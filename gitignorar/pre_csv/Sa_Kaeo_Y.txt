<p><b>Sa Kaeo</b> (<a href="/wiki/Thai_language" title="Thai language">Thai</a>: <span lang="th">สระแก้ว</span>, <small>pronounced </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Thai" title="Help:IPA/Thai">[sàʔ kɛ̂ːw]</a></span>) is a town (<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thesaban_mueang" title="Thesaban mueang">thesaban mueang</a></i>) in <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>, about 48 kilometers from the Thai-<a href="/wiki/Cambodia" title="Cambodia">Cambodian</a> border and 200 km east of Bangkok. It is the capital of <a href="/wiki/Sa_Kaeo_Province" title="Sa Kaeo Province">Sa Kaeo Province</a> and the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sa_Kaeo_District&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sa Kaeo District (page does not exist)">Sa Kaeo District</a>. As of 2005, it had a population of 16,591. The town covers parts of <i><a href="/wiki/Tambon" title="Tambon">tambons</a></i> Sa Kaeo and Tha Kasem in the Sa Kaeo District.
</p><p>In 1979-1980 Sa Kaeo was the site of the <a href="/wiki/Sa_Kaeo_Refugee_Camp" title="Sa Kaeo Refugee Camp">Sa Kaeo Refugee Camp</a>.
</p>
