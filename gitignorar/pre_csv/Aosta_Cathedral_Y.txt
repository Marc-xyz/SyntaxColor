<p><b>Aosta Cathedral</b> (<a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>: <i lang="it">Cattedrale di Aosta; Cattedrale di Santa Maria Assunta e San Giovanni Battista</i>; <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <i lang="fr">Cathédrale d'Aoste; Cathédrale Notre-Dame-de-l'Assomption et Saint-Jean-le-Baptiste</i>) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> <a href="/wiki/Cathedral" title="Cathedral">cathedral</a> in <a href="/wiki/Aosta" title="Aosta">Aosta</a>, in north-west <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, built in the 4th century. It is the episcopal seat of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diocese_of_Aosta" title="Diocese of Aosta">Diocese of Aosta</a>.
</p><p>In the 11th century the Palaeo-Christian structure was replaced by a new one, dedicated to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Assumption_of_the_Virgin_Mary" title="Assumption of the Virgin Mary">Assumption of the Virgin Mary</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_John_the_Baptist" title="Saint John the Baptist">Saint John the Baptist</a>. The architecture of the cathedral was modified during the 15th and 16th century.
</p><p>The present façade, in <a href="/wiki/Neoclassical_architecture" title="Neoclassical architecture">Neoclassical</a> style, was built between 1846 and 1848. The structures remaining from the <a href="/wiki/Romanesque_architecture" title="Romanesque architecture">Romanesque</a> period are two clock-towers and the crypt, and also the remaining part of an <a href="/wiki/Ottonian_art" title="Ottonian art">Ottonian</a> fresco cycle on the church ceiling.
</p>
