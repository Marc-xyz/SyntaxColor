	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Lake Tulane</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Lake Tulane</b> in Florida is one of numerous natural lakes that occupy <a href="/wiki/Sinkhole" title="Sinkhole">sinkholes</a>, which were formed by the dissolution of <a href="/wiki/Eocene" title="Eocene">Eocene</a> <a href="/wiki/Limestone" title="Limestone">limestone</a> at depth. Lake Wales Ridge, within which Tulane Lake lies, is a linear highland that consists of unconsolidated <a href="/wiki/Quartz" title="Quartz">quartz</a> <a href="/wiki/Sand" title="Sand">sands</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Pliocene" title="Pliocene">Pliocene</a> Cypresshead Formation. These sands rest upon impermeable <a href="/wiki/Miocene" title="Miocene">Miocene</a> sediments that overlie limestone, which was dissolved by groundwater and collapsed to form Tulane Lake. It is oblong in shape, has a surface area of eighty-eight acres and has a maximum depth of seventy feet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Beck(1986a)_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Beck(1986a)-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GrimmOthers(1993a)_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-GrimmOthers(1993a)-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-White(1958a)_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-White(1958a)-3">[3]</a></sup> It is surrounded by residential housing in <a href="/wiki/Avon_Park,_Florida" title="Avon Park, Florida">Avon Park, Florida</a>.
</p><p>The organic-rich sediments, which underlie the bottom of Lake Tulane, provide one of the few records of ancient climates within eastern North America that extends beyond the late glacial period (∼ 15,000 cal years BP).  Cores from the bottom of Lake Tulane have yielded a record of paleoclimates and paleovegetation, which date back to before 60,000 BP. The Pre-<a href="/wiki/Holocene" title="Holocene">Holocene</a> pollen record shows alternating periods of Pinus (<a href="/wiki/Pine" title="Pine">pine</a>)-dominated and Quercus-Ambrosia (<a href="/wiki/Oak" title="Oak">oak</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ragweed" title="Ragweed">ragweed</a>)-dominated vegetation. The periods of Pinus-dominated vegetation correlate with <a href="/wiki/Heinrich_event" title="Heinrich event">Heinrich events</a> H1 to H5. This correlation demonstrates a linkage between continental and oceanic climate events. The periods of Pinus-dominated vegetation are interpreted to represent periods of relatively high precipitation associated with relatively warm waters in the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a>. The periods of Quercus-Ambrosia-dominated vegetation are interpreted to represent period of arid climate associated with periods when the Gulf of Mexico and the North <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> were cooled by glacial meltwaters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GrimmOthers(1993a)_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-GrimmOthers(1993a)-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GrimmOthers(1996a)_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-GrimmOthers(1996a)-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Lake Tulane is used for swimming, boating and fishing.  There is a public boat ramp on its west side and a public swimming beach on its south side.  A person leaving a comment in the Fishing Works website rated the fishing at the lake as excellent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
