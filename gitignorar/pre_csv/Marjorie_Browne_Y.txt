<p><b>Marjorie Browne</b> (1910–1990) was a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> musical theatre actress who made occasional films.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Her <a href="/wiki/West_End_theatre" title="West End theatre">West End</a> appearances included the original productions of <a href="/wiki/Cole_Porter" title="Cole Porter">Cole Porter</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Wake_Up_and_Dream_(musical)" title="Wake Up and Dream (musical)">Wake Up and Dream</a></i> at the <a href="/wiki/London_Pavilion" title="London Pavilion">London Pavilion</a> in 1929; <a href="/wiki/Stanley_Lupino" title="Stanley Lupino">Stanley Lupino</a>'s  musical <i><a href="/wiki/Sporting_Love" title="Sporting Love">Sporting Love</a></i> at the <a href="/wiki/Gaiety_Theatre,_London" title="Gaiety Theatre, London">Gaiety</a> in 1934; and as Marjanah in the revival of <i><a href="/wiki/Chu_Chin_Chow" title="Chu Chin Chow">Chu Chin Chow</a></i> at the <a href="/wiki/Palace_Theatre,_London" title="Palace Theatre, London">Palace</a> in 1940.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
