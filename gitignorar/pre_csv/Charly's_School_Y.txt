</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Charly%27s_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Originally a kindergarten school called Jardín Infantil Jean Piaget, it began teaching primary education in 1988 under the initiative of Carlos Yáñez González and Melania Orellana Rojas, the owners of the <i>jardín infantil</i>, and renamed it to Colegio Jean Piaget. However, it was not recognized as a primary school by the Ministry of Education until 5 March 1990. It was then renamed to Colegio Particular Charly's School. In 1995, it was declared a state-subsidized private school by the Ministry of Education.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-historia_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-historia-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2010, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Embassy_in_Chile" title="U.S. Embassy in Chile">U.S. Embassy in Chile</a> donated computers and books to Charly's School.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_staff">Administrative staff</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Charly%27s_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administrative staff">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The administrative staff of Charly's School is composed by 23 teachers, five assistants and five others.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-historia_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-historia-3">[3]</a></sup> The principal of the school is Melania del Carmen Orellana Rojas, the president of the parents' center (<i>centro de padres</i>) is Dolly Rodríguez Pérez, and the president of the <a href="/wiki/Centro_de_alumnos" title="Centro de alumnos">students' center</a> (<i>centro de alumnos</i>) is Lina Manríquez Rojas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mineduc_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-mineduc-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
