<p>The United States Open Tennis Championships is a hardcourt tennis tournament held annually at <a href="/wiki/Flushing_Meadows%E2%80%93Corona_Park" title="Flushing Meadows–Corona Park">Flushing Meadows</a>, starting on the last Monday in August and lasting for two weeks. The tournament consists of five main championship events: men's and women's singles, men's and women's doubles, and mixed doubles, with additional tournaments for seniors, juniors, and wheelchair players.
</p><p>In 2007, the wheelchair men's doubles event was won by <a href="/wiki/Shingo_Kunieda" title="Shingo Kunieda">Shingo Kunieda</a> and <a href="/wiki/Satoshi_Saida" title="Satoshi Saida">Satoshi Saida</a>, both of Japan, who beat <a href="/wiki/Robin_Ammerlaan" title="Robin Ammerlaan">Robin Ammerlaan</a> of the Netherlands and <a href="/wiki/Micha%C3%ABl_Jeremiasz" title="Michaël Jeremiasz">Michaël Jeremiasz</a> of France, 6-3, 6-2 in the final.
</p>
