<p><b>Beatriz Jaguaribe</b> is a professor of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Comparative_communications&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Comparative communications (page does not exist)">comparative communications</a> in the School of Communications at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universidade_Federal_do_Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro">Universidade Federal do Rio de Janeiro</a> and the recipient of a <a href="/wiki/List_of_Guggenheim_Fellowships_awarded_in_2004" title="List of Guggenheim Fellowships awarded in 2004">Guggenheim Fellowship</a>. 
</p><p>Jaguaribe has written on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(classification_of_human_beings)" title="Race (classification of human beings)">race</a> and <a href="/wiki/Visual_culture" title="Visual culture">visual culture</a> in contemporary Brazil. Among her publications are <i>Fins de Século: Cidade e Cultura no Rio de Janeiro</i> (1998), published by Rocco, and <i>Mapa do Maravilhoso do Rio de Janeiro</i> (2001), published by Sextante Artes. Jaguaribe works on the relationship between artistic production and lived experiences in the production of cultural maps of urban Brazil. 
</p><p>In 2001 Jaguaribe was Visiting Chair in the Study of Brazilian Culture at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Notre_Dame" title="University of Notre Dame">University of Notre Dame</a>'s Kellogg School of International Studies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> She was awarded a <a href="/wiki/List_of_Guggenheim_Fellowships_awarded_in_2004" title="List of Guggenheim Fellowships awarded in 2004">Guggenheim Fellowship</a> in 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In the 2012-2013 academic year Jaguaribe was the Andrés Bello Chair In Latin American Cultures And Civilizations at <a href="/wiki/New_York_University" title="New York University">New York University</a>'s King Juan Carlos I of Spain Center.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> 
</p><p>Jaguaribe served as a contributing editor of <i><a href="/wiki/Public_Culture" title="Public Culture">Public Culture</a></i>, a scholarly journal published by <a href="/wiki/Duke_University_Press" title="Duke University Press">Duke University Press</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and she serves as a Corresponding Editor for the International Journal of Urban and Regional Research (IJURR).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
