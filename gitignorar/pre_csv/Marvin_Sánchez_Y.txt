<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">This article uses <a href="/wiki/Spanish_naming_customs" title="Spanish naming customs">Spanish naming customs</a>: the first or paternal <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">family name</a> is <i>Sánchez</i> and the second or maternal family name is <i>Vallecillo</i>.</div>
<p><b>Marvin Saúl Sánchez Vallecillo</b> (born 2 November 1986) is a Honduran <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfielder</a> who currently plays for <a href="/wiki/Platense_F.C." title="Platense F.C.">Platense</a> in the Primera Division of <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">Honduras</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marvin_S%C3%A1nchez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sánchez started his career at <a href="/wiki/Platense_F.C." title="Platense F.C.">Platense</a> before moving to <a href="/wiki/F.C._Motagua" title="F.C. Motagua">F.C. Motagua</a> for the 2009 Clausura.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He moved abroad to play for Salvadoran side <a href="/wiki/C.D._Municipal_Lime%C3%B1o" title="C.D. Municipal Limeño">Municipal Limeño</a> in January 2010<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and in summer 2010 he joined <a href="/wiki/C.D.S._Vida" title="C.D.S. Vida">Vida</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
In summer 2012, Sánchez reluctantly had to train with <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Choloma" title="Atlético Choloma">Atlético Choloma</a>, since they owned his registration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He later denied any problems with Choloma, though he was said to be close to signing for <a href="/wiki/Real_C.D._Espa%C3%B1a" title="Real C.D. España">Real España</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marvin_S%C3%A1nchez&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He was part of the <a href="/wiki/Honduras_national_football_team" title="Honduras national football team">Honduran</a> U-20 team at the 2005 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Youth_Championship" title="FIFA World Youth Championship">World Youth Championship</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>. Sánchez was also part of the U-23 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Honduran_national_football_team" title="Honduran national football team">Honduran national football team</a> who was coached by Colombian trainer <a href="/wiki/Alexis_Mendoza" title="Alexis Mendoza">Alexis Mendoza</a> but was then coached by <a href="/wiki/Gilberto_Yearwood" title="Gilberto Yearwood">Gilberto Yearwood</a>. The U-23 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Honduran_national_football_team" title="Honduran national football team">Honduran national football team</a> were CONCACAF champions and qualified to the <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">2008 Summer Olympics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He made his debut for the senior national side on 22 May 2008 in a friendly against <a href="/wiki/Belize_national_football_team" title="Belize national football team">Belize</a>.
</p>
