<p><b>Guttenberg</b> (<span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/ɡ/: 'g' in 'guy'">ɡ</span><span title="/ʌ/: 'u' in 'cut'">ʌ</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span><span title="/ən/: 'on' in 'button'">ən</span><span title="'b' in 'buy'">b</span><span title="/ɜːr/: 'ur' in 'fur'">ɜːr</span><span title="/ɡ/: 'g' in 'guy'">ɡ</span></span>/</a></span></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Town_(New_Jersey)" title="Town (New Jersey)">town</a> in the <a href="/wiki/North_Hudson,_New_Jersey" title="North Hudson, New Jersey">northern part</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hudson_County,_New_Jersey" title="Hudson County, New Jersey">Hudson County</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>, United States. As of the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 United States Census</a>, the town's population was 11,176.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census2010_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Census2010-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Districts2011_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Districts2011-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LWD2010_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-LWD2010-10">[10]</a></sup> Only four blocks wide, Guttenberg is the ninth-smallest municipality in the state<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GCTPH1NJ2010_13-2"><a href="#cite_note-GCTPH1NJ2010-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/List_of_United_States_cities_by_population_density" title="List of United States cities by population density">most densely populated incorporated municipality in the United States</a>, as well as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_the_most_densely_populated_cities" title="List of the most densely populated cities">one of the most densely populated municipalities worldwide</a>, with 57,116 people per square mile (22,052/km²) of land area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup>
</p><p>The population increased by 369 (+3.4%) from the 10,807 counted in the <a href="/wiki/2000_United_States_Census" title="2000 United States Census">2000 Census</a>, which had in turn increased by 2,539 (+30.7%) from the 8,268 counted in the <a href="/wiki/1990_United_States_Census" title="1990 United States Census">1990 Census</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> As of the 2010 Census, about one-fifth of the town's population resides in the <a href="/wiki/Galaxy_Towers" title="Galaxy Towers">Galaxy Towers</a>, a trio of residential skyscrapers overlooking the Hudson River.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup>
</p><p>The current population growth and <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">density</a> in Guttenberg represents a significant change since 1983, when it was described by <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i>, as "an old community of two-story row houses, small stores and light industry."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYTimes7.3.83_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYTimes7.3.83-26">[26]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guttenberg,_New_Jersey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Further information: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Bergen" title="North Bergen">North Bergen</a></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:10.7.09GalaxyByLuigiNovi1.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1600" data-file-width="1200" decoding="async" height="293" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/81/10.7.09GalaxyByLuigiNovi1.jpg/220px-10.7.09GalaxyByLuigiNovi1.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/81/10.7.09GalaxyByLuigiNovi1.jpg/330px-10.7.09GalaxyByLuigiNovi1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/81/10.7.09GalaxyByLuigiNovi1.jpg/440px-10.7.09GalaxyByLuigiNovi1.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:10.7.09GalaxyByLuigiNovi1.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A 2009 sign on <a href="/wiki/Boulevard_East" title="Boulevard East">Boulevard East</a> advertising the town's 150th anniversary. In the background is one of the three towers of the Galaxy apartments.</div></div></div>
<p>Guttenberg was formerly a farm owned by William Cooper, sold in 1853 to a group of New Yorkers, who had formed the Weehawken Land and Ferry Association.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Like nearby <a href="/wiki/Union_Hill,_New_Jersey" title="Union Hill, New Jersey">Union Hill</a>, it was subdivided and lots were sold mostly to Germans. The company ran two ferries, the <i>Hultz</i> and the <i>Flora</i>, which crossed the Hudson from the landings at the foot of <a href="/wiki/Bulls_Ferry" title="Bulls Ferry">Bulls Ferry Road</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shadyside,_Edgewater" title="Shadyside, Edgewater">Pleasant Valley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fort_Lee,_New_Jersey" title="Fort Lee, New Jersey">Fort Lee</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Spring_Street_(Manhattan)" title="Spring Street (Manhattan)">Spring Street</a> in <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>.
</p><p>Guttenberg was formed as a town on March 9, 1859, from portions of <a href="/wiki/North_Bergen,_New_Jersey" title="North Bergen, New Jersey">North Bergen Township</a>, but remained as a part of the township that was not fully independent. Guttenberg became part of <a href="/wiki/Union_Township,_Hudson_County,_New_Jersey" title="Union Township, Hudson County, New Jersey">Union Township</a> when it was formed on February 28, 1861, and became fully independent as of April 1, 1878.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Story_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-Story-28">[28]</a></sup> The municipality takes its name from <a href="/wiki/Johannes_Gutenberg" title="Johannes Gutenberg">Johannes Gutenberg</a>, the inventor of the <a href="/wiki/Printing_press" title="Printing press">printing press</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> though other sources indicate that the name derives from "good village" in <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Galaxy_Towers" title="Galaxy Towers">Galaxy Towers</a>, developed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prudential_Insurance" title="Prudential Insurance">Prudential Insurance</a> Company, were built in the late 1970s on <a href="/wiki/Boulevard_East" title="Boulevard East">Boulevard East</a>. The three octagonal skyscrapers rise 415 feet (126 m) and contain 1,075 apartments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guttenberg,_New_Jersey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Guttenberg is located atop the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hudson_Palisades" title="Hudson Palisades">Hudson Palisades</a>, south of <a href="/wiki/Woodcliff,_North_Bergen" title="Woodcliff, North Bergen">Woodcliff</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Racetrack_Section,_North_Bergen" title="Racetrack Section, North Bergen">Racetrack Section</a> in <a href="/wiki/North_Bergen,_New_Jersey" title="North Bergen, New Jersey">North Bergen</a> and north of <a href="/wiki/West_New_York,_New_Jersey" title="West New York, New Jersey">West New York</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Its western border is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kennedy_Boulevard_(Hudson_County)" title="Kennedy Boulevard (Hudson County)">Kennedy Boulevard</a>. <a href="/wiki/Bergenline_Avenue" title="Bergenline Avenue">Bergenline Avenue</a>, the commercial corridor of <a href="/wiki/North_Hudson,_New_Jersey" title="North Hudson, New Jersey">North Hudson</a>, runs north and south through the town, and is the heart of <i>"<a href="/wiki/Havana_on_the_Hudson" title="Havana on the Hudson">Havana on the Hudson</a>"</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Its eastern border is the <a href="/wiki/North_River_(Hudson_River)" title="North River (Hudson River)">Hudson River</a>, opposite <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Upper_West_Side" title="Upper West Side">Upper West Side</a>.
</p><p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the town had a total area of 0.243 square miles (0.628 km<sup>2</sup>), including 0.196 square miles (0.507 km<sup>2</sup>) of land and 0.047 square miles (0.121 km<sup>2</sup>) of water (19.24%).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CensusArea_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-CensusArea-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR1_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-GR1-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>The town is ten blocks long and four blocks wide. It takes less than a minute to drive through.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYTimes7.3.83_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYTimes7.3.83-26">[26]</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_Census">2010 Census</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guttenberg,_New_Jersey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2010 Census">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 United States Census</a>, there were 11,176 people, 4,473 households, and 2,683.800 families residing in the town. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 57,116.0 per square mile (22,052.6/km<sup>2</sup>). There were 4,839 housing units at an average density of 24,730.2 per square mile (9,548.4/km<sup>2</sup>). The racial makeup of the town was 67.44% (7,537) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 4.80% (537) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_(U.S._Census)" title="Black (U.S. Census)">Black or African American</a>, 0.91% (102) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 7.32% (818) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 0.04% (4) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander_(U.S._Census)" title="Pacific Islander (U.S. Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 14.25% (1,593) from <a href="/wiki/Race_and_ethnicity_in_the_United_States_Census#Race" title="Race and ethnicity in the United States Census">other races</a>, and 5.23% (585) from two or more races. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic or Latino</a> of any race were 64.83% (7,245) of the population.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census2010_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-Census2010-8">[8]</a></sup> 
</p><p>There were 4,473 households out of which 27.5% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 37.6% were married couples living together, 15.4% had a female householder with no husband present, and 40.0% were non-families. 32.5% of all households were made up of individuals, and 9.5% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.48 and the average family size was 3.12.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census2010_8-4"><a href="#cite_note-Census2010-8">[8]</a></sup> 
</p><p>In the town, the population was spread out with 20.5% under the age of 18, 8.6% from 18 to 24, 34.7% from 25 to 44, 24.9% from 45 to 64, and 11.3% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 36.4 years. For every 100 females there were 95.4 males.  For every 100 females ages 18 and older there were 93.0 males.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census2010_8-5"><a href="#cite_note-Census2010-8">[8]</a></sup>  
</p><p>The Census Bureau's 2006–2010 <a href="/wiki/American_Community_Survey" title="American Community Survey">American Community Survey</a> showed that (in 2010 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inflation_adjustment" title="Inflation adjustment">inflation-adjusted</a> dollars) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Median_household_income" title="Median household income">median household income</a> was $49,981 (with a margin of error of +/− $8,219) and the median family income was $53,945 (+/− $9,457). Males had a median income of $50,227 (+/− $8,459) versus $32,089 (+/− $6,483) for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the borough was $33,239 (+/− $8,416). About 14.8% of families and 16.0% of the population were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 28.7% of those under age 18 and 11.8% of those age 65 or over.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000_Census">2000 Census</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guttenberg,_New_Jersey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2000 Census">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/2000_United_States_Census" title="2000 United States Census">2000 United States Census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-17">[17]</a></sup> there were 10,807 people, 4,493 households, and 2,619 families residing in the town. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 56,012.0 inhabitants per square mile (21,961.1/km<sup>2</sup>), making it the most densely populated municipality in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_United_States" title="The United States">The United States</a>, with over twice the density of New York City.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> There were 4,650 housing units at an average density of 24,100.7 per square mile (9,449.3/km<sup>2</sup>). The racial makeup of the town was 64.98% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 3.81% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 0.38% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 7.30% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 0.01% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander_(U.S._Census)" title="Pacific Islander (U.S. Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 16.42% from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 7.10% from two or more races. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 54.33% of the population.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census2000_43-1"><a href="#cite_note-Census2000-43">[43]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census2000SF1_44-1"><a href="#cite_note-Census2000SF1-44">[44]</a></sup>
</p><p>There were 4,493 households out of which 27.8% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 40.3% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 13.0% had a female householder with no husband present, and 41.7% were non-families. 35.1% of all households were made up of individuals and 10.0% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.38 and the average family size was 3.13.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census2000_43-2"><a href="#cite_note-Census2000-43">[43]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census2000SF1_44-2"><a href="#cite_note-Census2000SF1-44">[44]</a></sup>
</p><p>In the town, the population was spread out with 21.2% under the age of 18, 8.7% from 18 to 24, 36.9% from 25 to 44, 21.4% from 45 to 64, and 11.8% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 36 years. For every 100 females, there were 92.9 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 90.6 males.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census2000_43-3"><a href="#cite_note-Census2000-43">[43]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census2000SF1_44-3"><a href="#cite_note-Census2000SF1-44">[44]</a></sup>
</p><p>The median income for a household in the town was $44,515, and the median income for a family was $47,440. Males had a median income of $38,628 versus $33,154 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the town was $27,931. About 11.1% of families and 13.0% of the population were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 18.7% of those under age 18 and 10.9% of those age 65 or over.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census2000_43-4"><a href="#cite_note-Census2000-43">[43]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census2000SF1_44-4"><a href="#cite_note-Census2000SF1-44">[44]</a></sup>
</p><p>The town is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bedroom_community" title="Bedroom community">bedroom community</a> with about 36% of its employed residents working in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, including 1,648 of the 4,993 Guttenberg residents in the workforce who commute to employment in <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a> as of the 2000 Census.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Local_government">Local government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guttenberg,_New_Jersey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Local government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Guttenberg operates under the <a href="/wiki/Town_(New_Jersey)" title="Town (New Jersey)">Town</a> form of New Jersey municipal government, and is governed by a mayor and a five-member council. Town council members are elected <a href="/wiki/At-large" title="At-large">at-large</a> to four-year terms on a staggered basis in partisan elections, with two seats up for election in one year, three seats together with the mayoral seat up for election in the following year, followed by two years with no elections.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DataBook_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-DataBook-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>As of 2019<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Guttenberg,_New_Jersey&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the <a href="/wiki/Mayor" title="Mayor">Mayor</a> of Guttenberg is <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">Democrat</a> Wayne D. Zitt Jr., whose term of office ends December 31, 2021. Members of the Guttenberg Town Council are Richard Delafuente (D, 2021), Monica Fundora (D, 2021) and John D. Habermann (D, 2021), William Hokien (D, 2022) and Juana Malave (D, 2022).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Officials_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Officials-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HudsonDirectory_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-HudsonDirectory-53">[53]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hudson2018_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hudson2018-54">[54]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hudson2017_55-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hudson2017-55">[55]</a></sup>
</p><p>In December 2015, Wayne Zitt was selected from three candidates nominated by the Democratic municipal committee to fill the vacant seat expiring in December 2017 of Efrain Velez, who resigned earlier that month.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_scandals">Political scandals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guttenberg,_New_Jersey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Political scandals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Recent scandals include the convictions of the mayor, David Delle Donna, and his wife, a member of the town planning board, on federal extortion and mail fraud charges, for which they were each sentenced to serve four years in federal prison;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> a former mayor, Peter LaVilla, who pleaded guilty in 2003 to misappropriating campaign funds and using the money for a private brokerage account; a councilman who resigned after being accused of receiving illegal advances on his salary; and a chief financial officer, who pleaded guilty in 2002 to misappropriation of funds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_safety">Public safety</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guttenberg,_New_Jersey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Public safety">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Guttenberg is served by <a href="/wiki/North_Hudson_Regional_Fire_and_Rescue" title="North Hudson Regional Fire and Rescue">North Hudson Regional Fire and Rescue</a>, a regional fire department established in 1999 serving communities in northern Hudson County, that also serves North Bergen, Union City, Weehawken and West New York, replacing the five independent departments that had served each of the communities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guttenberg,_New_Jersey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of March 23, 2011, there were a total of 4,991 registered voters in Guttenberg, of which 2,997 (60.0%) were registered as <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">Democrats</a>, 376 (7.5%) were registered as <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republicans</a>, 4 (0.08%) were registered to other parties, and 1,614 (32.3%) were registered as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unaffiliated_(New_Jersey)" title="Unaffiliated (New Jersey)">Unaffiliated</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-97"><a href="#cite_note-97">[97]</a></sup>
</p><p>In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_presidential_election_in_New_Jersey,_2012" title="United States presidential election in New Jersey, 2012">2012 presidential election</a>, Democrat <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a> received 77.4% of the vote (2,507 cast), ahead of Republican <a href="/wiki/Mitt_Romney" title="Mitt Romney">Mitt Romney</a> with 21.4% (692 votes), and other candidates with 1.3% (41 votes), among the 3,266 ballots cast by the town's 5,488 registered voters (26 ballots were <a href="/wiki/Spoilt_vote" title="Spoilt vote">spoiled</a>), for a turnout of 59.5%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2012Elections_98-0"><a href="#cite_note-2012Elections-98">[98]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2012VoterReg_99-0"><a href="#cite_note-2012VoterReg-99">[99]</a></sup> In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_presidential_election_in_New_Jersey,_2008" title="United States presidential election in New Jersey, 2008">2008 presidential election</a>, Democrat Barack Obama received 71.6% of the vote (2,524 cast), ahead of Republican <a href="/wiki/John_McCain" title="John McCain">John McCain</a> with 26.3% (928 votes) and other candidates with 1.3% (45 votes), among the 3,526 ballots cast by the town's 5,878 registered voters, for a turnout of 60.0%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-100"><a href="#cite_note-100">[100]</a></sup> In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_presidential_election_in_New_Jersey,_2004" title="United States presidential election in New Jersey, 2004">2004 presidential election</a>, Democrat <a href="/wiki/John_Kerry" title="John Kerry">John Kerry</a> received 67.0% of the vote (2,172 ballots cast), outpolling Republican <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">George W. Bush</a> with 31.9% (1,035 votes) and other candidates with 0.4% (19 votes), among the 3,241 ballots cast by the town's 5,122 registered voters, for a turnout percentage of 63.3.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-101"><a href="#cite_note-101">[101]</a></sup>
</p><p>In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Jersey_gubernatorial_election,_2013" title="New Jersey gubernatorial election, 2013">2013 gubernatorial election</a>, Democrat <a href="/wiki/Barbara_Buono" title="Barbara Buono">Barbara Buono</a> received 52.5% of the vote (740 cast), ahead of Republican <a href="/wiki/Chris_Christie" title="Chris Christie">Chris Christie</a> with 45.7% (645 votes), and other candidates with 1.8% (25 votes), among the 1,478 ballots cast by the town's 5,545 registered voters (68 ballots were spoiled), for a turnout of 26.7%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2013Elections_102-0"><a href="#cite_note-2013Elections-102">[102]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2013VoterReg_103-0"><a href="#cite_note-2013VoterReg-103">[103]</a></sup> In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Jersey_gubernatorial_election,_2009" title="New Jersey gubernatorial election, 2009">2009 gubernatorial election</a>, Democrat <a href="/wiki/Jon_Corzine" title="Jon Corzine">Jon Corzine</a> received 70.9% of the vote (1,341 ballots cast), ahead of Republican Chris Christie with 23.6% (447 votes), Independent <a href="/wiki/Chris_Daggett" title="Chris Daggett">Chris Daggett</a> with 2.4% (46 votes) and other candidates with 0.6% (12 votes), among the 1,892 ballots cast by the town's 4,983 registered voters, yielding a 38.0% turnout.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-104"><a href="#cite_note-104">[104]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guttenberg,_New_Jersey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>For <a href="/wiki/Pre-kindergarten" title="Pre-kindergarten">pre-kindergarten</a> through <a href="/wiki/Eighth_grade" title="Eighth grade">eighth grade</a>, public school students from Guttenberg attend the Anna L. Klein School as part of the <a href="/wiki/Guttenberg_Public_School_District" title="Guttenberg Public School District">Guttenberg Public School District</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-105"><a href="#cite_note-105">[105]</a></sup> The principal of Anna L. Klein School is Keith Petry. As of the 2017-18 school year, the district and its one school had an enrollment of 996 students<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCES_106-0"><a href="#cite_note-NCES-106">[106]</a></sup>
</p><p>For <a href="/wiki/Ninth_grade" title="Ninth grade">ninth</a> through <a href="/wiki/Twelfth_grade" title="Twelfth grade">twelfth grades</a>, public school students attend <a href="/wiki/North_Bergen_High_School" title="North Bergen High School">North Bergen High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/North_Bergen,_New_Jersey" title="North Bergen, New Jersey">North Bergen</a>, as part of a <a href="/wiki/Sending/receiving_relationship" title="Sending/receiving relationship">sending/receiving relationship</a> with the <a href="/wiki/North_Bergen_School_District" title="North Bergen School District">North Bergen School District</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-107"><a href="#cite_note-107">[107]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-108"><a href="#cite_note-108">[108]</a></sup> As of the 2017-18 school year, the high school had an enrollment of 2,412 students and 160.2 classroom teachers (on an <a href="/wiki/Full-time_equivalent" title="Full-time equivalent">FTE</a> basis), for a <a href="/wiki/Student%E2%80%93teacher_ratio" title="Student–teacher ratio">student–teacher ratio</a> of 15.1:1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-109"><a href="#cite_note-109">[109]</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Roads_and_highways">Roads and highways</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guttenberg,_New_Jersey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Roads and highways">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>As of May 2010<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Guttenberg,_New_Jersey&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the town had a total of 4.39 miles (7.07 km) of roadways, of which 3.95 miles (6.36 km) were maintained by the municipality and 0.44 miles (0.71 km) by Hudson County.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-110"><a href="#cite_note-110">[110]</a></sup>
</p><p>The only significant roads directly serving Guttenberg are county highways. <a href="/wiki/County_Route_501_(New_Jersey)" title="County Route 501 (New Jersey)">County Route 501</a> follows Kennedy Boulevard along the town's western border.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-111"><a href="#cite_note-111">[111]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/County_Route_505_(New_Jersey)" title="County Route 505 (New Jersey)">County Route 505</a> follows River Road on the east side of town.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-112"><a href="#cite_note-112">[112]</a></sup> Both roads are oriented north-south. <a href="/wiki/Boulevard_East" title="Boulevard East">Boulevard East</a> passes through the town atop the Palisades.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Interstate_95_in_New_Jersey" title="Interstate 95 in New Jersey">Interstate 95</a> (the <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey_Turnpike" title="New Jersey Turnpike">New Jersey Turnpike</a>), <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_1/9" title="U.S. Route 1/9">U.S. Route 1/9</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey_Route_495" title="New Jersey Route 495">New Jersey Route 495</a> are major highways located in adjacent <a href="/wiki/North_Bergen,_New_Jersey" title="North Bergen, New Jersey">North Bergen</a>.
</p>
