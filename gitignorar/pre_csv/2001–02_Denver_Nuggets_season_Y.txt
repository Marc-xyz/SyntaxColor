<p>The <b><a href="/wiki/2001%E2%80%9302_NBA_season" title="2001–02 NBA season">2001–02 NBA season</a></b> was the Nuggets' 26th season in the <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_Association" title="National Basketball Association">National Basketball Association</a>, and 35th season as a franchise.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> During the offseason, the Nuggets signed free agents <a href="/wiki/Isaiah_Rider" title="Isaiah Rider">Isaiah Rider</a> and <a href="/wiki/Avery_Johnson" title="Avery Johnson">Avery Johnson</a>. However, Rider would play in just ten games before being waived on November 20. After finishing just two games below .500 the previous season, the Nuggets suffered another disastrous setback as <a href="/wiki/Antonio_McDyess" title="Antonio McDyess">Antonio McDyess</a> suffered a preseason knee injury, which limited him to just ten games. Things would only get worse in December as head coach <a href="/wiki/Dan_Issel" title="Dan Issel">Dan Issel</a> got into hot water after yelling a racial slur at a Mexican fan. Issel was suspended for four games and was forced to resign, being replaced by his assistant <a href="/wiki/Mike_Evans_(basketball)" title="Mike Evans (basketball)">Mike Evans</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> At midseason, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Van_Exel" title="Nick Van Exel">Nick Van Exel</a> was traded along with Johnson, <a href="/wiki/Raef_LaFrentz" title="Raef LaFrentz">Raef LaFrentz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tariq_Abdul-Wahad" title="Tariq Abdul-Wahad">Tariq Abdul-Wahad</a> to the <a href="/wiki/2001%E2%80%9302_Dallas_Mavericks_season" title="2001–02 Dallas Mavericks season">Dallas Mavericks</a> for <a href="/wiki/Juwan_Howard" title="Juwan Howard">Juwan Howard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tim_Hardaway" title="Tim Hardaway">Tim Hardaway</a>. The Nuggets finished sixth in the Midwest Division with a 27–55 record.
</p><p>Following the season, McDyess was traded to the <a href="/wiki/2002%E2%80%9303_New_York_Knicks_season" title="2002–03 New York Knicks season">New York Knicks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Voshon_Lenard" title="Voshon Lenard">Voshon Lenard</a> signed as a free agent with the <a href="/wiki/2002%E2%80%9303_Toronto_Raptors_season" title="2002–03 Toronto Raptors season">Toronto Raptors</a>, <a href="/wiki/Calbert_Cheaney" title="Calbert Cheaney">Calbert Cheaney</a> signed with the <a href="/wiki/2002%E2%80%9303_Utah_Jazz_season" title="2002–03 Utah Jazz season">Utah Jazz</a>, Hardaway and <a href="/wiki/George_McCloud" title="George McCloud">George McCloud</a> were both released, and Evans was fired as coach.
</p>
