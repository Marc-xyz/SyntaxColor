<p><b>Dr. Saskia Estupiñán</b>, a native of <a href="/wiki/Quito" title="Quito">Quito</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuador</a>, is a leading figure in international <a href="/wiki/Public_health" title="Public health">public health</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oral_health" title="Oral health">oral health</a> research. Dr. Estupinan has worked extensively with international organizations and has advised agencies including <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kellogg_Foundation" title="Kellogg Foundation">Kellogg Foundation</a> and the <a href="/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">World Bank</a>.
</p><p>A dentist and <a href="/wiki/Public_health" title="Public health">public health</a> specialist who attended the Universidad Central in <a href="/wiki/Quito" title="Quito">Quito</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UCLA" title="UCLA">UCLA</a>, Dr. Estupinan is best known for work with the <a href="/wiki/World_Health_Organization" title="World Health Organization">World Health Organization</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Pan_American_Health_Organization" title="Pan American Health Organization">Pan American Health Organization</a> promoting the expansion of <a href="/wiki/Salt" title="Salt">salt</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fluoridation" title="Fluoridation">fluoridation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Saskia Estupiñán is a track and field Master's athlete, ranked 9th worldwide in the 80 meter hurdles among women in her age group.
</p>
