<b>Mary Dorothy Rose Leys</b> (8 October 1890<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-register_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-register-1">[1]</a></sup> – 6 September 1967) was a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> historian and academic, who was involved in the work of the Catholic Social Guild and the Catholic Record Society.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Leys was born in <a href="/wiki/Tylers_Green" title="Tylers Green">Tylers Green</a>, <a href="/wiki/Buckinghamshire" title="Buckinghamshire">Buckinghamshire</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Her obituary in <i>The Times</i> states that she was educated at home because her family were too poor to afford school fees.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Her Scottish father, John Kirkwood Leys, was a novelist and died in 1909.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1911, she was awarded a scholarship to <a href="/wiki/Somerville_College,_Oxford" title="Somerville College, Oxford">Somerville College, Oxford</a>. She taught History at <a href="/wiki/St_Anne%27s_College,_Oxford" title="St Anne's College, Oxford">St Anne's College, Oxford</a> from 1919 until her retirement in 1955.
</p>
