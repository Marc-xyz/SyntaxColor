<p><b>The Trilobites</b> were an Australian <a href="/wiki/Power_pop" title="Power pop">power pop</a>/rock band formed in Sydney in June 1984.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-McF_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-McF-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ARDb_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ARDb-2">[2]</a></sup> The original members of the Trilobites were Mike Dalton on vocals, Scott Leighton on bass guitar, Martin Martini &amp; Paul Skates on guitars and Paul Styman on drums.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-McF_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-McF-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ARDb_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-ARDb-2">[2]</a></sup> Their first two singles, "Venus in Leather"' and "American TV" reached number 1 on the alternative chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-McF_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-McF-1">[1]</a></sup> They released three albums, <i>Turn it Around</i> in 1987, <i>Savage Mood Swing</i> in 1990 and <i>The Lost Generation</i> in 1992.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-McF_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-McF-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ARDb_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-ARDb-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Trilobites&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Vocalist Mike Dalton and drummer Paul Styman played together in a high school band, The Pyschotics, in the late 1970s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NKVD_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-NKVD-3">[3]</a></sup> Bass guitarist Scott Leighton celebrated his 21st birthday in 1984 by showcasing his new band, The United Underworld.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NKVD_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-NKVD-3">[3]</a></sup> Included in The United Underworld were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Martin_Martini" title="Martin Martini">Martin Martini</a> on rhythm guitar, Paul Skates on lead guitar and Paul Styman on drums and Steven Stradbrook on Vocals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ARDb_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-ARDb-2">[2]</a></sup> Shortly after this performance Dalton was asked to join as vocalist. All members had attended the same high school together, Sydney Technical High School, albeit in different grades.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NKVD_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-NKVD-3">[3]</a></sup> They soon changed their name to The Trilobites and began gigging in Sydney's plethora of suburban and inner city pubs and clubs. They debuted on 20 June 1984, at the Vulcan Hotel in Ultimo (incidentally same as the debut of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Hard_Ons" title="The Hard Ons">The Hard Ons</a>). Their first release was a live cassette only album, 'Let's Pump' self-released on Atomic Records. Later, The Citadel label issued the band's first two singles : "Venus in Leather" (1985) and "American T.V." (1986). Both singles reached No. 1 on the alternative charts,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-McF_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-McF-1">[1]</a></sup> and the band toured to a strong response . Both songs appeared on the Citadel's compilation album <i>Take Everything, Leave Nothing</i> (March 1988). In the meantime, the Trilobites had signed a new deal with the <a href="/wiki/Big_Time_Records" title="Big Time Records">Big Time</a> label which resulted in the single, "Night of the Many Deaths<i> (May 1987).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-McF_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-McF-1">[1]</a></sup></i>
</p><p>On 24 October 1987 The Trilobites decided to record their show at The Caringbah Inn and turn it into a live LP, <i>Turn It Around</i>.  It was released on the Waterfront label and only as a vinyl release. Waterfront also put out the Trilobites fourth single, "Jenny's Wake" (June 1988).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-McF_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-McF-1">[1]</a></sup> In 1988 the Trilobites came to the attention of the fledgling <a href="/wiki/RooArt" title="RooArt">rooArt</a> label.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-McF_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-McF-1">[1]</a></sup> The band contributed "All Hail the New Right" to rooArt inaugural <i>Youngblood</i> compilation album. That led to a full recording contract which resulted in the release of their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extended_Play" title="Extended Play">EP</a>, <i>I Can't Wait for the Summer to End</i> which gained strong success on the American college radio circuit. During March 1989, The Trilobites' debut studio album, <i>Savage Mood Swing</i> was produced by Englishman, Steve James (<a href="/wiki/Toyah_(band)" title="Toyah (band)">Toyah</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Lambrettas" title="The Lambrettas">The Lambrettas</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Otway" title="John Otway">John Otway</a>) and released in March 1990.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-McF_1-8"><a href="#cite_note-McF-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ARDb_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-ARDb-2">[2]</a></sup> Between recording the album and its release, the Trilobites also undertook a successful European tour (late 1989).  The album was also released in the United States on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polygram" title="Polygram">Polygram</a> label, with a promised tour of the States. In 1991, following rooART's last minute decision to pull the band's U.S. tour, Dalton left the band to take employment at MTV Australia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and was replaced by Gary Slater (<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Voodoo_Lust&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Voodoo Lust (page does not exist)">Voodoo Lust</a>).  Paul Styman left at this point to be replaced by Glen Abbott.
</p><p>This version of the band then released a single, "Tears You Apart/Don't Hide", and a six track <a href="/wiki/Mini-LP" title="Mini-LP">mini-LP</a> on vinyl.  These songs formed the basis of their second album, <i>The Lost Generation</i>, which was produced by <a href="/wiki/Rob_Younger" title="Rob Younger">Rob Younger</a> (Radio Birdman, <a href="/wiki/New_Christs" title="New Christs">New Christs</a>). It was released in February 1992 by Citadel<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-McF_1-9"><a href="#cite_note-McF-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ARDb_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-ARDb-2">[2]</a></sup> and became the band's final release. The original line-up re-formed for a series of Sydney shows in 1996.
</p>
<ul><li>Gary Slater returned to Brisbane and performed in bands including The Pot Monsters and Grumpy, later rejoining <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Voodoo_Lust&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Voodoo Lust (page does not exist)">Voodoo Lust</a>.</li>
<li>Glen Abbott joined <a href="/wiki/Machine_Gun_Fellatio" title="Machine Gun Fellatio">Machine Gun Fellatio</a>.</li>
<li>Martin Martini joined <a href="/wiki/Spurs_For_Jesus" title="Spurs For Jesus">Spurs For Jesus</a> &amp; The Upsets</li>
<li>Michael Dalton has been a producer and reporter at Channel 9 since 1993 and has worked on numerous shows including the NRL 'Footy Show', 'Good Morning Australia' and 'A Current Affair'</li></ul>
