<p><b>Graeme McGeagh</b> is a Scottish screenwriter and actor. He is possibly best known for co-writing and playing the leading role of Tom Collins in the film <i>A Practical Guide to a Spectacular Suicide</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Graeme_McGeagh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in Kirkintilloch in Scotland, McGeagh formed a close film partnership with childhood friends <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hughes_(director)" title="Graham Hughes (director)">Graham Hughes</a> and Keith Grantham. In 2011, the trio writing their first film <i>The Big Slick</i>. The feature-length comedy film, which also starred the trio in acting roles, tells the story of six young guys whose efforts to spend a quiet night in, quickly spiral out of control.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The film was an overwhelming success and earned McGeagh, Hughes and Grantham a nomination for best achievement in writing at the <a href="/wiki/2011_British_Academy_Scotland_New_Talent_Awards" title="2011 British Academy Scotland New Talent Awards">2011 British Academy Scotland New Talent Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The trio were triumphant and took home the accolade at the ceremony which was held at the <a href="/wiki/Glasgow_Film_Theatre" title="Glasgow Film Theatre">Glasgow Film Theatre</a> on 11 March 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the success of <i>The Big Slick</i>, McGeagh reunited with Hughes and Grantham in 2013 to write their next feature film. Work began later that year on <i>A Practical Guide to a Spectacular Suicide</i>, a story about a man named Tom Collins whose string of suicide attempts leads him to plan what he dubs will be his own 'spectacular suicide'. With the film written, McGeagh was approached to play the lead role which he accepted. Director <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hughes_(director)" title="Graham Hughes (director)">Graham Hughes</a>, later said 'I think he's a talent—and was the right guy for the part.'<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>The film was later released in 2014 and was well received both at home and abroad. Its first festival appearance was at the Cinequest Film Festival in California,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and in the UK the film premiered at the <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh_International_Film_Festival" title="Edinburgh International Film Festival">Edinburgh International Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> <i>A Practical Guide to a Spectacular Suicide</i> earned McGeagh a 2nd Bafta nomination at the <a href="/wiki/2014_British_Academy_Scotland_New_Talent_Awards" title="2014 British Academy Scotland New Talent Awards">2014 British Academy Scotland New Talent Awards</a> for Best Acting Performance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>
