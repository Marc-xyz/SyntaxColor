	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Romanid</h1>

<p><b>Romanid</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Zonal_constructed_language" title="Zonal constructed language">zonal constructed language</a> for speakers of <a href="/wiki/Romance_languages" title="Romance languages">Romance languages</a>, intended to be understandable by speakers of the Romance languages without prior study. It was created by the <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungarian</a> language teacher <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Zolt%C3%A1n_Magyar_(linguist)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Zoltán Magyar (linguist) (page does not exist)">Zoltán Magyar</a>, who published a first version in May 1956 and a second in December 1957. In 1984, he published a phrasebook with a short grammar, in which he presents a slightly more simplified version of the language.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The language is based on the most common word senses in <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It is said to enjoy some popularity among Hungarians.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> According to the Russian newspaper <a href="/wiki/Trud_(Russian_newspaper)" title="Trud (Russian newspaper)">Trud</a>, Romanid, from a structural point of view, is "considerably simpler and easier to learn" than Esperanto.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Example">Example</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Romanid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Example">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>(1957 version)</i> Moy lingva project nominad Romanid fu publicad ja in may de pasad ano cam scientific studium in hungar lingva...<br/>
<i>(1984 version)</i> Mi lingua project nominat Romanid esed publicat ja in may de pasat an cam scientific studio in hungar lingua...<br/>
<i>(translation)</i> My language project called Romanid was published already in May of last year as a scientific study in Hungarian...
</p>
