<p>The <b>3rd Canadian Infantry Brigade</b> was a formation of the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Army" title="Canadian Army">Canadian Army</a> in both <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> and <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>.  The brigade fought on the Western Front during the First World War, and in Sicily and Italy during the Second World War.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=3rd_Canadian_Infantry_Brigade&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="World_War_I">World War I</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=3rd_Canadian_Infantry_Brigade&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: World War I">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>First formed on 29 August 1914, the brigade was initially made up from provisional battalions that were named after their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Canada" title="Provinces of Canada">province</a> of origin, but these titles were replaced with numerals before the brigade arrived in <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">Britain</a> on 14 October 1914.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> On arrival in Britain, it consisted of four infantry battalions, numbered 13th, 14th, 15th, and 16th.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nicholson22_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nicholson22-1">[1]</a></sup> As part of the <a href="/wiki/1st_Canadian_Division" title="1st Canadian Division">1st Canadian Division</a>, the brigade fought in every major Canadian engagement on the Western Front between 1915 and 1918.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Its first commander was Colonel <a href="/wiki/Richard_Ernest_William_Turner" title="Richard Ernest William Turner">Richard Turner</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nicholson22_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Nicholson22-1">[1]</a></sup> From March 1916 until the end of the war, the brigade was commanded by Brigadier General <a href="/wiki/George_Tuxford" title="George Tuxford">George Tuxford</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>After training on Salisbury Plain in late 1914, the brigade moved as part of the 1st Canadian Division to the Western Front.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> During its deployment on the Western Front, the 3rd Brigade fought in the following battles and engagements on the Western Front:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-longlongtrail_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-longlongtrail-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p><b>1915:</b>
</p>

<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Vimy_Ridge" title="Battle of Vimy Ridge">Battle of Vimy Ridge</a>: April 9–14</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Arleux" title="Battle of Arleux">Battle of Arleux</a>: April 28–29</li>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="World_War_II">World War II</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=3rd_Canadian_Infantry_Brigade&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: World War II">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> the brigade, again as part of the 1st Canadian Division, participated in the Allied <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Invasion_of_Sicily" title="Invasion of Sicily">Invasion of Sicily</a> and then later fought in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_Campaign_(World_War_II)" title="Italian Campaign (World War II)">Italian campaign</a>.  It was demobilized for the second time at the end of the war.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-canadiansoldiers_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-canadiansoldiers-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
