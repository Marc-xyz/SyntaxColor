	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en"><i>Blanco y Negro</i> (magazine)</h1>

<p><i><b>Blanco y Negro</b></i> (meaning <i>Black and White</i> in English)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RosHazbun2014_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-RosHazbun2014-1">[1]</a></sup> was a Spanish-language weekly art and literary magazine and later, the companion of the daily <i><a href="/wiki/ABC_(newspaper)" title="ABC (newspaper)">ABC</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Crow2005_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Crow2005-2">[2]</a></sup> The magazine was published in <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_profile">History and profile</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blanco_y_Negro_(magazine)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History and profile">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Blanco y Negro</i> was established in 1891.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RosHazbun2014_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-RosHazbun2014-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rg_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-rg-3">[3]</a></sup> The title of the magazine was a reference to the contrasts in life such as laughter and tears and the sad and happy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Charnon-Deutsch2010_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Charnon-Deutsch2010-4">[4]</a></sup> Its founder was Torcuato Luca de Tena.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rg_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-rg-3">[3]</a></sup> The magazine was controlled by the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic Church</a> through Editorial Catolica which also published it on a weekly basis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RosHazbun2014_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-RosHazbun2014-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The headquarters of the weekly was in Madrid.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rg_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-rg-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Blanco y Negro</i> employed color print, paper couché and advanced image printing techniques such as <a href="/wiki/Photoengraving" title="Photoengraving">photoengraving</a> and <a href="/wiki/Photogravure" title="Photogravure">photogravure</a> for the first time in Spain. In addition, it published the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Color_photo" title="Color photo">color photo</a> in the country on 15 May 1912.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rg_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-rg-3">[3]</a></sup> The magazine covered the articles of various Spanish writers and caricaturists, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cecilio_Pla" title="Cecilio Pla">Cecilio Pla</a>, Ramon Cilla among the others.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Charnon-Deutsch2010_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Charnon-Deutsch2010-4">[4]</a></sup> The weekly also published articles by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hilda_de_Toledano" title="Hilda de Toledano">Hilda de Toledano</a> (literary pseudonym of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maria_Pia_of_Saxe-Coburg_and_Gotha_Braganza" title="Maria Pia of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha Braganza">Maria Pia of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha Braganza</a>), a writer and famous pretender to the throne of <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>.
</p><p>In 1988 <i>Blanco y Negro</i> became a Sunday supplement to the daily newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/ABC_(newspaper)" title="ABC (newspaper)">ABC</a></i>. In 2005, it was renamed <i>ABCD Las Artes y Las Letras</i> and continues as a weekly supplement.
</p>
