<p>The <i><b>Taymyr</b></i> was an <a href="/wiki/Icebreaker" title="Icebreaker">icebreaking steamer</a> of 1200 tons built for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_Imperial_Navy" title="Russian Imperial Navy">Russian Imperial Navy</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Petersburg" title="St. Petersburg">St. Petersburg</a> in 1909. It was named after the <a href="/wiki/Taymyr_Peninsula" title="Taymyr Peninsula">Taymyr Peninsula</a>.
</p><p><i>Taymyr</i> and her sister ship <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Icebreaker_Vaygach" title="Icebreaker Vaygach">Vaygach</a></i> were built for the purpose of thoroughly exploring the uncharted areas of the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Sea_Route" title="Northern Sea Route">Northern Sea Route</a>. This venture became known as the <a href="/wiki/Arctic_Ocean_Hydrographic_Expedition" title="Arctic Ocean Hydrographic Expedition">Arctic Ocean Hydrographic Expedition</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Surveys">Surveys</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taymyr_(1909_icebreaker)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Surveys">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first of a series of surveys began in the autumn of 1910, when <i>Taymyr</i> and <i>Vaygach</i> left <a href="/wiki/Vladivostok" title="Vladivostok">Vladivostok</a>. They entered the <a href="/wiki/Chukchi_Sea" title="Chukchi Sea">Chukchi Sea</a> with scientists on board and began their exploration. For the next five years, these icebreakers went on sounding and carrying on vital surveys during the thaw. Before every winter, when ice conditions became too bad, they returned to Vladivostok and waited for the spring. In 1911 the scientists and crew aboard <i>Vaygach</i> and <i>Taymyr</i> made the first Russian landing on <a href="/wiki/Wrangel_Island" title="Wrangel Island">Wrangel Island</a>.
</p><p>In 1914, <a href="/wiki/Boris_Vilkitsky" title="Boris Vilkitsky">Boris Vilkitsky</a> was both the captain of <i>Taymyr</i> and the leader of the Arctic Ocean Hydrographic Expedition. The purpose of the icebreakers <i>Taymyr</i> and <i>Vaygach</i> was to force the whole Northern Passage in order to reach <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archangelsk" title="Archangelsk">Archangelsk</a>. Severe weather and ice conditions, however, didn't allow them to cross the <a href="/wiki/Kara_Sea" title="Kara Sea">Kara Sea</a> and they were forced to winter at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bukhta_Dika&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bukhta Dika (page does not exist)">Bukhta Dika</a>, close to the <a href="/wiki/Firnley_Islands" title="Firnley Islands">Firnley Islands</a>. Thus the sister icebreakers were able to complete the passage only in 1915. They were warmly welcomed in Archangelsk upon their arrival.
</p><p>Some of the biggest successes of the expedition were the accurate charting of the Northern Sea Route and the discovery of <a href="/wiki/Severnaya_Zemlya" title="Severnaya Zemlya">Severnaya Zemlya</a> in 1913. <i>Taymyr</i> and <i>Vaygach</i> were considered the best icebreakers in the world at the time.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag may use weasel words or too-vague attribution. (July 2013)">by whom?</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>The first scientific <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soviet_and_Russian_manned_drifting_ice_stations" title="Soviet and Russian manned drifting ice stations">drifting ice station</a> in the world, <a href="/wiki/North_Pole-1" title="North Pole-1">North Pole-1</a> was established on 21 May 1937 some 20 kilometres (12 mi) from the North Pole by the expedition into the high latitudes Sever-1, led by <a href="/wiki/Otto_Schmidt" title="Otto Schmidt">Otto Schmidt</a>. NP-1 operated for 9 months, during which the ice floe passed 2,850 kilometres (1,770 mi). On 19 February 1938, Soviet icebreaker <i>Taimyr</i>, along with <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Icebreaker_Murman&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Icebreaker Murman (page does not exist)"><i>Murman</i></a> took off four polar explorers from the station, who immediately became famous in the USSR and were awarded titles <a href="/wiki/Hero_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Hero of the Soviet Union">Hero of the Soviet Union</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nuclear_vessels">Nuclear vessels</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taymyr_(1909_icebreaker)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Nuclear vessels">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A nuclear icebreaker of the Soviet Navy, as well as a class of nuclear-powered river icebreakers, was named <i><a href="/wiki/Taymyr_(1987_icebreaker)" title="Taymyr (1987 icebreaker)">Taymyr</a></i> in 1988.
</p>
