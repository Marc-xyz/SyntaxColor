<p><b>Trzebież</b> <span class="IPA" style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Polish" title="Help:IPA/Polish">[ˈtʂɛbjɛʂ]</a></span> (formerly German <i>Ziegenort</i>) - is a village in the administrative district of <a href="/wiki/Gmina_Police" title="Gmina Police">Gmina Police</a>, within <a href="/wiki/Police_County" title="Police County">Police County</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Pomeranian_Voivodeship" title="West Pomeranian Voivodeship">West Pomeranian Voivodeship</a>, in north-western Poland, close to the German border.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TERYT_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-TERYT-1">[1]</a></sup> It lies approximately 15 kilometres (9 mi) north of <a href="/wiki/Police,_West_Pomeranian_Voivodeship" title="Police, West Pomeranian Voivodeship">Police</a> and 28 km (17 mi) north of the regional capital <a href="/wiki/Szczecin" title="Szczecin">Szczecin</a>.
</p><p>The village has a population of 2,500. It lies on the <a href="/wiki/Szczecin_Lagoon" title="Szczecin Lagoon">Szczecin Lagoon</a>, and has a harbour, a <a href="/wiki/Marina" title="Marina">marina</a>, a beach and a school of <a href="/wiki/Sailing" title="Sailing">sailing</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Trzebie%C5%BC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Before 1945 the area was part of Germany. For the history of the region, see <a href="/wiki/History_of_Pomerania" title="History of Pomerania">History of Pomerania</a>.
</p><p>Trzebież, known as <i>Ziegenort</i> to its residents while part of Germany until 1945, became part of <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a> after the end of World War II and changed its name to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polish_Language" title="Polish Language">Polish</a> <i>Trzebież</i>.
