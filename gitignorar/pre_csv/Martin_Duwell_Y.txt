	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Martin Duwell</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>

		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Martin Duwell</b> (born 1948, in England) is an <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australian</a> poetry editor, reviewer and publisher. Duwell is recognized as a leading poetry reviewer in Australia, as well as for his "significant contribution to the recognition and development of new poetry in Australia".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AustLit_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-AustLit-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_Duwell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Duwell was educated at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Queensland" title="University of Queensland">University of Queensland</a> and has maintained a long association with the university as a teacher, scholar and editor for the poetry magazine <i>Makar</i> (1968–80) and the related <a href="/wiki/Gargoyle_Poets_Series" title="Gargoyle Poets Series">Gargoyle Poets Series</a> (1972–80).
</p><p>Throughout his career, Duwell's reviews of new publications have appeared frequently in many Australian magazines and newspapers. In 1982 he published <i>A Possible Contemporary Poetry: Interviews with Thirteen Poets from the New Australian Poetry</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Review_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Review-2">[2]</a></sup> He was also the poetry editor for the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Queensland_Press" title="University of Queensland Press">University of Queensland Press</a> for several years during the 1980s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Duwell formalised his strength in contemporary poetry with a 1988 PhD thesis that explored the influence of contemporary <a href="/wiki/American_poetry" title="American poetry">American poetry</a> on a selection of Australian poets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Duwell_PhD_thesis_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Duwell_PhD_thesis-4">[4]</a></sup> He was also co-editor of the <i>Penguin Literary History of Australian Literature</i> (1988), and worked with <a href="/wiki/Robert_M._W._Dixon" title="Robert M. W. Dixon">Robert Dixon</a> on two collections of <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_Australians" title="Indigenous Australians">Aboriginal</a> song poems: <i>The Honey-Ant Men's Love Song and Other Aboriginal Song Poems</i> (1990), and <i>Little Eva at Moonlight Creek and Other Aboriginal Song Poems</i> (1994).
</p><p>A co-editor of <i><a href="/wiki/Australian_Literary_Studies" title="Australian Literary Studies">Australian Literary Studies</a></i> since 1982, Duwell also edited the <i>ALS Guide to Australian Writers</i> (1992) and several other publications, including <a href="/wiki/John_Blight" title="John Blight">John Blight</a>'s <i>Selected Poems 1939-1990</i> (1992). In 2003 he edited the first in a series of <i>Best Australian Poetry</i>, a selection of poems that appear in periodicals during each year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Publications">Publications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_Duwell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Publications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Duwell was the co-editor of <i>The Best Australian Poetry</i> series<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UQP_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-UQP-6">[6]</a></sup> and the editor of 37 books of poetry in the <a href="/wiki/Gargoyle_Poets_Series" title="Gargoyle Poets Series">Gargoyle Poets Series</a>, Makar Press<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-About_Makar_Press_and_Duwell_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-About_Makar_Press_and_Duwell-7">[7]</a></sup> and was the editor of <i>Makar</i> magazine from 1968 to 1980.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
