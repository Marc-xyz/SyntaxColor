<p><b>"School for Postmen"</b> (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <i lang="fr"><b>L'École des facteurs</b></i>) is a 1947 French short comedy film directed by <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Tati" title="Jacques Tati">Jacques Tati</a>. Tati plays a French <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Postman" title="Postman">postman</a> adamant to prove he can be just as fast as American postmen at delivering mail.  The film includes several sight gags that involve his <a href="/wiki/Bicycle" title="Bicycle">bicycle</a>. He replicated most of the action here in his first major <a href="/wiki/Feature_film" title="Feature film">feature film</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Jour_de_f%C3%AAte" title="Jour de fête">Jour de fête</a></i>, released two years later.
</p><p>The 15-minute film is included on the <i><a href="/wiki/Mon_Oncle" title="Mon Oncle">Mon Oncle</a></i> DVD, part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Criterion_Collection" title="Criterion Collection">Criterion Collection</a>.
</p>
