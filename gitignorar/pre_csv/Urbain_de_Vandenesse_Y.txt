		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Urbain de Vandenesse</b> (? – 1753, Paris) was an 18th-century French physician and <a href="/wiki/Encyclop%C3%A9distes" title="Encyclopédistes">Encyclopédiste</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Urbain_de_Vandenesse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After he presented six thesis at the Faculté de médecine de Paris, he was received with the title <i>docteur-régent</i> in 1742.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>The 270 articles he wrote for the <i><a href="/wiki/Encyclop%C3%A9die" title="Encyclopédie">Encyclopédie</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Denis_Diderot" title="Denis Diderot">Denis Diderot</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jean_le_Rond_d%27Alembert" title="Jean le Rond d'Alembert">d’Alembert</a> are the only texts we know of him. There were more than 150 articles for Volume I and about 100 for Volume II ; his sudden death interrupted his cooperation for the relevant sections of Volume III for which he could contribute only one article. 
</p><p>After the death of Vandenesse, Diderot needed a new author in the field of medicine and pharmacy. On the recommendation of <a href="/wiki/Gabriel_Fran%C3%A7ois_Venel" title="Gabriel François Venel">Gabriel François Venel</a>, he chose <a href="/wiki/Arnulphe_d%27Aumont" title="Arnulphe d'Aumont">Arnulphe d'Aumont</a>.
</p>
