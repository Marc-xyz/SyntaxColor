<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1890s_in_Zimbabwe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1890">1890</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1890s_in_Zimbabwe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1890">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li><b>6 May –</b> The <a href="/wiki/Pioneer_Column" title="Pioneer Column">Pioneer Column</a> left <a href="/wiki/Cecil_Rhodes" title="Cecil Rhodes">Cecil Rhodes</a>' farm at Kenilworth, a suburb of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kimberley,_South_Africa" title="Kimberley, South Africa">Kimberley</a>, for Macloutsie under Col. Pennefather and Sir John Willoughby</li>
<li><b>29 June –</b> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archibald_Ross_Colquhoun" title="Archibald Ross Colquhoun">Archibald Ross Colquhoun</a> becomes the Resident Commissioner of <a href="/wiki/Mashonaland" title="Mashonaland">Mashonaland</a></li>
<li>July – <a href="/wiki/Tuli,_Zimbabwe" title="Tuli, Zimbabwe">Fort Tuli</a> is built by the Pioneer Column at the place known as Selous Camp and used by <a href="/wiki/Frederick_Selous" title="Frederick Selous">Frederick Selous</a> as a base for his hunting expeditions.</li>
<li><b>13 September –</b> The <a href="/wiki/Pioneer_Column" title="Pioneer Column">Pioneer Column</a> ran up the <a href="/wiki/Union_Jack" title="Union Jack">Union Jack</a> on Harare Hill in <a href="/wiki/Harare" title="Harare">Salisbury</a> and claimed <a href="/wiki/Mashonaland" title="Mashonaland">Mashonaland</a> for the <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British Empire</a>, having arrived the previous day</li>
<li><b>1 October –</b> The Pioneer Column is disbanded and each member is granted land on which to farm</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1891">1891</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1890s_in_Zimbabwe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1891">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li><b>15 February –</b> A temporary frontier at Manica is settled.</li>
<li><b>9 May –</b> <a href="/wiki/Mashonaland" title="Mashonaland">Mashonaland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Matabeleland" title="Matabeleland">Matabeleland</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bechuanaland" title="Bechuanaland">Bechuanaland</a> are declared <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Protectorate" title="British Protectorate">British Protectorates</a> in London by <a href="/wiki/Order_in_Council" title="Order in Council">Order in Council</a>.</li>
<li><b>12 June –</b> The United Kingdom and Portuguese governments reach agreement on the means of determining boundaries between their territories.</li>
<li><b>27 June –</b> First edition of the <i>Mashonaland Herald</i>, later the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rhodesia_Herald" title="Rhodesia Herald">Rhodesia Herald</a></i> and later still the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zimbabwe_Herald" title="Zimbabwe Herald">Zimbabwe Herald</a></i></li>
<li><b>18 September –</b>  <a href="/wiki/Leander_Starr_Jameson" title="Leander Starr Jameson">Leander Starr Jameson</a> becomes the first Administrator of Mashonaland</li>
<li><b>22 December –</b> The <a href="/wiki/British_South_Africa_Company" title="British South Africa Company">British South Africa Company</a> holds its first annual meeting in London.</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1892">1892</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1890s_in_Zimbabwe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1892">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li><b>22 June –</b> Kopje Fort is completed at Salisbury.</li>
<li><b>29 October –</b> The Rhodesia Herald commences publication.</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1893">1893</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1890s_in_Zimbabwe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1893">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li><b>18 July –</b> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ndebele_people_(Zimbabwe)" title="Ndebele people (Zimbabwe)">Matabele</a> impi enter Victoria and attack the <a href="/wiki/Shona_people" title="Shona people">Mashona</a></li>
<li><b>2 October –</b> <a href="/wiki/Lobengula" title="Lobengula">Lobengula</a>'s impis attack patrols of the British South Africa company</li>
<li><b>3 October –</b> War on the Matabele is authorised – <a href="/wiki/First_Matabele_War" title="First Matabele War">First Matabele War</a></li>
<li><b>8 October –</b> 22 January 1894 – <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Andrew_Duncan_(Zimbabwe)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Andrew Duncan (Zimbabwe) (page does not exist)">Andrew Duncan</a> becomes acting administrator for <a href="/wiki/Leander_Starr_Jameson" title="Leander Starr Jameson">Leander Starr Jameson</a></li>
<li><b>4 November –</b> British forces occupy the site of <a href="/wiki/Bulawayo" title="Bulawayo">Bulawayo</a>. This marks the effective occupation of <a href="/wiki/Matabeleland" title="Matabeleland">Matabeleland</a> by British settlers</li>
<li><b>3 December –</b> British forces, in search of King Lobengula, suffer a humiliating defeat near the Shangani river. The incident becomes known as the <a href="/wiki/Shangani_Patrol" title="Shangani Patrol">Shangani Patrol</a>. The remains of the 34 Patrol members killed on that day were eventually interred next to the bodies of <a href="/wiki/Cecil_Rhodes" title="Cecil Rhodes">Cecil Rhodes</a> and Leander Starr Jameson at World's View in the <a href="/wiki/Matobo_District" title="Matobo District">Matobo</a> Hills. The American scout, <a href="/wiki/Frederick_Russell_Burnham" title="Frederick Russell Burnham">Frederick Russell Burnham</a>, is one of only three survivors of the Patrol.</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1894">1894</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1890s_in_Zimbabwe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 1894">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li>Spring – Matabele King Lobengula dies of unknown causes. His death is kept a secret from the British for many months.</li>
<li><b>23 January –</b> <a href="/wiki/Mashonaland" title="Mashonaland">Mashonaland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Matabeleland" title="Matabeleland">Matabeleland</a> protectorates merge to form <i>South Zambesia</i>
<ul><li>Andrew Duncan is made the acting Chief magistrate of South Zambesia</li></ul></li>
<li>May – <a href="/wiki/Leander_Starr_Jameson" title="Leander Starr Jameson">Leander Starr Jameson</a> is elected Chief magistrate of South Zambesia</li>
<li><b>9 September –</b> Leander Jameson becomes the Administrator of the South Zambesia Protectorate</li>
<li><b>28 October –</b> 1 April 1895 – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Francis_Rhodes" title="Francis Rhodes">Francis Rhodes</a> stands in for Leander Jameson as acting Administrator of the South Zambesia Protectorate</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1895">1895</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1890s_in_Zimbabwe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 1895">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li><b>3 May –</b> <i>South Zambesia</i> and <a href="/wiki/Zambia" title="Zambia">North Zambesia</a> are united as <a href="/wiki/Rhodesia" title="Rhodesia">Rhodesia</a> (after <a href="/wiki/Cecil_Rhodes" title="Cecil Rhodes">Cecil Rhodes</a>)</li>
<li>June – <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Vintcent" title="Joseph Vintcent">Joseph Vintcent</a> becomes acting Administrator of the Rhodesia Protectorate</li>
<li><b>29 December –</b> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jameson_raid" title="Jameson raid">Jameson raid</a> is commenced.</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1896">1896</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1890s_in_Zimbabwe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 1896">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li><b>2 January –</b> <a href="/wiki/Leander_Starr_Jameson" title="Leander Starr Jameson">Leander Starr Jameson</a> and his raiders surrender</li>
<li><b>20 March –</b> The <a href="/wiki/Second_Matabele_War" title="Second Matabele War">First Chimurenga</a> revolt against the <a href="/wiki/British_South_Africa_Company" title="British South Africa Company">British South Africa Company</a> starts among the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Matabele_people" title="Matabele people">Matabele</a></li>
<li><b>2 May –</b> <a href="/wiki/Albert_Grey,_4th_Earl_Grey" title="Albert Grey, 4th Earl Grey">Albert Grey, 4th Earl Grey</a> becomes the second Administrator of <a href="/wiki/Mashonaland" title="Mashonaland">Mashonaland</a> and Administrator of the Rhodesia Protectorate</li>
<li><b>14 June –</b> The Mashona join in the First Chimurenga.</li>
<li><b>13 October –</b> The Matabele are defeated by the British South Africa Company, and their chiefs concede</li>
<li>November – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arthur_Lawley" title="Arthur Lawley">Arthur Lawley</a> becomes the Administrator of <a href="/wiki/Matabeleland" title="Matabeleland">Matabeleland</a></li>
<li><b>29 November –</b> British forces leave Rhodesia as it is believed local troops can now defeat the Mashona</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1897">1897</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1890s_in_Zimbabwe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 1897">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li>January – Final determination of the boundary between Rhodesia and Portuguese possessions to the east</li>
<li><b>24 July –</b> <a href="/wiki/William_Henry_Milton" title="William Henry Milton">William Henry Milton</a> becomes the 3rd Administrator of <a href="/wiki/Mashonaland" title="Mashonaland">Mashonaland</a> and Administrator of the Rhodesia Protectorate</li>
<li><b>26 October –</b> The municipalities of Salisbury and Bulawayo are formally created.</li>
<li><b>27 October –</b> The <a href="/wiki/Shona_people" title="Shona people">Mashona</a> are defeated by the <a href="/wiki/British_South_Africa_Company" title="British South Africa Company">British South Africa Company</a> thus ending the <a href="/wiki/Second_Matabele_War" title="Second Matabele War">First Chimurenga</a>.</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1898">1898</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1890s_in_Zimbabwe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 1898">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li>Rhodesia south of the Zambezi is renamed the Colony of Southern Rhodesia</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1899">1899</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1890s_in_Zimbabwe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 1899">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li>An Order in Council grants Southern Rhodesia a part-elected Legislative council.</li>
<li><b>17 April:</b> Polling day in the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southern_Rhodesia_Legislative_Council_election,_1899" title="Southern Rhodesia Legislative Council election, 1899">Legislative Council election</a>.</li>
<li><b>15 May:</b> First session of the Legislative Council.</li></ul>
