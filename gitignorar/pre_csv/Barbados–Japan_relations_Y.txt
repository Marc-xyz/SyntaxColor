<p>Foreign relations between <a href="/wiki/Barbados" title="Barbados">Barbados</a> and Japan were formally established on 29 August 1967.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Japan is accredited to Barbados from its regional Embassy in <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Spain" title="Port of Spain">Port of Spain</a> (<a href="/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago" title="Trinidad and Tobago">Trinidad and Tobago</a>) and an honorary consulate in the parish of <a href="/wiki/Saint_George,_Barbados" title="Saint George, Barbados">Saint George</a>.  Barbados is represented in Japan through a non-resident ambassador in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bridgetown,_Barbados" title="Bridgetown, Barbados">Bridgetown</a>.  Japan's new Ambassador for Barbados, Mitsuhiko Okada announced a new direct embassy to Barbados would be established located in Bridgetown In February 2016 .<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barbados%E2%80%93Japan_relations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Japanese submarine <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_submarine_I-52_(1943)" title="Japanese submarine I-52 (1943)">I-52</a></i> was bombed off the coast of Barbados in 1944.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1999, Barbadian and Japanese officials held talks aimed at defusing tensions over the transshipment of nuclear materials through the Caribbean Sea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Barbadian and Japanese economic trade has grown steadily, in the favour of Japan. In 2005, Japan ranked as Barbados' fourth-largest financial contributor in projects. In 2009, Japan provided technical cooperation and support for the Barbados-based <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caribbean_Disaster_Emergency_Response_Agency" title="Caribbean Disaster Emergency Response Agency">Caribbean Disaster Emergency Response Agency</a> (CDERA).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The thirteenth meeting between Japan and CARICOM was convened in Bridgetown, Barbados, on 19 March 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2008, the Senior Vice-Minister of Japan, Hitoshi Kimura, visited Barbados for high-level talks.  During the discussions, both nations spoke of Barbados wanting to conclude a Double Taxation Agreement (DTA) with Japan to increase investment and trade between both countries.  Additionally, the Barbados government pledged to support Japan's bid for a seat on the United Nations Security Council for 2009–2010. It was also discussed that the through countries needed to concentrate on cooperation in climate change through the <a href="/wiki/Small_Island_Developing_States" title="Small Island Developing States">Small Island Developing States</a> (SIDS) organisation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> On 27 November 2011, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_broadcaster" title="National broadcaster">national broadcaster</a> of Barbados the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Caribbean Broadcasting Corporation">Caribbean Broadcasting Corporation</a> and <a href="/wiki/TV_Osaka" title="TV Osaka">TV Osaka</a> made a jointly produced series called <i>From Bridgetown To Tokyo</i> showcasing the Japanese culture and Barbadians working in Japan. One of the main parts showed a Barbadian English teacher recruited through the Japanese embassy in the Caribbean and her experiences teaching in Japan.
</p><p>In March 2015 the government of Japan announced it would be moving to form a new Eastern Caribbean diplomatic mission based-in Barbados to serve the Eastern Caribbean. It is expected to open by January 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2018 Japan's resident ambassador to Barbados and the Eastern Caribbean presented close to BDS$2 in emergency resiliency equipment to the Government of Barbados.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Agreements">Agreements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barbados%E2%80%93Japan_relations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Agreements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of 2009, Barbadian and Japanese officials have been negotiating the details of a bilateral Double-Taxation Agreement (DTA) treaty.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
