<p><b>Jonathan Parr</b> (born 21 October 1988) is a Norwegian <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)" title="Defender (association football)">full-back</a> for <a href="/wiki/Str%C3%B8msgodset" title="Strømsgodset">Strømsgodset</a>. He made his debut for the <a href="/wiki/Norway_national_football_team" title="Norway national football team">Norway national football team</a> in 2010.
</p><p>Hailing from <a href="/wiki/Oslo" title="Oslo">Oslo</a>, Parr started his professional career with the local team <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FK_Lyn" title="FK Lyn">Lyn</a>, where he made his debut in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tippeligaen" title="Tippeligaen">Tippeligaen</a>. He then transferred to <a href="/wiki/Aalesunds_FK" title="Aalesunds FK">Aalesund</a> in 2007, where he was a part of the team that won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Norwegian_Cup" title="Norwegian Cup">Norwegian Cup</a> in both <a href="/wiki/2009_Norwegian_Football_Cup" title="2009 Norwegian Football Cup">2009</a> and <a href="/wiki/2011_Norwegian_Football_Cup" title="2011 Norwegian Football Cup">2011</a>. Parr transferred to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" title="Football League Championship">Championship</a> side Crystal Palace in July 2011 and was a part of the team that won promotion to the Premier League in the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Football_League_Championship" title="2012–13 Football League Championship">2012–13 season</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jonathan_Parr&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jonathan_Parr&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Parr was born in <a href="/wiki/Oslo" title="Oslo">Oslo</a> where he grew up in <a href="/wiki/Holmlia" title="Holmlia">Holmlia</a> and played for <a href="/wiki/Holmlia_SK" title="Holmlia SK">Holmlia SK</a>. During his youth he also played for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KFUM_Oslo" title="KFUM Oslo">KFUM Oslo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hauger_FK" title="Hauger FK">Hauger</a> before he got enrolled in the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=NTG/Lyn&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="NTG/Lyn (page does not exist)">NTG/Lyn</a>-program.
</p><p>Parr made his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tippeligaen" title="Tippeligaen">Tippeligaen</a> debut for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FK_Lyn" title="FK Lyn">Lyn</a> on 30 April 2006, in a 1–4 loss against <a href="/wiki/Stab%C3%A6k_Fotball" title="Stabæk Fotball">Stabæk</a>. He played 14 senior matches for Lyn, 11 of them in Tippeligaen. Parr was mostly used as a striker or winger at Lyn.
</p><p>In March 2007 he signed a three-year contract for <a href="/wiki/Aalesunds_FK" title="Aalesunds FK">Aalesund</a>. He was in the starting line-up five times, and came off the bench 14 times during his first year in Aalesund. His first league goal came in the 4–1 victory against <a href="/wiki/Sandefjord_Fotball" title="Sandefjord Fotball">Sandefjord</a> on 29 July 2007. He won the <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_Football_Cup" title="Norwegian Football Cup">Norwegian Football Cup</a> with Aalesund in <a href="/wiki/2009_Norwegian_Football_Cup" title="2009 Norwegian Football Cup">2009</a>. In February 2010, he extended his contract an extra year, expiring at the end of 2011.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Crystal_Palace">Crystal Palace</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jonathan_Parr&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Crystal Palace">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 16 July 2011, <a href="/wiki/Aalesunds_FK" title="Aalesunds FK">Aalesund</a> agreed a fee for Parr to transfer to London-based <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" title="Football League Championship">Championship</a> side <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crystal_Palace_FC" title="Crystal Palace FC">Crystal Palace</a>, subject to a medical.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Three days later, Crystal Palace confirmed that Parr had signed a three-year deal for an undisclosed fee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> on 24 January 2012, Parr missed the final penalty when Crystal Palace lost a <a href="/wiki/Penalty_shoot-out_(association_football)" title="Penalty shoot-out (association football)">penalty shoot-out</a> 1–3 against <a href="/wiki/Cardiff_City_F.C." title="Cardiff City F.C.">Cardiff City</a> in the semi final of the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Football_League_Cup" title="2011–12 Football League Cup">2011–12 League Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> On 12 May 2012, he was voted Crystal Palace player of the year for the 2011–12 season, due to his work-ethic and his great fitness.
</p><p>Parr started the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Football_League_Championship" title="2012–13 Football League Championship">2012–13 season</a> well, and made the left-back position his own in <a href="/wiki/Dougie_Freedman" title="Dougie Freedman">Dougie Freedman</a>'s team. After Freedman's acrimonious departure from Selhurst Park in October, Parr's place in the starting XI was never under threat. New manager <a href="/wiki/Ian_Holloway" title="Ian Holloway">Ian Holloway</a> had to also deploy him in the right fullback position because of injuries. Parr's season ended in April against <a href="/wiki/Ipswich_Town_F.C." title="Ipswich Town F.C.">Ipswich Town</a> after damaging ligaments in his ankle. Nevertheless, Crystal Palace still managed to gain promotion to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2013-14_Premier_League" title="2013-14 Premier League">Premier League</a> at the end of the season. The Eagles beat <a href="/wiki/Watford_F.C." title="Watford F.C.">Watford</a> in the promotion-playoffs finals to return to the English top tier after an eight-year absence.
</p><p>When Parr returned from his ankle-injury, <a href="/wiki/Dean_Moxey" title="Dean Moxey">Dean Moxey</a> was the preferred choice as Palace's left-back.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lettet_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-lettet-4">[4]</a></sup> Parr got his Premier League debut for Crystal Palace on 21 December, replacing left-back rival <a href="/wiki/Dean_Moxey" title="Dean Moxey">Dean Moxey</a> at half time in the match against <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a>. Parr gave away a penalty in his first appearance in eight months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Parr then started the next eight matches for Palace under the new manager <a href="/wiki/Tony_Pulis" title="Tony Pulis">Tony Pulis</a>, before he was injured in the FA Cup fourth-round match against <a href="/wiki/Wigan_Athletic_F.C." title="Wigan Athletic F.C.">Wigan Athletic</a> on 25 January 2014. Palace lost the match 1–2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lettet_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-lettet-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>At the end of 2013–14 season, he was released by the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ipswich_Town">Ipswich Town</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jonathan_Parr&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Ipswich Town">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 7 July 2014, Parr signed for <a href="/wiki/Ipswich_Town_F.C." title="Ipswich Town F.C.">Ipswich Town</a> on a free transfer. The 25-year-old Norway international signed a two-year deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>Parr scored his first goal for Ipswich against Brighton and Hove Albion on Tuesday 16 September 2014.
</p>
<h3><span id="Str.C3.B8msgodset"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Strømsgodset">Strømsgodset</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jonathan_Parr&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Strømsgodset">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Parr signed a permanent deal with <a href="/wiki/Str%C3%B8msgodset_Toppfotball" title="Strømsgodset Toppfotball">Strømsgodset</a> on 13 January 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He signed a 3.5-year contract.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jonathan_Parr&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Parr played 14 games on national youth teams for Norway up to U-19, and was a regular at the <a href="/wiki/Norway_national_under-21_football_team" title="Norway national under-21 football team">Norway national under-21 football team</a> with 18 matches between 2008 and 2010. On 11 May 2010, he got selected for the <a href="/wiki/Norway_national_football_team" title="Norway national football team">senior national team</a> for the first time, as Norway were facing <a href="/wiki/Montenegro_national_football_team" title="Montenegro national football team">Montenegro</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ukraine_national_football_team" title="Ukraine national football team">Ukraine</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>Parr debuted on the Norway national team on 29 May 2010, in a <a href="/wiki/Exhibition_game" title="Exhibition game">friendly match</a> against Montenegro, and he became Aalesund's first player to have started a Norway national game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> As of February 2013, Parr has been <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cap_(football)" title="Cap (football)">capped</a> nine times.
</p>
