<p><b>Emil Salim</b> (born in Lahat, <a href="/wiki/South_Sumatra" title="South Sumatra">South Sumatra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>, 8 June 1930) is an <a href="/wiki/Indonesians" title="Indonesians">Indonesian</a> <a href="/wiki/Economist" title="Economist">economist</a> and former politician. Born of <a href="/wiki/Minangkabau_people" title="Minangkabau people">Minangkabau</a> parents, both from the village of Koto Gadang in <a href="/wiki/West_Sumatra" title="West Sumatra">West Sumatra</a>. His uncle is <a href="/wiki/Agus_Salim" title="Agus Salim">Agus Salim</a>, one of the founding fathers of the Republic of Indonesia and Minister of Foreign Affairs in the early 1950s.
</p><p>Salim graduated from the Faculty of Economics of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Indonesia" title="University of Indonesia">University of Indonesia</a> in 1959. He obtained a PhD in Economics from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Berkeley" title="University of California, Berkeley">University of California, Berkeley</a>, and returned to Indonesia to a teaching position at the Faculty of Economics of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Indonesia" title="University of Indonesia">University of Indonesia</a> in 1964. He became one of the well-known group of '<a href="/wiki/Berkeley_Mafia" title="Berkeley Mafia">Berkeley Mafia</a>' economic advisers, working closely with Professor <a href="/wiki/Widjojo_Nitisastro" title="Widjojo Nitisastro">Widjojo Nitisastro</a>.  In 1977 he was appointed to the position of professor of economic development at the University of Indonesia.
</p><p>Salim has held a number of government positions, including:
</p>
<ul><li>1966: member of the team of economic advisers to <a href="/wiki/Suharto" title="Suharto">President Suharto</a></li>
<li>1967-68: member of the team of advisers to the Minister of Manpower.</li>
<li>1967-1969: Chairman of the technical team of the Council for Economic Stability and a member of the <i>Gotong Royong</i> Parliament.</li>
<li>1969: Vice Chairman of Bappenas (the National Development Planning Agency)</li>
<li>1971: Minister of State for the Improvement of the State Apparatus.</li>
<li>1973-1978: Minister of Transportation</li>
<li>1978-1983: Minister of State for Development Supervision and the Environment</li>
<li>1983-1993: Minister of State for Population and the Environment</li>
<li>2007-2010: Member of the Advisory Council to <a href="/wiki/Susilo_Bambang_Yudhoyono" title="Susilo Bambang Yudhoyono">President Yudhoyono</a>, as the adviser for environment and sustainable development issues</li>
<li>2010-2014: Chairperson, The Advisory Council to <a href="/wiki/Susilo_Bambang_Yudhoyono" title="Susilo Bambang Yudhoyono">President Yudhoyono</a></li></ul>
<p>Salim has chaired the <i>Foundation for Sustainable Development</i> and the <i>Kehati Foundation</i>, and co-chaired the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States-Indonesia_Society" title="United States-Indonesia Society">United States-Indonesia Society</a>. He is a member of the <i>Association of Indonesian Moslem Intellectuals</i>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Extractive_Industries_Review">Extractive Industries Review</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emil_Salim&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Extractive Industries Review">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In July 2001 the <a href="/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">World Bank</a> launched an independent inquiry called the Extractive Industries Review (EIR). The review was headed by Salim. Salim held consultations with a wide range of stakeholders in 2002 and 2003. The EIR recommendations were published in January 2004 in a final report entitled <i>Striking a Better Balance</i>. The report concluded that fossil fuel and mining projects do not alleviate poverty and recommended that World Bank involvement with these sectors be phased out by 2008 to be replaced by investment in renewable energy and clean energy. The World Bank published its Management Response to the EIR in September 2004. The EIR served to alter the World Bank's policies on oil, gas and mining. Following the EIR process, the World Bank also issued a revised Policy on Indigenous Peoples.
</p>
