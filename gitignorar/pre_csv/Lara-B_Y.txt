<p><b>Lara-B</b> (born <b>Lara Baruca</b>, born August 1, 1979, in <a href="/wiki/Koper" title="Koper">Koper</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SR_Slovenia" title="SR Slovenia">SR Slovenia</a> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Singer" title="Singer">singer</a> who began her career in her early teens. Her musical style combines <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rock_(music)" title="Rock (music)">rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electronica" title="Electronica">electronic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Industrial_music" title="Industrial music">industrial</a> and <a href="/wiki/Soul_music" title="Soul music">soul</a>.
</p><p>Baruca began performing in 1992. After performing at various music festivals (MMS '95, Kraška popevka '95, Slovenska popevka '99, EMA '99, MMS '99, Malta Festival '99), she turned her music aspirations mainly to concert stages. She combines her live performances with her studio work, and has created several albums: <i>Hudič izgublja moč</i> in 1997, <i>Kar ne piše</i> in 1999, <i>Beenarni sistem</i> in 2001, <i>Mindhacker</i> in 2005, and X in 2007, which is a compilation consisting mainly of live performances of the previous material, and represents a 10-year anniversary from her debut. She has also written scores for several <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theatrical" title="Theatrical">theatrical</a> performances (Behind that courtain 2002, Confi-dance 2003, Glasba in gib 2004, Tihe resnice 2005). Her musical expression has been expressed in other genres as well (<a href="/wiki/House_music" title="House music">house</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ambient_music" title="Ambient music">ambient</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drum_%27n%27_bass" title="Drum 'n' bass">drum 'n' bass</a>).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mindhacker">Mindhacker</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lara-B&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Mindhacker">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lara's first major album release was <i>Mindhacker</i> on <a href="/wiki/Menart_Records" title="Menart Records">Menart Records</a>. Paul Flowers, in his review for WUSC-FM in Columbia, South Carolina, call the album a step forward that shows a continued maturity in singing, composition and a new creative edge.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> When discussing the album, Baruca jokingly alluded to certain aspects of the album being "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emo_(music)" title="Emo (music)">emo</a>" in appearance. <i>Mindhacker</i> encompasses a great amount of her personal energies and life experiences, dealing with personal loss and growth emotionally.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
