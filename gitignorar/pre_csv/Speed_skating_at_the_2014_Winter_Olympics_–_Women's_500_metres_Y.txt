<p>The <b>women's 500 metres <a href="/wiki/Speed_skating" title="Speed skating">speed skating</a></b> competition of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2014_Sochi_Olympics" title="2014 Sochi Olympics">2014 Sochi Olympics</a> was held at <a href="/wiki/Adler_Arena_Skating_Center" title="Adler Arena Skating Center">Adler Arena Skating Center</a> on 11 February 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The reigning <a href="/wiki/2014_World_Sprint_Speed_Skating_Championships" title="2014 World Sprint Speed Skating Championships">2014 World Sprint Speed Skating Champion</a> <a href="/wiki/Yu_Jing" title="Yu Jing">Yu Jing</a> did not start due to reoccurrence of an old waist injury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualification">Qualification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Speed_skating_at_the_2014_Winter_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_500_metres&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Qualification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A total of thirty-six speed skaters could qualify for this distance, with a maximum of four skaters per country. The top 20 of the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_ISU_Speed_Skating_World_Cup_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_500_metres" title="2013–14 ISU Speed Skating World Cup – Women's 500 metres">2013–14 ISU Speed Skating World Cup – Women's 500 metres</a> standings after the fourth World Cup race in Berlin secured a spot for their country. Then the additional 16 spots were awarded based on a time ranking of all times skated in the World Cup and the <a href="/wiki/2014_World_Sprint_Speed_Skating_Championships" title="2014 World Sprint Speed Skating Championships">2014 World Sprint Speed Skating Championships</a>. A reserve list was also made.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Results">Results</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Speed_skating_at_the_2014_Winter_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_500_metres&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Results">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The races were held at 16:45<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and 18:34.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Lee_Sang-hwa" title="Lee Sang-hwa">Lee Sang-hwa</a> became the third woman to win back-to-back golds, having previously won the distance at the 2010 Olympics, and the first Korean woman to do so.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>On 24 November 2017, silver medalist from Russia <a href="/wiki/Olga_Fatkulina" title="Olga Fatkulina">Olga Fatkulina</a> was disqualified for a doping violation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On 1 February 2018, her results were restored as a result of the successful appeal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
