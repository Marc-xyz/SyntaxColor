	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Eliza Potter</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Eliza Potter</b> (1820–1893)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xs_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-xs-1">[1]</a></sup> was an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African-American" title="African-American">African-American</a> hairdresser in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cincinnati,_Ohio" title="Cincinnati, Ohio">Cincinnati, Ohio</a>. In 1859 she published her autobiography, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A_Hairdresser%27s_Experience_In_High_Life" title="A Hairdresser's Experience In High Life">A Hairdresser's Experience In High Life</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Beaulieu_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Beaulieu-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Potter, a free black woman of mixed race grew up in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>. Upon marriage she moved to <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a> and gave birth to two children.  According to her memoir, she left her marital home to go "roving".  She traveled widely, including in Europe, and learned her trade.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xs_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-xs-1">[1]</a></sup> Potter's autobiography provides an intimate glimpse of the experiences of a mixed-race beautician at the hands of her white employers in Cincinnati. She also travelled widely, and recorded the various social customs of <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saratoga_Springs,_New_York" title="Saratoga Springs, New York">Saratoga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> and <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Beaulieu_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Beaulieu-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
