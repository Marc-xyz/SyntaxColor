	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Major Riddle</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Major Arteburn Riddle</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> (born 1906 or 1907; died 1980)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-obit_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-obit-2">[2]</a></sup> was an American businessperson who owned several <a href="/wiki/Casino" title="Casino">casinos</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a> area.
</p><p>Riddle was born in <a href="/wiki/Louisville,_Kentucky" title="Louisville, Kentucky">Louisville, Kentucky</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-obit_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-obit-2">[2]</a></sup> At the age of 11, he moved with his family to <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis" title="Indianapolis">Indianapolis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-exhoosier_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-exhoosier-3">[3]</a></sup> There, he eventually started working in his father's trucking and manufacturing businesses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-exhoosier_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-exhoosier-3">[3]</a></sup> By 1936, he was involved in an Indianapolis-area nightclub with illegal gambling, which was linked to mobster <a href="/wiki/Al_Capone" title="Al Capone">Al Capone</a> and his <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Outfit" title="Chicago Outfit">Chicago Outfit</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-exhoosier_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-exhoosier-3">[3]</a></sup> In 1940, he became involved with another gambling club in <a href="/wiki/Moline,_Illinois" title="Moline, Illinois">Moline, Illinois</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-exhoosier_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-exhoosier-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Around this time, he was romantically linked to mob figure <a href="/wiki/Virginia_Hill" title="Virginia Hill">Virginia Hill</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Riddle moved to Chicago in the 1940s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-exhoosier_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-exhoosier-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Riddle made a fortune in the 1930s and 1940s with an oil and gas drilling company in Texas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-obit_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-obit-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oilbaron_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-oilbaron-8">[8]</a></sup> He also owned a trucking company in <a href="/wiki/Evansville,_Indiana" title="Evansville, Indiana">Evansville, Indiana</a> from 1937 to 1954.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-exhoosier_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-exhoosier-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thestrip_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-thestrip-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>Riddle started his Las Vegas casino career in 1956, when he took over the <a href="/wiki/Dunes_(hotel_and_casino)" title="Dunes (hotel and casino)">Dunes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-obit_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-obit-2">[2]</a></sup> Riddle and his co-investors substantially expanded the hotel using loans from the mob-influenced <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Teamsters" title="Teamsters">Teamsters</a> pension fund.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thestrip_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-thestrip-9">[9]</a></sup> He was also noted for booking the first topless showgirl revue in Las Vegas, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minsky%27s_Follies" title="Minsky's Follies">Minsky's Follies</a>, into the casino.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>He opened the <a href="/wiki/Silver_Nugget" title="Silver Nugget">Silver Nugget</a> casino in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Las_Vegas" title="North Las Vegas">North Las Vegas</a> in 1964.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Around 1975, he took over the Riata Club and reopened it as the <a href="/wiki/Silver_City_Casino" title="Silver City Casino">Silver City Casino</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thunderbird_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-thunderbird-13">[13]</a></sup> In 1977, he took over operation of the <a href="/wiki/El_Rancho_Hotel_and_Casino" title="El Rancho Hotel and Casino">Thunderbird</a> hotel casino and renamed it as the Silver Bird.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thunderbird_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-thunderbird-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In 1978, he opened the casino at the <a href="/wiki/Main_Street_Station_Hotel_and_Casino_and_Brewery" title="Main Street Station Hotel and Casino and Brewery">Holiday International</a>, under a lease from the hotel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jobless_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-jobless-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1963, Riddle wrote <i>The Weekend Gambler's Handbook</i>, a book of advice for casino gamblers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oilbaron_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-oilbaron-8">[8]</a></sup> It became popular, despite containing some advice that was based on superstition rather than mathematics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup>
</p><p>Riddle's son, Charles, died of suicide in 1966 at the age of 18.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>
</p><p>Riddle died on July 8, 1980 at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cedars-Sinai_Hospital" title="Cedars-Sinai Hospital">Cedars-Sinai Hospital</a> in Los Angeles, from <a href="/wiki/Pneumonia" title="Pneumonia">pneumonia</a> after suffering from a rare <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blood_disease" title="Blood disease">blood disease</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-obit_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-obit-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-colorful_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-colorful-19">[19]</a></sup>
</p><p>Soon after his death, Riddle's casinos went into bankruptcy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> The Silverbird and the Holiday International closed down,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jobless_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-jobless-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> and the Silver Nugget and Silver City were sold off.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Riddle's estate sold his remaining shares in the Dunes around 1984.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-colorful_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-colorful-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup>
</p>
