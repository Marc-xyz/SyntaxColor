<p><b>56 Artillery Lane</b> is an 18th-century <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grade_I_listed_building" title="Grade I listed building">Grade I listed building</a> in <a href="/wiki/Spitalfields" title="Spitalfields">Spitalfields</a>, London.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[a]</a></sup> The building is situated in the Artillery Passage, and was merged with the now <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grade_II_listed_building" title="Grade II listed building">Grade II listed building</a> 58 Artillery Lane after the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>; their combined shop front is one of the oldest in London, and the combined building is used by <a href="/wiki/Raven_Row" title="Raven Row">Raven Row</a> as a free art exhibition centre.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=56_Artillery_Lane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is not known exactly when the first house at 56 Artillery Lane was built, although a house appears at the location on a map of 1677. The original name of the building was 3 Raven Row. During the early 18th century, <a href="/wiki/Deed" title="Deed">deed</a> documents show that the building was owned by a <a href="/wiki/Mercery" title="Mercery">mercer</a> named Mathew Hebart and later a weaver named Thomas Wilkes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BHO_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-BHO-3">[2]</a></sup> The building was rebuilt between 1750 and 1756, in order to accommodate <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Huguenot" title="Huguenot">Huguenot</a> silk merchants Nicholas Jourdain and Francis Rybot who wanted to use the building as a silk shop; it is believed that <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sir_Robert_Taylor" title="Sir Robert Taylor">Sir Robert Taylor</a> was the architect. The 1756 building, including its shop front, still exists today, making it one of the oldest shop fronts in London.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BHO_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-BHO-3">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guardian_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian-4">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[5]</a></sup> In the 19th and early 20th centuries, the building was used as a grocery shop.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dig_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dig-7">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>6</span></sup>
</p><p>After the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>, 56 and 58 Artillery Lane were merged into a single office building, and 56 Artillery Lane became a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grade_I_listed_building" title="Grade I listed building">Grade I listed building</a> in 1950.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Turkey_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Turkey-8">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[8]</a></sup> In 1972, the building was refurbished after a major fire.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Turkey_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Turkey-8">[7]</a></sup> In 2006, an excavation of 56 and 58 Artillery Lane took place,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dig_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Dig-7">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>13</span></sup> and the buildings were later sold to Alex Sainsbury, heir to the <a href="/wiki/Sainsbury%27s" title="Sainsbury's">Sainsbury's</a> food chain, who converted them into the <a href="/wiki/Raven_Row" title="Raven Row">Raven Row</a> free art exhibition space.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Turkey_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-Turkey-8">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guardian_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian-4">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
