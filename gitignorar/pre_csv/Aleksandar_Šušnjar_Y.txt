<p><b>Aleksandar Šušnjar</b> (<a href="/wiki/Serbian_language" title="Serbian language">Serbian</a>: <span lang="sr">Александар Шушњар</span>, born 19 August 1995) is an <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australian</a> international <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" title="Football (soccer)">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)" title="Defender (association football)">defender</a> for <a href="/wiki/Busan_IPark" title="Busan IPark">Busan IPark</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He represented Serbia initially at youth-level and is a <a href="/wiki/Serbian_Australians" title="Serbian Australians">Serbian Australian</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aleksandar_%C5%A0u%C5%A1njar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Perth" title="Perth">Perth</a>, Susnjar made his senior debut in Europe, playing with Lithuanian side <a href="/wiki/FK_Ekranas" title="FK Ekranas">Ekranas</a> in <a href="/wiki/A_Lyga" title="A Lyga">A Lyga</a> seasons <a href="/wiki/2013_A_Lyga" title="2013 A Lyga">2013</a> and <a href="/wiki/2014_A_Lyga" title="2014 A Lyga">2014</a>. Then he returned to Australia and played with <a href="/wiki/Perth_SC" title="Perth SC">Perth SC</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2015_National_Premier_Leagues" title="2015 National Premier Leagues">2015 National Premier Leagues</a>. Next he signed with <a href="/wiki/FK_Jonava" title="FK Jonava">Lietava Jonava</a> playing with them in the <a href="/wiki/2016_A_Lyga" title="2016 A Lyga">2016 A Lyga</a>. Romanian <a href="/wiki/Liga_I" title="Liga I">Liga I</a> club <a href="/wiki/CS_Gaz_Metan_Media%C8%99" title="CS Gaz Metan Mediaș">Gaz Metan Mediaș</a> signed him and Susnjar played with them until the winter-break when he moved to Czech Republic and joined <a href="/wiki/FK_Teplice" title="FK Teplice">FK Teplice</a>. A year later, another <a href="/wiki/Czech_First_League" title="Czech First League">Czech First League</a> signed him, <a href="/wiki/FK_Mlad%C3%A1_Boleslav" title="FK Mladá Boleslav">Mladá Boleslav</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nft_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-nft-3">[3]</a></sup> On 24 July 2018, Susnjar joined <a href="/wiki/M%C5%A0K_%C5%BDilina" title="MŠK Žilina">MŠK Žilina</a> on a six-month loan deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Susnjar then moved to <a href="/wiki/Busan_IPark" title="Busan IPark">Busan IPark</a> in South Korea's <a href="/wiki/K_League" title="K League">K League</a> on 28 February 2019.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aleksandar_%C5%A0u%C5%A1njar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Susnjar initially represented his country of origin, Serbia, having made one appearance in 2011 for the <a href="/wiki/Serbia_national_under-17_football_team" title="Serbia national under-17 football team">Serbian U-17</a> side. However, by January 2018, he already called the attention of the Australian FA and made a debut for the <a href="/wiki/Australia_national_under-23_soccer_team" title="Australia national under-23 soccer team">Australian U23</a> team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>Susnjar received his first senior call up when he was selected in <a href="/wiki/Bert_van_Marwijk" title="Bert van Marwijk">Bert van Marwijk</a>'s first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Socceroos" title="Socceroos">Socceroos</a> squad for the March 2018 friendlies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He made the debut for the <a href="/wiki/Australia_national_soccer_team" title="Australia national soccer team">Australian national team</a> in a friendly game against Norway played on 23 March 2018.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nft_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-nft-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aleksandar_%C5%A0u%C5%A1njar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In addition to his native English, Susnjar can also speak <a href="/wiki/Serbian_language" title="Serbian language">Serbian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lithuanian_language" title="Lithuanian language">Lithuanian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Czech_language" title="Czech language">Czech</a> and <a href="/wiki/Romanian_language" title="Romanian language">Romanian</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He has been often confused<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> with same named Serbian footballer, who previously played with <a href="/wiki/FK_Donji_Srem" title="FK Donji Srem">Donji Srem</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Serbian_First_League" title="Serbian First League">Serbian First League</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> along with older brother, Milan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p>
