<p><b>Peter Reulein</b> (born 1966) is a German composer, organ improviser, academic teacher and church musician, from 2000 at the church <a href="/wiki/Liebfrauen,_Frankfurt" title="Liebfrauen, Frankfurt">Liebfrauen</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frankfurt_am_Main" title="Frankfurt am Main">Frankfurt am Main</a>. In 2016 he composed for the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Limburg" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Limburg">Catholic Diocese of Limburg</a> the Franciscan <a href="/wiki/Oratorio" title="Oratorio">oratorio</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Laudato_si%27_(oratorio)" title="Laudato si' (oratorio)">Laudato si'</a></i>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Peter_Reulein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in Frankfurt am Main, Reulein studied Catholic church music at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hochschule_f%C3%BCr_Musik_und_Darstellende_Kunst_Frankfurt_am_Main" title="Hochschule für Musik und Darstellende Kunst Frankfurt am Main">Hochschule für Musik und Darstellende Kunst Frankfurt am Main</a> with <a href="/wiki/Wolfgang_Sch%C3%A4fer" title="Wolfgang Schäfer">Wolfgang Schäfer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Uwe_Gronostay" title="Uwe Gronostay">Uwe Gronostay</a>. After extended studies of organ improvisation with <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Roth_(organist)" title="Daniel Roth (organist)">Daniel Roth</a> in Paris, he won several national and international competitions in this field,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gottwals_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gottwals-1">[1]</a></sup> for example in 1993 the competition of Belgian radio and the second prize and the public's prize at the <a href="/wiki/Festival_Europ%C3%A4ische_Kirchenmusik" title="Festival Europäische Kirchenmusik">Festival Europäische Kirchenmusik</a> in <a href="/wiki/Schw%C3%A4bisch_Gm%C3%BCnd" title="Schwäbisch Gmünd">Schwäbisch Gmünd</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Liebfrauen_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Liebfrauen-2">[2]</a></sup> He continued his studies of interpretations with <a href="/wiki/Ludger_Lohmann" title="Ludger Lohmann">Ludger Lohmann</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ewald_Kooiman" title="Ewald Kooiman">Ewald Kooiman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wolfgang_R%C3%BCbsam" title="Wolfgang Rübsam">Wolfgang Rübsam</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Liebfrauen_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Liebfrauen-2">[2]</a></sup> He took master classes with choral conductors such as <a href="/wiki/Eric_Ericson" title="Eric Ericson">Eric Ericson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Helmuth_Rilling" title="Helmuth Rilling">Helmuth Rilling</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Orgelmeile_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Orgelmeile-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>From 1991, Reulein was the church musician at the <a href="/wiki/Heilig_Geist,_Frankfurt" title="Heilig Geist, Frankfurt">Heilig Geist</a> in Frankfurt-Riederwald, from 2000 he has held the position at <a href="/wiki/Liebfrauen,_Frankfurt" title="Liebfrauen, Frankfurt">Liebfrauen</a> in the centre of Frankfurt. There he directs a vocal ensemble, the choir Collegium Vocale, the orchestra Collegium Musicum and the youth choir Capuccinis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Liebfrauen_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Liebfrauen-2">[2]</a></sup> He was instrumental in having a new organ built which <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Karl_G%C3%B6ckel&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Karl Göckel (page does not exist)">Karl Göckel</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> [<a class="extiw" href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karl_G%C3%B6ckel" title="de:Karl Göckel">de</a>]</span> completed in 2008. Reulein inaugurated it in a concert on 9 August, playing works by Bach, <a href="/wiki/C%C3%A9sar_Franck" title="César Franck">César Franck</a> and improvisation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Arning_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Arning-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Reulein is known for composing new songs for church services (<i lang="de" title="German language text"><a href="/wiki/Neues_Geistliches_Lied" title="Neues Geistliches Lied">Neues Geistliches Lied</a></i>), many of them on texts by <a href="/wiki/Eugen_Eckert" title="Eugen Eckert">Eugen Eckert</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Liebfrauen_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Liebfrauen-2">[2]</a></sup> Reulein was from 2000<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Frank_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Frank-5">[5]</a></sup> until 2005 the head of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arbeitskreis_Kirchenmusik_und_Jugendseelsorge_im_Bistum_Limburg" title="Arbeitskreis Kirchenmusik und Jugendseelsorge im Bistum Limburg">Arbeitskreis Kirchenmusik und Jugendseelsorge im Bistum Limburg</a>, caring about more appropriate church music for young people. Reulein has been an instructor of liturgical organ playing and improvisation at the Frankfurt Hochschule für Musik und Darstellende Kunst from 2002. He was appointed <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bezirkskantor&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bezirkskantor (page does not exist)">Bezirkskantor</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> [<a class="extiw" href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bezirkskantor" title="de:Bezirkskantor">de</a>]</span> (regional cantor) for Frankfurt in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Liebfrauen_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-Liebfrauen-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Limburg_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Limburg-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2014, Reulein recorded at Liebfrauen a CD of <i>Französische Orgelsymphonik</i> (French symphonic organ music) with works by <a href="/wiki/L%C3%A9on_Bo%C3%ABllmann" title="Léon Boëllmann">Léon Boëllmann</a>, <a href="/wiki/Camille_Saint-Sa%C3%ABns" title="Camille Saint-Saëns">Camille Saint-Saëns</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alexandre_Guilmant" title="Alexandre Guilmant">Alexandre Guilmant</a> and <a href="/wiki/Charles-Marie_Widor" title="Charles-Marie Widor">Charles-Marie Widor</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CD_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-CD-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:292px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Limburger_Dom_Oratorium_Laudato_si_06112016.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2448" data-file-width="3264" decoding="async" height="218" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0a/Limburger_Dom_Oratorium_Laudato_si_06112016.jpg/290px-Limburger_Dom_Oratorium_Laudato_si_06112016.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0a/Limburger_Dom_Oratorium_Laudato_si_06112016.jpg/435px-Limburger_Dom_Oratorium_Laudato_si_06112016.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0a/Limburger_Dom_Oratorium_Laudato_si_06112016.jpg/580px-Limburger_Dom_Oratorium_Laudato_si_06112016.jpg 2x" width="290"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Limburger_Dom_Oratorium_Laudato_si_06112016.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Premiere of <i><a href="/wiki/Laudato_si%27_(oratorio)" title="Laudato si' (oratorio)">Laudato si'</a></i> on 6 November 2016 at the Limburg Cathedral, conducted by the composer</div></div></div>
<p>He was commissioned to compose an <a href="/wiki/Oratorio" title="Oratorio">oratorio</a> to celebrate in 2016 the 50th anniversary of church music in the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Limburg" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Limburg">Catholic Diocese of Limburg</a>, presenting different styles of church music. The text by <a href="/wiki/Helmut_Schlegel" title="Helmut Schlegel">Helmut Schlegel</a>, titled <i><a href="/wiki/Laudato_si%27_(oratorio)" title="Laudato si' (oratorio)">Laudato si' – Ein franziskanisches Magnificat</a></i> (A <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Friars_Minor" title="Order of Friars Minor">Franciscan</a> <a href="/wiki/Magnificat" title="Magnificat">Magnificat</a>) includes the Latin Magnificat, writings by <a href="/wiki/Francis_of_Assisi" title="Francis of Assisi">Francis of Assisi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Clare_of_Assisi" title="Clare of Assisi">Clare of Assisi</a>, writings by <a href="/wiki/Pope_Francis" title="Pope Francis">Pope Francis</a> from the German version of <i><a href="/wiki/Laudato_si%27" title="Laudato si'">Laudato si'</a></i> to German, and other sacred texts. Reulein scored the work for five soloists, children's choir, <a href="/wiki/Choralschola" title="Choralschola">Choralschola</a>, mixed choir, organ and orchestra. It was published by the <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Dehm" title="Patrick Dehm">Dehm-Verlag</a> in Limburg in 2016 and first performed in the <a href="/wiki/Limburg_Cathedral" title="Limburg Cathedral">Limburg Cathedral</a> on 6 November 2016, conducted by the composer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Laudato_si_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Laudato_si-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
