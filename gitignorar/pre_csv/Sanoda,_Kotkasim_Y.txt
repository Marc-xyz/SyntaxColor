<p><b>Sanoda</b> is a village in <a href="/wiki/Kotkasim" title="Kotkasim">Kotkasim</a> tehsil  <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alwar_District" title="Alwar District">Alwar District</a> in the state of <a href="/wiki/Rajasthan" title="Rajasthan">Rajasthan</a>, India.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Sanoda is a part of the <a href="/wiki/Ahirwal" title="Ahirwal">Ahirwal</a> region and about 75 percent of the total population are <a href="/wiki/Yadav" title="Yadav">Yadav</a>. Most of the Yadavs belong to the gotra (clan) of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pachwaniya&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Pachwaniya (page does not exist)">Pachwaniya</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dagar_(clan)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dagar (clan) (page does not exist)">Dagar</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sanoda,_Kotkasim&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sanoda has higher literacy rate compared to Rajasthan. In 2011, literacy rate of Sanoda village was 76.49% compared to 66.11% of Rajasthan. In Sanoda Ahir Male literacy stands at 91.62% while female literacy rate was 60.12%.
</p>
<ul><li>Govt UP School (GUPS) Sanoda Ahir, <a href="/wiki/Kotkasim" title="Kotkasim">Kotkasim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alwar" title="Alwar">Alwar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rajasthan" title="Rajasthan">Rajasthan</a>.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Location">Location</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sanoda,_Kotkasim&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Location">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sanoda is located in the National Capital Region, 130 kilometres south of <a href="/wiki/Delhi" title="Delhi">Delhi</a>, 145 kilometres north of state capital <a href="/wiki/Jaipur" title="Jaipur">Jaipur</a>, 60 kilometres north of <a href="/wiki/Alwar" title="Alwar">Alwar</a> city, 30 kilometres east of <a href="/wiki/Rewari" title="Rewari">Rewari</a> city, 25 kilometres south of <a href="/wiki/Dharuhera" title="Dharuhera">Dharuhera</a>, 25 kilometres south of <a href="/wiki/Bhiwadi" title="Bhiwadi">Bhiwadi</a> and 22 kilometres west of <a href="/wiki/Tijara" title="Tijara">Tijara</a>, 20 kilometres north  <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kishangarh,_Alwar" title="Kishangarh, Alwar">Kishangarh Bas</a>, 5 kilometres south of <a href="/wiki/Kotkasim" title="Kotkasim">Kotkasim</a>, 4 kilometres north of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bibirani&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bibirani (page does not exist)">Bibirani</a>, another town in Alwar district.
</p><p>Villages near Sanoda are Pur(1.8 km), <a href="/wiki/Khanpur_Dagran" title="Khanpur Dagran">Khanpur Dagran</a>(2 km), Gheekaka(1.5 km),  Chachiyawas (1 km) <a href="/wiki/Moonpur_Thakran" title="Moonpur Thakran">Moonpur Thakran</a>(1.2 km), Jalaka(1 km).
It is on the border of the Alwar District and Rewari District. Rewari District Bawal is west towards this place . It is near to the Haryana State Border.

<p>Sanoda is a medium size village located in Kotkasim of Alwar district, Rajasthan with total 145 families residing. The Sanoda village has population of 789 of which 422 are males while 367 are females as per the 2011 census.
</p><p>The population of children with age 0-6 is 100 which makes up 12.67% of total population of village. Average Sex Ratio of Sanoda village is 870 which is lower than Rajasthan state average of 928. Child Sex Ratio for the Sanoda Ahir as per census is 563, lower than Rajasthan average of 888.
</p><p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scheduled_Castes" title="Scheduled Castes">Scheduled Castes</a> constituted 17.40% while <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scheduled_Tribes" title="Scheduled Tribes">Scheduled Tribes</a> were 0.51% of total population.
</p>
