		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:179px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:G._van_Hall.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="217" data-file-width="177" decoding="async" height="217" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/11/G._van_Hall.JPG" width="177"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:G._van_Hall.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Gijs van Hall, November 25, 1968</div></div></div>
<p><b>Gijsbert van Hall</b> (21 April 1904, in <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a> – 22 May 1977, in Amsterdam) was a <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a> politician and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mayor_of_Amsterdam" title="Mayor of Amsterdam">Mayor of Amsterdam</a> between 1957 and 1967. His brother, <a href="/wiki/Walraven_van_Hall" title="Walraven van Hall">Walraven van Hall</a>, was a banker and resistance leader during the <a href="/wiki/Reichskommissariat_Niederlande" title="Reichskommissariat Niederlande">German occupation</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> in <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. Both were born into a prominent banking family in <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>, with Gijs subject to infections of <a href="/wiki/Tuberculosis" title="Tuberculosis">tuberculosis</a> in his younger years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HALL,_Gijsbert_van_(1904-1977)_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-HALL,_Gijsbert_van_(1904-1977)-1">[1]</a></sup> He joined Kean Taylor &amp; Co. in 1928, then became Director of Labouchère and Co. in 1948. He became a senator in 1956 before being elected mayor the following year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mr._G._(Gijs)_van_Hall_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mr._G._(Gijs)_van_Hall-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
