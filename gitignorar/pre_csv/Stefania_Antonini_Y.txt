<p><b>Stefania Antonini </b> (born 10 October 1970) is an Italian former <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who played as a <a href="/wiki/Goalkeeper_(association_football)" title="Goalkeeper (association football)">goalkeeper</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Italy_women%27s_national_football_team" title="Italy women's national football team">Italy women's national football team</a>. She was the national team's first choice goalkeeper at the <a href="/wiki/1991_FIFA_Women%27s_World_Cup" title="1991 FIFA Women's World Cup">1991 FIFA Women's World Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wc_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-wc-1">[1]</a></sup> At club level she collected five <a href="/wiki/Serie_A_(women%27s_football)" title="Serie A (women's football)">women's Serie A</a> winner's medals and three <a href="/wiki/Italian_Women%27s_Cup" title="Italian Women's Cup">Coppa Italia</a> winner's medals.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stefania_Antonini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Swiss-born Antonini began playing football at 12 years old when her family returned to Italy and settled in <a href="/wiki/Teramo" title="Teramo">Teramo</a>. She became Ascoli's starting goalkeeper aged 13 and helped the club to <a href="/wiki/Promotion_and_relegation" title="Promotion and relegation">promotion</a> from Serie C to Serie A. In 1989 she made her first transfer to <a href="/wiki/A.S.D._Reggiana_Calcio_Femminile" title="A.S.D. Reggiana Calcio Femminile">Zambelli Reggiana</a>, where an experienced team containing <a href="/wiki/Carolina_Morace" title="Carolina Morace">Carolina Morace</a>, <a href="/wiki/Elisabetta_Vignotto" title="Elisabetta Vignotto">Elisabetta Vignotto</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anne_O%27Brien_(footballer)" title="Anne O'Brien (footballer)">Anne O'Brien</a> captured Antonini's first league title. She won a further four league titles and three national cups but retired at 29 years old, dissatisfied that her teams kept going bust and unhappy with the general lack of career prospects on offer for elite female footballers in Italy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dizionario_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-dizionario-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stefania_Antonini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Antonini was drafted into the <a href="/wiki/Italy_women%27s_national_football_team" title="Italy women's national football team">Italy women's national football team</a> in 1990, following good form for title-winning Reggiana and the suspension of <a href="/wiki/Eva_Russo" title="Eva Russo">Eva Russo</a>. She made her debut in August 1990 in a 4–1 win over <a href="/wiki/England_women%27s_national_football_team" title="England women's national football team">England</a>, played at <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium_(1923)" title="Wembley Stadium (1923)">Wembley Stadium</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Curtain_raiser" title="Curtain raiser">curtain raiser</a> for the <a href="/wiki/1990_FA_Charity_Shield" title="1990 FA Charity Shield">1990 FA Charity Shield</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-figc_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-figc-3">[3]</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/1991_FIFA_Women%27s_World_Cup" title="1991 FIFA Women's World Cup">1991 FIFA Women's World Cup</a>, Antonini was ever-present as Italy reached the quarter-final and lost 3–2 to <a href="/wiki/Norway_women%27s_national_football_team" title="Norway women's national football team">Norway</a> after <a href="/wiki/Overtime_(sports)" title="Overtime (sports)">extra time</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wc_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-wc-1">[1]</a></sup> At <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Women%27s_Euro_1993" title="UEFA Women's Euro 1993">UEFA Women's Euro 1993</a> hosts Italy reached the final and suffered another defeat by Norway, 1–0 this time. Antonini had been replaced in the team for the tournament by <a href="/wiki/Giorgia_Brenzan" title="Giorgia Brenzan">Giorgia Brenzan</a>, with whom she enjoyed a friendly rivalry for the national team goalkeeper position.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dizionario_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-dizionario-2">[2]</a></sup> She returned to the number one role for <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Women%27s_Euro_1995" title="UEFA Women's Euro 1995">UEFA Women's Euro 1995</a>, but yet another defeat by Norway in the second-round cost the Italians a place at the <a href="/wiki/1995_FIFA_Women%27s_World_Cup" title="1995 FIFA Women's World Cup">1995 FIFA Women's World Cup</a>. In December 1996 Antonini won the last of her 32 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cap_(sports)" title="Cap (sports)">caps</a> in a 0–0 draw with <a href="/wiki/Germany_women%27s_national_football_team" title="Germany women's national football team">Germany</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-figc_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-figc-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Playing_style">Playing style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stefania_Antonini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Playing style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the <i>Dizionario del Calcio Italiano</i>, Antonini was a goalkeeper of rare talent. She was described as more instinctive and spectacular than her longstanding rival Giorgia Brenzan, and capable of making extraordinary acrobatic saves. However, her extroverted style sometimes left her prone to lapses in concentration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dizionario_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-dizionario-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
