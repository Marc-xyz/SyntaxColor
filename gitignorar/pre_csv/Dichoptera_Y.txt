<p><i><b>Dichoptera</b></i> is a genus of <a href="/wiki/Fulgoridae" title="Fulgoridae">plant-hoppers</a> found in tropical Asia. They were formerly placed in the family <a href="/wiki/Dictyopharidae" title="Dictyopharidae">Dictyopharidae</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Dichoptera6.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1652" data-file-width="1892" decoding="async" height="192" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Dichoptera6.jpg/220px-Dichoptera6.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Dichoptera6.jpg/330px-Dichoptera6.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Dichoptera6.jpg/440px-Dichoptera6.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Dichoptera6.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Head profile</div></div></div>
<p>They have large and stout bodies with long membranous forewings. The head is short and may have a long process. There are 11 species in the genus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Often found on the bark of <i>Ficus</i> trees, they are tended by ants and sometimes parasitized by <a href="/wiki/Dryinidae" title="Dryinidae">Dryinidae</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
