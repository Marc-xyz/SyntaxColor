<p><b>Sun Kil Moon</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Folk_rock" title="Folk rock">folk rock</a> act from <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco, California</a>, founded in 2002. Initially a continuation of the defunct <a href="/wiki/Indie_rock" title="Indie rock">indie rock</a> band <a href="/wiki/Red_House_Painters" title="Red House Painters">Red House Painters</a>, Sun Kil Moon is now the primary recording moniker of vocalist and guitarist <a href="/wiki/Mark_Kozelek" title="Mark Kozelek">Mark Kozelek</a>. The project is named after the Korean <a href="/wiki/Super_flyweight" title="Super flyweight">super flyweight</a> boxer <a href="/wiki/Moon_Sung-kil" title="Moon Sung-kil">Sung-Kil Moon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the delayed release of Red House Painters' final studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Old_Ramon" title="Old Ramon">Old Ramon</a></i> (2001), vocalist and guitarist Kozelek began recording new material under the name Sun Kil Moon with bandmates Anthony Koutsos (drums) and Jerry Vessel (bass), alongside Geoff Stanfield and <a href="/wiki/Tim_Mooney" title="Tim Mooney">Tim Mooney</a>. The resulting album, <i><a href="/wiki/Ghosts_of_the_Great_Highway" title="Ghosts of the Great Highway">Ghosts of the Great Highway</a></i>, was released in 2003 to widespread critical acclaim. The project's second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Tiny_Cities" title="Tiny Cities">Tiny Cities</a></i> (2005), was composed entirely of <a href="/wiki/Modest_Mouse" title="Modest Mouse">Modest Mouse</a> cover songs.
</p><p>In 2008, the band released its third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/April_(Sun_Kil_Moon_album)" title="April (Sun Kil Moon album)">April</a></i>. Recorded as a three-piece by Kozelek, Koutsos and Stanfield, the album was well received and featured guest vocal appearances from noted indie rock musicians <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Will_Oldham" title="Will Oldham">Will Oldham</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ben_Gibbard" title="Ben Gibbard">Ben Gibbard</a>.
</p><p>Inspired by <a href="/wiki/Classical_guitar" title="Classical guitar">classical guitar</a> music, Kozelek recorded Sun Kil Moon's fourth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Admiral_Fell_Promises" title="Admiral Fell Promises">Admiral Fell Promises</a></i> (2010), as a solo act, and continued to record mostly alone on its follow-up, <i><a href="/wiki/Among_the_Leaves" title="Among the Leaves">Among the Leaves</a></i> (2012). In 2013, Kozelek released three studio albums under his own name before releasing Sun Kil Moon's sixth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Benji_(album)" title="Benji (album)">Benji</a></i>, in February 2014, to widespread critical acclaim and increased exposure, with the band releasing their seventh studio effort, <i><a href="/wiki/Universal_Themes" title="Universal Themes">Universal Themes</a>,</i> in 2015. Sun Kil Moon's eighth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Common_as_Light_and_Love_Are_Red_Valleys_of_Blood" title="Common as Light and Love Are Red Valleys of Blood">Common as Light and Love Are Red Valleys of Blood</a></i>, was released in February 2017, which was followed by their ninth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/This_Is_My_Dinner" title="This Is My Dinner">This Is My Dinner</a>,</i> in November 2018. The tenth Sun Kil Moon studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/I_Also_Want_to_Die_in_New_Orleans" title="I Also Want to Die in New Orleans">I Also Want to Die in New Orleans</a></i>, was released digitally on March 1, 2019, and on CD on April 12, 2019.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sun_Kil_Moon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Following the dissolution of <a href="/wiki/Red_House_Painters" title="Red House Painters">Red House Painters</a> after the tumultuous release of their last album, <i><a href="/wiki/Old_Ramon" title="Old Ramon">Old Ramon</a></i>, Kozelek released a handful of solo recordings before forming Sun Kil Moon in 2002. The band's debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Ghosts_of_the_Great_Highway" title="Ghosts of the Great Highway">Ghosts of the Great Highway</a></i>, was released in November 2003 by <a href="/wiki/Jetset_Records" title="Jetset Records">Jetset Records</a>. It contains several songs concerned with true-life stories of deceased boxers, such as Mexican featherweight champion <a href="/wiki/Salvador_S%C3%A1nchez" title="Salvador Sánchez">Salvador Sánchez</a>, Filipino flyweight champion <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pancho_Villa_(boxer)" title="Pancho Villa (boxer)">Pancho Villa</a>, and also Korean boxer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Duk_Koo_Kim" title="Duk Koo Kim">Duk Koo Kim</a>, all three boxers died at the age of 23. The album was well received by critics, garnering an overall rating of 84/100 on <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>.com.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>The band's follow-up <i><a href="/wiki/Tiny_Cities" title="Tiny Cities">Tiny Cities</a></i> was released in November 2005 on Kozelek's own label <a href="/wiki/Caldo_Verde_Records" title="Caldo Verde Records">Caldo Verde Records</a>. The album covers eleven songs by the indie/alternative group <a href="/wiki/Modest_Mouse" title="Modest Mouse">Modest Mouse</a>. <i>Ghosts of the Great Highway</i> was re-issued as a double CD in February 2007 on Caldo Verde. The second disc features six bonus tracks, including a cover of "Somewhere" written by <a href="/wiki/Leonard_Bernstein" title="Leonard Bernstein">Leonard Bernstein</a>.
</p><p>Sun Kil Moon's third studio album <i><a href="/wiki/April_(Sun_Kil_Moon_album)" title="April (Sun Kil Moon album)">April</a></i> was released in April 2008<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SKM_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-SKM-5">[5]</a></sup> and features guest vocals from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Will_Oldham" title="Will Oldham">Will Oldham</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ben_Gibbard" title="Ben Gibbard">Ben Gibbard</a>. The album was issued as a double CD, featuring a bonus disc of alternate recordings of album tracks. The album was met with further critical acclaim, including a 4.5/5-star rating from <a href="/wiki/AllMusic" title="AllMusic">AllMusic</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and 4-star reviews from <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Mojo_(magazine)" title="Mojo (magazine)">Mojo</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Boston_Globe" title="The Boston Globe">The Boston Globe</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Drowned_in_Sound" title="Drowned in Sound">Drowned in Sound</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p><i><a href="/wiki/Admiral_Fell_Promises" title="Admiral Fell Promises">Admiral Fell Promises</a></i> was released on July 13, 2010,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SKM_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-SKM-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pitchfork_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pitchfork-8">[8]</a></sup> and whilst credited to Sun Kil Moon, only Kozelek plays on the record, on <a href="/wiki/Classical_guitar" title="Classical guitar">nylon stringed guitar</a>, and vocals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PM_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-PM-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p><i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Among_The_Leaves" title="Among The Leaves">Among The Leaves</a></i>, Sun Kil Moon's fifth studio album, was released on May 29, 2012. Tim Mooney, the band's former drummer, died in June 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>Sun Kil Moon's sixth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Benji_(album)" title="Benji (album)">Benji</a></i>, was released in February 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The record met with critical acclaim,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and was named in the Best of 2014 lists of <i><a href="/wiki/Fact_(UK_magazine)" title="Fact (UK magazine)">Fact</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-factaoty_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-factaoty-13">[13]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Stereogum" title="Stereogum">Stereogum</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stereogumaoty_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-stereogumaoty-14">[14]</a></sup> Pitchfork,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pitchforkaoty_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-pitchforkaoty-15">[15]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Rough_Trade_(shops)" title="Rough Trade (shops)">Rough Trade</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-roughtrade_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-roughtrade-16">[16]</a></sup> Drowned in Sound,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disaoty_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-disaoty-17">[17]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Tiny_Mix_Tapes" title="Tiny Mix Tapes">Tiny Mix Tapes</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tmtaoty_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-tmtaoty-18">[18]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/NME" title="NME">NME</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nmeaoty_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-nmeaoty-19">[19]</a></sup> Soundblab selected it as the best album of 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup>
</p><p>Sun Kil Moon's seventh album of original material, <i><a href="/wiki/Universal_Themes" title="Universal Themes">Universal Themes</a></i>, was released on June 2, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The album features contributions from drummer <a href="/wiki/Steve_Shelley" title="Steve Shelley">Steve Shelley</a>, who previously collaborated with Kozelek on the album, <i>Benji</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pitchforkannounce_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-pitchforkannounce-22">[22]</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 20, 2016 it was announced that Sun Kil Moon would release a new <a href="/wiki/Double_album" title="Double album">double album</a>, <i>Common as Light and Love Are Red Valleys of Blood</i>, on February 28, 2017. The announcement was accompanied by the release of a new song, <i>God Bless Ohio</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> The album was released on February 17, 2017 with <a href="/wiki/Caldo_Verde_Records" title="Caldo Verde Records">Caldo Verde Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Mark Kozelek subsequently toured <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>, and the United States, both as a solo act and with Sun Kil Moon, to promote the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup>
</p><p>On November 1, 2018, Sun Kil Moon released their ninth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/This_Is_My_Dinner" title="This Is My Dinner">This Is My Dinner</a></i>. The following year, their Tenth studio album was released, titled <i><a href="/wiki/I_Also_Want_to_Die_in_New_Orleans" title="I Also Want to Die in New Orleans">I Also Want to Die in New Orleans</a></i>, named after the <a href="/wiki/Suicideboys" title="Suicideboys">$uicideboy$</a> album <i><a href="/wiki/I_Want_to_Die_in_New_Orleans" title="I Want to Die in New Orleans">I Want to Die in New Orleans</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> This album saw Kozelek working with Donny McCaslin and Jim White, the latter being heavily present on <a href="/wiki/David_Bowie" title="David Bowie">David Bowie</a>'s 2016 album, <i><a href="/wiki/Blackstar_(album)" title="Blackstar (album)">Blackstar</a></i>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Boxing_references">Boxing references</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sun_Kil_Moon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Boxing references">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kozelek is fascinated with the sport of boxing, and The name Sun Kil Moon was inspired by that of boxer <a href="/wiki/Moon_Sung-kil" title="Moon Sung-kil">Moon Sung-kil</a>. Kozelek discussed boxing and the murder of <a href="/wiki/Agapito_S%C3%A1nchez" title="Agapito Sánchez">Agapito Sánchez</a> four years after a fight he witnessed in a 2012 interview:
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r886047036">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>Their backgrounds are extremely harsh and they work very hard to move up in their careers. I was in attendance at the <a href="/wiki/Manny_Pacquiao" title="Manny Pacquiao">Manny Pacquiao</a>-<a href="/wiki/Agapito_S%C3%A1nchez" title="Agapito Sánchez">Agapito Sánchez</a> fight in San Francisco in 2001. When I heard Sánchez was murdered shortly after, it had a profound effect on me. It hurts when anyone dies young, but when you see the backgrounds of these guys and the path they've taken to try to find some light in their lives, it hurts to see them die young.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TQR_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-TQR-27">[27]</a></sup>
</p></blockquote>
<p>Several of the band's songs are inspired by the sport, such as "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Duk_Koo_Kim" title="Duk Koo Kim">Duk Koo Kim</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TQR_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-TQR-27">[27]</a></sup> Duk Koo Kim was a Korean boxer who died after a nationally televised bout with <a href="/wiki/Ray_Mancini" title="Ray Mancini">Ray Mancini</a> on November 13, 1982. Mancini is from Youngstown, Ohio, which is very close to Kozelek's hometown of Massillon. The triumph of the local hero soon turned into a tragedy that included the suicide deaths of both Kim's mother and the bout's referee, <a href="/wiki/Richard_Green_(referee)" title="Richard Green (referee)">Richard Green</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> The song "Pancho Villa" is named after <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Guilledo" title="Francisco Guilledo">the Filipino boxer known by that name</a>, and also mentions <a href="/wiki/Salvador_S%C3%A1nchez" title="Salvador Sánchez">Salvador Sánchez</a> and <a href="/wiki/Benny_Paret" title="Benny Paret">Benny Paret</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup>.
</p>
