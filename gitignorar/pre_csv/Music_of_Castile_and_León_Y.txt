<p>Central <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> includes the cultural melting pot of <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a> and <a href="/wiki/Castile_(historical_region)" title="Castile (historical region)">Castile</a>.  A down-tempo version of <a href="/wiki/Jota_(music)" title="Jota (music)">jota</a> is common, as well as other dances as <a href="/wiki/Fandango" title="Fandango">fandango</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Habas_verdes&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Habas verdes (page does not exist)">habas verdes</a>, 5/8 <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Charrada&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Charrada (page does not exist)">charrada</a>.  <a href="/wiki/Bagpipes" title="Bagpipes">Bagpipes</a> are still used in northern <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Le%C3%B3n" title="Province of León">León</a> and <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Zamora" title="Province of Zamora">Zamora</a> provinces.  <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tabor_pipe" title="Tabor pipe">Tabor pipe</a> (in León) and <i>dulzaina</i> (<a href="/wiki/Shawm" title="Shawm">shawm</a>) enjoy rich repertoires.
The city of <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a> is known for keeping its own version of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chotis" title="Chotis">chotis</a> music.  <a href="/wiki/Salamanca" title="Salamanca">Salamanca</a> is home to <a href="/wiki/Tuna_(music)" title="Tuna (music)">tuna</a>, a form of <a href="/wiki/Serenade" title="Serenade">serenade</a> played on <a href="/wiki/Guitar" title="Guitar">guitar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bandurria" title="Bandurria">bandurria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tambourine" title="Tambourine">tambourine</a>, traditionally by students in medieval clothing.
