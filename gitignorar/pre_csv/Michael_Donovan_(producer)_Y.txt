<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Donovan_(producer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Salter Street Films</b>
</p><p>Michael Donovan co-founded <a href="/wiki/Salter_Street_Films" title="Salter Street Films">Salter Street Films</a> with his brother <a href="/wiki/Paul_Donovan_(writer)" title="Paul Donovan (writer)">Paul Donovan</a> and Charles Bishop in 1983. Though the company got off to a slow start, Donovan eventually had major success with projects such as the Canadian satirical comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/This_Hour_Has_22_Minutes" title="This Hour Has 22 Minutes">This Hour Has 22 Minutes</a></i>.
</p><p>Salter Street Films was acquired by <a href="/wiki/Alliance_Atlantis" title="Alliance Atlantis">Alliance Atlantis</a> in 2001, and was closed by them two years later, in 2003.
</p><p><b>Halifax Film Company / DHX Media</b>
</p><p>In May 2004, after Alliance Atlantis closed Salter Street Films, Michael Donovan founded <a href="/wiki/WildBrain_Studios" title="WildBrain Studios">Halifax Film Company</a> with many of the former Salter Street employees.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2006, Halifax Film Company merged with Toronto, Ontario-based Decode Entertainment to become <a href="/wiki/WildBrain" title="WildBrain">DHX Media</a>, where Donovan served as the company's CEO from 2006-2014 and again from 2018-2019, and as Executive Chair of the company from 2014-2019.
</p><p>In August 2019, Donovan stepped down from being CEO and Executive Chair of DHX Media, which rebranded as <a href="/wiki/WildBrain" title="WildBrain">WildBrain</a> the following month. He remained as a founding chair on the board of directors until October 17, 2019, when he stepped down from the board of directors and left the company. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_nominations">Awards and nominations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Donovan_(producer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Awards and nominations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Donovan won an <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Documentary_Feature" title="Academy Award for Best Documentary Feature">Academy Award for Best Documentary Feature</a> for producing the 2002 documentary <i><a href="/wiki/Bowling_for_Columbine" title="Bowling for Columbine">Bowling for Columbine</a>.</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>  He was nominated for a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Genie_Award_for_Best_Screenplay" title="Genie Award for Best Screenplay">Genie Award for Best Screenplay</a> for writing the 2007 film <i><a href="/wiki/Shake_Hands_with_the_Devil_(2007_film)" title="Shake Hands with the Devil (2007 film)">Shake Hands with the Devil</a>.</i>
</p>
