<p><b>Mat Dan</b> (born <b>Daniel Tyler</b>, 18 October 1989)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hijabista_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-hijabista-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup> also known by his Muslim name <b>Mohammad Kareef Daniel Abdullah</b> is an English traveller and host has been lived in <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a> since 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He first settled in Malaysia after his adventures to <a href="/wiki/Kapas_Island" title="Kapas Island">Kapas Island</a>. He is known for his eloquence speaking Terengganu dialect pretty well. He is also better known in television about his adventures with his friends entitled <i>Haramain Backpackers - Trans Siberian</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thestar_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-thestar-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2018, he has been officially granted permanent resident status by then the Deputy Prime Minister Datuk Seri Dr <a href="/wiki/Ahmad_Zahid_Hamidi" title="Ahmad Zahid Hamidi">Ahmad Zahid Hamidi</a>, who publicly announced his new status at the launch of Expo Terengganu 2018 on Monday (February 26).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He is married with local Terengganu lass.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mat_Dan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mat_Dan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Daniel Tyler was born in <a href="/wiki/Bristol" title="Bristol">Bristol</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> on 18 October 1989<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup> as a second child from three siblings. His younger brother is Will Tyler.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metro20180629_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-metro20180629-8">[8]</a></sup> He is the son of Kevin, a businessman, and Nicol Tyler, an administrator.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thestar_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-thestar-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Emigrant_to_Malaysia">Emigrant to Malaysia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mat_Dan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Emigrant to Malaysia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2009, he went on a trip to <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a> and <a href="/wiki/Laos" title="Laos">Laos</a> with a friend<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gegar_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-gegar-9">[9]</a></sup> after completing his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A-level" title="A-level">A-level</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thestar_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-thestar-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-utusan_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-utusan-10">[10]</a></sup> At that time, his friend had suggested that he visit the <a href="/wiki/Perhentian_Islands" title="Perhentian Islands">Perhentian Island</a>. When he got there, he learned about <a href="/wiki/Kapas_Island" title="Kapas Island">Kapas Island</a>. 10 days after residing in the island,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-star20180315_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-star20180315-11">[11]</a></sup> he came to Kapas Island. As long as he was there, he became a volunteer in a chalet. He took care of the reception desk and cleaned the guest room for four months as a retiree before proceeding. He returned to Pulau Kapas in 2010<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-star20180315_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-star20180315-11">[11]</a></sup> then settled for over 7 years there on the grounds of the good service of Malaysians and a beautiful place that caused him to remain on the island.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hijabista_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-hijabista-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thestar_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-thestar-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-utusan_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-utusan-10">[10]</a></sup> There he learned the way of living and the language of the locals with the mastery of the Terengganu dialect which took 3 to 4 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gegar_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-gegar-9">[9]</a></sup> On February 26, 2018, Tyler received the status of Malaysian Permanent Resident (PR) from the then Malaysian Deputy Prime Minister, Dato' Seri <a href="/wiki/Ahmad_Zahid_Hamidi" title="Ahmad Zahid Hamidi">Ahmad Zahid Hamidi</a> at the opening of Terengganu Expo 2018 at the compound of the Terengganu Sports Complex.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Embracing_Islam">Embracing Islam</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mat_Dan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Embracing Islam">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On January 2013, he convert to <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a> with the help from his adopted brother, Zakaria Sulong,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-utusan_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-utusan-10">[10]</a></sup> and changed his name to Mohammad Kareef Daniel Abdullah.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ambassador">Ambassador</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mat_Dan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Ambassador">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 22 October 2016, he was appointed as Terengganu State Environmental Ambassador in conjunction with the State Level National State Day at <a href="/wiki/Kerteh" title="Kerteh">Kerteh</a> by the Malaysian Department of Environment (DOE).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mat_Dan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He engaged and married Nadifa Adnan (born 15 April 1989),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> a local Malay girl,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> on March 12, 2017 at his wife's home in Bukit Chendering, Terengganu.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup>
</p><p>He has a son born on May 5, 2018 at 2.7 kilograms at 6.43 pm <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Malaysian_Standard_Time" title="Malaysian Standard Time">Malaysian Standard Time</a> at the Salam Specialist Hospital and was named as Muhammad Zayne Danial James Tyler.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metro20180629_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-metro20180629-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup>
</p>
