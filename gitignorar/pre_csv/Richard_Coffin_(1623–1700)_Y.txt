	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Richard Coffin (1623–1700)</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:CoffinOfMonkleighArms.PNG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1066" data-file-width="928" decoding="async" height="253" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/66/CoffinOfMonkleighArms.PNG/220px-CoffinOfMonkleighArms.PNG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/66/CoffinOfMonkleighArms.PNG/330px-CoffinOfMonkleighArms.PNG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/66/CoffinOfMonkleighArms.PNG/440px-CoffinOfMonkleighArms.PNG 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:CoffinOfMonkleighArms.PNG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Arms of Coffin: <i>Azure, three <a href="/wiki/Bezant" title="Bezant">bezants</a> between eight <a href="/wiki/Cross" title="Cross">crosses crosslet</a> or</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Often shown with varying number of crosses, in which case: <i>Azure semé of <a href="/wiki/Cross" title="Cross">crosses crosslet</a> or, three bezants</i></div></div></div>
<p><b>Richard Coffin</b> (1623–1700) of <a href="/wiki/Portledge_Manor" title="Portledge Manor">Portledge</a> in the parish of <a href="/wiki/Alwington" title="Alwington">Alwington</a> in North Devon, was <a href="/wiki/Lord_of_the_manor" title="Lord of the manor">lord of the manor</a> of <a href="/wiki/Alwington" title="Alwington">Alwington</a> and served as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sheriff_of_Devon" title="Sheriff of Devon">Sheriff of Devon</a> in 1683.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">Origins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Coffin_(1623%E2%80%931700)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:HenryHurdinge_Died1627_MonkleighChurch_Devon.PNG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2405" data-file-width="2110" decoding="async" height="251" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c1/HenryHurdinge_Died1627_MonkleighChurch_Devon.PNG/220px-HenryHurdinge_Died1627_MonkleighChurch_Devon.PNG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c1/HenryHurdinge_Died1627_MonkleighChurch_Devon.PNG/330px-HenryHurdinge_Died1627_MonkleighChurch_Devon.PNG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c1/HenryHurdinge_Died1627_MonkleighChurch_Devon.PNG/440px-HenryHurdinge_Died1627_MonkleighChurch_Devon.PNG 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:HenryHurdinge_Died1627_MonkleighChurch_Devon.PNG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Mural monument to Henry Hurding (d.1627), Monkleigh Church. Elizabeth Hurding, the mother of Richard Coffin, is one of his four daughters shown kneeling behind his two wives at right</div></div></div>
<p>He was the only son and heir of John Coffin (1593-1622) of Portledge by his wife Elizabeth Hurding, one of the two eldest daughters of Henry Hurding (d.1627) (<i>alias</i> Harding<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>) of <a href="/wiki/Long_Bredy" title="Long Bredy">Long Bredy</a>, Dorset,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> by his first wife Gertrude Bampfield, 6th daughter of <a href="/wiki/Richard_Bampfield" title="Richard Bampfield">Richard Bampfield</a> (1526–1594) of <a href="/wiki/Manor_of_Poltimore" title="Manor of Poltimore">Poltimore</a> and Bampfylde House in Exeter, both in Devon, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sheriff_of_Devon" title="Sheriff of Devon">Sheriff of Devon</a> in 1576 and ancestor of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bampfylde_Baronets" title="Bampfylde Baronets">Bampfylde Baronets</a> and <a href="/wiki/Baron_Poltimore" title="Baron Poltimore">Barons Poltimore</a>. Her brother was  Sir <a href="/wiki/Amyas_Bampfylde" title="Amyas Bampfylde">Amias Bampfield</a>, MP.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The mural monument to Henry Hurding survives in Monkleigh Church, Devon, on which are depicted his four daughters. Elizabeth Hurding survived her husband and remarried to Hugh Prust (d.1666)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> of Gorven in the parish of <a href="/wiki/Hartland,_Devon" title="Hartland, Devon">Hartland</a> and of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Annery" title="Annery">Annery</a> in the parish of <a href="/wiki/Monkleigh" title="Monkleigh">Monkleigh</a>, both in North Devon. His four times great-grandfather was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Richard_Coffin_(1456-1523)" title="Richard Coffin (1456-1523)">Richard Coffin</a> (1456-1523)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> of <a href="/wiki/Alwington" title="Alwington">Alwington</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heanton_Punchardon" title="Heanton Punchardon">Heanton Punchardon</a> in North Devon, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sheriff_of_Devon" title="Sheriff of Devon">Sheriff of Devon</a> in 1511.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The Coffin family had been established at Alwington since the reign of King Henry II (1154-1189), and remained there in unbroken male succession until 1766.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="Marriages_.26_progeny"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriages_&amp;_progeny">Marriages &amp; progeny</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Coffin_(1623%E2%80%931700)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Marriages &amp; progeny">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He married thrice:
</p>
<ul><li>Firstly in 1644 to Mary Dennis (d.pre-1648), of the family of Dennis of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orleigh" title="Orleigh">Orleigh</a>, North Devon, near Alwington, without progeny.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
<li>Secondly in 1648 at Monkleigh, to Dorothy Rowe (d.1666),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> by whom he had progeny 2 sons and 9 daughters:
<ul><li>John Coffin (1649-1704), eldest son and heir, of Alwington, baptised at Monkleigh. He died without progeny four years after his father and was succeeded by his youngest half-brother <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Richard_Coffin_(1684-1766)" title="Richard Coffin (1684-1766)">Richard Coffin</a> (1684-1766), a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a> for <a href="/wiki/Barnstaple_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Barnstaple (UK Parliament constituency)">Barnstaple</a> in North Devon in 1727<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> who also died without progeny and was the last surviving male member of the Coffin family.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></li>
<li>Richard Coffin (1659-1660), 2nd son, died an infant, buried at Alwington.</li>
<li>Dorothy Coffin (1651-1690), eldest daughter, who in 1672 married Edward Pyne (1648-1675) of <a href="/wiki/East_Down,_Devon" title="East Down, Devon">East Down</a> and of Chelfham in the parish of <a href="/wiki/Bratton_Fleming" title="Bratton Fleming">Bratton Fleming</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> both in North Devon, whose grandson Rev. John Pine-Coffin (1735-1824) in 1796 inherited the Coffin estates at Alwington and elsewhere on the death of Richard Bennett-Coffin (d.1796), the grandson of her brother <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Richard_Coffin_(1684-1766)" title="Richard Coffin (1684-1766)">Richard Coffin</a> (1684-1766). Her descendants remained seated at East Down manor house until 1866, the Pyne family having occupied it since the 13th century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The last in the Pine-Coffin family to occupy Portledge manor house was <a href="/wiki/Richard_Geoffrey_Pine-Coffin" title="Richard Geoffrey Pine-Coffin">Richard Geoffrey Pine-Coffin</a> (1908-1974).</li>
<li>Elizabeth Coffin (1654-1654), 2nd daughter, died an infant.</li>
<li>Katherine I Coffin (1654-1682), 3rd daughter, 1st wife of Giles Risdon (d.1697) of <a href="/wiki/Bableigh,_Parkham" title="Bableigh, Parkham">Bableigh, Parkham</a> in North Devon, the birthplace of the Devon historian <a href="/wiki/Tristram_Risdon" title="Tristram Risdon">Tristram Risdon</a> (d.1640), whose grandfather was their shared ancestor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></li>
<li>Elizabeth II Coffin (1656-1666), 4th daughter, died aged 10.</li></ul></li></ul>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:JaneCoffin_Died1717_HuishChurch_Devon.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3036" data-file-width="2154" decoding="async" height="310" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/JaneCoffin_Died1717_HuishChurch_Devon.png/220px-JaneCoffin_Died1717_HuishChurch_Devon.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/JaneCoffin_Died1717_HuishChurch_Devon.png/330px-JaneCoffin_Died1717_HuishChurch_Devon.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/JaneCoffin_Died1717_HuishChurch_Devon.png/440px-JaneCoffin_Died1717_HuishChurch_Devon.png 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:JaneCoffin_Died1717_HuishChurch_Devon.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Mural monument to Jane Coffin (1657-1717), Church of St James The Less, <a href="/wiki/Huish,_Devon" title="Huish, Devon">Huish</a></div></div></div>
<ul><li><ul><li>Jane Coffin (1657-1717), 5th daughter, who in 1682 married Roger Wollocombe (1658-1708) of Combe in the parish of <a href="/wiki/Roborough,_Torridge" title="Roborough, Torridge">Roborough</a>, North Devon, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sheriff_of_Devon" title="Sheriff of Devon">Sheriff of Devon</a> in 1706.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> She survived her husband and died without progeny, appointing as her executor to her will her nephew Richard Yeo (1687-1750) (3rd son and heir of her younger sister Gertrude Coffin) lord of the manor of <a href="/wiki/Huish,_Devon" title="Huish, Devon">Huish</a> in Devon, who erected the surviving inscribed mural monument in the Church of St James The Less, Huish, in her memory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></li>
<li>Mary Coffin (born 1658), 6th daughter, who in 1680 married John Martin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></li>
<li>Gertrude Coffin (1661-1701), 7th daughter, who in 1682 married George Yeo (1653-1714) lord of the manor of <a href="/wiki/Huish,_Devon" title="Huish, Devon">Huish</a> in Devon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Her 3rd son and heir was Richard Yeo (1687-1750) who was appointed executor of the will of his childless aunt Jane Coffin (1657-1717), and erected the surviving inscribed mural monument in the Church of St James The Less, Huish, in her memory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></li>
<li>Ann Coffin (1682-1682), 8th daughter, died an infant.</li></ul></li></ul>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:PrideauxArms2.PNG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1066" data-file-width="926" decoding="async" height="253" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/PrideauxArms2.PNG/220px-PrideauxArms2.PNG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/PrideauxArms2.PNG/330px-PrideauxArms2.PNG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/PrideauxArms2.PNG/440px-PrideauxArms2.PNG 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:PrideauxArms2.PNG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Arms of Prideaux: <i>Argent, a chevron sable in chief a label of three points gules</i></div></div></div>
<ul><li>Thirdly in 1674 he married Anne Prideaux (d.1705), a daughter of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Edmund_Prideaux_(1606%E2%80%931683)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Edmund Prideaux (1606–1683) (page does not exist)">Edmund Prideaux</a> (1606–1683), of <a href="/wiki/Prideaux_Place" title="Prideaux Place">Prideaux Place</a> in <a href="/wiki/Padstow" title="Padstow">Padstow</a>, Cornwall, MP for <a href="/wiki/Saltash_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Saltash (UK Parliament constituency)">Saltash</a>  and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sheriff_of_Cornwall" title="Sheriff of Cornwall">Sheriff of Cornwall</a> in 1664, by whom he had a further 2 sons and 2 daughters as follows:
<ul><li>John Coffin (1678-1703), 3rd son, who in 1703 at <a href="/wiki/Upton_Pyne" title="Upton Pyne">Upton Pyne</a> married Katherine Kellond (1684-1709), a daughter of <a href="/wiki/John_Kelland" title="John Kelland">John Kellond</a> (1635-1692) (<i>alias</i> Kelland) of <a href="/wiki/Painsford,_Ashprington" title="Painsford, Ashprington">Painsford</a> in the parish of <a href="/wiki/Ashprington" title="Ashprington">Ashprington</a> in Devon, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a> MP for <a href="/wiki/Totnes_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Totnes (UK Parliament constituency)">Totnes</a> in Devon and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sheriff_of_Devon" title="Sheriff of Devon">Sheriff of Devon</a> in 1683,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> by his wife Bridget Fownes (1641-1696), his first cousin and sister of John Fownes (1640–1670)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> of Whitley, Devon,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> the father of <a href="/wiki/John_Fownes" title="John Fownes">John Fownes</a> (1661-1731) of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kittery,_Kingswear&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kittery, Kingswear (page does not exist)">Kittery Court</a> in the parish of <a href="/wiki/Kingswear" title="Kingswear">Kingswear</a> and of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nethway,_Brixham&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nethway, Brixham (page does not exist)">Nethway</a> in the parish of <a href="/wiki/Brixham" title="Brixham">Brixham</a>, both in Devon, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a> for <a href="/wiki/Dartmouth_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Dartmouth (UK Parliament constituency)">Dartmouth</a>, Devon, in 1713-14.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> The monument of John Kellond survives in Ashprington Church.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Her brother was Charles Kellond (1660-1695) of Painsford, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a> for <a href="/wiki/Totnes_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Totnes (UK Parliament constituency)">Totnes</a> in Devon 1680-1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The marriage was without progeny. She survived her husband and in 1705 remarried firstly to John Prideaux of <a href="/wiki/Soldon,_Holsworthy" title="Soldon, Holsworthy">Soldon, Holsworthy</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> in Devon, her first husband's mother's cousin, without progeny, and secondly to Peter Prideaux, nephew of her second husband, by whom she had progeny who inherited the estate of Soldon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> She died aged only 25, as is recorded on her surviving <a href="/wiki/Ledger_stone" title="Ledger stone">ledger stone</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sutcombe" title="Sutcombe">Sutcombe</a> Church, the parish church of the Prideaux seat of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thuborough,_Sutcombe" title="Thuborough, Sutcombe">Thuborough</a>.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Richard_Coffin_(1684-1766)" title="Richard Coffin (1684-1766)">Richard Coffin</a> (1684-1766), 4th son, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a> for <a href="/wiki/Barnstaple_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Barnstaple (UK Parliament constituency)">Barnstaple</a> in North Devon in 1727.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> He died without progeny and was the last surviving male member of the Coffin family,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> established since the reign of King Henry II (1154-1189)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> at Alwington.</li>
<li>Bridget Coffin (1675-1697), 10th daughter, who in 1694 married (as his second wife) Charles Kellond (1660-1695) of Painsford, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a> MP for <a href="/wiki/Totnes_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Totnes (UK Parliament constituency)">Totnes</a> in Devon 1680-1,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> whose sister Katherine Kellond had married her brother John Coffin. She had one daughter Anne Kellond (1695-1709) who died aged 14.</li>
<li>Honor Coffin (born 1682), 11th daughter, who in 1708 at Alwington married Edward Bennett of Hexworthy<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> in the parish of <a href="/wiki/Lawhitton" title="Lawhitton">Lawhitton</a> in Cornwall, a descendant of Col. <a href="/wiki/Robert_Bennet_(Roundhead)" title="Robert Bennet (Roundhead)">Robert Bennet</a> (1605–1683) of Hexworthy, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a> during the <a href="/wiki/English_Civil_War" title="English Civil War">Civil War</a> and a noted commander of the <a href="/wiki/Roundhead" title="Roundhead">Parliamentarian</a> forces. Her son was Richard Bennett-Coffin (d.1796) who became heir to the Coffin estates following the death of his childless uncle <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Richard_Coffin_(1684-1766)" title="Richard Coffin (1684-1766)">Richard Coffin</a> (1684-1766). He adopted the name and arms of Coffin, but died without progeny at Esher in Surrey and was buried at Lawhitton where survives his monument.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup>  His heir was Rev. John Pine-Coffin (1735-1824), eldest surviving grandson of Dorothy Coffin (1651-1690), eldest daughter of Richard Coffin (1623–1700), who in 1672 married Edward Pyne (1648-1675) of <a href="/wiki/East_Down,_Devon" title="East Down, Devon">East Down</a>. Her descendants remained seated at East Down manor house until 1866, the Pyne family having occupied it since the 13th century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> The last in the Pine-Coffin family to occupy Portledge manor house was <a href="/wiki/Richard_Geoffrey_Pine-Coffin" title="Richard Geoffrey Pine-Coffin">Richard Geoffrey Pine-Coffin</a> (1908-1974).</li></ul></li></ul>
