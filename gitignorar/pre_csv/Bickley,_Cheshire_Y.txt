<p><b>Bickley</b> is a former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" title="Civil parishes in England">civil parish</a>, now in the parish of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=No_Man%27s_Heath_and_District&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="No Man's Heath and District (page does not exist)">No Man's Heath and District</a>, in the unitary authority of <a href="/wiki/Cheshire_West_and_Chester" title="Cheshire West and Chester">Cheshire West and Chester</a> and ceremonial county of <a href="/wiki/Cheshire" title="Cheshire">Cheshire</a>, England. According to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Census_2001" title="United Kingdom Census 2001">2001 Census</a> it had a population of 498,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2001census_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-2001census-1">[1]</a></sup> reducing slightly to 481 at the 2011 census.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The parish included the villages of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bickley_Town&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bickley Town (page does not exist)">Bickley Town</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bickley_Moss&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bickley Moss (page does not exist)">Bickley Moss</a>. The civil parish was abolished in 2015 to form No Man's Heath and District.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>The name is <a href="/wiki/Old_English" title="Old English">Anglo-Saxon</a> in origin, and relates to bees.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
