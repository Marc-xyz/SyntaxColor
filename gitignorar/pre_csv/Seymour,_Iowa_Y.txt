</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seymour,_Iowa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first coal mines in Seymour were opened in 1884.  In 1902, the <a href="/wiki/Numa,_Iowa" title="Numa, Iowa">Numa</a> Block Coal Company took over these mines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>  In 1908, "Big Jim", Numa Block number 2 was 1 mile east of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chicago,_Milwaukee_and_St._Paul_Railway" title="Chicago, Milwaukee and St. Paul Railway">Chicago, Milwaukee and St. Paul Railway</a> depot, was the largest coal mine in Appanoose-Wayne Coalfield, able to produce 100,000 tons per year at full capacity.  The <a href="/wiki/Mystic,_Iowa" title="Mystic, Iowa">Mystic</a> coal seam was just over 2 feet thick here, reached by a 202-foot shaft.  Numa Block Mine number 3, the Sunshine Mine, was in the southeast part of town, served by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chicago,_Rock_Island_and_Pacific" title="Chicago, Rock Island and Pacific">Rock Island</a>, with a 240-foot shaft to the Mystic seam.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1884, Local Assembly 3143 of the <a href="/wiki/Knights_of_Labor" title="Knights of Labor">Knights of Labor</a>, based in Seymour, had 40 members.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>  In 1912, <a href="/wiki/United_Mine_Workers" title="United Mine Workers">United Mine Workers</a> Local 206 in Seymour had 299 members, approximately 13% of the entire population.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 6, 2017 an EF-2 tornado (confirmed by National Weather Service WFO, Des Moines, IA damage survey) hit Seymour around 8:00pm CST damaging homes and the High School which had been undergoing a process to pass a bond to renovate the school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> As a result of the storm damage school had been closed on Tuesday, March 7, 2017, electricity remained out while Alliant Energy worked to assess the damage and expected to restore power by Tuesday morning.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seymour,_Iowa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Seymour is located at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Seymour,_Iowa&amp;params=40_40_58_N_93_7_15_W_type:city"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">40°40′58″N</span> <span class="longitude">93°7′15″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">40.68278°N 93.12083°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">40.68278; -93.12083</span></span></span></a></span> (40.682854, −93.120732).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR1_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR1-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the city has a total area of 2.35 square miles (6.09 km<sup>2</sup>), all of it land.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gazetteer_files_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Gazetteer_files-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seymour,_Iowa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Seymour is served by a county-maintained road which used to be Iowa Highway 55 and is now County Road S60.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>Seymour is served by the Canadian Pacific Railway which currently operates the former Milwaukee Railroad tracks.
</p><p>Seymour was also served by the CRI&amp;P (Rock Island) until the early 1980s.  This line passed on the southwest corner of the town square with the depot in this location.  This line was abandoned when the Rock Island went bankrupt.  It used to be part of the Golden State Route which was a luxury passenger train operated by the Rock Island and the Southern Pacific.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p><p>The two railroad lines crossed at the western edge of Seymour and was protected by an interlocking tower until the Rock Island was abandoned.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p><p>There had been talk of relaying the track from Allerton to Seymour on the old Rock Island right of way to provide a connection between the CP and the Union Pacific in Allerton.  This idea has since been dropped due to the objections of the adjacent landowners.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_census">2010 census</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seymour,_Iowa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2010 census">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FactFinder_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-FactFinder-2">[2]</a></sup> of 2010, there were 701 people, 295 households, and 192 families residing in the city. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 298.3 inhabitants per square mile (115.2/km<sup>2</sup>). There were 348 housing units at an average density of 148.1 per square mile (57.2/km<sup>2</sup>). The racial makeup of the city was 98.7% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 0.4% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, and 0.9% from two or more races. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 1.6% of the population.
</p><p>There were 295 households of which 26.1% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 45.1% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 16.3% had a female householder with no husband present, 3.7% had a male householder with no wife present, and 34.9% were non-families. 30.5% of all households were made up of individuals and 13.3% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.38 and the average family size was 2.80.
</p><p>The median age in the city was 47.1 years. 20.8% of residents were under the age of 18; 7.3% were between the ages of 18 and 24; 20% were from 25 to 44; 27.4% were from 45 to 64; and 24.5% were 65 years of age or older. The gender makeup of the city was 48.5% male and 51.5% female.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000_census">2000 census</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seymour,_Iowa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2000 census">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of the census<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-14">[14]</a></sup> of 2000, there were 810 people, 336 households, and 219 families residing in the city. The population density was 344.5 people per square mile (133.1/km<sup>2</sup>). There were 393 housing units at an average density of 167.1 per square mile (64.6/km<sup>2</sup>). The racial makeup of the city was 97.53% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 0.37% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 0.99% from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 1.11% from two or more races. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 2.10% of the population.
</p><p>There were 336 households out of which 27.1% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 53.3% were married couples living together, 8.9% had a female householder with no husband present, and 34.8% were non-families. 31.5% of all households were made up of individuals and 17.3% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.30 and the average family size was 2.89.
</p><p>In the city, the population was spread out with 23.0% under the age of 18, 6.4% from 18 to 24, 24.9% from 25 to 44, 20.9% from 45 to 64, and 24.8% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 42 years. For every 100 females, there were 91.0 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 90.8 males.
</p><p>The median income for a household in the city was $26,172, and the median income for a family was $32,692. Males had a median income of $24,531 versus $20,833 for females. The per capita income for the city was $13,581. About 12.7% of families and 22.0% of the population were below the poverty line, including 37.3% of those under age 18 and 12.3% of those age 65 or over.
</p>
