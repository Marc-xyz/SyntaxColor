<p>"<b>Fat Bottomed Girls</b>" is a song by the British <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> band <a href="/wiki/Queen_(band)" title="Queen (band)">Queen</a>. Written by guitarist <a href="/wiki/Brian_May" title="Brian May">Brian May</a>, the track featured on their 1978 album <i><a href="/wiki/Jazz_(Queen_album)" title="Jazz (Queen album)">Jazz</a></i> and later on their compilation album <i><a href="/wiki/Greatest_Hits_(Queen_album)" title="Greatest Hits (Queen album)">Greatest Hits</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> When released as a single with "<a href="/wiki/Bicycle_Race" title="Bicycle Race">Bicycle Race</a>", the song reached number 11 in the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a> and number 24 in the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> in the US.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-US_Chart_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-US_Chart-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UK_Chart_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-UK_Chart-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>The song is formed around an open <a href="/wiki/Blues" title="Blues">bluesy</a>, metallic guitar tuning, and opens with its chorus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Allmusic_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Allmusic-7">[7]</a></sup> It was one of the few Queen songs played in an alternative (<a href="/wiki/Drop_D_tuning" title="Drop D tuning">drop D</a>) guitar tuning.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The song's music video was filmed at the <a href="/wiki/Kay_Bailey_Hutchison_Convention_Center" title="Kay Bailey Hutchison Convention Center">Dallas Convention Center</a> in Texas in October 1978.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ultimatequeen.co.uk_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-ultimatequeen.co.uk-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Song_and_lyrical_content">Song and lyrical content</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fat_Bottomed_Girls&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Song and lyrical content">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The two songs were released together on a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Double_A-side" title="Double A-side">double A-sided</a> <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">single</a>, and both songs refer to each other. Near the end of "Fat Bottomed Girls," the song references "<a href="/wiki/Bicycle_Race" title="Bicycle Race">Bicycle Race</a>", with Mercury shouting, "Get on your bikes and ride!" "Bicycle Race" reciprocates with the lyric "fat bottomed girls, they'll be riding today".
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Live_performances">Live performances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fat_Bottomed_Girls&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Live performances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Queen performed "Fat Bottomed Girls" in concert from 1978 to 1982.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Since its release, the song has appeared on television and film, and has been <a href="/wiki/Fat_Bottomed_Girls#Cover_versions" title="Fat Bottomed Girls">covered</a> by a number of artists.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ultimatequeen.co.uk_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-ultimatequeen.co.uk-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>The song was performed on the setlists of their <a href="/wiki/Queen_%2B_Adam_Lambert" title="Queen + Adam Lambert">Queen + Adam Lambert</a> tours in <a href="/wiki/Queen_%2B_Adam_Lambert_Tour_2012" title="Queen + Adam Lambert Tour 2012">2012</a>, <a href="/wiki/Queen_%2B_Adam_Lambert_Tour_2014%E2%80%932015" title="Queen + Adam Lambert Tour 2014–2015">2014-2015</a>, <a href="/wiki/Queen_%2B_Adam_Lambert_2016_Summer_Festival_Tour" title="Queen + Adam Lambert 2016 Summer Festival Tour">2016</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Queen_%2B_Adam_Lambert_Tour_2017%E2%80%932018" title="Queen + Adam Lambert Tour 2017–2018">2017-2018</a> featuring <a href="/wiki/Adam_Lambert" title="Adam Lambert">Adam Lambert</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and at the <a href="/wiki/IHeartRadio_Music_Festival" title="IHeartRadio Music Festival">iHeartRadio Music Festival</a> 2013 as Queen + Adam Lambert featuring <a href="/wiki/Fun_(band)" title="Fun (band)">Fun</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> It was also performed during the <a href="/wiki/Queen_%2B_Paul_Rodgers_Tour" title="Queen + Paul Rodgers Tour">Return Of The Champions Tour</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Rock_the_Cosmos_Tour" title="Rock the Cosmos Tour">Rock the Cosmos Tour</a>, both tours by <a href="/wiki/Queen_%2B_Paul_Rodgers" title="Queen + Paul Rodgers">Queen + Paul Rodgers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Versions">Versions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fat_Bottomed_Girls&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Versions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song featured a different vocal arrangement from the studio recording when performed live. In live performances, the lead vocals during the chorus were sung by <a href="/wiki/Freddie_Mercury" title="Freddie Mercury">Freddie Mercury</a> and harmonised with an upper voice (<a href="/wiki/Roger_Taylor_(Queen_drummer)" title="Roger Taylor (Queen drummer)">Roger Taylor</a>) and a lower voice (<a href="/wiki/Brian_May" title="Brian May">Brian May</a>). In the studio version, there is no higher harmony. The lead vocals on the verses are sung by Freddie Mercury, while Brian May sings the lead vocals on the chorus.
</p><p>The single version (which can be found on <i><a href="/wiki/Greatest_Hits_(Queen_album)" title="Greatest Hits (Queen album)">Greatest Hits</a></i>, but not the 1992 US "Red Cover" version) omits the extended guitar interludes between the verses and fades out before the ending.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FBG_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-FBG-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p><p>In the US, the 1991 Hollywood Records reissue of <i>Jazz</i> included a bonus remix by <a href="/wiki/Matt_Wallace" title="Matt Wallace">Matt Wallace</a>.
</p><p>In 1992, <a href="/wiki/Organized_Konfusion" title="Organized Konfusion">Organized Konfusion</a> remixed the track for inclusion on the later-cancelled <i>BASIC Queen Bootlegs</i> compilation.  This version included an alternate vocal track by Mercury, as well as hip-hop production and added rap verses.
</p><p>In 1993, <a href="/wiki/Vic_Sotto" title="Vic Sotto">Vic Sotto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Francis_Magalona" title="Francis Magalona">Francis Magalona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Richie_D%27Horsie" title="Richie D'Horsie">Richie D'Horsie</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_V." title="Michael V.">Michael V.</a> covers a tagalog parody reprise version of "Fat Bottomed Girls" as from the movie "Ano Ba Yan? 2" under <a href="/wiki/OctoArts_Films" title="OctoArts Films">OctoArts Films</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup>
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/Glee_(season_2)" title="Glee (season 2)">Season 2</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Glee_(TV_series)" title="Glee (TV series)">Glee</a></i>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Noah_Puckerman" title="Noah Puckerman">Noah Puckerman</a> (played by <a href="/wiki/Mark_Salling" title="Mark Salling">Mark Salling</a>) performs a cover of "Fat Bottomed Girls" in the episode "<a href="/wiki/Silly_Love_Songs_(Glee)" title="Silly Love Songs (Glee)">Silly Love Songs</a>" as well as in the <a href="/wiki/Glee_Live!_In_Concert!" title="Glee Live! In Concert!">Glee Live! In Concert!</a> tour, which was filmed for the concert film <i><a href="/wiki/Glee:_The_3D_Concert_Movie" title="Glee: The 3D Concert Movie">Glee: The 3D Concert Movie</a></i>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fat_Bottomed_Girls&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During an interview with AVClub, <a href="/wiki/Michael_McKean" title="Michael McKean">Michael McKean</a> stated that "Fat Bottomed Girls" was an influence for the song "Big Bottom" by <a href="/wiki/Spinal_Tap_(band)" title="Spinal Tap (band)">Spinal Tap</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> featured in the movie ’’<a href="/wiki/This_Is_Spinal_Tap" title="This Is Spinal Tap">This Is Spinal Tap</a>’’.
</p>
