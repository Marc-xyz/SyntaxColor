	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Nemetin</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Nemetin</b> is a village in eastern <a href="/wiki/Slavonia" title="Slavonia">Slavonia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a>, located near <a href="/wiki/Osijek" title="Osijek">Osijek</a>. It has a population of 139 (census 2011).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It is administratively located within the area of the city of <a href="/wiki/Osijek" title="Osijek">Osijek</a>.
</p><p>The village was the site of the largest prisoner exchange in the <a href="/wiki/Croatian_War_of_Independence" title="Croatian War of Independence">Croatian War of Independence</a> when on August 14, 1992, 662 Croatian soldiers and civilians were returned from Yugoslavian custody.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jutarnji_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Jutarnji-2">[2]</a></sup> Most of the prisoners had been incarcerated in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sremska_Mitrovica_camp" title="Sremska Mitrovica camp">Sremska Mitrovica camp</a> in <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jutarnji_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Jutarnji-2">[2]</a></sup> A group of citizens of <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina">Bosnia and Herzegovina</a> were also freed as part of the transfer, including <a href="/wiki/Sulejman_Tihi%C4%87" title="Sulejman Tihić">Sulejman Tihić</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HRT_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-HRT-3">[3]</a></sup> A memorial to the event was unveiled on its fifteenth anniversary.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HRT_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-HRT-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>During the war the area was mined. In 2006, a business zone in the area was officially demined.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
