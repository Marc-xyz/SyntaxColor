<p><b>Obg-like ATPase 1</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Enzyme" title="Enzyme">enzyme</a> that in humans is encoded by the <i>OLA1</i> <a href="/wiki/Gene" title="Gene">gene</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid17430889_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid17430889-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-entrez_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-entrez-6">[6]</a></sup>
Ola1 belongs to the protein family of Obg-like <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GTPases" title="GTPases">GTPases</a> but defines an exceptional example of a protein that has evolved altered nucleotide specificity and binds <a href="/wiki/Adenosine_triphosphate" title="Adenosine triphosphate">adenosine triphosphate</a> (ATP) with higher affinity than <a href="/wiki/Guanosine_triphosphate" title="Guanosine triphosphate">guanosine triphosphate</a> (GTP).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid17430889_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-pmid17430889-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
