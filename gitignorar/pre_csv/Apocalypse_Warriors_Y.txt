<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Contents">Contents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Apocalypse_Warriors&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Contents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Apocalypse Warriors</i> is a set of 13 individual bubble-packed figures, eight of which come with a weapons set that includes a modern weapon, primitive weapon, archaic weapon, and a bizarre weapon, which allows the gamer to adapt the figure to the campaign.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SG_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-SG-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Apocalypse_Warriors&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bob Kindel reviewed <i>Apocalypse Warriors</i> in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Space_Gamer" title="Space Gamer">Space Gamer</a></i> No. 76.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SG_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-SG-1">[1]</a></sup> Kindel commented that "Their armor and clothing have an improvised feel that lends itself to the spirit of the games."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SG_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-SG-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
