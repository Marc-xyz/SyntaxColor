<p><b>Caffè Vita Coffee Roasting Company</b>, commonly known as <b>Caffè Vita</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">coffee</a> roasting company in <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Washington_(state)" title="Washington (state)">Washington</a>. Part of the <a href="/wiki/Third_wave_of_coffee" title="Third wave of coffee">third wave of coffee</a> movement, Caffè Vita has been named one of the top ten places "to get the best coffee in America."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Allison_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Allison-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Caffè Vita was founded in Seattle's <a href="/wiki/Queen_Anne,_Seattle" title="Queen Anne, Seattle">Queen Anne</a> neighborhood in 1995 by Mike McConnell. McConnell was an early proponent for developing direct relationships with coffee farmers, and bypassing the <i>fair trade</i> of coffee,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Allison_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Allison-3">[3]</a></sup> pioneering the Farm Direct movement.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Locations">Locations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Caff%C3%A9_Vita_Coffee_Roasting_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Locations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Caffè Vita now operates 13 cafes throughout <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Oregon" title="Portland, Oregon">Portland</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>. Caffe Vita coffee is always fresh, imported directly from the farm, and roasted on-site in Seattle, Brooklyn, and Portland.
</p><p>Caffè Vita's location at 1005 East Pike Street, Seattle, houses their flagship cafe, their roasteria, and company offices. The company operates eight other cafes around Seattle, and a roasteria in <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Oregon" title="Portland, Oregon">Portland</a>. In March 2012 they opened a cafe in the <a href="/wiki/Lower_East_Side" title="Lower East Side">Lower East Side</a> of <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In the Spring of 2013 Caffè Vita opened a location on <a href="/wiki/Sunset_Boulevard" title="Sunset Boulevard">Sunset Boulevard</a> in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In 2015, Caffè Vita expanded its New York presence with the opening of a roasteria and cafe in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bushwick" title="Bushwick">Bushwick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Caffè Vita also supplies coffee to cafes, restaurants, and hotels who value farm direct, organic, specialty coffee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Community_service">Community service</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Caff%C3%A9_Vita_Coffee_Roasting_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Community service">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2009, Caffè Vita partnered with Portland's p:ear, a homeless youth mentoring program, to create the p:ear Barista School. Caffè Vita provides equipment, coffee beans, milk, and training that allow the school to take homeless youth through an 8-week program that give them the necessary skills and experience to get a job as a barista.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> 
</p><p>Also in 2009, Caffè Vita underwrote and organized "GIVE Seattle": a 36-track compilation record, featuring artists such as <a href="/wiki/Ben_Gibbard" title="Ben Gibbard">Ben Gibbard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fleet_Foxes" title="Fleet Foxes">Fleet Foxes</a> to benefit four local food banks and Arts Corps.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>Caffè Vita currently supports <a href="/wiki/FareStart" title="FareStart">FareStart's</a> youth barista training program, which trains homeless youth and provides barista jobs at their coffee stands in <a href="/wiki/T-Mobile_Park" title="T-Mobile Park">T-Mobile Field</a>. They also sponsor FareStart's annual Guest Chef Night and Volunteer Server Night, which annually bring together Seattle chefs and winemakers to raise money for FareStart. 
</p><p>Every year, Caffè Vita hosts a holiday fundraising drive for <a class="external text" href="https://www.jwcenter.org/" rel="nofollow">Jubilee Women’s Center</a> in all Caffe Vita cafes, with donations matched dollar for dollar. 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sustainability">Sustainability</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Caff%C3%A9_Vita_Coffee_Roasting_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sustainability">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Caffe Vita owner Mike McConnell is a pioneer Farm Direct movement, meticulously sourcing the best coffee available while developing long-term, mutually fruitful relationships with coffee growers in more than 11 countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Caffè Vita serves many <a href="/wiki/Organic_farming" title="Organic farming">Organic</a>, Farm Direct, and <a href="/wiki/Rainforest_Alliance" title="Rainforest Alliance">Rainforest Alliance</a> Certified coffees.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> 
</p><p>In 2009, Caffè Vita starting working with <a href="/wiki/Woodland_Park_Zoo" title="Woodland Park Zoo">Woodland Park Zoo</a>'s Tree Kangaroo Project to develop the first-ever coffee from the YUS region made commercially available in the U.S. purchased directly from the growers at a fair price.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Papua New Guinea Yopno Uruwa Som coffee, which protects the endangered <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tree_Kangaroo" title="Tree Kangaroo">Tree Kangaroo</a>, is sold at all Seattle Caffe Vita locations and at Woodland Park Zoo ZooStores. In addition to coffee from PNG, Caffè Vita also sells their Zoo Special Reserve, in which every bag of this coffee purchased online or in our cafes, a dollar is donated to the Woodland Park Zoo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Partnerships">Partnerships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Caff%C3%A9_Vita_Coffee_Roasting_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Partnerships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Caffè Vita partners with <a href="/wiki/Easy_Street_Records" title="Easy Street Records">Easy Street Records</a> on their Easy Street Indo 30 coffee blend. Matt Vaughan was Caffè Vita's first wholesale customer in 1995. Throughout the next 20 years they set a model for local and independent businesses, with support from each other and the community, allowing them to tantalize the ears and tongues of many with the best in music and coffee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2017, all <a href="/wiki/Washington_State_Ferries" title="Washington State Ferries">Washington State Ferries</a> began serving Caffè Vita coffee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2019, Caffe Vita began barrel-aging their coffee in partnership with <a class="external text" href="http://batch206.com/" rel="nofollow">Batch 206 Distillery</a>. To date, they've released 3 coffees barrel aged with Bourbon, Rum, and Gin. 
</p><p>Caffe Vita sponsors many local events, including <a class="external text" href="https://summer.timbermusicfest.com/" rel="nofollow">Timber Music Fest</a>, and <a class="external text" href="https://winter.timbermusicfest.com/" rel="nofollow">Timbrrr!</a> by <a class="external text" href="http://artisthome.org/" rel="nofollow">Artist Home</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>, <a class="external text" href="https://doebay.com/doe-bay-fest-2019/" rel="nofollow">Doe Bay Fest</a> on <a href="/wiki/Orcas_Island" title="Orcas Island">Orcas Island</a>, <a class="external text" href="https://www.chomplocal.org/" rel="nofollow">CHOMP!</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Capitol_Hill_Block_Party" title="Capitol Hill Block Party">Capitol Hill Block Party</a>.  
</p>
