<p><i><b>Sex, Life &amp; Love</b></i> is the second full-length studio disc from <a href="/wiki/Rythm_Syndicate" title="Rythm Syndicate">Rythm Syndicate</a>, the dance-rock band founded by songwriter/producers <a href="/wiki/Carl_Sturken_and_Evan_Rogers" title="Carl Sturken and Evan Rogers">Carl Sturken</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Carl_Sturken_and_Evan_Rogers" title="Carl Sturken and Evan Rogers">Evan Rogers</a>.  Released in 1992 (with the name changed to "RHYTHM Syndicate"), the disc features much of the same personnel, both in the band and in the recording, as <a href="/wiki/Rythm_Syndicate_(album)" title="Rythm Syndicate (album)">their debut</a>.  With the loss of former guitarist Mike McDonald, multi-instrumentalist Ron Skies was called in to handle guitars as well.
</p><p>Released during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_riots_of_1992" title="Los Angeles riots of 1992">1992 L.A. riots</a>, the back cover features the plea "Pray for peace, justice, and an end to <a href="/wiki/Racism" title="Racism">racism</a> in L.A., America and the world."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The album features <a href="/wiki/Hypersexuality" title="Hypersexuality">hypersexual</a> lyrics and titles, with some being "Just for the Sex", "Can I Get Naked with You" and "I Wanna Make Love to You".
</p><p>It failed to chart in America, and only one single aired; "I Wanna Make Love to You".  After the release, both Sturken &amp; Rogers returned to  production and songwriting work for other artists.
</p>
