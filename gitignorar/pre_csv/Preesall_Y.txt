<p><b>Preesall</b> is a small town, <a href="/wiki/Civil_parish" title="Civil parish">civil parish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wards_and_electoral_divisions_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Wards and electoral divisions of the United Kingdom">electoral ward</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lancashire" title="Lancashire">Lancashire</a>, England. The parish (until 1910 known as <b>Preesall with Hackensall</b>) covers the eastern bank of the estuary of the <a href="/wiki/River_Wyre" title="River Wyre">River Wyre</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Knott_End-on-Sea" title="Knott End-on-Sea">Knott End-on-Sea</a>, Pilling Lane and the village of Preesall itself. The parish of Preesall had a population of 5,314 recorded in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Census_2001" title="United Kingdom Census 2001">2001 census</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lpc_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-lpc-1">[1]</a></sup> rising to 5,694 at the 2011 census.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Preesall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There is evidence that the eastern side of the <a href="/wiki/River_Wyre" title="River Wyre">River Wyre</a> was occupied during the <a href="/wiki/Danes_(Germanic_tribe)" title="Danes (Germanic tribe)">Danish</a> invasions of the 9th and 10th centuries. Preesall is mentioned in the <a href="/wiki/Domesday_Book" title="Domesday Book">Domesday Book</a> (1086) as being a part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hundred_(country_subdivision)" title="Hundred (country subdivision)">Hundred</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amounderness" title="Amounderness">Amounderness</a> and the Domesday place name is given as <i>Pressouede</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NA-Preesall_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-NA-Preesall-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>The names Preesall and Hackensall are both probably <a href="/wiki/Old_Norse" title="Old Norse">Norse</a> in origin, with Preesall meaning "a hill and a heath" and Hackensall probably deriving from "Haakon", a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Viking" title="Viking">Viking</a> seafarer who sailed up the River Wyre and set up an encampment in the 10th century. In 1190 the land was granted to a <a href="/wiki/Archery" title="Archery">bowman</a> in  the service of <a href="/wiki/John,_King_of_England" title="John, King of England">Prince John</a>, and in the 16th century, the land, like much in this area, passed into the possession of the Fleetwood family. Richard Fleetwood built Hackensall Hall in 1656 after their home at <a href="/wiki/Rossall_School" title="Rossall School">Rossall Hall</a> was flooded. Nearby Parrox Hall was built about the same time, and has been in the possession of the Elletson family since 1690.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>A known site of high archaeological importance is the former Hackensall Tide Mill (Site PRN15022, SD 35114659) at Preesall. This mill is known from documentary sources and may date from before 1260; its site is indicated on the OS 1:10,560 mapping of 1848 (sheet Lancashire 43). Other watermill sites of this period are known in the county, although this appears to be the only tide mill and very few simple medieval mill sites have been investigated in the field. Physical remains of medieval watermills are rare nationally and surviving remains are likely to be of national, rather than local, importance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Preesall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Preesall was an <a href="/wiki/Urban_district_(Great_Britain_and_Ireland)" title="Urban district (Great Britain and Ireland)">urban district</a> from 1900 to 1974. It was also known as Preesall-With-Hackensall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vision10026994_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-vision10026994-5">[5]</a></sup> It was abolished in 1974 and amalgamated under the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a> to form part of the district of <a href="/wiki/Borough_of_Wyre" title="Borough of Wyre">Wyre</a>, but retained its own <a href="/wiki/Mayor" title="Mayor">mayor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Town_council" title="Town council">town council</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Preesall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The beach surrounding Pilling, Preesall and Knott-End is known as Preesall Beach. Preesall is protected from high tides by a sea defence known as the "Sea Wall". There is a path on the sea wall that leads from Knott End-on-Sea to <a href="/wiki/Pilling" title="Pilling">Pilling</a> and at a point next to Preesall pumping station, the sea wall goes over an old, now filled in, stone pillbox from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a> which is camouflaged among the rocks.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>West of the village itself lies a number of bodies of water, known locally as the Flashes. These were formed in the 1920s and 1930s by subsidence from <a href="/wiki/Salt_mining" title="Salt mining">salt mining</a> and caused the mines' closure in 1931. <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Chemical_Industries" title="Imperial Chemical Industries">ICI</a> continued to use the mines and land nearby for <a href="/wiki/Brine" title="Brine">brine</a> pumping until the 1980s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Parrox_Hall">Parrox Hall</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Preesall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Parrox Hall">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Parrox Hall is a Grade II* listed country house, probably built in the early 17th century, which replaced and partly incorporated a previous building. It stands in its own grounds on the southern edge of the village of Preesall and is built of rendered brick in two storeys to an H-shaped floor plan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>The site of the hall has been continuously owned by members of the same family who have descended from the original Lord of the Manor of Preesall-with-Hackensall, Geoffrey the Crossbowman (Galfridus Arbalastarius). He was a <a href="/wiki/Normans" title="Normans">Norman</a> soldier who was granted six carucates of land by <a href="/wiki/John,_King_of_England" title="John, King of England">Prince John</a> in 1189. The last family member to own the hall was Daniel Hope Elletson, who set up a trust to safeguard the hall's future.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The Trust is currently undertaking a number of conservation and restoration projects.
The hall hosts meetings, seminars and discussions and is also open to visiting groups by appointment and to the general public on a number of open days each summer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_houses">Public houses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Preesall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Public houses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Preesall has one pub remaining: The Black Bull.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Preesall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The village has three schools—a <a href="/wiki/Secondary_school" title="Secondary school">secondary</a> and two <a href="/wiki/Primary_school" title="Primary school">primary schools</a>. <a href="/wiki/Saint_Aidan%27s_Church_of_England_High_School" title="Saint Aidan's Church of England High School">Saint Aidan's</a>, located on Cartgate, is a <a href="/wiki/Church_of_England" title="Church of England">Church of England</a> secondary school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc888_4628_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc888_4628-10">[10]</a></sup> Preesall Fleetwood's Charity School is a Church of England primary school on Mill Street.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PFCS_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-PFCS-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc888_3572_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc888_3572-12">[12]</a></sup> Carter's Charity Voluntary Controlled Primary School, is located on Pilling Lane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc888_3126_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc888_3126-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Preesall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Preesall used to lie on the long closed <a href="/wiki/Garstang_and_Knot-End_Railway" title="Garstang and Knot-End Railway">Garstang and Knot-End Railway</a> which was built to provide an outlet for farmers in <a href="/wiki/Over_Wyre" title="Over Wyre">Over Wyre</a> to transport their produce up and down the country. This section of the line opened on 30 July 1908, along with <a href="/wiki/Preesall_railway_station" title="Preesall railway station">Preesall railway station</a>. However, the line closed to passengers on 29 March 1930.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ECBRC_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-ECBRC-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DRS_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-DRS-15">[15]</a></sup> There are bus services to and from both <a href="/wiki/Lancaster,_Lancashire" title="Lancaster, Lancashire">Lancaster</a> and <a href="/wiki/Blackpool" title="Blackpool">Blackpool</a>.
</p>
