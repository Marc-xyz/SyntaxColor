<p><b>Bill Gratton</b> (born February 29, 1948) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadian" title="Canadian">Canadian</a> retired professional <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey" title="Ice hockey">ice hockey</a> player who played six games in the <a href="/wiki/World_Hockey_Association" title="World Hockey Association">World Hockey Association</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Calgary_Cowboys" title="Calgary Cowboys">Calgary Cowboys</a> during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1975-76_WHA_season" title="1975-76 WHA season">1975–76 season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He later coached the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Major_League_Hockey#Southern_Int._B" title="Major League Hockey">Paris 29ers</a>, a top tier <a href="/wiki/Canadians" title="Canadians">Canadian</a> <a href="/wiki/Senior_ice_hockey" title="Senior ice hockey">Senior</a> <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey" title="Ice hockey">ice hockey</a> team in  <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Major_League_Hockey" title="Major League Hockey">Major League Hockey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Gratton was born in <a href="/wiki/Brantford" title="Brantford">Brantford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>.
</p>
