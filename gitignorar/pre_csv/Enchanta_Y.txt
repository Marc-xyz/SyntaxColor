<p><b>Enchanta</b> <span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ɛ/: 'e' in 'dress'">ɛ</span><span title="'n' in 'nigh'">n</span><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/tʃ/: 'ch' in 'China'">tʃ</span><span title="/ɑː/: 'a' in 'father'">ɑː</span><span title="'n' in 'nigh'">n</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span><span title="/ɑː/: 'a' in 'father'">ɑː</span></span>/</a></span></span> (<i>Enchan</i> <span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ɛ/: 'e' in 'dress'">ɛ</span><span title="'n' in 'nigh'">n</span><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/tʃ/: 'ch' in 'China'">tʃ</span><span title="/ɑː/: 'a' in 'father'">ɑː</span><span title="'n' in 'nigh'">n</span></span>/</a></span></span> in the 2016 series) is a <a href="/wiki/Constructed_language" title="Constructed language">constructed language</a> spoken by the denizens of <a href="/wiki/Encantadia" title="Encantadia">Encantadia</a>, known as Encantado(s)/Encantada(s) or <a href="/wiki/Diwata" title="Diwata">Diwata</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fairies" title="Fairies">fairies</a>). It was initially created by <a href="/wiki/Suzette_Doctolero" title="Suzette Doctolero">Suzette Doctolero</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippine</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fantaserye" title="Fantaserye">fantasy</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_drama" title="Television drama">television drama</a> <a href="/wiki/Saga" title="Saga">saga</a> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Encantadia_Saga" title="Encantadia Saga">Encantadia</a></i>, which aired on <a href="/wiki/GMA_Network" title="GMA Network">GMA Network</a> in 2005,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and was later used in the show's sequels, <i><a href="/wiki/Etheria" title="Etheria">Etheria: Ang Ikalimang Kaharian ng Encantadia</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Encantadia:_Pag-ibig_Hanggang_Wakas" title="Encantadia: Pag-ibig Hanggang Wakas">Encantadia: Pag-ibig Hanggang Wakas</a></i>. The source material of the language appears to have been derived from <a href="/wiki/Philippine_languages" title="Philippine languages">Philippine languages</a> (particularly <a href="/wiki/Tagalog_language" title="Tagalog language">Tagalog</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Romance_languages" title="Romance languages">Romance languages</a>.  It has its own script, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Enchan" title="Enchan">Enchan</a>, which appears to be based on <a href="/wiki/Baybayin" title="Baybayin">Baybayin</a>. Three songs in the language are written by Doctolero, namely "Asshenti (Love song)", "Hade!! (Etheria theme song)", and "Ivo (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lireo" title="Lireo">Lireo</a> <a href="/wiki/National_anthem" title="National anthem">national anthem</a>)", which are all sung by <a href="/wiki/Bayang_Barrios" title="Bayang Barrios">Bayang Barrios</a> for the sequel <i><a href="/wiki/Etheria" title="Etheria">Etheria</a></i> in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enchanta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Enchanta was created by Suzette Doctolero originally for the Philippine fantasy series Encantadia to make the viewers feel that the world  of Encantados be somehow realistic and genuine. Doctolero created its vocabulary from many Indo-European languages with some influences of Philippine languages especially Tagalog. It can be traced from words like "corre", to love (from the root "cor" meaning heart) and "avoya", to travel (voyage). The language is also notable of being spoken as it sounds like its from some kind of European country because of the tone and the phonotactics. Most characters from the Encantadia saga know how to speak Enchanta, but it was Cassiopeia that had spoken the language most in the whole series, from the fact that she is the first <i>diwata</i> (fairy) of Lireo and ancestor of all the royal-blooded <i>diwatas</i> of the Queendom.
</p>

<b>Kanwu</b> — Near / Malapit <br/>
<b>Mashna/Mancha</b> — General (Military) <br/>
<b>Masne sera (name of person)</b> — Peaceful night to you, (name of person) / Mapayapang gabi, (name of person) <br/>
<b>Maste lesnu (name of person)</b> — Peace be with (name of person). Compare with Namaste. <br/>
<b>Mayale</b> — Pig / Baboy <br/>
<b>Menantre</b> — Mentor <br/>
<b>Morfe</b> — Nothing / Wala <br/>
<b>Nedanus muste</b> — You are so beautiful. / Nakakabighani ang iyong ganda. <br/>
<b>Neda-veda</b> — He/she is not here. <br/>
<b>Ne Sshuda</b> — You're wrong. / Mali ka.<br/>
<b>Neshda</b> — Whisper / Bulong <br/>
<b>Paneya</b> — Bread / Tinapay <br/>
<b>Pashnea</b> — Showhing angerness / Pagpapahayag ng galit/ Animal!/Hayop!<br/>
<b>Prosunteo (name of person)</b> — (Name of person) is suspicious. <br/>
<b>Quando</b> — Reason / Dahilan Shedamaste - Go forth<br/>
<b>Sang'gre</b> — Princess<br/>
<b>Ssheda!</b> — Stop!/Halt!/Desist! / Hinto/Tigil <br/>
<b>Ssheda dini luna</b> — Stop and face me. / Tigil at ako ay harapin. <br/>
<b>Ssheda lasta!</b> — It won't happen! / Hindi iyan mangyayari. <br/>
<b>Ssheda musni verom</b> — I will not give this to you. / Hindi ko maibibigay ang iyong nais. <br/>
<b>Tashi dac</b> — Disrespectful / Lapastangan <br/>
<b>Uste</b> — You / Ikaw <br/>
<b>Vashna</b> — Goodness; Kindness / Kabutihan <br/>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conversational">Conversational</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Enchanta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Conversational">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> translations of phrases in Enchanta are written in parentheses below. These phrases are derived from the <a href="/wiki/Folk_music" title="Folk music">folk</a> <a href="/wiki/War_song" title="War song">war song</a> <i>Hade!! (<a href="/wiki/Etheria" title="Etheria">Etheria</a> theme song)</i> sung by <a href="/wiki/Bayang_Barrios" title="Bayang Barrios">Bayang Barrios</a> and written by <a href="/wiki/Suzette_Doctolero" title="Suzette Doctolero">Suzette Doctolero</a>. Based on the lyrics, the song is about the creation of <a href="/wiki/Encantadia" title="Encantadia">Encantadia</a>, the five kingdoms including <a href="/wiki/Etheria" title="Etheria">Etheria</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Warfare" title="Warfare">warfare</a>. In the end, the author appears to question the unending violence in the land, the lack of <a href="/wiki/Love" title="Love">love</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peace" title="Peace">peace</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> 
</p>
