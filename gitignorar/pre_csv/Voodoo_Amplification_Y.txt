<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Voodoo_Amplification&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Trace_Allen_Davis_(businessman)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Trace Allen Davis (businessman) (page does not exist)">Trace Allen Davis</a> first went into business in the mid 1990s with a small shop in Ithaca, New York, repairing and modifying production guitar amps with his own custom circuits. In time, his high-gain circuit mods became in such high demand by guitar players that production line amplifiers based on these custom circuits were released.
</p><p>Voodoo Amps are hand-wired, point-to-point circuits. As such, their amps are often cited 
among those brands of high-end guitar amplifiers termed "boutique" amplifiers. The company backs their commitment to outstanding tone with rock-solid customer service.
</p><p>Noted users of Voodoo's production amps and circuit mods are (according to the manufacturer) include <a href="/wiki/Vivian_Campbell" title="Vivian Campbell">Vivian Campbell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Phil_Collen" title="Phil Collen">Phil Collen</a> (of <a href="/wiki/Def_Leppard" title="Def Leppard">Def Leppard</a>), <a href="/wiki/Doug_Aldrich" title="Doug Aldrich">Doug Aldrich</a> (of <a href="/wiki/Whitesnake" title="Whitesnake">Whitesnake</a>), <a href="/wiki/Michael_Wilton" title="Michael Wilton">Michael Wilton</a> (of <a href="/wiki/Queensr%C3%BFche" title="Queensrÿche">Queensrÿche</a>), <a href="/wiki/Kip_Winger" title="Kip Winger">Kip Winger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jack_Frost" title="Jack Frost">Jack Frost</a>, <a href="/wiki/Richard_Fortus" title="Richard Fortus">Richard Fortus</a> (of <a href="/wiki/Guns_N%27_Roses" title="Guns N' Roses">Guns N' Roses</a>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Buz_McGrath" title="Buz McGrath">Buz McGrath</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ken_Susi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ken Susi (page does not exist)">Ken Susi</a> (of <a href="/wiki/Unearth" title="Unearth">Unearth</a>) and famed producer <a href="/wiki/Michael_Wagener" title="Michael Wagener">Michael Wagener</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Current_Models">Current Models</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Voodoo_Amplification&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Current Models">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Voodoo Amps currently produces a number of amplifiers which are a mix of modern- and vintage-inspiried designs.  Most models are known for their variation on Voodoo's high-gain circuits.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="V-Series">V-Series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Voodoo_Amplification&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: V-Series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Voodoo's first entry into their "V-Series" was their <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=V-Plex&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="V-Plex (page does not exist)">V-Plex</a> model. This amp was designed 
after the best sounding 1968 Marshall "Plexi" guitar amplifier that had come through their 
shop for repair.  Voodoo purchased the amp from its original owner, then reverse engineered the amp down to exact specs. The Voodoo V-Plex model is the result of those efforts. The amp has been very positively reviewed in  major guitar magazines, and is available in 50wt and 100wt head configurations, as well as a 25wt or 50wt 1x12 Combo.
</p><p>The second entry into the V-Series was the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=V-Reck&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="V-Reck (page does not exist)">V-Reck</a> model.  The Voodoo V-Reck is a custom circuit amp that aims to capture the essence and vibe of the original Trainwreck amplifiers,  which were created by Ken Fisher in Colonia, New Jersey.  The V-Reck captures the essence of the original amps by allowing guitarists to go from shimmering clean tones to raging distortion — simply by manipulating the guitar's volume knob.  The V-Reck is available as a 50wt head or as a 50wt 1x12 Combo.
</p><p>The third entry in this series was the V-Rock model.  The Voodoo V-Rock is a 100wt amplifier whose inspiration came from the original high-gain modification circuit designed by Jose Arredondo.  The V-Rock faithfully replicate the guitar tones of the top players of the 1980s, including <a href="/wiki/John_Sykes" title="John Sykes">John Sykes</a>, <a href="/wiki/George_Lynch_(musician)" title="George Lynch (musician)">George Lynch</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Van_Halen" title="Eddie Van Halen">Eddie Van Halen</a>. The V-Rock is currently offered as a 100wt head, although a 50wt combo model may soon follow.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hex_series">Hex series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Voodoo_Amplification&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Hex series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Based upon one of Voodoo's original circuits, the Hex is a highly versatile 4-stage gain preamp with tremendous articulation, clarity and definition, even under the most extreme gain settings. It is a dual-channel design, which allows users to set up a clean/dirty arrangement, or two flavors of a distorted tone (rhythm/lead).  The Hex is available in 50wt and 100wt head configurations.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Baron_Samedi_series">Baron Samedi series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Voodoo_Amplification&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Baron Samedi series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>With the Baron Samedi, Voodoo Amps proudly presents the ultimate high gain dual channel amp.  A 5-stage preamp offers a wide variety of available tones. An extensive equalisation circuit across two channels further enhances the flexibility of this amp.  The Witchdoctor is available in 50wt and 100wt head configurations, as well as a 10wt 1x12 combo.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="CP-100_series">CP-100 series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Voodoo_Amplification&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: CP-100 series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Next on the horizon is a 2nd model amp featuring a 5-stage preamp.  The CP-100 is 100wt head currently offered as a "rebuild" option on select production amplifier chassis, including the Marshall JCM 800 2205/2210 models, as well as the older Laney AOR Pro-Tube models with the wide chassis.  Voodoo Amps plans to have an official 2-channel production version of this amp available in late 2008/early 2009.
</p>
