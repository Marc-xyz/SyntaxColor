<p>The "<b>National Anthem of the Islamic Republic of Mauritania</b>" (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar">نشيد وطني موريتاني</span>‎, <i>Nashid Wataniin Muritaniin</i>, <abbr title="literal translation">lit.</abbr><span style="white-space:nowrap;"> "National Chant [of] Mauritania[n]"</span>) was <a href="/wiki/Mauritania" title="Mauritania">Mauritania</a>'s former <a href="/wiki/National_anthem" title="National anthem">national anthem</a> between 1960 and 2017. 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_anthem_of_Mauritania_(1960%E2%80%932017)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Its lyrics are taken from a poem written in the late 18th century by Baba Ould Cheikh; the melody was arranged by <a href="/wiki/Tolia_Nikiprowetzky" title="Tolia Nikiprowetzky">Tolia Nikiprowetzky</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mauritania_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-mauritania-2">[2]</a></sup> The anthem was adopted upon independence <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/French_Mauritania" title="French Mauritania">from France</a> in 1960. The unusual and highly complex rhythm of it makes it extremely difficult to sing; for this reason it is often erroneously listed as wordless.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mauritania_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-mauritania-2">[2]</a></sup> It was replaced by the <a href="/wiki/National_anthem_of_Mauritania" title="National anthem of Mauritania">current national anthem</a> on 16 November 2017, after the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mauritanian_constitutional_referendum,_2017" title="Mauritanian constitutional referendum, 2017">Mauritanian constitutional referendum of 2017</a>.
</p>
