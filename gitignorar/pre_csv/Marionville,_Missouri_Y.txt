<p><b>Marionville</b> is a city in <a href="/wiki/Lawrence_County,_Missouri" title="Lawrence County, Missouri">Lawrence County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Missouri" title="Missouri">Missouri</a>, United States. The population was 2,225 at the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 census</a>. On March 12, 2006, a tornado hit the town, destroying houses and killing two people. It was rated as an F3.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marionville,_Missouri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Marionville was <a href="/wiki/Plat" title="Plat">platted</a> in 1854.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The city was named for <a href="/wiki/Francis_Marion" title="Francis Marion">Francis Marion</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> A post office called Marionville has been in operation since 1864.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marionville,_Missouri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Marionville is located at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Marionville,_Missouri&amp;params=37_0_1_N_93_38_17_W_type:city"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">37°0′1″N</span> <span class="longitude">93°38′17″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">37.00028°N 93.63806°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">37.00028; -93.63806</span></span></span></a></span> (37.000361, -93.638026).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR1_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR1-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the city has a total area of 1.76 square miles (4.56 km<sup>2</sup>), all land.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gazetteer_files_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Gazetteer_files-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Marionville has a population of white squirrels, which is one of a handful of such populations in the United States. They can be seen in yards near the Ozarks Methodist Manor.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government">Government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marionville,_Missouri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On April 21, 2014 the Marionville mayor Dan Clevenger resigned.  He resigned over anti-Semitic remarks he made in the wake of the arrest of resident <a href="/wiki/Frazier_Glenn_Miller_Jr." title="Frazier Glenn Miller Jr.">Frazier Glenn Miller Jr.</a> for committing a shooting at a Jewish Community Center in <a href="/wiki/Overland_Park,_Kansas" title="Overland Park, Kansas">Overland Park, Kansas</a>.  A 4-1 vote by aldermen to begin the <a href="/wiki/Impeachment" title="Impeachment">impeachment</a> process also prompted Clevenger's decision to resign.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-resigned_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-resigned-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_census">2010 census</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marionville,_Missouri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2010 census">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FactFinder_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-FactFinder-2">[2]</a></sup> of 2010, there were 2,225 people, 900 households, and 587 families residing in the city. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 1,264.2 inhabitants per square mile (488.1/km<sup>2</sup>). There were 1,018 housing units at an average density of 578.4 per square mile (223.3/km<sup>2</sup>). The racial makeup of the city was 96.3% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 0.4% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 0.4% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 0.3% from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(U.S._Census)" title="Race (U.S. Census)">other races</a>, and 2.6% from two or more races. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 1.5% of the population.
</p><p>There were 900 households of which 31.3% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 48.3% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 12.4% had a female householder with no husband present, 4.4% had a male householder with no wife present, and 34.8% were non-families. 30.0% of all households were made up of individuals and 16.8% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.39 and the average family size was 2.92.
</p><p>The median age in the city was 40.5 years. 24.4% of residents were under the age of 18; 7.8% were between the ages of 18 and 24; 22.1% were from 25 to 44; 22.6% were from 45 to 64; and 23% were 65 years of age or older. The gender makeup of the city was 45.7% male and 54.3% female.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000_census">2000 census</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marionville,_Missouri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2000 census">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-4">[4]</a></sup> of 2000, there were 2,113 people, 871 households and 562 families residing in the city. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 1,525.6 people per square mile (586.9/km²). There were 993 housing units at an average density of 716.9 per square mile (275.8/km²). The racial makeup of the city was 97.11% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 0.09% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 0.66% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 0.38% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 0.33% from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 1.42% from two or more races. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> or any other race accounted for 0.62% of the population.
</p><p>There were 871 households, out of which 30.1% had children under the age of 18, 48.8% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 11.5% had a female householder with no husband present, 0.12% had a female household with someone else's husband present, and 35.4% were non-families. Approximately 30.9% of all households were made up of individuals, and 17.3% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.30, and the average family size was 2.86.
</p><p>In the city, 24.5% of the population were under the age of 18, 8.5% were ages 18 to 24, 25.7% were ages 25 to 44, 18.4% were ages 45 to 64, and 22.9% were 65 or older. The median age was 38 years. For every 100 females, there were 83.6 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 78.8 males.
</p><p>The median household income in Marionville was $25,078 per year at the time of the census, and the median income for a family was $30,607 per year. Males had a median income of $24,792 per year, versus $17,188 per year for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the city was $13,552 annually. About 19.5% of families and 22.0% of the population were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 32.7% of those under age 18 and 9.0% of those age 65 or over.
</p>
