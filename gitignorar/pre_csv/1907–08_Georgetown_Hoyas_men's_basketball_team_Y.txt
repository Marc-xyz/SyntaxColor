<p>The <b>1907–08 Georgetown Hoyas men's basketball team</b> represented <a href="/wiki/Georgetown_University" title="Georgetown University">Georgetown University</a> during the 1907-08 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intercollegiate_Athletic_Association_of_the_United_States" title="Intercollegiate Athletic Association of the United States">Intercollegiate Athletic Association of the United States</a> <a href="/wiki/College_basketball" title="College basketball">college basketball</a> season. Maurice Joyce coached the team in his first season as head coach.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup> Georgetown was an independent and played its home games at the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Convention_Hall_(Washington,_D.C.)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Convention Hall (Washington, D.C.) (page does not exist)">Convention Hall</a> at 5th and K Streets NW in downtown <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-homecourt_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-homecourt-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Joyce had introduced the new sport of basketball to Washington, D.C., in 1892 – the year after its invention by <a href="/wiki/James_Naismith" title="James Naismith">James Naismith</a> – and had fostered its development there over the next 15 years as Director of <a href="/wiki/Physical_education" title="Physical education">Physical Education</a> at the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Carroll_Institute&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Carroll Institute (page does not exist)">Carroll Institute</a>. In the autumn of 1906, Georgetown had hired him as its <a href="/wiki/Athletic_director" title="Athletic director">athletic director</a> with an eye toward developing a men's basketball program at the school, and he had founded the program late that year, in time to field Georgetown<span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s first team in the <a href="/wiki/1906%E2%80%9307_Georgetown_Hoyas_men%27s_basketball_team" title="1906–07 Georgetown Hoyas men's basketball team">1906-07 season</a>. That team had had no coach, relying instead on an elected student manager to provide leadership, monitor the team<span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s finances, and schedule games and practices – roles now performed by the school<span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s athletic department and coach. For Georgetown<span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s second season in 1907-08, Joyce formally became the team<span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s first head coach, although this did not diminish the role of the student manager, who continued in his duties; rather, the head coach acted as a teacher and faculty advisor, available to provide advice to the players during games rather than lead the team on the court. It was not until the late 1920s that the modern role of the head coach as in-game leader emerged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Season_recap">Season recap</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1907%E2%80%9308_Georgetown_Hoyas_men%27s_basketball_team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Season recap">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During its inaugural season in <a href="/wiki/1906%E2%80%9307_Georgetown_Hoyas_men%27s_basketball_team" title="1906–07 Georgetown Hoyas men's basketball team">1906-07</a>, Georgetown had played only four games, three of them against <a href="/wiki/George_Washington_Colonials_men%27s_basketball" title="George Washington Colonials men's basketball">George Washington</a>. George Washington had won two of the three games, led by <a href="/wiki/Center_(basketball)" title="Center (basketball)">center</a> Fred Rice. When George Washington decided not to field a basketball team for the 1907-08 season, Joyce convinced Rice to enroll in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Georgetown_University_Law_School" title="Georgetown University Law School">Georgetown University Law School</a> and play for the Hoyas in 1907-08.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rice_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-rice-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>In a low-scoring era, Rice got off to a spectacular start with his new team, scoring 20 or more points in four of his first seven games. Starting all seven games he played, he had no <a href="/wiki/Free_throw" title="Free throw">free-throw</a> attempts, but he scored 52 <a href="/wiki/Field_goal_(basketball)" title="Field goal (basketball)">field goals</a>, giving him 104 points and an average of 14.9 points per game; only one other Georgetown player would equal his per-game scoring average in the next 35 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rice_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-rice-4">[4]</a></sup> Rice would star for Georgetown for two more seasons before graduating from the Law School.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rice_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-rice-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Considered the first great team in <a href="/wiki/Georgetown_Hoyas_men%27s_basketball" title="Georgetown Hoyas men's basketball">Georgetown men's basketball</a> history,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">[3]</a></sup> the 1907-08 squad won its first five games before losing an exhibition game to <a href="/wiki/Rutgers_Scarlet_Knights_men%27s_basketball" title="Rutgers Scarlet Knights men's basketball">Rutgers</a> and suffering its only regular-season loss to a <a href="/wiki/Penn_Quakers_men%27s_basketball" title="Penn Quakers men's basketball">Pennsylvania</a> team that went 23-4 for the year. The Hoyas finished the season with a record of 5-1 and won the mythical "Champions of the South" title for the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rice_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-rice-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
