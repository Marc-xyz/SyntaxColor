<p>The <b>Belfast quarters</b> are distinctive cultural zones within the city of <a href="/wiki/Belfast" title="Belfast">Belfast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>, whose identities have been developed as a spur to tourism and urban regeneration. These "quarters" differ from the traditional <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Districts_of_Belfast" title="Districts of Belfast">districts</a> into which Belfast is divided.
</p><p>The term "quarter" does not refer to one fourth (or one quarter) of the city, nor is every area in the centre of Belfast part of one of the quarters. Instead, each "quarter" reflects something about a neighbourhood's history and locale, with the "Quarter" label being something that has come into general use in recent years.
</p><p>Belfast's official tourism website describes four quarters in the city.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cathedral_Quarter">Cathedral Quarter</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belfast_quarters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Cathedral Quarter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Cathedral_Quarter,_Belfast" title="Cathedral Quarter, Belfast">Cathedral Quarter</a> takes its name from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St_Anne%E2%80%99s_Cathedral,_Belfast" title="St Anne’s Cathedral, Belfast">St. Anne's Cathedral</a> and has taken on the mantle of the city's key cultural locality.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cathedral_Quarter_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cathedral_Quarter-1">[1]</a></sup> It boasts a yearly <a href="/wiki/Cathedral_Quarter_Arts_Festival" title="Cathedral Quarter Arts Festival">visual and performing arts festival</a>. Custom House Square is one of the city's main outdoor venues for free concerts and street entertainment.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:%27Titanic_Quarter%27_development,_Abercorn_Basin,_Belfast_-_geograph.org.uk_-_860766.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="502" data-file-width="640" decoding="async" height="173" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/%27Titanic_Quarter%27_development%2C_Abercorn_Basin%2C_Belfast_-_geograph.org.uk_-_860766.jpg/220px-%27Titanic_Quarter%27_development%2C_Abercorn_Basin%2C_Belfast_-_geograph.org.uk_-_860766.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/%27Titanic_Quarter%27_development%2C_Abercorn_Basin%2C_Belfast_-_geograph.org.uk_-_860766.jpg/330px-%27Titanic_Quarter%27_development%2C_Abercorn_Basin%2C_Belfast_-_geograph.org.uk_-_860766.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/%27Titanic_Quarter%27_development%2C_Abercorn_Basin%2C_Belfast_-_geograph.org.uk_-_860766.jpg/440px-%27Titanic_Quarter%27_development%2C_Abercorn_Basin%2C_Belfast_-_geograph.org.uk_-_860766.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:%27Titanic_Quarter%27_development,_Abercorn_Basin,_Belfast_-_geograph.org.uk_-_860766.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Construction in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Titanic_Quarter,_Belfast" title="Titanic Quarter, Belfast">Titanic Quarter</a></div></div></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Titanic_Quarter">Titanic Quarter</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belfast_quarters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Titanic Quarter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Titanic_Quarter,_Belfast" title="Titanic Quarter, Belfast">Titanic Quarter</a> covers 75 hectares of reclaimed land adjacent to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Port_of_Belfast" title="Port of Belfast">Belfast harbour</a>, formerly known as <i>Queen's Island</i>. Named after the <a href="/wiki/RMS_Titanic" title="RMS Titanic"><i>Titanic</i></a>, which was built there in 1912,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Titanic_In_History_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Titanic_In_History-2">[2]</a></sup> work has begun which aims to transform the former shipyard into "one of the largest waterfront developments in Europe".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Titanic_Quarter_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Titanic_Quarter-3">[3]</a></sup> Plans also include new apartments, a riverside entertainment district, and a major Titanic-themed museum.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Titanic_Quarter_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Titanic_Quarter-3">[3]</a></sup>  The museum was opened in 2012 and called <a href="/wiki/Titanic_Belfast" title="Titanic Belfast">Titanic Belfast</a>.
</p><p>Titanic Quarter also features the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SSE_Arena,_Belfast" title="SSE Arena, Belfast">SSE Arena</a> and Odyssey Pavilion, a major entertainment complex which has hosted concerts by such artists as <a href="/wiki/Iron_Maiden" title="Iron Maiden">Iron Maiden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slipknot_(band)" title="Slipknot (band)">Slipknot</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kings_of_Leon" title="Kings of Leon">Kings of Leon</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Muse_(band)" title="Muse (band)">Muse</a>, as well as comedy stand-up shows by <a href="/wiki/Peter_Kay" title="Peter Kay">Peter Kay</a> and <a href="/wiki/Russell_Howard" title="Russell Howard">Russell Howard</a>. It also played host to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MTV_EMA" title="MTV EMA">MTV EMA</a> Awards for the very first time on 6 November 2011.
</p>
<h2><span id="Queen.27s_Quarter"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Queen's_Quarter">Queen's Quarter</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belfast_quarters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Queen's Quarter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Queen%27s_Quarter,_Belfast" title="Queen's Quarter, Belfast">Queens Quarter</a> is named after <a href="/wiki/Queen%27s_University_Belfast" title="Queen's University Belfast">Queen's University</a> and is located in South Belfast, famous for its leafy, suburban atmosphere despite its proximity to the city centre. The area has a large student population and hosts the annual <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belfast_Festival_at_Queen%27s" title="Belfast Festival at Queen's">Belfast Festival at Queen's</a> each autumn. It is also home to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belfast_Botanic_Gardens" title="Belfast Botanic Gardens">Botanic Gardens</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Ulster_Museum" title="Ulster Museum">Ulster Museum</a>, which underwent extensive refurbishment for several years, reopening in 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Golden_Mile_(Belfast)" title="Golden Mile (Belfast)">The Golden Mile</a> is the name given to the mile between <a href="/wiki/Belfast_City_Hall" title="Belfast City Hall">Belfast City Hall</a> and Queen's University. Taking in Dublin Road, Great Victoria Street, Shaftesbury Square and Bradbury Place, it contains some of the best bars, restaurants and nightclubs in the city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Since the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belfast_Agreement" title="Belfast Agreement">Good Friday Agreement</a> in 1998, the nearby <a href="/wiki/Lisburn_Road" title="Lisburn Road">Lisburn Road</a> has developed into the city's most exclusive shopping strip.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Mural,_Falls_Road,_Belfast_(5)_-_geograph.org.uk_-_802522.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="435" data-file-width="640" decoding="async" height="150" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Mural%2C_Falls_Road%2C_Belfast_%285%29_-_geograph.org.uk_-_802522.jpg/220px-Mural%2C_Falls_Road%2C_Belfast_%285%29_-_geograph.org.uk_-_802522.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Mural%2C_Falls_Road%2C_Belfast_%285%29_-_geograph.org.uk_-_802522.jpg/330px-Mural%2C_Falls_Road%2C_Belfast_%285%29_-_geograph.org.uk_-_802522.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Mural%2C_Falls_Road%2C_Belfast_%285%29_-_geograph.org.uk_-_802522.jpg/440px-Mural%2C_Falls_Road%2C_Belfast_%285%29_-_geograph.org.uk_-_802522.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Mural,_Falls_Road,_Belfast_(5)_-_geograph.org.uk_-_802522.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Falls Road, the centre of <a href="/wiki/Gaeltacht_Quarter,_Belfast" title="Gaeltacht Quarter, Belfast">Gaeltacht Quarter</a>, features the (locally) famous 'International Wall', which generally depicts the community's political stance on world affairs</div></div></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gaeltacht_Quarter">Gaeltacht Quarter</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belfast_quarters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Gaeltacht Quarter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Gaeltacht_Quarter,_Belfast" title="Gaeltacht Quarter, Belfast">Gaeltacht Quarter</a> (<a href="/wiki/Irish_language" title="Irish language">Irish</a>: <i lang="ga">An Cheathrú Ghaeltachta</i>)  is an area ranging from Castle Street in Belfast City centre and westwards along <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Falls_Road_(Belfast)" title="Falls Road (Belfast)">Falls Road</a> in <a href="/wiki/Belfast" title="Belfast">West Belfast</a> which promotes, displays and encourages the use of the <a href="/wiki/Irish_language" title="Irish language">Irish language</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
