<p><b>Blendi Nallbani</b> (born 30 May 1971 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tiran%C3%AB" title="Tiranë">Tiranë</a>) is a retired <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albanian</a> professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who played as a <a href="/wiki/Goalkeeper_(association_football)" title="Goalkeeper (association football)">goalkeeper</a>. Nallbani was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Albania_national_football_team" title="Albania national football team">Albanian national team</a> prior to his international retirement in 2002.
</p><p>Nicknamed <i>Tata</i>, he is the youngest player to play for the national team only aged 17 at <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium_(1923)" title="Wembley Stadium (1923)">Wembley</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He also holds many records, such as most <a href="/wiki/Albanian_Superliga" title="Albanian Superliga">Albanian Superleague</a> winner and the player with the most matches in Albanian Superleague.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blendi_Nallbani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After the <a href="/wiki/Fall_of_communism_in_Albania" title="Fall of communism in Albania">fall of communism</a>, he was able to move abroad to play in Romania, but then moved back to Albania for good. He initially played for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Partizani_Tirana" title="Partizani Tirana">Partizani Tirana</a> and then, as soon as moving between clubs was allowed in <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albania</a>, he moved to his current team <a href="/wiki/KF_Tirana" title="KF Tirana">KF Tirana</a> for a transfer fee of <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">$</a>1,000,000 where has won multiple Championships, Cups, and Supercups. That was the most expensive transfer in the <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albanian</a> Football History at that time and made front-page news of all major newspapers. In 2001 he returned to Partizani.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tirana">Tirana</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blendi_Nallbani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Tirana">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Nallbani moved back to Tirana for a fourth stint in the summer of 2004 after four seasons at rival club Partizani Tirana. He joined goalkeepers <a href="/wiki/Isli_Hidi" title="Isli Hidi">Isli Hidi</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nigerian" title="Nigerian">Nigerian</a> <a href="/wiki/Ndubuisi_Egbo" title="Ndubuisi Egbo">Ndubuisi Egbo</a> at the club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blendi_Nallbani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International_debut">International debut</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blendi_Nallbani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: International debut">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At only seventeen years old, Nallbani was the youngest ever player to play for the <a href="/wiki/Albania_national_football_team" title="Albania national football team">Albanian National Team</a>. He conceded five goals to a strong <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">English side</a> in his premiere at <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium" title="Wembley Stadium">Wembley Stadium</a>. However, had it not been for his strong performance, <a href="/wiki/Albania_national_football_team" title="Albania national football team">Albania</a> would have suffered an even greater defeat. After the game, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/English_media" title="English media">English media</a> praised Nallbani's performance as one of the greatest performances and <a href="/wiki/Peter_Shilton" title="Peter Shilton">Peter Shilton</a> was himself amazed by the performance of the young Albanian.
</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"He (Shilton) was 21 when he first pulled on an <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a> jersey and that helped to produce a fascinating statistic when <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a> met <a href="/wiki/Albania_national_football_team" title="Albania national football team">Albania</a> in a <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">World Cup</a> qualifier at <a href="/wiki/Wembley" title="Wembley">Wembley</a> in 1989. <i>Blendi Nallbani</i>, the <a href="/wiki/Goalkeeper_(association_football)" title="Goalkeeper (association football)">goalkeeper</a> at the other end, was only 17 and hadn't been born when Shilts made his debut."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
</blockquote>
<p>At 17 years old, Nallbani was also the youngest goalkeeper to play in an <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a> match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>After that famous English game and another one against <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, Nallbani received offers from other famed European Clubs (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barcelona_FC" title="Barcelona FC">Barcelona FC</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Juventus" title="Juventus">Juventus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anderlecht" title="Anderlecht">Anderlecht</a>, and some <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romanian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkish</a> clubs). However, due to the "communist Albania," he was not allowed to play for teams outside the country or his family would be persecuted and possibly jailed.
</p><p>In the year, 1990, he was selected in the Under-21 Best European Selection and was supposed to play a charity game against the Africa/Asia Under-21. However, he was not able to attend this game for the same reasons that he was not allowed to play for a European Club at that time.
</p><p>Nallbani earned a total of 18 caps, scoring no goals. His final international was an April 2002 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Friendly_match" title="Friendly match">friendly match</a> against <a href="/wiki/Andorra_national_football_team" title="Andorra national football team">Andorra</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Relations_with_the_media">Relations with the media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blendi_Nallbani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Relations with the media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nallbani is known in Albania as being a bubbly and extravagant person who likes to make jokes with the Albanian media. During the 2008–2009 season in a heated derby match against his former team, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Partizani_Tirana" title="Partizani Tirana">Partizani Tirana</a> Nallbani was booked for handling the ball outside of his area, which angered the Partizani players and fans as they thought that the keeper deserved a sending off for the offence. After the match ended, Nallbani tried to explain in the changing rooms that he did not handle the ball outside his area saying
</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"I swear on my daughter, I didn't touch the ball with my hand."
</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Nallbani is the father of two children, both of them boys.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The referee Elvis Peza had also not booked Nallbani for a foul in the 55th minute on Partizani's <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazilian</a> striker <a href="/wiki/Paulo_Marcel_Pereira_Merabet" title="Paulo Marcel Pereira Merabet">Paulo Marcel Pereira Merabet</a>. This prompted an investigation from the FSHF (Albanian FA) into Peza's performance during the match which received a lot of media coverage in Albania as Nallbani was involved in two key decisions during the match. Former player and now head of Refereeing in Albania <a href="/wiki/Ilir_Shulku" title="Ilir Shulku">Ilir Shulku</a> quickly jumped to Peza's defence and put him off the hook.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blendi_Nallbani&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nallbani is married and has two children, both of which are boys, Marvin and Dejvi. One of his children has followed in his father's footsteps as a footballer. His son born in 1992, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Marvin_Nallbani&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Marvin Nallbani (page does not exist)">Marvin Nallbani</a>, is also a footballer and played for some time at KF Tirana with his father. Unlike his father he plays outfield, mostly in midfield and has been training with the Tirana senior squad since the age of 8.
</p><p>As of 2018, he is head of the omnisports club <i>Studenti</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
