<p>The second <b>Manuel Biplane</b> was a simple <a href="/wiki/Biplane" title="Biplane">biplane</a> <a href="/wiki/Glider_(sailplane)" title="Glider (sailplane)">glider</a> that was designed and built by Bill (W. L.) Manuel in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UK" title="UK">UK</a> in the late 1920s. It mostly flew tethered as a <a href="/wiki/Kite" title="Kite">kite</a>, in the propwash of a stationary powered aircraft.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design_and_development">Design and development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_1929_Biplane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Design and development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The very basic Manuel glider was an all wood <a href="/wiki/Wing_configuration#Wing_support" title="Wing configuration">two bay</a> <a href="/wiki/Biplane" title="Biplane">biplane</a> without <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stagger_(aviation)" title="Stagger (aviation)">stagger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Swept_wing" title="Swept wing">sweep</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dihedral_(aircraft)" title="Dihedral (aircraft)">dihedral</a>.  The bays were divided by pairs of parallel <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Interplane_strut" title="Interplane strut">interplane struts</a>; the interplane gap was large. The upper and lower wings had the same span and were precisely rectangular in plan, with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ailerons" title="Ailerons">ailerons</a> on the upper planes. The tail surfaces were carried on a horizontally orientated flat <a href="/wiki/Girder" title="Girder">girder</a> formed from two long wooden members joined by four cross struts and diagonal wire bracing.  This was fixed to the wing, at mid gap, through another pair of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Interplane_strut" title="Interplane strut">interplane struts</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flying_wires" title="Flying wires">wire braced</a> from the wings against vertical deflections. The horizontal tail was all-moving and a <a href="/wiki/Rhomboid" title="Rhomboid">rhomboidal</a> <a href="/wiki/Rudder" title="Rudder">rudder</a> was attached to a slender triangular <a href="/wiki/Fin" title="Fin">fin</a>.  The pilot sat, exposed, ahead of the lower wing on a short platform with the same width as the fuselage girder and extending a little behind the <a href="/wiki/Trailing_edge" title="Trailing edge">trailing edge</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ell_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ell-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The first flight was at <a href="/wiki/RAF_Hawkinge" title="RAF Hawkinge">RAF Hawkinge</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kent" title="Kent">Kent</a>, where Corporal Manuel was stationed, on 5 May 1929.  In later tests the Biplane was tethered on a 20 ft (6.1 m) rope to a <a href="/wiki/Gloster_Grebe" title="Gloster Grebe">Gloster Grebe</a> and flown in its propwash for a few minutes before the Grebe's <a href="/wiki/Armstrong_Siddeley_Jaguar" title="Armstrong Siddeley Jaguar">Armstrong Siddeley Jaguar</a> engine began to overheat.  The Biplane was eventually destroyed in these experiments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ell_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ell-1">[1]</a></sup>  
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Specifications">Specifications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_1929_Biplane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Specifications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p style="font-size: 90%; margin:0;"><i>Data from</i> British Gliders and Sailplanes 1971 p.141<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ell_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Ell-1">[1]</a></sup></p><p><span style="font-size: 120%;"><b>General characteristics</b></span>
</p><ul><li><b>Crew:</b> 1</li>
<li><b>Length:</b> 14 ft 5 in (4.39 m)</li>
<li><b>Wingspan:</b> 22 ft 0 in (6.71 m)</li>
<li><b>Wing area:</b> 160 sq ft (15 m<sup>2</sup>)</li>
