<p>The <b>Power Macintosh 6400</b> (also sold under variations of the name <b>Performa 6400</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Personal_computer" title="Personal computer">personal computer</a> designed, manufactured and sold by <a href="/wiki/Apple_Inc." title="Apple Inc.">Apple Computer</a> from August 1996 to August 1997. It is the only <a href="/wiki/Macintosh" title="Macintosh">Macintosh</a> mini-tower system to be branded as a <a href="/wiki/Macintosh_Performa" title="Macintosh Performa">Performa</a>, and alongside the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Power_Macintosh_6360" title="Power Macintosh 6360">Performa 6360</a> was the last new Performa-branded model introduced by Apple.
</p><p>The 6400 and its Performa variants were discontinued in favor of the uniformly-named <a href="/wiki/Power_Macintosh_6500" title="Power Macintosh 6500">Power Macintosh 6500</a> in August 1997.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hardware">Hardware</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Power_Macintosh_6400&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Hardware">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Logic board</b>: The logic board, code-named "Alchemy", is shared with the <a href="/wiki/Power_Macintosh_5400" title="Power Macintosh 5400">Power Macintosh 5400</a> all-in-one and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Performa_6360" title="Performa 6360">Performa 6360</a> desktop models. Alchemy is generally believed to be superior to its 5200/6200 predecessors since it had a 64-bit data path to main memory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p><b>CPU</b>: All 6400 models use a <a href="/wiki/PowerPC" title="PowerPC">PowerPC</a> 603e processor that is soldered to the computer's logic board and cooled by a fanless heat-sink. Though initially considered non-upgradable, CPU upgrades did come to market that overrode the fixed CPU by use of the Level 2 cache slot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p><b>Sound</b>: The new "InstaTower" case features an integrated <a href="/wiki/Subwoofer" title="Subwoofer">subwoofer</a> speaker and supports SRS surround-sound.  A knob on the back of the case controls the bass level of the subwoofer; this knob only works if external speakers are connected, otherwise the internal speaker provides full sound range.  There are two audio output jacks, one on the front and one on the back.  Headphones plugged into the front jack will disable the internal speaker and rear jack.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p><b>Expansion</b>: There are two PCI expansion slots as well as a dedicated communications slot that supports AAUI, Ethernet and fast modem cards.  The communication slot is not compatible with similar slots on other Macintosh computers.  <a href="/wiki/NuBus" title="NuBus">NuBus</a> cards are not supported.
</p><p><b>Operating system</b>: The Performa 6400/180 and 6400/200 were shipped with System 7.5.3 Revision 2.1; this particular release was specific to these machines as there were stability problems with System 7.5.3 Release 2 on the new hardware, especially with the video card and transferring files over LocalTalk.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Models">Models</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Power_Macintosh_6400&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Models">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>All Performa models shipped with <a href="/wiki/SurfWatch" title="SurfWatch">SurfWatch</a>, a product from <a href="/wiki/Spyglass,_Inc." title="Spyglass, Inc.">Spyglass, Inc.</a> that provides adult content filtering capability;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Content-control_software" title="Content-control software">content-control software</a> was a significant issue at the time due to the recent passage of the <a href="/wiki/Communications_Decency_Act" title="Communications Decency Act">Communications Decency Act</a> in the United States.
</p><p>Introduced August 5, 1996:
</p>
<ul><li><b>Performa 6400/180</b>: North American consumer model, 180 MHz CPU.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Performa 6400/200</b>: North American consumer model, 200 MHz CPU.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Performa 6400/200 Video Editing Edition</b>: The Performa 6400/200 with an <a href="/wiki/Avid_Technology" title="Avid Technology">Avid Cinema</a> video I/O card and video editing software, and <span class="nowrap">32 MiB RAM</span><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-macworldsecrets_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-macworldsecrets-1">[1]</a></sup></li></ul>
<p>Introduced October 1, 1996: 
</p>
<ul><li><b>Power Macintosh 6400/200</b>: Sold in the North American and British education markets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> $2,200 USD.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></li></ul>
<p>Introduced November 12, 1996:
</p>
<ul><li><b>Performa 6410</b>: Identical to the Performa 6400/180,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> sold in Europe and Asia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Performa 6420</b>: Identical to the Performa 6400/200,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> sold in Europe and Asia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></li></ul>
