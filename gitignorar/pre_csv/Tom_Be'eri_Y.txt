<p><b>Tom Beeri</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hebrew_language" title="Hebrew language">Hebrew</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="he">תום בארי</span>‎; born 17 May 1986 in <a href="/wiki/Yagur" title="Yagur">Yagur</a>) is an Israeli <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swimmer" title="Swimmer">swimmer</a> who represented Israel at the <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">2008 Summer Olympics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tom_Be%27eri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Be'eri was born and raised in Kibbutz Yagur, Israel. He won multiple Israeli National Champion titles  in the 100 meter <a href="/wiki/Breaststroke" title="Breaststroke">Breaststroke</a> and the 200 meter Breaststroke. Upon completing mandatory military service, he set an Israeli national record and qualified for the 2008 Summer Olympic Games in Beijing, China. He also served as the Israeli Olympic swim team captain and set two Israeli national records at the Games. 
</p><p>After the Olympic Games, Be’eri was recruited by many of the top collegiate swimming programs in the U.S. before becoming a student-athlete at the University of Georgia in 2009. As a collegiate swimmer, he represented the UGA swimming and diving team at SEC championships and at the NCAA national championships. As a student he earned and was recognized for four Athletic Director’s academic awards as well as four SEC academic awards. In 2013, Be'eri graduated with a dual degree in International Affairs-Political Economy and Business Administration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>He competed on behalf of <a href="/wiki/Israel_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics" title="Israel at the 2008 Summer Olympics">Israel at the 2008 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>, China.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He is Jewish.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
