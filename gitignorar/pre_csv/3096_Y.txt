<p><i><b>3096 Days</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <i lang="de"><b>3096 Tage</b></i>) is a 2013 German <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drama_film" title="Drama film">drama film</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/Sherry_Hormann" title="Sherry Hormann">Sherry Hormann</a>. The film is based on the true story of <a href="/wiki/Natascha_Kampusch" title="Natascha Kampusch">Natascha Kampusch</a>, a 10-year-old girl and her eight-year ordeal being kidnapped by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wolfgang_P%C5%99iklopil" title="Wolfgang Přiklopil">Wolfgang Přiklopil</a>. Northern Irish actress <a href="/wiki/Antonia_Campbell-Hughes" title="Antonia Campbell-Hughes">Antonia Campbell-Hughes</a> portrays Kampusch,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> while <a href="/wiki/Thure_Lindhardt" title="Thure Lindhardt">Thure Lindhardt</a> plays Přiklopil.
</p><p>The film was writer <a href="/wiki/Bernd_Eichinger" title="Bernd Eichinger">Bernd Eichinger</a>'s last film before his sudden death, and Martin Moskowicz, head of TV and Film at <a href="/wiki/Constantin_Film" title="Constantin Film">Constantin Film</a>, and Ruth Toma (<i><a href="/wiki/Ein_Lied_von_Liebe_und_Tod" title="Ein Lied von Liebe und Tod">Gloomy Sunday</a></i>) took over.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Premise">Premise</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=3096_Days&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Premise">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 10-year-old <a href="/wiki/Natascha_Kampusch" title="Natascha Kampusch">Natascha Kampusch</a> (<a href="/wiki/Antonia_Campbell-Hughes" title="Antonia Campbell-Hughes">Antonia Campbell-Hughes</a>) is kidnapped by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wolfgang_P%C5%99iklopil" title="Wolfgang Přiklopil">Wolfgang Přiklopil</a> (<a href="/wiki/Thure_Lindhardt" title="Thure Lindhardt">Thure Lindhardt</a>) and spends 8 years in captivity in a small room until she manages to escape.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=3096_Days&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In late 2012, there were concerns about Campbell-Hughes' drastic <a href="/wiki/Weight_loss" title="Weight loss">weight-loss</a> for the film, however she stated in an interview in the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/London_Evening_Standard" title="London Evening Standard">Evening Standard</a></i> that she wanted "to suffer as much as she - Kampusch - did".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> She also noted that she has received a torn Achilles tendon, a broken toe, a fractured rib and various cuts and bruises, due to the film set being similar to the dungeon Kampusch was placed in.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
