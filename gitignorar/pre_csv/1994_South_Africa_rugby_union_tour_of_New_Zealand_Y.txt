<p>The <b>1994 South Africa rugby union tour of New Zealand</b> was a series of <a href="/wiki/Rugby_union" title="Rugby union">rugby union</a> matches played in <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_African_national_rugby_union_team" title="South African national rugby union team">South African national rugby union team</a>, from June - August 1994. The team played 14 matches in total; 11 against several <a href="/wiki/List_of_New_Zealand_rugby_union_teams" title="List of New Zealand rugby union teams">provincial rugby teams</a> and 3 against the <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_national_rugby_union_team" title="New Zealand national rugby union team">New Zealand national rugby union team</a>. South Africa won 10 out of the 11 matches against provincial sides, losing one match to Otago. They lost their first two test matches against the New Zealand national side and drew the last test. The South African coach for the tour was Ian McIntosh.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> This was the South African team's first tour to New Zealand since the controversial <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1981_South_Africa_rugby_union_tour" title="1981 South Africa rugby union tour">1981 South Africa rugby union tour</a>, which was received negatively by New Zealanders due to South Africa's <a href="/wiki/Apartheid" title="Apartheid">Apartheid</a> legislation at the time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
