<p><b>Plexin-A2</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Protein" title="Protein">protein</a> that in humans is coded by the <i>PLXNA2</i> <a href="/wiki/Gene" title="Gene">gene</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid8570614_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid8570614-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-entrez_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-entrez-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>This gene encodes a member of the <a href="/wiki/Plexin" title="Plexin">plexin</a>-A family of <a href="/wiki/Semaphorin" title="Semaphorin">semaphorin</a> <a href="/wiki/Co-receptor" title="Co-receptor">co-receptors</a>. Semaphorins are a large family of secreted or membrane-bound proteins that mediate repulsive effects on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Axon_pathfinding" title="Axon pathfinding">axon pathfinding</a> during <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nervous_system_development&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nervous system development (page does not exist)">nervous system development</a>. A subset of semaphorins are recognized by plexin-A/neuropilin transmembrane receptor complexes, triggering a cellular signal transduction cascade that leads to <a href="/wiki/Axon" title="Axon">axon</a> repulsion. This plexin-A family member is thought to transduce signals from <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Semaphorin-3A&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Semaphorin-3A (page does not exist)">semaphorin-3A</a> and -3C.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-entrez_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-entrez-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>In some studies, the PLXNA2 gene is associated with <a href="/wiki/Schizophrenia" title="Schizophrenia">schizophrenia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SZGene_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-SZGene-7">[7]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Anxiety" title="Anxiety">anxiety</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid17339520_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid17339520-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
