<p><b>AMR Research, Inc.</b> was an independent US research and industry analysis firm founded by Tony Friscia in 1986 and sold to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gartner_Research" title="Gartner Research">Gartner Research</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US$" title="US$">US$</a>64 million in 2009.  AMR was the leading firm in its sector focused on global supply chain best practices and all supporting technologies. Its services continue to be offered by Gartner.  The company, which serves many <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> companies,  provides subscription advisory and peer networking services to supply chain, sustainability, and IT executives in the consumer products, life sciences, manufacturing, and retail sectors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The company also researches and advises  technology and services vendors.  Major technology markets covered include ERP, supply chain planning and execution, green technologies, business intelligence / performance management, PLM, manufacturing operations, CRM, HCM, and sourcing and procurement.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AMR_Research&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>1986 - Founded by CEO Tony Friscia</li>
<li>1988 - Launched its first advisory service focused on Manufacturing Automation</li>
<li>1990 - Founded the Manufacturing Execution Systems Association (MESA) to serve industry need for standards in using off-the-shelf software to manage plant execution</li>
<li>1991 - Initiated coverage on the emerging ERP market and became a leader in supporting companies on the selection of these next generation systems</li>
<li>1994 - Launched <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Supply_Chain" title="Supply Chain">Supply Chain</a> advisory service—the first syndicated research service focused on supply chain software</li>
<li>1996 - Co-founded the <a href="/wiki/Supply-Chain_Council" title="Supply-Chain Council">Supply-Chain Council</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li>1998 - Co-developed the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Supply-Chain_Operations_Reference" title="Supply-Chain Operations Reference">Supply-Chain Operations Reference</a> (SCOR) model</li>
<li>2003 - Defined the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Demand-driven_supply_network" title="Demand-driven supply network">Demand-driven supply network</a> (DDSN) model</li>
<li>2004 - Published the first annual AMR Research Supply Chain Top 25</li>
<li>2006 - Launched Sustainability Strategies service</li>
<li>2007 - Made commitment to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clinton_Global_Initiative" title="Clinton Global Initiative">Clinton Global Initiative</a> to identify best practices in extending global supply chains into sub-Saharan Africa and to support local producers in this region</li>
<li>2009 - Acquired by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gartner_Inc." title="Gartner Inc.">Gartner Inc.</a></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Press_coverage">Press coverage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AMR_Research&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Press coverage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>AMR Research has been quoted in several publications:
</p>
<ul><li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BusinessWeek" title="BusinessWeek">BusinessWeek</a> quotes Kevin O'Marah, AMR Research's chief strategy officer, on the impact of the Swine Flu epidemic. <a class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20090901095253/http://www.businessweek.com/bwdaily/dnflash/content/apr2009/db20090427_311178.htm" rel="nofollow">Swine Flu: Lessons from SARS</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Supply_Chain_Management_Review" title="Supply Chain Management Review">Supply Chain Management Review</a> cites an AMR Research survey on the effort that companies are putting into measuring greenhouse gas emissions.<a class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110716020103/http://www.scmr.com/article/CA6668789.html?industryid=48316" rel="nofollow">Companies will focus on greenhouse gas in 2010</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/InformationWeek" title="InformationWeek">InformationWeek</a> discusses the AMR Research Supply Chain Top 25. <a class="external text" href="http://www.informationweek.com/blog/main/archives/2009/05/apple_dell_and.html;jsessionid=TKXU014T2P431QE1GHRSKHWATMY32JVN" rel="nofollow">Apple, Dell, And P&amp;G Head AMR's Supply Chain Top 25 List</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CIO_Magazine" title="CIO Magazine">CIO Magazine</a> reports on AMR Research's study of the best university supply chain programs. <a class="external text" href="http://www.cio.com/article/495302/Supply_Chain_U._AMR_Rates_Top_Colleges_for_Supply_Chain_Studies" rel="nofollow">Supply Chain U.: AMR Rates Top Colleges for Supply Chain Studies</a></li></ul>
