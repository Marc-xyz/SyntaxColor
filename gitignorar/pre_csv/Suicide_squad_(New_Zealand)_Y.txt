	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Suicide squad (New Zealand)</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>

The <b>suicide squad</b> in New Zealand was a so-nicknamed group of New Zealand Legislative Councillors appointed in 1950 by Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Sidney_Holland" title="Sidney Holland">Sidney Holland</a> tasked with voting the <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_Legislative_Council" title="New Zealand Legislative Council">New Zealand Legislative Council</a> out of existence.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Abolition_of_the_Upper_House">Abolition of the Upper House</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Suicide_squad_(New_Zealand)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Abolition of the Upper House">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>By the mid-20th century, the New Zealand Legislative Council was increasingly being looked on as ineffectual and making little difference to the legislative process, as it rarely criticised bills sent to it by the House. Many believed that it was now obsolete, with some favouring its reform, and others favouring its abolition. 
</p><p>The leader of the <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_National_Party" title="New Zealand National Party">National Party</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sidney_Holland" title="Sidney Holland">Sidney Holland</a>, introduced a <a href="/wiki/Private_member%27s_bill" title="Private member's bill">private member's bill</a> to abolish it in 1947. However, the Parliament of New Zealand was unable to amend the <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_Constitution_Act_1852" title="New Zealand Constitution Act 1852">New Zealand Constitution Act 1852</a> because it was an Act of the United Kingdom Parliament, meaning the New Zealand Parliament was barred from amending the parts of the Act dealing with the establishment of the Legislative Council.
</p><p>Therefore, it had to adopt the <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Westminster_1931" title="Statute of Westminster 1931">Statute of Westminster 1931</a>, which it did with the <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Westminster_Adoption_Act_1947" title="Statute of Westminster Adoption Act 1947">Statute of Westminster Adoption Act 1947</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-politics_source_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-politics_source-1">[1]</a></sup> Following the adoption of the Act, the Parliament of New Zealand passed the <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_Constitution_Amendment_(Request_and_Consent)_Act_1947" title="New Zealand Constitution Amendment (Request and Consent) Act 1947">New Zealand Constitution Amendment (Request and Consent) Act 1947</a>, and the Parliament of the United Kingdom passed the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Zealand_Constitution_Amendment_Act_1947" title="New Zealand Constitution Amendment Act 1947">New Zealand Constitution Amendment Act 1947</a>, which allowed the New Zealand Parliament to amend the Constitution Act and abolish the Legislative Council. 
</p><p>However, the Labour government did not actually enact the abolition itself, losing office in the <a href="/wiki/1949_New_Zealand_general_election" title="1949 New Zealand general election">1949 general election</a>. National formed the <a href="/wiki/First_National_Government_of_New_Zealand" title="First National Government of New Zealand">new government</a> under  Sidney Holland, and a key aide to Holland was <a href="/wiki/Clifton_Webb_(politician)" title="Clifton Webb (politician)">Clifton Webb</a>, who was appointed to <a href="/wiki/Attorney-General_(New_Zealand)" title="Attorney-General (New Zealand)">Attorney-General</a>. As <a href="/wiki/Minister_of_Justice_(New_Zealand)" title="Minister of Justice (New Zealand)">Minister of Justice</a>, Webb was responsible for drafting the legislation, the Legislative Council Abolition Act, that would result in the abolition of the <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_Legislative_Council" title="New Zealand Legislative Council">Legislative Council</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DNZB_Webb_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-DNZB_Webb-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>With nineteen of the existing twenty-five members of the council  known to be opposed to the bill, Holland appointed twenty-five new members on 22 June 1950, known as the <i>suicide squad</i>, to vote themselves out of existence, the same method the Australian state of <a href="/wiki/Queensland" title="Queensland">Queensland</a> had used to abolish its <a href="/wiki/Queensland_Legislative_Council" title="Queensland Legislative Council">upper house</a> in 1922.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> 
</p><p>Four more members were appointed on 27 July 1950, and to encourage co-operation from the remaining members, Holland promised to use the money saved through abolition to set up a fund for retired members, and a Statutes Revision Committee (now defunct) was established to carry out some of the scrutiny that the Legislative Council had been intended for. 
</p><p>On 1 December 1950, the Legislative Council met for the last time, and by a majority of ten voted itself out of existence. The Council was formally abolished on 1 January 1951.
</p><p>While abolition was intended as an interim measure, no serious attempts were made to introduce a new second chamber, and Parliament has remained unicameral ever since.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Members_of_the_suicide_squad">Members of the suicide squad</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Suicide_squad_(New_Zealand)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Members of the suicide squad">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Most members were appointed on 22 June 1950, although <a href="/wiki/William_Polson" title="William Polson">William Polson</a> was appointed 15 March 1950 and Allen, Bell, Croker, and Marumaru were appointed 27 July 1950.  <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Otto_Bishop" title="Thomas Otto Bishop">Thomas Otto Bishop</a> was first appointed on 9 March 1943 and after the expiry of his seven-year term, was reappointed on 15 March 1950; he was thus not part of the intake of new members.  All their terms lasted to 31 December 1950.
