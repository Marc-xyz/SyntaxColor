<p><b>Colmars</b> or <b>Colmars-les-Alpes</b> (<i>Còumars</i> in <a href="/wiki/Proven%C3%A7al_dialect" title="Provençal dialect">provençal</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/Communes_of_France" title="Communes of France">commune</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Alpes-de-Haute-Provence" title="Alpes-de-Haute-Provence">Alpes-de-Haute-Provence</a> <a href="/wiki/Departments_of_France" title="Departments of France">department</a> in southeastern <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>.
</p><p>The official name of the commune, according to the geographical code of the INSEE, is "Colmars", but it is always referred locally as "Colmars-les-Alpes", even though it is not recognised in legal situations. The signs on entering the village read "Colmars-les-Alpes".
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colmars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The village is situated at the confluence of the Verdon and Lance rivers, at an altitude of 1250 metres. The site of the village is a <i>site inscrit</i>, a site of special natural, scientific or historical interest.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hamlets">Hamlets</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colmars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Hamlets">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There are two other hamlets in the commune: Clignon-Haut and Clignon-Bas.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mountains_and_peaks">Mountains and peaks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colmars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Mountains and peaks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There are numerous high points in the commune. The main ones are:
</p>
<ul><li>Tête de l'Encombrette (2682m)</li>
<li>Autapie (2426m)</li>
<li>Roche Cline</li>
<li>Valpane</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colmars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name of the village comes from a hill dedicated to Mars by the Romans ("Collo Marto"). The village was originally situated further up a hill, but the site was judged to be too cramped and not possible to expand any further, so it was abandoned. It was destroyed by Raymond de Turenne in 1390.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Modern_era">Modern era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colmars&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Modern era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the French Wars of Religion, the site was the target of several attacks:
</p>
<ul><li>Paulon de Mauvans, a Protestant captain, pillaged the village in 1560;</li>
<li>Cartier, a captain operating on behalf of de Mauvans, took and ransomed the village in 1583;</li>
<li>The French Catholic League sacked the village a few years later.</li></ul>
