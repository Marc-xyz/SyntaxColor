<p><b>Tomás González Estrada</b> (born 1969) is a Colombian economist. He was an Economic Counselor in the government of <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9s_Pastrana_Arango" title="Andrés Pastrana Arango">Andrés Pastrana Arango</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Mines_and_Energy_(Colombia)" title="Ministry of Mines and Energy (Colombia)">Minister of Mines and Energy</a> in the second government of <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Santos" title="Juan Manuel Santos">Juan Manuel Santos</a>. On 7 March 2016, his resignation from the position was accepted due to the country's energy crisis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tom%C3%A1s_Gonz%C3%A1lez_Estrada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>González Estrada was born in <a href="/wiki/Bogot%C3%A1" title="Bogotá">Bogotá</a>. He holds a master's degree from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Los_Andes_(Colombia)" title="University of Los Andes (Colombia)">University of Los Andes</a>, with a Master's and Ph.D. in Economics from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_London" title="University of London">University of London</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He was a researcher and professor at the Faculty of Economics of the University of Los Andes.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tom%C3%A1s_Gonz%C3%A1lez_Estrada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tomás González was an Economic Counselor in the government of <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9s_Pastrana_Arango" title="Andrés Pastrana Arango">Andrés Pastrana</a> from 1998 to 2000. From 2000 and 2002 he was Deputy Director of National Planning under the direction of <a href="/wiki/Juan_Carlos_Echeverry_(politician)" title="Juan Carlos Echeverry (politician)">Juan Carlos Echeverry</a>. He has been technical secretary of the National Council of Economic and Social Policy (CONPES), External Affairs Manager of BP Colombia, member of the Council of Ministers, and a board member of ISA, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=ISAGEN&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="ISAGEN (page does not exist)">ISAGEN</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> [<a class="extiw" href="https://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/ISAGEN" title="es:ISAGEN">es</a>]</span>, FEN, IPSE, and UPME.
</p><p>González was appointed Deputy Minister of Mines and Energy under Ministers <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Rodado_Noriega" title="Carlos Rodado Noriega">Carlos Rodado Noriega</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mauricio_C%C3%A1rdenas_Santamar%C3%ADa" title="Mauricio Cárdenas Santamaría">Mauricio Cárdenas</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Federico_Renjifo_V%C3%A9lez" title="Federico Renjifo Vélez">Federico Renjifo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He was program coordinator of the Good Government Foundation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In 2014, in the second term of <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Santos" title="Juan Manuel Santos">Juan Manuel Santos</a>, he was appointed <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Mines_and_Energy_(Colombia)" title="Ministry of Mines and Energy (Colombia)">Minister of Mines and Energy</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Minister_of_Mines_and_Energy">Minister of Mines and Energy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tom%C3%A1s_Gonz%C3%A1lez_Estrada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Minister of Mines and Energy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Among González Estrada's initiatives were the elaboration and execution of Plan 5 Caribe – seeking to improve the provision of energy service in the Colombian Caribbean coast – the expansion and coverage for interconnected and non-interconnected areas of the country, and the production of crude oil in excess of one million barrels, that the country sustained for nine consecutive months for the first time in history. His pillars of management were supply, competitiveness, and equity.
</p><p>In March 2016 the <a href="/wiki/Office_of_the_Inspector_General_of_Colombia" title="Office of the Inspector General of Colombia">Inspector General of the Nation</a> opened a formal disciplinary investigation against González for contracts made between the Presidency of the Republic and a polling company, leading to his resignation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
