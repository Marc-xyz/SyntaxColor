<p><b>Leszno County</b> (<a href="/wiki/Polish_language" title="Polish language">Polish</a>: <i lang="pl">powiat leszczyński</i>) is a unit of territorial administration and local government (<a href="/wiki/Powiat" title="Powiat">powiat</a>) in <a href="/wiki/Greater_Poland_Voivodeship" title="Greater Poland Voivodeship">Greater Poland Voivodeship</a>, west-central <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>. It came into being on January 1, 1999, as a result of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polish_local_government_reforms" title="Polish local government reforms">Polish local government reforms</a> passed in 1998. Its administrative seat is the city of <a href="/wiki/Leszno" title="Leszno">Leszno</a>, although the city is not part of the county (it constitutes a separate city county). The only towns in Leszno County are <a href="/wiki/Rydzyna" title="Rydzyna">Rydzyna</a>, which lies 9 km (6 mi) south-east of Leszno, and <a href="/wiki/Osieczna" title="Osieczna">Osieczna</a>, 10 km (6 mi) north-east of Leszno.
</p><p>The county covers an area of 804.65 square kilometres (310.7 sq mi). As of 2006 its total population is 50,024, out of which the population of Rydzyna is 2,539, that of Osieczna is 2,018, and the rural population is 45,467.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Neighbouring_counties">Neighbouring counties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leszno_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Neighbouring counties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Apart from the city of <a href="/wiki/Leszno" title="Leszno">Leszno</a>, Leszno County is also bordered by <a href="/wiki/Ko%C5%9Bcian_County" title="Kościan County">Kościan County</a> to the north, <a href="/wiki/Gosty%C5%84_County" title="Gostyń County">Gostyń County</a> to the east, <a href="/wiki/Rawicz_County" title="Rawicz County">Rawicz County</a> to the south-east, <a href="/wiki/G%C3%B3ra_County" title="Góra County">Góra County</a> to the south, <a href="/wiki/Wschowa_County" title="Wschowa County">Wschowa County</a> to the west, and <a href="/wiki/Wolsztyn_County" title="Wolsztyn County">Wolsztyn County</a> to the north-west.
</p>
