<p><b>Saint-Damase-de-L'Islet</b> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Municipality_(Quebec)" title="Municipality (Quebec)">municipality</a> in <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, with a population of about 600 people nestled in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Appalachian_mountains" title="Appalachian mountains">Appalachian mountains</a>. It is located about 15 kilometres (9.3 mi) southeast of <a href="/wiki/Saint-Jean-Port-Joli,_Quebec" title="Saint-Jean-Port-Joli, Quebec">Saint-Jean-Port-Joli</a>. It is named after the <a href="/wiki/Pope_Damasus_I" title="Pope Damasus I">Pope Damasus I</a> and Damase Ouellet (1826–1908), which is known as the pioneer of the town.
</p><p>The town was first named "Municipalité du canton d'Ashford" in 1898 and got its current name in 1955.
</p><p>It is known for its local Chicken Festival every September.
</p>
