<p><b>Arad</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hebrew_language" title="Hebrew language">Hebrew</a>: <link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r886049626" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><span class="script-hebrew" dir="rtl" style="font-size: 115%;">עֲרָד</span> <small><span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap;margin-right:.25em;"><a href="/wiki/File:Arad.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" data-file-height="20" data-file-width="20" decoding="async" height="11" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" width="11"/></a></span><a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/c4/Arad.ogg" title="Arad.ogg">(audio)</a></span> <small class="metadata audiolinkinfo" style="cursor:help;">(<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Media_help" title="Wikipedia:Media help"><span style="cursor:help;">help</span></a>·<a href="/wiki/File:Arad.ogg" title="File:Arad.ogg"><span style="cursor:help;">info</span></a>)</small></span></small>) is a city in the <a href="/wiki/Southern_District_(Israel)" title="Southern District (Israel)">Southern District</a> of <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>. It is located on the border of the <a href="/wiki/Negev" title="Negev">Negev</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Judaean_Desert" title="Judaean Desert">Judean Deserts</a>, 25 kilometres (16 miles) west of the <a href="/wiki/Dead_Sea" title="Dead Sea">Dead Sea</a> and 45 kilometres (28 miles) east of <a href="/wiki/Beersheba" title="Beersheba">Beersheba</a>. The city is home to a diverse population of 26,451,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Israelpopulations_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Israelpopulations-1">[1]</a></sup> including <a href="/wiki/Ashkenazi_Jews" title="Ashkenazi Jews">Ashkenazi</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sephardi" title="Sephardi">Sephardi</a> <a href="/wiki/Jews" title="Jews">Jews</a>, both secular and religious, <a href="/wiki/Negev_Bedouin" title="Negev Bedouin">Bedouins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Black_Hebrew_Israelites" title="Black Hebrew Israelites">Black Hebrews</a>, as well as new immigrants.
</p><p>After attempts to settle the area in the 1920s, Arad was founded in November 1962 as an Israeli <a href="/wiki/Development_town" title="Development town">development town</a>, the first planned city in Israel. Arad's population grew significantly with the Aliyah from the former Soviet Union and peaked in 2002 at 24,500 residents.
</p><p>Landmarks in Arad include the ruins of <a href="/wiki/Tel_Arad" title="Tel Arad">Tel Arad</a>, Arad Park, a domestic airfield and Israel's first legal race circuit. The city is known for its annual summer music festival, the Arad Festival.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arad,_Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Antiquity">Antiquity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arad,_Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Antiquity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tel_Arad.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3000" data-file-width="4000" decoding="async" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/Tel_Arad.JPG/220px-Tel_Arad.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/Tel_Arad.JPG/330px-Tel_Arad.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/Tel_Arad.JPG/440px-Tel_Arad.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Tel_Arad.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Tel Arad</div></div></div>
<p>Arad is named after the Biblical <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Age" title="Bronze Age">Bronze Age</a> <a href="/wiki/Canaan" title="Canaan">Canaanite</a> town located at <a href="/wiki/Tel_Arad" title="Tel Arad">Tel Arad</a> (a <a href="/wiki/Biblical_archaeology" title="Biblical archaeology">Biblical archaeology</a> site famous for the discovery of <a href="/wiki/Ostracon" title="Ostracon">ostraca</a>), which is located approximately 8 kilometers (5.0 mi) west of modern Arad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ariel_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-ariel-3">[3]</a></sup> The Bible (Judges 1:16) describes it as a Canaanite stronghold whose king kept the <a href="/wiki/Israelites" title="Israelites">Israelites</a> from moving from the <a href="/wiki/Negev" title="Negev">Negev</a> to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Judean_Mountains" title="Judean Mountains">Judean Mountains</a>, although Tel Arad was destroyed over 1,200 years before the arrival of the Israelites. However, <a href="/wiki/Shoshenq_I" title="Shoshenq I">Shoshenq I</a>'s chronicles seem to mention a settlement in Tel Arad. After its destruction during the Canaanite era, the town lay abandoned for centuries before being resettled by the Israelites from the 11th century BC onward. The Israelites initially settled it as an unwalled piece of land cut off as an official or sacred domain was established on the upper hill. It was later a garrison-town known as "The Citadel". The citadel and sanctuary are believed to have been constructed at the time of Kings <a href="/wiki/David" title="David">David</a> and <a href="/wiki/Solomon" title="Solomon">Solomon</a>. Artifacts found within the sanctuary of the citadel mostly reflect offerings of oil, wine, wheat, etc. brought there by numerous people during the time of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Judah" title="Kingdom of Judah">Kingdom of Judah</a>, up to Judah's fall to the Babylonians. Under the Judaean kings, the citadel was periodically refortified, remodeled and rebuilt, until ultimately it was destroyed between 597 BCE and 577 BCE whilst Jerusalem was under siege by Babylonian king <a href="/wiki/Nebuchadnezzar_II" title="Nebuchadnezzar II">Nebuchadnezzar II</a>. However, during the <a href="/wiki/Persian_Empire" title="Persian Empire">Persian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maccabees" title="Maccabees">Maccabean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman</a>, and early <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> eras, locals continued to transport these items to the sacred precinct of the upper hill. Markers of these ancient Israelite rituals remain to this day, with broken pottery littering the entire site.
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine</a> period, the location was still correctly identified by <a href="/wiki/Eusebius" title="Eusebius">Eusebius</a>, and the name "Arad" was preserved by the Bedouins.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mapa_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-mapa-4">[4]</a></sup> Ancient Arad became a Christian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bishopric" title="Bishopric">bishopric</a>. Stephanus, one of its bishops, was a signatory of the synodal letter of John III of Jerusalem against <a href="/wiki/Severus_of_Antioch" title="Severus of Antioch">Severus of Antioch</a> in 518 and took part in the 536 synod of the three <a href="/wiki/Roman_province" title="Roman province">Roman provinces</a> of <a href="/wiki/Palaestina_Prima" title="Palaestina Prima">Palaestina Prima</a>, <a href="/wiki/Palaestina_Secunda" title="Palaestina Secunda">Palaestina Secunda</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Palaestina_Salutaris" title="Palaestina Salutaris">Palaestina Salutaris</a> (to the last of which Arad belonged) against <a href="/wiki/Anthimus_I_of_Constantinople" title="Anthimus I of Constantinople">Anthimus I of Constantinople</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> No longer a residential bishopric, Arad is today listed by the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic Church</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Titular_see" title="Titular see">titular see</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="British_Mandate_era">British Mandate era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arad,_Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: British Mandate era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first modern attempt to settle the area was made by the <a href="/wiki/Yishuv" title="Yishuv">Yishuv</a>, the body of Jewish residents in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mandate_Palestine" title="Mandate Palestine">Mandatory Palestine</a>, on 23 February 1921, when the British Mandate government allowed discharged soldiers from the <a href="/wiki/Jewish_Legion" title="Jewish Legion">Jewish Legion</a> to settle in the area. Nine men and two women attempted the task, but after four months were forced to leave because water was not found in the area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ariel_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-ariel-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="State_of_Israel">State of Israel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arad,_Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: State of Israel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:WikiAir_IL-13-06_015_-_Arad.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2592" data-file-width="3888" decoding="async" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/WikiAir_IL-13-06_015_-_Arad.JPG/220px-WikiAir_IL-13-06_015_-_Arad.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/WikiAir_IL-13-06_015_-_Arad.JPG/330px-WikiAir_IL-13-06_015_-_Arad.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/WikiAir_IL-13-06_015_-_Arad.JPG/440px-WikiAir_IL-13-06_015_-_Arad.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:WikiAir_IL-13-06_015_-_Arad.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Aerial view of Arad</div></div></div>
<p>On 15 November 1960, a planning team, followed by a full-fledged committee on 29 December, was appointed by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Israeli_cabinet" title="Israeli cabinet">Israeli cabinet</a> to examine the possibility of establishing a city in the northeastern <a href="/wiki/Negev" title="Negev">Negev</a> desert and Arad region. An initial budget of 50,000 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Israeli_lira" title="Israeli lira">Israel pounds</a> was granted for the project, headed by <a href="/wiki/Aryeh_Eliav" title="Aryeh Eliav">Aryeh Eliav</a>. On 31 January 1961, the final location was chosen (3.5 kilometers (2.2 mi) southwest of Mount Kidod), and plans were approved for roads and water connections. In March 1961, blueprints  for a city of 10,000- 20,000 residents were drawn up. Yona Pitelson was the chief architect and planner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-museum_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-museum-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p>The plan took into account topography and climate, with residential buildings constructed with large inner courtyards that offered protection  from the desert sun and wind. High density residential areas were built first in order to create an urban milieu and shorten walking distances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-idan24-53_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-idan24-53-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Arad_plan.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1693" data-file-width="2044" decoding="async" height="182" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Arad_plan.png/220px-Arad_plan.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Arad_plan.png/330px-Arad_plan.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Arad_plan.png/440px-Arad_plan.png 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Arad_plan.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Original city plan for Arad</div></div></div>
<p>The oil company Nefta built a work camp in the area in July 1961, consisting of six temporary sheds, after oil was found there in commercial quantities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-museum_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-museum-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-new_israel_guide_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-new_israel_guide-10">[10]</a></sup> The town itself was established in 1962 by a group of young ex-<a href="/wiki/Kibbutz" title="Kibbutz">kibbutz</a> and ex-<a href="/wiki/Moshav" title="Moshav">moshav</a> members seeking an environment free of  overcrowding, traffic, noise, and pollution.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lexicon_hareuveni_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-lexicon_hareuveni-11">[11]</a></sup> The founding ceremony was held on 21 November, and attended by then-Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/David_Ben-Gurion" title="David Ben-Gurion">David Ben-Gurion</a>. It was one of the last <a href="/wiki/Development_town" title="Development town">development towns</a> to be founded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> According to the city website, Arad was the first pre-planned city in Israel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-muni89_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-muni89-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p><p>Until 1964 Arad had about 160 families, most of whom were natives. After 1971 Arad began absorbing <i><a href="/wiki/Aliyah" title="Aliyah">olim</a></i> (Jewish immigrants), mostly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aliyah_from_the_Soviet_Union_in_the_1970s" title="Aliyah from the Soviet Union in the 1970s">from the Soviet Union</a>, but also from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/English_speaking_countries" title="English speaking countries">English speaking countries</a> and <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">Latin America</a>, and its population increased from 4,000 in 1969 to 10,500 in 1974 and 12,400 in 1983. During the first half of the 1990s, Arad absorbed 6,000 immigrants from the former Soviet Union.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mapa_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-mapa-4">[4]</a></sup> In 1995, the city had 20,900 residents. Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Yitzhak_Rabin" title="Yitzhak Rabin">Yitzhak Rabin</a> declared Arad a city on 29 June 1995.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-muni2_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-muni2-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arad,_Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:AradMap.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1048" data-file-width="1600" decoding="async" height="144" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/AradMap.png/220px-AradMap.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/AradMap.png/330px-AradMap.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/AradMap.png/440px-AradMap.png 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:AradMap.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Neighborhoods of Arad</div></div></div>
<p>Arad is located mostly on the western and southwestern Kidod Range, and the Arad Plain,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eretz_hanegev575_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-eretz_hanegev575-15">[15]</a></sup> which marks the southwestern end of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Judean_Desert" title="Judean Desert">Judean Desert</a>. It is located 23 kilometers (14.3 mi) west of the southern end of the <a href="/wiki/Dead_Sea" title="Dead Sea">Dead Sea</a>, and is by road, 45 kilometers (28.0 mi) east of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beer_Sheva" title="Beer Sheva">Beersheva</a>, 111 kilometers (69.0 mi) south of <a href="/wiki/Jerusalem" title="Jerusalem">Jerusalem</a>, 138 kilometers (85.7 mi) south east of <a href="/wiki/Tel_Aviv" title="Tel Aviv">Tel Aviv</a>, and 219 kilometers (136.1 mi) north of the southern-most city of <a href="/wiki/Eilat" title="Eilat">Eilat</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p><p>The city spans an area of 93,140 dunams (93.1 km<sup>2</sup>; 36.0 sq mi),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-profile_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-profile-17">[17]</a></sup> one of the largest municipal areas in Israel, even though its urban area is much smaller. In 1993, the city's jurisdiction was 73,934.3 dunams (73.9 km<sup>2</sup>; 28.5 sq mi), still many times larger than the urban area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The historical site of Tel Arad and the Arad Park (also known as Ran Grove) can also be found within its municipal area, west of the urban core. Arad also has a commercial landing strip located slightly to the south of its urban core. It borders the <a href="/wiki/Tamar_Regional_Council" title="Tamar Regional Council">Tamar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Abu_Basma_Regional_Council" title="Abu Basma Regional Council">Abu Basma</a> regional councils, and the closest local municipality to Arad is the <a href="/wiki/Negev_Bedouin" title="Negev Bedouin">Bedouin</a> local council <a href="/wiki/Kuseife" title="Kuseife">Kuseife</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geology_and_topography">Geology and topography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arad,_Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Geology and topography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Kidod_Stream.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1024" data-file-width="4797" decoding="async" height="53" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Kidod_Stream.jpg/250px-Kidod_Stream.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Kidod_Stream.jpg/375px-Kidod_Stream.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Kidod_Stream.jpg/500px-Kidod_Stream.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Kidod_Stream.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Kidod Stream (Wadi Kadada)</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Praim_Stream.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="666" data-file-width="1024" decoding="async" height="163" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Praim_Stream.jpg/250px-Praim_Stream.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Praim_Stream.jpg/375px-Praim_Stream.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Praim_Stream.jpg/500px-Praim_Stream.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Praim_Stream.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Pra'im Stream</div></div></div>
<p>The western part of Arad is made up of <a href="/wiki/Loess" title="Loess">Loess</a> land, while the eastern part is made up of <a href="/wiki/Sedimentary_rock" title="Sedimentary rock">sedimentary rock</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Chalk" title="Chalk">chalk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flint" title="Flint">flint</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dolomite_(rock)" title="Dolomite (rock)">dolomite</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eretz_hanegev573_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-eretz_hanegev573-19">[19]</a></sup>
</p><p>Arad's elevation ranges between approximately 361.5 and 631.1 metres (1,186 and 2,071 ft) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Above_mean_sea_level" title="Above mean sea level">above sea level</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-profile_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-profile-17">[17]</a></sup> a notable peak being Mount Kidod (600 m or 2,000 ft), located at the northeastern point of the city and named after Ras al-Kadadeh, the Arabic name for the hill.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ariel_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-ariel-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mapa_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-mapa-4">[4]</a></sup> Other peaks within the municipal borders include Mount Kina (635 m or 2,083 ft) and Mount Brir (537 m or 1,762 ft). There are several <a href="/wiki/Wadi" title="Wadi">wadis</a> that pass through Arad, notably the Ye'elim Stream (which runs along Highway 31) and <a href="/wiki/Tze%27elim_Stream" title="Tze'elim Stream">Tze'elim Stream</a>. Others include the Hesed, Keisan, Kidod, Kina, Malhata, Pra'im, Tavya, and other streams.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-topo_map_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-topo_map-20">[20]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arad,_Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Flickr_-_U.S._Embassy_Tel_Aviv_-_Ambassador_Shapiro_visits_the_city_of_Arad_No.293.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="931" data-file-width="1200" decoding="async" height="171" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Flickr_-_U.S._Embassy_Tel_Aviv_-_Ambassador_Shapiro_visits_the_city_of_Arad_No.293.jpg/220px-Flickr_-_U.S._Embassy_Tel_Aviv_-_Ambassador_Shapiro_visits_the_city_of_Arad_No.293.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Flickr_-_U.S._Embassy_Tel_Aviv_-_Ambassador_Shapiro_visits_the_city_of_Arad_No.293.jpg/330px-Flickr_-_U.S._Embassy_Tel_Aviv_-_Ambassador_Shapiro_visits_the_city_of_Arad_No.293.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Flickr_-_U.S._Embassy_Tel_Aviv_-_Ambassador_Shapiro_visits_the_city_of_Arad_No.293.jpg/440px-Flickr_-_U.S._Embassy_Tel_Aviv_-_Ambassador_Shapiro_visits_the_city_of_Arad_No.293.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Flickr_-_U.S._Embassy_Tel_Aviv_-_Ambassador_Shapiro_visits_the_city_of_Arad_No.293.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>US Ambassador Shapiro visits Arad solar plant</div></div></div>
<p>Between 1964 and 1974, Arad's average temperature in January was 11 °C (52 °F), and 27 °C (81 °F) in July. The average yearly precipitation between 1960 and 1990 was 150 millimetres (5.9 in),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-profile_17-2"><a href="#cite_note-profile-17">[17]</a></sup> and 158 millimetres (6.2 in) between 1962 and 1978.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eretz_hanegev573_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-eretz_hanegev573-19">[19]</a></sup> Arad is thus situated in an arid area, where most precipitation falls in the winter months of December, January and February. It does however, on rare occasions, snow. Two examples are the massive snowstorm during the 1991–92 winter that swept through entire mountainous region of the Negev and January's snowstorm of 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arad,_Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Arad_demographics.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="266" data-file-width="471" decoding="async" height="124" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/24/Arad_demographics.png/220px-Arad_demographics.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/24/Arad_demographics.png/330px-Arad_demographics.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/24/Arad_demographics.png/440px-Arad_demographics.png 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Arad_demographics.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Distribution of population by age</div></div></div>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/Israel_Central_Bureau_of_Statistics" title="Israel Central Bureau of Statistics">Israel Central Bureau of Statistics</a>, of Arad's 24,400 2015 population, 80.3% were Jews, and another 16.8% were other non-Arabs. 11,900 (~48.8%) were men and 12,500 were women. 33.8% (the second highest in the country) were immigrants since 1990.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-profile2015_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-profile2015-23">[23]</a></sup> Arad's population peaked in 2002 at 24,500.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cbs_2002_pop_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-cbs_2002_pop-24">[24]</a></sup> Arad's former mayor, Moty Brill, said that the reason for the city's decline is its failure to absorb the massive immigration from Russia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ben-simon_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-ben-simon-25">[25]</a></sup> In early 2007, a study ordered by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Housing_and_Construction_Minister_of_Israel" title="Housing and Construction Minister of Israel">Israeli Housing and Construction Ministry</a> found that Arad suffered from a negative image, and described it a "settlement that drives residents away".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mirkovsky_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-mirkovsky-26">[26]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2015, Arad had 10,983 (~45%) salaried workers and 529 (~2.2%) self-employed. 11,805 were receiving children's benefits, 321 were receiving unemployment benefits, and 1,168 were receiving income guarantee. The average monthly income for self-employed workers was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Israeli_Sheqel" title="New Israeli Sheqel">NIS</a> 6,934, with salaried employees earning an average of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Israeli_Sheqel" title="New Israeli Sheqel">NIS</a> 6,988 (NIS 9,008 for men and NIS 5,184 for women).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-profile2015_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-profile2015-23">[23]</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Israel_Defense_Forces" title="Israel Defense Forces">IDF</a>'s City of Training Bases, under construction near <a href="/wiki/Beersheba" title="Beersheba">Beersheba</a>, hopes to bring thousands of soldiers (mostly officers, senior NCOs and other staff) to live in the Negev, including Arad. According to Orli Yehezkel, CEO of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Development_of_the_Negev_and_Galilee_Minister_of_Israel" title="Development of the Negev and Galilee Minister of Israel">Ministry for the Development of the Negev and Galilee</a>, said <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Israeli_Shekel" title="New Israeli Shekel">NIS</a> 4.5 million will be invested in Arad, including a subsidy of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Israeli_Sheqel" title="New Israeli Sheqel">NIS</a> 1,000 per family per month for two years for families of soldiers wishing to move to Arad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ministry_for_the_Development_of_the_Negev_and_Galilee" title="Ministry for the Development of the Negev and Galilee">Negev and Galilee Development Ministry</a> envisions a tripling of Arad's population by 2025.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jpost-population_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-jpost-population-28">[28]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arad,_Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Arad_Mall_outside.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1018" data-file-width="2244" decoding="async" height="100" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/Arad_Mall_outside.jpg/220px-Arad_Mall_outside.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/Arad_Mall_outside.jpg/330px-Arad_Mall_outside.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/Arad_Mall_outside.jpg/440px-Arad_Mall_outside.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Arad_Mall_outside.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Arad Mall</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Arad_Market.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1024" data-file-width="2426" decoding="async" height="93" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fa/Arad_Market.jpg/220px-Arad_Market.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fa/Arad_Market.jpg/330px-Arad_Market.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fa/Arad_Market.jpg/440px-Arad_Market.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Arad_Market.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Arad Market on a closed day</div></div></div>
<p>Other than tourist venues, Arad's commerce is mostly concentrated in the central commerce area, as envisioned in the original plan. There is one shopping mall in the city, the Arad Mall, in the northern block of the commerce sector.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tourist_map_h3_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-tourist_map_h3-29">[29]</a></sup> In addition to the main area, there are small shop clusters in most neighborhoods in the city, notably a building called The Star (<i>HaKokhav</i>) in the Tlalim quarter. A lone supermarket outside the center, Mega in the City, is located in the industrial zone, near the entrance to the city, and a major shopping complex is planned near it by a real estate company called Zim Centers. The Arad Market, open on Mondays, is located in the light industry zone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup>
</p><p>Among the companies with manufacturing plants in Arad are Arad Textile Industries, one of Israel's largest producers of <a href="/wiki/Towel" title="Towel">towels</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flextronics" title="Flextronics">Flextronics</a> Israel, A.M.S. Electronics who manufacture <a href="/wiki/Printed_circuit_board" title="Printed circuit board">printed circuit boards</a> and electronic products, El-Ran Timber Industries, Jordael who manufacture cosmetic products, and a Unilever Shefa Israel cereal plant. Since 1971, Arad has been producing <a href="/wiki/Phosphoric_acid" title="Phosphoric acid">phosphoric acid</a>, made from <a href="/wiki/Brine" title="Brine">brine</a> collected from the Dead Sea, and phosphates from the nearby <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tzefa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tzefa (page does not exist)">Tzefa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-new_israel_guide_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-new_israel_guide-10">[10]</a></sup> Rotem Amfert Negev Ltd, a subsidiary of Israel Chemicals Ltd (ICL), has been planning a new phosphate plant in the Sdeh Brir area for several years. In 2008, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ministry_of_the_Environment_(Israel)" title="Ministry of the Environment (Israel)">Ministry of the Environment</a> decided not to contest the company's bid, despite studies showing it may increase air pollution-related deaths in the area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bendavid_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-bendavid-31">[31]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arad,_Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Tourism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Mitzpe_Moav.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="691" data-file-width="1024" decoding="async" height="148" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Mitzpe_Moav.jpg/220px-Mitzpe_Moav.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Mitzpe_Moav.jpg/330px-Mitzpe_Moav.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Mitzpe_Moav.jpg/440px-Mitzpe_Moav.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Mitzpe_Moav.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yigal_Tumarkin" title="Yigal Tumarkin">Yigal Tumarkin</a>'s monument in the Moav Outlook</div></div></div>
<p>In addition to the annual music festival, Arad's fresh air attracts asthmatics from all over the world, and its proximity to the <a href="/wiki/Dead_Sea" title="Dead Sea">Dead Sea</a> provides a less expensive accommodation alternative to the pricey hotel zone on the sea shore.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-new_israel_guide_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-new_israel_guide-10">[10]</a></sup>  The Arad municipality has begun to develop hiking and ecological tourism. Additionally, many visitors to <a href="/wiki/Masada" title="Masada">Masada</a> stay in Arad, which is the closest (22 km (13.7 mi) by road) urban settlement to the site and provides the only access to its western side.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mapa_42_v23_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-mapa_42_v23-32">[32]</a></sup> On the outskirts of Arad, near the hotel area, there is a large white monument conceived by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yigal_Tumarkin" title="Yigal Tumarkin">Yigal Tumarkin</a> in 1968, called <i>Mitzpe Mo'av</i> (Mo'av Lookout), which also offers a view of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Judean_Desert" title="Judean Desert">Judean Desert</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-muni_139_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-muni_139-33">[33]</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Israel_National_Trail" title="Israel National Trail">Israel National Trail</a> passes through Arad, between Mount Kina and <a href="/wiki/Tel_Arad" title="Tel Arad">Tel Arad</a>, a major archeological site and national park. Although not located within Arad's jurisdiction, the Zohar Peak (552 m or 1,811 ft) and Zohar Lookout (<i>Mitzpe Zohar</i>) are common hiking and biking destinations on the national trail. The trails codenamed 11335 and 11240 run along the national trail in this area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arad,_Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Arad's main cultural center is the Center for Culture, Youth and Sports (<a href="/wiki/Hebrew_language" title="Hebrew language">Hebrew</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="he">מתנ"ס</span>‎, <i>Matnas</i>), named after <a href="/wiki/Samuel_M._Rubin" title="Samuel M. Rubin">Samuel Rubin</a> and located on Ben Yair Street near the Arad Mall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tourist_map_h3_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-tourist_map_h3-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> It was built in 1983<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> and contains the Arad Museum, Arad's public library, a concert hall and the Arad Visitor Center, founded in 1989.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lexicon_hareuveni_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-lexicon_hareuveni-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Oron movie theater designed by the architect <a href="/wiki/Menachem_Cohen_(architect)" title="Menachem Cohen (architect)">Menachem Cohen</a> closed  during the tenure of Mayor Moty Brill. Eshet Lot, an artists quarter, is located in the city's industrial zone, utilizing old warehouses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> The city also has a <a href="/wiki/Music_school" title="Music school">conservatory</a> and concert hall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2007,  the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ministry_for_the_Development_of_the_Negev_and_Galilee" title="Ministry for the Development of the Negev and Galilee">Ministry for the Development of the Negev and Galilee</a> proposed moving the national archive of Israel from <a href="/wiki/Jerusalem" title="Jerusalem">Jerusalem</a> to Arad and opening a museum.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-grinberg_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-grinberg-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup>
</p><p>Arad was  known for its annual music festival, first held in  1982. It was a popular event and a major magnet for artists until 1995. On 18 July 1995, three teenagers were crushed to death by a falling gate during a farewell concert by the band <a href="/wiki/Mashina" title="Mashina">Mashina</a>. Despite the festival organizers' attempts to keep the festival going it was cut short. Five of the festival's organizers received prison sentences of up to one year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Gan_HaHamisha.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1888" data-file-width="1024" decoding="async" height="313" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Gan_HaHamisha.jpg/170px-Gan_HaHamisha.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Gan_HaHamisha.jpg/255px-Gan_HaHamisha.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Gan_HaHamisha.jpg/340px-Gan_HaHamisha.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Gan_HaHamisha.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Stone structure in Gan HaHamisha, marking Arad's place in the desert, according to Bedouin tradition</div></div></div>
<p>The main public park and picnic area is  Arad Park, also called the Ran Grove (after Ran Schochat, who was killed in the <a href="/wiki/Yom_Kippur_War" title="Yom Kippur War">Yom Kippur War</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aradnik-memorials_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-aradnik-memorials-42">[42]</a></sup>), which is located about 8 kilometers (5.0 mi) west of the city center and measures 3,000 dunams (3.0 km<sup>2</sup>; 1.2 sq mi).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mapa_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-mapa-4">[4]</a></sup> Other notable parks include:
</p>
<ul><li><i>Gan HaPsalim</i> (a.k.a. <i>Park HaNsharim</i>), the park at the entrance to the city, with monuments of various desert animals<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tourist_map_ch4_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-tourist_map_ch4-43">[43]</a></sup></li>
<li><i>Gan HaHamisha</i> (Garden of the Five), a memorial park for the five residents of Arad who were <a href="/wiki/Killed_in_action" title="Killed in action">killed in action</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Six-Day_War" title="Six-Day War">Six-Day War</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aradnik-memorials_42-1"><a href="#cite_note-aradnik-memorials-42">[42]</a></sup> Includes a monument for the soldiers, as well as a stone structure called <i>Amud HaBulbusim</i> (<i>lit.</i> Pillar of the Potatoes, because of its shape), which marks Arad's place in the desert and their residents' control of the territory according to Bedouin tradition – designed by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Yona_Pitelson&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Yona Pitelson (page does not exist)">Yona Pitelson</a>.</li>
<li>Northern Park, to the north of the Halamish neighborhood<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tourist_map_v2_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-tourist_map_v2-44">[44]</a></sup></li>
<li><i>Gan Harpatka'ot</i> (Adventure Park), a large playground and open grass area in the Rishonim neighborhood<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tourist_map_h4_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-tourist_map_h4-45">[45]</a></sup></li></ul>
<p>The main cluster of recreational facilities is located in the Ayanot neighborhood also called Kiryat HaSport. Facilities include a swimming pool, tennis courts, a <a href="/wiki/Country_club" title="Country club">country club</a> and  playgrounds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mapa_362_d3_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-mapa_362_d3-46">[46]</a></sup> The city's main <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> stadium is located nearby on the corner of Yehuda and Palmach Streets. The stadium is home to <a href="/wiki/Hapoel_Arad_F.C." title="Hapoel Arad F.C.">Hapoel Arad</a>, which  plays in <a href="/wiki/Liga_Bet" title="Liga Bet">Liga Bet</a>, the fourth tier of <a href="/wiki/Football_in_Israel" title="Football in Israel">Israeli football</a>.
</p><p>Since 2000, Arad also hosts an annual <a href="/wiki/Mountain_biking" title="Mountain biking">mountain biking</a> tournament, Riding Arad, in memory of Itamar Ilya, a soldier <a href="/wiki/Killed_in_action" title="Killed in action">killed in action</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanon</a> in 1997. In March 2008, the tournament became part of an international <a href="/wiki/Union_Cycliste_Internationale" title="Union Cycliste Internationale">Union Cycliste Internationale</a> competition, which is co-hosted by <a href="/wiki/Misgav_Regional_Council" title="Misgav Regional Council">Misgav</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ma%27alot-Tarshiha" title="Ma'alot-Tarshiha">Ma'alot</a>. Arad's professional course is 37 kilometers (23.0 mi) long, while the expert course is 22.5 kilometers (14.0 mi).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Schaller.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="800" data-file-width="2884" decoding="async" height="83" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Schaller.jpg/300px-Schaller.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Schaller.jpg/450px-Schaller.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Schaller.jpg/600px-Schaller.jpg 2x" width="300"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Schaller.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Schaller Medical Centre</div></div></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Healthcare">Healthcare</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arad,_Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Healthcare">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Arad does not have a hospital, but there are numerous medical clinics, including <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clalit" title="Clalit">Clalit</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leumit_Health_Fund" title="Leumit Health Fund">Leumit</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maccabi_Sherutei_Briut" title="Maccabi Sherutei Briut">Maccabi</a></i>. Emergencies are handled by the single <a href="/wiki/Magen_David_Adom" title="Magen David Adom">Magen David Adom</a> station, located in the municipal compound.
</p><p>A medical center was also built in the compound in 2004, named Schaller Medical Centre after Leon and Freda Schaller from London, who were the prime donors. It serves as an emergency ward, but does not have the equipment needed to perform operations, and such patients are transferred to the <a href="/wiki/Soroka_Medical_Center" title="Soroka Medical Center">Soroka Medical Center</a>. On average, 10% of the patients are transferred.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Law_enforcement">Law enforcement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arad,_Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Law enforcement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Arad is served by a local <a href="/wiki/Israel_Police" title="Israel Police">Israel Police</a> station, located next to the municipal compound and the Magen David Adom station. It is commanded by <a href="/wiki/Superintendent_(police)" title="Superintendent (police)">Superintendent</a> Yuval Paz<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aradnik_police2_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-aradnik_police2-50">[50]</a></sup> and has 58 members.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aradnik_police_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-aradnik_police-51">[51]</a></sup> Arad's station also serves the <a href="/wiki/Sodom_and_Gomorrah#Modern_Sodom" title="Sodom and Gomorrah">Sodom</a> area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-muni_crime_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-muni_crime-52">[52]</a></sup>
</p><p>Arad's police force was chosen as the exceptional force for 2007, and awarded an award of excellence on 5 March 2008. Among others, a severe reduction in <a href="/wiki/Property_crime" title="Property crime">property crime</a> was cited. Arad's force was able to reduce the amount of such crimes from 1,092 in 2003 to just 168 in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aradnik_police_51-1"><a href="#cite_note-aradnik_police-51">[51]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-muni_crime_52-1"><a href="#cite_note-muni_crime-52">[52]</a></sup> On 18 July 2008, Arad police, in a rare incident, shot dead a man threatening to kill his ex-wife with a knife.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ynet-crime_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-ynet-crime-53">[53]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education_and_religious_institutions">Education and religious institutions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arad,_Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Education and religious institutions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Alon_Pano.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1024" data-file-width="2186" decoding="async" height="103" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Alon_Pano.jpg/220px-Alon_Pano.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Alon_Pano.jpg/330px-Alon_Pano.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Alon_Pano.jpg/440px-Alon_Pano.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Alon_Pano.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>ORT Arad HS, formerly Yigal Allon Junior High</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Avishur_School.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="800" data-file-width="2793" decoding="async" height="63" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/Avishur_School.jpg/220px-Avishur_School.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/Avishur_School.jpg/330px-Avishur_School.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/Avishur_School.jpg/440px-Avishur_School.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Avishur_School.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Avishur elementary school</div></div></div>
<p>Arad has a public elementary school in every central neighborhood (Halamish, Tlalim, Avishur, Leva'ot and Ye'elim (called Ye'elim-Ofarim)), while Tlalim is a religious school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-elementary_schools_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-elementary_schools-54">[54]</a></sup> There is one public middle school, Re'ut, located near the junction of the Yoshiyahu and HaKana'im streets. The second middle school, Allon, was closed in 2007 and merged into <a href="/wiki/World_ORT" title="World ORT">ORT</a> Arad, the city's only public secondary school, which shares a building with the Re'ut middle school and the former <a href="/wiki/Yigal_Allon" title="Yigal Allon">Yigal Allon</a> school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hatzvi_2007_summary_55-0"><a href="#cite_note-hatzvi_2007_summary-55">[55]</a></sup> In the 2006–07 school year, 57.14% of Arad's 12th graders were eligible for a <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bagrut" title="Bagrut">Bagrut</a></i> (matriculation) certificate, compared to a national average of 45.9%. In the 2007–08 year, the number dropped to 49.67%, compared to a national average of 46.3%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hatzvi-bagrut_56-0"><a href="#cite_note-hatzvi-bagrut-56">[56]</a></sup>
</p><p>In addition, there are several private and <a href="/wiki/Haredi_Judaism" title="Haredi Judaism">Haredi</a> schools in Arad, such as the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Shuvu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Shuvu (page does not exist)">Shuvu</a> movement's grades 1–8 school, and the <a href="/wiki/Ger_(Hasidic_dynasty)" title="Ger (Hasidic dynasty)">Gerrer</a> Haredi school.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-elementary_schools_54-1"><a href="#cite_note-elementary_schools-54">[54]</a></sup> Other Haredi schools include the boys' Beit Ya'akov and girls' Kol Ya'akov in the Halamish neighborhood, the Lev Simcha Yeshiva, and the Beit Ya'akov High School. <a href="/wiki/Religious_Zionism" title="Religious Zionism">Religious Zionist</a> schools include the Ne'ot Avraham <a href="/wiki/Bnei_Akiva" title="Bnei Akiva">Bnei Akiva</a> <a href="/wiki/Ulpana" title="Ulpana">Ulpana</a>, which also provides young women with the guidance to perform volunteer activities in the community,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ulpana_57-0"><a href="#cite_note-ulpana-57">[57]</a></sup> and the Tlalim Elementary School, founded in 1971.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tlalim-school_58-0"><a href="#cite_note-tlalim-school-58">[58]</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Arad_shaked_synagogue.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="768" data-file-width="1024" decoding="async" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Arad_shaked_synagogue.jpg/220px-Arad_shaked_synagogue.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Arad_shaked_synagogue.jpg/330px-Arad_shaked_synagogue.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Arad_shaked_synagogue.jpg/440px-Arad_shaked_synagogue.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Arad_shaked_synagogue.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Rotem neighborhood synagogue</div></div></div>
<p>Also in Arad is the Kedem Democratic School, which is an alternative school for students up through the high school level. It allows students to create their own schedules, and choose how they would like to spend their entire day at school.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>SOS Children's Village Arad (known as <i>Kfar Neradim</i>) was built in the southern outskirts of Arad and inaugurated on 27 October 1981. It consists of twelve family houses, to accommodate up to 120 children. There is also an SOS Youth Facility to accommodate 12 to 14 youths from the SOS Children's Village. In January 2005, an SOS Social Centre was opened at Arad. It runs a day-care center and various community outreach programmes to support socially weak families.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> Until fall 2008, the World Union of Jewish Students (WUJS), funded by Jews from <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>, had <a href="/wiki/WUJS_Arad_Institute" title="WUJS Arad Institute">an institute</a> in Arad which allowed post-college young Jews from around the world to study Israeli society and the Hebrew language. When WUJS's ownership changed, the institute was moved to the center of the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wujs_60-0"><a href="#cite_note-wujs-60">[60]</a></sup>
</p><p>Arad has 22 synagogues, of which 11 are <a href="/wiki/Ashkenazi_Jews" title="Ashkenazi Jews">Ashkenazi</a>, 9 are <a href="/wiki/Sephardi_Jews" title="Sephardi Jews">Sephardi</a>, one is <a href="/wiki/Yemenite_Jews" title="Yemenite Jews">Yemenite</a>, and one is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ethiopian_Jews" title="Ethiopian Jews">Ethiopian</a>. Both the central Ashkenazi and Sephardi synagogues are located in the Ye'elim neighborhood. A Masorti synagogue, Shira Hadasha is in the Rishonim neighborhood. There are <a href="/wiki/Ger_(Hasidic_dynasty)" title="Ger (Hasidic dynasty)">Gerrer</a> synagogues in Avishur and Halamish, and a <a href="/wiki/Chabad" title="Chabad">Chabad</a> synagogue in Ye'elim.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aradnik-synagogues_61-0"><a href="#cite_note-aradnik-synagogues-61">[61]</a></sup> There are two <i><a href="/wiki/Mikveh" title="Mikveh">mikvehs</a></i> in the city–in the Tlalim and Ayanot neighborhoods.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aradnik-mikvas_62-0"><a href="#cite_note-aradnik-mikvas-62">[62]</a></sup> The city also has a small <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Messianic_Jews" title="Messianic Jews">Messianic</a> community that is being accused of illegal missionary activities by the <a href="/wiki/Haredi_Judaism" title="Haredi Judaism">Haredim</a> and certain right-wing groups.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> Arad's dead are mostly buried in the local cemetery, located in the northeastern outskirts of the city, close to the road to Masada.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media">Media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arad,_Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Arad's local newspaper, <i>Kidod</i>, was published by the entrepreneurs Eli and Rochale Ziv, early founders of Arad. <i>Kidod</i> started as a small leaflet in December 1966 and grew to a become a full paper. At its peak, it was circulated, free of charge, in groceries, kiosks, etc., in 3,000 copies each week. <i>Kidod</i> ran for 23 years and had a key role in the cultural and community life of Arad. <i>HaTzvi Arad</i> is the local newspaper today.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arad,_Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Metropoline_minibus.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="436" data-file-width="1024" decoding="async" height="128" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/Metropoline_minibus.jpg/300px-Metropoline_minibus.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/Metropoline_minibus.jpg/450px-Metropoline_minibus.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/Metropoline_minibus.jpg/600px-Metropoline_minibus.jpg 2x" width="300"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Metropoline_minibus.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Metropoline mini-bus on the internal Line 1</div></div></div>
<p>Arad is reached by <a href="/wiki/Highway_31_(Israel)" title="Highway 31 (Israel)">Highway 31</a>, which connects it with <a href="/wiki/Beersheba" title="Beersheba">Beersheba</a> to the west, and the <a href="/wiki/Dead_Sea" title="Dead Sea">Dead Sea</a> to the east. It has two junctions within Arad's municipal area—the Arad Junction (within the city itself, with Road 3199), and the Tel Arad Junction (with <a href="/wiki/Highway_80_(Israel)" title="Highway 80 (Israel)">Highway 80</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mapa_44_ch24_64-0"><a href="#cite_note-mapa_44_ch24-64">[64]</a></sup> The local Road 3199 connects Arad with <a href="/wiki/Masada" title="Masada">Masada</a>, but does not connect to the road next to the dead sea (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Highway_90_(Israel)" title="Highway 90 (Israel)">Highway 90</a>). It is therefore the only way to get to Masada's western side.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mapa_42_v23_32-1"><a href="#cite_note-mapa_42_v23-32">[32]</a></sup>
</p><p>Arad's airfield, first used during the music festival of 1994, is located south of the city, and serves domestic flights according to demand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> In May 2017, a new railway line to Arad via <a href="/wiki/Kuseife" title="Kuseife">Kuseife</a> was approved.  The line will connect to the existing <a href="/wiki/Beersheba" title="Beersheba">Beersheba</a>-<a href="/wiki/Dimona" title="Dimona">Dimona</a> rail line at <a href="/wiki/Nevatim" title="Nevatim">Nevatim</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aradnik-baha27_67-0"><a href="#cite_note-aradnik-baha27-67">[67]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ladaat_68-0"><a href="#cite_note-ladaat-68">[68]</a></sup> Its terminus will be adjacent to the central bus station.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aradnik-railway_69-0"><a href="#cite_note-aradnik-railway-69">[69]</a></sup>
</p><p>Arad Central Bus Station, once located on the corner of Yerushalayim Street and Yehuda Street was closed due to a legal battle between the municipality and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Egged_Bus_Cooperative" title="Egged Bus Cooperative">Egged Bus Cooperative</a>. A new station was subsequently built with seven platforms and a <a href="/wiki/Park_and_ride" title="Park and ride">park and ride</a> lot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup>
</p><p>Buses in Arad are served by the Egged Bus Cooperative and <a href="/wiki/Metropoline" title="Metropoline">Metropoline</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> A multitude of inter-city bus routes are served by the main station, two more inter-city <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Haredi" title="Haredi">Haredi</a> routes which leave from the north, as well as five inner routes (1, 2, 3, 11, 12). <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Local_government">Local government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arad,_Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Local government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Avraham_Sochat_1.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="768" data-file-width="576" decoding="async" height="227" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Avraham_Sochat_1.jpg/170px-Avraham_Sochat_1.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Avraham_Sochat_1.jpg/255px-Avraham_Sochat_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Avraham_Sochat_1.jpg/340px-Avraham_Sochat_1.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Avraham_Sochat_1.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Avraham "Beiga" Shochat, the first elected leader of Arad</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:GideonBarLev.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="796" data-file-width="528" decoding="async" height="256" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/GideonBarLev.jpg/170px-GideonBarLev.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/GideonBarLev.jpg/255px-GideonBarLev.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/GideonBarLev.jpg/340px-GideonBarLev.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:GideonBarLev.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Gideon Bar-Lev, former mayor</div></div></div>
<p>Arad has, since its inception in 1962, been under the governance of a <a href="/wiki/Regional_council_(Israel)" title="Regional council (Israel)">regional council</a> and <a href="/wiki/Local_council_(Israel)" title="Local council (Israel)">local council</a>, before being designated as a <a href="/wiki/City_council_(Israel)" title="City council (Israel)">city council</a> in 1995. Democratic elections have been held in Arad since 1966, although only Avraham Shochat, Betzalel Tabib and Moty Brill were ever elected or re-elected as the head of the municipality. The first mayor of the city of Arad was <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Betzalel_Tabib&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Betzalel Tabib (page does not exist)">Betzalel Tabib</a> who served as both head of the local council and city council. He was replaced by Mordechai Brill in 2003, although due to his inability to pass a yearly budget, Brill was dismissed by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Interior_Minister_of_Israel" title="Interior Minister of Israel">Minister of the Interior</a> <a href="/wiki/Meir_Sheetrit" title="Meir Sheetrit">Meir Sheetrit</a> in August 2007, and a government-appointed clerk, Gideon Bar-Lev, took his place.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup> An election took place again 13 April 2010, won by Tali Ploskov of the <a href="/wiki/Yisrael_Beiteinu" title="Yisrael Beiteinu">Yisrael Beiteinu</a> party.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup> The longest serving head of Arad's municipality was <a href="/wiki/Avraham_Shochat" title="Avraham Shochat">Avraham Shochat</a> who served as head of the local council between 1967 and 1986.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-museum_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-museum-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p>Arad's yearly budget is over 100 million <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Israeli_shekel" title="New Israeli shekel">NIS</a> and over $30 million <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_dollar" title="US dollar">USD</a>. On 27 December 2007, the 2008 budget was unanimously passed at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Israeli_Sheqel" title="New Israeli Sheqel">NIS</a> 123 million by the city council. The city's total income for the year is estimated at approximately <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Israeli_Sheqel" title="New Israeli Sheqel">NIS</a> 119 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup>
</p>

<p>While it is not customary for Israeli cities to have major unique/auxiliary laws, many cities have minor laws. In Arad, it is illegal to feed animals in public places.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup> It is also illegal to sell or consume <a href="/wiki/Sunflower_seed" title="Sunflower seed">sunflower seeds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peanut" title="Peanut">peanuts</a>, etc. in public places, due to an anti-littering law enacted in 1965.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup> There are in all 27 auxiliary laws in Arad, which were enacted between 1965 and 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-80"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Municipal_flag_and_emblem">Municipal flag and emblem</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arad,_Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Municipal flag and emblem">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Arad_Flag.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="954" data-file-width="1024" decoding="async" height="205" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Arad_Flag.jpg/220px-Arad_Flag.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Arad_Flag.jpg/330px-Arad_Flag.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Arad_Flag.jpg/440px-Arad_Flag.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Arad_Flag.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Flag of Arad</div></div></div>
<p>The emblem of Arad is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Square_(geometry)" title="Square (geometry)">square</a> with a hill and a flame. The hill represents Mount Kidod, a hill in the northeastern part of the city, and the flame represents natural gas, which was extracted in the area in Arad's early days and spurred the city's growth. The emblem was adopted on 19 May 1966.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fotw_81-0"><a href="#cite_note-fotw-81">[81]</a></sup> The flag of Arad is a rectangle with a 2:3 ratio, which has a light blue background and shows the emblem in the center with the Hebrew text for "Municipality of Arad" at the top and the English text "City of ARAD Israel" (or variations thereof) and the bottom. This is the de facto flag, and there is no law or edict making it official.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fotw_81-1"><a href="#cite_note-fotw-81">[81]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span id="Twin_towns_.E2.80.94_sister_cities"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Twin_towns_—_sister_cities">Twin towns — sister cities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arad,_Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Twin towns — sister cities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><span class="flagicon"><a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States"><img alt="United States" class="thumbborder" data-file-height="650" data-file-width="1235" decoding="async" height="12" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a4/Flag_of_the_United_States.svg/23px-Flag_of_the_United_States.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a4/Flag_of_the_United_States.svg/35px-Flag_of_the_United_States.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a4/Flag_of_the_United_States.svg/46px-Flag_of_the_United_States.svg.png 2x" width="23"/></a></span> <a href="/wiki/Wilmington,_Delaware" title="Wilmington, Delaware">Wilmington, Delaware</a> became a sister city of Arad in 1973, to commemorate the 25th anniversary of the State of <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-83"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup></li>
<li><span class="flagicon"><a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany"><img alt="Germany" class="thumbborder" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="1000" decoding="async" height="14" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/ba/Flag_of_Germany.svg/23px-Flag_of_Germany.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/ba/Flag_of_Germany.svg/35px-Flag_of_Germany.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/ba/Flag_of_Germany.svg/46px-Flag_of_Germany.svg.png 2x" width="23"/></a></span> <a href="/wiki/Dinslaken" title="Dinslaken">Dinslaken</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> signed a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Town_twinning" title="Town twinning">twinning agreement</a> with Arad in 1989.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RuhrTwins2010_84-0"><a href="#cite_note-RuhrTwins2010-84">[84]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-85"><a href="#cite_note-85">[85]</a></sup></li>
<li><span class="flagicon"><a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States"><img alt="United States" class="thumbborder" data-file-height="650" data-file-width="1235" decoding="async" height="12" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a4/Flag_of_the_United_States.svg/23px-Flag_of_the_United_States.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a4/Flag_of_the_United_States.svg/35px-Flag_of_the_United_States.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a4/Flag_of_the_United_States.svg/46px-Flag_of_the_United_States.svg.png 2x" width="23"/></a></span> <a href="/wiki/Burlington,_Vermont" title="Burlington, Vermont">Burlington, Vermont</a> developed a sister city relationship with Arad in 1991. Then Mayor Bezalel Tabib, along with Professor Walid Dajani, who represented <a href="/wiki/Bethlehem" title="Bethlehem">Bethlehem</a>, visited Burlington to sign a three cities cooperative agreement—the first-ever sister-city pacts among American, Palestinian, and Israeli communities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-86"><a href="#cite_note-86">[86]</a></sup></li></ul>
