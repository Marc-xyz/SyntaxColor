<p>The <b>Climax Blues Band</b> (originally known as <b>The Climax Chicago Blues Band</b>) is a British <a href="/wiki/Blues_rock" title="Blues rock">blues rock</a> band. The band was formed in <a href="/wiki/Stafford" title="Stafford">Stafford</a>, England, in 1967 by vocalist and harmonica player Colin Cooper (1939–2008), guitarist and vocalist <a href="/wiki/Pete_Haycock" title="Pete Haycock">Pete Haycock</a> (1951–2013), guitarist Derek Holt (b. 1949), bassist and keyboardist Richard Jones (b. 1949), drummer George Newsome (b. 1947), and keyboardist Arthur Wood (1929–2005).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMG_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-AMG-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Climax_Blues_Band&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1972 the group shortened its name to the Climax Blues Band. The band has released at least 19 albums and had a <a href="/wiki/Top_40" title="Top 40">Top 10</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hit_single" title="Hit single">hit</a> in the UK with "<a href="/wiki/Couldn%27t_Get_It_Right" title="Couldn't Get It Right">Couldn't Get It Right</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-British_Hit_Singles_&amp;_Albums_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-British_Hit_Singles_&amp;_Albums-2">[2]</a></sup> That song and "<a href="/wiki/I_Love_You_(Climax_Blues_Band_song)" title="I Love You (Climax Blues Band song)">I Love You</a>" were also hits in the United States; "Couldn't Get It Right" reached No. 3 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> in 1977, and "I Love You" peaked at No. 12 in 1981.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard_singles_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard_singles-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Jones left the group in 1969 and Holt began playing bass. The band switched labels to <a href="/wiki/Harvest_Records" title="Harvest Records">Harvest Records</a> in 1970 and subsequent records had a more rock-oriented feel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NME_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-NME-4">[4]</a></sup> John Cuffley replaced Newsome in 1971.
</p><p>Albums issued in the 1970s include <i>FM/Live</i> (1973), a double set recorded at a concert in New York,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-independent_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-independent-5">[5]</a></sup> and the studio albums <i>Stamp Album</i> (1975) and <i><a href="/wiki/Gold_Plated" title="Gold Plated">Gold Plated</a></i> (1976), featuring the single "<a href="/wiki/Couldn%27t_Get_It_Right" title="Couldn't Get It Right">Couldn't Get It Right</a>". In the 1970s, the band's concerts in the US were attended by up to 20,000 people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Obit_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Obit-6">[6]</a></sup> By 1981 the band was moving towards a pop-rock sound.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Obit_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Obit-6">[6]</a></sup> Holt and Cuffley left in 1983.
</p><p>A previously unknown recording of a live performance was released as <i>Climax Blues Band/World Tour 1976</i> by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Major_League_Productions_(MLP)" title="Major League Productions (MLP)">Major League Productions (MLP)</a> record label.
</p><p>The album <i>Sample and Hold</i> was recorded for Virgin Records in 1983 by Haycock, Cooper, and Glover, with a rhythm section composed of the session musicians Dave Marquee and Henry Spinetti.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> A follow-up album was in the works, but Cooper bowed out, citing personal reasons. Haycock went on to record several solo projects, the first of which was the album <i>Total Climax</i>, with his band, Pete Haycock's Climax; this band toured extensively in Europe, including Communist East Germany, and conducted a well-received tour in Australia. After that, Haycock was asked by his former Climax Blues Band manager, Miles Copeland, to record an instrumental album, <i>Guitar and Son</i>, and the live album <i>Night of the Guitars</i> (from the tour of the same name) for the I.R.S. No Speak label.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> After that tour, Haycock teamed up with the guitarist Steve Hunter and former Climax Blues bandmate Derek Holt to record the album <i>H Factor</i>. He was later recruited by <a href="/wiki/Bev_Bevan" title="Bev Bevan">Bev Bevan</a> to become a member of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electric_Light_Orchestra_Part_II" title="Electric Light Orchestra Part II">Electric Light Orchestra Part II</a> and recorded and toured with that group from 1990 onwards. He also started his <a href="/wiki/Film_score" title="Film score">film score</a> career at this time, playing the lead on <a href="/wiki/Hans_Zimmer" title="Hans Zimmer">Hans Zimmer</a>'s score to <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thelma_and_Louise" title="Thelma and Louise">Thelma and Louise</a></i>. He also performed on the <i>Night of the Guitars</i> tours, which included Holt on bass, keyboards and occasional vocals.
</p><p>Holt wrote "I Love You," one of the Climax Blues Band's biggest <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hit_record" title="Hit record">hits</a>. It is included on the Climax Blues Band <a href="/wiki/Double_album" title="Double album">double album</a>, <i>25 Years 1968–1993</i>, released by the German label Repertoire in 1993. "I Love You" still gets over 20,000 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radio_station" title="Radio station">radio</a> hits a year in the US and was used in <a href="/wiki/Finn_Taylor" title="Finn Taylor">Finn Taylor</a>'s 2002 film <i><a href="/wiki/Cherish_(film)" title="Cherish (film)">Cherish</a></i> and in <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Smith" title="Kevin Smith">Kevin Smith</a>'s 2008 film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zach_and_Miri_make_a_Porno" title="Zach and Miri make a Porno">Zach and Miri make a Porno</a></i>.  In 1983 Holt joined with drummer Brendan Day and Nektar guitarist Roye Albrighton, who recorded one self-titled album for the A&amp;M label.
</p><p>In 1985, Cooper and Glover recruited guitarist Lester Hunt, drummer Roy Adams, and original member Derek Holt to record the Climax Blues Band album <i>Drastic Steps</i>, and this line-up toured in support of the album in the UK, Europe and America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Obit_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-Obit-6">[6]</a></sup> The new line-up soon became established with Cooper, Glover, Hunt, Adams and Neil Simpson in the early 1990s, releasing the live album <i>Blues from the Attic</i> in 1993 and <i>Big Blues</i> in 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMG_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-AMG-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Climax_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Climax-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>Cooper died of cancer, aged 69, in July 2008. He is survived by his wife and two children. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Obit_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-Obit-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Obit_2_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Obit_2-11">[11]</a></sup>   He wanted the band to continue, as the other musicians had all been long serving (Glover since 1980, Adams and Hunt since 1985, and Simpson since 1991). Cooper was replaced by singer and saxophone and harmonica player Johnny Pugh, who retired in 2012, and was replaced in turn by vocalist Graham Dee and saxophone player Chris Aldridge. This lineup continues to tour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
