<p><b>Pierre Bilal Koko'o Lotti Abalintsina</b> (born January 18, 1986 in <a href="/wiki/Ebolowa" title="Ebolowa">Ebolowa</a>), known as <b>Koko'o</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroonian</a> football player last played for Finnish side <a href="/wiki/JIPPO" title="JIPPO">JIPPO</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Koko%27o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Koko'o always had a reputation for scoring goals, and this earned him several moves throughout his career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Prior to his move to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Al_Ahly_Tripoli" title="Al Ahly Tripoli">Al Ahly Tripoli</a>, the Cameroonian topped the Botola scoring charts with 17 goals in 21 games for <a href="/wiki/Moghreb_T%C3%A9touan" title="Moghreb Tétouan">Moghreb Tétouan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He signed a five-year deal with Al Ahly Tripoli, the first year being a loan, believed to be worth $800,000, 40 times the $20,000 <a href="/wiki/Moghreb_T%C3%A9touan" title="Moghreb Tétouan">Moghreb Tétouan</a> paid for him a year earlier to sign him from <a href="/wiki/ASEC_Mimosas" title="ASEC Mimosas">ASEC Mimosas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
