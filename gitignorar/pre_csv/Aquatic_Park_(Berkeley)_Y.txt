	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Aquatic Park (Berkeley)</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:AquaticParkPedBridge1.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1944" data-file-width="2592" decoding="async" height="188" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/AquaticParkPedBridge1.JPG/250px-AquaticParkPedBridge1.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/AquaticParkPedBridge1.JPG/375px-AquaticParkPedBridge1.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/AquaticParkPedBridge1.JPG/500px-AquaticParkPedBridge1.JPG 2x" width="250"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:AquaticParkPedBridge1.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Pedestrian Bridge at the north end of the park</div></div></div>
<p><b>Aquatic Park</b> is a public <a href="/wiki/Park" title="Park">park</a> in <a href="/wiki/Berkeley,_California" title="Berkeley, California">Berkeley, California</a>, United States, located just east of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastshore_Freeway" title="Eastshore Freeway">Eastshore Freeway</a> (<a href="/wiki/Interstate_80" title="Interstate 80">Interstate 80</a>) between Ashby and University Avenues. The <a href="/wiki/Works_Progress_Administration" title="Works Progress Administration">Works Progress Administration</a> created the park in the 1930s simultaneously with the nearby <a href="/wiki/Berkeley_Marina" title="Berkeley Marina">Berkeley Yacht Harbor</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Its centerpiece is an artificial mile-long <a href="/wiki/Lagoon" title="Lagoon">lagoon</a> that was cut off from <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Bay" title="San Francisco Bay">San Francisco Bay</a> by the creation of a causeway for the Eastshore Highway, during the construction of the approaches to the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco%E2%80%93Oakland_Bay_Bridge" title="San Francisco–Oakland Bay Bridge">San Francisco–Oakland Bay Bridge</a>, also in the 1930s. The lagoon still communicates with the Bay through <a href="/wiki/Culvert" title="Culvert">culverts</a> under the freeway (one in line with Ashby Avenue, the other in line with Grayson Street). The east shoreline of the lagoon used to be the original shoreline of San Francisco Bay.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Park_features">Park features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aquatic_Park_(Berkeley)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Park features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Aquatic Park features picnic areas, "Dream Land for Kids"—a large children's playground, a pedestrian-bicycle path, and a <a href="/wiki/Disc_golf" title="Disc golf">disc golf</a> course. The lagoon is used by the Berkeley Paddling and Rowing Club year round, and by the Berkeley Water Ski Club during the summer months.  The lagoon is also used by the nonprofit organization, Waterside Workshops, for their youth boating and sailing, and boat rentals to the general public on weekends.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>  Waterside Workshops also houses a community bikeshop at the intersection of Bolivar Drive and Bancroft Way.  Historically, the Aquatic Park lagoon was also a practice site for the University of California <a href="/wiki/College_rowing_(United_States)" title="College rowing (United States)">crew</a> teams.
</p>
