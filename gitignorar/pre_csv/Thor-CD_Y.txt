	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Thor-CD</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Thor-CD</b> was a re-recordable CD format proposed in 1988 by <a href="/wiki/Tandy_Corporation" title="Tandy Corporation">Tandy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-latimes_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-latimes-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Several years before <a href="/wiki/CD-R" title="CD-R">recordable compact discs</a> were introduced, <a href="/wiki/Tandy_Corporation" title="Tandy Corporation">Tandy Corporation</a> announced a similar CD format named Thor-CD,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> but after being pushed back for several years, it was finally cancelled due to steep manufacturing costs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>At the time Tandy proposed the new format, CDs were mostly used for digital music, but not for other digital data. Tandy aimed to change this with its new format.
</p><p>However, the introduction of the <a href="/wiki/CD-ROM" title="CD-ROM">CD-ROM</a> format, which was incompatible with Tandy's proposal, all but killed Tandy's product.  
</p>
