<p><b>Maria II Zaccaria</b> (14th-century - after 1404), was a <a href="/wiki/Principality_of_Achaea" title="Principality of Achaea">Princess of Achaia</a>.  
</p><p>She was daughter of <a href="/wiki/Centurione_I_Zaccaria" title="Centurione I Zaccaria">Centurione I Zaccaria</a>, Lord of <a href="/wiki/Barony_of_Veligosti" title="Barony of Veligosti">Veligosti–Damala</a> and <a href="/wiki/Barony_of_Chalandritsa" title="Barony of Chalandritsa">Chalandritsa</a>. She succeeded her spouse <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_San_Superano" title="Pedro de San Superano">Pedro de San Superano</a> in 1402 as regent for their son. In 1404, she ceded the regency to her nephew, <a href="/wiki/Centurione_II_Zaccaria" title="Centurione II Zaccaria">Centurione II Zaccaria</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
