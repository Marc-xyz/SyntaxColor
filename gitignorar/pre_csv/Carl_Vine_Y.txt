	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Carl Vine</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>

		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>

<b>Carl Vine</b>, <span class="noexcerpt nowraplinks" style="font-size:85%;"><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Officer_of_the_Order_of_Australia" title="Officer of the Order of Australia">AO</a></span> (born 8 October 1954), is an Australian composer of <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_classical_music" title="Contemporary classical music">contemporary classical music</a>. From 1975 he has worked as a freelance pianist and composer with a variety of theatre and dance companies, and ensembles. Vine's catalogue includes eight symphonies, twelve concertos, music for film, television and theatre, electronic music and numerous chamber works. Since 2000 he has been the Artistic Director of <a href="/wiki/Musica_Viva_Australia" title="Musica Viva Australia">Musica Viva Australia</a>. In 2005 he was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Don_Banks_Music_Award" title="Don Banks Music Award">Don Banks Music Award</a>. In the 2014 Queen's Birthday Honours List, Vine was appointed as an Officer of the Order of Australia (AO), "for distinguished service to the performing arts as a composer, conductor, academic and artistic director, and to the support and mentoring of emerging performers."
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carl_Vine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Vine was born in <a href="/wiki/Perth" title="Perth">Perth</a>, Western Australia. He played the cornet from the age of 5, and took up the piano when he was 10. A teenage fascination with the music of <a href="/wiki/Karlheinz_Stockhausen" title="Karlheinz Stockhausen">Karlheinz Stockhausen</a> inspired a period of <a href="/wiki/Modernism_(music)" title="Modernism (music)">Modernism</a>, which he explored until the mid-1980s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AG_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-AG-1">[1]</a></sup> He studied <a href="/wiki/Physics" title="Physics">physics</a>, then <a href="/wiki/Musical_composition" title="Musical composition">composition</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Western_Australia" title="University of Western Australia">University of Western Australia</a> (now the <a href="/wiki/UWA_Conservatorium_of_Music" title="UWA Conservatorium of Music">UWA Conservatorium of Music</a>), before moving to Sydney in 1975, where he worked as a freelance pianist and composer with a variety of theatre and dance companies, and ensembles.
</p><p>Vine first came to prominence in Australia as a composer of music for dance, with 25 dance scores to his credit. In 1979 he co-founded the contemporary music ensemble "<a href="/wiki/Flederman" title="Flederman">Flederman</a>", which presented many of Vine's own works. From 1980 to 1982 he lectured in <a href="/wiki/Electronic_music" title="Electronic music">electronic music</a> composition at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queensland_Conservatorium_of_Music" title="Queensland Conservatorium of Music">Queensland Conservatorium of Music</a> in Brisbane.
</p><p>His catalogue includes eight symphonies, twelve concertos, music for film, television and theatre, electronic music and numerous chamber works. Although primarily a composer of modern classical music, he has undertaken tasks as diverse as arranging the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australian_National_Anthem" title="Australian National Anthem">Australian National Anthem</a> and writing music for the <a href="/wiki/1996_Summer_Olympics_closing_ceremony#Sydney_2000" title="1996 Summer Olympics closing ceremony">1996 Atlanta Olympics closing ceremony</a>.
</p><p>Since 2000, Vine has been the Artistic Director of <a href="/wiki/Musica_Viva_Australia" title="Musica Viva Australia">Musica Viva Australia</a>, the world's largest chamber music presenter. In 2005, he was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Don_Banks_Music_Award" title="Don Banks Music Award">Don Banks Music Award</a>, the highest accolade the <a href="/wiki/Australia_Council_for_the_Arts" title="Australia Council for the Arts">Australia Council for the Arts</a> can confer on a musician. Since 2006, he has also been the Artistic Director of the Huntington Estate Music Festival.
</p><p>In 2012, his second piano concerto was premiered by <a href="/wiki/Piers_Lane" title="Piers Lane">Piers Lane</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Sydney_Symphony_Orchestra" title="Sydney Symphony Orchestra">Sydney Symphony Orchestra</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AG_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-AG-1">[1]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Chamber_Orchestra" title="Australian Chamber Orchestra">Australian Chamber Orchestra</a> with soprano <a href="/wiki/Danielle_de_Niese" title="Danielle de Niese">Danielle de Niese</a> premiered his solo <a href="/wiki/Cantata" title="Cantata">cantata</a>, <i>The Tree of Man</i>, after <a href="/wiki/The_Tree_of_Man" title="The Tree of Man">the 1955 novel</a> by <a href="/wiki/Patrick_White" title="Patrick White">Patrick White</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/2014_Queen%27s_Birthday_Honours_(Australia)" title="2014 Queen's Birthday Honours (Australia)">2014 Queen's Birthday Honours List</a>, Vine was appointed as an Officer of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Australia" title="Order of Australia">Order of Australia</a> (AO), "for distinguished service to the performing arts as a composer, conductor, academic and artistic director, and to the support and mentoring of emerging performers."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Vine is based in Sydney, where he works as a freelance composer.  His <a href="/wiki/Trombone" title="Trombone">trombone</a> concerto <i>Five Hallucinations</i> was premiered by the Chicago Symphony Orchestra in October 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
