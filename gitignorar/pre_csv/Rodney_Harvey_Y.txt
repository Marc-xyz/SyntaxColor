<p><b>Rodney Michael Harvey</b> (July 31, 1967 – April 11, 1998) was an American <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dancer" title="Dancer">dancer</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rodney_Harvey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>, Harvey was discovered by director <a href="/wiki/Paul_Morrissey" title="Paul Morrissey">Paul Morrissey</a> in 1984. Morrissey cast Harvey in two of his films <i><a href="/wiki/Mixed_Blood_(film)" title="Mixed Blood (film)">Mixed Blood</a></i> (1985) and <i><a href="/wiki/Spike_of_Bensonhurst" title="Spike of Bensonhurst">Spike of Bensonhurst</a></i> (1988).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> After signing with an agent, Harvey moved to <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> where he continued acting and also began modeling. He appeared in a layout for <i><a href="/wiki/Life_(magazine)" title="Life (magazine)">Life</a></i> magazine featuring <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a> photographed by <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Weber_(photographer)" title="Bruce Weber (photographer)">Bruce Weber</a>, and worked for <a href="/wiki/Calvin_Klein" title="Calvin Klein">Calvin Klein</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1990, he landed the role of Sodapop Curtis in the <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a> series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Outsiders_(U.S._TV_series)" title="The Outsiders (U.S. TV series)">The Outsiders</a></i>. After the series ended after one season, Harvey guest starred on <i><a href="/wiki/Twin_Peaks" title="Twin Peaks">Twin Peaks</a></i>, followed by a role in the <a href="/wiki/Gus_Van_Sant" title="Gus Van Sant">Gus Van Sant</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/My_Own_Private_Idaho" title="My Own Private Idaho">My Own Private Idaho</a></i>. Harvey also appeared in the 1988 film <i><a href="/wiki/Salsa_(1988_film)" title="Salsa (1988 film)">Salsa</a></i> alongside Draco Rosa. He made his last onscreen appearance in 1996 with a role in the drama <i>God's Lonely Man</i>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rodney_Harvey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the making of <i>My Own Private Idaho</i>, Harvey began using <a href="/wiki/Heroin" title="Heroin">heroin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>  After several stints in jail and attempts to get clean, he died of a heroin and <a href="/wiki/Cocaine" title="Cocaine">cocaine</a> overdose on April 11, 1998 at the Hotel Barbizon in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>After his death, photos of him in the depths of heroin addiction appeared in an Office of Drug Control Policy (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Partnership_for_a_Drug-Free_America" title="Partnership for a Drug-Free America">Partnership for a Drug-Free America</a>) <a href="/wiki/Public_service_announcement" title="Public service announcement">public service announcement</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
