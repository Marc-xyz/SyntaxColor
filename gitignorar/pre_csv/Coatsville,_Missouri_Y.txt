	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Coatsville, Missouri</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Coatsville</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Unincorporated_area" title="Unincorporated area">unincorporated community</a> in northern <a href="/wiki/Schuyler_County,_Missouri" title="Schuyler County, Missouri">Schuyler County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Missouri" title="Missouri">Missouri</a>, United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It is located on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Route_202_(Missouri)" title="Route 202 (Missouri)">Route 202</a>, approximately six miles northwest of <a href="/wiki/Lancaster,_Missouri" title="Lancaster, Missouri">Lancaster</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Iowa" title="Iowa">Iowa</a> state line. The community is part of the <a href="/wiki/Kirksville,_Missouri" title="Kirksville, Missouri">Kirksville</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kirksville_micropolitan_area" title="Kirksville micropolitan area">Micropolitan Statistical Area</a>.
</p><p>Coatsville was <a href="/wiki/Plat" title="Plat">platted</a> in 1869.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> A post office called Coatsville has been in operation since 1869.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
