<p><i><b>Spirit of Indiana</b></i> is a public artwork by <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> artist <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eugene_Francis_Savage" title="Eugene Francis Savage">Eugene Francis Savage</a>, located in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indiana_State_House" title="Indiana State House">Indiana State House</a>, which is in <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis" title="Indianapolis">Indianapolis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">Indiana</a>, United States. The mural is 21 feet high by 41.5 feet wide, oil on linen canvas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Agreement_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Agreement-1">[1]</a></sup> It was commissioned in 1961 and installation was finished in 1964.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NewsMural_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-NewsMural-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spirit_of_Indiana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The mural is on the east wall of the <a href="/wiki/Indiana_House_of_Representatives" title="Indiana House of Representatives">Indiana House of Representatives</a> in the Indiana State House. On the far left side, Education holds a torch and offers a cornucopia of "cherished things" as a burnt sacrifice as <a href="/wiki/Pegasus" title="Pegasus">Pegasus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Apollo" title="Apollo">Apollo</a> rise from the smoke. To their right, Indiana is depicted as a woman in an empire gown of 1816 entering into her statehood, escorted by <a href="/wiki/William_Henry_Harrison" title="William Henry Harrison">William Henry Harrison</a>. The pair stands in front of a <a href="/wiki/Sycamore" title="Sycamore">sycamore</a> tree. The right side of the mural depicts the Roman goddess <a href="/wiki/Ceres_(mythology)" title="Ceres (mythology)">Ceres</a> presiding over the work of Indiana's agriculture and industry as their products are loaded on ships and transported around the world. In the background, behind the sycamore, cloud formations represent the pioneer, settlement, and Civil War periods of Indiana's history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NewsMural_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-NewsMural-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Historical_information">Historical information</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spirit_of_Indiana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Historical information">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The mural was initially commissioned on May 31, 1961. It was originally titled "The <a href="/wiki/Apotheosis" title="Apotheosis">Apotheosis</a> of Indiana (1860-1960)." The preliminary designs were approved by a representative of then-Governor <a href="/wiki/Matthew_E._Welsh" title="Matthew E. Welsh">Matthew E. Welsh</a> and final approval of the work was made by a gubernatorial representative and the Abbey Fund Committee. It was completed in Savage's studio and moved to the State House to be installed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Agreement_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Agreement-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The work cost $40,000 and was paid for entirely by an Abbey Fund grant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NewsMural_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-NewsMural-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
