<p><b>Nicholas "Nick" Erickson</b> (July 18, 1870 – June 21, 1931) was a <a href="/wiki/Coxswain" title="Coxswain">Coxswain</a> serving in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish–American War">Spanish–American War</a> who received the <a href="/wiki/Medal_of_Honor" title="Medal of Honor">Medal of Honor</a> for bravery.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nick_Erickson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Erickson was born July 18, 1870 in <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a>, and after entering the navy he was sent to fight in the Spanish–American War aboard the U.S.S. Marblehead as a Coxswain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMOHW_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-AMOHW-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>He died June 21, 1931 and was buried in Woodlawn Cemetery Bronx, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-findagrave_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-findagrave-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Medal_of_Honor_citation">Medal of Honor citation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nick_Erickson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Medal of Honor citation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rank and organization: Coxswain, U.S. Navy. Born: 18 July 1870, Finland. Accredited to: New York. G.O. No.: 521, 7 July 1899.
</p><p><b>Citation:</b>
</p>
<blockquote><p>On board the U.S.S. Marblehead during the operation of cutting the cable leading from Cienfuegos, Cuba, 11 May 1898. Facing the heavy fire of the enemy, Erickson set an example of extraordinary bravery and coolness throughout this action.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMOHW_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-AMOHW-1">[1]</a></sup></p></blockquote>
