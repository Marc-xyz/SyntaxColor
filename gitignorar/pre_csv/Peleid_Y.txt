<p><b>Peleid</b> (foaled 1970) was a British <a href="/wiki/Thoroughbred" title="Thoroughbred">Thoroughbred</a> <a href="/wiki/Horse_racing" title="Horse racing">racehorse</a> and <a href="/wiki/Horse_breeding#Terminology" title="Horse breeding">sire</a> best known for winning the <a href="/wiki/British_Classic_Races" title="British Classic Races">classic</a> <a href="/wiki/St_Leger_Stakes" title="St Leger Stakes">St Leger Stakes</a> in 1973. After showing little promise in his early career he ran well in several important <a href="/wiki/Handicap_(horse_racing)" title="Handicap (horse racing)">handicap races</a> in 1973 before stepping up in class to record a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fractional_odds" title="Fractional odds">28/1</a> upset victory in the St Leger on 15 September. He was later sold and exported to stand as a breeding stallion in <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Peleid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Peleid was a bay horse bred by his owner Colonel William E. Behrens. He was from the fourth crop of foals sired by Derring-Do, a horse whose wins included the <a href="/wiki/Queen_Elizabeth_II_Stakes" title="Queen Elizabeth II Stakes">Queen Elizabeth II Stakes</a> in 1965 and whose other offspring included the 2000 Guineas winners <a href="/wiki/High_Top" title="High Top">High Top</a> and <a href="/wiki/Roland_Gardens" title="Roland Gardens">Roland Gardens</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ville_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ville-1">[1]</a></sup> Peleid's dam, Winning Bid, was a successful broodmare who also produced the <a href="/wiki/Eclipse_Stakes" title="Eclipse Stakes">Eclipse Stakes</a> winner Coup de Feu. The colt was trained by Bill Elsey at his Highfield stables in <a href="/wiki/Malton,_North_Yorkshire" title="Malton, North Yorkshire">Malton, North Yorkshire</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Racing_career">Racing career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Peleid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Racing career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1972:_two-year-old_season">1972: two-year-old season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Peleid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1972: two-year-old season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Peleid was not campaigned in the highest class, and won only once, when taking a <a href="/wiki/Maiden_race" title="Maiden race">maiden race</a> at <a href="/wiki/York_Racecourse" title="York Racecourse">York Racecourse</a> in July.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mortimer_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mortimer-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1973:_three-year-old_season">1973: three-year-old season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Peleid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1973: three-year-old season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As a three-year-old Peleid was mainly campaigned in handicap races and made steady progress. After winning over one mile at <a href="/wiki/Aintree_Racecourse" title="Aintree Racecourse">Liverpool</a> on <a href="/wiki/Grand_National" title="Grand National">Grand National</a> day in March<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thefreelibrary1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-thefreelibrary1-3">[3]</a></sup> he won the valuable <a href="/wiki/Zetland_Gold_Cup" title="Zetland Gold Cup">Zetland Gold Cup</a> over ten <a href="/wiki/Furlong" title="Furlong">furlongs</a> at <a href="/wiki/Redcar_Racecourse" title="Redcar Racecourse">Redcar Racecourse</a> in May. In June he was sent to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Royal_Ascot" title="Royal Ascot">Royal Ascot</a> where he finished second to Zab in the King George V Stakes. In July he won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_Smith%27s_Magnet_Cup" title="John Smith's Magnet Cup">John Smith's Magnet Cup</a> at York and was then sent to France where he finished second in the Prix de la Ville de Trouville. In August he returned to York for the <a href="/wiki/Ebor_Handicap" title="Ebor Handicap">Ebor Handicap</a> over one and three quarter miles. Carrying a weight of 114 <a href="/wiki/Pound_(mass)" title="Pound (mass)">pounds</a> he finished fourth to Bonne Noel.
</p><p>In September Peleid was one of thirteen three-year-olds to contest the St Leger Stakes at <a href="/wiki/Doncaster_Racecourse" title="Doncaster Racecourse">Doncaster Racecourse</a>. Ridden by the 47-year-old veteran Frankie Durr, he was not regarded as a serious contender, and started at odds of 28/1. It was believed that Peleid had raced too prominently in the early stages of the Ebor, and on this occasion Durr restrained the colt in the early stages before making his challenge in the straight. He took the lead inside the last quarter mile and accelerated clear of the field to win by two and a half lengths and a neck from <a href="/wiki/Buoy_(horse)" title="Buoy (horse)">Buoy</a> and Duke of Ragusa. He was the first winner of the race trained in the North of England since the filly <a href="/wiki/Cantelo" title="Cantelo">Cantelo</a>, trained by Elsey's father Charles Elsey won in 1959.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thefreelibrary1_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-thefreelibrary1-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1974:_four-year-old_season">1974: four-year-old season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Peleid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1974: four-year-old season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Peleid remained in training as a four-year-old but failed to win a race in Britain: he did finish second in the <a href="/wiki/John_Porter_Stakes" title="John Porter Stakes">John Porter Stakes</a> and third in the <a href="/wiki/Jockey_Club_Stakes" title="Jockey Club Stakes">Jockey Club Stakes</a>. In the autumn he was sold and exported to <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a>. He won the Great Breeders' Prize in his new country before being retired to stud.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mortimer_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Mortimer-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Assessment">Assessment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Peleid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Assessment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In their book <i>A Century of Champions</i>, John Randall and Tony Morris rated Peleid a "poor" St Leger winner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-century_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-century-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
