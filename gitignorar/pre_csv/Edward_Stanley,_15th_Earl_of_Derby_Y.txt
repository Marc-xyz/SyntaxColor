<p><b>Edward Henry Stanley, 15th Earl of Derby</b>, <span class="noexcerpt nowraplinks" style="font-size:100%;"><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Knight_of_the_Order_of_the_Garter" title="Knight of the Order of the Garter">KG</a><span style="font-size:14px;">,</span> <a href="/wiki/Privy_Council_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Privy Council of the United Kingdom">PC</a><span style="font-size:14px;">,</span> <a href="/wiki/Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society" title="Fellow of the Royal Society">FRS</a></span> (21 July 1826 – 21 April 1893), known as <b>Lord Stanley</b> from 1851 to 1869, was a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_Great_Britain_and_Ireland" title="United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland">British</a> statesman. He served as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Foreign_Affairs" title="Secretary of State for Foreign Affairs">Secretary of State for Foreign Affairs</a> twice, from 1866 to 1868 and from 1874 to  1878, and also twice as <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_the_Colonies" title="Secretary of State for the Colonies">Colonial Secretary</a> in 1858 and from 1882 to 1885.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_education">Background and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edward_Stanley,_15th_Earl_of_Derby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He was born to <a href="/wiki/Edward_Smith-Stanley,_14th_Earl_of_Derby" title="Edward Smith-Stanley, 14th Earl of Derby">Edward Smith-Stanley, 14th Earl of Derby</a>, who led the <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative Party</a> from 1846–1868 and served as Prime Minister three times, and <a href="/wiki/Emma_Caroline_Smith-Stanley,_Countess_of_Derby" title="Emma Caroline Smith-Stanley, Countess of Derby">Emma Caroline Bootle-Wilbraham</a>, daughter of <a href="/wiki/Edward_Bootle-Wilbraham,_1st_Baron_Skelmersdale" title="Edward Bootle-Wilbraham, 1st Baron Skelmersdale">Edward Bootle-Wilbraham, 1st Baron Skelmersdale</a>, and was the older brother of <a href="/wiki/Frederick_Stanley,_16th_Earl_of_Derby" title="Frederick Stanley, 16th Earl of Derby">Frederick Arthur Stanley, 16th Earl of Derby</a>. The Stanleys were one of the richest landowning families in England. Lord Stanley, as he was styled before acceding to the earldom, was educated at <a href="/wiki/Eton_College" title="Eton College">Eton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rugby_School" title="Rugby School">Rugby</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trinity_College,_Cambridge" title="Trinity College, Cambridge">Trinity College, Cambridge</a>, where he took a first in classics and became a member of the society known as the <a href="/wiki/Cambridge_Apostles" title="Cambridge Apostles">Cambridge Apostles</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edward_Stanley,_15th_Earl_of_Derby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In March 1848 he unsuccessfully contested the <a href="/wiki/Borough" title="Borough">borough</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lancaster,_England" title="Lancaster, England">Lancaster</a>, and then made a long tour in the <a href="/wiki/West_Indies" title="West Indies">West Indies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. During his absence he was elected member for <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Lynn_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="King's Lynn (UK Parliament constituency)">King's Lynn</a>, which he represented till October 1869, when he succeeded to the peerage. He took his place, as a matter of course, among the <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservatives</a>, and delivered his <a href="/wiki/Maiden_speech" title="Maiden speech">maiden speech</a> in May 1850 on the sugar duties. Just before, he had made a very brief tour in <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>. In 1852 he went to <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, and while travelling in that country he was appointed under-secretary for foreign affairs in his father's first administration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EB1911_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-EB1911-2">[2]</a></sup> On 11 March 1853, he was commissioned a captain in the 3rd Royal Lancashire Militia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>From the outset of his career he was known to be more politically sympathetic to the <a href="/wiki/Liberalism" title="Liberalism">Liberals</a> rather than the Conservatives, and in 1855 <a href="/wiki/Henry_John_Temple,_3rd_Viscount_Palmerston" title="Henry John Temple, 3rd Viscount Palmerston">Lord Palmerston</a> offered him the post of <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_the_Colonies" title="Secretary of State for the Colonies">Secretary of State for the Colonies</a>. He was much tempted by the proposal, and hurried down to Knowsley to consult his father, who called out when he entered the room, "Halo, Stanley! what brings you here?—Has <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Disraeli" title="Benjamin Disraeli">Dizzy</a> cut his throat, or are you going to be married?" When the object of his sudden appearance had been explained, the Conservative chief received the courteous suggestion of the prime minister with anything but favour, and the offer was declined. On 13 May 1856, he was appointed to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Royal_Commission" title="Royal Commission">Royal Commission</a> on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Purchase_of_commissions_in_the_British_army" title="Purchase of commissions in the British army">purchase of commissions in the British army</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In his father's second administration Lord Stanley held, at first, the office of Secretary of State for the Colonies (1858), but became <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_Board_of_Control" title="President of the Board of Control">President of the Board of Control</a> on the resignation of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lord_Ellenborough" title="Lord Ellenborough">Lord Ellenborough</a>. He had the charge of the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_India_Act_1858" title="Government of India Act 1858">India Bill of 1858</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_House_of_Commons" title="British House of Commons">House of Commons</a>, became the first <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_India" title="Secretary of State for India">Secretary of State for India</a>, and left behind him in the India Office an excellent reputation as a man of business.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EB1911_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-EB1911-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>After the revolution in Greece and the flight of <a href="/wiki/Otto_of_Greece" title="Otto of Greece">King Otto</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Victoria_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Victoria of the United Kingdom">Queen Victoria's</a> second son, <a href="/wiki/Alfred,_Duke_of_Saxe-Coburg_and_Gotha" title="Alfred, Duke of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha">Prince Alfred</a>, was the Greek people's choice as their next king. After he declined, the idea arose of electing a great and wealthy English nobleman, in the open hope that although they might have to offer him a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Civil_List" title="Civil List">Civil List</a> he would decline to receive it. Lord Stanley was favourite candidate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EB1911_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-EB1911-2">[2]</a></sup> However, despite reports to the contrary, he was in fact never formally offered the crown.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Foreign_minister_1866-68">Foreign minister 1866-68</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edward_Stanley,_15th_Earl_of_Derby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Foreign minister 1866-68">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the fall of the <a href="/wiki/John_Russell,_1st_Earl_Russell" title="John Russell, 1st Earl Russell">Russell</a> government in 1866 he became <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Foreign_Affairs" title="Secretary of State for Foreign Affairs">Secretary of State for Foreign Affairs</a> in his father's third administration. He compared his conduct in that great post to that of a man floating down a river and fending off from his vessel, as well as he could, the various obstacles it encountered.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EB1911_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-EB1911-2">[2]</a></sup> He enunciated the policy of <a href="/wiki/Splendid_isolation" title="Splendid isolation">Splendid isolation</a> in 1866:
</p>
<dl><dd>it is the duty of the Government of this country, placed as it is with regard to geographical position, to keep itself upon terms of goodwill with all surrounding nations, but not to entangle itself with any single or monopolizing alliance with any one of them; above all to endeavor not to interfere needlessly and vexatiously with the internal affairs of any foreign country."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></dd></dl>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:John_bevan_smith_passport_1.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2048" data-file-width="1448" decoding="async" height="283" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/4c/John_bevan_smith_passport_1.jpg/200px-John_bevan_smith_passport_1.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/4c/John_bevan_smith_passport_1.jpg/300px-John_bevan_smith_passport_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/4c/John_bevan_smith_passport_1.jpg/400px-John_bevan_smith_passport_1.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:John_bevan_smith_passport_1.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Passport for a railway engineer dated 1876 containing Lord Stanley's full title and his signature.</div></div></div><p>He arranged the collective guarantee of the neutrality of Luxembourg in 1867, likely to be a theatre of war with conflict between France and Prussia growing ever more likely. He also negotiated a convention with the USA about the <i><a href="/wiki/CSS_Alabama" title="CSS Alabama">CSS Alabama</a></i>, which, however, was not ratified, and refused to take any part in the troubles in Crete.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EB1911_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-EB1911-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Foreign_Minister_1874-78">Foreign Minister 1874-78</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edward_Stanley,_15th_Earl_of_Derby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Foreign Minister 1874-78">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1874 he again became Foreign Secretary in Disraeli's government.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EB1911_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-EB1911-2">[2]</a></sup> Disraeli usually gets the main credit for a series of achievements. In 1875 came the purchase of the controlling shares in the Suez Canal company. By negotiations, Russia gave up substantial gains in the Balkans and a foothold in the Mediterranean. Britain gained control of Cyprus from the Ottomans as a naval base covering the Eastern Mediterranean. In exchange, Britain guaranteed the Asiatic territories of the Ottoman Empire. Britain did not do well in conflicts in Afghanistan and South Africa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>Derby acquiesced in Disraeli's purchase of the <a href="/wiki/Suez_Canal" title="Suez Canal">Suez Canal</a> shares, a measure then considered dangerous by many people, but ultimately most successful. He accepted the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Julius_Andrassy" title="Julius Andrassy">Andrassy</a> Note, but declined to accede to the Berlin Memorandum.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EB1911_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-EB1911-2">[2]</a></sup> Derby's conduct during the Eastern Crisis was mysterious to many of his contemporaries and for some time thereafter. Derby's hope for peace with Russia led him (and his wife) to share Cabinet secrets with the Russian ambassador, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pyotr_Shuvalov" title="Pyotr Shuvalov">Pyotr Shuvalov</a>, in hopes of averting war with Russia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Blake_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Blake-8">[8]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Robert_Blake,_Baron_Blake" title="Robert Blake, Baron Blake">Robert Blake</a> commented that "Derby surely must be the only Foreign Secretary in British history to reveal the innermost secrets of the Cabinet to the ambassador of a foreign power in order to frustrate the presumed intentions of his own Prime Minister." Derby resigned in January 1878 when the Cabinet resolved to send the British fleet through the Dardanelles, but when that action soon proved unnecessary, Derby was allowed to withdraw his resignation. However, he resigned again and finally in the same year when the Cabinet agreed to call up the reserve.
</p><p>By October 1879 it was clear enough that he had thrown in his lot with the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_(UK)" title="Liberal Party (UK)">Liberal Party</a>, but it was not till March 1880 that he publicly announced this change of allegiance. He did not at first take office in the second <a href="/wiki/William_Ewart_Gladstone" title="William Ewart Gladstone">Gladstone</a> government, but became Colonial Secretary in December 1882, holding this position till the fall of that government in the summer of 1885. In 1886 the Liberal party split; Lord Derby became a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liberal_Unionist" title="Liberal Unionist">Liberal Unionist</a>, and took an active part in the general management of that party, leading it in the House of Lords till 1891, when <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spencer_Compton_Cavendish,_8th_Duke_of_Devonshire" title="Spencer Compton Cavendish, 8th Duke of Devonshire">Lord Hartington</a> became Duke of Devonshire. In 1892 he presided over the Labour Commission.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EB1911_2-7"><a href="#cite_note-EB1911-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>He served as <a href="/wiki/List_of_Presidents_of_Co-operative_Congress" title="List of Presidents of Co-operative Congress">President</a> of the first day of the 1881 <a href="/wiki/Co-operative_Congress" title="Co-operative Congress">Co-operative Congress</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PDF_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-PDF-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edward_Stanley,_15th_Earl_of_Derby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lord Derby married <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mary_Catherine_Stanley,_Lady_Derby" title="Mary Catherine Stanley, Lady Derby">Lady Mary</a>, daughter of <a href="/wiki/George_Sackville-West,_5th_Earl_De_La_Warr" title="George Sackville-West, 5th Earl De La Warr">George Sackville-West, 5th Earl De La Warr</a> and widow of <a href="/wiki/James_Gascoyne-Cecil,_2nd_Marquess_of_Salisbury" title="James Gascoyne-Cecil, 2nd Marquess of Salisbury">James Gascoyne-Cecil, 2nd Marquess of Salisbury</a>, in 1870. They had no children. Derby's health never recovered from an attack of influenza which he had in 1891, and he died at <a href="/wiki/Knowsley_Hall" title="Knowsley Hall">Knowsley</a> on 21 April 1893, aged 66.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EB1911_2-8"><a href="#cite_note-EB1911-2">[2]</a></sup> He was succeeded in the earldom by his younger brother, <a href="/wiki/Frederick_Stanley,_16th_Earl_of_Derby" title="Frederick Stanley, 16th Earl of Derby">Frederick</a>. Lady Derby died in December 1900.
</p>
