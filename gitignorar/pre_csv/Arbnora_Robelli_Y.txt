<p><b>Arbnora Robelli</b> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Albanians_in_Sweden" title="Albanians in Sweden">Swedish Albanian</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Women%27s_soccer" title="Women's soccer">football</a> <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">forward</a>, currently playing for <a href="/wiki/IS_Halmia" title="IS Halmia">IS Halmia</a> in Sweden's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swedish_Women%27s_Football_Division_1" title="Swedish Women's Football Division 1">Division 1</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arbnora_Robelli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Robelli was part of the inaugural <a href="/wiki/Albania_women%27s_national_football_team" title="Albania women's national football team">Albania women's national team</a> in 2011 where she received a call up in November for a friendly against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Macedonia_women%27s_national_football_team" title="Macedonia women's national football team">Macedonia</a>.
</p><p><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arbnora_Robelli&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Robelli was a contestant on Miss World Sweden 2012, where she reached the last 32, along with 4 other Swedish Albanian contestants.
</p><p>Robelli has a bachelor's degree in social psychology.
</p>
