	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Magomed Gadaborshev</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>

		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Magomed Gadaborshev</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Магомед Гадаборшев</span>) (? – July 9, 2009) was a <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ingushetia" title="Ingushetia">Ingushetian</a> <a href="/wiki/Colonel" title="Colonel">colonel</a> who headed <a href="/wiki/Ingushetia" title="Ingushetia">Ingushetia</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Forensics" title="Forensics">Forensics</a> and Investigations Center.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rfe_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-rfe-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 7, 2009, attackers opened fire on Gadaborshev's car as he drove through the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/City_center" title="City center">city center</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nazran" title="Nazran">Nazran</a>, the largest city in Ingushetia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rfe_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-rfe-1">[1]</a></sup> He suffered gunshot wounds in the attack and fell into a <a href="/wiki/Coma" title="Coma">coma</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rfe_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-rfe-1">[1]</a></sup> Doctors pronounced Gadaborshev dead on July 9, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rfe_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-rfe-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Gadaborshev's killing followed a string of attacks on and assassinations of Ingush officials in the preceding weeks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rfe_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-rfe-1">[1]</a></sup> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/President_of_Ingushetia" title="President of Ingushetia">President of Ingushetia</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yunus-Bek_Yevkurov" title="Yunus-Bek Yevkurov">Yunus-Bek Yevkurov</a>, was seriously wounded in a bombing on June 22, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rfe_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-rfe-1">[1]</a></sup> The deputy chief justice of the Supreme Court of Ingushetia <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aza_Gazgireeva" title="Aza Gazgireeva">Aza Gazgireeva</a> and former deputy prime minister <a href="/wiki/Bashir_Aushev" title="Bashir Aushev">Bashir Aushev</a> were also assassinated in separate shootings in June 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyt_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
