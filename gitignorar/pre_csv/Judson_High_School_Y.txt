<p><b>Judson High School</b> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_school_(government_funded)" title="Public school (government funded)">public</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coeducation" title="Coeducation">co-educational</a> <a href="/wiki/Secondary_school" title="Secondary school">secondary school</a> in <a href="/wiki/Converse,_Texas" title="Converse, Texas">Converse, Texas</a>, United States, 15 miles northeast of downtown <a href="/wiki/San_Antonio" title="San Antonio">San Antonio</a>. It was established in 1959 as part of the <a href="/wiki/Judson_Independent_School_District" title="Judson Independent School District">Judson Independent School District</a>, and is currently classified as a 6A school by the <a href="/wiki/University_Interscholastic_League" title="University Interscholastic League">University Interscholastic League</a> (UIL). Judson High School is the second oldest International Baccalaureate World School in Texas, since 1985. The school and the District were named after Moses Campbell Judson, who served on the <a href="/wiki/Bexar_County,_Texas" title="Bexar County, Texas">Bexar County</a> School Board from 1918 to 1939. His nephew Jack Judson was on the board when the decision was made to name the new rural high school Judson.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>For a portion of its history up through 2010, Judson High School used a dual campus system wherein juniors and seniors attended the "Red Campus" and freshmen and sophomores attended the "Gray Campus." Previous to this dual campus system, Judson also had an atypical structure because it only housed grades 10-12 with the middle schools supporting grades 7-9. These structural departures from a typical high school system were due to efforts to accommodate the area's rapid population growth.
</p><p>A single building now houses all departments with the exception of the agriculture facilities. All original buildings that made up the Red Campus were razed in 2011 to make way for new athletic fields and tennis courts, and the Gray Campus was re-purposed into Judson Middle School. The Judson ISD Performing Arts Center (PAC), constructed in 1998, houses the band, choir, orchestra, and drama classes. The PAC facility has a recital hall that seats 216 people, and an Auditorium that has 840 seats. The Judson ISD Performing Arts Center is physically connected to Judson High School via a vestibule.
</p><p>Judson was named a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Blue_Ribbon_School" title="National Blue Ribbon School">National Blue Ribbon School</a> in 1999-2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Before 2005 Judson was the only high school in the district. <a href="/wiki/Karen_Wagner_High_School" title="Karen Wagner High School">Karen Wagner High School</a> opened in Fall 2005, and in Fall 2016 the district opened a third high school, <a href="/wiki/Veterans_Memorial_High_School_(San_Antonio)" title="Veterans Memorial High School (San Antonio)">Veterans Memorial High School</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Athletics">Athletics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Judson_High_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Athletics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Judson's athletic programs have experienced state championship success in several sports.
</p><p><b>Football</b>
</p><p>The Judson Rocket football program emerged as a perennial power in the 1977 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Judson won its first state championship in 1983.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/D.W._Rutledge" title="D.W. Rutledge">D.W. Rutledge</a> coached the Rockets until 2000 amassing a record of 198-31-5 taking the Rockets to seven state championship games, and winning four state titles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The football stadium is named after Coach Rutledge in his honor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p>After the 2000 season the team went to the state playoffs in nine of eleven seasons making three championship game appearances and winning one state championship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>Mark Smith, formerly of Kerrville Tivy High School where he coached Heisman Trophy winner <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Manziel" title="Johnny Manziel">Johnny Manziel</a>, assumed head coaching duties at Judson from 2012 through 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>Sean McAuliffe, an alumnus of Judson High School and its football program, became the head coach starting with the 2014 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p><p>Judson offensive coordinator Rodney Williams became head coach in May 2019.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> In his first season Williams led the Rockets to a regular season record of 9-1, but the team lost to Lake Travis 48-35 in the Region 4 Final, finishing the season at 12-2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p><p>Judson has made it to the playoffs 41 times over 58 total seasons, advanced to the state semifinal level 18 times,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> reached the state title game 11 times, and won the state championship six times.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p><p>Judson holds the all-time Texas state record with 43 consecutive winning seasons from 1977 through 2019. The team last finished with a losing record in 1976 and is a combined 456-103-5 during the streak.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The previous record for consecutive winning seasons was 36 set by <a href="/wiki/Plano_Senior_High_School" title="Plano Senior High School">Plano Senior High School</a>. During the current streak, Judson has won over 80 percent of its games, and the worst record in 43 years is 7-5 with a worst regular season record of 6-4 (happened 4 times).
</p>

<p><b>Track and Field</b>
</p><p>Judson track and field programs have experienced significant recent success. The boys team won the 2013 Texas 5A state track meet,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> and the girls team won consecutive Texas 5A/6A state championships in 2014 and 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup>
</p><p><b>Basketball</b>
</p><p>The Judson Rocket boys basketball program has been a perennial contender under coach Michael Wacker.  Judson has been to the UIL state tournament three times: 1992-1993, 2000-2001, and 2013-2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup>  In all three instances, Judson lost to the eventual state champion. The Rockets finished the 2014 season with a record of 37-2, the best record in Judson boys basketball history.
