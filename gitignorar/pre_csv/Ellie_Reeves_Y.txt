<p><b>Eleanor Claire Reeves</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> (born 11 December 1980) is a British <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(UK)" title="Labour Party (UK)">Labour Party</a> politician, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament_(UK)" title="Member of Parliament (UK)">Member of Parliament</a> (MP) for <a href="/wiki/Lewisham_West_and_Penge_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Lewisham West and Penge (UK Parliament constituency)">Lewisham West and Penge</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-labourlist1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-labourlist1-3">[3]</a></sup> She is the sister of an MP, <a href="/wiki/Rachel_Reeves" title="Rachel Reeves">Rachel Reeves</a>, and married to another MP, <a href="/wiki/John_Cryer" title="John Cryer">John Cryer</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ellie_Reeves&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ellie Reeves was born in <a href="/wiki/Lewisham" title="Lewisham">Lewisham</a>, the daughter of teachers,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HuffPost10817_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-HuffPost10817-4">[4]</a></sup> Graham and Sally Reeves,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="reference does not support text (September 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and the sister of fellow Labour MP <a href="/wiki/Rachel_Reeves" title="Rachel Reeves">Rachel Reeves</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>Reeves grew up in <a href="/wiki/Sydenham,_London" title="Sydenham, London">Sydenham</a> and was educated at Adamsrill Primary School and Cator Park Secondary School (now <a href="/wiki/Harris_Academy_Bromley" title="Harris Academy Bromley">Harris Academy Bromley</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> She has a bachelor's degree in law from <a href="/wiki/St_Catherine%27s_College,_Oxford" title="St Catherine's College, Oxford">St Catherine's College, Oxford</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ellie_Reeves&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Reeves is an employment law barrister. She joined Labour at 15<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HuffPost10817_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-HuffPost10817-4">[4]</a></sup> and was vice-chair of the <a href="/wiki/London_Labour_Party" title="London Labour Party">London Labour Party</a> and a member of Labour's <a href="/wiki/National_Executive_Committee" title="National Executive Committee">National Executive Committee</a>. She is a supporter of <a href="/wiki/Progress_(organisation)" title="Progress (organisation)">Progress</a> and <a href="/wiki/Labour_First" title="Labour First">Labour First</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-labourlist1_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-labourlist1-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Parliamentary_career">Parliamentary career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ellie_Reeves&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Parliamentary career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She was elected for the Labour-held seat of Lewisham West and Penge at the <a href="/wiki/2017_United_Kingdom_general_election" title="2017 United Kingdom general election">2017 general election</a>, almost doubling the majority to 23,000.
</p><p>She was appointed PPS to the <a href="/wiki/Shadow_Secretary_of_State_for_International_Development" title="Shadow Secretary of State for International Development">Shadow Secretary of State for International Development</a> in March 2018.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>On 13 June 2018, Reeves and five other MPs resigned as frontbenchers for the Labour Party in order to vote in favour of remaining in the single market by joining the <a href="/wiki/European_Economic_Area" title="European Economic Area">European Economic Area</a> (EEA), defying party instructions to abstain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ellie_Reeves&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She is married to <a href="/wiki/John_Cryer" title="John Cryer">John Cryer</a>, chair of the parliamentary Labour Party.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-labourlist1_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-labourlist1-3">[3]</a></sup> She had a son in 2015, and another in late 2019.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HuffPost10817_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-HuffPost10817-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
