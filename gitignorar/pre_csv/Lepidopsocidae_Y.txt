<p><b>Lepidopsocidae</b> are an insect family of bark lice (Psocoptera) belonging to the suborder <a href="/wiki/Trogiomorpha" title="Trogiomorpha">Trogiomorpha</a>. Colloquially, Lepidopsocidae are referred to as the Scaly-winged <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barklice" title="Barklice">Barklice</a>. There are more than 220 species described worldwide. Phylogenetic analysis of relationships between families in Trogiomorpha, based on partial sequences of the nuclear 18S rDNA, mitochondrial 16S rDNA, and Histone 3 genes, propose that Lepidopsocidae is monophyletic, meaning that the taxa within share a common ancestor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> This is supported morphologically by the presence of scales and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Setae" title="Setae">setae</a> covering the body and forewings of Lepodopsocids. Sister families of Lepidoposcidae include <a href="/wiki/Trogiidae" title="Trogiidae">Trogiidae</a> and Psoquillidae.
</p><p>Lepidopsocidae was described by Gunther Enderlein in <i>The Scaly Winged Copeognatha (Monograph of the Amphientomidae, Lepidopsocidae, and Lepidillidae in relation to their Morphology and Taxonomy)</i>, published in 1906.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Subfamilies" title="Subfamilies">Subfamilies</a> of Lepidopsocidae include:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Johnson2019_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Johnson2019-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gbif_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-gbif-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bugref_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-bugref-5">[5]</a></sup>
