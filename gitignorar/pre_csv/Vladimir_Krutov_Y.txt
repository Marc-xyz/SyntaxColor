<p><b>Vladimir Yevgenyevich Krutov</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Владимир Евгеньевич Крутов</span>; 1 June 1960 – 6 June 2012), nicknamed "The Tank",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet</a> <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey" title="Ice hockey">hockey</a> <a href="/wiki/Forward_(ice_hockey)" title="Forward (ice hockey)">forward</a>. Together with <a href="/wiki/Igor_Larionov" title="Igor Larionov">Igor Larionov</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sergei_Makarov_(ice_hockey)" title="Sergei Makarov (ice hockey)">Sergei Makarov</a>, he was part of the famed <i>KLM Line</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SI_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-SI-2">[2]</a></sup> He is considered one of the best hockey wingers of the 1980s.
</p><p>For the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soviet_national_ice_hockey_team" title="Soviet national ice hockey team">Soviet Union national team</a>, Krutov won the <a href="/wiki/1981_Canada_Cup" title="1981 Canada Cup">1981 Canada Cup</a>, two golds (<a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey_at_the_1984_Winter_Olympics" title="Ice hockey at the 1984 Winter Olympics">1984</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey_at_the_1988_Winter_Olympics" title="Ice hockey at the 1988 Winter Olympics">1988</a>) and one silver (<a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey_at_the_1980_Winter_Olympics" title="Ice hockey at the 1980 Winter Olympics">1980</a>) in the <a href="/wiki/Winter_Olympic_Games" title="Winter Olympic Games">Olympics</a>, and five golds (1981, 1982, 1983, 1986, 1989), one silver (1987), and one bronze (1985) in the <a href="/wiki/Ice_Hockey_World_Championships" title="Ice Hockey World Championships">World Championships</a>.
</p><p>On the club level, Krutov played for <a href="/wiki/HC_CSKA_Moscow" title="HC CSKA Moscow">CSKA Moscow</a> from 1978 to 1989. He was one of the first Soviet players to make the jump to the NHL, doing so with the <a href="/wiki/Vancouver_Canucks" title="Vancouver Canucks">Vancouver Canucks</a> in 1989.  However, Krutov did not have a successful season, battling <a href="/wiki/Homesickness" title="Homesickness">homesickness</a> and <a href="/wiki/Obesity" title="Obesity">weight problems</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>, earning the derisive nickname "Vlad the Inhaler."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>  
</p><p>Krutov left the NHL after his lone season in North America and played for several clubs in the Swiss and the Swedish minor leagues before retiring to move into coaching.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SI_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-SI-2">[2]</a></sup> His son <a href="/wiki/Alexei_Krutov" title="Alexei Krutov">Alexei Krutov</a> is a former hockey player who played professionally from 1999 to 2017.
</p><p>In 2010, he was inducted into the International Ice Hockey Federation Hall of Fame.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ToSt_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-ToSt-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>Krutov died in a hospital in Moscow on 6 June 2012, of <a href="/wiki/Internal_bleeding" title="Internal bleeding">internal bleeding</a> and <a href="/wiki/Liver_failure" title="Liver failure">liver failure</a>, just five days after his 52nd birthday.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ToSt_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-ToSt-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
