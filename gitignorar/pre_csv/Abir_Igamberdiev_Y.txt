<p><b>Abir Ubayevich Igamberdiev</b> (Russian: Абир Убаевич Игамбердиев, also known as Andrei Igamberdiev) (born 4 July 1959) is a <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian</a>-<a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canadian</a> <a href="/wiki/Mathematical_and_theoretical_biology" title="Mathematical and theoretical biology">theoretical biologist</a> and <a href="/wiki/Plant" title="Plant">plant</a> <a href="/wiki/Scientist" title="Scientist">scientist</a>. He is <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">Professor</a> at <a href="/wiki/Memorial_University_of_Newfoundland" title="Memorial University of Newfoundland">Memorial University of Newfoundland</a>, Canada. He is most known for his research on organization of <a href="/wiki/Plant_physiology" title="Plant physiology">plant metabolism</a>, for the conceptual development of foundations of theoretical biology, and for his works on <a href="/wiki/Philosophy" title="Philosophy">philosophy</a> of <a href="/wiki/Science" title="Science">science</a>. 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abir_Igamberdiev&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Igamberdiev was born in <a href="/wiki/Almaty" title="Almaty">Alma-Ata</a> (<a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a>) and lived in <a href="/wiki/Voronezh" title="Voronezh">Voronezh</a> (Russia) where he studied <a href="/wiki/Biology" title="Biology">biology</a> at <a href="/wiki/Voronezh_State_University" title="Voronezh State University">Voronezh State University</a>. He earned a <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy">PhD</a> in biology from the same university in 1985 and a <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Science" title="Doctor of Science">Doctor of Science</a> degree at the Institute of Plant Physiology of the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Academy_of_Sciences" title="Russian Academy of Sciences">Russian Academy of Sciences</a> in <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a> in 1992. In the 1990s he was a guest scientist at <a href="/wiki/Ume%C3%A5_University" title="Umeå University">Umeå University</a> (<a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>), <a href="/wiki/Free_University_of_Berlin" title="Free University of Berlin">Free University of Berlin</a> (<a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>), <a href="/wiki/University_of_Wyoming" title="University of Wyoming">University of Wyoming</a> (<a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">USA</a>), and <a href="/wiki/Ris%C3%B8_DTU_National_Laboratory_for_Sustainable_Energy" title="Risø DTU National Laboratory for Sustainable Energy">Risø National Laboratory</a> (Denmark). In 2002 he permanently moved to Canada where he worked at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Manitoba" title="University of Manitoba">University of Manitoba</a> (<a href="/wiki/Winnipeg" title="Winnipeg">Winnipeg</a>) and then at the Memorial University of Newfoundland (<a href="/wiki/St._John%27s,_Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="St. John's, Newfoundland and Labrador">St. John’s</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Newfoundland" title="Newfoundland">Newfoundland</a>) where he is currently a professor at the Department of Biology.    
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_activity">Professional activity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abir_Igamberdiev&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Professional activity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Igamberdiev has published over 250 peer-reviewed <a href="/wiki/Scientific_journal" title="Scientific journal">journal</a> publications and several edited <a href="/wiki/Book" title="Book">books</a>. He currently is <a href="/wiki/Editor-in-chief" title="Editor-in-chief">editor-in-chief</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Elsevier" title="Elsevier">Elsevier</a> journal <a href="/wiki/BioSystems" title="BioSystems">BioSystems</a> and a subject editor of the <a href="/wiki/Journal_of_Plant_Physiology" title="Journal of Plant Physiology">Journal of Plant Physiology</a>.  Abir Igamberdiev’s research focuses on the organization of <a href="/wiki/Metabolism" title="Metabolism">metabolism</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>, <a href="/wiki/Bioenergetics" title="Bioenergetics">bioenergetics</a> of <a href="/wiki/Plant_cell" title="Plant cell">plant cells</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>, plant <a href="/wiki/Enzyme" title="Enzyme">enzymology</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>, plant <a href="/wiki/Adaptation" title="Adaptation">adaptation</a> to hypoxic <a href="/wiki/Stress_(biology)" title="Stress (biology)">stress</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>, <a href="/wiki/Nitric_oxide" title="Nitric oxide">nitric oxide</a> metabolism in plants and the role of plant <a href="/wiki/Hemoglobin" title="Hemoglobin">hemoglobins</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>, foundations of theoretical biology<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>, <a href="/wiki/Natural_philosophy" title="Natural philosophy">natural philosophy</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>, and <a href="/wiki/System_dynamics" title="System dynamics">dynamics</a> of <a href="/wiki/Social_system" title="Social system">social systems</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>.
</p><p>Abir Igamberdiev has contributed to the characterization of metabolic pathways and enzymes of plant <a href="/wiki/Cellular_respiration" title="Cellular respiration">respiratory metabolism</a> and to the discovery that plant <a href="/wiki/Mitochondrion" title="Mitochondrion">mitochondria</a> use <a href="/wiki/Nitrite" title="Nitrite">nitrite</a> as an alternative <a href="/wiki/Electron_acceptor" title="Electron acceptor">electron acceptor</a> under <a href="/wiki/Hypoxia_(environmental)" title="Hypoxia (environmental)">anoxia</a> (<a href="/wiki/Anaerobic_respiration" title="Anaerobic respiration">anaerobic respiration</a>) and produce <a href="/wiki/Nitric_oxide" title="Nitric oxide">nitric oxide</a>, which is scavenged by <a href="/wiki/Phytoglobin" title="Phytoglobin">phytoglobin</a>. He developed the concept of <a href="/wiki/Thermodynamics" title="Thermodynamics">thermodynamic</a> buffering in metabolism that supports stable <a href="/wiki/Non-equilibrium_thermodynamics" title="Non-equilibrium thermodynamics">non-equilibrium dynamics</a> of <a href="/wiki/Biological_system" title="Biological system">living systems</a>. He introduced the concept of internal <a href="/wiki/Quantum_state" title="Quantum state">quantum state</a> of living systems that maintains their <a href="/wiki/Computation" title="Computation">computational</a> properties and determines <a href="/wiki/Morphogenesis" title="Morphogenesis">morphogenesis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Evolution" title="Evolution">evolution</a>. Evolution of social systems is viewed by Igamberdiev as a continuous generation and interaction of <a href="/wiki/Reflexivity_(social_theory)" title="Reflexivity (social theory)">reflexive</a> models of externality that trigger social dynamics and establish the structure of <a href="/wiki/Society" title="Society">societies</a>.  
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Essays_and_works_of_fiction">Essays and works of fiction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abir_Igamberdiev&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Essays and works of fiction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Abir Igamberdiev is an author of <a href="/wiki/Short_story" title="Short story">short stories</a> and <a href="/wiki/Essay" title="Essay">essays</a> on <a href="/wiki/Classical_music" title="Classical music">classical music</a>, <a href="/wiki/Literature" title="Literature">literature</a> and <a href="/wiki/Philosophy" title="Philosophy">philosophy</a> in Russian (<a class="external text" href="http://samlib.ru/i/igamberdiew_a_u/" rel="nofollow">full texts here</a>).
</p>
