<p>The <b>Laughing Baby</b> is a <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> <a href="/wiki/Viral_video" title="Viral video">viral video</a> of a baby laughing. The video became an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Internet_phenomenon" title="Internet phenomenon">internet phenomenon</a> and has had a total of over 69 million views. Originally uploaded by a Swedish man under the pseudonym of spacelord72, and later re-uploaded and popularized by another user known as BlackOleg,   the "Laughing Baby" is one of the few <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Internet_memes" title="Internet memes">internet memes</a> that have entered <a href="/wiki/Popular_culture" title="Popular culture">popular culture</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-slate_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-slate-1">[1]</a></sup> The original uploaded by spacelord72, has had 11 million views as of April 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-THEVIDEO_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-THEVIDEO-2">[2]</a></sup> and the reposting by BlackOleg, titled HaHaHa, achieved 62 million views by Sep 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kullin_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-kullin-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_video">The video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Laughing_Baby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: The video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Laughing Baby video, which was shot by a Swedish man in his kitchen in 2006,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CollegeHumour_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-CollegeHumour-4">[4]</a></sup> has a running time of 1:40, and involves a fixed camera situated on William Nilsson,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> a blond-haired, blue eyed baby, who laughs after brief <a href="/wiki/Interjection" title="Interjection">interjections</a> from a voice in the background (presumably his father, Kjell-Åke Andersson).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-THEVIDEO_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-THEVIDEO-2">[2]</a></sup> The video opens with the <a href="/wiki/Swedish_language" title="Swedish language">Swedish text</a>: "kan man ha roligare" which translates as "Can you have any more fun?"
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Comments">Comments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Laughing_Baby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Comments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The clip has become known for its disparate variety of commenters – from the amused to those concerned for the baby's health.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CollegeHumour_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-CollegeHumour-4">[4]</a></sup> These comments were the focus of a <i><a href="/wiki/Slate_(magazine)" title="Slate (magazine)">Slate</a></i> article by Michael Agger, who remarked that <a href="/wiki/Upload" title="Upload">uploading</a> the video was like "dipping a bunny into acid," further adding that the "Laughing Baby has become an internet monument, and posting a remark is like tagging your name on the <a href="/wiki/Statue_of_Liberty" title="Statue of Liberty">Statue of Liberty</a>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-slate_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-slate-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Laughing_Baby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Laughing Baby video became a worldwide phenomenon and the tenth most viewed YouTube video of all time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-THEVIDEO_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-THEVIDEO-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kullin_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-kullin-3">[3]</a></sup> Its success is attributed to "<a href="/wiki/Cuteness" title="Cuteness">cuteness</a>" and its "adorable nature".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-slate_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-slate-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-last100_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-last100-6">[6]</a></sup> The video appeared on the front pages of <a href="/wiki/Web_portal" title="Web portal">web portals</a> such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Msn.com" title="Msn.com">msn.com</a>, and has been the theme of several <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ringtones" title="Ringtones">ringtones</a>, which helped to further increase its familiarity and popularity. The baby was featured in the <i><a href="/wiki/South_Park" title="South Park">South Park</a></i> episode "<a href="/wiki/Canada_on_Strike" title="Canada on Strike">Canada on Strike</a>", where he appeared alongside other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/YouTube_celebrity" title="YouTube celebrity">YouTube celebrities</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Tay_Zonday" title="Tay Zonday">Tay Zonday</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chris_Crocker_(Internet_celebrity)" title="Chris Crocker (Internet celebrity)">Chris Crocker</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-southparkstudios_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-southparkstudios-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>The "Laughing Baby" video was shown to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Elizabeth II of the United Kingdom">Queen Elizabeth II</a> and her husband <a href="/wiki/Prince_Philip,_Duke_of_Edinburgh" title="Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh">Prince Philip</a> during a 16 October 2008 visit to <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a> headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Victoria,_London" title="Victoria, London">Victoria, London</a>. The Queen remarked that the child was a "...lovely little thing, isn't it?" and that it was "Amazing [that] a child would laugh like that."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Queenvideo_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Queenvideo-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
