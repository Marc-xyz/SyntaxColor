<p><i><b>Mycobacterium hiberniae</b></i> is a species of the phylum <a href="/wiki/Actinobacteria" title="Actinobacteria">Actinobacteria</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gram-positive" title="Gram-positive">Gram-positive</a> <a href="/wiki/Bacteria" title="Bacteria">bacteria</a> with high <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/G%2BC_ratio" title="G+C ratio">guanine and cytosine content</a>, one of the dominant phyla of all bacteria), belonging to the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Mycobacterium" title="Mycobacterium">Mycobacterium</a></i>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Etymology" title="Etymology">Etymology</a> Hibernia, <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> for <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> where it was first isolated.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mycobacterium_hiberniae&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Polymorphic, beaded, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gram-positive" title="Gram-positive">gram-positive</a>, nonmotile and acid-fast rods (0.9 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%CE%9Cm" title="Îm">Âµm</a> x 1.2-1.5Â Âµm).
</p><p><b>Colony characteristics</b>
</p>
<ul><li>Smooth and glistening <a href="/wiki/Colony_(biology)" title="Colony (biology)">colonies</a> with rose-pink <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pigmentation" title="Pigmentation">pigmentation</a> but become rough and dry later. Colonies with unique pigment production are 1-1.5Â mm in diameter.</li></ul>
<p><b>Physiology</b>
</p>
<ul><li>Slow growth on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/L%C3%B6wenstein-Jensen" title="LÃ¶wenstein-Jensen">LÃ¶wenstein-Jensen</a> medium and <a href="/wiki/Middlebrook_7H10_Agar" title="Middlebrook 7H10 Agar">Middlebrook 7H10</a> agar at 37Â Â°C (range: 22-37Â Â°C). No growth at 42Â Â°C.</li>
<li>Resistant to <a href="/wiki/Isoniazid" title="Isoniazid">isoniazid</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rifampin" title="Rifampin">rifampin</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Streptomycin" title="Streptomycin">streptomycin</a></li>
<li>Sensitive to <a href="/wiki/Ethambutol" title="Ethambutol">ethambutol</a>.</li></ul>
<p><b>Differential characteristics</b>
</p>
<ul><li><i>M. hiberniae</i> has unusual rose-pink pigmentation, which is unique in the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Mycobacterium" title="Mycobacterium">Mycobacterium</a></i>.</li></ul>
