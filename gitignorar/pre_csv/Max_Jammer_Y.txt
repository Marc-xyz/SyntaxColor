<p><b>Max Jammer</b> (born <b>Moshe Jammer</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> <small>German: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German" title="Help:IPA/Standard German">[ˈjamɐ]</a></span>; April 13, 1915 – December 18, 2010), was an <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israeli</a> <a href="/wiki/Physics" title="Physics">physicist</a> and <a href="/wiki/Philosophy_of_physics" title="Philosophy of physics">philosopher of physics</a>. He was born in <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Max_Jammer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jammer studied physics, philosophy and <a href="/wiki/History_of_science" title="History of science">history of science</a>, first at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Vienna" title="University of Vienna">University of Vienna</a>, and then from 1935 at the <a href="/wiki/Hebrew_University_of_Jerusalem" title="Hebrew University of Jerusalem">Hebrew University of Jerusalem</a>, where he received a PhD in experimental physics in 1942. After serving in the British Army for the rest of the war, Jammer returned to Hebrew University, where he lectured on the history and philosophy of science, before moving in 1952 to <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a>. He subsequently became a lecturer there and a close colleague of <a href="/wiki/Albert_Einstein" title="Albert Einstein">Albert Einstein</a> at <a href="/wiki/Princeton_University" title="Princeton University">Princeton University</a>. He taught at Harvard, the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Oklahoma" title="University of Oklahoma">University of Oklahoma</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Boston_University" title="Boston University">Boston University</a>, before in 1956 establishing the Department and becoming Professor of Physics at <a href="/wiki/Bar-Ilan_University" title="Bar-Ilan University">Bar-Ilan University</a> in Israel, where he later also served as President and Rector. He also co-founded the Institute for Philosophy of Science at <a href="/wiki/Tel_Aviv_University" title="Tel Aviv University">Tel Aviv University</a>, and has been President of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Association_for_the_Advancement_for_Science_in_Israel&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Association for the Advancement for Science in Israel (page does not exist)">Association for the Advancement for Science in Israel</a>. He was Visiting Professor at the <a href="/wiki/ETH_Zurich" title="ETH Zurich">Swiss Federal Institute of Technology</a> in <a href="/wiki/Z%C3%BCrich" title="Zürich">Zürich</a>, the <a href="/wiki/University_of_G%C3%B6ttingen" title="University of Göttingen">University of Göttingen</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Institut_Henri_Poincar%C3%A9" title="Institut Henri Poincaré">Institut Henri Poincaré</a>, <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University" title="Columbia University">Columbia University</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Catholic_University_of_America" title="The Catholic University of America">the Catholic University of America</a> in Washington, D. C., and other universities in the United States and Canada.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Max_Jammer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Awards received by Jammer include:
</p>
<ul><li>the 1984 <a href="/wiki/Israel_Prize" title="Israel Prize">Israel Prize</a>, awarded for history of science;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prize_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-prize-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li>the 2003 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EMET_Prize" title="EMET Prize">EMET Prize</a> awarded by the Prime Minister of Israel;</li>
<li>the 2007 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abraham_Pais_Prize" title="Abraham Pais Prize">Abraham Pais Prize</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/History_of_Physics" title="History of Physics">History of Physics</a>, awarded by the <a href="/wiki/American_Physical_Society" title="American Physical Society">American Physical Society</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pais_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pais-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>the Monograph Prize of the <a href="/wiki/American_Academy_of_Arts_and_Sciences" title="American Academy of Arts and Sciences">American Academy of Arts and Sciences</a>; and</li>
<li>a prize for 'an outstanding book on theology and natural sciences' from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Templeton_Foundation" title="Templeton Foundation">Templeton Foundation</a>.</li></ul>
