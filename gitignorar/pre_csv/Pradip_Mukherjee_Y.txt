<p><b>Pradip Mukherjee</b> (also known as <i>Pradip Mukhopadhyay</i>) is an Indian actor and dramatist who primarily acts in <a href="/wiki/Cinema_of_West_Bengal" title="Cinema of West Bengal">Bengali movies</a>. Although not famous for acting in mainstream Bengali movies, Pradip Mukherjee has received critical acclaim for his acting in movies like <a href="/wiki/Satyajit_Ray" title="Satyajit Ray">Satyajit Ray</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Jana_Aranya" title="Jana Aranya">Jana Aranya</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Buddhadeb_Dasgupta" title="Buddhadeb Dasgupta">Buddhadeb Dasgupta</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Dooratwa" title="Dooratwa">Dooratwa</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Rituparno_Ghosh" title="Rituparno Ghosh">Rituparno Ghosh</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Utsab" title="Utsab">Utsab</a></i>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pradip_Mukherjee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pradip Mukherjee was born to middle-class parents Sammohan Mukhopadhyay and Bhakti Mukhopadhyay and grew up in Chorbagan area of Shimla in <a href="/wiki/Kolkata" title="Kolkata">Kolkata</a>. He completed his high school education from <a href="/wiki/Hare_School" title="Hare School">Hare School</a> in 1965 and graduated from <a href="/wiki/City_College,_Kolkata" title="City College, Kolkata">City College, Kolkata</a> in 1970. He took up law and graduated from law school in 1973.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting_career">Acting career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pradip_Mukherjee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Acting career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During his college days, Pradip Mukherjee took drama lessons and joined drama academies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup> He acted in several plays at Tapan Theater in Kolkata. After graduating from law school, he worked full-time as a lawyer and acted in plays during weekends. In 1974, he met <a href="/wiki/Satyajit_Ray" title="Satyajit Ray">Satyajit Ray</a>, who, impressed with his performance while he performed for Nakshatra Theater Group, cast him in his last movie of the <a href="/wiki/Calcutta_trilogy" title="Calcutta trilogy">Calcutta Trilogy</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Jana_Aranya" title="Jana Aranya">Jana Aranya</a></i>. The film received critical acclaim and won several awards including Karlovy Vary Prize in 1976.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERobinson1989366_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERobinson1989366-2">[2]</a></sup> He also earned <a href="/wiki/Filmfare_Awards_East" title="Filmfare Awards East">Filmfare Awards East</a> for Best Actor in 1976.
</p><p>Later in his career, he acted in critically acclaimed films like <i><a href="/wiki/Dooratwa" title="Dooratwa">Dooratwa</a></i> (1981) and <i><a href="/wiki/Utsab" title="Utsab">Utsab</a></i> (2001).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pradip_Mukherjee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pradip Mukherjee married in 1977 and has a son and a daughter.
</p><p>Besides acting, he works full-time as a tax consultant in <a href="/wiki/Lake_Town,_Kolkata" title="Lake Town, Kolkata">Lake Town</a>, <a class="external text" href="https://www.google.ae/maps/place/Patipukur,+Kolkata,+West+Bengal+700048,+India/@22.603607,88.3932671,16z/data=!3m1!4b1!4m5!3m4!1s0x3a027606dba67667:0x3e8fd59a3481b1fe!8m2!3d22.604442!4d88.3955409" rel="nofollow">Patipukur</a> area of East <a href="/wiki/Kolkata" title="Kolkata">Kolkata</a>.
</p>
