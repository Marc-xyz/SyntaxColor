<p><b>Altheim culture</b> (Altheim group) is a <a href="/wiki/Chalcolithic" title="Chalcolithic">Chalcolithic</a> culture that derives its name from the town of <a href="/wiki/Altheim,_Biberach" title="Altheim, Biberach">Altheim</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> It is also a subgroup of Lengyel culture.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>  Not defined until 1915 by the German Prehistorian <a href="/wiki/Paul_Reinecke" title="Paul Reinecke">Paul Reinecke</a> after finds from a <a href="/wiki/Causewayed_enclosure" title="Causewayed enclosure">Causewayed enclosure</a> 1000 m nw of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Altheim_(Essenbach)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Altheim (Essenbach) (page does not exist)">Altheim (Essenbach)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Landshut_(district)" title="Landshut (district)">Landshut (district)</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Lower_Bavaria" title="Lower Bavaria">Lower Bavaria</a>, Southeastern Germany.
</p><p>The Altheim culture sites range from the <a href="/wiki/Inn_(river)" title="Inn (river)">Inn (river)</a> in the East to the <a href="/wiki/Lech_(river)" title="Lech (river)">Lech (river)</a> in the West and from the Alb hills in the North to the foothills of the Alps in the South.
</p><p>The Altheim culture dates to between 3800 and 3400 BC, i.e. the Younger Neolithic of Southern Central Europe, formerly called the Chalcolithic.
</p><p>Contemporaneous phenomena are, to the West <a href="/wiki/Pfyn_culture" title="Pfyn culture">Pfyn culture</a> in sw Germany and Switzerland, to the Northwest and North <a href="/wiki/Michelsberg_culture" title="Michelsberg culture">Michelsberg culture</a>, to the East <a href="/wiki/Baalberge_group" title="Baalberge group">Baalberge group</a> in Bohemia and to the Southeast <a href="/wiki/Mondsee_group" title="Mondsee group">Mondsee group</a>.
</p><p><br/>
