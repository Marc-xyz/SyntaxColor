<p><i><b>Tordylium</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> of <a href="/wiki/Flowering_plant" title="Flowering plant">flowering plants</a> in the carrot family (<a href="/wiki/Apiaceae" title="Apiaceae">Apiaceae</a>). Members of the genus are known as <b>hartworts</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stac10p828_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Stac10p828-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tordylium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Tordylium</i> species are <a href="/wiki/Annual_plant" title="Annual plant">annuals</a> or <a href="/wiki/Biennial_plant" title="Biennial plant">biennials</a>, covered in long hairs. Their stems may be hollow or almost solid. The basal leaves are more-or-less undivided, and have usually disappeared when the plant flowers. The stem leaves are once <a href="/wiki/Pinnation" title="Pinnation">pinnate</a>. The flowers have persistent <a href="/wiki/Sepal" title="Sepal">sepals</a> and white <a href="/wiki/Petal" title="Petal">petals</a>, with those on one side much longer than the other. The fruits are about as long as they are wide. Their side ridges have whitish wings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stac10p828_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Stac10p828-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tordylium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Species assigned to the genus were first described by <a href="/wiki/Carl_Linnaeus" title="Carl Linnaeus">Carl Linnaeus</a> in 1753 in <i><a href="/wiki/Species_Plantarum" title="Species Plantarum">Species Plantarum</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IPNI_Tordylium_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-IPNI_Tordylium-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Linn53_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Linn53-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Species">Species</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tordylium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Species">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The number of species in the genus varies widely between sources. <i><a href="/wiki/Ainsworthia" title="Ainsworthia">Ainsworthia</a></i> and <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Synelcosciadium&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Synelcosciadium (page does not exist)">Synelcosciadium</a></i> were included in <i>Tordylium</i> by El-Aisawi &amp; Jury (1998).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AlEiJury88_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-AlEiJury88-4">[4]</a></sup> Gömürgen et al. (2011) say there are 17 species in <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> alone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GomuDogaOzmeBase11_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-GomuDogaOzmeBase11-5">[5]</a></sup> As of February 2015<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Tordylium&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, <a href="/wiki/The_Plant_List" title="The Plant List">The Plant List</a> accepts only six species in total, keeping <i>Ainsworthia</i> and <i>Synelcosciadium</i> separate:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TPL_Tordylium_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-TPL_Tordylium-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TPL_Ainsworthia_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-TPL_Ainsworthia-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TPL_Synelcosciadium_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-TPL_Synelcosciadium-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><i><a href="/wiki/Tordylium_apulum" title="Tordylium apulum">Tordylium apulum</a></i> <small>L.</small> – Mediterranean hartwort</li>
<li><i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tordylium_byzantinum&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tordylium byzantinum (page does not exist)">Tordylium byzantinum</a></i> <small>(Azn.) Hayek</small><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Note1_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Note1-9">[Note 1]</a></sup></li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Tordylium_elegans" title="Tordylium elegans">Tordylium elegans</a></i> <small>(Boiss. &amp; Balansa) Alava</small></li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Tordylium_maximum" title="Tordylium maximum">Tordylium maximum</a></i> <small>L.</small> – hartwort</li>
<li><i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tordylium_officinale&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tordylium officinale (page does not exist)">Tordylium officinale</a></i> <small>L.</small></li>
<li><i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tordylium_pestalozzae&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tordylium pestalozzae (page does not exist)">Tordylium pestalozzae</a></i> <small>Boiss.</small></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Uses">Uses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tordylium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Uses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Tordylium apulum</i>, the Mediterranean hartwort or Roman pimpernel, is used as a vegetable in <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a> and as a flavouring in <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Facc98_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Facc98-10">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>Tordylium officinale, the <a href="/wiki/Officinal" title="Officinal">Officinal</a> or <a href="/wiki/Crete" title="Crete">Cretan</a> Hartwort ( also a Mediterranean species ) , bears fruit formerly used as an <a href="/wiki/Emmenagogue" title="Emmenagogue">emmenagogue</a>, and the plant ( plant part unspecified ) has formed one of the ingredients of <a href="/wiki/Theriac" title="Theriac">Theriac</a>, a preparation believed to be an <a href="/wiki/Antidote" title="Antidote">antidote</a> to <a href="/wiki/Snake_venom" title="Snake venom">snake</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Venom" title="Venom">venoms</a>. <a href="/wiki/Lucien_D%C3%A9sir%C3%A9_Joseph_Courchet" title="Lucien Désiré Joseph Courchet">Courchet</a> further states of the genus Tordylium in general that the various species bear fruits that - like those of many other Umbellifers - are aromatic and <a href="/wiki/Carminative" title="Carminative">carminative</a>, but that those of Tordylium are seldom used.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
