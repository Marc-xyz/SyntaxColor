<p><i><b>The Conway Daily Sun</b></i> is a five-day (Tuesday through Saturday) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Free_daily_newspaper" title="Free daily newspaper">free daily newspaper</a> published in <a href="/wiki/North_Conway,_New_Hampshire" title="North Conway, New Hampshire">North Conway, New Hampshire</a>, United States, covering the <a href="/wiki/Mount_Washington_(New_Hampshire)" title="Mount Washington (New Hampshire)">Mount Washington Valley</a>. It has been published since 1989 by <a href="/wiki/Country_News_Club" title="Country News Club">Country News Club</a>, and was the forerunner of three other <i>Daily Sun</i> newspapers in <a href="/wiki/New_Hampshire" title="New Hampshire">New Hampshire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">Maine</a>.
</p><p><i>The Conway Daily Sun</i> was the first <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> daily to publish the popular numbers puzzle <a href="/wiki/Sudoku" title="Sudoku">Sudoku</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Today">Today</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Conway_Daily_Sun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Today">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mark Guerringue, one of the three founders of Country News Club, still serves as publisher of <i>The Conway Daily Sun</i> as of early 2012.
</p><p>The <i>Daily Sun</i> circulates in several towns of <a href="/wiki/Carroll_County,_New_Hampshire" title="Carroll County, New Hampshire">Carroll County, New Hampshire</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Albany,_New_Hampshire" title="Albany, New Hampshire">Albany</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bartlett,_New_Hampshire" title="Bartlett, New Hampshire">Bartlett</a> (including Glen), <a href="/wiki/Conway,_New_Hampshire" title="Conway, New Hampshire">Conway</a> (including <a href="/wiki/Intervale,_New_Hampshire" title="Intervale, New Hampshire">Intervale</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_Conway,_New_Hampshire" title="North Conway, New Hampshire">North Conway</a>), <a href="/wiki/Eaton,_New_Hampshire" title="Eaton, New Hampshire">Eaton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Freedom,_New_Hampshire" title="Freedom, New Hampshire">Freedom</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jackson,_New_Hampshire" title="Jackson, New Hampshire">Jackson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Madison,_New_Hampshire" title="Madison, New Hampshire">Madison</a> (including <a href="/wiki/Silver_Lake,_New_Hampshire" title="Silver Lake, New Hampshire">Silver Lake</a>), <a href="/wiki/Moultonborough,_New_Hampshire" title="Moultonborough, New Hampshire">Moultonborough</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tamworth,_New_Hampshire" title="Tamworth, New Hampshire">Tamworth</a> (including <a href="/wiki/Chocorua,_New_Hampshire" title="Chocorua, New Hampshire">Chocorua</a>), <a href="/wiki/Ossipee,_New_Hampshire" title="Ossipee, New Hampshire">Ossipee</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wolfeboro,_New_Hampshire" title="Wolfeboro, New Hampshire">Wolfeboro</a>; and two towns in <a href="/wiki/Oxford_County,_Maine" title="Oxford County, Maine">Oxford County, Maine</a>: <a href="/wiki/Fryeburg,_Maine" title="Fryeburg, Maine">Fryeburg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lovell,_Maine" title="Lovell, Maine">Lovell</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>A complete <a href="/wiki/PDF" title="PDF">PDF</a> of the newspaper is also available for free online; for an extra subscription fee, readers can even access the online version the night before the print edition is distributed. Online subscribers also have access to newspaper's stories on web pages.
</p><p>Lloyd Jones, Daymond Steer, Brett Guerringue and Tom Eastman are <i>The Conway Daily Sun</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s staff writers. Several stringers and part-time writers also contribute regularly. Margaret McKenzie is the managing editor, Terry Leavitt edits the health section and Berlin paper. Lloyd Jones also serves as the sports editor. Alec Kerr edits the wire pages and entertainment sections and serves as the paper's entertainment critic.
</p><p>Citing advertisement revenue issues, <i>The Conway Daily Sun</i> printed its final Monday edition on October 26, 2009.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The paper continues to be published Tuesday through Saturday.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sisters_and_competitors">Sisters and competitors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Conway_Daily_Sun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sisters and competitors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Conway was the first free daily to be launched by Dave Danforth, Mark Guerringue and Adam Hirshan. It was joined a few years later by <i><a href="/wiki/The_Berlin_Daily_Sun" title="The Berlin Daily Sun">The Berlin Daily Sun</a></i>. In 2000, Guerringue and Hirshan partnered with Ed Engler to launch <i><a href="/wiki/The_Laconia_Daily_Sun" title="The Laconia Daily Sun">The Laconia Daily Sun</a></i> in the neighboring <a href="/wiki/Lakes_Region_(New_Hampshire)" title="Lakes Region (New Hampshire)">Lakes Region</a> of <a href="/wiki/New_Hampshire" title="New Hampshire">New Hampshire</a>. In 2009, Guerringue and Hirshan teamed with Curtis Robinson to start <i><a href="/wiki/The_Portland_Daily_Sun" title="The Portland Daily Sun">The Portland Daily Sun</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Maine" title="Portland, Maine">Portland, Maine</a>.
</p><p>No other daily newspaper is based in <a href="/wiki/Carroll_County,_New_Hampshire" title="Carroll County, New Hampshire">Carroll County</a>. Country News Club's closest competitor geographically is <i><a href="/wiki/The_Citizen_(Laconia)" title="The Citizen (Laconia)">The Citizen</a></i> of <a href="/wiki/Laconia,_New_Hampshire" title="Laconia, New Hampshire">Laconia</a>.
</p>
