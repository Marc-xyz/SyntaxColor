	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">R. E. Latham</h1>

		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Ronald Edward Latham</b> (1907-1992) was an English classicist best known for his translation of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/On_the_Nature_of_the_Universe" title="On the Nature of the Universe">On the Nature of the Universe</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Lucretius" title="Lucretius">Lucretius</a>. He also translated <i><a href="/wiki/The_Travels_of_Marco_Polo" title="The Travels of Marco Polo">The Travels of Marco Polo</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Bede" title="Bede">Bede</a>'s 'Ecclesiastical History of the English People<i>.</i>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R._E._Latham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Latham was educated at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Royal_Grammar_School,_Newcastle" title="Royal Grammar School, Newcastle">Royal Grammar School, Newcastle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Balliol_College,_Oxford" title="Balliol College, Oxford">Balliol College, Oxford</a>, where he read <a href="/wiki/Literae_humaniores" title="Literae humaniores">Literae humaniores</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DMLBS_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-DMLBS-1">[1]</a></sup> In 1934 he was appointed Assistant Keeper at the <a href="/wiki/Public_Record_Office" title="Public Record Office">Public Record Office</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> His 1938 lectures at the St Pancras <a href="/wiki/Working_Men%27s_College" title="Working Men's College">Working Men's College</a> were published in book form as <i>In Quest of Civiliation</i> (1946).
</p><p>In 1968 he was appointed the first editor of the <i>Dictionary of Medieval Latin from British Sources</i>. In 1977 he retired, and was succeeded by David Howlett.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DMLBS_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-DMLBS-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Works">Works</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R._E._Latham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Works">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><i>In quest of civilization</i>. London: Jarrolds, 1946.</li>
<li>(tr.) <i>Lucretius on the nature of the universe</i> by <a href="/wiki/Lucretius" title="Lucretius">Lucretius</a>. Harmondsworth, England: Penguin, 1951.</li>
<li>(tr.) <i>The travels of Marco Polo, the Venetian</i> by <a href="/wiki/Marco_Polo" title="Marco Polo">Marco Polo</a>. Harmondsworth, England: Penguin, 1958.</li>
<li>(ed.) <i>Revised medieval Latin word-list from British and Irish sources</i>. London: Published for the British Academy by the Oxford University Press, 1965</li>
<li>(tr.) <i>Ecclesiastical history of the English people with Bede's letter to Egbert and Cuthbert's letter on the death of Bede</i>.</li></ul>
