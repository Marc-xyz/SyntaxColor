<p><b>Saint Felix School</b> is a 2–18 <a href="/wiki/Mixed-sex_education" title="Mixed-sex education">mixed</a>, <a href="/wiki/Independent_school_(United_Kingdom)" title="Independent school (United Kingdom)">independent</a>, <a href="/wiki/Day_school" title="Day school">day</a> and <a href="/wiki/Boarding_school" title="Boarding school">boarding school</a> in <a href="/wiki/Reydon" title="Reydon">Reydon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Southwold" title="Southwold">Southwold</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suffolk" title="Suffolk">Suffolk</a>, England. It was founded in 1897 as a girls' school by Margaret Isabella Gardiner.
</p><p>By September 1902, the present site of the school had been purchased and the first four boarding houses and teaching block completed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In 1910, the Gardiner Hall, Library, and Clough House were built. The school accommodates babies and toddlers in the St Felix Nursery, and children up to the age of 18 in the Sixth Form. The school offers boarding throughout the term, weekly, or 'flexi' boarding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The current head is Mr James Harrison.
</p>
