	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Cologne High Military and Escort Road</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>

		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>Cologne High Military and Escort Road</b> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <i lang="de">Cölnische Hohe Heer- und Geleitstraße</i>, also called the <i>Hohe Straße</i>) is a historical trading route that ran from the city of <a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a> via the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Imperial_cities" title="Imperial cities">imperial cities</a> of  <a href="/wiki/Wetzlar" title="Wetzlar">Wetzlar</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Friedberg_(Hesse)" title="Friedberg (Hesse)">Friedberg</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frankfurt_am_Main" title="Frankfurt am Main">Frankfurt</a> within the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Holy Roman Empire</a>.
</p><p>This <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Historic_road" title="Historic road">historic road</a> ran along the eastern edge of the <a href="/wiki/Westerwald" title="Westerwald">Westerwald</a> mountains via Greifenstein and crossed the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/River_Lahn" title="River Lahn">River Lahn</a> in Wetzlar. In addition to its function as a trading road this highway was also used as a <a href="/wiki/Pilgrims%27_way" title="Pilgrims' way">pilgrims' way</a> from Cologne to <a href="/wiki/Marburg" title="Marburg">Marburg</a>. The so-called Elizabeth Path (<i>Elisabethpfad</i>) had the grave of Saint <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Elizabeth_of_Thuringia" title="Elizabeth of Thuringia">Elizabeth of Thuringia</a> in Marburg as its destination. This historical pilgrimage route was waymarked as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Way_of_St._James" title="Way of St. James">Way of St. James</a> in 2007. The section of the old road between <a href="/wiki/Butzbach" title="Butzbach">Butzbach</a> and Wetzlar is recorded in 1315 and 1349 in deeds no. 441 and 758 at <a href="/wiki/Arnsburg_Abbey" title="Arnsburg Abbey">Arnsburg Abbey</a>.
</p>
