<p><b>Lite</b>, stylized as <b>LITE</b>, is a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Math_rock" title="Math rock">math rock</a> band that has been termed "one of Japan's top instrumental rock acts."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JTreview_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-JTreview-1">[1]</a></sup> The band's members are Nobuyuki Takeda (guitar), Kozo Kusumoto (guitar &amp; synthesizer), Jun Izawa (bass), and Akinori Yamamoto (drums). 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lite_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Formed in 2003 in Tokyo, LITE did many gigs around Tokyo and self-released two demo CDs. In 2006, they released one mini-album, <i>LITE</i>, and one full-length album, <i>Filmlets</i>, through Transduction in the UK and Cargo in Europe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nothing_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nothing-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>LITE's sound combines the precision and musicianship of prog rock with the emotionally charged cinematic compositions of art rock, in a heavier, more modern package that they describe as “<a href="/wiki/Math_rock" title="Math rock">math rock</a>”.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> 
</p><p>LITE toured the UK and Ireland in 2006. In July 2007, they played at Fuji Rock Festival in Japan and followed that with another UK &amp; Ireland tour in September, coinciding with a split release with Funanori (Kaori Tsuchida from <a href="/wiki/The_Go!_Team" title="The Go! Team">The Go! Team</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mike_Watt" title="Mike Watt">Mike Watt</a> from the Minutemen).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JT_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-JT-4">[4]</a></sup> Titled "A Tiny Twofer", the split CD features three songs each. In 2008 they released their second album <i>Phantasia</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nothing_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Nothing-2">[2]</a></sup> In May 2009, they made their American debut in New York City opening for Mike Watt's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Missingmen" title="The Missingmen">Missingmen</a>.
</p><p>LITE produced two EPs, <i>Turns Red</i> in 2009, and <i>Illuminate</i> in 2010, and third album <i>For All the Innocence</i> in 2011.
</p><p><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JTreview_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-JTreview-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Their mini-album <i>Past, Present, Future</i> was released in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>LITE released their 4th album <i>Installation</i> in 2013 from Japan (I want The Moon) and the US (Topshelf Records). 
</p><p>In 2015, the band released their US tour documentary DVD titled <i>Past 7 Days</i> (I want The Moon).
</p><p>In 2016, LITE and <a href="/wiki/Mouse_on_the_Keys" title="Mouse on the Keys">mouse on the keys</a> released LITE / mouse on the keys <i>split</i> and they did the US tour from November 2016 and March 2017.
</p><p>The band released their 5th album <i>Cubic</i> from Japan (I want The Moon) and the US (Topshelf Records). 
</p><p>In 2018, LITE released their new single <i>Blizzard</i> from Japan (I want The Moon) and the US (Topshelf Records). 
</p><p>In 2019, LITE released split 7 inch with Redneck Manifesto <i>TRM・LITE</i> from STIFFSLACK in Japan.
</p><p>The band released their 6th album <i>Multiple</i> from Japan (I want The Moon) and the US (Topshelf Records). 
</p><p><br/>
</p>
