<p><b>GMA News and Public Affairs</b> (formerly known as <i>RBS News Department</i>, <i>GMA Radio-Television News</i> and <i>GMA Rainbow Satellite News</i>; and commonly <b>GMA News</b>) is the news and public affairs programming division of <a href="/wiki/GMA_Network_(company)" title="GMA Network (company)">GMA Network Inc.</a>
</p><p>The division produces news, public affairs, infotainment, and lately, entertainment programs for <a href="/wiki/GMA_Network" title="GMA Network">GMA Network</a> and <a href="/wiki/GMA_News_TV" title="GMA News TV">GMA News TV</a> owned and affiliated television and radio stations in the Philippines, and internationally through <a href="/wiki/GMA_Pinoy_TV" title="GMA Pinoy TV">GMA Pinoy TV</a>, <a href="/wiki/GMA_Life_TV" title="GMA Life TV">GMA Life TV</a> and GMA News TV International.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=GMA_News_and_Public_Affairs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:GMANewsLogo.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="80" data-file-width="425" decoding="async" height="56" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/24/GMANewsLogo.png/300px-GMANewsLogo.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/24/GMANewsLogo.png 1.5x" width="300"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:GMANewsLogo.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>GMA News and Public Affairs logo with the "Serbisyong Totoo" tagline, an older logo as well.</div></div></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="News_division">News division</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=GMA_News_and_Public_Affairs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: News division">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The GMA News division traces its origin from the Republic Broadcasting System (RBS), established by <a href="/wiki/Robert_Stewart_(entrepreneur)" title="Robert Stewart (entrepreneur)">Robert Stewart</a> in 1950. The programming of its flagship <a href="/wiki/AM_broadcasting" title="AM broadcasting">AM radio station</a>, <a href="/wiki/DZBB-AM" title="DZBB-AM">DZBB</a>, depended mostly on news reports. The station covered the eruption of Mt. Hibok-Hibok in 1951 and the election and death of the former <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_Philippines" title="President of the Philippines">Philippine president</a> <a href="/wiki/Ramon_Magsaysay" title="Ramon Magsaysay">Ramon Magsaysay</a>.
</p><p>While <a href="/wiki/DZBB-TV" title="DZBB-TV">RBS Channel 7</a> was established in 1961, it was in the 1970s that GMA became one of the most-watched television news source in the country. In 1983, Channel 7 was the first to break the news of <a href="/wiki/Benigno_Aquino_Jr." title="Benigno Aquino Jr.">Ninoy Aquino</a>'s death and later would be the only television station to broadcast his funeral. The channel also became the first station to broadcast the <a href="/wiki/Fidel_Ramos" title="Fidel Ramos">Ramos</a>-<a href="/wiki/Juan_Ponce_Enrile" title="Juan Ponce Enrile">Enrile</a> break-away that led to the <a href="/wiki/People_Power_Revolution" title="People Power Revolution">People Power Revolution</a>, also known as EDSA Revolution. In 1998, GMA teamed up with The <i><a href="/wiki/Philippine_Daily_Inquirer" title="Philippine Daily Inquirer">Philippine Daily Inquirer</a></i> to produce the coverage of <a href="/wiki/1998_Philippine_presidential_election" title="1998 Philippine presidential election">that year's election</a>.
</p><p>GMA News became a pioneer in local television news in many ways. GMA became the first TV network to be fully reporting the news in <a href="/wiki/Filipino_language" title="Filipino language">Filipino language</a> with their late-night newscast <i><a href="/wiki/GMA_Network_News" title="GMA Network News">GMA Network News</a></i> in 1998 which was anchored by <a href="/wiki/Mike_Enriquez" title="Mike Enriquez">Mike Enriquez</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vicky_Morales" title="Vicky Morales">Vicky Morales</a>. GMA was one of the pioneers of women in broadcast journalism. <a href="/wiki/Tina_Monzon-Palma" title="Tina Monzon-Palma">Tina Monzon-Palma</a> (who moved to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TV5_(Philippines)" title="TV5 (Philippines)">TV5</a>, later ABS-CBN) was one of the first female co-anchors when she first presented <i><a href="/wiki/News_at_Seven" title="News at Seven">News at Seven</a></i>, one of the most-watched news programs in the 1970s, <a href="/wiki/Helen_Vela" title="Helen Vela">Helen Vela</a> was the first woman to anchor news in Filipino for <i><a href="/wiki/GMA_Balita" title="GMA Balita">GMA Balita</a></i> in 1986 and <a href="/wiki/Mel_Tiangco" title="Mel Tiangco">Mel Tiangco</a> was the first late-night sole anchor for <i><a href="/wiki/Frontpage:_Ulat_ni_Mel_Tiangco" title="Frontpage: Ulat ni Mel Tiangco">Frontpage</a></i> in 1999. During the time of <a href="/wiki/Martial_law_in_the_Philippines" title="Martial law in the Philippines">Martial Law</a> by then-President <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_Marcos" title="Ferdinand Marcos">Ferdinand Marcos</a>, GMA was the first to broadcast an hourly news bulletin program. <i><a href="/wiki/GMA_News_Roundup" title="GMA News Roundup">GMA News Roundup</a></i> aired from 1974 to 1976. The program was later replaced by <i><a href="/wiki/GMA_News_Digest" title="GMA News Digest">GMA News Digest</a></i> in 1976, <i><a href="/wiki/GMA_News_Live" title="GMA News Live">GMA News Live</a></i> in 1987, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GMA_Flash_Report" title="GMA Flash Report">GMA Flash Report</a></i> in 2002, and most recently, <i><a href="/wiki/GMA_News_Update" title="GMA News Update">GMA News Update</a></i> in 2016.
</p><p>The station was also the first to use a ticker for breaking news and traffic information. In 1995, GMA News was the first to use <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electronic_News_Gathering" title="Electronic News Gathering">Electronic News Gathering</a> Vans in the country. In 2004, it regularly began to use Satellite News Gathering facilities to reach more remote areas. GMA News also covered major events in the country such as the <a href="/wiki/World_Youth_Day_1995" title="World Youth Day 1995">World Youth Day 1995</a>, <a href="/wiki/2000_Today" title="2000 Today">2000 Today</a> and <a href="/wiki/World_Meeting_of_Families" title="World Meeting of Families">2003 World Meeting of Families</a>.
</p><p>In 2006, GMA News was praised by the former President <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gloria_Macapagal-Arroyo" title="Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo">Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo</a> for its news coverage, and said that it was the reason for the network's high ratings. In the 2006 SWS Media Trust survey, GMA News garnered 60% of public trust, second only to rival <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ABS-CBN_(television_network)" title="ABS-CBN (television network)">ABS-CBN</a> with 68%. But a similar Pulse Asia survey shows that GMA News is the more credible in the country and to key demographics. <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.journal.com.ph/index.php?page=news&amp;id=11709&amp;sid=6&amp;urldate=2006-09-10" rel="nofollow">[1]</a>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_Affairs_division">Public Affairs division</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=GMA_News_and_Public_Affairs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Public Affairs division">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:GMAPublicAffairs2015Logo.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="500" decoding="async" height="200" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/GMAPublicAffairs2015Logo.jpg/200px-GMAPublicAffairs2015Logo.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/GMAPublicAffairs2015Logo.jpg/300px-GMAPublicAffairs2015Logo.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/GMAPublicAffairs2015Logo.jpg/400px-GMAPublicAffairs2015Logo.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:GMAPublicAffairs2015Logo.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>GMA Public Affairs logo (2015)</div></div></div>
<p>GMA’s Public Affairs division was established in 1987<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gmapa20_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-gmapa20-1">[1]</a></sup> when Tina Monzon-Palma, then head of GMA News, recognized that a 30-minute newscast was not adequate and sufficient to inform the general Filipino public on what is happening to the recently established <a href="/wiki/Corazon_Aquino" title="Corazon Aquino">Aquino</a> government after the historic <a href="/wiki/People_Power_Revolution" title="People Power Revolution">People Power Revolution</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> in February 1986.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The public affairs division started with five news personnel including Marissa La Torre Flores (now handles GMA News and Public Affairs as senior vice president) and held office inside the cameramen’s locker room before moving into the state-of-the-art GMA Network Center with no experience, equipment, camera and an improvised set broadcasting at the old GMA building in EDSA with only a passion-to-work attitude.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gmapa20_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-gmapa20-1">[1]</a></sup> Now with more than 500 news personnel—here and abroad—and producing 16 of the most awarded programs on Philippine television today.
</p><p><i><a href="/wiki/Weekend_with_Velez" title="Weekend with Velez">Weekend with Velez</a></i> was the first network-produced public affairs program on GMA, afterward renamed to <i>Velez This Week</i> and was hosted by Jose Mari Velez.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gmapa20_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-gmapa20-1">[1]</a></sup> Later that year it was joined by other public affairs shows such as <i>Firing Line with Teddy Benigno</i> (later on hosted by Oscar Orbos); <i>View Point with Dong Puno</i>; <i>Straight from the Shoulder</i> hosted by Louie Beltran and <i>The Probe Team</i> by Che-Che Lazaro and <i>Issues and Answers</i> hosted by Art Borjal.
</p><p>From a makeshift and improvised set, the once GMA News garnered several honors and recognitions from local and international award-giving bodies, including two gold medals in the New York Festivals and their first <a href="/wiki/Peabody_Award" title="Peabody Award">Peabody Award</a> in 1999,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> one of the most distinguished merits in the broadcast industry, the only one awarded to an Asian country. The first Peabody was given for <i>Kidney for Sale</i>, an investigative work on the selling of kidneys in a depressed area along the coast of <a href="/wiki/Manila_Bay" title="Manila Bay">Manila Bay</a>. The award also recognizes Marissa Flores as the executive producer and a team of producers, writers, directors and reporters, as well as Jessica Soho, Michelle Seva-Recto, <a href="/wiki/Jay_Taruc" title="Jay Taruc">Jay Taruc</a>, Leogarda Sanchez and Rowel Cornejo, Melchor Quintos and Gregg Gonzales.
</p><p>When the Philippine longest-running noontime show, <i><a href="/wiki/Eat_Bulaga!" title="Eat Bulaga!">Eat Bulaga!</a></i>, celebrated its silver anniversary in 2004, Public Affairs co-produced <i>Eat Bulaga!: Silver Special</i> with the noontime program's production company, <a href="/wiki/TAPE_Inc." title="TAPE Inc.">TAPE Inc.</a> <a href="/wiki/Arnold_Clavio" title="Arnold Clavio">Arnold Clavio</a> hosted the program with his fellow <i><a href="/wiki/Unang_Hirit" title="Unang Hirit">Unang Hirit</a></i> host <a href="/wiki/Rhea_Santos" title="Rhea Santos">Rhea Santos</a>. Clavio was a contestant in a former segment of the noontime show.
</p><p>Coinciding with its 20th anniversary in broadcasting excellence, GMA News and Public Affairs aired a documentary entitled <i>20: Dalawampung Taon ng GMA Public Affairs</i> (<i>20: Twenty Years of GMA Public Affairs</i>) on October 28, 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>In the recent years, GMA Public Affairs has started producing scripted drama serials that usually tackles various issues that affect the Filipino populace.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accolades">Accolades</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=GMA_News_and_Public_Affairs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Accolades">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>GMA Network's then flagship newscast, <i><a href="/wiki/Saksi" title="Saksi">Saksi</a></i>, won the 1999 <a href="/wiki/Asian_Television_Awards" title="Asian Television Awards">Asian Television Awards</a> and 2002 New York Festival awards for Best Newscast.
</p><p>In 1999, <a href="/wiki/Peabody_Award" title="Peabody Award">George Foster Peabody Award</a> recognized the documentaries "Kidneys for Sale" and "Kamao" by Jessica Soho and the <i><a href="/wiki/I-Witness" title="I-Witness">I-Witness</a></i> team and Jay Taruc’s child labor story on <i><a href="/wiki/Brigada_Siete" title="Brigada Siete">Brigada Siete</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-peabody_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-peabody-6">[6]</a></sup> On December 9, 2003 the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Representatives_of_the_Philippines" title="House of Representatives of the Philippines">Philippine House of Representatives</a> 12th Congress commended GMA News and Public Affairs through Resolution 787<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> authored by Rep. Rodolfo Plaza for its efforts in uplifting the standards of the Philippine broadcast industry by receiving the first Peabody Award for television news awarded to an Asian country in 1999.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2010, <a href="/wiki/Kara_David" title="Kara David">Kara David</a>'s <i>I-Witness</i> documentary, "Ambulansiyang de Paa," won GMA News and Public Affairs second Peabody Award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-peabody_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-peabody-6">[6]</a></sup> GMA News TV's documentary program <i><a href="/wiki/Reel_Time" title="Reel Time">Reel Time</a>'</i>s "Salat" episode received a Peabody Award in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In 2014, Peabody awards recognized GMA News' <i><a href="/wiki/State_of_the_Nation_with_Jessica_Soho" title="State of the Nation with Jessica Soho">State of the Nation with Jessica Soho</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/24_Oras" title="24 Oras">24 Oras</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Saksi" title="Saksi">Saksi</a></i>, <i>24 Oras Weekend</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Kapuso_Mo,_Jessica_Soho" title="Kapuso Mo, Jessica Soho">Kapuso Mo, Jessica Soho</a></i> for its coverage of the <a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Haiyan" title="Typhoon Haiyan">Super Typhoon Yolanda (Haiyan)</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>
