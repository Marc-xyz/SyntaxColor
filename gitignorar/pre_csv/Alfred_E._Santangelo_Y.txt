	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Alfred E. Santangelo</h1>

<p><b>Alfred Edward Santangelo</b> (June 4, 1912 – March 30, 1978) was an American lawyer and politician from <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alfred_E._Santangelo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Santangelo was born on June 4, 1912, in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>. He graduated from <a href="/wiki/Curtis_High_School" title="Curtis High School">Curtis High School</a>, <a href="/wiki/Staten_Island" title="Staten Island">Staten Island</a>. He graduated from <a href="/wiki/City_College_of_New_York" title="City College of New York">City College of New York</a> where he joined <a href="/wiki/Alpha_Phi_Delta" title="Alpha Phi Delta">Alpha Phi Delta</a> in 1935, and <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Law_School" title="Columbia Law School">Columbia Law School</a> in 1938.
</p><p>He was a member of the <a href="/wiki/New_York_State_Senate" title="New York State Senate">New York State Senate</a> (22nd D.) from 1947 to 1950, sitting in the <a href="/wiki/166th_New_York_State_Legislature" title="166th New York State Legislature">166th</a> and <a href="/wiki/167th_New_York_State_Legislature" title="167th New York State Legislature">167th New York State Legislatures</a>. In 1950, he ran for re-election, but was defeated by Republican <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=William_J._Bianchi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="William J. Bianchi (page does not exist)">William J. Bianchi</a>.
</p><p>Santangelo was again a member of the State Senate from 1953 to 1956, sitting in the <a href="/wiki/169th_New_York_State_Legislature" title="169th New York State Legislature">169th</a> and <a href="/wiki/170th_New_York_State_Legislature" title="170th New York State Legislature">170th New York State Legislatures</a>.
</p><p>He was elected as a <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">Democrat</a> to the <a href="/wiki/85th_United_States_Congress" title="85th United States Congress">85th</a>, <a href="/wiki/86th_United_States_Congress" title="86th United States Congress">86th</a> and <a href="/wiki/87th_United_States_Congress" title="87th United States Congress">87th United States Congresses</a>, holding office from January 3, 1957, to January 3, 1963. After redistricting, due to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Census,_1960" title="United States Census, 1960">1960 Census</a>, Santangelo ran in November 1962 for re-election in <a href="/wiki/New_York%27s_24th_congressional_district" title="New York's 24th congressional district">New York's 24th congressional district</a>, but was defeated by Republican <a href="/wiki/Paul_A._Fino" title="Paul A. Fino">Paul A. Fino</a>.
</p><p>He died on March 30, 1978, in <a href="/wiki/Orlando,_Florida" title="Orlando, Florida">Orlando, Florida</a>.
</p>
