	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Daniel Morar</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Daniel Marius Morar</b> (born August 15, 1966) is a Romanian jurist, who was Chief Prosecutor of the <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romanian</a> <a href="/wiki/National_Anticorruption_Directorate" title="National Anticorruption Directorate">National Anticorruption Directorate</a> (DNA), the agency responsible for investigating, preventing and prosecuting <a href="/wiki/Political_corruption" title="Political corruption">corruption</a>-related offenses. The National Anticorruption Directorate under Morar's leadership is credited as one of the pivotal actors in Romania's <a href="/wiki/Anti-corruption" title="Anti-corruption">anti-corruption</a> fight. Since 2013 he has been a supreme judge on Romania's <a href="/wiki/Constitutional_Court_of_Romania" title="Constitutional Court of Romania">Constitutional Court</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Daniel_Morar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since September 2001, he has indicted nine <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chamber_of_Deputies_of_Romania" title="Chamber of Deputies of Romania">deputies</a>, three Secretaries of State and former <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Romania" title="Prime Minister of Romania">Prime Minister</a> <a href="/wiki/Adrian_N%C4%83stase" title="Adrian Năstase">Adrian Năstase</a>.
</p><p>As Chief of the National Anticorruption Directorate, he started re-opening files that had been previously closed by the former DNA management. The Năstase case was one of those. Morar's policy is to work on all the "shadow zones" that led the <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">European Commission</a> to label the fight against the corruption as one of the conditions Romania had to fulfill in order to accede to the European Union. While important steps have been made in this direction, the clearance of the old corruption and bribery involved personnel is compulsory. 
</p>
