<p><b>Guy Martin</b> (born 3 February 1957) is a <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> <a href="/wiki/Chef" title="Chef">chef</a>, who earned three stars from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guide_Michelin" title="Guide Michelin">Guide Michelin</a>. He is currently working the restaurant <i><a href="/wiki/Le_Grand_V%C3%A9four" title="Le Grand Véfour">Le Grand Véfour</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guy_Martin_(chef)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Self-taught, he began his career as a <a href="/wiki/Pizzaiolo" title="Pizzaiolo">pizzaiolo</a> at age 17. He received his first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Michelin_star" title="Michelin star">Michelin star</a> in 1984 while he had been a chef for only six months and they were only three people in his team. He then became head chef of the restaurant <i>Le Grand Vefour</i> that received three Michelin stars (reduced to two stars in 2008).
</p><p>In 2010 was chosen to prepare the celebratory meal of the annexation of <a href="/wiki/Savoie" title="Savoie">Savoie</a> to France, held at the Château of <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Savoy" title="Duchy of Savoy">the dukes of Savoie</a> in <a href="/wiki/Chamb%C3%A9ry" title="Chambéry">Chambéry</a> and cooked for the former French President <a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Sarkozy" title="Nicolas Sarkozy">Nicolas Sarkozy</a> himself.
</p><p>Since 2011, he has hosted the television program <i>Épicerie fine</i>, for two seasons of 35 episodes, in which he travels to discover the origin of the various foods and gives tips to prepare them. The program is broadcast on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TV5_Monde" title="TV5 Monde">TV5 Monde</a>, Cuisine TV and Voyage et Campagnes TV.
</p>
