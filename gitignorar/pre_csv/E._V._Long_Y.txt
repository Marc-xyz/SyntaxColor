<p><b>Earl Van Meter Long</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> (July 11, 1885 – January 28, 1941)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/College_football" title="College football">college football</a> coach.  His career record was 7–17–1.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_family">Early life and family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=E._V._Long&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Long was born on July 11, 1885, in <a href="/wiki/Maroa,_Illinois" title="Maroa, Illinois">Maroa, Illinois</a>, to Silaws and Mary Jane Long.  He married Jessie Lois Brown, of <a href="/wiki/Canton,_Illinois" title="Canton, Illinois">Canton, Illinois</a>, on July 10, 1911.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coaching_career">Coaching career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=E._V._Long&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Coaching career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Richmond">Richmond</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=E._V._Long&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Richmond">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Long was the 17th head football coach at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Richmond" title="University of Richmond">University of Richmond</a> and he held that position for the 1910 season.  His coaching record at Richmond was 2–5–1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wichita_State">Wichita State</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=E._V._Long&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Wichita State">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After leaving Richmond, Long was named the eighth head football coach at Fairmount College—now known as <a href="/wiki/Wichita_State_University" title="Wichita State University">Wichita State University</a>—and he held that position for two seasons, from 1912 until 1913.  His coaching record at Fairmount was 6–11.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
