<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Nigel_Shafran,_Brighton,_2016.jpg"><img alt="Nigel Shafran, Brighton, 2016.jpg" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="4046" data-file-width="2697" decoding="async" height="330" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Nigel_Shafran%2C_Brighton%2C_2016.jpg/220px-Nigel_Shafran%2C_Brighton%2C_2016.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Nigel_Shafran%2C_Brighton%2C_2016.jpg/330px-Nigel_Shafran%2C_Brighton%2C_2016.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Nigel_Shafran%2C_Brighton%2C_2016.jpg/440px-Nigel_Shafran%2C_Brighton%2C_2016.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Nigel_Shafran,_Brighton,_2016.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div></div></div></div><p><b>Nigel Shafran</b> (born 1964) is a photographer<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jobey-guardian_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-jobey-guardian-2">[2]</a></sup> and artist. His work has been exhibited at <a href="/wiki/Tate" title="Tate">Tate</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Victoria_and_Albert_Museum" title="Victoria and Albert Museum">Victoria and Albert Museum</a>. In the 1980s Shafran worked as a fashion photographer,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-constable-independent_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-constable-independent-3">[3]</a></sup> before turning to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fine_art_photography" title="Fine art photography">fine art photography</a>. Talking to <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> journalist Sarah Philips, Shafran described his work as, "a build-up of images, often in sequences. There is a connection between them all. Basically, I'm a one-trick pony: it's all life and death and that's it."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-phillips-guardian_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-phillips-guardian-4">[4]</a></sup>
