		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">This article is about <a href="/wiki/%C3%96sters_IF" title="Östers IF">Östers IF</a>'s former <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey" title="Ice hockey">ice hockey</a> department. <p><b>Östers IF</b> ("Östers Idrottsförening", or simply "Öster") competed in ice hockey until 1971, when their ice hockey department merged with the hockey department of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=V%C3%A4xj%C3%B6_IK&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Växjö IK (page does not exist)">Växjö IK</a> to form <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A4xj%C3%B6_HC" title="Växjö HC">Växjö HC</a>.  During its existence, Öster's hockey club managed many years<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (August 2013)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup> of play in the upper tiers of <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey_in_Sweden" title="Ice hockey in Sweden">Swedish ice hockey</a>, including one season (<a href="/wiki/1963%E2%80%9364_Swedish_Division_I_season" title="1963–64 Swedish Division I season">1963–64</a>) in Sweden's top league, at the time called <a href="/wiki/Hockeyettan" title="Hockeyettan">Division 1</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
