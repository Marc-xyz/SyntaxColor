<p>The <b>Ondava</b> is a 146.5 km long <a href="/wiki/River" title="River">river</a> in <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovakia</a>, the northern source river of the <a href="/wiki/Bodrog" title="Bodrog">Bodrog</a>. Its source is in the <a href="/wiki/Low_Beskids" title="Low Beskids">Low Beskids</a> (Eastern <a href="/wiki/Carpathian_Mountains" title="Carpathian Mountains">Carpathian Mountains</a>), near the village <a href="/wiki/Ni%C5%BEn%C3%A1_Polianka" title="Nižná Polianka">Nižná Polianka</a>, close to the border with <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>. The Ondava flows south through the towns <a href="/wiki/Svidn%C3%ADk" title="Svidník">Svidník</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stropkov" title="Stropkov">Stropkov</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trhovi%C5%A1te" title="Trhovište">Trhovište</a>, and through the <a href="/wiki/Ondavsk%C3%A1_Highlands" title="Ondavská Highlands">Ondavská Highlands</a>.
</p><p>Near the village <a href="/wiki/Cejkov" title="Cejkov">Cejkov</a>, the Ondava joins the <a href="/wiki/Latorica" title="Latorica">Latorica</a> and forms the <a href="/wiki/Bodrog" title="Bodrog">Bodrog</a> river, itself a tributary of the <a href="/wiki/Tisza" title="Tisza">Tisza</a>.  The Ondava river is 44% regulated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
