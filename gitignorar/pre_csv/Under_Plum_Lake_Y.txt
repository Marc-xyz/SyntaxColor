<p><i><b>Under Plum Lake</b></i> is a children's <a href="/wiki/Adventure_fiction" title="Adventure fiction">adventure</a> novel by <a href="/wiki/Lionel_Davidson" title="Lionel Davidson">Lionel Davidson</a>, first published in 1980.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Under_Plum_Lake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A young boy, Barry, explores a cave on the <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a> coast and discovers a highly advanced subterranean civilization called Egon. Located somewhere deep beneath Earth's oceans, Egon is unknown and inaccessible to humans unless an Egonian chooses to bring them there. On all such occasions, the human being's memories of Egon are erased and replaced with false memories before being returned to the surface.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Under_Plum_Lake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Kirkus_Reviews" title="Kirkus Reviews">Kirkus Reviews</a></i> characterised it as "a little tale, somewhere between fantasy and science fiction, that's less-than-magical yet more than nicely told", written by an author "whose oddly conceived books are usually--like this one--less than fully satisfying."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
