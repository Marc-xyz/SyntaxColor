<p><b>Shelton Woolright</b> (born 4 April 1979) is a drummer, musician and photographer. Born and raised in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Auckland,_New_Zealand" title="Auckland, New Zealand">Auckland</a>, he became the drummer of <a href="/wiki/Alternative_rock" title="Alternative rock">alternative rock</a> band <a href="/wiki/Blindspott" title="Blindspott">Blindspott</a> in 1997, before leaving the group in 2008. Later that year, Woolright formed the group <a href="/wiki/I_Am_Giant" title="I Am Giant">I Am Giant</a> in the United Kingdom, before the group returned to New Zealand to produce their music career. Woolright launched his solo project Deadbeat in November 2017.
</p><p>In March 2015, it was announced that Woolright would become a judge on the <a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(New_Zealand_series_2)" title="The X Factor (New Zealand series 2)">second series</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(New_Zealand_TV_series)" title="The X Factor (New Zealand TV series)">The X Factor</a></i>, replacing the sacked <a href="/wiki/Willy_Moon" title="Willy Moon">Willy Moon</a>. He mentored <a href="/wiki/Brendon_Thomas_and_The_Vibes" title="Brendon Thomas and The Vibes">Brendon Thomas and The Vibes</a> to the final, where they finished in third place. <i>The X Factor</i> was later cancelled following the airing of the second series.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shelton_Woolright&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Woolright was born in New Zealand. He is of Tongan and French Canadian heritage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVJudge_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-TVJudge-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Woolright was the original drummer for the New Zealand band Blindspott whose 2002 debut album reached No 1 and went platinum in New Zealand in its first week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Blindspott broke up in 2007, although Woolright retained an interest in the band. On 15 May 2010, it was announced on <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> that Blindspott had reunited and were writing new material. As Woolright was not involved this eventually ended with him taking legal action. On 5 March 2011, the Blindspott lead singer Damian Alexander announced it would be releasing its new single <i>From The Blind Spot</i> under the name <a href="/wiki/Blacklistt" title="Blacklistt">Blacklistt</a> for legal reasons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NZ_Herald_10710415_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-NZ_Herald_10710415-3">[3]</a></sup> Entertainment lawyer Mick Sinclair was handling the case for Blindspott.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NZ_Herald_10716773_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-NZ_Herald_10716773-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_career">Later career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shelton_Woolright&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Later career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="I_Am_Giant">I Am Giant</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shelton_Woolright&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: I Am Giant">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following his departure from <a href="/wiki/Blindspott" title="Blindspott">Blindspott</a>, Woolright formed the band <a href="/wiki/I_Am_Giant" title="I Am Giant">I Am Giant</a> in 2008 with <a href="/wiki/Paul_Matthews_(musician)" title="Paul Matthews (musician)">Paul Matthews</a>, Ed Martin and Max O'Donnell. The band currently consists of Woolright, Matthews and Aja Timu.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Deadbeat">Deadbeat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shelton_Woolright&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Deadbeat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Woolright launched new solo project Deadbeat in November 2017. Deadbeat is a high energy drum DJ set, which involves Woolright layering live drums over a mashup of the biggest party tunes on the planet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
Deadbeat is booked for gigs in upcoming 2018, including New Zealand festivals Rhythm and Vines, and Homegrown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Photography">Photography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shelton_Woolright&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Photography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 14 March Shelton put on exhibition of his photography <i>L’Art reflète la vie</i> at the Viaduct in Auckland. The Auckland exhibition was to raise funds for Cure Kids, a child cancer sponsor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He had first put his photography on display in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> at The Hub, 5 rue Montorgueil on 12 February 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVJudge_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-TVJudge-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_X_Factor"><i>The X Factor</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shelton_Woolright&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: The X Factor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In March 2015, it was announced that Woolright would replace the sacked <a href="/wiki/Willy_Moon" title="Willy Moon">Willy Moon</a> on the <a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(New_Zealand_series_2)" title="The X Factor (New Zealand series 2)">second series</a> of <a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(New_Zealand_TV_series)" title="The X Factor (New Zealand TV series)">the New Zealand version of <i>The X Factor</i></a>, along with <a href="/wiki/Natalie_Bassingthwaighte" title="Natalie Bassingthwaighte">Natalie Bassingthwaighte</a> replacing Moon's wife, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Natalia_Kills" title="Natalia Kills">Natalia Kills</a>, who was also sacked from the show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Woolright mentored <a href="/wiki/Brendon_Thomas_and_The_Vibes" title="Brendon Thomas and The Vibes">Brendon Thomas and The Vibes</a> to the final where they finished in third place.
</p>
