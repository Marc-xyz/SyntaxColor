<p><b>960 Music Group</b> (also known as <b>960 Music</b>), headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Lagos" title="Lagos">Lagos</a>, is a <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigerian</a> record company, content owner, and artist management firm focused on providing diverse audio and visual entertainment across Nigeria. The company is headed by Mark Redguard.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Independent record labels under 960 Music include Hypertek Digital (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2face_Idibia" title="2face Idibia">2 Face</a>, <a href="/wiki/Victor_Uwaifo" title="Victor Uwaifo">Sir Victor Uwaifo</a>, Rocksteady and <a href="/wiki/Dammy_Krane" title="Dammy Krane">Dammy Krane</a>), Aristokrat Records (Pucado, Kamar, Ozone, Mojeed and Leriq) and Ultima Productions (<a href="/wiki/Geoffrey_Oji" title="Geoffrey Oji">Geoffrey Oji</a>). On 19 November 2014, 960 Music Group announced the addition of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Password_(Nigerian_music_producer)" title="Password (Nigerian music producer)">Password</a> to the label on a management and promotions deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=960_Music_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>960 Music Group was founded in 2013. It is funded by Rio Partners, and is the first music group in Nigeria to acquire different music labels and bring them together to form a big diversified group. 960 Music is considered as part of the "new school" evolution of African Music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In 2013, 960 Music Group acquired a 40 percent stake in Hypertek Records and Aristokrat Records, leading 960 Music to be listed on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nigeria_Entertainment_Today" title="Nigeria Entertainment Today">Nigeria Entertainment Today</a>'</i>s list of the "65 People, Places, Events And Things To Watch Out For In 2014".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In mid 2013, Nigerian singer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2face_Idibia" title="2face Idibia">2face Idibia</a> officially signalled the end of his former label, Hypertek Entertainment, and launched a new imprint (<a href="/wiki/Hypertek_Digital" title="Hypertek Digital">Hypertek Digital</a>) under 960 Music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>On 29 November 2013, 960 Music Group, through one of its subsidiaries Hypertek Digital, signed <a href="/wiki/Victor_Uwaifo" title="Victor Uwaifo">Sir Victor Uwaifo</a>. Uwaifo has over five hundred songs to his name, and was the first African to win a Golden Record. This move was to show that 960 Music is in the market to push through "old-school" acts whose relevance transcend the generational gap.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Burna_Boy" title="Burna Boy">Burna Boy</a> also released his music video "Na So E Suppose Be" under the label.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Partnership_with_Ultima_Productions">Partnership with Ultima Productions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=960_Music_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Partnership with Ultima Productions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 27 October 2014, 960 Music announced a partnership with Ultima Productions aligning the two companies’ interests in driving digital growth, breaking new music barriers, and creating new marketing opportunities for MTN's <i><a href="/wiki/Project_Fame_West_Africa" title="Project Fame West Africa">Project Fame West Africa</a></i> Season 7 finalists. The partnership would initially focus on the A&amp;R, promotion, marketing, distribution and monetization of the Season 7 winner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> It would also provide winners the opportunity to gain real-world experience after the show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>Mark Redguard, CEO of 960 Music Group, remarked that he was excited to work with one of the biggest talent producing shows in Nigeria and firmly believes that the partnership would be one of the most fruitful partnerships the Nigerian Music Industry had ever seen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Redguard continued:
</p>

<p>960 Music Group, in conjunction with Ultima Productions, has commenced filming a reality TV show with <a href="/wiki/Geoffrey_Oji" title="Geoffrey Oji">Geoffrey Oji</a>. The show, titled <i>Project Fame Unplugged</i>, is a Behind the Scenes television series designed to showcase the making of the Geoffrey music brand, and get all access to his entire creative process.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p><p>Under Ultima Productions/960 Music, MTN Project Fame, West Africa Season 7 winner, Geoffrey Oji, released his debut single titled 'Bursting My Brain'. The song was very well received by his old and new fans off the show<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Best_of_MTN_Project_Fame_7.0"><i>Best of MTN Project Fame 7.0</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=960_Music_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Best of MTN Project Fame 7.0">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In February 2015, 960 Music Group released the compilation album by finalists of season 7 of <a href="/wiki/MTN_Group" title="MTN Group">MTN</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Project_Fame_West_Africa" title="Project Fame West Africa">Project Fame West Africa</a></i>. The album, which was executive produced by Mark Redguard and <a href="/wiki/Password_(record_producer)" title="Password (record producer)">Password</a>, consists of 13 songs produced by Password, <a href="/wiki/Cobhams_Asuquo" title="Cobhams Asuquo">Cobhams Asuquo</a>, Wilson Joel, Royal Priest and Prodo. The album was included in the March edition of <i>MyStreetz</i> magazine, and is being given away for free through e-commerce retailer, <a href="/wiki/Konga.com" title="Konga.com">Konga.com</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p>
