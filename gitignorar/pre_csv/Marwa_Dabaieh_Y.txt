	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Marwa Dabaieh</h1>

<p><b>Marwa Dabaieh</b> is an Egyptian architect and <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">professor</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environmental" title="Environmental">Environmental</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sustainable" title="Sustainable">Sustainable</a> <a href="/wiki/Architecture" title="Architecture">Architecture</a> at the Department of Architecture at <a href="/wiki/Aalborg_University" title="Aalborg University">Aalborg University</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>She is a BioGeometry® practitioner and expert architect in <a href="/wiki/Zero_emission" title="Zero emission">Zero emission</a> and passive earthen buildings. Since 2001, she has worked in the field of sustainable architectural conservation, <a href="/wiki/Environmental_design" title="Environmental design">Environmental design</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Energy-efficient_building_design" title="Energy-efficient building design">Energy-efficient building</a>s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marwa_Dabaieh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2011, she was awarded her doctoral degree <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PhD" title="PhD">PhD</a>. in <a href="/wiki/Vernacular_architecture" title="Vernacular architecture">Vernacular architecture</a> from <a href="/wiki/Lund_University" title="Lund University">Lund University</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>.
</p><p>She received her <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor's degree">Bachelor's degree</a> in architecture in 2001 from <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> followed by a <a href="/wiki/Master%27s_degree" title="Master's degree">Master's degree</a> in <a href="/wiki/Environmental_planning" title="Environmental planning">Environmental planning</a> and design in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
<br/>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career_and_achievements">Career and achievements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marwa_Dabaieh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career and achievements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She received the Swedish Elna Bengtssons foundation prize for scientific research in 2012 for her <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PhD" title="PhD">PhD</a>. project.
</p><p><b>Marwa Dabaieh</b> applies trans-disciplinary approaches in her research work following human-centered participatory design methods. She published four books on vernacular architecture beside over 50 publications in the fields of energy-efficient buildings, passive design, participatory action research, sustainable conservation, environmental design, vernacular architecture, and BioGeometry®. As a practitioner, she participated in several design projects in <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>, the <a href="/wiki/MENA" title="MENA">MENA</a> region and <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>. 
</p><p>Marwa started her professorship position at <a href="/wiki/Aalborg_University" title="Aalborg University">Aalborg University</a> in 2018 as a full professor of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environmental" title="Environmental">Environmental</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sustainable" title="Sustainable">Sustainable</a> <a href="/wiki/Architecture" title="Architecture">Architecture</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>She is also a reader (<a href="/wiki/Docent" title="Docent">Docent</a>) in <a href="/wiki/Zero_emission" title="Zero emission">Zero emission</a> buildings at <a href="/wiki/Malm%C3%B6_University" title="Malmö University">Malmö University</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
