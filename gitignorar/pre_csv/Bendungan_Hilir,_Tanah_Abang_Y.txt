<p><b>Bendungan Hilir</b> (or abbreviated <b>"Benhil"</b>) is an <a href="/wiki/Administrative_village" title="Administrative village">administrative village</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Tanah_Abang" title="Tanah Abang">Tanah Abang</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Districts_of_Indonesia" title="Districts of Indonesia">district</a> of <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>. It has a population of 20,025 people.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pejompongan_and_Bendungan_Hilir_neighborhood">Pejompongan and Bendungan Hilir neighborhood</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bendungan_Hilir,_Tanah_Abang&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Pejompongan and Bendungan Hilir neighborhood">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Pejompongan" title="Pejompongan">Pejompongan</a> and Bendungan Hilir were two residential neighborhoods located to the west side of <a href="/wiki/Krukut_River" title="Krukut River">Krukut River</a> and bounded by Penjernihan Road, <a href="/wiki/Jalan_Jenderal_Gatot_Subroto" title="Jalan Jenderal Gatot Subroto">Jalan Jenderal Gatot Subroto</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sudirman_Road" title="Sudirman Road">Sudirman Road</a>. The neighborhoods were located to the north of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gelora_Bung_Karno" title="Gelora Bung Karno">Gelora Bung Karno</a> sport complex. Pejompongan and Bendungan Hilir were among the first neighborhood developed after independence period.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMerrillees201519_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMerrillees201519-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>With the planning and development of Gatot Subroto Road in the 1950s, 25 hectares of land at Pejompongan and 6 hectares land at Bendungan Hilir were opened in 1952 for the development of a new housing. This housing were developed particularly for civil servants who needed to stay in Jakarta to do their duty.
</p><p>The other 15 hectares of land at Pejompongan was opened for a water purification plant that was completed in 1957 (hence the name of the road, <i>Penjernihan</i> ("purification") road. In the 1960s, both the northern part of Pejompongan and Bendungan Hilir had already been established.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMerrillees201519_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMerrillees201519-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Design of both neighborhoods follow the <a href="/wiki/Garden_city_movement" title="Garden city movement">Garden City</a> principle.
</p>
