<p>The <b>Pinka</b> is a river in Central Europe with a length of approximately 100 km. Its source is located in <a href="/wiki/Styria" title="Styria">Styria</a>, eastern <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>, next to the provincial border of <a href="/wiki/Burgenland" title="Burgenland">Burgenland</a>. It passes into Hungary between the villages of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Burg_(Burgenland)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Burg (Burgenland) (page does not exist)">Burg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fels%C5%91csat%C3%A1r" title="Felsőcsatár">Felsőcsatár</a>, and crosses the Austrian-Hungarian border five times. Further it flows into the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%A1ba" title="Rába">Rába</a> river near <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6rmend" title="Körmend">Körmend</a>. Its main tributary is the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Strem_(river)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Strem (river) (page does not exist)">Strem</a>.
</p><p>Important towns on its course are <a href="/wiki/Pinkafeld" title="Pinkafeld">Pinkafeld</a> (Hungarian: <i>Pinkafő</i>) and <a href="/wiki/Oberwart" title="Oberwart">Oberwart</a> (Hungarian: <i>Felsőőr</i>).
</p>
