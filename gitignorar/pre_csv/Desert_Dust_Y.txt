<p><i><b>Desert Dust</b></i> is a 1927 American <a href="/wiki/Silent_film" title="Silent film">silent</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Western_film" title="Western film">Western film</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/William_Wyler" title="William Wyler">William Wyler</a> and starring <a href="/wiki/Ted_Wells_(actor)" title="Ted Wells (actor)">Ted Wells</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lotus_Thompson" title="Lotus Thompson">Lotus Thompson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Gordon_(actor/director)" title="Bruce Gordon (actor/director)">Bruce Gordon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The film's sets were designed by the <a href="/wiki/Art_director" title="Art director">art director</a> <a href="/wiki/David_S._Garber" title="David S. Garber">David S. Garber</a>.
</p>
