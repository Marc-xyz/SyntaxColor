<p><b>Hanabusa Yoshitada</b><span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">花房 義質</span>, February 10, 1842 - July 9, 1917)</span> was a Japanese politician, diplomat and peer.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hanabusa_Yoshitada&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Yoshimoto_Hanabusa.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="566" data-file-width="372" decoding="async" height="228" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Yoshimoto_Hanabusa.JPG/150px-Yoshimoto_Hanabusa.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Yoshimoto_Hanabusa.JPG/225px-Yoshimoto_Hanabusa.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Yoshimoto_Hanabusa.JPG/300px-Yoshimoto_Hanabusa.JPG 2x" width="150"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Yoshimoto_Hanabusa.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Hanabusa Yoshitada as a young.</div></div></div>
<p>Hanabusa was the eldest son of Hanabusa Tanren, a <a href="/wiki/Samurai" title="Samurai">samurai</a> retainer of <a href="/wiki/Okayama_Domain" title="Okayama Domain">Okayama Domain</a> and the first mayor of the city of <a href="/wiki/Okayama" title="Okayama">Okayama</a>, Japan. He studied <i><a href="/wiki/Rangaku" title="Rangaku">rangaku</a></i> under the famed <a href="/wiki/Ogata_K%C5%8Dan" title="Ogata Kōan">Ogata Kōan</a>, and immediately after the <a href="/wiki/Meiji_Restoration" title="Meiji Restoration">Meiji Restoration</a> in 1867 was sent to Europe and North America as a student. On his return in 1870, he was accepted into the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Foreign_Affairs_(Japan)" title="Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Japan)">Foreign Ministry</a>. The same year, he was sent to <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a> as part of the Japanese delegation negotiating the opening of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diplomatic_relations" title="Diplomatic relations">diplomatic relations</a> between the <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Japan" title="Empire of Japan">Empire of Japan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Qing_dynasty" title="Qing dynasty">Qing dynasty</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Late_Imperial_China" title="Late Imperial China">Empire of China</a>.
</p><p>In 1872, he served as secretary to <a href="/wiki/Soejima_Taneomi" title="Soejima Taneomi">Soejima Taneomi</a> during the negotiations involving the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maria_Luz_Incident" title="Maria Luz Incident">Maria Luz Incident</a>. Soon afterwards, he was dispatched to <a href="/wiki/Saint_Petersburg" title="Saint Petersburg">Saint Petersburg</a> to assist <a href="/wiki/Enomoto_Takeaki" title="Enomoto Takeaki">Enomoto Takeaki</a> in negotiating the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Saint_Petersburg_(1875)" title="Treaty of Saint Petersburg (1875)">Treaty of Saint Petersburg (1875)</a>, which formalized the border between Japan and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Empire_of_Russia" title="Empire of Russia">Empire of Russia</a>.
</p><p>In 1877, Hanabusa was sent to <a href="/wiki/Busan" title="Busan">Busan</a> to oversee the opening of that port as per the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Japan%E2%80%93Korea_Treaty_of_1876" title="Japan–Korea Treaty of 1876">Japan–Korea Treaty of 1876</a> signed the previous year with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joseon_dynasty" title="Joseon dynasty">Joseon dynasty</a> <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a>. In 1879, Hanabusa was the first Japanese diplomat to take up residence and to establish a permanent legation in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Hanabusa was subsequently known for his involvement in the "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Imo_incident" title="Imo incident">Imo incident</a>,"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> which was a military revolt of some units of the <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korean</a> military in Seoul on July 23, 1882. While the exact causes and details of the incident remain a topic of controversy, violence did erupt,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and Hanabusa and his aides were forced to flee the legation,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iwao2314_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-iwao2314-4">[4]</a></sup>  and were rescued by a British ship, the <i>Flying Fish</i> then in port at <a href="/wiki/Incheon" title="Incheon">Chemulpo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Japanese government immediately sent Hanabusa back to Seoul, with his security ensured by four warships, three cargo ships and a battalion of armed soldiers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iwao2314_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-iwao2314-4">[4]</a></sup> Hanabusa was the chief Japanese negotiator for  the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Chemulpo" title="Treaty of Chemulpo">Treaty of Chemulpo</a>, which permitted the permanent garrison of Japanese troops in Seoul.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iwao2314_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-iwao2314-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 1882, Hanabusa was transferred from Korea to St. Petersburg as Japanese ambassador to Russia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ndl_hanabusa_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-ndl_hanabusa-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 1887, Hanabusa returned to Tokyo, where he was made a councilor to the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Household_Agency" title="Imperial Household Agency">Imperial Household Ministry</a>. In 1900, he was accorded the title of <a href="/wiki/Baron" title="Baron">baron</a> (<i>danshaku</i>) under the <i><a href="/wiki/Kazoku" title="Kazoku">kazoku</a></i> peerage system. In September 1907, his rank was elevated to <a href="/wiki/Viscount" title="Viscount">Viscount</a> (<i>shishaku</i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ndl_hanabusa_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-ndl_hanabusa-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>In December 1911, Hanabusa was made a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Privy_Council_(Japan)" title="Privy Council (Japan)">privy councillor</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ndl_hanabusa_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-ndl_hanabusa-6">[6]</a></sup> In December 1912, he became Chairman of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_Red_Cross" title="Japanese Red Cross">Japanese Red Cross</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ndl_hanabusa_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-ndl_hanabusa-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>Viscount Hanabusa died on July 9, 1917.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ndl_hanabusa_6-4"><a href="#cite_note-ndl_hanabusa-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
