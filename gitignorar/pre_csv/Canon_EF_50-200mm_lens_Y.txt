	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Canon EF 50-200mm lens</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <a href="/wiki/Canon_Inc." title="Canon Inc.">Canon</a> <b>EF 50-200mm <span style="font-style:italic;font-family:Trebuchet MS,Candara,Georgia,Calibri,Corbel,serif">f</span>/3.5-4.5</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Canon_EF_lens_mount" title="Canon EF lens mount">EF mount</a> <a href="/wiki/Normal_lens" title="Normal lens">normal</a>-to-<a href="/wiki/Telephoto_lens" title="Telephoto lens">telephoto</a> <a href="/wiki/Zoom_lens" title="Zoom lens">zoom lens</a>. It is one of the first lenses in the <a href="/wiki/Canon_EF_lens_mount" title="Canon EF lens mount">Canon EF</a> series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-canonmuseum_1st_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-canonmuseum_1st-1">[1]</a></sup> 
</p><p>In June 1988, Canon announced the <b>EF 50-200mm <span style="font-style:italic;font-family:Trebuchet MS,Candara,Georgia,Calibri,Corbel,serif">f</span>/3.5-4.5L</b> lens. This lens has very similar physical qualities compared to the EF50-200mm <span style="font-style:italic;font-family:Trebuchet MS,Candara,Georgia,Calibri,Corbel,serif">f</span>/3.5-4.5.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-canonmuseum_2nd_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-canonmuseum_2nd-2">[2]</a></sup> The main difference is that this is a <a href="/wiki/Canon_L_lens" title="Canon L lens">L lens</a>, meaning that the lens contains special lens elements which better correct for various aberrations. In the case of the EF 50-200mm 3.5-4.5 L, both an ultra-low dispersion ("UD") and a synthetic crystal fluorite ("Fluorite") were used. Aside from the different optical construction and slight variation in weight and length, the L and non-L lens were basically identical. Note that at this time period Canon did not protect its L zooms against adverse elements such as weather. Both this lens and the similar duo EF 100-300mm 5.6 and its L variant lacked weather sealing construction, sharing the same housing as their non-L counterparts. Canon did, however, supply the L versions with lens hoods and cases at the time of purchase, which were available for the non-L lenses for separate purchase.
</p>
