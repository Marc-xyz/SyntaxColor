	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Louise Cooper (financial analyst)</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Louise Cooper</b> is a British Chartered Financial Analyst, <a href="/wiki/Journalist" title="Journalist">journalist</a>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Times" title="The Times">Times</a></i> columnist, known for her work on the <a href="/wiki/BBC_World_Service" title="BBC World Service">BBC World Service</a> between 2002 and 2011 as a presenter and senior economics journalist for shows including <i><a href="/wiki/Newshour" title="Newshour">Newshour</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Europe_Today_(radio_programme)" title="Europe Today (radio programme)">Europe Today</a></i>. She writes a regular column for newspaper <i>The Times</i> and publishes the financial blog CooperCity.
</p><p>She came to the attention of the public in 2011 while working for <a href="/wiki/BGC_Partners" title="BGC Partners">BGC Partners</a> as a Senior Financial Analyst, appearing from their offices to provide opinion and economic insight on TV for major broadcasters such as <a href="/wiki/BBC_News" title="BBC News">BBC News</a>, <a href="/wiki/ITV_News" title="ITV News">ITV News</a>, Al-Jazeera, <a href="/wiki/Sky_News" title="Sky News">Sky News</a> and <a href="/wiki/CNBC" title="CNBC">CNBC</a> among others. She garnered praise for her no-nonsense style and the ability to break down complex business stories for a wider audience. Cooper is often quoted in the media, commenting on complex financial issues such as Greek debt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> billion dollar corporate mergers,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> gender equality in pay<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and the impact of finance on football<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>She has been, from time to time, a regular guest on BBC5 Live’s early morning financial programme <i><a href="/wiki/Wake_Up_to_Money" title="Wake Up to Money">Wake Up to Money</a></i>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Louise_Cooper_(financial_analyst)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cooper attended the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/City_University_Business_School" title="City University Business School">Cass Business School</a> graduating with first class honours in Business and Finance in 1992. From there she moved to <a href="/wiki/Goldman_Sachs" title="Goldman Sachs">Goldman Sachs</a> working in UK Equity Institutional Research Sales. In 1999 she moved to <a href="/wiki/The_Money_Channel" title="The Money Channel">The Money Channel</a>, working as a presenter until 2001.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In June 2002 she joined the <a href="/wiki/BBC_World_Service" title="BBC World Service">BBC World Service</a> Radio station as a Senior Business Journalist, she remained there until 2011 when she joined Lansons Communication as the Head of Lansons Live.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>In June 2011 Cooper moved to <a href="/wiki/BGC_Partners" title="BGC Partners">BGC Partners</a> as a Senior Financial Analyst, where she came to prominence on British Television, providing financial analysis from the BGC offices. Cooper featured on BBC News, Sky News, ITV, CNBC and Al Jazeera. She has also made appearances on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Daily_Politics" title="The Daily Politics">The Daily Politics</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> where she described then <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UKIP" title="UKIP">UKIP</a> leader <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Farage" title="Nigel Farage">Nigel Farage</a> as 'laughable' for his comments denying discrimination against women working in London's financial sector.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> She has also appeared on ITV's <i><a href="/wiki/Daybreak_(2010_TV_programme)" title="Daybreak (2010 TV programme)">Daybreak</a></i> as well as <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Question_Time_(TV_series)" title="Question Time (TV series)">Question Time</a></i> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/This_Week_(BBC_TV_series)" title="This Week (BBC TV series)">This Week</a></i>. In August 2011 an article was published by the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mail_on_Sunday" title="Mail on Sunday">Mail on Sunday</a></i>, calling Cooper the 'Downturn Diva' and the 'New face of the global economic downturn'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p>After leaving BGC Partners in November 2012,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Cooper published her blog CooperCity<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and has written articles for a number of newspapers and websites, including <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Daily_Mail" title="The Daily Mail">The Daily Mail</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/CNN" title="CNN">CNN</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Spectator" title="The Spectator">The Spectator</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/City_A.M." title="City A.M.">City A.M.</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> She also writes a regular column for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Times" title="The Times">The Times</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and works as a keynote speaker and host.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p>
