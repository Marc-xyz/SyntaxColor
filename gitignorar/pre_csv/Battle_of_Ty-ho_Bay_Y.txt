<p>The <b>Battle of Ty-ho Bay</b> was a significant naval engagement in 1855 involving the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> against Chinese <a href="/wiki/Piracy" title="Piracy">pirates</a>. The action off <a href="/wiki/Tai_O" title="Tai O">Tai O</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a> was to rescue captured merchant vessels, held by a fleet of armed <a href="/wiki/Junk_(ship)" title="Junk (ship)">war-junks</a>. British and American forces defeated the pirates in one of the last major battles between Chinese pirate fleets and western navies. It was also one of the first joint operations undertaken by British and American forces.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Ty-ho_Bay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Unlike the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> where piracy was largely ended by 1830, piracy in <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific" title="Pacific">Pacific</a> continued to thrive as it had for centuries. Chinese and <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> pirates constantly fought each other throughout China's coastal regions. Hundreds of pirate hideouts existed along the Chinese coast by 1855. This prompted western naval forces to fight them when they attacked shipping.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In September 1855, the pirates of Kuhlan seized four merchant vessels escorted by the <a href="/wiki/Paddle_steamer" title="Paddle steamer">paddle steamer</a> <a href="/wiki/HMS_Eaglet" title="HMS Eaglet">HMS <i>Eaglet</i></a>. The Royal Navy <a href="/wiki/Sloop-of-war" title="Sloop-of-war">sloop-of-war</a> <a href="/wiki/HMS_Rattler_(1843)" title="HMS Rattler (1843)">HMS <i>Rattler</i></a> was sent to rescue the merchantmen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-auto-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>HMS <i>Rattler</i> found the pirates in Kuhlan Harbor. When shallow water prevented her from attacking, she left to seek aid from the <i>Eaglet</i> and <a href="/wiki/USS_Powhatan_(1850)" title="USS Powhatan (1850)">USS <i>Powhatan</i></a>, a sidewheel <a href="/wiki/Steamship" title="Steamship">steam</a> <a href="/wiki/Frigate" title="Frigate">frigate</a> of the <a href="/wiki/East_India_Squadron" title="East India Squadron">East India Squadron</a> commanded by <a href="/wiki/Commodore_(United_States)" title="Commodore (United States)">Commodore</a> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=William_J._McCluney&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="William J. McCluney (page does not exist)">William J. McCluney</a>.  <i>Powhatan</i> later played a significant role during the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>.  <i>Rattler</i> was commanded by <a href="/wiki/Commodore_(Royal_Navy)" title="Commodore (Royal Navy)">Commodore</a> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=William_Fellowes&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="William Fellowes (page does not exist)">William Fellowes</a> and was manned by 180 officers and crew. The number of crew and armament of <i>Eaglet</i> is unknown. She was originally a civil vessel chartered for British naval service between 1855 and 1857, to tow British vessels through shallow water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-auto-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Battle">Battle</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Ty-ho_Bay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Battle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Taio.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="768" data-file-width="1024" decoding="async" height="150" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/Taio.png/200px-Taio.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/Taio.png/300px-Taio.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/Taio.png/400px-Taio.png 2x" width="200"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Taio.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i>Location of Tai O within Hong Kong.</i></div></div></div>
<p>On August 4, the steamer <i>Eaglet</i> arrived at Ty-ho Bay, she was towing at least six boats of different types, filled with British and American sailors and marines. Each boat was armed with a howitzer or cannon. The British first spotted a merchant junk that appeared to be fleeing the bay, so the <a href="/wiki/Pinnace_(ship%27s_boat)" title="Pinnace (ship's boat)">pinnace</a> of the <i>Rattler</i> and the <a href="/wiki/Cutter_(boat)" title="Cutter (boat)">cutter</a> of the <i>Powhatan</i> were sent to cut the junk out. Minutes later, when the pinnace and cutter disappeared from sight, the remaining British and American vessels sighted the pirate fleet, which included fourteen large junks and twenty-two smaller ones. Some 1,500 pirates crewed the vessels and they were armed with small cannons. Also in the bay were seven captured merchant ships, most of which were Chinese junks. When the Chinese sighted the approaching enemy, half of the pirate junks began to flee while the other remained behind to engage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-auto-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The pirates began a heavy fire on the British and Americans but most of the shot was not well directed and passed over the <i>Eaglet</i> and the armed boats. When the expedition was in range the boats started their return fire and six junks were sunk at this time. When the range decreased to close quarters, <i>Eaglet</i> detached the boats and they went off to board the junks. Fourteen were taken with heavy resistance and were burned just after. The seven merchant ships were also liberated but two were heavily damaged in the battle and subsequently burned as well. As result of the action, fourteen larger junks were destroyed along with six small ones while sixteen others escaped.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-auto-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>An estimated 500 pirates were killed in action, drowned, or were wounded. Around 1,000 pirates were taken prisoner. American casualties consisted of six wounded with five dead out of about 100 men, the dead were crewmen John Pepper, James A. Halsey, Isaac Coe, Landsman, S. Mullard and B. F. Addamson. The British suffered several wounded and four men killed, officer George Mitchell, and crewmen James Silvers, John Massey, and M. Oliff.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-auto-1">[1]</a></sup> The battle is largely forgotten but a monument was erected at <a href="/wiki/Happy_Valley,_Hong_Kong" title="Happy Valley, Hong Kong">Happy Valley</a> in commemoration, it was later moved to the city of <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (October 2010)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
