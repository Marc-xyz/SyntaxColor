<p><b>Taherpur</b> is a city and a notified area in <a href="/wiki/Ranaghat_subdivision" title="Ranaghat subdivision">Ranaghat subdivision</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nadia_district" title="Nadia district">Nadia district</a> in the <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">Indian</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/States_and_territories_of_India" title="States and territories of India">state</a> of <a href="/wiki/West_Bengal" title="West Bengal">West Bengal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taherpur&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nadia district is mostly alluvial plains lying to the east of <a href="/wiki/Hooghly_River" title="Hooghly River">Hooghly River</a>, locally known as Bhagirathi. The alluvial plains are cut across by such distributaries as Jalangi, Churni and Ichhamati. With these rivers getting silted up, floods are a recurring feature.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Police_station">Police station</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taherpur&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Police station">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rananghat and Taherpur police stations have jurisdiction over <a href="/wiki/Ranaghat" title="Ranaghat">Ranaghat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Birnagar" title="Birnagar">Birnagar</a>, Taherpur and <a href="/wiki/Ranaghat_I" title="Ranaghat I">Ranaghat I</a> CD Block.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The total area covered by the Taherpur police station is 154.74 km<sup>2</sup> and the population covered is 173,926 (2001 census).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taherpur&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As per the <a href="/wiki/2011_Census_of_India" title="2011 Census of India">2011 Census of India</a>, Taherpur (Notified Area + Out Growth) had a total population of 38,039, of which 19,410 (51%) were males and 18,629 (49%) were females. Population below 6 years was 3,200. The total number of literates in Taherpur was 30,166 (86.59% of the population over 6 years).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-census3-2011_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-census3-2011-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>As of 2001<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Taherpur&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup> India <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Taherpur had a population of 20,060. Males constitute 51% of the population and females 49%. Taherpur has an average literacy rate of 80%, higher than the national average of 59.5%: male literacy is 84%, and female literacy is 76%. In Taherpur, 9% of the population is under 6 years of age.
</p>
