	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Joseph-Ernst Graf Fugger von Glött</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b> Joseph-Ernst Graf Fugger von Glött </b>, since 1940: <b>Fürst Fugger von Glött</b> (October 26, 1895, <a href="/wiki/Kirchheim_in_Schwaben" title="Kirchheim in Schwaben">Kirchheim in Schwaben</a> – May 13, 1981, <a href="/wiki/Miesbach" title="Miesbach">Miesbach</a>)  was a <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> <a href="/wiki/Politician" title="Politician">politician</a> and representative of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christian_Social_Union_of_Bavaria" title="Christian Social Union of Bavaria">Christian Social Union of Bavaria</a>. He was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Bundestag" title="Bundestag">Bundestag</a> of Germany between 1949 and 1953.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> From 1954 to 1962 he was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Landtag_of_Bavaria" title="Landtag of Bavaria">Landtag of Bavaria</a>.  He is a member of the famed <a href="/wiki/Fugger" title="Fugger">Fugger</a> family, the preeminent bankers of the <a href="/wiki/Renaissance" title="Renaissance">renaissance</a> era.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joseph-Ernst_Graf_Fugger_von_Gl%C3%B6tt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Joseph-Ernst Fugger von Glött was born on 26 October 1895 in the <a class="external text" href="http://www.tourismus-unterallgaeu.de/index.php?id=1119" rel="nofollow">Kirchheim Castle</a> near <a href="/wiki/Augsburg" title="Augsburg">Augsburg</a>. He was the third child and first son of <a href="/wiki/Carl_Ernst_F%C3%BCrst_Fugger_von_Gl%C3%B6tt" title="Carl Ernst Fürst Fugger von Glött">Carl Ernst Fürst Fugger von Glött</a> and his wife Elisabeth (Gräfin von Quadt-Wykradt-Isny). As a member of the famous <a href="/wiki/Fugger" title="Fugger">Fugger</a> family, Fugger von Glött was born into wealth and power. His father gained further fame and power by serving as the president of the upper house of the Bavarian Landtag.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Fugger von Glött received a typical upbringing for a young, aristocratic country man of his time: he completed his <a href="/wiki/Abitur" title="Abitur">Abitur</a> at the elite <a href="/wiki/Gymnasium_(Germany)" title="Gymnasium (Germany)">Gymnasium</a> "<a href="/wiki/Stella_Matutina_(Jesuit_school)" title="Stella Matutina (Jesuit school)">Stella Matutina</a>," took part in <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant" title="Lieutenant">lieutenant</a> for an extremely prestigious <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">Bavarian</a> <a href="/wiki/Cavalry" title="Cavalry">cavalry</a> <a href="/wiki/Regiment" title="Regiment">regiment</a>, and completed his "<a href="/wiki/Higher_education" title="Higher education">Studium</a>" after the war at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Hohenheim" title="University of Hohenheim">University of Hohenheim</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>At the death of his father in 1940 Fugger von Glött took over the official leadership of his household.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nazi_Era">Nazi Era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joseph-Ernst_Graf_Fugger_von_Gl%C3%B6tt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Nazi Era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fugger von Glött was drafted as an <a href="/wiki/Officer_(armed_forces)" title="Officer (armed forces)">officer</a> into the <a href="/wiki/Wehrmacht" title="Wehrmacht">Wehrmacht</a> in 1939 but, because of the <a href="/wiki/Prinzenerlass" title="Prinzenerlass">Prinzenerlass</a>, was prevented from serving. After being dismissed from military service, Fugger von Glött joined the <a href="/wiki/Kreisau_Circle" title="Kreisau Circle">Kreisau Circle</a>.
</p><p>After the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/July_20_plot" title="July 20 plot">July 20 plot</a> failed, Fugger von Glött was arrested at his castle in Kirchheim on 3 September 1944 by the <a href="/wiki/Gestapo" title="Gestapo">Gestapo</a> for his role in the assassination attempt. Unlike the majority of conspirators, Fugger von Glött was sentenced to penal servitude rather than execution. He was freed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">American</a> troops in April 1945.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-War">Post-War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joseph-Ernst_Graf_Fugger_von_Gl%C3%B6tt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Post-War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After the war, Fugger von Glött returned to his hometown in order to devote himself once again to his family and chores. During this time he was responsible for the activities of the many institutions of the family, including the reconstruction and expansion of the <a href="/wiki/Fuggerei" title="Fuggerei">Fuggerei</a> in Augsburg.
</p><p>Fugger von Glött quickly began to invest himself in politics, and helped found the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Christian_Social_Union_of_Bavaria" title="Christian Social Union of Bavaria">CSU</a> in 1945/1946. He belonged to the first <a href="/wiki/Bundestag" title="Bundestag">Bundestag</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and was president of the <a class="external text" href="http://www.interparlamentarische.de/" rel="nofollow">Interparliamentary Committee</a> from 1953 until 1956. He was also a member of the <a href="/wiki/Landtag_of_Bavaria" title="Landtag of Bavaria">Landtag of Bavaria</a> from 1954 until 1962.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He assisted fellow Kreisauer Kreis survivor Rudolf Ruby in providing his castle as a venue for Ruby's <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Harmonia_Mundi" title="Deutsche Harmonia Mundi">Deutsche Harmonia Mundi</a> artists.
</p><p>He was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Merit_of_the_Federal_Republic_of_Germany" title="Order of Merit of the Federal Republic of Germany">Order of Merit of the Federal Republic of Germany</a> in 1965.
</p>
