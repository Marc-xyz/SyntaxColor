<p>The <b>Penang Island City Council</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Local_government_in_Malaysia" title="Local government in Malaysia">local government</a> that administers the <a href="/wiki/City" title="City">city</a> of <a href="/wiki/George_Town,_Penang" title="George Town, Penang">George Town</a>, which includes the entirety of <a href="/wiki/Penang_Island" title="Penang Island">Penang Island</a>. The City Council, which has jurisdiction over an area of nearly 306 km<sup>2</sup> (118 sq mi), falls under the purview of the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Penang" title="Government of Penang">Penang state government</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:2_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-:2-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Penang Island City Council is responsible for urban planning, heritage preservation, public health, sanitation, waste management, traffic management, environmental protection, building control, social and economic development, and general maintenance of urban infrastructure. In addition, the City Council, in a joint effort with <a href="/wiki/Rapid_Penang" title="Rapid Penang">Rapid Penang</a>, runs a free shuttle bus service within the heart of George Town.
</p><p>The headquarters of the Penang Island City Council is located within the <a href="/wiki/City_Hall,_Penang" title="City Hall, Penang">City Hall</a> in George Town, which had served as the seat of the George Town City Council until 1976. The City Council also has offices within <a href="/wiki/Komtar" title="Komtar">Komtar</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_tallest_buildings_in_Penang_Island" title="List of tallest buildings in Penang Island">tallest skyscraper</a> in George Town.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Penang_Island_City_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/History_of_George_Town,_Penang" title="History of George Town, Penang">History of George Town, Penang</a></div>
<p>A committee of assessors for <a href="/wiki/George_Town,_Penang" title="George Town, Penang">George Town</a> was established in 1800, making it the first local government to be established within <a href="/wiki/British_Malaya" title="British Malaya">British Malaya</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:31_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-:31-2">[2]</a></sup> The committee, which consisted of British and local Asian ratepayers, was tasked with the valuation of property within the new settlement.
</p><p>In 1857, the George Town Municipal Commission was officially established. It consisted of five members and was led by the <a href="/wiki/List_of_governors_of_Penang" title="List of governors of Penang">Resident-Councillor of Penang</a>. Three of the Municipal Commissioners were to be elected by expatriate ratepayers and Straits-born British citizens, making the Municipal Commission the first, albeit partially, elected local government within British Malaya. However, the local elections were abolished by 1913.
</p><p>In 1951, the British colonial authorities reintroduced municipal elections of nine of the fifteen municipal commissioners for George Town, the first <a href="/wiki/Municipal_council" title="Municipal council">municipal council</a> in Malaya to do so.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:8_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-:8-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:10_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-:10-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-:1-5">[5]</a></sup> For the municipal elections, George Town was divided into three wards - Tanjung, Kelawei and Jelutong. By 1956, George Town became the first <a href="/wiki/Municipality" title="Municipality">municipality</a> in Malaya to have a fully elected local government. Five wards were created to elect one councillor each year, while the President of the Municipal Council was voted from amongst the councillors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:10_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-:10-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Penang_City_Hall1.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1200" data-file-width="1600" decoding="async" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Penang_City_Hall1.JPG/220px-Penang_City_Hall1.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Penang_City_Hall1.JPG/330px-Penang_City_Hall1.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Penang_City_Hall1.JPG/440px-Penang_City_Hall1.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Penang_City_Hall1.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Built in 1903, the <a href="/wiki/City_Hall,_Penang" title="City Hall, Penang">City Hall</a> in <a href="/wiki/George_Town,_Penang" title="George Town, Penang">George Town</a> now houses the Penang Island City Council.</div></div></div>
<p>On 1 January 1957, George Town became a city by a <a href="/wiki/Royal_charter" title="Royal charter">royal charter</a> granted by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Elizabeth II of the United Kingdom">Queen Elizabeth II</a>, becoming the first city in the <a href="/wiki/Federation_of_Malaya" title="Federation of Malaya">Federation of Malaya</a>, and by extension, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>. George Town remained Malaysia's only city (other than <a href="/wiki/City_of_Singapore_(historical_entity)" title="City of Singapore (historical entity)">Singapore</a> between 1963 and 1965) until 1972, when <a href="/wiki/Kuala_Lumpur" title="Kuala Lumpur">Kuala Lumpur</a> was also granted city status. The first Mayor of George Town was <a href="/wiki/D._S._Ramanathan" title="D. S. Ramanathan">D. S. Ramanathan</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_of_Malaya" title="Labour Party of Malaya">Labour Party</a> politician.
</p><p>In 1965, the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Malaysia" title="Government of Malaysia">Malaysian federal government</a> suspended local elections as a result of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indonesian_Confrontation" title="Indonesian Confrontation">Indonesian Confrontation</a>. The George Town City Council was at the time the richest local council in the country, with annual revenue almost double that of the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Penang" title="Government of Penang">Penang state government</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In response to allegations of maladministration and misconduct, a Royal Commission of Enquiry was set up by the federal government under Senator Athi Nahappan, while the functions of the City Council were temporarily transferred to the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Chief_Ministers_of_Penang" title="List of Chief Ministers of Penang">Chief Minister of Penang</a> in 1966.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-:1-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Royal Commission cleared the George Town City Council of the allegations of corruption and recommended the restoration of municipal elections. However, this was never carried out. Instead, in 1971, the two local councils on <a href="/wiki/Penang_Island" title="Penang Island">Penang Island</a> - the George Town City Council and the Penang Island Rural District Council, the latter of which administered the rural southwest of Penang Island - were taken over by the Penang state government.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In addition, the state government, controlled at the time by <a href="/wiki/Parti_Gerakan_Rakyat_Malaysia" title="Parti Gerakan Rakyat Malaysia">Gerakan</a> and led by <a href="/wiki/Lim_Chong_Eu" title="Lim Chong Eu">Lim Chong Eu</a> as the Chief Minister, also decided to continue the suspension of Penang's local governments.
</p><p>In 1974, both the George Town City Council and the Penang Island Rural District Council were merged to form the Penang Island Municipal Council. This act also resulted in the consolidation of <a href="/wiki/Penang" title="Penang">Penang</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Local_government_in_Malaysia" title="Local government in Malaysia">local governments</a> into two local governments, each administering one halve of the state - Penang Island and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seberang_Perai_Municipal_Council" title="Seberang Perai Municipal Council">Seberang Perai</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy_over_city_status">Controversy over city status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Penang_Island_City_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Controversy over city status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/George_Town,_Penang" title="George Town, Penang">George Town</a>'s royal charter provided that:
</p>
<dl><dd>"<i>... the said Municipality of George Town shall on the First Day of January in the year of Our Lord One thousand nine hundred and fifty seven and forever thereafter be a city and shall be called and styled the CITY OF GEORGE TOWN instead of the Municipality of George Town and shall thenceforth have all such rank, liberties, privileges and immunities as are incident to a City.</i>"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:12_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-:12-8">[8]</a></sup></dd></dl>
<p>With the legal entity for George Town being superseded by the merger of the local governments in 1974, the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Malaysia" title="Government of Malaysia">Malaysian federal government</a> was of the view that George Town no longer existed as a city. The city of George Town was omitted in federal government publications and maps.
</p><p>Despite this, most citizens of <a href="/wiki/Penang" title="Penang">Penang</a> contend that George Town is still a city to this day, as George Town's city status was technically never revoked.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:19_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-:19-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:11_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-:11-10">[10]</a></sup> Several federal and municipal ordinances and by-laws still in use today refer to the City of George Town, such as the City of George Town Ordinance 1957 and the City of George Town Liquefied Petroleum Gases By-Laws 1971.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> According to Penang Heritage Trust (PHT) trustee, Anwar Fazal, a lawyer by profession, George Town "<i>legally has been and is still a city because the City of George Town Ordinance 1957 was never repealed</i>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:19_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-:19-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p><p>
In addition, Clause 3 of the Local Government (Merger of the City Council of George Town and the Rural District Council of Penang Island) Order 1974, which was sanctioned by the then <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Penang" title="Government of Penang">Penang state government</a>, stated that</p><blockquote><p>"<i>... the status of the City of George Town as a city shall continue to be preserved and maintained and shall remain unimpaired by the merger hereby effected.</i>"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p></blockquote><p>The clause above implies that, although the legal entity for George Town had been superseded, George Town's city status remains intact and unchanged by the merger of the local governments.
</p><p>In 2008, the newly-elected Penang state government, announced that they would revive the commemorations of George Town's city status from 1 January 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Expansion_of_city_limit">Expansion of city limit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Penang_Island_City_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Expansion of city limit">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2015, the Malaysian federal government elevated the Penang Island Municipal Council into the present-day Penang Island City Council.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In effect, the jurisdiction of the city of <a href="/wiki/George_Town,_Penang" title="George Town, Penang">George Town</a> was expanded to cover an area of 305.77 km<sup>2</sup> (118.06 sq mi), encompassing the entirety of <a href="/wiki/Penang_Island" title="Penang Island">Penang Island</a> as well as five of the surrounding islets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:2_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-:2-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-island_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-island-16">[16]</a></sup> This also makes George Town the only <a href="/wiki/List_of_cities_in_Malaysia" title="List of cities in Malaysia">city in Malaysia</a> to be conferred city status twice, first by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queen_Elizabeth_II" title="Queen Elizabeth II">Queen Elizabeth II</a>, and then by the Malaysian federal government. Patahiyah binti Ismail was subsequently installed as the <a href="/wiki/Mayor_of_Penang_Island" title="Mayor of Penang Island">Mayor of Penang Island</a>, the first female Mayor in <a href="/wiki/Penang" title="Penang">Penang</a>'s history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organisation">Organisation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Penang_Island_City_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Organisation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Menara_Komtar_Penang.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2560" data-file-width="1920" decoding="async" height="293" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Menara_Komtar_Penang.JPG/220px-Menara_Komtar_Penang.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Menara_Komtar_Penang.JPG/330px-Menara_Komtar_Penang.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Menara_Komtar_Penang.JPG/440px-Menara_Komtar_Penang.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Menara_Komtar_Penang.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Penang Island City Council operates several offices within <a href="/wiki/Komtar" title="Komtar">Komtar</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_tallest_buildings_in_Penang_Island" title="List of tallest buildings in Penang Island">tallest skyscraper</a> in <a href="/wiki/Penang" title="Penang">Penang</a>.</div></div></div><p>The City Council is headed by the <a href="/wiki/Mayor_of_Penang_Island" title="Mayor of Penang Island">Mayor of Penang Island</a>, who is assisted by the City Secretary and 24 councillors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The Mayor's term lasts for two years, while each of the 24 councillors is appointed for a one-year term by the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Penang" title="Government of Penang">Penang state government</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup>
</p><p>20 of the councillors are selected by the component parties of the ruling <a href="/wiki/Pakatan_Harapan" title="Pakatan Harapan">Pakatan Harapan</a> coalition. Of these, nine are appointed by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Democratic_Action_Party_(Malaysia)" title="Democratic Action Party (Malaysia)">Democratic Action Party</a> (DAP), seven by the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Justice_Party_(Malaysia)" title="People's Justice Party (Malaysia)">People's Justice Party</a> (PKR), and two each from the <a href="/wiki/National_Trust_Party_(Malaysia)" title="National Trust Party (Malaysia)">National Trust Party</a> (Amanah) and <a href="/wiki/Malaysian_United_Indigenous_Party" title="Malaysian United Indigenous Party">Malaysian United Indigenous Party</a> (Bersatu).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:3_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-:3-20">[20]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Penang" title="Penang">Penang</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Non-governmental_organization" title="Non-governmental organization">non-governmental organisations</a> (NGOs) are allocated the remaining four councillor posts to allow for the participation in policy-making by Penang's civil societies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:3_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-:3-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup>
</p><p>The current Mayor of Penang Island is Yew Tung Seang, who assumed office in 2018, whilst Addnan Mohd Razali holds the position of the City Secretary.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-22">[22]</a></sup>
</p>
