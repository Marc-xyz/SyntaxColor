<p><b>Hans Ulrik</b> (born 28 September 1965 in <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen" title="Copenhagen">Copenhagen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>) is a Danish <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jazz_saxophonist" title="Jazz saxophonist">jazz saxophonist</a>, composer and has several recordings as band leader with the like of <a href="/wiki/Gary_Peacock" title="Gary Peacock">Gary Peacock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adam_Nussbaum" title="Adam Nussbaum">Adam Nussbaum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Niels_Lan_Doky" title="Niels Lan Doky">Niels Lan Doky</a>, <a href="/wiki/Audun_Kleive" title="Audun Kleive">Audun Kleive</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marilyn_Mazur" title="Marilyn Mazur">Marilyn Mazur</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Scofield" title="John Scofield">John Scofield</a> and Steve Swallow.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-henk_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-henk-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hans_Ulrik&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ulrik studied jazz saxophone at <a href="/wiki/Berklee_College_of_Music" title="Berklee College of Music">Berklee College of Music</a> in <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a> (1984–86). He continued his studies after a visit in New York City (1986) until 1987. Since 1987 he has been the leader of various bands like <i>Pinocchio</i> (1987), <i>Hans Ulrik Fusion</i> (1988–89), <i>Ulrik/Hess Quartet</i> (1989–90), <i>Hans Ulrik Group</i> (1991–95), <i>Wombat</i> (1995–98), <i>Hans Ulrik Jazz &amp; Mambo</i> (1998–2003) and <i>Hans Ulrik Quartet</i> (from 2003).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>In addition to various Danish jazz awards Ulrik has received the award of the European Jazz Competition in Leverkusen 1988 the prize for best soloist and 1990. Third prize at the Jazz Festival in Rome. In 2002 he undertook a tour of China, Hong Kong, Malaysia and Australia. In 2005 he joined <a href="/wiki/Steve_Swallow" title="Steve Swallow">Steve Swallow</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Montreal_Jazz_Festival" title="Montreal Jazz Festival">Montreal Jazz Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-henk_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-henk-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
