<p><i><b>Leymus condensatus</b></i>, the <b>giant wildrye</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Leymus" title="Leymus">wild rye grass</a> native to <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> and northern <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leymus_condensatus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Leymus condensatus</i> grows in bunches or clumps, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bunch_grass" title="Bunch grass">bunch grass</a>, stays green all year, and has a distinctive silver blue foliage.  It is drought tolerant, growing in <a href="/wiki/Coastal_sage_scrub" title="Coastal sage scrub">coastal sage scrub</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chaparral" title="Chaparral">chaparral</a>, the <a href="/wiki/California_oak_woodland" title="California oak woodland">California oak woodlands</a> of southern oak woodland and foothill woodland, and <a href="/wiki/Yucca_brevifolia" title="Yucca brevifolia">Joshua tree</a> woodlands, rarely in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wetlands" title="Wetlands">wetlands</a>. It often <a href="/wiki/Hybrid_(biology)" title="Hybrid (biology)">hybridizes</a> with <i><a href="/wiki/Leymus_triticoides" title="Leymus triticoides">Leymus triticoides</a></i>, producing the common hybrid grass <i>Leymus x multiflorus</i>.
</p>
