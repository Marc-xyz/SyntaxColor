<p><b>Sönmez Köksal</b> (born 8 March 1940) is a career <a href="/wiki/Turkish_people" title="Turkish people">Turkish</a> civil servant. He served in a variety of positions largely in the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and in international organisations representing Turkey, and was Turkey's Ambassador to Iraq (1986–1990). He was head of the <a href="/wiki/National_Intelligence_Organization_(Turkey)" title="National Intelligence Organization (Turkey)">National Intelligence Organization</a> (<a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a>: <i lang="tr">Milli İstihbarat Teşkilatı</i>, MİT) from 1992 to 1998, and subsequently Turkey's Ambassador to France (1998–2002).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Köksal was the first civilian head of the MIT, breaking the tradition of appointing generals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
