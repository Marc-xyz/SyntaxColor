<p><i><b>Tenda dos Milagres</b></i> is a 1977 Brazilian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drama_film" title="Drama film">drama film</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Pereira_dos_Santos" title="Nelson Pereira dos Santos">Nelson Pereira dos Santos</a>, based on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tenda_dos_Milagres_(novel)" title="Tenda dos Milagres (novel)">novel of the same name</a> by <a href="/wiki/Jorge_Amado" title="Jorge Amado">Jorge Amado</a>. It stars Hugo Carvana, Sonia Dias and Severino Dada. The director satirizes and exposes racism in Brazilian society. The most notable example where this is done is in a <a href="/wiki/Flashback_(narrative)" title="Flashback (narrative)">flashback</a> scene where Brazilians are shown listening with approving interest to Nazi race theories in the late 1930s. <i>Tenda dos Milagres</i> was shot in <a href="/wiki/Salvador,_Bahia" title="Salvador, Bahia">Salvador, Bahia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cinemateca_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-cinemateca-1">[1]</a></sup> 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tenda_dos_Milagres_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It won the Best Film Award and Best Score Award, and dos Santos won the Best Director Award and Dias won the Best Supporting Actress Award at the 10th <a href="/wiki/Festival_de_Bras%C3%ADlia" title="Festival de Brasília">Festival de Brasília</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cinemateca_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-cinemateca-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It was entered into the <a href="/wiki/27th_Berlin_International_Film_Festival" title="27th Berlin International Film Festival">27th Berlin International Film Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-imdb_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-imdb-3">[3]</a></sup> The film was also selected as the Brazilian entry for the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Foreign_Language_Film" title="Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film">Best Foreign Language Film</a> at the <a href="/wiki/50th_Academy_Awards" title="50th Academy Awards">50th Academy Awards</a>, but was not accepted as a nominee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
