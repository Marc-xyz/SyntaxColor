<p><b>Nottingham Airport</b> (<a href="/wiki/IATA_airport_code" title="IATA airport code">IATA</a>: <b>NQT</b>, <a href="/wiki/ICAO_airport_code" title="ICAO airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>EGBN</b>), also known as <b>Nottingham City Airport</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> is located in <a href="/wiki/Tollerton,_Nottinghamshire" title="Tollerton, Nottinghamshire">Tollerton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nottinghamshire" title="Nottinghamshire">Nottinghamshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. It is situated 3 <a href="/wiki/Nautical_mile" title="Nautical mile">nautical miles</a> (5.6 km; 3.5 mi) south east<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aip_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-aip-1">[1]</a></sup> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nottingham_City_Centre" title="Nottingham City Centre">Nottingham City Centre</a>, and signposted on the <a href="/wiki/A52_road" title="A52 road">A52</a> at <a href="/wiki/Trent_Bridge_(bridge)" title="Trent Bridge (bridge)">Trent Bridge</a> and on the <a href="/wiki/A606_road" title="A606 road">A606</a>—this makes it one of the closest airports to a city centre in the UK. The <a href="/wiki/Aerodrome" title="Aerodrome">aerodrome</a> is equipped for <a href="/wiki/Private_aviation" title="Private aviation">private aviation</a>, business aviation and flight instruction. 
</p><p>The airport primarily serves <a href="/wiki/General_aviation" title="General aviation">general aviation</a> and has two <a href="/wiki/Runway" title="Runway">runways</a>: 09/27 and 03/21 which are 1,050 m × 30 m (3,445 ft × 98 ft) and 821 m × 23 m (2,694 ft × 75 ft) respectively. The longer runway is equipped with lighting for night flying, which operates during the winter flying season.
</p><p>Nottingham City Aerodrome has a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Civil_Aviation_Authority" title="United Kingdom Civil Aviation Authority">CAA</a> Ordinary Licence (Number P491) that allows flights for the public transport of passengers or for flying instruction as authorised by the licensee (Truman Aviation Limited).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nottingham_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nottingham (Tollerton) Airport was opened in 1930 for civilian aviation. During the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>, the airport was known as <b>RAF Tollerton</b> and acted as a relief landing ground for the Polish Training School based at <a href="/wiki/RAF_Newton" title="RAF Newton">RAF Newton</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Polish_Air_Force" title="Polish Air Force">Polish Air Force</a>, and was host to Field Aircraft Services, which repaired battle damaged heavy aircraft and later dismantled them. 
</p><p>After the war the airfield returned to civilian use including a short lived period as a commercial airport, with Blue Line Airways operating from there until 1949, when its aircraft passed on to <a href="/wiki/British_Eagle" title="British Eagle">British Eagle</a> Since that time, Tollerton has serviced small private aircraft only, this has included hosting airshows and three <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King%27s_Cup_Air_Race" title="King's Cup Air Race">King's Cup Air Races</a> 1967, 1968, and 1970.
</p><p>The 227-acre (0.92 km<sup>2</sup>) airport was purchased in December 2006 from Nottingham City Council, by a consortium called Nottingham City Airport plc., and a programme of improvement was promised.
</p><p>A comprehensive history of the airfield titled 'Tollerton - An Airfield For Nottingham 1929-2007' was published by the Keyworth and District Local History Society in 2008.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Truman_Aviation">Truman Aviation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nottingham_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Truman Aviation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Truman Aviation is the airport operator, as well as a <a href="/wiki/Flight_training" title="Flight training">flight training</a> organisation. It incorporates its own maintenance organization, flight training school and <a href="/wiki/Flying_club" title="Flying club">flying club</a>; it is also responsible for providing Air Traffic Services, Fire and Rescue Services and handling on the aerodrome. Truman Aviation operates a fleet of ten aircraft, including the <a href="/wiki/Piper_PA-38_Tomahawk" title="Piper PA-38 Tomahawk">Piper PA-38 Tomahawk</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Piper_Cherokee#PA-28-180_Archer" title="Piper Cherokee">Piper PA-28 Archer</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Piper_Cherokee#PA-28R-200_Arrow" title="Piper Cherokee">Piper PA-28 Arrow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Piper_PA-34_Seneca" title="Piper PA-34 Seneca">Piper PA-34 Seneca</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cessna_172" title="Cessna 172">Cessna 172</a> and a <a href="/wiki/De_Havilland_Tiger_Moth" title="De Havilland Tiger Moth">Tiger Moth</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sherwood_Flying_Club">Sherwood Flying Club</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nottingham_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sherwood Flying Club">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sherwood Flying Club is a members only flying club, based at Nottingham Airport. It was founded in 1957 and presently uses two Piper PA-28 Cherokees and two Grob G-115s for hire and training.
</p>
<h2><span id="2425_.28Nottingham_Airport_Squadron.29_Air_Training_Corps"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2425_(Nottingham_Airport_Squadron)_Air_Training_Corps">2425 (Nottingham Airport Squadron) Air Training Corps</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nottingham_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2425 (Nottingham Airport Squadron) Air Training Corps">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>2425 Squadron has been based at the airport since the early 1980s. This thriving Squadron is one of around 1,000 Squadrons that form the <a href="/wiki/Air_Training_Corps" title="Air Training Corps">Air Training Corps</a> or RAF Air Cadets. Cadets are from <a href="/wiki/Tollerton,_Nottinghamshire" title="Tollerton, Nottinghamshire">Tollerton</a> and the surrounding suburbs and villages within a 10-mile radius approximately.
</p>
