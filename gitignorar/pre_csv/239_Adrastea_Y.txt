<p><b>Adrastea</b> (<a href="/wiki/Minor_planet_designation" title="Minor planet designation">minor planet designation</a>: <b>239 Adrastea</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Asteroid_belt" title="Asteroid belt">main belt</a> <a href="/wiki/Asteroid" title="Asteroid">asteroid</a>. It was discovered by <a href="/wiki/Johann_Palisa" title="Johann Palisa">Johann Palisa</a> on 18 August 1884 in <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a>. It is named after the <a href="/wiki/Greek_mythology" title="Greek mythology">Greek</a> <a href="/wiki/Nymph" title="Nymph">nymph</a> <a href="/wiki/Adrasteia" title="Adrasteia">Adrasteia</a>. The asteroid is roughly 42 km in diameter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jpldata_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-jpldata-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
