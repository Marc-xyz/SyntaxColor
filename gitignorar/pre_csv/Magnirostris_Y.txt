<p><i><b>Magnirostris</b></i>, from the Latin <i>magnus</i> "large" and <i>rostrum</i> "beak", is the name given to a <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> of <a href="/wiki/Dinosaur" title="Dinosaur">dinosaur</a> from the upper <a href="/wiki/Campanian" title="Campanian">Campanian</a> stage in the Upper <a href="/wiki/Cretaceous" title="Cretaceous">Cretaceous</a>. It was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ceratopsian" title="Ceratopsian">ceratopsian</a> which lived in Inner Mongolia in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>. It is distinguished from other <a href="/wiki/Protoceratopsidae" title="Protoceratopsidae">protoceratopsids</a> by its large beak (hence the name) and incipient orbital horn cores.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Discovery_and_species">Discovery and species</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Magnirostris&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Discovery and species">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Magnirostris dodsoni</i> was described by You and <a href="/wiki/Dong_Zhiming" title="Dong Zhiming">Dong Zhiming</a> in 2003, from a near-complete skull collected from the Bayan Mandahu area in <a href="/wiki/Inner_Mongolia" title="Inner Mongolia">Inner Mongolia</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> by the Sino-Canadian Dinosaur Project. It was named after <a href="/wiki/Peter_Dodson" title="Peter Dodson">Peter Dodson</a>, a palaeontologist.
</p><p>It may be only a variant of <i><a href="/wiki/Bagaceratops" title="Bagaceratops">Bagaceratops</a></i>, and the incipient horn cores may be an artifact of preservation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MN06_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-MN06-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Paleoecology">Paleoecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Magnirostris&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Paleoecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Magnirostris</i> lived in deserts with dunes. Other dinosaurs discovered in the Bayan Mandahu include <i><a href="/wiki/Protoceratops" title="Protoceratops">Protoceratops</a> helleninkorhinus</i>, another primitive ceratopsian, and possibly <i><a href="/wiki/Velociraptor" title="Velociraptor">Velociraptor</a> osmolskae</i>, a small predatory <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theropod" title="Theropod">theropod</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Paul2016_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Paul2016-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
