</p><p><b>Naval Surface Warfare Center, Indian Head Division</b> (<b>NSWC IHD</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">naval</a> <a href="/wiki/United_States_Armed_Forces" title="United States Armed Forces">military</a> installation in <a href="/wiki/Charles_County,_Maryland" title="Charles County, Maryland">Charles County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maryland" title="Maryland">Maryland</a>, that is a <a href="/wiki/Naval_Sea_Systems_Command" title="Naval Sea Systems Command">NAVSEA</a> Warfare Center (WFC) enterprise dedicated to <a href="/wiki/Energetics" title="Energetics">energetics</a> (i.e., <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Explosive_material" title="Explosive material">explosives</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Propellants" title="Propellants">propellants</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pyrotechnics" title="Pyrotechnics">pyrotechnics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reactive_material" title="Reactive material">reactive materials</a>, and their application in <a href="/wiki/Propulsion" title="Propulsion">propulsion</a> systems and <a href="/wiki/Ammunition" title="Ammunition">ordnance</a>).  NSWC IHD began as the Naval Proving Ground, Indian Head during World War I.
</p><p>As a <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Defense" title="United States Department of Defense">United States Department of Defense</a> (DoD) Energetics Center, <a href="/wiki/Naval_Surface_Warfare_Center" title="Naval Surface Warfare Center">Naval Surface Warfare Center</a>, Indian Head Division, is a critical component of the <a href="/wiki/Naval_Sea_Systems_Command" title="Naval Sea Systems Command">NAVSEA</a> Warfare Center (WFC) enterprise. One of the WFC's nine divisions, Indian Head’s mission is to research, develop, test, evaluate, and produce <a href="/wiki/Energetics" title="Energetics">energetics</a> (i.e., explosives, propellants, <a href="/wiki/Pyrotechnics" title="Pyrotechnics">pyrotechnics</a>, reactive materials, related chemicals and fuels and their application in propulsion systems and ordnance) and energetic systems for U.S. fighting forces.
</p><p>As the largest DoD full-spectrum energetics facility, NSWC Indian Head employs a workforce of more than 1,900,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> of which more than 850 are scientists, engineers, and technicians that develop and sustain explosives, propellants, pyrotechnics, high-energy chemicals, and their application to warfighting systems. In addition, NSWC Indian Head has the largest concentration of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ph.D." title="Ph.D.">Ph.D.</a>'s working in Energetics in the WFC, including the highest number of <a href="/wiki/Chemical_synthesis" title="Chemical synthesis">synthetic chemists</a>, detonation physicists, and formulation scientists dedicated to the energetics national competency.
</p><p>The Division’s unique synergy and balanced capabilities address all aspects of energetics, including basic research, applied technology, technology demonstration, prototyping, engineering development, acquisition, low-rate production, in-service engineering/mishaps and failure investigations, surveillance, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Demilitarization" title="Demilitarization">demilitarization</a>.
</p><p>If the military experience problems with current weapon systems, or encounter new threats on the battlefield, Indian Head Division collaborates and provides the appropriate solution. As the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Navy" title="U.S. Navy">U.S. Navy</a>’s lead technical authority in the United States, NSWC Indian Head performs over 60% of all Navy energetics workload, and has an unmatched record of 13 Navy-qualified explosives transitioned into 47 Navy, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Army" title="U.S. Army">Army</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Air_Force" title="United States Air Force">Air Force</a>, and <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marine_Corps" title="United States Marine Corps">Marine Corps</a> weapons. Seventy-five percent of all explosives deployed in U.S. weapons were developed by NSWC Indian Head.
</p><p>One of ten divisions of the <a href="/wiki/Naval_Sea_Systems_Command" title="Naval Sea Systems Command">NAVSEA</a> Warfare Center Enterprise, the main site for NSWC IHD is located at Naval Support Facility Indian Head, a 3,500-acre <a href="/wiki/Peninsula" title="Peninsula">peninsula</a> along the <a href="/wiki/Potomac_River" title="Potomac River">Potomac River</a> in Southern Maryland. It also maintains operations in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/McAlester,_OK" title="McAlester, OK">McAlester, OK</a>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Colts_Neck,_NJ" title="Colts Neck, NJ">Colts Neck, NJ</a>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ogden,_UT" title="Ogden, UT">Ogden, UT</a>; <a href="/wiki/Louisville,_Kentucky" title="Louisville, Kentucky">Louisville, KY</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Picatinny_Arsenal" title="Picatinny Arsenal">Picatinny</a>, New Jersey.
</p>
