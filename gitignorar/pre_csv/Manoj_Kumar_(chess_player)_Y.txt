	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Manoj Kumar (chess player)</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>

		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Manoj Kumar</b> is a Fijian <a href="/wiki/Chess" title="Chess">chess</a> player who has won the Fiji National Championship eight times: 1998, 1999, 2000, 2003, 2007, 2008, 2009, and 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
The Fiji Sports Councils Awards named him Fiji national domestic Player of the year twice, in 2007 and 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fcf-awards_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-fcf-awards-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Kumar won the first Fiji Invitational Championship in 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Kumar represented Fiji in the most Oceania championships: 2001; 2007 Nadi, Fiji;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fcf-awards_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-fcf-awards-2">[2]</a></sup> 2009 Gold Coast; and 2011 Rotoura, New Zealand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Kumar also represented Fiji in three <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chess_Olympiads" title="Chess Olympiads">Chess Olympiads</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fcf-awards_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-fcf-awards-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
