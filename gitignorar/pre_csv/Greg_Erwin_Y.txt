<p><b>Gregory "Greg" Erwin</b> is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> who currently serves as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crew_chief_(auto_racing)" title="Crew chief (auto racing)">crew chief</a> for <a href="/wiki/Wood_Brothers_Racing" title="Wood Brothers Racing">Wood Brothers Racing</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Matt_DiBenedetto" title="Matt DiBenedetto">Matt DiBenedetto</a> in the <a href="/wiki/NASCAR_Cup_Series" title="NASCAR Cup Series">NASCAR Cup Series</a>. In 2013, he was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crew_chief_(auto_racing)" title="Crew chief (auto racing)">crew chief</a> for Penske Racing driver <a href="/wiki/Sam_Hornish_Jr." title="Sam Hornish Jr.">Sam Hornish Jr.</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nationwide_Series" title="Nationwide Series">Nationwide Series</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nascar_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-nascar-1">[1]</a></sup> In 2015, Erwin was the crew chief for the No. 22 car of Team Penske in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xfinity_Series" title="Xfinity Series">Xfinity Series</a>. Erwin became the team manager of Team Penske in the Xfinity Series in 2016. In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sprint_Cup_Series" title="Sprint Cup Series">Sprint Cup Series</a>, Erwin served as crew chief for <a href="/wiki/Greg_Biffle" title="Greg Biffle">Greg Biffle</a>. Erwin and Biffle made the NASCAR <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chase_for_the_Sprint_Cup" title="Chase for the Sprint Cup">Chase for the Sprint Cup</a> in 2009 and recorded Ford's first win of 2010 at <a href="/wiki/Pocono_Raceway" title="Pocono Raceway">Pocono Raceway</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usatoday_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday-2">[2]</a></sup> Erwin has also worked as crew chief for <a href="/wiki/Robby_Gordon_Motorsports" title="Robby Gordon Motorsports">Robby Gordon Motorsports</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-espn_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-espn-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greg_Erwin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Erwin was born in <a href="/wiki/Hatboro,_Pennsylvania" title="Hatboro, Pennsylvania">Hatboro, Pennsylvania</a> and in high school, his father bought his first oval track race car.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-espn_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-espn-3">[3]</a></sup> Erwin worked on the team during high school and every summer while attended <a href="/wiki/Clemson_University" title="Clemson University">Clemson University</a>. He earned an engineering degree from Clemson in 1992 returning for graduate school in 1993 with a motorsports engineering scholarship. During the program he spent his summers working with race teams such as Tri-Star Motorsports.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jr_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-jr-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="NASCAR_career">NASCAR career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greg_Erwin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: NASCAR career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greg_Erwin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Erwin was offered his first NASCAR job with Diamond Ridge Motorsports in 1995.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-espn_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-espn-3">[3]</a></sup> He worked as an engineer for the team that employed drivers such as Steve Grissom, Jeff Green and <a href="/wiki/Elliott_Sadler" title="Elliott Sadler">Elliott Sadler</a>. Erwin left Diamond Ridge at the end of 1995 to work for <a href="/wiki/Chip_Ganassi_Racing" title="Chip Ganassi Racing">Team SABCO Racing</a>. The operation grew quickly in the next few months going from one team to three. Erwin worked as the only engineer for all three cars. <a href="/wiki/Chip_Ganassi" title="Chip Ganassi">Chip Ganassi</a> bought into the race team in 2001 hiring additional engineers. Through the end of 2002, Erwin remained at Chip Ganassi Racing with Felix Sabates.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jr_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-jr-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Erwin began working for <a href="/wiki/Richard_Childress_Racing" title="Richard Childress Racing">Richard Childress Racing</a> in 2003 helping with their new seven-post research and development program. Erwin worked directly with the No. 31 team and crew chief Chris Andrews. At the end of 2004, early in 2005, Erwin left Richard Childress Racing for <a href="/wiki/Robby_Gordon_Motorsports" title="Robby Gordon Motorsports">Robby Gordon Motorsports</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jr_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-jr-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Erwin's first crew chief position was for the Robby Gordon Motorsports No. 7 Nextel Cup entry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-espn_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-espn-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Before Erwin joined in 2005, the team failed to qualify for four races but only missed two the rest of the season under his direction. The team started outside the top 35 in points and had to race their way in to the first few races in 2006. In its second year the team did not miss a race in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jr_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-jr-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Roush">Roush</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greg_Erwin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Roush">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Roush Fenway Racing hired Erwin as the crew chief for the No. 16 Nextel Cup car driven by Greg Biffle in May 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nascar_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-nascar-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Calgary_Chronicle_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Calgary_Chronicle-6">[6]</a></sup> On June 3, Erwin's first race with Biffle was at <a href="/wiki/Dover_International_Speedway" title="Dover International Speedway">Dover International Speedway</a>. Biffle started in the 10th position and finished sixth, his best finish in seven races. In September, his team went on to win at <a href="/wiki/Kansas_Speedway" title="Kansas Speedway">Kansas Speedway</a> and finish 14th in the Nextel Cup point standings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jr_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-jr-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Erwin and Biffle recorded two wins, twelve top-five finishes and 17 top 10 finishes, in 2009, their first full season together. The team made the Chase for the second time in Biffle's career finishing the season third in the point standings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jr_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-jr-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>The #16 car ran well at the <a href="/wiki/Brickyard_400" title="Brickyard 400">Brickyard 400</a> in 2010 thanks, in part, to the new FR9 Ford engine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> They ended an extended winless drought the next weekend at Pocono on August 1, 2010 after a late <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stop</a> decision to take 2 tires instead of 4.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usatoday_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Penske">Penske</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greg_Erwin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Penske">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2013, Erwin served as crew chief for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sam_Hornish,_Jr." title="Sam Hornish, Jr.">Sam Hornish, Jr.</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nationwide_Series" title="Nationwide Series">Nationwide Series</a>. In 2014, he became the competition director for <a href="/wiki/Team_Penske" title="Team Penske">Team Penske</a> in the Nationwide Series. Erwin served as interim crew chief for Sprint Cup driver <a href="/wiki/Brad_Keselowski" title="Brad Keselowski">Brad Keselowski</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2014_The_Profit_on_CNBC_500" title="2014 The Profit on CNBC 500">2014 The Profit on CNBC 500</a> after regular crew chief <a href="/wiki/Paul_Wolfe" title="Paul Wolfe">Paul Wolfe</a> was witnessing the birth of his child.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In 2015, Erwin became the crew chief for Team Penske's No. 22 car in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xfinity_Series" title="Xfinity Series">Xfinity Series</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Erwin became the team manager for Team Penske's Xfinity Series program in 2016, with Brian Wilson replacing him as crew chief of the No. 22 car.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wood_Brothers">Wood Brothers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greg_Erwin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Wood Brothers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2018, Erwin became the crew chief for <a href="/wiki/Wood_Brothers_Racing" title="Wood Brothers Racing">Wood Brothers Racing</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Paul_Menard" title="Paul Menard">Paul Menard</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monster_Energy_NASCAR_Cup_Series" title="Monster Energy NASCAR Cup Series">Monster Energy NASCAR Cup Series</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greg_Erwin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Erwin is married to wife Susan and they have three children; Curtis, Kimberly and Colin. The family lives in <a href="/wiki/Mooresville,_North_Carolina" title="Mooresville, North Carolina">Mooresville, North Carolina</a>.
</p>
