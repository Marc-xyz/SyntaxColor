<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kwok_Ka_Ming&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kwok graduated from <a href="/wiki/St._Francis_Xavier%27s_College" title="St. Francis Xavier's College">St. Francis Xavier's College</a> where he played in the school basketball and table tennis team. He joined the <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_Football_Association" title="Hong Kong Football Association">Hong Kong Football Association</a> junior training course in 1965.  Kwok Ka Ming joined Hong Kong first division team <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_Rangers" title="Hong Kong Rangers">Hong Kong Rangers</a> at the age of 18. He played at the right side of midfield for both Rangers and Hong Kong in the 1970s. He retired from playing in 1980, when he was just 30 years old.
</p><p>He was award the "10 Outstanding Youths of Hong Kong" award in 1977 for his contribution to <a href="/wiki/Football_in_Hong_Kong" title="Football in Hong Kong">Hong Kong football</a>, along with famed Hong Kong movie director <a href="/wiki/Michael_Hui" title="Michael Hui">Michael Hui</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="1985_match_against_China_PR">1985 match against China PR</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kwok_Ka_Ming&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1985 match against China PR">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/1985_China_v_Hong_Kong_football_match" title="1985 China v Hong Kong football match">1985 China v Hong Kong football match</a></div>
<p>Kwok later became the coach of the <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_national_football_team" title="Hong Kong national football team">Hong Kong football team</a>, guiding the team to a decisive victory against <a href="/wiki/China_national_football_team" title="China national football team">China PR</a> by 2-1 at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Workers_Stadium" title="Workers Stadium">Workers Stadium</a> in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>, China on 19 May 1985, during a <a href="/wiki/1986_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_(AFC)" title="1986 FIFA World Cup qualification (AFC)">1986 FIFA World Cup qualifying</a> game.
</p><p>Although the two teams drew 0-0 in their first match in Hong Kong, China PR was considered to be stronger and was favoured to win the group. Due to superior goal difference, China only needed a draw to proceed to the next round. However, a 20-yard freekick by Hong Kong's <a href="/wiki/Cheung_Chi_Tak" title="Cheung Chi Tak">Cheung Chi Tak</a> (張志德) opened the scoring. China PR replied with <a href="/wiki/Li_Hui_(footballer)" title="Li Hui (footballer)">Li Hui</a> (李輝)'s goal but another well taken goal by <a href="/wiki/Ku_Kam_Fai" title="Ku Kam Fai">Ku Kam Fai</a> (顧錦輝) sealed the win for Hong Kong and sent China PR out of the 1986 World Cup.
</p><p>The loss to Hong Kong delayed China PR's World Cup entry for another 16 years before they finally appeared in Korea Japan 2002. After the game, Chinese fans rioted outside the stadium, while the Hong Kong team received a heroic welcome at the <a href="/wiki/Kai_Tak_Airport" title="Kai Tak Airport">Kai Tak Airport</a>.
</p><p>The victory was passionately remembered by Hong Kong football fans as "The 519 Victory".
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="1986_FIFA_World_Cup_Asia_Qualifying_Play_offs">1986 FIFA World Cup Asia Qualifying Play offs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kwok_Ka_Ming&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1986 FIFA World Cup Asia Qualifying Play offs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hong Kong thus earned the right to play <a href="/wiki/Japan_national_football_team" title="Japan national football team">Japan</a> for two home and away ties in the second qualifying round. Kwok led the team to Japan but lost 3-0.
</p><p>For the return leg at the <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_Stadium" title="Hong Kong Stadium">Hong Kong Stadium</a>, the match was completely sold out but the Hong Kong team missed a penalty and lost again to Japan by 2-1.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_tournaments">Other tournaments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kwok_Ka_Ming&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Other tournaments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He has been, for many different tournaments, including the <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 FIFA World Cup</a> and <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a>, a member of The FIFA Technical Study Group.
</p>
