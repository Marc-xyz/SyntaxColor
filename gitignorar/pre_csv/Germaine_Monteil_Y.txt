	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Germaine Monteil</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>
<b>Germaine Monteil</b> was a New York-based French fashion designer and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cosmetician" title="Cosmetician">cosmetician</a> who founded the cosmetics and <a href="/wiki/Perfume" title="Perfume">perfume</a> company sharing her name.  
</p><p>Galore perfume, introduced in 1964, is not included on your list of Germaine Monteil fragrances.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fashion">Fashion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Germaine_Monteil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Fashion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in France in 1898, Monteil moved to the United States in the early 1930s. A high-end dressmaker as well as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cosmetician" title="Cosmetician">cosmetician</a>, she first established herself as a fashion designer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-asiv_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-asiv-1">[1]</a></sup> She made classic dresses with flaring circular or pleated skirts and slim silhouettes, and was renowned for her use of prints which appealed to the American market.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-asiv_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-asiv-1">[1]</a></sup> In 1938, Monteil won a <a href="/wiki/Neiman_Marcus_Fashion_Award" title="Neiman Marcus Fashion Award">Neiman Marcus Fashion Award</a> in recognition of her influence upon the fashion industry. This was the first year in which the awards were presented. She died in 1987.
</p>
<h2><span id="Cosmetics_.26_perfumes"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Cosmetics_&amp;_perfumes">Cosmetics &amp; perfumes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Germaine_Monteil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Cosmetics &amp; perfumes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1936, Monteil founded with her husband, Guy Bjorkman, <i>Germaine Monteil Cosmetiques Corp.</i> as a sideline to her fashion business, offering skin treatments and creams.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-asiv_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-asiv-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Her first perfume, <i>Laughter</i> (later renamed <i>Rigolade</i>), came out in 1941.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nph_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-nph-3">[3]</a></sup> The increasing success of this sideline led to Monteil abandoning fashion design in the late 1940s to focus on perfume and cosmetics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-asiv_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-asiv-1">[1]</a></sup> The company was acquired by cosmetics giant Revlon in 1987 from fashion house Yves Saint Laurent. Revlon then sold the brand on to the German company Wilde Cosmetics GmbH in 2006.
