<p><b>Georg Bohlmann</b> (23 April 1869 – 25 April 1928) was a <a href="/wiki/Germans" title="Germans">German</a> <a href="/wiki/Mathematician" title="Mathematician">mathematician</a> who specialized in <a href="/wiki/Probability_theory" title="Probability theory">probability theory</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Actuarial_mathematics" title="Actuarial mathematics">actuarial mathematics</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Georg_Bohlmann&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Georg Bohlmann went to school in <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Leipzig" title="Leipzig">Leipzig</a> and took his <i><a href="/wiki/Abitur" title="Abitur">Abitur</a></i> at the <i>Wilhelms-Gymnasium</i> in Berlin in 1888. After that, he began studying mathematics at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_Berlin" title="University of Berlin">University of Berlin</a> under <a href="/wiki/Leopold_Kronecker" title="Leopold Kronecker">Leopold Kronecker</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lazarus_Fuchs" title="Lazarus Fuchs">Lazarus Fuchs</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wilhelm_Dilthey" title="Wilhelm Dilthey">Wilhelm Dilthey</a>. As he advanced in his studies, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lie_groups" title="Lie groups">Lie groups</a> became the focus of his interest. Since this area was poorly represented at Berlin, he moved to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_Halle" title="University of Halle">University of Halle</a>, where he obtained his doctorate in 1892 under <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Albert_Wangerin" title="Albert Wangerin">Albert Wangerin</a> with a dissertation on the topic <i>Ueber eine gewisse Klasse continuierlicher Gruppen und ihren Zusammenhang mit den Additionstheoremen</i> ("On a certain class of continuous groups and their relation to addition theorems").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> After that, he worked at the Meteorological Institute of Berlin, where presumably his interest in applied mathematics developed.  At the invitation of <a href="/wiki/Felix_Klein" title="Felix Klein">Felix Klein</a>, he moved to the <a href="/wiki/University_of_G%C3%B6ttingen" title="University of Göttingen">University of Göttingen</a>, where he <a href="/wiki/Habilitation" title="Habilitation">habilitated</a> in 1894. In 1895, he was involved in starting a seminar on actuarial science at Göttingen.  However, since he held no permanent position there, he went to Berlin in 1903 to work as the Chief Actuary for the German subsidiary of the New York Mutual Life Insurance Company.
</p><p>In 1901, he wrote the entry on life insurance mathematics in the <i><a href="/wiki/Klein%27s_encyclopedia" title="Klein's encyclopedia">Enzyklopädie der mathematischen Wissenschaften</a></i> ("Encyclopaedia of Mathematical Sciences") in which he gave <a href="/wiki/Probability_axioms" title="Probability axioms">axioms for probability theory</a> long before <a href="/wiki/Andrey_Kolmogorov" title="Andrey Kolmogorov">Andrey Kolmogorov</a> did so in 1933. In particular, he was the first to give the modern definition of <a href="/wiki/Independence_(probability_theory)" title="Independence (probability theory)">statistical independence</a>. Compared to the current structure of probability theory, his work only lacked the technical condition of <a href="/wiki/Sigma_additivity" title="Sigma additivity">sigma additivity</a>. However, in contrast to Kolmogorov, Bohlmann failed to prove significant theorems within his axiomatic framework. As a result, his fundamental contributions to probability theory gained very little attention. In particular, though Kolmogorov had visited Göttingen several times in the late 1920s, he had no knowledge of Bohlmann's work.
</p><p>Bohlmann was an invited speaker in the <a href="/wiki/International_Congress_of_Mathematicians" title="International Congress of Mathematicians">International Congress of Mathematicians</a> in 1908 at Rome.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
