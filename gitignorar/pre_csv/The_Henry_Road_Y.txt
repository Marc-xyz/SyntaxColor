<p><b>The Henry Road</b> are a band from <a href="/wiki/Nottingham" title="Nottingham">Nottingham</a> in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>.  They are the primary and possibly only exponent of the style "wagon pop".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Henry_Road&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Birth_of_a_supergroup">Birth of a supergroup</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Henry_Road&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Birth of a supergroup">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Henry Road were originally to be called Le Cube 100<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-leftlion.co.uk_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-leftlion.co.uk-2">[2]</a></sup> but decided against this as they felt it implied greater knowledge of French and number than they could claim at the time.
</p><p>They are the self-styled founders of Wagon Pop, "music that doesn't have a compelling urge to be hip".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-leftlion.co.uk_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-leftlion.co.uk-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Their music draws heavily from <a href="/wiki/Progressive_rock" title="Progressive rock">progressive</a> musicians of the 60s and 70s, particularly the <a href="/wiki/Electric_Light_Orchestra" title="Electric Light Orchestra">Electric Light Orchestra</a>, who are celebrated in their song <i><a href="/wiki/Jeff_Lynne" title="Jeff Lynne">Jeff Lynne</a> is Five</i>.  It is also reminiscent of bands with more of a <a href="/wiki/Comedy" title="Comedy">comedy</a> element, such as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bonzo_Dog_Band" title="Bonzo Dog Band">Bonzo Dog Band</a>, with whom they have been compared.
</p><p>Their songs are often <a href="/wiki/Narrative" title="Narrative">narrative</a> in structure, but feature highly surreal characters and situations, such as Loggy Log, eponymous hero of the Loggy Log EP,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-os_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-os-3">[3]</a></sup> Crap Arrow (an arrow that points in every direction at once and is therefore a sphere) and Mrs. Screen.  Their own assumed identities are similarly strange and wonderful.
</p><p>In 2006 the band lost a founder member and went straight back to their studio to finish the recording of their first album 'Running at Dog 90'. During this time they recruited a new guitarist: The Cohain (music journalist Steph Coole - reminiscent in style to <a href="/wiki/Lester_Bangs" title="Lester Bangs">Lester Bangs</a>) and managed a handful of live dates.  During this time the live gigs (which were still accompanied by a visual feast of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Optikinetics&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Optikinetics (page does not exist)">Optikinetics</a> and other lighting effects, manned by the Henry Road's 'fifth member'- the mysterious 'Metempsychosis'; they were also joined on stage by 'DJ Dan Rattomatic' of the hip hop outfit 'First Blood'.
</p><p>The beginning of 2008 saw the band relocate to London, and prepare to release and tour the new album.
</p><p>2009 will see the release of debut single Jeff Lynne is 5 and long-awaited debut album Running at Dog 90.
</p><p>The current and new live line up are gigging the London circuit in prep for the upcoming releases, they are:
Bwen Lord (drums)
Phil Colin (keys and vox)
Steph Coole (guitars and vox)
Simon Ashley (bass)
Dan Rattomatic (effects and decks)
</p>
