<ul><li>28 February – the <a href="/wiki/Grand_National" title="Grand National">Grand National</a> at <a href="/wiki/Aintree" title="Aintree">Aintree</a> is won by the 5/1 joint favourite <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Discount_(racehorse)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Discount (racehorse) (page does not exist)">Discount</a>.</li>
<li>11 April – initiation of the <a href="/wiki/Ragged_school" title="Ragged school">Ragged Schools Union</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li>11 May – major fire at <a href="/wiki/Lyme_Regis" title="Lyme Regis">Lyme Regis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li>6 June – <a href="/wiki/George_Williams_(YMCA)" title="George Williams (YMCA)">George Williams</a> founds the <a href="/wiki/YMCA" title="YMCA">Young Men's Christian Association</a> in London.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pocket_On_This_Day_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pocket_On_This_Day-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>15 June – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Factory_Act_1844" title="Factory Act 1844">Factory Act</a> imposes a maximum 12-hour working day for women, and a maximum 6-hour day for children aged 6 to 13.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBH_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-CBH-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li>19 July – <a href="/wiki/Bank_Charter_Act_1844" title="Bank Charter Act 1844">Bank Charter Act</a> restricts powers of British banks other than the <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_England" title="Bank of England">Bank of England</a> to issue <a href="/wiki/Banknotes_of_the_pound_sterling" title="Banknotes of the pound sterling">banknotes of the pound sterling</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li>9 August – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Imprisonment_for_debt" title="Imprisonment for debt">imprisonment for debt</a> abolished in England.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li>21 &amp; 27 August – consecration of two new major urban <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> churches, both designed by <a href="/wiki/Augustus_Pugin" title="Augustus Pugin">Augustus Pugin</a>, which will in the 1850s be elevated to cathedral status: <a href="/wiki/St_Mary%27s_Cathedral,_Newcastle_upon_Tyne" title="St Mary's Cathedral, Newcastle upon Tyne">St Mary's Church, Newcastle upon Tyne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nottingham_Cathedral" title="Nottingham Cathedral">St Barnabas Church, Nottingham</a>. (In October, Pugin occupies <a href="/wiki/The_Grange,_Ramsgate" title="The Grange, Ramsgate">The Grange, Ramsgate</a>, a house designed for himself which is influential in the development of domestic <a href="/wiki/Gothic_Revival_architecture" title="Gothic Revival architecture">Gothic Revival architecture</a>.)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GA_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-GA-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li>28 September – a <a href="/wiki/Blackdamp" title="Blackdamp">blackdamp</a> <a href="/wiki/Explosion" title="Explosion">explosion</a> at <a href="/wiki/Haswell,_County_Durham" title="Haswell, County Durham">Haswell Colliery</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Durham_Coalfield" title="Durham Coalfield">Durham Coalfield</a> kills 95, with just four survivors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
<li>8 October – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Louis-Phillipe" title="Louis-Phillipe">Louis-Phillipe</a> arrives in Portsmouth</li>
<li>20 October – <a href="/wiki/Counties_(Detached_Parts)_Act_1844" title="Counties (Detached Parts) Act 1844">Counties (Detached Parts) Act 1844</a> comes into effect, eliminating many outliers or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Exclave" title="Exclave">exclaves</a> of <a href="/wiki/Counties_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Counties of the United Kingdom">counties</a> in <a href="/wiki/England_and_Wales" title="England and Wales">England and Wales</a> for civil purposes.</li>
<li>28 October – the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Royal_Exchange_(London)" title="Royal Exchange (London)">Royal Exchange</a> in London opened by <a href="/wiki/Queen_Victoria" title="Queen Victoria">Queen Victoria</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
<li>21 December – the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rochdale_Pioneers" title="Rochdale Pioneers">Rochdale Pioneers</a>, usually considered the first successful <a href="/wiki/Cooperative" title="Cooperative">cooperative</a> enterprise, open their store in <a href="/wiki/Rochdale" title="Rochdale">Rochdale</a>, forming the basis for the modern <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_cooperative_movement" title="History of the cooperative movement">cooperative movement</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pocket_On_This_Day_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Pocket_On_This_Day-3">[3]</a></sup></li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Undated">Undated</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1844_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Undated">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Henry_Hardinge,_1st_Viscount_Hardinge" title="Henry Hardinge, 1st Viscount Hardinge">Henry Hardinge, 1st Viscount Hardinge</a> appointed as <a href="/wiki/Governor-General_of_India" title="Governor-General of India">Governor-General of India</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pocket_Date_Book_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pocket_Date_Book-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Winsford" title="Winsford">Winsford</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rock_salt" title="Rock salt">rock salt</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salt_mine" title="Salt mine">mine</a> opens in <a href="/wiki/Cheshire" title="Cheshire">Cheshire</a>; by 2014 it will be Britain's oldest working mine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ring_of_bells" title="Ring of bells">Ring of bells</a> installed at <a href="/wiki/St_John_the_Evangelist%27s_Church,_Kirkham" title="St John the Evangelist's Church, Kirkham">St John the Evangelist's Church, Kirkham</a> (Lancashire), said to be the first peal rung in an English Roman Catholic church since the <a href="/wiki/English_Reformation" title="English Reformation">Reformation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></li>
<li>"<a href="/wiki/Surplice" title="Surplice">Surplice</a> riots" in <a href="/wiki/Exeter" title="Exeter">Exeter</a> and London break out in opposition to supposed Catholicisation of the Church of England.</li>
<li>King <a href="/wiki/Frederick_Augustus_II_of_Saxony" title="Frederick Augustus II of Saxony">Frederick Augustus II of Saxony</a> makes an informal summer tour of Britain.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Publications">Publications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1844_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Publications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Robert_Chambers_(publisher_born_1802)" title="Robert Chambers (publisher born 1802)">Robert Chambers</a>' anonymous <i><a href="/wiki/Vestiges_of_the_Natural_History_of_Creation" title="Vestiges of the Natural History of Creation">Vestiges of the Natural History of Creation</a></i>, which paves the way for acceptance of <a href="/wiki/Charles_Darwin" title="Charles Darwin">Darwin</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Origin_of_Species" title="The Origin of Species">The Origin of Species</a></i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Charles_Dickens" title="Charles Dickens">Charles Dickens</a>' novel <i><a href="/wiki/Martin_Chuzzlewit" title="Martin Chuzzlewit">Martin Chuzzlewit</a></i> (complete in book form) and his Christmas novella <i><a href="/wiki/The_Chimes" title="The Chimes">The Chimes</a></i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Disraeli" title="Benjamin Disraeli">Benjamin Disraeli</a>'s novel <i><a href="/wiki/Coningsby_(novel)" title="Coningsby (novel)">Coningsby</a></i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Henry_Fox_Talbot" title="Henry Fox Talbot">Henry Fox Talbot</a>'s book <i><a href="/wiki/The_Pencil_of_Nature" title="The Pencil of Nature">The Pencil of Nature</a></i>, the first illustrated with photographs from a camera (publication commences June).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/William_Makepeace_Thackeray" title="William Makepeace Thackeray">William Makepeace Thackeray</a>'s novel <i><a href="/wiki/The_Luck_of_Barry_Lyndon" title="The Luck of Barry Lyndon">The Luck of Barry Lyndon</a></i> (serialisation).</li></ul>
