	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Nicola Martinucci</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Nicola Martinucci</b> (born 28 March 1941) is an Italian opera singer, particularly noted for his performances in the <a href="/wiki/Spinto" title="Spinto">spinto tenor</a> of roles Calaf in <i><a href="/wiki/Turandot" title="Turandot">Turandot</a></i>, Radamès in <i><a href="/wiki/Aida" title="Aida">Aida</a></i>, and the title role in <i><a href="/wiki/Andrea_Ch%C3%A9nier" title="Andrea Chénier">Andrea Chénier</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bagnoli_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bagnoli-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grove_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Grove-2">[2]</a></sup></i>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nicola_Martinucci&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Martinucci was born in <a href="/wiki/Taranto" title="Taranto">Taranto</a>, Italy. He did not begin studying singing until his 20s. With the encouragement of <a href="/wiki/Mario_Del_Monaco" title="Mario Del Monaco">Mario Del Monaco</a> he studied in Milan with Marcello Del Monaco (Mario's brother) and with Sara Sforni. He made his debut in 1966 at the Teatro Nuovo in Milan as Manrico in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Il_Trovatore" title="Il Trovatore">Il Trovatore</a></i>. In 1966 he had won the <a href="/wiki/Viotti_International_Music_Competition" title="Viotti International Music Competition">Viotti International Music Competition</a> which led to further debuts in Italian opera houses. He later appeared <a href="/wiki/La_Scala" title="La Scala">La Scala</a> from 1983 where he reprised the role of Manrico as well as singing Calaf, Radamès, Luigi in <i><a href="/wiki/Il_tabarro" title="Il tabarro">Il tabarro</a></i> and Oronte in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/I_Lombardi" title="I Lombardi">I Lombardi</a></i>; <a href="/wiki/La_Fenice" title="La Fenice">La Fenice</a> where he appeared from 1984 as Calaf, Radames, and Cavaradossi in <i><a href="/wiki/Tosca" title="Tosca">Tosca</a></i>; and the <a href="/wiki/Arena_di_Verona_Festival" title="Arena di Verona Festival">Arena di Verona</a>, where he between 1982 and 1986 he sang Radamès, Calaf, and Andrea Chenier.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Repubblica_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Repubblica-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bagnoli_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Bagnoli-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grove_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Grove-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Outside his native Italy, Martinucci made his debut at the <a href="/wiki/Teatro_Col%C3%B3n" title="Teatro Colón">Teatro Colón</a> in Buenos Aires in 1976 as Des Grieux in <i><a href="/wiki/Manon_Lescaut_(Puccini)" title="Manon Lescaut (Puccini)">Manon Lescaut</a></i> and appeared there again in 1977 as Calaf, in 1978 in the title role of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Don_Carlo" title="Don Carlo">Don Carlo</a></i>, and in 1979 as Rodolfo in <i><a href="/wiki/La_boh%C3%A8me" title="La bohème">La bohème</a></i>. He made his debut at London's <a href="/wiki/Royal_Opera_House" title="Royal Opera House">Royal Opera House</a> in 1985 as Dick Johnson in <i><a href="/wiki/La_fanciulla_del_West" title="La fanciulla del West">La fanciulla del West</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Repubblica_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Repubblica-3">[3]</a></sup> His <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Opera" title="Metropolitan Opera">Metropolitan Opera</a> debut came in 1988 when he sang Calaf with <a href="/wiki/Ghena_Dimitrova" title="Ghena Dimitrova">Ghena Dimitrova</a> as Turandot. He went on to appear there until 1995 as Radamès, Dick Johnson, Andrea Chénier, and Manrico. His last performance at the Met was as Turiddu in <i><a href="/wiki/Cavalleria_rusticana" title="Cavalleria rusticana">Cavalleria rusticana</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>In his later years, Martinucci has taught masterclasses in singing and served on the juries of several singing competitions. His daughter Leyla is also an opera singer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Several of Martinucci's performances have been released on video, including <i>Aida</i> and <i>Turandot</i> from the Arena di Verona.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
