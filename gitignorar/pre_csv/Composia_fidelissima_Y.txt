<p><i><b>Composia fidelissima</b></i> the <b>faithful beauty</b> or <b>Uncle Sam moth</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Moth" title="Moth">moth</a> in the family <a href="/wiki/Erebidae" title="Erebidae">Erebidae</a>. The species was first described by <a href="/wiki/Gottlieb_August_Wilhelm_Herrich-Sch%C3%A4ffer" title="Gottlieb August Wilhelm Herrich-Schäffer">Gottlieb August Wilhelm Herrich-Schäffer</a> in 1866. It is found in southern <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a> and the <a href="/wiki/West_Indies" title="West Indies">West Indies</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Wingspan" title="Wingspan">wingspan</a> is 48–64 mm. Adults are on wing year round. They are day flying.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>The larvae feed on <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cynanchum_scoparium&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cynanchum scoparium (page does not exist)">Cynanchum scoparium</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Canavalia" title="Canavalia">Canavalia</a></i> (including <i><a href="/wiki/Canavalia_rosea" title="Canavalia rosea">Canavalia rosea</a></i>), <i><a href="/wiki/Nerium" title="Nerium">Nerium</a></i> (including <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nerium_oleander" title="Nerium oleander">Nerium oleander</a></i>) and <i><a href="/wiki/Echites" title="Echites">Echites</a></i> species (including <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Echites_umbellate&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Echites umbellate (page does not exist)">Echites umbellate</a></i>).
</p>
