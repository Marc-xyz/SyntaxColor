<p>The <b>Battle of Kápolna</b> was a battle in the <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_Revolution_of_1848" title="Hungarian Revolution of 1848">Hungarian Revolution of 1848</a>, fought on 26 and 27 February 1849.  The battle ended with Austrian victory and influenced the politics of central Europe: <a href="/wiki/Franz_Joseph_I_of_Austria" title="Franz Joseph I of Austria">Franz Joseph I</a> announced the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/March_Constitution_of_Austria" title="March Constitution of Austria">March Constitution of Austria</a> on 4 March 1849.
</p>

<h3><span id="Dembi.C5.84ski.2C_the_Polish_supreme_commander"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Dembiński,_the_Polish_supreme_commander">Dembiński, the Polish supreme commander</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_K%C3%A1polna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Dembiński, the Polish supreme commander">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/L%C3%A1szl%C3%B3_Teleki" title="László Teleki">László Teleki</a> sent <a href="/wiki/Henryk_Dembi%C5%84ski" title="Henryk Dembiński">Henryk Dembiński</a>, the Supreme Commander of the Polish <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/November_uprising" title="November uprising">November uprising</a>, from Paris to the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Hungary" title="Kingdom of Hungary">Kingdom of Hungary</a>. <a href="/wiki/Lajos_Kossuth" title="Lajos Kossuth">Lajos Kossuth</a> knew Dembiński well after his <a href="/wiki/Lithuania" title="Lithuania">Lithuanian</a> retreat.
</p>
<h3><span id="Mad_plan.2C_bad_tactics"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Mad_plan,_bad_tactics">Mad plan, bad tactics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_K%C3%A1polna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Mad plan, bad tactics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:T%C3%A9li_hadj%C3%A1rat_1848_49_2.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="458" data-file-width="582" decoding="async" height="157" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/T%C3%A9li_hadj%C3%A1rat_1848_49_2.png/200px-T%C3%A9li_hadj%C3%A1rat_1848_49_2.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/T%C3%A9li_hadj%C3%A1rat_1848_49_2.png/300px-T%C3%A9li_hadj%C3%A1rat_1848_49_2.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/T%C3%A9li_hadj%C3%A1rat_1848_49_2.png/400px-T%C3%A9li_hadj%C3%A1rat_1848_49_2.png 2x" width="200"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:T%C3%A9li_hadj%C3%A1rat_1848_49_2.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i>Movements in the Winter Campaign</i></div></div></div>
<p>Dembiński's appointment was misguided, because he was introverted, short-tempered, and distrustful. The Hungarian commanding officers did not like it. Dembiński had an argument with <a href="/wiki/M%C3%B3r_Perczel" title="Mór Perczel">Mór Perczel</a>, stood down <a href="/wiki/Gy%C3%B6rgy_Klapka" title="György Klapka">György Klapka</a>, and later had an argument with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Art%C3%BAr_G%C3%B6rgey" title="Artúr Görgey">Artúr Görgey</a>.
</p><p>Dembiński's plans were  to fight the final battle at <a href="/wiki/Mez%C5%91k%C3%B6vesd" title="Mezőkövesd">Mezőkövesd</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eger" title="Eger">Eger</a>, but before this to attack the Austrian army at <a href="/wiki/Szolnok" title="Szolnok">Szolnok</a>. He was so obsessive about his plans that he even refused to attack <a href="/wiki/Franz_Schlik" title="Franz Schlik">Franz Schlik</a>, even though Shlik was completely isolated. Dembiński's explanation was that if he did, <a href="/wiki/Alfred_I,_Prince_of_Windisch-Gr%C3%A4tz" title="Alfred I, Prince of Windisch-Grätz">Winfisch-Grätz</a> still wanted control in <a href="/wiki/Buda" title="Buda">Buda</a>, which meant that the battle plans for <a href="/wiki/Mez%C5%91k%C3%B6vesd" title="Mezőkövesd">Mezőkövesd</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eger" title="Eger">Eger</a> were null and void. Dembiński stuck to his plan and drew back one of his legions from <a href="/wiki/P%C3%A9terv%C3%A1s%C3%A1ra" title="Pétervására">Pétervására</a> when Klapka wanted to attack Schlik on 24 February.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Balance_of_forces">Balance of forces</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_K%C3%A1polna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Balance of forces">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Henryk_Dembi%C5%84ski.PNG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2009" data-file-width="1274" decoding="async" height="237" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Henryk_Dembi%C5%84ski.PNG/150px-Henryk_Dembi%C5%84ski.PNG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Henryk_Dembi%C5%84ski.PNG/225px-Henryk_Dembi%C5%84ski.PNG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Henryk_Dembi%C5%84ski.PNG/300px-Henryk_Dembi%C5%84ski.PNG 2x" width="150"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Henryk_Dembi%C5%84ski.PNG" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Henryk_Dembi%C5%84ski" title="Henryk Dembiński">Henryk Dembiński</a> Polish-born, Hungarian-Polish supreme commander in the Battle of Kápolna. After the defeat he lost his position.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Windisch-Graetz.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="778" data-file-width="530" decoding="async" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/81/Windisch-Graetz.jpg/150px-Windisch-Graetz.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/81/Windisch-Graetz.jpg/225px-Windisch-Graetz.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/81/Windisch-Graetz.jpg/300px-Windisch-Graetz.jpg 2x" width="150"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Windisch-Graetz.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Alfred_I,_Prince_of_Windisch-Gr%C3%A4tz" title="Alfred I, Prince of Windisch-Grätz">Alfred I, Prince of Windisch-Grätz</a>, captain of the Austrian army. During the Winter Campaign,  <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Joseph_Kriehuber" title="Joseph Kriehuber">Joseph Kriehuber</a>'s litrograph (1852)</div></div></div>
<p>Windisch-Grätz saw Schilck army's vulnerable position and the Hungarian army's activity, so he started to move his army towards the River <a href="/wiki/Tisza" title="Tisza">Tisza</a>. On the one hand Windisch-Grätz wanted to stop the Hungarian army, but on the other he wanted to make contact with Schilck, but they only communicated through a messenger.
</p><p>Windisch-Grätz's army had 30,000 men and 165 cannon. The Hungarian-Polish-Italian army had about 43,000 men and 150 cannon, but the supreme commander only counted 36,000 men and 136 cannon. So there was a chance that the Hungarians and Poles would win on numbers, but the sticking point was for Dembiński to close up gather the Hungarian forces. But he did not.
</p><p>On 26 February, at 10 a.m., the Austrian army started to move from <a href="/wiki/Gy%C3%B6ngy%C3%B6s" title="Gyöngyös">Gyöngyös</a> to <a href="/wiki/Tarna" title="Tarna">Tarna</a>, where they met with the Hungarian army. The first Hungarian legion, under the command of <a href="/wiki/Arisztid_Dessewffy" title="Arisztid Dessewffy">Arisztid Dessewffy</a>, remained at <a href="/wiki/Verpel%C3%A9t" title="Verpelét">Verpelét</a>. A small commando from this legion were at <a href="/wiki/Sirok" title="Sirok">Sirok</a>. The arriving Austrian army realised that the Hungarian army was much stronger than they, and after a short fight the Austrian army started to move towards <a href="/wiki/Feldebr%C5%91" title="Feldebrő">Feldebrő</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Battle">Battle</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_K%C3%A1polna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Battle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Demolished_bridges_on_the_River_Tarna">Demolished bridges on the River Tarna</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_K%C3%A1polna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Demolished bridges on the River Tarna">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:P%C3%B6ltenberg_Ern%C5%91.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1200" data-file-width="888" decoding="async" height="203" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/48/P%C3%B6ltenberg_Ern%C5%91.jpg/150px-P%C3%B6ltenberg_Ern%C5%91.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/48/P%C3%B6ltenberg_Ern%C5%91.jpg/225px-P%C3%B6ltenberg_Ern%C5%91.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/48/P%C3%B6ltenberg_Ern%C5%91.jpg/300px-P%C3%B6ltenberg_Ern%C5%91.jpg 2x" width="150"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:P%C3%B6ltenberg_Ern%C5%91.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A lithograph of <a href="/wiki/Ern%C5%91_Poeltenberg" title="Ernő Poeltenberg">Ernő Poeltenberg</a> by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Elek_Szamossy&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Elek Szamossy (page does not exist)">Elek Szamossy</a></div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Ern%C5%91_Poeltenberg" title="Ernő Poeltenberg">Ernő Poeltenberg</a> (German: <i lang="de">Pöltenberg</i>) and his Seventh Legion marched into <a href="/wiki/Feldebr%C5%91" title="Feldebrő">Feldebrő</a> around noon, and at 2 p.m. Poeltenberg moved his legion and crossed the River <a href="/wiki/Tarna" title="Tarna">Tarna</a>. First they fought with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Major_General" title="Major General">Major General</a> <a href="/wiki/Colloredo" title="Colloredo">Colloredo</a> at the forest of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Aldebr%C5%91&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Aldebrő (page does not exist)">Aldebrő</a>. At first neither of the armies could get a decisive victory, but Colloredo got more and more reinforcements, and Poeltenberg had to retreat.
</p><p>Poeltenberg got no news from his messenger, who he had sent to Káplona, so he crossed the Tarna in the middle of Austrian fire. He demolished the bridges on the Tarna, emptied Aldebrő (Lower Debrő) and <a href="/wiki/Feldebr%C5%91" title="Feldebrő">Feldebrő</a> (Upper Debrő) and retreated in the direction of <a href="/wiki/Kerecsend" title="Kerecsend">Kerecsend</a>.  The Austrian army occupied the forest but they did not cross the Tarna, or occupy Aldebrő or Feldebrő.
</p><p>In the meantime Dembiński arrived and gave an order to Poeltenberg to go back to the two villages. The next morning Poeltenberg was stationed at Aldebrő and Feldebrő.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Belated_order">Belated order</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_K%C3%A1polna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Belated order">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Franz_Wyss.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="551" data-file-width="542" decoding="async" height="152" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Franz_Wyss.jpg/150px-Franz_Wyss.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Franz_Wyss.jpg/225px-Franz_Wyss.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Franz_Wyss.jpg/300px-Franz_Wyss.jpg 2x" width="150"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Franz_Wyss.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Franz_Wyss&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Franz Wyss (page does not exist)">Franz Wyss</a>, who died in the Battle of Csorna. Contemporary lithograph</div></div></div>
<p>Colonel <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=J%C3%A1nos_M%C3%A1ri%C3%A1ssy&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="János Máriássy (page does not exist)">János Máriássy</a>’s Driquet brigade, part of the First Hungarian Division, crossed the Tarna at the beginning of the battle and was stationed between the <a href="/wiki/Kompolt" title="Kompolt">Kompolt</a> Forest and the <a href="/wiki/K%C3%A1polna" title="Kápolna">Kápolna</a> vineyard.
</p><p>There was a legion available for Máriássy, and with it he tried a covert attack to the rear of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Franz_Wyss&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Franz Wyss (page does not exist)">Franz Wyss</a>, but it was discovered and repelled. But Máriássy's cavalrymen still managed to blockade the Austrian army. The Hungarian army retreated in the direction of Tarna. The Second Hungarian Legion under the command of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=M%C3%B3ric_Kisfaludy&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Móric Kisfaludy (page does not exist)">Móric Kisfaludy</a> was stationed at <a href="/wiki/K%C3%A1l" title="Kál">Kál</a>. Kisfaludy crossed the river after he heard the first cannon fire and attacked the Austrian army. Even though Kisfaludy's army was weaker than the Austrian army, the Hungarian army defended its position and defeated Wyss.
</p><p>Dembiński was in conference with Görgei in <a href="/wiki/Eger" title="Eger">Eger</a> when he heard the cannon, and they both immediately went to Kápolna. Dembiński gave an order for the rear army to advance, but only two of the legions received the order in time. Thus the legion under the command of Sulcz joined together with the legions at Verpelét, and <a href="/wiki/Lajos_Aulich" title="Lajos Aulich">Lajos Aulich</a>’s legion advanced to Kál.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sirok_Channel">Sirok Channel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_K%C3%A1polna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Sirok Channel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Kriehuber_Franz_Schlick.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1200" data-file-width="555" decoding="async" height="324" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Kriehuber_Franz_Schlick.jpg/150px-Kriehuber_Franz_Schlick.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Kriehuber_Franz_Schlick.jpg/225px-Kriehuber_Franz_Schlick.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Kriehuber_Franz_Schlick.jpg/300px-Kriehuber_Franz_Schlick.jpg 2x" width="150"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Kriehuber_Franz_Schlick.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Franz_Schlik" title="Franz Schlik">Franz Schlik</a>, a lithograph by <a href="/wiki/Josef_Kriehuber" title="Josef Kriehuber">Josef Kriehuber</a></div></div></div>
<p>During the night the Hungarian army's position became worse because Schlik took control of the Sirok <a href="/wiki/Channel_(geography)" title="Channel (geography)">Channel</a> without a fight and he was nearer to the Hungarian army at Verpelét.
</p><p>The battle continued on 27 February. Wyss' main aim was to occupied Kápolna which meant that he would cross through the Hungarian army and destroy its right flank, because he knew that Schlik occupied Sirok canal and was advancing towards the right flank.
</p><p>Klapka had to defend Verpelét with only one division. Dembiński gave an order to Klapka to reoccupy the Sirok Channel and sent reinforcements under the command of Poeltenberg. At the same time Dembiński ordered Görgei to lead the Hungarians at Verpelét and stop the separated divisions of the Austrian army coming together. Poeltenberg had just arrived at Verpelét when Schlik occupied it, and he tried to attack the Austrian army. Klapla, seeing the Hungarian army's success, tried to reoccupy Verpelét, but it wasn't successful.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ineffective_attack">Ineffective attack</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_K%C3%A1polna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Ineffective attack">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Kapolnai_csata-EN.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="415" data-file-width="242" decoding="async" height="343" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/Kapolnai_csata-EN.svg/200px-Kapolnai_csata-EN.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/Kapolnai_csata-EN.svg/300px-Kapolnai_csata-EN.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/Kapolnai_csata-EN.svg/400px-Kapolnai_csata-EN.svg.png 2x" width="200"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Kapolnai_csata-EN.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Battle of Kápolna</div></div></div>
<p>By this time Görgei had arrived in Verpelét. As the commander of the Dessewffy and Poeltenberg legions, Görgei held up Schlik, but he needed to retreat in the direction of <a href="/wiki/Kerecsend" title="Kerecsend">Kerecsend</a> because he did not want the Austrian army to get trapped. Dembiński commanded the Márriássy legion in a retreat from Kápolna eastwards towards the Tarna riverbank and blockaded the bridge too. Máriássy warned Dembiński that defence of Kápolna would be almost impossible, because the Austrian army, who were on the other bank of the river, still controlled Dembiński's side too. Dembiński ignored Máriássy's warning, and started to attack to retake Kápolna.
</p><p>The Hungarian divisions, commanded by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=M%C3%B3ric_Psotta&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Móric Psotta (page does not exist)">Móric Psotta</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=C%C3%A9z%C3%A1r_Medny%C3%A1nszky&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cézár Mednyánszky (page does not exist)">Cézár Mednyánszky</a>, were advancing to the bridgehead, but there they encountered the Austrians and capitulated. Máriássy also attacked the Austrians at a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_house" title="Public house">pub</a> they were occupying in Kápolna. Because Psotta and Mednyánszky capitulated, Wyss could concentrate on Máriássy's army and expelled it from the pub. Máriássy tried to retake, but he had to retreat in the direction of Kerecsend. However, the Austrian attack got stuck here and Windisch-Grätz tried a covert attack against the Hungarians at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=T%C3%B3falu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tófalu (page does not exist)">Tófalu</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Relief_of_supreme_commander">Relief of supreme commander</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_K%C3%A1polna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Relief of supreme commander">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:SzemereBertalan.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="788" data-file-width="568" decoding="async" height="208" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/SzemereBertalan.jpg/150px-SzemereBertalan.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/SzemereBertalan.jpg/225px-SzemereBertalan.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/SzemereBertalan.jpg/300px-SzemereBertalan.jpg 2x" width="150"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:SzemereBertalan.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Bertalan_Szemere" title="Bertalan Szemere">Bertalan Szemere</a></div></div></div>
<p>Dembiński drew back the Hungarian army from Kál in the direction of the eastern bank of the Tarna. On 27 February Aulich's legions defended against the Austrian army, but before he went to reoccupy Kápolna, Aulich received an order from Dembiński to advance north and attack from there. Aulich was advancing there when he received the news that the Austrian army had defeated the Hungarian army at Kál. Because of this Aulich turned back towards Kál, but before he arrived Dembiński ordered him to retreat. At the same time that Guyon and <a href="/wiki/Gy%C3%B6rgy_Kmety" title="György Kmety">György Kmety</a>'s legions arrived to Kerecsend, but Dembiński didn't continue the battle and gave the order for retreat. Klapka's legion's officers didn't want to follow Dembiński's advice unless one of the commanding officers confirmed it. On 3 March <a href="/wiki/Bertalan_Szemere" title="Bertalan Szemere">Bertalan Szemere</a> arrived at <a href="/wiki/Tiszaf%C3%BCred" title="Tiszafüred">Tiszafüred</a> for a debriefing military meeting where he became the intermediary between Dembiński and the Hungarian army. Szemere visited Dembiński and tried to convince him to hold another debriefing. Because Dembiński didn't want to, Szemere relieved him of his command and gave his post to Görgey, who became the supreme commander.
</p>
