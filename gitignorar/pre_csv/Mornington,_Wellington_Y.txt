<p><b>Mornington</b> is a suburb of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wellington,_New_Zealand" title="Wellington, New Zealand">Wellington</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>, on the southern hills behind <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn,_New_Zealand" title="Brooklyn, New Zealand">Brooklyn</a>. It is named after the <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Wellesley,_1st_Duke_of_Wellington" title="Arthur Wellesley, 1st Duke of Wellington">Duke of Wellington</a>'s father, the <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Mornington" title="Earl of Mornington">Earl of Mornington</a>. The original Mornington is in <a href="/wiki/County_Meath" title="County Meath">County Meath</a>, and was the Irish seat of the Duke's father. It was named in 1878 from the time when J.F.E. Wright (1827-1891) subdivided his land in the south-west of the city in partnership with Jacob Joseph, and created the suburbs of Mornington and <a href="/wiki/Vogeltown,_Wellington" title="Vogeltown, Wellington">Vogeltown</a>.
</p><p>According to the <a href="/wiki/2013_New_Zealand_census" title="2013 New Zealand census">2013 New Zealand census</a>, the area including <a href="/wiki/Kingston,_Wellington" title="Kingston, Wellington">Kingston</a> has a population of 2,208, a decrease of 48 people since the 2006 census.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
