<p><b>Christian Henry Pozer</b> (December 26, 1835 – July 18, 1884) was a <a href="/wiki/Canadians" title="Canadians">Canadian</a> lawyer and politician.
</p><p>Born in St-Georges d'Aubert Gallion (now <a href="/wiki/Saint-Georges,_Quebec" title="Saint-Georges, Quebec">St-Georges-de-Beauce</a>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beauce_(Quebec)" title="Beauce (Quebec)">Beauce</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lower_Canada" title="Lower Canada">Lower Canada</a> (now <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>), the son of William Pozer and Ann Milbourne, he was called to the Lower Canada bar in 1860.
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/List_of_elections_in_the_Province_of_Canada" title="List of elections in the Province of Canada">1863</a>, he ran as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_of_Quebec" title="Liberal Party of Quebec">Liberal</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Legislative_Assembly_of_the_Province_of_Canada" title="Legislative Assembly of the Province of Canada">Legislative Assembly of the Province of Canada</a> in the riding of <a href="/wiki/Beauce_(provincial_electoral_district)" title="Beauce (provincial electoral district)">Beauce</a> and was defeated. He was elected in <a href="/wiki/1867_Quebec_general_election" title="1867 Quebec general election">1867</a> and re-elected in <a href="/wiki/1871_Quebec_general_election" title="1871 Quebec general election">1871</a>. He resigned in 1874.
</p><p>At the same time, he was elected to the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Commons_of_Canada" title="House of Commons of Canada">House of Commons of Canada</a> for the riding of Beauce in <a href="/wiki/1867_Canadian_federal_election" title="1867 Canadian federal election">1867</a>. A <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_of_Canada" title="Liberal Party of Canada">Liberal</a> he was re-elected in <a href="/wiki/1872_Canadian_federal_election" title="1872 Canadian federal election">1872</a> and <a href="/wiki/1874_Canadian_federal_election" title="1874 Canadian federal election">1874</a>.
</p><p>He resigned from the House of Commons when he was appointed a <a href="/wiki/Senate_of_Canada" title="Senate of Canada">Senator</a> in 1876 representing the senatorial division of Lauzon, Quebec. He died in office in 1884 and was buried in the Pozer Cemeterey in <a href="/wiki/Saint-Georges,_Quebec" title="Saint-Georges, Quebec">St-Georges-de-Beauce</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
