<p><i><b>Nations and Nationalism</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Peer_review" title="Peer review">peer-reviewed</a> <a href="/wiki/Academic_journal" title="Academic journal">academic journal</a> that covers research on <a href="/wiki/Nationalism" title="Nationalism">nationalism</a> and related issues. It is published quarterly on behalf of the <a href="/wiki/Association_for_the_Study_of_Ethnicity_and_Nationalism" title="Association for the Study of Ethnicity and Nationalism">Association for the Study of Ethnicity and Nationalism</a> by <a href="/wiki/Wiley-Blackwell" title="Wiley-Blackwell">Wiley-Blackwell</a>. <a href="/wiki/Anthony_D._Smith" title="Anthony D. Smith">Anthony D. Smith</a> was the founding editor and the editor until his death in 2016.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aims">Aims</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nations_and_Nationalism_(journal)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Aims">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first issue of <i>Nations and Nationalism</i> was published in March 1995. In their <a href="/wiki/Editorial" title="Editorial">editorial</a> for that issue, Anthony D. Smith, Obi Igwara, Athena Leoussi, and Terry Mulhall described the need for a journal devoted to the study of nations and nationalism, and identified the three basic aims of the journal as "(1) to be the vehicle of new research, both theoretical and empirical, and act as a forum for the exchange of views in the field; (2) to identify and develop a separate subject-area as a field of study in its own right, and unify the body of scholars in the field; [and] (3) to bring to the attention of the wider scholarly community, and the public, the need to treat the subject-area as a well-defined field of interdisciplinary study, which requires the collaboration of scholars from a variety of intellectual backgrounds".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Abstracting_and_indexing">Abstracting and indexing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nations_and_Nationalism_(journal)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Abstracting and indexing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Nations and Nationalism</i> is abstracted and indexed in <a href="/wiki/EBSCO_Industries" title="EBSCO Industries">Academic Search Premier</a>, <a href="/wiki/CSA_(database_company)" title="CSA (database company)">CSA Biological Sciences</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environmental_Sciences_%26_Pollution_Management" title="Environmental Sciences &amp; Pollution Management">Environmental Sciences &amp; Pollution Management</a>, <a href="/wiki/Current_Contents" title="Current Contents">Current Contents</a>/Social &amp; Behavioral Sciences, <a href="/wiki/EBSCO_Industries" title="EBSCO Industries">EBSCO Sociological Collection</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GEOBASE_(database)" title="GEOBASE (database)">GEOBASE</a>, <a href="/wiki/International_Bibliography_of_the_Social_Sciences" title="International Bibliography of the Social Sciences">International Bibliography of the Social Sciences</a>, <a href="/wiki/ProQuest" title="ProQuest">ProQuest</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scopus" title="Scopus">Scopus</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Social_Sciences_Citation_Index" title="Social Sciences Citation Index">Social Sciences Citation Index</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>According to the <i><a href="/wiki/Journal_Citation_Reports" title="Journal Citation Reports">Journal Citation Reports</a></i>, the journal has a 2014 <a href="/wiki/Impact_factor" title="Impact factor">impact factor</a> of 0.444, ranking it 12th out of 15 journals in the category "Ethnic Studies", 22nd out of 87 journals in the category "History", 107th out of 142 journals in the category "Sociology" and 111th out of 161 journals in the category "Political Science".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WoS_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-WoS-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
