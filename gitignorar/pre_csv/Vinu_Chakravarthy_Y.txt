<p><b>Vinu Chakravarthee</b> (15 December 1945 – 27 April 2017)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> was an Indian actor, script writer and director who is known for acting in more than 1000 movies in <a href="/wiki/Tamil_language" title="Tamil language">Tamil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Telugu_language" title="Telugu language">Telugu</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kannada_language" title="Kannada language">Kannada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Badaga_language" title="Badaga language">Badaga</a> and <a href="/wiki/Malayalam" title="Malayalam">Malayalam</a> mostly as a comedian, supporting actor or in a villainous role. The 2007 released Tamil movie <i><a href="/wiki/Muni_(film)" title="Muni (film)">Muni</a></i> was his landmark 1000th movie.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He had been hospitalised for the last few months of his life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vinu_Chakravarthy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Chakravarthy was born on 15 December 1945, in <a href="/wiki/Usilampatti" title="Usilampatti">Usilampatti</a> to Adimoola Thevar and Manjuvani Ammal. He studied at Wesley School, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Royapettah" title="Royapettah">Royapettah</a> and graduated in <a href="/wiki/Commerce" title="Commerce">commerce</a> from <a href="/wiki/A._M._Jain_College" title="A. M. Jain College">A. M. Jain College</a>. He was eldest son among other 3 sons and a daughter.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vinu_Chakravarthy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On completion of his education, Chakravarthy worked as a Reserve <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sub-Inspector" title="Sub-Inspector">Sub-Inspector</a> at the Ice House <a href="/wiki/Police_station" title="Police station">police station</a> for six months before joining the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southern_Railway_Zone_(India)" title="Southern Railway Zone (India)">Southern Railways</a> where he worked for four years.
</p><p>Chakravarthy was working as a script writer for <a href="/wiki/Kannada" title="Kannada">Kannada</a> director <a href="/wiki/Puttanna_Kanagal" title="Puttanna Kanagal">Puttanna Kanagal</a> when he was noticed by film producer Tiruppur Mani who, in 1977, gave him a role in the Kannada film <i><a href="/wiki/Parasangada_Gendethimma" title="Parasangada Gendethimma">Parasangada Gendethimma</a></i> which was later remade into Tamil as <i><a href="/wiki/Rosappu_Ravikkaikari" title="Rosappu Ravikkaikari">Rosappu Ravikkaikari</a></i> (1979) with Chakravarthy returning. Since then, Chakravarthy has made over 1,000 films in four South Indian languages - 900 in Tamil, 30 in Malayalam, 5 in Telugu and one in Badaga language. He has also directed movies on his own and is credited with introducing <a href="/wiki/Silk_Smitha" title="Silk Smitha">Silk Smitha</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-toi_20110221_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-toi_20110221-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vinu_Chakravarthy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Controversies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Chakravarthy criticised television and film producer <a href="/wiki/Ekta_Kapoor" title="Ekta Kapoor">Ekta Kapoor</a> for her depiction of <a href="/wiki/Silk_Smitha" title="Silk Smitha">Silk Smitha</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dirty_Picture" title="The Dirty Picture">The Dirty Picture</a></i>, a biographical film on the actress. He also expressed displeasure over Kapoor's choice of casting. He also said Smitha had wide expressive, inviting eyes, which <a href="/wiki/Vidya_Balan" title="Vidya Balan">Vidya Balan</a> does not have.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-toi_20110221_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-toi_20110221-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
