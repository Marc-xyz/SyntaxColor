<p>The <b>Men's time trial</b> of the <a href="/wiki/2010_UCI_Road_World_Championships" title="2010 UCI Road World Championships">2010 UCI Road World Championships</a> cycling event took place on 30 September in <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geelong,_Victoria" title="Geelong, Victoria">Geelong</a>), <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, the first time in <a href="/wiki/UCI_Road_World_Championships" title="UCI Road World Championships">the event's</a> 77 times that it has been held in Australia.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Fabian_Cancellara" title="Fabian Cancellara">Fabian Cancellara</a> defended his title, and in doing so, clinched a record fourth time trial world championship, recording a time more than a minute faster than any of his rivals. <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>'s <a href="/wiki/David_Millar" title="David Millar">David Millar</a> took silver, with <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Tony_Martin_(cyclist)" title="Tony Martin (cyclist)">Tony Martin</a> claiming bronze. The two Australian entrants, <a href="/wiki/Richie_Porte" title="Richie Porte">Richie Porte</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Rogers_(cyclist)" title="Michael Rogers (cyclist)">Michael Rogers</a>, came in fourth and fifth respectively in their home country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Route">Route</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_UCI_Road_World_Championships_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_time_trial&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Route">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
