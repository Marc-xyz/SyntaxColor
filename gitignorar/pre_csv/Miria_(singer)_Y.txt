<p><b>Miria</b> is a Los Angeles-based singer-songwriter. She is also one of the co-producers and directors of the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Women%27s_Music_Festival" title="Los Angeles Women's Music Festival">Los Angeles Women's Music Festival</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miria_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Los Angeles based singer-songwriter Miria has released the album, <i>Under the Surface</i> and several singles. Her song <i>Break Your Heart</i>, from her album, won second place in the DBSA Facing Us 2008 Song Contest,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Her song, <i>Lullabye of Mars</i>, won second place at the 2004 <a href="/wiki/Mars_Society" title="Mars Society">Mars Society</a> Roget de Lisle song contest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2007 Miria and <a href="/wiki/Gayle_Day" title="Gayle Day">Gayle Day</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Gilli_Moon" title="Gilli Moon">Gilli Moon</a>'s <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Warrior_Girl_Music&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Warrior Girl Music (page does not exist)">Warrior Girl Music</a>, co-produced the Los Angeles Women's Music Festival, the first festival of its kind in the Los Angeles area.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Trivia">Trivia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miria_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Trivia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In addition to being a singer-songwriter, Miria is also a <a href="/wiki/User_experience_design" title="User experience design">UI/UX Designer</a> and former employee of the <a href="/wiki/Free_Software_Foundation" title="Free Software Foundation">Free Software Foundation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> She also performed at the <a href="/wiki/Car_Talk" title="Car Talk">Car Talk</a> anniversary show as a member of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Museum_School_Cheerleaders" title="Museum School Cheerleaders">Museum School Cheerleaders</a>.
</p>
