	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">African Regional Intellectual Property Organization</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:African_Regional_Intellectual_Property_Organization.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="628" data-file-width="1357" decoding="async" height="139" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/African_Regional_Intellectual_Property_Organization.png/300px-African_Regional_Intellectual_Property_Organization.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/African_Regional_Intellectual_Property_Organization.png/450px-African_Regional_Intellectual_Property_Organization.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/African_Regional_Intellectual_Property_Organization.png/600px-African_Regional_Intellectual_Property_Organization.png 2x" width="300"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:African_Regional_Intellectual_Property_Organization.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Map of current ARIPO members in blue, nations with observer status in green.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The text near this tag is dated. (November 2010)">needs update</span></a></i>]</sup></div></div></div>
<p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">African</a> Regional <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intellectual_Property" title="Intellectual Property">Intellectual Property</a> Organization</b> (<b>ARIPO</b>), formerly <b>African Regional Industrial Property Organization</b>, is an intergovernmental organization for cooperation among African states in <a href="/wiki/Patent" title="Patent">patent</a> and other intellectual property matters. ARIPO was established by the Lusaka Agreement<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AripoTreaties_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-AripoTreaties-1">[1]</a></sup> of 1976. It has the capacity to hear applications for patents and registered <a href="/wiki/Trademark" title="Trademark">trademarks</a> in its member states who are parties to the Harare (patents), Banjul (marks) and Arusha (<a href="/wiki/Plant_variety_(law)" title="Plant variety (law)">plant varieties</a>) protocols.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AripoTreaties_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-AripoTreaties-1">[1]</a></sup> ARIPO also features a protocol on the protection of <a href="/wiki/Traditional_knowledge" title="Traditional knowledge">traditional knowledge</a>, the Swakopmund Protocol,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AripoTreaties_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-AripoTreaties-1">[1]</a></sup> signed in 2010 by 9 member states of the organization which entered into force on May 11, 2015, and was amended on December 6, 2016.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>ARIPO has the <a href="/wiki/World_Intellectual_Property_Organization" title="World Intellectual Property Organization">WIPO</a> ST.3 code AP. Its 19 member states<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> are mostly <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>-speaking countries. Rwanda became the 18th member state on March 24, 2010,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rwanda-ARIPO_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rwanda-ARIPO-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rwanda-PCT_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rwanda-PCT-4">[4]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Tom%C3%A9_and_Pr%C3%ADncipe" title="São Tomé and Príncipe">São Tomé and Príncipe</a> on May 19, 2014 (the Harare Protocol entered into force on August 19, 2014 with respect to São Tomé and Príncipe).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>The name of the organization changed from <i>African Regional Industrial Property Organization</i> to <i>African Regional Intellectual Property Organization</i> in 2005.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organs">Organs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=African_Regional_Intellectual_Property_Organization&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Organs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>ARIPO’s organisational structure consists of four organs: the Council of Ministers, the Administrative Council, the Secretariat, and the Board of Appeal.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rights_covered">Rights covered</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=African_Regional_Intellectual_Property_Organization&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Rights covered">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Together, the two protocols constituting the organisation cover <a href="/wiki/Copyright" title="Copyright">copyright</a>, <a href="/wiki/Industrial_design" title="Industrial design">industrial design</a>, <a href="/wiki/Patent" title="Patent">patent</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trademark" title="Trademark">trademark</a>, <a href="/wiki/Traditional_knowledge" title="Traditional knowledge">traditional knowledge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Utility_model" title="Utility model">utility model</a> rights.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
