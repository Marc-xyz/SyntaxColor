	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Joseph L. Ullman</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Joseph Leonard Ullman</b> (30 January 1923, <a href="/wiki/Buffalo,_New_York" title="Buffalo, New York">Buffalo, New York</a> – 11 September 1995, <a href="/wiki/Chelsea,_Michigan" title="Chelsea, Michigan">Chelsea, Michigan</a>) was a mathematician who worked on <a href="/wiki/Mathematical_analysis" title="Mathematical analysis">classical analysis</a> with a focus on <a href="/wiki/Approximation_theory" title="Approximation theory">approximation theory</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Ullman received his A.B. from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_Buffalo" title="University of Buffalo">University of Buffalo</a> and his graduate study was interrupted by service in the U.S. Army in World War II. He was injured, received a <a href="/wiki/Purple_Heart" title="Purple Heart">Purple Heart</a>, and spent the rest of the war as a mathematics instructor. He received a Ph.D. in 1949 from <a href="/wiki/Stanford_University" title="Stanford University">Stanford University</a> with thesis <i>Studies on Faber Polynomials</i> under the direction of <a href="/wiki/G%C3%A1bor_Szeg%C5%91" title="Gábor Szegő">Gábor Szegő</a>. Ullman became an instructor at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Michigan" title="University of Michigan">University of Michigan</a> in 1949, an assistant professor in 1954, an associate professor in 1962, and a professor in 1966.
</p><p>He wrote forty-three research papers. During his career at the University of Michigan he supervised eleven doctoral theses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
