<p><b>Jam Raidhan</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Urdu_language" title="Urdu language">Urdu</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ur"><b>جام رائےدھن</b></span>‎). On <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jam_Sikandar" title="Jam Sikandar">Sikandar</a>’s death, Raidhan became the 13th Jam (Sultan) of Sindh. He ruled the <a href="/wiki/Sindh" title="Sindh">Sindh</a> from 1444-1453 A.D, from the ocean to Kajur, Mullee, and Khoondee, the boundaries of Matheluh, and Oobawruh.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto-1">[1]</a></sup> Historians have different opinions about Jam Raidhan, many have argued that Jam Raidhan is no other than <a href="/wiki/Jam_Sanjar" title="Jam Sanjar">Jam Sanjar</a> and they two are not different personalities but the two names for the single ruler of Sind.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto1-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jam_Raidhan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the time of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jam_Tughluq&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jam Tughluq (page does not exist)">Jam Tughluq</a>, he lived in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kutch" title="Kutch">Kutch</a>. When the news of the decease of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jam_Sikandar" title="Jam Sikandar">Jam Sikandar</a> reached him, taking many men, he went to <a href="/wiki/Thatta" title="Thatta">Thatta</a>, and collecting the men of that city, he said: “I have not come to take the country, but I have come to save the property of the Musulmans. I do not consider myself fit to be king; he whom you consider fit for the situation, make him king, and I will be the first of any to give him the hand of homage.” Amongst those men there was no one fit to be king, so all of them agreeing, placed him on the throne of the kingdom. In the course of one year and a half, he pulled beneath his rule the whole of Sind, from the ocean to Kajur, Mullee, and Khoondee, the boundaries of Matheluh, and Oobawruh.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-auto-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Jam_Sanjar_and_Jam_Raidhan">Jam Sanjar and Jam Raidhan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jam_Raidhan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Jam Sanjar and Jam Raidhan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The accounts of earlier historians <a href="/wiki/Mir_Ali_Sher_Qaune_Thattvi" title="Mir Ali Sher Qaune Thattvi">Mir Ali Sher Qaune Thattvi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Masum_Shah" title="Masum Shah">Mir Muhammad Masoom Shah Bakhri</a> has placed Jam Rai Dinu (Raidhan) and Jam Sanjar as two different rulers of Samaa dynasty with Jam Rai Dinu predecing Jam Sanjar in the history of Samaa rule over Sind. Jam Sanjar was also entitled as Sultan Saddarudin Shah. According to the commentary of remarkable historian of 20th century Mr. M.H Panhwar over the rule of Samaa dynasty he sujests the name Jam Raidhan for Jam Rai Dinu as it is being used in the folklores of Sind and the Samaat tribes of Sind still name their children with the same name.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto1_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-auto1-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jam_Raidhan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jam Roydhun was drinking wine in private, when <a href="/wiki/Jam_Sanjar" title="Jam Sanjar">Jam Sanjar</a>, putting some poison in a bottle, gave it into the hands of one of his attendants, three days after drinking which Jam Roydhun died.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-auto-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
