<p><b>Mimi Lerner</b> (May 20, 1945 — March 29, 2007) was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polish-American" title="Polish-American">Polish-American</a> <a href="/wiki/Mezzo-soprano" title="Mezzo-soprano">mezzo-soprano</a> and later head of the voice department at <a href="/wiki/Carnegie_Mellon_University" title="Carnegie Mellon University">Carnegie Mellon University</a>.
</p><p>Lerner was born <b>Emilia Lipczer</b> in <a href="/wiki/Sambir" title="Sambir">Sambir, Ukraine</a>,  in 1945 to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jewish" title="Jewish">Jewish</a> parents who hid in the woods to avoid <a href="/wiki/The_Holocaust" title="The Holocaust">Nazi persecution</a> until she was one. They then moved to <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> and later to <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">the Bronx</a>. She graduated from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queens_College" title="Queens College">Queens College</a> with a bachelor's degree in <a href="/wiki/Music_education" title="Music education">music education</a>. She was teaching in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pittsburgh,_Pennsylvania" title="Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania">Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania</a> while earning a master's degree at Carnegie Mellon. What started as a singing hobby led to her debut at the <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_Opera" title="New York City Opera">New York City Opera</a> in 1979, singing Sextus in <i><a href="/wiki/La_clemenza_di_Tito" title="La clemenza di Tito">La clemenza di Tito</a></i>.  Later NYCO assignments included Adalgisa in <i><a href="/wiki/Norma_(opera)" title="Norma (opera)">Norma</a></i>, Bradamante in <i><a href="/wiki/Alcina" title="Alcina">Alcina</a></i>, Smeton in <i><a href="/wiki/Anna_Bolena" title="Anna Bolena">Anna Bolena</a></i>, and leading roles in the <i>Central Park</i> trilogy (which consists of <a href="/wiki/Deborah_Drattell" title="Deborah Drattell">Deborah Drattell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wendy_Wasserstein" title="Wendy Wasserstein">Wendy Wasserstein</a>'s <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=The_Festival_of_Regrets&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="The Festival of Regrets (page does not exist)">The Festival of Regrets</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Torke" title="Michael Torke">Michael Torke</a> and <a href="/wiki/A._R._Gurney" title="A. R. Gurney">A. R. Gurney</a>'s <i>Strawberry Fields</i>, and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Beaser" title="Robert Beaser">Robert Beaser</a> and <a href="/wiki/Terrence_McNally" title="Terrence McNally">Terrence McNally</a>'s <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=The_Food_of_Love&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="The Food of Love (page does not exist)">The Food of Love</a></i>).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>Since the early 1980s she was a regular guest artist with opera companies throughout the United States, including the <a href="/wiki/Dallas_Opera" title="Dallas Opera">Dallas Opera</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Glimmerglass_Opera" title="Glimmerglass Opera">Glimmerglass Opera</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Grand_Opera" title="Houston Grand Opera">Houston Grand Opera</a>, the <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy_Center_for_the_Performing_Arts" title="John F. Kennedy Center for the Performing Arts">John F. Kennedy Center for the Performing Arts</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Opera" title="Metropolitan Opera">Metropolitan Opera</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Opera_Theater_of_Pittsburgh" title="Opera Theater of Pittsburgh">Opera Theater of Pittsburgh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Opera" title="Pittsburgh Opera">Pittsburgh Opera</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Santa_Fe_Opera" title="Santa Fe Opera">Santa Fe Opera</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Opera" title="Seattle Opera">Seattle Opera</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Washington_National_Opera" title="Washington National Opera">Washington National Opera</a>. She appeared on the international stage at <a href="/wiki/La_Scala" title="La Scala">La Scala</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Th%C3%A9%C3%A2tre_du_Ch%C3%A2telet" title="Théâtre du Châtelet">Théâtre du Châtelet</a>, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Glyndebourne_Festival" title="Glyndebourne Festival">Glyndebourne Festival</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mimi_Lerner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She died in the Pittsburgh neighborhood of <a href="/wiki/Oakland_(Pittsburgh)" title="Oakland (Pittsburgh)">Oakland</a> from complications of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heart_tumor" title="Heart tumor">heart tumor</a>, which had been diagnosed a dozen years earlier. She was 61 years old.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytobit_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-nytobit-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
