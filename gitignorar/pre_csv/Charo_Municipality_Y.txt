	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Charo Municipality</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Charo</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Mexico" title="Municipalities of Mexico">municipality</a> located in the <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexican</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mexican_state" title="Mexican state">state</a> of <a href="/wiki/Michoac%C3%A1n" title="Michoacán">Michoacán</a>. The municipality has an area of 322.50  square kilometres (0.29% of the surface of the state) and is bordered to the north by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tar%C3%ADmbaro_(municipality)" title="Tarímbaro (municipality)">Tarímbaro</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C3%81lvaro_Obreg%C3%B3n_(municipality)" title="Álvaro Obregón (municipality)">Álvaro Obregón</a>, to the east by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Indaparapeo_(municipality)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Indaparapeo (municipality) (page does not exist)">Indaparapeo</a>, to the south by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tzitz%C3%ADo_(municipality)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tzitzío (municipality) (page does not exist)">Tzitzío</a>, and to the west and southwest by <a href="/wiki/Morelia" title="Morelia">Morelia</a>.  The municipality had a population of 19,417 inhabitants according to the 2005 census.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>  Its municipal seat is the city of the same name.
</p><p>The economy of the municipality is mostly based on ranching and agriculture, with maize, carrots and beans the main crops.
</p><p>In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pre-Hispanic" title="Pre-Hispanic">pre-Hispanic</a> times the area was inhabited by the Matlaltzingas, who were allies of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pur%C3%A9pecha_people" title="Purépecha people">Purépecha</a>, the dominant force in the region at the time.
</p>
