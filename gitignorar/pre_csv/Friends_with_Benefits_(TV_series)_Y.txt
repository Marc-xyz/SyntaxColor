<p><i><b>Friends with Benefits</b></i> (stylized as <b>friends ♥♥ith benefits</b>) is an American <a href="/wiki/Romantic_comedy" title="Romantic comedy">romantic</a> <a href="/wiki/Sitcom" title="Sitcom">sitcom</a> television series created by <a href="/wiki/Scott_Neustadter" title="Scott Neustadter">Scott Neustadter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_H._Weber" title="Michael H. Weber">Michael H. Weber</a> that aired on <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> from August 5 to September 9, 2011. It was originally set to air on  as a <a href="/wiki/Mid-season_replacement" title="Mid-season replacement">mid-season replacement</a> during the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_United_States_network_television_schedule" title="2010–11 United States network television schedule">2010–11 television season</a>, but was ultimately delayed until summer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The series ended after twelve episodes aired with the thirteenth being only available on <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a>, Amazon.com, and <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Friends_with_Benefits_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The series follows a group of friends living in Chicago. It involves a very close friendship between two of the friends, who are each looking for the perfect mate, and the others who question their relationship, but at the same time, have their own romantic problems to deal with.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cast">Cast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Friends_with_Benefits_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Cast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Ryan_Hansen" title="Ryan Hansen">Ryan Hansen</a> as Ben Lewis, an office drone who is in no rush to find the right girl for him. He has been sleeping with his friend, Sara.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Danneel_Harris" title="Danneel Harris">Danneel Harris</a> as Sara Maxwell, an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/OB/GYN" title="OB/GYN">OB/GYN</a> and Ben's closest friend. Unlike Ben, she is desperate to find the right guy.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jessica_Lucas" title="Jessica Lucas">Jessica Lucas</a> as Riley Elliot, a bartender and Sara's roommate.  She is laid-back and lives a very casual love life.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Zach_Cregger" title="Zach Cregger">Zach Cregger</a> as Aaron Greenway, a rich, nerdy engineer who wants to meet women but has trouble interacting with them.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Andre_Holland" title="Andre Holland">Andre Holland</a> as Julian 'Fitz' Fitzgerald, Aaron's roommate. He takes it upon himself to help Aaron with women and keep the group on good terms with each other.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production_and_development">Production and development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Friends_with_Benefits_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production and development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Friends with Benefits</i> was originally developed for <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> with a script order given by the network in September 2009. However, the network did not <a href="/wiki/Green-light" title="Green-light">green-light</a> a pilot for the potential series, allowing <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> to pick up the project.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> For the pilot episode, <a href="/wiki/Fran_Kranz" title="Fran Kranz">Fran Kranz</a> was originally cast as Aaron,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EW0516_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-EW0516-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TV0521_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-TV0521-4">[4]</a></sup> and Ian Reed Kesler portrayed Hoon (another version of Fitz).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EW0516_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-EW0516-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TV0521_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-TV0521-4">[4]</a></sup>  The series was officially picked up on May 16, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
