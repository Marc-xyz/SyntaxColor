	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">John Langley (bishop)</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>John Douse Langley</b> was the second <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bishop_of_Bendigo" title="Bishop of Bendigo">Bishop of Bendigo</a> from 1907 to 1920.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Ballyduff,_County_Waterford" title="Ballyduff, County Waterford">Ballyduff, County Waterford</a> on 17 May 1836 he was educated at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trinity_College,_Dublin" title="Trinity College, Dublin">Trinity College, Dublin</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>  and at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moore_College" title="Moore College">Moore College</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales" title="New South Wales">New South Wales</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>He came to Australia with his family in 1853,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and after an earlier career with the <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_Australasia" title="Bank of Australasia">Bank of Australasia</a> was ordained in 1873. He held <a href="/wiki/Incumbent_(ecclesiastical)" title="Incumbent (ecclesiastical)">incumbencies</a> in <a href="/wiki/Berrima,_New_South_Wales" title="Berrima, New South Wales">Berrima</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a> before becoming <a href="/wiki/Archdeacon" title="Archdeacon">Archdeacon</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cumberland_County,_New_South_Wales" title="Cumberland County, New South Wales">Cumberland</a> in 1895 and a <a href="/wiki/Canon_(priest)" title="Canon (priest)">Canon</a> of <a href="/wiki/St_Andrew%27s_Cathedral,_Sydney" title="St Andrew's Cathedral, Sydney">St Andrew's Cathedral, Sydney</a> in  1902.
</p><p>Dr. Langley succeeded his younger brother <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Henry_Archdall_Langley" title="Henry Archdall Langley">Henry Archdall Langley</a> (1844–1906) who was the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bishop_of_Bendigo" title="Bishop of Bendigo">Bishop of Bendigo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
He died on 8 November 1930.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
