<p><b>Lalsawma</b> is an Indian social worker, writer and a Christian missionary and Pastor of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mizoram_Presbyterian_Church" title="Mizoram Presbyterian Church">Mizoram Presbyterian Church</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mizoram_-_the_land_of_over_80_religious_sects_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mizoram_-_the_land_of_over_80_religious_sects-1">[1]</a></sup> His contributions are reported in the reconciliation efforts between the Government of India and the <a href="/wiki/Mizo_National_Front" title="Mizo National Front">Mizo National Front</a> (MNF) on two occasions. In the wake of the <a href="/wiki/March_1966_Mizo_National_Front_uprising" title="March 1966 Mizo National Front uprising">March 1966 Mizo National Front uprising</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Mizoram_Presbyterian_Church_Synod" title="Mizoram Presbyterian Church Synod">Mizoram Presbyterian Church Synod</a> appointed missionaries for peace talks with MNF in 1969<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mirzoram,_Dimensions_and_Perspectives:_Society,_Economy,_and_Polit_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mirzoram,_Dimensions_and_Perspectives:_Society,_Economy,_and_Polit-2">[2]</a></sup> and Lalsawma was one the two missionaries who contacted the MNF leaders.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Church_Role_in_the_Mizoram_Peace_Accord_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Church_Role_in_the_Mizoram_Peace_Accord-3">[3]</a></sup> In 1982, he was again a member of the three-men delegation who held talks with MNF which, untimaltely, led to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mizo_Accord" title="Mizo Accord">Mizo Accord</a> of 1986.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Mizo_Uprising:_Assam_Assembly_Debates_on_the_Mizo_Movement,_1966-1971_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Mizo_Uprising:_Assam_Assembly_Debates_on_the_Mizo_Movement,_1966-1971-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ethnic_Life-Worlds_in_North-East_India:_An_Analysis_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ethnic_Life-Worlds_in_North-East_India:_An_Analysis-5">[5]</a></sup> He has written several articles<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mizoram_The_Dagger_Brigade_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mizoram_The_Dagger_Brigade-6">[6]</a></sup> and is the author of the book, <i>Four Decades of Revivals, the Mizo Way</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Four_Decades_of_Revivals,_the_Mizo_Way_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Four_Decades_of_Revivals,_the_Mizo_Way-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Christianity_in_India:_Two_Thousand_Years_of_Faith_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Christianity_in_India:_Two_Thousand_Years_of_Faith-8">[8]</a></sup> The Government of India awarded him the fourth highest civilian honour of the <a href="/wiki/Padma_Shri" title="Padma Shri">Padma Shri</a>, in 2005, for his contributions to Indian society.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Padma_Awards_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Padma_Awards-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
