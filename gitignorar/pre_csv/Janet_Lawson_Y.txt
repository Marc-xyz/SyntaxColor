	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Janet Lawson</h1>

<p><b>Janet Lawson</b> (born Janet Ann Polun; November 13, 1940, in Baltimore, Maryland) is a jazz singer and educator. Her primary influences as a singer were saxophonists such as <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Parker" title="Charlie Parker">Charlie Parker</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lester_Young" title="Lester Young">Lester Young</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sonny_Rollins" title="Sonny Rollins">Sonny Rollins</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Her voice was described by jazz critic <a href="/wiki/John_S._Wilson_(music_critic)" title="John S. Wilson (music critic)">John S. Wilson</a> in a 1977 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_Times" title="New York Times">New York Times</a> article entitled <i>Janet Lawson: The Dream Jazz Voice</i> writing that she  "has the kind of voice that most jazz singers probably wish they had. It is a full, well‐developed, remarkably pliant voice with a lower range whose dark sonorities compare favorably with the deep power of Sarah Vaughan, and a high register in which she does not have to strain to project very fast, often complex, lines."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Janet_Lawson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lawson was born in Baltimore to a Jewish father and Catholic mother from Eastern Europe. Her father was a jazz drummer and her mother was a singer and lyricist who sometimes sang in her father's band. At home, they worked on songs together at the piano. She performed on the radio and regional television as a child. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Lawson began singing with a local big band in her teens. When she was eighteen, she moved to New York City and got a job as a secretary at <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Records" title="Columbia Records">Columbia Records</a>. Lawson appeared regularly on <a href="/wiki/Steve_Allen" title="Steve Allen">Steve Allen</a>'s television show (1968-1969) and worked in theater.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> She lived across the street from Al Jeter, the head of <a href="/wiki/Riverside_Records" title="Riverside Records">Riverside Records</a>, and made contacts when she attended parties at his penthouse apartment. She went to jazz clubs and was inspired by seeing <a href="/wiki/Thelonious_Monk" title="Thelonious Monk">Thelonious Monk</a>. She made her debut at the <a href="/wiki/Village_Vanguard" title="Village Vanguard">Village Vanguard</a> with <a href="/wiki/Art_Farmer" title="Art Farmer">Art Farmer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Weeks_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Weeks-5">[5]</a></sup> In 1976, Lawson formed the Janet Lawson Quintet, which in 1983 included <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Rosenberg&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Roger Rosenberg (page does not exist)">Roger Rosenberg</a>, a saxophonist and flutist, <a href="/wiki/Bill_O%27Connell" title="Bill O'Connell">Bill O'Connell</a>, piano, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ratzo_Harris&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ratzo Harris (page does not exist)">Ratzo Harris</a>, bass, and <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Madison_(musician)" title="Jimmy Madison (musician)">Jimmy Madison</a>, drums. Lawson became known as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scat_singer" title="Scat singer">scat singer</a> and improviser.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Weeks_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Weeks-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> 
</p><p>Lawson has worked with <a href="/wiki/Art_Farmer" title="Art Farmer">Art Farmer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chick_Corea" title="Chick Corea">Chick Corea</a>,  <a href="/wiki/Ron_Carter" title="Ron Carter">Ron Carter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bob_Dorough" title="Bob Dorough">Bob Dorough</a>, <a href="/wiki/Duke_Ellington" title="Duke Ellington">Duke Ellington</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Flanagan" title="Tommy Flanagan">Tommy Flanagan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sheila_Jordan" title="Sheila Jordan">Sheila Jordan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barry_Harris" title="Barry Harris">Barry Harris</a>, <a href="/wiki/Milt_Hinton" title="Milt Hinton">Milt Hinton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Jefferson" title="Eddie Jefferson">Eddie Jefferson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barney_Kessel" title="Barney Kessel">Barney Kessel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dave_Liebman" title="Dave Liebman">Dave Liebman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joe_Newman_(trumpeter)" title="Joe Newman (trumpeter)">Joe Newman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rufus_Reid" title="Rufus Reid">Rufus Reid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Clark_Terry" title="Clark Terry">Clark Terry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ed_Thigpen" title="Ed Thigpen">Ed Thigpen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cedar_Walton" title="Cedar Walton">Cedar Walton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Duke_Pearson" title="Duke Pearson">Duke Pearson</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=David_Lahm&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="David Lahm (page does not exist)">David Lahm</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tatu_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tatu-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>In the early 2000s, she was diagnosed with <a href="/wiki/Lyme_disease" title="Lyme disease">Lyme disease</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bell%27s_palsy" title="Bell's palsy">Bell's palsy</a>, suffering damage to her vocal cords. She has taught voice at <a href="/wiki/New_York_University" title="New York University">New York University</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_New_School" title="The New School">the New School</a>, given private lessons, taught elementary school children, and has made trips every year to Latvia to attend a youth music camp.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Weeks_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-Weeks-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
