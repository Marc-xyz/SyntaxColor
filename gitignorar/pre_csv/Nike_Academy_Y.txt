<p>The <b>Nike Football Academy</b> was an <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> <a href="/wiki/Youth_system" title="Youth system">football academy</a> funded and administered by <a href="/wiki/Nike,_Inc." title="Nike, Inc.">Nike, Inc.</a>. The academy had a revolving squad of unsigned under-20 players and is run with the intention of helping them find a professional club. The academy was based at <a href="/wiki/St_George%27s_Park_National_Football_Centre" title="St George's Park National Football Centre">St George's Park National Football Centre</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The squad was made up of players scouted worldwide and drafted to the squad through Nike Most Wanted trials. Over 50 players have gone on to play professional football over the years. The programme was led by Manager <a href="/wiki/Jon_Goodman" title="Jon Goodman">Jon Goodman</a>, with former <a href="/wiki/Wolverhampton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.">Wolverhampton Wanderers</a> player <a href="/wiki/Matt_Murray_(English_footballer)" title="Matt Murray (English footballer)">Matt Murray</a> who worked as Assistant Manager.
</p><p>The programme was also known for its collaborations with world class professional players, most notably <a href="/wiki/Sami_Khedira" title="Sami Khedira">Sami Khedira</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joe_Hart" title="Joe Hart">Joe Hart</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rio_Ferdinand" title="Rio Ferdinand">Rio Ferdinand</a>, <a href="/wiki/Francesco_Totti" title="Francesco Totti">Francesco Totti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mario_G%C3%B6tze" title="Mario Götze">Mario Gotze</a> and others.
</p><p>Players such as <a href="/wiki/David_Accam" title="David Accam">David Accam</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Lewis_Cunningham&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Michael Lewis Cunningham (page does not exist)">Michael Lewis Cunningham</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tom_Rogi%C4%87" title="Tom Rogić">Tom Rogic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Abdul_Majeed_Waris" title="Abdul Majeed Waris">Majeed Waris</a>, <a href="/wiki/Petar_Golubovi%C4%87" title="Petar Golubović">Petar Golubovic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Salazar" title="Kevin Salazar">Kevin Salazar</a> and Sergio Galeano are graduates of the programme.
</p><p>The Academy regularly played teams like <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">FC Barcelona</a>, <a href="/wiki/A.S._Roma" title="A.S. Roma">Arsenal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Inter_Milan" title="Inter Milan">Inter Milan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a> and others.
</p><p>The Academy closed at the end of the 2016/17 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
