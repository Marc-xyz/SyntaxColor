<p><b>Central Bank of India</b>, a government-owned bank, is one of the oldest and largest commercial banks in India. It is based in <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">Mumbai</a> which is the financial capital of India and capital city of state of <a href="/wiki/Maharashtra" title="Maharashtra">Maharashtra</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>It is one of eighteen public sector banks in India to get <a href="/wiki/Recapitalization" title="Recapitalization">recapitalised</a> in 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> 
</p><p>Central Bank of India has approached the <a href="/wiki/Reserve_Bank_of_India" title="Reserve Bank of India">Reserve Bank of India</a> (RBI) for permission to open representative offices in five more locations – <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dubai" title="Dubai">Dubai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Doha" title="Doha">Doha</a> and <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>As on 30 September 2019, the bank has a network of 4,681 branches, 3,477 ATMs, ten satellite offices and one extension counter.  It has a pan-India presence covering all 28 states, seven out of nine union territories and NCT Delhi, 574 district headquarters and 626 districts out of 707 districts in the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Annual_Report_2017-18_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-Annual_Report_2017-18-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Central_Bank_of_India_2010_stamp.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="466" data-file-width="622" decoding="async" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Central_Bank_of_India_2010_stamp.jpg/220px-Central_Bank_of_India_2010_stamp.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Central_Bank_of_India_2010_stamp.jpg/330px-Central_Bank_of_India_2010_stamp.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Central_Bank_of_India_2010_stamp.jpg/440px-Central_Bank_of_India_2010_stamp.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Central_Bank_of_India_2010_stamp.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A 2010 stamp dedicated to Sorabji Pochkhanawala and the 100th anniversary of the Central Bank of India</div></div></div>
<p>The Central Bank of India was established on 21 December 1911 by Sir <a href="/wiki/Sorabji_Pochkhanawala" title="Sorabji Pochkhanawala">Sorabji Pochkhanawala</a> with Sir Pherozeshah Mehta as Chairman,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Hindu_2007_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Hindu_2007-6">[6]</a></sup> and claims to have been the first commercial Indian bank completely owned and managed by Indians.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early-20th_century">Early-20th century</span></h3>
<p>By 1918 it had established a branch in <a href="/wiki/Hyderabad" title="Hyderabad">Hyderabad</a>. A branch in nearby <a href="/wiki/Secunderabad" title="Secunderabad">Secunderabad</a> followed in 1925.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>In 1923, it acquired the <b>Tata Industrial Bank</b> in the wake of the failure of the <a href="/wiki/Alliance_Bank_of_Simla" title="Alliance Bank of Simla">Alliance Bank of Simla</a>. The Tata bank, established in 1917, had opened a branch in Madras in 1920 that became the Central Bank of India, Madras.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>Central Bank of India was instrumental in the creation of the first Indian exchange bank, the Central Exchange Bank of India, which opened in London in 1936. However, <a href="/wiki/Barclays" title="Barclays">Barclays</a> Bank acquired Central Exchange Bank of India in 1938.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>Also before World War II, Central Bank of India established a branch in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rangoon" title="Rangoon">Rangoon</a>. The branch's operations concentrated on business between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Burma" title="Burma">Burma</a> and India, and especially money transmission via telegraphic transfer. Profits derived primarily from foreign exchange and margins. The bank also lent against land, produce, and other assets, mostly to Indian businesses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-World_War_II">Post-World War II</span></h3>
<p>In 1963, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Politics_of_Burma#Military_socialist_era" title="Politics of Burma">revolutionary government</a> in Burma nationalized Central Bank of India's operations there, which became People's Bank No. 1.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1969, the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_India" title="Government of India">Indian Government</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indian_banking#Nationalisation" title="Indian banking">nationalized</a> the bank on 19 July, together with 13 others.
</p>
 <div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Nameboard_of_Central_Bank_of_India_.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3120" data-file-width="4160" decoding="async" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Nameboard_of_Central_Bank_of_India_.jpg/220px-Nameboard_of_Central_Bank_of_India_.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Nameboard_of_Central_Bank_of_India_.jpg/330px-Nameboard_of_Central_Bank_of_India_.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Nameboard_of_Central_Bank_of_India_.jpg/440px-Nameboard_of_Central_Bank_of_India_.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Nameboard_of_Central_Bank_of_India_.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Nameboard of Central Bank of India, Shankar Sheth Road Branch, Pune</div></div></div> 
<p>In the 1980s the managers of the London branches of Central Bank of India, <a href="/wiki/Punjab_National_Bank" title="Punjab National Bank">Punjab National Bank</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Union_Bank_of_India" title="Union Bank of India">Union Bank of India</a> were caught up in a fraud in which they made dubious loans to the Bangladeshi jute trader Rajender Singh Sethia. The regulatory authorities in England and India forced all three Indian banks to close their London branches.
</p><p>Central Bank of India was one of the first banks in India to issue credit cards in the year 1980 in collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Mastercard" title="Mastercard">Mastercard</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>On its 108th Foundation day Central Bank of India launched its first step towards robotic banking, a robot named "MEDHA".
</p>
