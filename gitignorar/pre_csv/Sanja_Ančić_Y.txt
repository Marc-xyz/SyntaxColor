<p><b>Sanja Ančić</b> (<small>Croatian pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Serbo-Croatian" title="Help:IPA/Serbo-Croatian">[sâɲa âːntʃitɕ]</a></span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> born 18 July 1988) is a <a href="/wiki/Tennis" title="Tennis">tennis</a> player from Croatia. She won a total of eight <a href="/wiki/International_Tennis_Federation" title="International Tennis Federation">ITF</a> tournaments in singles and one in doubles. Her highest singles ranking was world no. 159, which she reached on 11 September 2006, and her highest doubles ranking was no. 240, reached on 5 March 2007. She retired in 2007.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sanja_An%C4%8Di%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ančić was born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Split_(city)" title="Split (city)">Split</a> to Stipe and Nilda Ančić. She plays right-handed, has a two-handed backhand and is the sister of professional tennis players <a href="/wiki/Ivica_An%C4%8Di%C4%87" title="Ivica Ančić">Ivica</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mario_An%C4%8Di%C4%87" title="Mario Ančić">Mario Ančić</a>. She played with her brother in the <a href="/wiki/2007_Hopman_Cup" title="2007 Hopman Cup">2007 Hopman Cup</a>.
</p>
