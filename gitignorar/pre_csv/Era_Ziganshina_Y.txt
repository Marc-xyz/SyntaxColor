<p><b>Era Garafovna Ziganshina</b> (<a href="/wiki/Tatar_language" title="Tatar language">Tatar</a>: <span lang="tt" title="Tatar language text">Эра Гарәф кызы Җиһаншина</span>, <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Э́ра Гара́фовна Зига́ншина</span>; born February 1, 1944) is a <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet</a> and Russian film and stage actress, <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Artist_of_Russia" title="People's Artist of Russia">People's Artist of Russia</a> (2005).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1153-2005_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-1153-2005-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Era_Ziganshina&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ziganshina was born in <a href="/wiki/Kazan" title="Kazan">Kazan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">USSR</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> She studied at drama school at the <a href="/wiki/Tovstonogov_Bolshoi_Drama_Theater" title="Tovstonogov Bolshoi Drama Theater">Tovstonogov Bolshoi Drama Theater</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leningrad" title="Leningrad">Leningrad</a> (now Saint Petersburg). From 1965 to 1976 he worked at the <a href="/wiki/Baltic_House_Festival_Theatre" title="Baltic House Festival Theatre">Baltic House Festival Theatre</a> (with a break in 1970 he worked at the Russian drama theater in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chisinau" title="Chisinau">Chisinau</a>).
</p>
