<p><i><b>Don't Give Me Names</b></i> is the second album by the German band <a href="/wiki/Guano_Apes" title="Guano Apes">Guano Apes</a>, released in 2000. It includes the hit <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">single</a> "<a href="/wiki/Big_in_Japan_(Alphaville_song)#Guano_Apes_version" title="Big in Japan (Alphaville song)">Big in Japan</a>" (a <a href="/wiki/Cover_version" title="Cover version">cover</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Alphaville_(band)" title="Alphaville (band)">Alphaville</a> song), which peaked at #9 on the German charts and remains one of Guano Apes' most popular songs.
</p><p>The album was certified <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gold_album" title="Gold album">gold</a> in Germany<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and in Switzerland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Don%27t_Give_Me_Names&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After the success of their debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Proud_Like_a_God" title="Proud Like a God">Proud Like a God</a></i>, the members of Guano Apes felt pressed to come up with a new album that could hold its own against its predecessor, and spent a lot of time on writing and demoing new songs. According to Dennis Poschwatta in the documentary <i><a href="/wiki/Planet_of_the_Apes:_The_Documentary" title="Planet of the Apes: The Documentary">Planet of the Apes</a></i>, “the way we worked […] was not tot let anybody trick us. The album had to be awesome. It had to be out of sight.”
</p><p>Recording for <i>Don’t Give Me Names</i> began in October 1999 and was done at three different studios: Horus Studio in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hannover" title="Hannover">Hannover</a>, Vox Klangstudio in <a href="/wiki/Bendestorf" title="Bendestorf">Bendestorf</a>, and Galaxy Studios in <a href="/wiki/Mol,_Belgium" title="Mol, Belgium">Mol</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>. Wanting to achieve maximum result, the band tried to handle as much tasks as possible themselves, with the working process soon becoming chaotic, costly and time-consuming. Henning Rümenapp related how, eventually, producers Fabio Trentini and Wolfgang Stach were instrumental in “[getting] us all into the groove, bringing four oddballs together and focusing our ideas.”
</p><p>A number of songs featured on <i>Don't Give Me Names</i>, including “Innocent Greed”, “I Want It” and “Dödel Up”, had already been performed live by the band by the time they were recorded, with a few of them dating back to the period before the release of <i>Proud Like a God</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> "<a href="/wiki/Big_in_Japan_(Alphaville_song)#Guano_Apes_version" title="Big in Japan (Alphaville song)">Big in Japan</a>", the first single, was initially recorded for a compilation album called <i>Pop 2000</i>, released to celebrate 50 years of German pop and rock music. The band originally intended to include “<a href="/wiki/Don%27t_You_Turn_Your_Back_on_Me" title="Don't You Turn Your Back on Me">Don't You Turn Your Back on Me</a>” (released as a standalone single in March 1999) on the album as well, but eventually decided against it due to the song's low chart ratings and overall fan feedback.
</p><p>Four tracks from <i>Don’t Give Me Names</i> were released as singles: “Big in Japan”, ”<a href="/wiki/No_Speech" title="No Speech">No Speech</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Living_in_a_Lie" title="Living in a Lie">Living in a Lie</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/D%C3%B6del_Up" title="Dödel Up">Dödel Up</a>".
</p>
