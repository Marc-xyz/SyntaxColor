<p><b>Rovereto railway station</b> (<a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>: <i lang="it">Ferrovie Stazione di Rovereto</i>) serves the <a href="/wiki/Comune" title="Comune">Comune</a> of <a href="/wiki/Rovereto" title="Rovereto">Rovereto</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Autonomous_regions_with_special_statute" title="Autonomous regions with special statute">autonomous</a> <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Italy" title="Provinces of Italy">province</a> of <a href="/wiki/Trentino" title="Trentino">Trentino</a>, northeastern Italy.
</p><p>The station was opened in 1859 by the Austrian Empire's <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Southern_Railway" title="Austrian Southern Railway">Südbahn</a> and transferred to Italy in 1919. Rovereto station is located on the trans-Alpine <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brenner_railway" title="Brenner railway">Brenner Railway</a> connecting <a href="/wiki/Verona_Porta_Nuova_railway_station" title="Verona Porta Nuova railway station">Verona</a> and <a href="/wiki/Innsbruck_Hauptbahnhof" title="Innsbruck Hauptbahnhof">Innsbruck</a>.
</p><p>The station is currently managed by <a href="/wiki/Rete_Ferroviaria_Italiana" title="Rete Ferroviaria Italiana">Rete Ferroviaria Italiana</a> (RFI). The commercial area of the passenger building, however, is managed by <a href="/wiki/Centostazioni" title="Centostazioni">Centostazioni</a>, whereas train services are operated by <a href="/wiki/Trenitalia" title="Trenitalia">Trenitalia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Federal_Railways" title="Austrian Federal Railways">ÖBB-DB</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Location">Location</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rovereto_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Location">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rovereto railway station is situated at <i>Piazzale <a href="/wiki/Paolo_Orsi" title="Paolo Orsi">Paolo Orsi</a></i>, the western edge and a five-minute walk to the city centre.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rovereto_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station was opened on 23 March 1859 upon the completion of the Trento-Ala section of the Brenner Railway.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-trenidicarta.it_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-trenidicarta.it-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Initial train operations were entrusted to the Austrian Empire's <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Southern_Railway" title="Austrian Southern Railway">Südbahn</a> (Imperial Royal Privileged Southern Railway Company of Austria, Venice and Central Italy / <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <i lang="de"><a class="extiw" href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/S%C3%BCdbahngesellschaft" title="de:Südbahngesellschaft">Kaiserlich königliche privilegierte Südbahngesellschaft</a></i>).
</p><p>After Austria-Hungary's defeat in the <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">First World War</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Saint-Germain-en-Laye_(1919)" title="Treaty of Saint-Germain-en-Laye (1919)">Treaty of Saint-Germain-en-Laye</a> (1919) rewarded Italy with annexation of County of Tyrol's territory south of the Brenner Pass. Ownership of all railway stations from Ala/Ahl-am-Etsch to Brenner, including Rovereto, were consequently transferred to <i>Ferrovie dello Stato</i> (FS).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Features">Features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rovereto_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Stazionerovereto.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1796" data-file-width="2112" decoding="async" height="187" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Stazionerovereto.jpg/220px-Stazionerovereto.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Stazionerovereto.jpg/330px-Stazionerovereto.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Stazionerovereto.jpg/440px-Stazionerovereto.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Stazionerovereto.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The station yard</div></div></div>
<p>The station has three tracks with platforms for passenger trains and additional tracks in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Goods_yard" title="Goods yard">goods yard</a>, which can be accessed by trucks and other vehicles from <i>Via Zeni</i> on the opposite side of the station building.
</p><p>There have been plans to transfer the goods yard southwards to <a href="/wiki/Mori,_Trentino" title="Mori, Trentino">Mori <u>railway</u> aistation</a> to make room for a larger, bifrontal station, thus providing access on both sides from <i>Piazzale Orsi</i> and <i>Via Zeni</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<div style="clear:left;"></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Train_services">Train services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rovereto_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Train services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station has about two million passenger movements per year and is therefore, in terms of passenger numbers, the third biggest within the region, after <a href="/wiki/Bolzano/Bozen_railway_station" title="Bolzano/Bozen railway station">Bolzano/Bozen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trento_railway_station" title="Trento railway station">Trento</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>The following services call at this station:
</p><p><b>Domestic</b>
</p>
<ul><li>High-speed train (<a href="/wiki/Trenitalia" title="Trenitalia">Trenitalia</a> Frecciargento) <b>Bolzano/Bozen-Naples</b>: Bolzano/Bozen - Trento/Trient - Rovereto/Rofreit - Verona - Bologna - Florence - Rome - (Naples)</li>
<li>Regional train (<a href="/wiki/Trenitalia" title="Trenitalia">Trenitalia</a> Regional) <b>Brennero/Brenner-Bologna</b>: Brennero/Brenner - Vipiteno/Sterzing - Fortezza/Franzensfeste - Bressanone/Brixen - Chuisa/Klausen - Bolzano/Bozen - Ora/Auer - Trento/Trient - Rovereto/Rofreit - Ala/Ahl-am-Etsch - Verona - Isola della Scala - Nogara - Mirandola - Bologna</li>
<li>Regional Train (<a href="/wiki/Trenitalia" title="Trenitalia">Trenitalia</a> Regional) <b>Bolzano/Bozen-Ala/Ahl-am-Etsch</b>: Bolzano/Bozen - Laives/Leifers - Ora/Aura - Egna/Neumarkt - Salorno/Salurn - Mezzocorona/Kronmetz - Trento/Trient - Rovereto/Rofreit - Mori - Ala/Ahl-am-Etsch</li></ul>
<p><b>Cross-border</b>
</p><p>(A for Austria, D for Germany)
</p>
<ul><li>Intercity train (<a href="/wiki/Austrian_Federal_Railways" title="Austrian Federal Railways">ÖBB</a> EuroCity) <b>Munich-Verona/Venice</b>: Munich (Hbf) (D) - Rosenheim (D) - Kufstein (A) - Jenbach (A) - Innsbruck (A) - Bolzano/Bozen - Trento/Trient - Rovereto/Rofreit - Verona - (Padua) - (Venice)</li>
<li>Intercity train (<a href="/wiki/Austrian_Federal_Railways" title="Austrian Federal Railways">ÖBB</a> EuroCity) <b>Munich-Verona/Bologna</b>: Munich (Hbf) (D) - Rosenheim (D) - Kufstein (A) - Jenbach (A) - Innsbruck (A) - Bolzano/Bozen - Trento/Trient - Rovereto/Rofreit - Verona - (Bologna)</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Interchange">Interchange</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rovereto_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Interchange">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At <i>Piazzale Orsi,</i> close to railway station building, urban bus routes A, 4, 5, 6 and 7 stop here as well as suburban services to <a href="/wiki/Trento" title="Trento">Trento</a>, <a href="/wiki/Riva_del_Garda" title="Riva del Garda">Riva del Garda</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ala,_Trentino" title="Ala, Trentino">Ala</a>.
</p>
