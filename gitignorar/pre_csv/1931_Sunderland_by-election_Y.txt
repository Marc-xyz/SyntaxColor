	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">1931 Sunderland by-election</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">UK Parliamentary by-election</div>
<p>

The <b><a href="/wiki/Sunderland_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Sunderland (UK Parliament constituency)">Sunderland</a> by-election of 1931</b> was held on 26 March 1931.  The by-election was held due to the death of the incumbent Labour MP, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alfred_Smith_(UK_politician)" title="Alfred Smith (UK politician)">Alfred Smith</a>. Smith and his Labour colleague Dr <a href="/wiki/Marion_Phillips" title="Marion Phillips">Marion Phillips</a> had gained the two-member seat at <a href="/wiki/1929_United_Kingdom_general_election" title="1929 United Kingdom general election">the last general election</a> from the Conservatives <a href="/wiki/Luke_Thompson_(politician)" title="Luke Thompson (politician)">Luke Thompson</a> and Walter Raine, who had first won it in <a href="/wiki/1922_United_Kingdom_general_election" title="1922 United Kingdom general election">1922</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TimesHoC1929_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-TimesHoC1929-1">[1]</a></sup> Another defeated candidate in 1929 was the Liberal <a href="/wiki/Betty_Morgan_(politician)" title="Betty Morgan (politician)">Dr Betty Morgan</a>, then aged 24.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TimesHoC1929_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-TimesHoC1929-1">[1]</a></sup> Both Thompson and Morgan contested the by-election.
</p><p>The by-election saw Luke Thompson narrowly regain the seat he had lost in 1929 for the Conservatives. At the <a href="/wiki/1931_United_Kingdom_general_election" title="1931 United Kingdom general election">general election held later in the year</a>, Thompson was returned with a greatly increased majority of over 23,000 votes, and his fellow Conservative <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Storey,_Baron_Buckton" title="Samuel Storey, Baron Buckton">Samuel Storey</a> was also comfortably elected as the seat's second member.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TimesHoC1931_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-TimesHoC1931-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
