<p><b>Chō Tsuratatsu</b><span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">長 連龍</span>, September 9, 1546 – March 18, 1619)</span> was a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Samurai" title="Samurai">samurai</a> of the late <a href="/wiki/Sengoku_period" title="Sengoku period">Sengoku period</a> to early <a href="/wiki/Edo_period" title="Edo period">Edo period</a>, who served the <a href="/wiki/Hatakeyama_clan" title="Hatakeyama clan">Hatakeyama clan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oda_clan" title="Oda clan">Oda clan</a>, and then the <a href="/wiki/Maeda_clan" title="Maeda clan">Maeda clan</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Kaga_Domain" title="Kaga Domain">Kaga Domain</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ch%C5%8D_Tsuratatsu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The son of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ch%C5%8D_Tsugutsura&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Chō Tsugutsura (page does not exist)">Chō Tsugutsura</a>, Tsuratatsu was originally from <a href="/wiki/Noto_Province" title="Noto Province">Noto Province</a>. He became a monk later in life, remaining as such until 1577. That year, Tsuratatsu's elder brother joined <a href="/wiki/Oda_Nobunaga" title="Oda Nobunaga">Oda Nobunaga</a> during his invasion of Noto, and convinced Tsuratatsu to do the same, thus the Chō family became the established power within the province. Tsuratatsu supported his master by repeatedly engaging in conflicts against the <a href="/wiki/Ikk%C5%8D-ikki" title="Ikkō-ikki">Ikkō-ikki</a>, and most notably assisted <a href="/wiki/Shibata_Katsuie" title="Shibata Katsuie">Shibata Katsuie</a> in his campaign to liberate <a href="/wiki/Kaga_Province" title="Kaga Province">Kaga Province</a> from the influence of the Ikkō in 1580. In return for Tsuratatsu's conviction<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (July 2012)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup>, Nobunaga granted him land confiscated from the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Isurugi_Shrine&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Isurugi Shrine (page does not exist)">Isurugi Shrine</a> of <a href="/wiki/Echizen_Province" title="Echizen Province">Echizen Province</a>. That same year Tsuratatsu became a high ranking <i><a href="/wiki/Yoriki" title="Yoriki">yoriki</a></i> under the jurisdiction of <a href="/wiki/Maeda_Toshiie" title="Maeda Toshiie">Maeda Toshiie</a>, but was later made a direct vassal of the Maeda, following the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Incident_at_Honn%C5%8D-ji" title="Incident at Honnō-ji">Incident at Honnō-ji</a>. Tsuratatsu assisted <a href="/wiki/Tokugawa_Ieyasu" title="Tokugawa Ieyasu">Tokugawa Ieyasu</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sekigahara_Campaign" title="Sekigahara Campaign">Sekigahara Campaign</a> of 1600. Tsuratatsu died in what is now <a href="/wiki/Nanao,_Ishikawa" title="Nanao, Ishikawa">Nanao</a>, in 1619. His descendants continued to serve the Maeda clan, received a stipend of 30,000 <a href="/wiki/Koku" title="Koku">koku</a>, and had <a href="/wiki/Kar%C5%8D" title="Karō">karō</a> status.
</p><p>One of Tsuratatsu's descendants, <a href="/wiki/Ch%C5%8D_Tsurahide" title="Chō Tsurahide">Chō Tsurahide</a>, was one of the assassins of <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Ckubo_Toshimichi" title="Ōkubo Toshimichi">Ōkubo Toshimichi</a>.
</p>
