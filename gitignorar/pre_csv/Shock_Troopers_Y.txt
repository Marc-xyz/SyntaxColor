<p><i><b>Shock Troopers</b></i><span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">ショックトルーパーズ</span><span style="display:none">,</span> <i>Shokku Torūpāzu</i>)</span> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Run_and_gun_(video_game)" title="Run and gun (video game)">run and gun</a> <a href="/wiki/Arcade_game" title="Arcade game">arcade game</a> developed by Saurus and published by <a href="/wiki/SNK" title="SNK">SNK</a> in 1997 for the <a href="/wiki/Neo_Geo" title="Neo Geo">Neo-Geo</a> arcade and home platform. Gameplay involves taking command of one or three soldiers in an eight-way shooter. A second game in the series, <i><a href="/wiki/Shock_Troopers:_2nd_Squad" title="Shock Troopers: 2nd Squad">Shock Troopers: 2nd Squad</a></i>, followed up in 1998.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gameplay">Gameplay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shock_Troopers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Gameplay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the beginning of each session, players choose whether to go through the game in one of two modes, "Lonely Wolf" or "Team Battle". In "Lonely Wolf", one character is controlled throughout the game, while "Team Battle" allows selecting a band of three soldiers through their missions, switching between them <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/On-the-fly" title="On-the-fly">on-the-fly</a>. Choosing to go it alone gives a higher starting life total, while proceeding as a team will give a wider variety of special weapons as well as a higher starting total (ten for each of the three characters instead of twenty for one).
</p><p>At the beginning of the game, there is also a choice to travel through the Mountain, Jungle, or Valley route. Halfway through the game, a different route can optionally be chosen otherwise stay on the current path. Characters and paths chosen determine how much life bonus is received at the beginning of each level. Each stage is interspersed with boss battles (both in the middle and at the end of each stage). Until the very end, battling the enemy commander atop an aircraft.
</p><p>Each character has their own virtues over other selections. Some might start with higher life totals, while others could move faster. Regardless of choice, the soldiers each possess a unique special weapon or "bomb", which varies in distance and range. Controls consist of an eight-way joystick and four buttons. Players move in eight directions with the joystick and fire, do evasive maneuvers, use their special weapon, and switch characters with the A, B, C, and D buttons, respectively.
</p><p>Players must progress through six stages, each ending in a boss fight. The weapons can be fired in all eight directions, but holding down the fire button allows strafing. Attacking within close range of an enemy will yield items, including point bonuses, weapon power-ups, and life. Enemy fire can be avoided with the dodge button. In "Team Battle" mode, the three selected soldiers can be cycled to utilize the traits and special weapons of each.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shock_Troopers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Bloody Scorpions terrorist group have kidnapped a scientist and his granddaughter Cecilia in order to gain the powerful drug, Alpha-301, which converts normal people into superhuman soldiers. A special team composed of eight soldiers from different countries must fight through their ranks in order to get to their leader and save the scientist's granddaughter and the world. (The sequel, <i><a href="/wiki/Shock_Troopers:_2nd_Squad" title="Shock Troopers: 2nd Squad">Shock Troopers: 2nd Squad</a></i>, has a completely unrelated story and different characters.)
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ports_and_re-releases">Ports and re-releases</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shock_Troopers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ports and re-releases">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Shock Troopers</i> was included in <i><a href="/wiki/SNK_Arcade_Classics_Vol._1" title="SNK Arcade Classics Vol. 1">SNK Arcade Classics Vol. 1</a></i>, a video game compilation for <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_2" title="PlayStation 2">PlayStation 2</a>, <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Portable" title="PlayStation Portable">PlayStation Portable</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wii" title="Wii">Wii</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/SNK#2003–2016:_SNK_Playmore" title="SNK">SNK Playmore</a> released ports for <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_3" title="PlayStation 3">PlayStation 3</a> and PlayStation Portable developed by <a href="/wiki/M2_(game_developer)" title="M2 (game developer)">M2</a> on August 25, 2011 on <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Network" title="PlayStation Network">PlayStation Network</a> via <a href="/wiki/NEOGEO_Station" title="NEOGEO Station">NEOGEO Station</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/D4_Enterprise" title="D4 Enterprise">D4 Enterprise</a> developed and published a port for the <a href="/wiki/Virtual_Console" title="Virtual Console">Virtual Console</a> for Wii, which was released in Japan on May 22, 2012;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[2]</a></sup> in North America on October 25, 2012;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[3]</a></sup> and in Europe on November 8, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>A port developed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DotEmu" title="DotEmu">DotEmu</a> for <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">Microsoft Windows</a>, <a href="/wiki/MacOS" title="MacOS">OS X</a>, <a href="/wiki/Linux" title="Linux">Linux</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asm.js" title="Asm.js">asm.js</a> was released by SNK Playmore as part of the <a href="/wiki/Humble_Bundle" title="Humble Bundle">Humble NEOGEO 25th Anniversary Bundle</a> on December 8, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[5]</a></sup> It was released on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Steam_(software)" title="Steam (software)">Steam</a> on May 18, 2016 and on <a href="/wiki/GOG.com" title="GOG.com">GOG.com</a> on May 30, 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shock_Troopers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The game was very well received. <a href="/wiki/Classic_Game_Room" title="Classic Game Room">Classic Game Room</a> described <i>Shock Troopers</i> for the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_3" title="PlayStation 3">PlayStation 3</a> as an "amazing" shooter with "fantastic" gameplay, controls, and the best game of 2011, despite being just a re-release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[8]</a></sup> Nintendo Life's Corbie Dillard scored the <a href="/wiki/Virtual_Console" title="Virtual Console">Virtual Console</a> release a 7/10 and opined it "succeeds in offering up an intense and explosive run-and-gun experience."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[9]</a></sup> Digitally Downloaded reviewer awarded it four-and-half stars out of five, stating: "If you’ve never played a run-n’-gun shooter before, Shock Troopers should be one of your top contenders, regardless of your skill level."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[10]</a></sup> According to Hardcore Gaming 101, unlike the version included in <i><a href="/wiki/SNK_Arcade_Classics_Vol._1" title="SNK Arcade Classics Vol. 1">SNK Arcade Classics Vol. 1</a></i>, the VC version "runs almost perfectly." The website especially hailed the game's "fantastic presentation that ranks among the best in the genre."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[11]</a></sup>
</p>
