	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Ellen Chapman</h1>

<table class="infobox biography vcard" style="width:22em"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" style="text-align:center;font-size:125%;font-weight:bold"><div class="fn" style="display:inline">Ellen Chapman</div></th></tr><tr><th scope="row">Nationality</th><td class="category"><a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Occupation</th><td class="role">Politician</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Known for</th><td>First woman councillor for <a href="/wiki/Worthing_Borough_Council" title="Worthing Borough Council">Worthing</a></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p><b>Ellen Chapman</b> was an <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a> suffragist and local politician, and the first woman councillor for <a href="/wiki/Worthing_Borough_Council" title="Worthing Borough Council">Worthing</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ellen_Chapman&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ellen Chapman was the first woman to run for election to <a href="/wiki/Worthing_Borough_Council" title="Worthing Borough Council">Worthing Borough Council</a>, and in 1910 became the first woman councillor, and one of the first woman councillors in England.  In 1920, Chapman became Worthing's first female mayor, and the first female mayor anywhere in Sussex.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ShHS_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ShHS-2">[2]</a></sup>  She was a great benefactor of the poor in Worthing and lived in Ardsheal Road, Broadwater.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> 
</p><p>Chapman had been selected to be mayor in 1914 but the choice was vetoed at the last minute because – to quote a member of the all-male mayoral selection committee – “it would be inadvisable to have a woman mayor while the country is in a state of war”.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Chapman had also been a poor law guardian at the East Preston <a href="/wiki/Poor_law_union" title="Poor law union">Union</a>.  She was also a member of the <a href="/wiki/National_Union_of_Women%27s_Suffrage_Societies" title="National Union of Women's Suffrage Societies">National Union of Women's Suffrage Societies</a> and founder and president of the Worthing Women's Franchise Society, a branch of the NUWSS, in 1913.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>  She was also a member of the <a href="/wiki/Conservative_and_Unionist_Women%27s_Franchise_Association" title="Conservative and Unionist Women's Franchise Association">Conservative and Unionist Women's Franchise Association</a> and the Catholic Women's Suffrage Society.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ShHS_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-ShHS-2">[2]</a></sup>  Ellen Chapman was largely responsible for the Worthing Women's Franchise Society taking a leading role in the Sussex-wide campaign for ‘Votes for Women’.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ShHS_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-ShHS-2">[2]</a></sup>  Chapman also led several deputations to meet Government ministers and met members of the local establishment including the <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Social_and_Political_Union" title="Women's Social and Political Union">Women's Social and Political Union</a> and the fledgling local <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(UK)" title="Labour Party (UK)">Labour Party</a> to lobby and persuade people of the merits of women's suffrage and non-violence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>Chapman was elected to <a href="/wiki/West_Sussex_County_Council" title="West Sussex County Council">West Sussex County Council</a> in 1919 and was the first woman to do so, with Evelyn Gladys Cecil from <a href="/wiki/Bognor_Regis" title="Bognor Regis">Bognor Regis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>  Chapman was elected unopposed to the <a href="/wiki/Broadwater,_West_Sussex" title="Broadwater, West Sussex">Broadwater</a> ward.
</p>
