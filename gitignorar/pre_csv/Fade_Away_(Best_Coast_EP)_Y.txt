<p><i><b>Fade Away</b></i> is the sixth <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended play</a> (EP) by American <a href="/wiki/Indie_rock" title="Indie rock">indie rock</a> duo <a href="/wiki/Best_Coast" title="Best Coast">Best Coast</a>. It was released on October 22, 2013, on frontwoman Bethany Cosentino's label Jewel City. Produced by <a href="/wiki/Wally_Gagel" title="Wally Gagel">Wally Gagel</a>, the EP was recorded at <a href="/wiki/Wax_Ltd" title="Wax Ltd">Wax Ltd</a>'s studios, and was inspired by musical acts like <a href="/wiki/Mazzy_Star" title="Mazzy Star">Mazzy Star</a>, <a href="/wiki/Patsy_Cline" title="Patsy Cline">Patsy Cline</a>, and <a href="/wiki/My_Bloody_Valentine_(band)" title="My Bloody Valentine (band)">My Bloody Valentine</a>. Cosentino wrote the song "Fear of My Identity" about "having an <a href="/wiki/Existential_crisis" title="Existential crisis">existential crisis</a>" after using the sedative <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ambien" title="Ambien">Ambien</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>

<p><i>Fade Away</i> received generally positive reviews from music critics. At <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, which assigns a <a href="/wiki/Standard_score" title="Standard score">normalized</a> rating out of 100 to reviews from mainstream publications, the EP received an <a href="/wiki/Weighted_arithmetic_mean" title="Weighted arithmetic mean">average</a> score of 67, based on 15 reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MC_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-MC-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Jon Dolan of <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> viewed <i>Fade Away</i> as a combination of the band's first two albums, describing it as "masterfully archetypal but utterly [Cosentino's] own."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RS_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-RS-12">[12]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Alternative_Press_(magazine)" title="Alternative Press (magazine)">Alternative Press</a></i> called the EP the band's "best work to date", remarking, "Lyrically introspective, musically tight and vocally resolute [...] <i>Fade Away</i> sounds like a reclamation record for Bethany Cosentino."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AP_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-AP-6">[6]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Pitchfork_(website)" title="Pitchfork (website)">Pitchfork</a></i> deemed it a return to form for the band, noting that "[t]here are touches of sophistication across <i>Fade Away</i> that Best Coast haven't been able to achieve until now."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PF_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-PF-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Accolades">Accolades</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fade_Away_(Best_Coast_EP)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Accolades">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Rolling Stone</i> placed <i>Fade Away</i> at number 35 on their list of the 50 Best Albums of 2013, writing, "It's just seven songs but still felt like a breakthrough."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p>
