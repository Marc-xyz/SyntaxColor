<p>"<b>I'm Still in Love with You</b>" is a song recorded by Jamaican recording artist <a href="/wiki/Sean_Paul" title="Sean Paul">Sean Paul</a> for his second studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Dutty_Rock" title="Dutty Rock">Dutty Rock</a></i> (2002). It features vocals from <a href="/wiki/Sasha_(Jamaican_musician)" title="Sasha (Jamaican musician)">Sasha</a>. It reached number six in the United Kingdom, and number 14 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> chart. It also became a top ten hit in Hungary, Italy, Ireland and Switzerland.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Composition">Composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I%27m_Still_in_Love_with_You_(Sean_Paul_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Interpolation_(music)" title="Interpolation (music)">interpolation</a> of "I'm Still in Love with You" by <a href="/wiki/Alton_Ellis" title="Alton Ellis">Alton Ellis</a>.
</p><p>Sean Paul and Sasha (the female counterpart) are discussing a stalled and seemingly unfulfilled relationship. Paul's character is trying to explain that the relationship was somewhat of an extended one-night stand, and that it has no future; Sasha's character seems to want to continue the relationship, despite Paul's admittance of "thug" love because she remains in love with him.
</p><p>Though the original version's message was more vague, the message in this version seems much clearer in explaining the relationship between the characters in the song's story. Alton Ellis' version was originally produced as a one-man song, but he later teamed up with his sister, <a href="/wiki/Hortense_Ellis" title="Hortense Ellis">Hortense Ellis</a> to perform a duet. It wasn't until this moment that the song showed a romantic battle between two parties to continue a seemingly ruined relationship.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cover_version">Cover version</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I%27m_Still_in_Love_with_You_(Sean_Paul_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Cover version">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hortense Ellis also did her own version of her brother's song entitled "I'm Still In Love with You (Boy)", which was later done by <a href="/wiki/Marcia_Aitken" title="Marcia Aitken">Marcia Aitken</a>. Aitken's version of the song featured a slight but noticeable difference in vocal pitch from either of the Ellis' versions. Sasha's vocal patterns more closely resemble Marcia's interpretation than that of the Ellis'.
</p><p>In 2019, Brazilian singer <a href="/wiki/Anitta_(singer)" title="Anitta (singer)">Anitta</a> remade and reenacted the visuals for Sean Paul's original video with her song "Terremoto," featuring Brazilian rapper Kevinho. The video ends with the following messages: "Thank You Sean Paul" and "D̶i̶r̶e̶c̶t̶e̶d̶  Remade by João Papa".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
