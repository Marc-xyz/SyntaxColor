	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Edward R. Moore</h1>

		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">For other people named Edward Moore, see <a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Edward_Moore_(disambiguation)" title="Edward Moore (disambiguation)">Edward Moore (disambiguation)</a>.</div>
<p><b>Edward Roberts Moore</b> (1894–1952) was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Priesthood_(Catholic_Church)" title="Priesthood (Catholic Church)">Roman Catholic priest</a>, <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">professor</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Social_worker" title="Social worker">social worker</a> and <a href="/wiki/Author" title="Author">author</a>. He served as <a href="/wiki/Pastor" title="Pastor">pastor</a> of historic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Peter%27s_Roman_Catholic_Church,_New_York" title="St. Peter's Roman Catholic Church, New York">St. Peter's Roman Catholic Church, New York</a> from 1937 until his death in 1952. He was Director of the Division of Social Action of the Catholic Charities of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Archdiocese_of_New_York" title="Roman Catholic Archdiocese of New York">Roman Catholic Archdiocese of New York</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Social_action">Social action</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edward_R._Moore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Social action">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ordained a priest in 1919, Moore was a professor at the <a href="/wiki/Fordham_Graduate_School_of_Social_Service" title="Fordham Graduate School of Social Service">Fordham Graduate School of Social Service</a> before retiring from social work to devote his full attention to his duties as Pastor of St Peter's. Moore was a member of the city's <a href="/wiki/Slum" title="Slum">Slum</a> Clearance Committee under <a href="/wiki/Mayor" title="Mayor">Mayor</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fiorello_La_Guardia" title="Fiorello La Guardia">Fiorello La Guardia</a> and a member of the first Board of the <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_Housing_Authority" title="New York City Housing Authority">New York City Housing Authority</a> in 1934. According to a history of the NYC housing authority, Moore "once escorted the former King of England, then the <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Windsor" title="Duke of Windsor">Duke of Windsor</a> on a tour of Housing Authority Developments."
</p><p>"Moore Houses" a New York City Housing Authority Development is named in honor of Moore and is located in the Melrose section of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bronx" title="Bronx">Bronx</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Youth_work">Youth work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edward_R._Moore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Youth work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A former Archdiocesan Director of the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Youth_Organization" title="Catholic Youth Organization">Catholic Youth Organization</a>, Moore served as National Director of the Catholic Committee on <a href="/wiki/Scouting" title="Scouting">Scouting</a> from 1933-1947. In 1940 he was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Silver_Buffalo_Award" title="Silver Buffalo Award">Silver Buffalo Award</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Boy_Scouts_of_America" title="Boy Scouts of America">Boy Scouts of America</a>.
</p>
