<p><i><b>Chhaap Tilak Sab Chheeni</b></i> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Urdu_language" title="Urdu language">Urdu</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ur"><span class="Nastaliq" dir="rtl" style="font-family: 'Jameel Noori Nastaleeq', 'Urdu Typesetting', 'Noto Nastaliq Urdu', 'Noto Nastaliq Urdu Draft', 'Hussaini Nastaleeq', 'AlQalam Taj Nastaleeq', IranNastaliq, 'Awami Nastaliq', 'Awami Nastaliq Beta3', 'Awami Nastaliq Beta2', 'Awami Nastaliq Beta1', 'Nafees Nastaleeq', 'Nafees Nastaleeq v1.01', 'Pak Nastaleeq', 'PDMS_Jauhar', 'Alvi Lahori Nastaleeq'; font-size: 110%; font-style: normal;" title="Nastaliq">چھاپ تلک سب چھینی</span></span>‎), is a ghazal (poem with a certain structure) written and composed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amir_Khusro" title="Amir Khusro">Amir Khusro</a>, a 14th-century <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sufi" title="Sufi">Sufi</a> mystic, in the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Awadhi_Bhasha&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Awadhi Bhasha (page does not exist)">Awadhi Boli</a>  of Hindi language. Due to the resonance of its melody and mystical lyrics, it is frequently heard in <a href="/wiki/Qawwali" title="Qawwali">Qawwali</a> concerts across <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">South Asia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> This  is a romantic poem expressing the feelings of a woman how she fell in love with her lover and how she feels about it, although as it is widely sung by sufi singers some may take it as a devotional composition describing how a devotee feels about God.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>This poem has been sung in Qawwali format by notable Indian and Pakistani Qawwals, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ustad_Nusrat_Fateh_Ali_Khan" title="Ustad Nusrat Fateh Ali Khan">Ustad Nusrat Fateh Ali Khan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Naheed_Akhtar" title="Naheed Akhtar">Naheed Akhtar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mehnaz_Begum" title="Mehnaz Begum">Mehnaz Begum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Abida_Parveen" title="Abida Parveen">Abida Parveen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sabri_Brothers" title="Sabri Brothers">Sabri Brothers</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Iqbal_Hussain_Khan_Bandanawazi" title="Iqbal Hussain Khan Bandanawazi">Iqbal Hussain Khan Bandanawazi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fareed_Ayaz" title="Fareed Ayaz">Farid Ayaz</a> &amp; Abu Muhammad Qawwal, Ustad Jafar Hussain Khan, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ustad_Vilayat_Khan" title="Ustad Vilayat Khan">Ustad Vilayat Khan</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ustad_Shujaat_Khan" title="Ustad Shujaat Khan">Ustad Shujaat Khan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zila_Khan" title="Zila Khan">Zila Khan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hadiqa_Kiani" title="Hadiqa Kiani">Hadiqa Kiani</a> , <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Smita_Rao_Bellur&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Smita Rao Bellur (page does not exist)">Smita Rao Bellur</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rahat_Fateh_Ali_Khan" title="Rahat Fateh Ali Khan">Rahat Fateh Ali Khan</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Popular_culture">Popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chhaap_Tilak_Sab_Chheeni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 1978 <a href="/wiki/Bollywood" title="Bollywood">Bollywood</a> film <a href="/wiki/Main_Tulsi_Tere_Aangan_Ki" title="Main Tulsi Tere Aangan Ki">Main Tulsi Tere Aangan Ki</a> featured a version by <a href="/wiki/Lata_Mangeshkar" title="Lata Mangeshkar">Lata Mangeshkar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asha_Bhosle" title="Asha Bhosle">Asha Bhosle</a>. Another popular version, by <a href="/wiki/Abida_Parveen" title="Abida Parveen">Abida Parveen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rahat_Fateh_Ali_Khan" title="Rahat Fateh Ali Khan">Rahat Fateh Ali Khan</a>, appeared on the Pakistani musical variety show <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coke_Studio_(Pakistan)" title="Coke Studio (Pakistan)">Coke Studio</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The song has maintained its popularity over the years, being regularly heard on television talent shows in India and Pakistan, and on social media.
</p>
