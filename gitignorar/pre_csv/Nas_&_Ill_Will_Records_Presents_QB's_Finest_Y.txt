<p><i><b>Nas &amp; Ill Will Records Presents QB's Finest</b></i>, commonly known as simply <i><b>QB's Finest</b></i>, is a <a href="/wiki/Compilation_album" title="Compilation album">compilation album</a> released on November 21, 2000 and the second release from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapper" title="Rapper">rapper</a> <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>' new <a href="/wiki/Ill_Will_Records" title="Ill Will Records">Ill Will Records</a> imprint, distributed by <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Records" title="Columbia Records">Columbia Records</a>. It featured Nas and a number of other rappers from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queensbridge,_Queens" title="Queensbridge, Queens">Queensbridge</a> <a href="/wiki/Public_housing" title="Public housing">housing projects</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Mobb_Deep" title="Mobb Deep">Mobb Deep</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nature_(rapper)" title="Nature (rapper)">Nature</a>, Nashawn, Littles, <a href="/wiki/Bravehearts" title="Bravehearts">Bravehearts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cormega" title="Cormega">Cormega</a>, who had briefly reconciled a longtime feud with Nas.
</p><p>The album also featured guest appearances from Queensbridge's hip hop legends <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roxanne_Shant%C3%A9" title="Roxanne Shanté">Roxanne Shanté</a>, <a href="/wiki/MC_Shan" title="MC Shan">MC Shan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marley_Marl" title="Marley Marl">Marley Marl</a>, both of whom appeared on the lead single "Da Bridge 2001" (based on MC Shan's and Marley Marl's 1986 classic "The Bridge"). "Da Bridge 2001" also featured a response from Nas to <a href="/wiki/Memphis_Bleek" title="Memphis Bleek">Memphis Bleek</a>, in which Nas calls out most of the <a href="/wiki/Roc-A-Fella_Records" title="Roc-A-Fella Records">Roc-A-Fella Records</a> roster, including Memphis Bleek, <a href="/wiki/Damon_Dash" title="Damon Dash">Damon Dash</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beanie_Sigel" title="Beanie Sigel">Beanie Sigel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>. The album's biggest hit single was "<a href="/wiki/Oochie_Wally" title="Oochie Wally">Oochie Wally</a>", a <a href="/wiki/Nightclub" title="Nightclub">club</a> anthem recorded by the Bravehearts.
</p>

<p>Though numbered correctly in the liner notes, the original <a href="/wiki/Compact_disc" title="Compact disc">compact disc</a> pressing erroneously combined tracks one and two during mastering, throwing off the count for the rest of the tracks by one number (i.e., "We Live This" is track two, "Real Niggas" is track three, and so on). Later, corrected masters feature the "Oochie Wally" remix (which features Nas in addition to the Bravehearts) in place of the original.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Samples">Samples</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nas_%26_Ill_Will_Records_Presents_QB%27s_Finest&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Samples">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>"Da Bridge 2001" contains a sample from "The Bridge" by <a href="/wiki/MC_Shan" title="MC Shan">MC Shan</a></li>

<li>"Straight Outta Q.B." contains a sample from "Straight Outta Compton" by <a href="/wiki/N.W.A" title="N.W.A">N.W.A</a>. Cormega's verse contains many recycled lyrics from <a href="/wiki/Ice_Cube" title="Ice Cube">Ice Cube</a>'s verse on the original song.</li>
<li>"Oochie Wally" contains a sample from "Bambooji" by <a href="/wiki/Gong_(band)" title="Gong (band)">Gong</a></li>
<li>"Street Glory" contains a sample from "Once Upon A Time" by <a href="/wiki/Donna_Summer" title="Donna Summer">Donna Summer</a></li>
<li>"Money" contains a sample from the theme song of <i><a href="/wiki/Phantasm_(film)" title="Phantasm (film)">Phantasm</a></i> by Fred Myrow</li>
<li>"Self Conscience" contains a sample from <i><a href="/wiki/The_Exorcist_(film)" title="The Exorcist (film)">The Exorcist</a></i> theme</li>
<li>"Die 4" contains a sample from <i><a href="/wiki/Halloween_(1978_film)" title="Halloween (1978 film)">Halloween</a></i> theme</li>
