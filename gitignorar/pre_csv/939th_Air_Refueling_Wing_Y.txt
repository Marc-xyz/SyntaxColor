<p>The <b>939th Air Refueling Wing</b> is an inactive <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Air_Force_Reserve" title="United States Air Force Reserve">United States Air Force Reserve</a> unit.  It was last active with the <a href="/wiki/Fourth_Air_Force" title="Fourth Air Force">Fourth Air Force</a>, based at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Portland_Air_Reserve_Station" title="Portland Air Reserve Station">Portland Air Reserve Station</a>, Oregon.  It was inactivated on 30 June 2008.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=939th_Air_Refueling_Wing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Need_for_reserve_troop_carrier_groups">Need for reserve troop carrier groups</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=939th_Air_Refueling_Wing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Need for reserve troop carrier groups">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After May 1959, the reserve flying force consisted of 45 troop carrier squadrons assigned to 15 troop carrier wings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[note 1]</a></sup>  The squadrons were not all located with their parent wings, but were spread over thirty-five Air Force, Navy and civilian airfields under what was called the Detached Squadron Concept.  The concept offered several advantages.  Communities were more likely to accept the smaller <a href="/wiki/Squadron_(aviation)" title="Squadron (aviation)">squadrons</a> than the large <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wing_(military_aviation_unit)" title="Wing (military aviation unit)">wings</a> and the location of separate squadrons in smaller population centers would facilitate recruiting and manning.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[1]</a></sup>  However, under this concept, all support organizations were located with the wing headquarters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[2]</a></sup>   Although this was not a problem when the entire wing was called to active service, mobilizing a single flying squadron and elements to support it proved difficult.  This weakness was demonstrated in the partial mobilization of reserve units during the <a href="/wiki/Berlin_Crisis_of_1961" title="Berlin Crisis of 1961">Berlin Crisis of 1961</a>.  To resolve this, at the start of 1962, <a href="/wiki/Continental_Air_Command" title="Continental Air Command">Continental Air Command</a>, (ConAC) determined to reorganize its reserve wings by establishing groups with support elements for each of its troop carrier squadrons.  This reorganization would facilitate mobilization of elements of wings in various combinations when needed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Activation_of_the_939th_Troop_Carrier_Group">Activation of the 939th Troop Carrier Group</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=939th_Air_Refueling_Wing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Activation of the 939th Troop Carrier Group">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As a result, the <b>939th Troop Carrier Group</b> was established at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Portland_Air_Reserve_Station" title="Portland Air Reserve Station">Portland International Airport</a>, Oregon on 11 February 1963 as the headquarters for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/313th_Troop_Carrier_Squadron" title="313th Troop Carrier Squadron">313th Troop Carrier Squadron</a>, which had been stationed there since November 1957.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Maurer313TCS_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Maurer313TCS-5">[4]</a></sup>  Along with group <a href="/wiki/Headquarters" title="Headquarters">headquarters</a>, a Combat Support Squadron, Materiel Squadron and a Tactical Infirmary were organized to support the 313th.  The group was equipped with <a href="/wiki/Fairchild_C-119_Flying_Boxcar" title="Fairchild C-119 Flying Boxcar">Fairchild C-119 Flying Boxcars</a> for <a href="/wiki/Tactical_Air_Command" title="Tactical Air Command">Tactical Air Command</a> airlift operations.
</p><p>The group was one of four C-119 groups assigned to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/349th_Troop_Carrier_Wing" title="349th Troop Carrier Wing">349th Troop Carrier Wing</a> in 1963, the others were the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/938th_Troop_Carrier_Group" title="938th Troop Carrier Group">938th Troop Carrier Group</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hamilton_Air_Force_Base" title="Hamilton Air Force Base">Hamilton Air Force Base</a>, California, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/940th_Troop_Carrier_Group" title="940th Troop Carrier Group">940th Troop Carrier Group</a> at <a href="/wiki/McClellan_Air_Force_Base" title="McClellan Air Force Base">McClellan Air Force Base</a>, California, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/941st_Troop_Carrier_Group" title="941st Troop Carrier Group">941st Troop Carrier Group</a> at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Paine_Air_Force_Base&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Paine Air Force Base (page does not exist)">Paine Air Force Base</a>, Washington.
</p><p>The 939th performed routine reserve airlift operations, being upgraded to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/C-124_Globemaster_II" title="C-124 Globemaster II">C-124 Globemaster II</a> intercontinental airlifter in 1966. On  26 January 1968 the group was activated for combat duty in the <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_War" title="Vietnam War">Vietnam War</a>.  The group flew overseas missions, particularly to the Far East and Southeast Asia during that period, being relieved from active service in June 1969.
</p><p>Inactivated on 1 July 1973 as part of a reorganization of Military Airlift Command assets, personnel and equipment being assigned directly to its host 349th MAW.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Air_rescue_operations">Air rescue operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=939th_Air_Refueling_Wing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Air rescue operations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 1 April 1985 the group was again activated as the <b>939th Aerospace Rescue and Recovery Group</b> when the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/304th_Aerospace_Rescue_and_Recovery_Squadron" title="304th Aerospace Rescue and Recovery Squadron">304th Aerospace Rescue and Recovery Squadron</a> at Portland International Airport equipped with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lockheed_HC-130_Hercules" title="Lockheed HC-130 Hercules">Lockheed HC-130 Hercules</a> aircraft transferred from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/March_Air_Force_Base" title="March Air Force Base">March Air Force Base</a>, California in addition to its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bell_HH-1_Huey" title="Bell HH-1 Huey">Bell HH-1 Hueys</a>.  The mobilization gaining command for the group was <a href="/wiki/Military_Airlift_Command" title="Military Airlift Command">Military Airlift Command</a>, with the intermediate command being <a href="/wiki/Twenty-Third_Air_Force" title="Twenty-Third Air Force">Twenty-Third Air Force</a> until August 1989, then <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Air_Rescue_Service" title="Air Rescue Service">Air Rescue Service</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-939thHistory_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-939thHistory-6">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>In December 1986, the group began converting its helicopter element to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sikorsky_HH-3_Jolly_Green_Giant" title="Sikorsky HH-3 Jolly Green Giant">Sikorsky HH-3 Jolly Green Giants</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-939thHistory_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-939thHistory-6">[5]</a></sup>  In October 1987, the group became the headquarters for all reserve rescue units, when the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/301st_Aerospace_Rescue_and_Recovery_Squadron" title="301st Aerospace Rescue and Recovery Squadron">301st Aerospace Rescue and Recovery Squadron</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Homestead_Air_Force_Base" title="Homestead Air Force Base">Homestead Air Force Base</a>, Florida and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/305th_Aerospace_Rescue_and_Recovery_Squadron" title="305th Aerospace Rescue and Recovery Squadron">305th Aerospace Rescue and Recovery Squadron</a> at <a href="/wiki/Selfridge_Air_National_Guard_Base" title="Selfridge Air National Guard Base">Selfridge Air National Guard Base</a>, Michigan were assigned to the group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-939thHistory_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-939thHistory-6">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>On 1 April 1990, recognizing that the group was parent for several operational squadrons, it was expanded as the <b>939th Air Rescue Wing</b>.  The three flying squadrons also became  "air rescue" squadrons the same day.  In February 1991, the 939th and its subordinate squadrons began conversion  to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sikorsky_HH-60G_Pave_Hawk" title="Sikorsky HH-60G Pave Hawk">Sikorsky HH-60G Pave Hawk</a> helicopters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-939thHistory_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-939thHistory-6">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 1992, the 939th Operations Group was activated and the wing's rescue squadrons were assigned to it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-939OGfacts_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-939OGfacts-7">[6]</a></sup>  Following the devastation of <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Andrew" title="Hurricane Andrew">Hurricane Andrew</a> in the same month, the 301st Rescue Squadron provided extensive service, but was forced to abandon Homestead and move to <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Air_Force_Base" title="Patrick Air Force Base">Patrick Air Force Base</a>, Florida.
</p><p>The 939th also deployed aircrews from each of its rescue squadrons to provide <a href="/wiki/Search_and_rescue" title="Search and rescue">search and rescue</a> coverage worldwide, including to <a href="/wiki/Naval_Air_Station_Keflavik" title="Naval Air Station Keflavik">Naval Air Station Keflavik</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a>, during and after the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southwest_Asia" title="Southwest Asia">Southwest Asia</a> War and to provide combat search and rescue coverage in the <a href="/wiki/Persian_Gulf" title="Persian Gulf">Persian Gulf</a> area between 1993 and 2003.
</p><p>In April 1997, the regular <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1st_Rescue_Group" title="1st Rescue Group">1st Rescue Group</a> at Patrick inactivated and a second reserve rescue squadron, the <a href="/wiki/39th_Rescue_Squadron" title="39th Rescue Squadron">39th Rescue Squadron</a> was formed there.  Both squadrons were placed under the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/920th_Rescue_Group" title="920th Rescue Group">920th Rescue Group</a>, which was assigned to the wing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-920RWfacts_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-920RWfacts-8">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Air_refueling_operations">Air refueling operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=939th_Air_Refueling_Wing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Air refueling operations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2003, the 939th converted to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Air_refueling" title="Air refueling">air refueling</a> mission with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boeing_KC-135R_Stratotanker" title="Boeing KC-135R Stratotanker">Boeing KC-135R Stratotanker</a> aircraft and was redesignated as the <b>939th Air Refueling Wing</b>.  Some of its rescue squadrons were inactivated while others, including the 304th Rescue Squadron (304 RQS) at Portland ARS, were reassigned to the 920th, which was expanded as the 920th Rescue Wing and became the new headquarters for Air Force Reserve rescue units.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-920RWfacts_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-920RWfacts-8">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>The 939th was inactivated in 2008 following the recommendations of the <a href="/wiki/2005_Base_Realignment_and_Closure_Commission" title="2005 Base Realignment and Closure Commission">2005 Base Realignment and Closure Commission</a> and its KC-135Rs redistributed to other units.
</p>
