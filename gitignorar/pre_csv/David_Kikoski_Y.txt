<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Kikoski&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/New_Brunswick,_New_Jersey" title="New Brunswick, New Jersey">New Brunswick, New Jersey</a>, Kikoski learned piano from his father and played with him in bars as a teenager. He studied at the <a href="/wiki/Berklee_College_of_Music" title="Berklee College of Music">Berklee College of Music</a> in the early 1980s, then moved to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> in 1985, touring and recording subsequently with <a href="/wiki/Roy_Haynes" title="Roy Haynes">Roy Haynes</a> (from 1986), <a href="/wiki/Randy_Brecker" title="Randy Brecker">Randy Brecker</a> (1986–88), <a href="/wiki/Bob_Berg" title="Bob Berg">Bob Berg</a> (1988), and <a href="/wiki/Billy_Hart" title="Billy Hart">Billy Hart</a> (1989). He has also played or recorded with <a href="/wiki/George_Garzone" title="George Garzone">George Garzone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barry_Finnerty" title="Barry Finnerty">Barry Finnerty</a>, <a href="/wiki/Red_Rodney" title="Red Rodney">Red Rodney</a>, <a href="/wiki/Craig_Handy" title="Craig Handy">Craig Handy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Moore" title="Ralph Moore">Ralph Moore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Didier_Lockwood" title="Didier Lockwood">Didier Lockwood</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Joe_Locke&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Joe Locke (page does not exist)">Joe Locke</a>, <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Ker_Ourio" title="Olivier Ker Ourio">Olivier Ker Ourio</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mingus_Big_Band" title="Mingus Big Band">Mingus Big Band</a>. Kikoski won a 2011 Grammy Award with the Mingus Big Band for the Best Live Jazz Ensemble Album, "Live at the Jazz Standard".  He also had a Grammy nomination with Roy Haynes for the "Birds of a Feather" CD.
</p><p>Kikoski has been a resident of <a href="/wiki/Jersey_City,_New_Jersey" title="Jersey City, New Jersey">Jersey City, New Jersey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
