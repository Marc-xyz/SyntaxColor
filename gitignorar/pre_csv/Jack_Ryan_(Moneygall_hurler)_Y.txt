<p><b>Jack Ryan</b> (born 1946) is an <a href="/wiki/Irish_people" title="Irish people">Irish</a> retired <a href="/wiki/Hurling" title="Hurling">hurler</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gaelic_football" title="Gaelic football">Gaelic footballer</a>. His <a href="/wiki/All-Ireland_Senior_Hurling_Championship" title="All-Ireland Senior Hurling Championship">championship</a> career as a <a href="/wiki/Dual_player" title="Dual player">dual player</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Tipperary_GAA" title="Tipperary GAA">Tipperary</a> senior teams spanned eleven seasons from 1967 until 1977.
</p><p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Dunkerrin" title="Dunkerrin">Dunkerrin</a>, <a href="/wiki/County_Offaly" title="County Offaly">County Offaly</a>, Ryan was raised in a family that had a strong association with <a href="/wiki/Gaelic_games" title="Gaelic games">Gaelic games</a>. His father, <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9amus_%C3%93_Riain" title="Séamus Ó Riain">Séamus Ó Riain</a>, had played junior hurling and football with Tipperary in the 1940s before becoming a Gaelic games administrator. He served in numerous roles with the <a href="/wiki/Tipperary_GAA" title="Tipperary GAA">Tipperary County Board</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Munster_GAA" title="Munster GAA">Munster Council</a> before becoming <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/President_of_the_Gaelic_Athletic_Association" title="President of the Gaelic Athletic Association">President of the Gaelic Athletic Association</a> in 1967. Ryan's brothers - <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Philip_Ryan_(hurler)" title="Philip Ryan (hurler)">Philip</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A9amus_Ryan_(hurler)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Séamus Ryan (hurler) (page does not exist)">Séamus</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Eugene_Ryan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Eugene Ryan (page does not exist)">Eugene</a> - would all later play for Tipperary at various levels.
</p><p>Ryan first played competitive Gaelic football and hurling with the <a href="/wiki/Moneygall_GAA" title="Moneygall GAA">Moneygall</a> club at juvenile and underage levels. After winning several divisional titles in both codes, Ryan won back-to-back <a href="/wiki/Tipperary_Senior_Hurling_Championship" title="Tipperary Senior Hurling Championship">county senior championship</a> medals in 1975 and 1976.
</p><p>Ryan made his debut on the inter-county scene at the age of sixteen when he was selected for the Tipperary minor hurling team in 1963. He enjoyed two championship seasons with the minor team, however, he was a <a href="/wiki/Munster_Minor_Hurling_Championship" title="Munster Minor Hurling Championship">Munster</a> runner-up on both occasions. After a year with the Tipperary junior football team, Ryan spent three years as a dual player with the under-21 teams, culminating with the winning of an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/All-Ireland_Under-21_Hurling_Championship" title="All-Ireland Under-21 Hurling Championship">All-Ireland</a> medal in 1967. That same year he was added to the Tipperary senior football panel before joining the senior hurling panel the following year. Over the course of the next decade Ryan was a regular player with the hurlers, the highlight of his career being the winning of <a href="/wiki/All-Ireland_Senior_Hurling_Championship" title="All-Ireland Senior Hurling Championship">All-Ireland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Munster_Senior_Hurling_Championship" title="Munster Senior Hurling Championship">Munster</a> medals as a non-playing substitute in 1971. He retired from inter-county hurling following the conclusion of the <a href="/wiki/1977_All-Ireland_Senior_Hurling_Championship" title="1977 All-Ireland Senior Hurling Championship">1977 championship</a>.
</p><p>Ryan's wife, Orla Ní Síocháin, was a three-time <a href="/wiki/All-Ireland_Senior_Camogie_Championship" title="All-Ireland Senior Camogie Championship">All-Ireland</a> medal winner with the Dublin <a href="/wiki/Camogie" title="Camogie">camogie</a> team and a daughter of GAA General-Secretary <a href="/wiki/Se%C3%A1n_%C3%93_S%C3%ADoch%C3%A1in" title="Seán Ó Síocháin">Seán Ó Síocháin</a>, while his son, <a href="/wiki/Shane_Ryan_(Gaelic_footballer)" title="Shane Ryan (Gaelic footballer)">Shane Ryan</a>, was a six-time <a href="/wiki/Leinster_Senior_Football_Championship" title="Leinster Senior Football Championship">Leinster</a> medal winner with the Dublin senior football team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
