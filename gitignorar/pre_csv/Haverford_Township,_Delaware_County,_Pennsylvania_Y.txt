<p><b>Haverford Township</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Home_Rule_Municipality_(Pennsylvania)" title="Home Rule Municipality (Pennsylvania)">Home Rule Municipality</a> <a href="/wiki/Township_(Pennsylvania)" title="Township (Pennsylvania)">township</a> in <a href="/wiki/Delaware_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Delaware County, Pennsylvania">Delaware County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Charter_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Charter-3">[3]</a></sup>  Haverford is named after the town of <a href="/wiki/Haverfordwest" title="Haverfordwest">Haverfordwest</a> in Wales, UK.  It is a commuting suburb located due west of <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a> and is officially known as the <b>Township of Haverford</b>.  Despite being under a home rule charter since 1977, it continues to operate under a Board of Commissioners divided into wards,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Charter_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Charter-3">[3]</a></sup> as do "First Class" townships that are still under the Pennsylvania Township Code. Haverford Township was founded in 1682 and incorporated in 1911.
</p><p>Haverford Township contains portions of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unincorporated_community" title="Unincorporated community">unincorporated communities</a> of <a href="/wiki/Haverford,_Pennsylvania" title="Haverford, Pennsylvania">Haverford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ardmore,_Pennsylvania" title="Ardmore, Pennsylvania">Ardmore</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wynnewood,_Pennsylvania" title="Wynnewood, Pennsylvania">Wynnewood</a> as well as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Census_designated_places" title="Census designated places">census designated places</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bryn_Mawr,_Pennsylvania" title="Bryn Mawr, Pennsylvania">Bryn Mawr</a> and <a href="/wiki/Drexel_Hill,_Pennsylvania" title="Drexel Hill, Pennsylvania">Drexel Hill</a>. The unincorporated community of <a href="/wiki/Havertown,_Pennsylvania" title="Havertown, Pennsylvania">Havertown</a> lies wholly within Haverford Township.  The township population as of the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 census</a> was 48,491.
</p><p>Haverford Township holds the distinction of having hosted two different men's major golf championships at two different golf clubs: <a href="/wiki/Merion_Golf_Club" title="Merion Golf Club">Merion Golf Club</a> hosted the <a href="/wiki/1934_U.S._Open_(golf)" title="1934 U.S. Open (golf)">1934</a>, <a href="/wiki/1950_U.S._Open_(golf)" title="1950 U.S. Open (golf)">1950</a>, <a href="/wiki/1971_U.S._Open_(golf)" title="1971 U.S. Open (golf)">1971</a>, <a href="/wiki/1981_U.S._Open_(golf)" title="1981 U.S. Open (golf)">1981</a>, and <a href="/wiki/2013_U.S._Open_(golf)" title="2013 U.S. Open (golf)">2013</a> <a href="/wiki/U.S._Open_(golf)" title="U.S. Open (golf)">U.S. Open</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Llanerch_Country_Club" title="Llanerch Country Club">Llanerch Country Club</a> hosted the <a href="/wiki/1958_PGA_Championship" title="1958 PGA Championship">1958 PGA Championship</a>.
</p><p>Haverford, along with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Upper_Darby_Township" title="Upper Darby Township">Upper Darby</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cheltenham_Township,_PA" title="Cheltenham Township, PA">Cheltenham</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lower_Merion_Township,_PA" title="Lower Merion Township, PA">Lower Merion</a> together form as the major <a href="/wiki/Inner_suburb" title="Inner suburb">inner ring</a> <a href="/wiki/List_of_inner_suburbs_in_the_United_States#Philadelphia" title="List of inner suburbs in the United States">suburbs</a> of Philadelphia.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Haverford_Township,_Delaware_County,_Pennsylvania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Haverford Township was laid out by <a href="/wiki/William_Penn" title="William Penn">William Penn</a> as part of the <a href="/wiki/Welsh_Tract" title="Welsh Tract">Welsh Tract</a> or Barony. In 1681, a representative group of Welsh <a href="/wiki/Quakers" title="Quakers">Quakers</a> met with Penn to discuss their settlement having purchased forty thousand acres of land which today comprises all of Haverford, <a href="/wiki/Radnor_Township,_Delaware_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Radnor Township, Delaware County, Pennsylvania">Radnor</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lower_Merion_Township,_Montgomery_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Lower Merion Township, Montgomery County, Pennsylvania">Lower Merion</a> Townships. "Companies of Adventurers" were formed, with the most prominent person in each taking out the patent on 5,000 acres of land as trustee. The first three families arrived in Haverford Township in 1682. Lewis David, Henry Lewis and William Howell selected land along the southern border. The original settlers, led by John Roberts who had negotiated with William Penn in 1684 to constitute the Tract as a separate county whose local government would use the Welsh language never saw their dream realized. Early records indicate that the area was often called "Harford" a Welsh contraction of Haverford.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The earliest map of Penn's province dates from 1687. (<i>Note: on the map to the right, the word "ye" is read as "the." The "y" is the old letter "thorn" which equals "th.")</i>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:1681_32_settlements.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="427" data-file-width="956" decoding="async" height="98" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/1681_32_settlements.png/220px-1681_32_settlements.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/1681_32_settlements.png/330px-1681_32_settlements.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/1681_32_settlements.png/440px-1681_32_settlements.png 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:1681_32_settlements.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>"The City of Philadelphia two Miles in Length and one in Breadth.,Inset to,A Mapp of Ye Improved Part of Pennsylvania in America, Divided into Countyes, Townships and Lotts. Surveyed by Thomas Holme. Sold by George Willdey at the Great Toy, Spectacle, and Print Shop, at the corner of Ludgate Street, near St. Paul's, London. 1687."</div></div></div> 
<p>The area was primarily agricultural until the second decade of the twentieth century. The census returns of 1860 show the value of livestock as $62,485.00; animals slaughtered $11,255.00; 46,049 bushels of grain harvested and the value of orchard and garden produce, $4,090.00.
</p><p>The two creeks that mark part of the township boundaries, Cobbs Creek and Darby Creek provided mill seats for the early settlers.  "As early as the year 1688 a small grist-mill, known as the "Haverford Mill," was built on Cobb's Creek, near where that stream is crossed by the road leading past <a href="/wiki/Old_Haverford_Friends_Meetinghouse" title="Old Haverford Friends Meetinghouse">Haverford meeting-house</a>. Its original owner is unknown. By searching the records, however, a little light is thrown on the history of this, one of the first mills built in Pennsylvania. Thus, Fourth month (June) 12, 1700, Richard Hayes, attorney for William Howell, acknowledged a deed to David Lloyd, attorney for Rowland Powell, " for ninety -seven acres of land with a mill called Haverford Mill, and all other appurtenances and improvements thereunto belonging," the deed bearing date Third month 30th, 1700. This seems to indicate that William Howell, one of the first settlers of the township, was the original owner."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> (The Indian name of Cobb's Creek was Karakung, as it is still called in that area.) 
</p><p>Daniel Humphreys purchased the property in 1703 and built a sawmill and fulling and dyeing mill. These were added to Dennis Kelly's holding in 1826 and became the Castle Hill Mills. This site is just north of Eagle Road on the west bank of Cobb's Creek.
</p><p>On <a href="/wiki/Darby_Creek_(Pennsylvania)" title="Darby Creek (Pennsylvania)">Darby Creek</a> to the west, Richard Hayes Jr., David Morris and Samuel Lewis erected a gristmill, known as Haverford New Mill, in 1707. Later a sawmill was attached. This mill operated until 1904. Near the border with <a href="/wiki/Marple_Township,_Delaware_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Marple Township, Delaware County, Pennsylvania">Marple Township</a>, Humphrey Ellis operated a very early fulling mill. In 1807 Henry Lawrence built a sawmill on this site, and in 1832 his son, William, built a gristmill close by. The sawmill remained in the family ownership for more than 125 years, and was, until its demolition, the oldest industry extant in Haverford Township.
</p><p>About 1800 grist and sawmills were built on the headwaters of Cobb's Creek by Peter Brown, and in 1810 Jonathan Miller built grist and sawmills at what is now the Juncture of Mill Road and Karakung Drive.
</p><p>Israel Whelen, Sr., built the <a href="/wiki/Nitre_Hall" title="Nitre Hall">Nitre Hall</a> Powder Mills on Cobb's Creek, which were in operation by 1810. During the <a href="/wiki/American_Revolution" title="American Revolution">American Revolution</a> very little black powder was manufactured in this country, and it was needed for engineering and mining. Between 1810 and 1840 these mills were the second largest powder mills in the U.S. with production of 800,000 pounds in 1812. The mills ceased operations in 1840 and were sold to Dennis Kelly, who converted them for the manufacture of cotton and wooden goods.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1814, Dennis Kelly borrowed capital to purchase a mill-seat on Cobb's Creek. He erected a small wooden factory known as Clinton Mills. This was a highly successful venture and, after enlarging the mill, he built Cedar Grove Mill further downstream. Dennis Kelly provided material for the U.S. government from 1817 until 1860 with contracts ranging from $1,800.00 to $41,370.00. Improved transportation opened the township to development.
</p><p><b>The Railroads and Haverford Township</b>
</p><p>The transformation of Haverford Township from an agrarian spread of farms and mills began just after the turn of the 20th century. The railroads bought acres of land west of Cobb's Creek for their planned developments. Rail lines ran from 69th Street in Upper Darby outward to Ardmore, West Chester and Norristown. Along the rail lines the railroad built the first planned suburban communities <a class="external text" href="http://www.llanerchadvocacygroup.com/history-of-llanerch.html" rel="nofollow">Llanerch</a>, 1897 and the most fully developed, <a href="/wiki/Havertown,_Pennsylvania" title="Havertown, Pennsylvania">Brookline</a>, 1909. While Beechwood, Oakmont and Manoa date from the same period the major housing tracts do not appear until after World War II along with the development of Chatham Park. These communities are now included under the fictitious postal address "Havertown." The first Welsh settlers called this area "Harford."
</p><p><b>Golf Courses</b>
</p><p>Several golf courses were developed. Merion Cricket Club Golf Association incorporated Clifton Hall an old farmhouse, in their clubhouse. They have 140 acres in the main course or East Course and 160 acres in the West Course. The Llanerch Country Club consists of 126 acres. The Pennsylvania Railroad Golf Club was established in 1925 on 109 acres bounded by Earlington Road, Manoa Road and City Line. This property was sold in 1943 to Warner West Corporation, which developed Chatham Park.
</p><p><b>Education</b>
</p><p>The first building erected for educational purposes was the Federal School, in 1797. During the 1800s four additional one-room schools and one parochial school were built. The latter part of the 19th century was the beginning of the present school system. After World War II, additions were made to existing schools, a new high school and three one-story elementary schools were built. Today there is one middle school, one senior high, five elementary schools, four parochial schools and four private schools.
</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Lawrence_Cabin">Lawrence Cabin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Haverford_Township,_Delaware_County,_Pennsylvania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Lawrence Cabin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:LawrenceCabin.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1224" data-file-width="1632" decoding="async" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/LawrenceCabin.JPG/220px-LawrenceCabin.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/LawrenceCabin.JPG/330px-LawrenceCabin.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/LawrenceCabin.JPG/440px-LawrenceCabin.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:LawrenceCabin.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Lawrence Cabin</div></div></div>
<p>David Lawrence was one of the early Welsh settlers in Haverford Township. He emigrated with his wife Elinor Ellis and her family in 1684, and took up part of his father-in-law's land grant. His son Henry Lawrence purchased 209 acres along Cobbs Creek in 1709. It has not been determined whether the log house predates this purchase, but a ​<span class="frac nowrap">2<span class="visualhide"> </span><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>2</sub></span>-story stone addition was built c. 1730, and later a clapboard summer kitchen was added. It became known as the Three Generation House, and remained in Lawrence family ownership until 1942.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="1797_Federal_School">1797 Federal School</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Haverford_Township,_Delaware_County,_Pennsylvania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1797 Federal School">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:FederalSchool.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1054" data-file-width="1632" decoding="async" height="142" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/FederalSchool.JPG/220px-FederalSchool.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/FederalSchool.JPG/330px-FederalSchool.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/FederalSchool.JPG/440px-FederalSchool.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:FederalSchool.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>1797 Federal School</div></div></div>
<p>The first record of the purchase of land for a school in Haverford Township was October 29, 1797. Alexander Symington received 5 shillings for an irregularly shaped building lot of one quarter of an acre and three perches near the present day intersection of Darby and Coopertown Roads. Payment for the land was made by five trustees: Philip Sheaff, William Brook, Francis Lee, David Lyons and Benjamin Hayes Smith, "for the purpose of erecting a school thereon for the use of said Township of Haverford". The <a href="/wiki/Federal_School" title="Federal School">1797 Federal School</a> is on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Pont_Reading_House">Pont Reading House</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Haverford_Township,_Delaware_County,_Pennsylvania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Pont Reading House">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Pont_Reading133671pu.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3277" data-file-width="4523" decoding="async" height="159" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Pont_Reading133671pu.jpg/220px-Pont_Reading133671pu.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Pont_Reading133671pu.jpg/330px-Pont_Reading133671pu.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Pont_Reading133671pu.jpg/440px-Pont_Reading133671pu.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Pont_Reading133671pu.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Pont Reading earliest section c. 1683</div></div></div>
<p>"Pont Reading House" is situated on the Easterly side of Haverford Road, Haverford Township, <a href="/wiki/Delaware_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Delaware County, Pennsylvania">Delaware County</a>, Pennsylvania, at the corner where the Ardmore Trolley passes en route between Brookline—Oakmont and Ardmore, and near Ardmore Junction Station of the Philadelphia and Western Railway. It was the home of <a href="/wiki/Joshua_Humphreys" title="Joshua Humphreys">Joshua Humphreys</a>, the shipbuilder and naval architect known for constructing the <a href="/wiki/Original_six_frigates_of_the_United_States_Navy" title="Original six frigates of the United States Navy">original six frigates of the United States Navy</a>. Here, this beautiful specimen of early American homes still stands, in an excellent state of preservation and repair, as it has since 1813, when the front section was built and added to the middle three-story section erected 1730–60, which in turn was then added to the rear and original two-story structure, built possibly as early as 1683.  One of the log walls of this first and original section can still be seen as one enters a door on one of the sides thereof, All surrounded and shaded with some large trees, of the original forest, still surviving.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Nitre_Hall">Nitre Hall</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Haverford_Township,_Delaware_County,_Pennsylvania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Nitre Hall">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:NitreHall.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="793" data-file-width="1131" decoding="async" height="154" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/NitreHall.JPG/220px-NitreHall.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/NitreHall.JPG/330px-NitreHall.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/NitreHall.JPG/440px-NitreHall.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:NitreHall.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Nitre Hall c. 1800</div></div></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Nitre_Hall" title="Nitre Hall">Nitre Hall</a> Powder Mills, which gave the valley its name, were built by Israel Whelen shortly after 1800. The young nation had growing needs for explosives, and the mills prospered with an output of 800,000 pounds during the War of 1812. Nitre Hall Mills produced a quality and quantity of black powder in the U.S. second only to the Dupont Mills on the Brandywine. After the powder mills closed in 1840, Dennis Kelly bought the property and converted the buildings to the manufacture of textiles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Nitre Hall is on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Grange_Estate">The Grange Estate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Haverford_Township,_Delaware_County,_Pennsylvania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: The Grange Estate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Grange_Estate" title="Grange Estate">Grange Estate</a>, also known as <b>Maen-Coch</b> and <b>Clifton Hall</b>, is a historic mansion in Havertown, Pennsylvania, near Philadelphia, in Delaware County, Pennsylvania. Built in 1700 and expanded several times through the 1850s, it was purchased by Haverford Township in 1974. The building was added to the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> in 1976 as The Grange.
</p><p><b>Leedom Hall</b>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Leedom-Dickenson_Mansion.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="450" data-file-width="700" decoding="async" height="141" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Leedom-Dickenson_Mansion.jpg/220px-Leedom-Dickenson_Mansion.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Leedom-Dickenson_Mansion.jpg/330px-Leedom-Dickenson_Mansion.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Leedom-Dickenson_Mansion.jpg/440px-Leedom-Dickenson_Mansion.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Leedom-Dickenson_Mansion.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Leedom Hall, original cabin William Howell, c. 1688</div></div></div>
<p>Leedom Hall: The date of the original cabin (under the far left wing), since demolished, is uncertain. It may well have been the home of William Howell whose early mill dates from 1688. The first stone section of the mansion dates from about 1700. This first wing measures one perch (18 steps) in length, a measurement that dates from medieval times. Its original fireplace complete with its crane is intact. The second wing of Leedom hall dates from about 1720 and the third from around 1740. The house, in the Georgian style, retains its original appearance. From a 1917 account: <small>“The Leedom Mansion stands a short distance from the mills on the bank to the right of the public road. It is an imposing, well-built home, the yard adorned with noble trees, aged box bushes and cheerful lowers.” <i>Cobb's Creek in the Days of the Old Powder Mills,</i> Eckfeldt, John W. 1917.</small>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography"><small>Geography</small></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Haverford_Township,_Delaware_County,_Pennsylvania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Haverford Township is located in the northeast portion of Delaware County.  According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the township has a total area of 10.0 square miles (26 km<sup>2</sup>), all of it land.  The township is part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pennsylvania_Main_Line" title="Pennsylvania Main Line">Pennsylvania Main Line</a> and of the <a href="/wiki/Welsh_Tract" title="Welsh Tract">Welsh Tract</a>.  Waterways in Haverford Township include <a href="/wiki/Cobbs_Creek" title="Cobbs Creek">Cobbs Creek</a>, <a href="/wiki/Darby_Creek_(Pennsylvania)" title="Darby Creek (Pennsylvania)">Darby Creek</a>, <a href="/wiki/Naylors_Run" title="Naylors Run">Naylors Run</a> and <a href="/wiki/Meadowbrook_Run" title="Meadowbrook Run">Meadowbrook Run</a>.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Haverford_Township,_Delaware_County,_Pennsylvania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Haverford Township straddles the boundary between a hot-summer <a href="/wiki/Humid_continental_climate" title="Humid continental climate">humid continental climate</a> (<i>Dfa</i>) and a <a href="/wiki/Humid_subtropical_climate" title="Humid subtropical climate">humid subtropical climate</a> (<i>Cfa</i>). The <a href="/wiki/Hardiness_zone" title="Hardiness zone">hardiness zone</a> is 7a. The average monthly temperature in the Brookline/Oakmont vicinity ranges from 32.1° F in January to 77.2° F in July. <a class="external autonumber" href="http://prism.oregonstate.edu/explorer/" rel="nofollow">[1]</a>
</p>

<p>As of Census 2010, the racial makeup of the township was 91.2% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 2.7% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 0.1% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 4.2% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 0.5% from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 1.3% from two or more races. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 1.9% of the population <a class="external autonumber" href="https://factfinder.census.gov/faces/tableservices/jsf/pages/productview.xhtml?src=CF" rel="nofollow">[2]</a>.
</p><p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-13">[13]</a></sup> of 2000, there were 48,498 people, 18,061 households, and 13,021 families residing in the township.  The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 4,844.6 people per square mile (1,870.6/km²).  There were 18,378 housing units at an average density of 1,835.8 per square mile (708.9/km²).  The racial makeup of the township was 93.99% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_American" title="White American">White</a>, 2.12% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American" title="African American">African American</a>, 0.10% <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native American</a>, 2.76% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_American" title="Asian American">Asian</a>, 0.01% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander_American" title="Pacific Islander American">Pacific Islander</a>, 0.20% from <a href="/wiki/Race_and_ethnicity_in_the_United_States_Census#Race" title="Race and ethnicity in the United States Census">other races</a>, and 0.81% from two or more races. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_and_Latino_Americans" title="Hispanic and Latino Americans">Hispanics or Latinos</a> of any race were 0.89% of the population.
</p><p>There were 18,061 households, out of which 33.3% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 60.9% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 8.6% had a female householder with no husband present, and 27.9% were non-families. 23.3% of all households were made up of individuals, and 12.4% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older.  The average household size was 2.65 and the average family size was 3.17.
</p><p>The age distribution was 24.9% under the age of 18, 6.3% from 18 to 24, 28.6% from 25 to 44, 22.7% from 45 to 64, and 17.5% who were 65 years of age or older.  The median age was 39 years. For every 100 females, there were 90.6 males.  For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 86.4 males.
</p><p>According to a 2010 estimate, the median income for a household in the township was $87,283, and the median income for a family was $103,138. Males had a median income of $52,471 versus $38,852 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the township was $29,749.  About 2.0% of families and 3.7% of the population were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 2.1% of those under age 18 and 4.2% of those age 65 or over.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Haverford_Township_and_Championship_Golf">Haverford Township and Championship Golf</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Haverford_Township,_Delaware_County,_Pennsylvania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Haverford Township and Championship Golf">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A total of 6 men's <a href="/wiki/Men%27s_major_golf_championships" title="Men's major golf championships">Major Championships</a> and 11 men's and women's US Amateur Championships, have been staged within Haverford Township. <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Jones_(golfer)" title="Bobby Jones (golfer)">Bobby Jones</a> completed his <a href="/wiki/Grand_Slam_(golf)" title="Grand Slam (golf)">grand slam</a> at Merion in 1930, <a href="/wiki/Ben_Hogan" title="Ben Hogan">Ben Hogan</a>'s comeback from a horrific car accident in the <a href="/wiki/1950_U.S._Open_(golf)" title="1950 U.S. Open (golf)">1950 US Open</a> staged at <a href="/wiki/Merion_Golf_Club" title="Merion Golf Club">Merion</a>.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Haverford_Township,_Delaware_County,_Pennsylvania&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first recorded purchase of land for educational purposes in Haverford Township was made on October 28, 1797, five shillings "for the purpose of erecting a school thereon for the use of said Township of Haverford".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>  A stone structure erected on a site along <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Darby_Road" title="Darby Road">Darby Road</a> at the crossroads Coopertown served as a school until 1872. Today, known as the Federal School, the building still stands and serves as a window to history for Haverford Township children. Every fourth-grade student spends a day at the <a href="/wiki/Federal_School" title="Federal School">Federal School</a> learning what it was like to be a student in 1797.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:HHS2014.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="267" data-file-width="500" decoding="async" height="117" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/HHS2014.jpg/220px-HHS2014.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/HHS2014.jpg/330px-HHS2014.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/HHS2014.jpg/440px-HHS2014.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:HHS2014.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Haverford High School</div></div></div>
<p>Public school students living in Haverford Township attend schools in the <a href="/wiki/School_District_of_Haverford_Township" title="School District of Haverford Township">School District of Haverford Township</a>. The District currently has a student enrollment of approximately 5,475, and, residents number over 49,000. There are seven schools in the District: Chatham Park, Chestnutwold, Coopertown, Lynnewood, and <a href="/wiki/Manoa" title="Manoa">Manoa</a> elementary schools, with grades kindergarten through 5; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Haverford_Middle_School" title="Haverford Middle School">Haverford Middle School</a>, with grades 6-8; and <a href="/wiki/Haverford_High_School" title="Haverford High School">Haverford High School</a>, with grades 9-12.
</p><p>Residents of Haverford Township benefit from a wide range of school supported services, including Adult Evening School; the Golden Age Program, the Senior Citizen Prom and the Tax-Aide Program for senior citizens; and <a href="/wiki/Delaware_County_Community_College" title="Delaware County Community College">Delaware County Community College</a> courses for all residents. Haverford, which is a member of the Delaware County Chamber of Commerce and the Main Line Chamber of Commerce, maintains partnerships with local universities and colleges, businesses, service organizations, realtors, community groups and senior citizen organizations.
