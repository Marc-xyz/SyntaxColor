<p><b>Parvatii Nair</b> (born <b>Parvathy Venugopal Nair</b>) is an <a href="/wiki/Indian_people" title="Indian people">Indian</a> model and actress who works in the <a href="/wiki/Cinema_of_South_India" title="Cinema of South India">South Indian film industry</a>. Born into a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Malayali_people" title="Malayali people">Malayali</a> family in <a href="/wiki/Abu_Dhabi" title="Abu Dhabi">Abu Dhabi</a>, she trained as a software professional while pursuing a career in modelling, before becoming an actress. . During her modelling career, she appeared in a number of commercials and won the titles "Miss Karnataka" and "Miss Navy Queen".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-breakthrough_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-breakthrough-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Nair made her acting debut with the 2012 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Malayalam_language" title="Malayalam language">Malayalam</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Poppins" title="Poppins">Poppins</a></i> directed by <a href="/wiki/V._K._Prakash" title="V. K. Prakash">V. K. Prakash</a>, before appearing in a number of critically acclaimed films across all four South Indian film industries, including <i><a href="/wiki/Story_Kathe" title="Story Kathe">Story Kathe</a></i> (2013) and <i><a href="/wiki/Yennai_Arindhaal" title="Yennai Arindhaal">Yennai Arindhaal</a></i> (2015). Her performance as a young journalist in <i>Story Kathe</i> won her the Best Kannada Debut Actress Award at the <a href="/wiki/3rd_South_Indian_International_Movie_Awards" title="3rd South Indian International Movie Awards">South Indian International Movie Awards</a>, while the role of a vengeful wife in <i>Yennai Arindhaal</i> won her praise from critics and received a Filmfare nomination.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2015, she starred in the critical and commercial success <i><a href="/wiki/Vascodigama" title="Vascodigama">Vascodigama</a></i>, portraying an <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> lecturer.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_family">Background and family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parvatii_Nair&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Parvatii Nair was born in <a href="/wiki/Abu_Dhabi" title="Abu Dhabi">Abu Dhabi</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a> into a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Malayali_people" title="Malayali people">Malayali</a> family and has an elder brother named Shankar. She attended <a href="/wiki/Our_Own_English_High_School" title="Our Own English High School">Our Own English High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Abu_Dhabi" title="Abu Dhabi">Abu Dhabi</a>, and went on to graduate in <a href="/wiki/Computer_Science_and_Engineering" title="Computer Science and Engineering">Computer Science and Engineering</a> from <a href="/wiki/Manipal_Institute_of_Technology" title="Manipal Institute of Technology">Manipal Institute of Technology</a> in <a href="/wiki/Manipal" title="Manipal">Manipal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Karnataka" title="Karnataka">Karnataka</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, hoping to work as a software engineer. As a school student, she won a Guinness certificate for being part of the team that made the longest painting in the world. She became a full-time model after she participated in "Miss Karnataka", and went on to be an ambassador for <a href="/wiki/Mysore_Sandal_Soap" title="Mysore Sandal Soap">Mysore Sandal soap</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-breakthrough_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-breakthrough-2">[2]</a></sup> She won "Navy Queen Kerala", before appearing in numerous print and television commercials.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-breakthrough_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-breakthrough-2">[2]</a></sup> As a model, she has endorsed the brands Anchor, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_Paints_Ltd" title="Asian Paints Ltd">Asian Paints</a>, Malabar Gold, Mazaa, <a href="/wiki/Prestige_Group" title="Prestige Group">Prestige</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reliance_Industries" title="Reliance Industries">Reliance</a> and Tata Diamonds. Nair had also expressed interest in participating in the Femina Miss India pageant after winning the state-level title, but could not apply as a result of the competition's height eligibility.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> While working as a model, she began appearing in short films, music videos and documentaries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-breakthrough_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-breakthrough-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parvatii_Nair&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Early_work.2C_2012-2013"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_work,_2012-2013">Early work, 2012-2013</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parvatii_Nair&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early work, 2012-2013">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After working in a jewellery commercial for director <a href="/wiki/V._K._Prakash" title="V. K. Prakash">V. K. Prakash</a>, Nair was offered a role in his feature film <i><a href="/wiki/Beautiful_(2011_film)" title="Beautiful (2011 film)">Beautiful</a></i> (2011), but refused as she was unsure whether to pursue a career in films. She later made her acting début in Prakash's anthology film <i><a href="/wiki/Poppins" title="Poppins">Poppins</a></i> (2012), and worked on the project alongside commitments for other Malayalam films.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> She played further roles as a traditional bride in the horror film <i><a href="/wiki/Yakshi_%E2%80%93_Faithfully_Yours" title="Yakshi – Faithfully Yours">Yakshi – Faithfully Yours</a></i> (2012) and in <a href="/wiki/Shalil_Kallur" title="Shalil Kallur">Shalil Kallur</a>'s drama film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> She subsequently began working on bigger budget films in the Malayalam film industry and signed on to portray <a href="/wiki/Jayasurya" title="Jayasurya">Jayasurya</a>'s wife in the action anthology <i><a href="/wiki/D_Company_(film)" title="D Company (film)">D Company</a></i> (2013), as a part of the segment <i>Gangs of Vadakkumnathan</i>, which won positive reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indiatimes4_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-indiatimes4-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Nair made her debut in the Kannada film industry through <i><a href="/wiki/Story_Kathe" title="Story Kathe">Story Kathe</a></i> (2013) which earned her recognition as the Best Debut Actress in Kannada films at the <a href="/wiki/3rd_South_Indian_International_Movie_Awards" title="3rd South Indian International Movie Awards">3rd South Indian International Movie Awards</a>. Portraying a television journalist who tries to win patent rights for a medical student, her role was appreciated by critics and the film performed well at the box office.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In 2013 Nair appeared as a judge in the first modelling based Malayalam reality show <i>Super Models</i> on Amrita Television.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> She also appeared in a music video, "Ariyathe Ninayathe" by <a href="/wiki/Vineeth_Sreenivasan" title="Vineeth Sreenivasan">Vineeth Sreenivasan</a> and was included in <a href="/wiki/The_Times_of_India" title="The Times of India"><i>Bangalore Times</i></a>' "Most Desirable Women of 2013" list. She was also in the most desirable women of 2015, 2016, and 2017 lists from Kochi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indiatimes6_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-indiatimes6-16">[16]</a></sup> She gave motivational talks at TEDx Bangalore and was invited to speak at a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rotary_Club" title="Rotary Club">Rotary Club</a> and various other venues.
</p>
<h3><span id="Breakthrough.2C_2014"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Breakthrough,_2014">Breakthrough, 2014</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parvatii_Nair&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Breakthrough, 2014">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Nair moved on to work on two big-budget Tamil films during the course of 2014 and shot for <a href="/wiki/Gautham_Menon" title="Gautham Menon">Gautham Menon</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Yennai_Arindhaal" title="Yennai Arindhaal">Yennai Arindhaal</a></i> (2015) and <a href="/wiki/Kamal_Haasan" title="Kamal Haasan">Kamal Haasan</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Uttama_Villain" title="Uttama Villain">Uttama Villain</a></i> (2015). Nair was cast alongside <a href="/wiki/Ajith_Kumar" title="Ajith Kumar">Ajith Kumar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trisha_(actress)" title="Trisha (actress)">Trisha</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anushka_Shetty" title="Anushka Shetty">Anushka Shetty</a> in <i>Yennai Arindhaal</i> after a successful screen test. The actress stated it was "a dream-come-true" and to be cast in the project was "huge encouragement", and made reference to Menon's dignified portrayals of female characters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yennai_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-yennai-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Portraying Elizabeth, the vengeful wife of <a href="/wiki/Arun_Vijay" title="Arun Vijay">Arun Vijay</a>'s character, the film and her performance received praise from Silverscreen.com, who stated she displays "awesome ruthlessness" and "nails the part".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Nair also auditioned for <i><a href="/wiki/Uttama_Villain" title="Uttama Villain">Uttama Villain</a></i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Ramesh_Aravind" title="Ramesh Aravind">Ramesh Aravind</a>, and portrayed the girlfriend of <a href="/wiki/Kamal_Haasan" title="Kamal Haasan">Kamal Haasan</a>'s son.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Featuring alongside an ensemble cast, her role was extended as the shoot progressed and Nair expressed her delight at working with Kamal Haasan, stating it was like "going to an acting school".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> The film opened to positive reviews and marked the actress's most high-profile project at that time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> She then appeared in the Kannada film, <i><a href="/wiki/Vascodigama" title="Vascodigama">Vascodigama</a></i> (2015), as a college lecturer alongside <a href="/wiki/Kishore_(actor)" title="Kishore (actor)">Kishore</a>. Nair also made her debut as a singer through the film, in a song which featured Kannada and English lyrics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The film opened to positive reviews from critics and performed well at the box office.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup>  Indiaglitz.com noting that she "suits the role precisely" and another reviewer stating she was "utilised well" in the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup>
</p><p>She played the lead in the first Tamil production of <a href="/wiki/Eros_International" title="Eros International">Eros International</a>, the period film <i><a href="/wiki/Enkitta_Mothathe_(2017_film)" title="Enkitta Mothathe (2017 film)">Enkitta Mothathe</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> She later appeared <i><a href="/wiki/James_%26_Alice" title="James &amp; Alice">James and Alice</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> Her next release was the Tamil film <i><a href="/wiki/Koditta_Idangalai_Nirappuga" title="Koditta Idangalai Nirappuga">Koditta Idangalai Nirappuga</a></i> directed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/R._Parthiepan" title="R. Parthiepan">R. Parthiepan</a>  with <a href="/wiki/Shanthanu_Bhagyaraj" title="Shanthanu Bhagyaraj">Shanthanu Bhagyaraj</a> as male lead in which her performance stood out and was widely appreciated. The road thriller <i>Overtake</i> was her next Malayalam film in which she performed stunts without a double. The movie opened to good reviews at the Kerala box office and the concept was fresh to Malayalam cinema .<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> She paired up with <a href="/wiki/Mohanlal" title="Mohanlal">Mohanlal</a> for the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Neerali" title="Neerali">Neerali</a></i>, directed by AJoy Varma which opened to positive reviews.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> She later appeared in Amazon Prime’s first exclusive series in Tamil named <i><a href="/wiki/Vella_Raja" title="Vella Raja">Vella Raja</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Nair received the Indian Affairs "Most Promising Actress 2017" award at the <a href="/wiki/India_Leadership_Conclave" title="India Leadership Conclave">8th Annual India Leadership Conclave</a> in Mumbai on 4 August 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup>
</p><p>Parvatii Nair collaborated with an USA based fashion brand Yog The Label<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> selling her pictures featured products manufactured in USA and Europe worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_the_media">In the media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parvatii_Nair&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: In the media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nair has been a regular guest model at Indian fashion weeks, often appearing in the main display of shows. At the 2013 Chennai International Fashion Week, Nair was the main model for Sakshi Bindra's clothing line and was the first day's "showstopper".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> She wore costumes styled by Vivek Karunakaran as a part of the Walk of Pride charity event held at the Park, <a href="/wiki/Chennai" title="Chennai">Chennai</a> in April 2015. During August 2015, she attended the India Luxury Style Week and Chennai's Fashion Culture Week within a fortnight, to promote the fashion industry in the city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> In 2016, she was showstopper in the first day of the Bangalore Fashion Week, wearing traditional wear by designer Mable Thomas, and then at the Keralan Fashion League.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> Her experience as a successful model saw her as a main judge on Amrita TV's reality show, <i>Supermodels</i> during 2013. She also appeared alongside four other actresses as the judges of the beauty pageant, Miss India Bangalore 2015 during December of that year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> Her popularity as a model had initially helped her make a breakthrough into the film industry, and saw her get recognition across all four South Indian states.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> 
</p><p>Nair was announced as the brand ambassador of the Karnataka Bulldozers team during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2016_Celebrity_Cricket_League" title="2016 Celebrity Cricket League">2016 Celebrity Cricket League</a> and attended the team's fixtures across South India as a part of her role. She shared ambassadorial duties with actress <a href="/wiki/Sharmiela_Mandre" title="Sharmiela Mandre">Sharmiela Mandre</a>, stating that she had grown up following the sport during her childhood in the <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a>, where her father and brother had played regularly. The actress also attended matches played by <a href="/wiki/Royal_Challengers_Bangalore" title="Royal Challengers Bangalore">Royal Challengers Bangalore</a> during the IPL Season of 2015 and played cricket as part of shooting for <i><a href="/wiki/Uttama_Villain" title="Uttama Villain">Uttama Villain</a></i> (2015).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> In 2016, she was announced as one of the ambassadors of the Nellai Dragons team led by <a href="/wiki/Jayam_Ravi" title="Jayam Ravi">Jayam Ravi</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Nadigar_Sangam" title="Nadigar Sangam">Nadigar Sangam</a>'s Star Cricket competition. Nair has also been a part of charity events including the Clean Bengaluru Challenge in November 2014 initiated by Times Foundation, where she helped sweep the streets of Vasanthnagar in the city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> She has been chosen by Peta India as an ambassador for the adopt a stray dog campaign.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> She was brand ambassador for the World Junior Squash Championships held in Chennai along with Ashok Selvan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup>
</p>
