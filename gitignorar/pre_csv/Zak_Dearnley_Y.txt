<p><b>Zachary Harry Dearnley</b> (born 28 September 1998) is an English professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">forward</a> for <a href="/wiki/New_Mills_A.F.C." title="New Mills A.F.C.">New Mills</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Zak_Dearnley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Sheffield" title="Sheffield">Sheffield</a>, Dearnley began his career with <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MUFC_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-MUFC-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MEN_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-MEN-3">[3]</a></sup> He was on the substitutes bench in May 2017 for their final day <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> encounter with <a href="/wiki/Crystal_Palace_F.C." title="Crystal Palace F.C.">Crystal Palace</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> During the <a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_Manchester_United_F.C._season" title="2018–19 Manchester United F.C. season">2018–19</a> season under <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Mourinho" title="José Mourinho">José Mourinho</a>, Dearnley trained with the club's senior squad in the build-up to a <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">UEFA Champions League</a> tie with <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MEN_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-MEN-3">[3]</a></sup> 
</p><p>On 31 January 2019, Dearnley was loaned to <a href="/wiki/EFL_League_Two" title="EFL League Two">EFL League Two</a> side <a href="/wiki/Oldham_Athletic_A.F.C." title="Oldham Athletic A.F.C.">Oldham Athletic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He made his professional debut on 9 February against <a href="/wiki/Crawley_Town_F.C." title="Crawley Town F.C.">Crawley Town</a>, scoring Oldham's second goal in a 3–0 victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2018-19_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-2018-19-6">[6]</a></sup> Dearnley's loan, after nine appearances, was terminated on 30 April due to injury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>On 7 June 2019, it was announced that Dearnley would leave Manchester United following the expiration of his contract on 30 June.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Manchester_United_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Manchester_United-8">[8]</a></sup> In the succeeding August, Dearnley had a trial with <a href="/wiki/Rotherham_United_F.C." title="Rotherham United F.C.">Rotherham United</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>On 19 November 2019, Dearnley joined <a href="/wiki/North_West_Counties_Football_League" title="North West Counties Football League">North West Counties Football League</a> Division One South side <a href="/wiki/New_Mills_A.F.C." title="New Mills A.F.C.">New Mills</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> His debut arrived later that day against <a href="/wiki/Ellesmere_Rangers_F.C." title="Ellesmere Rangers F.C.">Ellesmere Rangers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NMS_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-NMS-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Zak_Dearnley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dearnley represented England at <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-16_football_team" title="England national under-16 football team">U16</a> and <a href="/wiki/English_Schools%27_Football_Association" title="English Schools' Football Association">Schools U18</a> level.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MUFC_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-MUFC-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
