<p><i><b>Hugtto! PreCure Futari wa Pretty Cure: All Stars Memories</b></i><span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">映画 HUGっと!プリキュア♡ふたりはプリキュア オールスターズメモリーズ</span><span style="display:none">,</span> <i>Eiga Hagutto! Purikyua ♡ Futari wa Purikyua Ōru Sutāzu Memorīzu</i>)</span> is the 25th <i><a href="/wiki/Pretty_Cure" title="Pretty Cure">Pretty Cure</a></i> movie, as well as the 11th All Stars movie produced by <a href="/wiki/Toei_Animation" title="Toei Animation">Toei Animation</a>. To celebrate the 15th anniversary of the franchise, the <i><a href="/wiki/Hugtto!_PreCure" title="Hugtto! PreCure">Hugtto! PreCure</a></i> team joins forces with the <i><a href="/wiki/Futari_wa_Pretty_Cure" title="Futari wa Pretty Cure">Futari wa Pretty Cure</a></i> team, alongside all the other teams as well. The movie was released in theaters on October 27th, 2018 with <a href="/wiki/Memory" title="Memory">memories</a> as its theme.
</p><p>The film topped Japanese box office records in its first release in theaters and currently owns the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guinness_World_Record" title="Guinness World Record">Guinness World Record</a> for "Most Magical Warriors in an Anime Film", with a total of 55 Pretty Cures, spanning thirteen generations.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Synopsis">Synopsis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hugtto!_PreCure_Futari_wa_Pretty_Cure:_All_Stars_Memories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Synopsis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A vengeful teru teru bozu-like monster called Miden is stealing all of the Cures' magical powers and memories, turning them into helpless infants, barring Nagisa Misumi and Hana Nono. They must help the affected Cures to regain their abilities and fight off this new menance. 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hugtto!_PreCure_Futari_wa_Pretty_Cure:_All_Stars_Memories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This is the 15th anniversary film for the <i><a href="/wiki/Pretty_Cure" title="Pretty Cure">Pretty Cure</a></i> franchise.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> After the 17 March 2018 release of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pretty_Cure_Super_Stars!" title="Pretty Cure Super Stars!">Pretty Cure Super Stars!</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> the film's title was announced on June 17 of the same year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>The film is a crossover between the current series HUGtto! Precure and the inaugural series <i><a href="/wiki/Futari_wa_Pretty_Cure" title="Futari wa Pretty Cure">Futari wa Pretty Cure</a></i>, and marks the first time since 2016 where all of the Pretty Cures appear at once.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>Similar to <i><a href="/wiki/Pretty_Cure_Dream_Stars!" title="Pretty Cure Dream Stars!">Pretty Cure Dream Stars!</a>,</i> in addition to Hiroshi Miyamoto returning as director, the film combines cel animation and computer-generated animation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The Pretty Cure dialogue was recorded twice, and the CGI sequence started in January 2018, ahead of <i>HUGtto! PreCure'</i>s premiere. Production of the cel animation started in August 2018.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Miracle Light for the movie is the Miracle Memories Light<span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">ミラクル♡メモリーズライト</span><span style="display:none">,</span> <i>Mirakuru♡Memorīzu Raito</i>)</span>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> and the first 200,000 children received the Mirai Crystal~Cure Yell and Cure Black~<span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">ミライクリスタル〜キュアエール&amp;キュアブラック〜</span><span style="display:none">,</span> <i>Mirai Kurisutaru〜Kyua Ēru &amp; Kyua Burakku〜</i>)</span> as a present.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p><p>There were several tie-ins with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yokohama_City" title="Yokohama City">Yokohama City</a>. A Pretty Cure special stage dance involving the <a href="/wiki/Kanto_Gakuin_University" title="Kanto Gakuin University">Kanto Gakuin University</a> cheerleading dance team Fits was held at Minato Mirai Smart Festival 2018.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> A fifteenth anniversary exhibition was held at <a href="/wiki/Yokohama_Landmark_Tower" title="Yokohama Landmark Tower">Yokohama Landmark Tower</a> for eight days, and <a href="/wiki/Mizuki_Yamamoto" title="Mizuki Yamamoto">Mizuki Yamamoto</a>, who appeared in the film as the reporter, hosted a <a href="/wiki/Women-only_space" title="Women-only space">women-only space</a> called "PRECURE GIRLS 'NIGHT" on September 21.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> On October 21, the week before the film release, a premiere screening was held at Yokohama Burg 13, and all the 55 Pretty Cure costumes appeared at Grand Mall Park.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> In addition, various places in Yokohama City including Yokohama Minato Mirai 21 are used as a part of the stage everywhere in the work including the first scene.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception_and_accolades">Reception and accolades</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hugtto!_PreCure_Futari_wa_Pretty_Cure:_All_Stars_Memories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Reception and accolades">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film earned 357 million yen during the first two days of release (October 27 and 28).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-animate20181029_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-animate20181029-21">[21]</a></sup> This was the third <i>Pretty Cure</i> film in a row to rank first place.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-animate20181029_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-animate20181029-21">[21]</a></sup> Pia gave the film a first place ranking at 92.4. Toei described it as "the biggest hit start of Spring and Autumn compared to the 24 films so far".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup>
</p><p>With a revenue of 1.15 billion yen,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> <i>All Stars Memories</i> is the highest-grossing <i>Pretty Cure</i> film since <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pretty_Cure_All_Stars_DX2:_Light_of_Hope_-_Protect_the_Rainbow_Jewel!" title="Pretty Cure All Stars DX2: Light of Hope - Protect the Rainbow Jewel!">Pretty Cure All Stars DX2: Light of Hope - Protect the Rainbow Jewel!</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup>
</p><p>With 55 Pretty Cures in the movie, <a href="/wiki/Guinness_World_Records" title="Guinness World Records">Guinness World Records</a> approved the movie for the world record of "Most Magical Warriors in An Anime Film" on 27 October, 2018.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> The film also won the Best 3D in an Anime award at the 2018 CGWorld Awards<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> and the Best Film award at the 2019 VFX Japan Awards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hugtto!_PreCure_Futari_wa_Pretty_Cure:_All_Stars_Memories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Single">Single</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hugtto!_PreCure_Futari_wa_Pretty_Cure:_All_Stars_Memories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Single">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>DANZEN! Futari wa Pretty Cure-The One and Only Twin Lights~</i><span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">DANZEN!ふたりはプリキュア〜唯一無二の光たち〜</span><span style="display:none">,</span> <i>DANZEN! Futari wa Purikyua 〜Yuiitsumuni no Hikaritachi〜</i>)</span> is the insert song of the film, sung by <a href="/wiki/Mayumi_Gojo" title="Mayumi Gojo">Mayumi Gojo</a>. it is paired with <i>Rewind Memory</i><span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">リワインドメモリー</span><span style="display:none">,</span> <i>Riwaindo Memorī</i>)</span>, sung by Gojo and Kanako Miyamoto. The single, released on October 24, 2018, topped at #35 in the Oricon Singles Chart on November 5, 2018.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup>
</p>
