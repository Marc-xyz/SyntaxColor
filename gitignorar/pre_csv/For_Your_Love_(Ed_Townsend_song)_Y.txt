<p>"<b>For Your Love</b>" is a song written and performed by <a href="/wiki/Ed_Townsend" title="Ed Townsend">Ed Townsend</a>. It reached #7 on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Songs" title="Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs">US R&amp;B chart</a> and #13 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> in 1958.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_charting_versions">Other charting versions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=For_Your_Love_(Ed_Townsend_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Other charting versions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/The_Righteous_Brothers" title="The Righteous Brothers">The Righteous Brothers</a> released a version of the song as a single in 1965 that reached #103 on the <i>Billboard</i> chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Peaches_%26_Herb" title="Peaches &amp; Herb">Peaches &amp; Herb</a> released a version of the song as a single in 1967 that reached #10 on the R&amp;B chart and #20 on the <i>Billboard</i> chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bobby_Austin_(musician)" title="Bobby Austin (musician)">Bobby Austin</a> released a version of the song as a single in 1970 that reached #65 on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">country chart</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gwen_McCrae" title="Gwen McCrae">Gwen McCrae</a> released a version of the song as a single in 1973 that reached #17 on the R&amp;B chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bobby_Lewis_(country_singer)" title="Bobby Lewis (country singer)">Bobby Lewis</a> released a version of the song as a single in 1976 that reached #52 on the country chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_versions">Other versions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=For_Your_Love_(Ed_Townsend_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Other versions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Prentice_Moreland" title="Prentice Moreland">Prentice Moreland</a> released a version of the song as the <a href="/wiki/A-side_and_B-side" title="A-side and B-side">B-side</a> to his 1962 single "Lover Supreme".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Joe_Tex" title="Joe Tex">Joe Tex</a> released a version of the song as a single in <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> in 1967.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Carla_Thomas" title="Carla Thomas">Carla Thomas</a> released a version of the song as a single as part of her <i>Carla</i> <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> in 1967.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Frankie_Avalon" title="Frankie Avalon">Frankie Avalon</a>  released a version of the song as a single in 1969.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Houston_Person" title="Houston Person">Houston Person</a> released a version of the song as the B-side to his 1970 single "Wadin"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and was featured on his album, <i><a href="/wiki/Truth!" title="Truth!">Truth!</a></i></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/The_Supremes" title="The Supremes">The Supremes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Four_Tops" title="Four Tops">Four Tops</a> released a version of the song on their 1970 album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Magnificent_7_(album)" title="The Magnificent 7 (album)">The Magnificent 7</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></i></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cynthia_Richards" title="Cynthia Richards">Cynthia Richards</a> released a version of the song as a single in <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a> in 1975.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bruce_Springsteen" title="Bruce Springsteen">Bruce Springsteen</a> released a version of the song as a soundchecked track from the Nugs.net recording of his May 23, 1988 concert at <a href="/wiki/Madison_Square_Garden" title="Madison Square Garden">Madison Square Garden</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></li></ul>
