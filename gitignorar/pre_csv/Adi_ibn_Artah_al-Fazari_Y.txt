	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Adi ibn Artah al-Fazari</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Adi ibn Artah al-Fazari</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar">عدي بن أرطاة الفزاري</span>‎) (died 720) was a governor of <a href="/wiki/Basra" title="Basra">Basra</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Umayyad_dynasty" title="Umayyad dynasty">Umayyad dynasty</a>, serving during the caliphate of <a href="/wiki/Umar_II" title="Umar II">'Umar ibn 'Abd al-'Aziz</a>. He was killed during the revolt of <a href="/wiki/Yazid_ibn_al-Muhallab" title="Yazid ibn al-Muhallab">Yazid ibn al-Muhallab</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career_under_Umar_II">Career under Umar II</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adi_ibn_Artah_al-Fazari&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career under Umar II">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Adi was appointed to the governorship of Basra by the caliph <a href="/wiki/Umar_II" title="Umar II">Umar II</a>, shortly after the latter's ascension in 717.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> After receiving his appointment, he established himself in Basra; he also ordered the arrest of his predecessor <a href="/wiki/Yazid_ibn_al-Muhallab" title="Yazid ibn al-Muhallab">Yazid ibn al-Muhallab</a>, who Umar had dismissed from the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Umayyad_governors_of_Iraq" title="List of Umayyad governors of Iraq">governorship of Iraq</a>. Once Yazid was captured, Adi shipped him to the caliph's residence in <a href="/wiki/Bilad_al-Sham" title="Bilad al-Sham">Syria</a>, where he was cast into prison.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Adi served as governor of Basra for the duration of Umar's caliphate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KIK322_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-KIK322-3">[3]</a></sup> Unlike Yazid, who had been governor of all of Iraq and the eastern provinces, Adi did not have authority over <a href="/wiki/Kufa" title="Kufa">Kufa</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Khurasan" title="Khurasan">Khurasan</a>, which were placed under separate governors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> His area of responsibility, however, was still considerable, and extended from Basra and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahrain_(historical_region)" title="Bahrain (historical region)">Bahrayn</a> in the west to <a href="/wiki/Sindh" title="Sindh">Sind</a> in the east.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KIK322_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-KIK322-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Oman" title="Oman">Oman</a> was also initially under Adi's authority, but Umar revoked his jurisdiction over it after receiving complaints about his lieutenant's administration there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>During Adi's governorship, he authorized public works projects in Basra; a canal was dug to provide water to the city's residents and was named after him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> A second canal which was completed was named after Adi's chief of <a href="/wiki/Shurta" title="Shurta">police</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He also wanted to enlarge the governor's residence in the city, but was reportedly dissuaded from doing so by Umar.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> During his administration, a plague struck in 718-719 and was named the "plague of Adi ibn Artah."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Muhallabid_revolt">The Muhallabid revolt</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adi_ibn_Artah_al-Fazari&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The Muhallabid revolt">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shortly after the death of Umar in February 720, Adi received a message from the new caliph <a href="/wiki/Yazid_II" title="Yazid II">Yazid II</a> informing him that Yazid ibn al-Muhallab had escaped from prison and that he was likely heading toward Iraq. On the caliph's orders, Adi arrested and imprisoned the <a href="/wiki/Muhallabids" title="Muhallabids">Muhallabids</a> who were in Basra and prepared for an confrontation with Yazid; the local army was organized and a trench was dug around the city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>As Yazid advanced toward Basra, however, Adi's defense began to fall apart. Many of his men refused to fight against Yazid, and contingents that were sent out to stop his march instead allowed him to pass by unmolested. Yazid also began offering higher stipends to the Basrans, which increased his popularity among them. Consequently, when he arrived at Basra he was able to enter the city without much difficulty. The Syrians and Basrans who remained loyal to Adi attempted to mount a defense outside the fortress of Basra, but they were defeated and the fortress was stormed by Yazid's men. The remainder of Adi's forces fled to Kufa; Adi himself was captured and brought before Yazid, who ordered his confinement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>Following Yazid's conquest of Basra, Adi was transferred to <a href="/wiki/Wasit,_Iraq" title="Wasit, Iraq">Wasit</a>, which was put under the command of Yazid's son <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mu%27awiya_ibn_Yazid_al-Muhallabi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mu'awiya ibn Yazid al-Muhallabi (page does not exist)">Mu'awiya</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> He remained incarcerated there until Yazid was killed in battle against <a href="/wiki/Maslama_ibn_Abd_al-Malik" title="Maslama ibn Abd al-Malik">Maslama ibn Abd al-Malik</a> in August 720. When news of Yazid's fate reached Wasit, Mu'awiya took out a number of prisoners under his charge, including Adi and his son Muhammad, and executed them in retaliation for his father's death.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p>
