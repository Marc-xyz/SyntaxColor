<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Dunfermline_Palace_engraving_by_William_Miller_after_W_Brown.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1830" data-file-width="2479" decoding="async" height="162" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/Dunfermline_Palace_engraving_by_William_Miller_after_W_Brown.jpg/220px-Dunfermline_Palace_engraving_by_William_Miller_after_W_Brown.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/Dunfermline_Palace_engraving_by_William_Miller_after_W_Brown.jpg/330px-Dunfermline_Palace_engraving_by_William_Miller_after_W_Brown.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/Dunfermline_Palace_engraving_by_William_Miller_after_W_Brown.jpg/440px-Dunfermline_Palace_engraving_by_William_Miller_after_W_Brown.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Dunfermline_Palace_engraving_by_William_Miller_after_W_Brown.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Dunfermline Palace seen from the Lyne burn, in an engraving by <a href="/wiki/William_Miller_(engraver)" title="William Miller (engraver)">William Miller</a></div></div></div>
<p><b>Dunfermline Palace</b> is a ruined former <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scottish</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scottish_royalty" title="Scottish royalty">royal</a> <a href="/wiki/Palace" title="Palace">palace</a> and important tourist attraction in Dunfermline, <a href="/wiki/Fife" title="Fife">Fife</a>, Scotland. It is currently, along with other buildings of the adjacent <a href="/wiki/Dunfermline_Abbey" title="Dunfermline Abbey">Dunfermline Abbey</a>, under the care of <a href="/wiki/Historic_Scotland" title="Historic Scotland">Historic Scotland</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Scheduled_monument" title="Scheduled monument">scheduled monument</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Dunfermline was a favourite residence of many <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kings_of_Scotland" title="Kings of Scotland">Scottish monarchs</a>. Documented history of royal residence there begins in the 11th century with <a href="/wiki/Malcolm_III_of_Scotland" title="Malcolm III of Scotland">Malcolm III</a> who made it his capital. His seat was the nearby <a href="/wiki/Malcolm%27s_Tower" title="Malcolm's Tower">Malcolm's Tower</a>, a few hundred yards to the west of the later palace. In the medieval period <a href="/wiki/David_II_of_Scotland" title="David II of Scotland">David II</a> and <a href="/wiki/James_I_of_Scotland" title="James I of Scotland">James I of Scotland</a> were both born at Dunfermline. 
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Dunfermline_Abbey_gatehouse_and_pend,_Dunfermline.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2448" data-file-width="3264" decoding="async" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Dunfermline_Abbey_gatehouse_and_pend%2C_Dunfermline.jpg/220px-Dunfermline_Abbey_gatehouse_and_pend%2C_Dunfermline.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Dunfermline_Abbey_gatehouse_and_pend%2C_Dunfermline.jpg/330px-Dunfermline_Abbey_gatehouse_and_pend%2C_Dunfermline.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Dunfermline_Abbey_gatehouse_and_pend%2C_Dunfermline.jpg/440px-Dunfermline_Abbey_gatehouse_and_pend%2C_Dunfermline.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Dunfermline_Abbey_gatehouse_and_pend,_Dunfermline.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <a href="/wiki/Gatehouse" title="Gatehouse">gatehouse</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pend" title="Pend">pend</a> which link Dunfermline palace and abbey.</div></div></div>
<p>Dunfermline Palace is attached to the historic <a href="/wiki/Dunfermline_Abbey" title="Dunfermline Abbey">Dunfermline Abbey</a>, occupying a site between the abbey and deep gorge to the south. It is connected to the former monastic residential quarters of the abbey via a gatehouse above a <a href="/wiki/Pend" title="Pend">pend</a> (or <i>yett</i>), one of Dunfermline's medieval <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Town_gate" title="Town gate">gates</a>. The building therefore occupies what was originally the guest house of the abbey. However, its remains largely reflect the form in which the building was remodelled by <a href="/wiki/James_IV_of_Scotland" title="James IV of Scotland">James IV</a> around 1500. In November 1504 James IV was in residence when two people were suspected of having plague. James IV and <a href="/wiki/Margaret_Tudor" title="Margaret Tudor">Margaret Tudor</a> and their servants left for Edinburgh, including <a href="/wiki/Ellen_More" title="Ellen More">African women</a> known as the "More lasses".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Throughout the sixteenth century, Scotland's monarchs and royal family members were frequently in residence. 
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Dunfermline_Palace_plaque_King_Charles_1.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1215" data-file-width="1603" decoding="async" height="167" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Dunfermline_Palace_plaque_King_Charles_1.JPG/220px-Dunfermline_Palace_plaque_King_Charles_1.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Dunfermline_Palace_plaque_King_Charles_1.JPG/330px-Dunfermline_Palace_plaque_King_Charles_1.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Dunfermline_Palace_plaque_King_Charles_1.JPG/440px-Dunfermline_Palace_plaque_King_Charles_1.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Dunfermline_Palace_plaque_King_Charles_1.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Plaque commemorating King Charles I who was born at Dunfermline Palace in 1600</div></div></div>
<p>In 1589 the palace was given as a wedding present by the king, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_VI_of_Scotland" title="James VI of Scotland">James VI</a>, to <a href="/wiki/Anne_of_Denmark" title="Anne of Denmark">Anne of Denmark</a> after their marriage. She gave birth to three of their children there; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Elizabeth_of_Bohemia" title="Elizabeth of Bohemia">Elizabeth</a> (1596),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Charles_I_of_England" title="Charles I of England">Charles</a> (1600) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Robert_Stuart,_Duke_of_Kintyre" title="Robert Stuart, Duke of Kintyre">Robert</a> (1602). 
</p><p>After the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Union_of_Crowns" title="Union of Crowns">Union of Crowns</a> in 1603, the removal of the Scottish court to <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> meant that the building came to be rarely visited by a monarch.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Ten tapestries from the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Royal_tapestry_collection" title="Scottish Royal tapestry collection">royal tapestry collection</a> were still there in 1616, left from the time the infant Prince Charles resided at the Palace.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> When Charles I returned in 1633 for his Scottish <a href="/wiki/Coronation" title="Coronation">coronation</a> he only made a brief visit to his place of birth. The last monarch to occupy the palace was <a href="/wiki/Charles_II_of_England" title="Charles II of England">Charles II</a> who stayed at Dunfermline in 1650 just before the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Pitreavie" title="Battle of Pitreavie">Battle of Pitreavie</a>. Soon afterwards, during the <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Cromwell#Scottish_campaign:_1650–1651" title="Oliver Cromwell">Cromwellian occupation</a> of Scotland, the building was abandoned and by 1708 it had been unroofed. 
</p><p>All that remains of the palace today is the kitchen, its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cellars" title="Cellars">cellars</a>, and the impressive south wall with a commanding prospect over the <a href="/wiki/Firth_of_Forth" title="Firth of Forth">Firth of Forth</a> to the south.
</p>
