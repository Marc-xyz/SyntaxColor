<p><b>Diocesan Shrine and Parish of St. Mary Magdalene</b> (Filipino: <i>Dambanang Pangdiyosesis at Parokya ni Santa Maria Magdalena and Simbahan ng Pililla)</i> is a <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Roman Catholic Church</a> located in the <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_the_Philippines" title="Municipalities of the Philippines">municipality</a> of <a href="/wiki/Pililla,_Rizal" title="Pililla, Rizal">Pillila</a>, Province of Rizal, Philippines. The church was built by the <a href="/wiki/Franciscans" title="Franciscans">Franciscans</a> in 1583, under the patronage of <a href="/wiki/Mary_Magdalene" title="Mary Magdalene">St. Mary Magdalene</a>. It is a few kilometers away from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Ildefonso_Parish_Church_(Tanay,_Rizal)" title="San Ildefonso Parish Church (Tanay, Rizal)">San Ildefonso Parish Church</a>, a historical church in Tanay.
</p><p>On January 16, 1977, the <a href="/wiki/National_Historical_Commission_of_the_Philippines" title="National Historical Commission of the Philippines">National Historical Commission of the Philippines</a> unveiled the historical marker of St. Mary Magdalene Parish Church.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 22, 2018, The Parish was declared as a diocesan shrine.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Mary_Magdalene_Parish_Church_(Pililla,_Rizal)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1571, the Spaniards conquered and inhabited the towns along <a href="/wiki/Laguna_de_Bay" title="Laguna de Bay">Laguna de Bay</a>. Prior to Spanish colonization, Pililla was named <i>Pilang Munti</i>. It was incorporated to the administration of <a href="/wiki/Morong,_Rizal" title="Morong, Rizal">Morong</a> and was named <i>Pilang Morong</i>.
</p><p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Franciscan" title="Franciscan">Franciscan</a> priests led by Fr. Diego de Oropesa and Fr. <a href="/wiki/Juan_de_Plasencia" title="Juan de Plasencia">Juan de Plasencia</a> arrived at <i>Pilang Morong</i> in 1572. As part of the established <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reducciones" title="Reducciones">reducciones</a></i> system, the Franciscans built the first church made from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cogon_grass" title="Cogon grass">cogon</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nipa_palm" title="Nipa palm">nipa</a> in 1583.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> When <i>Pilang Morong</i> was given autonomy in 1599, the central government granted an authorization to construct a church of stone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-faithweb_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-faithweb-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1632, a conflagration destroyed the church and the whole town. When a new church was built, another destruction by fire ruined the church in 1668. A new church was immediately completed between 1670 and 1673. Renovation of the church altar and the adjacent convent were completed in 1848. Several renovation projects were also completed between 1962 and 1976.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-faithweb_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-faithweb-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Features">Features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Mary_Magdalene_Parish_Church_(Pililla,_Rizal)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The church is made of <a href="/wiki/Adobe" title="Adobe">adobe</a>, similar to other churches in Laguna. The façade and belfry of the church are simple in design.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-faithweb_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-faithweb-3">[3]</a></sup> The Baptistery is located at the bottom of the bell tower, on the left side from the vestibule of the church. A small <a href="/wiki/Retablo" title="Retablo">retablo</a> housing the image of St. Mary Magdalene can be found inside the baptistery.
</p>
