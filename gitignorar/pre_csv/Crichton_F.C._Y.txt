<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crichton_F.C.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The most recent incarnation of the club formed in 1972 as <b>Auldgirth Football Club</b> playing in local amateur leagues. Auldgirth FC renamed to <b>Blackwood Dynamos Football Club</b> before a 1999 re-name to reflect their home ground is Crichton Hospital Park.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> They were originally to adopt the title <b>Crichton Royal Football Club</b>, but never assumed the <i>Royal</i> part of the name. Their strip consisted of blue and white.
</p><p>They formerly competed in the <a href="/wiki/South_of_Scotland_Football_League" title="South of Scotland Football League">South of Scotland Football League</a>, which they joined in the <a href="/wiki/1992%E2%80%9393_in_Scottish_football" title="1992–93 in Scottish football">1992–93 season</a>. They won the South of Scotland League Cup in the <a href="/wiki/2002%E2%80%9303_in_Scottish_football" title="2002–03 in Scottish football">2002–03 season</a>.
</p><p>Manager Jim Thompson guided the club to their best ever season in <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_in_Scottish_football" title="2007–08 in Scottish football">2007–08</a>, as they won the league title. Winning the South of Scotland League gave the Crichton passage to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scottish_Cup_2008%E2%80%9309" title="Scottish Cup 2008–09">2008–09 Scottish Cup</a>. Having received a bye in the first round, they played <a href="/wiki/Inverness" title="Inverness">Inverness</a> side <a href="/wiki/Clachnacuddin_F.C." title="Clachnacuddin F.C.">Clachnacuddin</a> away in the second round on 25 October 2008, where they lost 1–0.
</p><p>On 3 May 2015, it was announced that the club would fold after 43 years. This was due to their Chairman and two committee members leaving a committee which was already at the bare bones. In June 2015, it was announced that the club would merge with local amateur side Lochmaben Amateurs. The club played its home fixtures for the 2015–16 season at Hospital Park after a season playing away from their home ground. Before the start of the 2016-17 season, Crichton effectively folded as part of a merger with Lochmaben Amateurs and the club moved to <a href="/wiki/Lockerbie" title="Lockerbie">Lockerbie</a> and now play under the Lochmaben name.
</p>
