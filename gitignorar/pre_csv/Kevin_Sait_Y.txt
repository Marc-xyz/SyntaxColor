<p><b>Kevin Sait</b> (born 11 November 1956) is a former <a href="/wiki/Australian_rules_football" title="Australian rules football">Australian rules footballer</a> who played with <a href="/wiki/Western_Bulldogs" title="Western Bulldogs">Footscray</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Football_League" title="Australian Football League">Victorian Football League</a> (VFL).
</p><p>Sait, a rover from <a href="/wiki/Yarraville_Football_Club" title="Yarraville Football Club">Yarraville</a>, was a star in the second division of the <a href="/wiki/Victorian_Football_League" title="Victorian Football League">Victorian Football Association</a>, where he won the Field Trophy, which was awarded to the <a href="/wiki/Best_and_fairest" title="Best and fairest">best and fairest</a> player in the second division, in 1980;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> he placed second for the same award in 1977,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and third in 1979.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>A former <a href="/wiki/Richmond_Football_Club" title="Richmond Football Club">Richmond</a> seconds player, Sait spent the <a href="/wiki/1981_VFL_season" title="1981 VFL season">1981 VFL season</a> at Footscray, where he made 13 senior appearances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AFLTables_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-AFLTables-5">[5]</a></sup> On debut, against <a href="/wiki/Essendon_Football_Club" title="Essendon Football Club">Essendon</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Western_Oval" title="Western Oval">Western Oval</a>, Sait kicked two last quarter goals, to help secure an eight-point win.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AFLTables_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-AFLTables-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> It would be one of only two wins that year for Footscray and the only time Sait didn't finish on a losing side.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AFLTables_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-AFLTables-5">[5]</a></sup> He averaged 21 disposals from his 13 games in 1981 and kicked 18 goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AFLTables_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-AFLTables-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>From 1982 to 1987, Sait played for <a href="/wiki/Williamstown_Football_Club" title="Williamstown Football Club">Williamstown</a>, back in the Victorian Football Association.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fox_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fox-8">[8]</a></sup> He made 94 appearances for the club and was a member of their 1986 premiership team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fox_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Fox-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Williamstown_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Williamstown-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
