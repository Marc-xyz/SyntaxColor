<p><i><b>The Kovak Box</b></i> is a 2006 <a href="/wiki/Psychological_thriller" title="Psychological thriller">psychological thriller</a> <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">film</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Monz%C3%B3n" title="Daniel Monzón">Daniel Monzón</a> and starring <a href="/wiki/Timothy_Hutton" title="Timothy Hutton">Timothy Hutton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luc%C3%ADa_Jim%C3%A9nez" title="Lucía Jiménez">Lucía Jiménez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Annette_Badland" title="Annette Badland">Annette Badland</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Kelly_(actor)" title="David Kelly (actor)">David Kelly</a>. The film concerns an American <a href="/wiki/Horror_fiction" title="Horror fiction">horror</a>/<a href="/wiki/Science_fiction" title="Science fiction">science fiction</a> novelist who finds the plot of one of his stories unfold around him after a conference in <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Synopsis">Synopsis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Kovak_Box&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Synopsis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On the island city of <a href="/wiki/Palma_de_Mallorca" title="Palma de Mallorca">Palma de Mallorca</a>, David Norton (Hutton) promotes his latest book, <i>The Kovak Box</i>, before a small private gathering of European fans. While nothing comes across as unusual, one of the audience members has a distinctive <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_Mafia" title="Russian Mafia">Russian Mafia</a> arm tattoo and the hotel <a href="/wiki/Concierge" title="Concierge">concierges</a> are subtly condescending towards him, despite the writer's mannered patronage and celebrity status.
</p><p>Later in the evening, Norton's fiancee, Jane, receives an anonymous phone call and <a href="/wiki/Jumper_(person)" title="Jumper (person)">jumps off</a> their hotel balcony. After dying from her injuries in the hospital, Norton discovers a nearby patient, Silvia Mendez (Jiménez), did the same thing on the same night: While taking a shower, Silvia answered a call on her cellphone consisting only of the 1933 song <i><a href="/wiki/Gloomy_Sunday" title="Gloomy Sunday">Gloomy Sunday</a></i> by composer <a href="/wiki/Rezs%C5%91_Seress" title="Rezső Seress">Rezső Seress</a>. After jumping from her window, she only remembers waking up nude under a crushed bar canopy. Silvia is a young nightclub debutante with no enemies or connections to the American couple. While recuperating in her apartment, a mysterious man attempts to stab her in the back of the neck but flees during a struggle. After surviving the attack, Silvia reunites with Norton, who is also being pursued by unknown assailants.
</p><p>As he begins piecing answers together, Norton finds that the events coincide with the plot of his latest book, which is based on a real individual: Frank Kovak (Kelly), a disgraced <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungarian</a> doctor who was driven to exile after his experiments were passed off as <a href="/wiki/Psychological_warfare" title="Psychological warfare">psychological warfare</a>. Kovak has used <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neural_implants" title="Neural implants">neural implants</a> and paid a network of criminals to emulate the experiment on Norton, in which everyone takes their lives (or suffers a brutal death) while the <i>Gloomy Sunday</i> song plays. Ailing from a terminal <a href="/wiki/Brain_tumor" title="Brain tumor">brain tumor</a>, Kovak has nothing to lose except Norton winning his deadly game.
</p>
