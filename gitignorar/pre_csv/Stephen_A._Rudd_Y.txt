<p><b>Stephen Andrew Rudd</b> (December 11, 1874 – March 31, 1936) was an American politician from <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stephen_A._Rudd&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He was born on December 11, 1874, in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kings_County,_New_York" title="Kings County, New York">Kings County, New York</a>, the son of Assemblyman <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_J._Rudd&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Robert J. Rudd (page does not exist)">Robert J. Rudd</a>.
</p><p>He was elected as a <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">Democrat</a> to the <a href="/wiki/71st_United_States_Congress" title="71st United States Congress">71st United States Congress</a>, to fill the vacancy caused by the death of <a href="/wiki/David_J._O%27Connell" title="David J. O'Connell">David J. O'Connell</a>, and was re-elected to the <a href="/wiki/72nd_United_States_Congress" title="72nd United States Congress">72nd</a>, <a href="/wiki/73rd_United_States_Congress" title="73rd United States Congress">73rd</a> and <a href="/wiki/74th_United_States_Congress" title="74th United States Congress">74th United States Congresses</a>, holding office from February 17, 1931 until his death on March 31, 1936.
</p><p>He died on March 31, 1936, in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, aged 61.
</p><p>State Senator <a href="/wiki/Roy_H._Rudd" title="Roy H. Rudd">Roy H. Rudd</a> (1906–1997) was his son.
</p>
