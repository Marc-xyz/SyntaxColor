<p><i><b>Rebel Heart Tour</b></i> is the fifth <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Live_album" title="Live album">live album</a> by American singer and songwriter <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a>, chronicling her tenth worldwide <a href="/wiki/Rebel_Heart_Tour" title="Rebel Heart Tour">concert tour of the same name</a>, recorded at Sydney's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Allphones_Arena" title="Allphones Arena">Allphones Arena</a>. It was released on September 15, 2017 by <a href="/wiki/Eagle_Rock_Entertainment" title="Eagle Rock Entertainment">Eagle Vision</a> on <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> and <a href="/wiki/Blu-ray" title="Blu-ray">Blu-ray</a> formats and by <a href="/wiki/Eagle_Records" title="Eagle Records">Eagle Records</a> for audio versions. <i>Rebel Heart Tour</i> also contains bonus content like the <a href="/wiki/Madonna:_Tears_of_a_Clown" title="Madonna: Tears of a Clown">Tears of a Clown</a> show (2016) at <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Forum_Theatre" title="Forum Theatre">Forum Theatre</a>, as well as a 22-song double CD. <a href="/wiki/Danny_Tull" title="Danny Tull">Danny Tull</a> and Nathan Rissman, who had worked on Madonna's previous concert films, directed <i>Rebel Heart Tour</i>.
</p><p>The album received generally positive feedback from <a href="/wiki/Music_journalism" title="Music journalism">critics</a>, who picked Madonna's live singing, dancing and the production as highlights. <i>Rebel Heart Tour</i> reached the top of the DVD and video charts in most of the countries it charted in, and top ten of the album charts in Belgium (Flanders and Wallonia), Germany, Hungary, Italy, Portugal and Spain. At the 32nd <a href="/wiki/Japan_Gold_Disc_Award" title="Japan Gold Disc Award">Japan Gold Disc Award</a>, <i>Rebel Heart Tour</i> won in the category of Best Music Video for Western Artists.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rebel_Heart_Tour_(album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Madonna_Rebel_Heart_Tour_2015_-_Stockholm_(22791069684)_(cropped).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3056" data-file-width="4075" decoding="async" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Madonna_Rebel_Heart_Tour_2015_-_Stockholm_%2822791069684%29_%28cropped%29.jpg/220px-Madonna_Rebel_Heart_Tour_2015_-_Stockholm_%2822791069684%29_%28cropped%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Madonna_Rebel_Heart_Tour_2015_-_Stockholm_%2822791069684%29_%28cropped%29.jpg/330px-Madonna_Rebel_Heart_Tour_2015_-_Stockholm_%2822791069684%29_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Madonna_Rebel_Heart_Tour_2015_-_Stockholm_%2822791069684%29_%28cropped%29.jpg/440px-Madonna_Rebel_Heart_Tour_2015_-_Stockholm_%2822791069684%29_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Madonna_Rebel_Heart_Tour_2015_-_Stockholm_(22791069684)_(cropped).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Madonna performing "<a href="/wiki/Like_a_Prayer_(song)" title="Like a Prayer (song)">Like a Prayer</a>" on the <a href="/wiki/Rebel_Heart_Tour" title="Rebel Heart Tour">Rebel Heart Tour</a>; the performance of the song was included as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bonus_track" title="Bonus track">bonus track</a> on the video release.</div></div></div>
<p>Madonna had embarked on the <a href="/wiki/Rebel_Heart_Tour" title="Rebel Heart Tour">Rebel Heart Tour</a> (2015–2016) to promote her thirteenth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Rebel_Heart" title="Rebel Heart">Rebel Heart</a></i>. Performed in 82 shows across 55 cities, the tour was a commercial success earning US$169.8 million; it was attended by an audience of over 1.045 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The performances at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Allphones_Arena" title="Allphones Arena">Allphones Arena</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a>, Australia were recorded for the video release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>In September 2016, Madonna announced on her <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a> that she had finished watching a "rough assembly" of the tour's film, and it would be out in the next two months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i> subsequently announced that the concert film would premiere on December 9, 2016 on American cable channel <a href="/wiki/Showtime_(TV_network)" title="Showtime (TV network)">Showtime</a>. Titled <i>Madonna: Rebel Heart Tour</i>, it featured behind-the-scenes footage from the Australian performances of the tour,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> which had been exclusively previewed by <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> on December 2, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Danny_Tull" title="Danny Tull">Danny Tull</a> and Nathan Rissman, who had worked on Madonna's previous concert films, directed <i>Rebel Heart Tour</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Madonna explained in an interview with <a href="/wiki/BBC_News" title="BBC News">BBC News</a>:
</p>
<blockquote>
<p>I was there every step of the way, every day for months and months. It's really hard to capture the true feeling of the excitement and the passion and the heat and the blood, sweat and tears. I'm pleased with the way it came out... [W]hen I look back at the DVD it almost brings a tear to my eye because everyone seems so in love.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
</blockquote>
<p>The film was released on September 15, 2017 on DVD, Blu-ray, and digital formats,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> containing bonus content like the <a href="/wiki/Madonna:_Tears_of_a_Clown" title="Madonna: Tears of a Clown">Tears of a Clown</a> show at <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Forum_Theatre" title="Forum Theatre">Forum Theatre</a> in 2016, as well as a 22-song double CD.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The cover image was shot by fashion photographer Joshua Brandão.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/British_Board_of_Film_Classification" title="British Board of Film Classification">British Board of Film Classification</a> (BBFC) listed the total running time of the album at over 138 minutes, with almost two hours for the main tour film and the rest for Tears of a Clown and a bonus performance of "<a href="/wiki/Like_a_Prayer_(song)" title="Like a Prayer (song)">Like a Prayer</a>" (1989) during one of the shows. The release of <i>Rebel Heart Tour</i> was distributed by <a href="/wiki/Eagle_Rock_Entertainment" title="Eagle Rock Entertainment">Eagle Rock Entertainment</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> It was made available for pre-order on August 16, 2017, Madonna's birthday, in all physical and digital-file-based stores. Along with the pre-order, a live version of "<a href="/wiki/Material_Girl" title="Material Girl">Material Girl</a>" was purchasable from the digital outlets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The Japanese edition of the DVD/ Blu-ray included a performance of "<a href="/wiki/Take_a_Bow_(Madonna_song)" title="Take a Bow (Madonna song)">Take a Bow</a>" (1994) as a bonus track.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-japan_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-japan-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rebel_Heart_Tour_(album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Madonna_-_Rebel_Heart_Tour_Cologne_2_(22851518577)_(cropped)2.jpg"><img alt="Madonna, dressed in a jeweled black dress and wearing long see-through fingerless gloves, plays the ukulele as she sings to a microphone." class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3264" data-file-width="2641" decoding="async" height="210" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Madonna_-_Rebel_Heart_Tour_Cologne_2_%2822851518577%29_%28cropped%292.jpg/170px-Madonna_-_Rebel_Heart_Tour_Cologne_2_%2822851518577%29_%28cropped%292.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Madonna_-_Rebel_Heart_Tour_Cologne_2_%2822851518577%29_%28cropped%292.jpg/255px-Madonna_-_Rebel_Heart_Tour_Cologne_2_%2822851518577%29_%28cropped%292.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Madonna_-_Rebel_Heart_Tour_Cologne_2_%2822851518577%29_%28cropped%292.jpg/340px-Madonna_-_Rebel_Heart_Tour_Cologne_2_%2822851518577%29_%28cropped%292.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Madonna_-_Rebel_Heart_Tour_Cologne_2_(22851518577)_(cropped)2.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Madonna performing "<a href="/wiki/La_Vie_en_rose" title="La Vie en rose">La Vie en rose</a>" during the Rebel Heart Tour. Her live singing on the video release was critically acclaimed.</div></div></div>
<p>Markos Papadatos from the <i>Digital Journal</i> gave the album a rating of A describing the release as a "must for any die-hard fan of Madonna".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Jeffrey Kaufman from Blu-ray.com gave a rating of four stars out of five for the release, saying that Madonna knows "how to provide amazing visuals for a Blu-ray concert release to accompany an interesting if kind of random assortment of tunes from her by now pretty long career." He further complimented the production, the video and the audio quality of the album, recommending it for being "constantly over the top and often quite breathtaking".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blu-ray_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-blu-ray-16">[16]</a></sup> Writing for <a href="/wiki/New_York_Post" title="New York Post">Decider</a>, Benjamin H. Smith opined that "the 2017 concert film, <i>Madonna: Rebel Heart Tour</i>, shows the Material Girl hasn’t lost any steps now that she’s more a grande dame [...] what she debatably lacks as a vocalist, she has always made up for with her ability to craft her persona, like <a href="/wiki/David_Bowie" title="David Bowie">David Bowie</a>, altering it to keep up with the latest trends, but always maintaining her singular identity", but pointed out that  "it was her ‘80s songbook that got the biggest response".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup>
</p><p>Daryl Deino from <a href="/wiki/Inquisitr" title="Inquisitr">Inquisitr</a> gave a positive review for the live album of the release, but criticized its DVD and Blu-ray versions for poor editing. He named <i>Rebel Heart Tour</i> Madonna's best live album to date and found it superior to the DVD and Blu-ray. He also complimented Madonna's live, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Autotune" title="Autotune">unprocessed</a> vocals on the album, praising in particular her cover of "<a href="/wiki/La_Vie_en_rose" title="La Vie en rose">La Vie en rose</a>", calling it "the best vocal performance of her career so far [...] this is a song nobody could imagine [Madonna] covering at the beginning of her career. Now, it sounds natural". Although the singer's voice sounded "strained" to Deino during the dance sequences, it was not noticeable in the video versions since "Madonna's dancing is the key element in these performances". He ended his review saying that the "whole album makes you want to run to a Madonna concert, and that's exactly what a live album is supposed to do."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-inquisitr_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-inquisitr-18">[18]</a></sup>
</p><p>Simon Button from <i><a href="/wiki/Attitude_(magazine)" title="Attitude (magazine)">Attitude</a></i> commended the release, describing it as "a work of theatrical genius", complimenting the dancers, Madonna's camaraderie onstage and the "smoke-and-mirrors element" in the product. However, he criticized the inclusion of the Tears of a Clown show, finding it to be "as much a car crash as the two hours of the <i>Rebel Heart Tour</i> are a triumph".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-attitude_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-attitude-19">[19]</a></sup> Writing for DVD Movie Guide, Colin Jacobson felt that "as a concert, Madonna’s <i>Rebel Heart Tour</i> falls into the 'good but not great' category. As a video, it disappoints because it fails to effectively recapture the live experience".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Roger Wink, from music portal Vintage Vinyl News, was critical of the CD release; "much of the allure of a Madonna show is the visual aspect which doesn't come across on CD, so the DVD/Blu-Ray is probably a much better experience but that doesn't excuse the low points, and there are many of them, in the listening experience".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_reception">Commercial reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rebel_Heart_Tour_(album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Commercial reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the United Kingdom, <i>Rebel Heart Tour</i> sold 1,993 copies in its first week of release, and debuted at number 42 on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Albums_Chart" title="UK Albums Chart">UK Albums Chart</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> In the United States, <i>Rebel Heart Tour</i> failed to chart on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>, but reached numbers 20 and 45 on the Digital Album and Top Album Sales charts for one week each, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usdigital_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-usdigital-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usalbumsales_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-usalbumsales-24">[24]</a></sup> The album also reached number two on the Top Music Video chart, being kept from the pole position by <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jackson" title="Alan Jackson">Alan Jackson</a>'s <i>Precious Memories: Live at the Ryman</i> (2009).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usvideo_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-usvideo-25">[25]</a></sup> <i>Rebel Heart Tour</i> reached number one in Mexico, and placed at number 69 on the year-end chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-amprofon_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-amprofon-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-amproyear_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-amproyear-27">[27]</a></sup> In France, the live album peaked at number 20 on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/French_Albums_Chart" title="French Albums Chart">French Albums Chart</a> selling 1,921 copies, while the video versions topped the French DVD Charts selling 5,044 units.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Syndicat_National_de_l%27%C3%89dition_Phonographique" title="Syndicat National de l'Édition Phonographique">Syndicat National de l'Édition Phonographique</a> (SNEP) certified the video Platinum for selling over 15,000 units.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-snep_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-snep-29">[29]</a></sup> Across the rest of Europe, the album reached the top ten of the charts in Belgium (Flanders and Wallonia), Germany, Hungary, Italy, Portugal and Spain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fla_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-fla-30">[30]</a></sup> At the 32nd <a href="/wiki/Japan_Gold_Disc_Award" title="Japan Gold Disc Award">Japan Gold Disc Award</a>, <i>Rebel Heart Tour</i> won in the category of Best Music Video for Western Artists.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup>
</p>
