<p>"<b>Fly</b>" is a song written and performed by <a href="/wiki/Jars_of_Clay" title="Jars of Clay">Jars of Clay</a>. It was the first mainstream single and second <a href="/wiki/Christian_radio" title="Christian radio">Christian radio</a> single from their 2002 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album">studio album</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Eleventh_Hour_(Jars_of_Clay_album)" title="The Eleventh Hour (Jars of Clay album)">The Eleventh Hour</a></i>.  A live version of the song appears on disc two of the band's 2003 <a href="/wiki/Double_album" title="Double album">double album</a>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Furthermore:_From_the_Studio,_From_the_Stage" title="Furthermore: From the Studio, From the Stage">Furthermore: From the Studio, From the Stage</a></i>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fly_(Jars_of_Clay_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Fly" is based on the story of a couple fighting a losing battle against the wife's cancer. Specifically, it is about the moment at which her husband "lets her go". The unnamed couple were friends or close acquaintances of band members.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
