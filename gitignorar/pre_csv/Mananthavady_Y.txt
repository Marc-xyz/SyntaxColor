<p><b>Mananthavady</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Municipality" title="Municipality">municipality</a> and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taluk" title="Taluk">taluk</a></i> in the <a href="/wiki/Wayanad_district" title="Wayanad district">Wayanad district</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kerala" title="Kerala">Kerala</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>. The town is located on the banks of Mananthavady River,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources in the body of the article. (September 2019)">not verified in body</span></a></i>]</sup> a tributary of the <a href="/wiki/Kabini_River" title="Kabini River">Kabini River</a>. The Adivasi Gothra Mahasabha is headquartered in the town,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources in the body of the article. (September 2019)">not verified in body</span></a></i>]</sup> and the district hospital in Mananthavady is the only major treatment facility for the tribal communities of Wayanad.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources in the body of the article. (September 2019)">not verified in body</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>The area had once been ruled by the Pazhassi dynasty and the tomb of <a href="/wiki/Pazhassi_Raja" title="Pazhassi Raja">Pazhassi Raja</a> is an important heritage site located in the heart of the town.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources in the body of the article. (September 2019)">not verified in body</span></a></i>]</sup> Mananthavady had also been a major army camp for the British during their campaign against Pazhassi.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources in the body of the article. (September 2019)">not verified in body</span></a></i>]</sup> Vestiges of the <a href="/wiki/British_Raj" title="British Raj">colonial period</a> can still be seen around Mananthavady.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources in the body of the article. (September 2019)">not verified in body</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>Mananthavady has been referred to as <i>Hosenkadi</i> in a dictum scribbled under a copper artefact found at the Ananthanathaswamy Temple at Varadoor. The dominant view on the etymology is that the word is derived from "Mane Eytha Vady" (English: "The place where an arrow was shot at the deer"). This view is strengthened by the existence of a place called <i>Ambukuthy</i>, literally "the location where the arrow pierced", on the outskirts of the town.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Mananthavady is located 28 kilometres (17 mi) north-east of the district headquarters of <a href="/wiki/Kalpetta" title="Kalpetta">Kalpetta</a>, 38 km (24 mi) from <a href="/wiki/Sultan_Bathery" title="Sultan Bathery">sultan Bathery</a>, 80 km (50 mi) east of <a href="/wiki/Thalassery" title="Thalassery">Thalassery</a> and 92 km (57 mi) north-east of <a href="/wiki/Kozhikode" title="Kozhikode">Kozhikode</a> (via Kutyady).
</p><p>Mananthavady is the nearest municipality in Kerala to Mysore, 105 km (65 mi) away via Kartikulam, Bavali and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/H_D_Kote" title="H D Kote">H D Kote</a> through <a href="/wiki/Nagarhole_National_Park" title="Nagarhole National Park">Nagarhole National Park</a>.
</p>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Government_College_Mananthavady" title="Government College Mananthavady">Government College Mananthavady</a> was established in 1981, and was the only centre for higher learning in North Wayanad for more than a decade.  In 1995, <a href="/wiki/Mary_Matha_Arts_%26_Science_College" title="Mary Matha Arts &amp; Science College">Mary Matha Arts &amp; Science College</a> opened in the government-aided sector.
</p><p>The two private colleges are P.K.K.M. College of Applied Science and W.M.O. Imam Gazzali Arts &amp; Science College.
</p><p>All of the above colleges are affiliated with <a href="/wiki/Kannur_University" title="Kannur University">Kannur University</a>, which has a campus in Mananthavady providing instruction in teacher education, zoology, and rural and tribal sociology.
</p><p>In 1999, <a href="/wiki/Government_Engineering_College,_Mananthavady" title="Government Engineering College, Mananthavady">Government Engineering College, Mananthavady</a>, opened as the first engineering college in Wayanad district. It is affiliated with Kerala Technological University, and offers bachelor's and master's degree courses in computer science, electronics, mechanical engineering, electrical engineering, network security and communications engineering.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Boys_Town">Boys Town</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mananthavady&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Boys Town">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Valliyoorkavu_Temple">Valliyoorkavu Temple</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mananthavady&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Valliyoorkavu Temple">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Valliyoorkkavu" title="Valliyoorkkavu">Valliyoorkkavu</a></b> is a temple located 3 km (1.9 mi) from the town, dedicated to the mother goddess which is worshipped in three principal forms of <a href="/wiki/Durga" title="Durga">Vana Durga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bhadrakali" title="Bhadrakali">Bhadrakali</a> and <a href="/wiki/Durga" title="Durga">Jala Durga</a>. It is the most important place of worship for the tribal communities. An annual 15-day festival held in March is the grandest of all festivals in the district, and is the largest congregation of tribal peoples in the district.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Minor_attractions">Minor attractions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mananthavady&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Minor attractions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kerala_Lalita_Kala_Akademi" title="Kerala Lalita Kala Akademi">Kerala Lalithakala Akademi</a> Art Gallery is located on the Mysore Road. It regularly hosts exhibitions of various Indian artists and organizes cultural events.</li>
<li>A Latin church is situated at the heart of the town, and dates to the colonial past.</li>
<li>Paalamandhapam Devi Temple and Malakkari Temple Koolivayal are located 12 km (7.5 mi) from Mananthavady, towards Panamaram town.</li>
<li>Thrissilery Siva Temple is a well-known shrine in Wayanad situated in Thrissilery. There is a Jala Durga temple in the same complex.</li>
<li>Muthireri kavu is a temple associated with <a href="/wiki/Kottiyoor_Vysakha_Mahotsavam" title="Kottiyoor Vysakha Mahotsavam">Kottiyoor Vysakha Mahotsavam</a>, situated in the small village of Muthireri, about 8 km (5.0 mi) from Mananthavady.</li>
<li>Holy Trinity CSI Church Mananthavady is an 18th-century church in Wayanad, located near the municipal bus stand.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mananthavady&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The nearest railway station is at <a href="/wiki/Vatakara" title="Vatakara">Vatakara</a>, 65 km (40 mi) away. A Thalassery–Manathavady–Mysore railway line is being planned.  The nearest airport is <a href="/wiki/Kannur_International_Airport" title="Kannur International Airport">Kannur International Airport</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mattannur" title="Mattannur">Mattannur</a>, 66 km (41 mi) away. <a href="/wiki/Mysore_Airport" title="Mysore Airport">Mysore Airport</a> is 109 km (68 mi) away, followed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kozhikode_International_Airport" title="Kozhikode International Airport">Kozhikode International Airport</a> at 120 km (75 mi) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bengaluru_International_Airport" title="Bengaluru International Airport">Bengaluru International Airport</a> at 290 km (180 mi).
</p><p>The Thalassery–Bavali Road passes through Mananthavady and is the town's main road, allowing connection to <a href="/wiki/Mysore" title="Mysore">Mysore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Karnataka" title="Karnataka">Karnataka</a>. The road to Mysore through Nagarhole National Park has been declared a National Highway by the central government; it is parallel to the <a href="/wiki/Kabini_River" title="Kabini River">Kabini River</a> and closed at night due to animal crossings. The highway goes from Kainatty (at the junction of NH 212[766]) to Mysore via Bavali, Jayapura.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Another road permits access to <a href="/wiki/Gonikoppal" title="Gonikoppal">Gonikoppal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kodagu_district" title="Kodagu district">Kodagu district</a>, 60 km (37 mi) away via  Kartikulam, Tholpetty forest, Kutta and <a href="/wiki/Ponnampet" title="Ponnampet">Ponnampet</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-"><a href="#cite_note-">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
