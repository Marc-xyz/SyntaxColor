<p><b>Los Túneles subterráneos de San Germán</b> is a vaulted brick storm sewer system built in 1835 underneath the urban center of <a href="/wiki/San_Germ%C3%A1n,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Germán, Puerto Rico">San Germán, Puerto Rico</a>.  The system is made of a central tunnel and several smaller side tunnels. The construction is of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brick_and_rubble" title="Brick and rubble">brick and rubble</a> with modern concrete repairs. The main part of the system covers the underground course of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Quebrada_Manzanares&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Quebrada Manzanares (page does not exist)">Quebrada Manzanares</a> from its headwaters to its resurgence as a surface stream, 842.53 metres (2,764.2 ft) away.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nps_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-nps-4">[3]</a></sup>  The vaulted brick branches, most of them abandoned and sealed, date mostly from before 1910. The tunnel sections built up to 1918 are listed on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> as <b>Alcantarilla Pluvial sobre la Quebrada Manzanares</b>.
</p>
