<p>The <b>2008–09 Philadelphia 76ers season</b> was the 70th season of the franchise, 60th in the <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_Association" title="National Basketball Association">National Basketball Association</a> (NBA). The team finished with a .500 record.
The only two things memorable about the season is that the team went back to the classic uniform style the team wore in the 1980s, and the Sixers defeated the Chicago Bulls in a game played at The Spectrum, the teams home from 1967–1996.
</p><p>The team signed Elton Brand to a 5-year $79 million dollar contract.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Key_dates">Key dates</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008%E2%80%9309_Philadelphia_76ers_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Key dates">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b>June 26</b>: The <a href="/wiki/2008_NBA_draft" title="2008 NBA draft">2008 NBA draft</a> took place in New York City.</li>
<li><b>July 1</b>: The <a href="/wiki/Free_agent" title="Free agent">free agency</a> period started.</li>
<li><b>July 9</b>: Signed 2-time All-Star PF <a href="/wiki/Elton_Brand" title="Elton Brand">Elton Brand</a>.</li>
<li><b>October 8</b>: The Sixers' preseason started with a game against the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Celtics" title="Boston Celtics">Boston Celtics</a>.</li>
<li><b>October 29</b>: The Sixers' regular season started with a game against the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Raptors" title="Toronto Raptors">Toronto Raptors</a>.</li>
<li><b>December 13</b>: The Sixers fired head coach <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Cheeks" title="Maurice Cheeks">Maurice Cheeks</a> after a 9–14 start and appoint Assistant general manager <a href="/wiki/Tony_DiLeo" title="Tony DiLeo">Tony DiLeo</a> as interim head coach.</li>
<li><b>March 13</b>: The Sixers played their last game at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wachovia_Spectrum" title="Wachovia Spectrum">Wachovia Spectrum</a> against the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Bulls" title="Chicago Bulls">Chicago Bulls</a>.</li>
<li><b>April 4</b>: The Sixers clinched a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NBA_Playoffs" title="NBA Playoffs">playoff berth</a> to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009_NBA_Playoffs" title="2009 NBA Playoffs">2009 NBA Playoffs</a>.</li>
<li><b>April 30</b>: The Sixers were eliminated from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009_NBA_Playoffs" title="2009 NBA Playoffs">2009 NBA Playoffs</a> with their game 6 loss to <a href="/wiki/Orlando_Magic" title="Orlando Magic">Orlando Magic</a> finishing the series 4–2.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Summary">Summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008%E2%80%9309_Philadelphia_76ers_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2008_NBA_draft">2008 NBA draft</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008%E2%80%9309_Philadelphia_76ers_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2008 NBA draft">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On June 26, the Sixers selected power forward/center <a href="/wiki/Marreese_Speights" title="Marreese Speights">Marreese Speights</a> from <a href="/wiki/Florida_Gators_men%27s_basketball" title="Florida Gators men's basketball">Florida</a> with the 16th overall pick. Their second round pick was previously traded to the <a href="/wiki/Utah_Jazz" title="Utah Jazz">Utah Jazz</a> on June 7, 2009 for a second round pick in the <a href="/wiki/2009_NBA_draft" title="2009 NBA draft">2009 NBA draft</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_Agency">Free Agency</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008%E2%80%9309_Philadelphia_76ers_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Free Agency">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Sixers headed into the off-season with free agents <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Louis_Amundson" title="Louis Amundson">Louis Amundson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Calvin_Booth" title="Calvin Booth">Calvin Booth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Herbert_Hill_(basketball)" title="Herbert Hill (basketball)">Herbert Hill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andre_Iguodala" title="Andre Iguodala">Andre Iguodala</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Ollie" title="Kevin Ollie">Kevin Ollie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shavlik_Randolph" title="Shavlik Randolph">Shavlik Randolph</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lou_Williams" title="Lou Williams">Louis Williams</a>.
</p><p>On June 24, the Sixers extended qualifying offers to key restricted free agents <a href="/wiki/Andre_Iguodala" title="Andre Iguodala">Andre Iguodala</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lou_Williams" title="Lou Williams">Louis Williams</a>. The Sixers brought in <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Hawks" title="Atlanta Hawks">Atlanta Hawks</a> restricted free agent <a href="/wiki/Josh_Smith" title="Josh Smith">Josh Smith</a>  on July 3 for a tour of Philadelphia but left the city without signing an offer sheet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 9 after denouncing their rights to their unrestricted free agents,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and trading <a href="/wiki/Rodney_Carney" title="Rodney Carney">Rodney Carney</a> and <a href="/wiki/Calvin_Booth" title="Calvin Booth">Calvin Booth</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> the Sixers signed 2-time NBA All-Star Power Forward <a href="/wiki/Elton_Brand" title="Elton Brand">Elton Brand</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Clippers" title="Los Angeles Clippers">Los Angeles Clippers</a> to a 5-year contract reportedly worth 78–82 Million dollars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>After the Brand signing, the first weeks of July were quiet in terms of free agency. Rumors spread that key restricted free agents Andre Iguodala and Louis Williams were being pursued by the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Clippers" title="Los Angeles Clippers">Los Angeles Clippers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Golden_State_Warriors" title="Golden State Warriors">Golden State Warriors</a> respectively.
</p><p>On July 24 it was announced by Philly.com that the Sixers had come to terms with Point Guard <a href="/wiki/Royal_Ivey" title="Royal Ivey">Royal Ivey</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Bucks" title="Milwaukee Bucks">Milwaukee Bucks</a> for a 2-year league minimum contract. The deal was finalized and signed on Monday July 28.
</p><p>Also on July 28, Philly.com released that the Sixers had come to terms with Shooting Guard <a href="/wiki/Kareem_Rush" title="Kareem Rush">Kareem Rush</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Indiana_Pacers" title="Indiana Pacers">Indiana Pacers</a>. The deal was finalized and signed on Tuesday July 29.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>After news reports stated that <a href="/wiki/Lou_Williams" title="Lou Williams">Louis Williams</a> rejected a $3.9M deal from the Sixers and he was talking to the <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Cavaliers" title="Cleveland Cavaliers">Cleveland Cavaliers</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> he signed a 5-year deal on July 31.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 12, ESPN announced that the 76ers had come to terms with restricted free agent <a href="/wiki/Andre_Iguodala" title="Andre Iguodala">Andre Iguodala</a> on a reported 6-year 80 million dollar deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> After several days, it was officially announced on August 18 that Iguodala had signed the deal.
</p><p>On August 19 Philly.com announced that veteran backup big man <a href="/wiki/Theo_Ratliff" title="Theo Ratliff">Theo Ratliff</a> had agreed to terms on veterans league minimum 1 year 1.4 million dollar deal with the 76ers. Ratliff had previously played with Philadelphia from 1997–2001. Ratliff was named an All-Star in 2001, but was unable to play because of an injury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>On September 1, 2008 following his placement on waivers <a href="/wiki/Donyell_Marshall" title="Donyell Marshall">Donyell Marshall</a> agreed to terms with the Philadelphia 76ers for a one-year contract for the league minimum $1,262,275 (for veterans with at least 10 years of experience).  The story was first reported by The Philadelphia Daily News<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>

<p>In the first round of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009_NBA_Playoffs" title="2009 NBA Playoffs">2009 NBA Playoffs</a>, the sixth seeded <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_76ers" title="Philadelphia 76ers">Philadelphia 76ers</a> were paired with the third seed <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Division_(NBA)" title="Southeast Division (NBA)">Southeast Division</a> champions and eventual <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Conference_(NBA)" title="Eastern Conference (NBA)">Eastern Conference</a> champion <a href="/wiki/Orlando_Magic" title="Orlando Magic">Orlando Magic</a> in a best-of-seven series. The Sixers managed to split the series with the Magic at two games a piece after four games, but the Magic went on to win what would be the final two games of the series to eliminate the Sixers.
</p>
