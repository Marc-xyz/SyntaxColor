<p>The <b>tropical pewee</b> or <b>southern tropical pewee</b> (<i>Contopus cinereus</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Tyrant_flycatcher" title="Tyrant flycatcher">tyrant flycatcher</a> family.  It breeds from southern <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trinidad" title="Trinidad">Trinidad</a> south to  <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">Bolivia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Taxon" title="Taxon">taxon</a> <i>punensis</i>, found in south-western <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuador</a> and western <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>, is usually included as a <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> of the tropical pewee, but it has been suggested it should be considered a separate <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a>, the <b><a href="/wiki/Tumbes_pewee" title="Tumbes pewee">Tumbes pewee</a></b> (<i>Contopus punensis</i>) .
</p><p>This <a href="/wiki/Pewee" title="Pewee">pewee</a> is found at the edges of <a href="/wiki/Forest" title="Forest">forests</a> and cultivated areas with tall trees.  The <a href="/wiki/Bird_nest" title="Bird nest">nest</a> is a small open saucer of fibre and grasses, lined with grass and decorated with <a href="/wiki/Lichen" title="Lichen">lichen</a> on its exterior. It is placed in a tree fork or on a branch. The female builds the nest and incubates the typical <a href="/wiki/Clutch_(eggs)" title="Clutch (eggs)">clutch</a> of two creamy-white <a href="/wiki/Bird_egg" title="Bird egg">eggs</a>, which are marked with red-brown spots at the larger end, for 15–16 days to hatching.
</p><p>The tropical pewee is 14 cm long and weighs 12 g. The upperparts are dark brown or grey with a blackish crown and two whitish wing bars. The throat and centre of the breast are whitish, the abdomen is pale yellow, and the sides of the flanks and breast are grey-brown. There are large variation in the overall darkness of the <a href="/wiki/Plumage" title="Plumage">plumage</a>, and especially the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nominate_subspecies" title="Nominate subspecies">nominate subspecies</a> from south-eastern <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> and adjacent parts of <a href="/wiki/Paraguay" title="Paraguay">Paraguay</a> and Argentina can be very dark, almost approaching the <a href="/wiki/Blackish_pewee" title="Blackish pewee">blackish pewee</a> in colour. The <a href="/wiki/Beak" title="Beak">beak</a> is short, with a black upper mandible and orange lower mandible. Sexes are similar.
</p><p>Tropical pewees perch on a high watchpoint from which they sally forth to catch flying <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a>, returning to the same exposed perch.
</p><p>This is a conspicuous species, with a trilled <i>threeee</i> call, or a sharp <i>weet</i>. There are, however, some geographical variations in its <a href="/wiki/Bird_vocalization" title="Bird vocalization">voice</a>. It will defend the nest aggressively against species as large as a <a href="/wiki/Great_kiskadee" title="Great kiskadee">great kiskadee</a>.
</p>
