	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Vaca Valley and Clear Lake Railroad</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><table class="infobox" style="width:22em"><caption>Vaca Valley and Clear Lake Railroad</caption><tbody><tr><th scope="row">Locale</th><td>Elmira-Vacaville-Winters-Esparto-Rumsey, California</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Dates of operation</th><td>1877–1988</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Successor</th><td><a href="/wiki/Northern_Railway_(California)" title="Northern Railway (California)">Northern Railway</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row"><a href="/wiki/Track_gauge" title="Track gauge">Track gauge</a></th><td><span class="nowrap">4 ft <span class="frac">8<span class="visualhide"> </span><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>2</sub></span> in</span> (<span class="nowrap">1,435 mm</span>) <span class="nowrap"><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Standard_gauge" title="Standard gauge">standard gauge</a></span></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Headquarters</th><td>Vacaville, California</td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>The <b>Vaca Valley and Clear Lake Railroad</b> was a standard gauge railroad that operated at Vacaville, California in the late 19th century.  The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vaca_Valley_Railroad" title="Vaca Valley Railroad">Vaca Valley Railroad</a> was incorporated on April 12, 1869 to run a branch from the mainline of the <a href="/wiki/California_Pacific_Railroad" title="California Pacific Railroad">California Pacific Railroad</a> (later <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southern_Pacific_Railroad" title="Southern Pacific Railroad">Southern Pacific Railroad's</a> mainline between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sacramento" title="Sacramento">Sacramento</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oakland" title="Oakland">Oakland, CA</a>) at <a href="/wiki/Elmira,_California" title="Elmira, California">Elmira</a> to <a href="/wiki/Rumsey,_California" title="Rumsey, California">Rumsey</a>.
</p><p>The Vaca Valley Railroad ran 4.35 miles from Elmira to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vacaville" title="Vacaville">Vacaville</a>.  In June 1869 the line was opened for service.  The Vaca Valley was sold at the Solano County Sheriff's auction on September 17, 1870 but retained the same name.  The railroad was then extended beyond Vacaville, reaching <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Winters,_CA" title="Winters, CA">Winters</a> on August 26, 1875.
</p><p>In 1877, just 10 weeks after completing the track between Winters and <a href="/wiki/Madison,_California" title="Madison, California">Madison</a> the Vaca Valley Railroad became the Vaca Valley and Clear Lake Railroad.
</p><p>In 1888 this railroad fell under control of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southern_Pacific_Railroad" title="Southern Pacific Railroad">Southern Pacific's</a> subsidiary, the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Railway_(California)" title="Northern Railway (California)">Northern Railway</a>.  By 1888, the Northern Railway extended the line northwest from Madison to <a href="/wiki/Capay,_California" title="Capay, California">Capay</a> and Rumsey.
</p><p>In 1898 the line was operated by Southern Pacific (SP) and ran from the Cal-P Oakland-Sacramento mainline at Elmira to Rumsey.  SP abandoned the track between Rumsey and Capay in 1934.  The track between Rumsey and <a href="/wiki/Esparto,_California" title="Esparto, California">Esparto</a> was removed in 1937.  Passenger service continued between Elmira and Esparto until 1957.<a class="external autonumber" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20090514084303/http://www.capayvalleyvision.org/timeline.html" rel="nofollow">[1]</a>
</p><p>The line between Winters and Vacaville, which ran parallel to the west side of <a href="/wiki/Interstate_505" title="Interstate 505">Interstate 505</a>, was abandoned in the 1970s, ending at the grocery warehouses just south of Midway Road/I-505 in Vacaville.  The Vacaville Branch's last train operated in about 1985 and the line was removed to Elmira by the 1990s.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Timeline">Timeline</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vaca_Valley_and_Clear_Lake_Railroad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Timeline">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>July 27, 1868 <a href="/wiki/California_Pacific_Railroad" title="California Pacific Railroad">California Pacific Railroad</a> completes track from Vallejo – Elmira via Suisun. At Vallejo the Cal-P had a railroad ferry to San Francisco.</li>
<li>August 10, 1868 California Pacific completes Elmira – Dixon track, and reaches Sacramento in 1869/1870.</li>
<li>April 12, 1869 Vaca Valley Railroad incorporated in Vacaville to build line from California Pacific at Elmira to Vacaville.</li>
<li>May 10, 1869 On the same day the last spike was driven at Promontory Point, Utah to complete the Central Pacific Railroad/Union Pacific Railroad, grading commenced on the Vaca Valley Railroad.</li>
<li>June 7, 1869 Rails laid on Vaca Valley Railroad</li>
<li>June 16, 1869 The first train of the Vaca Valley Railroad was operated</li>
<li>June 21, 1869 track between Elmira and Vacaville (5 miles) placed into operation.</li>
<li>August 26, 1875 track between Vacaville and Winters (Winter's) (10 miles) opened via Allendale.</li>
<li>June 30, 1876 California Pacific Railroad sold to Central Pacific Railroad.  Vaca Valley Railroad owns 19.5 miles of track.</li>
<li>February 19, 1877 Vaca Valley Railroad continues northwest expansion towards Madison and is renamed the Vaca Valley &amp; Clear Lake Railroad.</li>
<li>May 1, 1877 track opened between Winters and Madison, a total of 27.4 miles from Elmira.</li>
<li>May 15, 1888 Vaca Valley &amp; Clear Lake RR merged into the Northern Railway, (a Southern Pacific Railroad subsidiary.  Northern Railway was created on this date as a consolidation of several smaller railroads.</li>
<li>July 1, 1888 Northern Railway opens 23.65 miles of track between Madison and Rumsey via Capay (Esparto).</li>
<li>April 14, 1898 Northern Railway consolidated into Southern Pacific Railroad.  Line is known as the Rumsey Branch of SP.</li>
<li>April 15, 1934 SP abandons track between Rumsey and Capay.</li>
<li>1937 Track removed between Rumsey and Capay.  Line becomes SP's Capay Branch.</li>
<li>1943 Track abandoned from Esparto – Capay.  Line becomes SP's Esparto Branch.</li>
<li>1957 Last passenger train operated between Elmira and Esparto.</li>
<li>March 7, 1975 Line abandoned between Vacaville – Esparto (ICC Fin Doc AB-12).  Line becomes SP's Vacaville Branch.</li>
<li>1985 Last freight train operates on Vacaville Branch and Vacaville Branch abandoned between Elmira and Vacaville.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Locomotives">Locomotives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vaca_Valley_and_Clear_Lake_Railroad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Locomotives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>These locomotives were listed on the Vaca Valley &amp; Clear Lake Railroad's roster.
</p>
<ul><li>No. 1 "Vacaville" was built by <a href="/wiki/Vulcan_Iron_Works" title="Vulcan Iron Works">Vulcan Iron Works</a> in May 1867 (Builder No. 9) and was a Type <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2-2-4" title="2-2-4">2-2-4</a>.  It was sold to Union Coal Company in 1888 when the railroad was sold to SP's subsidiary, the Northern Railway.</li>
<li>No. 2 "Ben Ely" was built by <a href="/wiki/Baldwin_Locomotive_Works" title="Baldwin Locomotive Works">Baldwin Locomotive Works</a> in July 1875 (Builder No. 3753) and was a Type <a href="/wiki/2-6-0" title="2-6-0">2-6-0</a> (Mogul).  In 1888 the engine was renumbered Northern Railway #1022.</li></ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_abandoned_grade">The abandoned grade</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vaca_Valley_and_Clear_Lake_Railroad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The abandoned grade">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Traces of the abandoned grade can be found.
From the Vacaville/Allendale area, the line crossed Midway Road on the west side of I-505 and ran north to Winters on the east side of Hartley Road.  At Udell Road the line ran northbound on the east side of Winters Road.  The trestle where the line crossed Putah Creek is on the east side of Winters Road.  Present-day Railroad Ave in Winters was the old grade through town and continued north along the east side of Yolo County Road 89.  At Madison near Highway 16 the railroad crossed 89 and ran on the east side of Railroad Street where it then turned west along Highway 16 towards Esparto.  From there, Highway 16 generally follows the path (albeit with straighter curves) of the old railroad up to Rumsey.  The railroad was originally planned to continue up the twisty route to Clearlake, but investment never came through.  Each of the train stations still exist, and have been converted into residences (except for the Esparto one).
</p>
