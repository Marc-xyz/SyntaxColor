<p><b>Helmuth Förster</b> (19 April 1889 – 7 April 1965) was a German general' in the <a href="/wiki/Luftwaffe" title="Luftwaffe">Luftwaffe</a> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. A decorated <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> aviator, he returned to military service in 1934 as an <i>Oberstleutnant</i> in the Luftwaffe. Promoted to <i>Oberst</i> in 1936, he was appointed to command the <a href="/wiki/Kampfgeschwader_4" title="Kampfgeschwader 4">4th Bomber Wing</a>. During the <a href="/wiki/Invasion_of_Poland" title="Invasion of Poland">invasion of Poland</a>, Förster commanded the <i>Lehrdivision</i> with significant success. He was then appointed as chief of staff of the <a href="/wiki/Luftflotte_5" title="Luftflotte 5">5th Air Fleet</a> during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Invasion_of_Norway" title="Invasion of Norway">invasion of Norway</a>. After serving on the German-French Peace Commission, he was appointed as military governor of the <a href="/wiki/Territory_of_the_Military_Commander_in_Serbia" title="Territory of the Military Commander in Serbia">German-occupied territory of Serbia</a> from April to June 1941, then commanded the <a href="/wiki/1st_Air_Corps_(Germany)" title="1st Air Corps (Germany)">1st Air Corps</a> during <a href="/wiki/Operation_Barbarossa" title="Operation Barbarossa">the invasion of the Soviet Union</a> until October 1942. Whilst in command of the 1st Air Corps he was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Knight%27s_Cross_of_the_Iron_Cross" title="Knight's Cross of the Iron Cross">Knight's Cross of the Iron Cross</a>. He spent the remainder of the war as chief of administration at the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Aviation_(Nazi_Germany)" title="Ministry of Aviation (Nazi Germany)">Reich Ministry of Aviation</a>. He was pensioned as an <i>Oberstleutnant</i> in 1952.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMitcham2007131_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMitcham2007131-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
