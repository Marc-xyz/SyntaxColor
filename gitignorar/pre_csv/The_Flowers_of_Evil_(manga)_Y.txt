<p><i><b>The Flowers of Evil</b></i><span style="font-weight: normal"> (<a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>: <span lang="ja">惡の華</span><span style="display:none">,</span> <a href="/wiki/Hepburn_romanization" title="Hepburn romanization">Hepburn</a>: <i>Aku no Hana</i>)</span> is a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> series written and illustrated by <a href="/wiki/Sh%C5%ABz%C5%8D_Oshimi" title="Shūzō Oshimi">Shūzō Oshimi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ANN1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-ANN1-3">[3]</a></sup> It was serialized in <a href="/wiki/Kodansha" title="Kodansha">Kodansha</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Bessatsu_Sh%C5%8Dnen_Magazine" title="Bessatsu Shōnen Magazine">Bessatsu Shōnen Magazine</a></i> between September 9, 2009, and May 9, 2014,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-natalie_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-natalie-4">[4]</a></sup> and licensed by <a href="/wiki/Vertical_(company)" title="Vertical (company)">Vertical</a> in North America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The story follows a middle school student named Takao Kasuga who's forced into a "contract" by fellow student Sawa Nakamura, after being caught stealing the gym clothes of his crush Nanako Saeki, and the series of events afterwards that follow these three characters. The title of the manga comes from <a href="/wiki/Charles_Baudelaire" title="Charles Baudelaire">Charles Baudelaire</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Les_Fleurs_du_Mal" title="Les Fleurs du Mal">Les Fleurs du Mal</a></i>.
</p><p>The manga received a nomination at the fifth <a href="/wiki/Manga_Taish%C5%8D" title="Manga Taishō">Manga Taishō</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-taisho_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-taisho-6">[6]</a></sup> An <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> television series adaptation of the manga, produced by <a href="/wiki/Zexcs" title="Zexcs">Zexcs</a>, aired in Japan between April and June 2013. It was <a href="/wiki/Simulcast" title="Simulcast">simulcast</a> outside Japan by <a href="/wiki/Crunchyroll" title="Crunchyroll">Crunchyroll</a>. The anime was animated using <a href="/wiki/Rotoscoping" title="Rotoscoping">rotoscoping</a> techniques, causing some controversy among fans of the manga. The anime has been licensed by <a href="/wiki/Sentai_Filmworks" title="Sentai Filmworks">Sentai Filmworks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sentai-ANN_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-sentai-ANN-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>A live-action film adaptation released on September 27, 2019.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-liveaction1_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-liveaction1-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Flowers_of_Evil_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Kiryu_Honcho_2-chome.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3240" data-file-width="4320" decoding="async" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Kiryu_Honcho_2-chome.JPG/220px-Kiryu_Honcho_2-chome.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Kiryu_Honcho_2-chome.JPG/330px-Kiryu_Honcho_2-chome.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Kiryu_Honcho_2-chome.JPG/440px-Kiryu_Honcho_2-chome.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Kiryu_Honcho_2-chome.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The series is set in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kiryu,_Gunma" title="Kiryu, Gunma">Kiryu, Gunma</a></div></div></div>
<p>The story starts in a small town in <a href="/wiki/Gunma_Prefecture" title="Gunma Prefecture">Gunma Prefecture</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oktakumode_interview_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-oktakumode_interview-9">[9]</a></sup> and follows Takao Kasuga, a middle school bookworm whose favorite book is <a href="/wiki/Charles_Baudelaire" title="Charles Baudelaire">Charles Baudelaire</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Les_Fleurs_du_mal" title="Les Fleurs du mal">Les Fleurs du mal</a></i>. One day after school, he impulsively steals the gym clothes of Nanako Saeki, the classmate he idolizes. However, a girl named Sawa Nakamura sees him and blackmails Kasuga into a "contract". At the same time, Kasuga grows closer to Saeki and manages to become her boyfriend.
</p><p>As Kasuga spends more time with both girls, he finds the guilt of his theft weighing down on him. He attempts to confess by vandalizing his classroom with Nakamura, but Saeki refuses to break up with him. When his mother finds out he was responsible for the vandalism, he runs from home and attempts to bike with Nakamura past the mountain adjacent to the town. Saeki catches up to the pair as they rest and tries to make Kasuga come back. Unable to choose between them, he estranges both girls and the police collect them.
</p><p>A month later, Kasuga breaks up with Saeki and resolves to help Nakamura. He writes a composition to convey his feelings to her. When she runs from him, Kasuga goes to her house and reads in her diary about her disappointment in not being able to reach "the other side". He steals the panties of all the girls in his class except Saeki's and uses them to decorate a makeshift hideout, winning Nakamura's approval.
</p><p>At the start of summer vacation, they create a plan to nail the panties to a piece of plywood to display at the upcoming festival. Saeki discovers the plan and lures Kasuga to the hideout, trying to seduce him and make him stay in their town. When he chooses Nakamura over her, she rapes him but Kasuga resists, causing her to burn the hideout down.
</p><p>Saeki later turns herself in to the police for setting the fire, prompting her best friend Ai Kinoshita to tell their school about Kasuga's crimes. The school doesn't involve the police and his parents decide to move over the vacation. The day before the festival, Nakamura breaks into Kasuga's house, attacking his father, and the two escape. At the festival, they don disguises and climb to the top of a float while wielding a knife. They curse their town and pour kerosene on themselves, but before they can use a lighter, Nakamura pushes Kasuga over the float and she gets tackled by her father.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tokawamachi_(tokawa-town)_(17983634474).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3349" data-file-width="5025" decoding="async" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Tokawamachi_%28tokawa-town%29_%2817983634474%29.jpg/220px-Tokawamachi_%28tokawa-town%29_%2817983634474%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Tokawamachi_%28tokawa-town%29_%2817983634474%29.jpg/330px-Tokawamachi_%28tokawa-town%29_%2817983634474%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Tokawamachi_%28tokawa-town%29_%2817983634474%29.jpg/440px-Tokawamachi_%28tokawa-town%29_%2817983634474%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Tokawamachi_(tokawa-town)_(17983634474).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The beach in Tokawa Town where Kasuga and Tokiwa speak with Nakamura</div></div></div>
<p>A few years later, Kasuga is going to high school in <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Cmiya-ku,_Saitama" title="Ōmiya-ku, Saitama">Ōmiya-ku, Saitama</a> and still can't forget Nakamura. Kasuga finds his classmate Aya Tokiwa looking at <i>Les Fleurs du mal</i> in a used bookstore and she starts lending him novels, rekindling his love of literature.
</p><p>Kasuga discovers that she is working on a novel and is brought to tears upon reading the manuscript because he can identify with the protagonist. Kasuga encounters Saeki and when they meet up for lunch, she accuses him of using Tokiwa as a stand-in for Nakamura. Kasuga visits Tokiwa at her workplace and asks her out, saying that he will save her, and she accepts.
</p><p>Kasuga visits his hometown for his dying grandfather and ends up meeting Kinoshita, who regrets being left behind by Saeki and tells Kasuga where Nakamura moved. Tokiwa finishes her novel, but Kasuga tells her about his past and his desire to meet Nakamura again. They take the train to <a href="/wiki/Tokawa_Station" title="Tokawa Station">Tokawa Station</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ch%C5%8Dshi,_Chiba" title="Chōshi, Chiba">Chōshi, Chiba</a> and find her eatery, where her mother warns them that Nakamura is "peaceful now", but Tokiwa insists on speaking to her.
</p><p>They talk at a nearby beach and Kasuga questions her, but doesn't get satisfactory answers. The three roughhouse and Kasuga tells Nakamura that he is happy that she didn't disappear. Later, Kasuga is in college and still dating Tokiwa, who is working on another novel. After falling asleep, Kasuga dreams of the wilting flower of evil, its scar no longer present on his hand. In his dream he envisions the futures of the series' characters; he marries and has a child with Tokiwa, Saeki starts her own family and reunites with Kinoshita, and Nakamura finds contentment, eventually moving away to the city. At the end of the dream Nakamura looks up at her own wilting flower of evil. When he wakes up, Kasuga starts writing in his empty book just as he was in his dream, presumably about the manga's events.
</p><p>The final chapter depicts Nakamura's perspective of her first meeting with Kasuga. Through her eyes she sees everyone and everything around her as monochrome and deformed, symbolizing her distaste for normality. When she sees Kasuga steal the gym suit, his features become clear to her. Later on, Nakamura feels herself reverting to the normalcy she despises. Before she does, Kasuga appears on his bike, fully defining her world in both detail and a deep crimson color.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characters">Characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Flowers_of_Evil_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl><dt>Takao Kasuga<span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">春日 高男</span><span style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kasuga Takao</i>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: Shin'ichirō Ueda</span></dd>
<dd>Played by: Kentaro Ito</dd>
<dd>An introverted middle school student with a strong interest in literature. He feels alienation as well as suffocation from the world around him.  His view of other people is dim-he considers them to be ignorant as well as incapable of understanding the abstract themes present in the books he reads, namely Les Fleurs Du Mal by Charles Baudelaire. He idolizes his classmate Saeki from afar as the pinnacle of beauty and virtue, calling her his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Muse" title="Muse">muse</a> and an angel. Although he considers his worship of Saeki to be purely platonic, he cannot help experiencing feelings of sexual attraction to her. Kasuga is deeply disturbed by these feelings because he thinks they devalue his worship of Saeki and are a sin against her purity.</dd>
<dd>While initially viewing Nakamura with fear and apprehension, Kasuga gradually develops a bond with her. The feelings he develops for Nakamura are not romantic in the traditional sense, but rather are a combination of deep fascination, sympathy and servitude. He rejects Saeki to pursue a vision of the future with Nakamura in which the two of them try to make it to "the other side".</dd>
<dt>Sawa Nakamura<span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">仲村 佐和</span><span style="display:none">,</span> <i>Nakamura Sawa</i>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Mariya_Ise" title="Mariya Ise">Mariya Ise</a></span></dd>
<dd>Played by: <a href="/wiki/Tina_Tamashiro" title="Tina Tamashiro">Tina Tamashiro</a></dd>
<dd>A classmate of Kasuga who shows utter disinterest in the world around her as well as contempt for anyone she comes in contact with. Her existence is ignored by her classmates, who view her as disturbed. She maintains a flat affect for most of her interactions, which she keeps very brief. Nakamura views the world as a "sea of shit-bugs" only interested in sex.  She is deeply tortured by the inescapability of her own sexual nature and also disgusted by the world's facade of respectability.</dd>
<dd>Nakamura views Kasuga as a person with a nature similar to hers although his is still buried beneath the layers of respectability engendered by society. She decides to make a contract with him in which she plans to gradually remove the "layers of skin" he is hiding behind. Nakamura's feelings toward Kasuga start out somewhat impersonal, with her treating him like a test subject or play-thing.  However as time goes on she develops a deeply personal investment in his progress.</dd>
<dt>Nanako Saeki<span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">佐伯 奈々子</span><span style="display:none">,</span> <i>Saeki Nanako</i>)</span></dt>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: <a href="/wiki/Y%C5%8Dko_Hikasa" title="Yōko Hikasa">Yōko Hikasa</a></span></dd>
<dd>Played by: Shiori Akita</dd>
<dd>Another girl from Kasuga's class, on whom he has a crush, Saeki is deemed to be the top student of their class, and is considered to be a popular and beautiful girl. After he defends Nakamura from the rest of their class and supports him, Saeki begins to take an interest in Kasuga, especially his passion for <i>Les Fleurs du mal</i>, though she struggles to understand the content. Viewing herself as stifled by her parents, she particularly connects with how he is able to show his true self without having to hide his personality, desires or interests. The two start dating and Saeki reveals how she longs for a life in which she doesn't have to fake herself with being well-mannered, composed and focused on study. Indeed, her relationship with Kasuga reflects his with Nakamura, her self hatred and idolization of him drives her to commit acts of arson and rape. She later lives in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Utsunomiya,_Tochigi" title="Utsunomiya, Tochigi">Utsunomiya</a>, and has gotten a new boyfriend who, according to Tokiwa, "looks a lot" like Kasuga.</dd>
<dt>Aya Tokiwa<span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">常磐 文</span><span style="display:none">,</span> <i>Tokiwa Aya</i>)</span></dt>
<dd>Played by: <a href="/wiki/Marie_Iitoyo" title="Marie Iitoyo">Marie Iitoyo</a></dd>
<dd>A character first introduced in the second part of the story, she is portrayed as a tall, red-haired beauty of Kasuga's year, revered by his friends, but known to date an older guy from another school. Kasuga notices her holding a copy of <i>Les Fleurs du mal</i> in a bookstore and discovers her passion for books. She helps him regain his interest in literature once more, and becomes close to him, to the point of inciting her boyfriend Koji's suspicion towards Kasuga. Kasuga supports her in writing her novel.</dd>
<dt>Ai Kinoshita<span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">木下 亜衣</span><span style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kinoshita Ai</i>)</span></dt>
<dd><i>Portrayed by:</i> Tsugumi Nakamura</dd>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: Ayako Uemura</span></dd>
<dd>Saeki's best friend. Outspoken and hotheaded, she cares deeply for Saeki and begins to resent Kasuga for the pain he's caused her. She later gets the truth from Saeki when she realises her friend has been spying on Kasuga and Nakamura. She feels the urgency to tell the police the deeds of the couple, only to be scolded by Saeki, who believes she'll be able to pull Kasuga out of his mess. As events develops and she realises Saeki's corruption as well, she is unable to hold out and becomes responsible for exposing Kasuga and Nakamura, but in the process also affects her best friend.</dd>
<dt>Yamada<span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">山田</span><span style="display:none">,</span> <i>Yamada</i>)</span></dt>
<dd>Portrayed by: Katsutoshi Matsuzaki</dd>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: Katsutoshi Matsuzaki</span></dd>
<dd>A friend of Kasuga. He's a chubby guy, a blabbermouth mocker and doesn't understand Kasuga's fascination with <i>Les Fleurs du mal</i>.</dd>
<dt>Ken Kojima<span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">小島 建</span><span style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kojima Ken</i>)</span></dt>
<dd>Portrayed by: Ryōsuke Tani</dd>
<dd><span style="font-weight: normal">Voiced by: Shin'ya Hamazoe</span></dd>
<dd>Another friend of Kasuga's.</dd></dl>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Flowers_of_Evil_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manga">Manga</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Flowers_of_Evil_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Manga">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Redon_les_fleurs_du_mal_flower.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="400" data-file-width="278" decoding="async" height="245" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Redon_les_fleurs_du_mal_flower.jpg/170px-Redon_les_fleurs_du_mal_flower.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Redon_les_fleurs_du_mal_flower.jpg/255px-Redon_les_fleurs_du_mal_flower.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/89/Redon_les_fleurs_du_mal_flower.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Redon_les_fleurs_du_mal_flower.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The illustration of a flower by <a href="/wiki/Odilon_Redon" title="Odilon Redon">Odilon Redon</a> in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Les_Fleurs_du_Mal" title="Les Fleurs du Mal">Les Fleurs du Mal</a></i> which Oshimi modified for the manga</div></div></div>
<p>Oshimi first read <a href="/wiki/Charles_Baudelaire" title="Charles Baudelaire">Charles Baudelaire</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Les_Fleurs_du_Mal" title="Les Fleurs du Mal">Les Fleurs du Mal</a></i> in middle school and found the book to feel "suspicious, indecent, yet nastily noble". He based the eye-flower in the series on an illustration for the book by <a href="/wiki/Odilon_Redon" title="Odilon Redon">Odilon Redon</a>, his favorite artist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Oshimi also makes references to other artists such as with the cover of the tenth chapter parodying <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Goya" title="Francisco Goya">Francisco Goya</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Clothed_Maja" title="The Clothed Maja">The Clothed Maja</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>One influence for the manga that Oshimi cited was <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tetsu_Adachi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tetsu Adachi (page does not exist)">Tetsu Adachi's</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> [<a class="extiw" href="https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%AE%89%E9%81%94%E5%93%B2" title="ja:安達哲">ja</a>]</span> <i>Song of Cherry Blossoms</i>, where he could relate to the feelings of the protagonist and described the manga as "depicting murky self-consciousness and the dark side of youth with astounding intensity..." Other manga that influenced him were <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Masahiko_Kikuni&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Masahiko Kikuni (page does not exist)">Masahiko Kikuni's</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> [<a class="extiw" href="https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%96%9C%E5%9B%BD%E9%9B%85%E5%BD%A6" title="ja:喜国雅彦">ja</a>]</span> <i><a href="/wiki/Moonlight_Whispers" title="Moonlight Whispers">Moonlight Whispers</a></i> and Haruko Kashiwagi's <i>Dog</i>, which he felt had in common that they were coming-of-age stories about "discovering 'a value system of one's own<span style="padding-right:.15em;">'</span>" which he hoped his own manga could do.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> After the publication of the second volume of the manga, Oshimi learned of the French film <i><a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Deliver_Us_from_Evil" title="Don't Deliver Us from Evil">Don't Deliver Us from Evil</a></i> and in retrospect he felt he could relate to the themes of the film where "the girls worship Baudelaire and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lautr%C3%A9mont" title="Lautrémont">Lautrémont</a>, create a kingdom of evil all for themselves, and fall into ruin..."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p><p>Oshimi drew on many aspects of his life for the manga such as the settings and characters. The settings are based on real locations from Oshimi's hometown including: the school library,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Oshimi_Flowers_v1p108_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Oshimi_Flowers_v1p108-14">[14]</a></sup> the riverbank,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and the park where Kasuga is splashed with water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Many of the characters also have real-life models: Nakamura is based on a person who used to call Oshimi things like "shitbug" and other phrases that Nakamura uses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Oshimi_Flowers_v1p108_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-Oshimi_Flowers_v1p108-14">[14]</a></sup> Kasuga's friend Hiruta was based on Oshimi's middle school friend who betrayed him to the class bully.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Oshimi_Flowers_v1p172_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Oshimi_Flowers_v1p172-17">[17]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Anime">Anime</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Flowers_of_Evil_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Anime">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Aku_no_hana_rotoscope_process.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="445" data-file-width="224" decoding="async" height="437" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/3/32/Aku_no_hana_rotoscope_process.png/220px-Aku_no_hana_rotoscope_process.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/3/32/Aku_no_hana_rotoscope_process.png 1.5x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Aku_no_hana_rotoscope_process.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>An example of the <a href="/wiki/Rotoscoping" title="Rotoscoping">rotoscoping</a> process used for the anime with sequentially: a video still, tracing, and the colored result</div></div></div>
<p>Initially, the director <a href="/wiki/Hiroshi_Nagahama" title="Hiroshi Nagahama">Hiroshi Nagahama</a> declined the offer to direct the anime because he felt it would be best presented as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Live-action" title="Live-action">live-action</a>, but reconsidered when he figured out how to adapt it and pitched the idea of using <a href="/wiki/Rotoscoping" title="Rotoscoping">rotoscoping</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-natalie_interview_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-natalie_interview-18">[18]</a></sup> He felt it was the most appropriate choice because to him, the manga felt very realistic and close to daily life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nagahama_interview_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nagahama_interview-19">[19]</a></sup> The live-action filming took around three months to complete on location in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kiryu,_Gunma" title="Kiryu, Gunma">Kiryu, Gunma</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oktakumode_interview_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-oktakumode_interview-9">[9]</a></sup> Along with the editing after, it took about twice the amount of time to produce than a regular anime. The end cards at the end of each episode were animated by Nagahama himself in traditional anime style to differentiate them and he made Oshimi's hair wave around to emulate <a href="/wiki/Flash_animation" title="Flash animation">flash animation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nagahama_interview_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-Nagahama_interview-19">[19]</a></sup>
</p><p>Before the premiere of the anime, the producer opted to not release any character artwork.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> When the anime aired, the use of rotoscoping resulted in criticism from fans of the manga.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Nagahama knew that the rotoscoping would be controversial, but he felt it was worth it because it would make the anime leave an impact, even if it was viewed as "creepy". When asked at a Q&amp;A panel at <a href="/wiki/Animazement" title="Animazement">Animazement</a> 2013 why he didn't make the adaptation a traditional live-action series, he responded that in live-action, the focus is on the actors and not the characters. When further asked about what kind of atmosphere he was trying to create with the pacing and soundtrack of the anime, Nagahama replied that he wanted to make an anime that feels fresh.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nagahama_interview_19-2"><a href="#cite_note-Nagahama_interview-19">[19]</a></sup> In an interview with <a href="/wiki/Natalie_(website)" title="Natalie (website)">Natalie</a> including both Nagahama and Oshimi, Oshimi said that he was aware of the rotoscoping idea and approved of the direction Nagahama took the adaptation, especially of his intent to "leave the viewer with a scar" which he felt aligned with his goal of figuratively "murdering" the readers. He also felt that Nagahama had deep insight into the story and that the anime along with the manga formed two versions of the story from his original idea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-natalie_interview_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-natalie_interview-18">[18]</a></sup> Oshimi was also involved in providing feedback on the adaptation as it went along and when shown anime-style drawings, preferred the different look of rotoscoping.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oktakumode_interview_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-oktakumode_interview-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Themes">Themes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Flowers_of_Evil_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Themes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>One of the issues that Oshimi wanted to tackle with the manga was the concept of "perversion", which he felt was misunderstood and actually a hidden quality of everyone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Oshimi_Flowers_v1p172_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-Oshimi_Flowers_v1p172-17">[17]</a></sup> Another question he wanted to answer was where the process of <a href="/wiki/Adolescence" title="Adolescence">adolescence</a> ends; the beginning clearly demarcated by the physical characteristics of puberty, but the ending unclear, which he felt has to be discovered by an individual by themselves.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Anime_2">Anime</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Flowers_of_Evil_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Anime">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The anime adaptation is directed by <a href="/wiki/Hiroshi_Nagahama" title="Hiroshi Nagahama">Hiroshi Nagahama</a> and produced by Zexcs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sentai-ANN_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-sentai-ANN-7">[7]</a></sup> It was the first anime to use rotoscoping exclusively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> It aired in Japan between April 5 and June 29, 2013 and has been licensed by <a href="/wiki/Sentai_Filmworks" title="Sentai Filmworks">Sentai Filmworks</a> in North America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sentai-ANN_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-sentai-ANN-7">[7]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Crunchyroll" title="Crunchyroll">Crunchyroll</a> has the streaming rights in Australia, North America, UK and other territories.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> The opening theme is "Aku no Hana"<span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">惡の華</span>)</span> by Uchūjin with guest vocalists <a href="/wiki/Shinsei_Kamattechan" title="Shinsei Kamattechan">Noko</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mariko_Got%C5%8D" title="Mariko Gotō">Mariko Gotō</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Shiho_Nanba&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Shiho Nanba (page does not exist)">Shiho Nanba</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> [<a class="extiw" href="https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%8D%97%E6%B3%A2%E5%BF%97%E5%B8%86" title="ja:南波志帆">ja</a>]</span>. The ending theme is "〜花〜 A Last Flower" by <a href="/wiki/Asa-Chang_%26_Junray" title="Asa-Chang &amp; Junray">Asa-Chang &amp; Junray</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> At <a href="/wiki/Sakura-Con" title="Sakura-Con">Sakura-Con</a> 2014, Nagahama explained the <a href="/wiki/Flashforward" title="Flashforward">flashforward</a> at the end of the last episode as a declaration of his intention for a second season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Flowers_of_Evil_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manga_3">Manga</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Flowers_of_Evil_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Manga">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Japan, the manga charted several times on the <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Oricon</a> lists: Volume 5 was the 30th best-selling manga for the week of January 2, 2012,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> rising to 17th the next week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> Volume 7 was the 44th best-selling manga for the week of December 10, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> Volume 8 was the 22nd best-selling manga for the week of June 3, 2013,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> rising to 17th the next week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> Volume 9 was the 22nd best-selling manga for the week of August 5, 2013,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> falling to 29th the next week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> Volume 10 was the 17th best-selling manga for the week of January 6, 2014,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> falling to 25th the next week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> Volume 11 was the 5th best-selling manga for the week of June 9, 2014,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> falling to 42nd the next week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> The first 9 volumes of the manga sold over 2<span class="nowrap"> </span>million copies in Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> The manga was also 18th on the Top 20 Manga for Male Readers in the guidebook <i>Kono Manga ga Sugoi!</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Vertical_(company)" title="Vertical (company)">Vertical</a>'s localization of the manga was able to reach <a href="/wiki/List_of_The_New_York_Times_Manga_Best_Sellers" title="List of The New York Times Manga Best Sellers"><i>The New York Times</i> Manga Best Sellers</a> chart on two different occasions: Volume 1 was the 3rd best-selling manga for the week of May 13, 2012<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_May_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT_May-51">[51]</a></sup> and Volume 3 was the 8th best-selling manga for the week of October 28, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_OctNov_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT_OctNov-52">[52]</a></sup>
</p><p>The manga was nominated for the fifth <a href="/wiki/Manga_Taish%C5%8D" title="Manga Taishō">Manga Taishō</a> (Cartoon Grand Prize)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-taisho_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-taisho-6">[6]</a></sup> but lost to <a href="/wiki/Hiromu_Arakawa" title="Hiromu Arakawa">Hiromu Arakawa</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Silver_Spoon_(manga)" title="Silver Spoon (manga)">Silver Spoon</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Anime_3">Anime</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Flowers_of_Evil_(manga)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Anime">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Anime_News_Network" title="Anime News Network">Anime News Network</a> (ANN) editor Carl Kimlinger gave the series high praise when it was streaming, giving credit to both director <a href="/wiki/Hiroshi_Nagahama" title="Hiroshi Nagahama">Hiroshi Nagahama</a> and composer <a href="/wiki/Hideyuki_Fukasawa" title="Hideyuki Fukasawa">Hideyuki Fukasawa</a> for doing Oshimi's work justice with direction that's parts psychological thriller and darkly romantic with the inclusion of rotoscoping and a score that's atonally sinister, saying that, "[I]t is a series that can be attacked from any direction and not crack. It is, in its delightfully unpleasant way, a masterpiece."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> Fellow ANN editor Nick Creamer also liked the series, praising the story for conveying themes of human identity and codependence with amoral people and the direction for its slow and methodical pace, concluding that "<i>The Flowers of Evil</i> is one of the best shows of recent years, an absurdly confident mood piece with a uniquely effective aesthetic and a true understanding of and sympathy for the emotional trials of youth. It should be watched by anyone interested in the potential of animation for conveying human truth."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> Richard Eisenbeis of <a href="/wiki/Kotaku" title="Kotaku">Kotaku</a>, on the other hand, heavily panned the series for having an unlikable main cast, lack of any stakes or tension, and horrendous technical aspects in terms of animation and no musical score in places, concluding that, "It's just an utter train wreck of a series. I recommend it to no one."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> Tim Jones, writing for THEM Anime Reviews, was also put off by the rotoscoping style, saying that from a distance it looks "comical and oft-putting (sic)." But Jones gave praise to the cinematography, the main trio's development and Nagahama's direction on a typical high school setting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup>
</p>
