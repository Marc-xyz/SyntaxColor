<p><b>Very Rev. Everard Mercurian, S.J.</b> (1514 – 1 August 1580) was the fourth <a href="/wiki/Superior_General_of_the_Society_of_Jesus" title="Superior General of the Society of Jesus">Superior General</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Society_of_Jesus" title="Society of Jesus">Society of Jesus</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Everard_Mercurian&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born '<b>Lardinois'</b> into a humble family in <a href="/wiki/Marcourt" title="Marcourt">Marcourt</a>, near <a href="/wiki/La_Roche-en-Ardenne" title="La Roche-en-Ardenne">La Roche-en-Ardenne</a> in what is now the province of <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg_(Belgium)" title="Luxembourg (Belgium)">Luxembourg</a> in 1514, in the south-east corner of what is now <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>. This is the origin of his name, which he signed <b>Everard de Marcour</b>. After study in the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Paris" title="University of Paris">University of Paris</a>, he was ordained and became a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parish_priest" title="Parish priest">parish priest</a>. He became acquainted with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jesuit" title="Jesuit">Jesuits</a> at the University and on 8 September 1548 entered the Jesuits in the <a href="/wiki/Low_Countries" title="Low Countries">Low Countries</a>. The Society was expanding rapidly and he became Visitor of the German Province and later Provincial of the Lower German Province and lastly the German Assistant in 1565.
</p>
<h2><span id="General_Congregation_III_.281573.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="General_Congregation_III_(1573)">General Congregation III (1573)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Everard_Mercurian&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: General Congregation III (1573)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Francis_Borgia" title="Francis Borgia">Francis Borgia</a>, then Superior General of the Society, died just before the Third General Congregation was about to convene. <a href="/wiki/Pope_Gregory_XIII" title="Pope Gregory XIII">Pope Gregory XIII</a>, who was a good friend of the Society, expressed his desire that the delegates elect a General who was not a Spaniard. Thus Mercurian became the first non-Spanish general of the Society, even though Polanco, a Spaniard, seemed to be the natural choice.  This, however, caused dissatisfaction and opposition among a number of the Spanish and Portuguese members, which came to a crisis during the generalate of Father Mercurian's successor, Father <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Claudius_Acquaviva" title="Claudius Acquaviva">Claudius Acquaviva</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>National sensitivities of the time are thought to have motivated the Pope. The first three Generals, <a href="/wiki/Ignatius_of_Loyola" title="Ignatius of Loyola">Ignatius</a>, <a href="/wiki/Diego_Laynez" title="Diego Laynez">Lainez</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Francis_Borgia" title="Francis Borgia">Borgia</a> had been Spaniards and there was some concern that "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Christians" title="New Christians">New Christians</a>" — converted <a href="/wiki/Jews" title="Jews">Jews</a> or <a href="/wiki/Muslims" title="Muslims">Muslims</a>, — might enter into the mainstream of the Society and Spanish Christians were more likely to have such backgrounds. Polanco, a close associate of <a href="/wiki/Ignatius_of_Loyola" title="Ignatius of Loyola">St. Ignatius</a>, was a Spaniard and was suspected of having a racial background that would not be acceptable. The Fathers of the Congregation voted 27 out of 47 on April 23, 1573 for the election of Everard Mercurian, a Belgian. It was a choice which pleased the Pope because Mercurian was a good friend, a non-Spaniard, and besides, there was no chance of his having tainted blood.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Achievements_as_General">Achievements as General</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Everard_Mercurian&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Achievements as General">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Constitutions">Constitutions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Everard_Mercurian&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Constitutions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Father Mercurian brought the rules of the Society to their final form during his seven years and three months as General, compiling the "Summary of the Constitutions" from the manuscripts of St. Ignatius, and drawing up the "Common Rules" of the Society, and the particular rules of each office.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="English_College">English College</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Everard_Mercurian&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: English College">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>From his friend Gregory, the Society received charge of the English College and Gregory's beneficence to the Roman College was much appreciated.
</p>
<h3><span id="Polanco.27s_Census"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Polanco's_Census">Polanco's Census</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Everard_Mercurian&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Polanco's Census">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During this time, too, Polanco traveled the length and breadth of Europe making a census of the Society's activities and of its men. When it was finally finished it filled six large volumes and gave a detailed account of the progress the Society had made from 1537 until the death of Ignatius.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Foreign_Missions">Foreign Missions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Everard_Mercurian&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Foreign Missions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He was greatly interested in the foreign missions and established the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maronite" title="Maronite">Maronite</a> mission of <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanon</a>. He appointed <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Valignano" title="Alessandro Valignano">Alessandro Valignano</a> General Visitor of all the missions in Asia (India, Japan, Moluccas). The Visitor had extensive power and changed completely the missionary outlook, insisting on the learning of the oriental languages, understanding and adopting local customs as well as admitting to the seminary and priesthood local youth. This new missionary approach allowed the emergence of such outstanding missionaries and orientalists as <a href="/wiki/Matteo_Ricci" title="Matteo Ricci">Matteo Ricci</a> (in China) <a href="/wiki/Roberto_de_Nobili" title="Roberto de Nobili">Roberto de Nobili</a> (in India) and others.<br/>
He was very reluctant to get the Jesuits involved in the attempt to influence politics in England under Queen <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Elizabeth_I" title="Elizabeth I">Elizabeth I</a>, but he was finally persuaded to send <a href="/wiki/Edmund_Campion" title="Edmund Campion">Edmund Campion</a> and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Persons" title="Robert Persons">Robert Persons</a> to support the Catholics in England, with strict instructions to avoid politics or treason. However, <a href="/wiki/Pope_Gregory_XIII" title="Pope Gregory XIII">Pope Gregory XIII</a> met with Campion and Persons before their departure from Rome and completely subverted Mercurian's instructions. He had also, unbeknownst to them, sent support before them to the Irish rebel <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_Fitzmaurice_Fitzgerald" title="James Fitzmaurice Fitzgerald">James Fitzmaurice</a>, thereby seriously compromising their non-political stance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Everard_Mercurian&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Father Everard Mercurian passed thirty-two years in the Society, and died at the age of sixty-six. At that time the Society numbered 5000 members in eighteen provinces. He died, a <a href="/wiki/Martyr_of_charity" title="Martyr of charity">martyr of charity</a>, during the <a href="/wiki/Influenza" title="Influenza">influenza</a> epidemic of 1580 while visiting the sick in their homes. He was buried in the Church of St. Andrea al Quirinale, at that time the church of the Novitiate. Later on, his remains were transferred to the Ossuary in the crypt of the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_the_Ges%C3%B9" title="Church of the Gesù">church of the Gesù</a>.
</p>
