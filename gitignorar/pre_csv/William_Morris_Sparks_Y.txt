<p><b>William Morris Sparks</b> (April 28, 1872 – January 7, 1950) was a <a href="/wiki/United_States_federal_judge" title="United States federal judge">United States Circuit Judge</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Court_of_Appeals_for_the_Seventh_Circuit" title="United States Court of Appeals for the Seventh Circuit">United States Court of Appeals for the Seventh Circuit</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education_and_career">Education and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=William_Morris_Sparks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Education and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Charlottesville,_Indiana" title="Charlottesville, Indiana">Charlottesville</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">Indiana</a>, Sparks received an <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">Artium Baccalaureus</a> degree from <a href="/wiki/DePauw_University" title="DePauw University">DePauw University</a> in 1896, attended <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indiana_Law_School" title="Indiana Law School">Indiana Law School</a> (now <a href="/wiki/Indiana_University_Robert_H._McKinney_School_of_Law" title="Indiana University Robert H. McKinney School of Law">Indiana University Robert H. McKinney School of Law</a>), and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Read_law" title="Read law">read law</a> to enter the Bar in 1896. He was a deputy prosecuting attorney of <a href="/wiki/Rush_County,_Indiana" title="Rush County, Indiana">Rush County</a>, Indiana from 1896 to 1898, then entered private practice in <a href="/wiki/Rushville,_Indiana" title="Rushville, Indiana">Rushville</a>, Indiana from 1897 to 1901. He was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Indiana_House_of_Representatives" title="Indiana House of Representatives">Indiana House of Representatives</a> from 1901 to 1903. He was a Judge of the 16th Judicial Circuit Court of Indiana from 1904 to 1910, returning to private practice in Rushville from 1910 to 1914, and again assuming his Circuit Court judgeship from 1914 to 1929.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Federal_judicial_service">Federal judicial service</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=William_Morris_Sparks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Federal judicial service">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On October 25, 1929, Sparks was nominated by President <a href="/wiki/Herbert_Hoover" title="Herbert Hoover">Herbert Hoover</a> to a seat on the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Court_of_Appeals_for_the_Seventh_Circuit" title="United States Court of Appeals for the Seventh Circuit">United States Court of Appeals for the Seventh Circuit</a> vacated by Judge <a href="/wiki/Albert_Barnes_Anderson" title="Albert Barnes Anderson">Albert Barnes Anderson</a>. Sparks was confirmed by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">United States Senate</a> on October 31, 1929, and received his commission the same day. He served as Chief Judge and as a member of the <a href="/wiki/Judicial_Conference_of_the_United_States" title="Judicial Conference of the United States">Judicial Conference of the United States</a> in 1948, assuming <a href="/wiki/Senior_status" title="Senior status">senior status</a> on November 13, 1948. Sparks served in that capacity until his death on January 7, 1950, in Rushville.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-auto-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
