	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Kidero (rural locality)</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Rural locality in Dagestan, Russia</div>
<p><b>Kidero</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Кидеро</span>, <a href="/wiki/Chechen_language" title="Chechen language">Chechen</a>: <span lang="ce-Cyrl">Кидеро</span>) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">rural locality</a> (a <a href="/wiki/Village#Russia" title="Village">selo</a>) in <a href="/wiki/Tsuntinsky_District" title="Tsuntinsky District">Tsuntinsky District</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Dagestan" title="Dagestan">Republic of Dagestan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>. Population: <span class="nowrap">752 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2010)" title="Russian Census (2010)">2010 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2010Census_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-2010Census-1">[1]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">612 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2002)" title="Russian Census (2002)">2002 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PopCensus_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-PopCensus-2">[2]</a></sup> 
</p><p>Between 1991 and 2017 Kidero served as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> of Tsuntinsky District, however, it lacks the necessary infrastructure. In 2017, the district center was moved into the selo of <a href="/wiki/Tsunta" title="Tsunta">Tsunta</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
