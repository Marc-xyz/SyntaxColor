<div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Title in the Baronetage of the UK</div>
<p>The <b>Schuster Baronetcy</b>, of Collingham Road in the Royal Borough of Kensington, was a title in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baronetage_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Baronetage of the United Kingdom">Baronetage of the United Kingdom</a>. It was created on 24 July 1906 for the banker <a href="/wiki/Felix_Schuster" title="Felix Schuster">Felix Schuster</a>. He was a senior partner in the family firm of Schuster, Son &amp; Co, and was also a Finance Member of the Council of India from 1906 to 1916. The third Baronet was a <a href="/wiki/Colonel" title="Colonel">Colonel</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rifle_Brigade_(Prince_Consort%27s_Own)" title="Rifle Brigade (Prince Consort's Own)">Rifle Brigade</a>. The baronetcy became extinct on his death in 1996.
</p><p>The physicist <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Schuster" title="Arthur Schuster">Sir Arthur Schuster</a> was the elder brother of the first Baronet.
</p>
