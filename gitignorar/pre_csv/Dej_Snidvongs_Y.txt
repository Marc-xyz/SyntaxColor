<p><b><i>Mom Luang</i> Dej Snidvongs</b> (<a href="/wiki/Thai_language" title="Thai language">Thai</a>: <span lang="th">เดช สนิทวงศ์</span>; born 18 February 1898 - 	8 September 1975) is a <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thai</a> honorary academic. He was the <a href="/wiki/Privy_Council_of_Thailand" title="Privy Council of Thailand">President of the Privy Council of Thailand</a> to King <a href="/wiki/Bhumibol_Adulyadej" title="Bhumibol Adulyadej">Bhumibol Adulyadej</a> and Governor of the <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_Thailand" title="Bank of Thailand">Bank of Thailand</a> in 1949 to 1952.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He was given the title of <a href="/wiki/Thai_royal_and_noble_titles#Luang" title="Thai royal and noble titles">Luang</a> Dejsahakorn.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family_and_education">Family and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dej_Snidvongs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Family and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mom Luang Dej Sanitwong is the third son of  Chaopraya Wongsanaprapat (Mom Rajawongse Sathand Snidvongs) and Thanpuying Wongsanuprapat (Tat Singhaseni). He was born at the palace of Prince <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sai_Sanidvongs&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sai Sanidvongs (page does not exist)">Sai Sanidvongs</a> at <a href="/wiki/Pom_Prap_Sattru_Phai_District" title="Pom Prap Sattru Phai District">Pom Prap Sattru Phai District</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phra_Nakhon" title="Phra Nakhon">Phra Nakhon</a> Province on 18 February 1898.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He has 14 half-brother and sister, one of whom was <a href="/wiki/Bua_Kitiyakara" title="Bua Kitiyakara">Bua Snidvongs</a>, the sister of different mothers which is the mother of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queen_Sirikit" title="Queen Sirikit">Queen Sirikit</a>, Therefore, Dej Snidvongswas was an uncle of Queen Sirikit.
</p><p>He began to study at Praya Singhaseni's wife house, Khunying Hong who was his aunt. Later in 1906, he attended the <a href="/wiki/Debsirin_School" title="Debsirin School">Debsirin School</a>. Later, the moved to King's College under the Ministry of Justice at Bang Khwang Sub-district, Nonthaburi Province. In 1914 he receive government scholarships to study in Germany. Subsequently, <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> occurred on 4 August 1914 when the Thai government declared war on Germany on 22 July 1917 he was arrested by German government with 4 Thai students, namely Tua Lapanukrom, Term Bunnag, Prachuap Bunnag and Mom Luang Udom Snidvongs. Later in 1918 has a revolution in Germany Thai captive students were released. Therefore, joined the <a href="/wiki/Siamese_Expeditionary_Forces" title="Siamese Expeditionary Forces">Siamese Expeditionary Forces</a> together with the Allies invading Germany. After the end of the war, he moved to study in Bern, Switzerland in 1919 and was able to take the entrance examination at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Bern" title="University of Bern">University of Bern</a> in the year 1920 and graduated with a Doktorrerum Politicarum (Cum Laude) degree in 1925. After that, he traveled to study and research the history of economics and trade between Thailand and the United Kingdom 6 months before returning to Thailand in 1926.
</p><p>Dej Snidvongs married with Prayong Snidvongs na Ayudhya in 1928. There are 4 children, Warunyapha Snidvongs na Ayudhya, Phisit Snidvongs na Ayudhya, Manu-seri Snidvongs na Ayudhya and Kasem Snidvongs na Ayudhya. He died with lung cancer at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chulalongkorn_Hospital" title="Chulalongkorn Hospital">Chulalongkorn Hospital</a> on 8 September 1975 at 22.33 hrs at the age of 77 years old.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Careers">Careers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dej_Snidvongs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Careers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dej Snidvongs started the first government service in 1926 as the commissioner of the Department of Commerce's, <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Commerce_(Thailand)" title="Ministry of Commerce (Thailand)">Minister of Economic Affairs</a>. In 1935 he was the Permanent Secretary of the Ministry of Agriculture and then in 1942 as Permanent Secretary of the Ministry of Economy and acting in the position of Permanent Secretary of the Ministry of Commerce in the same year. In 1949 he was the Governor of the <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_Thailand" title="Bank of Thailand">Bank of Thailand</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>For that political position Dej Snidvongs joined with the first constitutional <a href="/wiki/Cabinet_of_Thailand" title="Cabinet of Thailand">Cabinet of Siam</a> as <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Committee_of_Siam" title="People's Committee of Siam">People's Committee of Siam</a> of <a href="/wiki/Phraya_Manopakorn_Nititada" title="Phraya Manopakorn Nititada">Phraya Manopakorn Nititada</a> government in 1932. Later, in the period of Field Marshal <a href="/wiki/Plaek_Phibunsongkhram" title="Plaek Phibunsongkhram">Plaek Phibunsongkhram</a> as Prime Minister in 1938, he held the position of Deputy Minister of Agriculture. Later in 1974 as Deputy Minister of Economic Affairs in <a href="/wiki/Khuang_Aphaiwong" title="Khuang Aphaiwong">Khuang Aphaiwong</a> government.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 1944, he also joined the cabinet by being the Minister of Economic Affairs and joined the government of <a href="/wiki/Khuang_Aphaiwong" title="Khuang Aphaiwong">Khuang Aphaiwong</a>. In 1947, he served as Minister of Economic Affairs for a period and in 1948 he was appointed as Minister to the <a href="/wiki/Office_of_the_Prime_Minister_(Thailand)" title="Office of the Prime Minister (Thailand)">Office of the Prime Minister</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>Important position of Dej Snidvongs is to receive the King <a href="/wiki/Bhumibol_Adulyadej" title="Bhumibol Adulyadej">Bhumibol Adulyadej</a> appointed as a <a href="/wiki/Privy_Council_of_Thailand" title="Privy Council of Thailand">Privy Councilor</a> on 24 April 1947 and was President of the Privy Council on 18 February 1975 until his death.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Royal_decorations">Royal decorations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dej_Snidvongs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Royal decorations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
