<p><b>Kaina Station</b><span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">荷稲駅</span><span style="display:none">,</span> <i>Kaina-eki</i>)</span> is a railway station on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tosa_Kuroshio_Railway_Nakamura_Line" title="Tosa Kuroshio Railway Nakamura Line">Tosa Kuroshio Railway Nakamura Line</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kuroshio,_K%C5%8Dchi" title="Kuroshio, Kōchi">Kuroshio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hata_District,_K%C5%8Dchi" title="Hata District, Kōchi">Hata District</a>, <a href="/wiki/K%C5%8Dchi_Prefecture" title="Kōchi Prefecture">Kōchi Prefecture</a>, Japan. It is operated by the <a href="/wiki/Tosa_Kuroshio_Railway" title="Tosa Kuroshio Railway">Tosa Kuroshio Railway</a> and has the station number "TK28".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kuroshiomap_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-kuroshiomap-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hacchi_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-hacchi-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lines_and_Trains">Lines and Trains</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kaina_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Lines and Trains">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station is served by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tosa_Kuroshio_Railway_Nakamura_Line" title="Tosa Kuroshio Railway Nakamura Line">Tosa Kuroshio Railway Nakamura Line</a>, and is located 13.8 km from the starting point of the line at <a href="/wiki/Kubokawa_Station" title="Kubokawa Station">Kubokawa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-terada2013_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-terada2013-3">[3]</a></sup> Only local trains stop at the station.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tosasagatimetable_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-tosasagatimetable-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Layout">Layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kaina_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station consists of a side platform serving a single track on a side hill cutting. There is no station building and the station is unstaffed. A shelter is provided on the platform for waiting passengers. A bike shed has been set up near the station entrance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sanyukai_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-sanyukai-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hacchi_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-hacchi-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kaina_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Japanese_National_Railways" title="Japanese National Railways">Japanese National Railways</a> (JNR) opened the station on 18 December 1963 as an intermediate station when it laid down theNakamura Line from <a href="/wiki/Kubokawa_Station" title="Kubokawa Station">Kubokawa</a> to <a href="/wiki/Tosa-Saga_Station" title="Tosa-Saga Station">Tosa-Saga</a>. After the privatization of JNR, control of the station passed to Tosa Kuroshio Railway on 1 April 1988.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-terada2013_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-terada2013-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-teishajo1_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-teishajo1-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Passenger_statistics">Passenger statistics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kaina_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Passenger statistics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In fiscal 2011, the station was used by an average of 31 passengers daily.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-terada2013_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-terada2013-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
