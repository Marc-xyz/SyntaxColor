<p>The third <b>USS <i>Emerald</i> (PYC-1)</b>, was a <a href="/wiki/Yacht" title="Yacht">yacht</a> built in 1922 as <i>Tamarack IV</i> by the  <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Consolidated_Shipbuilding_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Consolidated Shipbuilding Company (page does not exist)">Consolidated Shipbuilding Company</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Morris_Heights,_New_York" title="Morris Heights, New York">Morris Heights, New York</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> She was acquired by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_Navy" title="US Navy">US Navy</a> on 25 October 1940 and commissioned 27 December 1940, with Lieutenant (junior grade) Robert W. Graham in command.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DANFS_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-DANFS-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="World_War_II_service">World War II service</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=USS_Emerald_(PYc-1)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: World War II service">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After calling at <a href="/wiki/Norfolk,_Virginia" title="Norfolk, Virginia">Norfolk, Virginia</a> <i>Emerald</i> arrived at <a href="/wiki/Jacksonville,_Florida" title="Jacksonville, Florida">Jacksonville, Florida</a>, 13 February 1941 for patrol duty off the <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a> coast, and served as harbor entrance control station in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Johns_River" title="Saint Johns River">Saint Johns River</a> between December 1941 and March 1942. Her last service was as <a href="/wiki/Examination_vessel" title="Examination vessel">examination ship</a> at <a href="/wiki/Key_West" title="Key West">Key West</a>, and on 31 May <i>Emerald</i> arrived at <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a>, where she lay until decommissioned 11 August 1942. She was transferred to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maritime_Commission" title="Maritime Commission">Maritime Commission</a> for disposal 13 November 1945.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DANFS_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-DANFS-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
