<p><i><b>Metamorphosis Alpha</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction" title="Science fiction">science fiction</a> <a href="/wiki/Role-playing_game" title="Role-playing game">role-playing game</a>. It was created by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_M._Ward" title="James M. Ward">James M. Ward</a> and originally produced by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TSR,_Inc." title="TSR, Inc.">TSR</a>, the publisher of <i><a href="/wiki/Dungeons_%26_Dragons" title="Dungeons &amp; Dragons">Dungeons &amp; Dragons</a></i>. It was the first science fiction role-playing game,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> published in July 1976.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Metamorphosis_Alpha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The original edition of the game takes place on a <a href="/wiki/Generation_ship" title="Generation ship">generation spaceship</a>, the starship <i>Warden</i> that has been struck by an unknown cataclysmic event that killed many of the colonists and crew.  Thus, the characters must survive their missions in this ship (which they believe to be a world) where they no longer understand the technology around them and they encounter numerous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mutant_(fictional)" title="Mutant (fictional)">mutated creatures</a>.  In essence, <i>Metamorphosis Alpha</i> is a <a href="/wiki/Dungeon_crawl" title="Dungeon crawl">dungeon crawl</a> in space.
</p><p>Players can opt to create a human, a mutated human, a mutated plant or a mutated creature as their character.  A number of articles in <i><a href="/wiki/Dragon_(magazine)" title="Dragon (magazine)">Dragon</a></i> expanded upon these options to include clones and robot characters as well as adding rules for cybernetics. There are five common player characteristics: radiation resistance, mental resistance, dexterity, strength, and constitution.  Human players added a sixth characteristic, leadership potential, while mutated humans and creatures add a random number of mutations, both physical and mental.  <i>Metamorphosis Alpha's</i> combat rules are very similar to those used in the original edition of <i><a href="/wiki/Dungeons_%26_Dragons" title="Dungeons &amp; Dragons">Dungeons &amp; Dragons</a></i> (<i>D&amp;D</i>).
</p><p><i>Metamorphosis Alpha</i> is the intellectual precursor to <i><a href="/wiki/Gamma_World" title="Gamma World">Gamma World</a></i> (1978), also produced by TSR.
</p>
<h2><span id="Science_fiction_or_science_fantasy.3F"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Science_fiction_or_science_fantasy?">Science fiction or science fantasy?</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Metamorphosis_Alpha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Science fiction or science fantasy?">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Metamorphosis Alpha</i> has an emphasis on super science and an element of <a href="/wiki/Science_fantasy" title="Science fantasy">science fantasy</a> (as confirmed by creator <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_M._Ward" title="James M. Ward">James M. Ward</a> in 2006).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Ward has stated that the game is not "hard sci-fi".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>In 1980, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TSR,_Inc." title="TSR, Inc.">TSR</a> released <i><a href="/wiki/Editions_of_Dungeons_%26_Dragons#Advanced_Dungeons_&amp;_Dragons" title="Editions of Dungeons &amp; Dragons">AD&amp;D</a></i> module S3: <i><a href="/wiki/Expedition_to_the_Barrier_Peaks" title="Expedition to the Barrier Peaks">Expedition to the Barrier Peaks</a></i>. The module was based on the idea that a starship such as the <i>Warden</i> (though not the <i>Warden</i> itself, according to TSR founder <a href="/wiki/Gary_Gygax" title="Gary Gygax">Gary Gygax</a>) becomes marooned in a <i>D&amp;D</i> universe. In the module's foreword, Gygax writes that the module was written to introduce <i>Metamorphosis Alpha</i> to the wider <i>D&amp;D</i> audience and to demonstrate how one might undertake science fiction/fantasy crossover.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Inspiration">Inspiration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Metamorphosis_Alpha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Inspiration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ward, the game's author, stated that the original inspiration for the game was <a href="/wiki/Brian_Aldiss" title="Brian Aldiss">Brian Aldiss</a>'s classic science fiction novel <i><a href="/wiki/Non-Stop_(novel)" title="Non-Stop (novel)">Non-Stop</a></i> (also known as <i>Starship</i>, 1958). In the British RPG magazine <i><a href="/wiki/White_Dwarf_(magazine)" title="White Dwarf (magazine)">White Dwarf</a></i>, issue 1, <a href="/wiki/Ian_Livingstone" title="Ian Livingstone">Ian Livingstone</a> wrote a review of the game and published his own additional rules for playing <i>Metamorphosis Alpha</i> on Aldiss's ship. However, it is often suggested that the game was inspired by <a href="/wiki/Robert_A._Heinlein" title="Robert A. Heinlein">Robert A. Heinlein</a>'s 1941 novel <i><a href="/wiki/Orphans_of_the_Sky" title="Orphans of the Sky">Orphans of the Sky</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metamorphosis_Alpha_Review_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Metamorphosis_Alpha_Review-3">[3]</a></sup> James M. Ward has stated that he was not familiar with the 1973 Canadian TV series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Starlost" title="The Starlost">The Starlost</a></i>, a show with a similar concept, and that <i>Metamorphosis Alpha</i> is not based upon that series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metamorphosisalpha.net_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-metamorphosisalpha.net-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ownership_and_rights">Ownership and rights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Metamorphosis_Alpha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Ownership and rights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The rights to <i>Metamorphosis Alpha</i> are owned by James M. Ward. Ward also owns the rights to all <i>Metamorphosis Alpha</i> material previously published in <i>Dragon</i>. WardCo. (Ward's publishing business) has been granted permission by Steve Jackson Games and by Judges Guild (the estate of Bob Bledsaw Sr.) to host and republish some material. In 2008, Ward requested via public forums that sites hosting unlicensed copies of his original rules and the material previously published in <i>Dragon</i> magazine remove them, as they were a breach of his copyright and directly competed with products that he is selling. All permissions to host the <i>Metamorphosis Alpha</i> rules and the <i>Metamorphosis Alpha</i> articles in <i>Dragon</i> magazine, other than those being sold on behalf of WardCo., at sites other than at the official homepage have been rescinded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
In 2010, Signal Fire Studios acquired the rights to produce a new edition of the game based upon Hasbro's 4th edition <i>D&amp;D</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
In 2011, Ward announced that he had negotiated a deal to produce a graphic novel of <i>Metamorphosis Alpha</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-metamorphosisalpha.net_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-metamorphosisalpha.net-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Editions_and_supplements">Editions and supplements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Metamorphosis_Alpha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Editions and supplements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Metamorphosis Alpha</i> was first published in 1976 by TSR. It was written by James M. Ward and illustrated by Dave Sutherland III.

It was designed by Ward himself.
</p><p>In 2006, <i>Metamorphosis Alpha 4th Edition</i> was released by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mudpuppy_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mudpuppy Games (page does not exist)">Mudpuppy Games</a> (<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r886058088" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><a href="/wiki/International_Standard_Book_Number" title="International Standard Book Number">ISBN</a> <a href="/wiki/Special:BookSources/0-9763601-2-8" title="Special:BookSources/0-9763601-2-8">0-9763601-2-8</a>).  This edition contained original material by Ward and additional new material and photography by Craig J. Brain. The book cover and interior illustrations were painted and drawn by Jim Holloway with most of the cartography by Ryan Wolfe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-amazon-ma4_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-amazon-ma4-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2007, WardCo. released an official errata sheet for <i>Metamorphosis Alpha First Edition</i>, which is available from the official website and released a revised edition of the first-edition rules through OneBookShelf as a <a href="/wiki/PDF" title="PDF">PDF</a>.
</p><p>In 2010, WardCo. released <i>The House On The Hill</i> adventure module (<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r886058088" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><a href="/wiki/International_Standard_Book_Number" title="International Standard Book Number">ISBN</a> <a href="/wiki/Special:BookSources/145360412X" title="Special:BookSources/145360412X">145360412X</a>) for <i>Metamorphosis Alpha</i> (1st edition) as a PDF through OneBookshelf and the printed edition through Amazon. The adventure was written by Craig J. Brain, with bonus content by James M. Ward. The illustrations for the adventure were by Lee Smith and Dave Sutherland III.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, WardCo. released a reprint of the original edition of the game via Lulu with the addition of a new adventure and a number of corrections.
</p><p>In 2014, WardCo and Goodman Games. released a hardbound deluxe edition of the original 1st edition via kickstarter including new adventures and consolidated other content from magazines and new content.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Metamorphosis_Alpha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Robert R. Taylor reviewed <i>Metamorphosis Alpha</i> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Space_Gamer" title="The Space Gamer">The Space Gamer</a></i> No. 10.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SG_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-SG-9">[9]</a></sup> Taylor concluded that "The game is definitely first rate and quite excellent. <i>MA</i> is highly recommended to someone interested in buying their first role-playing game since the rules are rich with guidelines to help the player in constructing his own ship."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SG_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-SG-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Eric_Goldberg_(game_designer)" title="Eric Goldberg (game designer)">Eric Goldberg</a> reviewed <i>Lords of the Middle Sea</i> in <i><a href="/wiki/Ares_(magazine)" title="Ares (magazine)">Ares Magazine</a></i> #1, rating it a 3 out of 9.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ares_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ares-10">[10]</a></sup> Goldberg commented that "<i>Metamorphosis: Alpha</i> is <i>Dungeons and Dragons</i> in space. Regrettably, one can only stretch a great idea so far; this offering is far too contrived to gain acceptance in the minds of most players.."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ares_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ares-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Gary_Gygax" title="Gary Gygax">Gary Gygax</a> comments that "<i>Metamorphosis Alpha</i> is a game that breaks the typical level-progression reward mold but nevertheless offers a rich, if sometimes difficult to gain, array of player rewards — from the knowledge of the environment to beneficial character mutations, learned skills to the acquisition of tech items and other equipment. Furthermore it blends fantasy with weird and super science in a unique manner that is captivating to players with imaginations suited to such a startling mixture. <i>Metamorphosis Alpha</i> — in any edition — stimulates the imagination, encourages keen thinking, and breaks the mold of typical fantasy and science fiction roleplaying games. If that doesn't make it one of the best hobby games ever, I don't know what would."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HG_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-HG-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/David_M._Ewalt" title="David M. Ewalt">David M. Ewalt</a>, in his book <i>Of Dice and Men</i>, commented that <i>Metamorphosis Alpha</i> was "notable as the first role-playing game with a science-fiction setting. The game takes place on the starship <i>Warden</i>, a vast spaceship built by the player characters' ancestors; in the aftermath of some unknown disaster, their progeny survive on the ship but don't understand its technology and must fight mutated creatures to ensure their survival."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ODaM_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-ODaM-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
