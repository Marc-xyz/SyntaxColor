<p>The <b>1977 <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City_Royals" title="Kansas City Royals">Kansas City Royals</a> season</b> was a season in American <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a>. It involved the Royals finishing first in the <a href="/wiki/American_League_West" title="American League West">American League West</a> with a record of 102 wins and 60 losses. They went on to lose the <a href="/wiki/1977_American_League_Championship_Series" title="1977 American League Championship Series">1977 American League Championship Series</a> to the <a href="/wiki/1977_New_York_Yankees_season" title="1977 New York Yankees season">New York Yankees</a>, 3 games to 2.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Offseason">Offseason</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1977_Kansas_City_Royals_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Offseason">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>November 5, 1976: Ruppert Jones was drafted from the Royals by the <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Mariners" title="Seattle Mariners">Seattle Mariners</a> with the first pick in the <a href="/wiki/1976_Major_League_Baseball_expansion_draft" title="1976 Major League Baseball expansion draft">1976 Major League Baseball expansion draft</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li>December 6, 1976: <a href="/wiki/Jamie_Quirk" title="Jamie Quirk">Jamie Quirk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jim_Wohlford" title="Jim Wohlford">Jim Wohlford</a> and a player to be named later were traded by the Kansas City Royals to the <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Brewers" title="Milwaukee Brewers">Milwaukee Brewers</a> for <a href="/wiki/Jim_Colborn" title="Jim Colborn">Jim Colborn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Darrell_Porter" title="Darrell Porter">Darrell Porter</a>. The Kansas City Royals completed the trade by sending <a href="/wiki/Bob_McClure" title="Bob McClure">Bob McClure</a> to the Brewers on March 15, 1977.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li>December 7, 1976: <a href="/wiki/Frank_Ortenzio" title="Frank Ortenzio">Frank Ortenzio</a> was traded by the Royals to the <a href="/wiki/Montreal_Expos" title="Montreal Expos">Montreal Expos</a> for Rudy Kinard (minors).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li></ul>
