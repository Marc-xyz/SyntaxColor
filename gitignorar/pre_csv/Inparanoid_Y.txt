	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Inparanoid</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Inparanoid</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Algorithm" title="Algorithm">algorithm</a> that finds <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ortholog" title="Ortholog">orthologous</a> <a href="/wiki/Gene" title="Gene">genes</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paralog" title="Paralog">paralogous</a> genes that arose—most likely by <a href="/wiki/Gene_duplication" title="Gene duplication">duplication</a>—after some <a href="/wiki/Speciation" title="Speciation">speciation</a> event. Such <a href="/wiki/Protein" title="Protein">protein</a>-coding genes are called <i>in-paralogs</i>, as opposed to <i>out-paralogs</i> (which arose prior to a species split).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Remm2001_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Remm2001-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p><b>Inparanoid</b> (with varying capitalization) may refer to a program that uses the INPARANOID algorithm, or to the derived database of orthologous clusters of genes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-O'Brien2005_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-O'Brien2005-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
