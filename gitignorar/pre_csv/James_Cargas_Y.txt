<p><b>James Cargas</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Americans" title="Americans">American</a> energy attorney, and was the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">Democratic</a> nominee in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives_elections_in_Texas,_2012#District_7" title="United States House of Representatives elections in Texas, 2012">2012</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives_elections_in_Texas,_2014#District_7" title="United States House of Representatives elections in Texas, 2014">2014</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives_elections_in_Texas,_2012#District_7" title="United States House of Representatives elections in Texas, 2012">2016</a> United States House of Representatives election in Texas' 7th Congressional District. He is a life-long Democrat, who garnered the highest vote percentage (44%) in 2016 (opposing <a href="/wiki/John_Culberson" title="John Culberson">John Culberson</a>) of any Democrat in Texas Congressional District 7 since 1964.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-washingtonpost.com_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-washingtonpost.com-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto1-3">[3]</a></sup> Cargas ran for the Democratic nomination in District 7 again in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives_elections_in_Texas,_2018#District_7" title="United States House of Representatives elections in Texas, 2018">2018</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ballotpedia.org_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-ballotpedia.org-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_career">Early life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_Cargas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1988 after graduating from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Michigan" title="University of Michigan">University of Michigan</a> with a double major in English and Communications, James Cargas became the Deputy Press Secretary to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/David_E._Bonior" title="David E. Bonior">David Bonior</a>, then Member of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._House_of_Representatives" title="U.S. House of Representatives">U.S. House of Representatives</a> from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Michigan%27s_12th_Congressional_District" title="Michigan's 12th Congressional District">Michigan's 12th Congressional District</a>.
</p><p>In 1992, he graduated from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_University_Washington_College_of_Law" title="American University Washington College of Law">American University Washington College of Law</a> where he served as President of the Environmental Law Society and Articles' Editor of <a class="external text" href="http://www.auilr.org/" rel="nofollow">The American University Journal of International Law and Policy</a>. Upon graduation, he worked first in a large law firm and later as environmental counsel to a major interstate pipeline company. He is admitted to practice law in Texas, Washington DC, Michigan and West Virginia.
</p><p>He left the private sector and joined the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clinton_White_House" title="Clinton White House">Clinton White House</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Samuel_Curtis_Johnson,_Jr.#The_President.E2.80.99s_Council_on_Sustainable_Development" title="Samuel Curtis Johnson, Jr.">President’s Council on Sustainable Development</a>.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Cargas,_Clinton,_Papageorgiou.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="412" data-file-width="550" decoding="async" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Cargas%2C_Clinton%2C_Papageorgiou.png/220px-Cargas%2C_Clinton%2C_Papageorgiou.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Cargas%2C_Clinton%2C_Papageorgiou.png/330px-Cargas%2C_Clinton%2C_Papageorgiou.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Cargas%2C_Clinton%2C_Papageorgiou.png/440px-Cargas%2C_Clinton%2C_Papageorgiou.png 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Cargas,_Clinton,_Papageorgiou.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>James Cargas, President Bill Clinton, and Dr. Dorina Papageorgiou at an event.</div></div></div>
<p>After leaving the White House to work on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Al_Gore_presidential_campaign,_2000#Primaries" title="Al Gore presidential campaign, 2000">Al Gore Presidential primary campaign</a> in Washington DC, Iowa and Texas, Cargas rejoined the administration as a Special Assistant in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Department_of_Energy" title="U.S. Department of Energy">U.S. Department of Energy</a> in the <a class="external text" href="http://www.fossil.energy.gov/" rel="nofollow">Office of Fossil Energy</a> under Secretary of Energy <a href="/wiki/Bill_Richardson" title="Bill Richardson">Bill Richardson</a>.  He also served as the Energy Department's liaison to the President's Southwest Border Task Force. He was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Awards_and_decorations_of_the_United_States_government" title="Awards and decorations of the United States government">Exceptional Service Award of the Department of Energy</a>
</p><p>He permanently moved to Houston and became Deputy Director of the non-profit organization <a class="external text" href="http://naesb.org/" rel="nofollow">North American Energy Standards Board</a>. After three years, he returned to private practice where he represented several startup companies in the energy marketing, <a href="/wiki/Directional_drilling" title="Directional drilling">directional drilling</a>, and other industries.
</p><p>In 2008, <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mayor_Bill_White" title="Mayor Bill White">Mayor Bill White</a> hired Cargas as the Senior Assistant City Attorney for Energy of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/City_of_Houston" title="City of Houston">City of Houston</a> responsible for advising the Mayor on all aspects of energy procurement and energy transactional matters. Today, he works for  <a href="/wiki/Sylvester_Turner" title="Sylvester Turner">Mayor Sylvester Turner</a> in the same function and also advises the City on contract, environmental, renewable energy,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> real estate and regulatory matters.
</p><p>Cargas is a founding member of the <a class="external text" href="http://oilpatchdemocrats.blogspot.com/" rel="nofollow">Oil Patch Democrats</a>, a Texas-based political organization promoting "realistic energy policy" and Democratic candidates.
</p><p>Cargas is married to <a class="external text" href="http://www.jamescargas.com/about_dorina/" rel="nofollow">Dr. Dorina Papageorgiou</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Neuroscientist" title="Neuroscientist">neuroscientist</a> at <a href="/wiki/Baylor_College_of_Medicine" title="Baylor College of Medicine">Baylor College of Medicine</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Houston,_Texas" title="Houston, Texas">Houston, Texas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2012_Congressional_Election">2012 Congressional Election</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_Cargas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2012 Congressional Election">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cargas ran in the <a href="/wiki/2012_United_States_House_of_Representatives_elections" title="2012 United States House of Representatives elections">2012 election for the United States House of Representatives</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Texas%27s_7th_congressional_district" title="Texas's 7th congressional district">Texas' 7th Congressional District</a>. He won the Democratic nomination for Texas' 7th Congressional District after a contested primary.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Cargas received 36.43% of the vote against incumbent <a href="/wiki/John_Culberson" title="John Culberson">John Culberson</a> (R) in the general election on November 6, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> He has been endorsed by the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Chronicle" title="Houston Chronicle">Houston Chronicle</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/The_National_Herald" title="The National Herald">The National Herald</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> NEO Magazine,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Washington_Monthly" title="Washington Monthly">Washington Monthly</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:James_Cargas.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="356" data-file-width="530" decoding="async" height="148" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/James_Cargas.png/220px-James_Cargas.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/James_Cargas.png/330px-James_Cargas.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/James_Cargas.png/440px-James_Cargas.png 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:James_Cargas.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Congressional candidate James Cargas during a 2012 interview with Houston television channel KHOU.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Cargas_Logo.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="177" data-file-width="233" decoding="async" height="167" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/81/Cargas_Logo.png/220px-Cargas_Logo.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/81/Cargas_Logo.png 1.5x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Cargas_Logo.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The 2014 James Cargas campaign logo.</div></div></div>
<p>The Texas House of Representatives district maps were the subject of courtroom battles in the lead up to the 2012 election.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Although the federal court rejected maps drawn by the State Legislature due to racial discrimination, the final court-appointed maps included several of the original boundaries at the request of the Texas attorney general. The 7th District lost Memorial Park, Montrose, Spring Branch and Rice University to the 2nd District.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup>
</p><p>Cargas campaigned on a platform consisting of the following central issues:
</p>
<ul><li>Increasing federal funding for science and biotechnology.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> His positions are included in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Research!America&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Research!America (page does not exist)">Research!America</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></li>
<li>Comprehensive national energy policy<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2014_Congressional_Election">2014 Congressional Election</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_Cargas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2014 Congressional Election">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cargas won the Democratic nomination in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives_elections_in_Texas,_2014" title="United States House of Representatives elections in Texas, 2014">2014 Democratic Primary</a> for <a href="/wiki/Texas%27s_7th_congressional_district" title="Texas's 7th congressional district">Texas' 7th Congressional District</a> with 62.2% of the vote following an <a class="external text" href="http://www.chron.com/opinion/recommendations/article/For-Congress-Dems-should-consider-James-Cargas-5267558.php" rel="nofollow">endorsement by the Houston Chronicle</a>. In the general election on November 4, 2014 Cargas received 34.55% of the vote against incumbent John Culberson (R).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2016_Congressional_Election">2016 Congressional Election</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_Cargas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2016 Congressional Election">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cargas ran again in <a href="/wiki/Texas%27s_7th_congressional_district" title="Texas's 7th congressional district">Texas' 7th Congressional District</a> in 2016. </p>
