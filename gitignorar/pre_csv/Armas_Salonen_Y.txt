	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Armas Salonen</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Finnish Assyriologist</div>
<p><b>Armas Immanuel Salonen</b> (17 January 1915 – 22 October 1981) was a Finnish Assyriologist.
</p><p>Salonen graduated from high school in 1933.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-:1-1">[1]</a></sup> He studied at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Helsinki" title="University of Helsinki">University of Helsinki</a> under professor <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Knut_Tallqvist&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Knut Tallqvist (page does not exist)">Knut Tallqvist</a>. Having obtained his bachelor's degree in 1936, he continued his studies in Germany and later on in the US, where he joined the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_Chicago_Oriental_Institute" title="University of Chicago Oriental Institute">Oriental Institute of the University of Chicago</a> in 1947. There he participated in the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Assyrian_Dictionary" title="Chicago Assyrian Dictionary">Chicago Assyrian Dictionary</a> project under professor <a href="/wiki/A._Leo_Oppenheim" title="A. Leo Oppenheim">A. Leo Oppenheim</a>, focusing on etymological references.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Salonen received his PhD in 1950. From 1949 to 1978 he held the position of associate professor of Assyrian and Semitic Philology at the University of Helsinki.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:uvf_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-:uvf-3">[3]</a></sup> His research interests focused on the <a href="/wiki/Material_culture" title="Material culture">material culture</a> and everyday life of <a href="/wiki/Mesopotamia" title="Mesopotamia">Mesopotamia</a>, such as its watercraft, vehicles and doors; a large part of his work consists of thematic glossaries of specialized terminology. He published the first Finnish translation of the <a href="/wiki/Epic_of_Gilgamesh" title="Epic of Gilgamesh">Epic of Gilgamesh</a> in 1943.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup> He was also interested in the history of Persia and ancient Egypt, and had a command of Arabic. In 1957, together with <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jussi_Aro&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jussi Aro (page does not exist)">Jussi Aro</a>, he published the first Finnish translation of the <a href="/wiki/Quran" title="Quran">Quran</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Salonen was elected member of the <a href="/wiki/Finnish_Academy_of_Science_and_Letters" title="Finnish Academy of Science and Letters">Finnish Academy of Science and Letters</a> in 1950 and honorary member of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Finnish_Egyptological_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Finnish Egyptological Society (page does not exist)">Finnish Egyptological Society</a> in 1975.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-:1-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
