<p><b>Gonzalo "Gonchi" Rodríguez Bongoll</b> (January 22, 1971 – September 11, 1999) was a Uruguayan <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Racing_driver" title="Racing driver">racing driver</a>. He was killed in an accident at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Laguna_Seca_Raceway" title="Laguna Seca Raceway">Laguna Seca Raceway</a> during qualifying for a <a href="/wiki/Champ_Car" title="Champ Car">CART</a> race.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gonzalo_Rodr%C3%ADguez_(racing_driver)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He showed promise in <a href="/wiki/International_Formula_3000" title="International Formula 3000">Formula 3000</a> for three seasons, taking two wins in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1998_Formula_3000_season" title="1998 Formula 3000 season">1998</a> at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps" title="Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps">Spa-Francorchamps</a> and <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburgring" title="Nürburgring">Nürburgring</a>, winning the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1999_Formula_3000_season" title="1999 Formula 3000 season">following season</a> in <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Monaco</a> and finishing third in both championships. Following a rotation of drivers as team mates to <a href="/wiki/Al_Unser_Jr." title="Al Unser Jr.">Al Unser Jr.</a> in <a href="/wiki/Champ_Car" title="Champ Car">CART</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Penske_Racing" title="Penske Racing">Penske Racing</a>'s second car, he was given his opportunity at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Detroit_Indy_Grand_Prix" title="Detroit Indy Grand Prix">Detroit Grand Prix</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1999_CART_World_Series_Season" title="1999 CART World Series Season">1999</a> and scored a point in his only race.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gonzalo_Rodr%C3%ADguez_(racing_driver)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Laguna_Seca_Raceway" title="Laguna Seca Raceway">Laguna Seca Raceway</a> during the qualification session for his second CART race (in 5th place while qualifying), he was fatally injured in a crash. A stuck throttle was initially thought to be the cause for his car to overshoot the braking point, leave the track and slam into a concrete barrier at the entry of the Corkscrew corner. Review of the in-car telemetry refuted this supposition. The impact caused his car to flip over the barrier and land upside down on the other side of the wall. Rodríguez was killed instantly by a <a href="/wiki/Basilar_skull_fracture" title="Basilar skull fracture">basilar skull fracture</a> caused by the impact with the wall, which was lined by only a thin layer of tires. Because of the incident, runoff was installed at the end of the straight.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>Rodríguez had a contract in place to compete in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2000_CART_World_Series_Season" title="2000 CART World Series Season">2000</a> CART championship with <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Racing" title="Patrick Racing">Patrick Racing</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gonzalo_Rodr%C3%ADguez_(racing_driver)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In July 2013, Autosport magazine named Rodríguez one of the 50 greatest drivers never to have raced in Formula One, citing both his mental and physical strength, and his race craft.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In 2014, a Spanish-language documentary of his life, <i>Gonchi</i>, was released.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
