<p><b>Hartwell</b> is a city in <a href="/wiki/Hart_County,_Georgia" title="Hart County, Georgia">Hart County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a>, United States. The population was 4,469 at the 2010 census.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census_2010_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Census_2010-4">[4]</a></sup> The city is the <a href="/wiki/County_seat" title="County seat">county seat</a> of Hart County.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR6_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR6-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hartwell,_Georgia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hartwell was founded in 1854 as seat of the newly formed Hart County. It was incorporated as a town in 1856 and as a city in 1904.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The town was named for Revolutionary War figure <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nancy_Morgan_Hart" title="Nancy Morgan Hart">Nancy Morgan Hart</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hartwell,_Georgia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hartwell is located in central Hart County at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Hartwell,_Georgia&amp;params=34_21_10_N_82_55_52_W_type:city"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">34°21′10″N</span> <span class="longitude">82°55′52″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">34.35278°N 82.93111°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">34.35278; -82.93111</span></span></span></a></span> (34.352738, -82.931161).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR1_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR1-8">[8]</a></sup> It sits 4 miles (6 km) southwest of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Hartwell" title="Lake Hartwell">Lake Hartwell</a>, which acquired its name from the city. Hartwell is in the <a href="/wiki/Piedmont_(United_States)" title="Piedmont (United States)">Piedmont</a> region of Georgia, or the <a href="/wiki/Upland_South" title="Upland South">Upland South</a>, and lies 30 miles (48 km) southeast of the foothills of the <a href="/wiki/Appalachian_Mountains" title="Appalachian Mountains">Appalachian Mountains</a> at <a href="/wiki/Toccoa,_Georgia" title="Toccoa, Georgia">Toccoa</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_29" title="U.S. Route 29">U.S. Route 29</a> passes through the center of Hartwell, leading east 7 miles (11 km) to the <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">South Carolina</a> border at Hartwell Dam on the <a href="/wiki/Savannah_River" title="Savannah River">Savannah River</a>, and southwest 12 miles (19 km) to <a href="/wiki/Royston,_Georgia" title="Royston, Georgia">Royston</a>. <a href="/wiki/Anderson,_South_Carolina" title="Anderson, South Carolina">Anderson, South Carolina</a>, is 23 miles (37 km) to the northeast via US 29, and <a href="/wiki/Athens,_Georgia" title="Athens, Georgia">Athens, Georgia</a>, is 43 miles (69 km) to the southwest. <a href="/wiki/Georgia_State_Route_51" title="Georgia State Route 51">Georgia State Route 51</a> also passes through Hartwell, leading north 7 miles (11 km) to <a href="/wiki/Reed_Creek,_Georgia" title="Reed Creek, Georgia">Reed Creek</a> and west 9 miles (14 km) to <a href="/wiki/Bowersville,_Georgia" title="Bowersville, Georgia">Bowersville</a>.
</p><p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, Hartwell has a total area of 5.1 square miles (13.2 km<sup>2</sup>), of which 0.02 square miles (0.04 km<sup>2</sup>), or 0.32%, are water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census_2010_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Census_2010-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>

<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-2">[2]</a></sup> of 2010, there were 4,469 people.  There were 2,266 housing units.  The racial makeup of the city was 61.33% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 34.53% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 0.13% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 0.62% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 0% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander_(U.S._Census)" title="Pacific Islander (U.S. Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 0.33% from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 1.77% from two or more races. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 3.07% of the population.
</p><p>The median income for a household in the city was $29,128 and the median income for a family was $45,909. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the city was $18,937.  About 15.4% of families and 23.1% of the population were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 21.5% of those under age 18 and 20.6% of those age 65 or over.
</p>

<p>Hartwell is located in the <a href="/wiki/Humid_subtropical_climate" title="Humid subtropical climate">humid subtropical climate</a> zone of the <a href="/wiki/Southeastern_United_States" title="Southeastern United States">Southeastern United States</a>. It is in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Piedmont_Plateau" title="Piedmont Plateau">Piedmont Plateau</a> region, along the eastern side of the <a href="/wiki/Appalachian_Mountains" title="Appalachian Mountains">Appalachian Mountains</a>, at an elevation just above 800 feet (240 m). Due to some of the highest of elevations in the Appalachians being between Hartwell and <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, this allows for slightly warmer conditions than areas further south such as <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>.  One exception to this rule is called the "wedge" or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cold_air_damming" title="Cold air damming">cold air damming</a>. This is due to a large high pressure system in eastern Canada driving colder drier air down against the eastern side of the Appalachian Mountain range. When this phenomenon is joined by gulf moisture, it frequently causes ice storms or <a href="/wiki/Freezing_rain" title="Freezing rain">freezing rain</a> in the region. This same geographical feature can also cause frequent <a href="/wiki/Drought" title="Drought">drought</a>.
</p><p>Hartwell averages 51.96 inches (1,320 mm) of precipitation annually. The average snowfall is 2 inches (51 mm), although more can fall, and the city is just as likely to have no measurable snowfall in any given year. The <a href="/wiki/1993_Storm_of_the_Century" title="1993 Storm of the Century">1993 Storm of the Century</a> brought 6 inches (150 mm) of snow to the area.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hart_County_School_District">Hart County School District</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hartwell,_Georgia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Hart County School District">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hart_County_Public_Library">Hart County Public Library</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hartwell,_Georgia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Hart County Public Library">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Hart County Public Library was begun in 1938 with rooms over Homer Herndon's drug store, then moved to the County Courthouse in 1941 until 1968 when the courthouse burned down. It was then located in the County School Board building until funds were raised for a permanent building in 1975. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p>
