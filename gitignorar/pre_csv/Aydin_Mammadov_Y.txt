<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aydin_Mammadov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Aydin Mammadov was born in 1967 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sumgait" title="Sumgait">Sumgait</a>.
</p><p>In 1975-1983, he studied at secondary school number 10 in Sumgait.
</p><p>In 1983, he graduated with honors from the Baku Pedagogical College named after <a href="/wiki/Mirza_Alakbar_Sabir" title="Mirza Alakbar Sabir">M.A.Sabir</a>. In 1987-1989, he served in the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Army" title="Soviet Army">Soviet Army</a>.
</p><p>In 1989-1994, he graduated with honors from the History faculty of <a href="/wiki/Baku_State_University" title="Baku State University">Baku State University</a>. He was awarded a <a href="/wiki/Scholarship" title="Scholarship">scholarship</a> named after <a href="/wiki/Karl_Marx" title="Karl Marx">K. Marx</a> for excellent study at the university.
</p><p>In 1994-1997, he worked at <a href="/wiki/Sumqayit_State_University" title="Sumqayit State University">Sumqayit State University</a> and at various preparatory courses.
</p><p>In 2013, he received a Ph.D. in history on the topic "Turkish policy of Germany and its historiography at the end of the 19th and beginning of the 20th centuries”, specialty 5509.01 - “Historiography, source study and methods".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Since 2001, he has been working at the Department of Baku State University, "Source Studies, Historiography, and Methods".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
