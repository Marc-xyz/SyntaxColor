<p><b>WBRV</b> (900 <a href="/wiki/AM_broadcasting" title="AM broadcasting">AM</a>) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radio_station" title="Radio station">radio station</a> broadcasting a <a href="/wiki/Classic_rock" title="Classic rock">classic rock</a> format. Licensed to <a href="/wiki/Boonville,_New_York" title="Boonville, New York">Boonville, New York</a>, United States, the station is owned by The Flack Broadcasting Group.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_history">Station history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WBRV_(AM)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Station history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>WBRV AM signed on in the 1950s. The station was built by local newspaper owner Livingston Lansing. The station was a member of the Northeast Radio Network. <a href="/wiki/WBRV-FM" title="WBRV-FM">WBRV-FM</a>, and its repeater WLLG, both act as translators. In May 2007, WBRV became independent and began broadcasting an Oldies format. The new station was called "Oldies 900".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wbrv_1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-wbrv_1-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>On January 1, 2017, WBRV changed their format from classic hits to classic rock, still branded as "The Blizzard".  (info taken from stationintel.com)
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="FM_rebroadcasters">FM rebroadcasters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WBRV_(AM)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: FM rebroadcasters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In February 2010, Flack Broadcasting acquired a former <a href="/wiki/North_Country_Public_Radio" title="North Country Public Radio">North Country Public Radio</a> translator after it moved to a more powerful signal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wbrv_2_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-wbrv_2-2">[2]</a></sup> The 105.9 FM translator allows WBRV to be heard more widely 24 hours a day, since WBRV is only allowed a nighttime power of 52 watts. Shortly after signing on the FM translator, WBRV changed its name from "Oldies 900" to "The Blizzard", but kept the same format.
In November 2011, Flack Broadcasting was able to acquire another <a href="/wiki/North_Country_Public_Radio" title="North Country Public Radio">North Country Public Radio</a> translator that was being upgraded to a more powerful signal. This translator's frequency is 98.3 and should give <a href="/wiki/Lowville_(village),_New_York" title="Lowville (village), New York">Lowville, New York</a> a stronger signal.
</p>
