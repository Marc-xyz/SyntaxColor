	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Stefan Czarnowski</h1>

		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Stefan Czarnowski</b> (1879 – 1937) was a <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Polish</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sociologist" title="Sociologist">sociologist</a>, professor of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Warsaw" title="University of Warsaw">University of Warsaw</a>.
</p><p>Member of the Polish pro-independence movements, he fought in the <a href="/wiki/Polish_Legions_in_World_War_I" title="Polish Legions in World War I">Polish Legions</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polish-Soviet_War" title="Polish-Soviet War">Polish-Soviet War</a>. At first supporter of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Endecja" title="Endecja">endecja</a>, he gravitated towards supporting <a href="/wiki/Polish_Socialist_Party" title="Polish Socialist Party">Polish Socialist Party</a>.
</p><p>His works centered on sociology of culture and religion. He studied social movements, theory of heroes as well as history of the Celts.
</p>
