<p><i><b>Empire Carpenter</b></i> was a 7,025 GRT <a href="/wiki/Cargo_ship" title="Cargo ship">cargo ship</a> which was built in 1942 for the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_War_Transport" title="Ministry of War Transport">Ministry of War Transport</a> (MoWT). In 1944 she was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lend-lease" title="Lend-lease">leased</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> and renamed <i><b>Dickson</b></i>. In 1946, she was returned to the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> and regained her former name of <i>Empire Carpenter</i>. She was sold in 1947 and renamed <i><b>Petfrano</b></i>. In 1955, she was sold to <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">Panama</a> owners and renamed <i><b>Amipa</b></i>, further sales saw her renamed <i><b>Apex</b></i>. In 1968, she was sold to <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cypriot</a> owners and renamed <i><b>Afros</b></i>, serving until scrapped in 1971.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SS_Empire_Carpenter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The ship was built by C Connell &amp; Co Ltd, <a href="/wiki/Scotstoun" title="Scotstoun">Scotstoun</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ships_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ships-1">[1]</a></sup> as yard number 440.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Clyde_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Clyde-2">[2]</a></sup> She was launched on 21 November 1942, and completed in January 1943.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ships_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ships-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The ship was 431 feet 3 inches (131.45 m) long, with a beam of 56 feet 2 inches (17.12 m), a depth of 33 feet 6 inches (10.21 m),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Carpenter42_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Carpenter42-3">[3]</a></sup> and a draught of  27 feet 3 inches (8.31 m).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Clyde_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Clyde-2">[2]</a></sup> Her GRT was 7,025, with a NRT of 4,857.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Carpenter42_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Carpenter42-3">[3]</a></sup> Her DWT was 10,318.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Clyde_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Clyde-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>She was propelled by a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Triple_expansion_steam_engine" title="Triple expansion steam engine">triple expansion steam engine</a>, which had cylinders of <span class="frac nowrap">24<span class="visualhide"> </span><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>2</sub></span> inches (62 cm), 39 inches (99 cm) and 70 inches (180 cm) diameter and 48 inches (120 cm) stroke. The engine was built by D Rowan &amp; Co Ltd, <a href="/wiki/Glasgow" title="Glasgow">Glasgow</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Carpenter42_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Carpenter42-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=SS_Empire_Carpenter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Empire Carpenter</i> was built for the MoWT.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ships_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Ships-1">[1]</a></sup> The United Kingdom Official Number 168745 and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Code_Letters" title="Code Letters">Code Letters</a> BFLJ were allocated. She was placed under the management of the Hain Steamship Co Ltd.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Carpenter42_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-Carpenter42-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Empire Carpenter</i> was a member of a number of convoys during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>.
</p>
<dl><dt>NKS9</dt></dl>
<p>Convoy MKS departed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/B%C3%B4ne" title="Bône">Bône</a>, <a href="/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algeria</a> on 4 March 1943 and arrived at <a href="/wiki/Liverpool" title="Liverpool">Liverpool</a> on 18 March. <i>Empire Caribou</i> was carrying a cargo of <a href="/wiki/Steel" title="Steel">steel</a> bound for <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MKS9_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-MKS9-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<dl><dt>JW 54A</dt></dl>
<p>Convoy JW 54A departed <a href="/wiki/Loch_Ewe" title="Loch Ewe">Loch Ewe</a> on 15 November 1943 and arrived at the <a href="/wiki/Kola_Peninsula" title="Kola Peninsula">Kola Inlet</a> on 24 November. <i>Empire Carpenter</i> was carrying the <a href="/wiki/Convoy_commodore" title="Convoy commodore">Vice Commodore</a> of the convoy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JW54A_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-JW54A-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<dl><dt>JW 57</dt></dl>
<p>Convoy JW 57 departed Liverpool on 20 February 1944 and arrived at the Kola Inlet on 28 February.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JW57_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-JW57-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<dl><dt>RA 55A</dt></dl>
<p>Convoy RA 55A departed the Kola Inlet on 23 December 1943 and arrived at Loch Ewe on 1 January 1944.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RA55A_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-RA55A-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1944, <i>Empire Carpenter</i> was transferred to the Soviet Union under Lend-Lease and was renamed <i>Dickson</i>. She was returned to the United Kingdom in 1946 and regained her former name of <i>Empire Carpenter</i>. She was placed under the management of the Petrinovic Steamship Co Ltd.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Clyde_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Clyde-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1947, <i>Empire Carpenter</i> was sold to the Petrinovic Steamship Co Ltd and was renamed <i>Petfrano</i>. She served until 1955 when she was sold to Compagnia di Navigazione Amipa SA, <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">Panama</a> and was renamed <i>Amipa</i>. In 1958, she was sold to Compagnia Maritime Apex SA and renamed <i>Apex</i>. In 1968, she was renamed <i>Afros</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ships_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Ships-1">[1]</a></sup> In 1970, she was sold to Campos Shipping Co, <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a>. She was <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_convenience" title="Flag of convenience">reflagged</a> to <a href="/wiki/Liberia" title="Liberia">Liberia</a>, with her port of registry being <a href="/wiki/Monrovia" title="Monrovia">Monrovia</a>. She served until 1971, arriving for scrapping on 17 March at <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Clyde_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-Clyde-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
