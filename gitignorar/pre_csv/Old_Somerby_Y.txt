<p><b>Old Somerby</b> (pronounced <i>Summerby</i>) is a village and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" title="Civil parishes in England">civil parish</a> in the <a href="/wiki/South_Kesteven" title="South Kesteven">South Kesteven</a> <a href="/wiki/Non-metropolitan_district" title="Non-metropolitan district">district</a> of <a href="/wiki/Lincolnshire" title="Lincolnshire">Lincolnshire</a>, England, 3 miles (5 km) south-east of <a href="/wiki/Grantham" title="Grantham">Grantham</a>. It lies on the B1176 road about1 mile (1.6 km) east of its junction with the <a href="/wiki/A52_road" title="A52 road">A52</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/B6403_road" title="B6403 road">B6403</a>, and 0.2 miles (250 m) from the <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_Main_Line" title="East Coast Main Line">East Coast Main Line</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-os_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-os-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Structure">Structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Old_Somerby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The civil-parish population at the 2011 census was 224.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Adjacent villages are <a href="/wiki/Ropsley" title="Ropsley">Ropsley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Boothby_Pagnell" title="Boothby Pagnell">Boothby Pagnell</a>. The village divides into Old Somerby, High Somerby and Low Somerby.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-os_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-os-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Amenities">Amenities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Old_Somerby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Amenities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Parish" title="Parish">church parish</a> is part of The North Beltisloe Group of <a href="/wiki/Beltisloe" title="Beltisloe">Beltisloe</a> <a href="/wiki/Deanery" title="Deanery">Deanery</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Diocese_of_Lincoln" title="Diocese of Lincoln">Diocese of Lincoln</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The parish church in High Somerby is dedicated to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St_Mary_Magdalene" title="St Mary Magdalene">St Mary Magdalene</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nb_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-nb-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> the same dedication as at nearby <a href="/wiki/Bitchfield" title="Bitchfield">Bitchfield</a>.
</p><p>There is bed-and-breakfast accommodation in School Lane. The village <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_house" title="Public house">public house</a> is the <i>Fox and Hounds</i> in Grantham Road (B1176). Schools, shops and other amenities are available in Grantham (4 miles, 6.5 km), to which there are occasional daytime, weekday buses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Heritage">Heritage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Old_Somerby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Heritage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:182px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:St.Mary_Magdalen%27s_church,_Old_Somerby,_Lincs._-_geograph.org.uk_-_130303.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="427" data-file-width="640" decoding="async" height="120" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/St.Mary_Magdalen%27s_church%2C_Old_Somerby%2C_Lincs._-_geograph.org.uk_-_130303.jpg/180px-St.Mary_Magdalen%27s_church%2C_Old_Somerby%2C_Lincs._-_geograph.org.uk_-_130303.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/St.Mary_Magdalen%27s_church%2C_Old_Somerby%2C_Lincs._-_geograph.org.uk_-_130303.jpg/270px-St.Mary_Magdalen%27s_church%2C_Old_Somerby%2C_Lincs._-_geograph.org.uk_-_130303.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/St.Mary_Magdalen%27s_church%2C_Old_Somerby%2C_Lincs._-_geograph.org.uk_-_130303.jpg/360px-St.Mary_Magdalen%27s_church%2C_Old_Somerby%2C_Lincs._-_geograph.org.uk_-_130303.jpg 2x" width="180"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:St.Mary_Magdalen%27s_church,_Old_Somerby,_Lincs._-_geograph.org.uk_-_130303.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>St Mary Magdalene's Church</div></div></div>
<p>Somerby was a colony of the <a href="/wiki/Danes_(Germanic_tribe)" title="Danes (Germanic tribe)">Danes</a> who ruled Lincolnshire after peace was made with <a href="/wiki/Alfred_the_Great" title="Alfred the Great">King Alfred</a> in 878. The village is listed in the <i><a href="/wiki/Domesday_Book" title="Domesday Book">Domesday Book</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> where the King's tenant-in-chief is stated to be <a href="/wiki/Walter_D%27Aincourt" title="Walter D'Aincourt">Walter of Aincourt</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p>The village belonged to the historical <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hundred_(county_subdivision)" title="Hundred (county subdivision)">wapentake</a> of <a href="/wiki/Winnibriggs_and_Threo" title="Winnibriggs and Threo">Winnibriggs and Threo</a>. The population of the 51 houses of the village in 1870–1872 was given as 234.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
<div style="clear:left;"></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Historic_buildings">Historic buildings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Old_Somerby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Historic buildings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The church is Grade II* listed. Initially from the 12th–15th centuries, it underwent alterations and additions in the 18th century and in 1876.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Old Rectory in School Lane is Grade II listed. It dates from about 1700, with later alterations and additions in the late 18th and the 19th and 20th centuries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The other Grade II listed buildings in the village are a Barn at the Manor House, the Manor House itself, the Stable Block at Cold Harbour Farm, and the Old Farm House.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p><p>Nearby the church, there is the site of a moated manor house.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_people">Notable people</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Old_Somerby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Notable people">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Edward_Weston_(politician)" title="Edward Weston (politician)">Edward Weston</a> (1703–1770), didactic writer and politician, purchased the parish of Somerby and lived at Somerby Hall, as did his descendants into the 1930s. His monument remains in the churchyard.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/William_Henry_Brookfield" title="William Henry Brookfield">William Henry Brookfield</a> (1809–1874), formerly inspector of schools and chaplain-in-ordinary to Queen Victoria, became Rector of Somerby-cum-Humby in 1865. <a href="/wiki/Alfred,_Lord_Tennyson" title="Alfred, Lord Tennyson">Lord Tennyson</a>, a friend, wrote a sonnet in his memory.</li></ul>
