	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Adamson Mushala</h1>

<p><b>Adamson Batson Mushala</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Zambia" title="Zambia">Zambian</a> rebel leader who rebelled against the Zambian government of Dr. Kenneth David Kaunda from 1975 to 1982.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rebellion">Rebellion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adamson_Mushala&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Rebellion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mushala initially belonged to the <a href="/wiki/United_National_Independence_Party" title="United National Independence Party">United National Independence Party</a>, UNIP fighting colonial minority rule.  He was sent to China for guerrilla training with a view to coming back to fight the colonial government, but upon his return, Zambia was already independent. He demanded a high post in the UNIP government which Kaunda denied. Out of frustration, he left Zambia together with his followers and went to the then South West Africa (<a href="/wiki/Namibia" title="Namibia">Namibia</a>) under the control of South Africa. He advanced in his guerrilla training and in 1975, after inspiration from Jonas Savimbi of <a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Angola</a>, he came back to Zambia and rebelled against the UNIP government until 1982.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>It was believed that Mushala was strongly believed to be surviving through a complexity of witchcraft and supernatural powers. There were some unconfirmed reports in Solwezi that the Government had even hired some witchdoctors locally and abroad, to come and try to capture the gangster but all was in vain.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adamson_Mushala&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Adamson Mushala was gunned down in 1982 in a military ambush in the remote areas of Northwestern Province near <a href="/wiki/Solwezi" title="Solwezi">Solwezi</a>, where his rebellion began with a post-independence dispute in the 1960s and ended with a bullet through his eye.
</p><p>His body was paraded public for public viewing at Solwezi General Hospital.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Pashala Mooshala 
Alexandros Sotos
Pashamel
Pashoola
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adamson_Mushala&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After the death of Mushala, his second-in-command Alexander Saimbwende took over the reins and continued the fight until September 25, 1990, when he gave up after a dialogue with the then Member of the Central Committee for North Western Province Alexander Kamalondo. He was flown to Lusaka where he was later pardoned by President Dr. Kenneth Kaunda.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
