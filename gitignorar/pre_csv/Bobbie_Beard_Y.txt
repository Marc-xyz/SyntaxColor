<p><b>Bobbie Beard</b> (August 2, 1930 – October 16, 1999) was an American <a href="/wiki/Child_actor" title="Child actor">child actor</a>, best known for portraying "Cotton" in several <i><a href="/wiki/Our_Gang" title="Our Gang">Our Gang</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Short_film" title="Short film">short films</a> from 1932 to 1934. He was a native of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Los_Angeles,_California" title="Los Angeles, California">Los Angeles, California</a>. His older brother was <a href="/wiki/Matthew_Beard_(American_actor)" title="Matthew Beard (American actor)">Matthew "Stymie" Beard</a>, one of the series' most popular and best-remembered characters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gideonseniors1959.com_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-gideonseniors1959.com-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Our_Gang_years"><i>Our Gang</i> years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bobbie_Beard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Our Gang years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As older brother Stymie was the main breadwinner for the Beard family, his success with the <i>Our Gang</i> series opened the door for his siblings. Bobbie Beard appeared as Stymie's younger brother in <i><a href="/wiki/Hi%27-Neighbor!" title="Hi'-Neighbor!">Hi'-Neighbor!</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Forgotten_Babies" title="Forgotten Babies">Forgotten Babies</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Fish_Hooky" title="Fish Hooky">Fish Hooky</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/A_Lad_an%27_a_Lamp" title="A Lad an' a Lamp">A Lad an' a Lamp</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Birthday_Blues" title="Birthday Blues">Birthday Blues</a></i>. His most memorable appearance was in <i>A Lad an' a Lamp</i>, in which <a href="/wiki/George_McFarland" title="George McFarland">Spanky McFarland</a> keeps wishing that Cotton could be a monkey. Despite his notable presence in several films, Beard never spoke a word.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_years_and_death">Later years and death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bobbie_Beard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Later years and death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After departing <i>Our Gang</i>, Beard became an auction dealer in the Los Angeles area. In later years, Beard worked at the <a href="/wiki/Hillcrest_Country_Club_(Los_Angeles)" title="Hillcrest Country Club (Los Angeles)">Hillcrest Country Club</a> in Los Angeles and became good friends with <a href="/wiki/Groucho_Marx" title="Groucho Marx">Groucho Marx</a>. He later served in the <a href="/wiki/Korean_War" title="Korean War">Korean War</a>, and spent his final years working for the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Unified_School_District" title="Los Angeles Unified School District">Los Angeles School Board</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Beard died on October 16, 1999.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gideonseniors1959.com_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-gideonseniors1959.com-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
