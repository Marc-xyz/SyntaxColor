<p><i><b>Here I Am, Here I Stay</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <i lang="de"><b>Hier bin ich – hier bleib ich</b></i>) is a 1959 <a href="/wiki/Cinema_of_Germany" title="Cinema of Germany">West German</a> <a href="/wiki/Musical_film" title="Musical film">musical</a> <a href="/wiki/Comedy_film" title="Comedy film">comedy film</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/Werner_Jacobs" title="Werner Jacobs">Werner Jacobs</a> and produced by <a href="/wiki/Artur_Brauner" title="Artur Brauner">Artur Brauner</a>. It stars <a href="/wiki/Caterina_Valente" title="Caterina Valente">Caterina Valente</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hans_Holt" title="Hans Holt">Hans Holt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ruth_Stephan" title="Ruth Stephan">Ruth Stephan</a>. The film also features a guest appearance by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bill_Haley_(musician)" title="Bill Haley (musician)">Bill Haley</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Here_I_Am,_Here_I_Stay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The singer Caterine, owner of a bar/night club in Paris, wants to marry her boyfriend Pierre. However, at the registrar's office she finds out that she is allegedly already married to one Baron Hubert von Löwenherz, owner of a castle. A former employee had stolen Caterine's documents and married the Baron under her assumed identity — before disappearing with the family jewels. Caterine travels to the Baron's castle, where a visit by his rich uncle forces the Baron to stage a life of married harmony with Caterine as his wife. Eventually, Caterine and the Baron fall for each other, whilst Pierre finds a new romance with the castle's cook.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Filmportal_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Filmportal-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Here_I_Am,_Here_I_Stay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Hier bin ich - hier bleib ich</i> was directed by <a href="/wiki/Werner_Jacobs" title="Werner Jacobs">Werner Jacobs</a> and produced by <a href="/wiki/Artur_Brauner" title="Artur Brauner">Artur Brauner</a>. The screenplay was written by Curth Flatow and Eckart Hachfeld, based on the play <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=J%27y_suis,_j%27y_reste&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="J'y suis, j'y reste (page does not exist)">J'y suis, j'y reste</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> [<a class="extiw" href="https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/J%27y_suis,_j%27y_reste_(pi%C3%A8ce_de_th%C3%A9%C3%A2tre)" title="fr:J'y suis, j'y reste (pièce de théâtre)">fr</a>]</span></i> by Raymond Vincy and Jean Valmy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Filmportal_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Filmportal-1">[1]</a></sup> An earlier <a class="extiw" href="https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/J%27y_suis,_j%27y_reste_(film)" title="fr:J'y suis, j'y reste (film)">French film of 1954</a> is also based on this play.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-French_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-French-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Filming took place from 16 October to 11 November 1958 in <a href="/wiki/Bamberg" title="Bamberg">Bamberg</a> and Schloss Seehof in <a href="/wiki/Franconia" title="Franconia">Franconia</a>, as well as at the <a href="/wiki/Spandau_Studios" title="Spandau Studios">Spandau Studios</a> in Berlin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Filmportal_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Filmportal-1">[1]</a></sup> The line producer of the film was <a href="/wiki/Horst_Wendlandt" title="Horst Wendlandt">Horst Wendlandt</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Filmportal_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Filmportal-1">[1]</a></sup> The film's sets were designed by the <a href="/wiki/Art_director" title="Art director">art directors</a> <a href="/wiki/Walter_Kutz" title="Walter Kutz">Walter Kutz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Helmut_Nentwig" title="Helmut Nentwig">Helmut Nentwig</a>,
</p><p>The film premiered at the Gloria-Palast in Stuttgart on 8 January 1959.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Filmportal_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-Filmportal-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Soundtrack">Soundtrack</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Here_I_Am,_Here_I_Stay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Soundtrack">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the late 1980s Bear Family Records of Germany released a soundtrack album for the film, which was notable for including two of the three Bill Haley recordings which had been made exclusively for the movie (including "Vive la Rock and Roll" which featured the then-unprecedented duet between Haley and Caterina Valente, and "Hot Dog Buddy Buddy"). The third Haley recording made for the film, "Whoa Mabel", has not surfaced as of 2011 (like the other two it is not the same recording as released by Haley on <a href="/wiki/Decca_Records" title="Decca Records">Decca Records</a>).
</p>
