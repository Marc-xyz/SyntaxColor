<p><b>A. Scott Frank</b> (born March 10, 1960) is an American <a href="/wiki/Screenwriter" title="Screenwriter">screenwriter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Film_director" title="Film director">film director</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Author" title="Author">author</a>. He has earned two <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Adapted_Screenplay" title="Academy Award for Best Adapted Screenplay">Academy Award for Best Adapted Screenplay</a> nominations, for <i><a href="/wiki/Out_of_Sight" title="Out of Sight">Out of Sight</a></i> (1998) and <i><a href="/wiki/Logan_(film)" title="Logan (film)">Logan</a></i> (2017).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scott_Frank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Frank was born to a <a href="/wiki/American_Jews" title="American Jews">Jewish</a> family<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jewish_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Jewish-1">[1]</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Fort_Walton_Beach,_Florida" title="Fort Walton Beach, Florida">Fort Walton Beach, Florida</a> on March 10, 1960.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>He attended the <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Santa_Barbara" title="University of California, Santa Barbara">University of California, Santa Barbara</a>, graduating in 1982 with a B.A. in film studies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-carsey_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-carsey-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scott_Frank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2008, Scott Frankâs directorial debut, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lookout_(2007_film)" title="The Lookout (2007 film)">The Lookout</a></i>, won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Independent_Spirit" title="Independent Spirit">Independent Spirit</a> award for Best First Feature.
</p><p>Along with <i>The Lookout</i>, Mr. Frankâs other screenplays include <i>Little Man Tate</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Dead_Again" title="Dead Again">Dead Again</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Malice_(1993_film)" title="Malice (1993 film)">Malice</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Heaven%27s_Prisoners" title="Heaven's Prisoners">Heaven's Prisoners</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Get_Shorty_(film)" title="Get Shorty (film)">Get Shorty</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Out_of_Sight" title="Out of Sight">Out of Sight</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Minority_Report_(film)" title="Minority Report (film)">Minority Report</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Interpreter" title="The Interpreter">The Interpreter</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marley_%26_Me(film)" title="Marley &amp; Me(film)">Marley &amp; Me</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wolverine_(film)" title="The Wolverine (film)">The Wolverine</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/A_Walk_Among_the_Tombstones_(film)" title="A Walk Among the Tombstones (film)">A Walk Among the Tombstones</a></i> (which he also directed).
</p><p><i>Out of Sight</i>, nominated for an <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Adapted_Screenplay" title="Academy Award for Best Adapted Screenplay">Academy Award for Best Adapted Screenplay</a>, won the <a href="/wiki/Writers_Guild_of_America_Award_for_Best_Adapted_Screenplay" title="Writers Guild of America Award for Best Adapted Screenplay">Writers Guild of America Award for Best Adapted Screenplay</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Edgar_Award" title="Edgar Award">Edgar Award</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Mystery_Writers_of_America" title="Mystery Writers of America">Mystery Writers of America</a> as well as Best Screenplay awards from the <a href="/wiki/National_Society_of_Film_Critics" title="National Society of Film Critics">National Society of Film Critics</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Society_of_Film_Critics" title="Boston Society of Film Critics">Boston Society of Film Critics</a>.
</p><p><i>Minority Report</i> won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saturn_Award" title="Saturn Award">Saturn Award</a> for Best Science Fiction Screenplay. <i>Get Shorty</i> was nominated for both a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Golden_Globe" title="Golden Globe">Golden Globe</a> and a Writers Guild Award for Best Adapted Screenplay and, as was <i>Dead Again</i>, was nominated for an Edgar Award for Best Mystery Screenplay.
</p><p>In January 2016, Frank debuted as a <a href="/wiki/Novelist" title="Novelist">novelist</a> with <i>Shaker</i>. The <a href="/wiki/Novel" title="Novel">novel</a> is a <a href="/wiki/Crime" title="Crime">crime</a> <a href="/wiki/Mystery_fiction" title="Mystery fiction">mystery</a> published by Penguin/Random House.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
