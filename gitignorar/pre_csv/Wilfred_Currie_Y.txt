	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Wilfred Currie</h1>

<div aria-label="Portals" class="noprint portal plainlist tright" role="navigation" style="margin:0.5em 0 0.5em 1em;border:solid #aaa 1px">
<ul style="display:table;box-sizing:border-box;padding:0.1em;max-width:175px;background:#f9f9f9;font-size:85%;line-height:110%;font-style:italic;font-weight:bold">
<li style="display:table-row"><span style="display:table-cell;padding:0.2em;vertical-align:middle;text-align:center"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:P_christianity.svg"><img alt="icon" class="noviewer" data-file-height="360" data-file-width="400" decoding="async" height="28" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/P_christianity.svg/31px-P_christianity.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/P_christianity.svg/47px-P_christianity.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/P_christianity.svg/62px-P_christianity.svg.png 2x" width="31"/></a></span><span style="display:table-cell;padding:0.2em 0.2em 0.2em 0.3em;vertical-align:middle"><a href="/wiki/Portal:Christianity" title="Portal:Christianity">Christianity portal</a></span></li></ul></div><p><b> Wilfred Bennetto Currie </b> was an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anglican" title="Anglican">Anglican</a> <a href="/wiki/Priest" title="Priest">priest</a> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> in the 20th century.
</p><p>Educated at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Edinburgh" title="University of Edinburgh">University of Edinburgh</a> and ordained in 1933, Currie was a curate at St John’s <a href="/wiki/Aberdeen" title="Aberdeen">Aberdeen</a> and then <a href="/wiki/Priest_in_charge" title="Priest in charge">Priest in charge</a> of St Mark’s in  <a href="/wiki/Aberdeen" title="Aberdeen">the same city</a>. He was a <a href="/wiki/Chaplain" title="Chaplain">Chaplain</a> to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_armed_forces" title="British armed forces">British armed forces</a> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> after which he held <a href="/wiki/Vicar" title="Vicar">incumbencies</a> at <a href="/wiki/Longside" title="Longside">Longside</a>  and <a href="/wiki/Stirling" title="Stirling">Stirling</a> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> before he became <a href="/wiki/St_Ninian%27s_Cathedral,_Perth" title="St Ninian's Cathedral, Perth">Provost of St Ninian’s Cathedral, Perth</a> in 1955, a post he held until 1969.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
