<p><b>Charles Henry Morgan</b> (July 5, 1842 – January 4, 1912) was a <a href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives" title="United States House of Representatives">United States Representative</a> for <a href="/wiki/Missouri" title="Missouri">Missouri</a>, 1875–1879, 1883–1885, 1893–1895, and 1909–1911.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Charles_Henry_Morgan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Morgan was born in <a href="/wiki/Cuba,_New_York" title="Cuba, New York">Cuba, New York</a>. His family moved to <a href="/wiki/Pewaukee,_Wisconsin" title="Pewaukee, Wisconsin">Pewaukee, Wisconsin</a> in 1845.  He attended school in <a href="/wiki/Fond_du_Lac,_Wisconsin" title="Fond du Lac, Wisconsin">Fond du Lac, Wisconsin</a>. During the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a> he enlisted in the <a href="/wiki/Union_Army" title="Union Army">Union Army</a> and rose from <a href="/wiki/Private_(rank)" title="Private (rank)">Private (rank)</a> to <a href="/wiki/Captain_(United_States)" title="Captain (United States)">captain</a> in the First Regiment and Twenty-first Regiment, Wisconsin Volunteer Infantry.
</p><p>He graduated from <a href="/wiki/Albany_Law_School" title="Albany Law School">Albany Law School</a> in 1865 and began <a href="/wiki/Practice_of_law" title="Practice of law">practicing law</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lamar,_Missouri" title="Lamar, Missouri">Lamar, Missouri</a>.  In 1868 he was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prosecuting_attorney" title="Prosecuting attorney">prosecuting attorney</a> of <a href="/wiki/Barton_County,_Missouri" title="Barton County, Missouri">Barton County, Missouri</a>; member of the <a href="/wiki/Missouri_House_of_Representatives" title="Missouri House of Representatives">Missouri House of Representatives</a> in 1872–74.
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish–American War">Spanish–American War</a> he was a <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant_colonel_(United_States)" title="Lieutenant colonel (United States)">lieutenant colonel</a> of the Fifth Missouri Volunteer Infantry.
</p><p>In 1907 he moved to <a href="/wiki/Joplin,_Missouri" title="Joplin, Missouri">Joplin, Missouri</a>.
</p><p>He served as a Democratic congressman in 1875–79, 1883–85 (during which he was chairman, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_House_Committee_on_Oversight_and_Government_Reform" title="United States House Committee on Oversight and Government Reform">Committee on Expenditures in the Post Office Department</a>) and 1893–95.  He was elected as a Republican in 1908 and served 1909–1911.
</p><p>He died in <a href="/wiki/Joplin,_Missouri" title="Joplin, Missouri">Joplin, Missouri</a> and is interred in Mount Hope Cemetery.
</p>
