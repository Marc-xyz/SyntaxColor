<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma_state_budget" title="Oklahoma state budget">Oklahoma state budget</a></div>
<p>The <b>Oklahoma State Budget for Fiscal Year 2004</b> was the <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma_state_budget" title="Oklahoma state budget">spending request</a> by <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Oklahoma" title="Governor of Oklahoma">Governor</a> <a href="/wiki/Brad_Henry" title="Brad Henry">Brad Henry</a> to fund <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Oklahoma" title="Government of Oklahoma">government operations</a> for <a href="/wiki/Fiscal_year" title="Fiscal year">July 1, 2003–June 30, 2004</a>. Governor Henry and legislative leaders approved the budget in May 2003. This was Henry's first budget submitted as governor.
</p><p>Figures shown in the spending request do not reflect the actual appropriations for fiscal year 2004, which must be authorized by the <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma_Legislature" title="Oklahoma Legislature">Legislature</a>. 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2004_Oklahoma_state_budget&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fiscal year 2003 saw a $593 million revenue shortfall. For FY2004, as in most budget years, the top eleven appropriated agencies made up 90% of the total budget. For FY2004, the governor and legislative leaders imposed a 6.3% across-the-board cut to state appropriations from 2003 levels. Education, healthcare, human services and public safety agencies saw only minor cuts or even slight increases. More dramatic budget cuts (ranging from 10% to 20%) were made to the agencies in the remaining 10% of the state budget.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Total_revenue">Total revenue</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2004_Oklahoma_state_budget&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Total revenue">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>All revenue of the fiscal year 2004 was $5.16 billion, down 8.5% from FY2003. The breakdown is as follows:
</p>
<ul><li>$4.935 billion - All Taxes
<ul><li>$1.99 billion - Individual Income Tax</li>
<li>$1.24 billion - Sales Tax</li>
<li>$359 million - Gross Production Tax</li>
<li>$214 million - Motor Vehicle Tax</li>
<li>$87.3 million - Corporate Income Tax</li>
<li>$1.05 billion - All Other Taxes</li></ul></li>
<li>$72 million - Rainy Day Fund</li>
<li>$22 million - Other Sources</li>
<li>$131 million - Revenue Adjustment</li></ul>
