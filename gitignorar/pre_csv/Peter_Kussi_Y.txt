		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Peter Kussi</b> (died 2012) was a <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech</a> scholar and translator.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Born in <a href="/wiki/Prague" title="Prague">Prague</a>, he taught <a href="/wiki/Czech_language" title="Czech language">Czech language</a> and <a href="/wiki/Czech_literature" title="Czech literature">Czech literature</a> at <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University" title="Columbia University">Columbia University</a> for many years. He is best known for his translation of the works of <a href="/wiki/Milan_Kundera" title="Milan Kundera">Milan Kundera</a>, among them the novel <i>Immortality</i> which won the <a href="/wiki/Independent_Foreign_Fiction_Prize" title="Independent Foreign Fiction Prize">Independent Foreign Fiction Prize</a>. He also edited an anthology of the works of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Karel_Capek" title="Karel Capek">Karel Capek</a>. 
</p>
