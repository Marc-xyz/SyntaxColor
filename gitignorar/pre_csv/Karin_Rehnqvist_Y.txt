<p><b>Karin Rehnqvist</b> (born 21 August 1957) is a <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Swedish</a> composer and conductor of classical music. She composes chamber music, orchestral works, music for the stage, and particularly vocal music, incorporating elements of folk music such as the vocal technique of <a href="/wiki/Kulning" title="Kulning">Kulning</a>. In 2009 she was appointed the first female professor of composition at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_College_of_Music,_Stockholm" title="Royal College of Music, Stockholm">Royal College of Music</a> in Stockholm.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Karin_Rehnqvist&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rehnqvist was born in Stockholm and grew up in <a href="/wiki/Nybro" title="Nybro">Nybro</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mugi_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-mugi-1">[1]</a></sup> She studied music pedagogy at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_College_of_Music,_Stockholm" title="Royal College of Music, Stockholm">Royal College of Music</a> in Stockholm from 1976 to 1980, and continued to study composition to 1984, with <a href="/wiki/Gunnar_Bucht" title="Gunnar Bucht">Gunnar Bucht</a>, <a href="/wiki/P%C3%A4r_Lindgren" title="Pär Lindgren">Pär Lindgren</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brian_Ferneyhough" title="Brian Ferneyhough">Brian Ferneyhough</a>, among others.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Reimers_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Reimers-2">[2]</a></sup> Between 1976 and 1991 she was the artistic director and conductor of the choir <i>Stans Kör</i>. From 2000 to 2003 she was Composer in Residence with the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Chamber_Orchestra" title="Scottish Chamber Orchestra">Scottish Chamber Orchestra</a> and the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Svenska_Kammarorkestern" title="Svenska Kammarorkestern">Svenska Kammarorkestern</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Reimers_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Reimers-2">[2]</a></sup> For them she composed a series of works including a concerto for clarinettist <a href="/wiki/Martin_Fr%C3%B6st" title="Martin Fröst">Martin Fröst</a>, and the symphonic work <i>Arktis Arktis!</i>, inspired by a polar expedition in the summer of 1999. These two works were recorded in May 2005. Her choral symphony <i>Light of Light</i>, which features children's choir and orchestra, was premiered in Paris in 2004.
</p><p>In 2006, a retrospective of her work was presented by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Stockholm_Philharmonic_Orchestra" title="Royal Stockholm Philharmonic Orchestra">Royal Stockholm Philharmonic Orchestra</a>, conducted by <a href="/wiki/Niklas_Will%C3%A9n" title="Niklas Willén">Niklas Willén</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mugi_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-mugi-1">[1]</a></sup> In 2009, Rehnqvist was appointed the first female professor of composition at the Royal College of Music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Columbia_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Columbia-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Composition">Composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Karin_Rehnqvist&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Several of Rehnqvist's works were composed for the voices and interpretation of <a href="/wiki/Lena_Willemark" title="Lena Willemark">Lena Willemark</a> and <a href="/wiki/Susanne_Rosenberg" title="Susanne Rosenberg">Susanne Rosenberg</a>, both having a past as folk singers, such as <i>Davids nimm</i> and <i>Puksånger-lockrop</i> (1989). Rehnqvist includes <a href="/wiki/Kulning" title="Kulning">kulning</a> (cattle calling) in her works, a high-pitched vocal technique used by <a href="/wiki/Scandinavia" title="Scandinavia">Scandinavia</a> shepherds to communicate over long distances, calling livestock down from high mountain and possibly scaring away <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Predator" title="Predator">predators</a>. <i>Puksånger &amp; lockrop</i> (Timpanum Songs – Herding Calls) was her second major vocal composition, set for two singers and percussion, in which kulning "begins the piece and sets the atmosphere for the entire work", followed by a section based on "condescending traditional Finnish proverbs" about women, described as "highly effective satiric attacks on misogyny. This section is followed immediately by a kulning section, which represents a rebellion against the previous ideas".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WhiteChristensen2002_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-WhiteChristensen2002-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Rehnqvist composed <i>Solsången</i> (Sun Song) in 1994, scored for chamber orchestra with one female solo singer and two narrators. The text is partly based on the <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Icelandic</a> <i><a href="/wiki/S%C3%B3larlj%C3%B3%C3%B0" title="Sólarljóð">Sólarljóð</a></i> (The Song of the Sun), an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Old_Norse_poem" title="Old Norse poem">Old Norse poem</a> written c. 1200 in the traditional metric style of the <i><a href="/wiki/Poetic_Edda" title="Poetic Edda">Poetic Edda</a></i>, but with content from Christian visionary poems.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Karin_Rehnqvist&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1996 Rehnqvist received the Läkerol Arts Award “for her renewal of the relationship between folk music and art music” and the Spelmannen Prize by the newspaper <i>Expressen</i>. In 1997 she was awarded the Christ Johnson Prize for <i>Solsången</i> (Sun Song).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Reimers_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Reimers-2">[2]</a></sup> In 2001 she received the Kurt Atterberg Prize and in 2005/06 the Rosenberg Award. In March 2006 Rehnqvist was awarded the honour of a major retrospective by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Stockholm_Philharmonic_Orchestra" title="Royal Stockholm Philharmonic Orchestra">Royal Stockholm Philharmonic Orchestra</a>. In 2007 Rehnqvist received the Hugo Alfvén Prize.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sveriges_Radio_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sveriges_Radio-5">[5]</a></sup> In 2014 she was awarded a <a href="/wiki/Grammis" title="Grammis">Grammis</a> in the category Classic for the CD <i>Live</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grammis_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Grammis-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
