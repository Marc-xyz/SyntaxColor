<p><b>Preguiça</b> is a settlement in the central part of the island of <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Nicolau,_Cape_Verde" title="São Nicolau, Cape Verde">São Nicolau</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde" title="Cape Verde">Cape Verde</a>. It is situated on the south coast, 6 km south of <a href="/wiki/Ribeira_Brava,_Cape_Verde" title="Ribeira Brava, Cape Verde">Ribeira Brava</a>. It served as the port of Ribeira Brava, after the older <i>Porto de Lapa</i>, 6 km to the northeast, had been abandoned in 1653 due to pirate attacks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> 
The settlement was mentioned as <i>Paraghisi</i> in the 1747 map by <a href="/wiki/Jacques-Nicolas_Bellin" title="Jacques-Nicolas Bellin">Jacques-Nicolas Bellin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In 1820 the <a href="/wiki/Forte_do_Pr%C3%ADncipe_Real" title="Forte do Príncipe Real">Forte do Príncipe Real</a>, now ruined, was built to protect the port.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Preguiça's port consists of a stone quay and a short <a href="/wiki/Mole_(architecture)" title="Mole (architecture)">mole</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pregui%C3%A7a_Airport" title="Preguiça Airport">Preguiça Airport</a> is located 3 km north of the village.
</p>
