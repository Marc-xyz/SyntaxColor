<p><b>Brian Medavoy</b> (born June 30, 1965) is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Talent_manager" title="Talent manager">talent manager</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Entrepreneur" title="Entrepreneur">entrepreneur</a> and <a href="/wiki/Television_producer" title="Television producer">television producer</a>.  He is the son of film producer and executive, <a href="/wiki/Mike_Medavoy" title="Mike Medavoy">Mike Medavoy</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brian_Medavoy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Medavoy began his career in 1985 in the mail room at the <a href="/wiki/Creative_Artists_Agency" title="Creative Artists Agency">Creative Artists Agency</a>.  He then moved to <a href="/wiki/ICM_Partners" title="ICM Partners">ICM Partners</a>' mail room and eventually became the assistant to acclaimed talent agent <a href="/wiki/Ed_Limato" title="Ed Limato">Ed Limato</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1990, he co-founded More Medavoy Management with his partner Erwin More.  The company grew quickly, managing <a href="/wiki/Bill_Bellamy" title="Bill Bellamy">Bill Bellamy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jason_Biggs" title="Jason Biggs">Jason Biggs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bon_Jovi" title="Bon Jovi">Bon Jovi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mariah_Carey" title="Mariah Carey">Mariah Carey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Morris_Chestnut" title="Morris Chestnut">Morris Chestnut</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jenna_Elfman" title="Jenna Elfman">Jenna Elfman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Fallon" title="Jimmy Fallon">Jimmy Fallon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Melissa_Gilbert" title="Melissa Gilbert">Melissa Gilbert</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mariska_Hargitay" title="Mariska Hargitay">Mariska Hargitay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lucy_Lawless" title="Lucy Lawless">Lucy Lawless</a>, <a href="/wiki/Howie_Long" title="Howie Long">Howie Long</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marlee_Matlin" title="Marlee Matlin">Marlee Matlin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jenny_McCarthy" title="Jenny McCarthy">Jenny McCarthy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Swayze" title="Patrick Swayze">Patrick Swayze</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cicely_Tyson" title="Cicely Tyson">Cicely Tyson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maria_Bello" title="Maria Bello">Maria Bello</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kristen_Bell" title="Kristen Bell">Kristen Bell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jason_Bateman" title="Jason Bateman">Jason Bateman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Josh_Brolin" title="Josh Brolin">Josh Brolin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Reynolds" title="Ryan Reynolds">Ryan Reynolds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tobey_Maguire" title="Tobey Maguire">Tobey Maguire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Berg" title="Peter Berg">Peter Berg</a> among others during operation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>From 1994 until 2002, Brian Medavoy received producer credits on a slew of television movies and series including <a href="/wiki/Sweet_Justice" title="Sweet Justice">Sweet Justice</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Single_Guy" title="The Single Guy">The Single Guy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Since_You%27ve_Been_Gone_(film)" title="Since You've Been Gone (film)">Since You've Been Gone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Getting_Personal" title="Getting Personal">Getting Personal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ringmaster_(film)" title="Ringmaster (film)">Ringmaster</a>, <a href="/wiki/American_High_(TV_series)" title="American High (TV series)">American High</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dharma_%26_Greg" title="Dharma &amp; Greg">Dharma &amp; Greg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Just_Shoot_Me!" title="Just Shoot Me!">Just Shoot Me!</a>.  In 1999, More Medavoy Management merged with Susan Bymell and Evelyn O'Neill becoming the Talent Entertainment Group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>  It was while working at Talent Entertainment Group that Brian Medavoy received an Emmy for his work on <a href="/wiki/American_High_(TV_series)" title="American High (TV series)">American High</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>After a hiatus, Medavoy and Erwin More joined forces again in 2014 with a client roster that included <a href="/wiki/Paula_Patton" title="Paula Patton">Paula Patton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Neal_McDonough" title="Neal McDonough">Neal McDonough</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bella_Heathcote" title="Bella Heathcote">Bella Heathcote</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kristoffer_Polaha" title="Kristoffer Polaha">Kristoffer Polaha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reno_Wilson" title="Reno Wilson">Reno Wilson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Beach" title="Michael Beach">Michael Beach</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ben_Koldyke" title="Ben Koldyke">Ben Koldyke</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eric_Balfour" title="Eric Balfour">Eric Balfour</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
