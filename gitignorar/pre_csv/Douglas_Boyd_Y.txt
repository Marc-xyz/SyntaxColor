	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Douglas Boyd</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">For the oral historian and folklorist, see <a href="/wiki/Douglas_A._Boyd" title="Douglas A. Boyd">Douglas A. Boyd</a>.</div>
<p>

<b>Douglas Boyd</b> (born 1959, <a href="/wiki/Glasgow" title="Glasgow">Glasgow</a>, Scotland) is a British <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oboist" title="Oboist">oboist</a> and conductor.  He studied oboe at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Academy_of_Music" title="Royal Academy of Music">Royal Academy of Music</a>, London, as a pupil of <a href="/wiki/Janet_Craxton" title="Janet Craxton">Janet Craxton</a>. He later was a student with <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Bourgue" title="Maurice Bourgue">Maurice Bourgue</a> in Paris. In 1984 he won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Young_Concert_Artists_International_Auditions" title="Young Concert Artists International Auditions">Young Concert Artists International Auditions</a>, which led to his New York City recital debut at <a href="/wiki/Carnegie_Hall" title="Carnegie Hall">Carnegie Hall</a>.
</p><p>Boyd was one of the founding members of the <a href="/wiki/Chamber_Orchestra_of_Europe" title="Chamber Orchestra of Europe">Chamber Orchestra of Europe</a> (COE), and served as its principal oboist from 1981 to 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> During his time with the COE, he developed an interest in conducting, and counted as his first conducting mentors <a href="/wiki/Claudio_Abbado" title="Claudio Abbado">Claudio Abbado</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nikolaus_Harnoncourt" title="Nikolaus Harnoncourt">Nikolaus Harnoncourt</a>.  He also had guidance from <a href="/wiki/Paavo_Berglund" title="Paavo Berglund">Paavo Berglund</a> and Sir <a href="/wiki/Colin_Davis" title="Colin Davis">Colin Davis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shulgold_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Shulgold-2">[2]</a></sup> In 2006, Boyd ceased performing on the oboe to focus full-time on his conducting career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2001, Boyd became music director of the <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Camerata" title="Manchester Camerata">Manchester Camerata</a>, his first major conducting post.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He conducted several recordings with the Manchester Camerata for the Avie label, including music of Beethoven, Mozart<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and Mahler.  Boyd concluded his Manchester tenure after the 2010–2011 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Boyd has also served as principal guest conductor of the <a href="/wiki/City_of_London_Sinfonia" title="City of London Sinfonia">City of London Sinfonia</a>.  In November 2012, Boyd was named artistic director of <a href="/wiki/Garsington_Opera" title="Garsington Opera">Garsington Opera</a>, with immediate effect.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>Outside of the UK, in April 2008, the <a href="/wiki/Orchester_Musikkollegium_Winterthur" title="Orchester Musikkollegium Winterthur">Orchester Musikkollegium Winterthur</a> announced the appointment of Boyd as its principal conductor, as of the 2009–2010 season.  Boyd has since extended his Winterthur contract through the 2015–2016 season,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and concluded his Winterthur tenure after the 2015–2016 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Boyd has conducted the Winterthur orchestra in commercial recordings of music of <a href="/wiki/Wolfgang_Amadeus_Mozart" title="Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart">Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Felix_Mendelssohn" title="Felix Mendelssohn">Felix Mendelssohn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Josef_Rheinberger" title="Josef Rheinberger">Josef Rheinberger</a>.  On 1 July 2015, Boyd became the new music director of the <a href="/wiki/Orchestre_de_chambre_de_Paris" title="Orchestre de chambre de Paris">Orchestre de chambre de Paris</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>In the US, Boyd's first conducting appearance was with the Gardner Chamber Orchestra in 2000.  In 2002, Boyd became co-artistic director of the orchestra, along with <a href="/wiki/Paula_Robison" title="Paula Robison">Paula Robison</a>.  In 2004, he became one of the first Artistic Partners with the <a href="/wiki/Saint_Paul_Chamber_Orchestra" title="Saint Paul Chamber Orchestra">Saint Paul Chamber Orchestra</a>.  In 2006, he extended his contract with the SPCO into the 2009–2010 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> He concluded his SPCO Artistic Partnership in September 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> He first conducted the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Colorado_Symphony_Orchestra" title="Colorado Symphony Orchestra">Colorado Symphony Orchestra</a> in December 2006,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> and was named their principal guest conductor in January 2008, for a contract of 3 years with 2 weeks of guest appearances per season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shulgold_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Shulgold-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Boyd and his wife Sally Pendlebury, a cellist,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> have three children, Iona, Sebastian and Sam.  The family reside in London.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shulgold_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Shulgold-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
