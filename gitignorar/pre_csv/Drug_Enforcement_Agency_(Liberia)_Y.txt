	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Drug Enforcement Agency (Liberia)</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Not to be confused with <a href="/wiki/Drug_Enforcement_Administration" title="Drug Enforcement Administration">Drug Enforcement Administration</a>.</div>
<p>The <b>Drug Enforcement Agency</b> (<b>DEA</b>) is an <a href="/wiki/Government_agency" title="Government agency">agency</a> within the <a href="/wiki/Liberia" title="Liberia">Liberian</a> government charged with fighting <a href="/wiki/Drug-related_crime" title="Drug-related crime">drug-related crimes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-million_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-million-1">[1]</a></sup> Before its creation, fighting drug crimes was a responsibility of the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_National_Defense_(Liberia)" title="Ministry of National Defense (Liberia)">Ministry of Defense</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>  The agency began as the National Drug Committee of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Interim_Government_of_National_Unity&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Interim Government of National Unity (page does not exist)">Interim Government of National Unity</a>; it was created in 1993 during the presidency of <a href="/wiki/Amos_Sawyer" title="Amos Sawyer">Amos Sawyer</a>. Five years later, the committee was converted into its present form: President <a href="/wiki/Charles_Taylor_(Liberian_politician)" title="Charles Taylor (Liberian politician)">Taylor</a> signed a bill passed by the <a href="/wiki/Legislature_of_Liberia" title="Legislature of Liberia">National Legislature</a> that created the DEA and patterned it after the <a href="/wiki/Drug_Enforcement_Administration" title="Drug Enforcement Administration">Drug Enforcement Administration</a> in the United States. The DEA is charged with fighting drug trafficking at the country's borders,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-million_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-million-1">[1]</a></sup> arresting <a href="/wiki/Illegal_drug_trade" title="Illegal drug trade">traffickers and dealers</a>, and destroying illegal drugs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-destroys_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-destroys-3">[3]</a></sup> According to DEA boss Anthony Souh, the agency nevertheless suffers from substantial internal <a href="/wiki/Political_corruption" title="Political corruption">corruption</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-heritage_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-heritage-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2011, the DEA boss was Director Henry Shaw,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-destroys_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-destroys-3">[3]</a></sup> but by 2012 he had been replaced by Anthony Souh.  Directors are appointed by the <a href="/wiki/President_of_Liberia" title="President of Liberia">President</a> subject to <a href="/wiki/Advice_and_consent" title="Advice and consent">confirmation</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Senate_of_Liberia" title="Senate of Liberia">Senate of Liberia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-heritage_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-heritage-4">[4]</a></sup> The DEA is not responsible for overseeing <a href="/wiki/Legal_drug_trade" title="Legal drug trade">commerce in legal drugs</a> and other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pharmaceutical_drug" title="Pharmaceutical drug">pharmaceuticals</a>; such substances are within the purview of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pharmaceutical_Board_of_Liberia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Pharmaceutical Board of Liberia (page does not exist)">Pharmaceutical Board of Liberia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
