<p><b>Guzhen County</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">simplified Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans">固镇县</span>; <a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">traditional Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant">固鎮縣</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Gùzhèn Xiàn</span></i>) is a county in the north of <a href="/wiki/Anhui" title="Anhui">Anhui Province</a>, China. It is under the administration of <a href="/wiki/Bengbu" title="Bengbu">Bengbu</a> city.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guzhen_County&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>A 4500 year old city in what is called as the Nanchengzi Ruins is found in this county.</li>
<li>At the end of the Qin Dynasty (202 BC), the battle of Chu and Han happened in Guzhen，leaving the "besieged on all sides", "Farewell My Concubine" and other stories.</li>
<li>During Taihe Period, the Northern Wei Dynasty, establish a town called Guyang, then evolve Guzhen County.</li>
<li>October 31, 1964, each part of Xuxian, Lingbi, Huaiyuan, Wuhe formed the Administrative Region Establishment of Guzhen County. July 1, 1965, Guzhen county was  under the administration of Xuxian area; 1983 is Bengbu City.</li></ul>
