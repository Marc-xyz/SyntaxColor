<p>The <b>first season</b> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Philippine_television" title="Philippine television">Philippine television</a> reality competition show, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Clash_(TV_series)" title="The Clash (TV series)">The Clash</a></i> was broadcast by <a href="/wiki/GMA_Network" title="GMA Network">GMA Network</a>. Hosted by <a href="/wiki/Regine_Velasquez" title="Regine Velasquez">Regine Velasquez</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Andre_Paras" title="Andre Paras">Andre Paras</a> and <a href="/wiki/Joyce_Pring" title="Joyce Pring">Joyce Pring</a>, it premiered on July 7, 2018 on the network's Sabado Star Power sa Gabi and Sunday Grande sa Gabi line up replacing <i><a href="/wiki/Celebrity_Bluff" title="Celebrity Bluff">Celebrity Bluff</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Lip_Sync_Battle_Philippines" title="Lip Sync Battle Philippines">Lip Sync Battle Philippines</a></i> in their respective timeslot. The judges composed of <a href="/wiki/Ai-Ai_delas_Alas" title="Ai-Ai delas Alas">Ai-Ai delas Alas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lani_Misalucha" title="Lani Misalucha">Lani Misalucha</a> and <a href="/wiki/Christian_Bautista" title="Christian Bautista">Christian Bautista</a>. The season concluded on September 30, 2018 with a total of 26 episodes. <a href="/wiki/Golden_Ca%C3%B1edo" title="Golden Cañedo">Golden Cañedo</a> was declared as the winner. It was replaced by <i><a href="/wiki/Daddy%27s_Gurl" title="Daddy's Gurl">Daddy's Gurl</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Studio_7_(TV_program)" title="Studio 7 (TV program)">Studio 7</a></i> in its timeslot.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Top_62">Top 62</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Clash_(season_1)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Top 62">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The top 62 clashers were selected by respected personalities from the music industry including songwriter Vehnee Saturno; songwriter, music producer, and South Border lead singer <a href="/wiki/Jay_Durias" title="Jay Durias">Jay Durias</a>; and TV director Bert de Leon.
</p><p>The 62 clashers (competitors) were electronically paired to battle it out in a singing duel with the winner advancing to the next round. The losers were temporarily eliminated in the first round until two of them were electronically selected to return for a breakout as wild card contender in the single seat remaining out of the 32 seats that qualify for round two.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
