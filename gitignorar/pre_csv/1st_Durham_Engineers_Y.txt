<p>The <b>1st Durham Engineers</b>, later <b>Durham Fortress Engineers</b>, was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Volunteer_Force_(Great_Britain)" title="Volunteer Force (Great Britain)">Volunteer</a> unit of the <a href="/wiki/British_Army" title="British Army">British Army</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Royal_Engineers" title="Royal Engineers">Royal Engineers</a>. First founded in 1868 it was sometimes united with the <a href="/wiki/Tyne_Electrical_Engineers" title="Tyne Electrical Engineers">Tyne Electrical Engineers</a>, at other times it formed an independent unit. Although its main role was defence of the North East Coast of England, the unit sent detachments on active service to the <a href="/wiki/Suakin_Expedition" title="Suakin Expedition">Suakin Expedition</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Second_Boer_War" title="Second Boer War">Second Boer War</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Western_Front_(World_War_I)" title="Western Front (World War I)">Western Front</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_Front_(World_War_I)" title="Italian Front (World War I)">Italy</a> during the <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">First World War</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Volunteer_Force">Volunteer Force</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1st_Durham_Engineers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Volunteer Force">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1868 a new unit of Engineer Volunteers (EV) was formed at <a href="/wiki/Jarrow" title="Jarrow">Jarrow</a> on the <a href="/wiki/County_Durham" title="County Durham">County Durham</a> bank of the <a href="/wiki/River_Tyne" title="River Tyne">River Tyne</a>. The <b>1st Durham EV</b> was raised and commanded by <a href="/wiki/Sir_Charles_Palmer,_1st_Baronet" title="Sir Charles Palmer, 1st Baronet">Charles Palmer</a> (1822–1907), founder of <a href="/wiki/Palmers_Shipbuilding_and_Iron_Company" title="Palmers Shipbuilding and Iron Company">Palmers Shipbuilding and Iron Company</a> and later the first mayor and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a> for Jarrow.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The 1st Durham initially comprised six companies and Palmer was commissioned as <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant_colonel_(United_Kingdom)" title="Lieutenant colonel (United Kingdom)">Lieutenant-Colonel</a> Commandant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-List68_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-List68-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Westlake_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Westlake-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DurhamFort_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-DurhamFort-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>By 1874 the 1st Durham EV (now 8 companies strong) and the 1st Newcastle EV (1 company) were united into the 1st Durham Administrative Battalion EV, consolidated in 1880 as the <b>1st Newcastle upon Tyne and Durham EV</b>, with Palmer as commanding officer and an establishment of 1300 men.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-List68_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-List68-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Westlake_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Westlake-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DurhamFort_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-DurhamFort-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1885, the Inspector-General of Fortifications, <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Clarke_(British_Army_officer)" title="Andrew Clarke (British Army officer)">Lieutenant-General Sir Andrew Clarke</a>, sent a detachment of Volunteers to the <a href="/wiki/Red_Sea" title="Red Sea">Red Sea</a> port of <a href="/wiki/Suakin_Expedition" title="Suakin Expedition">Suakin</a> to assist the Regular <a href="/wiki/Royal_Engineers" title="Royal Engineers">Royal Engineers</a> (RE) in railway construction for the British force engaged there. The detachment of 40 men was drawn from the 1st Newcastle &amp; Durham EV and the 1st Lancashire EV.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>Palmer, by now created Sir Charles Palmer, 1st Baronet of Grinkle Park, retired from the unit in 1888 with the rank of Colonel. The same year, the 1st Newcastle &amp; Durham was split into three separate units: the <b>1st Durham RE (Volunteers)</b>, at Jarrow,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Westlake_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Westlake-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DurhamFort_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-DurhamFort-5">[5]</a></sup> and the <b><a href="/wiki/Tyne_Electrical_Engineers" title="Tyne Electrical Engineers">Tyne Division RE (V), Submarine Miners</a></b> at <a href="/wiki/North_Shields" title="North Shields">North Shields</a>, with Palmer as Honorary Commandant of both units,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Regts_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Regts-11">[11]</a></sup> together with a new <b><a href="/wiki/1st_Newcastle_Engineers" title="1st Newcastle Engineers">1st Newcastle-on-Tyne RE (V)</a></b> at <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_upon_Tyne" title="Newcastle upon Tyne">Newcastle</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p><p>The 1st Durham RE (V) sent a detachment of one officer and 25 other ranks to assist the regular REs during the <a href="/wiki/Second_Boer_War" title="Second Boer War">Second Boer War</a> in 1900, and a second section the following year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Forum_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Forum-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jarrow_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Jarrow-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Territorial_Force">Territorial Force</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1st_Durham_Engineers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Territorial Force">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>When the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Volunteer_Force_(Great_Britain)" title="Volunteer Force (Great Britain)">Volunteers</a> were subsumed into the new <a href="/wiki/Territorial_Force" title="Territorial Force">Territorial Force</a> (TF) under the <a href="/wiki/Haldane_Reforms" title="Haldane Reforms">Haldane Reforms</a> of 1908, the original plan was for the 1st Durham RE to provide the 1st Northumbrian Field Company and Divisional Signal Company in the TF's <a href="/wiki/50th_(Northumbrian)_Division" title="50th (Northumbrian) Division">Northumbrian Division</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> However, by 1910 this plan had been altered, and instead the 1st Durham was redesignated the <b>Durham Fortress Engineers</b>, with the addition of an Electric Light Company provided by the <a href="/wiki/Tyne_Electrical_Engineers" title="Tyne Electrical Engineers">Tyne Electrical Engineers</a> (formerly Submarine Miners).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Westlake_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-Westlake-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DurhamFort_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-DurhamFort-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>
</p><p>The organisation of the unit on the eve of the First World War was as follows:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DurhamFort_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-DurhamFort-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Trail_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Trail-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup>
</p><p><b>Durham Fortress Engineers, RE</b>
</p>
<ul><li>HQ at Western Road, Jarrow</li>
<li>No 1 Works Company at Jarrow</li>
<li>No 2 Works Company at Jarrow</li>
<li>No 3 Works Company at <a href="/wiki/Gateshead" title="Gateshead">Gateshead</a></li></ul>
<p>The unit formed part of North Eastern Coastal Defences.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="First_World_War">First World War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1st_Durham_Engineers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: First World War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Territorial_shoulder_title_of_the_Durham_engineers.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2089" data-file-width="2459" decoding="async" height="187" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Territorial_shoulder_title_of_the_Durham_engineers.jpg/220px-Territorial_shoulder_title_of_the_Durham_engineers.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Territorial_shoulder_title_of_the_Durham_engineers.jpg/330px-Territorial_shoulder_title_of_the_Durham_engineers.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Territorial_shoulder_title_of_the_Durham_engineers.jpg/440px-Territorial_shoulder_title_of_the_Durham_engineers.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Territorial_shoulder_title_of_the_Durham_engineers.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Shoulder title of Durham Territorial Engineers</div></div></div>
<p>When the TF was mobilised on the outbreak of war in August 1914, the Durham Fortress Engineers took up their places in the North Eastern Coastal Defences. At first they were employed on constructing field defences, erecting hutted camps, <i>etc</i>. By the summer of 1915 there were three strong lines of entrenchments, bomb-proof shelters and dug-outs linking strongpoints and gun positions along the coast as well as the fixed defences at Tynemouth and Hartlepool. In August 1916 the Durham Fortress Engineers overcame shifting sand dunes to construct Link House Battery to defend <a href="/wiki/Blyth,_Northumberland" title="Blyth, Northumberland">Blyth Harbour</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup>
</p><p>Although the coastal towns of NE England were bombarded by the German Navy on 16 December 1914 (<a href="/wiki/Raid_on_Scarborough,_Hartlepool_and_Whitby" title="Raid on Scarborough, Hartlepool and Whitby">Raid on Scarborough, Hartlepool and Whitby</a>) and by <a href="/wiki/Zeppelin" title="Zeppelin">Zeppelins</a> in January and June 1915, it became clear that a fullscale German invasion of Britain was unlikely, while the armies in the field required large numbers of engineers. The Fortress Engineer units therefore began organising field companies for overseas service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-W&amp;R62_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-W&amp;R62-23">[23]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Durham Fortress Engineers found three field companies, while the remainder of the unit continued to be employed around the Tyne Garrison. These companies were numbered 1st to 3rd (or formally 1/1st to 1/3rd, since they were composed of '1st-Line' Territorials who had volunteered for overseas service) and went to France in September 1915.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Trail_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-Trail-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-W&amp;R62_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-W&amp;R62-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Forum1_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-Forum1-24">[24]</a></sup> A 530th (Durham) Reserve Field Company was also formed, from 2nd or even 3rd Line Territorials. This did not proceed overseas, and was probably quickly absorbed into the central training organisation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1st_Durham_Field_Company">1st Durham Field Company</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1st_Durham_Engineers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1st Durham Field Company">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>1st Durham Field Company joined <a href="/wiki/4th_Infantry_Division_(United_Kingdom)" title="4th Infantry Division (United Kingdom)">4th Division</a> in France on 20 September 1915 and served with it on the <a href="/wiki/Western_Front_(World_War_I)" title="Western Front (World War I)">Western Front</a> to the end of the war. It was renumbered 526th (Durham) Field Company, RE, in February 1917.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Trail_20-2"><a href="#cite_note-Trail-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-W&amp;R62_23-2"><a href="#cite_note-W&amp;R62-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Forum1_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-Forum1-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2nd_Durham_Field_Company">2nd  Durham Field Company</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1st_Durham_Engineers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2nd Durham Field Company">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>2nd Durham Field Company joined <a href="/wiki/5th_Infantry_Division_(United_Kingdom)" title="5th Infantry Division (United Kingdom)">5th Division</a> in France on 20 September 1915 and served with it on the Western Front until November 1917, when the division was transferred to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_Front_(World_War_I)" title="Italian Front (World War I)">Italian Front</a>. 5th Division returned to the Western Front in April 1918 and fought there until the end of the war. It was renumbered 527th (Durham) Field Company, RE, in February 1917.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Forum_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-Forum-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Trail_20-3"><a href="#cite_note-Trail-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-W&amp;R62_23-3"><a href="#cite_note-W&amp;R62-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="3rd_Durham_Field_Company">3rd Durham Field Company</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1st_Durham_Engineers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 3rd Durham Field Company">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>3rd Durham Field Company landed at Le Havre on 18 September 1915 and joined <a href="/wiki/51st_(Highland)_Division" title="51st (Highland) Division">51st (Highland) Division</a> on 19 September, during the latter part of the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Somme" title="Battle of the Somme">Battle of the Somme</a>. On 30 January 1916 it transferred to <a href="/wiki/7th_Infantry_Division_(United_Kingdom)" title="7th Infantry Division (United Kingdom)">7th Division</a> (in exchange for a Highland Field Company) and served with it on the Western Front until November 1917 when the division was transferred to the Italian front. 7th Division then served in Italy until the end of the war. It was renumbered 528th (Durham) Field Company, RE, in February 1917.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Trail_20-4"><a href="#cite_note-Trail-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-W&amp;R62_23-4"><a href="#cite_note-W&amp;R62-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Inter-War_period">Inter-War period</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1st_Durham_Engineers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Inter-War period">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>When the TF was reconstituted (as the Territorial Army (TA)) in 1920, the Durham Fortress Engineers was not reformed, but was absorbed into the <a href="/wiki/Tyne_Electrical_Engineers" title="Tyne Electrical Engineers">Tyne Electrical Engineers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DurhamFort_5-5"><a href="#cite_note-DurhamFort-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-Second_World_War">Post-Second World War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1st_Durham_Engineers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Post-Second World War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>When the TA was reconstituted again in 1947, no less than seven units were reformed in the Tyne Electrical Engineers' lineage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Regts_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-Regts-11">[11]</a></sup> These included 132 Field Engineer Regiment, RE, which was considered to be the successor of the Durham Fortress Engineers, with the following organisation:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DurhamFort_5-6"><a href="#cite_note-DurhamFort-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45on_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-45on-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Watson_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-Watson-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-W&amp;R297_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-W&amp;R297-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-W&amp;R304_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-W&amp;R304-37">[37]</a></sup>
</p><p><b>132 Field Engineer Regiment, RE (TA)</b>
</p>
<ul><li>HQ at Sunderland</li>
<li>333 Field Squadron – <i>redesignated 608 Field Squadron in 1953</i></li>
<li>334 Field Squadron</li>
<li>335 Field Squadron – <i>transferred to 118 Construction Regiment, RE, in 1950</i></li>
<li>336 Field Park Squadron</li></ul>
<p>The unit formed part of 22 Engineer Group in North East England, which was composed of units derived from the Tyne Electrical Engineers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45on_34-1"><a href="#cite_note-45on-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Watson_35-1"><a href="#cite_note-Watson-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> 
</p><p>On 31 October the regiment was joined by a new field squadron formed by conversion of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/426th_(Durham)_Coast_Regiment,_Royal_Artillery" title="426th (Durham) Coast Regiment, Royal Artillery">426th (Durham) Coast Regiment, Royal Artillery</a>. It was numbered 336 Fd Sqn and absorbed the existing 336 Fd Park Sqn; it was redesignated 336 (Durham Coast) Crane Operating Squadron by 31 December 1956.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-W&amp;R304_37-1"><a href="#cite_note-W&amp;R304-37">[37]</a></sup> 
</p><p>The regiment was redesignated <b>132 Corps Engineer Regiment</b> in 1957, but was disbanded in 1961. 336 Crane Operating Sqn had left the regiment in 1957; in 1965 it transferred to the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Corps_of_Transport" title="Royal Corps of Transport">Royal Corps of Transport</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-W&amp;R304_37-2"><a href="#cite_note-W&amp;R304-37">[37]</a></sup> The regiment was disbanded in 1961, 608 Sqn disbanding and 334 Fd Sqn being absorbed into the Sunderland-based 233 Field Squadron of <a href="/wiki/Tyne_Electrical_Engineers" title="Tyne Electrical Engineers">105 Construction Regiment (Tyne Electrical Engineers)</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DurhamFort_5-7"><a href="#cite_note-DurhamFort-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Regts_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-Regts-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45on_34-2"><a href="#cite_note-45on-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-W&amp;R297_36-1"><a href="#cite_note-W&amp;R297-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Honorary_Colonel">Honorary Colonel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1st_Durham_Engineers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Honorary Colonel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Sir_Charles_Palmer,_1st_Baronet" title="Sir Charles Palmer, 1st Baronet">Sir Charles Palmer, Bt</a> appointed 17 November 1888, died 1907.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Memorials">Memorials</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1st_Durham_Engineers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Memorials">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A memorial plaque was set up in Jarrow Town Hall to honour the two service sections of the 1st Durham RE who went to South Africa during the 2nd Boer War.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Forum_14-2"><a href="#cite_note-Forum-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jarrow_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-Jarrow-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p><p>A war memorial to the men of the Durham Fortress Engineers and the three field companies who served in the First World War was unveiled in Jarrow Drill Hall in 1928. An additional inscription to <a href="/wiki/1st_Newcastle_Engineers" title="1st Newcastle Engineers">233 Field Company, RE</a> was added after the <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">Second World War</a>. The memorial is now in the RE TA Centre at Debdon Gardens, Gateshead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup>
</p>
