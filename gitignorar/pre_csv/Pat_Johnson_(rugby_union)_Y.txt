<p><b>Pat Johnson</b> (born 17 March 1960) is a former American <a href="/wiki/Rugby_union" title="Rugby union">rugby union</a> player.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He played at Hooker for the <a href="/wiki/United_States_national_rugby_union_team" title="United States national rugby union team">United States</a> at two <a href="/wiki/Rugby_World_Cup" title="Rugby World Cup">World Cups</a>, in <a href="/wiki/1987_Rugby_World_Cup" title="1987 Rugby World Cup">1987</a> and <a href="/wiki/1991_Rugby_World_Cup" title="1991 Rugby World Cup">1991</a>. He made his <a href="/wiki/Test_match_(rugby_union)" title="Test match (rugby union)">test</a> debut for the <a href="/wiki/United_States_national_rugby_union_team" title="United States national rugby union team">United States</a> against Canada in <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a> on 10 May 1987. His last test match was also against <a href="/wiki/Canada_national_rugby_union_team" title="Canada national rugby union team">Canada</a> in <a href="/wiki/Denver" title="Denver">Denver</a>, 13 June 1992.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability#Self-published_sources" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag may rely on a self-published source. (December 2017)">self-published source</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
