<p><b>Michael Urukalo</b>, born <b>Milorad Urukalo</b>, is a Serbian-born Australian <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football (soccer)</a> coach who has worked at several clubs in Africa, Asia and Australia.
</p><p>Urukalo coached in Malaysia, Singapore Pro League, Kuwait National League and Guam Football Federation. He was also the first coach to achieve the Malaysian <a href="/wiki/Double_(association_football)" title="Double (association football)">Double</a>, winning both the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Malaysian_Super_League" title="Malaysian Super League">Malaysian League</a> and <a href="/wiki/Malaysia_Cup" title="Malaysia Cup">Malaysia Cup</a> in 1991 with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Johor_FA" title="Johor FA">Johor</a>.
</p><p>He also had a brief stint with <a href="/wiki/Enyimba_International_F.C." title="Enyimba International F.C.">Enyimba</a> of Nigeria, with whom he won the 2004 <a href="/wiki/CAF_Super_Cup" title="CAF Super Cup">African Super Cup</a>.
</p>
