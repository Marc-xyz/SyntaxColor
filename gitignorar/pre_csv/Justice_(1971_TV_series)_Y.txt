<p><i><b>Justice</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Television_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Television in the United Kingdom">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Drama" title="Drama">drama</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_series" title="Television series">television series</a> that originally aired on <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> in 39 hour-long episodes between 8 August 1971 and 16 October 1974.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Margaret_Lockwood" title="Margaret Lockwood">Margaret Lockwood</a> stars as Harriet Peterson, a female <a href="/wiki/Barrister" title="Barrister">barrister</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_of_England" title="North of England">North of England</a>. It was made by <a href="/wiki/ITV_Yorkshire" title="ITV Yorkshire">Yorkshire Television</a> and was based loosely on <i>Justice Is a Woman</i>, an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/ITV_Playhouse" title="ITV Playhouse">ITV Playhouse</a></i> broadcast in 1969 in which Lockwood had previously also played a barrister. The theme music was <i><a href="/wiki/Crown_Imperial_(march)" title="Crown Imperial (march)">Crown Imperial</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/William_Walton" title="William Walton">William Walton</a>.
</p>
