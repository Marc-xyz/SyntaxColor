<p><b>Wild T &amp; the Spirit</b> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadian" title="Canadian">Canadian</a> <a href="/wiki/Blues_rock" title="Blues rock">blues rock</a> band, fronted by guitarist <a href="/wiki/Tony_Springer" title="Tony Springer">Tony Springer</a>. 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wild_T_and_the_Spirit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Wild T &amp; the Spirit was formed in 1990 by Springer with bassist Brian Dickie and drummer Danny Bilan (formerly of <a href="/wiki/Moxy_(band)" title="Moxy (band)">Moxy</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-1">[1]</a></sup> The band released its Warner Bros. debut album, <i>Love Crazy</i>, in 1991. Bassist Kojo Ferguson toured with the band in support of <i>Love Crazy</i>, appearing in the video for <i>"Midnight Blues"</i>.  Nazeem Lakay soon replaced Dickie on bass. 
</p><p>The following year, Springer was invited to play guitar on <a href="/wiki/David_Bowie" title="David Bowie">David Bowie</a>'s album <i><a href="/wiki/Black_Tie_White_Noise" title="Black Tie White Noise">Black Tie White Noise</a></i>. Following that album and tour, Wild T &amp; the Spirit released their second Warner Bros. album, <i>Givin' Blood</i>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-1">[1]</a></sup> in 1993, and the band toured Canada as a supporting act for <a href="/wiki/Bon_Jovi" title="Bon Jovi">Bon Jovi</a> to promote the album. <i>Givin' Blood</i> earned a nomination for <a href="/wiki/Juno_Award_for_Rock_Album_of_the_Year" title="Juno Award for Rock Album of the Year">Best Hard Rock Album</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Juno_Awards_of_1994" title="Juno Awards of 1994">1994 Juno Awards</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and gave the band a Canadian chart hit with "Loveland". 
</p><p>In the latter half of the 1990s, Springer spend time in Chicago, Ill. performing as a sideman with various blues artists; he returned to Toronto and the band continued to play concert venues throughout Eastern Canada with new members, bassist Chas Laurie and former <a href="/wiki/Frozen_Ghost" title="Frozen Ghost">Frozen Ghost</a> drummer John Bouvette. In 2000, band released the album <i>Strange Modern</i>.  Laurie left the band and was replaced by bassist Guenther Kapelle.
</p><p>The band followed up with <i>True Bliss</i> on Bullseye/Taxim Records in 2004, with Kapelle. <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Healey" title="Jeff Healey">Jeff Healey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carole_Pope" title="Carole Pope">Carole Pope</a>, and keyboardist Peter Nunn also appeared on <i>True Bliss</i> as guest musicians.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The band toured throughout Canada, USA, UK and Europe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bwz_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-bwz-5">[5]</a></sup> In 2008 the band released the album <i>Fender Bender</i> on Jazzhaus Records and continued on with their tour schedule. The new album was received well by fans and critic. 
</p><p>The band released an album in 2015 called <i>Live</i>, which was recorded live in Toronto.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The lineup at that time was Springer, Kapelle and drummer Dave Langguth. That year they performed at the East Coast Guitar Fest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
