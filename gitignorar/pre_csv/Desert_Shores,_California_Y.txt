<p><b>Desert Shores</b> (formerly <b>Fish Springs</b>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gnis_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-gnis-1">[1]</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Census-designated_place" title="Census-designated place">census-designated place</a> (CDP) in <a href="/wiki/Imperial_County,_California" title="Imperial County, California">Imperial County, California</a>, US.  It is part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/El_Centro_Metropolitan_Area" title="El Centro Metropolitan Area">El Centro Metropolitan Statistical Area</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Desert_Shores,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The town is located at the western shore of the <a href="/wiki/Salton_Sea" title="Salton Sea">Salton Sea</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Valley" title="Imperial Valley">Imperial Valley</a>, within the <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Desert" title="Colorado Desert">Colorado Desert</a> ecoregion. It is 36 miles (58 km) west-northwest of <a href="/wiki/Calipatria,_California" title="Calipatria, California">Calipatria</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CGN_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-CGN-3">[3]</a></sup> According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the CDP has a total area of 0.7 square miles (1.8 km<sup>2</sup>), all land.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010">2010</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Desert_Shores,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2010">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 United States Census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> reported that Desert Shores had a population of 1,104. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 1,619.9 people per square mile (625.5/km²). The racial makeup of Desert Shores was 709 (64.2%) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 8 (0.7%) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 26 (2.4%) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 4 (0.4%) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 1 (0.1%) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander_(U.S._Census)" title="Pacific Islander (U.S. Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 307 (27.8%) from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 49 (4.4%) from two or more races.  <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 848 persons (76.8%).
</p><p>The Census reported that 1,104 people (100% of the population) lived in households, 0 (0%) lived in non-institutionalized group quarters, and 0 (0%) were institutionalized.
</p><p>There were 344 households, out of which 167 (48.5%) had children under the age of 18 living in them, 181 (52.6%) were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">opposite-sex married couples</a> living together, 37 (10.8%) had a female householder with no husband present, 27 (7.8%) had a male householder with no wife present.  There were 29 (8.4%) <a href="/wiki/POSSLQ" title="POSSLQ">unmarried opposite-sex partnerships</a>, and 1 (0.3%) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Same-sex_partnerships" title="Same-sex partnerships">same-sex married couples or partnerships</a>. 80 households (23.3%) were made up of individuals and 42 (12.2%) had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 3.21.  There were 245 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Family_(U.S._Census)" title="Family (U.S. Census)">families</a> (71.2% of all households); the average family size was 3.84.
</p><p>The population was spread out with 352 people (31.9%) under the age of 18, 124 people (11.2%) aged 18 to 24, 263 people (23.8%) aged 25 to 44, 218 people (19.7%) aged 45 to 64, and 147 people (13.3%) who were 65 years of age or older.  The median age was 29.9 years. For every 100 females, there were 108.7 males.  For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 98.4 males.
</p><p>There were 421 housing units at an average density of 617.7 per square mile (238.5/km²), of which 344 were occupied, of which 225 (65.4%) were owner-occupied, and 119 (34.6%) were occupied by renters. The homeowner vacancy rate was 4.2%; the rental vacancy rate was 2.4%.  704 people (63.8% of the population) lived in owner-occupied housing units and 400 people (36.2%) lived in rental housing units.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000">2000</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Desert_Shores,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2000">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-6">[6]</a></sup> of 2000, there were 792 people, 279 households, and 181 families residing in the CDP.  The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 1,208.4 people per square mile (463.3/km²).  There were 406 housing units at an average density of 619.5 per square mile (237.5/km²).  The racial makeup of the CDP was 73.5% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">White</a>, 1.4% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Black</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">African American</a>, 0.9% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Native American</a>, 0.3% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Asian</a>, 21.1% from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 2.9% from two or more races.  60.9% of the population were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Latino</a> of any race.
</p><p>There were 279 households out of which 28.3% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 50.5% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 5.4% had a female householder with no husband present, and 35.1% were non-families. 29.0% of all households were made up of individuals and 18.3% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older.  The average household size was 2.8 and the average family size was 3.6.
</p><p>In the CDP, the population was spread out with 26.9% under the age of 18, 8.5% from 18 to 24, 25.4% from 25 to 44, 18.8% from 45 to 64, and 20.5% who were 65 years of age or older.  The median age was 37 years. For every 100 females, there were 128.2 males.  For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 125.3 males.
</p><p>The median income for a household in the CDP was $24,712, and the median income for a family was $29,550. Males had a median income of $26,176 versus $19,375 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the CDP was $8,352.  About 12.0% of families and 7.9% of the population were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including none of those under age 18 and 14.9% of those age 65 or over.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government">Government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Desert_Shores,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Local">Local</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Desert_Shores,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Local">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Desert Shores and its neighboring community of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salton_City" title="Salton City">Salton City</a> are governed by the Salton Community Services District (SCSD), which is a special district per California Government Code. The legislative body of the SCSD is a five-member Board of Directors who are elected by the registered voters in the district every two years.
</p><p>The SCSD has seven services that it is authorized to provide:
</p>
<ol><li>Collect, treat, or dispose of sewage.</li>
<li>Collect, transfer, and dispose of solid waste.</li>
<li>Provide fire protection.</li>
<li>Acquire, construct recreational facilities.</li>
<li>Organize, promote community recreation.</li>
<li>Acquire, construct, and improve street lighting and landscaping.</li>
<li>Provide emergency medical services.</li></ol>
<p>Aside from acting as the architectural committee for architectural compliance with the Covenants, Conditions, and Restrictions (CC&amp;R) of the 80 plus tracts within the district, the SCSD has no land use authority under existing law; that responsibility falls on the Imperial County Board of Supervisors.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="State_and_federal">State and federal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Desert_Shores,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: State and federal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/California_State_Legislature" title="California State Legislature">state legislature</a>, Desert Shores is in <a href="/wiki/California%27s_40th_State_Senate_district" title="California's 40th State Senate district">the  40th Senate District</a>, represented by <a href="/wiki/California_Democratic_Party" title="California Democratic Party">Democrat</a> <a href="/wiki/Ben_Hueso" title="Ben Hueso">Ben Hueso</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/California%27s_56th_State_Assembly_district" title="California's 56th State Assembly district">the  56th Assembly District</a>, represented by <a href="/wiki/California_Democratic_Party" title="California Democratic Party">Democrat</a> <a href="/wiki/Eduardo_Garcia_(politician)" title="Eduardo Garcia (politician)">Eduardo Garcia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p>Federally, Desert Shores is in <a href="/wiki/California%27s_51st_congressional_district" title="California's 51st congressional district">California's  51st congressional district</a>, represented by <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">Democrat</a> <a href="/wiki/Juan_Vargas" title="Juan Vargas">Juan Vargas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
