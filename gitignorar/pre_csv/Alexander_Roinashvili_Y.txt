	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Alexander Roinashvili</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>

		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>

		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:165px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Alexander_Roinashvili._1897.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="270" data-file-width="163" decoding="async" height="270" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/c4/Alexander_Roinashvili._1897.jpg" width="163"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Alexander_Roinashvili._1897.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Alexander Roinashvili, 1897</div></div></div>
<p><b>Alexander Roinashvili</b> (<a href="/wiki/Georgian_language" title="Georgian language">Georgian</a>: <span lang="ka" title="Georgian language text">ალექსანდრე როინაშვილი</span>; also known by his Russified name Alexander Solomonovich Roinov, <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Александр Соломонович Роинов</span>) (1846 – 1898) was the first <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgian</a> <a href="/wiki/Photographer" title="Photographer">photographer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He is known for his photos of the <a href="/wiki/Caucasus" title="Caucasus">Caucasian</a> landscapes and portraits of contemporary Georgian intellectuals. 
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ilia_Chavchavadze_by_Alexander_Roinashvili.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="408" data-file-width="293" decoding="async" height="306" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/27/Ilia_Chavchavadze_by_Alexander_Roinashvili.png/220px-Ilia_Chavchavadze_by_Alexander_Roinashvili.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/27/Ilia_Chavchavadze_by_Alexander_Roinashvili.png 1.5x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ilia_Chavchavadze_by_Alexander_Roinashvili.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Phototgraph of <a href="/wiki/Ilia_Chavchavadze" title="Ilia Chavchavadze">Ilia Chavchavadze</a> by Alexander Roinashvili</div></div></div>
<p>Born in the mountainous community of <a href="/wiki/Dusheti" title="Dusheti">Dusheti</a>, east Georgia, then part of the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a>, Roinashvili took photographic classes at the Khlamov studio in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tiflis" title="Tiflis">Tiflis</a>. He began his career as a photographer in Tiflis in 1865 and soon set up his own studio. Closely associated with the Georgian national movement, he was involved in documenting cultural heritage in Georgia and organized a mobile museum of photography which toured across the Caucasus and Russia proper.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
