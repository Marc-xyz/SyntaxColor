	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Scientific citation</h1>

<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Scientific_citations.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="790" data-file-width="1166" decoding="async" height="149" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Scientific_citations.png/220px-Scientific_citations.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Scientific_citations.png/330px-Scientific_citations.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Scientific_citations.png/440px-Scientific_citations.png 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Scientific_citations.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Reference section in scientific paper.</div></div></div>
<p><b>Scientific <a href="/wiki/Citation" title="Citation">citation</a></b> is providing detailed reference in a scientific publication, typically a <a href="/wiki/Academic_publishing#Scholarly_paper" title="Academic publishing">paper</a> or book, to previous published (or occasionally private) communications which have a bearing on the subject of the new publication. The purpose of citations in original work is to allow readers of the paper to refer to cited work to assist them in judging the new work, source background information vital for future development, and acknowledge the contributions of earlier workers. Citations in, say, a review paper bring together many sources, often recent, in one place.
</p><p>To a considerable extent the quality of work, in the absence of other criteria, is judged on the number of citations received, adjusting for the volume of work in the relevant topic.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> While this is not necessarily a reliable measure, counting citations is trivially easy; judging the merit of complex work can be very difficult.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>Previous work may be cited regarding experimental <a href="/wiki/Procedure_(term)" title="Procedure (term)">procedures</a>, apparatus, <a href="/wiki/Goal" title="Goal">goals</a>, previous theoretical results upon which the new work builds, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theses" title="Theses">theses</a>, and so on. Typically such citations establish the general <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/framework" title="wikt:framework">framework</a> of <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/influence" title="wikt:influence">influences</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Mindset" title="Mindset">mindset</a> of <a href="/wiki/Research" title="Research">research</a>, and especially as "part of what science" it is, and to help determine who conducts the <a href="/wiki/Peer_review" title="Peer review">peer review</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>Disciplined citation of prior works in mathematics and science is known at least as far back as <a href="/wiki/Euclid" title="Euclid">Euclid</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Late in the first millennium, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Islamic_scholar" title="Islamic scholar">Islamic scholars</a> developed their practice of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Isnad" title="Isnad">isnad</a>, or "backing", which established the <a href="/wiki/Validity_(logic)" title="Validity (logic)">validity</a> of sayings of <a href="/wiki/Muhammad" title="Muhammad">Muhammad</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Hadith" title="Hadith">hadith</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asharite" title="Asharite">Asharite</a> school of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Early_Muslim_philosophy" title="Early Muslim philosophy">early Muslim philosophy</a> extended this into <a href="/wiki/Fiqh" title="Fiqh">fiqh</a> or <a href="/wiki/Jurisprudence" title="Jurisprudence">jurisprudence</a>, while the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mutazilite" title="Mutazilite">Mutazilite</a> school used the traditional methods and applied them to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Islamic_science" title="Islamic science">science</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>In some form, then, achieving authority for new work by citing accepted authorities is a near-universal idea among the peoples of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mediterranean" title="Mediterranean">Mediterranean</a>, whose educated people were exposed to one or other of these practices well before the European <a href="/wiki/Renaissance" title="Renaissance">Renaissance</a> and the emergence of the formal <a href="/wiki/Scientific_method" title="Scientific method">scientific method</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Patent_references">Patent references</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scientific_citation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Patent references">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Patent_law" title="Patent law">patent law</a> the citation of previous works, or <a href="/wiki/Prior_art" title="Prior art">prior art</a>, helps establish the <a href="/wiki/Uniqueness" title="Uniqueness">uniqueness</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Invention" title="Invention">invention</a> being described.  The focus in this practice is to claim <a href="/wiki/Originality" title="Originality">originality</a> for <a href="/wiki/Commerce" title="Commerce">commercial</a> purposes, and so the author is motivated to avoid citing works that cast doubt on its originality.  Thus this does not appear to be "scientific" citation. Inventors and lawyers have a legal obligation to cite all relevant art; not to do so risks invalidating the patent.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The patent examiner is obliged to list all further prior art found in searches.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Citation_frequency">Citation frequency</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scientific_citation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Citation frequency">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Modern scientists are sometimes judged by the number of times their work is cited by others—this is actually a key indicator of the relative <a href="/wiki/Impact_factor" title="Impact factor">importance</a> of a work in science.  Accordingly, individual scientists are motivated to have their own work cited early and often and as widely as possible, but all other scientists are motivated to eliminate unnecessary citations so as not to devalue this means of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Judgment" title="Judgment">judgment</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> A formal <a href="/wiki/Citation_index" title="Citation index">citation index</a> tracks which referred and reviewed papers have referred which other such papers. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Baruch_Lev&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Baruch Lev (page does not exist)">Baruch Lev</a> and other advocates of <a href="/wiki/Accounting_reform" title="Accounting reform">accounting reform</a> consider the number of times a <a href="/wiki/Patent" title="Patent">patent</a> is cited to be a significant metric of its quality, and thus of <a href="/wiki/Innovation" title="Innovation">innovation</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
