<p><b>Malindo Airways Sdn Bhd</b> (doing business as <b>Malindo Air</b>) is a Malaysian hybrid-full service carrier<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> owned by Indonesian <a href="/wiki/Lion_Air" title="Lion Air">Lion Air</a> Group with headquarters in Petaling Jaya, Selangor, Malaysia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thestar1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-thestar1-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The name Malindo signifies a cooperative pact between <b>Mal</b>aysia and <b>Indo</b>nesia.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malindo_Air&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The entry of <a href="/wiki/AirAsia" title="AirAsia">AirAsia</a> from Malaysia in the home turf of <a href="/wiki/Lion_Air" title="Lion Air">Lion Air</a> has encouraged the Indonesian airline to enter the <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysian</a> market with a subsidiary airline. AirAsia's subsidiary <a href="/wiki/Indonesia_AirAsia" title="Indonesia AirAsia">Indonesia AirAsia</a>, in partnership with its parent firm, attempted to buy Indonesian carrier <a href="/wiki/Batavia_Air" title="Batavia Air">Batavia Air</a> to gain a foothold in Indonesia, but the deal did not go through due to regulatory complications and Batavia Air ended up going bankrupt. The attempted deal resulted in a turf war between <a href="/wiki/Lion_Air" title="Lion Air">Lion Air</a>, Indonesia's biggest <a href="/wiki/Low-cost_carrier" title="Low-cost carrier">low-cost carrier</a>, and <a href="/wiki/AirAsia" title="AirAsia">AirAsia</a>, Asia's biggest <a href="/wiki/Low-cost_carrier" title="Low-cost carrier">low-cost carrier</a>.
</p><p>Mr Chandran Rama Muthy, personal assistant executive to the president director of Lion Air, has been appointed as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CEO" title="CEO">CEO</a> of Malindo Air. The airline's inaugural flights began operations on 22 March 2013.
</p><p>For its jet routes, Malindo Air provides a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Personal_televisions" title="Personal televisions">personal TV</a> (<a href="/wiki/In-flight_entertainment" title="In-flight entertainment">in-flight entertainment system</a>) in every seat, light snacks and free meal, seat pitches of 402" and 605" for <a href="/wiki/Economy_class" title="Economy class">economy class</a> and <a href="/wiki/Business_class" title="Business class">business class</a> respectively, and free baggage allowances of 55 kg &amp; 100 kg (with exception of flights to <a href="/wiki/Shah_Amanat_International_Airport" title="Shah Amanat International Airport">Shah Amanat International Airport</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shahjalal_International_Airport" title="Shahjalal International Airport">Shahjalal International Airport</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a> with limits of 35 kg &amp; 40 kg).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In June 2015, Malindo became the first Malaysian airline to offer in-flight <a href="/wiki/Wi-Fi" title="Wi-Fi">Wi-Fi</a> service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The combination of the amenities, services and low cost fares puts Malindo Air in the category of hybrid airline.
</p><p>The IFE is based on Panasonic eXLite with 9 inch touchscreens in Economy class and 11.1 inch touchscreens in Business Class, capable of gestures (swiping, scrolling). Full meals are served in Business class.
</p><p>The airline also has expanded on a parallel <a href="/wiki/Turboprop" title="Turboprop">turboprop</a> service, focusing mainly on secondary routes within a 2-hour radius from <a href="/wiki/Sultan_Abdul_Aziz_Shah_Airport" title="Sultan Abdul Aziz Shah Airport">Sultan Abdul Aziz Shah Airport</a>. Pioneer destinations include <a href="/wiki/Penang" title="Penang">Penang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Johor_Bahru" title="Johor Bahru">Johor Bahru</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kota_Bharu" title="Kota Bharu">Kota Bharu</a>, the flights have commenced in early June 2013.
</p><p>In early 2017, the airline is expected to re-brand Malindo Air to "Batik Air Malaysia" which will focus on international routes while its associate airline, <a href="/wiki/Batik_Air" title="Batik Air">Batik Air</a> serves domestic routes in <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>. The airline has begun to feature the Batik Air Malaysia logo and livery on its aircraft gradually.
</p><p>In 2016 Malindo initiated interline agreements with <a href="/wiki/Turkish_Airlines" title="Turkish Airlines">Turkish Airlines</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Qatar_Airways" title="Qatar Airways">Qatar Airways</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>In April 2017, Malindo Air received its International Air Transport Association (IATA) certificate as IATA member. The airline has also successfully retained its IOSA registration, which is valid from 3 June 2018 to 3 June 2020.
</p><p>In an official press release issued by Malindo dated 26 August 2019, Captain Mushafiz Mustafa Bakri, Director of Safety, Security &amp; Quality of Thai Lion Air has been announced as the new CEO, replacing the current CEO effective 23 September 2019, while Chandra Rama Muthy to be appointed as Group Strategy Director, Lion Group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Incidents_and_accidents">Incidents and accidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malindo_Air&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Incidents and accidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>19 April 2018 – A Malindo Air Boeing 737-900ER flying from <a href="/wiki/Tribhuvan_International_Airport" title="Tribhuvan International Airport">Tribhuvan International Airport</a> in Kathmandu to Kuala Lumpur overran the runway at Kathmandu after a high-speed <a href="/wiki/Rejected_takeoff" title="Rejected takeoff">rejected takeoff</a>. The aircraft came to a stop in the grass 250 feet (75 metres) past the runway end. All 132 passengers and seven crew members escaped injury. Damage to the aircraft was minor and the airport was closed for 12 hours for it to be moved. The flight crew opted to abort the takeoff because of a warning indicating the aircraft was not correctly configured.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></li>
<li>On 20 June 2019, Malindo Air Flight 301, a Boeing 737-8GP(WL) (registration 9M-LCK) from <a href="/wiki/Bandung" title="Bandung">Bandung</a> to <a href="/wiki/Kuala_Lumpur" title="Kuala Lumpur">Kuala Lumpur</a>, made a runway excursion at <a href="/wiki/Husein_Sastranegara_International_Airport" title="Husein Sastranegara International Airport">Husein Sastranegara International Airport</a> shortly before takeoff. There were no fatalities.</li></ul>
