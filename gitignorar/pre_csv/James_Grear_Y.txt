<p><b>James Thomas Grear</b> (born June 5, 1962) is an American <a href="/wiki/Gospel_music" title="Gospel music">gospel musician</a>. He started his music career, in 1998, with Company that are based out of <a href="/wiki/Minneapolis" title="Minneapolis">Minneapolis, Minnesota</a>. He has released eight albums, with three of them charting on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)"><i>Billboard</i> magazine</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Top_Gospel_Albums" title="Top Gospel Albums">Gospel Albums</a> chart. He has released albums with a few labels, such as the following: Born Again Records,  Diamante Records, Liquid 8 Records, Alliant Music Group, Habakkuk Music, Echo Park Records.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_Grear&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Grear was born James Thomas Grear,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Intelius_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Intelius-1">[1]</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Gary,_Indiana" title="Gary, Indiana">Gary, Indiana</a> on June 5, 1962, and was raised as a member of The Church of God in Christ by his parents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GE_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-GE-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_Grear&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>His music recording career began in 1998, and he has released eight albums with a myriad of labels, which are the following: Born Again Records,  Diamante Records, Liquid 8 Records, Alliant Music Group, Habakkuk Music, Echo Park Records.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AM_Disc_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-AM_Disc-3">[3]</a></sup> He had three albums place on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)"><i>Billboard</i> magazine</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Top_Gospel_Albums" title="Top Gospel Albums">Gospel Albums</a> chart, with one placing on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/R%26B_Albums" title="R&amp;B Albums">R&amp;B Albums</a> chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AM_Awards_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-AM_Awards-4">[4]</a></sup> The three that charted on the Gospel Albums chart were 1998's <i>Don't Give Up</i> at No. 8, 2001's <i>What Will Your Life Say</i> at No. 14, and <i>A Special Place</i> in 2004 at No. 18.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AM_Awards_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-AM_Awards-4">[4]</a></sup> The album <i>Don't Give Up</i>, also charted on the R&amp;B Albums chart at No. 34.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AM_Awards_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-AM_Awards-4">[4]</a></sup> He released, <i>It's My Season</i>, on May 5, 2015 by Universal Records alongside EchoPark JDI Records.
</p>
