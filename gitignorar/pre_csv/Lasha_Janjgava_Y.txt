	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Lasha Janjgava</h1>

<p><b>Lasha Janjgava</b> (<a href="/wiki/Georgian_language" title="Georgian language">Georgian</a>: <span lang="ka" title="Georgian language text">ლაშა ჯანჯღავა</span>; born 5 May 1970) is a <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgian</a> <a href="/wiki/Chess" title="Chess">chess</a> <a href="/wiki/Grandmaster_(chess)" title="Grandmaster (chess)">Grandmaster (GM)</a> (1990), two-times <a href="/wiki/Georgian_Chess_Championship" title="Georgian Chess Championship">Georgian Chess Championship</a> winner (1994, 1996).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lasha_Janjgava&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the 1980s and 1990s Lasha Janjgava was one of the leading Georgian chess players. At the turn of 1989/90 he was very successful in <a href="/wiki/Hastings_International_Chess_Congress" title="Hastings International Chess Congress">Hastings</a>, where in the <i><a href="/wiki/Scheveningen_system" title="Scheveningen system">Scheveningen system</a></i> tournament he shared 1st place with <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Gallagher" title="Joseph Gallagher">Joseph Gallagher</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gregory_Kaidanov" title="Gregory Kaidanov">Gregory Kaidanov</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sergey_Smagin" title="Sergey Smagin">Sergey Smagin</a>. In 1991, Lasha Janjgava shared the 3rd place with <a href="/wiki/Maia_Chiburdanidze" title="Maia Chiburdanidze">Maia Chiburdanidze</a> (behind <a href="/wiki/Zurab_Sturua" title="Zurab Sturua">Zurab Sturua</a> and <a href="/wiki/Giorgi_Giorgadze" title="Giorgi Giorgadze">Giorgi Giorgadze</a>) in <a href="/wiki/Tbilisi" title="Tbilisi">Tbilisi</a>. In 1992, he won in <a href="/wiki/Antwerp" title="Antwerp">Antwerp</a> Academic World Chess Championship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He twice won <a href="/wiki/Georgian_Chess_Championship" title="Georgian Chess Championship">Georgian Chess Championships</a>: in 1994 and 1996.
</p><p>Lasha Janjgava played for Georgia in the <a href="/wiki/Chess_Olympiad" title="Chess Olympiad">Chess Olympiads</a>:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>In 1992, at second reserve board in the <a href="/wiki/30th_Chess_Olympiad" title="30th Chess Olympiad">30th Chess Olympiad</a> in <a href="/wiki/Manila" title="Manila">Manila</a> (+0, =4, -0),</li>
<li>In 1994, at first reserve board in the <a href="/wiki/31st_Chess_Olympiad" title="31st Chess Olympiad">31st Chess Olympiad</a> in <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a> (+0, =2, -3),</li>
<li>In 1996, at second board in the <a href="/wiki/32nd_Chess_Olympiad" title="32nd Chess Olympiad">32nd Chess Olympiad</a> in <a href="/wiki/Yerevan" title="Yerevan">Yerevan</a> (+1, =0, -2),</li>
<li>In 1998, at second reserve board in the <a href="/wiki/33rd_Chess_Olympiad" title="33rd Chess Olympiad">33rd Chess Olympiad</a> in <a href="/wiki/Elista" title="Elista">Elista</a> (+1, =3, -1).</li></ul>
<p>Lasha Janjgava played for Georgia in the <a href="/wiki/European_Team_Chess_Championship" title="European Team Chess Championship">European Team Chess Championships</a>:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>In 1992, at third board in the 10th European Team Chess Championship in <a href="/wiki/Debrecen" title="Debrecen">Debrecen</a> (+1, =4, -1),</li>
<li>In 2005, at first reserve board in the 15th European Team Chess Championship in <a href="/wiki/Gothenburg" title="Gothenburg">Gothenburg</a> (+1, =1, -0).</li></ul>
<p>In 1989, Lasha Janjgava was awarded the FIDE International Master (IM) title and received the FIDE Grandmaster (GM) title year later. He is FIDE Trainer (2010).
</p><p>In recent years Lasha Janjgava has rarely participated in chess tournaments.
</p>
