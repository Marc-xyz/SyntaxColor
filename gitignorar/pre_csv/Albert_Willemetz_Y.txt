<p><b>Albert Willemetz</b> (14 February 1887 – 7 October 1964) was a <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> <a href="/wiki/Libretto" title="Libretto">librettist</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Albert_Willemetz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Albert Willemetz was a prolific lyricist. He invented a new type of musical, with a humorous and "sexy" style. He was the author of more than 3000 songs, including "Mon homme" (popularized in English as "My Man"),  "<a href="/wiki/Valentine_(Maurice_Chevalier_song)" title="Valentine (Maurice Chevalier song)">Valentine</a>," "Dans la vie faut pas s'en faire," "Les palétuviers," "Ramona," "Est-ce que je te demande," "Ah si vous connaissiez ma poule," "Amusez-vous," and "<a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9licie_aussi" title="Félicie aussi">Félicie aussi</a>"), more than 100 musicals (including <i><a href="/wiki/Phi-Phi" title="Phi-Phi">Phi-Phi</a></i>, <i>Ta Bouche</i>, <i>Là-Haut</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/D%C3%A9d%C3%A9_(op%C3%A9rette)" title="Dédé (opérette)">Dédé</a></i>, <i>3 jeunes filles nues</i>, <i>Florestan 1er</i>, and <i>Trois Valses</i>), more than 100 revues (including seven with <a href="/wiki/Sacha_Guitry" title="Sacha Guitry">Sacha Guitry</a>), and work for films.
</p><p>He worked with some of the notable musicians of his day, including <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_Messager" title="André Messager">André Messager</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Yvain" title="Maurice Yvain">Maurice Yvain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Honegger" title="Arthur Honegger">Arthur Honegger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Henri_Christin%C3%A9" title="Henri Christiné">Henri Christiné</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Padilla_S%C3%A1nchez" title="José Padilla Sánchez">José Padilla</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vincent_Scotto" title="Vincent Scotto">Vincent Scotto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reynaldo_Hahn" title="Reynaldo Hahn">Reynaldo Hahn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Raoul_Moretti" title="Raoul Moretti">Raoul Moretti</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Moises_Simons" title="Moises Simons">Moises Simons</a>, <a href="/wiki/Georges_Van_Parys" title="Georges Van Parys">Georges Van Parys</a>, <a href="/wiki/Henri_Betti" title="Henri Betti">Henri Betti</a>, Szulc, Borel-Clerc, Oberfeld, Romberg, Lopez, Richepin, and Lattès.
</p><p>Some of the singers who have sung his lyrics include <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Chevalier" title="Maurice Chevalier">Maurice Chevalier</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fanny_Brice" title="Fanny Brice">Fanny Brice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yvonne_Printemps" title="Yvonne Printemps">Yvonne Printemps</a>, Mistinguett, <a href="/wiki/Billie_Holiday" title="Billie Holiday">Billie Holiday</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ella_Fitzgerald" title="Ella Fitzgerald">Ella Fitzgerald</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barbra_Streisand" title="Barbra Streisand">Barbra Streisand</a>, Arletty, <a href="/wiki/Josephine_Baker" title="Josephine Baker">Josephine Baker</a>, <a href="/wiki/Danielle_Darrieux" title="Danielle Darrieux">Danielle Darrieux</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pauline_Carton" title="Pauline Carton">Pauline Carton</a>, Fernandel, Bourvil, Dranem, Henri Garat, <a href="/wiki/Victor_Boucher" title="Victor Boucher">Victor Boucher</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jean_Gabin" title="Jean Gabin">Jean Gabin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Fresnay" title="Pierre Fresnay">Pierre Fresnay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michel_Simon" title="Michel Simon">Michel Simon</a> and <a href="/wiki/L%C3%A9o_Ferr%C3%A9" title="Léo Ferré">Léo Ferré</a>.
</p><p>Additionally, Albert Willemetz served as Secretary to Clemenceau, the Director of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Th%C3%A9%C3%A2tre_des_Bouffes_Parisiens" title="Théâtre des Bouffes Parisiens">Théâtre des Bouffes Parisiens</a> for 30 years, and was President of the <a href="/wiki/Soci%C3%A9t%C3%A9_des_auteurs,_compositeurs_et_%C3%A9diteurs_de_musique" title="Société des auteurs, compositeurs et éditeurs de musique">SACEM</a> (from 1945), and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conf%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_des_Soci%C3%A9t%C3%A9s_d%C2%B4Auteurs_et_Compositeurs" title="Confédération Internationale des Sociétés d´Auteurs et Compositeurs">CISAC</a> (1956). He was the only president of both organizations not to be able to read music. He died in <a href="/wiki/Marnes-la-Coquette" title="Marnes-la-Coquette">Marnes-la-Coquette</a>, near Paris.
</p>
