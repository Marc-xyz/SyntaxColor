	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Kannada flag</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Flag_of_Karnataka.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="300" data-file-width="450" decoding="async" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/Flag_of_Karnataka.svg/220px-Flag_of_Karnataka.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/Flag_of_Karnataka.svg/330px-Flag_of_Karnataka.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/Flag_of_Karnataka.svg/440px-Flag_of_Karnataka.svg.png 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Flag_of_Karnataka.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Kannada flag</div></div></div>
<p>The <b>Kannada flag</b> is a flag that is in popular use in the state of <a href="/wiki/Karnataka" title="Karnataka">Karnataka</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>. It is also used by <i>Kannada Paksha</i>, a political party in Karnataka. It was widely identified with <a href="/wiki/Karnataka" title="Karnataka">Karnataka</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kannadigas" title="Kannadigas">Kannadigas</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Kannada" title="Kannada">Kannada</a> language. The flag does not represent separatist ideology and was used across the length and breadth of the state by Kannada-centric organizations and private individuals to show solidarity with Kannada causes and demonstrate their unity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It finds a place in the official website of the Government of Karnataka.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kannada_flag&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The flag was first conceived by Ma Ramamurthy, a Bangalore-based writer, journalist and social activist, often considered as a commander of Kannada movement in 1960s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Ma Ramamurthy was the son of noted freedom fighter and litterateur, Veerakesari Seetharama Shastri. In 1964, Ma Ramamurthy went on a padayatra, protesting against the hoisting of flags by a group from a neighboring State in Bangalore. He then realized the need for a flag representing Karnataka and designed a yellow flag depicting Karnataka map with a paddy crown in the center which was simplified later to its current form.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He used the flag for the political party called "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kannada_Paksha&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kannada Paksha (page does not exist)">Kannada Paksha</a>" which he founded in 1965. The political party is active now the flag has been consistently used all over Karnataka to represent the state, Kannada language and culture. This flag is prominently hoisted on the occasion of <a href="/wiki/Karnataka_Rajyotsava" title="Karnataka Rajyotsava">Karnataka Rajyotsava</a> celebrated on November 1 as the state's Formation Day. It is routine in many educational and government institutions. After hoisting the flag, the official State anthem written in Kannada, <i><a href="/wiki/Jaya_Bharata_Jananiya_Tanujate" title="Jaya Bharata Jananiya Tanujate">Jaya Bharata Jananiya Tanujate</a></i> is sung.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> One can find the yellow-red flag fluttering atop buildings, flag posts at road junctions, and on vehicles along the length and breadth of Karnataka State. Many institutions host the flag to protect their buildings from local agitations.
</p><p>In several pro-Kannada movements including <a href="/wiki/Gokak_agitation" title="Gokak agitation">Gokak agitation</a> the flag has been used as the symbol of the unity of Kannadigas. In 1998, Kannada Development Authority recommended for granting it state flag status but the state law department cited legal hurdles for the same.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2009, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/B._S._Yeddyurappa" title="B. S. Yeddyurappa">B. S. Yeddyurappa</a>, then <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chief_Minister_of_Karnataka" title="Chief Minister of Karnataka">Chief Minister of Karnataka</a>, issued a circular enforcing restrictions on hoisting Kannada flag on government buildings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Later, this move was challenged in Karnataka High Court in 2012 after then Chief Minister <a href="/wiki/D._V._Sadananda_Gowda" title="D. V. Sadananda Gowda">D. V. Sadananda Gowda</a> mentioned in his budget speech that the hoisting of the Kannada flag on November 1 will be made compulsory in all government offices, schools and colleges. During hearing, a division bench of the high court headed by Chief Justice Vikramajit Sen questioned the government's move to hoist "any flag" other than the national flag. The court had also sought a response from the government regarding "whether it is possible for the State to have any flag other than the national flag" while also asking the Union government to clarify the law in this connection.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Subsequently, the state Govt decided to withdraw its circular before the eve of Rajyotsava in 2012.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
