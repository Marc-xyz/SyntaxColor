<p><b>Eino Vihtori Kaakkolahti</b> (April 25, 1929 — June 25, 2014)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> was a Finnish <a href="/wiki/Pes%C3%A4pallo" title="Pesäpallo">pesäpallo</a> player.
</p><p>During his 18 seasons in the Finnish championship league, he won a total of 10 medals and 6 awards,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and was chosen Pesäpallo Player of the Year in 1961.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BPotY_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-BPotY-3">[3]</a></sup> In 1973 and '74, Kaakkolahti was vice chairman of Suomen Pesäpalloliitto (The Finnish Pesäpallo Association, or PPL for short).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Eino Kaakkolahti's little brother, Matti Kaakkolahti, is a ski jumping medalist and has played pesäpallo in the Finnish series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
