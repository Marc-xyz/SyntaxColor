<p><b>Pond Mill Bridge</b>, also known as Pondtown Mill Bridge, is a historic stone <a href="/wiki/Arch_bridge" title="Arch bridge">arch bridge</a> in <a href="/wiki/Latimore_Township,_Adams_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Latimore Township, Adams County, Pennsylvania">Latimore Township</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adams_County,_Pennsylvania" title="Adams County, Pennsylvania">Adams County, Pennsylvania</a>.  It is a 91-foot-long (28 m), triple-arched rubble stone bridge. The bridge crosses <a href="/wiki/Bermudian_Creek" title="Bermudian Creek">Bermudian Creek</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-arch_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-arch-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>It was added to the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> in 1988.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nris_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-nris-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
