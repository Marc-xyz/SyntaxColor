	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Arrowhead State Trail</h1>

<p>The <b>Arrowhead State Trail</b> is a recreational trail in the <a href="/wiki/Arrowhead_Region" title="Arrowhead Region">Arrowhead Region</a> of northern <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a>, USA, geared primarily for winter <a href="/wiki/Snowmobile" title="Snowmobile">snowmobile</a> use.  It runs 135 miles (217 km) from an intersection with the <a href="/wiki/Taconite_State_Trail" title="Taconite State Trail">Taconite State Trail</a> 10 miles (16 km) west of <a href="/wiki/Tower,_Minnesota" title="Tower, Minnesota">Tower</a> to an intersection with the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Blue_Ox_Trail&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Blue Ox Trail (page does not exist)">Blue Ox Trail</a> 3 miles (4.8 km) south of <a href="/wiki/International_Falls,_Minnesota" title="International Falls, Minnesota">International Falls</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Map_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Map-1">[1]</a></sup>  In summer about 69 miles (111 km) are suitable for <a href="/wiki/Hiking" title="Hiking">hiking</a>, <a href="/wiki/Equestrianism" title="Equestrianism">horseback riding</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mountain_biking" title="Mountain biking">mountain biking</a>, while the northern section is blocked by areas of wetness and standing water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Website_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Website-2">[2]</a></sup>  
</p><p>The Arrowhead State Trail was authorized by the <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Legislature" title="Minnesota Legislature">Minnesota Legislature</a> in 1974–75.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Map_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Map-1">[1]</a></sup>  It is managed by the <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Department_of_Natural_Resources" title="Minnesota Department of Natural Resources">Minnesota Department of Natural Resources</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arrowhead_State_Trail&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The northern section of the trail from International Falls to the <a href="/wiki/Ash_River" title="Ash River">Ash River</a> is fairly flat, passing through forests of <a href="/wiki/Aspen" title="Aspen">aspen</a> on higher ground and <a href="/wiki/Spruce" title="Spruce">spruce</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fraxinus" title="Fraxinus">ash</a> on lower ground.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Map_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Map-1">[1]</a></sup>  The southern section is more rugged, featuring rolling hills with large, exposed boulders interspersed with many lakes and streams.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Arrowhead135_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Arrowhead135-3">[3]</a></sup>  This thickly wooded area is part of the <a href="/wiki/Laurentian_Mixed_Forest_Province" title="Laurentian Mixed Forest Province">Laurentian Mixed Forest Province</a>.  There are intersections with numerous local snowmobile trails.  The route of the Arrowhead State Trail passes within 1 mile (1.6 km) of <a href="/wiki/Voyageurs_National_Park" title="Voyageurs National Park">Voyageurs National Park</a> and within 10 miles (16 km) of the <a href="/wiki/Boundary_Waters_Canoe_Area_Wilderness" title="Boundary Waters Canoe Area Wilderness">Boundary Waters Canoe Area Wilderness</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>  The trail, which very roughly follows <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_53" title="U.S. Route 53">U.S. Route 53</a>, offers nine shelters with firepits and restrooms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Arrowhead135_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Arrowhead135-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arrowhead_State_Trail&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since 2005 the trail has hosted the Arrowhead 135, a 135-mile (217 km) extreme endurance race in which contestants are challenged to carry all of their own gear under their own power via foot, ski, or bicycle, in mid-winter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
