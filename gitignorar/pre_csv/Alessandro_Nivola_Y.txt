<p><b>Alessandro Antine Nivola</b> (born June 28, 1972) is an American actor and producer who has starred in feature films such as <i><a href="/wiki/Face/Off" title="Face/Off">Face/Off</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Jurassic_Park_III" title="Jurassic Park III">Jurassic Park III</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/American_Hustle" title="American Hustle">American Hustle</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/A_Most_Violent_Year" title="A Most Violent Year">A Most Violent Year</a></i>, and a brief appearance in <i><a href="/wiki/Selma_(film)" title="Selma (film)">Selma</a></i>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alessandro_Nivola&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nivola was born in <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>. His mother, Virginia (née Davis), is an artist, and his father, Pietro Salvatore Nivola (born 1944), was a professor of political science.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Nivola's paternal grandfather was the <a href="/wiki/Italians" title="Italians">Italian</a> sculptor <a href="/wiki/Costantino_Nivola" title="Costantino Nivola">Costantino Nivola</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and his paternal grandmother, Ruth Guggenheim,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Judaism" title="Judaism">Jewish</a> refugee from <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Germany</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-German_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-German-11">[11]</a></sup> Nivola has described his mother as "a WASP, from the South",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and has stated that she is a relative of Confederate President <a href="/wiki/Jefferson_Davis" title="Jefferson Davis">Jefferson Davis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> He has a brother, Adrian, and attended <a href="/wiki/Phillips_Exeter_Academy" title="Phillips Exeter Academy">Phillips Exeter Academy</a> and Yale University. Alessandro's family also lived in <a href="/wiki/Burlington,_Vermont" title="Burlington, Vermont">Burlington, Vermont</a>, where he attended Mater Christi School, a ministry of the Sisters of Mercy.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alessandro_Nivola&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nivola earned a Drama Desk Award nomination for his first performance on the New York stage in the 1995 Broadway production of <i>A Month In The Country</i> opposite <a href="/wiki/Helen_Mirren" title="Helen Mirren">Helen Mirren</a>. Since then he has starred in numerous films including David O. Russell's <i>American Hustle</i> which earned him a Screen Actors Guild Award. Among his other films are <a href="/wiki/Lisa_Cholodenko" title="Lisa Cholodenko">Lisa Cholodenko</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Laurel_Canyon_(film)" title="Laurel Canyon (film)">Laurel Canyon</a></i> (Independent Spirit Award nomination for best supporting actor), <i>Coco Before Chanel</i>, <i>Junebug</i>, <i>Mansfield Park</i>, <a href="/wiki/Kenneth_Branagh" title="Kenneth Branagh">Kenneth Branagh</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Love%27s_Labour%27s_Lost_(film)" title="Love's Labour's Lost (film)">Love's Labour's Lost</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Jurassic_Park_III" title="Jurassic Park III">Jurassic Park III</a></i>, <i>Goal 1</i> and <i>2</i>, <i>The Eye</i>, <i>I Want You</i>, <i>Best Laid Plans</i>, <i>Janie Jones</i>, <i>Five Dollars A Day</i>, and John Woo's <i><a href="/wiki/Face/Off" title="Face/Off">Face/Off</a></i> (Blockbuster Entertainment Award nomination). In 2009 he was given the Excellence in Acting Award at the Provincetown International Film Festival.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>In 2013, Nivola was among <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> critics' pics for an Oscar nomination for his performance opposite <a href="/wiki/Elle_Fanning" title="Elle Fanning">Elle Fanning</a> and <a href="/wiki/Annette_Bening" title="Annette Bening">Annette Bening</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sally_Potter" title="Sally Potter">Sally Potter</a>'s film <i><a href="/wiki/Ginger_%26_Rosa" title="Ginger &amp; Rosa">Ginger &amp; Rosa</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In 2013, he appeared in the drama film <i><a href="/wiki/Devil%27s_Knot_(film)" title="Devil's Knot (film)">Devil's Knot</a></i>, about the <a href="/wiki/West_Memphis_Three" title="West Memphis Three">West Memphis Three</a>, and in 2014, in the thriller <i><a href="/wiki/A_Most_Violent_Year" title="A Most Violent Year">A Most Violent Year</a></i>. He returned to the theater and starred in the 2010 Off-Broadway revival of <a href="/wiki/Sam_Shepard" title="Sam Shepard">Sam Shepard</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A_Lie_Of_The_Mind" title="A Lie Of The Mind">A Lie Of The Mind</a></i> directed by <a href="/wiki/Ethan_Hawke" title="Ethan Hawke">Ethan Hawke</a>. In 2013 he starred as Sir Robert Morton in the Broadway revival of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Terrence_Rattigan" title="Terrence Rattigan">Terrence Rattigan</a>'s <i>The Winslow Boy</i>. He returned to Broadway in the fall of 2014 starring opposite <a href="/wiki/Bradley_Cooper" title="Bradley Cooper">Bradley Cooper</a> in a revival of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Elephant_Man" title="The Elephant Man">The Elephant Man</a></i>. In 2012, Nivola established the film and television production company King Bee Productions with his wife, actress <a href="/wiki/Emily_Mortimer" title="Emily Mortimer">Emily Mortimer</a>. Their first project was a six-part television series called <i><a href="/wiki/Doll_%26_Em" title="Doll &amp; Em">Doll &amp; Em</a></i> which Nivola produced and Mortimer wrote and starred in. The program aired on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sky_Living" title="Sky Living">Sky Living</a> in the UK and on <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> in the United States in 2014.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alessandro_Nivola&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nivola married British actress <a href="/wiki/Emily_Mortimer" title="Emily Mortimer">Emily Mortimer</a> in <a href="/wiki/Buckinghamshire" title="Buckinghamshire">Buckinghamshire</a> in January 2003. The couple have two children and live in <a href="/wiki/Boerum_Hill" title="Boerum Hill">Boerum Hill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vincentelli_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Vincentelli-16">[16]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup>
</p>
