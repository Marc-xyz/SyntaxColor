	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Paul Boyle</h1>

<p><b>Paul Joseph Boyle</b>, <span class="noexcerpt nowraplinks" style="font-size:100%;"><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commander_of_the_Order_of_the_British_Empire" title="Commander of the Order of the British Empire">CBE</a><span style="font-size:14px;">,</span> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society_of_Edinburgh" title="Fellow of the Royal Society of Edinburgh">FRSE</a><span style="font-size:14px;">,</span> <a href="/wiki/Fellow_of_the_British_Academy" title="Fellow of the British Academy">FBA</a></span> (born 16 November 1964) is a British <a href="/wiki/Geographer" title="Geographer">geographer</a>, academic, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academic_administrator" title="Academic administrator">academic administrator</a>. He was the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vice-Chancellor" title="Vice-Chancellor">Vice-Chancellor</a> of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Leicester" title="University of Leicester">University of Leicester</a> between 2014 and 2019.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He had been <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Professor_(highest_academic_rank)" title="Professor (highest academic rank)">Professor</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Human_Geography" title="Human Geography">Human Geography</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_St_Andrews" title="University of St Andrews">University of St Andrews</a> from 1999 to 2014, and Chief Executive of the <a href="/wiki/Economic_and_Social_Research_Council" title="Economic and Social Research Council">Economic and Social Research Council</a> (ESRC) from 2010 to 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WW_2017_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-WW_2017-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VC_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-VC-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Q&amp;A_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Q&amp;A-4">[4]</a></sup> He took over as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vice-Chancellor" title="Vice-Chancellor">Vice-Chancellor</a> of <a href="/wiki/Swansea_University" title="Swansea University">Swansea University</a> at the end of the 2018/2019 academic year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Honours">Honours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Paul_Boyle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Honours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2006, Boyle was elected a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society_of_Edinburgh" title="Fellow of the Royal Society of Edinburgh">Fellow of the Royal Society of Edinburgh</a> (FRSE).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WW_2017_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-WW_2017-2">[2]</a></sup> In 2013, he was elected a <a href="/wiki/Fellow_of_the_British_Academy" title="Fellow of the British Academy">Fellow of the British Academy</a> (FBA), the United Kingdom's national academy for the social sciences and humanities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FBA_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-FBA-6">[6]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/2016_New_Year_Honours" title="2016 New Year Honours">2016 New Year Honours</a>, he was appointed a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commander_of_the_Order_of_the_British_Empire" title="Commander of the Order of the British Empire">Commander of the Order of the British Empire</a> (CBE) "for services to social science".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LG_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-LG-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
