<p><b>Boatswain Point</b> is a locality in the Australian state of <a href="/wiki/South_Australia" title="South Australia">South Australia</a> located on the state’s south-east coast overlooking both <a href="/wiki/Guichen_Bay" title="Guichen Bay">Guichen Bay</a> and the body of water known in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> as the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Ocean" title="Southern Ocean">Southern Ocean</a> and by international authorities as the <a href="/wiki/Great_Australian_Bight" title="Great Australian Bight">Great Australian Bight</a> about 260 kilometres (160 mi) south east of the state capital of <a href="/wiki/Adelaide_city_centre" title="Adelaide city centre">Adelaide</a> and about 11 kilometres (6.8 mi) north of the municipal seat of <a href="/wiki/Robe,_South_Australia" title="Robe, South Australia"> Robe</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LMV_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-LMV-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-postcode_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-postcode-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iho_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-iho-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ahs_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-ahs-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>Boundaries for the locality were created on 28 January 1999 for “the long established name” and include the site of the <i>Boatswain Point Shack Site</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LMV_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-LMV-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SAGG_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-SAGG-3">[3]</a></sup>  The name is derived from a coastal feature within the locality called <i>Boatswain Point</i> which is itself is reportedly named after a local person known as “Tom the Bosun” who profited from ferrying <a href="/wiki/History_of_Chinese_Australians#Gold_Rush:_1851_to_1854" title="History of Chinese Australians">Chinese immigrants</a> from ships off the coast and guiding them to the <a href="/wiki/Australian_gold_rushes#Notable_gold_finds_which_started_rushes" title="Australian gold rushes">goldfields</a> in <a href="/wiki/Victoria_(Australia)" title="Victoria (Australia)">Victoria</a> during the 1850s.  In 1977, the name was used for a private sub-division in part section 477 of the cadastral unit of the <a href="/wiki/Hundred_of_Waterhouse_(South_Australia)" title="Hundred of Waterhouse (South Australia)">Hundred of Waterhouse</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-manning_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-manning-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>The locality overlooks to its west, a portion of the continental coastline located just north of Guichen Bay and to its south, the coastline of the bay from Cape Thomas in the west to Boatswains Point in the east.  Two settlements are located along its west coast behind an area of reserved land.  The road system associated with these settlements is connected to the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Ports_Highway" title="Southern Ports Highway">Southern Ports Highway</a> in the east by a road called Boatswain Point Road.  Land in the remainder of the locality is used for agricultural purposes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LMV_1-8"><a href="#cite_note-LMV-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LMVa_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-LMVa-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/2016_Australian_census" title="2016 Australian census">2016 Australian census</a> which was conducted in August 2016 reports that Boatswain Point had a population of 50 people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census2016_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Census2016-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Boatswain Point is located within the federal <a href="/wiki/Division_of_Barker" title="Division of Barker">division of Barker</a>, the state <a href="/wiki/Electoral_district_of_MacKillop" title="Electoral district of MacKillop">electoral district of MacKillop</a> and the local government area of the <a href="/wiki/District_Council_of_Robe" title="District Council of Robe">District Council of Robe</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AEC_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-AEC-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ECSA_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-ECSA-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LMV_1-9"><a href="#cite_note-LMV-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
