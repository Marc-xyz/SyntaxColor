<p><b>CIBC Retail Markets</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Retail_banking" title="Retail banking">retail banking</a> division of the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Imperial_Bank_of_Commerce" title="Canadian Imperial Bank of Commerce">Canadian Imperial Bank of Commerce</a>. It offers <a href="/wiki/Deposit_account" title="Deposit account">savings</a> and <a href="/wiki/Loan" title="Loan">loan</a> services for personal and business customers. CIBC Retail Markets is currently headed by Sonia A. Baxendale, Senior Executive Vice-President.
</p><p>It offers <a href="/wiki/Branch_(banking)" title="Branch (banking)">branch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Online_banking" title="Online banking">online</a> and <a href="/wiki/Telephone_banking" title="Telephone banking">telephone banking</a>, as well as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Automatic_Banking_Machine" title="Automatic Banking Machine">ABMs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Credit_card" title="Credit card">credit card</a> services. <a href="/wiki/Private_banking" title="Private banking">Private banking</a> is also available to retail customers.
</p><p>CIBC's <a href="/wiki/Co-branding" title="Co-branding">co-branded</a> retail electronic banking business, <b>President's Choice Financial</b>, is a co-venture with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Loblaw_Companies_Limited" title="Loblaw Companies Limited">Loblaw Companies Limited</a>.
</p><p>In 2003 the bank had 1,106 branches, 4,421 ABM's, over 9 million individual customers (with slightly fewer than half online) and 400,000 corporate customers, with an annual profit of $535 million profit for the year 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
