<p><b>Jarmo Kekäläinen</b> (born July 3, 1966) is a <a href="/wiki/Finns" title="Finns">Finnish</a> professional <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey" title="Ice hockey">ice hockey</a> executive and former player who briefly played in the <a href="/wiki/National_Hockey_League" title="National Hockey League">National Hockey League</a> (NHL) with the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Bruins" title="Boston Bruins">Boston Bruins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ottawa_Senators" title="Ottawa Senators">Ottawa Senators</a>. Kekäläinen serves as the general manager of the <a href="/wiki/Columbus_Blue_Jackets" title="Columbus Blue Jackets">Columbus Blue Jackets</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Playing_career">Playing career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jarmo_Kek%C3%A4l%C3%A4inen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Playing career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kekäläinen began his hockey career in his native Finland, playing for four years in Finland with a number of teams as well as for Finland in the 1986 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Junior_Ice_Hockey_Championship" title="World Junior Ice Hockey Championship">World Junior Ice Hockey Championship</a>. Kekäläinen then played for the <a href="/wiki/Clarkson_Golden_Knights" title="Clarkson Golden Knights">Clarkson Golden Knights</a> for three years, establishing himself and gaining interest from professional teams. In his second year with the Knights, he scored 44 points in 31 games and was named to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ECAC_Hockey_League" title="ECAC Hockey League">ECAC Hockey League</a> first all star team. In his third year with the Knights, he scored 18 points in eight games before signing with the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Bruins" title="Boston Bruins">Boston Bruins</a>. Kekäläinen played parts of two seasons with the Bruins before returning to Finland, where he played for another two years. For the 1993-1994 season, Kekäläinen went back to North America, where he signed with the <a href="/wiki/Ottawa_Senators" title="Ottawa Senators">Ottawa Senators</a>. He played briefly with the Senators that season, playing in 28 games. The following season, Kekäläinen played in <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> before retiring in 1995 because of knee problems which were caused by injuries.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Executive_career">Executive career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jarmo_Kek%C3%A4l%C3%A4inen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Executive career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Immediately after retirement Kekäläinen became involved with front office work in many capacities. He was General Manager of <a href="/wiki/HIFK" title="HIFK">IFK Helsinki</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SM-liiga" title="SM-liiga">Finnish Elite League</a> from 1995 to 1999, during which time IFK won one <a href="/wiki/Kanada-malja" title="Kanada-malja">Kanada-malja</a> and finished as the runner-up to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TPS_(ice_hockey)" title="TPS (ice hockey)">TPS</a> the following year. During this time with IFK, Kekäläinen also worked in several capacities with the Ottawa Senators. After he left Finland in 1999 Kekäläinen became director of Player Personnel of the Senators until 2002. While with the Senators he helped in picking future NHL stars <a href="/wiki/Mari%C3%A1n_Hossa" title="Marián Hossa">Marian Hossa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Martin_Havl%C3%A1t" title="Martin Havlát">Martin Havlat</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ray_Emery" title="Ray Emery">Ray Emery</a>. In 2002 Kekäläinen joined the St. Louis Blues as assistant general manager and director of amateur scouting, where he helped draft much of the core of the team in the early 2010s, including defenseman <a href="/wiki/Alex_Pietrangelo" title="Alex Pietrangelo">Alex Pietrangelo</a> and forwards <a href="/wiki/T._J._Oshie" title="T. J. Oshie">T. J. Oshie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Patrik_Berglund" title="Patrik Berglund">Patrik Berglund</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Perron" title="David Perron">David Perron</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/David_Backes_(ice_hockey)" title="David Backes (ice hockey)">David Backes</a>. After being passed over for the Blues' general manager job (losing out to <a href="/wiki/Doug_Armstrong" title="Doug Armstrong">Doug Armstrong</a>), Kekäläinen returned to Finland to take the general manager job with <a href="/wiki/Jokerit" title="Jokerit">Jokerit</a>.
</p><p>While with Jokerit, Kekäläinen had an unwritten escape clause with ownership that permitted him to leave if he was offered a general manager job in the NHL. That opportunity came on February 13, 2013, when after <a href="/wiki/Scott_Howson" title="Scott Howson">Scott Howson</a> was fired the previous day, the <a href="/wiki/Columbus_Blue_Jackets" title="Columbus Blue Jackets">Columbus Blue Jackets</a>' president of hockey operations <a href="/wiki/John_Davidson_(ice_hockey)" title="John Davidson (ice hockey)">John Davidson</a>, under whom Kekäläinen had worked while with the Blues, hired Kekäläinen to be the new general manager of the Blue Jackets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> As a result, Kekäläinen became the first European general manager in the National Hockey League.
</p>
