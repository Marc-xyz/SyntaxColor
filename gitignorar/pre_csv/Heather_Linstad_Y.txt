<p><b>Heather Linstad</b> is an ice hockey coach, most prominently with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Connecticut_Huskies_women%27s_ice_hockey" title="Connecticut Huskies women's ice hockey">Connecticut Huskies</a> from 2000 to 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Coach_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Coach-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coaching_career">Coaching career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heather_Linstad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Coaching career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prior to beginning her coaching career, she was a college <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey" title="Ice hockey">ice hockey</a> player for the <a href="/wiki/Providence_Friars_women%27s_ice_hockey" title="Providence Friars women's ice hockey">Providence Friars women's ice hockey</a> team, earning the <a href="/wiki/ECAC_Hockey" title="ECAC Hockey">ECAC</a> player of the year in 1989.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LinstadAwarded_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-LinstadAwarded-5">[5]</a></sup> She was then head coach for eight seasons at <a href="/wiki/Northeastern_Huskies_women%27s_ice_hockey" title="Northeastern Huskies women's ice hockey">Northeastern Huskies</a> from 1992 to 2000, winning the ECAC coach of the year 1994. In 2000, Linstad became the women's ice hockey coach at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Connecticut" title="University of Connecticut">University of Connecticut</a>, coaching the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Connecticut_Huskies_women%27s_ice_hockey" title="Connecticut Huskies women's ice hockey">Connecticut Huskies</a> for thirteen seasons. On February 13, 2010, Linstad obtained her 300th win as a head coach, with a 4–1 over her alma matter, the Providence Friars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Win_300_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Win_300-3">[3]</a></sup> Linstad abruptly resigned from her coaching position at the University of Connecticut on March 13, 2013, after two poor seasons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-resign_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-resign-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2016, she would be awarded the Women's Hockey Founders Award by the <a href="/wiki/American_Hockey_Coaches_Association" title="American Hockey Coaches Association">American Hockey Coaches Association</a> for her contributions to the growth and development of women's ice hockey in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LinstadAwarded_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-LinstadAwarded-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>Linstad was the head coach of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_women%27s_national_ice_hockey_team" title="United States women's national ice hockey team">United States</a> U-18 women's ice hockey team through the 2011–12 year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-U18_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-U18-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>On January 29, 2016, Lindstad was named as head coach of the <a href="/wiki/Connecticut_Whale_(NWHL)" title="Connecticut Whale (NWHL)">Connecticut Whale</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Women%27s_Hockey_League_(2015%E2%80%93)" title="National Women's Hockey League (2015–)">National Women's Hockey League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Coach_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Coach-4">[4]</a></sup> She left the team after the 2016–17 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heather_Linstad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A lawsuit filed against the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Connecticut" title="University of Connecticut">University of Connecticut</a> alleges Linstad dismissed a female hockey player from the team for not being stable enough following an alleged on-campus sexual assault by a male hockey player in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-coverup_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-coverup-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unstable_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-unstable-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
