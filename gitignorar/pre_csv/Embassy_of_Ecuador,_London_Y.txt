<p>The <b>Embassy of Ecuador in London</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Diplomatic_mission" title="Diplomatic mission">diplomatic mission</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuador</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_London_Diplomatic_List_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_London_Diplomatic_List-1">[1]</a></sup> It is headed by the ambassador of Ecuador to the United Kingdom. It is located in the <a href="/wiki/Knightsbridge" title="Knightsbridge">Knightsbridge</a> area of London, in the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Borough_of_Kensington_and_Chelsea" title="Royal Borough of Kensington and Chelsea">Royal Borough of Kensington and Chelsea</a>.  It is in an apartment building which also houses the <a href="/wiki/Embassy_of_Colombia,_London" title="Embassy of Colombia, London">Embassy of Colombia</a> as well as a number of residential apartments, near <a href="/wiki/Harrods" title="Harrods">Harrods</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hyde_Park,_London" title="Hyde Park, London">Hyde Park</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hans_Place" title="Hans Place">Hans Place</a>, at 3 Hans Crescent<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> at the intersection with Basil Street, and it is close to <a href="/wiki/Knightsbridge_tube_station" title="Knightsbridge tube station">Knightsbridge</a> Underground station.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>For almost seven years the embassy was famously home to the Australian activist and journalist <a href="/wiki/Julian_Assange" title="Julian Assange">Julian Assange</a>, who initially entered on 19 June 2012 claiming <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diplomatic_asylum" title="Diplomatic asylum">diplomatic asylum</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ft20120815_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-ft20120815-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytimes20120816_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes20120816-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC19259623_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC19259623-6">[6]</a></sup> which was granted by the Ecuadorian government on 16 August 2012. He had absconded in breach of bail after dismissal of his appeal by the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Supreme Court of the United Kingdom">Supreme Court of the United Kingdom</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytimes20120816_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes20120816-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBCNews_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBCNews-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mmrree.gob.ec_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-mmrree.gob.ec-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Functions">Functions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Embassy_of_Ecuador,_London&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Functions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The embassy is charged with representing the interests of the <a href="/wiki/President_of_Ecuador" title="President of Ecuador">president</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Government_of_Ecuador" title="Government of Ecuador">government of Ecuador</a>, improving diplomatic relations between Ecuador and the accredited countries, promoting and improving the image and standing of Ecuador in the accredited nations, promoting the <a href="/wiki/Culture_of_Ecuador" title="Culture of Ecuador">culture of Ecuador</a>, encouraging and facilitating tourism to and from Ecuador, and ensuring the safety of Ecuadorians abroad.
</p><p>The structure that houses the embassy is a white <a href="/wiki/Stucco" title="Stucco">stucco</a>-fronted red-brick building on <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hans_Crescent&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hans Crescent (page does not exist)">Hans Crescent</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Knightsbridge" title="Knightsbridge">Knightsbridge</a> area of <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>. The embassy is a suite of rooms occupying part of the <a href="/wiki/Storey" title="Storey">ground floor</a> of the building, which is an apartment block.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytimes20120816_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes20120816-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>Ecuador also maintains a consulate at 144-146 Kings Cross Road, London WC1X 9DU<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and an Office of the Naval Assistant and Permanent Representative to the <a href="/wiki/International_Maritime_Organization" title="International Maritime Organization">International Maritime Organization</a> at 61 Wimbledon Hill Road, <a href="/wiki/Wimbledon,_London" title="Wimbledon, London">Wimbledon</a>, London.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_London_Diplomatic_List_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-The_London_Diplomatic_List-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="Julian_Assange.27s_refuge"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Julian_Assange's_refuge">Julian Assange's refuge</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Embassy_of_Ecuador,_London&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Julian Assange's refuge">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Julian_Assange_in_Ecuadorian_Embassy.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2848" data-file-width="4288" decoding="async" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Julian_Assange_in_Ecuadorian_Embassy.jpg/220px-Julian_Assange_in_Ecuadorian_Embassy.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Julian_Assange_in_Ecuadorian_Embassy.jpg/330px-Julian_Assange_in_Ecuadorian_Embassy.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Julian_Assange_in_Ecuadorian_Embassy.jpg/440px-Julian_Assange_in_Ecuadorian_Embassy.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Julian_Assange_in_Ecuadorian_Embassy.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Julian Assange at the Ecuadorian embassy in August 2012</div></div></div>
<p>The co-founder of <a href="/wiki/WikiLeaks" title="WikiLeaks">WikiLeaks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Julian_Assange" title="Julian Assange">Julian Assange</a>, was a resident of the embassy for seven years after entering it on 19 June 2012 to claim <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diplomatic_asylum" title="Diplomatic asylum">diplomatic asylum</a> after being wanted by Swedish authorities for questioning over four alleged sexual offences.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ft20120815_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-ft20120815-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytimes20120816_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes20120816-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC19259623_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-BBC19259623-6">[6]</a></sup> Assange's asylum request was eventually granted by the Ecuadorian government in August 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytimes20120816_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes20120816-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBCNews_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-BBCNews-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mmrree.gob.ec_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-mmrree.gob.ec-8">[8]</a></sup> The Swedish prosecutors dropped their investigation in May 2017, claiming they could not expect the Ecuadorian Embassy to communicate reliably with Assange with respect to the case.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>The British government had suggested it could use its discretionary powers under the <a href="/wiki/Diplomatic_and_Consular_Premises_Act_1987" title="Diplomatic and Consular Premises Act 1987">Diplomatic and Consular Premises Act 1987</a> to enter the embassy and arrest Assange<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC19259623_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-BBC19259623-6">[6]</a></sup> after giving the embassy due notice. However, it later retracted the suggestion, following condemnation from Ecuador's Foreign Minister <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Pati%C3%B1o" title="Ricardo Patiño">Ricardo Patiño</a> and President <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Correa" title="Rafael Correa">Rafael Correa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aljazeera_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-aljazeera-11">[11]</a></sup> Patiño described the British government's statement as "a clear breach of international law and the protocols set out in the <a href="/wiki/Vienna_Convention_on_Diplomatic_Relations" title="Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations">Vienna Convention</a>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ecuador%27s_London_embassy_16_August_2012.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3079" data-file-width="3959" decoding="async" height="171" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Ecuador%27s_London_embassy_16_August_2012.jpg/220px-Ecuador%27s_London_embassy_16_August_2012.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Ecuador%27s_London_embassy_16_August_2012.jpg/330px-Ecuador%27s_London_embassy_16_August_2012.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Ecuador%27s_London_embassy_16_August_2012.jpg/440px-Ecuador%27s_London_embassy_16_August_2012.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ecuador%27s_London_embassy_16_August_2012.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Press outside the embassy in August 2012</div></div></div>
<p>On 16 August 2012, police and protesters gathered outside the embassy, with reports of minor scuffles between the two groups and arrests of some of the protesters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> On 19 August 2012, Assange made a speech from a low<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian-balcony-2012-08-19_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-balcony-2012-08-19-14">[14]</a></sup> balcony of the embassy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian-live-2012-08-19_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-live-2012-08-19-15">[15]</a></sup> Assange's remarks were prefaced by a statement from <a href="/wiki/Baltasar_Garz%C3%B3n" title="Baltasar Garzón">Baltasar Garzón</a>, who is heading his legal team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian-live-2012-08-19_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-live-2012-08-19-15">[15]</a></sup> This was followed by protests in Ecuador outside the <a href="/wiki/Embassy_of_the_United_Kingdom,_Quito" title="Embassy of the United Kingdom, Quito">British embassy in Quito</a>, as well as support for Correa's approval of the asylum request.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aljazeera_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-aljazeera-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>On 22 August 2012, the Ecuadorian President Rafael Correa stated that Assange would be allowed to stay in the embassy indefinitely, but that Ecuador would be open to negotiations with the British government if it withdrew its threats to storm the embassy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2014, Assange called a press conference and announced he would be leaving the embassy "soon".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AssangeEcuador_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-AssangeEcuador-18">[18]</a></sup>
</p><p>Press photographs taken outside the embassy were reported to have shown police notes stating that Assange was to be arrested "under all circumstances".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The policing of the embassy during the first two years of Assange's stay reportedly cost £6.5 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Before the police guard was lifted in February 2015, costs of policing Assange had reached £10 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup>
</p><p><i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> revealed in May 2018 that Ecuador had employed an international security company for more than five years to monitor Assange's visitors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup>
</p><p>On 3 April 2019, WikiLeaks claimed that the Ecuadorian embassy would expel Assange within a few hours or days but Jose Valencia, Foreign Minister of Ecuador called it a rumour. Two days later the statements of Assange's departure came after the Ecuadorian President Lenin Moreno accused Assange of repeatedly violating the terms of asylum.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> On 11 April 2019, Assange was arrested by British authorities who were invited into the embassy by the Ecuadorian government.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup>
</p>
