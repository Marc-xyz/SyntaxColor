<p><b>George Edward Stanley</b> (July 15, 1942 – February 7, 2011) was a teacher at <a href="/wiki/Cameron_University" title="Cameron University">Cameron University</a> and author of <a href="/wiki/Short_story" title="Short story">short stories</a> for boys under the pseudonym <b>M. T. Coffin</b>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Edward_Stanley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>George Edward Stanley was born in <a href="/wiki/Memphis,_Texas" title="Memphis, Texas">Memphis, Texas</a> in 1942 and died on February 7, 2011 from an <a href="/wiki/Aneurysm" title="Aneurysm">aneurysm</a> at the age of 68 years.
</p><p>From 1967 to 1969, he taught English to foreigners of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/East_Texas_State_University" title="East Texas State University">East Texas State University</a>; the following year he held the same role at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Kansas" title="University of Kansas">University of Kansas</a> (<a href="/wiki/Lawton,_Kansas" title="Lawton, Kansas">Lawton</a>). In 1970 he moved to Cameron University (always in Lawton), where he worked until 1973 as an assistant professor, associate professor from 1973 to 1976, later becoming a professor of Middle Eastern and African languages in 1979.
</p><p>In 1979 he published his first book <i>Mini-Mysteries</i> began to write children's books, career that led him to write 102 novels (including also one republished in Italian).
</p>
