<p><b>WRFT</b> (91.5 FM) is a <a href="/wiki/High_school_radio" title="High school radio">high school radio</a> station <a href="/wiki/Broadcasting" title="Broadcasting">broadcasting</a> from <a href="/wiki/Franklin_Central_High_School" title="Franklin Central High School">Franklin Central High School</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indianapolis,_Indiana" title="Indianapolis, Indiana">Indianapolis, Indiana</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. The station is currently owned by <a href="/wiki/Franklin_Township_Community_School_Corporation" title="Franklin Township Community School Corporation">Franklin Township Community School Corporation</a>.
</p><p>WRFT is a high school radio station operating at 91.5FM. It is located in the southern part of Indianapolis in Franklin Township and is housed in Franklin Central High School.  The station has 130 watts of power and covers about two-thirds of Indianapolis. WRFT has always been operated by high school students with a general manager in charge of the overall operation. It is on the air 24 hours a day, every day of the year after going automated three years ago.
</p><p>Generally, WRFT programs to serve Franklin Township and broadcasts programs and announcements pertaining to the immediate area. WRFT was the first station in the area, and one of the first in the state, to feature a regular remote broadcast during the high school football season.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> "The Friday Morning Sports Show," originates from the local Chick-fil-A every Friday morning from 6–7 o'clock.
</p>
