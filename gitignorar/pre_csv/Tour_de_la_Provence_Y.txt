<p>The <b>Tour de la Provence</b> is an early-season <a href="/wiki/Road_bicycle_racing#Stage_races" title="Road bicycle racing">bicycle stage race</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Provence" title="Provence">Provence</a> region of <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>. It is organized by southern French newspaper <a href="/wiki/La_Provence" title="La Provence">La Provence</a>, which serves as the race's title sponsor for its first three years. It is held in February, as a <a href="/wiki/UCI_race_classifications" title="UCI race classifications">2.1 event</a> on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UCI_Europe_Tour" title="UCI Europe Tour">UCI Europe Tour</a> calendar.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-La_Provence_lance_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-La_Provence_lance-2">[2]</a></sup>The race will become part of the new <a href="/wiki/UCI_ProSeries" title="UCI ProSeries">UCI ProSeries</a> in 2020. <i>La Provence</i> has entrusted the organization of the event to Serge Pascal, who also organizes the Tour du Haut Var. The inaugural edition of 2016 was won by <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Voeckler" title="Thomas Voeckler">Thomas Voeckler</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tour_de_la_Provence&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Organizers intend to build on the tradition of the former <a href="/wiki/Tour_du_Vaucluse" title="Tour du Vaucluse">Tour du Vaucluse</a> and the Tour du Sud-Est, two races in the same region that have disappeared over time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-La_Provence_lance_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-La_Provence_lance-2">[2]</a></sup> The event is part of a series of cycling stage races being held in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_of_France" title="South of France">south of France</a> in February, following the <a href="/wiki/%C3%89toile_de_Bess%C3%A8ges" title="Étoile de Bessèges">Étoile de Bessèges</a>, <a href="/wiki/La_M%C3%A9diterran%C3%A9enne" title="La Méditerranéenne">La Méditerranéenne</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Tour_du_Haut_Var" title="Tour du Haut Var">Tour du Haut Var</a>. The Tour La Provence takes place mid-week between the Tour du Haut-Var and the <a href="/wiki/Classic_Sud-Ard%C3%A8che" title="Classic Sud-Ardèche">Classic Sud-Ardèche</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-new_French_race_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-new_French_race-5">[5]</a></sup> These races are competed mainly by French teams and are considered early-season preparations for <a href="/wiki/Paris%E2%80%93Nice" title="Paris–Nice">Paris–Nice</a>, the first European <a href="/wiki/UCI_World_Tour" title="UCI World Tour">World Tour</a> event in March.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-new_French_race_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-new_French_race-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Route">Route</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tour_de_la_Provence&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Route">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The race is run over three stages in the <a href="/wiki/Bouches-du-Rh%C3%B4ne" title="Bouches-du-Rhône">Bouches-du-Rhône</a> department of the <a href="/wiki/Provence-Alpes-C%C3%B4te_d%27Azur" title="Provence-Alpes-Côte d'Azur">Provence-Alpes-Côte d'Azur</a> region, in the <a href="/wiki/Southern_France" title="Southern France">south of France</a>. The final stage finishes on the <a href="/wiki/Canebi%C3%A8re" title="Canebière">Canebière</a>, the historic main street in the old quarter of <a href="/wiki/Marseille" title="Marseille">Marseille</a>, in front of the city hall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-La_Provence_lance_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-La_Provence_lance-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
