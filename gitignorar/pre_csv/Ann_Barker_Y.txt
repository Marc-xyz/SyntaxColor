<p><b>Ann Patricia Barker</b> (born 19 January 1952) is a former <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australian</a> politician. She was a <a href="/wiki/Australian_Labor_Party_(Victorian_Branch)" title="Australian Labor Party (Victorian Branch)">Labor Party</a> member of the <a href="/wiki/Victorian_Legislative_Assembly" title="Victorian Legislative Assembly">Victorian Legislative Assembly</a> from 1999 to 2014, representing the electorate of <a href="/wiki/Electoral_district_of_Oakleigh" title="Electoral district of Oakleigh">Oakleigh</a>. She previously represented the electorate of <a href="/wiki/Electoral_district_of_Bentleigh" title="Electoral district of Bentleigh">Bentleigh</a> from 1988 to 1992.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-parlbio_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-parlbio-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Barker was born in <a href="/wiki/Tasmania" title="Tasmania">Tasmania</a>. She worked as an electorate officer to former federal MP <a href="/wiki/Joan_Child" title="Joan Child">Joan Child</a> before being elected to the <a href="/wiki/Victorian_Legislative_Assembly" title="Victorian Legislative Assembly">Victorian Legislative Assembly</a> seat of <a href="/wiki/Electoral_district_of_Bentleigh" title="Electoral district of Bentleigh">Bentleigh</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1988_Victorian_state_election" title="1988 Victorian state election">1988 state election</a>, succeeding retiring ALP member <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Hockley" title="Gordon Hockley">Gordon Hockley</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-parlbio_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-parlbio-1">[1]</a></sup> She was seen as a potential ministerial candidate towards the end of her first term, but was twice overlooked by then-Premier <a href="/wiki/Joan_Kirner" title="Joan Kirner">Joan Kirner</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> These ambitions were to be short-lived, as she was one of many Labor members to be defeated amidst the party's landslide defeat at the <a href="/wiki/1992_Victorian_state_election" title="1992 Victorian state election">1992 state election</a>, losing to Liberal <a href="/wiki/Inga_Peulich" title="Inga Peulich">Inga Peulich</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-parlbio_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-parlbio-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>After her 1992 election defeat, Barker was employed as an advisor to federal MP <a href="/wiki/Simon_Crean" title="Simon Crean">Simon Crean</a>, then a minister in the <a href="/wiki/Paul_Keating" title="Paul Keating">Keating</a> government. She left Crean's office in 1996 to work as an office manager with the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Victorian_Court_Information_and_Welfare_Network&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Victorian Court Information and Welfare Network (page does not exist)">Victorian Court Information and Welfare Network</a>, but returned to Crean's office the following year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-parlbio_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-parlbio-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Parkinson_1998_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Parkinson_1998-4">[4]</a></sup> She was the Labor candidate for the seat of <a href="/wiki/Electoral_district_of_Oakleigh" title="Electoral district of Oakleigh">Oakleigh</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1996_Victorian_state_election" title="1996 Victorian state election">1996 election</a>, but was very narrowly defeated by incumbent Liberal member <a href="/wiki/Denise_McGill" title="Denise McGill">Denise McGill</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> She was again the Labor candidate for the seat at the <a href="/wiki/1999_Victorian_state_election" title="1999 Victorian state election">1999 election</a>, having resisted pressure to stand aside for star candidate <a href="/wiki/Mary_Delahunty" title="Mary Delahunty">Mary Delahunty</a>, and amidst the party's statewide victory, succeeded in defeating McGill.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Parkinson_1998_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Parkinson_1998-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>Barker was appointed Parliamentary Secretary for Training and Higher Education after the party's victory at the <a href="/wiki/2002_Victorian_state_election" title="2002 Victorian state election">2002 election</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-parlbio_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-parlbio-1">[1]</a></sup> A relatively low-profile MP, she spoke less than only one other member of either house of parliament in 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In 2006, she was a vocal supporter of <a href="/wiki/Martin_Pakula" title="Martin Pakula">Martin Pakula</a>'s unsuccessful challenge to the preselection of her former employer, Simon Crean.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> She was appointed Deputy Speaker of the Legislative Assembly after the <a href="/wiki/2006_Victorian_state_election" title="2006 Victorian state election">2006 election</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-parlbio_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-parlbio-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>On 25 May 2013, Barker became the longest serving female Labor MP in the Victorian Parliament.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
