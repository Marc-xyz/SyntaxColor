<p><b>Jo Manning</b> (born 11 December 1923) is a Canadian etcher and painter.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jo_Manning&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Joan Elizabeth Manning was born in <a href="/wiki/Sidney,_British_Columbia" title="Sidney, British Columbia">Sidney, British Columbia</a>, on 11 December 1923.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEJo_Manning_Canadian_Etching,_Worthpoint_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEJo_Manning_Canadian_Etching,_Worthpoint-1">[1]</a></sup>
She spent her early childhood on <a href="/wiki/Vancouver_Island" title="Vancouver Island">Vancouver Island</a>, then moved to <a href="/wiki/Amherstburg" title="Amherstburg">Amherstburg</a>, Ontario.
She studied at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ontario_College_of_Art" title="Ontario College of Art">Ontario College of Art</a> in Toronto and graduated in 1945.
She married, and became known as Jo Rothfels.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEManning2014_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEManning2014-2">[2]</a></sup>
She returned to the OCA in 1960 to study printmaking as a special student.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHellerHeller2013361_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHellerHeller2013361-3">[3]</a></sup>
She took up etching in 1962. After a divorce in 1971 she reverted to her maiden name.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEManning2014_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEManning2014-2">[2]</a></sup>
In 1971 she studied process camera techniques and color separation at <a href="/wiki/George_Brown_College" title="George Brown College">George Brown College</a>, Toronto.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHellerHeller2013361_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHellerHeller2013361-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_career">Later career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jo_Manning&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Later career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Manning taught in a mobile printmaking workshop between 1965 and 1970 for a community program of the Ontario Department of Education. She taught or ran workshops at <a href="/wiki/Centennial_College" title="Centennial College">Centennial College</a> (1967–71) and <a href="/wiki/Sheridan_College" title="Sheridan College">Sheridan College</a> (1971-74), and in the summer at <a href="/wiki/Hockley_Valley" title="Hockley Valley">Hockley Valley</a> School of Art (1970–74), <a href="/wiki/Elliot_Lake" title="Elliot Lake">Elliot Lake</a> (1970–72), <a href="/wiki/University_of_Toronto" title="University of Toronto">University of Toronto</a> (1975) and various other places.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHellerHeller2013361_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHellerHeller2013361-3">[3]</a></sup>
She became an executive member of the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Society_of_Graphic_Art" title="Canadian Society of Graphic Art">Canadian Society of Graphic Art</a> and a member of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadian_Society_of_Painter-Etchers_and_Engravers" title="Canadian Society of Painter-Etchers and Engravers">Canadian Society of Painter-Etchers and Engravers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEJo_Manning,_Westland_Gallery_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEJo_Manning,_Westland_Gallery-4">[4]</a></sup>
The two organizations merged in 1976 to form the Print and Drawing Council of Canada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTELacroix2006_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTELacroix2006-5">[5]</a></sup>
Jo Manning was a founding member of the new Council.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEJo_Manning,_Westland_Gallery_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEJo_Manning,_Westland_Gallery-4">[4]</a></sup>
Around 1980 she became ill from exposure to chemicals and moved away from etching into ink drawing and oil and watercolor painting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEManning2014_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEManning2014-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Exhibitions_and_reception">Exhibitions and reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jo_Manning&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Exhibitions and reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Manning's work has been included in many group shows in North America, Europe and Australia. 
She has held a number of solo exhibitions including:
</p>
<ul><li>Pollack Gallery, Toronto (1965, 1968)</li>
<li>Gallery Pascal, Toronto (1974, 1977, 1980)</li>
<li>Mira Godard Gallery, Montreal (1976)</li>
<li>Earlscourt Gallery, Hamilton, Ontario (1979)</li>
<li>Gadatsky Gallery, Toronto (1984)</li>
<li>Bishop's University, Lennoxville, Quebec</li></ul>
<p>Manning has won many awards including a gold medal at the 2nd Print Biennale (1970) in <a href="/wiki/Florence" title="Florence">Florence</a>, Italy, first prize at the 4th American Print Biennale (1970) in <a href="/wiki/Santiago" title="Santiago">Santiago</a>, Chile, and the medal of honor at the International Graphic Biennale (1980) in <a href="/wiki/Frechen" title="Frechen">Frechen</a>, Germany. Her work is held in many private, corporate and public collections including the <a href="/wiki/National_Gallery_of_Canada" title="National Gallery of Canada">National Gallery of Canada</a> in Ottawa, <a href="/wiki/Montreal_Museum_of_Fine_Arts" title="Montreal Museum of Fine Arts">Montreal Museum of Fine Arts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Art_Gallery_of_Windsor" title="Art Gallery of Windsor">Art Gallery of Windsor</a> and many others.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHellerHeller2013361_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHellerHeller2013361-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
