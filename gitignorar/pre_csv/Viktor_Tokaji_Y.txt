<p><b>Viktor Tokaji</b> (born January 11, 1977 in <a href="/wiki/Duna%C3%BAjv%C3%A1ros" title="Dunaújváros">Dunaújváros</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a>) is a Hungarian former professional <a href="/wiki/Ice_hockey" title="Ice hockey">ice hockey</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Defenseman_(ice_hockey)" title="Defenseman (ice hockey)">defenceman</a> who most notably played for <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Hockey_League" title="Austrian Hockey League">Austrian Hockey League</a> (EBEL) side, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alba_Vol%C3%A1n_Sz%C3%A9kesfeh%C3%A9rv%C3%A1r" title="Alba Volán Székesfehérvár">Alba Volán Székesfehérvár</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Playing_career">Playing career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Viktor_Tokaji&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Playing career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tokaji originally played for Dunaferr SE Dunaújváros from 1993 to 2006. In the 2006–07 season he transferred to <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>, where he played for <a href="/wiki/Huddinge_IK" title="Huddinge IK">Huddinge IK</a>. From 2007 to 2015, he had been one of the leaders of his team, Alba Volán Székesfehérvár.
</p><p>Tokaji has played over 150 games for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hungary_national_ice_hockey_team" title="Hungary national ice hockey team">Hungary national ice hockey team</a>.
</p>
