<p><i><b>Otaku no Seiza: An Adventure in the Otaku Galaxy</b></i><span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">おたくの星座</span>, lit. "Constellation of the Geeks")</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Role-playing_video_game" title="Role-playing video game">role-playing video game</a> that was developed by Advance Communications and published by M&amp;M for the <a href="/wiki/Nintendo_Entertainment_System" title="Nintendo Entertainment System">Famicom</a> in 1991. <i><b>Aurora Quest: Otaku no Seiza in Another World</b></i><span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">オーロラクエスト おたくの星座</span>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Video_game_remake" title="Video game remake">remake</a> of the Famicom version for the <a href="/wiki/TurboGrafx-16#TurboGrafx-CD" title="TurboGrafx-16">Turbo CD</a> by <a href="/wiki/Pack-In-Video" title="Pack-In-Video">Pack-In-Video</a> in December 1993 and featured updated graphics. Neither version was released outside Japan. An <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> based on the games was also released in Japan in 1994.
</p><p>The game is a combination of <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction" title="Science fiction">science fiction</a> and <a href="/wiki/Comedy" title="Comedy">comedy</a> in which a nameless hero arrives in a city where women have stolen the rights of men. The player controls the man as he tries to free men in the city. <i>Otaku no Seiza</i> features turn-based battles, similar to the <i><a href="/wiki/Dragon_Quest" title="Dragon Quest">Dragon Quest</a></i> series.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gameplay">Gameplay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Otaku_no_Seiza:_An_Adventure_in_the_Otaku_Galaxy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Gameplay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Otaku no Seiza</i> features standard role-playing video gameplay. Players control a party of characters through <a href="/wiki/Video_game_graphics#Top-down_perspective" title="Video game graphics">overhead</a> environments, including towns, dungeons, and a <a href="/wiki/Overworld" title="Overworld">world map</a>. While on the world map or in a dungeon, the party will <a href="/wiki/Random_encounter" title="Random encounter">randomly encounter</a> enemies. The perspective switches to a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/First-person_(video_games)" title="First-person (video games)">first-person</a> mode and the enemies and party members take <a href="/wiki/Turn-based_strategy" title="Turn-based strategy">turns</a> attacking or healing.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Otaku_no_Seiza:_An_Adventure_in_the_Otaku_Galaxy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Otaku no Seiza</i> tells the story of Fuyuu City, a place built in space far in the future. Aurora, a group of five attractive and powerful women, control the city. Men in the city are treated poorly compared to women, until the protagonist finds himself in the middle of the city with <a href="/wiki/Amnesia" title="Amnesia">amnesia</a>. Outraged, the protagonist decides to defeat Aurora and gain rights for the discriminated men of the city.
</p>
