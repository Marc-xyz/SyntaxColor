<p><b>Mayur Puri</b> is an Indian screenwriter, lyricist, actor and film-maker working in <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">Mumbai</a>. He wrote songs, screenplays and dialogue for several movies, including <i> <a href="/wiki/Om_Shanti_Om" title="Om Shanti Om">Om Shanti Om</a></i> (2007) starring <a href="/wiki/Shah_Rukh_Khan" title="Shah Rukh Khan">Shahrukh Khan</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/Farah_Khan" title="Farah Khan">Farah Khan</a>, where Mayur also did a cameo appearance (as the wanna be Gujju Director of 'Apahij Pyar'<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Om_Shanti_Om_(2007)_Yahoo_Movies_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Om_Shanti_Om_(2007)_Yahoo_Movies-1">[1]</a></sup>). He wrote dialogue for the Farah Khan film <i><a href="/wiki/Happy_New_Year_(2014_film)" title="Happy New Year (2014 film)">Happy New Year</a></i> (2014).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Happy_New_Year_Credits_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Happy_New_Year_Credits-2">[2]</a></sup> On 17 November 2014 Library of the Academy of Motion Picture Arts &amp; Sciences selected the screenplay of <i>Happy New Year</i> for its permanent Core Collection.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HNY_in_Oscar_Library_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-HNY_in_Oscar_Library-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Mayur wrote the dialogue for India's first 3D Dance film <i><a href="/wiki/ABCD:_Any_Body_Can_Dance" title="ABCD: Any Body Can Dance">ABCD: Any Body Can Dance</a></i>. He also penned the songs for 'ABCD: Any Body Can Dance'. After the success of 'ABCD: Any Body Can Dance', Mayur has written dialogue and songs for the second installment of the franchise <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ABCD:_Any_Body_Can_Dance_2" title="ABCD: Any Body Can Dance 2">ABCD: Any Body Can Dance 2</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ABCD2_Full_Cast_&amp;_Crew_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-ABCD2_Full_Cast_&amp;_Crew-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Mayur recently directed Firdaws (2017), his first short film that has been doing festival rounds and has been the official selection in over 36 international festivals. Mayur has won 16 International Awards for Firdaws.
</p><p>Mayur Puri has donned many hats over his two-decades-long Bollywood career as director, film writer, lyricist and actor, but he primarily is and always will remain a passionate storyteller at heart - a role he’d never grow out of. Mayur has been one of the game changers responsible for the localization, transcreation and adaptations of various big studio Hollywood films into Hindi translated blockbusters. He has penned the Hindi translation of big-ticket Hollywood films like <a href="/wiki/The_Lion_King_(2019_film)" title="The Lion King (2019 film)">The Lion King</a> (2019), <a href="/wiki/Avengers:_Endgame" title="Avengers: Endgame">Avengers – Endgame (2019)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mowgli:_Legend_of_the_Jungle" title="Mowgli: Legend of the Jungle">Mowgli – Legend of the Jungle</a> (2018), <a href="/wiki/Pirates_of_the_Caribbean:_Dead_Men_Tell_No_Tales" title="Pirates of the Caribbean: Dead Men Tell No Tales">Pirates of the Caribbean – Dead Men Tell No Tales</a> (2017) and <a href="/wiki/Thor:_Ragnarok" title="Thor: Ragnarok">Thor Ragnarok</a> (2017) to name a few. As of now, Mayur is gearing up for his directorial debut.
</p><p><br/>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mayur_Puri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mayur hails from <a href="/wiki/Ahmedabad" title="Ahmedabad">Ahmedabad</a>, Gujarat, where he did most of his education, including a Master of Arts degree from University School of Languages and F.Y. Diploma in Dramatics from the Drama Department of Gujarat College (topped the university)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Diploma_in_Dramatics_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Diploma_in_Dramatics-5">[5]</a></sup>. He spent his early years writing and directing for local theatre and television, and after 8 years of experience, he moved from Ahmedabad to Mumbai in late 1999 and immediately landed a job with <a href="/wiki/Sanjay_Gadhvi" title="Sanjay Gadhvi">Sanjay Gadhvi</a>, an Indian film director as his Chief Assistant Director. From the year 2000, he started writing screenplay and dialogues for films that he was assisting on. During his first year in Mumbai, he also wrote and acted in lead for a Gujarati play: <i>Amastaa Amastaa (2001)</i>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_career">Film career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mayur_Puri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Film career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1999, Mayur started in the film industry, by assisting Sanjay Gadhvi. They worked on three films together, one of which was <a href="/wiki/Tere_Liye_(film)" title="Tere Liye (film)">Tere Liye</a>, where Mayur was the chief assistant director. <a href="/wiki/Mere_Yaar_Ki_Shaadi_Hai" title="Mere Yaar Ki Shaadi Hai">Mere Yaar Ki Shaadi Hai</a> (2002) was next and was Mayur's first as a screenwriter as well. Mere Yaar ki Shaadi Hai was made by <a href="/wiki/Yash_Raj_Films" title="Yash Raj Films">Yash Raj Films</a>. He next worked as an associate director on <a href="/wiki/Dhoom" title="Dhoom">Dhoom</a> (2004), an action film which was a departure from the production house's usual romantic and family-oriented movies.
</p><p>In 2004, Mayur left Yash Raj Films and worked as an independent screenwriter and lyricist. His first film as an independent was <a href="/wiki/My_Name_Is_Anthony_Gonsalves_(film)" title="My Name Is Anthony Gonsalves (film)">My Name is Anthony Gonsalves</a>, an adaptation of <a href="/wiki/Julius_Caesar_(play)" title="Julius Caesar (play)">Julius Caesar</a>). It released to a lukewarm response.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-My_Name_is_Anthony_Gonsalves_Box_Office_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-My_Name_is_Anthony_Gonsalves_Box_Office-6">[6]</a></sup> His big break as a screenwriter came with <a href="/wiki/Om_Shanti_Om" title="Om Shanti Om">Om Shanti Om</a>. The dialogues in Om Shanti Om achieved widespread public appreciation, with phrases 'Ek chukti sindoor' and 'Picture abhi baaki hai mere dost' acquiring a cult status upon the release. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10_Most_memorable_closing_scenes_and_dialogue_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-10_Most_memorable_closing_scenes_and_dialogue-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>Next, Mayur was the creative director for the animation film <a href="/wiki/Jumbo" title="Jumbo">Jumbo</a> (2008) by <a href="/wiki/Percept_Picture_Company" title="Percept Picture Company">Percept Picture Company</a>, where <a href="/wiki/Akshay_Kumar" title="Akshay Kumar">Akshay Kumar</a> voiced the titular character. He co-wrote <a href="/wiki/Blue_(2009_film)" title="Blue (2009 film)"><i>Blue</i></a> (2009) which was the first Indian film to be shot extensively underwater<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Blue_to_be_India's_first_underwater_thriller_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Blue_to_be_India's_first_underwater_thriller-8">[8]</a></sup> in the Bahamas with top technicians from Hollywood.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-James_Bomalick_from_Hollywood_is_the_Action_Director_on_Blue_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-James_Bomalick_from_Hollywood_is_the_Action_Director_on_Blue-9">[9]</a></sup> However, the film failed at the box-office. Mayur then wrote the dialogues for <i><a href="/wiki/Prince_(2010_film)" title="Prince (2010 film)">Prince</a></i>, and also had a supporting role as P.K.
</p><p>In 2009, Mayur took a sabbatical from Bollywood and founded a story-telling institute for young children called Story Circus and conducted a workshop for aspiring film-makers in Goa with the help of Goa University.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Filmgoa_with_Goa_University_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Filmgoa_with_Goa_University-10">[10]</a></sup> While he was busy with his activities Beyond Bollywood, Mayur's next hit at the box-office came in 2011 with <a href="/wiki/Remo_D%27Souza" title="Remo D'Souza">Remo D'Souza</a>'s directorial debut <a href="/wiki/F.A.L.T.U" title="F.A.L.T.U">F.A.L.T.U</a> - the screenplay and dialogues for which Mayur had written a few years back.
</p><p>After one of the sessions in Story Circus, Mayur bumped into <a href="/wiki/Farah_Khan" title="Farah Khan">Farah Khan</a> who had come to pick her kids. While Mayur was still off Bollywood, Farah's <a href="/wiki/Tees_Maar_Khan_(2010_film)" title="Tees Maar Khan (2010 film)">Tees Maar Khan</a> had released and she was starting to script her next project <i><a href="/wiki/Happy_New_Year_(2014_film)" title="Happy New Year (2014 film)">Happy New Year</a></i> which she offered Mayur immediately. Also, around the same time, Pritam came back to Mayur and offered him to work on the songs of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/I_Love_New_Year" title="I Love New Year">I Love New Year</a> and <a href="/wiki/Race_2" title="Race 2">Race 2</a>. By 2012 Mayur returned to full-time writing with additional dialogue and lyrics of Remo D'Souza's next musical 'ABCD: Any Body Can Dance'. For this film's songs, Mayur collaborated with music director duo - Sachin-Jigar. In 2013 he wrote 26 songs as a lyricist.
</p><p><i><a href="/wiki/Happy_New_Year_(2014_film)" title="Happy New Year (2014 film)">Happy New Year</a></i> released in 2014, the dialogues of the film written by Mayur <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Happy_New_Year_-_Trailer_Dialogues_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Happy_New_Year_-_Trailer_Dialogues-11">[11]</a></sup> were very well received. Apart from this, Mayur worked on the script of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ABCD:_Any_Body_Can_Dance_2" title="ABCD: Any Body Can Dance 2">ABCD: Any Body Can Dance 2</a></i>. His recent releases as a lyricist are <i><a href="/wiki/Humshakals" title="Humshakals">Humshakals</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_(2014_film)" title="Entertainment (2014 film)">Entertainment</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Kick_(2014_film)" title="Kick (2014 film)">Kick</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/All_Is_Well_(2015_film)" title="All Is Well (2015 film)">All is Well</a></i> and <i>ABCD: Any Body Can Dance 2</i>.
</p><p>In a TV Interview with Komal Nahata<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mayur_Puri's_Interview_with_Komal_Nahata_(2013)_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mayur_Puri's_Interview_with_Komal_Nahata_(2013)-12">[12]</a></sup> it was indicated that Mayur is working on his directorial debut film which will be announced soon.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_songwriter">Film songwriter</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mayur_Puri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Film songwriter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a songwriter, Mayur has closely worked with composer <a href="/wiki/Pritam" title="Pritam">Pritam</a> on the albums for <a href="/wiki/Race_2" title="Race 2">Race 2</a> (2013), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Singh_Is_Kinng" title="Singh Is Kinng">Singh Is Kinng</a> (2009)  and <a href="/wiki/Pyaar_Ke_Side_Effects" title="Pyaar Ke Side Effects">Pyaar Ke Side Effects</a> (2006), <a href="/wiki/Chocolate_(2005_film)" title="Chocolate (2005 film)">Chocolate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Garam_Masala_(2005_film)" title="Garam Masala (2005 film)">Garam Masala</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pyaar_Ke_Side_Effects" title="Pyaar Ke Side Effects">Pyaar Ke Side Effects</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fight_Club_%E2%80%93_Members_Only" title="Fight Club – Members Only">Fight Club – Members Only</a>, <a href="/wiki/Singh_is_Kinng" title="Singh is Kinng">Singh is Kinng</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jannat_2" title="Jannat 2">Jannat 2</a>, <a href="/wiki/Race_2" title="Race 2">Race 2</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shaadi_Ke_Side_Effects" title="Shaadi Ke Side Effects">Shaadi Ke Side Effects</a>. The latest release is 'Selfie Le Le Re' from <a href="/wiki/Bajrangi_Bhaijaan" title="Bajrangi Bhaijaan">Bajrangi Bhaijaan</a> (2015) starring <a href="/wiki/Salman_Khan" title="Salman Khan">Salman Khan</a>.
</p><p>According to a Times Poll, "Be Intehaan" from the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Race_2" title="Race 2">Race 2</a></i> was ranked as the 7th most Romantic songs of 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Times_Poll_Most_Romantic_Song_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Times_Poll_Most_Romantic_Song-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p><p>Mayur also worked with <a href="/wiki/A._R._Rahman" title="A. R. Rahman">A. R. Rahman</a> on the songs "Aaj Dil Gustakh Hai" for <i>Blue</i> and "Alive" (Hindi version - Zindagi) for '<a href="/wiki/Provoked_(film)" title="Provoked (film)">Provoked</a>'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Exclusive:_Provoked_:_Alive_-_A_R_Rahman_feat._Karen_David_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Exclusive:_Provoked_:_Alive_-_A_R_Rahman_feat._Karen_David-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p><p>Mayur has collaborated with music directors <a href="/wiki/Sachin%E2%80%93Jigar" title="Sachin–Jigar">Sachin–Jigar</a> on <i>ABCD</i> (2013), <i>ABCD 2</i> (2015) and revamped <a href="/wiki/Zoom_(Indian_TV_channel)" title="Zoom (Indian TV channel)">Zoom (TV channel)</a>'s song called "Turn On" and with <a href="/wiki/Himesh_Reshammiya" title="Himesh Reshammiya">Himesh Reshammiya</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/Kick_(2014_film)" title="Kick (2014 film)">Kick</a></i>.

<p>Mayur recently directed Firdaws (2017), his first short film that has been doing festival rounds and has been the official selection in over 36 international festivals. Mayur has won 16 International Awards for Firdaws.

</p><p>MUMBAI: Tere Liye was the first Hindi Film Mayur started to work as a First Assistant Director. It marked the launch of the Music duos <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jeet-Pritam&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jeet-Pritam (page does not exist)">Jeet-Pritam</a> who are now working as independent music composers. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Beyond_Bollywood">Beyond Bollywood</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mayur_Puri&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Beyond Bollywood">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Story Circus</b> - In 2010, Mayur founded a theatre production group that creates live theatre and storytelling productions for educational institutions, corporates, and theatre enthusiasts. Read more about <a class="external text" href="https://thestorycircus.com/" rel="nofollow">Story Circus here.</a>
</p><p><b>FilmGoa</b>
</p><p>In 2011, Mayur started an initiative called FilmGoa - an effort to decentralize the mainstream film-making in India. It aimed to provide training and job support to aspiring film-makers and to facilitate film making activities in Goa. Mayur went on to give orientation lectures in more than 30 colleges in Goa, interacting with over 6000 students during this year long event. Out of hundreds of applicants from all over the country, 85 promising ones were chosen for a yearlong workshop, hosted by Goa University and Goa Tourism Development Corporation.
</p><p>Mayur also undertakes Corporate Workshops for Middle and Upper Management, the sessions usually focus on <i>creating ‘Brand Ambassadors through Broadcast and Social Media Content’</i> and <i>‘Decoding Creativity at Workplace.</i> His clients include Radio Mirchi and Viacom.
</p><p>For more info on Mayur's work and community initiatives please visit www.mayurpuri.com
</p>
