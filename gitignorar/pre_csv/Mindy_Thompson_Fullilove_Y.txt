	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Mindy Thompson Fullilove</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>

		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Mindy Thompson Fullilove</b>, M.D. (born October 15, 1950) is an American <a href="/wiki/Psychiatrist" title="Psychiatrist">clinical psychiatrist</a> who focuses on the ways social and environmental factors affect the <a href="/wiki/Mental_health" title="Mental health">mental health</a> of communities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> She is currently a professor of Urban Policy and Health at <a href="/wiki/The_New_School" title="The New School">The New School</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Trained at <a href="/wiki/Bryn_Mawr_College" title="Bryn Mawr College">Bryn Mawr College</a> and <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University" title="Columbia University">Columbia University</a>, Fullilove has conducted research on <a href="/wiki/HIV/AIDS" title="HIV/AIDS">AIDS</a> and other epidemics of poor communities and studied the links between the environment and mental health.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Her research examines the mental health effects of environmental processes such as violence, segregation, and urban renewal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mindy_Thompson_Fullilove&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fullilove grew up in <a href="/wiki/Orange,_New_Jersey" title="Orange, New Jersey">Orange, N.J</a>. Her father, Ernie Thompson, was a labor organizer in Jersey City and was the first black field organizer hired by the United Electrical Radio and Machine Workers Union. Her mother, Maggie, was a white woman from <a href="/wiki/Chippewa_Lake,_Ohio" title="Chippewa Lake, Ohio">Chippewa Lake, Ohio</a>, who worked as a union hall secretary. Fullilove’s parents launched a successful campaign in Orange to desegregate local schools. She attended <a href="/wiki/Unitarian_Universalism" title="Unitarian Universalism">Unitarian Universalist Church</a> as a child.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mindy_Thompson_Fullilove&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fullilove graduated cum laude from Bryn Mawr College in 1971 with a BA in History. She received an MS in Nutrition from Columbia University in 1974 and an MD in Medicine from Columbia University in 1978. She received a <a href="/wiki/Board_certification" title="Board certification">Board Certification</a> in Psychiatry in 1984.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mindy_Thompson_Fullilove&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fullilove has been elected to the New York Psychiatric Society, the <a href="/wiki/American_College_of_Psychiatrists" title="American College of Psychiatrists">American College of Psychiatrists</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Academy_of_Medicine" title="New York Academy of Medicine">New York Academy of Medicine</a>. She is also a member of the <a href="/wiki/American_Psychiatric_Association" title="American Psychiatric Association">American Psychiatric Association</a> and the <a href="/wiki/American_Public_Health_Association" title="American Public Health Association">American Public Health Association</a>. Fullilove was an Assistant Clinical Professor of Psychiatry at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_San_Francisco" title="University of California, San Francisco">UCSF</a> Center for AIDS Prevention Studies from 1983 until 1990.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> She joined the faculty at Columbia University in 1990 as an Associate Professor of Clinical Psychiatry and Public Health, becoming Professor of Clinical Psychiatry and Sociomedical Sciences in 1999,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> a position she held until 2016 when she joined the New School faculty.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> She is a Professor of Urban Policy and Health at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Milano_School_of_Management,_Policy,_and_Environment" title="Milano School of Management, Policy, and Environment">Milano School of Management, Policy, and Environment</a> at the New School.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-:1-10">[10]</a></sup> There, she has been working on a project entitled 400 Years of Inequality, which examines events that have shaped inequality in the United States, going as far back as 1619.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_work">Other work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mindy_Thompson_Fullilove&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Other work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="The_CLIMB_Project">The CLIMB Project</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mindy_Thompson_Fullilove&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The CLIMB Project">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In 2004, Fullilove helped start the CLIMB (City Life is Moving Bodies) Project,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> a community-based initiative in Upper Manhattan that promotes physical, social, and civic activity among northern Manhattan residents<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and is committed to ensuring parks are safe and accessible to all.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The project has sparked millions of investments, including one of $30 million in 2016 dedicated to updating <a href="/wiki/Highbridge_Park" title="Highbridge Park">Highbridge Park</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-auto-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="University_of_Orange">University of Orange</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mindy_Thompson_Fullilove&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: University of Orange">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In 2007, Fullilove and other community activists founded the University of Orange, a free <a href="/wiki/Popular_education" title="Popular education">popular education</a> center located in her hometown, to promote <a href="/wiki/Civic_engagement" title="Civic engagement">civic engagement</a> and active citizen participation in the community.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> She currently serves as the university’s President of the Board of Directors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Interdisciplinary_Research_Leaders">Interdisciplinary Research Leaders</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mindy_Thompson_Fullilove&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Interdisciplinary Research Leaders">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Fullilove is a member of one of the fifteen teams assembled by Interdisciplinary Research Leaders, a program led by the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Minnesota" title="University of Minnesota">University of Minnesota</a> to build healthier and more equitable communities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Her project, Making the Just City: An Examination of Organizing for Equity and Health in Shaw and Orange, is focused on helping people stay in neighborhoods that are facing the economic and social pressures of <a href="/wiki/Gentrification" title="Gentrification">gentrification</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>
</p>
