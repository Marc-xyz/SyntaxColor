<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Belley is of <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Roman</a> origin, and in the 5th century became an <a href="/wiki/Episcopal_see" title="Episcopal see">episcopal see</a>. It was the capital of the province of <a href="/wiki/Bugey" title="Bugey">Bugey</a>, which was a dependency of <a href="/wiki/Savoy" title="Savoy">Savoy</a> till 1601, when it was ceded to France. In 1385 the town was almost entirely destroyed by an act of incendiarism, but was subsequently rebuilt by the dukes of Savoy, who surrounded it with <a href="/wiki/Defensive_wall" title="Defensive wall">ramparts</a> of which little is left.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Belley was the birthplace of the epicure <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brillat-Savarin" title="Brillat-Savarin">Jean-Anthelme Brillat-Savarin</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ecclesiastical_history">Ecclesiastical history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ecclesiastical history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Belley was the seat of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bishop_of_Belley" title="Bishop of Belley">Bishop of Belley</a> and the location of <a href="/wiki/Belley_Cathedral" title="Belley Cathedral">Belley Cathedral</a>.  Belley is the home region of St. <a href="/wiki/Peter_Chanel" title="Peter Chanel">Peter Chanel</a>, the famous 19th-century <a href="/wiki/Society_of_Mary_(Marists)" title="Society of Mary (Marists)">Marist</a> missionary martyr and proto-martyr of <a href="/wiki/Oceania" title="Oceania">Oceania</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The town is famed for its <a href="/wiki/Cheese" title="Cheese">cheese</a>, <i>la Tome de Belley,</i> also known as <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Chevret&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Chevret (page does not exist)">Chevret</a> or still "Le pavé d'Affinois". It is also at the centre of the Bugey wine region. It is also home to a sizeable <a href="/wiki/Volvo" title="Volvo">Volvo</a> production unit producing compact excavators, Comatel and Ciat.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personalities">Personalities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personalities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Gertrude_Stein" title="Gertrude Stein">Gertrude Stein</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alice_B._Toklas" title="Alice B. Toklas">Alice B. Toklas</a> lived in Belley.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>French gastronome <a href="/wiki/Jean_Anthelme_Brillat-Savarin" title="Jean Anthelme Brillat-Savarin">Jean Anthelme Brillat-Savarin</a> was born and lived in Belley and served as its mayor for some time.</li>
<li>French writer <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9e_Martinerie" title="Andrée Martinerie">Andrée Martinerie</a> (1917–1997)  writer winner of the 1961 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prix_des_Libraires" title="Prix des Libraires">Prix des Libraires</a> was born in Belley.</li></ul>
