<p><b>Old Crow</b> (<i>Teechik</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Gwich%CA%BCin_language" title="Gwichʼin language">Gwich’in</a>) is a community in the Canadian <a href="/wiki/Provinces_and_territories_of_Canada" title="Provinces and territories of Canada">territory</a> of <a href="/wiki/Yukon" title="Yukon">Yukon</a>. It had 221 inhabitants as of 2016, most of them <a href="/wiki/Gwich%27in" title="Gwich'in">Gwich'in</a>. A <a href="/wiki/First_Nations" title="First Nations">First Nations</a> peoples who belong to the <a href="/wiki/Vuntut_Gwitchin_First_Nation" title="Vuntut Gwitchin First Nation">Vuntut Gwitchin First Nation</a>.
</p><p>Located in a <a href="/wiki/Periglaciation" title="Periglaciation">periglacial</a> environment, the community is situated on the <a href="/wiki/Porcupine_River" title="Porcupine River">Porcupine River</a> in the far northern part of the territory. Old Crow is the only Yukon community that cannot be reached by motor vehicle, requiring visitors to fly in to <a href="/wiki/Old_Crow_Airport" title="Old Crow Airport">Old Crow Airport</a> in order to reach it. It is a <a href="/wiki/Prohibition_in_Canada" title="Prohibition in Canada">dry community</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Old_Crow,_Yukon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A large number of apparently human modified animal bones have been discovered in the Old Crow area, notably at <a href="/wiki/Bluefish_Caves" title="Bluefish Caves">Bluefish Caves</a>, about 380 km (240 mi) south, and the <a href="/wiki/Old_Crow_Flats" title="Old Crow Flats">Old Crow Flats</a>, located about 14 km (8.7 mi) south, that have been dated to 25,000-40,000 years ago by <a href="/wiki/Radiocarbon_dating" title="Radiocarbon dating">carbon dating</a>, several thousand years earlier than generally accepted human <a href="/wiki/History_of_North_America" title="History of North America">habitation of North America</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>An <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_in_Canada" title="Indigenous peoples in Canada">Indigenous</a> chief named Deetru` K`avihdik, literally "Crow-May-I-Walk", helped settle a community here around the 1870s and the town was named after him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The village was founded around <a href="/wiki/Muskrat" title="Muskrat">muskrat</a> trapping, which continues to provide basic income.
</p><p>The people of Old Crow are dependent on the <a href="/wiki/Porcupine_caribou" title="Porcupine caribou">Porcupine caribou</a> herd for food and clothing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fpost5nov2009_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-fpost5nov2009-5">[5]</a></sup> The Porcupine caribou herd migrates to the coastal plain in the <a href="/wiki/Arctic_National_Wildlife_Refuge" title="Arctic National Wildlife Refuge">Arctic National Wildlife Refuge</a> (ANWR) in <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a> to give birth to their young. Many citizens of Old Crow believe the herd is being seriously threatened by <a href="/wiki/Oil_well" title="Oil well">oil-drilling</a> in the ANWR and have been heavily involved in lobbying to prevent it.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Old_Crow,_Yukon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Old_Crow_Airport" title="Old Crow Airport">Old Crow Airport</a> provides year-round transportation to other communities. During winter time, a temporary winter road is sometimes built to transport freight into Old Crow; it is not annual, but only when a significant need arises such as a major building project.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Communications">Communications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Old_Crow,_Yukon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Communications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Old Crow is served by <a href="/wiki/Northwestel" title="Northwestel">Northwestel</a> since 1971.  The long distance connection originally relied on a microwave relay at Rat Pass near the Yukon/NWT border, which also provide a radio-telephone base station along the <a href="/wiki/Dempster_Highway" title="Dempster Highway">Dempster Highway</a>, but it was frequently out of service in winter when weather conditions made helicopter access hazardous. In the late 1980s, a satellite ground station was installed in Old Crow, providing more reliable service.
</p><p>The long distance connection is noteworthy for two minor incidents.  In 1985, the Yukon territorial election was covered by live television coverage for the first time, sent by satellite to Toronto for switching into the network stations in Yukon; however, results from Old Crow were unavailable due to the microwave failure.  On 11 September 2001, the satellite connection went out of service, and an aircraft bringing a technician to repair it was challenged by Canadian Forces aircraft for violating the closure of airspace throughout North America.
</p><p>The community operated its own television transmitter to carry CBC television signals from satellite.  The current<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (April 2018)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup> status of this transmitter is unknown since the CBC in 2012 closed down analog transmitters it owned.  The community has APTN television, as well as CHON-FM and CKRW transmitters and a community-owned transmitter picking up CBC station CHAK-AM in Inuvik.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Old_Crow,_Yukon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Old_Crow,_Yukon8.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="800" decoding="async" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Old_Crow%2C_Yukon8.jpg/220px-Old_Crow%2C_Yukon8.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Old_Crow%2C_Yukon8.jpg/330px-Old_Crow%2C_Yukon8.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Old_Crow%2C_Yukon8.jpg/440px-Old_Crow%2C_Yukon8.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Old_Crow,_Yukon8.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>View from the river bank</div></div></div>
<p>Old Crow has a <a href="/wiki/Subarctic_climate" title="Subarctic climate">subarctic climate</a> (<a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen climate classification</a> <i>Dfc</i>) with mild summers and severely cold winters. Average annual temperature is −8.3 °C (17.1 °F).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ccn_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-ccn-7">[7]</a></sup> Old Crow experiences annual temperature average daily highs of 20.2 °C (68.4 °F) in July and average daily lows of −33.5 °C (−28.3 °F) in January.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ccn_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-ccn-7">[7]</a></sup> Record high temperature was 32.8 °C (91.0 °F) on August 30, 1976 and the lowest was −59.4 °C (−74.9 °F) on January 5, 1975.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ccn_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-ccn-7">[7]</a></sup> Precipitation is very low, but is somewhat higher in the summer. Average annual snowfall is 141.4 cm (55.67 in) and rainfall is 154.8 mm (6.09 in).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ccn_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-ccn-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>As Old Crow is located north of the <a href="/wiki/Arctic_Circle" title="Arctic Circle">Arctic Circle</a>, it experiences polar day or <a href="/wiki/Midnight_sun" title="Midnight sun">midnight sun</a> in summer and <a href="/wiki/Polar_night" title="Polar night">polar night</a> in winter.
The midnight sun is typically between May 5 and August 8 inclusive, while the polar night usually starts around December 14 and ends by December 29.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
