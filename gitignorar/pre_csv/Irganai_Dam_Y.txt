<p><b>Irganai Dam</b> is a hydroelectric dam in the Untskul region of <a href="/wiki/Dagestan" title="Dagestan">Dagestan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>. It is located on the river Avar Koisu.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Irganai_Dam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Construction of the dam and power station started in 1979.  The first generation unit of the Irganai hydropower station was launched in 1998.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hydroproject_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-hydroproject-1">[1]</a></sup>  The second generation unit was launched in 2001.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Technical_description">Technical description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Irganai_Dam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Technical description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The dam is 111 metres (364 ft) high and 317 metres (1,040 ft) long at the crest.  It is filled of gravel with asphalt-concrete diaphragm.  The complex includes tunnel spillway, intake structure, two diversion tunnels 5.2 kilometres (3.2 mi) each, underground surge tanks, steel-reinforced concrete penstocks, and a powerhouse.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hydroproject_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-hydroproject-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Irganai is the largest derivational hydroelectric power station in Russia, with two radial-axial hydraulic units with a capacity of 200 MW each.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rian090910_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-rian090910-2">[2]</a></sup>
The power station has a total installed capacity of 400 megawatts (MW) and projected capacity of 800 MW.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hydroproject_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-hydroproject-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hydro080910_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-hydro080910-3">[3]</a></sup>  Its mean annual electric energy output is 1,280 GWh.
</p><p>The dam creates the Irganai reservoir. The reservoir has a surface area of 18 km<sup>2</sup> (6.9 sq mi).  Its full capacity is 705,000,000 cubic metres (2.49<span style="margin:0 .15em 0 .25em">×</span>10<sup>10</sup> cu ft) and alive storage capacity is 397,000,000 cubic metres (1.40<span style="margin:0 .15em 0 .25em">×</span>10<sup>10</sup> cu ft).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hydroproject_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-hydroproject-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The dam and power station is projected by <a href="/wiki/Lenhydroproject" title="Lenhydroproject">Lenhydroproject</a> and operated by <a href="/wiki/RusHydro" title="RusHydro">RusHydro</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Incidents">Incidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Irganai_Dam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Incidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 7 September 2010, fire broke out at power station's hall. On 9 September 2010, Russian security forces defused an explosive device equivalent to 3 kilograms (6.6 lb) of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trinitrotoluene" title="Trinitrotoluene">trinitrotoluene</a> in the room.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hydro090910_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-hydro090910-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ruvr090910_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-ruvr090910-5">[5]</a></sup>  On 31 January 2011, a stick of <a href="/wiki/Dynamite" title="Dynamite">dynamite</a> was discovered at the power station.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rian310111_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-rian310111-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
