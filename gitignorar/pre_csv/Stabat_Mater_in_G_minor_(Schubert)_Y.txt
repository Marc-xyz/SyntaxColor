	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Stabat Mater in G minor (Schubert)</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:201px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:LichtentalerPfarrkirche060128.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1599" data-file-width="1088" decoding="async" height="292" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/LichtentalerPfarrkirche060128.jpg/199px-LichtentalerPfarrkirche060128.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/LichtentalerPfarrkirche060128.jpg/299px-LichtentalerPfarrkirche060128.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/LichtentalerPfarrkirche060128.jpg/398px-LichtentalerPfarrkirche060128.jpg 2x" width="199"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:LichtentalerPfarrkirche060128.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lichtental_Church" title="Lichtental Church">parish church</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lichtental" title="Lichtental">Lichtental</a>, also known as <i><i lang="de" title="German language text">Schubertkirche</i></i>. The Stabat Mater is thought to have been composed for liturgical use in the church.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Franz_Schubert_c1827.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="457" data-file-width="356" decoding="async" height="218" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Franz_Schubert_c1827.jpg/170px-Franz_Schubert_c1827.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Franz_Schubert_c1827.jpg/255px-Franz_Schubert_c1827.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Franz_Schubert_c1827.jpg/340px-Franz_Schubert_c1827.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Franz_Schubert_c1827.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Portrait of Franz Schubert by <a href="/wiki/Franz_Eybl" title="Franz Eybl">Franz Eybl</a> (1827)</div></div></div>
<p><b><span lang="la" style="font-style:normal;" title="Latin language text">Stabat Mater</span></b> in <a href="/wiki/G_minor" title="G minor">G minor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Schubert_Thematic_Catalogue" title="Schubert Thematic Catalogue">D</a> 175, is a musical setting of the Latin <span lang="la" style="font-style:normal;" title="Latin language text"><a href="/wiki/Stabat_Mater" title="Stabat Mater">Stabat Mater</a></span> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sequence_(poetry)" title="Sequence (poetry)">sequence</a>, composed by <a href="/wiki/Franz_Schubert" title="Franz Schubert">Franz Schubert</a> in April 1815.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It is scored for <a href="/wiki/SATB" title="SATB">SATB</a> choir, 2 <a href="/wiki/Oboe" title="Oboe">oboes</a>, 2 <a href="/wiki/Clarinet" title="Clarinet">clarinets</a>, 2 <a href="/wiki/Bassoon" title="Bassoon">bassoons</a>, 3 <a href="/wiki/Trombone" title="Trombone">trombones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Violin" title="Violin">violin</a> I and II, <a href="/wiki/Viola" title="Viola">viola</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Basso_continuo" title="Basso continuo">basso continuo</a> (<a href="/wiki/Cello" title="Cello">cello</a>, <a href="/wiki/Double_bass" title="Double bass">double bass</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pipe_organ" title="Pipe organ">organ</a>).
</p><p>This setting contains four stanzas of the twenty stanzas of the sequence. After a short orchestral interlude, these four stanzas are repeated with "far-reaching variation".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Newbould_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Newbould-2">[2]</a></sup> Its structure as a single continuous movement is unusual; most of Schubert's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sacred_music" title="Sacred music">sacred works</a> (not including <a href="/wiki/Mass_(music)" title="Mass (music)">masses</a>) were composed as one movement divided into three sections.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> While settings of the <span lang="la" style="font-style:normal;" title="Latin language text">Stabat Mater</span> developed into a staple of concert music by the late 19th century, it is thought that this piece would have been performed for liturgical use in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lichtental_Church" title="Lichtental Church">Lichtental Church</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Schubert's original manuscript indicates that he wished to score the piece with <a href="/wiki/French_horn" title="French horn">horns</a>, rather than trombones. However, the <a href="/wiki/French_horn#History" title="French horn">early horn</a> was valveless, limiting it from producing certain notes; the minor key setting made it impossible to perform the work with horns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Newbould_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Newbould-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>A year later, Schubert composed his <a href="/wiki/Stabat_Mater_in_F_minor_(Schubert)" title="Stabat Mater in F minor (Schubert)"><i>Stabat Mater</i> in F minor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Schubert_Thematic_Catalogue" title="Schubert Thematic Catalogue">D</a> 383. This was a far longer work in the form of a short <a href="/wiki/Oratorio" title="Oratorio">oratorio</a>, and the text used was a German <a href="/wiki/Paraphrase" title="Paraphrase">paraphrase</a> of the Latin text, as written by <a href="/wiki/Friedrich_Gottlieb_Klopstock" title="Friedrich Gottlieb Klopstock">F. G. Klopstock</a>.
</p>
