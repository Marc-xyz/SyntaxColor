	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">John Wesley (actor)</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>

		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><table class="infobox biography vcard" style="width:22em"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" style="text-align:center;font-size:125%;font-weight:bold"><div class="fn" style="display:inline">John Wesley</div></th></tr><tr><th scope="row">Born</th><td><span style="display:none">(<span class="bday">1947-08-03</span>)</span>August 3, 1947<br/><div class="birthplace" style="display:inline"><a href="/wiki/Lake_Charles,_Louisiana" title="Lake Charles, Louisiana">Lake Charles, Louisiana</a></div></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Died</th><td>September 7, 2019<span style="display:none">(2019-09-07)</span> (aged 72)</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Occupation</th><td class="role">Actor</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Years active</th><td>1968–2019</td></tr></tbody></table>
<p><b>John Wesley</b> (August 3, 1947 – September 7, 2019) was an American actor who worked in many <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_series" title="Television series">television series</a>, including <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Fresh_Prince_of_Bel_Air" title="The Fresh Prince of Bel Air">The Fresh Prince of Bel Air</a></i>, and in <i><a href="/wiki/Superhuman_Samurai_Syber-Squad" title="Superhuman Samurai Syber-Squad">Superhuman Samurai Syber-Squad</a></i>, where he played the role of Principal Pratchert.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cyclo_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-cyclo-1">[1]</a></sup> He also had roles in many feature films as well as theater productions.
</p><p>He was Mr. Jim on the series <i><a href="/wiki/Martin_(TV_series)" title="Martin (TV series)">Martin</a></i> for 3 seasons, and was the series regular "Sweets Walker" on <i>Dirty Dancing</i>, the television series. He has also appeared in numerous commercials and voiceovers including "Rafaki". His first main film role was as Frankie in <i><a href="/wiki/Missing_in_Action_2:_The_Beginning" title="Missing in Action 2: The Beginning">Missing in Action 2: The Beginning</a></i> starring <a href="/wiki/Chuck_Norris" title="Chuck Norris">Chuck Norris</a> but his first credited film role was in the 1968 <a href="/wiki/Clint_Eastwood" title="Clint Eastwood">Clint Eastwood</a> western <i><a href="/wiki/Hang_%27Em_High" title="Hang 'Em High">Hang 'Em High</a></i>. Wesley was also an award-winning stage actor and a Vietnam veteran.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cyclo_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-cyclo-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Wesley earned degrees from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_San_Diego" title="University of California, San Diego">University of California, San Diego</a> graduate acting program and the <a href="/wiki/University_of_San_Diego" title="University of San Diego">University of San Diego</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wesley_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Wesley-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Wesley died on September 7, 2019, from complications of <a href="/wiki/Multiple_myeloma" title="Multiple myeloma">multiple myeloma</a>, five weeks after his 72nd birthday.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wesley_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Wesley-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
