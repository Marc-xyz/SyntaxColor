<p>The <b>Robert Lindemann House</b>, also known as the <b>Karl Lindemann House</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Queen_Anne_style_architecture_in_the_United_States" title="Queen Anne style architecture in the United States">Queen Anne</a> style house built in 1913 by Robert Lindemann in rural <a href="/wiki/Enderlin,_North_Dakota" title="Enderlin, North Dakota">Enderlin, North Dakota</a>, United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nris_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-nris-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nrhpinv3_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpinv3-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>The building is locally significant as the best preserved Queen Anne style property in the area.  It is a three-story building with an octagonal turret.  The house had remained in the family from its construction in 1913 until at least 1994, although it was unoccupied since 1981.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nrhpinv3_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpinv3-2">[2]</a></sup> It was listed on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> in 1994.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nris_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-nris-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
