<p>The <b>Robert F. Carey Memorial Handicap</b> is an American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thoroughbred_horse_race" title="Thoroughbred horse race">Thoroughbred horse race</a> run annually during the first week of October at <a href="/wiki/Hawthorne_Race_Course" title="Hawthorne Race Course">Hawthorne Race Course</a> in <a href="/wiki/Stickney,_Illinois" title="Stickney, Illinois">Stickney</a>/<a href="/wiki/Cicero,_Illinois" title="Cicero, Illinois">Cicero</a>, <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">Illinois</a>. A <a href="/wiki/Graded_stakes_race" title="Graded stakes race">Grade III</a> event since 1998, it is open to horses age three and older and is contested on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grass" title="Grass">turf</a> over a distance of one mile (8 <a href="/wiki/Furlong" title="Furlong">furlongs</a>).
</p><p>Inaugurated on October 9, 1983, the race is named in honor of Robert Francis Carey, founder of the American Brick Company and the Managing Director of Hawthorne from 1947 to his death in 1980.
</p><p>Since inception it has been contested at various distances:
</p>
<ul><li>1983 : ​<span class="frac nowrap">1<span class="visualhide"> </span><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>16</sub></span> miles</li>
<li>1984-1987 :  ​<span class="frac nowrap">1<span class="visualhide"> </span><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>4</sub></span> miles</li>
<li>1988-1989 : ​<span class="frac nowrap">1<span class="visualhide"> </span><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>8</sub></span> miles</li>
<li>1990-1993, 1995–present : 1 mile</li>
<li>1994 : 1 mile, 70 yards</li></ul>
<p>In  1985, 1994 and again in 2009, soft course conditions from heavy rains resulted in the race being transferred to the main dirt track.
</p>
