<p><b>Muuhime</b><span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">牟宇姫</span>, 1608 – March 17, 1683)</span> was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Date_clan" title="Date clan">Date</a> family and the wife of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ishikawa_Munetaka&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ishikawa Munetaka (page does not exist)">Ishikawa Munetaka</a>. Her father was <a href="/wiki/Date_Masamune" title="Date Masamune">Date Masamune</a>, and her mother was his concubine, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Oyama-no-Kata&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Oyama-no-Kata (page does not exist)">Oyama-no-Kata</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Muuhime&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She was born in <a href="/wiki/Aoba_Castle" title="Aoba Castle">Sendai Castle</a> as the second daughter – ninth child overall – of Masamune in 1608, and her mother was the daughter of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Shibata_Muneyoshi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Shibata Muneyoshi (page does not exist)">Shibata Muneyoshi</a>.
</p><p>On March 25, 1619, at the age of twelve, she was married to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ishikawa_Munetaka&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ishikawa Munetaka (page does not exist)">Ishikawa Munetaka</a>. During the wedding, Masamune celebrated the Ishikawa family coming to the Sendai house, he stayed overnight and went home the next day. Thereafter, Michitaka Ishikawa was awarded three sons and two daughters – <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ishikawa_Munehiro&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ishikawa Munehiro (page does not exist)">Munehiro</a>, Munenobu, Sadahiro, Chiyotsuruhime, and Kahokuhime.
</p><p>Masamune would worry about Muuhime, and he would write letters that addressed to her as "Omuu".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> After Masamune died, Muuhime's mother, Oyama-no-Kata, left Sendai Castle. Muuhime built a <a href="/wiki/D%C5%8D_(architecture)#Types" title="Dō (architecture)">mi-dō</a> for her mother, and the latter spent the rest of her life there. In September of 1646, her husband, Michitaka, elected Munehiro's child to lead the Ishikawa clan and retired, living in the rice preservative in the territory.
</p><p>On September 30, 1668, Oyama-no-Kata died. The following November 20, Munetaka died as well.
</p><p>On March 17, 1683, Muuhime died at 76. The <a href="/wiki/Dharma_name" title="Dharma name">precept name</a> given to her was <b>Shōjuin Tentokuunjo Daishi</b><span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">松寿院殿徳雲如高大姉</span>)</span>.
</p>
