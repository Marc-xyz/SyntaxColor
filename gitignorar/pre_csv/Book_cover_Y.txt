<p>A <b>book cover</b> is any protective covering used to bind together the pages of a <a href="/wiki/Book" title="Book">book</a>. Beyond the familiar distinction between <a href="/wiki/Hardcover" title="Hardcover">hardcovers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paperback" title="Paperback">paperbacks</a>, there are further alternatives and additions, such as <a href="/wiki/Dust_jacket" title="Dust jacket">dust jackets</a>, ring-binding, and older forms such as the nineteenth-century "paper-boards" and the traditional types of <a href="/wiki/Bookbinding" title="Bookbinding">hand-binding</a>. The term "Bookcover" is often used for a book cover image in library management software. This article is concerned with modern mechanically produced covers.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Book_cover&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ivory_cover_of_the_Lorsch_Gospels,_c._810,_Carolingian,_Victoria_and_Albert_Museum.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1190" data-file-width="866" decoding="async" height="234" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0e/Ivory_cover_of_the_Lorsch_Gospels%2C_c._810%2C_Carolingian%2C_Victoria_and_Albert_Museum.jpg/170px-Ivory_cover_of_the_Lorsch_Gospels%2C_c._810%2C_Carolingian%2C_Victoria_and_Albert_Museum.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0e/Ivory_cover_of_the_Lorsch_Gospels%2C_c._810%2C_Carolingian%2C_Victoria_and_Albert_Museum.jpg/255px-Ivory_cover_of_the_Lorsch_Gospels%2C_c._810%2C_Carolingian%2C_Victoria_and_Albert_Museum.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0e/Ivory_cover_of_the_Lorsch_Gospels%2C_c._810%2C_Carolingian%2C_Victoria_and_Albert_Museum.jpg/340px-Ivory_cover_of_the_Lorsch_Gospels%2C_c._810%2C_Carolingian%2C_Victoria_and_Albert_Museum.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ivory_cover_of_the_Lorsch_Gospels,_c._810,_Carolingian,_Victoria_and_Albert_Museum.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Ivory" title="Ivory">Ivory</a> cover of the <a href="/wiki/Codex_Aureus_of_Lorsch" title="Codex Aureus of Lorsch">Codex Aureus of Lorsch</a>, c. 810, <a href="/wiki/Carolingian_dynasty" title="Carolingian dynasty">Carolingian dynasty</a>, <a href="/wiki/Victoria_and_Albert_Museum" title="Victoria and Albert Museum">Victoria and Albert Museum</a></div></div></div>
<p>Before the early nineteenth century, books were hand-bound, in the case of luxury medieval manuscripts in <a href="/wiki/Treasure_binding" title="Treasure binding">treasure bindings</a> using materials such as gold, silver and jewels. For hundreds of years, book bindings had functioned as a protective device for the expensively printed or hand-made pages, and as a decorative tribute to their cultural authority. In the 1820s great changes began to occur in how a book might be covered, with the gradual introduction of techniques for mechanical book-binding. Cloth, and then paper, became the staple materials used when books became so cheap—thanks to the introduction of steam-powered presses and mechanically produced paper—that to have them hand-bound became disproportionate to the cost of the book itself.
</p><p>Not only were the new types of book-covers cheaper to produce, they were also printable, using multi-colour <a href="/wiki/Lithography" title="Lithography">lithography</a>, and later, <a href="/wiki/Halftone" title="Halftone">halftone</a> illustration processes. Techniques borrowed from the nineteenth-century poster-artists gradually infiltrated the book industry, as did the professional practice of graphic design. The book cover became more than just a protection for the pages, taking on the function of advertising, and communicating information about the text inside.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cover_design">Cover design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Book_cover&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Cover design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hanna_book_cover.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="7798" data-file-width="4863" decoding="async" height="273" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/Hanna_book_cover.jpg/170px-Hanna_book_cover.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/Hanna_book_cover.jpg/255px-Hanna_book_cover.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/Hanna_book_cover.jpg/340px-Hanna_book_cover.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hanna_book_cover.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Early 20th century leather book cover, with <a href="/wiki/Gold_leaf" title="Gold leaf">gold leaf</a> ornamentation.</div></div></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Arts_and_Crafts_movement" title="Arts and Crafts movement">Arts and Crafts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Art_Nouveau" title="Art Nouveau">Art Nouveau</a> movements at the turn of the twentieth century stimulated a modern renaissance in book cover design that soon began to infiltrate the growing mass book industry through the more progressive publishers in Europe, London and New York. Some of the first radically modern cover designs were produced in the Soviet Union during the 1920s by avant-gardists such as <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Rodchenko" title="Alexander Rodchenko">Alexander Rodchenko</a> and <a href="/wiki/El_Lissitzky" title="El Lissitzky">El Lissitzky</a>. Another highly influential early book cover designer was <a href="/wiki/Aubrey_Beardsley" title="Aubrey Beardsley">Aubrey Beardsley</a>, thanks to his striking covers for the first four volumes of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Yellow_Book" title="The Yellow Book">The Yellow Book</a></i> (1894–5).
</p><p>In the post-war era, book covers have become vitally important as the book industry has become commercially competitive. Covers now give detailed hints about the style, genre and subject of the book, while many push design to its limit in the hope of attracting sales attention.
</p><p>This can differ from country to country because of other tastes of the markets. So translated books can also have different book-accessories such as toys belonging to children's books, for example <a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a>.
</p><p>The era of internet sales has arguably not diminished the importance of the book cover, as it now continues its role in a two-dimensional digital form, helping to identify and promote books online.
</p><p><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/wraparound" title="wikt:wraparound">Wraparound</a> covers are also common.
</p>

<li>A summary</li></ul></li></ul>
