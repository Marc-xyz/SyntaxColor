<p>The <b>Presbyterian Parsonage</b> is a historic <a href="/wiki/Clergy_house" title="Clergy house">parsonage</a> in the Central College neighborhood of <a href="/wiki/Westerville,_Ohio" title="Westerville, Ohio">Westerville</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">Ohio</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.  Constructed in the 1840s, when Central College was a separate community, it has been named a <a href="/wiki/Historic_site" title="Historic site">historic site</a>.
</p><p>The parsonage is an <a href="/wiki/Aluminium" title="Aluminium">aluminum</a>-sided structure, two <a href="/wiki/Storey" title="Storey">stories</a> tall with a multi-level <a href="/wiki/Gable" title="Gable">gabled</a> roof.  Two chimneys sit near the <a href="/wiki/Roofline" title="Roofline">roofline</a>, while both the front and side are divided into three <a href="/wiki/Bay_(architecture)" title="Bay (architecture)">bays</a> with windows.  The main entrance, placed near the center of the facade, is <a href="/wiki/Post_and_lintel" title="Post and lintel">trabeated</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sidelight" title="Sidelight">sidelights</a> are placed on both sides of the doorway,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> along with high-style <a href="/wiki/Pilaster" title="Pilaster">pilasters</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Entablature" title="Entablature">entablature</a>, and a <a href="/wiki/Frieze" title="Frieze">frieze</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mps_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-mps-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>2</span></sup>  These elements combine to make the house an example of the <a href="/wiki/Greek_Revival_architecture" title="Greek Revival architecture">Greek Revival</a> style of architecture.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nris_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-nris-1">[1]</a></sup>  Despite the aluminum siding, the house's core structure is <a href="/wiki/Framing_(construction)" title="Framing (construction)">frame</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mps_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-mps-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>2</span></sup>
</p><p>Timothy Lee <a href="/wiki/Plat" title="Plat">platted</a> Central College in 1849, surrounding a <a href="/wiki/Presbyterian_Church_in_the_United_States_of_America" title="Presbyterian Church in the United States of America">Presbyterian</a> college founded seven years earlier, but financial problems saw the college close in 1850; although it was resurrected as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University-preparatory_school" title="University-preparatory school">prep school</a> for the <a href="/wiki/College_of_Wooster" title="College of Wooster">College of Wooster</a>, the school again closed and was sold to a group that operated it as a <a href="/wiki/Retirement_home" title="Retirement home">retirement home</a> for the deaf.  Lee had owned much land in the vicinity before platting the village; he reduced his holdings in 1841 by donating the land upon which the parsonage sits,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mps_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-mps-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>4</span></sup> across Sunbury Road from the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Home_for_the_Aged_Deaf&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Home for the Aged Deaf (page does not exist)">former college building</a>, and one <a href="/wiki/City_block" title="City block">block</a> north of the <a href="/wiki/Central_College_Presbyterian_Church" title="Central College Presbyterian Church">Central College Presbyterian Church</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mps_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-mps-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>6</span></sup>  Although it was used as a parsonage for much of the nineteenth century, it has since been sold to private owners, who in 1927 moved the house 100 feet (30 m) to the east and turned it to face westward, toward the road.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mps_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-mps-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>2</span></sup>
</p><p>In 1980, the Presbyterian Parsonage was listed on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a>, qualifying because of its place in local history and because of its historically significant architecture.  It was part of a small <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Multiple_property_submission" title="Multiple property submission">multiple property submission</a> of Central College buildings, along with the Central College Presbyterian Church, the old college building, and the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ebenezer_Washburn_House&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ebenezer Washburn House (page does not exist)">Ebenezer Washburn House</a> to the north.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nris_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-nris-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
