<p><i><b>Ecsenius caeruliventris</b></i>, known commonly as the <b>bluebelly blenny</b> in <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FishBase_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-FishBase-2">[2]</a></sup> is a species of <a href="/wiki/Combtooth_blenny" title="Combtooth blenny">combtooth blenny</a> in the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Ecsenius" title="Ecsenius">Ecsenius</a></i>. It is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical" title="Tropical">tropical</a> blenny found in the western <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific" title="Pacific">Pacific</a> ocean, specifically in <a href="/wiki/Sulawesi" title="Sulawesi">Sulawesi</a>, Indonesia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FishBase_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-FishBase-2">[2]</a></sup> Male <i>Ecsenius caeruliventris</i> can reach a maximum length of 2.4 centimetres, while females can reach a maximum length of 2.37 centimetres;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FishBase_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-FishBase-2">[2]</a></sup> it is one of the smallest blennies in <i>Ecsenius</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Springer_&amp;_Allen_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Springer_&amp;_Allen-3">[3]</a></sup> The species name means "sky-blue belly" from the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> words <i>caeruleus</i> and <i>ventris</i>, referring to the blue abdomen on the species; the common name is derived from this. The species is most similar to its sister taxon <i><a href="/wiki/Ecsenius_shirleyae" title="Ecsenius shirleyae">E. shirleyae</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Ecsenius_bandanus" title="Ecsenius bandanus">E. bandanus</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Springer_&amp;_Allen_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Springer_&amp;_Allen-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Ecsenius caeruliventris</i> are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oviparous" title="Oviparous">oviparous</a>, and guard their eggs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FishBase_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-FishBase-2">[2]</a></sup> The blennies swim at a depth range of 3–5 metres.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FishBase_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-FishBase-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
