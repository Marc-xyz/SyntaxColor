<p>The <b>1996 ARL premiership</b> (also known as the <b>1996 Optus Cup</b> due to sponsorship from <a href="/wiki/Optus" title="Optus">Optus</a>) was the 89th season of professional <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league" title="Rugby league">rugby league</a> football in Australia, and the second to be administered by the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Rugby_League" title="Australian Rugby League">Australian Rugby League</a> (ARL). Twenty teams contested the premiership, including five <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a>-based foundation teams, another six from Sydney, two from greater <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales" title="New South Wales">New South Wales</a>, four from <a href="/wiki/Queensland" title="Queensland">Queensland</a>, and one each from New Zealand, the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Capital_Territory" title="Australian Capital Territory">Australian Capital Territory</a> and <a href="/wiki/Western_Australia" title="Western Australia">Western Australia</a>. Ultimately two Sydney clubs, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manly-Warringah_Sea_Eagles" title="Manly-Warringah Sea Eagles">Manly-Warringah Sea Eagles</a> and <a href="/wiki/St._George_Dragons" title="St. George Dragons">St. George Dragons</a> contested the <a href="/wiki/Grand_final" title="Grand final">grand final</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Regular_season">Regular season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1996_ARL_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Regular season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>With the <a href="/wiki/Super_League_war" title="Super League war">Super League war</a> in full effect off the field, those clubs affiliated with the breakaway competition refused to participate in five games of Round 1, all forfeited to ARL-aligned clubs and only four of the ten scheduled games took place. Of the two games between two Super League clubs, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canterbury_Bulldogs" title="Canterbury Bulldogs">Canterbury</a> versus <a href="/wiki/North_Queensland_Cowboys" title="North Queensland Cowboys">North Queensland</a> was cancelled, whilst Auckland flew a team consisting of players from the <a href="/wiki/Otahuhu_Leopards" title="Otahuhu Leopards">Otahuhu Leopards</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ellerslie_Eagles" title="Ellerslie Eagles">Ellerslie Eagles</a> clubs to Brisbane and were thus declared winners over the <a href="/wiki/Brisbane_Broncos" title="Brisbane Broncos">Broncos</a> by forfeit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Following up on their performance in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ARL_season_1995" title="ARL season 1995">1995 season</a> up to the grand final, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Manly-Warringah_Sea_Eagles" title="Manly-Warringah Sea Eagles">Manly-Warringah</a> dominated the season with their defence, which conceded only 34 tries in 25 matches, the best record of any team since the six-tackle rule was introduced in <a href="/wiki/1971_NSWRFL_season" title="1971 NSWRFL season">1971</a>. Indeed, the Sea Eagles only conceded 191 points during the minor round, an average of only 8.7 points per game, while scoring 549 points at 24.9 points per game. Their 1995 rivals <a href="/wiki/Canberra_Raiders" title="Canberra Raiders">Canberra</a> were hit by injuries which wiped out the seasons of key players including captain <a href="/wiki/Ricky_Stuart" title="Ricky Stuart">Ricky Stuart</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bradley_Clyde" title="Bradley Clyde">Bradley Clyde</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jason_Croker" title="Jason Croker">Jason Croker</a>, and suspensions to Kiwi props <a href="/wiki/John_Lomax_(rugby_league)" title="John Lomax (rugby league)">John Lomax</a> and <a href="/wiki/Quentin_Pongia" title="Quentin Pongia">Quentin Pongia</a>.
</p><p>Super League-aligned Canterbury were also hit by the loss of key players <a href="/wiki/Jim_Dymock" title="Jim Dymock">Jim Dymock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dean_Pay" title="Dean Pay">Dean Pay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jason_Smith_(rugby_league)" title="Jason Smith (rugby league)">Jason Smith</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jarrod_McCracken" title="Jarrod McCracken">Jarrod McCracken</a> to ARL-loyal <a href="/wiki/Parramatta_Eels" title="Parramatta Eels">Parramatta</a>. <a href="/wiki/Sydney_Roosters" title="Sydney Roosters">Sydney City</a> started the season in good form, but fell off after winning their first ten games, whilst <a href="/wiki/Brisbane_Broncos" title="Brisbane Broncos">Brisbane</a> (with <a href="/wiki/Allan_Langer" title="Allan Langer">Allan Langer</a> putting in some strong performances) dominated early but as had become their custom, lost ground mid-season during the <a href="/wiki/1996_State_of_Origin_series" title="1996 State of Origin series">Origin</a> period. <a href="/wiki/North_Sydney_Bears" title="North Sydney Bears">North Sydney</a>, with a powerful <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league_positions#Forwards" title="Rugby league positions">forward</a> pack and skillful goal-kicking half <a href="/wiki/Jason_Taylor_(rugby_league)" title="Jason Taylor (rugby league)">Jason Taylor</a> feeding a superb set of outside <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league_positions#Backs" title="Rugby league positions">backs</a>, were expected to make the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grand_Final" title="Grand Final">Grand Final</a>, but as had become their habit in the 1990s they lost the preliminary final, this time to <a href="/wiki/St._George_Dragons" title="St. George Dragons">St. George</a>.
</p><p>The 20-team competition in <a href="/wiki/1995_ARL_season" title="1995 ARL season">1995</a> and 1996 caused frequent jackpots in FootyTAB's "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pick_The_Margins" title="Pick The Margins">Pick The Margins</a>" and after three successive rounds without a single winner, on 8 July 1996 after a last-minute Sydney City penalty goal, one punter received an all-time record for any form of <a href="/wiki/Sports_betting" title="Sports betting">sports betting</a> in Australia: <a href="/wiki/Australian_dollar" title="Australian dollar">$</a>2,006,217.
</p><p>This year Canterbury-Bankstown back <a href="/wiki/Terry_Lamb" title="Terry Lamb">Terry Lamb</a> set new record for most first-grade premiership games at 350 before retiring at the end of the season.
</p><p>North Sydney's Jason Taylor won the official player of the year award, the <a href="/wiki/Rothmans_Medal" title="Rothmans Medal">Rothmans Medal</a>, while the <a href="/wiki/Dally_M_Medal" title="Dally M Medal">Dally M Medal</a> was awarded to Brisbane's <a href="/wiki/Allan_Langer" title="Allan Langer">Allan Langer</a>.
</p><p>At the end of the season, ARL chief executive <a href="/wiki/John_Quayle_(rugby_league)" title="John Quayle (rugby league)">John Quayle</a> resigned and was replaced by Balmain president (and former hooker) <a href="/wiki/Neil_Whittaker" title="Neil Whittaker">Neil Whittaker</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<p><sup>1</sup> - Due to the <a href="/wiki/Super_League_war" title="Super League war">Super League war</a>, clubs aligned with the Super League refused to take part in round 1. As such, only 4 games were played, all between two ARL-aligned teams. Of the remaining 6 games, Super League teams Canberra, Penrith, Cronulla and the Western Reds forfeited their games to the ARL-aligned clubs South Queensland, Parramatta, Newcastle and St George respectively. The match between Canterbury and North Queensland was cancelled, and Brisbane forfeited to Auckland due to Auckland fielding a team consisting of players from the <a href="/wiki/Otahuhu_Leopards" title="Otahuhu Leopards">Otahuhu Leopards</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ellerslie_Eagles" title="Ellerslie Eagles">Ellerslie Eagles</a> clubs. As a result of the forfeitures, 9 teams were in the top 8 after the first round due to ties on points differential.

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Grand_Final">Grand Final</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1996_ARL_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Grand Final">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This was the last grand final to feature two Sydney-based teams until <a href="/wiki/2003_NRL_Grand_Final" title="2003 NRL Grand Final">2003</a>. 40,985 people were at the <a href="/wiki/Sydney_Football_Stadium" title="Sydney Football Stadium">Sydney Football Stadium</a> for the game, the lowest attendance since <a href="/wiki/1989_NSWRL_season#Grand_Final" title="1989 NSWRL season">1989</a>. The match was refereed by Queenslander David Manson. For St. George it was their third Grand Final appearance in the 1990s and would prove to be their last as a stand-alone club. Manly, looking for their 6th premiership, had been beaten Grand Finalists in <a href="/wiki/1995_ARL_season#Grand_Final" title="1995 ARL season">1995</a>.
</p><p>This would be the third and final time the two clubs would meet in a Grand Final with St George having been victorious on both previous occasions in <a href="/wiki/1957_NSWRFL_season#Grand_Final" title="1957 NSWRFL season">1957</a> and <a href="/wiki/1959_NSWRFL_season#Grand_Final" title="1959 NSWRFL season">1959</a>.
</p><p>The pre-game entertainment focused on the 40th anniversary of television in Australia as match broadcaster <a href="/wiki/Nine_Network" title="Nine Network">Channel 9</a> had been the first TV station in 1956. Music artists who performed in the pre-game included <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Glen_Shorrock" title="Glen Shorrock">Glen Shorrock</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Delltones" title="The Delltones">The Delltones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ross_Wilson_(musician)" title="Ross Wilson (musician)">Ross Wilson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Christine_Anu" title="Christine Anu">Christine Anu</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kate_Ceberano" title="Kate Ceberano">Kate Ceberano</a> who sang a video replay duet of <a href="/wiki/I_Still_Call_Australia_Home" title="I Still Call Australia Home">I Still Call Australia Home</a> with late Australian entertainer <a href="/wiki/Peter_Allen_(musician)" title="Peter Allen (musician)">Peter Allen</a> (as Allen had died in 1992 he only appeared on the stadiums video replay screen).
</p><p>Kate Ceberano also performed the <a href="/wiki/Advance_Australia_Fair" title="Advance Australia Fair">Australian national anthem</a>.
</p>

<p><b>1st half</b><br/>
In the 5th minute, Manly centre Craig Innes won the chase and scored after a grubber kick by his skipper Geoff Toovey.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Matthew Ridge converted from the sideline for 6–0. The Dragons played on after being awarded a penalty in front of the posts in the 8th minute but failed to score. At the 15-minute mark Saints' halfback Noel Goldthorpe conceded a penalty right in front of their goalpost after committing a head high tackle on Manly's Daniel Gartner. Ridge took the kick, extending the lead to 8–0. St. George sent in forward replacements Lance Thompson and David Barnhill for Scott Gourley and Kevin Campion (head cut). For Manly, Tierney came off the interchange bench to replace Gillespie. Up until the 19th minute mark when Manly veteran five-eighth Cliff Lyons took the field, their coach Bob Fulton was using six running forwards with Toovey as dummy half.
</p><p>The Dragons' first points came in the 37th minute when Wayne Bartrim kicked a penalty awarded when Manly forward Cunningham stripped the ball. From the ensuing kick-off just before half-time came the game's controversial moment and a hotly disputed try. Ridge made a spectacular short kick-off and regathered, catching the Dragons unaware. St George hooker Nathan Brown appeared to tackle Ridge albeit one-handedly and by the collar. Ridge got up and ran when Brown was expecting him to stop and play the ball. Referee David Manson ruled that Brown did not complete the tackle. Ridge was eventually tackled just a few metres from the line. From dummy-half Nik Kosef then passed the ball to Steve Menzies who stormed his way through Saints' defense of Thompson, Raper, Goldthorpe and Bartrim to score next to the posts, giving Ridge an easy conversion kick. The controversial ruling by referee Manson gave Manly a 14–2 half time lead and broke Saints' resolve. In the process of scoring Menzies injured his groin/hamstring and although he returned for the second half, he was unable to run and was eventually replaced by coach Fulton.
</p><p><b>2nd half</b><br/>
In the 53rd minute Manly's Danny Moore scored a try from a Terry Hill pass after Hill drew Saints defenders, Adrian Brunker and Nick Zisti. With Ridge off the field after being concussed in a tackle, Innes converted from 5m off the sideline for the Sea-Eagles to take a 20–2 lead. Five minutes later Dragons' winger Zisti scored a try from a Bartrim cut-out pass. Bartrim then converted from the sideline for a final scoreline of 20–8.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The final twenty minutes were scoreless with two <a href="/wiki/Drop_goal#Rugby_league" title="Drop goal">field goal</a> attempts from Ridge charged down by Dragons' defenders.
</p>
