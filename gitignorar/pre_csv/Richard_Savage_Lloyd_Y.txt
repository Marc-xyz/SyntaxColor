	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Richard Savage Lloyd</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>

		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>

<b>Richard Savage Lloyd</b> (c.1730–1810), of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hintlesham_Hall" title="Hintlesham Hall">Hintlesham Hall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suffolk" title="Suffolk">Suffolk</a>, was a British landowner and Member of Parliament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Thomas_Gainsborough_-_Lady_Lloyd_and_Her_Son,_Richard_Savage_Lloyd,_of_Hintlesham_Hall,_Suffolk_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3949" data-file-width="4917" decoding="async" height="177" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/24/Thomas_Gainsborough_-_Lady_Lloyd_and_Her_Son%2C_Richard_Savage_Lloyd%2C_of_Hintlesham_Hall%2C_Suffolk_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg/220px-Thomas_Gainsborough_-_Lady_Lloyd_and_Her_Son%2C_Richard_Savage_Lloyd%2C_of_Hintlesham_Hall%2C_Suffolk_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/24/Thomas_Gainsborough_-_Lady_Lloyd_and_Her_Son%2C_Richard_Savage_Lloyd%2C_of_Hintlesham_Hall%2C_Suffolk_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg/330px-Thomas_Gainsborough_-_Lady_Lloyd_and_Her_Son%2C_Richard_Savage_Lloyd%2C_of_Hintlesham_Hall%2C_Suffolk_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/24/Thomas_Gainsborough_-_Lady_Lloyd_and_Her_Son%2C_Richard_Savage_Lloyd%2C_of_Hintlesham_Hall%2C_Suffolk_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg/440px-Thomas_Gainsborough_-_Lady_Lloyd_and_Her_Son%2C_Richard_Savage_Lloyd%2C_of_Hintlesham_Hall%2C_Suffolk_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Thomas_Gainsborough_-_Lady_Lloyd_and_Her_Son,_Richard_Savage_Lloyd,_of_Hintlesham_Hall,_Suffolk_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Richard Savage Lloyd and mother. Painting by Thomas Gainsborough, 1745</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Driveway_to_Hintlesham_Hall_-_geograph.org.uk_-_472463.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="427" data-file-width="640" decoding="async" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e8/Driveway_to_Hintlesham_Hall_-_geograph.org.uk_-_472463.jpg/220px-Driveway_to_Hintlesham_Hall_-_geograph.org.uk_-_472463.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e8/Driveway_to_Hintlesham_Hall_-_geograph.org.uk_-_472463.jpg/330px-Driveway_to_Hintlesham_Hall_-_geograph.org.uk_-_472463.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e8/Driveway_to_Hintlesham_Hall_-_geograph.org.uk_-_472463.jpg/440px-Driveway_to_Hintlesham_Hall_-_geograph.org.uk_-_472463.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Driveway_to_Hintlesham_Hall_-_geograph.org.uk_-_472463.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Hintlesham Hall, Suffolk</div></div></div>
<p>He was the eldest son of <a href="/wiki/Richard_Lloyd_(died_1761)" title="Richard Lloyd (died 1761)">Sir Richard Lloyd</a> of Hintlesham, lawyer and solicitor-general and was educated at <a href="/wiki/Eton_College" title="Eton College">Eton College</a> (1742–48) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._John%E2%80%99s_College,_Cambridge" title="St. John’s College, Cambridge">St. John’s College, Cambridge</a>. Like his father before him, he then entered the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Temple" title="Middle Temple">Middle Temple</a> to study law. He succeeded his father in 1761 to Hintlesham Hall, now a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grade_I_listed_building" title="Grade I listed building">Grade I listed building</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>He sat in the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Commons_of_Great_Britain" title="House of Commons of Great Britain">House of Commons of Great Britain</a> from 1759 to 1768 as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a> (MP)  for <a href="/wiki/Totnes_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Totnes (UK Parliament constituency)">Totnes</a>.
</p><p>He married and had 2 sons and 2 daughters and was succeeded by Richard Savage Lloyd, jnr.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
