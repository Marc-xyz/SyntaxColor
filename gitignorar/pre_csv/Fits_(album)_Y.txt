<p><i><b>Fits</b></i> is <a href="/wiki/White_Denim" title="White Denim">White Denim</a>'s second LP on European label Full Time Hobby Records,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> following up the critically acclaimed releases of 2008's <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">EU</a> debut <i><a href="/wiki/Workout_Holiday_LP" title="Workout Holiday LP">Workout Holiday</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">US</a> debut <i><a href="/wiki/Exposion" title="Exposion">Exposion</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The band's third full-length album was released in Europe on June 22, 2009, and was released in the United States on October 20, 2009 on <a href="/wiki/Downtown_Records" title="Downtown Records">Downtown Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p><p>Prior to the album's release, on April 24, 2009, <i><a href="/wiki/NME" title="NME">NME</a></i> posted the song "Mirrored and Reverse" on their website, giving fans their first taste of <i>Fits</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> On June 8, 2009, Dallas-based music blog Gorilla vs. Bear released the song "I Start To Run."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> A week before the album's official release, <i>NME</i> allowed readers to stream the entire album via their website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>
</p>
