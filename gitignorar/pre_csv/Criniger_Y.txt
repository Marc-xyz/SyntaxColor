<p><i><b>Criniger</b></i> is a genus of <a href="/wiki/Songbird" title="Songbird">songbirds</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Bulbul" title="Bulbul">bulbul</a> family, Pycnonotidae. The species of <i>Criniger</i> are found in western and central Africa.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy_and_systematics">Taxonomy and systematics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Criniger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy and systematics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Established by <a href="/wiki/Coenraad_Jacob_Temminck" title="Coenraad Jacob Temminck">Coenraad Jacob Temminck</a> in 1820, the name <i>Criniger</i> is <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> for "long-haired" (from <i>crinis</i>, meaning "hair" and <i>gerere</i>, meaning "to carry").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jobling_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Jobling-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Extant_species">Extant species</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Criniger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Extant species">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There are five extant species in the genus <i>Criniger</i>:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Western_bearded_greenbul" title="Western bearded greenbul">Western bearded greenbul</a> (<i>Criniger barbatus</i>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Eastern_bearded_greenbul" title="Eastern bearded greenbul">Eastern bearded greenbul</a> (<i>Criniger chloronotus</i>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Red-tailed_greenbul" title="Red-tailed greenbul">Red-tailed greenbul</a> (<i>Criniger calurus</i>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/White-bearded_greenbul" title="White-bearded greenbul">White-bearded greenbul</a> (<i>Criniger ndussumensis</i>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Yellow-bearded_greenbul" title="Yellow-bearded greenbul">Yellow-bearded greenbul</a> (<i>Criniger olivaceus</i>)</li></ul>
