<p><i><b>Camellia taliensis</b></i> (also <b>Yunnan large leaf varietal tea</b>, <b>wild tea</b>, å¤§çè¶) is a <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> of evergreen <a href="/wiki/Shrub" title="Shrub">shrub</a> or small <a href="/wiki/Tree" title="Tree">tree</a> whose leaves and leaf buds are used to produce <a href="/wiki/Tea" title="Tea">tea</a>.
</p><p>It is of the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Camellia" title="Camellia">Camellia</a></i> of flowering plants in the family <a href="/wiki/Theaceae" title="Theaceae">Theaceae</a>.
</p><p><i>C. taliensis</i> is an important wild relative to the cultivated the tea plant <i><a href="/wiki/Camellia_sinensis" title="Camellia sinensis">Camellia sinensis</a>.</i> It also belongs to the same <a href="/wiki/Section_(botany)" title="Section (botany)">section</a> <i>Thea</i> as <i>C. sinensis.</i>
</p><p>It is an endangered species due to <a href="/wiki/Human_overpopulation" title="Human overpopulation">human overpopulation</a> causing <a href="/wiki/Habitat_fragmentation" title="Habitat fragmentation">fragmentation</a> of the plant's natural habitat and from <a href="/wiki/Overexploitation" title="Overexploitation">overpicking</a> of the leaves for the tea market.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Camellia_taliensis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="box-Expand_section plainlinks metadata ambox mbox-small-left ambox-content" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg"><img alt="[icon]" data-file-height="31" data-file-width="44" decoding="async" height="14" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/20px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/30px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/40px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png 2x" width="20"/></a></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>needs expansion</b>. <small>You can help by <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Camellia_taliensis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=">adding to it</a>.</small>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">April 2017</span>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p><i>Camellia talensis</i> has five <a href="/wiki/Locule" title="Locule">locules</a> per <a href="/wiki/Ovary" title="Ovary">ovary</a> while in comparison <i>C. sinensis</i> has three locules per ovary.
</p><p>It grows primarily in the southwestern portion of <a href="/wiki/Yunnan" title="Yunnan">Yunnan</a> province in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> and in neighboring areas in Thailand and northern Myanmar.
</p><p><i>C. taliensis</i> has larger leaves than <i>C. sinensis</i> var. <i>sinensis</i> closer to the size of <i>C. sinensis</i> var. <i>assamica.</i> And, in several chemical composition and morphological comparisons, <i>C. taliensis</i> is also closer to <i>C. sinensis</i> var. <i><a href="/wiki/Assam_tea" title="Assam tea">assamica</a></i> than to <i>C. sinensis</i> var. <i>sinensis.</i> However, the closer similarity may also be due to human selection (which causes reduction in genetic diversity) as <i>C. sinensis</i> var. <i>assamica</i> is the tea variety traditionally cultivated in Yunnan.
</p><p>Like <i>C. sinensis, C. taliensis</i> contains both <a href="/wiki/Theanine" title="Theanine">theanine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Caffeine" title="Caffeine">caffeine</a>.
</p><p><i>C. talensis</i> can be easily <a href="/wiki/Hybrid_(biology)" title="Hybrid (biology)">crossed</a> with <i>C. sinensis,</i> and the resulting crossbred plants are intermediate between species both morphologically and chemically indicating true hybrids.
</p>

<table class="box-Expand_section plainlinks metadata ambox mbox-small-left ambox-content" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg"><img alt="[icon]" data-file-height="31" data-file-width="44" decoding="async" height="14" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/20px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/30px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/40px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png 2x" width="20"/></a></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>needs expansion</b>. <small>You can help by <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Camellia_taliensis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=">adding to it</a>.</small>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">April 2017</span>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p><i>Camellia taliensis</i> is locally used to make <a href="/wiki/White_tea" title="White tea">white tea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Black_tea" title="Black tea">black tea</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pu%27er_tea" title="Pu'er tea">pu'er tea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Yue_Guang_Bai&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Yue Guang Bai (page does not exist)">Yue Guang Bai</a> (æåç½ "Moonlight White") is a white tea made from the plant.
</p><p>Yunnan pu-erh tea made from <i>C. taliensis</i> can command a much higher price than pu'er made from the more common <i>C. sinensis.</i>
</p>
