	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Politics of Qinghai</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>Politics of <a href="/wiki/Qinghai" title="Qinghai">Qinghai</a></b> Province in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People's Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a> are structured in a dual party-government system like all other governing institutions in mainland China.
</p><p>The Governor of Qinghai (青海省省长) is the highest-ranking official in the People's Government of Qinghai. However, in the province's dual party-government governing system, the Governor has less power than the Qinghai <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_China" title="Communist Party of China">Communist Party of China</a> Provincial Committee Secretary (青海省委书记), colloquially termed the "Qinghai Party Chief".
</p>
