<p><b>Maxwell Greevey</b> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fictional_character" title="Fictional character">fictional character</a> played by <a href="/wiki/George_Dzundza" title="George Dzundza">George Dzundza</a> on <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a>'s long-running <a href="/wiki/Police_procedural" title="Police procedural">police procedural</a> and <a href="/wiki/Legal_drama" title="Legal drama">legal drama</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_series" title="Television series">television series</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order" title="Law &amp; Order">Law &amp; Order</a></i>. Following Dzundza's departure from the cast at the end of the first season, Greevey was written off the series with his death in the second season premiere.
</p>

<h2><span id="In_Law_.26_Order"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="In_Law_&amp;_Order">In <i>Law &amp; Order</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Max_Greevey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: In Law &amp; Order">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Greevey is a <a href="/wiki/Homicide" title="Homicide">homicide</a> detective working in <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>'s 27th Detective Squad. He began his career in the 1960s, partnered with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Don_Cragen" title="Don Cragen">Don Cragen</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dann_Florek" title="Dann Florek">Dann Florek</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-S1E06_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-S1E06-1">[1]</a></sup> By the beginning of the series, he is promoted to sergeant and partnered with <a href="/wiki/Mike_Logan_(Law_%26_Order)" title="Mike Logan (Law &amp; Order)">Mike Logan</a> (<a href="/wiki/Chris_Noth" title="Chris Noth">Chris Noth</a>), with Cragen as their captain. He is very close to Logan, even while having authority over him, and provides his younger partner advice and someone in whom to confide.
</p><p>Greevey is happily married to his wife Marie, with whom he has three children. A <a href="/wiki/Conservatism" title="Conservatism">conservative</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Irish_Catholic" title="Irish Catholic">Irish Catholic</a>, he sometimes looks down upon people, even <a href="/wiki/Murder" title="Murder">murder</a> victims, whose lifestyles conflict with his beliefs. For example, his opposition to <a href="/wiki/Abortion" title="Abortion">abortion</a> makes him uncomfortable investigating the bombing of a <a href="/wiki/Family_planning" title="Family planning">family planning</a> clinic, which causes some friction with Logan, who is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pro-choice" title="Pro-choice">pro-choice</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-S1E12_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-S1E12-2">[2]</a></sup> However, he seems at least tolerant of <a href="/wiki/Adultery" title="Adultery">adultery</a>, noting that while he himself has never cheated on his wife, "I don't judge. You can never know someone else's story".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-S1E07_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-S1E07-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Greevey also harbors an open distrust of doctors, which began after a doctor incorrectly diagnosed him with an inoperable <a href="/wiki/Brain_tumor" title="Brain tumor">brain tumor</a>. After seeking a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_opinion_(medicine)" title="Second opinion (medicine)">second opinion</a>, he learned that it was actually a <a href="/wiki/Subdural_hematoma" title="Subdural hematoma">subdural hematoma</a>, and recovered shortly thereafter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-S1E01_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-S1E01-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Greevey carries a <a href="/wiki/Smith_%26_Wesson_Model_36" title="Smith &amp; Wesson Model 36">Smith &amp; Wesson Model 36</a> revolver as his duty weapon. He draws it several times during the series, but never fires it.
</p><p>Greevey's partner prior to Logan was killed in the line of duty during a traffic stop.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Departure">Departure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Max_Greevey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Departure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dzundza was disappointed when he realized that <i>Law &amp; Order</i> would be more of an ensemble show rather than a show starring him. Though the cast liked his portrayal of Greevey, they increasingly felt uncomfortable around Dzundza, who was also under stress from the constant commute between New York City and his home in Los Angeles. Dzundza quit after the first season of the show,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Courrier_and_Green_111_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Courrier_and_Green_111-6">[6]</a></sup> making his last full appearance in the season finale, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Blue_Wall" title="The Blue Wall">The Blue Wall</a>". In the opening scene of the second season premiere, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Confession_(Law_%26_Order_episode)" title="Confession (Law &amp; Order episode)">Confession</a>", Greevey (played in the scene by an uncredited stunt double) is shot and killed by a hit man working for a crime syndicate he is investigating. He is succeeded by <a href="/wiki/Phil_Cerreta" title="Phil Cerreta">Sgt. Phil Cerreta</a> (<a href="/wiki/Paul_Sorvino" title="Paul Sorvino">Paul Sorvino</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-S2E01_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-S2E01-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>Greevey is the first detective within the <a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order_(franchise)" title="Law &amp; Order (franchise)"><i>Law &amp; Order</i> franchise</a> to have been killed in the line of duty.
</p><p>Along with <a href="/wiki/Nina_Cassady" title="Nina Cassady">Nina Cassady</a> (<a href="/wiki/Milena_Govich" title="Milena Govich">Milena Govich</a>), he is the second shortest serving detective in the series after <a href="/wiki/Nick_Falco" title="Nick Falco">Nick Falco</a> (<a href="/wiki/Michael_Imperioli" title="Michael Imperioli">Michael Imperioli</a>).
</p>
<h2><span id="Connection_to_Law_.26_Order:_Special_Victims_Unit"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Connection_to_Law_&amp;_Order:_Special_Victims_Unit">Connection to <i>Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Max_Greevey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Connection to Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Greevey is mentioned in a 2001 episode of the spinoff series <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Special_Victims_Unit" title="Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit">Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-S3E03_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-S3E03-8">[8]</a></sup> Prior to his death, Greevey and Cragen had worked on a case involving the murder of a college student named Jennifer Talmadge, and the disappearance of her infant son, Stephen. He took the case very seriously, working on it on his off time. After Greevey's death, Cragen took the case over and kept in touch with her parents. In the episode, Cragen, by now the head of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sex_crime" title="Sex crime">sex crimes</a> unit, finds Stephen Talmadge while investigating a corrupt <a href="/wiki/Adoption" title="Adoption">adoption</a> agency. Cragen and his detectives later determine that the wife of Stephen's biological father killed Jennifer Talmadge and gave Stephen to the adoption agency. In the end, Stephen's biological father wins custody of him.
</p>
