<p><b>John Sharp Williams</b> (July 30, 1854 – September 27, 1932) was a prominent <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Politician" title="Politician">politician</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Democratic_Party" title="United States Democratic Party">Democratic Party</a> from the 1890s through the 1920s, and served as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minority_Leader_of_the_United_States_House_of_Representatives" title="Minority Leader of the United States House of Representatives">Minority Leader of the United States House of Representatives</a>  from 1903 to 1908.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Sharp_Williams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Williams was born in <a href="/wiki/Memphis,_Tennessee" title="Memphis, Tennessee">Memphis, Tennessee</a>, but raised in <a href="/wiki/Yazoo_County,_Mississippi" title="Yazoo County, Mississippi">Yazoo County, Mississippi</a>, after he was orphaned during the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>. After graduating from the <a href="/wiki/Kentucky_Military_Institute" title="Kentucky Military Institute">Kentucky Military Institute</a> in 1870, he studied at the <a href="/wiki/Sewanee:_The_University_of_the_South" title="Sewanee: The University of the South">University of the South</a> before transferring to the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Virginia" title="University of Virginia">University of Virginia, Charlottesville</a>, where he was <a href="/wiki/Phi_Beta_Kappa" title="Phi Beta Kappa">Phi Beta Kappa</a> but did not complete all his science courses for his <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor's degree">bachelor's degree</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MS_History_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-MS_History-1">[1]</a></sup> He spent two years in Europe at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_Heidelberg" title="University of Heidelberg">University of Heidelberg</a> and what is now the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Burgundy" title="University of Burgundy">University of Burgundy</a> before returning to the University of Virginia to receive his law degree in 1876.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MS_History_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-MS_History-1">[1]</a></sup> After a brief return to Memphis (where he married Elizabeth Dial Webb in 1877), Williams returned to Yazoo County, where from 1878 to 1893 he ran the family plantation and kept a law practice.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Sharp_Williams&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Elected to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives" title="United States House of Representatives">United States House of Representatives</a> in 1893, Williams soon became a leader of the Democratic minority, renowned for his speaking skill and wit. Like most other Southern Democrats of the day, he was a proponent of coining silver and an opponent of high tariffs; unlike them, he refused to use racebaiting to build political popularity. In 1906, when Great Britain launched HMS <i>Dreadnought</i>, Congressman Williams introduced a bill to change the name of <a href="/wiki/USS_Michigan_(BB-27)" title="USS Michigan (BB-27)">USS <i>Michigan</i></a> to USS <i>Skeered O' Nothin'</i> as a challenge to the prestigious English.
</p><p>During his time as ranking Democrat in the Republican-controlled House, Williams was given the privilege of choosing the Democrats assigned to committees by the <a href="/wiki/Speaker_of_the_United_States_House_of_Representatives" title="Speaker of the United States House of Representatives">House Speaker</a> <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Gurney_Cannon" title="Joseph Gurney Cannon">Joseph Gurney Cannon</a> (by the rules of the House, Cannon was entitled to make all appointments himself), giving him tremendous power within the minority party. In gratitude, Williams was known to omit Democrats whom Cannon found particularly objectionable from committee assignments. Recognizing his status vis-à-vis Cannon, Williams jokingly described his relative political impotence in the Cannon-dominated <a href="/wiki/United_States_House_Committee_on_Rules" title="United States House Committee on Rules">Committee on Rules</a>, "I am invited to the seances but I am never consulted about the spiritualistic appearances." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>By beating one of Mississippi's leading racebaiters, <a href="/wiki/James_K._Vardaman" title="James K. Vardaman">James K. Vardaman</a>, Williams moved to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">United States Senate</a> in 1911. He became one of <a href="/wiki/Woodrow_Wilson" title="Woodrow Wilson">Woodrow Wilson</a>'s strongest supporters, from Wilson's nomination for the Presidency in 1912 to the losing battle to ratify American participation in the <a href="/wiki/League_of_Nations" title="League of Nations">League of Nations</a> in 1920. During his time as a senator, he also served as a chairman of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Senate_Committee_to_Establish_a_University_of_the_United_States" title="U.S. Senate Committee to Establish a University of the United States">Committee to Establish a University of the United States</a>.
</p><p>He gave a classic denunciation of the black race when he declared on December 20, 1898: "You could ship-wreck 10,000 illiterate white Americans on a desert island, and in three weeks they would have a fairly good government, conceived and administered upon fairly democratic lines. You could ship-wreck 10,000 negroes, every one of whom was a graduate of <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a>, and in less than three years, they would have retrograded governmentally; half of the men would have been killed, and the other half would have two wives apiece."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>After retiring from the Senate in 1923, Williams returned to his family plantation, where he spent the last decade of his life.
</p>
