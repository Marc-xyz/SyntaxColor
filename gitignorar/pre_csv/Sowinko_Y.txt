<p><b>Sowinko</b> <span class="IPA" style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Polish" title="Help:IPA/Polish">[sɔˈvinkɔ]</a></span> (formerly German <i>Neu Zowen</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Village" title="Village">village</a> in the administrative district of <a href="/wiki/Gmina_Polan%C3%B3w" title="Gmina Polanów">Gmina Polanów</a>, within <a href="/wiki/Koszalin_County" title="Koszalin County">Koszalin County</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Pomeranian_Voivodeship" title="West Pomeranian Voivodeship">West Pomeranian Voivodeship</a>, in north-western Poland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TERYT_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-TERYT-1">[1]</a></sup> It lies approximately 16 kilometres (10 mi) north-west of <a href="/wiki/Polan%C3%B3w" title="Polanów">Polanów</a>, 21 km (13 mi) east of <a href="/wiki/Koszalin" title="Koszalin">Koszalin</a>, and 153 km (95 mi) north-east of the regional capital <a href="/wiki/Szczecin" title="Szczecin">Szczecin</a>.
</p><p>Before 1945 the area was part of <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. For the history of the region, see <a href="/wiki/History_of_Pomerania" title="History of Pomerania">History of Pomerania</a>.
</p><p>The village has a population of 100.
</p>
