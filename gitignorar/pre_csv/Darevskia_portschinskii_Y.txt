<p><i><b>Darevskia portschinskii</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> of <a href="/wiki/Lizard" title="Lizard">lizard</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> <a href="/wiki/Lacertidae" title="Lacertidae">Lacertidae</a>. The species is <a href="/wiki/Endemism" title="Endemism">endemic</a> to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Caucasus" title="South Caucasus">South Caucasus</a> region of <a href="/wiki/Eurasia" title="Eurasia">Eurasia</a>. There are two recognized <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Darevskia_portschinskii&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Specific_name_(zoology)" title="Specific name (zoology)">specific name</a>, <i>portschinskii</i>, is in honor of Russian entomologist <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Josef_Aloizievitsch_Portschinski&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Josef Aloizievitsch Portschinski (page does not exist)">Josef Aloizievitsch Portschinski</a> (1848–1916).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geographic_range">Geographic range</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Darevskia_portschinskii&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geographic range">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>D. portschinskii</i> is found in <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RDB_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-RDB-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Habitat">Habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Darevskia_portschinskii&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The natural <a href="/wiki/Habitat" title="Habitat">habitats</a> of <i>D. portschinskii</i> are rocky areas and <a href="/wiki/Shrubland" title="Shrubland">shrubland</a> at altitudes of 300–1,700 m (980–5,580 ft).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iucn_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-iucn-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reproduction">Reproduction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Darevskia_portschinskii&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reproduction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>D. portschinskii</i> is <a href="/wiki/Oviparity" title="Oviparity">oviparous</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RDB_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-RDB-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Subspecies">Subspecies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Darevskia_portschinskii&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Subspecies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

</p>
