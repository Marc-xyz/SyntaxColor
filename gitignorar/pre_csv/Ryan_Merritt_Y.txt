<p><b>Ryan Adam Merritt</b> (born February 21, 1992) is an American <a href="/wiki/Professional_baseball" title="Professional baseball">professional baseball</a> <a href="/wiki/Pitcher" title="Pitcher">pitcher</a> who is a free agent. He previously played in <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> (MLB) for the <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Indians" title="Cleveland Indians">Cleveland Indians</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Merritt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Merritt was raised in <a href="/wiki/Celina,_Texas" title="Celina, Texas">Celina, Texas</a>, and is a graduate of <a href="/wiki/Celina_High_School_(Texas)" title="Celina High School (Texas)">Celina High School</a>, where he <a href="/wiki/Letterman_(sports)" title="Letterman (sports)">lettered</a> in baseball and football. He then attended <a href="/wiki/McLennan_Community_College" title="McLennan Community College">McLennan Community College</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cleveland_Indians">Cleveland Indians</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Merritt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Cleveland Indians">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Indians" title="Cleveland Indians">Cleveland Indians</a> selected Merritt in the 16th round of the <a href="/wiki/2011_Major_League_Baseball_draft" title="2011 Major League Baseball draft">2011 Major League Baseball draft</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The Indians added him to their 40-man roster on November 20, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fillout_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-fillout-3">[3]</a></sup> Merritt started Game 5 of the <a href="/wiki/2016_American_League_Championship_Series" title="2016 American League Championship Series">2016 American League Championship Series</a>, just his second major league start; he pitched 4 1/3 shutout innings against Toronto, giving up two hits and striking out three. Merritt is one of only two pitchers in MLB history to have started just one game in the regular season before pitching in the postseason, the other being <a href="/wiki/Matt_Moore_(baseball)" title="Matt Moore (baseball)">Matt Moore</a>.
</p><p>On July 13, 2018, Merritt was designated for assignment. After clearing waivers, Merritt was outrighted to the minor leagues on July 21. He elected free agency on November 3, 2018.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tampa_Bay_Rays">Tampa Bay Rays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Merritt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Tampa Bay Rays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On November 14, 2018, Merritt signed a minor league deal with the <a href="/wiki/Tampa_Bay_Rays" title="Tampa Bay Rays">Tampa Bay Rays</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He became a free agent following the 2019 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
