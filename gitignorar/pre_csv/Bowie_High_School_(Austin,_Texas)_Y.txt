<p><b>James Bowie High School</b> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_high_school" title="Public high school">public high school</a> in southwest <a href="/wiki/Austin,_Texas" title="Austin, Texas">Austin, Texas</a>. With an estimated student body of 3,000, and a campus sitting on 60 acres (240,000 m<sup>2</sup>), Bowie is the largest school in the <a href="/wiki/Austin_Independent_School_District" title="Austin Independent School District">Austin Independent School District</a> and also the second-largest secondary school in Central Texas. The school was established in 1988 on land donated to the school district by <a href="/wiki/Circle_C_Ranch" title="Circle C Ranch">Circle C Ranch</a>, which again in 2011 donated 100 acres (400,000 m<sup>2</sup>) for athletic purposes. James Bowie High School's principal is Mark Robinson.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Academics">Academics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bowie_High_School_(Austin,_Texas)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Academics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bowie was named a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Blue_Ribbon_School" title="National Blue Ribbon School">National Blue Ribbon School</a> in 1992-93.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Other awards include 2002 "Exemplary" School of Excellence by <a href="/wiki/Texas_Education_Agency" title="Texas Education Agency">Texas Education Agency</a>, 2002 "Gold Performance Acknowledgments" by Texas Education Agency, 2002 5 Star High School by <a href="/wiki/Texas_Monthly" title="Texas Monthly">Texas Monthly</a> &amp; National Center for Educational Accountability, and 2004 Recognized High School by Texas Education Agency. <a href="/wiki/Children_at_Risk" title="Children at Risk">Children at Risk</a> ranked Bowie the #8 public high school in Austin in 2012.
</p>
