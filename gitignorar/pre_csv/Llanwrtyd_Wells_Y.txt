<p><b>Llanwrtyd Wells</b> (<a href="/wiki/Welsh_language" title="Welsh language">Welsh</a>: <i lang="cy">Llanwrtyd</i>) is a small town and <a href="/wiki/Community_(Wales)" title="Community (Wales)">community</a> in mid <a href="/wiki/Powys" title="Powys">Powys</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Historic_counties_of_Wales" title="Historic counties of Wales">historic county</a> of <a href="/wiki/Brecknockshire" title="Brecknockshire">Brecknockshire</a> (Breconshire) on the <a href="/wiki/Afon_Irfon" title="Afon Irfon">Afon Irfon</a>. The town is on the <a href="/wiki/A483_road" title="A483 road">A483</a> between <a href="/wiki/Llandovery" title="Llandovery">Llandovery</a> and <a href="/wiki/Builth_Wells" title="Builth Wells">Builth Wells</a> and is located near the pass between the Tywi and Irfon valleys.
</p><p>The community also includes the smaller settlements of <a href="/wiki/Llanwrtyd" title="Llanwrtyd">Llanwrtyd</a> and <a href="/wiki/Abergwesyn" title="Abergwesyn">Abergwesyn</a>, the valley of the <a href="/wiki/Afon_Irfon" title="Afon Irfon">Afon Irfon</a>, and a large part of the "<a href="/wiki/Desert_of_Wales" title="Desert of Wales">Desert of Wales</a>".
</p><p>With a population of 850 (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Census_2011" title="United Kingdom Census 2011">United Kingdom Census 2011</a>), it claims to be the smallest town in Britain,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> although <a href="/wiki/Fordwich" title="Fordwich">Fordwich</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kent" title="Kent">Kent</a> has a smaller population.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Llanwrtyd_Wells&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Llanwrtyd Wells grew in the 19th century as a <a href="/wiki/Spa_town" title="Spa town">spa town</a> around the <i>Ffynnon Ddrewllyd</i> ("stinking <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Water_well" title="Water well">well</a>"), 1.5 miles from the much older settlement of Llanwrtyd. The town was also known as an <a href="/wiki/Eisteddfod" title="Eisteddfod">eisteddfod</a> site, and is the site of both the World <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bog_Snorkelling" title="Bog Snorkelling">Bog Snorkelling</a> Championships and the annual <a href="/wiki/Man_versus_Horse_Marathon" title="Man versus Horse Marathon">Man versus Horse Marathon</a>, as well as other annual events.
</p><p>The Abernant Lake Hotel<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> was built on the site of an old farm, to cater for the many visitors, keen to take the spa waters. The hotel grounds include a 5 acres (2 ha) lake created in 1903 by damming an <a href="/wiki/Oxbow_lake" title="Oxbow lake">oxbow</a> of the Irfon. During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, the hotel was home to <a href="/wiki/Bromsgrove_School" title="Bromsgrove School">Bromsgrove School</a>, evacuated from <a href="/wiki/Worcestershire" title="Worcestershire">Worcestershire</a>, and from 1943-45 to the <a href="/wiki/Czechoslovakia" title="Czechoslovakia">Czechoslovak</a> State School for Refugee Children. It remained as a hotel until 2007 when it became home to a multi-activity centre for school groups and families.
</p><p>Llanwrtyd Wells had two golf clubs and courses during the 20th century, both now defunct. The early course was at the Dol-y-coed Hotel and the later one at Abernant Lake Hotel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cambrian_Mill">Cambrian Mill</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Llanwrtyd_Wells&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Cambrian Mill">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Cambrian_Woollen_Mill" title="Cambrian Woollen Mill">Cambrian Woollen Mill</a></div>
<p>Built in 1852, the <a href="/wiki/Cambrian_Woollen_Mill" title="Cambrian Woollen Mill">Cambrian Woollen Mill</a> was the largest <a href="/wiki/Textile_manufacturing" title="Textile manufacturing">woolen mill</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Llanidloes" title="Llanidloes">Llanidloes</a> area, employing over 250 workers at its peak.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> It was reduced to an empty shell by a fire in November 1889, but rebuilt in 1902 and has been in continuous operation since then.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> It is one of very few woolen mills still operating in Wales. According to the mill's website, visitors can experience "700 years of weaving history".
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Spa">Spa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Llanwrtyd_Wells&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Spa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Following the discovery in 1732 by the Rev. Theophilus Evans of waters claimed to have healing properties, Llanwrtyd Wells became a spa town. (In 1740 Wales' most famous hymn-writer, <a href="/wiki/William_Williams_Pantycelyn" title="William Williams Pantycelyn">William Williams Pantycelyn</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> was appointed curate to Evans.) 
The spa was referred to as <i>Ffynnon Ddrewllyd</i> "Stinking Well" because of the strong smell of <a href="/wiki/Hydrogen_sulfide" title="Hydrogen sulfide">hydrogen sulfide</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The spa is located at the site of the Dol-y-Coed Hotel,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> now the home of Charcroft Electronics.
</p><p>The fashion for spas reached its peak in the <a href="/wiki/Victorian_era" title="Victorian era">Victorian era</a> and many of the hotels in the town date back to that time. The Belle Vue Hotel,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> the only purpose-built hotel in Llanwrtyd, was built in 1843.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Modern_times">Modern times</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Llanwrtyd_Wells&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Modern times">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In contrast to its past history as a spa town, when hundreds flocked to take the waters for their claimed medicinal effects, the area is now better known for recreations such as pony trekking, mountain biking, walking and birdwatching, and for its annual <a href="/wiki/Man_versus_Horse_Marathon" title="Man versus Horse Marathon">Man versus Horse Marathon</a>, Beer Festival and World Bog Snorkeling Championship. The town's largest employer Charcroft Electronics<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> is located in buildings which were previously the Dol-y-Coed Hotel. The hotel, on the banks of the Irfon, dates from about 1535.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Llanwrtyd_Wells&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Llanwrtyd_Wells_(electoral_ward)" title="Llanwrtyd Wells (electoral ward)">Llanwrtyd Wells</a> <a href="/wiki/Wards_and_electoral_divisions_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Wards and electoral divisions of the United Kingdom">electoral ward</a> exists, which also includes the neighbouring communities of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Llangamarch" title="Llangamarch">Llangamarch</a> and <a href="/wiki/Treflys" title="Treflys">Treflys</a>. This ward had a population of 1,875 at the 2011 Census.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>Unlike many small towns in rural Wales, <a href="/wiki/Llanwrtyd_railway_station" title="Llanwrtyd railway station">Llanwrtyd station</a> still has a passenger train service. <a href="/wiki/Heart_of_Wales_line" title="Heart of Wales line">Heart of Wales line</a> trains between <a href="/wiki/Swansea" title="Swansea">Swansea</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shrewsbury" title="Shrewsbury">Shrewsbury</a> call four times a day each way Monday to Saturday and twice each way on Sunday (2013 timetable).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Famous_residents">Famous residents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Llanwrtyd_Wells&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Famous residents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The town is the home of <a href="/wiki/Robin_Kevan" title="Robin Kevan">Robin Kevan</a>, also known as "Rob the Rubbish", who has become famous for his efforts to clean up Britain's countryside.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>
