<p><i><b>The Swan Book</b></i> is the third novel by the <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_Australians" title="Indigenous Australians">Indigenous Australian</a> author <a href="/wiki/Alexis_Wright" title="Alexis Wright">Alexis Wright</a>.  It met with critical acclaim when it was published, and was short-listed for Australia's premier literary prize, the <a href="/wiki/Miles_Franklin_Award" title="Miles Franklin Award">Miles Franklin Award</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot_Introduction">Plot Introduction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Swan_Book&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot Introduction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>The Swan Book</i> is set in the future, with Aboriginal people still living under the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Territory_National_Emergency_Response" title="Northern Territory National Emergency Response">Intervention</a> in the north, in an environment fundamentally altered by climate change.  It follows a girl who is pulled from a tree as a child after having been lost and gang-raped, and how she grows up raised by a European immigrant and seemingly guided by swans.  After the death of her guardian, she is betrothed to a boy who grows up to become the first Indigenous President of Australia (Prime Minister has been abandoned in this future), and later marries him, despite retaining a childlike mind even as an adult.
</p>

<li>2014 shortlisted the <a href="/wiki/Stella_Prize" title="Stella Prize">Stella Prize</a></li>
<li>2014 winner ASAL Awards — <a href="/wiki/ALS_Gold_Medal" title="ALS Gold Medal">ALS Gold Medal</a></li>
<li>2014 shortlisted <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miles_Franklin_Literary_Award" title="Miles Franklin Literary Award">Miles Franklin Literary Award</a></li>
<li>2014 shortlisted <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales_Premier%27s_Literary_Awards" title="New South Wales Premier's Literary Awards">New South Wales Premier's Literary Awards</a> — Christina Stead Prize for Fiction</li>
