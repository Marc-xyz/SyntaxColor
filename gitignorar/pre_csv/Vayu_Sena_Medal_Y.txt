<p>The <b>Vayu Sena Medal</b> is an <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">Indian</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Military_decoration" title="Military decoration">military decoration</a>, usually awarded in peacetime for a job well done. However it has been granted during times of conflict for acts of gallantry in the face of enemy, though not in the same numbers as the <i><a href="/wiki/Vir_Chakra" title="Vir Chakra">Vir Chakra</a></i>.
</p><p>Posthumous awards may be made and a bar is authorized for subsequent awards of the <i>Vayu Sena Medal</i>.
</p><p>The VM was established on 17 June 1960 by the <a href="/wiki/President_of_India" title="President of India">President of India</a> and awards have been made from 1961 onwards. Awards over the past decade or more have been classified into two categories. One is <i>Vayu Sena</i> Medal (Gallantry) and the other for <i>Vayu Sena</i> Medal (Devotion to Duty).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vayu_Sena_Medal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Obverse:  A four-armed silver star with the points shaped like lotus blooms. In the centre, the national emblem. Suspended by a straight bar suspender and normally named and dated on the edge.
</p><p>Reverse: A Himalayan Eagle with wings spread, and the legend, above and below, in Hindi "Vayu Sena Medal" or "Air Force / Medal".
</p><p>Ribbon: 30 mm, alternating 2 mm diagonal (lower left to upper right) stripes of grey and orange-saffron. The ribbon has an overall "woven" appearance.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Examples_of_recipients">Examples of recipients</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vayu_Sena_Medal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Examples of recipients">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>To understand the award better, a sample recipient would be that of the second-award bar to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Squadron_Leader" title="Squadron Leader">Squadron Leader</a> Harchand Singh Gill, <a href="/wiki/Indian_Air_Force" title="Indian Air Force">Indian Air Force</a>:
</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"In October 1953, a composite column of Assam Rifles and a Civil Administrative party was attacked by armed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tagin_(people)" title="Tagin (people)">Tagins</a> and some officers and men were killed. Sqn-Ldr. H. S. Gill was in command of the detachment of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/C-47_Skytrain" title="C-47 Skytrain">Dakotas</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Airlift" title="Airlift">airlanding</a> of troops. The work of airlanding personnel and supplies on the two small available <a href="/wiki/Airport" title="Airport">landing grounds</a> was difficult. Notwithstanding the difficulties Sqn.-Ldr. Gill with his keen enthusiasm, boundless energy and courage personally carried out the major portion of the landings at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Doporijo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Doporijo (page does not exist)">Doporijo</a>. By his tireless work, the airlift which had appeared impossible, became a reality. He showed courage and devotion to duty of the highest order." 
</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Sqn-Ldr. H.S. Gill was born in Mullanpur, Punjab, India. He was born on 25 March 1925.
</p>
