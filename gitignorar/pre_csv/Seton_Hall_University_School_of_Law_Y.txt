<p><b>Seton Hall University School of Law</b> (also known as <b>Seton Hall Law School</b>) is the <a href="/wiki/Law_school" title="Law school">law school</a> of  <a href="/wiki/Seton_Hall_University" title="Seton Hall University">Seton Hall University</a>. Located in downtown <a href="/wiki/Newark,_New_Jersey" title="Newark, New Jersey">Newark</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>, Seton Hall Law is one of two law schools in the state of New Jersey. The school confers three law degrees: <a href="/wiki/Juris_Doctor" title="Juris Doctor">Juris Doctor</a> (J.D.), <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Laws" title="Master of Laws">Master of Laws</a> (LL.M.), and <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Science" title="Master of Science">Master of Science</a> in Jurisprudence. The law school is accredited by the <a href="/wiki/American_Bar_Association" title="American Bar Association">American Bar Association</a> (ABA), and has been a member of the <a href="/wiki/Association_of_American_Law_Schools" title="Association of American Law Schools">Association of American Law Schools</a> since its founding in 1951.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seton_Hall_University_School_of_Law&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On February 5, 1951, Seton Hall University School of Law opened on the old John Marshall site, 40 Journal Square, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jersey_City" title="Jersey City">Jersey City</a> with an entering class of 72 students.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
The school was also fully accredited by the <a href="/wiki/American_Bar_Association" title="American Bar Association">American Bar Association</a> in that same year. <a href="/wiki/Kathleen_M._Boozang" title="Kathleen M. Boozang">Kathleen M. Boozang</a> became Dean in 2015 succeeding Patrick E. Hobbs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Seton Hall Law is part of <a href="/wiki/Seton_Hall_University" title="Seton Hall University">Seton Hall University</a>, a private Catholic university with a main campus in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Orange" title="South Orange">South Orange</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NJ" title="NJ">NJ</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="JD_Programs">JD Programs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seton_Hall_University_School_of_Law&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: JD Programs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Juris_doctor" title="Juris doctor">J.D.</a> degree program of 88 credits can be pursued as a full-time or a weekend student. Full-time students can complete the program in three years; weekend students can complete in four years or fewer if they accelerate their studies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Weekend students spend alternating weekends on campus each semester and engage in asynchronous online coursework while off-campus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_Degree_Offerings">Other Degree Offerings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seton_Hall_University_School_of_Law&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Other Degree Offerings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Seton Hall Law offers a Master of Science in Jurisprudence (M.S.J.) in Financial Services Compliance, Health and Hospital Law, Pharmaceutical and Medical Device Law and Compliance, Intellectual Property Law, or Privacy Law &amp; Cyber Security.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p>A Master of Laws (LL.M.) is also offered with concentrations in Financial Services Compliance, Health Law, and Intellectual Property Law.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>The school also offers several joint degree programs with other faculties of the University. For example, there is a combined J.D./M.A. (or MADIR) program with the University's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_C._Whitehead_School_of_Diplomacy_and_International_Relations" title="John C. Whitehead School of Diplomacy and International Relations">Whitehead School of Diplomacy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>In Fall 2019, 216 students matriculated as 1Ls.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Centers_of_Excellence">Centers of Excellence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seton_Hall_University_School_of_Law&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Centers of Excellence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>The Center for Health &amp; Pharmaceutical Law &amp; Policy</b> educates lawyers and health care sector professionals regarding the complex set of laws that govern patients, health care providers, and life science companies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p><p><b>The Institute for Privacy Protection</b> educates consumers and businesses to provide inter-disciplinary forums to address emerging privacy issues by educating professionals, businesses, and organizations in this rapidly evolving area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p><p><b>The Seton Hall Law Gibbons Institute of Law, Science &amp; Technology</b> educates and trains the next generation of attorneys and professionals for the complex issues they will face as scientific and technological advances challenge business, law, and legal institutions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p><p><b>The Center for Social Justice</b> is one of the nation's strongest pro bono and clinical programs, enabling students to gain hands-on experience while providing legal services to economically disadvantaged area residents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p><p><b>The Center for Policy and Research</b> provides law students with an uncommon opportunity to gain experience in forensic analysis and investigation through research into national policies and practices.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ranking">Ranking</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seton_Hall_University_School_of_Law&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Ranking">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 2019 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._News_and_World_Report" title="U.S. News and World Report">U.S. News and World Report</a> ranked Seton Hall Law #59 in its Best Law Schools ranking.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> For twenty years, the school's health law program has consistently ranked in the Top 10 by <a href="/wiki/U.S._News_%26_World_Report" title="U.S. News &amp; World Report">U.S. News &amp; World Report</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Above_the_Law_(website)" title="Above the Law (website)">Above the Law</a> ranked the school #35 out of the top 50 law schools in the nation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Law_Journal" title="National Law Journal">National Law Journal</a> ranked Seton Hall's 2018 graduating class as 1st in the nation for state and federal clerkships and 11th in the nation for employment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Employment_and_Bar_Passage">Employment and Bar Passage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seton_Hall_University_School_of_Law&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Employment and Bar Passage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Employment Status for Class of 2018 Graduates: Total employment rate for the class of 2018 was 98.1%; Bar Pass required or J.D. Advantage totaled 94.3%. Employed students hold positions in Judicial Clerkships (58.23%), Private Practice (26.58%), Corporate or Business (6.96%), Government or Public Interest (6.33%), and 1.9% unemployed graduates are seeking employment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto1_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto1-21">[21]</a></sup>
</p><p>Seton Hall Law class of 2018's overall bar passage rate for first-time applicants in July 2018 was 82.28%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto1_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-auto1-21">[21]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Costs">Costs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seton_Hall_University_School_of_Law&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Costs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The tuition and fees for the Law School are $55,788 for incoming full-time students and $42,076 for incoming part-time students for the 2018-19 academic year. However, 81% of incoming students in 2019 received scholarship funding, and 75%-85% of funded students typically renew scholarships after the first year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup>
</p><p>The median grant amount was $25,000 for full-time students and $19,400 for weekend students, bringing net-tuition (tuition less scholarship and grants) for those receiving the median grant amount to $27,206 for full-time students and $19,754 for weekend students.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-:1-23">[23]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Publications">Publications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seton_Hall_University_School_of_Law&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Publications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The school produces two journals: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seton_Hall" title="Seton Hall">Seton Hall</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Law_Review" title="Law Review">Law Review</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> and the <a class="external text" href="http://scholarship.shu.edu/shlj/" rel="nofollow">Seton Hall Legislative Journal</a>.

<p>At One Newark Center, the Law School is housed in a 22-story building in <a href="/wiki/Downtown_Newark" title="Downtown Newark">Downtown Newark</a> completed in 1991.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Seton_Hall_Law_Virtual_Tour_30-1"><a href="#cite_note-Seton_Hall_Law_Virtual_Tour-30">[30]</a></sup> The Newark Campus building provides 210,000 square feet (20,000Â m<sup>2</sup>) including 65,000 square feet (6,000Â m<sup>2</sup>) of library, named for Congressman <a href="/wiki/Peter_W._Rodino" title="Peter W. Rodino">Peter W. Rodino</a>, Jr.. It is at the corner of <a href="/wiki/Raymond_Boulevard" title="Raymond Boulevard">Raymond Boulevard</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/McCarter_Highway" title="McCarter Highway">McCarter Highway</a>, two blocks west of <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania_Station_(Newark)" title="Pennsylvania Station (Newark)">Penn Station Newark</a>, where numerous connections can be made to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Jersey_Transit_rail_operations" title="New Jersey Transit rail operations">New Jersey Transit</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Port_Authority_Trans_Hudson" title="Port Authority Trans Hudson">PATH</a> (an approximate 20 minute ride to <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> While many students commute from around the <a href="/wiki/New_York_metropolitan_area" title="New York metropolitan area">New York metropolitan area</a>, other students choose to reside at <a href="/wiki/Eleven_80" title="Eleven 80">Eleven 80</a>, the Union Building, and Renaissance Towers. One Newark Center is one of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_tallest_buildings_in_Newark" title="List of tallest buildings in Newark">tallest buildings</a> in the city and also contains commercial offices. Nearby attractions include the <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey_Performing_Arts_Center" title="New Jersey Performing Arts Center">New Jersey Performing Arts Center</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Newark_Museum" title="Newark Museum">Newark Museum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prudential_Center" title="Prudential Center">Prudential Center</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Arena_(Harrison)" title="Red Bull Arena (Harrison)">Red Bull Arena</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup>
</p>
