<p>The <b><i>Sava</i> class</b> was a class of diesel-electric <a href="/wiki/Attack_submarine" title="Attack submarine">attack submarines</a> built for the <a href="/wiki/Yugoslav_Navy" title="Yugoslav Navy">Yugoslav Navy</a> during the late 1970s and early 1980s. Developed by the Brodarski Institute (BI) from <a href="/wiki/Zagreb" title="Zagreb">Zagreb</a>, the two strong class was built by the Brodogradilište specijalnih objekata (Special objects shipyard) in <a href="/wiki/Split,_Croatia" title="Split, Croatia">Split</a>. They were intended as a replacement for the aging <a href="/wiki/Sutjeska-class_submarine" title="Sutjeska-class submarine"><i>Sutjeska</i>-class submarines</a>. Compared to the earlier <a href="/wiki/Heroj-class_submarine" title="Heroj-class submarine"><i>Heroj</i> class</a>, the <i>Sava</i> class was longer with more powerful armament that consisted of six <a href="/wiki/Torpedo_tube" title="Torpedo tube">torpedo tubes</a> that could also be used for <a href="/wiki/Minelayer" title="Minelayer">minelaying</a>.
</p><p>With the start of the <a href="/wiki/Croatian_War_of_Independence" title="Croatian War of Independence">Croatian War of Independence</a>, both submarines were relocated to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boka_Kotorska" title="Boka Kotorska">Boka Kotorska</a> where they would be commissioned with the new FR Yugoslav Navy of, what would eventually become, <a href="/wiki/Serbia_and_Montenegro" title="Serbia and Montenegro">Serbia and Montenegro</a>. Both were decommissioned during the early 2000s.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development_and_building">Development and building</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sava-class_submarine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Development and building">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <i>Sava</i> class was developed by the Brodarski Institute (BI) from <a href="/wiki/Zagreb" title="Zagreb">Zagreb</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SR_Croatia" title="SR Croatia">SR Croatia</a> as a replacement for the two <a href="/wiki/Sutjeska-class_submarine" title="Sutjeska-class submarine"><i>Sutjeska</i>-class</a> boats in service. The project, designated B-72, was led by Colonel Dušan Radanović who was succeeded by Branko Ryšlavy. Compared to the previous <i>Heroj</i>-class attack submarines, the new class was to have a greater degree of automatation with improved armament and autonomy including greater diving depths.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPodmorničarska_tradicija_na_hrvatskom_Jadranu_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPodmorničarska_tradicija_na_hrvatskom_Jadranu-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Both submarines, <i>Sava</i> (P-831) and <i>Drava</i> (P-832), were built by the Brodogradilište specijalnih objekata (Special objects shipyard) in Split, SR Croatia and named after rivers in SFR Yugoslavia. <i>Sava</i> was <a href="/wiki/Keel_laying" title="Keel laying">laid down</a> in 1975, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ship_naming_and_launching" title="Ship naming and launching">launched</a> in 1977 and was <a href="/wiki/Ship_commissioning" title="Ship commissioning">commissioned</a> in 1978. It was the second submarine in the Yugoslav Navy to carry that name, the first one being <i>Sava</i> (P-802). <i>Drava</i> was laid down in 1978, launched in 1981 and commissioned in 1982.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sava-class_submarine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The submarines measured 55.9 <a href="/wiki/Metre" title="Metre">m</a> (183 ft 5 in) in length <a href="/wiki/Displacement_(ship)" title="Displacement (ship)">displacing</a> 770 <a href="/wiki/Tonne" title="Tonne">t</a> (760 long tons) surfaced and 964 t (949 long tons) when submerged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTENapadne_podmornice_klase_Sava_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTENapadne_podmornice_klase_Sava-2">[2]</a></sup> The diesel-electric drive consisted of two MTU diesel engines and a <a href="/wiki/KON%C4%8CAR_Group" title="KONČAR Group">Končar</a> 1 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Megawatt" title="Megawatt">MW</a> (1,300 hp) electric motor mounted on a single shaft, enabling the boats a speed of 10 <a href="/wiki/Knot_(unit)" title="Knot (unit)">knots</a> (19 km/h; 12 mph) when surfaced and a maximum speed of 16.5 knots (30.6 km/h; 19.0 mph) underwater.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEDevelopment_and_Production_of_Electric_Vehicles_in_Croatia_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDevelopment_and_Production_of_Electric_Vehicles_in_Croatia-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTENapadne_podmornice_klase_Sava_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTENapadne_podmornice_klase_Sava-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESaunders2004650_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESaunders2004650-4">[4]</a></sup> The boat's maximum diving depth was 300 m (984 ft 3 in) and were manned by a crew of 35.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTENapadne_podmornice_klase_Sava_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTENapadne_podmornice_klase_Sava-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Armament consisted of six 533 mm (21.0 in) forward facing torpedo tubes used for launching a complement of up to ten TEST-71ME <a href="/wiki/Torpedo" title="Torpedo">torpedoes</a> with a maximum range of 10.8 <a href="/wiki/Nautical_mile" title="Nautical mile">nmi</a> (20 km; 12 mi) or up to twenty <a href="/wiki/Naval_mine" title="Naval mine">naval mines</a> if used for minelaying operations. Sensors include a "Stop Light" <a href="/wiki/Radar_warning_receiver" title="Radar warning receiver">radar warning</a> system and a "Snoop Group" surface search <a href="/wiki/Radar" title="Radar">radar</a>. Sources are somewhat conflicting regarding installed <a href="/wiki/Sonar" title="Sonar">sonar</a> equipment.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Service_and_aftermath">Service and aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sava-class_submarine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Service and aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Both <i>Sava</i> and <i>Drava</i> were part of the 88th Submarine Flotilla of the Yugoslav Navy, with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lora_Naval_Base" title="Lora Naval Base">Lora Naval Base</a> in Split being their homeport. In October 1983 while underwater <i>Drava</i> suffered a hull breach which almost resulted in the submarine and its entire crew being lost. It was revealed that the cause of the breach was a design flaw in the submarine itself.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEVranešević29_November_2007_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEVranešević29_November_2007-5">[5]</a></sup> With the start of the <a href="/wiki/Croatian_War_of_Independence" title="Croatian War of Independence">Croatian War of Independence</a> both submarines were relocated to Montenegro where they were eventually commissioned with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Military_of_Serbia_and_Montenegro#Navy" title="Military of Serbia and Montenegro">FR Yugoslav Navy</a>. The major overhaul of <i>Drava</i> was stopped in 1996 and the boat was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ship_decommissioning" title="Ship decommissioning">decommissioned</a> sometime around 1999.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKratki_istorijat_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKratki_istorijat-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPodmorničarska_tradicija_na_hrvatskom_Jadranu_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPodmorničarska_tradicija_na_hrvatskom_Jadranu-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEŠoštarić7_August_2008_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEŠoštarić7_August_2008-7">[7]</a></sup> <i>Sava</i> was decommissioned in 2002 and was officially declared redundant in 2004, being not operational for some time by that point. The submarines were passed on to <a href="/wiki/Montenegro" title="Montenegro">Montenegro</a> after the country <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Montenegrin_independence_referendum,_2006" title="Montenegrin independence referendum, 2006">declared independence</a> in 2006, and after unsuccessful attempts at selling the submarines as complete warships, in 2008 it was decided that they were to be sold for scrap or as museum exhibits.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECrnogorci_prodaju_podmornice_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECrnogorci_prodaju_podmornice-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKratki_istorijat_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKratki_istorijat-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEIzjava_komandanta_Mornarice_VCG_kbb_Rajka_Bulatovića_u_vezi_prodaje_podmornice_P-831_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEIzjava_komandanta_Mornarice_VCG_kbb_Rajka_Bulatovića_u_vezi_prodaje_podmornice_P-831-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Drava</i> was sold and <a href="/wiki/Ship_breaking" title="Ship breaking">scrapped</a> in June 2008 in <a href="/wiki/Tivat" title="Tivat">Tivat</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKratki_istorijat_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKratki_istorijat-6">[6]</a></sup> On 8 May 2008 <i>Sava</i> was sold as scrap for 174,500 Euros to the <a href="/wiki/Podgorica" title="Podgorica">Podgorica</a> firm "Mont Metal" and was towed to <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> for scrapping. An article published in 2010 suggested that the submarine was sold along with the "Orca-2000" system still on board, although it was supposed to be removed along with the other sensitive combat equipment. "Orca-2000" was a torpedo fire control system that could also be used for navigation and was developed in Serbia and Montenegro .<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESa_„Savom“_otplovila_i_„Orca“_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESa_„Savom“_otplovila_i_„Orca“-10">[10]</a></sup> A few days later <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Rajko_Bulatovi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Rajko Bulatović (page does not exist)">Rajko Bulatović</a>, commander of the <a href="/wiki/Montenegrin_Navy" title="Montenegrin Navy">Montenegrin Navy</a> at the time, made a statement explaining the "Orca-2000" system was never installed on <i>Sava</i>, and was in fact, developed for a foreign customer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEIzjava_komandanta_Mornarice_VCG_kbb_Rajka_Bulatovića_u_vezi_prodaje_podmornice_P-831_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEIzjava_komandanta_Mornarice_VCG_kbb_Rajka_Bulatovića_u_vezi_prodaje_podmornice_P-831-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
