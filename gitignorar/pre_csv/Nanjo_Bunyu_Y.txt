	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Nanjo Bunyu</h1>

<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:173px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bunyiu_Nanjio.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="227" data-file-width="171" decoding="async" height="227" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/4/40/Bunyiu_Nanjio.jpg" width="171"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Bunyiu_Nanjio.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Nanjo Bunyu</div></div></div>
<p><b>Nanjō Bun'yū</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> (南条文雄) (1 July 1849 – 9 November 1927) was a Buddhist priest and one of the most important modern Japanese scholars of <a href="/wiki/Buddhism" title="Buddhism">Buddhism</a>. Nanjō was born to the abbot of Seiunji Temple (誓運寺), part of the Shinshu Ōtani sect (真宗大谷派) of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Higashi_Honganji" title="Higashi Honganji">Higashi Honganji</a> (東本願寺) branch of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jodo_Shinshu" title="Jodo Shinshu">Jodo Shinshu</a>.
</p><p>Nanjō studied Classical Chinese texts and Buddhist doctrine in his youth before being sent to <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> in 1876 to study <a href="/wiki/Sanskrit" title="Sanskrit">Sanskrit</a> and <a href="/wiki/Indian_philosophy" title="Indian philosophy">Indian philosophy</a> from European scholars, including <a href="/wiki/Max_M%C3%BCller" title="Max Müller">Max Müller</a>, under whom Bunyu studied in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> While there he met the Chinese Buddhist <a href="/wiki/Yang_Wenhui" title="Yang Wenhui">Yang Wenhui</a>, whom he helped to acquire some three hundred Chinese Buddhist texts that had been lost in China to be reprinted at Yang's printing house in Nanjing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He returned to Japan in 1884 and served as a professor or head of a number of Buddhist seminaries and universities until his death.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
