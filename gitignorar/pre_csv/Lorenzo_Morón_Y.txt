<p><b>Lorenzo Morón Vizcaíno</b> (born 16 February 1970), commonly known as <b>Loren</b>, is a Spanish retired <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who played as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Centre-back" title="Defender (association football)">central defender</a>, and is a <a href="/wiki/Manager_(association_football)" title="Manager (association football)">manager</a>.
</p><p>He amassed <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Segunda División">Segunda División</a> totals of 279 games and 11 goals, representing five clubs. He appeared in 99 <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> matches during four seasons (five goals), with <a href="/wiki/CP_M%C3%A9rida" title="CP Mérida">Mérida</a>, <a href="/wiki/UD_Salamanca" title="UD Salamanca">Salamanca</a> and <a href="/wiki/Recreativo_de_Huelva" title="Recreativo de Huelva">Recreativo</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Playing_career">Playing career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lorenzo_Mor%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Playing career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Marbella" title="Marbella">Marbella</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andalusia" title="Andalusia">Andalusia</a>, Loren kickstarted his 18-year senior career with <a href="/wiki/CA_Marbella" title="CA Marbella">CA Marbella</a>, helping the club promote from <a href="/wiki/Tercera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Tercera División">Tercera División</a> to <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Segunda División">Segunda División</a> in only two seasons. He made his debut in the latter competition on 6 September 1992, in a 1–1 home draw against <a href="/wiki/CD_Castell%C3%B3n" title="CD Castellón">CD Castellón</a>.
</p><p>In the summer of 1993, Loren signed with second level side <a href="/wiki/CP_M%C3%A9rida" title="CP Mérida">CP Mérida</a>, helping them promote to <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> for the first time ever in <a href="/wiki/1994%E2%80%9395_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="1994–95 Segunda División">his second year</a>. In <a href="/wiki/1995%E2%80%9396_La_Liga" title="1995–96 La Liga">the following campaign</a>, he started in all his 22 league appearances in an eventual relegation as second from bottom.
</p><p>Loren split the following nine years between the top flight and division two, in representation of <a href="/wiki/UD_Salamanca" title="UD Salamanca">UD Salamanca</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sevilla_FC" title="Sevilla FC">Sevilla FC</a> and <a href="/wiki/Recreativo_de_Huelva" title="Recreativo de Huelva">Recreativo de Huelva</a>. He scored a career-best three goals – at the professional level – with the second team in <a href="/wiki/2000%E2%80%9301_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="2000–01 Segunda División">2000–01</a>, adding his second championship in the second tier in the process; <a href="/wiki/2002%E2%80%9303_La_Liga" title="2002–03 La Liga">two seasons later</a>, his two goals from 32 appearances could not help prevent Recreativo from being relegated from the top tier, and he also started in <a href="/wiki/2003_Copa_del_Rey_Final" title="2003 Copa del Rey Final">the final</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Copa_del_Rey" title="Copa del Rey">Copa del Rey</a>, a 0–3 loss to <a href="/wiki/RCD_Mallorca" title="RCD Mallorca">RCD Mallorca</a> in <a href="/wiki/Elche" title="Elche">Elche</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PAIS_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-PAIS-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Loren retired in 2007 at the age of 37, after two years in <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n_B" title="Segunda División B">Segunda División B</a> with his first club, now renamed <a href="/wiki/Marbella_FC" title="Marbella FC">UD Marbella</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coaching_career">Coaching career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lorenzo_Mor%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Coaching career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Loren started working as a head coach in late 2014, with amateurs <a href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_Mancha_Real" title="Atlético Mancha Real">Atlético Mancha Real</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> On 23 March 2015, he replaced <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pablo_Alfaro_Armengot" title="Pablo Alfaro Armengot">Pablo Alfaro</a> at the helm of Marbella as the team was placed inside the relegation zone,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> eventually leading them to a comfortable <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n_B" title="2014–15 Segunda División B">tenth-place finish in the third division</a>.
</p><p>After leaving Marbella by mutual consent in April 2016,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Loren took over at nearby <a href="/wiki/Tercera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Tercera División">Tercera División</a> club <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A9lez_CF" title="Vélez CF">Vélez CF</a> in October.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In December 2017, over two decades after playing in the <a href="/wiki/Extremadura" title="Extremadura">Extremaduran</a> capital, he was hired as manager of <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A9rida_AD" title="Mérida AD">Mérida AD</a>, replacing <a href="/wiki/Mehdi_Nafti" title="Mehdi Nafti">Mehdi Nafti</a> who returned to the helm the following March when the situation had not improved.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lorenzo_Mor%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

A <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Striker" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a>, he represented <a href="/wiki/Real_Betis" title="Real Betis">Real Betis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
