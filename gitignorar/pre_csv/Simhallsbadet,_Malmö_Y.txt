<p><b>Simhallsbadet</b> is a swimming complex in <a href="/wiki/Malm%C3%B6" title="Malmö">Malmö</a> city, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> owned by the <a href="/wiki/Malm%C3%B6_Municipality" title="Malmö Municipality">Malmö Municipality</a>. It was known as <b>Aq-va-kul</b> 1988–2015. Today it is mainly used by swimming clubs and schools.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simhallsbadet,_Malm%C3%B6&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Simhallsbadet_.281956.E2.80.931988.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Simhallsbadet_(1956–1988)">Simhallsbadet (1956–1988)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simhallsbadet,_Malm%C3%B6&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Simhallsbadet (1956–1988)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Simhallsbadet was built as a public swimming venue in 1956 with a 25 m pool, and in 1977 a 50 m pool was added.
</p>
<h3><span id="Aq-va-kul_.281988.E2.80.932015.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Aq-va-kul_(1988–2015)">Aq-va-kul (1988–2015)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simhallsbadet,_Malm%C3%B6&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Aq-va-kul (1988–2015)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1988 a <a href="/wiki/Water_park" title="Water park">water park</a> was built and the name was changed to <i>Aq-va-kul</i>. There were also a <a href="/wiki/Turkish_bath" title="Turkish bath">Turkish bath</a> and a relax division. The facility had 600,000 visitors yearly. The name Aq-va-kul is a word play on the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> word for water, aqua, and the <a href="/wiki/Swedish_language" title="Swedish language">Swedish</a> phrase <i>Ack, vad kul</i> (English: "Oh!, how fun")
</p><p>Aq-va-kul was the arena for seven <a href="/wiki/Swedish_Short_Course_Swimming_Championships" title="Swedish Short Course Swimming Championships">Swedish Short Course Swimming Championships</a> (last time in 2003), four <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Swedish_Junior%27s_Swimming_Championships&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Swedish Junior's Swimming Championships (page does not exist)">Swedish Junior's Swimming Championships</a> (last time in 2002), four <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Swedish_Youth_Short_Course_Swimming_Championships&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Swedish Youth Short Course Swimming Championships (page does not exist)">Swedish Youth Short Course Swimming Championships</a> finals (last time in 2006), and several meets in the <a href="/wiki/FINA_Swimming_World_Cup" title="FINA Swimming World Cup">FINA Swimming World Cup</a> series in the 1990s. The current <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_records_in_swimming" title="European records in swimming">European record</a> on men's 800 m freestyle was set in the 25 m pool in January 1997 by <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=J%C3%B6rg_Hoffman&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jörg Hoffman (page does not exist)">Jörg Hoffman</a> with the time of 7:36.24.
</p>
<h3><span id="Simhallsbadet_.282015_.E2.80.93.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Simhallsbadet_(2015_–)">Simhallsbadet (2015 –)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simhallsbadet,_Malm%C3%B6&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Simhallsbadet (2015 –)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2015 <a href="/wiki/Hylliebadet" title="Hylliebadet">Hylliebadet</a> replaced Aq-va-kul as a public swimming venue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mnd_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-mnd-1">[1]</a></sup> At the same time Aq-va-kul changed name to the former <i>Simhallsbadet</i> and is now mainly used by swimming clubs and schools.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-simhallsbadet_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-simhallsbadet-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
