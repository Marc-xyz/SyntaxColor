<p><i><b>Anelasmocephalus hadzii</b></i> is a species of <a href="/wiki/Trogulidae" title="Trogulidae">trogulid</a>, a group of <a href="/wiki/Opiliones" title="Opiliones">opiliones</a>, native to <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zipcodezoo_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-zipcodezoo-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characteristic_Features">Characteristic Features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anelasmocephalus_hadzii&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Characteristic Features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>A. hadzii</i> has all legs except for the <a href="/wiki/Arthropod_leg#Calcaneus" title="Arthropod leg">calcaneus</a> are very hairy and warty. Soil particles, as with most trogulidae, are tightly glued to these warts. The glans of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Penis_(harvestmen)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Penis (harvestmen) (page does not exist)">penis</a> is long and has a hook at the end. The distal end of this is long and thin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-martens1978_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-martens1978-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
