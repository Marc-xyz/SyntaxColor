<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1809_in_literature&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Jane_Austen%27s_House,_Chawton_-_geograph.org.uk_-_946021.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="455" data-file-width="640" decoding="async" height="156" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Jane_Austen%27s_House%2C_Chawton_-_geograph.org.uk_-_946021.jpg/220px-Jane_Austen%27s_House%2C_Chawton_-_geograph.org.uk_-_946021.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Jane_Austen%27s_House%2C_Chawton_-_geograph.org.uk_-_946021.jpg/330px-Jane_Austen%27s_House%2C_Chawton_-_geograph.org.uk_-_946021.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Jane_Austen%27s_House%2C_Chawton_-_geograph.org.uk_-_946021.jpg/440px-Jane_Austen%27s_House%2C_Chawton_-_geograph.org.uk_-_946021.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Jane_Austen%27s_House,_Chawton_-_geograph.org.uk_-_946021.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Jane_Austen%27s_House_Museum" title="Jane Austen's House Museum">Chawton Cottage</a></div></div></div>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/February_24" title="February 24">February 24</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Theatre_Royal,_Drury_Lane" title="Theatre Royal, Drury Lane">Theatre Royal, Drury Lane</a>, London, is destroyed by fire. On being found drinking wine in the street while watching the conflagration, <a href="/wiki/Richard_Brinsley_Sheridan" title="Richard Brinsley Sheridan">Richard Brinsley Sheridan</a>, the proprietor, is reported as saying: "A man may surely be allowed to take a glass of wine by his own fireside."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The putative manuscript of <i><a href="/wiki/The_History_of_Cardenio" title="The History of Cardenio">The History of Cardenio</a></i> may have been lost in the blaze.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_1" title="March 1">March 1</a> – The literary and political periodical <a href="/wiki/Quarterly_Review" title="Quarterly Review"><i>The Quarterly Review</i></a> is first published by <a href="/wiki/John_Murray_(publisher)" title="John Murray (publisher)">John Murray</a> in London.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_1" title="June 1">June 1</a> – <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Taylor_Coleridge" title="Samuel Taylor Coleridge">Samuel Taylor Coleridge</a> founds <i>The Friend</i>, a weekly periodical which runs for some 25 issues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_7" title="July 7">July 7</a> – <a href="/wiki/Jane_Austen" title="Jane Austen">Jane Austen</a> settles with her sister and mother at <a href="/wiki/Jane_Austen%27s_House_Museum" title="Jane Austen's House Museum">Chawton Cottage</a> in <a href="/wiki/Chawton" title="Chawton">Chawton</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Alton,_Hampshire" title="Alton, Hampshire">Alton, Hampshire</a>, where she resumes writing regularly.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_18" title="September 18">September 18</a> – A new <a href="/wiki/Royal_Opera_House" title="Royal Opera House">Theatre Royal, Covent Garden</a>, London opens to replace the first, burnt down in 1808, with a performance of <i><a href="/wiki/Macbeth" title="Macbeth">Macbeth</a></i>. An increase in ticket prices causes the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Old_Price_Riots,_1809" title="Old Price Riots, 1809">Old Price Riots</a>, which last for 64 days, until the manager, <a href="/wiki/John_Philip_Kemble" title="John Philip Kemble">John Philip Kemble</a>, reverses the price rise.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Walnut_Street_Theatre" title="Walnut Street Theatre">Walnut Street Theatre</a> in <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a> is established as "The New Circus" by the <a href="/wiki/Circus_of_Pepin_and_Breschard" title="Circus of Pepin and Breschard">Circus of Pepin and Breschard</a>. It is the oldest continually operating <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theatre_(structure)" title="Theatre (structure)">theatre</a> in the English-speaking world and the oldest in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/William_Combe" title="William Combe">William Combe</a> begins publication of the verse <i>Tour of Dr Syntax in search of the Picturesque</i> in Ackermann's <i>Political Magazine</i> (London), illustrated by <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Rowlandson" title="Thomas Rowlandson">Thomas Rowlandson</a>, satirising <a href="/wiki/William_Gilpin_(priest)" title="William Gilpin (priest)">William Gilpin</a>'s views on the <a href="/wiki/Picturesque" title="Picturesque">picturesque</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tom_Stoppard" title="Tom Stoppard">Tom Stoppard</a>'s play <i><a href="/wiki/Arcadia_(play)" title="Arcadia (play)">Arcadia</a></i> (<a href="/wiki/1993_in_literature" title="1993 in literature">1993</a>) is set partly in this year.</li></ul>
