<p>The <b>Erdbeerkopf</b> is an <span style="border-width:0px; padding:0px; margin:0px;">847.7 m</span>-high mountain<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DE_BFN-Karten_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-DE_BFN-Karten-1">[1]</a></sup> in the <a href="/wiki/Harz" title="Harz">High Harz</a> in central Germany, northeast of <a href="/wiki/Schierke" title="Schierke">Schierke</a> in the state of <a href="/wiki/Saxony-Anhalt" title="Saxony-Anhalt">Saxony-Anhalt</a>.
</p><p>To the north and east, the forested summit is bordered by the <a href="/wiki/Wormsgraben" title="Wormsgraben">Wormsgraben</a>, the valley of the <a href="/wiki/Wormke" title="Wormke">Wormke</a>. To the south runs the <a href="/wiki/Brocken_Railway" title="Brocken Railway">Brocken Railway</a> along the slopes of the Erdbeerkopf.
</p><p>There are two paths leading to the mountain, from the east and west. To the southwest a field runs downhill that used to be a ski slope. The remains of a derelict ski lift can still be seen there. The mountain is very rarely climbed despite its accessible location. On the summit is a wooden bench from where part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Harz" title="South Harz">South Harz</a> can be seen through the gap in the trees.
</p>
