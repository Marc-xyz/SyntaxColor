	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Maca Likutabua</h1>

<p><b>Lady Liku</b> (23 April 1935 – 24 March 2000) was a woman of chiefly title in the Fiji Islands and was the wife of the late <a href="/wiki/Ratu" title="Ratu">Ratu</a> Sir <a href="/wiki/Lala_Sukuna" title="Lala Sukuna">Lala Sukuna</a>.
</p><p><b>Maca Likutabua Gucake</b> was born in the village of <a href="/wiki/Tubou" title="Tubou">Tubou</a>, on the island of <a href="/wiki/Lakeba" title="Lakeba">Lakeba</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Lau_Islands" title="Lau Islands">Lau Islands</a> to a chiefly family.  Her father, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Roko_Lenaitasi_Gucake&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Roko Lenaitasi Gucake (page does not exist)">Roko Lenaitasi Gucake</a> was from the Yavusa <a href="/wiki/Vuanirewa" title="Vuanirewa">Vuanirewa</a>, the ruling tribe of the Lau islands, and belonged to the Noble <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Vatuwaqa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Vatuwaqa (page does not exist)">Vatuwaqa</a> household. Her mother, Mereani Naisua was the daughter of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ratu_Jone_Tawake&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ratu Jone Tawake (page does not exist)">Ratu Jone Tawake</a> of the chiefly <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tui_Kaba&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tui Kaba (page does not exist)">Tui Kaba</a> household of <a href="/wiki/Bau_(island)" title="Bau (island)">Bau</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriage">Marriage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maca_Likutabua&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Marriage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lady Liku was known for her beauty and was considered to be the most beautiful young lady in <a href="/wiki/Lau_Islands" title="Lau Islands">Lau</a>.  Her beauty did not go unnoticed, in fact it caught the eye of <a href="/wiki/Ratu" title="Ratu">Ratu</a> Sir <a href="/wiki/Lala_Sukuna" title="Lala Sukuna">Lala Sukuna</a> who was then the <a href="/wiki/Tui_Lau" title="Tui Lau">Tui Lau</a>.  <a href="/wiki/Ratu" title="Ratu">Ratu</a> Sukuna, using his status as <a href="/wiki/Tui_Lau" title="Tui Lau">Tui Lau</a>, easily arranged his marriage to Lady Liku.
</p><p>She travelled from Lakeba to Viti Levu for the arranged marriage, and as she got off the boat, it was said that the people saw her and shook their heads in disappointment because they felt her beauty had gone to waste.
</p><p>They finally married in September 1957 when she was 21 years old, and at the time, <a href="/wiki/Ratu" title="Ratu">Ratu</a> Sukuna was 69 years old.  The marriage did not even last a year as Ratu Sukuna died at sea en route to England on the ship Arcadia.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maca_Likutabua&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lady Liku lived on as a widow and never married again, nor did she have any children.  She died on March 24, 2000 from Diabetes in the Colonial War Memorial Hospital in <a href="/wiki/Suva" title="Suva">Suva</a>.
</p>
