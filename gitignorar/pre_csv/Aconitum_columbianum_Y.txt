<p><i><b>Aconitum columbianum</b></i> is a species of flowering plant in the <a href="/wiki/Ranunculaceae" title="Ranunculaceae">buttercup family</a> known by the common names <b>Columbian monkshood</b> or <b>western monkshood</b>.
</p><p>This wildflower is native to western North America where it grows in riparian and other moist areas, in meadows and coniferous forests.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It is found from 600â2,900 metres (2,000â9,500Â ft) in elevation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aconitum_columbianum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Aconitum columbianum</i> is a tall spindly erect to scandent forb which is perennial from rhizomes. It has lobed or toothed leaves and long stems with far-spaced flowers.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Aconitum_columbianum_seeds.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2000" data-file-width="3008" decoding="async" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Aconitum_columbianum_seeds.jpg/220px-Aconitum_columbianum_seeds.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Aconitum_columbianum_seeds.jpg/330px-Aconitum_columbianum_seeds.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Aconitum_columbianum_seeds.jpg/440px-Aconitum_columbianum_seeds.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Aconitum_columbianum_seeds.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Seeds</div></div></div>
<p>The folded, wrinkly flowers are often deep blue or purple, but may also be white or yellowish, and they usually have a spur. The fruits are pod-like <a href="/wiki/Follicle_(fruit)" title="Follicle (fruit)">follicles</a>.
</p><p>Like other <a href="/wiki/Aconitum" title="Aconitum">monkshoods</a> (<i>Aconitum</i> species), this plant is poisonous to humans and livestock, although some species have been used to make drugs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
