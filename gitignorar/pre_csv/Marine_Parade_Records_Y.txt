<p><b>Marine Parade</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Independent_record_label" title="Independent record label">independent record label</a> set up and owned by the <a href="/wiki/Breakbeat" title="Breakbeat">breakbeat</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DJ" title="DJ">DJ</a> and <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">producer</a> <a href="/wiki/Adam_Freeland" title="Adam Freeland">Adam Freeland</a> in 1998. The name of the label originates from the street on which Freeland used to live in his hometown of <a href="/wiki/Brighton" title="Brighton">Brighton</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>.
</p><p>The label underwent some financial difficulties in mid-2004 when one of its distributors went into <a href="/wiki/Liquidation" title="Liquidation">liquidation</a>. This difficulty caused problems with the supply of the album <i><a href="/wiki/You_Can_Be_Special_Too" title="You Can Be Special Too">You Can Be Special Too</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Evil_Nine" title="Evil Nine">Evil Nine</a>. Only 1000 copies reached the stores, and it became a much sought-after record, with copies fetching up to $90 on <a href="/wiki/EBay" title="EBay">eBay</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The label has since recovered and re-released the record.
</p><p>The imprint’s output has spanned everything from classic dance-music anthems like Freeland's <i><a href="/wiki/We_Want_Your_Soul" title="We Want Your Soul">We Want Your Soul</a></i> and ILS' <i>Next Level</i> to Jape's space-folk hit <i>Floating</i>.
</p><p>Marine Parade has released some enduring electronic albums, such as Freeland's <i>Now&amp;Them</i>, <a href="/wiki/Evil_Nine" title="Evil Nine">Evil Nine</a>'s <i>You Can Be Special Too</i>, and ILS' <i>Soul Trader</i>. Many remixes have been commissioned from other esteemed dance-music acts, like <a href="/wiki/Prins_Thomas" title="Prins Thomas">Prins Thomas</a>, Switch and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=D.I.M&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="D.I.M (page does not exist)">D.I.M</a>, and featured collaborations with artists ranging from <a href="/wiki/Aesop_Rock" title="Aesop Rock">Aesop Rock</a> to <a href="/wiki/Josh_Wink" title="Josh Wink">Josh Wink</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tom_Middleton" title="Tom Middleton">Tom Middleton</a>.
</p><p>Marine Parade has proven itself to be a launch-pad for new talent, having helped the career of Australian band Infusion, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UNKLE" title="UNKLE">UNKLE</a> collaborator Richard File (as Forme), and <a href="/wiki/Bj%C3%B6rk" title="Björk">Björk</a> musical director Damian Taylor (as Stone Lions). 2008 saw the second album from Evil Nine <i>They Live</i>.
</p><p>The second Freeland artist album <i>Cope™</i> was released on the label in 2009, featuring contributions from Tommy Lee of Mötley Crüe, <a href="/wiki/Alex_Metric" title="Alex Metric">Alex Metric</a> and <a href="/wiki/Twiggy_Ramirez" title="Twiggy Ramirez">Twiggy Ramirez</a> amongst others. A double disc <i>Cope™ Remixed</i> compilation was released at the end of the year with remixes covering myriad genre, from Joker, <a href="/wiki/Gui_Boratto" title="Gui Boratto">Gui Boratto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Emalkay" title="Emalkay">Emalkay</a>, Prins Thomas and <a href="/wiki/Siriusmo" title="Siriusmo">Siriusmo</a>. The label also released the debut album of PANTyRAiD (<i>Ooah of Glitch Mob and MartyParty</i>).
</p><p>In 2010 Marine Parade released a single by label boss Adam Freeland titled 'How To Fake Your Own Life', which included a remix by Om Unit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
