<p><b>Malik Fiaz Ahmad Awan</b> is a Pakistani politician who was a Member of the <a href="/wiki/Provincial_Assembly_of_the_Punjab" title="Provincial Assembly of the Punjab">Provincial Assembly of the Punjab</a>, from 2008 to May 2018.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malik_Fiaz_Ahmad_Awan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He was born on 6 September 1960 in <a href="/wiki/Hafizabad" title="Hafizabad">Hafizabad</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pap_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-pap-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>He has the degree of Bachelor of Arts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pap_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-pap-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Malik_Fiaz_Ahmad_Awan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He ran for the seat of the <a href="/wiki/Provincial_Assembly_of_the_Punjab" title="Provincial Assembly of the Punjab">Provincial Assembly of the Punjab</a> as an independent candidate from Constituency PP-79 (Gujranwala-III) in <a href="/wiki/1990_Pakistani_general_election" title="1990 Pakistani general election">1990 Pakistani general election</a> but was unsuccessful. He received 11,625 votes and lost the seat to Noor Muhammad, a candidate of <a href="/wiki/Islami_Jamhoori_Ittehad" title="Islami Jamhoori Ittehad">Islami Jamhoori Ittehad</a> (IJI).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ecp/pa-88-97_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ecp/pa-88-97-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>He ran for the seat of the Provincial Assembly of the Punjab as an independent candidate from Constituency PP-79 (Hafizabad-I) in <a href="/wiki/1993_Pakistani_general_election" title="1993 Pakistani general election">1993 Pakistani general election</a> but was unsuccessful. He received 5,848 votes and lost the seat to Syed Muhammad Arif Hussain, a candidate of <a href="/wiki/Pakistan_Muslim_League_(N)" title="Pakistan Muslim League (N)">Pakistan Muslim League (N)</a> (PML-N).
</p><p>He ran for the seat of the Provincial Assembly of the Punjab as a candidate of <a href="/wiki/Pakistan_Peoples_Party" title="Pakistan Peoples Party">Pakistan Peoples Party</a> (PPP) from Constituency PP-105 (Hafizabad-I) in <a href="/wiki/2002_Pakistani_general_election" title="2002 Pakistani general election">2002 Pakistani general election</a>, but was unsuccessful. He received 24,638 votes and lost the seat to Muzaffar Ali Sheikh, a candidate of <a href="/wiki/Pakistan_Muslim_League_(Q)" title="Pakistan Muslim League (Q)">Pakistan Muslim League (Q)</a> (PML-Q).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>He was elected to the Provincial Assembly of the Punjab as a candidate of PPP from Constituency PP-105 (Hafizabad-I) in <a href="/wiki/2008_Pakistani_general_election" title="2008 Pakistani general election">2008 Pakistani general election</a>. He received 34,134 votes and defeated Haji Rai Riasat Ali, a candidate of PML-Q.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>He was re-elected to the Provincial Assembly of the Punjab as a candidate of PML-N from Constituency PP-105 (Hafizabad-I) in <a href="/wiki/2013_Pakistani_general_election" title="2013 Pakistani general election">2013 Pakistani general election</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He received 55,021 votes and defeated an independent candidate, Muzaffar Ali Sheikh.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
