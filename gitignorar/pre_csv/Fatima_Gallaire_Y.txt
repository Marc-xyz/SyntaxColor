	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Fatima Gallaire</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Fatima Gallaire</b>, née <b>Bourega</b>, (born 1944) is a Franco-<a href="/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algerian</a> playwright and author of short stories, who writes in <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>. Born in Algeria, she holds a degree in French literature from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_Algiers" title="University of Algiers">University of Algiers</a>, and one in cinema from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paris_8_University" title="Paris 8 University">Paris 8 University</a>. She has written over twenty plays, many of which have been translated and performed in languages including English, Italian, German, Spanish and Uzbek. These include <i>Princesses</i>, translated as <i>You have come back</i>, and <i>Les Co-épouses</i>, translated as <i>House of Wives</i>. She received the Arletty Prize for Drama in 1990 and the <a href="/wiki/Acad%C3%A9mie_fran%C3%A7aise" title="Académie française">Académie française</a> AMIC award in 1994.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fatima_Gallaire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gallaire was born as Fatima Bourega on 7 August 1944 in <a href="/wiki/El_Harrouch" title="El Harrouch">El Harrouch</a> in north-eastern Algeria. After studying French literature at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_Algiers" title="University of Algiers">University of Algiers</a> (1963–67), she moved to <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> where she studied cinema until 1970.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gall_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-gall-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>After working for three years at the Algiers Cinëmathèque, she returned to Paris in 1975 where she earned a degree in Cinema Studies at Paris 8 University, <a href="/wiki/Vincennes" title="Vincennes">Vincennes</a>, in 1980. The following year she married a Frenchman, adopting his family name for her publications.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Déjeux1994_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Déjeux1994-2">[2]</a></sup> She gave birth to twins, a boy and a girl, in 1981.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MakwardCottenet-Hage1996_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-MakwardCottenet-Hage1996-3">[3]</a></sup> In 1985, she began to write for the theatre, her <i>Ah vous êtes venus...</i> (later retitled <i>Princesses</i>) being presented at the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Th%C3%A9%C3%A2tre_Essaion&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Théâtre Essaion (page does not exist)">Théâtre Essaion</a> in Paris in February 1986.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Scherer1995_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Scherer1995-4">[4]</a></sup> In 1991, she wrote her first novel <i>Le Mendigot</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gall_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-gall-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Works_translated_into_English">Works translated into English</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fatima_Gallaire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Works translated into English">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><i>You have come back</i> (<i>Princesses</i>), in "Plays by women: an International Anthology", ed. Francoise Kourilsky et Catherine Temerson, Ubu Repertory Theater Publications, 1988.</li>
<li><i>Madame Bertin's Testimony</i> (<i>Témoignage contre un homme stérile</i>), in "Monologues : plays from Martinique, France, Algeria, Quebec", Ubu Repertory Theater, New York, 1995.</li>
<li><i>House of Wives</i> (<i>Les Co-épouses</i>), in "Four Plays from North Africa", ed. Marvin Carlson, Martin E. Segal Theatre Center Publications, New York, 2008.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fatima_Gallaire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1990, she was awarded the Arletty Prize for her contributions to drama.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MakwardCottenet-Hage1996_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-MakwardCottenet-Hage1996-3">[3]</a></sup> She also received the AMIC Award from the Académie française in 1994.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sleeman2001_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sleeman2001-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
