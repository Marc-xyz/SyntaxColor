	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Garw/Ffaldau Colliery</h1>

<p>The <b>Garw/Ffaldau Colliery</b> was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Colliery" title="Colliery">colliery</a> formed in 1975 in <a href="/wiki/Pontycymer" title="Pontycymer">Pontycymer</a>, Wales. It was formed from the joining together of the <b>Garw Colliery</b> and the <b>Ffaldau Colliery</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Curtis2013_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Curtis2013-2">[2]</a></sup> The Ffaldau Colliery had 1,100 workers in 1927, though this had declined by half by the mid 1930s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CampbellFishman2016_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-CampbellFishman2016-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>The pit closed in November 1985; a total of 630 miners lost their jobs. A potential buy out of the pit by workers had been mooted, but this was deemed to have been prohibitively expensive after the <a href="/wiki/National_Coal_Board" title="National Coal Board">National Coal Board</a> cut the ropes on the caged lift and 200,000 tonnes of hardcore were poured down the mine shaft. A reunion was held of former mine workers in 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WONov15_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-WONov15-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Garw Colliery (also known as the Ocean Colliery) was one of six pits in the Garw valley.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DigGarw_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-DigGarw-5">[5]</a></sup> Following the merger of the two pits workers entering at <a href="/wiki/Blaengarw" title="Blaengarw">Blaengarw</a> had a 1-2 mile walk underground due to the closure of the access shaft at Pontycymer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WONov15_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-WONov15-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
