	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">James Burgess (archaeologist)</h1>

<b>James Burgess</b> <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Indian_Empire" title="Order of the Indian Empire">CIE</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FRSE" title="FRSE">FRSE</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FRGS" title="FRGS">FRGS</a> MRAS LLD (14 August 1832<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IndBio_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-IndBio-1">[1]</a></sup> – 3 October 1916), was the founder of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Indian_Antiquary" title="The Indian Antiquary">The Indian Antiquary</a></i> in 1872<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Temp_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Temp-2">[2]</a></sup> and an important archaeologist of India in the 19th century.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_Burgess_(archaeologist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:22_Seton_Place,_Edinburgh.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2056" data-file-width="3088" decoding="async" height="200" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/22_Seton_Place%2C_Edinburgh.jpg/300px-22_Seton_Place%2C_Edinburgh.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/22_Seton_Place%2C_Edinburgh.jpg/450px-22_Seton_Place%2C_Edinburgh.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/22_Seton_Place%2C_Edinburgh.jpg/600px-22_Seton_Place%2C_Edinburgh.jpg 2x" width="300"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:22_Seton_Place,_Edinburgh.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>22 Seton Place, Edinburgh</div></div></div>
<p>Burgess was born on 14 August 1832 in <a href="/wiki/Kirkmahoe" title="Kirkmahoe">Kirkmahoe</a>, Dumfriesshire. He was educated at Dumfries and then the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Glasgow" title="University of Glasgow">University of Glasgow</a> and the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Edinburgh" title="University of Edinburgh">University of Edinburgh</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sewell_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sewell-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>He did educational work in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Calcutta" title="Calcutta">Calcutta</a>, 1856 and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bombay" title="Bombay">Bombay</a>, 1861, and was Secretary of the Bombay Geographical Society 1868-73. He was Head of the Archaeological Survey, Western India, 1873, and of South India, 1881. From 1886-89 he was Director General, <a href="/wiki/Archaeological_Survey_of_India" title="Archaeological Survey of India">Archaeological Survey of India</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IndBio_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-IndBio-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1881 the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Edinburgh" title="University of Edinburgh">University of Edinburgh</a> awarded him an honorary Doctor of Letters (LLD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-royalsoced.org.uk_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-royalsoced.org.uk-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>He retired to <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a> around 1892.
</p><p>He was elected a Fellow of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society_of_Edinburgh" title="Royal Society of Edinburgh">Royal Society of Edinburgh</a> in 1894. He won its <a href="/wiki/Keith_Medal" title="Keith Medal">Keith Medal</a> for 1897-99, and served as their Vice President 1908 to 1914.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-royalsoced.org.uk_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-royalsoced.org.uk-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>He died on 3 October 1916, at 22 Seton Place in <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sewell_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Sewell-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
