<p><b>Ela Pitam</b> (born 10 February 1977, <a href="/wiki/Riga" title="Riga">Riga</a>) is an <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israeli</a> <a href="/wiki/Chess" title="Chess">chess</a> player who holds the title of <a href="/wiki/FIDE_titles" title="FIDE titles">Woman Grandmaster</a> (WGM, 1997). Winner the <a href="/wiki/Israeli_Chess_Championship" title="Israeli Chess Championship">Israeli Women Chess Championship</a> (2000).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ela_Pitam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1991 Ela Pitam left <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> for Israel. She graduated from <a href="/wiki/University_of_Haifa" title="University of Haifa">University of Haifa</a>. 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chess_career">Chess career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ela_Pitam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Chess career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pitam began to study chess in Latvia. In 1990 she took part in the Open Chess Championship of Latvia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> From 1992 to 1997 she participated in <a href="/wiki/European_Youth_Chess_Championship" title="European Youth Chess Championship">European Youth Chess Championships</a> and <a href="/wiki/World_Youth_Chess_Championship" title="World Youth Chess Championship">World Youth Chess Championships</a>. The best result - 3rd place in 1994 in European Girl Chess Championship U18 in <a href="/wiki/Chania" title="Chania">Chania</a>.
</p><p>In 1998, in <a href="/wiki/Dresden" title="Dresden">Dresden</a> she shared the first place with in the <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_World_Chess_Championship" title="Women's World Chess Championship">Women's World Chess Championship</a> zone tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Ela Pitam participated in <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_World_Chess_Championship" title="Women's World Chess Championship">Women's World Chess Championship</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Knock-out_tournament" title="Knock-out tournament">knock-out</a> tournament in <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_World_Chess_Championship_2000" title="Women's World Chess Championship 2000">2000</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_Delhi" title="New Delhi">New Delhi</a> and lost <a href="/wiki/Ekaterina_Kovalevskaya" title="Ekaterina Kovalevskaya">Ekaterina Kovalevskaya</a> in the 1st round.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2000 she won the Israeli Women's Chess Championship. In 2001 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tel-Aviv" title="Tel-Aviv">Tel-Aviv</a> she shared the third place in the International Women's Chess tournament (won <a href="/wiki/Ildik%C3%B3_M%C3%A1dl" title="Ildikó Mádl">Ildikó Mádl</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Pitam played for Israel in the <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Chess_Olympiad" title="Women's Chess Olympiad">Women's Chess Olympiads</a>:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>In 1996, at first reserve board in the <a href="/wiki/32nd_Chess_Olympiad" title="32nd Chess Olympiad">32nd Chess Olympiad (women)</a> in <a href="/wiki/Yerevan" title="Yerevan">Yerevan</a> (+8, =3, -1) and won individual silver medal,</li>
<li>In 1998, at second board in the <a href="/wiki/33rd_Chess_Olympiad" title="33rd Chess Olympiad">33rd Chess Olympiad (women)</a> in <a href="/wiki/Elista" title="Elista">Elista</a> (+4, =7, -1),</li>
<li>In 2000, at second board in the <a href="/wiki/34th_Chess_Olympiad" title="34th Chess Olympiad">34th Chess Olympiad (women)</a> in <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a> (+4, =2, -5),</li>
<li>In 2010, at third board in the <a href="/wiki/39th_Chess_Olympiad" title="39th Chess Olympiad">39th Chess Olympiad (women)</a> in <a href="/wiki/Khanty-Mansiysk" title="Khanty-Mansiysk">Khanty-Mansiysk</a> (+3, =1, -3).</li></ul>
<p>Pitam played for Israel in the <a href="/wiki/European_Team_Chess_Championship" title="European Team Chess Championship">European Team Chess Championships</a>:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>In 1997, at second board in the 2nd European Team Chess Championship (women) in <a href="/wiki/Pula" title="Pula">Pula</a> (+2, =2, -1),</li>
<li>In 1999, at second board in the 3rd European Team Chess Championship (women) in <a href="/wiki/Batumi" title="Batumi">Batumi</a> (+2, =5, -2),</li>
<li>In 2009, at third board in the 8th European Team Chess Championship (women) in <a href="/wiki/Novi_Sad" title="Novi Sad">Novi Sad</a> (+3, =1, -3).</li></ul>
