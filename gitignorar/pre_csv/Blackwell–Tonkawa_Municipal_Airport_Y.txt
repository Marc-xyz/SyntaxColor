<p><b>Blackwell–Tonkawa Municipal Airport</b> (<a href="/wiki/IATA_airport_code" title="IATA airport code">IATA</a>: <b>BWL</b>, <a href="/wiki/ICAO_airport_code" title="ICAO airport code">ICAO</a>: <b>KBKN</b>, <a href="/wiki/Federal_Aviation_Administration" title="Federal Aviation Administration">FAA</a> <a href="/wiki/Location_identifier" title="Location identifier">LID</a>: <b>BKN<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r886047488">.mw-parser-output .nobold{font-weight:normal}</style><span class="nobold">, formerly</span> 4O3</b>) is a public use <a href="/wiki/Airport" title="Airport">airport</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Kay_County,_Oklahoma" title="Kay County, Oklahoma">Kay County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">Oklahoma</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. The airport is five nautical miles (9 km) southwest of the <a href="/wiki/Central_business_district" title="Central business district">central business district</a> of <a href="/wiki/Blackwell,_Oklahoma" title="Blackwell, Oklahoma">Blackwell, Oklahoma</a> and is owned by the cities of Blackwell and <a href="/wiki/Tonkawa,_Oklahoma" title="Tonkawa, Oklahoma">Tonkawa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FAA_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-FAA-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Although most U.S. airports use the same three-letter <a href="/wiki/Location_identifier" title="Location identifier">location identifier</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Aviation_Administration" title="Federal Aviation Administration">FAA</a> and <a href="/wiki/International_Air_Transport_Association" title="International Air Transport Association">IATA</a>, this airport is assigned <b>BKN</b> by the FAA and <b>BWL</b> by the IATA<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IATAsearch_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-IATAsearch-1">[1]</a></sup> (which assigned <b>BKN</b> to <a href="/wiki/Balkanabat_Airport" title="Balkanabat Airport">Balkanabat Airport</a> in <a href="/wiki/Balkanabat" title="Balkanabat">Balkanabat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkmenistan" title="Turkmenistan">Turkmenistan</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IATAsearch_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-IATAsearch-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Facilities_and_aircraft">Facilities and aircraft</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blackwell%E2%80%93Tonkawa_Municipal_Airport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Facilities and aircraft">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Blackwell–Tonkawa Municipal Airport covers an area of 209 acres (85 ha) at an <a href="/wiki/Elevation" title="Elevation">elevation</a> of 1,030 feet (314 m) above <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mean_sea_level" title="Mean sea level">mean sea level</a>. It has one <a href="/wiki/Runway" title="Runway">runway</a> designated 17/35 with an asphalt surface measuring 3,501 by 60 feet (1,067 x 18 m).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FAA_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-FAA-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>For the 12-month period ending May 6, 2008, the airport had 2,400 <a href="/wiki/General_aviation" title="General aviation">general aviation</a> aircraft operations, an average of 200 per month. At that time there were 15 aircraft based at this airport: 100% single-<a href="/wiki/Aircraft_engine" title="Aircraft engine">engine</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FAA_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-FAA-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
