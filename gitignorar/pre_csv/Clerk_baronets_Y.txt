	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Clerk baronets</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>

		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Baronetcy from Penicuik, Scotland</div>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">This article is about the Clerk baronetcy of Penicuik, Scotland. For the Clerks of Accra, Ghana, see <a href="/wiki/Clerk_family" title="Clerk family">Clerk family</a>.</div>
<p>There has been one creation of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baronetcy" title="Baronetcy">baronetcy</a> with the surname <b>Clerk</b> (<span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span><span title="'l' in 'lie'">l</span><span title="/ɑːr/: 'ar' in 'far'">ɑːr</span><span title="'k' in 'kind'">k</span></span>/</a></span></span>) (as distinct from <a href="/wiki/Clark_baronets" title="Clark baronets">Clark</a>, <a href="/wiki/Clarke_baronets" title="Clarke baronets">Clarke</a> and <a href="/wiki/Clerke_baronets" title="Clerke baronets">Clerke</a>). It was created in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baronetage_of_Nova_Scotia" title="Baronetage of Nova Scotia">Baronetage of Nova Scotia</a> by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Letters_Patent" title="Letters Patent">Letters Patent</a> dated 24 March 1679, for <b>John Clerk of Pennycuik</b> (or Penicuik; see <a href="/wiki/Penicuik_House" title="Penicuik House">Penicuik House</a>), whose father, also <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_Clerk_of_Penicuik" title="John Clerk of Penicuik">John Clerk of Penicuik</a>, had returned from Paris in 1647 with a considerable fortune and purchased the lands of <a href="/wiki/Penicuik" title="Penicuik">Penicuik</a> in <a href="/wiki/Midlothian" title="Midlothian">Midlothian</a>. The 1st Baronet acquired the lands of <a href="/wiki/Lasswade" title="Lasswade">Lasswade</a>, Midlothian, in 1700. The second Baronet built <a href="/wiki/Mavisbank_House" title="Mavisbank House">Mavisbank House</a> near <a href="/wiki/Loanhead" title="Loanhead">Loanhead</a> between 1723 and 1727. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> 
</p><p>The 3rd Baronet, James, laid out plans for a new town in 1770, inspired by the local plans for a New Town in <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a> which were by then coming into reality.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The rebuilding included a new church, St Mungos, in 1771, reputedly by Sir James himself.
</p><p>The family are said by Anderson (1867) to date from at least 1180 AD when one of them appeared as a witness to a donation to <a href="/wiki/Holyrood_Abbey" title="Holyrood Abbey">Holyrood Abbey</a> by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_I_of_Scotland" title="William I of Scotland">William The Lion</a>. <a href="/wiki/John_Scougal" title="John Scougal">John Scougal</a> is known to have painted at least two portraits of the first baronets.
</p><p>A number of the lineage are buried in Penicuik Old Parish Churchyard, with both a memorial gateway and a mausoleum to their memories.
</p>
