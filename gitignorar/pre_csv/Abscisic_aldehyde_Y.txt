<p><b>Abscisic aldehyde</b> is an intermediate in the <a href="/wiki/Biosynthesis" title="Biosynthesis">biosynthesis</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Plant_hormone" title="Plant hormone">plant hormone</a> <a href="/wiki/Abscisic_acid" title="Abscisic acid">abscisic acid</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It is produced by the dehydrodgenation of <a href="/wiki/Xanthoxin" title="Xanthoxin">xanthoxin</a> by xanthtoxin dehydrogenases, which is an NAD+ dependent short-chain dehydrogenas,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> followed by selective oxidation by abscisic aldehyde oxygenase.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Xanthtoaba.svg"><img alt="Xanthtoaba.svg" data-file-height="98" data-file-width="554" decoding="async" height="98" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Xanthtoaba.svg/554px-Xanthtoaba.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Xanthtoaba.svg/831px-Xanthtoaba.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Xanthtoaba.svg/1108px-Xanthtoaba.svg.png 2x" width="554"/></a>
</p>
