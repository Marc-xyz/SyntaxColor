<p><b>James Francis Conway</b> (14 June 1925 – 28 December 2003)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Medal_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Medal-1">[1]</a></sup> was an <a href="/wiki/Australian_rules_football" title="Australian rules football">Australian rules football</a> player and coach. The winner of the 1950 <a href="/wiki/Sandover_Medal" title="Sandover Medal">Sandover Medal</a>, Conway played 180 games for <a href="/wiki/East_Fremantle_Football_Club" title="East Fremantle Football Club">East Fremantle</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Western_Australian_National_Football_League" title="Western Australian National Football League">Western Australian National Football League</a> (WANFL) between 1943 and 1956, also representing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Sandgropers" title="The Sandgropers">Western Australia</a> in 15 interstate matches. In 1964, he was appointed coach of <a href="/wiki/Claremont_Football_Club" title="Claremont Football Club">Claremont</a>, a position which he held for five years, until 1968. He was inducted into the <a href="/wiki/West_Australian_Football_Hall_of_Fame" title="West Australian Football Hall of Fame">West Australian Football Hall of Fame</a> in 2009.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jim_Conway_(footballer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/North_Fremantle,_Western_Australia" title="North Fremantle, Western Australia">North Fremantle</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ww2_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ww2-2">[2]</a></sup> Conway originally played football for the North Fremantle team in the Ex-Scholars league.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He made his debut for <a href="/wiki/East_Fremantle_Football_Club" title="East Fremantle Football Club">East Fremantle</a> in 1943 at the age of 17 in the war-time underage competition, and, as captain, was involved in East Fremantle's premiership in his first year. In August 1944, he enlisted in the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Australian_Air_Force" title="Royal Australian Air Force">Royal Australian Air Force</a> as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leading_Aircraftman" title="Leading Aircraftman">Leading Aircraftman</a>, and was stationed with the 12 Aircraft Repair Depot until June 1946, when he was discharged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ww2_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-ww2-2">[2]</a></sup> On his return to <a href="/wiki/Western_Australia" title="Western Australia">Western Australia</a>, Conway immediately resumed playing with East Fremantle, and again played in a premiership. He made his interstate debut for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Sandgropers" title="The Sandgropers">Western Australia</a> in 1947, and the following season was appointed <a href="/wiki/Captain_(Australian_rules_football)" title="Captain (Australian rules football)">captain</a> of East Fremantle, a role he would reprise in 1951 and 1956.
</p><p>Playing mainly as a rover, and on occasion at centre half-forward, Conway won both East Fremantle's <a href="/wiki/Best_and_fairest" title="Best and fairest">best and fairest</a> award, the Lynn Medal, and the <a href="/wiki/Sandover_Medal" title="Sandover Medal">Sandover Medal</a> for the best player in the competition in 1950. The following season he kicked 77 goals to be East Fremantle's leading goalkicker. Conway retired in 1956 having played 180 games for East Fremantle, kicking 343 goals, as well as representing Western Australia on 15 occasions, kicking 21 goals. In total, he record 78 Sandover Medal votes over his career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> After his retirement, Conway was recruited as captain-coach of <a href="/wiki/Coolamon,_New_South_Wales" title="Coolamon, New South Wales">Coolamon</a> in country <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales" title="New South Wales">New South Wales</a>, taking over from ex-<a href="/wiki/South_Fremantle_Football_Club" title="South Fremantle Football Club">South Fremantle</a> player <a href="/wiki/Frank_Treasure" title="Frank Treasure">Frank Treasure</a>. In 1964, <a href="/wiki/Claremont_Football_Club" title="Claremont Football Club">Claremont</a> signed Conway as coach, replacing <a href="/wiki/Peter_Pianto" title="Peter Pianto">Peter Pianto</a>. After finishing last the previous two seasons, Claremont finished fourth in the regular season for 1964, with 12 wins and nine losses, qualifying for the finals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The club defeated <a href="/wiki/Subiaco_Football_Club" title="Subiaco Football Club">Subiaco</a> in the opening semi-final, and then went on to defeat <a href="/wiki/Perth_Football_Club" title="Perth Football Club">Perth</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Preliminary_final" title="Preliminary final">preliminary final</a>, qualifying for the <a href="/wiki/Grand_final" title="Grand final">grand final</a> against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minor_premier" title="Minor premier">minor premiers</a> East Fremantle, Conway's old team. In the grand final, Claremont defeated East Fremantle 14.18 (102) to 15.8 (98), to win their first premiership since 1940.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Conway spent four more seasons in charge of Claremont, with the club making the finals in 1965 only. He also coached the state team on a number of occasions.
</p><p>In 1997, Conway was included as the rover in East Fremantle's Team of the Century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He was included in the <a href="/wiki/Fremantle_Football_Hall_of_Legends" title="Fremantle Football Hall of Legends">Fremantle Football Hall of Legends</a> in 1998,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and inducted into the <a href="/wiki/West_Australian_Football_Hall_of_Fame" title="West Australian Football Hall of Fame">West Australian Football Hall of Fame</a> in 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
