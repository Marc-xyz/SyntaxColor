<p><i><b>Baurutitan</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sauropod" title="Sauropod">sauropod</a> <a href="/wiki/Dinosaur" title="Dinosaur">dinosaur</a> that lived during the <a href="/wiki/Late_Cretaceous" title="Late Cretaceous">Late Cretaceous</a> in what is now <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Type_species" title="Type species">type species</a>, <i>Baurutitan britoi</i>, was described in 2005 by <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Kellner" title="Alexander Kellner">Kellner</a> and colleagues, although the fossil remains had already been discovered in 1957. <i>Baurutitan</i> is classified as a <a href="/wiki/Lithostrotia" title="Lithostrotia">lithostrotian</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Titanosaur" title="Titanosaur">titanosaur</a>, and is distinguished from related genera based on its distinctive caudal <a href="/wiki/Vertebra" title="Vertebra">vertebrae</a>. This South American dinosaur was found in the <a href="/wiki/Mar%C3%ADlia_Formation" title="Marília Formation">Marília Formation</a> near <a href="/wiki/Uberaba" title="Uberaba">Uberaba</a>, in the Brazilian state of <a href="/wiki/Minas_Gerais" title="Minas Gerais">Minas Gerais</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kellner2005_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-kellner2005-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Discovery">Discovery</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baurutitan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Discovery">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The remains of <i>Baurutitan</i> were found in 1957 by <a href="/wiki/Llewellyn_Ivor_Price" title="Llewellyn Ivor Price">Llewellyn Ivor Price</a>, famous Brazilian paleontologist, in the region of Peirópolis, <a href="/wiki/Minas_Gerais" title="Minas Gerais">Minas Gerais</a>. It was, however, not until 2005 that it was officially published and named. The works of Price in Peirópolis began in 1947 after Jesuíno Felicíssimo Junior, from the Instituto Geográfico e Geológico of São Paulo, told him about the presence of fossils in the region. Price then conducted excavations in an old quarry, known as Quarry Caieira, on the in São Luis farm. Recovered fossils included those of <a href="/wiki/Turtle" title="Turtle">turtles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crocodylomorpha" title="Crocodylomorpha">crocodylomorphs</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theropod" title="Theropod">theropods</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sauropod" title="Sauropod">sauropods</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">fish</a>, freshwater invertebrates (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gastropod" title="Gastropod">gastropods</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bivalve" title="Bivalve">bivalves</a>), <a href="/wiki/Ichnotaxon" title="Ichnotaxon">trace fossils</a>, fragments of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ootaxon" title="Ootaxon">eggs</a>, and plant debris. Dynamite was occasionally used to remove materials embedded in the matrix. There were expeditions to Caieira between 1949 and 1961, after which fieldwork was not continued due to a lack of new relevant findings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kellner2005_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-kellner2005-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>A series of 19 vertebrae (the last sacral and 18 caudals) found at this locality were recognized as belong to those of a titanosaur; they were initially named 'Series "C"', and subsequently became the holotype of the new genus <i>Baurutitan</i> (specimen number MCT 1490-R). All material found was stored in the collection of Museu de Ciência da Terra (Earth Science Museum – MCT) of the Departamento Nacional de Produção Mineral (National Department of Mineral Production) in <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kellner2005_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-kellner2005-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baurutitan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name <i>Baurutitan</i> comes from the junction of the word "bauru", which alludes to the geographical region of the finding, within the <a href="/wiki/Bauru_Group" title="Bauru Group">Bauru Group</a>, and the word "titan" of Greek myths. The specific name "britoi" is given in honor of Ignácio Aureliano Machado Brito, a <a href="/wiki/Brazilians" title="Brazilians">Brazilian</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paleontologist" title="Paleontologist">paleontologist</a> who advised the study and description of this <a href="/wiki/Dinosaur" title="Dinosaur">dinosaur</a>, whose fossils have remained stored for many years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kellner2005_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-kellner2005-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baurutitan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Baurutitan</i> was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sauropod" title="Sauropod">sauropod</a> which was estimated to have measured 12–14 metres (39–46 ft) long and had a height of around 3.5 metres (11 ft). The holotype specimen was found in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marilia_Formation" title="Marilia Formation">Marilia Formation</a>, dating to the <a href="/wiki/Maastrichtian" title="Maastrichtian">Maastrichtian</a> epoch, some 72-66 million years ago. <i>Baurutitan</i> was the fourth sauropod described in <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, after <i><a href="/wiki/Antarctosaurus" title="Antarctosaurus">Antarctosaurus</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Gondwanatitan" title="Gondwanatitan">Gondwanatitan</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Amazonsaurus" title="Amazonsaurus">Amazonsaurus</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kellner2005_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-kellner2005-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>A 2017 cladistic analysis by José Carballido and colleagues places <i>Baurutitan</i> as a member of Lithostrotia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
