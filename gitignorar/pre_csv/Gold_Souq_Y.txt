	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Gold Souq</h1>

		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>A <b>Gold Souq</b> or <b>Gold Souk</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar">سوق الذهب</span>‎) is a <a href="/wiki/Gold" title="Gold">gold</a> market in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arab" title="Arab">arab</a> countries of <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabian Peninsula</a> and particularly in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cooperation_Council_for_the_Arab_States_of_the_Gulf" title="Cooperation Council for the Arab States of the Gulf">GCC</a> countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-time_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-time-1">[1]</a></sup> The word <i>Souq</i> is mostly used by Arabs for open markets. The term evolved through the expatriates settled in <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Cooperation_Council" title="Gulf Cooperation Council">Gulf Cooperation Council</a> countries, and spread to other Asian and western parts due to the growing numbers of expatriates in GCC countries. The term is often used to refer to the gold market in Arabized or Muslim cities, but also appears in Western and Asian cities.
</p><p>The world's largest indoor gold souq is located at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dubai_Mall" title="Dubai Mall">Dubai Mall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dubai" title="Dubai">Dubai</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UAE" title="UAE">UAE</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gulfnews_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-gulfnews-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
