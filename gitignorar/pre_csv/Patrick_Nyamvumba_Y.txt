<p>Gen. <b>Patrick Nyamvumba</b> (born 11 June 1967) is the immediate past Chief of Defence Staff of the <a href="/wiki/Rwanda_Defence_Force" title="Rwanda Defence Force">Rwanda Defence Force</a> (RDF) and the Minister of Internal Security of the Republic of Rwanda.
</p><p>From 2013-2019, he served as the Chief of the Rwandan Defence Force. Prior to that, from 2009 to 2013, he served in <a href="/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudan</a> as Force Commander of the AU-UN Hybrid Operation in <a href="/wiki/Darfur" title="Darfur">Darfur</a> (<a href="/wiki/United_Nations%E2%80%93African_Union_Mission_in_Darfur" title="United Nations–African Union Mission in Darfur">UNAMID</a>). A graduate of the <a href="/wiki/Nigerian_Defence_Academy" title="Nigerian Defence Academy">Nigerian Defence Academy</a>, Nyamvumba previously served the RDF as a commander of infantry forces, Commandant of the Rwanda Military Academy in Nyakinama, <a href="/wiki/Musanze_District" title="Musanze District">Musanze District</a>, President of the Military High Court (2007-2009), Chief of Logistics, and Chief of Operations, Plans, and Training (1998-1999).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
