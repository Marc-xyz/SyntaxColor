<p><b>Posthuman</b> are an electronic music duo consisting of cousins Richard Bevan and Joshu Doherty. 
</p><p>Establishing themselves in the early 2000's as part of the London <a href="/wiki/Intelligent_dance_music" title="Intelligent dance music">IDM</a> scene, through the following years the pair experimented with various styles and genres. Their <a href="/wiki/2002_in_music" title="2002 in music">2002</a> self-titled EP combined <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">Heavy Metal</a> with <a href="/wiki/Electronica" title="Electronica">electronica</a>, and their <a href="/wiki/2006_in_music" title="2006 in music">2006</a> album <i>The People's Republic</i> was heavily influenced by <a href="/wiki/Post-rock" title="Post-rock">post-rock</a>. From 2007 onward their output focused more on <a href="/wiki/Techno" title="Techno">techno</a> and <a href="/wiki/Electronica" title="Electronica">electronica</a>. Since 2010 their sound has been most heavily influenced by <a href="/wiki/Acid_house" title="Acid house">Acid house</a> and <a href="/wiki/Detroit_techno" title="Detroit techno">Detroit techno</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Band_biography">Band biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Posthuman_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Band biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Richard and Joshu are cousins, and first wrote music together as teenagers. In 1999, they sent their first demo CD to <a href="/wiki/Skam_Records" title="Skam Records">Skam Records</a>, resulting in a release on the label's SMAK series.
</p><p>In 2000, the pair moved to London and began running a series of electronic music clubnights under the name 'Seed'. These nights increased in size and soon relocated to <a href="/wiki/Aldwych_tube_station" title="Aldwych tube station">Aldwych tube station</a>, a disused underground train station. A number of these events took place between 2001 and 2004, among the artists who appeared were <a href="/wiki/Aphex_Twin" title="Aphex Twin">Aphex Twin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alison_Goldfrapp" title="Alison Goldfrapp">Alison Goldfrapp</a>, <a href="/wiki/Richard_X" title="Richard X">Richard X</a>, <a href="/wiki/Plaid_(band)" title="Plaid (band)">Plaid</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Funkst%C3%B6rung" title="Funkstörung">Funkstörung</a>. On the back of this series of events, the pair set up their own record label <a href="/wiki/Seed_Records" title="Seed Records">Seed Records</a>, as an outlet for their own material, and music by other artists who had appeared at the 'Seed' events. 
</p><p>The last of these events were to be held on 13 and 14 November 2004, however, <a href="/wiki/John_Peel" title="John Peel">John Peel</a>, who was booked to DJ, passed away around a fortnight before the event.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The following week, the event was canceled due to London Underground rescinding the venue hire, and on the night of the event, <a href="/wiki/John_Balance" title="John Balance">John Balance</a> of <a href="/wiki/Coil_(band)" title="Coil (band)">Coil</a> (who were also on the bill to play) also died.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>. Soon after, Joshu left Seed Records to work briefly for <a class="external text" href="http://www.b12records.com" rel="nofollow">B12 Records</a>. In 2010 he founded a new imprint <a class="external text" href="http://www.balkanvinyl.com" rel="nofollow">Balkan Vinyl</a>.
</p><p>Since 2007, Joshu has run the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Acid_House" title="Acid House">Acid House</a> clubnight <a class="external text" href="http://www.iheartacid.com" rel="nofollow">I Love Acid</a> and it's associated vinyl-only record label from 2014, named after the track by <a href="/wiki/Luke_Vibert" title="Luke Vibert">Luke Vibert</a>, who features regularly. 
</p><p>The artwork for their 2010 album <i>Syn Emergence</i> was based around Bevan's award winning visual project of the same name<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>.
</p><p>In 2018, their album <i>Mutant City Acid</i> was pressed on randomly coloured vinyl, with the entire process livestreamed on video from the pressing plant <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>. As opposed to manual pressing, marbled or mixed colour vinyl, this experimental randomised process was the first time this has been done successfully, leading to almost 100 different colour variations of the double album.
</p>
