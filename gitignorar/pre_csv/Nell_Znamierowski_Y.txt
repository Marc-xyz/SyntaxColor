	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Nell Znamierowski</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Nell Znamierowski</b> (born 1931) is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Textile_art" title="Textile art">textile artist</a>.
</p><p>Znamierowski was born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amsterdam,_New_York" title="Amsterdam, New York">Amsterdam</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-si.edu_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-si.edu-1">[1]</a></sup> She received a Bachelor of Fine Arts in Textile Design from the <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island_School_of_Design" title="Rhode Island School of Design">Rhode Island School of Design</a> in 1953,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tunis1982_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tunis1982-2">[2]</a></sup> and with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fulbright_Fellowship" title="Fulbright Fellowship">Fulbright Fellowship</a> traveled to <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a> to work at the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=School_of_Industrial_Design&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="School of Industrial Design (page does not exist)">School of Industrial Design</a>, where she developed an interest in <a href="/wiki/Rya_(rug)" title="Rya (rug)">Rya rugs</a>. During her career she has worked variously as a print designer, color consultant, and designer of woven fabric in addition to teaching at the school of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Museum_of_Art" title="Brooklyn Museum of Art">Brooklyn Museum of Art</a> – where she also coordinated the fiber program – and the <a href="/wiki/Fashion_Institute_of_Technology" title="Fashion Institute of Technology">Fashion Institute of Technology</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-weavolution.com_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-weavolution.com-3">[3]</a></sup> Works created or designed by Znamierowski can be found in the collections of the <a href="/wiki/Smithsonian_American_Art_Museum" title="Smithsonian American Art Museum">Smithsonian American Art Museum</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-si.edu_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-si.edu-1">[1]</a></sup> the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cooper-Hewitt_Museum" title="Cooper-Hewitt Museum">Cooper-Hewitt Museum</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CH_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-CH-4">[4]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Art_Institute_of_Chicago" title="Art Institute of Chicago">Art Institute of Chicago</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and she has been featured in many solo and group exhibitions throughout the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tunis1982_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Tunis1982-2">[2]</a></sup> She is the author of a number of guides to weaving, including <i>Step-by-step weaving: a complete introduction to the craft of weaving, including photographs in full color</i> (1967) and <i>Step-by-step rugmaking: a complete introduction to the craft of rugmaking</i> (1972).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> She has also written for numerous textile-based publications, including <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Craft_Horizons&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Craft Horizons (page does not exist)">Craft Horizons</a></i>, <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Piecework_(magazine)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Piecework (magazine) (page does not exist)">Piecework</a></i>, <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=American_Craft&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="American Craft (page does not exist)">American Craft</a></i>, <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Handwoven&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Handwoven (page does not exist)">Handwoven</a></i>, and <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Fiberarts&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Fiberarts (page does not exist)">Fiberarts</a></i>, and has lectured widely and given workshops throughout her career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-weavolution.com_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-weavolution.com-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
