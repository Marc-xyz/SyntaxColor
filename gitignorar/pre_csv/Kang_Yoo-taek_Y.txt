<p><b>Kang Yootaek</b> (born 26 November 1991) is a <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">Korean</a> 4-dan <a href="/wiki/Go_professional" title="Go professional">professional</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Go_players" title="Go players">Go player</a>, ranked #13<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> (as of April 2011) in the official rating system of the <a href="/wiki/Korea_Baduk_Association" title="Korea Baduk Association">Korea Baduk Association</a> (the Hanguk Kiwon).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kang_Yoo-taek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kang started to play Go at the age of 7. He is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Yangchun" title="Yangchun">Yangchun</a> Dail <a href="/wiki/Go_(game)" title="Go (game)">Baduk</a> School.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> He became professional in 2007 by winning the Korean <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Insei" title="Insei">Insei</a> league. He is currently ranked <a href="/wiki/Go_ranks_and_ratings" title="Go ranks and ratings">4-dan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
