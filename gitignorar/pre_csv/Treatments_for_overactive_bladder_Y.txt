<p><b>Treatments for overactive bladder</b> are therapies used to treat <a href="/wiki/Overactive_bladder" title="Overactive bladder">overactive bladder</a> or related conditions, such as <a href="/wiki/Urinary_incontinence" title="Urinary incontinence">urinary incontinence</a> and <a href="/wiki/Frequent_urination" title="Frequent urination">frequent urination</a>. Drugs are commonly prescribed for this. Other non-drug treatments are also used.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Efficacy_of_treatments">Efficacy of treatments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Treatments_for_overactive_bladder&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Efficacy of treatments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Studies have shown that few people get complete relief from overactive bladder drugs and that all available drugs are no more than moderately effective.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBDbladder_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBDbladder-1">[1]</a></sup> A typical person with overactive bladder may urinate 12 times per day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBDbladder_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-BBDbladder-1">[1]</a></sup> Medication may reduce this number by 2-3 and reduce <a href="/wiki/Urinary_incontinence" title="Urinary incontinence">urinary incontinence</a> events by 1-2 per day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBDbladder_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-BBDbladder-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Oxybutynin is sometimes used in combination with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kegel_exercises" title="Kegel exercises">Kegel exercises</a> and <a href="/wiki/Functional_electrical_stimulation" title="Functional electrical stimulation">Functional electrical stimulation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>A 2009 <a href="/wiki/Literature_review" title="Literature review">literature review</a> on women found that no drug seemed better than others, and paid special attention to comparing newer drugs to older ones.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hartmann2009_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hartmann2009-3">[3]</a></sup> There is not sufficient evidence to guide the choices doctors and patients make in treating overactive bladder with experimental treatments including <a href="/wiki/Sacral_nerve_stimulation" title="Sacral nerve stimulation">sacral nerve stimulation</a>,  oxybutynin instillation, and <a href="/wiki/Botulinum_toxin" title="Botulinum toxin">botulinum toxin</a> injections.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hartmann2009_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Hartmann2009-3">[3]</a></sup> The research literature shows that in <a href="/wiki/Alternative_medicine" title="Alternative medicine">alternative medicine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Acupuncture" title="Acupuncture">acupuncture</a> has shown some efficacy while <a href="/wiki/Reflexology" title="Reflexology">reflexology</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hypnosis" title="Hypnosis">hypnosis</a> have not.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hartmann2009_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Hartmann2009-3">[3]</a></sup> In all cases the strength of evidence is not sufficient to allow fully informed recommendations of treatment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hartmann2009_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-Hartmann2009-3">[3]</a></sup> Some <a href="/wiki/Behavior_modification" title="Behavior modification">behavior modification</a> practices were associated with benefits comparable to any other treatment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hartmann2009_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-Hartmann2009-3">[3]</a></sup> The strength of the evidence is insufficient to fully inform choice of these treatments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hartmann2009_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-Hartmann2009-3">[3]</a></sup> Some <a href="/wiki/Behavior_modification" title="Behavior modification">behavior modification</a> practices produced symptom improvements comparable to medications, but the limited evidence available shows no improvement for patients who combine behavior changes with drugs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hartmann2009_3-6"><a href="#cite_note-Hartmann2009-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Non-drug_treatments">Non-drug treatments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Treatments_for_overactive_bladder&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Non-drug treatments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Non-drug treatments for overactive bladder include <a href="/wiki/Sacral_nerve_stimulation" title="Sacral nerve stimulation">sacral nerve stimulation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Acupuncture" title="Acupuncture">acupuncture</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Behavior_modification" title="Behavior modification">behavior modification</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Drugs">Drugs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Treatments_for_overactive_bladder&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Drugs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Classification_of_drugs">Classification of drugs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Treatments_for_overactive_bladder&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Classification of drugs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Most drugs used to treat overactive bladder are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Muscarinic_antagonists" title="Muscarinic antagonists">muscarinic antagonists</a>.
</p>
