<p><b>Ian Paul Cassidy</b><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="Why is 'Ian' in inverted commas? (December 2018)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> born November 4, 1964 in <a href="/wiki/Harrogate" title="Harrogate">Harrogate</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a>) is an <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a> who has appeared mainly in <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australian" title="Australian">Australian</a> productions. In 2000 he played Cracker Bob in <i><a href="/wiki/Highlander:_Endgame" title="Highlander: Endgame">Highlander: Endgame</a></i> and in 2001 he had a starring role in the drama series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Beast_(2001_TV_series)" title="The Beast (2001 TV series)">The Beast</a></i>, which was cancelled after five episodes. Cassidy first came to fame as the host of Australian television show <i>Kids Company</i>, on Channel 10.  He also appeared in many Shakespeare productions across Australia.  During the 1980s he participated in a number of Australian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Independent_films" title="Independent films">independent films</a>. During the first years of the 1990s he appeared in many U.S. films like <i><a href="/wiki/For_the_Boys" title="For the Boys">For the Boys</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Wind_(film)" title="Wind (film)">Wind</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/In_the_Line_of_Fire" title="In the Line of Fire">In the Line of Fire</a></i>, and Dimension Film's Sci-FiÂ :<i><a href="/wiki/Highlander:_Endgame" title="Highlander: Endgame">Highlander: Endgame</a></i>.  He has mainly appeared in American television series like <i><a href="/wiki/The_Pretender_(TV_series)" title="The Pretender (TV series)">The Pretender</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/EZ_Streets" title="EZ Streets">EZ Streets</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Pensacola:_Wings_of_Gold" title="Pensacola: Wings of Gold">Pensacola: Wings of Gold</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Walker,_Texas_Ranger" title="Walker, Texas Ranger">Walker, Texas Ranger</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Drake_%26_Josh" title="Drake &amp; Josh">Drake &amp; Josh</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Desperate_Housewives" title="Desperate Housewives">Desperate Housewives</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Numb3rs" title="Numb3rs">Numb3rs</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/24_(TV_series)" title="24 (TV series)">24</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Pacific_(TV_miniseries)" title="The Pacific (TV miniseries)">The Pacific</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Monk_(TV_series)" title="Monk (TV series)">Monk</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bold_and_the_Beautiful" title="The Bold and the Beautiful">The Bold and the Beautiful</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Young_and_the_Restless" title="The Young and the Restless">The Young and the Restless</a></i> and numerous others.  He has also appeared in many Video Games for <a href="/wiki/MachineGames" title="MachineGames">MachineGames</a>, including the highly acclaimed  <i><a href="/wiki/Wolfenstein:_The_New_Order" title="Wolfenstein: The New Order">Wolfenstein: The New Order</a></i> as Bobby Bram.
</p>
