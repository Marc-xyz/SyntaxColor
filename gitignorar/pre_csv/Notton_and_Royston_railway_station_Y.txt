<p><b>Notton and Royston railway station</b> was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Railway_station" title="Railway station">railway station</a> that served the village of <a href="/wiki/Royston,_South_Yorkshire" title="Royston, South Yorkshire">Royston</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Yorkshire" title="South Yorkshire">South Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. It was situated on the <a href="/wiki/Barnsley_Coal_Railway" title="Barnsley Coal Railway">Barnsley Coal Railway</a> between <a href="/wiki/Staincross_and_Mapplewell_railway_station" title="Staincross and Mapplewell railway station">Staincross and Mapplewell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ryhill_railway_station" title="Ryhill railway station">Ryhill</a>.
</p><p>The first section of the line to open was that from Stairfoot to Applehaigh (just north of Notton and Royston station) in 1870 so that Rosa Colliery could be served. The station opened, along with two others on the line, on 1 September 1882, and was closed to passengers by the <a href="/wiki/London_and_North_Eastern_Railway" title="London and North Eastern Railway">LNER</a> on 22 September 1930.
</p><p>It had flanking platforms and simple buildings to house all the facilities constructed in wood.
</p>
