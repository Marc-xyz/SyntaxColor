<p>The <b>New Socialist Party</b> (<a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>: <i lang="it">Nuovo Partito Socialista</i>, NPS) was a <a href="/wiki/Social_democracy" title="Social democracy">social-democratic</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nordsieck_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Nordsieck-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_San_Marino" title="List of political parties in San Marino">political party</a> in <a href="/wiki/San_Marino" title="San Marino">San Marino</a>.
</p><p>It was formed in 2005 as a split by those among the members of the <a href="/wiki/Sammarinese_Socialist_Party" title="Sammarinese Socialist Party">Sammarinese Socialist Party</a> who did not accept the merger with the post-communist <a href="/wiki/Party_of_Democrats" title="Party of Democrats">Party of Democrats</a> into the <a href="/wiki/Party_of_Socialists_and_Democrats" title="Party of Socialists and Democrats">Party of Socialists and Democrats</a>. Its Italian counterpart was the <a href="/wiki/New_Italian_Socialist_Party" title="New Italian Socialist Party">New Italian Socialist Party</a>, also affiliated with the centre-right.
</p><p>In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sammarinese_parliamentary_election,_2006" title="Sammarinese parliamentary election, 2006">2006 general election</a> NPS won 5.4% of the votes cast and took three seats and stood in opposition to the 2006-2008 governing coalition formed by the Party of Socialists and Democrats, <a href="/wiki/Popular_Alliance_(San_Marino)" title="Popular Alliance (San Marino)">Popular Alliance</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_Left_(San_Marino)" title="United Left (San Marino)">United Left</a>.
</p><p>In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sammarinese_parliamentary_election,_2008" title="Sammarinese parliamentary election, 2008">2008 general election</a> the party participated within the <a href="/wiki/Freedom_List" title="Freedom List">Freedom List</a> that won 1,317 votes (6.28%) and got 4 seats. The Freedom List is part of the centre-right <a href="/wiki/Pact_for_San_Marino" title="Pact for San Marino">Pact for San Marino</a> coalition. This electoral coalition won 35 seats out of 60 in the Grand and General Council in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sammarinese_parliamentary_election,_2008" title="Sammarinese parliamentary election, 2008">Sammarinese parliamentary election, 2008</a> gaining 54.22% of the national vote and a governmental majority of 5. The alliance has formed the new government of San Marino. Thus, the New Socialist Party (as part of the <a href="/wiki/Pact_for_San_Marino" title="Pact for San Marino">Pact for San Marino</a> coalition and of the Freedom List), became part of the official new government.
</p><p>On 30 May 2012 the party merged with the <a href="/wiki/Sammarinese_Reformist_Socialist_Party" title="Sammarinese Reformist Socialist Party">Sammarinese Reformist Socialist Party</a> to form the modern-day <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Party_(San_Marino)" title="Socialist Party (San Marino)">Socialist Party</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
