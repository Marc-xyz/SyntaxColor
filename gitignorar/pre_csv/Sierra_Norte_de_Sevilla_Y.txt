<p><b>Sierra Norte de Sevilla</b> or <b>Sierra Norte</b> (Northern Range) is one of the western <a href="/wiki/Mountain_range" title="Mountain range">mountain ranges</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Morena" title="Sierra Morena">Sierra Morena</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andalusia" title="Andalusia">Andalusia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>. Its name derives from its location at the northern part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sevilla_Province" title="Sevilla Province">Sevilla Province</a>. The highest point of the range is the 960 m high <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cerro_de_La_Capitana&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cerro de La Capitana (page does not exist)">Cerro de La Capitana</a>.
</p><p>This range gives its name to an administrative comarca in the region, the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sierra_Norte_de_Sevilla_Comarca&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sierra Norte de Sevilla Comarca (page does not exist)">Sierra Norte Comarca</a>, as well as to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vino_de_la_Tierra" title="Vino de la Tierra">wine of the area</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Norte_de_Sevilla_(Vino_de_la_Tierra)" title="Sierra Norte de Sevilla (Vino de la Tierra)">Vino de la Tierra Sierra Norte de Sevilla</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sierra_Norte_de_Sevilla&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Sierra Norte is not very high and its mountains have generally a rounded shape except for the occasional <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Karstic" title="Karstic">karstic</a> crags. 962 m high <i>Cerro de La Capitana</i> is the highest point of the range. The <a href="/wiki/Odiel" title="Odiel">Odiel</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Murtigas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Murtigas (page does not exist)">Murtigas</a> are the main rivers in the area of the range.
</p><p>The Sierra Norte is properly a <a href="/wiki/Massif" title="Massif">massif</a> that includes the subranges of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sierra_del_Agua&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sierra del Agua (page does not exist)">Sierra del Agua</a>, highest point 910 m high <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pico_Hamapega&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Pico Hamapega (page does not exist)">Pico Hamapega</a>, the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sierra_de_La_Grana&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sierra de La Grana (page does not exist)">Sierra de La Grana</a> at the edge of the <a href="/wiki/Guadalcanal,_Seville" title="Guadalcanal, Seville">Plains of Guadalcanal</a>, and the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sierra_de_San_Miguel&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sierra de San Miguel (page does not exist)">Sierra de San Miguel</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>The climate is less humid than in the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_de_Aracena" title="Sierra de Aracena">Sierra de Aracena</a> further west. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Holm_oak" title="Holm oak">Holm oaks</a> are more common, growing in clarified forests where there are also large clearings, known locally as <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dehesa_(pastoral_management)" title="Dehesa (pastoral management)">dehesas</a></i>. These are characteristic of the Sierra Morena landscape and are traditional pasture zones for cattle and pigs.
Also typical are the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gallery_forests" title="Gallery forests">gallery forests</a> shading the rivers of the range that provide a home to birds such as the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_imperial_eagle" title="Spanish imperial eagle">Spanish imperial eagle</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Cinereous_vulture" title="Cinereous vulture">cinereous vulture</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Black_stork" title="Black stork">black stork</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Cerro del Hierro</i> (iron hillock) is an interesting karstic site within the range which has been declared a <a href="/wiki/Natural_monument" title="Natural monument">natural monument</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Geology">Geology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sierra_Norte_de_Sevilla&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The geological structure of the Sierra Norte is quite complex. It includes terrain similar to the <a href="/wiki/Geology_of_the_Appalachians" title="Geology of the Appalachians">geology of the Appalachians</a>, together with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Granitic" title="Granitic">granitic</a> terrain, <a href="/wiki/Sill_(geology)" title="Sill (geology)">tabular</a> <a href="/wiki/Sheet_intrusion" title="Sheet intrusion">sheet intrusions</a>, as well as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Karstic" title="Karstic">karstic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pure_shear" title="Pure shear">pure shear</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Protected_areas">Protected areas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sierra_Norte_de_Sevilla&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Protected areas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sierra_Norte_de_Sevilla_Natural_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sierra Norte de Sevilla Natural Park (page does not exist)">Sierra Norte de Sevilla Natural Park</a> is a <a href="/wiki/Protected_area" title="Protected area">protected area</a> with <a href="/wiki/Natural_park_(Spain)" title="Natural park (Spain)">natural park</a>  status located in the Sierra Norte range.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
