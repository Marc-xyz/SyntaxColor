<p>The <b>IAR 12</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romanian</a> low-wing monoplane fighter-trainer aircraft designed before <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design_and_development">Design and development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=IAR-12&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Design and development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The failure of the first fighter designed at Brasov did not discourage the energetic team of the I.A.R. Works. Even before the second prototype of the C.V. 11 was disqualified from the fighter contest, the first details of its successor had already been laid out by Engineer-in-Chief Carafoli.  A new tail with enlarged rudder was fitted to essentially the same fuselage, giving a more conventional look and offering better control during flight. As a consequence, the overall height increased by more than a meter, i.e. 40% of the original dimension. Unlike the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IAR_11" title="IAR 11">IAR 11</a> design, the wingtips were rounded, and the span had been increased as well, giving a 19.80 m² wing area compared to the original 18.20 m² of the C.V. 11. An anti-crash pylon with a minuscule Venturi-tube installed at its top appeared behind the cockpit to protect the pilot in case the aircraft overturned.
</p><p>The powerplant chosen for the new aircraft, named I.A.R. 12, was again a Lorraine-Dietrich 12Eb, that offered 450 h.p. (336 kW) at 1,900 r.p.m., similar to the type fitted to the first C.V. 11. However, due to the increased aerodynamic drag, the maximum speed at ground level decreased to 294 km/h. This unsatisfactory result, combined with poor handling characteristics experienced during early test flights, constrained Carafoli to improve the construction and try a new engine, 
fitted to essentially the same fuselage, giving a more conventional look and offering better control during flight. An anti-crash pylon with a minuscule Venturi-tube installed at its top appeared behind the cockpit to protect the pilot in case the aircraft overturned. Such a feature on the C.V. 11 C1 could have saved the life of Cpt. Popescu on that fatal day in early December 1931.
</p>

<h2><span id="Specifications_.28IAR_12.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Specifications_(IAR_12)">Specifications (IAR 12)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=IAR-12&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Specifications (IAR 12)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p style="font-size: 90%; margin:0;"><i>Data from</i> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JAWA38_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-JAWA38-1">[1]</a></sup></p><p><span style="font-size: 120%;"><b>General characteristics</b></span>
</p><ul><li><b>Crew:</b> one</li>
<li><b>Length:</b> 7.2 m (24 ft 7 in)</li>
<li><b>Wingspan:</b> 11.70 m (38 ft 5 in)</li>
<li><b>Height:</b> 3.5 m (12 ft 6 in)</li>
<li><b>Wing area:</b> 19.8 m<sup>2</sup> (213.13 ft<sup>2</sup>)</li>
<li><b>Empty weight:</b> 1150 kg (2535 lb)</li>
<li><b>Gross weight:</b> 1,540 kg (3395 lb)</li>
<li><b>Powerplant:</b> 1 × <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IAR_LD_450" title="IAR LD 450">IAR LD 450</a> 12-cylinder W-form watercooled in-line built under licience <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lorraine-Dietrich_12_Eb&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lorraine-Dietrich 12 Eb (page does not exist)">Lorraine-Dietrich 12 Eb</a>, 340 kW (450 hp)</li></ul>
<p><span style="font-size: 120%;"><b>Performance</b></span>
</p>
<ul><li><b>Maximum speed:</b> at sea level 294 km/h (183 mph)</li>
<li><b>Endurance:</b> 2 hours  10 min</li>
<li><b>Service ceiling:</b> 7,900 m (25,915 ft)</li></ul>
<p><span style="font-size: 120%;"><b>Armament</b></span>
</p>
<ul><li>2 x 7.7 mm Vickers machine-guns in the nose of the aircraft firing through airscrew.</li></ul>
