<p><b>KXFN</b> (1380 <a href="/wiki/Hertz" title="Hertz">kHz</a>) is an <a href="/wiki/AM_broadcasting" title="AM broadcasting">AM</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radio_station" title="Radio station">radio station</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Louis,_Missouri" title="St. Louis, Missouri">St. Louis, Missouri</a>. It is owned by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Immaculate_Heart_Media,_Inc." title="Immaculate Heart Media, Inc.">Immaculate Heart Media, Inc.</a> and airs a <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish language</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Catholic_radio" title="Catholic radio">Catholic</a> <a href="/wiki/Radio_format" title="Radio format">format</a>. The station has a colorful history as a <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_hit_radio" title="Contemporary hit radio">Top 40</a> station <b>KWK</b>.
</p><p>KXFN employs separate daytime and nighttime <a href="/wiki/Transmitter" title="Transmitter">transmitter</a> sites; the daytime transmitter is located on <a href="/wiki/Chouteau_Island" title="Chouteau Island">Chouteau Island</a> near <a href="/wiki/Granite_City,_Illinois" title="Granite City, Illinois">Granite City, Illinois</a>, while the nighttime site is located further south near <a href="/wiki/Dupo,_Illinois" title="Dupo, Illinois">Dupo, Illinois</a>. Listeners in St. Louis and its adjacent communities can also hear KXFN programming on an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FM_radio" title="FM radio">FM</a> <a href="/wiki/Broadcast_relay_station" title="Broadcast relay station">translator station</a>, 105.3 <b>K287BY</b>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=KXFN&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=KXFN&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The station is among the oldest in St. Louis. It began broadcasting in 1925, as <b>KFVE</b>, licensed to the Film Corporation of America in St. Louis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>  In November 1927 it changed its <a href="/wiki/Call_sign" title="Call sign">call sign</a> to <b>KWK</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> At first, KWK was an <a href="/wiki/Network_affiliate" title="Network affiliate">affiliate</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NBC_Blue_Network" title="NBC Blue Network">NBC Blue Network</a>.
</p><p>KWK was owned by Thomas Patrick and had its offices and studios in <a href="/wiki/The_Chase_Park_Plaza_Hotel" title="The Chase Park Plaza Hotel">The Chase Park Plaza Hotel</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>  Later, it was the <a href="/wiki/Mutual_Broadcasting_System" title="Mutual Broadcasting System">Mutual Broadcasting System</a> affiliate in St. Louis until August 1969, when the station switched from <a href="/wiki/Adult_standards" title="Adult standards">adult standards</a> to an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/R%26B" title="R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> format.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="From_No_Rock_to_Rock">From No Rock to Rock</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=KXFN&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: From No Rock to Rock">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>KWK's claim to national fame was a film clip where a <a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">disc jockey</a> at the station is seen smashing one of <a href="/wiki/Elvis_Presley" title="Elvis Presley">Elvis Presley</a>'s records and declaring "<a href="/wiki/Rock_and_roll" title="Rock and roll">Rock and roll</a> has got to go!"  It was a clear sign that KWK had veered away from the rock format. This clip can be seen in the 1981 film "<a href="/wiki/This_Is_Elvis" title="This Is Elvis">This Is Elvis</a>."
</p><p>On July 31, 1973, the station went off the air until November 1, 1978, when it returned as a <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_hit_radio" title="Contemporary hit radio">Top 40</a> station, and in March 1979, it began <a href="/wiki/Simulcast" title="Simulcast">simulcasting</a> with its sister station <a href="/wiki/WARH" title="WARH">WWWK-FM</a>. In June 1984, KWK became <b>KGLD</b>, an <a href="/wiki/Oldies" title="Oldies">oldies</a> station. The call letters stood for "Gold."
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports_and_hot_talk">Sports and hot talk</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=KXFN&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sports and hot talk">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On January 1, 1992, KGLD changed to all-<a href="/wiki/Sports_radio" title="Sports radio">sports radio</a> <b>KASP</b>. The station went back to simulcasting with WKBQ-FM in 1993. (WKBQ-FM, then at 106.5 FM, would swap frequencies with <a href="/wiki/WHHL" title="WHHL">WKKX</a> (104.1) in January 1994.) In December 1994, the station flipped to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_talk" title="Hot talk">hot talk</a> as "Straight Talk 1380." Programming on "Straight Talk" included <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Steve_and_DC_Morning_Show" title="Steve and DC Morning Show">Steve &amp; DC</a> in mornings (simulcast with WKBQ-FM), <a href="/wiki/The_Fabulous_Sports_Babe" title="The Fabulous Sports Babe">The Fabulous Sports Babe</a>, Ken Hamblin, <a href="/wiki/Tom_Leykis" title="Tom Leykis">Tom Leykis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jim_Bohannon" title="Jim Bohannon">Jim Bohannon</a>.
</p><p>On February 22, 1995, the station changed call letters to <b>KRAM</b>, shortly after the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Rams" title="Los Angeles Rams">Los Angeles Rams</a> football team re-located to St. Louis. On March 21, 1996, 1380 AM returned to simulcasting WKBQ-FM, switching its call sign to <b>WKBQ</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>  That November, <a href="/wiki/Emmis_Communications" title="Emmis Communications">Emmis Communications</a> bought it in a deal with WKBQ-FM and WKKX. Emmis donated the station to a ministry, which changed the call letters to  <b>KKWK</b>, with a short lived urban talk format.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Jazz_and_gospel">Jazz and gospel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=KXFN&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Jazz and gospel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:157px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:KSLG_(AM)_logo.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="86" data-file-width="155" decoding="async" height="86" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/0/04/KSLG_%28AM%29_logo.png" width="155"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:KSLG_(AM)_logo.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>KSLG's logo as "Team 1380"</div></div></div>
<p>KKWK switched to a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jazz_music" title="Jazz music">jazz</a> format with new <b>KZJZ</b> call letters adopted on September 1, 1998. KZJZ played mostly classic <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a>, had a full-time air staff, and won a Marconi Award.
</p><p>Having low advertising revenues, the station switched to a satellite-run <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southern_Gospel" title="Southern Gospel">Southern Gospel</a> format as <b>KSLG</b> in November 1999. KSLG switched back to sports in 2004, initially carrying <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sporting_News_Radio" title="Sporting News Radio">Sporting News Radio</a> programming, and later switched to <a href="/wiki/ESPN_Radio" title="ESPN Radio">ESPN Radio</a>. On December 3, 2007, KSLG switched affiliations from ESPN to <a href="/wiki/Fox_Sports_Radio" title="Fox Sports Radio">Fox Sports Radio</a> and rebranded as "Team 1380".
</p><p>On July 1, 2010, "Grand Slam Sports", owner of fellow St. Louis sports station <a href="/wiki/KFNS_(AM)" title="KFNS (AM)">KFNS</a>, announced its intention to purchase KSLG pending FCC approval. It began managing the station immediately under a <a href="/wiki/Local_marketing_agreement" title="Local marketing agreement">local marketing agreement</a>. This resulted in the return of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radio_syndication" title="Radio syndication">syndicated</a> "<a href="/wiki/Jim_Rome" title="Jim Rome">Jim Rome</a> Show" to the St. Louis market after an absence of approximately a year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>  On June 20, 2012, KSLG changed its call letters to <b>KXFN</b> with the FN referring to "Fan," similar to now co-owned KFNS.
</p>
<h3><span id="Women.27s_talk"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Women's_talk">Women's talk</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=KXFN&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Women's talk">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Citing increased competition and declining ratings, KXFN changed its format in May 2013 to a female-oriented talk format, branded as "1380 The Woman."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Concurrently, KFNS switched to a male-focused hot talk/comedy format as "590 The Man".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The dual-format experiment was a failure for both stations.
</p><p>Less than ten months later, KXFN dropped its talk format to carry <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yahoo!_Sports_Radio" title="Yahoo! Sports Radio">Yahoo! Sports Radio</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>  On April 1, 2014, it assumed much of KFNS's hot talk format and airstaff as "1380 The X, Xtreme Talk Radio."  KFNS itself reverted to sports, but retained the "Man" nickname.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Staff_squabbles">Staff squabbles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=KXFN&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Staff squabbles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>For several months, KFNS and KXFN staffers were publicly critical of station management.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> There was on-air sparring between hosts, and even a physical altercation between KFNS's morning host and the station manager (who subsequently resigned).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p><p>On October 1, 2014, KXFN changed to a music/talk format with multiple styles of shows, offering music of different genres as well as comedy talk content. That lasted until the following March, when TalkSTL.com began leasing the airtime on KXFN, once again restoring the previous sports talk format.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> By that December, TalkSTL.com's parent company, Markel Radio Group, bought and began programming KFNS, which had fallen silent the previous November after "Grand Slam Sports" went into bankruptcy. Markel would then relaunch a new version of "590 The Fan", moving some of the staff and programming over from KXFN. After a brief simulcast on both stations, KXFN fell silent on December 19, 2015.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Salem_Media_ownership">Salem Media ownership</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=KXFN&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Salem Media ownership">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:192px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:KXFN_AM1380thepulse_logo.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="169" data-file-width="378" decoding="async" height="85" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/e/ef/KXFN_AM1380thepulse_logo.jpg/190px-KXFN_AM1380thepulse_logo.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/e/ef/KXFN_AM1380thepulse_logo.jpg/285px-KXFN_AM1380thepulse_logo.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/e/ef/KXFN_AM1380thepulse_logo.jpg 2x" width="190"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:KXFN_AM1380thepulse_logo.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Pulse's logo</div></div></div>
<p>On August 1, 2016, the <a href="/wiki/Salem_Media_Group" title="Salem Media Group">Salem Media Group</a> announced the $190,000 purchase of KXFN through "Grand Slam Sports" bankruptcy receiver Detalus Consulting, pairing it with the recently purchased and relaunched <a href="/wiki/WSDZ" title="WSDZ">WSDZ</a>. Salem was able to secure FM translators for KXFN and WSDZ as part of the FCC's AM Revitalization Translator Waiver Period.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p><p>On January 6, 2017, KXFN returned to the air and launched a health and wellness talk format, branded as "1380 The Pulse".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>  The health talk format would last less than a year. On October 16, 2017, KXFN changed to conservative talk, branded as "The Answer." WSDZ, which had been carrying Salem's conservative talk line-up, would simultaneously flip to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Urban_gospel" title="Urban gospel">urban gospel</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2019, the station was sold to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Immaculate_Heart_Media,_Inc." title="Immaculate Heart Media, Inc.">Immaculate Heart Media, Inc.</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup>
</p>
