<p><b>Chignik Bay Seaplane Base</b> (<a href="/wiki/IATA_airport_code" title="IATA airport code">IATA</a>: <b>KBW</b>, <a href="/wiki/Federal_Aviation_Administration" title="Federal Aviation Administration">FAA</a> <a href="/wiki/Location_identifier" title="Location identifier">LID</a>: <b>Z78</b>) is a public-use <a href="/wiki/Seaplane" title="Seaplane">seaplane</a> base serving <a href="/wiki/Chignik,_Alaska" title="Chignik, Alaska">Chignik</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FAA_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-FAA-1">[1]</a></sup> a city in the <a href="/wiki/Lake_and_Peninsula_Borough,_Alaska" title="Lake and Peninsula Borough, Alaska">Lake and Peninsula Borough</a> of the <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">U.S. state</a> of <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Facilities_and_aircraft">Facilities and aircraft</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chignik_Bay_Seaplane_Base&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Facilities and aircraft">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Chignik Bay Seaplane Base has two seaplane landing areas: the E/W waterway is 6,000 by 4,000 feet (1,829 x 1,219 m) and the NE/SW waterway is 10,000 by 4,000 feet (3,048 x 1,219 m). The beach is used for aircraft pull-up. For the 12-month period ending December 31, 2006, the airport had 210 aircraft operations, an average of 17 per month: 95% <a href="/wiki/General_aviation" title="General aviation">general aviation</a> and 5% <a href="/wiki/Air_taxi" title="Air taxi">air taxi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FAA_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-FAA-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
