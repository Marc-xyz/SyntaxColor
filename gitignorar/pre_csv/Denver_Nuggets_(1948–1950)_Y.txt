<p>The <b>Denver Nuggets</b> were a professional <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a> <a href="/wiki/Team" title="Team">team</a> based in <a href="/wiki/Denver" title="Denver">Denver, Colorado</a>. The Nuggets joined the <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_League_(United_States)" title="National Basketball League (United States)">National Basketball League</a> (NBL) for the 1948–49 season, and then joined the <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_Association" title="National Basketball Association">National Basketball Association</a> when the NBL was absorbed by the <a href="/wiki/Basketball_Association_of_America" title="Basketball Association of America">Basketball Association of America</a> to create the NBA for the 1949–50 season. The Nuggets were the first major professional sports franchise in <a href="/wiki/Colorado" title="Colorado">Colorado</a>, and the first NBA franchise west of the <a href="/wiki/Mississippi_River" title="Mississippi River">Mississippi</a>.
</p><p>In 1950, the Nuggets were one of seven teams, including <a href="/wiki/Anderson_Packers" title="Anderson Packers">Anderson Packers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Stags" title="Chicago Stags">Chicago Stags</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sheboygan_Red_Skins" title="Sheboygan Red Skins">Sheboygan Red Skins</a>, <a href="/wiki/St._Louis_Bombers" title="St. Louis Bombers">St. Louis Bombers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Washington_Capitols" title="Washington Capitols">Washington Capitols</a>, <a href="/wiki/Waterloo_Hawks" title="Waterloo Hawks">Waterloo Hawks</a>, that dropped out of the <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_Association" title="National Basketball Association">National Basketball Association</a> altogether.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Nuggets started the 1949–50 season with a record of 0–15, and finished the season 11–51.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Denver_Nuggets_(1948%E2%80%931950)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The team's roots can be traced back to 1932 when the original amateur Nuggets were formed as a member of the <a href="/wiki/Amateur_Athletic_Union" title="Amateur Athletic Union">Amateur Athletic Union</a>. Led by player-coach <a href="/wiki/Jack_McCracken" title="Jack McCracken">Jack McCracken</a>, the Nuggets were one of the most powerful amateur athletic basketball teams in the country, winning the 1939 AAU championship and losing the 1940 championship to the <a href="/wiki/Phillips_66ers" title="Phillips 66ers">Phillips 66ers</a>. Not playing professionally, its players were given extra jobs by team sponsors. Featuring players such as McCracken, <a href="/wiki/Ace_Gruenig" title="Ace Gruenig">Ace Gruenig</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vince_Boryla" title="Vince Boryla">Vince Boryla</a>, the Nuggets continued as an AAU power throughout their existence, and the AAU has been credited by historians for helping put Denver on the national sports map.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>After the 1947–48 season, the Nuggets decided to step out of their amateur status. Rather than join the <a href="/wiki/National_Industrial_Basketball_League" title="National Industrial Basketball League">National Industrial Basketball League</a> (NIBL), which was founded in 1947 and remained amateur, the Nuggets' general manager, <a href="/wiki/Hal_Davis" title="Hal Davis">Hal Davis</a>, secured a franchise in the nine-team <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_League_(United_States)" title="National Basketball League (United States)">National Basketball League</a> to play in the 1948–49 season. The Nuggets finished last in the Western Division in the 1948–49 season.
</p><p>In the 1949–50 season, the team played in the newly-formed <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_Association" title="National Basketball Association">National Basketball Association</a> (NBA). The Nuggets were led by player-coach <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Darden" title="Jimmy Darden">Jimmy Darden</a>, a star shooting guard who joined the Nuggets after leaving the Army as a <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> veteran in 1946. The Nuggets played one season in the NBA, compiling an 11–51 record in the Western Division, and were then disbanded in 1950. Briefly, in the 1950–51 season, the <a href="/wiki/Denver_Refiners" title="Denver Refiners">Denver Refiners</a> played in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Professional_Basketball_League_(1950%E2%80%931951)" title="National Professional Basketball League (1950–1951)">National Professional Basketball League</a> (NPBL), which was made up of franchises that left the NBA. That team moved to become the <a href="/wiki/Evansville_Agogans" title="Evansville Agogans">Evansville Agogans</a> the last six games of the season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> After the demise of the Nuggets and the Refiners, Colorado was without any major league sports teams until the <a href="/wiki/American_Football_League" title="American Football League">AFL</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Denver_Broncos" title="Denver Broncos">Denver Broncos</a> began play in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1960_AFL_season" title="1960 AFL season">1960</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Arena">Arena</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Denver_Nuggets_(1948%E2%80%931950)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Arena">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Nuggets played on the stage of the <a href="/wiki/Denver_Auditorium_Arena" title="Denver Auditorium Arena">Denver Auditorium Arena</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Denver_Nuggets_(1948%E2%80%931950)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>When the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Denver_Rockets" title="Denver Rockets">Denver Rockets</a> of the <a href="/wiki/American_Basketball_Association" title="American Basketball Association">American Basketball Association</a> (ABA) joined the NBA, a contest was held in 1974 to give the team a new nickname since the NBA already had the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Rockets" title="Houston Rockets">Houston Rockets</a>. The Nuggets' name was selected as the moniker of the new team. In 1985, <a href="/wiki/Vince_Boryla" title="Vince Boryla">Vince Boryla</a> joined the franchise as its president and general manager, and was awarded NBA Executive of the Year that same year. He was president and general manager of the Nuggets from 1985 to 1988. The current Denver Nuggets also started out in the same venue as the original Nuggets, the Denver Auditorium Arena, playing there from 1967 to 1975.
</p>
