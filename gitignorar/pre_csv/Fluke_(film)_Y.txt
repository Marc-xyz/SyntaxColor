<p><i><b>Fluke</b></i> is a 1995 drama film directed by <a href="/wiki/Carlo_Carlei" title="Carlo Carlei">Carlo Carlei</a> and starring <a href="/wiki/Matthew_Modine" title="Matthew Modine">Matthew Modine</a> as the voice of the title character with supporting roles featuring <a href="/wiki/Eric_Stoltz" title="Eric Stoltz">Eric Stoltz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nancy_Travis" title="Nancy Travis">Nancy Travis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Max_Pomeranc" title="Max Pomeranc">Max Pomeranc</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bill_Cobbs" title="Bill Cobbs">Bill Cobbs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ron_Perlman" title="Ron Perlman">Ron Perlman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jon_Polito" title="Jon Polito">Jon Polito</a> and <a href="/wiki/Samuel_L._Jackson" title="Samuel L. Jackson">Samuel L. Jackson</a> as the voice of Rumbo. The film was based on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fluke_(novel)" title="Fluke (novel)">novel of the same name</a> by <a href="/wiki/James_Herbert" title="James Herbert">James Herbert</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot <span id="Synopsis"></span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fluke_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fluke is a mutt puppy (a wrong colored Golden Retriever, played by dog actor Buddy<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>, voiced by <a href="/wiki/Sam_Gifaldi" title="Sam Gifaldi">Sam Gifaldi</a>), who has flashing memories and dreams of having lived a human life. After being taken to a pound and eventually escaping, he is raised by an elderly homeless woman named Bella (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Collin_Wilcox_Paxton" title="Collin Wilcox Paxton">Collin Wilcox Paxton</a>), who gives him the name Fluke, stating that he is a "fluke by nature, Fluke by name." Fluke supports Bella by helping her earn money from passing strangers impressed with Fluke's ability to beat Bella's shell game. 
</p><p>After Bella dies of an illness due to poor conditions, Fluke meets a street-wise dog named Rumbo (voiced by <a href="/wiki/Samuel_L._Jackson" title="Samuel L. Jackson">Samuel L. Jackson</a>) who takes him to see a man named Bert (<a href="/wiki/Bill_Cobbs" title="Bill Cobbs">Bill Cobbs</a>) who feeds Fluke and Rumbo. Fluke matures into an adult dog (now voiced by <a href="/wiki/Matthew_Modine" title="Matthew Modine">Matthew Modine</a>) and eventually realizes that he was once a man named Thomas P. Johnson (also portrayed by Matthew Modine), who died in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Car_crash" title="Car crash">car crash</a>. 
</p><p>Fluke is then abducted by a man named Sylvester (<a href="/wiki/Ron_Perlman" title="Ron Perlman">Ron Perlman</a>) to be used in makeup experiments at a <a href="/wiki/Cosmetics" title="Cosmetics">cosmetics</a> company. During his captivity, Rumbo comes to the rescue, and Rumbo is shot by Sylvester as he and Fluke escape. A dying Rumbo tells Fluke that the black-and-white snapshot of a man in a sailor suit on Bert's wall was him and Bert was his brother and that he wishes to smell the sea again, suggesting that he died in the line of duty.
</p><p>After Rumbo's death, Fluke seeks out his surviving wife Carol (<a href="/wiki/Nancy_Travis" title="Nancy Travis">Nancy Travis</a>) and son Brian (<a href="/wiki/Max_Pomeranc" title="Max Pomeranc">Max Pomeranc</a>) and reunites with them by becoming their new family dog. Though Carol, implied to be afraid of dogs, is apprehensive about adopting Fluke, she caves in seeing how Brian has quickly bonded with Fluke. During his life as a dog, Fluke gets to know his family better and bitterly realizes, much to his regret, that he had been a distant workaholic.
</p><p>As more memories return, Fluke suspects that his human death was caused by his former business partner Jeff Newman (<a href="/wiki/Eric_Stoltz" title="Eric Stoltz">Eric Stoltz</a>). Enraged by this and the fact that Jeff is now dating Carol, Fluke viciously attacks him when he visits the house, and ends up getting put outside. Jeff calls for Animal Control against the protests of Brian, and so Fluke is forced to run from the premises.
</p><p>The next night, Brian goes missing while looking for Fluke and Carol implores Jeff to help, so Jeff drives back to their house. Fluke, who had been hiding in the backseat of Jeff's car while the latter was at work, comes close to killing Jeff by causing him to get into a car accident like his own. Fluke then has another flashback and realizes that Jeff wasn't responsible for his death. Instead, Fluke had caused his own death when he recklessly drove on the wrong side of the road just to argue with Jeff one day and swerved off to avoid hitting an oncoming truck. Jeff later tried to save him but failed. An injured Jeff, implied to have realized Fluke's true identity by bearing no ill will, tells Fluke to go find Brian before the latter catches hypothermia from the falling snow. Regretful over his actions, Fluke barks for a passing driver to help Jeff before running off.
</p><p>On a hunch, Fluke goes to the graveyard where he had been buried and finds Brian there, who had been locked in by an unaware groundskeeper. Fluke huddles with Brian to keep him warm. Carol, having come to the same conclusion from one Brian's drawings of his father, uses her car to break open the cemetery gates and picks up Brian. Brian tells Carol that he heard Fluke tell him in a vision that Fluke had to leave them. Carol tries to coax Fluke to come home with them. Instead, Fluke digs away at the snow in front of his tombstone to show Carol who he really is by uncovering the word "forever" at the bottom, a phrase he often said to her as a human. Carol is left speechless, and lets Fluke leave without objection. With a heavy heart, Fluke departs and entrusts his family to Jeff for their happiness. He monologues that he had to leave because he finally accepted that he can no longer be the family man he should have been, and that he should just cherish the life he has now, which he had not done back then.
</p><p>Far away and sometime later, Fluke is resting under a tree on a farm by himself. To his surprise and happiness, he is reunited with Rumbo, now reincarnated as a <a href="/wiki/Squirrel" title="Squirrel">squirrel</a>. Rumbo tells Fluke about life as a squirrel and about reincarnation.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cast">Cast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fluke_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Cast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Air_Buddy_(dog)" title="Air Buddy (dog)">Buddy</a> as Fluke, a dog that was reincarnated after a business man's death.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Matthew_Modine" title="Matthew Modine">Matthew Modine</a> as Thomas P. Johnson, a workaholic, Jeff's best friend, Carol's husband, and Brian's father. As Fluke the dog, his struggle to return to his former human life ultimately teaches him the importance of moving on.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Nancy_Travis" title="Nancy Travis">Nancy Travis</a> as Carol Johnson, Thomas's wife, Brian's mother. Throughout the movie, she's shown to be not fond of dogs and is even borderline hostile towards Fluke. By the end of the movie, she is implied to have increased empathy for animals after realizing Fluke's true identity.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Max_Pomeranc" title="Max Pomeranc">Max Pomeranc</a> as Brian Johnson, Thomas and Carol's son. Despite Thomas's workaholic ways, Brian remembers his dad fondly, and is shown to miss him dearly.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Eric_Stoltz" title="Eric Stoltz">Eric Stoltz</a> as Jeff Newman, Thomas's best friend. In the years since Thomas's death, he has begun to fill the void Thomas left behind.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bill_Cobbs" title="Bill Cobbs">Bill Cobbs</a> as Bert</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ron_Perlman" title="Ron Perlman">Ron Perlman</a> as Sylvester</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jon_Polito" title="Jon Polito">Jon Polito</a> as Boss</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Collin_Wilcox_Paxton" title="Collin Wilcox Paxton">Collin Wilcox Paxton</a> as Bella, a kindly old homeless lady who cares for Fluke for some time until her death.</li></ul>
