<p>The <b>1995 NCAA Division I Men's Lacrosse Tournament</b> was the 25th annual Division I <a href="/wiki/NCAA_Men%27s_Lacrosse_Championship" title="NCAA Men's Lacrosse Championship">NCAA Men's Lacrosse Championship</a> tournament. Twelve NCAA Division I <a href="/wiki/College_lacrosse" title="College lacrosse">college men's lacrosse</a> teams met after having played their way through a regular season, and for some, a conference tournament.
</p><p>The championship game was played at Maryland's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Byrd_Stadium" title="Byrd Stadium">Byrd Stadium</a> in front of 26,229 fans,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-attend_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-attend-2">[2]</a></sup>  The game saw <a href="/wiki/Syracuse_Orange_men%27s_lacrosse" title="Syracuse Orange men's lacrosse">Syracuse University</a> defeat <a href="/wiki/University_of_Maryland,_College_Park" title="University of Maryland, College Park">University of Maryland</a> by the score of 13–9.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncaa_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-ncaa-1">[1]</a></sup> Despite the loss, Maryland goalie <a href="/wiki/Brian_Dougherty" title="Brian Dougherty">Brian Dougherty</a> was named the tournament's Most Outstanding Player.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-look_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-look-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Brian Dougherty showed why he earned the award as Division I top goalie, making 23 saves on 59 shots, in the semi-finals.
</p><p>In the first quarter Hopkins' attack took 19 shots, and Dougherty had 12 saves, allowing Maryland to walk away with a 4-1 first-quarter lead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
