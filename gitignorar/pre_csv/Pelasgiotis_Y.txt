	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Pelasgiotis</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>

		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Thessaly.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="806" data-file-width="894" decoding="async" height="198" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Thessaly.jpg/220px-Thessaly.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Thessaly.jpg/330px-Thessaly.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Thessaly.jpg/440px-Thessaly.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Thessaly.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Pelasgiotis in the centre of Thessaly</div></div></div>
<p><b>Pelasgiotis</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek_language" title="Ancient Greek language">Ancient Greek</a>: <span lang="grc">Πελασγιῶτις</span>, <small><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Romanization_of_Ancient_Greek" title="Romanization of Ancient Greek">romanized</a>: </small><i lang="grc-Latn" title="Ancient Greek-language romanization">Pelasgiōtis</i>) was an elongated district of <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Thessaly" title="Ancient Thessaly">ancient Thessaly</a>, extending from the <a href="/wiki/Vale_of_Tempe" title="Vale of Tempe">Vale of Tempe</a> in the north to the city of <a href="/wiki/Pherae" title="Pherae">Pherae</a> in the south. The Pelasgiotis included the following localities: <a href="/wiki/Argos_Pelasgikon" title="Argos Pelasgikon">Argos Pelasgikon</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Argyra_(Thessaly)" title="Argyra (Thessaly)">Argyra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Armenium" title="Armenium">Armenium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Atrax_(Thessaly)" title="Atrax (Thessaly)">Atrax</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crannon" title="Crannon">Crannon</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cynoscephalae_(Thessaly)" title="Cynoscephalae (Thessaly)">Cynoscephalae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Elateia_(Thessaly)" title="Elateia (Thessaly)">Elateia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gyrton_(Thessaly)" title="Gyrton (Thessaly)">Gyrton</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mopsion" title="Mopsion">Mopsion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Larissa" title="Larissa">Larissa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kondaia" title="Kondaia">Kondaia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Onchestos" title="Onchestos">Onchestos</a> river and town, <a href="/wiki/Phayttos" title="Phayttos">Phayttos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pherae" title="Pherae">Pherae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotussa" title="Scotussa">Scotussa</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sykourion" title="Sykourion">Sykourion</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Demonym" title="Demonym">demonym</a> of the district's inhabitants is Pelasgiotae 
or Pelasgiotes (Πελασγιῶται, <i>Pelasgiōtai</i>).
</p><p>Along with <a href="/wiki/Achaea_Phthiotis" title="Achaea Phthiotis">Achaea Phthiotis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thessaliotis" title="Thessaliotis">Thessaliotis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Histiaeotis" title="Histiaeotis">Histiaeotis</a>, the Pelasgiotis comprised the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thessalian_tetrarchy" title="Thessalian tetrarchy">Thessalian tetrarchy</a>, governed by a <i><a href="/wiki/Tagus_(title)" title="Tagus (title)">tagus</a></i>, when occasion required.
</p><p>The territory is  mentioned by <a href="/wiki/Strabo" title="Strabo">Strabo</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> but not by <a href="/wiki/Herodotus" title="Herodotus">Herodotus</a>, who seems to include it in the district of Thessaliotis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> 
</p><p>In epigraphy, Pelasgiotes are mentioned among other Thessalian ambassadors in Athens <abbr title="circa">c.</abbr><span style="white-space:nowrap;"> 353 BC</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> A fragment of a marble stele at Larissa records that on request of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_consul" title="Roman consul">Roman consul</a> <a href="/wiki/Quintus_Caecilius_Metellus_Macedonicus" title="Quintus Caecilius Metellus Macedonicus">Quintus Caecilius Metellus</a>, son of Quintus, "friend and benefactor of our country [<i>ethnei hēmōn</i>]" in return for services rendered by him, his family and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Senate_and_People" title="Roman Senate and People">Roman Senate and People</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Thessalian_League" title="Thessalian League">Thessalian League</a> decreed to send 43,000 coffers of wheat to <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>, to be taxed from different regions under the league. The Pelasgiotes and the Phthiotes are to provide 32,000 while the Histiaeotes and Thessaliotes must provide the remaining 11,000, with 25% going to the army, all in different months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>The regional and ethnic toponym is a reminiscent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pelasgian" title="Pelasgian">Pelasgian</a> element from the Thessalian past. As in other parts of Thessaly, <a href="/wiki/Aeolic_Greek" title="Aeolic Greek">Aeolic Greek</a> inscriptions are attested and after 2nd century BC, <a href="/wiki/Koine_Greek" title="Koine Greek">Koine Greek</a>.
</p><p>During the Thessalian Games at Larissa to <a href="/wiki/Zeus" title="Zeus">Zeus</a> Eleuthereus in the 1st century BC, several winner athletes are described as "Thessalian from Larissa of Pelasgis" (<span lang="grc" title="Ancient Greek language text">Θεσσαλὸς ἀπὸ Λαρίσης τῆς Πελασγίδος</span>, <i>Thessalos apo Larisēs tēs Pelasgidos</i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The 3rd-century BC funerary epigram for Erilaos of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kalchedon" title="Kalchedon">Kalchedon</a> mentions also <span lang="grc" title="Ancient Greek language text">Λάρισα τᾶι Πελασγίδι</span>, <i>Larisa tai Pelasgidi</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
