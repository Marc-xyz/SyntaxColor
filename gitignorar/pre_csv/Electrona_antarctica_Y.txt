<p>The <b>Antarctic lanternfish</b> or <i><b>Electrona antarctica</b></i> mainly inhabits the Antarctic deep, warm waters. It the dominant species in the Southern Ocean. Their life span is about 4–5 years and they mature after 2–3 years. Their maximum length is 12.5 cm. Their feeding depends upon area to area. Small fish primarily feed on <a href="/wiki/Copepod" title="Copepod">copepods</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Euphausiid" title="Euphausiid">euphausiid</a> larvae, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hyperiid" title="Hyperiid">hyperiids</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Turchini_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Turchini-1">[1]</a></sup> This species is mainly the nektonic prey species of seabirds in open water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Biology_of_the_Southern_Ocean,_Second_Edition_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Biology_of_the_Southern_Ocean,_Second_Edition-2">[2]</a></sup> It is an important krill predator and serves as prey for a majority of seabirds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-greeley_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-greeley-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
