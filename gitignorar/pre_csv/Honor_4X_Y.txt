<p>The <b>Huawei Honor 4X</b> is a mid-range <a href="/wiki/Android_(operating_system)" title="Android (operating system)">Android</a> <a href="/wiki/Smartphone" title="Smartphone">smartphone</a> manufactured by <a href="/wiki/Huawei" title="Huawei">Huawei</a> as part of the <a href="/wiki/Huawei_Honor_X_series" title="Huawei Honor X series">Huawei Honor X series</a>. It was released in October 2014,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gsmarena_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-gsmarena-1">[1]</a></sup> and received generally positive reviews.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Features">Features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Honor_4X&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hardware">Hardware</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Honor_4X&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Hardware">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Huawei Honor 4X supports <a href="/wiki/GSM" title="GSM">GSM</a>, <a href="/wiki/High_Speed_Packet_Access" title="High Speed Packet Access">HSPA</a> and <a href="/wiki/LTE_(telecommunication)" title="LTE (telecommunication)">LTE</a> networking. It uses a 5.5-inch <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/In-plane_switching" title="In-plane switching">IPS</a> display with 720p resolution. It uses either the <a href="/wiki/HiSilicon" title="HiSilicon">HiSilicon</a> Kirin 620 octa-core 1.2 GHz <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cortex-A53" title="Cortex-A53">Cortex-A53</a> CPU with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mali-450" title="Mali-450">Mali-450</a> graphics, or the <a href="/wiki/Qualcomm_Snapdragon" title="Qualcomm Snapdragon">Qualcomm Snapdragon</a> 410 quad-core 1.2 GHz Cortex-A53 CPU with <a href="/wiki/Adreno" title="Adreno">Adreno</a> 306 graphics. It contains 2GB of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RAM_memory" title="RAM memory">RAM memory</a> and 8GB storage, expandable with an SD card up to 32GB. It features a 13 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Megapixel" title="Megapixel">MP</a> rear camera with autofocus and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LED_flash" title="LED flash">LED flash</a> that supports 1080p video recording, and a 5 MP front camera that supports 720p video recording. It uses a <a href="/wiki/Lithium_polymer_battery" title="Lithium polymer battery">lithium polymer</a> 3000 mAh battery that supports up to 200 hours of 3G standby time and 15 hours of 3G talk time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gsmarena_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-gsmarena-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Software">Software</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Honor_4X&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Software">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Honor 4X comes preinstalled with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Android_4.4.2_KitKat" title="Android 4.4.2 KitKat">Android 4.4.2 KitKat</a> with Huawei's custom Emotion UI. The launcher does not include an app drawer, with apps spread across multiple home screens, thus the interface resembles iOS more than Android. Huawei plans an upgrade to <a href="/wiki/Android_Lollipop" title="Android Lollipop">Android Lollipop</a> and marshmallow.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-techradar_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-techradar-2">[2]</a></sup> A simple home screen style is also available.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-toi_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-toi-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Honor_4X&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Huawei Honor 4X received generally positive reviews.
</p><p>Tomos Ellis of <a href="/wiki/TechRadar" title="TechRadar">TechRadar</a> reviewed the version with the octa-core processor, and gave the Honor 4X a 4 out of 5. The review praised the performance of the 64-bit octa-core processor and 2 GB of RAM, long battery life provided with the 3000 mAh battery, dual-sim functionality, good build quality and camera quality, and opined that the online-only availability was likely to reduce costs, but criticized the lack of an app drawer in the custom Emotion UI Android skin, noting that separating apps across multiple homescreens was "too cluttered" and that the icons were "garish, cartoon-like". The review also noted that while competitors like the <a href="/wiki/Moto_G_(2nd_generation)" title="Moto G (2nd generation)">2014 Moto G</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sony_Xperia_M2" title="Sony Xperia M2">Sony Xperia M2</a> Aqua exist in a similar price range, none of them were <a href="/wiki/Phablet" title="Phablet">phablet</a>-sized like the Honor 4X.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-techradar_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-techradar-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Katharine Byrne of Expert Reviews reviewed the version with the octa-core processor, and also gave the Honor 4X a 4 out of 5, comparing the device favorably against the 2014 Moto G. The review appreciated the camera, saying that the outdoor photo quality was "much brighter and more vivid" than photos taken with the Moto G, and that the Honor 4X also has longer battery life and higher benchmark, web browsing, and graphics performance. The review noted that the display had high brightness and contrast, but criticized the colour accuracy, noting low sRGB colour gamut coverage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-expert_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-expert-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Komal Mohan of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Times_of_India" title="The Times of India">The Times of India</a></i> reviewed the version with the slower quad-core processor, and gave the Honor 4X a 3.5 out of 5. The review noted that the lack of backlighting for the capacitive front keys are an inconvenience, and said that the device "is on the chunky side" with its thickness of 8.7 mm. The sunlight legibility and touch sensitivity of the display were praised, but the viewing angles were disappointing. The review pointed out that the device's 3000 mAh battery was "one of its biggest strengths", but said that the Adreno 306 GPU of the quad-core Snapdragon 410 processor created noticeable lag when playing resource-intensive games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-toi_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-toi-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
