<p><i><b>Colin McRae: Dirt 2</b></i> (or simply <i><b>Dirt 2</b></i>, stylised as <i><b>DiRT2</b></i> or <i><b>COLIN McRAE DiRT2</b></i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Racing_video_game" title="Racing video game">racing video game</a> developed and published by <a href="/wiki/Codemasters" title="Codemasters">Codemasters</a> in 2009, first for the <a href="/wiki/Nintendo_DS" title="Nintendo DS">Nintendo DS</a>, <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_3" title="PlayStation 3">PlayStation 3</a>, <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Portable" title="PlayStation Portable">PlayStation Portable</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wii" title="Wii">Wii</a> and <a href="/wiki/Xbox_360" title="Xbox 360">Xbox 360</a>, and then later for PC platforms (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Windows" title="Windows">Windows</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/OS_X" title="OS X">OS X</a>). It is the seventh game in the <a href="/wiki/Colin_McRae_Rally" title="Colin McRae Rally"><i>Colin McRae Rally</i> series</a> and was the first game in the <i>McRae</i> series since <a href="/wiki/Colin_McRae#Death" title="Colin McRae">McRae's death</a> in 2007.
</p><p>It features <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ken_Block_(rally_driver)" title="Ken Block (rally driver)">Ken Block</a>, <a href="/wiki/Travis_Pastrana" title="Travis Pastrana">Travis Pastrana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tanner_Foust" title="Tanner Foust">Tanner Foust</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dave_Mirra" title="Dave Mirra">Dave Mirra</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mohammed_bin_Sulayem" title="Mohammed bin Sulayem">Mohammed ben Sulayem</a>. The game includes many new race-events, including stadium events. Along with the player, an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RV" title="RV">RV</a> travels from one event to another, and serves as "headquarters" for the player. It features a roster of contemporary off-road events, taking players to diverse and challenging real-world environments. The game takes place across Asia, Europe, Africa and North America. The game includes five different event types, plus three more "special" game modes. The World Tour mode sees players competing in multi-car and solo races at new locations, and also includes a new multiplayer mode. The sequel <i><a href="/wiki/Dirt_3" title="Dirt 3">Dirt 3</a></i> was released in 2011.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gameplay">Gameplay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colin_McRae:_Dirt_2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Gameplay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Colin McRae: Dirt 2</i> features five racing disciplines, all of which are playable offline (against AI bots when applicable) and online. In addition, three "special modes" are included. <i>Colin McRae: Dirt 2</i> runs on an updated version of the EGO engine, which powered Codemasters' <i>Race Driver: Grid</i> as well. The engine features, most notably, an updated physics engine, which models realistic weight transfer during turning maneuvers, allowing the player to incorporate advanced driving techniques, such as the <a href="/wiki/Scandinavian_flick" title="Scandinavian flick">Scandinavian flick</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>Rally racing occurs on non-<a href="/wiki/Race_track" title="Race track">circuit</a> roads which usually involve public (closed) routes and technically challenging courses. Usually involving staggered starts, each driver competes against the clock. In addition, during rally events, a <a href="/wiki/Co-driver" title="Co-driver">co-driver</a> is present, constantly reading <a href="/wiki/Pacenotes" title="Pacenotes">pacenotes</a> to guide the driver along narrow and meandering tracks of up to 6 km in length.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Niet_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Niet-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li>Rallycross races involve cars identical to those used in rally events; the races themselves, however, are very different. Each rally-cross race involves up to eight racers competing in multiple lap events on circuit tracks of mixed surface type. The tracks, half tarmac and half dirt, exist in closed settings, such as in or around stadiums, and are approximately 1 to 1.5 km in length.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Niet_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Niet-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li>The most similar real-world equivalent of Trailblazer events are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hill_Climb" title="Hill Climb">hill climbs</a>. Unlike hill climb events, however, trailblazer races do not require a positive change in elevation. Similar to rallying, trailblazing features staggered starts. Trailblazer (and hill climb) cars, unlike rally cars, usually feature massive aerodynamic aids, such as spoilers, diffusers, and splitters to help keep traction at higher speeds, regardless of track surface or conditions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Niet_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-Niet-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li>Raid races are multi-car events involving heavyweight vehicles, such as buggies and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trophy_Truck" title="Trophy Truck">trophy trucks</a>. Each race begins with a simultaneous start of up to eight competitors, with tracks averaging 5 km in length.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Niet_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-Niet-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li>Landrush events are similar to raid races, but take place on a circuit. Each event features eight racers on a dirt track of about 1 km, and involve multiple laps.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Niet_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-Niet-5">[5]</a></sup></li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Special_Modes">Special Modes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colin_McRae:_Dirt_2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Special Modes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In addition to the five straight game types, there are three special variants, each applicable to multiple modes.
</p>
<ul><li>Gatecrasher, which uses Rally courses and cars, involves smashing yellow barriers; competitors race against the clock. When smashed, the "gates", strategically placed along the best racing line, add time to the clock; whichever racer finishes the event with the most time on the clock wins. The format of the race is more similar to a multi-competitor time trial than to a traditional rally race.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Niet_5-5"><a href="#cite_note-Niet-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li>Domination, playable Rallycross and Land Rush stages, involves multiple drivers competing to set the lowest time in any given track sector. Points are awarded based on the final number of controlled sectors, as well as the final race position.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Niet_5-6"><a href="#cite_note-Niet-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li>Last Man Standing is an elimination-style race based on rallycross, during which the driver in last place is eliminated at 20-second intervals, the winner being the final driver left after all others are eliminated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Niet_5-7"><a href="#cite_note-Niet-5">[5]</a></sup></li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Multiplayer">Multiplayer</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colin_McRae:_Dirt_2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Multiplayer">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Colin McRae: Dirt 2</i> features a set of multiplayer modes which the user can partake in over, the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Network" title="PlayStation Network">PlayStation Network</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Games_For_Windows_-_Live" title="Games For Windows - Live">Games For Windows - Live</a> and <a href="/wiki/Xbox_Live" title="Xbox Live">Xbox Live</a>, as well as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LAN" title="LAN">local</a> multiplayer. Up to eight players can compete in ranked (Pro Tour) or unranked matches (Jam Session). Within the "Jam Session" category, any class of cars may be used on any track; the game lets hosts know when a combination is not necessarily advisable, such as Trailblazer cars on a Rallycross circuit. Ranked matches are, by comparison, much more restrained; only the prescribed cars may be used on a track.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_DiRT_Tour">The DiRT Tour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colin_McRae:_Dirt_2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The DiRT Tour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The DiRT TOUR, which is how the main single-player game mode is called, takes the player along all the nine locations on the world map, which serves as an event selection screen. Individual events appear on the map, as the player levels up, eventually unlocking all the locations. The money won by participating in the events can be used for buying and upgrading more cars. There are three tiers of difficulty, marked by stars - Rookie (1 star), Pro (2 stars) and All-Star (3 stars). For each tier, one <a href="/wiki/X_Games" title="X Games">X Games</a> event exists. Accessed via posters hanging above the world map, X Games Europe are unlocked at XP level 8, X Games Asia on level 16 and X Games America on level 24. Each X Games event features three races, Rally, Rallycross and Trailblazer, one in each country of given region. 
</p><p>After reaching the All-star tier, "World Tour" events are unlocked. There are five World Tour events, one for each of the main race types. These events are accessed via "event tags" on the right side of the world map. To be able to participate, you have to get at least 3rd on one of the three qualifying events. Each world tour offers five races across three locations, where the certain race type is available. After successful participation in all world tours, sixth event tag appears on the bottom of the world map, providing access to special event dedicated to <a href="/wiki/Colin_McRae" title="Colin McRae">Colin McRae</a>. In this event, you race against unique opponents, and winning it rewards the player with a special car, set of liveries and unlocks the "Colin McRae tribute" video.
</p><p>One of the aspects of the DiRT tour is also becoming famous and known to the celebrity racers. There are seven of them, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ken_Block_(rally_driver)" title="Ken Block (rally driver)">Ken Block</a>, <a href="/wiki/Travis_Pastrana" title="Travis Pastrana">Travis Pastrana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tanner_Foust" title="Tanner Foust">Tanner Foust</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dave_Mirra" title="Dave Mirra">Dave Mirra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mohammed_bin_Sulayem" title="Mohammed bin Sulayem">Mohammed ben Sulayem</a>, Jayde Taylor and Katie Justice (the two female drivers are not real). Initially, all of the celebrities "Have heard of you". By competing, they reach the states "Is your buddy", "Respects you" and "Thinks you are legend". By reaching the final state of the celebrity's, the player is rewarded by his/hers custom set of liveries. To improve the reputation state, the player has to win in the events where the celebrities compete, or by participating in challenges. Each celebrity can challenge the player for "one-on-one race", which is a one-time only event and doesn't win any money, just experience points and celebrity reputation. After challenging, a celebrity head-icon appears on the map, offering the challenge to be played.
</p><p>Becoming friends with the celebrity racers is important for "Team events". RallyCross, Raid, LandRush can be marked as team events, and that esseantially means that racers are competing in couples, so the total score of each player is the sum of both of the racers' individual score gained from the race. Before starting this type of event, you have to choose a partner, and to be able to choose certain driver, he has to have certain reputation state tier, depending on the event tier. Winning in these events can significantly improve the partners' opinion.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development_and_release">Development and release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colin_McRae:_Dirt_2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Development and release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Dirt_2_girls_at_Igromir_2009_(4081165231).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2444" data-file-width="2805" decoding="async" height="192" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ef/Dirt_2_girls_at_Igromir_2009_%284081165231%29.jpg/220px-Dirt_2_girls_at_Igromir_2009_%284081165231%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ef/Dirt_2_girls_at_Igromir_2009_%284081165231%29.jpg/330px-Dirt_2_girls_at_Igromir_2009_%284081165231%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ef/Dirt_2_girls_at_Igromir_2009_%284081165231%29.jpg/440px-Dirt_2_girls_at_Igromir_2009_%284081165231%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Dirt_2_girls_at_Igromir_2009_(4081165231).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i>Dirt 2</i> promotion at <a href="/wiki/IgroMir" title="IgroMir">IgroMir</a> 2009</div></div></div>
<p>Shortly before the release of the first game in the <i>Dirt</i> series, <i><a href="/wiki/Colin_McRae:_Dirt" title="Colin McRae: Dirt">Colin McRae: Dirt</a></i>, <i>Colin McRae: Dirt 2</i> was described by Matthew Horsman, chief game designer for the game, as a game more improved and refined than its predecessor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The game was later announced officially on 19 November 2008. The engine, EGO, initially featured in <i><a href="/wiki/Race_Driver:_Grid" title="Race Driver: Grid">Race Driver: Grid</a></i>, was updated to better take advantage of the PlayStation 3 specifications, as well as improving multi-core processor utilization on the Xbox 360 and PC. The Flashback ability, allowing players to rewind time mid-race, a new feature in <i>Grid</i>, was kept and improved due to its popularity. In addition, the features and expanse of the multiplayer component, which was often criticized in <i>Colin McRae: Dirt</i> as being fairly anaemic, was completely new to the series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> 
</p><p><i>Colin McRae: Dirt 2</i> used the audio technology <a href="/wiki/Ambisonics" title="Ambisonics">Ambisonics</a>. It was the first PC video game to use Blue Ripple Sound's <a href="/wiki/OpenAL#Rapture3D_OpenAL_Driver" title="OpenAL">Rapture3D</a> sound engine by default.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> It was also one of the first video games to implement the then newly released DirectX 11 graphical standard on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Evergreen_(GPU_family)" title="Evergreen (GPU family)">ATI 5000</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GeForce_400_Series" title="GeForce 400 Series">NVIDIA Geforce 400</a> series chipsets.
</p><p><i>Colin McRae: Dirt 2</i> was an official sponsor of Ken Block, Travis Pastrana, Tanner Foust and Dave Mirra of the <a href="/wiki/Subaru_Rally_Team_USA" title="Subaru Rally Team USA">Subaru Rally Team USA</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Niet_5-8"><a href="#cite_note-Niet-5">[5]</a></sup> 
</p><p>A demo of the game was released on the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Store" title="PlayStation Store">PlayStation Store</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xbox_Live_Marketplace" title="Xbox Live Marketplace">Xbox Live Marketplace</a> on 20 August 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The demo appeared for the PC on 29 November 2009; it features the same content as the console demo with the addition of higher graphic settings and a benchmark tool.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mac_OS_X" title="Mac OS X">Mac OS X</a> version of the game was released by <a href="/wiki/Feral_Interactive" title="Feral Interactive">Feral Interactive</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-feralinteractive.com_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-feralinteractive.com-1">[1]</a></sup> 
</p><p>
In February 2009, Codemasters revealed plans to release "a number of small DLC packs soon after launch which players can use to personalise and augment the boxed game".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> At the time of release, however, only two DLC options were available: "Trust Fund", which unlocks all vehicles, liveries and upgrades, and "Access All Areas", which unlocks all events and tracks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> There have been no further DLC releases. </p><div style="clear:both;"></div>
