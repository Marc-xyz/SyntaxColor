<p><i><b>Streptanthus gracilis</b></i> is an uncommon species of flowering plant in the <a href="/wiki/Brassicaceae" title="Brassicaceae">mustard family</a> known by the common name <b>alpine jewelflower</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Streptanthus_gracilis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is <a href="/wiki/Endemism" title="Endemism">endemic</a> to <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, where it is known only from the Kings-Kern Divide in the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Nevada_(U.S.)" title="Sierra Nevada (U.S.)">Sierra Nevada</a>, a series of high mountain peaks near the intersection of <a href="/wiki/Tulare_County,_California" title="Tulare County, California">Tulare</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fresno_County,_California" title="Fresno County, California">Fresno</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Inyo_County,_California" title="Inyo County, California">Inyo Counties</a>. It grows in weathered rocky habitat such as <a href="/wiki/Scree" title="Scree">talus</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Streptanthus_gracilis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Streptanthus gracilis</i> is an annual herb producing a slender, hairless, waxy stem up to 30 or 35 centimeters tall. The basal leaves have toothed oblong blades borne on <a href="/wiki/Petiole_(botany)" title="Petiole (botany)">petioles</a>. Leaves higher on the stem have shorter blades which may have short petioles or may clasp the stem at their bases.
</p><p>Flowers occur at intervals along the upper stem. Each has an urn-shaped calyx of pink <a href="/wiki/Sepal" title="Sepal">sepals</a> about half a centimeter long with pink petals emerging from the tip. The fruit is a thin, straight or curving <a href="/wiki/Silique" title="Silique">silique</a> up to 7 centimeters long.
</p>
