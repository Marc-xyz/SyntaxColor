<p><b>Oriol Ayala Serra</b> (born 19 October 1996) is a Spanish <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for <a href="/wiki/UE_Figueres" title="UE Figueres">UE Figueres</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Centre-back" title="Defender (association football)">central defender</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oriol_Ayala&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Girona" title="Girona">Girona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">Catalonia</a>, Ayala joined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_C.F." title="Real Madrid C.F.">Real Madrid</a>'s <a href="/wiki/La_F%C3%A1brica" title="La Fábrica">La Fábrica</a> in 2014, after stints at <a href="/wiki/Girona_FC" title="Girona FC">Girona FC</a>, UE Fornells and CEF Gironès-Sàbat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> On 7 July of the following year, after finishing his formation, he moved to <a href="/wiki/Palam%C3%B3s_CF" title="Palamós CF">Palamós CF</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tercera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Tercera División">Tercera División</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Ayala made his senior debut during the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_Tercera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="2015–16 Tercera División">2015–16 campaign</a>, being a regular starter. On 13 June 2017, he joined <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n_B" title="Segunda División B">Segunda División B</a> side <a href="/wiki/UE_Llagostera" title="UE Llagostera">UE Llagostera</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> but only featured rarely for the club.
</p><p>In January 2018, Ayala signed for <a href="/wiki/CF_Reus_Deportiu" title="CF Reus Deportiu">CF Reus Deportiu</a> and was assigned to <a href="/wiki/CF_Reus_Deportiu_B" title="CF Reus Deportiu B">the reserves</a> in the fourth division.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He made his professional debut on 18 October, starting in a 1–3 away loss against <a href="/wiki/UD_Almer%C3%ADa" title="UD Almería">UD Almería</a>, for <a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_Copa_del_Rey" title="2018–19 Copa del Rey">the season</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Copa_del_Rey" title="Copa del Rey">Copa del Rey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2019, after Reus' severe economic problems, Ayala joined fellow fourth division side <a href="/wiki/UE_Figueres" title="UE Figueres">UE Figueres</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
