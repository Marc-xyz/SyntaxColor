<p><b>Sir Toke Tufukia Talagi</b> <span class="noexcerpt nowraplinks" style="font-size:85%;"><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Knight_Companion_of_The_New_Zealand_Order_of_Merit" title="Knight Companion of The New Zealand Order of Merit">KNZM</a></span><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is a Niuean politician and current <a href="/wiki/Premier_of_Niue" title="Premier of Niue">Premier of Niue</a> since 2008. He was elected premier by the <a href="/wiki/Niue_Assembly" title="Niue Assembly">Niue Assembly</a> on 19 June 2008, defeating the standing premier, <a href="/wiki/Young_Vivian" title="Young Vivian">Young Vivian</a>, by fourteen votes to five, with one abstaining.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RNZI_40453_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-RNZI_40453-3">[3]</a></sup> He is the current <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Foreign_Minister_of_Niue" title="Foreign Minister of Niue">Foreign Minister of Niue</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toke_Talagi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are <a href="/wiki/Politics_of_Niue#Political_parties_and_elections" title="Politics of Niue">no political parties</a> in Niue; Talagi is therefore an independent. In addition to his tenure as Premier of Niue since 2008, Talagi has been, at various times, deputy prime minister,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> minister of Finance,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-webcache.googleusercontent.com_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-webcache.googleusercontent.com-5">[5]</a></sup> and minister of Education.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Elected to the national Assembly in 2002, he obtained the same number of votes as an opponent in the <a href="/wiki/2005_Niuean_general_election" title="2005 Niuean general election">2005 election</a>, and retained his seat only thanks to a favourable random draw to designate a winner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-webcache.googleusercontent.com_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-webcache.googleusercontent.com-5">[5]</a></sup> He was <a href="/wiki/2005_Niuean_general_election" title="2005 Niuean general election">re-elected</a> easily in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2008, he was elected chair of the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Islands_Forum" title="Pacific Islands Forum">Pacific Islands Forum</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In May 2009, Talagi co-chaired the 5th <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Alliance_Leaders_Meeting" title="Pacific Alliance Leaders Meeting">Pacific Alliance Leaders Meeting</a> (PALM) with then <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_Prime_Minister" title="Japanese Prime Minister">Japanese Prime Minister</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taro_Aso" title="Taro Aso">Taro Aso</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hokkaido" title="Hokkaido">Hokkaido</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-matangi_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-matangi-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>It was under Talagi's premiership that Niue became, in November 2011, a founding member of the <a href="/wiki/Polynesian_Leaders_Group" title="Polynesian Leaders Group">Polynesian Leaders Group</a>, a regional grouping intended to cooperate on a variety of issues including culture and language, education, responses to climate change, and trade and investment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RNZI_64516_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-RNZI_64516-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p><p>Talagi is the president of the Niue Rugby Union.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2017, Talagi made the announcement he would run in that year's election and for a fourth term as premier.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rnz_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-rnz-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recognition">Recognition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Toke_Talagi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Recognition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/2017_New_Year_Honours" title="2017 New Year Honours">2017 New Year Honours</a>, Talagi was appointed Knight Companion of the <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_Order_of_Merit" title="New Zealand Order of Merit">New Zealand Order of Merit</a> (KNZM).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> He received his knighthood from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Zealand_Governor_General" title="New Zealand Governor General">New Zealand Governor General</a> <a href="/wiki/Patsy_Reddy" title="Patsy Reddy">Patsy Reddy</a> in March 2017, who flew to Niue for the ceremony.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rnz0322_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-rnz0322-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p>
