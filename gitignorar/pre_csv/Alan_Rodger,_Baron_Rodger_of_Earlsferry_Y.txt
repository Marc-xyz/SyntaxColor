<p><b>Alan Ferguson Rodger, Baron Rodger of Earlsferry</b> <span class="noexcerpt nowraplinks" style="font-size:85%;"><a href="/wiki/Privy_Council_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Privy Council of the United Kingdom">PC</a> <a href="/wiki/Fellow_of_the_British_Academy" title="Fellow of the British Academy">FBA</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society_of_Edinburgh" title="Fellow of the Royal Society of Edinburgh">FRSE</a></span> (18 September 1944 – 26 June 2011) was a Scottish academic, lawyer, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Justice_of_the_Supreme_Court_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Justice of the Supreme Court of the United Kingdom">Justice of the Supreme Court of the United Kingdom</a>.
</p><p>He served as <a href="/wiki/Lord_Advocate" title="Lord Advocate">Lord Advocate</a>, the senior <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Law_Officers_of_the_Crown" title="Law Officers of the Crown">Law Officer</a> of Scotland, before becoming <a href="/wiki/Lord_President_of_the_Court_of_Session" title="Lord President of the Court of Session">Lord Justice General and Lord President of the Court of Session</a>, the head of the country's judiciary. He was then appointed a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lord_of_Appeal_in_Ordinary" title="Lord of Appeal in Ordinary">Lord of Appeal in Ordinary</a> (Law Lord) and became a Justice of the Supreme Court when the <a href="/wiki/Judicial_functions_of_the_House_of_Lords" title="Judicial functions of the House of Lords">judicial functions of the House of Lords</a> were transferred to that Court.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_career">Early life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Rodger,_Baron_Rodger_of_Earlsferry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Alan Rodger was born on 18 September 1944 in Glasgow, to Professor <a href="/wiki/Ferguson_Rodger" title="Ferguson Rodger">T Ferguson Rodger</a>, Professor of Psychological Medicine at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Glasgow" title="University of Glasgow">University of Glasgow</a>, and Jean Margaret Smith Chalmers, and educated at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Independent_school_(UK)" title="Independent school (UK)">independent</a> <a href="/wiki/Kelvinside_Academy" title="Kelvinside Academy">Kelvinside Academy</a> in the city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-whoswho_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-whoswho-2">[2]</a></sup> He studied at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Glasgow" title="University of Glasgow">University of Glasgow</a>, graduating with an <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Arts_(Scotland)" title="Master of Arts (Scotland)">MA</a>, and at the University's <a href="/wiki/University_of_Glasgow_School_of_Law" title="University of Glasgow School of Law">School of Law</a>, taking an <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Laws" title="Bachelor of Laws">LLB</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-whoswho_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-whoswho-2">[2]</a></sup> He then studied at <a href="/wiki/New_College,_Oxford" title="New College, Oxford">New College, Oxford</a>—under <a href="/wiki/David_Daube" title="David Daube">David Daube</a>, <a href="/wiki/Regius_Professor_of_Civil_Law_(Oxford)" title="Regius Professor of Civil Law (Oxford)">Regius Professor of Civil Law</a>—where he graduated with an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Master_of_Arts_(Oxbridge_and_Dublin)" title="Master of Arts (Oxbridge and Dublin)">MA (by decree)</a> and <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy">DPhil</a>, and was Dyke Junior Research Fellow at <a href="/wiki/Balliol_College,_Oxford" title="Balliol College, Oxford">Balliol College, Oxford</a>, from 1969 to 1970 and a Fellow of New College from 1970 to 1972.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-whoswho_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-whoswho-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>He became an advocate in 1974<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GN_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-GN-3">[3]</a></sup> and was Clerk of the <a href="/wiki/Faculty_of_Advocates" title="Faculty of Advocates">Faculty of Advocates</a> from 1976 to 1979. He was a Member of the <a href="/wiki/Mental_Welfare_Commission_for_Scotland" title="Mental Welfare Commission for Scotland">Mental Welfare Commission for Scotland</a> from 1981 to 1984, and was appointed <a href="/wiki/Queen%27s_Counsel" title="Queen's Counsel">Queen's Counsel</a> in 1985.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-whoswho_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-whoswho-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GN_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-GN-3">[3]</a></sup> He was an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Advocate_Depute" title="Advocate Depute">Advocate Depute</a> from 1985 to 1988 and was appointed <a href="/wiki/Solicitor_General_for_Scotland" title="Solicitor General for Scotland">Solicitor General for Scotland</a> in 1989, being promoted to <a href="/wiki/Lord_Advocate" title="Lord Advocate">Lord Advocate</a> in 1992, at which time he became a <a href="/wiki/Life_peer" title="Life peer">life peer</a> as <b>Baron Rodger of Earlsferry</b>, of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Earlsferry" title="Earlsferry">Earlsferry</a> in the District of <a href="/wiki/North-East_Fife_(district)" title="North-East Fife (district)">North East Fife</a>, and was appointed to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Her_Majesty%27s_Most_Honourable_Privy_Council" title="Her Majesty's Most Honourable Privy Council">Privy Council</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-whoswho_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-whoswho-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GN_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-GN-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Judicial_career">Judicial career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Rodger,_Baron_Rodger_of_Earlsferry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Judicial career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rodger was appointed a <a href="/wiki/Senator_of_the_College_of_Justice" title="Senator of the College of Justice">Senator of the College of Justice</a>, a judge of the <a href="/wiki/High_Court_of_Justiciary" title="High Court of Justiciary">High Court of Justiciary</a> and <a href="/wiki/Court_of_Session" title="Court of Session">Court of Session</a>, in 1995,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-herald-1995-11-09-judge_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-herald-1995-11-09-judge-4">[4]</a></sup> He became <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lord_Justice_General" title="Lord Justice General">Lord Justice General</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lord_President_of_the_Court_of_Session" title="Lord President of the Court of Session">Lord President</a> in 1996. He was appointed a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lord_of_Appeal_in_Ordinary" title="Lord of Appeal in Ordinary">Lord of Appeal in Ordinary</a> in 2001, upon the retirement of <a href="/wiki/James_Clyde,_Baron_Clyde" title="James Clyde, Baron Clyde">Lord Clyde</a>. He and nine other Lords of Appeal in Ordinary became <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Justice_of_the_Supreme_Court_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Justice of the Supreme Court of the United Kingdom">Justices</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Supreme Court of the United Kingdom">Supreme Court</a> upon that body's inauguration on 1 October 2009.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Rodger,_Baron_Rodger_of_Earlsferry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lord Rodger of Earlsferry died on 26 June 2011 after a short illness.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SCUK_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-SCUK-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-death_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-death-5">[5]</a></sup> Scottish First Minister <a href="/wiki/Alex_Salmond" title="Alex Salmond">Alex Salmond</a>, who provoked fury after criticising Rodger less than a month earlier,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> said he had made an "outstanding contribution" to Scottish public life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-death_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-death-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Honours">Honours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Rodger,_Baron_Rodger_of_Earlsferry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Honours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lord Rodger of Earlsferry was elected a Fellow of the <a href="/wiki/British_Academy" title="British Academy">British Academy</a> in 1991, and the same year was the Maccabaean Lecturer at the Academy. He was appointed a Fellow of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society_of_Edinburgh" title="Royal Society of Edinburgh">Royal Society of Edinburgh</a> and an Honorary <a href="/wiki/Bencher" title="Bencher">Bencher</a> at <a href="/wiki/Lincoln%27s_Inn" title="Lincoln's Inn">Lincoln's Inn</a> in 1992, and an Honorary Bencher of the <a href="/wiki/Bar_of_Northern_Ireland" title="Bar of Northern Ireland">Inn of Court of Northern Ireland</a> in 1998. Hon. Mem., SPTL, subseq. SLS, 1992; Corresp. Mem., Bayerische Akad. der Wissenschaften, 2001. Pres., Holdsworth Club, 1998–99. Hon. Fellow, American Coll. of Trial Lawyers, 2008. He has received honorary degrees of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Laws" title="Doctor of Laws">Doctor of Laws</a> (LLD) from the Universities of <a href="/wiki/University_of_Glasgow" title="University of Glasgow">Glasgow</a> (1995), <a href="/wiki/University_of_Aberdeen" title="University of Aberdeen">Aberdeen</a> (1999) and <a href="/wiki/University_of_Edinburgh" title="University of Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a> (2001).
</p><p>Lord Rodger of Earlsferry had been the <a href="/wiki/Visitor" title="Visitor">Visitor</a> of <a href="/wiki/St_Hugh%27s_College,_Oxford" title="St Hugh's College, Oxford">St Hugh's College, Oxford</a> since 2003,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/High_Steward_(academia)" title="High Steward (academia)">High Steward</a> of the University of Oxford since 2008,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and an Honorary Professor at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Glasgow_School_of_Law" title="University of Glasgow School of Law">University of Glasgow School of Law</a> since July 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GN_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-GN-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
