<p><b>Scranton</b> is a city in <a href="/wiki/Greene_County,_Iowa" title="Greene County, Iowa">Greene County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iowa" title="Iowa">Iowa</a>, United States. The population was 557 at the 2010 census. It was also named after <a href="/wiki/Scranton,_Pennsylvania" title="Scranton, Pennsylvania">Scranton, Pennsylvania</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scranton,_Iowa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Scranton was <a href="/wiki/Plat" title="Plat">platted</a> in 1869, shortly after the Cedar Rapids &amp; Missouri River Railroad was built through that territory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-history-4">[4]</a></sup> It was named for Joseph H. Scranton of <a href="/wiki/Scranton,_Pennsylvania" title="Scranton, Pennsylvania">Scranton, Pennsylvania</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> who supplied most of the iron rails in the construction of the railroad. Scranton was incorporated in 1880.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-history-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Scranton's <a href="/wiki/Water_tower" title="Water tower">water tower</a>, built in 1897, is the oldest working water tower in Iowa and 9th oldest in the United States.  A fire occurred at the tower in 1907, initially started to melt ice that had caused the town to be without water.  Three men were injured attempting to put the fire out, although the tower itself did not suffer any major damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GreeneCDC_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-GreeneCDC-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scranton,_Iowa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Scranton is located along <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_30" title="U.S. Route 30">U.S. Route 30</a> at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Scranton,_Iowa&amp;params=42_1_20_N_94_32_46_W_type:city"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">42°1′20″N</span> <span class="longitude">94°32′46″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">42.02222°N 94.54611°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">42.02222; -94.54611</span></span></span></a></span> (42.022197, -94.546047),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR1_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR1-7">[7]</a></sup> in the valley of the <a href="/wiki/Raccoon_River" title="Raccoon River">North Raccoon River</a>.
</p><p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the city has a total area of 1.88 square miles (4.87 km<sup>2</sup>), all of it land.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gazetteer_files_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Gazetteer_files-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_census">2010 census</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scranton,_Iowa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2010 census">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FactFinder_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-FactFinder-2">[2]</a></sup> of 2010, there were 557 people, 238 households, and 143 families residing in the city. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 296.3 inhabitants per square mile (114.4/km<sup>2</sup>). There were 267 housing units at an average density of 142.0 per square mile (54.8/km<sup>2</sup>). The racial makeup of the city was 96.4% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 0.2% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(U.S._Census)" title="Race (U.S. Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 2.7% from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(U.S._Census)" title="Race (U.S. Census)">other races</a>, and 0.7% from two or more races. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 2.9% of the population.
</p><p>There were 238 households of which 26.5% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 50.8% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 5.0% had a female householder with no husband present, 4.2% had a male householder with no wife present, and 39.9% were non-families. 34.5% of all households were made up of individuals and 12.2% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.34 and the average family size was 3.06.
</p><p>The median age in the city was 43.2 years. 23.2% of residents were under the age of 18; 6.7% were between the ages of 18 and 24; 22.2% were from 25 to 44; 27.7% were from 45 to 64; and 20.1% were 65 years of age or older. The gender makeup of the city was 51.2% male and 48.8% female.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000_census">2000 census</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scranton,_Iowa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2000 census">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-9">[9]</a></sup> of 2000, there were 604 people, 258 households, and 157 families residing in the city. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 321.6 people per square mile (124.0/km²). There were 285 housing units at an average density of 151.8 per square mile (58.5/km²). The racial makeup of the city was 96.52% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 0.17% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 2.65% from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 0.66% from two or more races. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 5.13% of the population.
</p><p>There were 258 households out of which 28.7% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 50.0% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 7.8% had a female householder with no husband present, and 38.8% were non-families. 34.9% of all households were made up of individuals and 17.8% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.34 and the average family size was 3.05.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Scranton_Iowa_20080118_City_Banner.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1849" data-file-width="1565" decoding="async" height="236" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Scranton_Iowa_20080118_City_Banner.JPG/200px-Scranton_Iowa_20080118_City_Banner.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Scranton_Iowa_20080118_City_Banner.JPG/300px-Scranton_Iowa_20080118_City_Banner.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Scranton_Iowa_20080118_City_Banner.JPG/400px-Scranton_Iowa_20080118_City_Banner.JPG 2x" width="200"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Scranton_Iowa_20080118_City_Banner.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>City banner located along Main Street</div></div></div>
<p>In the city, the population was spread out with 26.5% under the age of 18, 7.8% from 18 to 24, 22.4% from 25 to 44, 24.2% from 45 to 64, and 19.2% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 39 years. For every 100 females, there were 93.6 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 90.6 males.
</p><p>As of 2000 the median income for a household was $29,375, and the median income for a family was $38,542. Males had a median income of $27,411 versus $17,188 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the city was $13,836. About 10.6% of families and 13.1% of the population were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 13.8% of those under age 18 and 9.1% of those age 65 or over.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scranton,_Iowa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Residents are in the <a href="/wiki/Greene_County_Community_School_District" title="Greene County Community School District">Greene County Community School District</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> which operates Greene County Elementary, Greene County Middle, and Greene County High schools in <a href="/wiki/Jefferson,_Iowa" title="Jefferson, Iowa">Jefferson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> It was a part of the <a href="/wiki/Jefferson-Scranton_Community_School_District" title="Jefferson-Scranton Community School District">Jefferson-Scranton Community School District</a> until it merged into the Greene County district on July 1, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p><p>Scranton formerly had a public school building of its own, which had a three-story section built in 1915. The school closed in 2008; the school district had notified the city government in 2006 so the city could plan for the future of the school. In 2010 the city government acquired the building, paying $1 for it, and converted it into a community center; the city government considered converting the school facility into an apartment block, but it determined it would have required too much money. The city government determined that it could not find a purpose for the 1913 section; therefore its razing occurred in 2012.  The current Greene County district, as of 2014, rented the school gymnasium for athletic meetups.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Whenschoolsclose_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Whenschoolsclose-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Community">Community</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scranton,_Iowa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Community">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Annual events in Scranton include the Mid-Summer Festival, held in July, and a Christmas celebration.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>Every 5 years the town holds an all school reunion. Next reunion will be in 2020.
</p><p>Three churches exist in Scranton; <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">St. Paul's Catholic Church</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_Methodist_Church" title="United Methodist Church">United Methodist Church</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Churches_of_Christ" title="Churches of Christ">Church of Christ</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GreeneCDC_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-GreeneCDC-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
