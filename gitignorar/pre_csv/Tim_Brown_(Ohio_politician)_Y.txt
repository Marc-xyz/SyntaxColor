<p><b>Tim Brown</b> (born October 25, 1962) is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> politician who is the current President of the <a href="/wiki/Toledo_Metropolitan_Area_Council_of_Governments" title="Toledo Metropolitan Area Council of Governments">Toledo Metropolitan Area Council of Governments</a>. He previously served in the <a href="/wiki/Ohio_House_of_Representatives" title="Ohio House of Representatives">Ohio House of Representatives</a>, representing the 3rd District from 2013 to 2016.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tim_Brown_(Ohio_politician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Brown received a bachelor's degree in business from <a href="/wiki/Bowling_Green_State_University" title="Bowling Green State University">Bowling Green State University</a> in 1986.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tim_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-tim-1">[1]</a></sup> He formerly served as a <a href="/wiki/Wood_County,_Ohio" title="Wood County, Ohio">Wood County</a> Commissioner and as a district representative for United States Congressman <a href="/wiki/Paul_Gillmor" title="Paul Gillmor">Paul Gillmor</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ohio_House_of_Representatives">Ohio House of Representatives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tim_Brown_(Ohio_politician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ohio House of Representatives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A member of the <a href="/wiki/Ohio_Republican_Party" title="Ohio Republican Party">Ohio Republican Party</a>, Brown represents the 3rd District.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He won his first term in 2012 to succeed Randy Gardner by defeating Democrat Kelly Wicks and Libertarian Nathan Eberly with 51% of the vote.
</p><p>Brown is the first openly <a href="/wiki/Gay" title="Gay">gay</a> man to serve in the Ohio state legislature, and only the second LGBT person following <a href="/wiki/Nickie_Antonio" title="Nickie Antonio">Nickie Antonio</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>  He will also be one of just two openly gay Republican state legislators in the United States, alongside Pennsylvania state representative <a href="/wiki/Mike_Fleck" title="Mike Fleck">Mike Fleck</a>.
</p>
