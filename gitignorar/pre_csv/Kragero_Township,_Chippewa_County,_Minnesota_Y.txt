<p><b>Kragero Township</b> is a township in <a href="/wiki/Chippewa_County,_Minnesota" title="Chippewa County, Minnesota">Chippewa County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a>, United States. The population was 164 at the 2000 census.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kragero_Township,_Chippewa_County,_Minnesota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kragero Township was organized in 1873, and named for Hans H. Kragero, a Norwegian immigrant and early settler.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kragero_Township,_Chippewa_County,_Minnesota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the township has a total area of 44.7 square miles (116 km<sup>2</sup>), of which, 41.5 square miles (107 km<sup>2</sup>) of it is land and 3.2 square miles (8.3 km<sup>2</sup>) of it (7.22%) is water.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kragero_Township,_Chippewa_County,_Minnesota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-1">[1]</a></sup> of 2000, there were 164 people, 64 households, and 49 families residing in the township. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 4.0 people per square mile (1.5/km²). There were 79 housing units at an average density of 1.9/sq mi (0.7/km²). The racial makeup of the township was 99.39% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a> and 0.61% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, or 163 Whites and 1 Asian.
</p><p>There were 64 households out of which 31.3% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 70.3% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 3.1% had a female householder with no husband present, and 21.9% were non-families. 18.8% of all households were made up of individuals and 12.5% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.56 and the average family size was 2.88.
</p><p>In the township the population was spread out with 24.4% under the age of 18, 7.9% from 18 to 24, 23.2% from 25 to 44, 29.3% from 45 to 64, and 15.2% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 41 years. For every 100 females, there were 100.0 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 103.3 males.
</p><p>The median income for a household in the township was $42,292, and the median income for a family was $48,125. Males had a median income of $26,250 versus $21,607 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the township was $17,119. None of the families and 2.5% of the population were living below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>.
</p>
