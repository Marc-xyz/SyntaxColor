		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">For other people named Robert Sands, see <a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Robert_Sands_(disambiguation)" title="Robert Sands (disambiguation)">Robert Sands (disambiguation)</a>.</div>
<p><b>Robert Charles Sands</b> (May 11, 1799 <a href="/wiki/Flatbush,_Brooklyn" title="Flatbush, Brooklyn">Flatbush</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kings_County,_New_York" title="Kings County, New York">Kings County, New York</a> – December 17, 1832 <a href="/wiki/Hoboken,_New_Jersey" title="Hoboken, New Jersey">Hoboken</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hudson_County,_New_Jersey" title="Hudson County, New Jersey">Hudson County, New Jersey</a>) was an American writer and poet.
</p><p>He was the son of Auditor-General <a href="/wiki/Comfort_Sands" title="Comfort Sands">Comfort Sands</a>. He was a scholar and a writer of many literary types, but without much originality. His best work is considered to be in his short stories. His most well-known poem is <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Yamoyden&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Yamoyden (page does not exist)">Yamoyden</a></i> which is an Indian story written in collaboration with a friend. He is considered part of the "<a href="/wiki/The_Knickerbocker" title="The Knickerbocker">Knickerbocker group</a>", a group which also included <a href="/wiki/Washington_Irving" title="Washington Irving">Washington Irving</a>, <a href="/wiki/William_Cullen_Bryant" title="William Cullen Bryant">William Cullen Bryant</a>, <a href="/wiki/James_Kirke_Paulding" title="James Kirke Paulding">James Kirke Paulding</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gulian_Crommelin_Verplanck" title="Gulian Crommelin Verplanck">Gulian Crommelin Verplanck</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fitz-Greene_Halleck" title="Fitz-Greene Halleck">Fitz-Greene Halleck</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Rodman_Drake" title="Joseph Rodman Drake">Joseph Rodman Drake</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lydia_M._Child" title="Lydia M. Child">Lydia M. Child</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nathaniel_Parker_Willis" title="Nathaniel Parker Willis">Nathaniel Parker Willis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
