<p>The <b>peroneus tertius</b> (also known as <b>fibularis tertius</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Muscle" title="Muscle">muscle</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Human_body" title="Human body">human body</a> located in the lower limb.
</p><p>The muscle arises from the lower third of the anterior surface of the <a href="/wiki/Fibula" title="Fibula">fibula</a> (anterior compartment of lower leg); from the lower part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Interosseous_membrane_of_the_leg" title="Interosseous membrane of the leg">interosseous membrane</a>; and from an intermuscular septum between it and the <a href="/wiki/Peroneus_brevis" title="Peroneus brevis">peroneus brevis</a> muscle. The septum is sometimes called the intermuscular septum of Otto.
</p><p>The tendon, after passing under the <a href="/wiki/Superior_extensor_retinaculum_of_foot" title="Superior extensor retinaculum of foot">superior extensor retinaculum of foot</a> and <a href="/wiki/Inferior_extensor_retinaculum_of_foot" title="Inferior extensor retinaculum of foot">inferior extensor retinaculum of foot</a> in the same canal as the extensor digitorum longus, is inserted into the dorsal surface of the base of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metatarsus" title="Metatarsus">metatarsal</a> bone of the fifth digit.
</p><p>It is innervated by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deep_fibular_nerve" title="Deep fibular nerve">deep fibular nerve</a>, unlike the other <a href="/wiki/Peroneus_muscles" title="Peroneus muscles">peroneal muscles</a> which are innervated by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Superficial_fibular_nerve" title="Superficial fibular nerve">superficial fibular nerve</a>, since the peroneus tertius is a member of the anterior compartment.
</p><p>Its action is that of weak <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dorsiflexion" title="Dorsiflexion">dorsiflexion</a> of the ankle joint and to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eversion_(kinesiology)" title="Eversion (kinesiology)">evert</a> the foot at the ankle joint.
</p><p>The muscle may be absent in humans; it is seldom found in other primates, a fact that has linked its function to efficient terrestrial bipedalism.
</p>
