	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Yale Debate Association</h1>

<p>The <b>Yale Debate Association (YDA)</b> is <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale University</a>'s only competitive intercollegiate debate team. Founded in 1908, it is the most prolific winner of the <a href="/wiki/American_Parliamentary_Debate_Association" title="American Parliamentary Debate Association">American Parliamentary Debate Association</a>'s Club of the Year award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The YDA was also the first American team to win and have the top speaker at the modern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Universities_Debating_Championships" title="World Universities Debating Championships">World Championships</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-firstfinalist_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-firstfinalist-2">[2]</a></sup> Currently, the YDA is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Universities_Debating_Championships#World_Universities_Debating_Rankings" title="World Universities Debating Championships">fourth-ranked</a> collegiate debate society in the world, and the highest in North America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-worldranking_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-worldranking-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yale_Debate_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sterling_Memorial_Library_2,_September_1,_2008.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3097" data-file-width="2479" decoding="async" height="250" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Sterling_Memorial_Library_2%2C_September_1%2C_2008.jpg/200px-Sterling_Memorial_Library_2%2C_September_1%2C_2008.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Sterling_Memorial_Library_2%2C_September_1%2C_2008.jpg/300px-Sterling_Memorial_Library_2%2C_September_1%2C_2008.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Sterling_Memorial_Library_2%2C_September_1%2C_2008.jpg/400px-Sterling_Memorial_Library_2%2C_September_1%2C_2008.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Sterling_Memorial_Library_2,_September_1,_2008.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Yale's Sterling Memorial Library, location of the YDA Records</div></div></div>
<p>Founded in 1908, the Yale Debate Association initially hosted debates with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harvard" title="Harvard">Harvard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Princeton_University" title="Princeton University">Princeton</a> three times a year. Teams of three students each debated topics that covered a wide range of issues. Prominent officials— from judges, to university presidents, and even former United States president Grover Cleveland— determined the winners of each round. The debates were open to the public, free of charge, and drew large crowds on campus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The New York Times reported that “It is generally as important to win this debate [Yale v. Harvard] as to win the football match in the fall.” <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1922, Yale helped create an intercollegiate debate league with eight other institutions. The league grew to a national level when West Point hosted an open tournament in 1947.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>The YDA's most recent style of debate began with the creation of the <a href="/wiki/American_Parliamentary_Debate_Association" title="American Parliamentary Debate Association">American Parliamentary Debate Association</a> (APDA) in 1982. The format of parliamentary debate differs from those used by earlier leagues; teams consist of two debaters rather than three, and the order of the speeches is structured differently. The team continues to compete in APDA today.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Coaches">Coaches</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yale_Debate_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Coaches">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The YDA has had two official faculty coaches. The first, John Chester Adams graduated from Yale in 1886 and coached the team from when he began teaching in 1914 to his retirement in 1941.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The annual Adams Cup Debate Tournament between residential colleges at Yale is named in his honor. The second, professor of history and oratory Rollin G. Osterweis, coached the team after Adams’ retirement until his own in 1979. Former students of Osterweis include 2004 presidential candidates John Kerry and George Bush.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> "Rollie Osterweis, who coached the debaters, always said that Bill Buckley and John Kerry were the best speakers he'd ever worked with," Yale graduate Harvey Bundy told <a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a> in 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Among those who acted as assistant coaches under Osterweis was <a href="/wiki/John_O%27Leary_(ambassador)" title="John O'Leary (ambassador)">John O'Leary</a>, then a student at the <a href="/wiki/Yale_Law_School" title="Yale Law School">law school</a> and later the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Ambassador" title="United States Ambassador">United States Ambassador</a> to <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a>. As noted <a href="/wiki/Yale_Debate_Association#Osterweis" title="Yale Debate Association">below</a>, the team now hosts a tournament for local Connecticut schools in memory of Rollin Osterweis.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Structure">Structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yale_Debate_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Yale Debate Association is run by a board of students who are elected by the team as a whole each year. This board is entrusted to handle nearly all responsibilities associated with running the team. Currently, the Executive Board consists of five members: the President, the Director of Development, the Director of Membership, the Tournament Coordinator, and the Treasurer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-board_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-board-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>YDA membership is competitive and tryouts are held in the beginning of Yale's fall semester for those wishing to join the team. Once admitted, members remain on the team for the remainder of their time at Yale. The team consists of roughly 40-50 members.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_Competition">National Competition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yale_Debate_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: National Competition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Yale Debate Association primarily competes in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parliamentary_Debate#American_Parliamentary_Debate" title="Parliamentary Debate">American Parliamentary</a> style of debate as a member of the American Parliamentary Debate Association, which is an intercollegiate debate association with dozens of member universities across the United States. The YDA competes weekly at tournaments throughout the country.
</p><p>Members of the team have also been active in the governance of the league, including election to the executive board of APDA. YDA members who have served as President of APDA include Andrew Rohrbach (2008–2009), Andrew Korn (2004–2005), and Scott Luftglass (2000–2001).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_Competition">International Competition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yale_Debate_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: International Competition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In addition to competition within APDA, the YDA competes internationally. The YDA competes at the <a href="/wiki/North_American_Debating_Championship" title="North American Debating Championship">North American Debating Championships</a>, at the World Universities Debating Championships, and at other Inter-Varsity debate tournaments. Currently, the YDA is ranked as the best collegiate debate organization in the world, ahead of the University of Sydney Union and the Oxford Union, according to the ranking methodology developed by iDebate, a major debating news website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-worldranking_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-worldranking-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>The World Universities Debating Championships (WUDC) is conducted in the <a href="/wiki/British_Parliamentary_Style" title="British Parliamentary Style">British Parliamentary</a> style of debate. The YDA competes annually at WUDC and has had more top speakers at WUDC than any other American university,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>  and was the first team from the United States to reach the WUDC final round.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-firstfinalist_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-firstfinalist-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Teams from across the United States and Canada attend the annual North American Championships. The style of debate is similar to American parliamentary, however speeches are shortened. The YDA has won the tournament three times, more than any other American team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-northams_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-northams-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p><p>In addition to these tournaments, the YDA competes at the Oxford and Cambridge Inter-varsity tournaments. Yale debaters won the Oxford Invitational most recently in 2009,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> as well as in 2006 (Yale also won the Cambridge Invitational in 2006)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Yale_High_School_Invitational">The Yale High School Invitational</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yale_Debate_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: The Yale High School Invitational">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Yale Invitational is a nationwide <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Forensic_League" title="National Forensic League">National Forensic League</a> (NFL) tournament for high school students. Students come from across the country to compete in twelve events. Traditionally, quarter-finalists in Varsity <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lincoln-Douglas" title="Lincoln-Douglas">Lincoln-Douglas</a>, octo-finalists in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_Forum_Debate" title="Public Forum Debate">Public Forum Debate</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Congressional_Debate" title="Congressional Debate">Congressional Debate</a> Super Session participants earn a bid to the <a href="/wiki/Tournament_of_Champions_(debate)" title="Tournament of Champions (debate)">Tournament of Champions</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Yale Invitational has been hosted on Yale University's campus every fall since 1993. The organizational aspects of the tournament are entirely run by student members of the YDA. The competition itself is directed by a core of experienced coaches who are familiar with tabulation and tournament direction.
</p>
<h3><span id="The_Yale_Inter-Varsity_.28IV.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Yale_Inter-Varsity_(IV)">The Yale Inter-Varsity (IV)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yale_Debate_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: The Yale Inter-Varsity (IV)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Yale Inter-Varsity Debate Tournament is an annual international debate tournament for college students held in British Parliamentary (BP) style. It was first held in 2006. The organizational aspects of the tournament are run by members of the YDA. The adjudication team is led by a Chief Adjudicator and Deputy Chief Adjudicator, both of which are often current or former team members. The Yale IV was the only British Parliamentary tournament held by a team that is part of the American Parliamentary Debate Association until Fall 2012, with the inaugural Brandeis IV.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Triangulars">Triangulars</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yale_Debate_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Triangulars">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Yale continues to debate Harvard and Princeton in the annual tradition of the Triangulars debates. The competition began near the end of the 19th century as an ad-hoc debate against Harvard. A more formal association began in 1908, when Yale joined forces with Harvard and Princeton to hold annual debates called “Triangulars” that would discuss current political and economic affairs. The debates gained national attention and were attended by high-level officials and large audiences. Today, the YDA continues this tradition by fielding two three-member teams to debate both the Harvard and Princeton delegations every year.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Osterweis">Osterweis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yale_Debate_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Osterweis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Founded in 1980, The <a href="/wiki/Rollin_G._Osterweis" title="Rollin G. Osterweis">Rollin G. Osterweis</a> Tournament is a free tournament for high school students in Connecticut. The tournament includes a demonstration round from four current debaters and a training session for current high school debaters. The tournament is named after YDA Coach Rollin G. Osterweis and is funded by <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Edwards_College" title="Jonathan Edwards College">Jonathan Edwards College</a> in coordination with the Rollin G. Osterweis Fund.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Adams">Adams</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yale_Debate_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Adams">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The YDA also runs the Adams Cup, an intramural debate tournament between the residential colleges at Yale.  The tournament features students from Yale’s twelve <a href="/wiki/Residential_colleges_of_Yale_University" title="Residential colleges of Yale University">residential colleges</a>; the winner keeps the rotating trophy until the next year’s tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Alumni">Alumni</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yale_Debate_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Alumni">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>As one of the oldest debating institutions in the United States, the YDA has developed a large alumni community. Famous alumni have included 2004 presidential candidate <a href="/wiki/John_Kerry" title="John Kerry">John Kerry</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup>  President and Chief Justice of the Supreme Court <a href="/wiki/William_Howard_Taft" title="William Howard Taft">William Howard Taft</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> and publisher and journalist <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_F._Buckley" title="William F. Buckley">William F. Buckley</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Other former Yale debate team members include conservative writer and activist <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/L._Brent_Bozell,_Jr." title="L. Brent Bozell, Jr.">L. Brent Bozell, Jr.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stanford_Law_School" title="Stanford Law School">Stanford Law School</a> professor <a href="/wiki/Deborah_Rhode" title="Deborah Rhode">Deborah Rhode</a> and environmental attorney <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Cavanagh" title="Ralph Cavanagh">Ralph Cavanagh</a>. The YDA recently celebrated its 100th anniversary with the Centennial Reunion in October 2008, where the Yale Association for Debate Alumni was formally created.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Centennial_Reunion">Centennial Reunion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yale_Debate_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Centennial Reunion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Over one hundred current and former members of the team attended the Centennial Reunion in October 2008. The event included demonstration debates by former members of the YDA,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> as well as an alumni debate and a humorous debate, in addition to presentations demonstrating the current structure of the team. The event also paid tribute to the late coach of the YDA, Rollin Osterweis.
</p>
<h3><span id="Yale_Association_for_Debate_Alumni_.28YADA.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Yale_Association_for_Debate_Alumni_(YADA)">Yale Association for Debate Alumni (YADA)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yale_Debate_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Yale Association for Debate Alumni (YADA)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>YADA was founded in October 2008 to serve as a community for former members of the YDA. The organization holds social gatherings for alumni members and serves as a mechanism for current YDA members to contact former members of the team.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Service">Service</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yale_Debate_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Service">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In addition to its usual activities, the YDA is a partner organization of the New Haven <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Urban_Debate_League" title="Urban Debate League">Urban Debate League</a>, which seeks to provide the local community with the opportunity to participate in the activity of debate. The UDL provides weekly coaching lessons to local area high school students, as well as three charity tournaments in each semester. The program has expanded from its inception in 2004 to include ten public schools in New Haven.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_Appearances">Public Appearances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yale_Debate_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Public Appearances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_PETA_Debate">The PETA Debate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yale_Debate_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: The PETA Debate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On March 31, 2010 <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Friedrich" title="Bruce Friedrich">Bruce Friedrich</a>, Vice President of Policy for <a href="/wiki/People_for_the_Ethical_Treatment_of_Animals" title="People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals">People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals</a> (PETA) came to Yale to debate two YDA members on whether it is ethical to eat animals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Friedrich has debated meat, fur, and animal-experimentation industry representatives on many television and radio programs, including NBC's Today show as well as other programs on stations including CNN and MSNBC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> The YDA defended eating animals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Yale-Howard_Debate">The Yale-Howard Debate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yale_Debate_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: The Yale-Howard Debate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale University</a> and <a href="/wiki/Howard_University" title="Howard University">Howard University</a> held a joint event in 2009 and in 2010 that was sponsored by the <a href="/wiki/NAACP" title="NAACP">NAACP</a> and YDA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The debates centered on several topics addressing important questions facing the United States with relation to race and equality. The event’s style is shortened parliamentary and no winner is named. The 2009 and 2010 Yale-Howard Debates were attended by over 2,700 people in <a href="/wiki/Woolsey_Hall" title="Woolsey Hall">Woolsey Hall</a> on Yale University Campus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup>  The audience also included prominent figures of the Yale administration.
</p>
<h3><span id="The_VH1_.22Great_Debate.22"></span><span class="mw-headline" id='The_VH1_"Great_Debate"'>The VH1 "Great Debate"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yale_Debate_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title='Edit section: The VH1 "Great Debate"'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Over the summer of 2009, the YDA participated in an exhibition debate in the middle of Harold Square in New York City against the Harvard team debating various pop culture topics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> Part of this debate was later shown on <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a> news.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_Awards">National Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yale_Debate_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: National Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Over the past 25 years, members of the YDA have received many awards for their performances throughout the course of a season in the American Parliamentary Debate Association. Since APDA's founding, six Yale teams have won the Team of the Year award, granted annually to the league's top performing team of two individuals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> The league also gives the Speaker of the Year (SOTY) award, presented every year to its highest-ranked individual debater. YDA members have won this award six times in total.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> Finally, the Novice of the Year (NOTY) award, given annually to APDA's top performing first-year debater, has been awarded to Yale debaters four times.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup>
</p>
