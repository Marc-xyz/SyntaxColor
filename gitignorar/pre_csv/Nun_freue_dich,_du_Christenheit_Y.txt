	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Nun freue dich, du Christenheit</h1>

<p>"<b><span lang="de" style="font-style:normal;" title="German language text">Nun freue dich, du Christenheit</span></b>" (Now be joyful, you Christendom) is a <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic</a> <a href="/wiki/Hymn" title="Hymn">hymn</a> for <a href="/wiki/Easter" title="Easter">Easter</a>. It goes back to a 1390 hymn, which later appeared as "<b>Freu dich, du werte Christenheit</b>". The final version appeared first in the Catholic hymnal <i><a href="/wiki/Gotteslob_(1975)" title="Gotteslob (1975)">Gotteslob of 1975</a></i>, later in several regional sections of the <i><a href="/wiki/Gotteslob" title="Gotteslob">Gotteslob</a></i>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nun_freue_dich,_du_Christenheit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The current hymn goes back to around 1390, when a <i>Ein Mainzer Prozessionale</i> (A Mainz processional]], meaning a book of <a href="/wiki/Processional_hymn" title="Processional hymn">processional hymns</a>) has a chant with the <a href="/wiki/Incipit" title="Incipit">incipit</a> "Disse oisterliche dage" (These Easter days). The five stanzas appear as verses of the Marian antiphon "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Regina_Coeli" title="Regina Coeli">Regina celi letare</a>". A Breslau manuscript from 1478 lists three stanzas which are related to three stanzas of the Mainz source (1,2,5) but in reverse order, now beginning "Frew dich, alle Christenheit" (Be joyful, all Christendom).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Albert-Zerlik_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Albert-Zerlik-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The hymn, now "Freu dich, du werte Christenheit", became part of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Einheitslieder&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Einheitslieder (page does not exist)">Einheitslieder</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> [<a class="extiw" href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Einheitslieder" title="de:Einheitslieder">de</a>]</span> of 1947, in an attempt to unify Catholic hymn singing in German. The current text, finally "Nun freue dich, du Christenheit", was adapted for the first common Catholic hymnal, the <i><a href="/wiki/Gotteslob_(1975)" title="Gotteslob (1975)">Gotteslob of 1975</a></i>, which had the hymn in its common section. The following edition <i><a href="/wiki/Gotteslob" title="Gotteslob">Gotteslob</a></i> of 2013 has it no longer in the common section, but in 19 of the 24 regional sections. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diocese_of_Mainz" title="Diocese of Mainz">Diocese of Mainz</a> has it as GL 817.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Albert-Zerlik_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Albert-Zerlik-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Melody">Melody</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nun_freue_dich,_du_Christenheit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Melody">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The melody of "Freu dich, du werte Christenheit", and "<span lang="de" style="font-style:normal;" title="German language text">Nun freue dich, du Christenheit</span>" appeared in Mainz around in 1410. It was later used also for "<span lang="de" style="font-style:normal;" title="German language text"><a href="/wiki/Es_ist_das_Heil_uns_kommen_her" title="Es ist das Heil uns kommen her">Es ist das Heil uns kommen her</a></span>".
</p>
