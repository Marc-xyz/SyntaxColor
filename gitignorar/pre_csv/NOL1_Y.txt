<p><b>Putative ribosomal RNA methyltransferase NOP2</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Enzyme" title="Enzyme">enzyme</a> that in humans is encoded by the <i>NOP2</i> <a href="/wiki/Gene" title="Gene">gene</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid8088812_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid8088812-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-entrez_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-entrez-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Protein" title="Protein">protein</a> encoded by this gene is a <a href="/wiki/Nucleolus" title="Nucleolus">nucleolar</a> <a href="/wiki/Antigen" title="Antigen">antigen</a> expressed in <a href="/wiki/Cell_growth" title="Cell growth">proliferating cells</a>. It is not detectable in non-proliferating normal tissue but is detectable in many human tumors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid3422591_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid3422591-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>Overexpression of p120 leads to <a href="/wiki/Malignant_transformation" title="Malignant transformation">malignant transformation</a> of <a href="/wiki/3T3_cells" title="3T3 cells">3T3 cells</a> while treatment with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antisense" title="Antisense">antisense</a> p120 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MRNA" title="MRNA">mRNA</a> causes the transformed cells to revert to their original non-malignant <a href="/wiki/Phenotype" title="Phenotype">phenotype</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid1728415_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid1728415-8">[8]</a></sup> The p120 protein displays a dramatic increase in expression at the <a href="/wiki/G1/S_transition" title="G1/S transition">G1/S transition</a> suggesting that p120 regulates the <a href="/wiki/Cell_cycle" title="Cell cycle">cell cycle</a> and nucleolar activity that is required for cell proliferation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid8419402_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid8419402-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
