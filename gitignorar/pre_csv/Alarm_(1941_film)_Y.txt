<p><i><b>Alarm</b></i> is a 1941 German <a href="/wiki/Crime_film" title="Crime film">crime film</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/Herbert_B._Fredersdorf" title="Herbert B. Fredersdorf">Herbert B. Fredersdorf</a> and starring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Karl_Martell_(actor)" title="Karl Martell (actor)">Karl Martell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maria_von_Tasnady" title="Maria von Tasnady">Maria von Tasnady</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paul_Klinger" title="Paul Klinger">Paul Klinger</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The production was made by the <a href="/wiki/Independent_film" title="Independent film">independent</a> Aco-Film rather than one of Germany's major film companies. It was shot at the <a href="/wiki/Althoff_Studios" title="Althoff Studios">Althoff Studios</a> and various locations around <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a> including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tempelhof_Airport" title="Tempelhof Airport">Tempelhof Airport</a> and the Karstadt Department Store. The film's sets were designed by the <a href="/wiki/Art_director" title="Art director">art director</a> <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Lutz" title="Bruno Lutz">Bruno Lutz</a>.
</p>
