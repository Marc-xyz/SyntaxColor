<p>The <b>1<sup>er</sup> Régiment de Fusiliers Marins</b> 1<sup>er</sup> RFM French ( <b>1<sup>er</sup> RFM</b>, or  <b>first regiment of <a href="/wiki/Fusiliers_Marins" title="Fusiliers Marins">Fusiliers Marins</a></b>) was a unit of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Free_French_Navy" title="Free French Navy">Free French Navy</a> during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_Campaign_(World_War_II)" title="Italian Campaign (World War II)">campaign of Italy</a>, then in the campaign of France.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Creation">Creation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1er_R%C3%A9giment_de_Fusiliers_Marins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Creation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On September 24, 1943, the Fusiliers Marins Battalion increased effectifs by integrating volunteers from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Naval" title="Naval">naval</a> corps present in North Africa (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <i lang="fr">marine d'Afrique du Nord</i>) ( particularly radio and mechanic specialists), accordingly being designated as 1<sup>er</sup> Régiment de fusiliers marins (1<sup>er</sup> RFM), an armored reconnaissance unit of the <a href="/wiki/1st_Free_French_Division" title="1st Free French Division">1st Free French Division</a> 1<sup>er</sup> DFL. Command was entrusted to naval <a href="/wiki/Ranks_in_the_French_Navy" title="Ranks in the French Navy">Corvette captain</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hubert_Amyot_d%27Inville" title="Hubert Amyot d'Inville">Hubert Amyot d'Inville</a>. The regiment was equipped with U.S. American equipment, notably the <a href="/wiki/M24_Chaffee" title="M24 Chaffee">M24 Chaffee</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Campaigns">Campaigns</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1er_R%C3%A9giment_de_Fusiliers_Marins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Campaigns">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Italy">Italy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1er_R%C3%A9giment_de_Fusiliers_Marins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Italy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following a trained supported phase, the 1<sup>er</sup> RFM disembarked in Naples at the corps of the <a href="/wiki/1st_Free_French_Division" title="1st Free French Division">1<sup>er</sup> DFL</a>, on April 22, 1944. Since May 12, the regiment engaged in intense combats on Garigliano. While bearing the forefront of the division on three axes, the RFM led combats at Montefiascone and Radicofani. The regiment counted 61 killed out of which Amyot d'Inville and 140 wounded.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Provence">Provence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1er_R%C3%A9giment_de_Fusiliers_Marins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Provence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the end of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, the regiment was <a href="/wiki/Operation_Dragoon" title="Operation Dragoon">disembarked in Provence</a>, at Cavalaire-sur-Mer, under the command of <a href="/wiki/Ranks_in_the_French_Navy" title="Ranks in the French Navy">Corvette captain</a> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pierre_de_Morsier&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Pierre de Morsier (page does not exist)">Pierre de Morsier</a>.
</p><p>The unit combatted for the liberation of Toulon and Hyères, then reclimbed through the valley of the Rhône, penetrated in Lyon, then reached Autun; the Savary squadron entered following a hard confrontation during which five men were killed and four were wounded. Savary done, in Côte d'Or, the liaison were assumed with units of the <a href="/wiki/2nd_Armored_Division_(France)" title="2nd Armored Division (France)">2<sup>e</sup> DB</a> (Leclerc) which disembarked in Normandy. The RFM pursues the respective advancement in direction of the Vosgues.
</p>
<h3><span id="Vall.C3.A9e_du_Rh.C3.B4ne"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Vallée_du_Rhône">Vallée du Rhône</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1er_R%C3%A9giment_de_Fusiliers_Marins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Vallée du Rhône">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The regiment conducted the juncture with the <a href="/wiki/1st_Spahi_Regiment" title="1st Spahi Regiment">1st Spahi Regiment</a> (<a href="/wiki/Normandy_landings" title="Normandy landings">Normandy landings</a> with the <a href="/wiki/2nd_Armored_Division_(France)" title="2nd Armored Division (France)">2<sup>e</sup> DB</a>) at Châtillon-sur-Seine on September 12. This juncture is considered as one the junctures between Allied troops of Normandy and those of Provence.
</p>
<h3><span id="Haute-Sa.C3.B4ne"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Haute-Saône">Haute-Saône</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1er_R%C3%A9giment_de_Fusiliers_Marins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Haute-Saône">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On September 27, the tank squadron led the attack on Clairegoutte before apprehending Ronchamp on October 8, then Vescemont, Rougegoutte, Romagny and Rougemont-le-Château the following month. Distinguished particularly in the following operations: <a href="/wiki/Ranks_in_the_French_Navy" title="Ranks in the French Navy">Vessel Ensign</a> Bokanowski, <a href="/wiki/Ranks_in_the_French_Navy" title="Ranks in the French Navy">Aspirant</a> Vasseur and, alongside the sailors, the men of the 11th Cuir-Vercors (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <i lang="fr"><a class="extiw" href="https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/11e_r%C3%A9giment_de_cuirassiers" title="fr:11e régiment de cuirassiers">11<sup>e</sup> Cuir-Vercors</a></i>) placed under the orders of the 1<sup>er</sup> RFM.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Battle_of_the_Royan_pocket">Battle of the Royan pocket</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1er_R%C3%A9giment_de_Fusiliers_Marins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Battle of the Royan pocket">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following the campaign in the Vosges, the <a href="/wiki/1st_Free_French_Division" title="1st Free French Division">1<sup>re</sup> DFL</a> was sent to the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic</a> front to reduce the Royan pocket and Grave cap (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <i lang="fr"><a class="extiw" href="https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/poche_de_Royan_et_de_la_pointe_de_Grave" title="fr:poche de Royan et de la pointe de Grave">poche de Royan</a></i>), however was it recalled urgently on the eastern front due to a German offensive launched in December 1944 in Alsace.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Alsace">Alsace</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1er_R%C3%A9giment_de_Fusiliers_Marins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Alsace">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In January 1945, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fusiliers_marins" title="Fusiliers marins">fusiliers marins</a> distinguished themselves again in Alsace, at Herbsheim and Rossfeld, before pursuing their march towards the Rhin.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Alpes">Alpes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1er_R%C3%A9giment_de_Fusiliers_Marins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Alpes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Retrieved from the Alsace front, the division was assigned to the detachment of the <a href="/wiki/Army_of_the_Alps" title="Army of the Alps">Army of the Alps</a> (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <i lang="fr"><a class="extiw" href="https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arm%C3%A9e_des_Alpes_(1939)" title="fr:Armée des Alpes (1939)">armée des Alpes</a></i>) in April 1945, in the massif of Authion, where the 1st squadron fought with distinction, enduring the loss in the offensive of five officers out of six and nearly 50% of its forces that were engaged.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Traditions">Traditions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1er_R%C3%A9giment_de_Fusiliers_Marins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Traditions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Ordre_de_la_lib.C3.A9ration"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Ordre_de_la_libération">Ordre de la libération</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1er_R%C3%A9giment_de_Fusiliers_Marins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Ordre de la libération">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The mechanic <a href="/wiki/Sailor" title="Sailor">sailor</a> Georges Brières, killed at Giromagny, rests in the <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A9morial_de_la_France_combattante" title="Mémorial de la France combattante">Mémorial de la France combattante</a>. Brières represents the sacrifice of all sailors lost for the Liberation of France.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Regimental_Colors">Regimental Colors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1er_R%C3%A9giment_de_Fusiliers_Marins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Regimental Colors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The regimental colors, memory and tradition of the 1<sup>er</sup> Régiment de fusiliers marins are confined to the fusiliers school. With the <a href="/wiki/French_Navy" title="French Navy">Naval</a> leadership of the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%A9giment_Blind%C3%A9_de_Fusiliers-Marins" title="Régiment Blindé de Fusiliers-Marins">Régiment Blindé de Fusiliers-Marins</a> <a href="/wiki/Fusiliers_Marins" title="Fusiliers Marins">RBFM</a> of the <a href="/wiki/French_Navy" title="French Navy">French Navy</a> which was not part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Free_French_Forces" title="Free French Forces">Free French Forces</a> FFL, nor part of the <a href="/wiki/Free_French_Naval_Forces" title="Free French Naval Forces">Free French Naval Forces</a> FNFL, the <a href="/wiki/Ranks_in_the_French_Navy" title="Ranks in the French Navy">Naval</a> leadership of the 1<sup>er</sup> Régiment de Fusiliers Marins showcased the valor of French arms.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Regiment_combat_casualties">Regiment combat casualties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1er_R%C3%A9giment_de_Fusiliers_Marins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Regiment combat casualties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Between October 1940 and May 1945, the ensemble of the 1<sup>er</sup> BFM/ 1<sup>e</sup> RFM endured the loss of 195 men amongst them 12 officers out of which 2 commandants.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Decorations">Decorations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1er_R%C3%A9giment_de_Fusiliers_Marins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Decorations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Awarded to the men of this regiment:
</p>
<ul><li>200 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Croix_de_guerre_1939-1945" title="Croix de guerre 1939-1945">croix de guerre</a>,</li>
<li>70 <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A9daille_militaire" title="Médaille militaire">médailles militaires</a>,</li>
<li>32 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ordre_national_de_la_L%C3%A9gion_d%27honneur" title="Ordre national de la Légion d'honneur">Légion d'honneur</a>,</li>
<li>31 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ordre_de_la_Lib%C3%A9ration" title="Ordre de la Libération">croix de la Libération</a>.</li></ul>
<p>The regimental colors of the 1<sup>er</sup> RFM was awarded 5 citations at the orders of the armed forces obtained for 1939-1945 with attribution of the croix de la Libération, the <a href="/wiki/Resistance_Medal" title="Resistance Medal">médaille de la Résistance française</a> and la croix de guerre.
</p><p>In August 1945, the 1<sup>er</sup> RFM was reassigned at the disposition of <a href="/wiki/French_Navy" title="French Navy">Naval authorities</a>.
</p>
