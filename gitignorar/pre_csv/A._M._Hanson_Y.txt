<b>Alexander Mark</b> "<b>A. M.</b>" <b>Hanson</b> (born 1969) is an English artist and photographer. He is based in London.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A._M._Hanson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in 1969, adopted and raised in the <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a> countryside, near <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a>, in north-east England.
His mother, A.A. Hanson (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leeds_College_of_Art" title="Leeds College of Art">Leeds College of Art</a>), is a <a href="/wiki/Pottery" title="Pottery">potter</a> and former art teacher.
</p><p>He sang in a church <a href="/wiki/Choir" title="Choir">choir</a> (St. Edmund's, Roundhay, c.1980). After schools in Leeds and <a href="/wiki/York" title="York">York</a>, he moved from home at age 17, holding <a href="/wiki/Happening" title="Happening">happenings</a> in the basement of a shared house in the <a href="/wiki/Harehills" title="Harehills">Harehills</a> area of Leeds. Around the same time he attended courses with the <a href="/wiki/National_Youth_Theatre" title="National Youth Theatre">National Youth Theatre</a> and performed in productions at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leeds_University" title="Leeds University">Leeds University</a> Workshop Theatre. His first exhibited work (photo montages) shown at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leeds_City_Art_Gallery" title="Leeds City Art Gallery">Leeds City Art Gallery</a> in a group display about new <a href="/wiki/Surrealism" title="Surrealism">surrealism</a>. He left the formal education system early (after a brief period at a local arts college) and moved to London in 1989. Later that year he travels to <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a> and witnesses and documents the historic collapse of the <a href="/wiki/Berlin_Wall" title="Berlin Wall">Berlin Wall</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="1990s_work">1990s work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A._M._Hanson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1990s work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>First published imagery features in <a href="/wiki/I-D" title="I-D">i-D</a> magazine (1991) of nightclubbers at a rave. His social and performance based photos appear in various music, scene and listings <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Publications" title="Publications">publications</a> throughout the early to mid '90s, sometimes using the moniker 'Alex Sparks', and form print folios. Appears in a French TV commercial (1993), playing a catwalk photographer, for a Mod's Hair product. He makes <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Film_stills" title="Film stills">film stills</a> and appears in the award-winning short <i>A Smashing Night Out</i> (dir: M. Glamorre, 1994 <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> 10 x 10 series). His photo series includes an early picture of designer <a href="/wiki/Alexander_McQueen" title="Alexander McQueen">Alexander McQueen</a> on the verge of fame, alongside other progressive characters, at a time when London was witnessing the so-called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brit_Art" title="Brit Art">Brit Art</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brit_Pop" title="Brit Pop">Brit Pop</a> cultural explosion. Prints from this period later featured in <i>QueerNation</i>, a large 20th-century group <a href="/wiki/Retrospective" title="Retrospective">retrospective</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polysexual" title="Polysexual">polysexual</a> London <a href="/wiki/Nightlife" title="Nightlife">nightlife</a> (Elms Lesters Painting Rooms, London 2002). 
</p><p>Hanson was the only photographer to record performance artist and cultural icon <a href="/wiki/Leigh_Bowery" title="Leigh Bowery">Leigh Bowery</a>'s legendary last show at the Freedom Cafe in London in late 1994. Some of the work was later exhibited (including at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Fine_Art_Society" title="The Fine Art Society">The Fine Art Society</a>, London 1995) and published, firstly in the 1998 monograph <i>Leigh Bowery</i> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> prompting publisher Robert Violette to say that the photographs; "represent a crucial, defining moment of London in the 1990s". An audio/visual book, featuring collaborations between artists and musicians, <i>We Love You</i> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> shows his photo work, of Bowery's Minty collaborator Richard Torry and his Soho living studio space, alongside that of more established art world figures such as <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_%26_George" title="Gilbert &amp; George">Gilbert &amp; George</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tracey_Emin" title="Tracey Emin">Tracey Emin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marc_Quinn" title="Marc Quinn">Marc Quinn</a>.
</p><p>He sometimes performs with The Offset and The Paper People collectives, often singing a song about a 'side kick' Susan Tripod, first in shows in his kitchen and at LUX Centre London, 1998. 
</p><p>Drawings based on aerial photos turned into island maps, with related travel ephemera, are displayed at Vexed Generation in Soho (London, April 1999).
</p>
<h2><span id="2000.27s"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2000's">2000's</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A._M._Hanson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2000's">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A collaborative venture Alexcalledsimon projects forms to produce and present work based around a concept 'photo-related family' of made up characters and scenarios. These performance and mixed media works and recordings are exhibited, form part of live events, web based videos, screenings and in publications. He appears in "Battle of the Boutiques" <a href="/wiki/London_Fashion_Week" title="London Fashion Week">London Fashion Week</a> (A/W 2004) wearing <i>Missy island</i> map drawings evoked as paper hats. Paper cut outs from this series form a message trail across the Greek Cyclades islands the same year. 
</p><p>Exhibits at home and abroad, including Japan at Rice+ Tokyo 2003, various London galleries and project spaces including; The Centre of Attention 2003, The Photographer's Gallery / group display 2007, Residence Gallery 2008, <a href="/wiki/Wolfgang_Tillmans" title="Wolfgang Tillmans">Wolfgang Tillmans</a> London studio space 2008, Donlon Books 2009, at UK <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Art_festival" title="Art festival">art festivals</a> <i>Hackney Wicked</i> 2008, exhibits across Europe <i>24London</i> – <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a>, Berlin, <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a> 2006, and in a series of department store windows for t.a.g galleries, <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a> 2009. 
</p><p>Performance associated photo work continues, including studies (often forming extended series) of performers such as <a href="/wiki/David_Hoyle_(performance_artist)" title="David Hoyle (performance artist)">David Hoyle</a> and others at the cutting edge of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Avant_garde" title="Avant garde">avant garde</a> and offshoots of <a href="/wiki/Popular_culture" title="Popular culture">pop culture</a>. Some of this imagery forms photo blogs, other material is published including featuring in <i>Dance Theatre Journal</i> (vol. 23 No. 1, 2008 UK, 'Hoyle's Humility' cover and lead story images) and <i>Gazelland</i> magazine USA (with group display at Leo Kesting Gallery, New York) 2008 and 2009.
</p>
<h2><span id="2010.27s"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010's">2010's</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A._M._Hanson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2010's">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Formulates new ideas and work with studio based studies first at LCC <a href="/wiki/University_of_the_Arts_London" title="University of the Arts London">University of the Arts London</a> UAL, then at the <a href="/wiki/University_for_the_Creative_Arts" title="University for the Creative Arts">University for the Creative Arts</a> at Canterbury, BA (Hons) Fine Art graduating in 2013 with first class honours. New work first shown at various academic spaces and then at Galleria Uno+Uno in <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a> (2012). Contributions to other group shows include touring show <i>Business As Usual</i> at various venues including for North Contemporary 2015, and the summer salon at Lubomirov / Angus-Hughes Gallery, London 2016. Hanson curates a group test site event at Satellite Festival, <a href="/wiki/Whitstable" title="Whitstable">Whitstable</a> Biennale 2012, and several <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Interdisciplinary" title="Interdisciplinary">interdisciplinary</a> shows whilst at UCA. 
</p><p>In <i>A Distant, Darkened Lobby</i> a solo show at Limbo Arts, <a href="/wiki/Margate" title="Margate">Margate</a> 2014 (part of the town and <a href="/wiki/Turner_Contemporary" title="Turner Contemporary">Turner Contemporary</a>'s <i>Summer of Colour</i> season), interconnected installations or 'photo sets' comprise oversized layered prints and backlit film, ready made and adapted objects and office furniture with applied transfers, and films. The show reimagines office spaces as fragments of artist's studios, a theme and motif that becomes central to his practice. Some of this work, in a new arrangement, then transfers to <a href="/wiki/Regent%27s_Place" title="Regent's Place">Regent's Place</a> Plaza, London 2014, during <a href="/wiki/Frieze_Art_Fair" title="Frieze Art Fair">Frieze Art Fair</a> Week. 
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Archive" title="Archive">Archive</a> photographs are published in <i>Alexander McQueen: Fashion Visionary</i> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>  and <i>The Life and The Legacy</i> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> with quotes from the photographer, and alongside another interview text in a biography of the late designer <i>Blood Beneath The Skin</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> in the German book <i>Leigh Bowery Verwandlungskünstler</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Photographs of McQueen are acquired by <a href="/wiki/Central_Saint_Martins" title="Central Saint Martins">Central Saint Martins</a> Museum and Study Collection.
</p><p>Collaborative short films and a live video are made with Dale Cornish for the musician's 2014 <i>Xeric</i> recordings (<i>Entr'acte</i> Records). 
</p><p>Hanson curates a studios project which first shows as <i>Studio Life Lines</i> in a Docklands atrium as part of Photo Month Festival 2016, with emerging and established artists, film makers and photographers. A print based show and shop <i>These Studio Cities</i> looks at a variety of makers' processes, during open studios in Woolwich 2017. This evolving project aims to highlight the energy, ideas and productivity of studio situations and systems and their vital role for individuals, groups and creative communities especially in cities.
</p>
