<p>"<b>You Can't Deny It</b>" is a song by English singer <a href="/wiki/Lisa_Stansfield" title="Lisa Stansfield">Lisa Stansfield</a> from her debut solo <a href="/wiki/Album" title="Album">album</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Affection_(Lisa_Stansfield_album)" title="Affection (Lisa Stansfield album)">Affection</a></i> (1989). The song was written by Stansfield, Ian Devaney and Andy Morris, produced by Devaney and Morris, and remixed by Gail "Sky" King and Yvonne Turner. "You Can't Deny It" was released by <a href="/wiki/Arista_Records" title="Arista Records">Arista Records</a> as the second North American single on 2 May 1990.
</p><p>"You Can't Deny It" received positive reviews from <a href="/wiki/Music_journalism" title="Music journalism">music critics</a> and was commercially successful. The accompanying <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> for "You Can't Deny It" was directed by Steve Lowe and Jimmy Fletcher.
</p><p>In 2003, "You Can't Deny It" was included on the North American edition of <i><a href="/wiki/Biography:_The_Greatest_Hits" title="Biography: The Greatest Hits">Biography: The Greatest Hits</a></i>. In 2014, the remixes of "You Can't Deny It" were included on the deluxe 2CD + DVD re-release of <i>Affection</i> and on <i><a href="/wiki/People_Hold_On_..._The_Remix_Anthology" title="People Hold On ... The Remix Anthology">People Hold On ... The Remix Anthology</a></i> (also on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Collection_1989%E2%80%932003" title="The Collection 1989–2003">The Collection 1989–2003</a></i>). The new version 24/7 was included on the deluxe edition of <i><a href="/wiki/Seven_(Lisa_Stansfield_album)" title="Seven (Lisa Stansfield album)">Seven</a></i> and on <i><a href="/wiki/Seven_(Lisa_Stansfield_album)" title="Seven (Lisa Stansfield album)">Seven+</a></i> in 2014.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=You_Can%27t_Deny_It&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"You Can't Deny It" was released as the second North American single on 2 May 1990. "You Can't Deny It" was also released as a single in Japan on 21 June 1990. The single also included previously unreleased songs, "Lay Me Down" and "Something's Happenin'."
</p><p>In September 1990, just before Stansfield's European tour, the <a href="/wiki/A-side_and_B-side#Double_A-side" title="A-side and B-side">double A-side</a> single "<a href="/wiki/This_Is_the_Right_Time" title="This Is the Right Time">This Is the Right Time</a>"/"You Can't Deny It" with US remixes was released in selected European countries.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=You_Can%27t_Deny_It&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <i><a href="/wiki/Gavin_Report" title="Gavin Report">Gavin Report</a></i> wrote about the song: "There are lots of choice selections on Lisa's debut album, and here is a sweet example of her brand of whirling, sophisticated soul. No denying she must be considered among the most promising new artists to emerge this year."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chart_performance">Chart performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=You_Can%27t_Deny_It&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Chart performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"You Can't Deny It" became a hit in the United States, reaching number fourteen on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> chart, number one on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Songs" title="Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs">Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs</a> chart, number two on the <a href="/wiki/Dance_Club_Songs" title="Dance Club Songs">Dance Club Songs</a> chart and number 26 on the <a href="/wiki/Adult_Contemporary_(chart)" title="Adult Contemporary (chart)">Adult Contemporary</a> chart.
</p><p>In Canada, it peaked at number fourteen on the Top Singles chart and Adult Contemporary chart, and also reached number two on the Dance chart.
</p>
