<p>The <b>1963 Preakness Stakes</b> was the 88th running of the $200,000 <a href="/wiki/Preakness_Stakes" title="Preakness Stakes">Preakness Stakes</a> thoroughbred horse race. The race took place on May 18, 1963, and was televised in the United States on the <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> <a href="/wiki/Television_network" title="Television network">television network</a>. <a href="/wiki/Candy_Spots" title="Candy Spots">Candy Spots</a>, who was jockeyed by <a href="/wiki/Bill_Shoemaker" title="Bill Shoemaker">Bill Shoemaker</a>, won the race by three and one half lengths over runner-up <a href="/wiki/Chateaugay_(horse)" title="Chateaugay (horse)">Chateaugay</a>. Approximate post time was 5:48 p.m. Eastern Time. The race was run over a distance of a mile and one-sixteenth on a fast track in a final time of 1:56-<span style="font-size:80%">1/5</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>  The Maryland Jockey Club reported total attendance of <b>35,263</b>, this is recorded as second highest on the list of <a href="/wiki/American_thoroughbred_racing_top_attended_events" title="American thoroughbred racing top attended events">American thoroughbred racing top attended events</a> for North America in 1963.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2010guide_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-2010guide-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
