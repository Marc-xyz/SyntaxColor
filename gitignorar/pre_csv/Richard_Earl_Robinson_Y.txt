<p><b>Richard Earl Robinson</b> (February 3, 1903 – January 28, 1991) was a <a href="/wiki/United_States_federal_judge" title="United States federal judge">United States District Judge</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_District_Court_for_the_District_of_Nebraska" title="United States District Court for the District of Nebraska">United States District Court for the District of Nebraska</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education_and_career">Education and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Earl_Robinson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Education and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Omaha,_Nebraska" title="Omaha, Nebraska">Omaha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nebraska" title="Nebraska">Nebraska</a>, Robinson received a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Laws" title="Bachelor of Laws">Bachelor of Laws</a> from <a href="/wiki/Creighton_University_School_of_Law" title="Creighton University School of Law">Creighton University School of Law</a> in 1927. He was in private practice in Omaha from 1927 to 1956.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Federal_judicial_service">Federal judicial service</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Earl_Robinson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Federal judicial service">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On June 21, 1956, Robinson was nominated by President <a href="/wiki/Dwight_D._Eisenhower" title="Dwight D. Eisenhower">Dwight D. Eisenhower</a> to a seat on the <a href="/wiki/United_States_District_Court_for_the_District_of_Nebraska" title="United States District Court for the District of Nebraska">United States District Court for the District of Nebraska</a> vacated by Judge <a href="/wiki/James_A._Donohoe" title="James A. Donohoe">James A. Donohoe</a>. Robinson was confirmed by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">United States Senate</a> on July 2, 1956, and received his commission the next day. He served as Chief Judge from 1957 to 1972. He assumed <a href="/wiki/Senior_status" title="Senior status">senior status</a> on January 7, 1972. Robinson served in that capacity until his death on January 28, 1991.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-auto-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
