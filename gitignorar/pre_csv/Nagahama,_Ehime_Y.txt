	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Nagahama, Ehime</h1>

		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Nagahama</b><span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">長浜町</span><span style="display:none">,</span> <i>Nagahama-chō</i>)</span> was a <a href="/wiki/List_of_towns_in_Japan" title="List of towns in Japan">town</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Kita_District,_Ehime" title="Kita District, Ehime">Kita District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ehime_Prefecture" title="Ehime Prefecture">Ehime Prefecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.
</p><p>In 2003, the town had an estimated <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 8,765 and a <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">density</a> of 117.19 persons per km². The total area was 74.79 km².
</p><p>On January 11, 2005, Nagahama, along with the town of <a href="/wiki/Hijikawa,_Ehime" title="Hijikawa, Ehime">Hijikawa</a> and the village of <a href="/wiki/Kawabe,_Ehime" title="Kawabe, Ehime">Kawabe</a> (all from <a href="/wiki/Kita_District,_Ehime" title="Kita District, Ehime">Kita District</a>), was merged into the expanded city of <a href="/wiki/%C5%8Czu,_Ehime" title="Ōzu, Ehime">Ōzu</a> and no longer exists as an independent <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Japan" title="Municipalities of Japan">municipality</a>.
</p><p>Located on the coast, some of Nagahama's industries include fishing, logging, and a traditional wooden sandal factory. It is renowned in its area for its <a href="/wiki/Fugu" title="Fugu">fugu</a> (blowfish) sushi.
</p><p>There is an observatory in Nagahama from which a view of the Hijikawa River feeding into the <a href="/wiki/Seto_Inland_Sea" title="Seto Inland Sea">Seto Inland Sea</a> can be seen. There is a famous old drawbridge that is the oldest of its kind in Japan still in use. <a href="/wiki/Iyo-Nagahama_Station" title="Iyo-Nagahama Station">Iyo-Nagahama Station</a> serves Nagahama and has been featured in several movies<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Avoid_weasel_words" title="Wikipedia:Avoid weasel words"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too vague attribution or weasel words. (February 2017)">which?</span></a></i>]</sup>.  
</p><p>Nagahama is also known as Iyo-Nagahama and is not to be confused with <a href="/wiki/Nagahama,_Shiga" title="Nagahama, Shiga">Nagahama City</a> in <a href="/wiki/Shiga_Prefecture" title="Shiga Prefecture">Shiga Prefecture</a>.
</p>
