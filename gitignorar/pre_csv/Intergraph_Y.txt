	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Intergraph</h1>

<p><b>Intergraph Corporation</b> was an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> software development and services company, which now forms part of <a href="/wiki/Hexagon_AB" title="Hexagon AB">Hexagon AB</a>. It provides enterprise engineering and geospatially powered software to businesses, governments, and organizations around the world, and operates through three divisions: Hexagon PPM, Hexagon Safety &amp; Infrastructure, and Hexagon Geospatial. The company’s headquarters is in Huntsville, Alabama, United States.
</p><p>In 2008, Intergraph was one of the one hundred largest software companies in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In July 2010, Intergraph was acquired by <a href="/wiki/Hexagon_AB" title="Hexagon AB">Hexagon AB</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intergraph&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:M%26S_Computing_exhibit_1978.agr.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2796" data-file-width="3502" decoding="async" height="176" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/M%26S_Computing_exhibit_1978.agr.jpg/220px-M%26S_Computing_exhibit_1978.agr.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/M%26S_Computing_exhibit_1978.agr.jpg/330px-M%26S_Computing_exhibit_1978.agr.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/M%26S_Computing_exhibit_1978.agr.jpg/440px-M%26S_Computing_exhibit_1978.agr.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:M%26S_Computing_exhibit_1978.agr.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>M&amp;S Computing exhibit at a trade show in 1978</div></div></div>
<p>Intergraph was founded in 1969 as M&amp;S Computing, Inc., by former <a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM">IBM</a> engineers Jim
Meadlock, his wife Nancy, Terry Schansman (the S of M&amp;S), Keith Schonrock, and
Robert Thurber who had been working with <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Army" title="U.S. Army">U.S. Army</a> in developing systems that would apply <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Digital_computing" title="Digital computing">digital computing</a> to real time missile guidance. The company was later renamed to <i>Intergraph Corporation</i> in 1980.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>One of Intergraph's major hardware projects was developing a line of workstations using the <a href="/wiki/Clipper_architecture" title="Clipper architecture">Clipper architecture</a> created by <a href="/wiki/Fairchild_Semiconductor" title="Fairchild Semiconductor">Fairchild Semiconductor</a>. Intergraph was one of only two companies to use the chips in a major product line. Intergraph developed their own version of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UNIX" title="UNIX">UNIX</a> for the architecture, which they called <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=CLIX_(operating_system)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="CLIX (operating system) (page does not exist)">CLIX</a>. In 1987, Intergraph bought the Fairchild division responsible for the chip.
</p><p>In 1997, Intergraph began pursuing litigation against Intel and other computer hardware manufacturers based on the intellectual property developed in Clipper. Intergraph negotiated major settlements with Intel,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> HP,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> TI <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and Gateway,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> earning the company over $394M. In 2000, Intergraph exited the hardware business and became purely a software company. On July 21, 2000, it sold its Intense3D graphics accelerator division to <a href="/wiki/3Dlabs" title="3Dlabs">3Dlabs</a>, and its <a href="/wiki/Workstation" title="Workstation">workstation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Server_(computing)" title="Server (computing)">server</a> division to <a href="/wiki/Silicon_Graphics" title="Silicon Graphics">Silicon Graphics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>On November 29, 2006, Intergraph was acquired by an investor group led by <a href="/wiki/Hellman_%26_Friedman" title="Hellman &amp; Friedman">Hellman &amp; Friedman</a> LLC, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Texas_Pacific_Group" title="Texas Pacific Group">Texas Pacific Group</a> and <a href="/wiki/JMI_Equity" title="JMI Equity">JMI Equity</a>, making the company privately held. On October 28, 2010, Intergraph was acquired by <a href="/wiki/Hexagon_AB" title="Hexagon AB">Hexagon AB</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The transaction marks the return of Intergraph as part of a publicly traded company.
</p><p>As part of the Hexagon acquisition, Hexagon moved the management of ERDAS, Inc. from under Leica Geosystems to Intergraph, and Z/I Imaging airborne imaging sensors from under Intergraph to Leica Geosystems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>On December 2, 2013, the geospatial technology portfolio was split out from  under the Intergraph Security, Government and Infrastructure division to form the Hexagon Geospatial division.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> On October 13, 2015, the Intergraph Security, Government &amp; Infrastructure division was rebranded as Hexagon Safety &amp; Infrastructure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On January 9, 2017, the Intergraph Government Solutions division was rebranded as Hexagon US Federal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 5, 2017, the Intergraph Process, Power &amp; Marine division was rebranded as Hexagon PPM.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p>
