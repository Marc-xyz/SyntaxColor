<p><b>Sarah "Sasoun" Guyard-Guillot</b> (<small>French pronunciation: ​</small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/French" title="Help:IPA/French">[sa.ʁa sa.sun ɡi.jaʁ.ɡi.jo]</a></span>); (September 12, 1981 – June 29, 2013) was a French <a href="/wiki/Acrobatics" title="Acrobatics">acrobat</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aerialist" title="Aerialist">aerialist</a> who fell to her death during a performance of the <a href="/wiki/Cirque_du_Soleil" title="Cirque du Soleil">Cirque du Soleil</a> show <i><a href="/wiki/K%C3%A0" title="Kà">Kà</a></i> at <a href="/wiki/MGM_Grand_Las_Vegas" title="MGM Grand Las Vegas">MGM Grand</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Las_Vegas,_Nevada" title="Las Vegas, Nevada">Las Vegas, Nevada</a>, on June 29, 2013.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Death_of_Sarah_Guyard-Guillot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/K%C3%A0" title="Kà">Kà</a></div>
<p><i>Kà</i> is a Cirque du Soleil show that plays at the <a href="/wiki/MGM_Grand_Las_Vegas" title="MGM Grand Las Vegas">MGM Grand</a> in Las Vegas, Nevada.  According to the <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a></i> the show "may well be the most lavish production in the history of Western theater [and] is surely the most technologically advanced."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>  It features a 360 degree rotating stage and 86 cast members.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USA_Today_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-USA_Today-2">[2]</a></sup>  The stage does not have a permanent floor; it has several moving platforms that appear to float.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Guyard-Guillot's fall was the second accident to halt a performance in less than a week. On June 26, 2013, a cast member suffered a minor concussion after slipping from a slack rope during a preview performance of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson:_One" title="Michael Jackson: One">Michael Jackson: One</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LV_Sun_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-LV_Sun-4">[4]</a></sup>  Previously, two cast members were seriously injured during a 2007 performance of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zumanity" title="Zumanity">Zumanity</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LV_Sun_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-LV_Sun-4">[4]</a></sup> In 2009, an acrobat died during a <a href="/wiki/Trampoline" title="Trampoline">trampoline</a> training accident.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Variety_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Variety-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Death_of_Sarah_Guyard-Guillot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sarah Guyard-Guillot was born in Paris, France, and began her circus career in childhood.  She graduated from the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Annie_Fratellini_Art_%26_Circus_Academy&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Annie Fratellini Art &amp; Circus Academy (page does not exist)">Annie Fratellini Art &amp; Circus Academy</a>.  She worked as an acrobat and aerialist for 22 years.  In 2006, Guyard-Guillot joined the cast of <i>Kà</i> as it opened at the MGM Grand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LV_Sun_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-LV_Sun-4">[4]</a></sup>  She ran a circus school class for children in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Valley" title="Las Vegas Valley">Las Vegas</a> called Cirquefit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USA_Today_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-USA_Today-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>  She was formerly married to Mathieu Guyard, another <i>Kà</i> performer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>   At the time of her death, Guyard-Guillot's two daughters were 5 and 8.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Death_of_Sarah_Guyard-Guillot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the final battle scene of <i><a href="/wiki/K%C3%A0" title="Kà">Kà</a></i>, the stage is oriented vertically and performers use <a href="/wiki/Safety_harness" title="Safety harness">harnesses</a> attached to cables to move about.  During this scene at the 9:30 pm show on June 29, 2013, Guyard-Guillot was hoisted up the side of the stage to a height of at least 94 feet (29 m) (some eyewitnesses said it may have been over 100 feet (30 m)).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LV_Sun_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-LV_Sun-4">[4]</a></sup>  According to an eyewitness, she "then just plummeted down."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USA_Today_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-USA_Today-2">[2]</a></sup>  Her harness apparently slipped free from its safety wire, causing her to fall into a pit, out of the audience's view.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LV_Sun_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-LV_Sun-4">[4]</a></sup>  A spokesperson confirmed that the harness itself remained on Guyard-Guillot during her fall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Variety_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Variety-5">[5]</a></sup>  "Initially, a lot of people in the audience thought it was part of the choreographed fight," said an eyewitness.  "But you could hear screaming, then groaning, and we could hear a female artist crying from the stage."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USA_Today_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-USA_Today-2">[2]</a></sup>  After her fall, the show was quickly stopped and a recording asked the audience to leave and said refunds would be offered.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USA_Today_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-USA_Today-2">[2]</a></sup> The other performers watched helplessly as they dangled in the air, before being lowered to safety, one by one.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LV_Sun_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-LV_Sun-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Guyard-Guillot died in the ambulance on the way to the hospital.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Variety_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-Variety-5">[5]</a></sup>  She was pronounced dead at 11:43 pm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LV_Sun_4-6"><a href="#cite_note-LV_Sun-4">[4]</a></sup>  Guyard-Guillot's death was the first on-stage accident resulting in death in Cirque du Soleil's 30-year history.  She is survived by her two children. She was 31 at the time of her death.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USA_Today_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-USA_Today-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 2, 2013, the Clark County Coroner's office said Guyard-Guillot fell around 90 feet (27 m), which is almost twice the distance reported initially. She died of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blunt_force_trauma" title="Blunt force trauma">blunt force trauma</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reaction">Reaction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Death_of_Sarah_Guyard-Guillot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reaction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>An official statement by Cirque du Soleil read, "The entire Cirque du Soleil family is deeply saddened by the accidental death of Sarah (Sasoun) Guyard ... Our thoughts are with her family and the entire Cirque du Soleil family."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LV_Sun_4-7"><a href="#cite_note-LV_Sun-4">[4]</a></sup>  Company founder <a href="/wiki/Guy_Lalibert%C3%A9" title="Guy Laliberté">Guy Laliberté</a> said he was "heartbroken", and remarked, "We are all completely devastated with this news ... [Guyard-Guillot] has been an integral part of our Cirque du Soleil tight family. We are reminded, with great humility and respect, how extraordinary our artists are each and every night."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USA_Today_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-USA_Today-2">[2]</a></sup>  A memorial website  was quickly flooded with condolences.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LV_Sun_4-8"><a href="#cite_note-LV_Sun-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Performances of <i>Kà</i> were put on hold indefinitely.  On July 14, 2013, it was announced that performances of the show (in a modified form) would resume on July 16, 2013, and that a full schedule would be resumed on July 23, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>  The first performance was dedicated to Guillot-Guyard, who was described as "an exceptional artist".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>  A formal investigation into Guyard-Guillot's death was launched by Nevada <a href="/wiki/Occupational_Safety_and_Health_Administration" title="Occupational Safety and Health Administration">OSHA</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LV_Sun_4-9"><a href="#cite_note-LV_Sun-4">[4]</a></sup>  John Fudenberg of the <a href="/wiki/Clark_County,_Nevada" title="Clark County, Nevada">Clark County</a> Coroner’s office, who worked with OSHA on the investigation, said, "There is certainly safety apparatus that is used during any Cirque du Soleil show, and I know that company is very safety-conscious. We have to make sure we inspect the safety harnesses ... Our priority is to find out how this happened."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Variety_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-Variety-5">[5]</a></sup>  The investigation was expected to take up to six months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p><p>On October 29, 2013, the <a href="/wiki/Occupational_Safety_and_Health_Administration" title="Occupational Safety and Health Administration">Nevada Occupational Safety and Health Administration</a> (NVOSHA) issued six citations totaling $25,000 in fines and penalties for Cirque du Soleil Nevada, and three citations totaling $7,000 for the MGM Grand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p>
