<p><b>Percival James "Perce" Wilson</b> (February 22, 1890 – September 20, 1936), sometimes shown as <b>Percy Wilson</b>, was an <a href="/wiki/American_football" title="American football">American football</a> player.  He was the <a href="/wiki/Quarterback" title="Quarterback">quarterback</a> for the <a href="/wiki/1920_Detroit_Heralds_season" title="1920 Detroit Heralds season">1920 Detroit Heralds</a> during the first regular season of the <a href="/wiki/National_Football_League" title="National Football League">National Football League</a> (then known as the American Professional Football Association).  He was one of the first Canadian-born players, and the first Canadian-born quarterback, to play in the NFL.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Perce_Wilson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Wilson was born in 1890 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lifford,_Ontario" title="Lifford, Ontario">Lifford, Ontario</a>, Canada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OLFP_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-OLFP-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-C00_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-C00-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Draft_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Draft-3">[3]</a></sup>  His father, James Wilson, was born in Canada to Irish immigrants. His mother, Anna, was also born in Canada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-C00_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-C00-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Wilson moved to Detroit as a young boy and attended Detroit's <a href="/wiki/Western_International_High_School" title="Western International High School">Western High School</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-C00_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-C00-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PFR_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-PFR-4">[4]</a></sup> By 1917, Wilson had become a naturalized United States citizen and was employed in the milk and creamery business in Detroit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Draft_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Draft-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_football">Professional football</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Perce_Wilson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Professional football">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1920, he played for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Detroit_Heralds" title="Detroit Heralds">Detroit Heralds</a> during the first regular season of the <a href="/wiki/National_Football_League" title="National Football League">National Football League</a> (then known as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_Professional_Football_Association" title="American Professional Football Association">American Professional Football Association</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PFR_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-PFR-4">[4]</a></sup>  The Heralds opened their season with a game against the <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Panthers" title="Cleveland Panthers">Cleveland Panthers</a>, played at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Navin_Field" title="Navin Field">Navin Field</a> on October 10, 1920. The Heralds won by a 40-14 score, and Wilson, the Heralds' starting quarterback, scored the team's fifth touchdown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DFP1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-DFP1-5">[5]</a></sup>  Two weeks later, Wilson appeared in the Heralds' game against the Columbus Panhandles as a substitute at the quarterback position.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>  On November 28, 1920, he played at the left halfback position for the Heralds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>Wilson was one of the first Canadian-born players, and the first Canadian-born quarterback, to play in the NFL.  The other three Canadian-born players to play in the NFL during its inaugural 1920 season were <a href="/wiki/Buck_MacDonald" title="Buck MacDonald">Buck MacDonald</a>, a guard, <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Hughitt" title="Tommy Hughitt">Tommy Hughitt</a>, a halfback, and Jim Bryant, a halfback.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_years">Later years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Perce_Wilson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Later years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Wilson and his brother, Ernie Wilson, also played basketball for the Detroit Rayls, a team that existed in the late 1910s and early 1920s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OLFP_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-OLFP-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Wilson died in 1936 at age 46.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
