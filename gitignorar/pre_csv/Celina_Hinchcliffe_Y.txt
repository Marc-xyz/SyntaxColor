<b>Celina Alexandra Hinchcliffe</b> (born 21 March 1976 in <a href="/wiki/Bracknell" title="Bracknell">Bracknell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Berkshire" title="Berkshire">Berkshire</a>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is an English television sports broadcaster, currently a reporter for <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celina_Hinchcliffe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The daughter of television producer <a href="/wiki/Philip_Hinchcliffe" title="Philip Hinchcliffe">Philip Hinchcliffe</a>, her uncle is the actor <a href="/wiki/Geoffrey_Whitehead" title="Geoffrey Whitehead">Geoffrey Whitehead</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Hinchcliffe was educated at the independent <a href="/wiki/Sir_William_Perkins%27s_School" title="Sir William Perkins's School">Sir William Perkins's School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Chertsey" title="Chertsey">Chertsey</a>, and then gained a first class degree in English Literature and Drama from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Birmingham_University" title="Birmingham University">University of Birmingham</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celina_Hinchcliffe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She initially worked with various theatre companies, and travelled.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="BBC">BBC</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celina_Hinchcliffe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: BBC">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Hinchcliffe got her first break in broadcasting in 2001 at <a href="/wiki/BBC_Southern_Counties_Radio" title="BBC Southern Counties Radio">BBC Southern Counties Radio</a>, reporting on the likes of <a href="/wiki/Crawley_Town_F.C." title="Crawley Town F.C.">Crawley Town</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lewes_F.C." title="Lewes F.C.">Lewes</a>. From there, she worked as a reporter on African Football for the <a href="/wiki/BBC_World_Service" title="BBC World Service">BBC World Service</a>, and then at <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_5_Live" title="BBC Radio 5 Live">BBC Radio 5 Live</a>.
</p><p>In the summer of 2002, she joined <a href="/wiki/BBC_News_(TV_channel)" title="BBC News (TV channel)">BBC News</a> and <a href="/wiki/BBC_World_News" title="BBC World News">BBC World News</a> as a sports presenter. In 2005, she fronted <a href="/wiki/BBC_Two" title="BBC Two">BBC Two</a>'s live coverage of <a href="/wiki/England_women%27s_national_football_team" title="England women's national football team">England</a>'s matches in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2005_UEFA_Women%27s_Championship" title="2005 UEFA Women's Championship">women's European football championship</a>. On 21 January 2006, she became the first woman to present <a href="/wiki/BBC_One" title="BBC One">BBC One</a>'s Saturday lunchtime show <i><a href="/wiki/Football_Focus" title="Football Focus">Football Focus</a></i>. In 2005 she was the first woman to present Match of the Day. In 2006, she presented <a href="/wiki/BBC_Three" title="BBC Three">BBC Three</a>'s coverage of some England's qualifiers for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FIFA_Women%27s_World_Cup_2007" title="FIFA Women's World Cup 2007">FIFA Women's World Cup 2007</a>. In 2008, she also appeared on the <i><a href="/wiki/BBC_News_Summary" title="BBC News Summary">BBC 8pm News summary</a></i>, while usual presenter <a href="/wiki/Ellie_Crisell" title="Ellie Crisell">Ellie Crisell</a> was on maternity leave. In light of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BBC_Sports" title="BBC Sports">BBC Sports</a> presentation moving to <a href="/wiki/MediaCityUK" title="MediaCityUK">MediaCityUK</a> in <a href="/wiki/City_of_Salford" title="City of Salford">Salford</a>, Hinchcliffe made her final on-air presentation on the BBC on 21 February 2012, presented with her favourite chocolate cake by the floor manager.
</p><p>As a freelancer, she covers the French Open tennis for ITV alongside John Inverdale and presents the sport on Sky News.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="SkySports">SkySports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celina_Hinchcliffe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: SkySports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>She joined <a href="/wiki/Sky_News" title="Sky News">Sky News</a> in March 2012, and began presenting sports bulletins on <a href="/wiki/Sky_News" title="Sky News">Sky News</a> from January 2013.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Celina_Hinchcliffe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hinchcliffe married in May 2009, and gave birth to a son the following year.  She suffered from post-natal depression but recovered after receiving counselling.  She is a patron of the Cedar House Support Group which seeks to assist people experiencing post-natal depression.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>  She had her second son in 2013.
</p><p>She lists her interests as film, theatre, and live music. Hinchcliffe is also a passionate supporter of <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_Wednesday_F.C." title="Sheffield Wednesday F.C.">Sheffield Wednesday</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
