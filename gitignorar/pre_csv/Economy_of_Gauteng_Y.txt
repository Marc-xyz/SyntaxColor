width:1.5em; height:1.5em; margin:1px 0; border:1px solid black; background-color: #0000FF; color:black; font-size:100%; text-align:center;"> </span> <a href="/wiki/Free_State_(province)" title="Free State (province)">Free State</a> (5.5%)</div>
</div>
</div></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Gauteng" title="Gauteng">Gauteng</a> Province's total GDP for 2010 was <a href="/wiki/South_African_rand" title="South African rand">R</a>811 billion, making the province the single largest contributor to South Africa's GDP with a contribution of 33.8%,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GautengGDP_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-GautengGDP-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GautengGDP2_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-GautengGDP2-7">[7]</a></sup> despite having only 1.4% of South Africa's land area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GautengGDP3_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-GautengGDP3-8">[8]</a></sup> Gauteng also generates approximately 10% of the entire African continent's GDP.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GautengGDP3_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-GautengGDP3-8">[8]</a></sup> Gauteng's <a href="/wiki/Gini_coefficient" title="Gini coefficient">Gini coefficient</a> of 0.62 makes it more equal than South Africa (the Gini coefficient of which is 0.75<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-elsenburg_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-elsenburg-2">[2]</a></sup>) as a whole, although this is still a very high figure by international standards. The cities <a href="/wiki/Johannesburg" title="Johannesburg">Johannesburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Midrand" title="Midrand">Midrand</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pretoria" title="Pretoria">Pretoria</a>, which are all economic powerhouses, and <a href="/wiki/Vanderbijlpark" title="Vanderbijlpark">Vanderbijlpark</a>, which is an industrial powerhouse,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vdbpark1_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-vdbpark1-9">[9]</a></sup> are all in Gauteng.
</p><p>Gauteng is home to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/JSE_Securities_Exchange" title="JSE Securities Exchange">Johannesburg Stock Exchange</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JSEinJozi_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-JSEinJozi-10">[10]</a></sup> the largest stock exchange in Africa, as well as the head offices of over 140 local and international banks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GautengGDP_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-GautengGDP-1">[1]</a></sup> Some of the largest companies in Africa and abroad are based in Gauteng, or have offices and branches there, such as <a href="/wiki/Vodacom" title="Vodacom">Vodacom</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VodacomHistory_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-VodacomHistory-11">[11]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/MTN_Group" title="MTN Group">MTN</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MTNInfo_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-MTNInfo-12">[12]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Neotel" title="Neotel">Neotel</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NeotelInfo_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-NeotelInfo-13">[13]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft South Africa</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MicrosoftZA_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-MicrosoftZA-14">[14]</a></sup> and the largest <a href="/wiki/Porsche" title="Porsche">Porsche</a> Centre in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PorscheZA_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-PorscheZA-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Economy_of_Gauteng&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Mineral_Revolution" title="Mineral Revolution">Mineral Revolution</a> and <a href="/wiki/Witwatersrand_Gold_Rush" title="Witwatersrand Gold Rush">Witwatersrand Gold Rush</a></div>
<p>Gauteng was, for all intents and purposes, established with the establishment of administratively orientated Pretoria in 1855 and the establishment of Johannesburg thereafter in 1886<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JHBFoundingDate_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-JHBFoundingDate-16">[16]</a></sup> as a gold mining town. The economy of the Western Cape and Cape Town was dominant in South Africa until the mid 19th century. The establishment of Gauteng via Pretoria and Johannesburg ended this dominance for the Western Cape as economic and political power now shifted to the Gauteng region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMaban_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-AMaban-17">[17]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Imports_and_exports">Imports and exports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Economy_of_Gauteng&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Imports and exports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From 2002 to 2009, Gauteng's share of national imports and exports shares grew from 58.1% and 57% respectively to 59% and 66.7%, which is indicative of the high economic activity within the Gauteng region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GautengOnlineEconomicOverview_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-GautengOnlineEconomicOverview-18">[18]</a></sup> In 2009, Gauteng's total imports amounted to R316 billion,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GautengOnlineEconomicOverview2_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-GautengOnlineEconomicOverview2-19">[19]</a></sup> while exports amounted to R337,6 billion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GautengOnlineEconomicOverview3_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-GautengOnlineEconomicOverview3-20">[20]</a></sup> The most imported commodity to Gauteng for 2009 was, by far, machinery and electrical equipment, which took up over one third of the province's imports.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GautengOnlineEconomicOverview4_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-GautengOnlineEconomicOverview4-21">[21]</a></sup> The most exported commodity from Gauteng for 2009 was <a href="/wiki/Gemstone" title="Gemstone">precious or semi-precious stones</a> and <a href="/wiki/Precious_metal" title="Precious metal">metals</a>, comprising 35.39% of Gauteng's total exports.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GautengOnlineEconomicOverview3_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-GautengOnlineEconomicOverview3-20">[20]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Imports">Imports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Economy_of_Gauteng&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Imports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Gauteng's chief imports include electrical equipment and machinery, mineral products, transporting equipment and other such industrial goods. Most of the imports come from Asia and Europe, namely from China, Japan, <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, Germany, the United Kingdom, France, Italy and <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GautengOnlineEconomicOverview2_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-GautengOnlineEconomicOverview2-19">[19]</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Exports">Exports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Economy_of_Gauteng&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Exports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Gauteng's chief exports are precious minerals, base metals and mineral products, altogether accounting for 61% of its total export share.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GautengOnlineEconomicOverview3_20-2"><a href="#cite_note-GautengOnlineEconomicOverview3-20">[20]</a></sup> Gauteng exports its goods mostly to Asia and Europe, to countries like China, Japan, India, Germany, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a> and the United Kingdom. However, a significant amount of goods is also exported to the United States of America and to other African countries, such as <a href="/wiki/Zimbabwe" title="Zimbabwe">Zimbabwe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mozambique" title="Mozambique">Mozambique</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zambia" title="Zambia">Zambia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GautengOnlineEconomicOverview5_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-GautengOnlineEconomicOverview5-22">[22]</a></sup>
</p>
