<p><i><b>Tenkai Knights</b></i><span style="font-weight: normal"> (<a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>: <span lang="ja">テンカイナイト</span><span style="display:none">,</span> <a href="/wiki/Hepburn_romanization" title="Hepburn romanization">Hepburn</a>: <i>Tenkai Naito</i>)</span> is a 2013 <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canadian</a>-<a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> anime series, produced by <a href="/wiki/Shogakukan-Shueisha_Productions" title="Shogakukan-Shueisha Productions">Shogakukan-Shueisha Productions</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spin_Master" title="Spin Master">Spin Master</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tenkai_Knights_Coming_to_Cartoon_Network_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tenkai_Knights_Coming_to_Cartoon_Network-1">[1]</a></sup> and animated by <a href="/wiki/Bones_(studio)" title="Bones (studio)">Bones</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bones_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bones-2">[2]</a></sup> and Shogakukan Music &amp; Digital Entertainment. It officially aired in <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network" title="Cartoon Network">Cartoon Network</a> in the United States on August 24, 2013 and in Canada on <a href="/wiki/Teletoon" title="Teletoon">Teletoon</a> on September 28, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tenkai_Knights_Coming_to_Cartoon_Network_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Tenkai_Knights_Coming_to_Cartoon_Network-1">[1]</a></sup> After a year, the series premiered in <a href="/wiki/TV_Tokyo" title="TV Tokyo">TV Tokyo</a> and its Japanese affiliates on April 5, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crunchyroll_cast_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-crunchyroll_cast-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Tenkai Knights</i> is released to promote Spin Master's line of <a href="/wiki/Lego_clone" title="Lego clone">Ionix</a> brand of construction bricks.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Story">Story</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tenkai_Knights&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Story">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Eons ago, a cube–like peaceful planet named Quarton, populated by shape–shifting living bricks called Tenkai, was ravaged by a war between two opposite factions: the Corekai Soldiers, led by the valiant and fearless Commander Beagle ("Beag"); and the Corrupted Army, led by the ruthless Lord Vilius, who desired to obtain the source of Tenkai energy for his own purposes. However, the only ones who were strong enough to defeat him were the  4 Legendary Tenkai Knights of Quarton: Bravenwolf, Tributon, Valorn and Lydendor. Facing defeat, Vilius unleashed the mighty Tenkai Dragon, who was later defeated by the 4 Knights, its fragments scattered across the planet. These heroes were never seen again, but now, a year later, Lord Vilius and the infamous Corrupted Army have returned, more powerful than ever before.
</p><p>Now, on planet Earth, in the year 2034, within Benham City, four young teenage boys Guren, Ceylan, Toxsa, and Chooki find an interdimensional portal to Quarton where they are chosen by Boreas of the Guardians to become the new generation of Tenkai Knights and prevent Lord Vilius and his Corrupted Army empire from taking control of both worlds.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media">Media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tenkai_Knights&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Animation">Animation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tenkai_Knights&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Animation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Tenkai_Knights_episodes" title="List of Tenkai Knights episodes">List of Tenkai Knights episodes</a></div>
<p>The series is first aired in the United States on the <a href="/wiki/Cable_television" title="Cable television">cable TV</a> channel <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network" title="Cartoon Network">Cartoon Network</a> on August 24, 2013, at 7:30 am, and in Canada on <a href="/wiki/Teletoon" title="Teletoon">Teletoon</a> on September 28, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The English dub is produced by <a href="/wiki/Studiopolis" title="Studiopolis">Studiopolis</a>. To coincide with the launch of the property, Spin Master and Kickstart Entertainment produced a 10-episode prequel web-series entitled <i>Tenkai Knights Origins</i>. The series features music by John Majkut and was released onto <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> as well as the brand's official website between September and December 2013. They also aired as <a href="/wiki/Interstitial_program" title="Interstitial program">interstitals</a> on Cartoon Network and Teletoon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>In Japan, the television series was delayed until April, where it would be aired on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TV_Tokyo_Network" title="TV Tokyo Network">TV Tokyo Network</a>, with the start of its broadcast on April 5, 2014, running at 9:00 am. It would then replace Shogakukan's Oha Coliseum block and <i><a href="/wiki/Robocar_Poli" title="Robocar Poli">Robocar Poli</a></i> that currently air in the half-hour slot. Several of the affiliates would have the show delayed and begin broadcast on April 12, 2014, airing at 7:00 am. BS-Japan would further delay its broadcast until April 15, 2014, and begin broadcasting at 5:30 pm.
</p><p>A second season of the series was announced to be in development but was cancelled prior to entering production.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Toys">Toys</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tenkai_Knights&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Toys">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Tenkai Knights toy line is produced by Spin Master under the <i>Ionix</i> brand of construction bricks, along with a new <a href="/wiki/Pok%C3%A9mon" title="Pokémon">Pokémon</a> line. Spin Master refers to Ionix as the next generation of construction, with "Bricks that shapeshift." Their line of products include bricks that look very similar to traditional construction bricks, but actually shapeshift and unfold into unique mini-figures. Spin Master distributes the toys in the United States and Canada while <a href="/wiki/Happinet" title="Happinet">Happinet</a> handles the distribution rights of the toys in Japan.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Print">Print</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tenkai_Knights&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Print">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> adaptation of the anime series by <a href="/wiki/Ryo_Takamisaki" title="Ryo Takamisaki">Ryo Takamisaki</a> was serialized in <a href="/wiki/CoroCoro_Comic" title="CoroCoro Comic">CoroCoro Ichiban!</a> beginning with its June 2014 issue, published April 21, 2014. The manga concluded shortly after and was later compiled into two <a href="/wiki/Tank%C5%8Dbon" title="Tankōbon">tankōbon</a> by <a href="/wiki/Shogakukan" title="Shogakukan">Shogakukan</a>, the last of which, released on March 27, 2015, featured an additional 20 pages of material.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The series was not released in English.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Penguin_Random_House" title="Penguin Random House">Penguin Random House</a> published four <a href="/wiki/Novelization" title="Novelization">novelizations</a> written by <a href="/wiki/Brandon_T._Snider" title="Brandon T. Snider">Brandon T. Snider</a>, as well as two <a href="/wiki/Gamebook" title="Gamebook">choose your own adventure books</a> by Ray Santos in 2014 and 2015. iStoryTime released an interactive book on <a href="/wiki/IOS" title="IOS">iOS</a> in January 2014.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Home_video">Home video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tenkai_Knights&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Home video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Phase_4_Films" title="Phase 4 Films">Phase 4 Films</a> distributed a single home video release of the series in Canada and the United States, titled <i>Tenkai Knights: Rise of the Knights</i>. It presents the first several episodes of the show edited together into a movie and was released on <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> as well as digital platforms on February 4, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> A <a href="/wiki/Manufacture_on_demand" title="Manufacture on demand">manufacture on demand</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blu-Ray" title="Blu-Ray">Blu-Ray</a> version followed on May 4, 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p><p>In Japan, Happinet and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Victor_Entertainment" title="Victor Entertainment">Victor Entertainment</a> released the entire series over four DVD box sets between August 2, 2014, and May 2, 2015. The first 18 episodes were also released through six DVDs for the rental market.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tenkai_Knights&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Four pieces of theme music are used for the episodes: one opening theme and three closing themes. The opening theme is "Get the Glory" by Ayako Nakanomori. The first ending theme is "Shunkan Diamond"<span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">瞬間Diamond</span><span style="display:none">,</span> <i>Shunkan Diamond</i>, lit. "Diamond Moment")</span> by <a href="/wiki/Rurika_Yokoyama" title="Rurika Yokoyama">Rurika Yokoyama</a>, while the second ending is "Shōri no Hanataba o -gonna gonna be hot!-"<span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">勝利の花束を-gonna gonna be hot!-</span><span style="display:none">,</span> <i>lit. "Victory Bouquet -gonna gonna be hot!-"</i>)</span> by <a href="/wiki/Cyntia" title="Cyntia">Cyntia</a>. The third ending, "Densetsu no FLARE"<span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">伝説のFLARE -</span><span style="display:none">,</span> <i>lit. "Legendary Flare"</i>)</span>, is sung by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pile_(voice_actress)" title="Pile (voice actress)">Pile</a>. For the English version, John Majkut composed its opening theme with an instrumental version of the same track used for the credits. The series' background score is composed by Majkut and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=MONACA&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="MONACA (page does not exist)">MONACA</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Driver" title="Star Driver">Star Driver</a></i> fame.
</p><p>Happinet released an 82-song soundtrack on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Compact_Disc" title="Compact Disc">CD</a> on March 25, 2015, in Japan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_games">Video games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tenkai_Knights&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Video games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Tenkai_Knights:_Brave_Battle" title="Tenkai Knights: Brave Battle">Tenkai Knights: Brave Battle</a></div>
<p>On January 30, 2014, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Namco_Bandai_Games" title="Namco Bandai Games">Namco Bandai Games</a> announced a video game entitled <i>Tenkai Knights: Brave Battle</i><span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">テンカイナイト ブレイブバトル</span><span style="display:none">,</span> <i>Tenkai Naito Bureibu Batoru</i>)</span> for <a href="/wiki/Nintendo_3DS" title="Nintendo 3DS">Nintendo 3DS</a>, released in Japan on September 25, 2014, and in North America on October 7, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p><p>On February 27, 2014, Spin Master and Game Pill launched the <a href="/wiki/Free-to-play" title="Free-to-play">free-to-play</a> <i>Tenkai Knights: Battle for Quarton</i> game for <a href="/wiki/IOS" title="IOS">iOS</a> devices.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The game was also available through <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Web_browsers" title="Web browsers">web browsers</a> via the <a href="/wiki/Unity_(game_engine)" title="Unity (game engine)">Unity</a> plug-in. Other browser titles include <i>Bravenwolf's Run</i>, <i>Race to Redemption</i> and <i>Tenkai Boost</i>.
</p>
