	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Theodore Henry Broadhead</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Theodore Henry Broadhead</b> (3 December 1767 – 12 December 1820) was an English army officer and politician.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HoP_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-HoP-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Theodore_Henry_Broadhead&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The son of Theodore Henry Broadhead the elder, whose original surname was <b>Brinckman</b>, and his wife Mary Bingley, he was educated at <a href="/wiki/Eton_College" title="Eton College">Eton College</a>, and matriculated at <a href="/wiki/Emmanuel_College,_Cambridge" title="Emmanuel College, Cambridge">Emmanuel College, Cambridge</a> in 1784, graduating in B.A. 1788, and M.A.in 1791.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HoP_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-HoP-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He became a cornet in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1st_Life_Guards" title="1st Life Guards">1st Life Guards</a> in 1790, and continued in a number of militia posts. From 1807 he lived in <a href="/wiki/Windsor,_Berkshire" title="Windsor, Berkshire">Windsor</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HoP_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-HoP-1">[1]</a></sup> His residence, "Holly Grove", had been designed by <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Sandby" title="Thomas Sandby">Thomas Sandby</a>. It was later known as "Forest Lodge", and was absorbed into <a href="/wiki/Windsor_Great_Park" title="Windsor Great Park">Windsor Great Park</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Holly_Grove_House.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="282" data-file-width="429" decoding="async" height="145" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/Holly_Grove_House.jpg/220px-Holly_Grove_House.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/Holly_Grove_House.jpg/330px-Holly_Grove_House.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/01/Holly_Grove_House.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Holly_Grove_House.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Holly Grove House, residence of Theodore Henry Broadhead in Windsor</div></div></div>
<p>Broadhead entered politics as Member of Parliament for <a href="/wiki/Wareham_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Wareham (UK Parliament constituency)">Wareham</a> in 1812, holding the seat until 1818. He is not known to have contributed to debates. He returned as M.P. for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yarmouth,_Isle_of_Wight_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Yarmouth, Isle of Wight (UK Parliament constituency)">Yarmouth, Isle of Wight</a> in 1820, the year of his death.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Theodore_Henry_Broadhead&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Brinckmans were from <a href="/wiki/Hanover" title="Hanover">Hanover</a>, and Theodore Brinckman who moved to Great Britain in the time of George I was grandfather to Theodore Henry Broadhead the elder (1714–1810).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Debrett_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Debrett-4">[4]</a></sup> The family became landowners in Yorkshire when John Richard Brinckman, father of Theodore Henry the elder, married Anne Bingley, heiress to the Broadhead and Bingley estates.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>Broadhead married in 1797 Elizabeth Macdougall, daughter of William Gordon Macdougall of St. George's Hanover Square; they had eight sons and four daughters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HoP_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-HoP-1">[1]</a></sup> Macdougall is also given as from <a href="/wiki/Saint_Croix" title="Saint Croix">Saint Croix</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Debrett_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Debrett-4">[4]</a></sup> The witnesses were W. P. Georges, Junior, i.e. William Payne Georges II,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Frances Lavington, i.e. the wife of <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Payne,_1st_Baron_Lavington" title="Ralph Payne, 1st Baron Lavington">Ralph Payne, 1st Baron Lavington</a>, and John Dashwood King, i.e. <a href="/wiki/Sir_John_Dashwood-King,_4th_Baronet" title="Sir John Dashwood-King, 4th Baronet">Sir John Dashwood-King, 4th Baronet</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>The eight sons were:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Burke1865_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Burke1865-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Sir_Theodore_Brinckman,_1st_Baronet" title="Sir Theodore Brinckman, 1st Baronet">Theodore Henry Lavington</a></li>
<li>John Richard (born 1798), of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/60th_Rifles" title="60th Rifles">60th Rifles</a></li>
<li>Brinckman (born 1800), of the <a href="/wiki/Coldstream_Guards" title="Coldstream Guards">Coldstream Guards</a></li>
<li>William Augustus (born 1802), of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/7th_Hussars" title="7th Hussars">7th Hussars</a>, married in 1828 Louisa Augusta Paget, daughter of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sir_Charles_Paget" title="Sir Charles Paget">Sir Charles Paget</a></li>
<li>Bingley (1803–1851), of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/80th_Foot" title="80th Foot">80th Foot</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
<li>Henry R.N. (born 1806), commanded <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=HMS_Lynx_(1833)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="HMS Lynx (1833) (page does not exist)">HMS Lynx (1833)</a> active against the African slave trade.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
<li>George, cleric (1807–1870), rector of <a href="/wiki/West_Wycombe" title="West Wycombe">West Wycombe</a> from 1845<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li>Alfred (1808–1857), entered <a href="/wiki/Lincoln%27s_Inn" title="Lincoln's Inn">Lincoln's Inn</a> in 1831<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li></ul>
<p>In 1842 the first three brothers were able to change surname to Brinckman, by royal license.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p><p>The four daughters were:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Burke1865_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Burke1865-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
