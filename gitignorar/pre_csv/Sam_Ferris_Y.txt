<p><b>Samuel "Sam" Ferris</b> (29 August 1900 – 21 March 1980) was a <a href="/wiki/British_nationality_law" title="British nationality law">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Long-distance_running" title="Long-distance running">long-distance running</a> athlete who competed mainly in the <a href="/wiki/Marathon" title="Marathon">marathon</a>. He was born in Magherabeg, near <a href="/wiki/Dromore,_County_Down" title="Dromore, County Down">Dromore</a>, <a href="/wiki/County_Down" title="County Down">County Down</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>. He won a silver medal for <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> in the marathon at the <a href="/wiki/1932_Summer_Olympics" title="1932 Summer Olympics">1932 Summer Olympics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sports-reference_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-sports-reference-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>He served as the second president of the then recently formed <a href="/wiki/Road_Runners_Club_(UK)" title="Road Runners Club (UK)">UK Road Runners Club</a> during 1954.
</p>
