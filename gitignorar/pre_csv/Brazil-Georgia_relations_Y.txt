<p><b>Brazil-Georgia relations</b> describes relations between the governments of the two countries. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diplomatic_relations" title="Diplomatic relations">Diplomatic relations</a> between <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Federative Republic of Brazil</a> were established on April 28, 1993. An Inter-parliamentary Group of Friendship Brazil-Georgia was also established.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2015 visa-free travel was implemented between Georgia and Brazil.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The agreement was the result of coordinated political and logistical diplomacy and negotiation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2017, Brazil’s Ambassador to Georgia, H.E. Cicero Martins Garcia, stated that Brazil exported $250 million to Georgia, while Georgia exported only $1.3 million to Brazil, creating an imbalance that should be diplomatically negotiated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He stated that political dialogue had been "very good".
</p>
