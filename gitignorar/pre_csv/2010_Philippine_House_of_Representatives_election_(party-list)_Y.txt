<p>The <b>2010 <a href="/wiki/House_of_Representatives_of_the_Philippines" title="House of Representatives of the Philippines">House of Representatives</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> party-list election</b> was on May 10, 2010. The whole country was one <a href="/wiki/At-large" title="At-large">at-large</a> district, where parties <a href="/wiki/Closed_list" title="Closed list">nominate three persons to be their candidates, ranked in order of which they'll be seated</a> if elected. The elected representatives will serve in the <a href="/wiki/15th_Congress_of_the_Philippines" title="15th Congress of the Philippines">15th Congress</a>.
</p><p>Candidates from the district elections are not allowed to be nominated by the parties participating in the party-list election, nor are parties who have candidates in the district elections may be allowed to join the party-list election; the parties in the party-list election must represent a distinct "sector" in the society such as women, laborers and the like.
</p><p>In the election, the voter elects the <i>party</i>, not the nominees of the party; a voter may not be able to influence how the nominees are listed on election day, except for joining the party beforehand. If the party surpasses 2% of the national vote, the person first nominated by the party will be seated. Additional seats can be won by multiplying the percentage of the votes the party got, with the difference of number of seats the party already has (1), and the number of seats allocated for sectoral organizations (57), disregarding decimals, with no party getting more than two additional seats. However, usually only the party with the most votes gets the two additional seats, the other parties will get only one additional seat. If the number of seated representatives is less than the 20% quota of party-list representatives in Congress (57 for the 15th Congress), the unfilled seats will be filled up by the remaining parties in descending order of votes garnered until are seats are filled up. Note that with more than a hundred parties participating, and with the three-seat cap, the seats that the parties with 2% of the vote or more will always be less than the 20% allocation.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Philippine_House_of_Representatives_election_(party-list)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prior to the election, the <a href="/wiki/Commission_on_Elections_(Philippines)" title="Commission on Elections (Philippines)">Commission on Elections</a> (COMELEC) expected an increase of party-list organizations seeking accreditation from the 153 organizations that applied for accreditation during the 2007 elections.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The COMELEC also de-listed 25 party-list organizations for either failing to participate in the last two elections or did not obtain two percent of the votes cast. However, the COMELEC said the two percent vote requirement does not cover the organizations that won a congressional seat based on a recent Supreme Court ruling.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Militant groups Migrante and <a href="/wiki/Sanlakas" title="Sanlakas">Sanlakas</a> protested such de-listment, saying that they were not covered by the 2% rule, and that such rule is unconstitutional. Another 8 such organizations also filed a motion for reconsideration or a verified opposition against their de-listment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>After purging the party-list roster, the COMELEC then acted upon the organizations that filed their manifestations of intent to run. <a href="/wiki/LGBT" title="LGBT">LGBT</a> group <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ang_Ladlad" title="Ang Ladlad">Ang Ladlad</a> appealed their disqualification after the COMELEC's First Division disqualified the organization due to "moral grounds," citing the <a href="/wiki/Bible" title="Bible">Bible</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qur%27an" title="Qur'an">Qur'an</a>. Party leader <a href="/wiki/Danton_Remoto" title="Danton Remoto">Danton Remoto</a>, a professor at <a href="/wiki/Ateneo_de_Manila_University" title="Ateneo de Manila University">Ateneo de Manila University</a>, cited the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Declaration_on_Human_Rights" title="Universal Declaration on Human Rights">Universal Declaration on Human Rights</a> and the <a href="/wiki/International_Covenant_on_Civil_and_Political_Rights" title="International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights">International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights</a> as reasons why Ang Ladlad should be re-instated. Remoto would then appeal to the commission <i><a href="/wiki/En_banc" title="En banc">en banc</a>.</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The COMELEC also disqualified <a href="/wiki/Alliance_of_Concerned_Teachers" title="Alliance of Concerned Teachers">Alliance of Concerned Teachers</a> (ACT) and government employees union Courage. The commission's First Division disqualified ACT after it had failed to prove that it exists "in most of the regions;" the commission said that, Courage on the other hand, "exists in <a href="/wiki/Western_Visayas" title="Western Visayas">Western Visayas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Davao_del_Sur" title="Davao del Sur">Davao del Sur</a>, a town in <a href="/wiki/Lanao_del_Norte" title="Lanao del Norte">Lanao del Norte</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rizal" title="Rizal">Rizal</a> and some cities in <a href="/wiki/Metro_Manila" title="Metro Manila">Metro Manila</a>," and that it failed to prove that it represents a "marginalized and under-represented sector."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>In December, the commission <i>en banc</i> denied Ang Ladlad's motion for reconsideration with finality as Remoto said he would elevate the case to the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_the_Philippines" title="Supreme Court of the Philippines">Supreme Court</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In January 2010, the Supreme Court issued a temporary restraining order to the COMELEC on disqualifying Ang Ladlad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>With the COMELEC prevented from disqualifying Ang Ladlad, the organization along with 143 others were included in the final list of accredited party-list organizations, although the status may change if the court sides with COMELEC on the issue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Under Resolution 8745, six additional party-list groups were accredited, bringing the total to 150.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>The party-list election has been hit by allegations that several parties are fronts by the ruling administration. <a href="/wiki/Bagong_Alyansang_Makabayan" title="Bagong Alyansang Makabayan">Bagong Alyansang Makabayan</a> (BAYAN), a coalition of left-leaning party-lists, and election watchdog <a href="/wiki/Kontra_Daya" title="Kontra Daya">Kontra Daya</a> said that nine party-lists were connected to the Arroyo political family. The parties and their first nominee cited were:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ang_Galing_Pinoy" title="Ang Galing Pinoy">Ang Galing Pinoy</a>, a party that claims to represents <a href="/wiki/Security_guard" title="Security guard">security guards</a>: outgoing second district of Pampanga congressman <a href="/wiki/Mikey_Arroyo" title="Mikey Arroyo">Mikey Arroyo</a>. Arroyo, the son of president Gloria Macapagal Arroyo willingly dropped his intention to run in Pampanga's 2nd district to let his mother run.</li>
<li>Transport group <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=1-UTAK&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="1-UTAK (page does not exist)">1-UTAK</a>: <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Energy_(Philippines)" title="Department of Energy (Philippines)">Secretary of Energy</a> <a href="/wiki/Angelo_Reyes" title="Angelo Reyes">Angelo Reyes</a></li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pilipino_Association_for_Country_%E2%80%93_Urban_Poor_Youth_Advancement_and_Welfare&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Pilipino Association for Country – Urban Poor Youth Advancement and Welfare (page does not exist)">Pilipino Association for Country – Urban Poor Youth Advancement and Welfare</a> (PACYAW), which claims to advocate sports development in the country: former <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Tourism_(Philippines)" title="Department of Tourism (Philippines)">Tourism assistant secretary</a> Janet Lazatin as its first nominee</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kabalikat_ng_Mamamayan" title="Kabalikat ng Mamamayan">Kabalikat ng Mamamayan</a> (KABAYAN)</li></ul>
<p>The administration distanced itself from the six pro-government parties insisting it has not endorsed any group supposedly to augment the administration's representation in Congress, saying that "it is up to the COMELEC to judge and issue a decision on the matter."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p><p>On April 8, 2010, the Supreme Court ruled that Ang Ladlad was allowed to run in the 2010 election,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> saying with a unanimous vote that what is immoral is not necessary illegal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p>

</p><h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Philippine_House_of_Representatives_election_(party-list)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On July 11, 2010, the Commission on Elections (COMELEC) disqualified two nominees of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kasangga_sa_Kaunlaran&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kasangga sa Kaunlaran (page does not exist)">Kasangga sa Kaunlaran</a> namely <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Teodoro_Haresco&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Teodoro Haresco (page does not exist)">Teodoro Haresco</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Eugenio_Lacson&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Eugenio Lacson (page does not exist)">Eugenio Lacson</a> on proving they do not support the marginalized sectors. <a href="/wiki/Bagong_Alyansang_Makabayan" title="Bagong Alyansang Makabayan">Bagong Alyansang Makabayan</a> (BAYAN) secretary-general Renato Reyes says that "this should now serve as a benchmark for other pending petitions against party-list nominees who do not belong to nor represent the marginalized sectors."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 20, 2010, the COMELEC allowed Rep. Arroyo to sit as the representative for Ang Galing Pinoy after he was accepted as the first nominee of the party-list group. The Commission voted 4–2 while one abstained.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The COMELEC also proclaimed three more parties as winners: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Advocacy_for_Teacher_Empowerment_Through_Action,_Cooperation_and_Harmony_Towards_Educational_Reforms" title="Advocacy for Teacher Empowerment Through Action, Cooperation and Harmony Towards Educational Reforms">A TEACHER</a>, <a href="/wiki/Butil_Farmers_Party" title="Butil Farmers Party">Butil Farmers Party</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=1-UTAK&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="1-UTAK (page does not exist)">1-UTAK</a>, although the latter's first nominee, <a href="/wiki/Angelo_Reyes" title="Angelo Reyes">Angelo Reyes</a>, was not allowed to take office as he has a pending disqualification case.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup>
</p><p>While the commission upheld <a href="/wiki/Angelo_Reyes" title="Angelo Reyes">Angelo Reyes</a> nomination by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=1-UTAK&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="1-UTAK (page does not exist)">1-UTAK</a>, on late July, the party withdrew Reyes from their list after the commission's decision was appealed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Reyes, who was prohibited from having his seat at the House of Representatives, appealed to the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_the_Philippines" title="Supreme Court of the Philippines">Supreme Court</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> With the suicide of Reyes, the Supreme Court dismissed Reyes' petition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Supreme Court dismissed petition filed by the <a href="/wiki/Alliance_for_Barangay_Concerns" title="Alliance for Barangay Concerns">Alliance for Barangay Concerns</a> (ABC) on their disqualification by the commission, on March 22. The commission's decision, in which ABC was disqualified for being a front of the <a href="/wiki/Members_Church_of_God_International" title="Members Church of God International">Members Church of God International</a>, a religious group that produces the <i><a href="/wiki/Ang_Dating_Daan" title="Ang Dating Daan">Ang Dating Daan</a></i> television program, was upheld as it stated that the constitution gave the commission the power to register and cancel the registration of party-list groups. The commission originally dismissed the petition filed by lawyer and journalist <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Melanio_Mauricio,_Jr.&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Melanio Mauricio, Jr. (page does not exist)">Melanio Mauricio, Jr.</a> in 2010, but was reconsidered on August 2010; ABC has already won a seat in Congress but has not been sworn in. ABC, through its chairman James Marty Lim, argued that since the party had already won a seat, the jurisdiction should be under the House of Representatives Electoral Tribunal. However, the court ruled that commission maintains jurisdiction as the case does not refer to qualifications of <i>members</i> of Congress.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup>
</p>
