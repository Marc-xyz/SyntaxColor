<p><b>Hernar</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">island</a> in <a href="/wiki/%C3%98ygarden" title="Øygarden">Øygarden</a> municipality in <a href="/wiki/Hordaland" title="Hordaland">Hordaland</a> county, <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>.  It is one of the northernmost islands in the Øygarden <a href="/wiki/Archipelago" title="Archipelago">archipelago</a>.  The 0.5-square-kilometre (120-acre) island is rocky and barren.  Most of the settlement is in one cluster on the southwestern part of the island along the small harbor area.  The eastern third of the island is uninhabited.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>It sits in a cluster of mostly uninhabited islands, about 3.5 kilometres (2.2 mi) north of the island of <a href="/wiki/Sel%C3%B8yna" title="Seløyna">Seløyna</a>, the northernmost large, populated island in Øygarden which is connected to the mainland by a series of bridges.  Hernar has about 15 permanent inhabitants and many <a href="/wiki/Holiday_cottage" title="Holiday cottage">holiday cabins</a>. It is connected to the mainland via a passenger ferry service to the small island of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Helles%C3%B8yna&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hellesøyna (page does not exist)">Hellesøyna</a>, just off the coast of Seløyna.  The ferry service is operated by <a href="/wiki/Bergen_Nordhordland_Rutelag" title="Bergen Nordhordland Rutelag">Bergen Nordhordland Rutelag</a>.
</p>
