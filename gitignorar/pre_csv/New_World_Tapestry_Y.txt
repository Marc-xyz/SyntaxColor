	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">New World Tapestry</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>New World Tapestry</b> was for a time the largest stitched <a href="/wiki/Embroidery" title="Embroidery">embroidery</a> in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Parsons_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Parsons-1">[1]</a></sup> It depicts <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_England" title="Kingdom of England">English</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_colonisation_of_the_Americas" title="British colonisation of the Americas">colonisation</a> in <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guyana" title="Guyana">Guyanas</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a> between the years 1583 and 1642, when the <a href="/wiki/English_Civil_War" title="English Civil War">English Civil War</a> began.
</p><p>Work began on the tapestry in 1980 and continued for twenty years. The tapestry was at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Empire_%26_Commonwealth_Museum" title="British Empire &amp; Commonwealth Museum">British Empire &amp; Commonwealth Museum</a> until its closure in 2013. It is now in the collection of the <a href="/wiki/Bristol_City_Museum_and_Art_Gallery" title="Bristol City Museum and Art Gallery">Bristol City Museum and Art Gallery</a> in storage.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_panels">The panels</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_World_Tapestry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: The panels">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The New World Tapestry, which in its entirety measures 267 ft × 4 ft (81.4 m × 1.2 m), consists of twenty-four panels,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Move_to_Bristol_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Move_to_Bristol-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Visit_Britain_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Visit_Britain-3">[3]</a></sup> each of which depicts the <a href="/wiki/Narrative" title="Narrative">narrative</a> of a particular phase in the period between 1583 and 1642.
</p><p>Each panel measures 11 ft × 4 ft (3.4 m × 1.2 m).
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r886047036">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>The figures of the <a href="/wiki/Tapestry" title="Tapestry">tapestry</a> are rendered in an unmistakably modern, <a href="/wiki/Cartoon" title="Cartoon">cartoon</a>-like style, but it also follows in the tradition of <a href="/wiki/Tudor_period" title="Tudor period">Tudor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jacobean_era" title="Jacobean era">Jacobean</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canvas_work" title="Canvas work">canvas work</a> embroidery. The panels are worked in <a href="/wiki/Gobelin" title="Gobelin">gobelin</a> stitch which entirely covers the ground, and along with pictures of the main scenes of the story, the panels also feature birds, animals, flowers and insects all beautifully worked in bold colours.</p><div class="templatequotecite">— <cite>"Bristol and the New World", <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aztec" title="Aztec">Aztecs</a> at MexicoLore <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MexicoLore_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-MexicoLore-4">[4]</a></sup></cite></div>
</blockquote>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_creation_of_the_tapestry">The creation of the tapestry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_World_Tapestry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The creation of the tapestry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The designer was Tom Mor,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-City_London_anniversary_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-City_London_anniversary-5">[5]</a></sup> who also designed the Plymouth Tapestry at <a href="/wiki/Prysten_House" title="Prysten House">Prysten House</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Frommers_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Frommers-6">[6]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Plymouth" title="Plymouth">Plymouth</a>, the Adventurers for Virginia (<a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>) Tapestry,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-City_London_anniversary_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-City_London_anniversary-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HNN_anniversary_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-HNN_anniversary-7">[7]</a></sup>  and was the consultant on the <a href="/wiki/History_of_Jersey#20th_century" title="History of Jersey">Jersey</a> Liberation Tapestry (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St_Helier" title="St Helier">St Helier</a>, <a href="/wiki/Channel_Islands" title="Channel Islands">Channel Islands</a>) and the Plympton Tapestry (<a href="/wiki/Plympton" title="Plympton">Plympton</a>, Devon). The panel was researched by Tom Mor, Tom Maddock, Paul Presswell and Freda Simpson. Chief tapissiers were Joan Roncarelli and Renée Harvey. A New World Tapestry Website has been developed as of December 2008 and will soon include 120 pages, showing all complete panels.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OfficialWebsite_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-OfficialWebsite-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p>Research for the New World Tapestry's twenty-four panels began in 1980. Tom Mor was joined by Tom Maddock, a retired friend from <a href="/wiki/Ivybridge" title="Ivybridge">Ivybridge</a>. Over the months they travelled hundreds of miles together, researching the two hundred sixty four people who would be named on the tapestry. <a href="/wiki/Heraldry" title="Heraldry">Heraldic</a> expert Paul Presswell of <a href="/wiki/Buckfastleigh" title="Buckfastleigh">Buckfastleigh</a> identified all the <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">coats of arms</a> of the people, colleges and companies involved. The result has been the creation alongside the tapestry of a <a href="/wiki/Library" title="Library">library</a> of files on each person and a collection of <a href="/wiki/Reference_work" title="Reference work">reference books</a> of great use to <a href="/wiki/Research" title="Research">researchers</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academia" title="Academia">scholars</a> and <a href="/wiki/Teacher" title="Teacher">teachers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-City_London_anniversary_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-City_London_anniversary-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>Two hundred sixty four <a href="/wiki/Escutcheon_(heraldry)" title="Escutcheon (heraldry)">armorial shields</a> run along the top and bottom tapestry borders throughout its length, alternating with illustrations of the same number of flowers of herbs, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Herbalism" title="Herbalism">medicinal plants</a>, trees and shrubs. The latter are shown because the colonists took ointments and cure-alls with them on their voyages and plant hunters returned with such things as the <a href="/wiki/Potato" title="Potato">potato</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tobacco" title="Tobacco">tobacco</a>.
</p><p>All the flowers and florets depicted were drawn from nature by Tom Mor, who studied them under a <a href="/wiki/Watchmaker" title="Watchmaker">watchmaker</a>'s glass. He was helped from the very early days by Freda Simpson of Plymouth, who was passionately interested in herbs and old herbal remedies. She identified and gave him over 230 flower <a href="/wiki/Biological_specimen" title="Biological specimen">specimens</a> in the years that Mor lived with his wife and family in <a href="/wiki/Plymouth" title="Plymouth">Plymouth</a>. Later they moved to <a href="/wiki/Cambridge" title="Cambridge">Cambridge</a> where he was able to complete the set of 264 drawings with the help of Clive King and Caroline Lawes of the <a href="/wiki/Cambridge_University_Botanic_Garden" title="Cambridge University Botanic Garden">Cambridge University Botanic Garden</a>, Lady Jane Renfrew of <a href="/wiki/Lucy_Cavendish_College,_Cambridge" title="Lucy Cavendish College, Cambridge">Lucy Cavendish College</a> and Alison Davies, Monica Stokes and Edna Norman.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_stitchers">The stitchers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_World_Tapestry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The stitchers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Tom Mor could not have seen his canvasses brought to life without the help of his friends and the expertise of the dedicated tapissiers. When the very first stitch was made in the New World Tapestry in 1980, the team working in Prysten House numbered twenty. By the time the last stitch was made in March 2000, the number of tapissiers had increased to two hundred fifty-six with the addition of another eight centres. In <a href="/wiki/Devon" title="Devon">Devon</a> there was a second in Plymouth at <a href="/wiki/HMNB_Devonport" title="HMNB Devonport">HMS <i>Drake</i></a> (the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a>'s panel), <a href="/wiki/Ivybridge" title="Ivybridge">Ivybridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chillington,_Devon" title="Chillington, Devon">Chillington</a>, <a href="/wiki/Exeter" title="Exeter">Exeter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bideford" title="Bideford">Bideford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Totnes" title="Totnes">Totnes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tiverton,_Devon" title="Tiverton, Devon">Tiverton</a> Castle. <a href="/wiki/Dorset" title="Dorset">Dorset</a>'s Tapestry centre was in the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Guildhall,_Lyme_Regis&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Guildhall, Lyme Regis (page does not exist)">Guildhall</a> at <a href="/wiki/Lyme_Regis" title="Lyme Regis">Lyme Regis</a> and it was there that the Great Gardeners and Herbalists panel was stitched.
</p><p>The first <a href="/wiki/Gobelin_stitch" title="Gobelin stitch">oblique Gobelin stitch</a> was made on 26 September 1980 in Prysten House<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Frommers_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Frommers-6">[6]</a></sup>  in Plymouth, by U.S. Ambassador the Hon. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kingman_Brewster" title="Kingman Brewster">Kingman Brewster</a>. The coat of arms of His Excellency's ancestor, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pilgrim_(Plymouth_Colony)" title="Pilgrim (Plymouth Colony)">Pilgrim</a> leader <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_Brewster_(pilgrim)" title="William Brewster (pilgrim)">William Brewster</a>, appears on the 1620 <i><a href="/wiki/Mayflower" title="Mayflower">Mayflower</a></i> Panel.
</p><p>The last oblique Gobelin stitch was made by <a href="/wiki/Charles,_Prince_of_Wales" title="Charles, Prince of Wales">The Prince of Wales</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-City_London_anniversary_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-City_London_anniversary-5">[5]</a></sup> on 3 March 2000 in the Orchard Room of his home at <a href="/wiki/Highgrove_House" title="Highgrove House">Highgrove House</a> in <a href="/wiki/Gloucestershire" title="Gloucestershire">Gloucestershire</a>. Most fittingly, with his interest in history and a keen gardener himself, the Prince put his golden wool stitch in the date of the 1642 Great Gardeners and Herbalists' Panel.
</p><p>Stitches have also been added by <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">The Queen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Queen_Elizabeth_The_Queen_Mother" title="Queen Elizabeth The Queen Mother">Queen Elizabeth The Queen Mother</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prince_Philip,_Duke_of_Edinburgh" title="Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh">The Duke of Edinburgh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anne,_Princess_Royal" title="Anne, Princess Royal">The Princess Royal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Birgitte,_Duchess_of_Gloucester" title="Birgitte, Duchess of Gloucester">The Duchess of Gloucester</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Racism_controversy">Racism controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_World_Tapestry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Racism controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2017, representatives of the <a href="/wiki/National_Congress_of_American_Indians" title="National Congress of American Indians">National Congress of American Indians</a>, who had previously been unaware of the tapestry project, issued statements to the effect that the final product was racist in its depiction of <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_of_the_Americas" title="Indigenous peoples of the Americas">Native American</a> people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>   "It shamelessly perpetuates a centuries-long artistic tradition that seeks to portray Native people as subhuman, warlike savages,” according to Jacqueline Pata, the executive director of National Congress of American Indians.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Staff_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Staff-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>"What I wanted to avoid was the picture of English people coming ashore from their galleon to the New World as peaceful and quiet. It wasn’t so," Mor told The Telegraph. "It's no use pretending about it or being shocked by it. Mine is a cartoon, but it is a reality. I tell it as it is and I tell it with humour."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Staff_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-Staff-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Library">The Library</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_World_Tapestry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The Library">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The New World Tapestry Library<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-City_London_anniversary_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-City_London_anniversary-5">[5]</a></sup> material includes histories of the years 1583-1642, much of it original research, files on the two hundred sixty four people named on the tapestry, plus <a href="/wiki/Heraldry" title="Heraldry">heraldic</a> information on over three hundred individuals, companies, towns, counties and universities.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Supporters">Supporters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=New_World_Tapestry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Supporters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Supporters of the New World Tapestry include the Adventurers for Virginia<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Casing_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Casing-11">[11]</a></sup> patrons of the New World Tapestry and Library. Their names are inscribed for posterity in the Adventurers for Virginia Record Book. Supporters who join the Adventurers for Virginia may also:
</p>
<ul><li>In London, celebrate the Adventurers for Virginia Day every 10 April to commemorate the granting of <a href="/wiki/Royal_charter" title="Royal charter">royal charters</a> by <a href="/wiki/James_II_of_England" title="James II of England">James II of England</a> to the <a href="/wiki/London_Company" title="London Company">London Company</a> and the Plymouth Adventurers (<a href="/wiki/Plymouth_Company" title="Plymouth Company">Plymouth Company</a>) in 1606 to establish colonies in Virginia</li>
<li>Help fund the production of the book, <i>The <a href="/wiki/Jamestown,_Virginia" title="Jamestown, Virginia">Jamestown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kennebec_River" title="Kennebec River">Sagadahoc</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a> Story</i>, for schools and researchers.</li>
<li>Help fund the production of the <a href="/wiki/George_Yeardley" title="George Yeardley">Yeardley</a>/<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_River_plantations" title="James River plantations">Flowerdew</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brasses" title="Brasses">Brasses</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Flowerdew_brasses_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Flowerdew_brasses-12">[12]</a></sup>  for presentation in England and America.</li>
<li>Help make grants to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Empire_%26_Commonwealth_Museum" title="British Empire &amp; Commonwealth Museum">British Empire &amp; Commonwealth Museum</a> at <a href="/wiki/Bristol_Temple_Meads_railway_station" title="Bristol Temple Meads railway station">Bristol Temple Meads railway station</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bristol" title="Bristol">Bristol</a> to create the permanent exhibition of the New World Tapestry, expand and enhance the New World Tapestry Library and help the development of three-way educational research between <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">Americas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a>.</li>
<li>Receive a tie showing the Adventurers' badge plus a lifetime pass to view the tapestry at the museum in Bristol.</li></ul>
