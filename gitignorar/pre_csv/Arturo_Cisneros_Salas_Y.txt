	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Arturo Cisneros Salas</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Arthuro Cisneros Salas</b> (born 28 November 1978) is a former Mexican footballer who had stints in the <a href="/wiki/V.League_1" title="V.League 1">V-League</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ascenso_MX" title="Ascenso MX">Ascenso MX</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Soccer_League" title="Canadian Soccer League">Canadian Soccer League</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Playing_career">Playing career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arturo_Cisneros_Salas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Playing career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Salas began his career in his native city with <a href="/wiki/Guerreros_Acapulco" title="Guerreros Acapulco">Guerreros Acapulco</a>, where he played for five years, and served as the team's captain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In 2002, he went abroad to Canada to sign with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Montreal_Dynamites" title="Montreal Dynamites">Montreal Dynamites</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadian_Professional_Soccer_League_(1998-2005)" title="Canadian Professional Soccer League (1998-2005)">Canadian Professional Soccer League</a>. During his tenure with Montreal he helped the club finish second in the Eastern Conference, and reached the semi-finals in the playoffs. In 2003, Salas went overseas to Asia to sign with <a href="/wiki/Ti%E1%BB%81n_Giang_F.C." title="Tiền Giang F.C.">Tiền Giang F.C.</a>, and the following year he signed with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ho_Chi_Minh_City_F.C." title="Ho Chi Minh City F.C.">Ho Chi Minh City F.C.</a>. In 2005, he signed with <a href="/wiki/Atlante_F.C." title="Atlante F.C.">Atlante F.C.</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Ascenso_MX" title="Ascenso MX">Ascenso MX</a>. On 26 March 2006 he returned to Canada to sign with the <a href="/wiki/Laval_Dynamites" title="Laval Dynamites">Laval Dynamites</a> for the 2006 CSL season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He helped Laval finish third in the National Division, and featured in the quarterfinal match against <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Croatia" title="Toronto Croatia">Toronto Croatia</a>, which resulted in a 1-0 victory for Toronto.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
