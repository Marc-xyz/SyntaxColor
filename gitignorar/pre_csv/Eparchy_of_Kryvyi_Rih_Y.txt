<p><b>Kryvyi Rih and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nikopol,_Dnipropetrovsk_Oblast" title="Nikopol, Dnipropetrovsk Oblast">Nikopol</a> diocese</b> - <a href="/wiki/Eparchy" title="Eparchy">Eparchy</a> of the Ukrainian Orthodox Church (Moscow Patriarchate) centered in Krivoy Rog, Ukraine; includes the western districts of Dnepropetrovsk region. Cathedral city - <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Krivoy_Rog" title="Krivoy Rog">Krivoy Rog</a>. Cathedral - Spaso-Preobrazhensky (Krivoy Rog), Transfiguration (Nikopol).
</p><p>Founded on July 27, 1996 by decision of the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Synod_of_the_Russian_Orthodox_Church" title="Holy Synod of the Russian Orthodox Church">Holy Synod</a> of the Ukrainian Orthodox Church of the release of the Dnepropetrovsk diocese. Decision of the Holy Synod on September 12, 1996 by Bishop Krivoy Rog and Nikopol was defined as Archimandrite Ephraim, inhabitants of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kiev-Pechersk_Lavra" title="Kiev-Pechersk Lavra">Kiev-Pechersk Lavra</a>.
</p><p>For ten years, the number of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parishes" title="Parishes">parishes</a> in the territory of the diocese increased from 85 to 224, and <a href="/wiki/Clergy" title="Clergy">clergy</a> from 98 to 202.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Churches">Churches</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eparchy_of_Kryvyi_Rih&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Churches">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The oldest churches in the cathedral city of Kryvyi Rih diocese are:
</p>
<ul><li>church of St. Nicolas in Kryvyi Rih (1761);</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_church_of_the_Nativity_of_the_All-holy_Theotokos_(Kryvyi_Rih)" title="The church of the Nativity of the All-holy Theotokos (Kryvyi Rih)">church of the Nativity of the Theotokos</a> (1886);</li>
<li>church of the Protection of the Most Blessed Virgin Mary (1888);</li>
<li>church of the Ascension of the Lord (1904).</li></ul>
