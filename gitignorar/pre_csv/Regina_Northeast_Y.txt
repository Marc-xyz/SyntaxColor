<p><b>Regina Northeast</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Provinces_and_territories_of_Canada" title="Provinces and territories of Canada">provincial</a> <a href="/wiki/Electoral_district_(Canada)" title="Electoral district (Canada)">electoral district</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Legislative_Assembly_of_Saskatchewan" title="Legislative Assembly of Saskatchewan">Legislative Assembly</a> of <a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan" title="Saskatchewan">Saskatchewan</a>, Canada. This district includes the neighbourhoods of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Parkridge&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Parkridge (page does not exist)">Parkridge</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Uplands,_Regina&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Uplands, Regina (page does not exist)">Uplands</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Glencairn,_Regina&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Glencairn, Regina (page does not exist)">Glencairn</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Glencairn_Village&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Glencairn Village (page does not exist)">Glencairn Village</a>.
</p><p>Between <a href="/wiki/1991_Saskatchewan_general_election" title="1991 Saskatchewan general election">1991</a> and <a href="/wiki/1995_Saskatchewan_general_election" title="1995 Saskatchewan general election">1995</a>, this riding was called "Regina Churchill Downs".
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Kevin_Doherty_(politician)" title="Kevin Doherty (politician)">Kevin Doherty</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan_Party" title="Saskatchewan Party">Saskatchewan Party</a> won the riding in the general elections of 2011 and 2016, but announced his retirement from politics in the spring of 2018 and resigned the seat.  A <a href="/wiki/By-election" title="By-election">by-election</a> was held on September 12, 2018, and was won by <a href="/wiki/Yens_Pedersen" title="Yens Pedersen">Yens Pedersen</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan_New_Democratic_Party" title="Saskatchewan New Democratic Party">New Democratic Party</a>.  Gary Grewal was the candidate for the Saskatchewan Party.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
