<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">For the San Diego Trolley station, see <a href="/wiki/Barrio_Logan_station" title="Barrio Logan station">Barrio Logan station</a>.</div><p><b>Barrio Logan</b> is a neighborhood in south central <a href="/wiki/San_Diego" title="San Diego">San Diego</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>. It is bordered by the neighborhoods of <a href="/wiki/East_Village,_San_Diego" title="East Village, San Diego">East Village</a> and <a href="/wiki/Logan_Heights,_San_Diego" title="Logan Heights, San Diego">Logan Heights</a> to the north, <a href="/wiki/Shelltown,_San_Diego" title="Shelltown, San Diego">Shelltown</a> and <a href="/wiki/Southcrest,_San_Diego" title="Southcrest, San Diego">Southcrest</a> to the east, <a href="/wiki/San_Diego_Bay" title="San Diego Bay">San Diego Bay</a> to the southwest, and <a href="/wiki/National_City,_California" title="National City, California">National City</a> to the southeast. <a href="/wiki/Interstate_5_in_California" title="Interstate 5 in California">Interstate 5</a> forms the northeastern boundary.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The Barrio Logan Community Plan Area comprises approximately 1,000 acres, of which slightly more than half is under the jurisdiction of the <a href="/wiki/Port_of_San_Diego" title="Port of San Diego">Port of San Diego</a> or the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a> rather than the city of San Diego.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-plan_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-plan-2">[2]</a></sup> The community is subject to the California Coastal Act.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barrio_Logan,_San_Diego&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>When the Spanish first entered the region, they found a Kumeyaay rancheria at the mouth of <a href="/wiki/Chollas_Creek" title="Chollas Creek">Chollas Creek</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBrian_F._Smith_and_Associates201117,_20_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBrian_F._Smith_and_Associates201117,_20-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1871, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._House_of_Representatives" title="U.S. House of Representatives">Congressman</a> <a href="/wiki/John_A._Logan" title="John A. Logan">John A. Logan</a> wrote legislation to provide federal land grants and subsidies for a transcontinental railroad ending in San Diego.  A street laid in 1881 was named Logan Heights after him, and the name came to be applied to the general area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Plans for a railroad never successfully materialized, and the area was predominantly residential by the turn of the century, becoming one of San Diego's oldest communities.  Its transformation began in 1910 with the influx of refugees from the <a href="/wiki/Mexican_Revolution" title="Mexican Revolution">Mexican Revolution</a>, who soon became the majority ethnic group.  For this reason, the southern part of the original Logan Heights neighborhood came to be called Barrio Logan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> (<i><a href="/wiki/Barrio" title="Barrio">Barrio</a></i> is a Spanish word for "neighborhood".)
</p><p>The area was originally residential with access to the beach at <a href="/wiki/San_Diego_Bay" title="San Diego Bay">San Diego Bay</a>. During World War II this beach access was lost due to the expansion of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Naval_Station_San_Diego" title="Naval Station San Diego">Naval Station San Diego</a> and other military facilities on the waterfront. The neighborhood continued to degrade during the 1950s and 1960s due to rezoning that permitted industrial uses, the construction of <a href="/wiki/Interstate_5" title="Interstate 5">Interstate 5</a> through the heart of the community in 1963, and the construction of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Diego-Coronado_Bridge" title="San Diego-Coronado Bridge">San Diego-Coronado Bridge</a> in 1969, which covered much of the community with a concrete "roof" supported by gray concrete pillars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The city council promised to build a community park under the bridge approaches, and a site was approved in June 1969. When construction began in April 1970 at the designated site, but the community learned that the work was intended to create a state building instead of a park, there was a nonviolent community uprising. Students and others occupied the site and forced a halt to the construction. The occupation of the site lasted twelve days. Residents planted landscaping, and a local artist, <a href="/wiki/Salvador_Torres" title="Salvador Torres">Salvador Torres</a>, proclaimed his vision of covering the freeway support pillars with murals. After intense negotiation between the city and the state (which owned the land in question), the site was reclaimed for park use, and Chicano Park was built and dedicated. It was expanded several times and in 1990 it was extended all the way to the bay, restoring beach access to the community.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p>The community contains many industrial areas, primarily shipbuilding and maritime uses, as well as many residences. In 2013 the neighborhood planning group drew up, and the City Council approved, a revised community plan which would have created a "buffer zone" of a commercial area separating residential uses from industrial uses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> However, the ship-building industry, which objected to the buffer zone, organized and led a petition drive to overturn the plan via citywide vote. In June 2014 the voters rejected the community plan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> A revised plan is in the draft stage as of 2015.
</p><p>Since 2015, during the same time as <a href="/wiki/San_Diego_Comic-Con" title="San Diego Comic-Con">San Diego Comic-Con</a> is occurring less than two miles away, there has been <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Chicano-Con&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Chicano-Con (page does not exist)">Chicano-Con</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chicano_Park">Chicano Park</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barrio_Logan,_San_Diego&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Chicano Park">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Barrio Logan, in southeast San Diego, is referred to as el ombligo or navel, the center of the world.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:272px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Chicano_Park_Mural.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="480" data-file-width="640" decoding="async" height="203" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Chicano_Park_Mural.JPG/270px-Chicano_Park_Mural.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Chicano_Park_Mural.JPG/405px-Chicano_Park_Mural.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Chicano_Park_Mural.JPG/540px-Chicano_Park_Mural.JPG 2x" width="270"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Chicano_Park_Mural.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Murals in Chicano Park</div></div></div>
<p>Barrio Logan is the home of <a href="/wiki/Chicano_Park" title="Chicano Park">Chicano Park</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Chicano" title="Chicano">Chicano</a>-themed public park created in large part by the local residents. It is located at the site of a 1970s demonstration, land takeover, and cultural renaissance for the Mexican-American community. It features more than 60 colorful murals painted on the concrete support piers for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Diego-Coronado_Bridge" title="San Diego-Coronado Bridge">San Diego-Coronado Bridge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Interstate_5" title="Interstate 5">Interstate 5</a>. It was designated an official historic site by the San Diego Historical Site Board in 1980 and listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 2013.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barrio_Logan,_San_Diego&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Barrio Logan is home to Naval Base San Diego, also known as 32nd Street Naval Station, as well as the <a href="/wiki/National_Steel_and_Shipbuilding_Company" title="National Steel and Shipbuilding Company">NASSCO</a> shipyard and other military-related facilities.
</p><p>The neighborhood is also home to artist studios and galleries and hosts periodic Art Crawl.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
The painting of murals in Chicano Park had been discussed as part of the total park development since 1967.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barrio_Logan,_San_Diego&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Barrio Logan is part of City Council District 8 represented by Councilman <a href="/wiki/David_Alvarez_(politician)" title="David Alvarez (politician)">David Alvarez</a>. The Barrio Logan Community Planning Group was established in October of 2014 when City Council gave approval at the recommendation of representative Alvarez. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>The Barrio Logan Community Planning Area was granted status as a redevelopment area,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> but the state of California abolished all redevelopment areas in 2012.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barrio_Logan,_San_Diego&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area is served by the <a href="/wiki/San_Diego_Trolley" title="San Diego Trolley">San Diego Trolley</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Barrio_Logan_station" title="Barrio Logan station">Barrio Logan station</a>.
</p>
