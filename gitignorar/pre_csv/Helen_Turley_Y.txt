	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Helen Turley</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>

<table class="infobox biography vcard" style="width:22em"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" style="text-align:center;font-size:125%;font-weight:bold"><div class="fn" style="display:inline">Helen Turley</div></th></tr><tr><th scope="row">Nationality</th><td class="category">American</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Alma mater</th><td><a href="/wiki/Cornell_University" title="Cornell University">Cornell University</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Occupation</th><td class="role">Winemaker, entrepreneur, wine consultant</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Known for</th><td><a href="/wiki/Cabernet_Sauvignon" title="Cabernet Sauvignon">Cabernet Sauvignon</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pinot_Noir" title="Pinot Noir">Pinot Noir</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Chardonnay" title="Chardonnay">Chardonnay</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row"><div style="white-space:nowrap;">Notable work</div></th><td>Marcassin Pinot Noir</td></tr><tr><th scope="row"><span class="nowrap">Spouse(s)</span></th><td>John Wetlaufer</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Awards</th><td><a href="/wiki/Wine_Spectator" title="Wine Spectator">Wine Spectator</a> Distinguished Service Award, 2010; <a href="/wiki/Food_%26_Wine" title="Food &amp; Wine">Food &amp; Wine</a> Achievement Winemaker of the Year, 1999</td></tr></tbody></table>
<p><b>Helen Turley</b> is a pioneering <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Winemaking" title="Winemaking">winemaker</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wine" title="Wine">wine</a> consultant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> She is known for bringing several <a href="/wiki/California_wine" title="California wine">Californian</a> <a href="/wiki/Cult_wine" title="Cult wine">cult wines</a> to the public awareness, and as the owner of a 20-acre (8.1 ha) boutique <a href="/wiki/Winery" title="Winery">winery</a>, Marcassin Vineyard.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> She is the recipient of several prestigious American wine awards, to include <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wine_Spectator_Magazine" title="Wine Spectator Magazine">Wine Spectator's</a> Distinguished Service Award and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Food_%26_Wine_Magazine" title="Food &amp; Wine Magazine">Food &amp; Wine's</a> Achievement Winemaker of the Year.
</p><p>Turley has mentored many California wine consultants, such as Mark Aubert, <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Melka" title="Philippe Melka">Philippe Melka</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Heidi_Barrett" title="Heidi Barrett">Heidi Barrett</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rpfw30_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-rpfw30-4">[4]</a></sup> In addition to consulting for wineries such as Pahlmeyer, Bryant Family Vineyard, <a href="/wiki/Colgin_Cellars" title="Colgin Cellars">Colgin</a>, Martinelli and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blankiet_estate" title="Blankiet estate">Blankiet</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rpfw30_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-rpfw30-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> until 1995, she was the winemaker for <a href="/wiki/Turley_Wine_Cellars" title="Turley Wine Cellars">Turley Wine Cellars</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wsht_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-wsht-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Helen_Turley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Turley graduated from <a href="/wiki/Cornell_University" title="Cornell University">Cornell's</a> agricultural school in the 1970s, and moved to the Napa Valley, landing at <a href="/wiki/Robert_Mondavi" title="Robert Mondavi">Robert Mondavi</a>'s lab.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup> By 1984 she had her first job as a winemaker but began thinking differently about winemaking when she made two barrels of wine using wild yeast in 1989.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>During her career, Turley evangelized a variety of winemaking methods that were unique for their time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-:1-8">[8]</a></sup> She was one of the first winemakers in California to use cold soaks and to focus on harvesting phenolically ripe fruit to as to make the wine as natural as possible.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-:1-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p>During the 1990s, Turley helped usher in the era of the superconsultant in California, taking startups such as Peter Michael, Colgin and Bryant Family to fame as her winegrowing regime pushed wine quality to new heights.  She gained renown helping many small wineries make exceptional wine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> "Don't get in the way of the wine," Turley drilled in her pupils and mentees; "If you've got the right vineyard—the right grapes—you can make great wine."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>At one point, Turley simultaneously made wines for Colgin, Bryant, Turley, Martinelli, Pahlmeyer, Green &amp; Red, Harrison, La Jota, Landmark and Canepa, as well her and husband John Wetlaufer’s own label, Marcassin. Some of her early triumphs came with the Napa Valley Cabernets of Bryant and Colgin, which shared her signature style of bold and extracted flavors underscored by finesse, polish and refinement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Turley is also credited with helping raise the quality of Chardonnay in both <a href="/wiki/Napa_County,_California" title="Napa County, California">Napa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sonoma_County,_California" title="Sonoma County, California">Sonoma</a>. With Marcassin, and the wines she made for Martinelli, Turley was instrumental in putting <a href="/wiki/Sonoma_Coast_AVA" title="Sonoma Coast AVA">Sonoma Coast</a> on the map.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lawsuits">Lawsuits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Helen_Turley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Lawsuits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2001, the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Roy_Estate&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Roy Estate (page does not exist)">Roy Estate</a> hired Turley and her husband, John Wetlaufer, to design their 17-acre (6.9 ha) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Napa_Valley_(wine)" title="Napa Valley (wine)">Napa Valley</a> vineyard and for Turley to serve as consulting winemaker. After making Roy Estate's 2003 &amp; 2004 vintages, Turley canceled the arrangement in March 2005. The following September, the owners of Roy Estate filed a lawsuit against Turley and Wetlaufer alleging breach of contract. The lawsuit was eventually settled out of court in May 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
