<p><i><b>The Very Merry Widows</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <i lang="fr"><b>Mariées mais pas trop</b></i>) is a 2003 Franco-Belgian film directed and co-written by <a href="/wiki/Catherine_Corsini" title="Catherine Corsini">Catherine Corsini</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Very_Merry_Widows&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film is a black comedy. Renée (<a href="/wiki/Jane_Birkin" title="Jane Birkin">Jane Birkin</a>) is a wealthy widow several times over. When her orphaned granddaughter Laurence (<a href="/wiki/%C3%89milie_Dequenne" title="Émilie Dequenne">Émilie Dequenne</a>) turns up looking for a place to stay, she gives the naïve young woman some instruction on marriage to the rich and terminal as a means of self-enrichment. After trying a couple of local men, Laurence sets her sights on the insurance agent investigating her grandmother's latest loss, Thomas (<a href="/wiki/J%C3%A9r%C3%A9mie_Elka%C3%AFm" title="Jérémie Elkaïm">Jérémie Elkaïm</a>). Renée herself, on the other hand, finds herself falling in love: with Maurice (<a href="/wiki/Pierre_Richard" title="Pierre Richard">Pierre Richard</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Very_Merry_Widows&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
