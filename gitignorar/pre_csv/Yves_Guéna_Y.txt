<p><b>Yves Guéna</b> (6 July 1922 – 3 March 2016) was a French politician. In 1940, he joined the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Free_French_Forces" title="Free French Forces">Free French Forces</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>. He received several decorations for his courage.
</p><p>He belonged to various right wing parties: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Union_pour_la_nouvelle_R%C3%A9publique" title="Union pour la nouvelle République">Union pour la nouvelle République</a> (1962–1968), the <a href="/wiki/Union_of_Democrats_for_the_Republic" title="Union of Democrats for the Republic">Union of Democrats for the Republic</a> (1968–1978) and the <a href="/wiki/Rally_for_the_Republic" title="Rally for the Republic">Rally for the Republic</a> (1978–1997).
</p><p>He occupied several posts as minister. In 1968, he was the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Information_Ministers_of_France" title="List of Information Ministers of France">minister of information</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He was a member of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parliament_of_France" title="Parliament of France">Parliament</a> between 1962 and 1981 and then again between 1986 and 1988. He was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Senate_of_France" title="Senate of France">senator</a> between 1989 and 1997.
</p><p>In 2000, Guéna was named president of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Constitutional_Council_of_France" title="Constitutional Council of France">Constitutional Council of France</a>. In 2004 he left to become president of the <a href="/wiki/Arab_World_Institute" title="Arab World Institute">Arab World Institute</a> until 2007.
</p>
