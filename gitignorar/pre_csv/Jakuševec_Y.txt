	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Jakuševec</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Jakuševec</b> (sometimes also <b>Jakuševac</b>) is a neighborhood in the <a href="/wiki/Novi_Zagreb_%E2%80%93_istok" title="Novi Zagreb – istok">Novi Zagreb – istok</a> city district of <a href="/wiki/Zagreb" title="Zagreb">Zagreb</a>, the capital of <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a>. Located on Sajmišna Road east of Sarajevska Road and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Utrine,_Zagreb" title="Utrine, Zagreb">Utrine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Travno" title="Travno">Travno</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dugave" title="Dugave">Dugave</a> apartment block complexes, the neighborhood is mostly uninhabited, with an area of 455.21 <a href="/wiki/Hectare" title="Hectare">ha</a> and a total population of around 2,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It is the site of the city's largest <a href="/wiki/Landfill" title="Landfill">landfill</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> the city's biggest <a href="/wiki/Used_car" title="Used car">used car</a> market<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> (referred to as "Hrelić" which is actually a separate settlement further south<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>) and the Zlatni Konj art gallery.
</p><p>The first records of inhabitants in Jakuševec area date back to 1334 as "Parochia Sancti Marci Ev. in campo" (English: <span lang="en">Parish of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Mark_the_Evangelist" title="St. Mark the Evangelist">St. Mark the Evangelist</a> in the field</span>), which is the name recorded up to 1501. At that time, the parish' name is changed to "Parocha Sancti Marci Ev. in spinis" (English: <span lang="en">Parish of St. Mark the Evangelist among the thorns</span>). However, during the leadership of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parish_priest" title="Parish priest">pastor</a> Josip Vitković, the parish is renamed in 1871 to "Parish of Jakuševec" (<a href="/wiki/Croatian_language" title="Croatian language">Croatian</a>: <i lang="hr">Jakuševečka župa</i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uzoj_jak_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-uzoj_jak-5">[5]</a></sup>
Jakuševec remained a standalone village until 1991 when it was integrated along with several adjacent villages into the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/City_of_Zagreb" title="City of Zagreb">City of Zagreb</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
