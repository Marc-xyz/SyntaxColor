<p><b>Saint Thomas Anglicans</b> (aka <b>Anglican Syrian Christians</b> or <b><a href="/wiki/Church_of_South_India" title="Church of South India">CSI</a> Syrian Christians</b>) are the <a href="/wiki/Saint_Thomas_Christians" title="Saint Thomas Christians">Saint Thomas Christian</a> members of the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_South_India" title="Church of South India">Church of South India</a>; the autonomous <a href="/wiki/South_India" title="South India">South Indian</a> province of the <a href="/wiki/Anglican_Communion" title="Anglican Communion">Anglican Communion</a>. They are among the several different ecclesiastical communities that splintered out of the once undivided <a href="/wiki/Saint_Thomas_Christians" title="Saint Thomas Christians">Saint Thomas Christians</a>; an ancient Christian community whose origins goes back to the first century missionary activities of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Thomas_the_Apostle" title="Saint Thomas the Apostle">Saint Thomas the Apostle</a>, in the present day South Indian state of <a href="/wiki/Kerala" title="Kerala">Kerala</a>. The Apostle, as legend has it, arrived in Malankara (derived from <a href="/wiki/Maliankara" title="Maliankara">Maliankara</a> near <a href="/wiki/Muziris" title="Muziris">Muziris</a>) in AD 52.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGregoriosRoberson2008285_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGregoriosRoberson2008285-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTENeill2002247&amp;ndash;249_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTENeill2002247&amp;ndash;249-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">Origins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Thomas_Anglicans&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In November 1795, a treaty of perpetual friendship and tributary alliance was signed between the Rajah of <a href="/wiki/Travancore" title="Travancore">Travancore</a> and the <a href="/wiki/East_India_Company" title="East India Company">East India Company</a>. The treaty was again modified in 1805, which established British paramountcy over Travancore.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEAiya1906389&amp;ndash;400_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAiya1906389&amp;ndash;400-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMenon1878225&amp;ndash;239_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMenon1878225&amp;ndash;239-6">[6]</a></sup> The beginning of the relationship between the Anglican <a href="/wiki/Church_Mission_Society" title="Church Mission Society">Church Mission Society</a> and the ancient <a href="/wiki/Malankara_Church" title="Malankara Church">Malankara Church</a> could be traced to the Rev R H Kerr and the Rev <a href="/wiki/Claudius_Buchanan" title="Claudius Buchanan">Claudius Buchanan</a>, who paid visits to the Malabar Syrians in 1806, during the episcopate of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mar_Dionysius_I" title="Mar Dionysius I">Mar Dionysius I</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETovey2015_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETovey2015-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-csimkd.net_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-csimkd.net-8">[8]</a></sup> The missionaries found the Malabar Syrian Christians in poor and depressed conditions. This is clear in the words of the Syrian Metropolitan <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mar_Dionysius_I" title="Mar Dionysius I">Mar Dionysius I</a>, in his interview with <a href="/wiki/Claudius_Buchanan" title="Claudius Buchanan">Claudius Buchanan</a>, recorded in Dr. Buchanan's famous book "Christian Researches in Asia";  in which <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mar_Dionysius_I" title="Mar Dionysius I">Mar Dionysius I</a> says, "you have come to visit a declining church".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBuchanan181198_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBuchanan181198-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTENeill2002247&amp;ndash;249_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTENeill2002247&amp;ndash;249-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBayly2004281&amp;ndash;286_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBayly2004281&amp;ndash;286-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1810, Colonel <a href="/wiki/John_Munro,_9th_of_Teaninich" title="John Munro, 9th of Teaninich">John Munro</a>, a man with deep Christian convictions became the British Resident of Travancore, an office he held for the next 10 years. Col Munro persuaded the <a href="/wiki/Rani" title="Rani">Rani</a> of Travancore, with whom he was in very good terms to donate land in <a href="/wiki/Kottayam" title="Kottayam">Kottayam</a> as well as the money and timber, in-order to build the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orthodox_Pazhaya_Seminary" title="Orthodox Pazhaya Seminary">Orthodox Pazhaya Seminary</a> (founded 1815) for the Malankara Church.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETovey2015_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETovey2015-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEVarma_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEVarma-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMcKee2018_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMcKee2018-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific page or range of pages in which the material appears. (July 2019)">page needed</span></a></i>]</sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTENeill2002241_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTENeill2002241-13">[13]</a></sup> He also petitioned the Anglican <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Church_Missionary_Society" title="Church Missionary Society">Church Missionary Society</a> to send missionaries on a Help Mission, to educate and train the clergy of the Malankara Church.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETovey2015_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETovey2015-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEVarma_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEVarma-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMcKee2018_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMcKee2018-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific page or range of pages in which the material appears. (July 2019)">page needed</span></a></i>]</sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTENeill2002241_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTENeill2002241-13">[13]</a></sup> In the coming years, several pious Christian men like <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Bailey_(missionary)" title="Benjamin Bailey (missionary)">Benjamin Bailey</a>, Joseph Fenn and Henry Baker (Sr) arrived in <a href="/wiki/Kottayam" title="Kottayam">Kottayam</a> and worked at the Pazhaya Seminary and among the Malankara Syrians. The missionaries took charge of the college as it early Principals for training the younger Malankara Church clergy and worked on the translation of the Holy Bible to the native language <a href="/wiki/Malayalam" title="Malayalam">Malayalam</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTENeill2002247&amp;ndash;249_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTENeill2002247&amp;ndash;249-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEChatterton1924_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEChatterton1924-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETovey2015_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETovey2015-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>However the cordial relations between the missionaries and the Malankara Syrians did not last very long. The younger missionaries who arrived later were uncompromising evangelists who insisted on major reforms to the Malankara Jacobite Church, which the changed Jacobite leadership didn't want. The discord and rifts eventually led to the 1836 Malankara Jacobite Synod of <a href="/wiki/Mavelikkara" title="Mavelikkara">Mavelikkara</a>, in which the Jacobite Syrian Community under Mar Dionysios IV, decided to keep all their Syriac ecclesiastical traditions and be subject to the authority of the Syriac Patriarch of Antioch.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTENeill2002247&amp;ndash;249_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTENeill2002247&amp;ndash;249-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEChatterton1924_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEChatterton1924-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMcKee2018_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMcKee2018-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific page or range of pages in which the material appears. (July 2019)">page needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Inevitably, the missionaries and the Malankara Jacobites parted ways and the missionaries continued their work on their own. Nevertheless, the two decades of their association and involvement left a profound and lasting impact on the Malankara Syrian community; calls for reformation were to come from within the Church, later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTENeill2002247&amp;ndash;249_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTENeill2002247&amp;ndash;249-3">[3]</a></sup> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-marthoma.in_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-marthoma.in-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1836, as soon as the missionaries separated from the Malankara Syrian Church, a minority from the Church who were in favor of the reformed ideologies of the missionaries sought membership in the <a href="/wiki/Anglicanism" title="Anglicanism">Anglican Church</a> and were admitted. These St. Thomas Anglicans were the first reformed group to emerge from the St. Thomas Christian community. Initially the Anglican Syrian community was concentrated in the areas of Travancore where the missionaries had earlier worked with the Malankara Syrians.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTENeill2002247&amp;ndash;249_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTENeill2002247&amp;ndash;249-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEChatterton1924_14-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEChatterton1924-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-csimkd.net_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-csimkd.net-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="British_Period">British Period</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Thomas_Anglicans&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: British Period">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>St. Thomas Anglicans benefited from the English education imparted by the missionaries and joined them in their work.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-csimkd.net_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-csimkd.net-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTENeill2002247&amp;ndash;249_3-6"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTENeill2002247&amp;ndash;249-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEChatterton1924_14-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEChatterton1924-14">[14]</a></sup> The missionaries were the pioneers who promoted mass education in <a href="/wiki/Travancore" title="Travancore">Travancore</a> and people from all sections of society joined the various schools and the <a href="/wiki/CMS_College_Kottayam" title="CMS College Kottayam">Cotym College</a> established by the <a href="/wiki/Church_Mission_Society" title="Church Mission Society">Church Mission Society</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETH2016a_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETH2016a-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECMS2016_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECMS2016-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMathew20157&amp;ndash;8_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMathew20157&amp;ndash;8-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERajasekharanKumar20035,19,25_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERajasekharanKumar20035,19,25-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMenon1996339,348,349_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMenon1996339,348,349-20">[20]</a></sup> They also started the <a href="/wiki/C.M.S._Press" title="C.M.S. Press">C.M.S. Press</a> (first printing press of Kerala) in 1821, in <a href="/wiki/Kottayam" title="Kottayam">Kottayam</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CMSatlasHin_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-CMSatlasHin-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERajasekharanKumar20035,19,25_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERajasekharanKumar20035,19,25-19">[19]</a></sup> Anglican Syrian Christians served as teachers in the educational institutions and worked in the other institutions established by the Church Mission Society, taking charge of them, later. Some worked along with the missionaries in their evangelical and reformative activities among the poor and backward communities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETH2016a_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTETH2016a-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECMS2016_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECMS2016-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMathew20157&amp;ndash;8_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMathew20157&amp;ndash;8-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERajasekharanKumar20035,19,25_19-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERajasekharanKumar20035,19,25-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMenon1996339,348,349_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMenon1996339,348,349-20">[20]</a></sup> Some served the Government. Several were ordained into priesthood in the Anglican Church and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anglican_Bishop_of_Travancore_and_Cochin" title="Anglican Bishop of Travancore and Cochin">Anglican Diocese of Travancore and Cochin</a> was established in 1879.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEChatterton1924_14-4"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEChatterton1924-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kerala_window_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kerala_window-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTENeill2002247&amp;ndash;249_3-7"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTENeill2002247&amp;ndash;249-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceB_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceB-24">[24]</a></sup> St. Thomas Anglicans provided the greater part of the leadership of the Anglican Diocese of Travancore and Cochin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kerala_window_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-Kerala_window-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTENeill2002247&amp;ndash;249_3-8"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTENeill2002247&amp;ndash;249-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation_of_the_Church_of_South_India">Formation of the Church of South India</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Thomas_Anglicans&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Formation of the Church of South India">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Church_of_South_India#Origins" title="Church of South India">Church of South India § Origins</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Church_of_South_India#Formation" title="Church of South India">Church of South India § Formation</a></div>
<p>During the extensive dialogues that preceded the formation of the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_South_India" title="Church of South India">Church of South India</a>, the Anglican party while accepting the ministries of all uniting denominations, argued for the introduction of an episcopate in <a href="/wiki/Apostolic_succession" title="Apostolic succession">historic succession</a> (from the <a href="/wiki/Historic_episcopate_(Anglican_views)" title="Historic episcopate (Anglican views)">Anglican Church</a>) into the envisioned United Church, by bestowing <a href="/wiki/Episcopal_polity" title="Episcopal polity">episcopal ordinations</a> upon all candidates to bishoprics drawn from non-episcopal traditions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWilliams1930_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWilliams1930-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific page or range of pages in which the material appears. (July 2019)">page needed</span></a></i>]</sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHood1935_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHood1935-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTELivingstone2006_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTELivingstone2006-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-biblehub.com_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-biblehub.com-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEDouglas1999_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDouglas1999-29">[29]</a></sup> They also insisted that all ordinations after the union should be exclusively episcopal, conferred only by existing <a href="/wiki/Bishop" title="Bishop">bishops</a> with the imposition of hands, so that in the fullness of time the entire ministry of the United Church would be in <a href="/wiki/Apostolic_succession" title="Apostolic succession">apostolic succession</a>. These were eventually accepted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWilliams1930_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWilliams1930-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific page or range of pages in which the material appears. (July 2019)">page needed</span></a></i>]</sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEHood1935_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHood1935-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTELivingstone2006_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTELivingstone2006-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-biblehub.com_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-biblehub.com-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEDouglas1999_29-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDouglas1999-29">[29]</a></sup> Accordingly on 27 September 1947, as part of the inauguration of the Church of South India, the presiding bishop Rt. Rev. Dr. <a href="/wiki/Cherakarottu_Korula_Jacob" title="Cherakarottu Korula Jacob">Cherakarottu Korula Jacob</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anglican_Bishop_of_Travancore_and_Cochin" title="Anglican Bishop of Travancore and Cochin">Anglican diocese of Travancore and Cochin</a>, along with other Anglican bishops and senior presbyters of the uniting denominations, vested all new candidates to bishoprics with episcopal ordinations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The Church of South India was thus realized and since then the <b>Anglican Syrian Christians</b> came to be known as <b><a href="/wiki/Church_of_South_India" title="Church of South India">CSI</a> Syrian Christians</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Anglican_Syrian_Christians_today">Anglican Syrian Christians today</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Thomas_Anglicans&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Anglican Syrian Christians today">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After acceding to the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_South_India" title="Church of South India">CSI</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anglican_Bishop_of_Travancore_and_Cochin" title="Anglican Bishop of Travancore and Cochin">Anglican Diocese of Travancore and Cochin</a> was renamed as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Madhya_Kerala_Diocese" title="Madhya Kerala Diocese">Madhya Kerala Diocese</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kerala_window_23-2"><a href="#cite_note-Kerala_window-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceB_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceB-24">[24]</a></sup> Although the majority of CSI Syrian Christians have their roots in this diocese, like other Saint Thomas Christian communities, many moved out of Kerala after Indian independence to other <a href="/wiki/States_and_union_territories_of_India" title="States and union territories of India">Indian states</a> and the rest of the world, starting new congregations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEChatterton1924_14-5"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEChatterton1924-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Many of these congregations are out side of the South Indian Anglican province and hence fall under the ecclesiastical jurisdiction of the respective provincial bishops, at least technically.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Although the cumulative number of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Thomas_Christians" title="Saint Thomas Christians">Syrian Christians</a> in the South Indian and other Anglican provinces is difficult to determine, it is roughly estimated that they constitute about 4% (approximately 152,000) of the 3.8 million members of the Church of South India.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Relations_with_other_Saint_Thomas_Christians">Relations with other Saint Thomas Christians</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Thomas_Anglicans&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Relations with other Saint Thomas Christians">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Church_of_South_India#Ecumenism" title="Church of South India">Church of South India § Ecumenism</a></div>
<p>Due to familial and social ties, Anglican Syrian Christians have always been in cordial relations with the <a href="/wiki/Malankara_Church" title="Malankara Church">Malankara Syrians</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Jacobite_Syrian_Christian_Church" title="Jacobite Syrian Christian Church">Jacobite</a> and <a href="/wiki/Malankara_Orthodox_Syrian_Church" title="Malankara Orthodox Syrian Church">Orthodox</a> factions that came of it. This continued due to the historic reality that for well over half a century since their split in 1836, the Malankara Church was the only Church that existed in <a href="/wiki/Travancore" title="Travancore">Travancore</a>-<a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Cochin" title="Kingdom of Cochin">Cochin</a> area, from which the Anglican Syrians could get partners in marriage, outside of their own community.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEChatterton1924_14-6"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEChatterton1924-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> By 1889, the reformists of the Malankara Church separated as an independent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reformed_Eastern_Christianity" title="Reformed Eastern Christianity">Reformed Eastern</a> denomination, choosing the name <a href="/wiki/Mar_Thoma_Syrian_Church" title="Mar Thoma Syrian Church">Mar Thoma Syrian Church</a> in 1898. Since then ecumenical ties have developed with them as well; the <a href="/wiki/Mar_Thoma_Syrian_Church" title="Mar Thoma Syrian Church">Mar Thoma Church</a> is in partnership and full communion with the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_South_India" title="Church of South India">Church of South India</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-marthoma.in_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-marthoma.in-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup>
</p>
