<p><b>Edwin L. Crawford</b> (April 10, 1925 – September 27, 1993) was an American politician, most notable for having served as <a href="/wiki/Broome_County,_New_York" title="Broome County, New York">Broome County, New York</a>'s first <a href="/wiki/County_executive" title="County executive">county executive</a>, and was "a leader in efforts to modernize county governments through the United States."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edwin_L._Crawford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Crawford was born in Broome County and served in the 17th Airborne Division in Europe during World War II. After the war, he graduated from <a href="/wiki/Cornell_University" title="Cornell University">Cornell University</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cornell_Law_School" title="Cornell Law School">Cornell Law School</a> and practiced law from 1950 to 1977.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>He was appointed the first county executive of Broome County in 1968<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and left office in 1976.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>  After retiring from politics, he served as executive director of the New York State Association of Counties.
</p><p>Crawford died of prostate cancer in 1993.  The Edwin L. Crawford County Office Building in downtown Binghamton is named after him<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> as is a memorial lecture at <a href="/wiki/Albany_Law_School" title="Albany Law School">Albany Law School</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
