<p>The <b>1945–46 Ranji Trophy</b> was the 12th season of the <a href="/wiki/Ranji_Trophy" title="Ranji Trophy">Ranji Trophy</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Holkar_cricket_team" title="Holkar cricket team">Holkar</a> won the title defeating <a href="/wiki/Baroda_cricket_team" title="Baroda cricket team">Baroda</a> in the final.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Highlights">Highlights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1945%E2%80%9346_Ranji_Trophy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Highlights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Holkar scored 912 for 8 decl against <a href="/wiki/Karnataka_cricket_team" title="Karnataka cricket team">Mysore</a> in the semifinal. This was the highest score in Indian first class cricket till <a href="/wiki/Hyderabad_cricket_team" title="Hyderabad cricket team">Hyderabad</a> made 944 for 6 against <a href="/wiki/Andhra_cricket_team" title="Andhra cricket team">Andhra</a> in <a href="/wiki/1993%E2%80%9394_Ranji_Trophy" title="1993–94 Ranji Trophy">1993-94</a></li></ul>
<dl><dd><ul><li>Six batsmen scored hundreds for Holkar - <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kamal_Bhandarkar&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kamal Bhandarkar (page does not exist)">Kamal Bhandarkar</a> 142, <a href="/wiki/Chandu_Sarwate" title="Chandu Sarwate">Chandu Sarwate</a> 101, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Madhav_Jagdale&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Madhav Jagdale (page does not exist)">Madhav Jagdale</a> 164, <a href="/wiki/C._K._Nayudu" title="C. K. Nayudu">C. K. Nayudu</a> 101, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bhausaheb_Nimbalkar" title="Bhausaheb Nimbalkar">Bhausaheb Nimbalkar</a> 172 and Pratap Singh 100. This has never been equalled.</li>
<li>There were seven century partnerships in the Holkar innings. All wickets from one to eight, except the second, made more than a hundred runs</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/B._K._Garudachar" title="B. K. Garudachar">B. K. Garudachar</a> took 4 wickets for 301 runs. This is the most expensive bowling in Indian cricket and third in all first class cricket.</li>
<li>Chandu Sarwate took 9 for 61 in the Mysore first innings in addition to his hundred. It is the only instance of a century and nine wickets in an innings in Indian cricket.</li>
<li>Mysore conceded the match on the fourth day.</li></ul></dd></dl>
<ul><li>Baroda won the Zonal finals and the Semifinals by spin of coin, as both match couldn't produce a winner. The Zonal finals drew with first innings incomplete and the semifinal ended in a tie.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Vijay_Merchant" title="Vijay Merchant">Vijay Merchant</a> averaged 405.00 with the bat (405 in two innings, once out). This is the highest batting average achieved for any Ranji season.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/C._K._Nayudu" title="C. K. Nayudu">C. K. Nayudu</a> scored 200 for Holkar v Baroda in the final at the age of 50 years and 142 days. He is the oldest Indian cricketer to score a double hundred in first class cricket. Nayudu is the fifth oldest double centurion in all first class cricket, after <a href="/wiki/Dave_Nourse" title="Dave Nourse">Dave Nourse</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_King_(cricketer,_born_1871)" title="John King (cricketer, born 1871)">John King</a>, <a href="/wiki/Archie_MacLaren" title="Archie MacLaren">Archie MacLaren</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jack_Hobbs" title="Jack Hobbs">Jack Hobbs</a>.</li></ul>
