<p><b>Kinef-Surgutneftegaz</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">КИНЕФ-Сургутнефтегаз</span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian</a> men's and women's <a href="/wiki/Water_polo" title="Water polo">water polo</a> club from <a href="/wiki/Kirishi" title="Kirishi">Kirishi</a> owned by <a href="/wiki/Kinef" title="Kinef">Kinef</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Surgutneftegaz" title="Surgutneftegaz">Surgutneftegaz</a>. 
</p><p>The women's club was founded in the 1993–94 season as <b>Fakel</b>. In the 1996–97 season the club was renamed <b>Kinef</b>. Since 2003, receiving its current name <b>Kinef-Surgutneftegaz</b>, the Russian club emerged in the mid-2000s as the leading team in Russia, dominating the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Women%27s_Water_Polo_Championship" title="Russian Women's Water Polo Championship">Russian Championship</a> in subsequent years. Most recently it was won its tenth title in a row <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and qualified for the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_LEN_Women%27s_Champions%27_Cup" title="2011–12 LEN Women's Champions' Cup">2012 European Cup</a>'s final four.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
