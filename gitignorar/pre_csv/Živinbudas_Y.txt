	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Živinbudas</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Živinbudas</b> was one of the five senior <a href="/wiki/Lithuania" title="Lithuania">Lithuanian</a> dukes mentioned in the <a href="/wiki/Treaty" title="Treaty">treaty</a> with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Halych-Volhynia" title="Halych-Volhynia">Halych-Volhynia</a> in 1219. The treaty lists a total of <a href="/wiki/List_of_early_Lithuanian_dukes" title="List of early Lithuanian dukes">21 dukes</a>, five of them being elder or superior. Since Živinbudas is mentioned first in the list, it is presumed that he was the supreme ruler of Lithuania.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He is not mentioned in any other sources and the mention in the treaty is the only bit of information available about him. However, some historians argue he was ancestor of <a href="/wiki/Traidenis" title="Traidenis">Traidenis</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grand_Duke_of_Lithuania" title="Grand Duke of Lithuania">Grand Duke of Lithuania</a> ca. 1270-1282.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> That is probably influence of the <a href="/wiki/Palemonids" title="Palemonids">Palemonids</a> legends popularized by fake 16th century genealogies that connected a mythical <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palemon" title="Palemon">Palemon</a>, a prince of <a href="/wiki/Venice" title="Venice">Venice</a> who settled in Lithuania in the 10th century, to the <a href="/wiki/Gediminids" title="Gediminids">Gediminids</a>, an established dynasty.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Russian Gustinskaya Chronicle mentions Živibundas under the year 1218.
</p>
