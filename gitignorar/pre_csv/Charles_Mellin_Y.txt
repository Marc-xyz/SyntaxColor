<p><b>Charles Mellin</b> (1597, <a href="/wiki/Nancy,_France" title="Nancy, France">Nancy</a> – 21 September 1649, <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-malyon_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-malyon-1">[1]</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> painter of the <a href="/wiki/Baroque" title="Baroque">Baroque</a> era. He was from <a href="/wiki/Nancy,_France" title="Nancy, France">Nancy</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lorraine_(duchy)" title="Lorraine (duchy)">Lorraine</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-artfortune_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-artfortune-2">[2]</a></sup> but spent his artistic career in <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rykner_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-rykner-3">[3]</a></sup>  where he was nicknamed <i>Carlo Lorenese</i> ("Charles the Lorrainer").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-artfortune_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-artfortune-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span id="Life_.26_work"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_&amp;_work">Life &amp; work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Charles_Mellin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life &amp; work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He worked on murals<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rykner_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-rykner-3">[3]</a></sup> and decorated the Chapel of the Virgin at the church of <a href="/wiki/San_Luigi_dei_Francesi" title="San Luigi dei Francesi">San Luigi dei Francesi</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a> in 1631.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-artfortune_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-artfortune-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rykner_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-rykner-3">[3]</a></sup>  He had competed with <a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Poussin" title="Nicolas Poussin">Nicolas Poussin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Giovanni_Lanfranco" title="Giovanni Lanfranco">Giovanni Lanfranco</a> for the job of decorating this chapel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rykner_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-rykner-3">[3]</a></sup> During the early part of his career, Mellin collaborated with, and was influenced by, <a href="/wiki/Simon_Vouet" title="Simon Vouet">Simon Vouet</a>, but Vouet’s influence diminished after Vouet left Italy for <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rykner_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-rykner-3">[3]</a></sup> He is also said to have been influenced by <a href="/wiki/Domenichino" title="Domenichino">Domenichino</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-artfortune_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-artfortune-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>After Vouet’s departure, he worked for the Muti Papazzurri family as official painter. He decorated the <a href="/wiki/Palazzo_Muti" title="Palazzo Muti">Palazzo Muti</a> between 1628 and 1631, painting the vaults of the Galleria, and remnants of his work there still survive.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rykner_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-rykner-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jacobite_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-jacobite-4">[4]</a></sup> He also taught painting to the two sons of the Muti Papazzurri family.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rykner_3-6"><a href="#cite_note-rykner-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>In Rome, he painted a fresco, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Francis_de_Paul" title="St. Francis de Paul">St. Francis de Paul</a> before <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sixtus_V" title="Sixtus V">Sixtus V</a></i>, for the church of <a href="/wiki/Trinit%C3%A0_dei_Monti" title="Trinità dei Monti">Trinità dei Monti</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-artfortune_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-artfortune-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>He also decorated the choir at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abbey_of_Monte_Cassino" title="Abbey of Monte Cassino">Abbey of Monte Cassino</a> from 1636 until 1637<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-artfortune_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-artfortune-2">[2]</a></sup> with a cycle of 15 paintings,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-artfortune_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-artfortune-2">[2]</a></sup> which was destroyed during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Monte_Cassino" title="Battle of Monte Cassino">Battle of Monte Cassino</a> in <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rykner_3-7"><a href="#cite_note-rykner-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>From 1643 to 1647 he worked in <a href="/wiki/Naples" title="Naples">Naples</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-artfortune_2-7"><a href="#cite_note-artfortune-2">[2]</a></sup>  He created a <i>Purification of the Virgin</i> for the high altar of the church of the <a href="/wiki/Santissima_Annunziata_Maggiore,_Naples" title="Santissima Annunziata Maggiore, Naples">Santissima Annunziata Maggiore</a> in the city, which was completed in 1645.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-artfortune_2-8"><a href="#cite_note-artfortune-2">[2]</a></sup>  This painting was lost in a 1757 fire, but there is an etched copy that survives.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-artfortune_2-9"><a href="#cite_note-artfortune-2">[2]</a></sup>  In Naples, he painted an <i>Immaculate Conception</i> (1646) and an <i>Annunciation</i> (1647) for the church of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Santa_Donna_Regina_Nuova" title="Santa Donna Regina Nuova">Santa Donna Regina Nuova</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-artfortune_2-10"><a href="#cite_note-artfortune-2">[2]</a></sup>  The revolt of <a href="/wiki/Masaniello" title="Masaniello">Masaniello</a> caused him to leave Naples for Rome.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-artfortune_2-11"><a href="#cite_note-artfortune-2">[2]</a></sup>      
</p><p>He trained painters, such as <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nicolas_Labb%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nicolas Labbé (page does not exist)">Nicolas Labbé</a>, but did not have a workshop per se.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rykner_3-8"><a href="#cite_note-rykner-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
