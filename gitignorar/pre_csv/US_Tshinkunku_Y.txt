<p><b>Union Sportive Tshinkunku</b> commonly known as <b>US Tshinkunku</b>, is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DR_Congo" title="DR Congo">Congolese</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> club based in <a href="/wiki/Kananga" title="Kananga">Kananga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kasa%C3%AF-Central" title="Kasaï-Central">Kasaï-Central</a> province. 
</p><p>They currently playing in the <a href="/wiki/Linafoot_Ligue_2" title="Linafoot Ligue 2">Linafoot Ligue 2</a>, the second level of the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Democratic Republic of the Congo">Congolese</a> football and their home games are played at <a href="/wiki/Stade_des_Jeunes" title="Stade des Jeunes">Stade des Jeunes</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=US_Tshinkunku&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The club was founded in 1954 as <b>Union St-Gilloise</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p><b>US Tshinkunku</b> had their first major success in 1985, when they won the National Championship Cup called Cup of Zaire (Coupe du Zaire) when Georges Mwanza Mande, alias Tshikem was the club president. 
</p><p>In 2011,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Trésor Kapuku, then the governor of Kasai Occidental brought second title back to the province when he was the president of <i>US Tshinkunku</i> club. 
</p><p>In January 2014 the governor of then Kasai Occidental, Alex Kande Mupompa <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>, was elected as the new club president, two years later, the club performed badly in the league and many fans were not happy with the management committee performance. Alexy Kayembe De Bampende, a long-time fan and a <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">US</a> based businessman who lives in Texas invited Georges Mwanza Tshikem to come to rebuild the team. Tshikem went to Kananga where he organized the team and the election for management committee. 
</p><p>On 4 December 2016, club fans and players elected Georges Mwanza Mande, alias Tshikem as club president and Alexy Kayembe De Bampende was elected vice president of <i>Union Sportive Tshinkunku</i>. 
</p><p>Tshikem-Kayembe's vision is to make <i>Tshinkunku</i> a competitive club in national and international level. So far they managed to recruit Congolese qualified coaches to manage the team, Eric Tshibasu, the <a href="/wiki/DR_Congo_national_under-20_football_team" title="DR Congo national under-20 football team">under-20 DR Congo</a> national team, was hired as a team coach, but was fired three months later. Kayembe approached <a href="/wiki/Jean-Claude_Mukanya" title="Jean-Claude Mukanya">Jean-Claude Mukanya</a>, the former <a href="/wiki/DR_Congo_national_football_team" title="DR Congo national football team">DR Congo national football team</a> captain to become <i>US Tshinkunku</i> manager. <a href="/wiki/Jean-Claude_Mukanya" title="Jean-Claude Mukanya">Mukanya</a> signed a three year contract in Kinshasa on 3 July 2017 in front of hundreds of Kinshasa based <i>US Tshinkunku</i> fans.
</p>
