<p><b>Akabane-iwabuchi Station</b><span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">赤羽岩淵駅</span><span style="display:none">,</span> <i>Akabane-iwabuchi-eki</i>)</span> is a subway station in <a href="/wiki/Kita,_Tokyo" title="Kita, Tokyo">Kita, Tokyo</a>, Japan, operated jointly by the Tokyo subway operator <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Metro" title="Tokyo Metro">Tokyo Metro</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Public%E2%80%93private_partnership#Japan" title="Public–private partnership">third-sector</a> railway operator <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saitama_Railway_Corporation" title="Saitama Railway Corporation">Saitama Railway Corporation</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lines">Lines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Akabane-iwabuchi_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Lines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Akabane-iwabuchi Station is the northern terminus of the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Metro_Namboku_Line" title="Tokyo Metro Namboku Line">Tokyo Metro Namboku Line</a>, with most services continuing northward on the <a href="/wiki/Saitama_Rapid_Railway_Line" title="Saitama Rapid Railway Line">Saitama Rapid Railway Line</a> to <a href="/wiki/Urawa-Misono_Station" title="Urawa-Misono Station">Urawa-Misono</a>. The station is numbered "N-19" on the Tokyo Metro network.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_layout">Station layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Akabane-iwabuchi_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Station layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station concourse with ticket vending machines and barriers is located on the 2nd basement ("B2F") level, and the tracks are located on the 3rd basement ("B3F") level. 
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Platforms">Platforms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Akabane-iwabuchi_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Platforms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The station has one <a href="/wiki/Island_platform" title="Island platform">island platform</a> serving two tracks. The platforms are equipped with full-height <a href="/wiki/Platform_screen_doors" title="Platform screen doors">platform screen doors</a>.
</p><p><br/>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Akabane-iwabuchi_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Akabane-Iwabuchi Station opened on the TRTA (present-day Tokyo Metro) Namboku Line on 29 November 1991.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-terada2013_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-terada2013-1">[1]</a></sup> The Saitama Rapid Railway Line extending northward from the station opened on 28 March 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-terada2013_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-terada2013-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Passenger_statistics">Passenger statistics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Akabane-iwabuchi_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Passenger statistics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In fiscal 2011, the station was used by an average of 72,807 Tokyo Metro passengers daily and 34,395 Saitama Rapid Railway Line (boarding passengers only).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-terada2013_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-terada2013-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
