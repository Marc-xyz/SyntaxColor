<p>The <b>Balkan whip snake</b> (<i>Hierophis gemonensis</i>, formerly known as <i>Coluber gemonensis</i>) is a species of snake in the <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> <a href="/wiki/Colubridae" title="Colubridae">Colubridae</a>. It is found in <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a> (including the Greek islands) and most of the Balkan countries (specifically in <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina">Bosnia and Herzegovina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Macedonia" title="North Macedonia">North Macedonia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Slovenia" title="Slovenia">Slovenia</a>) where its natural <a href="/wiki/Habitat" title="Habitat">habitats</a> are Mediterranean-type shrubby <a href="/wiki/Vegetation" title="Vegetation">vegetation</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pastureland" title="Pastureland">pastureland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Plantation" title="Plantation">plantations</a> and rural gardens. It is threatened by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Habitat_loss" title="Habitat loss">habitat loss</a> in parts of its range but overall is assessed by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IUCN" title="IUCN">IUCN</a> as being of "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Least_concern" title="Least concern">least concern</a>".
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Balkan_whip_snake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Balkan whip snake is a slender snake with smooth scales usually under a metre (yard) long but exceptionally reaching 130 cm (50 ins). The head is fairly distinct from the body and has prominent eyes with round pupils. The head and front of the body are olive-grey or yellowish-brown with dark spots separated by paler areas which may form irregular bars. There are often small white specks on some of the scales. Further back the dark spots are usually arranged in longitudinal lines giving a striped effect. The underparts are whitish or pale yellow, with some spotting on the sides of the neck and sometimes elsewhere. There are usually 19 dorsal scales around the mid-body, 160 to 187 belly scales and 80 to 116  pairs of under-tail scales.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Collins_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Collins-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Balkan_whip_snake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:282px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hierophis_gemonensis_in_Pindus.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3120" data-file-width="4160" decoding="async" height="210" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Hierophis_gemonensis_in_Pindus.jpg/280px-Hierophis_gemonensis_in_Pindus.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Hierophis_gemonensis_in_Pindus.jpg/420px-Hierophis_gemonensis_in_Pindus.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Hierophis_gemonensis_in_Pindus.jpg/560px-Hierophis_gemonensis_in_Pindus.jpg 2x" width="280"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hierophis_gemonensis_in_Pindus.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Balkan whip snake, South Pindus</div></div></div>
<p>The Balkan whip snake is found in extreme northeastern Italy, the former Yugoslavia, Albania and mainland Greece as well as many offshore islands. It is also present on <a href="/wiki/Crete" title="Crete">Crete</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Ionian_Islands" title="Ionian Islands">Ionian Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Euboea" title="Euboea">Euboea</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kythera" title="Kythera">Kythera</a> and <a href="/wiki/Karpathos" title="Karpathos">Karpathos</a>. Its typical habitat is stony areas, scree, scrub, open woodland, road banks and ruins.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Collins_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Collins-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Balkan_whip_snake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Balkan whip snake is a diurnal, ground-dwelling species though it does sometimes clamber through low vegetation. It is fast and agile and feeds on lizards, large insects such as grasshoppers, nestling birds and small mammals. Females lay clutches of four to ten oval eggs measuring about 30 by 17 mm (1.2 by 0.7 in). It <a href="/wiki/Hibernation" title="Hibernation">hibernates</a> in winter in such places as rock fissures, animal burrows or outbuildings and sometimes several snakes will share a hibernating site.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Collins_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Collins-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conservation_status">Conservation status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Balkan_whip_snake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Conservation status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Balkan whip snake has a wide range and is common within much of its range. No particular threats have been identified but it is subject to some loss of habitat through agriculture, fire and pollution. The <a href="/wiki/International_Union_for_Conservation_of_Nature" title="International Union for Conservation of Nature">International Union for Conservation of Nature</a> has assessed it as being of "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Least_concern" title="Least concern">least concern</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iucn_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-iucn-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
