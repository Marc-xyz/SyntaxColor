	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">List of Indian states by wildlife population</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bengal_Tiger" title="Bengal Tiger">Bengal Tiger</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indian_Elephant" title="Indian Elephant">Indian Elephant</a> are <a href="/wiki/Endangered_species" title="Endangered species">endangered species</a> which are protected by <a href="/wiki/Project_Tiger" title="Project Tiger">Project Tiger</a> and <a href="/wiki/Project_Elephant" title="Project Elephant">Project Elephant</a> programmes run by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Environment_and_Forests" title="Ministry of Environment and Forests">Ministry of Environment and Forests</a>, <a href="/wiki/Government_of_India" title="Government of India">Government of India</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PE_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-PE-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PT_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-PT-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PE1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-PE1-3">[3]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indian_Leopard" title="Indian Leopard">Indian Leopards</a> are <a href="/wiki/Near-threatened_species" title="Near-threatened species">threatened</a> and protected species. The tiger numbers are of animals aged above 1.5 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>The South Indian states of <a href="/wiki/Karnataka" title="Karnataka">Karnataka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kerala" title="Kerala">Kerala</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tamil_Nadu" title="Tamil Nadu">Tamil Nadu</a> are home to nearly 44% of the elephants, 35% of the tigers and 31% of the leopards in India.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li>The state of <a href="/wiki/Karnataka" title="Karnataka">Karnataka</a> alone is home to 22% of the elephants, 18% of the tigers and 14% of the leopards in India.</li>
<li>The Northeast Indian states of <a href="/wiki/Assam" title="Assam">Assam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arunachal_Pradesh" title="Arunachal Pradesh">Arunachal Pradesh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mizoram" title="Mizoram">Mizoram</a>, <a href="/wiki/Meghalaya" title="Meghalaya">Meghalaya</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tripura" title="Tripura">Tripura</a>  together with <a href="/wiki/West_Bengal" title="West Bengal">West Bengal</a> account for 30% of the elephants and 5% of the tiger population.</li>
<li>The state of <a href="/wiki/Gujarat" title="Gujarat">Gujarat</a> is the only state with 100% of <a href="/wiki/Asiatic_lion" title="Asiatic lion">Asiatic lion</a> population in the world.</li></ul>
