<p>The <b>snowy-cheeked laughingthrush</b> (<i>Ianthocincla sukatschewi</i>), also known as <b>Sukatschev's laughingthrush</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> is a species of <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> in the family <a href="/wiki/Leiothrichidae" title="Leiothrichidae">Leiothrichidae</a>. It is <a href="/wiki/Endemism" title="Endemism">endemic</a> to northern <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> where its natural <a href="/wiki/Habitat" title="Habitat">habitat</a> is temperate <a href="/wiki/Forest" title="Forest">forests</a>. It is threatened by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Habitat_loss" title="Habitat loss">habitat loss</a>.
</p><p>The snowy-cheeked laughingthrush was at one time placed in the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Garrulax" title="Garrulax">Garrulax</a></i> but following the publication of a comprehensive <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Molecular_phylogenetic" title="Molecular phylogenetic">molecular phylogenetic</a> study in 2018, it was moved to the resurrected genus <i><a href="/wiki/Ianthocincla" title="Ianthocincla">Ianthocincla</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ioc_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-ioc-4">[4]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Binomial_nomenclature" title="Binomial nomenclature">specific name</a> honours the Russian merchant, philanthropist and collector Vladimir Platonovich Sukachev (1849-1919).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hbwkey_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-hbwkey-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
