<p><b>Annie Pokorny</b> (born November 16, 1992)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is an American Cross-Country Skier, currently a member of the Sun Valley Ski Education Foundation's Gold Team.
</p><p>Before finding her way to the Sun Valley Ski Education Foundation,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Pokorny was a student at <a href="/wiki/Middlebury_College" title="Middlebury College">Middlebury College</a> in <a href="/wiki/Vermont" title="Vermont">Vermont</a>. Pokorny, in her freshman year, placed 9th in the 15K classic mass start at the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=2012_NCAA_Championships&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="2012 NCAA Championships (page does not exist)">2012 NCAA Championships</a>.
</p><p>Pokorny finished in the 10k freestyle at the U.S. nationals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Her performances at the nationals earned her a spot on the U23 Team, which would take her to her first international competition. That competition was the U23 World Championships in <a href="/wiki/Liberec" title="Liberec">Liberec, Czech Republic</a>. Pokorny finished in the top 30 in two races at her first international competition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Upon her return to the United States, Pokorny won her first <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Intercollegiate_Ski_Association" title="Eastern Intercollegiate Ski Association">Eastern Intercollegiate Ski Association</a> (EISA) in <a href="/wiki/Stowe,_Vermont" title="Stowe, Vermont">Stowe, Vermont</a>. She mounted five podiums, helping her qualify for the NCAAs second-ranked eastern skier.
</p><p>Pokorny cut her collegiate skiing career at Middlebury short and go to professional skiing. She joined the Stratton Mountain School T2 Team, training alongside notable skiers; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jessie_Diggins" title="Jessie Diggins">Jessie Diggins</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sophie_Caldwell" title="Sophie Caldwell">Sophie Caldwell</a>.
</p><p>In 2015, Pokorny received a Travel and Training Fund Grant from The Women's Sports Foundation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> With those funds, Pokorny continuously trained for a spot on the US Ski Team for the 2018 Olympic Games. 
</p>
