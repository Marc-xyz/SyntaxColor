<p>The <b>Meggitt Vindicator II</b>, is a Canadian-built Unmanned Aerial Vehicle – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Target_Drone" title="Target Drone">Training Target</a> used by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadian_Forces" title="Canadian Forces">Canadian Forces</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>The UAV was used to simulate various types of targets like missiles, aircraft (including helicopter).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>The vehicle was not powered and launched via pneumatic catapult.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Specifications_Meggitt_Vindicator_II">Specifications Meggitt Vindicator II</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meggitt_Vindicator_II&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Specifications Meggitt Vindicator II">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p style="font-size: 90%; margin:0;"><i>Data from</i> <a class="external free" href="http://targetsystems.qinetiq.com/static/media/files/Vindicator_UAV-T_DS-AT-VN-V01_sw_final.pdf" rel="nofollow">http://targetsystems.qinetiq.com/static/media/files/Vindicator_UAV-T_DS-AT-VN-V01_sw_final.pdf</a></p><p><span style="font-size: 120%;"><b>General characteristics</b></span>
</p><ul><li><b>Crew:</b> None</li>
<li><b>Length:</b> 2.72 m (8 ft 11 in)</li>
<li><b>Wingspan:</b> 2.59 m (8 ft 6 in)</li>
<li><b>Height:</b> 0.51 m (1 ft 8 in)</li>
<li><b>Wing area:</b> 1.7 m<sup>2</sup> (18 sq ft)</li>
<li><b>Empty weight:</b> 68 kg (150 lb)</li>
<li><b>Gross weight:</b> 77 kg (170 lb)</li>
<li><b>Fuel capacity:</b> 23L</li></ul>
<p><span style="font-size: 120%;"><b>Performance</b></span>
</p>
<ul><li><b>Maximum speed:</b> 322 km/h (200 mph, 174 kn)</li>
<li><b>Range:</b> 190 km (120 mi, 100 nmi)</li>
<li><b>Endurance:</b> 1.5 hours</li>
<li><b>Service ceiling:</b> 910 m (3,000 ft)</li></ul>
