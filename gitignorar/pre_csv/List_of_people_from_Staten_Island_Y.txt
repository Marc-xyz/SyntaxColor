<p>This is a list of notable people who were either born or have lived in <a href="/wiki/Staten_Island" title="Staten Island">Staten Island</a>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Borough_(New_York_City)" title="Borough (New York City)">borough</a> of <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>, at some time in their lives. The list does not include people who were only in Staten Island as college students, military personnel, or prisoners. Approximately 750 people (or performing groups) are listed alphabetically under their primary vocations, which themselves are listed alphabetically.
</p>

<li><a href="/wiki/Arthur_Anderson_(actor)" title="Arthur Anderson (actor)">Arthur Anderson</a> (1922–2016) – actor of radio, stage, film, and television; voice of <a href="/wiki/Lucky_Charms" title="Lucky Charms">Lucky Charms</a> leprechaun</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Leslie_Barrett" title="Leslie Barrett">Leslie Barrett</a> (1919–2010) – frequent actor in theater and television, especially 1950s–1960s</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Maurice_Barrymore" title="Maurice Barrymore">Maurice Barrymore</a> (1849–1905) – vaudeville actor; father of <a href="/wiki/John_Barrymore" title="John Barrymore">John</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lionel_Barrymore" title="Lionel Barrymore">Lionel</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ethel_Barrymore" title="Ethel Barrymore">Ethel Barrymore</a>; lived in Fort Wadsworth, with their grandmother <a href="/wiki/Louisa_Lane_Drew" title="Louisa Lane Drew">Louisa Lane Drew</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/John_Dehner" title="John Dehner">John Dehner</a> (1915–1992) – television and movie actor; born on Staten Island</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Raoul_Pene_Du_Bois" title="Raoul Pene Du Bois">Raoul Pene Du Bois</a> (1914–1985) – costume and scenic designer for stage and film; two-time <a href="/wiki/Tony_Award" title="Tony Award">Tony Award</a> winner</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/David_Dukes" title="David Dukes">David Dukes</a> (1945–2000) – stage, television and movie actor; lived briefly in <a href="/wiki/Brighton_Heights,_Staten_Island" title="Brighton Heights, Staten Island">Brighton Heights, Staten Island</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Joey_Faye" title="Joey Faye">Joey Faye</a> (born Joseph Antony Palladino, c.1909–1997) – comic actor in vaudeville, theater, films and television</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/David_Froman" title="David Froman">David Froman</a> (1938–2010) – portrayed twins Gunther and Bruno on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Edge_of_Night" title="The Edge of Night">The Edge of Night</a></i>, and Lt. Bob Brooks on <i><a href="/wiki/Matlock_(TV_series)" title="Matlock (TV series)">Matlock</a></i>; once lived in Livingston</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ben_Grauer" title="Ben Grauer">Ben Grauer</a> (1908–1977) – longtime broadcaster on radio and television</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Neal_Hart" title="Neal Hart">Neal Hart</a> (1879–1949) – actor and director of silent films</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Allen_Jenkins" title="Allen Jenkins">Allen Jenkins</a> (born Alfred McGonegal, 1900–1974) – character actor, voice of cartoon <i><a href="/wiki/Top_Cat" title="Top Cat">Top Cat</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s Officer Dibble; born on Staten Island</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Betsy_Joslyn" title="Betsy Joslyn">Betsy Joslyn</a> (born 1954) – Broadway actress, best known for <a href="/wiki/Sweeney_Todd:_The_Demon_Barber_of_Fleet_Street" title="Sweeney Todd: The Demon Barber of Fleet Street"><i>Sweeney Todd</i></a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Thomas_W._Keene" title="Thomas W. Keene">Thomas W. Keene</a> (born Thomas R. Eagleson, 1840–1898) – Shakespearean actor</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Guy_Kibbee" title="Guy Kibbee">Guy Kibbee</a> (1882–1956) – stage and film actor, father of CUNY Chancellor <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kibbee" title="Robert Kibbee">Robert Kibbee</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Paul_Land" title="Paul Land">Paul Land</a> (1956–2007) – actor, <i><a href="/wiki/Spring_Break_(film)" title="Spring Break (film)">Spring Break</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Idolmaker" title="The Idolmaker">The Idolmaker</a></i></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Robert_Loggia" title="Robert Loggia">Robert Loggia</a> (1930–2015) – actor, <i><a href="/wiki/Scarface_(1983_film)" title="Scarface (1983 film)">Scarface</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sopranos" title="The Sopranos">The Sopranos</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Big_(film)" title="Big (film)">Big</a></i>; attended <a href="/wiki/New_Dorp_High_School" title="New Dorp High School">New Dorp High School</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wagner_College" title="Wagner College">Wagner College</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Allan_Manings" title="Allan Manings">Allan Manings</a> (1924–2010) – television producer and comedy writer, best known for <i><a href="/wiki/Rowan_%26_Martin%27s_Laugh-In" title="Rowan &amp; Martin's Laugh-In">Rowan &amp; Martin's Laugh-In</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Times" title="Good Times">Good Times</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/One_Day_at_a_Time_(1975_TV_series)" title="One Day at a Time (1975 TV series)">One Day at a Time</a></i></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Paul_Newman" title="Paul Newman">Paul Newman</a> (1925–2008) – <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">Academy Award</a> winning actor; married to Joanne Woodward; lived in <a href="/wiki/St._George,_Staten_Island" title="St. George, Staten Island">St. George, Staten Island</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT2007_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT2007-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mabel_Normand" title="Mabel Normand">Mabel Normand</a> (1892–1930) – silent film actress, director, writer, producer; frequent collaborator with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fatty_Arbuckle" title="Fatty Arbuckle">Fatty Arbuckle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Chaplin" title="Charlie Chaplin">Charlie Chaplin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mack_Sennett" title="Mack Sennett">Mack Sennett</a>; grew up in New Brighton</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Edward_Platt" title="Edward Platt">Edward Platt</a> (1916–1974) – actor who played "The Chief" on the 1960s TV show <i><a href="/wiki/Get_Smart" title="Get Smart">Get Smart</a></i>; born on Staten Island</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Frances_Robinson_(actress)" title="Frances Robinson (actress)">Frances Robinson</a> (born Marion Frances Ladd, 1916–1971) – actress in early films</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ivy_Scott" title="Ivy Scott">Ivy Scott</a> (1886–1947) – actress on radio and Broadway</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Raymond_Serra" title="Raymond Serra">Raymond Serra</a> (1936–2003) – actor in television and films, including <i><a href="/wiki/Gotti_(1996_film)" title="Gotti (1996 film)">Gotti</a></i> and the first two <i><a href="/wiki/Teenage_Mutant_Ninja_Turtles_in_film" title="Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles in film">Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles</a></i> movies</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Don_Stewart_(actor)" title="Don Stewart (actor)">Don Stewart</a> (1935–2006) – actor, best known for his long-running role as attorney Mike Bauer on <i><a href="/wiki/Guiding_Light" title="Guiding Light">Guiding Light</a></i></li>

<li><a href="/wiki/Alan_Seeger" title="Alan Seeger">Alan Seeger</a> (1888–1916) – poet and World War I hero (<i><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/A_treasury_of_war_poetry,_British_and_American_poems_of_the_world_war,_1914-1919/Poets_Militant#I_Have_a_Rendezvous_with_Death" title="s:A treasury of war poetry, British and American poems of the world war, 1914-1919/Poets Militant">I Have a Rendezvous with Death</a></i>); brother of <a href="/wiki/Charles_Seeger" title="Charles Seeger">Charles Seeger</a>; uncle of <a href="/wiki/Pete_Seeger" title="Pete Seeger">Pete Seeger</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Theodore_Sturgeon" title="Theodore Sturgeon">Theodore Sturgeon</a> (1918–1985) – science fiction author; born on Staten Island</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Henry_David_Thoreau" title="Henry David Thoreau">Henry David Thoreau</a> (1817–1862) – essayist, philosopher, naturalist; spent his longest time away from <a href="/wiki/Concord,_Massachusetts" title="Concord, Massachusetts">Concord, Massachusetts</a> on Staten Island in the 1840s</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Amy_Vanderbilt" title="Amy Vanderbilt">Amy Vanderbilt</a> (1908–1974) – author of the best-selling <i>Complete Book of Etiquette</i>; distant cousin of <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Vanderbilt" title="Cornelius Vanderbilt">Cornelius Vanderbilt</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Phyllis_A._Whitney" title="Phyllis A. Whitney">Phyllis A. Whitney</a> (1903–2008) – prolific mystery writer; Staten Island resident during 1950s–1960s</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/William_Winter_(author)" title="William Winter (author)">William Winter</a> (1836–1917) – poet, critic, biographer, theater historian</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Paul_Zindel" title="Paul Zindel">Paul Zindel</a> (1936–2003) – novelist and Pulitzer-winning playwright, whose stories usually took place on Staten Island</li></ul>
