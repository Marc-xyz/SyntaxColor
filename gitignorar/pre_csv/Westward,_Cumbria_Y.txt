<p><b>Westward</b> is a small village and <a href="/wiki/Civil_parish" title="Civil parish">civil parish</a> in the <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> county of <a href="/wiki/Cumbria" title="Cumbria">Cumbria</a>. It had a population of 814,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> at the 2001 census, increasing slightly to 838 at the 2011 Census.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>  Westward is located on the south side of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Wiza_Beck&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Wiza Beck (page does not exist)">Wiza Beck</a>. It is 3.3 miles (5.3 km) to the south of the town of <a href="/wiki/Wigton" title="Wigton">Wigton</a>. Administratively it forms part of the district of <a href="/wiki/Allerdale" title="Allerdale">Allerdale</a>.
</p><p>The Nobel prize winner <a href="/wiki/William_Henry_Bragg" title="William Henry Bragg">William Henry Bragg</a> was born in Westward in 1862.
