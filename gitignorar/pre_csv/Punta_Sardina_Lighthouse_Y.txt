<p>The <b>Punta Sardina Lighthouse</b> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">Faro de Punta Sardina</i>) is an active <a href="/wiki/Lighthouse" title="Lighthouse">lighthouse</a> on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canary_island" title="Canary island">Canary island</a> of <a href="/wiki/Gran_Canaria" title="Gran Canaria">Gran Canaria</a>. It is located north of the small town of Sardina, in the municipality of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/G%C3%A1ldar,_Las_Palmas" title="Gáldar, Las Palmas">Gáldar</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rowlett_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-rowlett-2">[2]</a></sup> The Punta Sardina light marks the north-western extremity of the island, and lies between the <a href="/wiki/Punta_del_Castillete_Lighthouse" title="Punta del Castillete Lighthouse">Punta del Castillete lighthouse</a> near <a href="/wiki/Puerto_de_Mog%C3%A1n" title="Puerto de Mogán">Puerto de Mogán</a> to the south and the <a href="/wiki/La_Isleta_Lighthouse" title="La Isleta Lighthouse">La Isleta lighthouse</a> of <a href="/wiki/Las_Palmas" title="Las Palmas">Las Palmas</a> to the east.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NGIA2009_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-NGIA2009-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Punta_Sardina_Lighthouse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The lighthouse is the second to be constructed on the rocky headland of Punta Sardina (Sardine Point), which overlooks the Atlantic Ocean. The first was completed in 1891, as part of the first maritime lighting plan for the Canaries. It remained in service until it was demolished in the 1980s to make way for the new modern tower.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telde_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-telde-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>The new light first entered service in 1985, and consists of a 24 metres (79 ft) high cylinder-shaped tower, which is white with four red bands. It supports twin galleries and a lantern with a grey cupola.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rowlett_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-rowlett-2">[2]</a></sup> With a focal height of 47 metres (154 ft) above sea level, the light can be seen for 20 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nautical_miles" title="Nautical miles">nautical miles</a>. Its <a href="/wiki/Light_characteristic" title="Light characteristic">light characteristic</a> is made up of four flashes of white light every twenty seconds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rowlett_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-rowlett-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ngall_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-ngall-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guias_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Guias-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>The lighthouse is maintained by the <a href="/wiki/Port_authority" title="Port authority">Port authority</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Las_Palmas" title="Province of Las Palmas">Province of Las Palmas</a>. It is registered under the international Admiralty number D2816 and has the <a href="/wiki/National_Geospatial-Intelligence_Agency" title="National Geospatial-Intelligence Agency">NGA</a> identifier of 113-23928.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rowlett_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-rowlett-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
