<p><b>Jean-Adolphe Beaucé</b> (2 August 1818 - 13 July 1875) was a French battle-scene painter. Born in Paris, he followed the French army on campaign from 1843 onwards in <a href="/wiki/French_North_Africa" title="French North Africa">North Africa</a>, the Middle East and <a href="/wiki/Second_French_intervention_in_Mexico" title="Second French intervention in Mexico">Mexico</a>. He also painted portraits of military figures and produced illustrations for several works by <a href="/wiki/Alexandre_Dumas" title="Alexandre Dumas">Alexandre Dumas pere</a> such as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Three_Musketeers" title="The Three Musketeers">The Three Musketeers</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Vicomte_of_Bragelonne:_Ten_Years_Later" title="The Vicomte of Bragelonne: Ten Years Later">The Viscount of Bragelone</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/La_Dame_de_Monsoreau" title="La Dame de Monsoreau">The Lady of Monsoreau</a></i>. He died in the Parisian suburb of <a href="/wiki/Boulogne-Billancourt" title="Boulogne-Billancourt">Boulogne-Billancourt</a> and is buried in the 49th division of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cimeti%C3%A8re_du_P%C3%A8re-Lachaise" title="Cimetière du Père-Lachaise">cimetière du Père-Lachaise</a> in Paris.
</p>
