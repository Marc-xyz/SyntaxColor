<p>The <b>2018 European Championship</b> was the 14th <a href="/wiki/European_Championship_of_American_football" title="European Championship of American football">European Championship</a> in <a href="/wiki/American_football" title="American football">American football</a>. The final tournament was played in <a href="/wiki/Vantaa" title="Vantaa">Vantaa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a> from 29 July to 4 August 2018.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualification">Qualification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_European_Championship_of_American_football&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Qualification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Qualification for the European Championship 2018 was held in three stages. In the first round, twelve teams played a single playoff round. The six winners and two further teams played the second round, in two tournaments of four teams. Another two teams entered the qualification in the third round in playoff games against the two tournament winners of the second round. Four teams were already qualified for the European Championship 2018. Altogether an all-time record number of twenty teams competed to win the European title in 2018. One team, <a href="/wiki/Germany_national_American_football_team" title="Germany national American football team">Germany</a>, did not play a single game.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="First_round">First round</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_European_Championship_of_American_football&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: First round">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first round was played from August to October 2015. Six of the twelve teams took part in one of the tournaments of the last championship. These were the seeded teams playing against an unseeded team. The seeded teams were the hosts of their playoff game. <a href="/wiki/Israel_national_American_football_team" title="Israel national American football team">Israel</a> defeating <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_American_football_team" title="Spain national American football team">Spain</a> was the only unseeded team to make it to the next round.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_round">Second round</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_European_Championship_of_American_football&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Second round">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The second round tournaments were played in 2016. As runner-up and third in the B Group Tournament in 2013, <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_American_football_team" title="Italy national American football team">Italy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain_national_American_football_team" title="Great Britain national American football team">Great Britain</a> earned a spot as the host nation of one of the tournaments.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Third_round">Third round</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_European_Championship_of_American_football&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Third round">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Italy and Great Britain, as the winners of the tournaments in Italy and Great Britain, were respectively assigned to play against Sweden and Denmark (fifth and sixth place at the 2014 EFAF Championship), with the winners advancing to the 2018 tournament.
</p><p>In June 2017, Italy was announced to have qualified for the final tournament<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ifaf_paris_ny_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-ifaf_paris_ny-1">[1]</a></sup> as only one of the four teams without any match played. Finland, who was already qualified, did not appear on the schedule. No reasons were given.
</p><p>In March 2018, IFAF New York announced to have Sweden, Denmark, and Great Britain advancing to the final tournament. Now reigning Champion Germany and Italy were not on the list. Again, no reasons were given. On 28 March, the website American Football International reported that the reason for Germany not joining was due to the teams in the <a href="/wiki/German_Football_League" title="German Football League">German Football League</a> voting not to change the league schedule to accommodate the European Championships.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-american_football_International_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-american_football_International-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
