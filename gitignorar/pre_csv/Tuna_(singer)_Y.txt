<p><b>Altuna Sejdiu</b> (born July 14, 1985 in <a href="/wiki/Skopje" title="Skopje">Skopje</a>), known professionally as <b>Tuna</b>, is  an <a href="/wiki/Albanians_in_North_Macedonia" title="Albanians in North Macedonia">Albanian</a> singer-songwriter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BIO_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-BIO-1">[1]</a></sup> She has released two albums, and a number of singles. Her discography includes songs in <a href="/wiki/Albanian_language" title="Albanian language">Albanian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Macedonian_language" title="Macedonian language">Macedonian</a> and <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tuna_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tuna is the daughter of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C5%A0aban_Sejdiu" title="Šaban Sejdiu">Shaban Sejdiu</a>, an ethnic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Albanian_people" title="Albanian people">Albanian</a> former Olympic wrestler, who had won bronze medal in two Olympiads (1980 and 1984) for Yugoslavia. Tuna started singing at a very young age, participating in various festivals. Her professional career began in 2002 when she competed in the talent search competition <i>Play - Search For A Star</i> organized by the Macedonian record label M2 Production.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Sejdiu was one of two winners from the competition, which resulted in the release of her first single "Najdi den, najdi nokj, najdi vreme" (Find a day, Find a night, Find time).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Tuna later began to establish her career in Albania and turned focus to releasing material in the Albanian language.  In 2003, she released her debut self-titled album <i>Tuna</i>, which was followed by her second album, <i>S'ka me diktaturë</i> (No more dictatorship), in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BIO_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-BIO-1">[1]</a></sup> In June 2012, Tuna released "I Asaj" (Hers) which rose huge popularity on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>. Albanian newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/Koha_Jon%C3%AB" title="Koha Jonë">Koha Jonë</a></i> believed that the song would undoubtedly be a summer hit.  She released in November 2012 her 6th Album titled N.E.R.D.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In 2012, Tuna joined <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/X_Factor_Albania" title="X Factor Albania">X Factor Albania</a></i> as a judge.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> She is a supporter of the LGBT rights.
</p>
