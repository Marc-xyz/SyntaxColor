<p><b>Elon John Farnsworth</b> (July 30, 1837 – July 3, 1863) was a <a href="/wiki/Union_Army" title="Union Army">Union Army</a> captain in the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>. He commanded Brigade 1, Division 3 of the <a href="/wiki/Cavalry_Corps_(Union_Army)" title="Cavalry Corps (Union Army)">Cavalry Corps (Union Army)</a> from June 28, 1863 to July 3, 1863, when he was mortally wounded and died at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Gettysburg" title="Battle of Gettysburg">Battle of Gettysburg</a>. He was nominated by <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln" title="Abraham Lincoln">Abraham Lincoln</a> for appointment to the grade of <a href="/wiki/Brigadier_general_(United_States)" title="Brigadier general (United States)">brigadier general</a> on June 29, 1863 but was not confirmed by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">United States Senate</a> before his death at Gettysburg.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_career">Early life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elon_J._Farnsworth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Farnsworth was born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Green_Oak,_Michigan" title="Green Oak, Michigan">Green Oak, Michigan</a> on July 30, 1837.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eicher596_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Eicher596-1">[1]</a></sup> 
</p><p>Elon Farnsworth's uncle, <a href="/wiki/John_F._Farnsworth" title="John F. Farnsworth">John F. Farnsworth</a>, served in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives" title="United States House of Representatives">United States House of Representatives</a> from Illinois between March 4, 1857 and March 3, 1861 and again between March 4, 1863 and March 3, 1873.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eicher596_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Eicher596-1">[1]</a></sup> John Farnsworth was a Union Army <a href="/wiki/Colonel_(United_States)" title="Colonel (United States)">colonel</a> who also commanded cavalry brigades (September 1862 to February 1862) and was nominated for appointed to the grade of brigadier general (November 1862).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eicher596_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Eicher596-1">[1]</a></sup> His appointment also was not confirmed by the United States Senate after the nomination was ordered returned to President Lincoln on February 12, 1863.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eicher596_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Eicher596-1">[1]</a></sup> 
</p><p>Elon Farnsworth's family moved to Illinois in 1854.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eicher596_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-Eicher596-1">[1]</a></sup> A member of the Chi Psi Fraternity, Farnsworth was expelled from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Michigan" title="University of Michigan">University of Michigan</a> following a drinking party in which a classmate died after being thrown from a window.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He joined the Army as a civilian foragemaster in 1857<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eicher596_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-Eicher596-1">[1]</a></sup> and served on the staff of <a href="/wiki/Albert_Sidney_Johnston" title="Albert Sidney Johnston">Albert Sidney Johnston</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Utah_War" title="Utah War">Utah War</a> of 1857–58.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> He also worked as a buffalo hunter, scout and freighter in the <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Territory" title="Colorado Territory">Colorado Territory</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eicher596_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-Eicher596-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Civil_War">Civil War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elon_J._Farnsworth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Civil War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the outbreak of the Civil War, Farnsworth was appointed a <a href="/wiki/First_lieutenant#United_States" title="First lieutenant">first lieutenant</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/8th_Regiment_Illinois_Volunteer_Cavalry" title="8th Regiment Illinois Volunteer Cavalry">8th Illinois Cavalry</a>, the regiment commanded by his uncle,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eicher596_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-Eicher596-1">[1]</a></sup> serving with distinction throughout the early stages of the war. Being promoted to <a href="/wiki/Captain_(United_States)" title="Captain (United States)">captain</a> on December 25, 1861, he was made Assistant Chief Quartermaster of the <a href="/wiki/IV_Corps_(Union_Army)" title="IV Corps (Union Army)">IV Corps</a>, and in early 1863, he served as <a href="/wiki/Aide-de-camp" title="Aide-de-camp">aide-de-camp</a> to Brigadier General <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Pleasonton" title="Alfred Pleasonton">Alfred Pleasonton</a> through the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Chancellorsville" title="Battle of Chancellorsville">Battle of Chancellorsville</a> and early stages of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gettysburg_Campaign" title="Gettysburg Campaign">Gettysburg Campaign</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eicher596_1-8"><a href="#cite_note-Eicher596-1">[1]</a></sup> Pleasanton, then in command of the Union Cavalry Corp, gave Farnsworth command of 1st Brigade, 3rd Division, Cavalry Corps, <a href="/wiki/Army_of_the_Potomac" title="Army of the Potomac">Army of the Potomac</a> on June 28, 1863, three days before the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Gettysburg" title="Battle of Gettysburg">Battle of Gettysburg</a>. On June 29, 1863, just two days before the battle, President Lincoln nominated Farnsworth to the grade <a href="/wiki/Brigadier_general_(United_States)" title="Brigadier general (United States)">brigadier general</a> of volunteers but the appointment was never confirmed by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">United States Senate</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eicher596_1-9"><a href="#cite_note-Eicher596-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Death_at_Gettysburg">Death at Gettysburg</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elon_J._Farnsworth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Death at Gettysburg">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Gettysburg_South_Cavalry_Field.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1025" data-file-width="2166" decoding="async" height="104" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/29/Gettysburg_South_Cavalry_Field.png/220px-Gettysburg_South_Cavalry_Field.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/29/Gettysburg_South_Cavalry_Field.png/330px-Gettysburg_South_Cavalry_Field.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/29/Gettysburg_South_Cavalry_Field.png/440px-Gettysburg_South_Cavalry_Field.png 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Gettysburg_South_Cavalry_Field.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>South Cavalry Field at Gettysburg <div class="legend" style="-webkit-column-break-inside: avoid;page-break-inside: avoid;break-inside: avoid-column"><span class="legend-color" style="display:inline-block; width:1.5em; height:1.5em; margin:1px 0; border:1px solid black; background-color: #ff0000; color:black; font-size:100%; text-align:center;"> </span> Confederate</div> <div class="legend" style="-webkit-column-break-inside: avoid;page-break-inside: avoid;break-inside: avoid-column"><span class="legend-color" style="display:inline-block; width:1.5em; height:1.5em; margin:1px 0; border:1px solid black; background-color: #0000ff; color:black; font-size:100%; text-align:center;"> </span> Union</div></div></div></div>
<p>After the collapse of <a href="/wiki/Pickett%27s_Charge" title="Pickett's Charge">Pickett's Charge</a> and the defeat of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Major_General_(CSA)" title="Major General (CSA)">Major General</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J.E.B._Stuart" title="J.E.B. Stuart">J.E.B. Stuart</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Confederate_States_Army" title="Confederate States Army">Confederate</a> cavalry on July 3, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Gettysburg,_Third_Day_cavalry_battles" title="Battle of Gettysburg, Third Day cavalry battles">third day of the Battle of Gettysburg</a>, Brigadier General <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Judson_Kilpatrick" title="Hugh Judson Kilpatrick">Hugh Judson Kilpatrick</a>, commanding the 3rd Division, ordered Farnsworth to make a charge with his brigade against Confederate positions south of the <a href="/wiki/Devil%27s_Den" title="Devil's Den">Devil's Den</a> area of the battlefield, below <a href="/wiki/Little_Round_Top" title="Little Round Top">Little Round Top</a>. Farnsworth initially balked, arguing there was no hope of success, and only agreed to it when Kilpatrick allegedly accused him of cowardice. Farnsworth made the charge, against elements of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_B._Hood" title="John B. Hood">John B. Hood</a>'s division, under <a href="/wiki/Evander_M._Law" title="Evander M. Law">Evander M. Law</a> (Hood having been wounded the previous day). Farnsworth rode with the second battalion of the <a href="/wiki/1st_Vermont_Cavalry" title="1st Vermont Cavalry">1st Vermont Cavalry</a>, alongside Major <a href="/wiki/William_Wells_(general)" title="William Wells (general)">William Wells</a>.
</p><p>The charge was repulsed with heavy losses, and Farnsworth himself was shot five times in the chest. An account by Confederate Colonel <a href="/wiki/William_C._Oates" title="William C. Oates">William C. Oates</a> claimed that Farnsworth was surrounded by Confederate soldiers and committed suicide to avoid capture, but this has been disputed by other witnesses and discounted by most historians.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eicher596_1-10"><a href="#cite_note-Eicher596-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Kilpatrick received much criticism for ordering the charge, but no official action was taken against him.
</p><p>Farnsworth is buried in Rockton Cemetery, <a href="/wiki/Rockton,_Illinois" title="Rockton, Illinois">Rockton, Illinois</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eicher596_1-11"><a href="#cite_note-Eicher596-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Memorials">Memorials</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elon_J._Farnsworth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Memorials">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Battery Farnsworth, a coastal defense built between 1897 and 1899 near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fort_Constitution" title="Fort Constitution">Fort Constitution</a> at <a href="/wiki/New_Castle,_New_Hampshire" title="New Castle, New Hampshire">New Castle, New Hampshire</a>, was named in his honor.
</p>
