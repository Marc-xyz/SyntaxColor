<p>The <b>2010 Lisbon summit</b> was a meeting of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heads_of_state" title="Heads of state">heads of state</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heads_of_government" title="Heads of government">heads of government</a> of <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a> held in <a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a>, Portugal, on 19 and 20 November 2010.
</p><p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Members_of_NATO" title="Members of NATO">member states</a> adopted a new "Strategic Concept", the alliance's new ten-year plan following the expiration of the previous plan adopted at the <a href="/wiki/1999_Washington_summit" title="1999 Washington summit">1999 Washington summit</a>. In addition to accepting the Strategic Concept that addressed the alliance's modern challenges such as terrorism and cyber attacks, the members agreed to develop a mutual <a href="/wiki/Missile_defense" title="Missile defense">missile defense</a> system. The members met with <a href="/wiki/President_of_Afghanistan" title="President of Afghanistan">President of Afghanistan</a> <a href="/wiki/Hamid_Karzai" title="Hamid Karzai">Hamid Karzai</a> regarding the group's operations in the country. They agreed to gradually withdraw combat forces from the country with a completion date of 2014. NATO reaffirmed their commitment to remain in Afghanistan to provide training and advice to Afghan forces and police.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Summit">Summit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Lisbon_summit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Summit">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The summit took place in the Feira Internacional de Lisboa in <a href="/wiki/Parque_das_Na%C3%A7%C3%B5es" title="Parque das Nações">Parque das Nações</a> (Park of the Nations). It was the first summit presided over by <a href="/wiki/Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen" title="Anders Fogh Rasmussen">Anders Fogh Rasmussen</a>, who began his term as the <a href="/wiki/Secretary_General_of_NATO" title="Secretary General of NATO">Secretary General of NATO</a> in August 2009.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Agenda">Agenda</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Lisbon_summit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Agenda">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/NATO_Lisbon_Summit_Declaration" title="NATO Lisbon Summit Declaration">NATO Lisbon Summit Declaration</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Strategic_Concept">Strategic Concept</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Lisbon_summit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Strategic Concept">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Going into the summit, the member states were expected to adopt a new Strategic Concept for the first time since 1999. Prior to the summit, Secretary General Rasmussen personally prepared a draft of the plan. Rasmussen said the goal of the new Strategic Concept "must reconfirm Nato's core task – territorial defence – but modernise how we do it, including cyber defence and missile defence."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> A group of experts led by former <a href="/wiki/United_States_Secretary_of_State" title="United States Secretary of State">United States Secretary of State</a> <a href="/wiki/Madeleine_Albright" title="Madeleine Albright">Madeleine Albright</a> drafted a report to help prepare the plan. At meetings prior to the summit, officials said that the plan was "98% there" and that any additional disagreements would be addressed at the summit. The draft acknowledged that modern threats to the alliance's members come in non-traditional forms such as terrorism, possessing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weapons_of_mass_destruction" title="Weapons of mass destruction">weapons of mass destruction</a>, and cyber attacks that could disrupt power infrastructure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-economist11nov_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-economist11nov-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>During the first day of the summit on Friday 19 November, the member states agreed to the new Strategic Concept that will serve as the alliance's mission plan for the next 10 years. The document addressed the importance of evolving threats to international security, and reemphasized the alliance's commitment to cooperation with prospective members and Russia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The 11-page document is titled "Active Engagement, Modern Defence".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-statconpdf_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-statconpdf-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Lisbon_summit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Afghanistan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:2010_Lisbon_NATO_summit_(2).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1469" data-file-width="2100" decoding="async" height="140" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/06/2010_Lisbon_NATO_summit_%282%29.jpg/200px-2010_Lisbon_NATO_summit_%282%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/06/2010_Lisbon_NATO_summit_%282%29.jpg/300px-2010_Lisbon_NATO_summit_%282%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/06/2010_Lisbon_NATO_summit_%282%29.jpg/400px-2010_Lisbon_NATO_summit_%282%29.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:2010_Lisbon_NATO_summit_(2).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>President of the United States <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a> meeting with President of Afghanistan <a href="/wiki/Hamid_Karzai" title="Hamid Karzai">Hamid Karzai</a>.</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/President_of_Afghanistan" title="President of Afghanistan">President of Afghanistan</a> <a href="/wiki/Hamid_Karzai" title="Hamid Karzai">Hamid Karzai</a> addressed the summit on Saturday 20 November. Karzai had said that he wants NATO to return control of the country by the end of 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc18nov_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-bbc18nov-7">[7]</a></sup> Before the summit, British Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/David_Cameron" title="David Cameron">David Cameron</a> said that the "NATO Summit in Lisbon is set to mark the starting point for passing responsibility for security progressively to Afghan forces."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p><p>While meeting with Karzai, the members agreed to a gradual phase-out of combat operations until 2014. After that date, NATO states would continue to contribute to training and advising the <a href="/wiki/Afghan_National_Army" title="Afghan National Army">Afghan National Army</a>. Secretary-General Rasmussen said "we will launch the process by which the Afghan government will take leadership for security throughout the country, district by district."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytafghan_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-nytafghan-14">[14]</a></sup> Though the members set the 2014 target date, many have already stated that their withdrawals will take place outside of any NATO decision.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytafghan_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-nytafghan-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Russia">Russia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Lisbon_summit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Russia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NATO-Russia_relations" title="NATO-Russia relations">NATO-Russia relations</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Medvedev_Obama_and_Sarkozy_at_2010_NATO_Summit_cropped.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1380" data-file-width="1642" decoding="async" height="185" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Medvedev_Obama_and_Sarkozy_at_2010_NATO_Summit_cropped.jpg/220px-Medvedev_Obama_and_Sarkozy_at_2010_NATO_Summit_cropped.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Medvedev_Obama_and_Sarkozy_at_2010_NATO_Summit_cropped.jpg/330px-Medvedev_Obama_and_Sarkozy_at_2010_NATO_Summit_cropped.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Medvedev_Obama_and_Sarkozy_at_2010_NATO_Summit_cropped.jpg/440px-Medvedev_Obama_and_Sarkozy_at_2010_NATO_Summit_cropped.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Medvedev_Obama_and_Sarkozy_at_2010_NATO_Summit_cropped.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Russian President <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dimitry_Medvedev" title="Dimitry Medvedev">Dimitry Medvedev</a>, American President <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a>, and French President <a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Sarkozy" title="Nicolas Sarkozy">Nicolas Sarkozy</a> on the summit's second day.</div></div></div>
<p>In October 2010, Rasmussen announced that Russian President Dimitry Medvedev would be present at the summit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Medvedev agreed to attend after meeting with French President Nicolas Sarkozy and German Chancellor Angela Merkel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The summit then held the first meeting of the NATO-Russia Council since it was created in 2002. Relations between Russia and the alliance had been strained following the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2008_South_Ossetia_war" title="2008 South Ossetia war">2008 South Ossetia war</a>, when Russia supported and <a href="/wiki/International_recognition_of_Abkhazia_and_South_Ossetia" title="International recognition of Abkhazia and South Ossetia">recognized</a> the breakaway regions of South Ossetia and Abkhazia in Georgia.
</p><p>Prior to the summit, an article in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Economist" title="The Economist">The Economist</a></i> noted that Russia may be more willing to cooperate on NATO issues and may pledge to contribute troops to Afghanistan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-economist11nov_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-economist11nov-9">[9]</a></sup> At the meeting, Russia agreed to cooperate on a missile defense programme that NATO sought to develop. Russia also committed to further support NATO's operations in Afghanistan by permitting more supplies to be transported through Russia and providing <a href="/wiki/Mil_Mi-17" title="Mil Mi-17">Mil Mi-17</a> helicopters to Afghan forces.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Missile_defense">Missile defense</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Lisbon_summit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Missile defense">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On the first day of the summit, leaders agreed to establish a <a href="/wiki/Missile_defense" title="Missile defense">missile defense</a> system that would have the capability of covering all member states in Europe, as well as the United States and Canada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The proposed system had previously been a point of debate between NATO and Russia, but Russian President <a href="/wiki/Dmitry_Medvedev" title="Dmitry Medvedev">Dmitry Medvedev</a>, who attended the summit, indicated more willingness to cooperate with the alliance on the issue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup>
</p>
