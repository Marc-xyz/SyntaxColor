<p><b>Loxley</b> is a town in <a href="/wiki/Baldwin_County,_Alabama" title="Baldwin County, Alabama">Baldwin County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">Alabama</a>, United States. As of the 2010 census, the population of the town was 1,632.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census_2010_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Census_2010-3">[3]</a></sup> It is part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daphne-Fairhope-Foley_micropolitan_area" title="Daphne-Fairhope-Foley micropolitan area">Daphne-Fairhope-Foley micropolitan area</a>. Loxley is becoming a popular location for the expansion of the suburbs from <a href="/wiki/Daphne,_Alabama" title="Daphne, Alabama">Daphne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Fort,_Alabama" title="Spanish Fort, Alabama">Spanish Fort, Alabama</a>, because it is served by an <a href="/wiki/Interstate_10" title="Interstate 10">Interstate 10</a> exit and is almost directly between the cities of <a href="/wiki/Mobile,_Alabama" title="Mobile, Alabama">Mobile, Alabama</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pensacola,_Florida" title="Pensacola, Florida">Pensacola, Florida</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loxley,_Alabama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="19th_century">19th century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loxley,_Alabama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 19th century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1870, a man from <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a> by the name of John Loxley established a lumber camp in the area previously known as Bennet. The lumber camp included a commissary and saw mill. The men that traveled with Loxley settled in what later became the town of Loxley. John Loxley returned to Chicago when the lumber was exhausted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Baldwin_County_Express_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Baldwin_County_Express-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="20th_century">20th century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loxley,_Alabama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 20th century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The train depot opened on May 5, 1906, on the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bay_Minette_%26_Fort_Morgan_Railroad&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bay Minette &amp; Fort Morgan Railroad (page does not exist)">Fort Morgan Line</a>. Before that, there were only wagon roads to <a href="/wiki/Bay_Minette,_Alabama" title="Bay Minette, Alabama">Bay Minette</a>. Also in 1906, the Loxley post office was opened by Octavia Sauer. She served as the postmistress and depot agent. By 1920, the businesses in Loxley consisted of an egg store, grocery store, two general merchandise stores, train depot, drug store, telegraph office, land office, repair garage, post office, bank, hotel, butcher shop, orange packing shed, cement block plant, a blacksmith, a feed and lumber store. A grammar school was built in 1925.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Baldwin_County_Express_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Baldwin_County_Express-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Town of Loxley incorporated in March 1957.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Baldwin_County_Express_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Baldwin_County_Express-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loxley,_Alabama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Loxley is located in south-central Baldwin County at 30°37'24.600" North, 87°45'17.035" West (30.623500, -87.754732).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR1-5">[5]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_90" title="U.S. Route 90">U.S. Route 90</a> (Hickory Street) passes through the center of the town, leading eastbound 5 miles (8 km) to <a href="/wiki/Robertsdale,_Alabama" title="Robertsdale, Alabama">Robertsdale, Alabama</a>, and 39 miles (63 km) to <a href="/wiki/Pensacola,_Florida" title="Pensacola, Florida">Pensacola, Florida</a>, and westbound 11 miles (18 km) to <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Fort,_Alabama" title="Spanish Fort, Alabama">Spanish Fort</a> and 20 miles (32 km) to <a href="/wiki/Mobile,_Alabama" title="Mobile, Alabama">Mobile</a>. <a href="/wiki/Interstate_10" title="Interstate 10">Interstate 10</a> passes through the northern end of town, with access via Exit 44 (<a href="/wiki/Alabama_State_Route_59" title="Alabama State Route 59">Alabama State Route 59</a>).
</p><p>According to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Census_Bureau" title="U.S. Census Bureau">U.S. Census Bureau</a>, the town has a total area of 32.2 square miles (83.4 km<sup>2</sup>), of which 31.9 square miles (82.5 km<sup>2</sup>) is land and 0.39 square miles (1.0 km<sup>2</sup>), or 1.15%, is water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census_2010_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Census_2010-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>

<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-8">[8]</a></sup> of 2010, there were 1,632 people, 643 households, and 453 families residing in the town. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 51 people per square mile (19.8/km²). There were 737 housing units at an average density of 22.9 per square mile (8.8/km²). The racial makeup of the town was 84.4% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">White</a>, 9.4% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Black</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">African American</a>, 0.7% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Native American</a>, 0.6% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Asian</a>, 3.4% from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 1.5% from two or more races. 5.1% of the population were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Latino</a> of any race.
</p><p>There were 643 households out of which 29.5% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 49.9% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 14.6% had a female householder with no husband present, and 29.5% were non-families. 25.8% of all households were made up of individuals and 10.6% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.53 and the average family size was 3.00.
</p><p>In the town, the population was spread out with 23.9% under the age of 18, 10.4% from 18 to 24, 29.4% from 25 to 44, 26.0% from 45 to 64, and 12.3% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 36.2 years. For every 100 females, there were 99.0 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 102.3 males.
</p><p>The median income for a household in the town was $32,803, and the median income for a family was $36,042. Males had a median income of $36,563 versus $27,566 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the town was $19,796. About 16.7% of families and 20.8% of the population were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 34.6% of those under age 18 and 13.1% of those age 65 or over.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government">Government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Loxley,_Alabama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Loxley is an incorporated town located in <a href="/wiki/Baldwin_County,_Alabama" title="Baldwin County, Alabama">Baldwin County, Alabama</a>. It is governed by a mayor and city council, both of which are elected by popular vote every four years.
</p>
