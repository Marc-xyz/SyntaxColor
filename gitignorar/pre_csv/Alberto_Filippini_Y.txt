<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alberto_Filippini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Atalanta">Atalanta</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alberto_Filippini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Atalanta">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in Brescia, Lombardy, Filippini started his career at another Lombard club <a href="/wiki/Atalanta_B.C." title="Atalanta B.C.">Atalanta</a>, which based in Bergamo. Filippini made his professional debut on 21 August 2005, against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A.C._Siena" title="A.C. Siena">Siena</a> in <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_Coppa_Italia" title="2005–06 Coppa Italia">2005–06 Coppa Italia</a>. He was a substitute of <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Soncin" title="Andrea Soncin">Andrea Soncin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Filippini also played for the reserve team of Atalanta from 2004 to 2006. From 2006 to 2008, Filippini was loaned to four <a href="/wiki/Lega_Pro_Prima_Divisione" title="Lega Pro Prima Divisione">Serie C1</a> clubs, namely <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salernitana_Sport" title="Salernitana Sport">Salernitana</a> (with <a href="/wiki/Dario_Bergamelli" title="Dario Bergamelli">Bergamelli</a>), <a href="/wiki/U.S._Sassuolo_Calcio" title="U.S. Sassuolo Calcio">Sassuolo</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/S.S.C._Venezia" title="S.S.C. Venezia">Venezia</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/S.S._Manfredonia_Calcio" title="S.S. Manfredonia Calcio">Manfredonia</a> (with <a href="/wiki/Davide_Mandorlini" title="Davide Mandorlini">Mandorlini</a> from Venice).
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Padova">Padova</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alberto_Filippini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Padova">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In summer 2008 <a href="/wiki/Calcio_Padova" title="Calcio Padova">Padova</a> signed Filippini in a temporary deal, as his fifth Serie C1 club. The club won promotion to Serie B in 2009. In June 2009 Filippini was signed by Padova in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Co-ownership_(football)" title="Co-ownership (football)">co-ownership deal</a> in a 3-year contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Padova also signed his ex-Atalanta team-mate <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Soncin" title="Andrea Soncin">Andrea Soncin</a> in August 2009, who also plays as a forward.
</p><p>On 1 February 2010 Filippini returned to the third level of Italian football for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Calcio_Como" title="Calcio Como">Calcio Como</a>, as his sixth Serie C1 club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In June 2010 Padova acquired him outright<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> for free.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Lega_Pro">Return to Lega Pro</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alberto_Filippini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Return to Lega Pro">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the second half of <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Serie_B" title="2010–11 Serie B">2010–11 Serie B</a> season, Filippini returned to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Calcio_Como" title="Calcio Como">Calcio Como</a> again in a temporary deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In July 2011 Filippini was sold to Como outright<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> for <a href="/wiki/Free_transfer_(association_football)" title="Free transfer (association football)">free</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>On 21 January 2012 Filippini was signed by <a href="/wiki/U.S._Cremonese" title="U.S. Cremonese">Cremonese</a>, his seventh Serie C1 club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>Since 2013 Filippini had played for <a href="/wiki/S.E.F._Torres_1903" title="S.E.F. Torres 1903">Torres</a> (his first Serie C2 club), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/S.P.A.L._2013" title="S.P.A.L. 2013">SPAL</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pordenone_Calcio" title="Pordenone Calcio">Pordenone</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Cosenza_Calcio" title="Cosenza Calcio">Cosenza</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
