<p><b>Sotirios "Sotiris" Gioulekas</b> (<a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>: <span lang="el">Σωτήρης Γκιουλέκας</span>, <small>Greek pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Greek" title="Help:IPA/Greek">[so'tiɾios ɟu'lekas]</a></span>, born November 28, 1979) is a Greek professional <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a> player.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He is a 6 ft 7.5 in (2.02 m) tall <a href="/wiki/Small_forward" title="Small forward">small forward</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">Professional career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sotirios_Gioulekas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Professional career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In his pro career, Gioulekas has played with the following clubs: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gymnastikos_S._Larissas_BC" title="Gymnastikos S. Larissas BC">GS Larissas</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olympia_Larissas_BC" title="Olympia Larissas BC">Olympia Larissa</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sellbytel_Baskets_N%C3%BCrnberg" title="Sellbytel Baskets Nürnberg">Baskets Nürnberg</a>. In the later stages of the <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_Lega_Basket_Serie_A" title="2005–06 Lega Basket Serie A">2005–06 season</a> he signed for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Virtus_Bologna" title="Virtus Bologna">Virtus Bologna</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In April 2007 he signed for <a href="/wiki/Orlandina_Basket" title="Orlandina Basket">Orlandina</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In July 2007 he signed an annual contract with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Societa_Veroli_Basket" title="Societa Veroli Basket">Veroli</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In December 2008 Gioulekas reached an agreement with <a href="/wiki/New_Basket_Brindisi" title="New Basket Brindisi">Brindisi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In 2009 Gioulekas signed for the <a href="/wiki/Serie_A2_Basket" title="Serie A2 Basket">Italian A2</a> club <a href="/wiki/Scafati_Basket" title="Scafati Basket">Scafati</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2011 he returned to Greece for third-tier team <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aries_Trikala_B.C." title="Aries Trikala B.C.">Aries Trikala</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In the summer of 2013 he signed for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Koroivos_BC" title="Koroivos BC">Koroivos</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Greek_A2_Basket_League" title="Greek A2 Basket League">Greek A2 League</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> On 29 August 2014 he signed for Greek A2 team <a href="/wiki/Filippos_Veria" title="Filippos Veria">Filippos Veria</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In June 2015, he signed to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iraklis_Thessaloniki_B.C." title="Iraklis Thessaloniki B.C.">Iraklis Thessaloniki</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sotirios_Gioulekas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gioulekas has represented <a href="/wiki/Greece_national_basketball_team" title="Greece national basketball team">Greece</a> at youth level. He has played a total of 25 games for the youth sides averaging 7.8 points per game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hbf_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-hbf-11">[11]</a></sup> Gioulekas was a in the <a href="/wiki/Greece_men%27s_national_under-16_basketball_team" title="Greece men's national under-16 basketball team">Greek</a> roster that won the bronze medal at the <a href="/wiki/1995_FIBA_Europe_Under-16_Championship" title="1995 FIBA Europe Under-16 Championship">1995 FIBA Europe Under-16 Championship</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hbf_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-hbf-11">[11]</a></sup> During the tournament he played in all 7 games averaging 11.6 points and 3.4 rebounds per game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal">Personal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sotirios_Gioulekas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2013 Gioulekas received his degree in <a href="/wiki/Sports_science" title="Sports science">Sports science</a> from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Thessaly" title="University of Thessaly">University of Thessaly</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tefaa_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-tefaa-13">[13]</a></sup> He is married and the father of twins.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tefaa_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-tefaa-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p>
