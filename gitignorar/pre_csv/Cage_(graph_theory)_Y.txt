	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Cage (graph theory)</h1>

<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">mathematical</a> area of <a href="/wiki/Graph_theory" title="Graph theory">graph theory</a>, a <b>cage</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Regular_graph" title="Regular graph">regular graph</a> that has as few <a href="/wiki/Vertex_(graph_theory)" title="Vertex (graph theory)">vertices</a> as possible for its <a href="/wiki/Girth_(graph_theory)" title="Girth (graph theory)">girth</a>.
</p><p>Formally, an (<i>r</i>,<i>g</i>)-graph is defined to be a graph in which each vertex has exactly <i>r</i> neighbors, and in which the shortest <a href="/wiki/Cycle_graph" title="Cycle graph">cycle</a> has length exactly <i>g</i>. It is known that an (<i>r</i>,<i>g</i>)-graph exists for any combination of <i>r</i> ≥ 2 and <i>g</i> ≥ 3. An (<i>r</i>,<i>g</i>)-cage is an (<i>r</i>,<i>g</i>)-graph with the fewest possible number of vertices, among all (<i>r</i>,<i>g</i>)-graphs.
</p><p>If a <a href="/wiki/Moore_graph" title="Moore graph">Moore graph</a> exists with degree <i>r</i> and girth <i>g</i>, it must be a cage. Moreover, the bounds on the sizes of Moore graphs generalize to cages: any cage with odd girth <i>g</i> must have at least

<p>vertices. Any (<i>r</i>,<i>g</i>)-graph with exactly this many vertices is by definition a Moore graph and therefore automatically a cage.
</p><p>There may exist multiple cages for a given combination of <i>r</i> and <i>g</i>. For instance there are three nonisomorphic (3,10)-cages, each with 70 vertices : the <a href="/wiki/Balaban_10-cage" title="Balaban 10-cage">Balaban 10-cage</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Harries_graph" title="Harries graph">Harries graph</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Harries%E2%80%93Wong_graph" title="Harries–Wong graph">Harries–Wong graph</a>. But there is only one (3,11)-cage : the <a href="/wiki/Balaban_11-cage" title="Balaban 11-cage">Balaban 11-cage</a> (with 112 vertices).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Known_cages">Known cages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cage_(graph_theory)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Known cages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A degree-one graph has no cycle, and a connected degree-two graph has girth equal to its number of vertices, so cages are only of interest for <i>r</i> ≥ 3. The (<i>r</i>,3)-cage is a <a href="/wiki/Complete_graph" title="Complete graph">complete graph</a> <i>K</i><sub><i>r</i>+1</sub> on <i>r</i>+1 vertices, and the (<i>r</i>,4)-cage is a <a href="/wiki/Complete_bipartite_graph" title="Complete bipartite graph">complete bipartite graph</a> <i>K</i><sub><i>r</i>,<i>r</i></sub> on 2<i>r</i> vertices.
</p><p>Other notable cages include the Moore graphs:
</p>
<ul><li>(3,5)-cage: the <a href="/wiki/Petersen_graph" title="Petersen graph">Petersen graph</a>, 10 vertices</li>
<li>(3,6)-cage: the <a href="/wiki/Heawood_graph" title="Heawood graph">Heawood graph</a>, 14 vertices</li>
<li>(3,8)-cage: the <a href="/wiki/Tutte%E2%80%93Coxeter_graph" title="Tutte–Coxeter graph">Tutte–Coxeter graph</a>, 30 vertices</li>
<li>(3,10)-cage: the <a href="/wiki/Balaban_10-cage" title="Balaban 10-cage">Balaban 10-cage</a>, 70 vertices</li>
<li>(3,11)-cage: the <a href="/wiki/Balaban_11-cage" title="Balaban 11-cage">Balaban 11-cage</a>, 112 vertices</li>
<li>(4,5)-cage: the <a href="/wiki/Robertson_graph" title="Robertson graph">Robertson graph</a>, 19 vertices</li>
<li>(7,5)-cage: The <a href="/wiki/Hoffman%E2%80%93Singleton_graph" title="Hoffman–Singleton graph">Hoffman–Singleton graph</a>, 50 vertices.</li>
<li>When <i>r</i> − 1 is a prime power, the (<i>r</i>,6) cages are the incidence graphs of <a href="/wiki/Projective_plane" title="Projective plane">projective planes</a>.</li>
<li>When <i>r</i> − 1 is a prime power, the (<i>r</i>,8) and (<i>r</i>,12) cages are <a href="/wiki/Generalized_polygon" title="Generalized polygon">generalized polygons</a>.</li></ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Asymptotics">Asymptotics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cage_(graph_theory)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Asymptotics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

</p><p>It is unlikely that these graphs are themselves cages, but their existence gives an upper bound to the number of vertices needed in a cage.
