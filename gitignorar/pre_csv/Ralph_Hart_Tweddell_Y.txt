	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Ralph Hart Tweddell</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>

<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ralph_Hart_Tweddell_-_Cassier%27s_1895-01.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2850" data-file-width="2036" decoding="async" height="308" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/Ralph_Hart_Tweddell_-_Cassier%27s_1895-01.png/220px-Ralph_Hart_Tweddell_-_Cassier%27s_1895-01.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/Ralph_Hart_Tweddell_-_Cassier%27s_1895-01.png/330px-Ralph_Hart_Tweddell_-_Cassier%27s_1895-01.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/Ralph_Hart_Tweddell_-_Cassier%27s_1895-01.png/440px-Ralph_Hart_Tweddell_-_Cassier%27s_1895-01.png 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ralph_Hart_Tweddell_-_Cassier%27s_1895-01.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Ralph Hart Tweddell was featured in an article in <i><a href="/wiki/Cassier%27s_Magazine" title="Cassier's Magazine">Cassier's Magazine</a></i> in January 1895, accompanied by this photo.</div></div></div>
<p><b>Ralph Hart Tweddell</b> (25 May 1843 – 3 September 1895) was a British mechanical engineer, known particularly for inventing the portable hydraulic <a href="/wiki/Rivet" title="Rivet">riveter</a>, which greatly facilitated the construction of boilers, bridges and ships.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ralph_Hart_Tweddell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tweddell was born in <a href="/wiki/South_Shields" title="South Shields">South Shields</a> in 1843. His father, Marshall Tweddell, was a shipowner. He was educated at <a href="/wiki/Cheltenham_College" title="Cheltenham College">Cheltenham College</a>, with the intention of entering the army. However, more interested in engineering, he was apprenticed to <a href="/wiki/R_and_W_Hawthorn" title="R and W Hawthorn">R and W Hawthorn</a>, a locomotive manufacturer in Newcastle upon Tyne.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wikis_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-wikis-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-grace_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-grace-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>During his apprenticeship he took out a patent in 1865 for a portable hydraulic apparatus for fixing the ends of <a href="/wiki/Fire-tube_boiler" title="Fire-tube boiler">boiler tubes in tube plates</a>. The encouraging results suggested that hydraulic power should be used for machines used in boiler construction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wikis_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-wikis-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1865 he designed a stationary hydraulic riveting machine. This was more effective than the existing mechanical riveting machines, which usually did not make steam-tight joints in the boilers of steamships, which were by then operating under higher pressure than before. The plant, consisting of a pump, an accumulator, and a riveter, was first used by Thompson, Boyd &amp; Co., of Newcastle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wikis_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-wikis-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-grace_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-grace-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_portable_riveter">The portable riveter</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ralph_Hart_Tweddell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The portable riveter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1871 Tweddell invented a portable riveter, so that the work did not have to be brought to the machine. It was manufactured by <a href="/wiki/Fielding_%26_Platt" title="Fielding &amp; Platt">Fielding &amp; Platt</a>; an early user was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Armstrong,_Mitchell_and_Company" title="Armstrong, Mitchell and Company">Armstrong, Mitchell and Company</a> in Newcastle. In 1873 it was used in riveting a <a href="/wiki/Lattice_girder" title="Lattice girder">lattice girder</a> bridge carrying Primrose Street over the Great Eastern railway at <a href="/wiki/Bishopsgate_railway_station" title="Bishopsgate railway station">Bishopsgate railway station</a> in London. The success of this led to its use in the construction of other bridges.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wikis_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-wikis-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-grace_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-grace-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>The portable riveter was first used for locomotive work by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F._W._Webb" title="F. W. Webb">F. W. Webb</a> at <a href="/wiki/Crewe_Works" title="Crewe Works">Crewe Works</a>. It was also used for agricultural machinery, for underframes of railway carriages, and by the Italian government for gun-carriages. In France, Tweddell's system was used when in 1874 the French government began to build iron warships in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Military_port_of_Toulon" title="Military port of Toulon">Toulon</a>, and it was later used at the shipyard at Penhoët near <a href="/wiki/Saint-Nazaire" title="Saint-Nazaire">Saint-Nazaire</a>, and at <a href="/wiki/Brest,_France" title="Brest, France">Brest</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wikis_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-wikis-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-grace_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-grace-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1885 he was awarded a gold medal, under the Howard Trust, at the <a href="/wiki/International_Inventions_Exhibition" title="International Inventions Exhibition">International Inventions Exhibition</a> in South Kensington for "his system of applying hydraulic power to the working of machine tools, and for the rivetting and other machines which he has invented in connection with that system".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>He wrote the paper "On Machine Tools and Labour-saving Appliances worked by Hydraulic Pressure" for the <a href="/wiki/Institution_of_Civil_Engineers" title="Institution of Civil Engineers">Institution of Civil Engineers</a>, for which he was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Telford_Medal" title="Telford Medal">Telford Medal</a> and premium in 1883. He sent three papers to the <a href="/wiki/Institution_of_Mechanical_Engineers" title="Institution of Mechanical Engineers">Institution of Mechanical Engineers</a> including "On the Application of Water Pressure to Shop-tools and Mechanical Engineering Works". In 1890 a paper entitled "The Application of Water Pressure to Machine Tools and Appliances" was awarded a Bessemer Premium by the <a href="/wiki/Society_of_Engineers_UK" title="Society of Engineers UK">Society of Engineers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wikis_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-wikis-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>He became a member of the Institution of Mechanical Engineers in 1867. From 1879 he was a member of the Institution of Civil Engineers. He was also a member of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Soci%C3%A9t%C3%A9_des_ing%C3%A9nieurs_civils_de_France&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Société des ingénieurs civils de France (page does not exist)">Société des ingénieurs civils de France</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;"> [<a class="extiw" href="https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soci%C3%A9t%C3%A9_des_ing%C3%A9nieurs_civils_de_France" title="fr:Société des ingénieurs civils de France">fr</a>]</span> from 1879.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wikis_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-wikis-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-grace_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-grace-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1875 Tweddell married Hannah Mary Grey. In his spare time he was interested in hunting, shooting and fishing. He died in 1895 at his home near <a href="/wiki/Gravesend" title="Gravesend">Gravesend</a> in Kent, two years after a riding accident which affected his health.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wikis_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-wikis-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-grace_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-grace-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
