<p><b>Frelsdorf</b> is a village and a former municipality in the <a href="/wiki/Cuxhaven_(district)" title="Cuxhaven (district)">district of Cuxhaven</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Lower_Saxony" title="Lower Saxony">Lower Saxony</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. Since 1 November 2011, it is part of the municipality <a href="/wiki/Beverstedt" title="Beverstedt">Beverstedt</a>.
</p><p>Frelsdorf belonged to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archdiocese_of_Bremen" title="Archdiocese of Bremen">Prince-Archbishopric of Bremen</a>, established in 1180. In 1648 the Prince-Archbishopric was transformed into the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Bremen" title="Duchy of Bremen">Duchy of Bremen</a>, which was first ruled in <a href="/wiki/Personal_union" title="Personal union">personal union</a> by the Swedish Crown - interrupted by a Danish occupation (1712–1715) - and from 1715 on by the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Hanover" title="House of Hanover">Hanoverian</a> Crown. In 1807 the ephemeric <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Westphalia" title="Kingdom of Westphalia">Kingdom of Westphalia</a> annexed the Duchy, before <a href="/wiki/First_French_Empire" title="First French Empire">France</a> annexed it in 1810. In 1813 the Duchy was restored to the <a href="/wiki/Electorate_of_Hanover" title="Electorate of Hanover">Electorate of Hanover</a>, which - after its upgrade to the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Hanover" title="Kingdom of Hanover">Kingdom of Hanover</a> in 1814 - incorporated the Duchy in a <a href="/wiki/Real_union" title="Real union">real union</a> and the Ducal territory, including Frelsdorf, became part of the new <a href="/wiki/Stade_(region)" title="Stade (region)">Stade Region</a>, established in 1823.
</p>
