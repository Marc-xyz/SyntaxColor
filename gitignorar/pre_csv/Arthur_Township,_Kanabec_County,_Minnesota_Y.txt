<p><b>Arthur Township</b> is a township in <a href="/wiki/Kanabec_County,_Minnesota" title="Kanabec County, Minnesota">Kanabec County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a>, United States. The population was 1,905 at the 2000 census.
</p><p>Arthur Township was organized in 1883, and named for <a href="/wiki/Chester_A._Arthur" title="Chester A. Arthur">Chester A. Arthur</a>, 21st President of the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arthur_Township,_Kanabec_County,_Minnesota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the township has a total area of 31.3 square miles (81 km<sup>2</sup>), of which 30.3 square miles (78 km<sup>2</sup>) of it is land and 1.0 square mile (2.6 km<sup>2</sup>) of it (3.16%) is water.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arthur_Township,_Kanabec_County,_Minnesota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-1">[1]</a></sup> of 2000, there were 1,905 people, 672 households, and 538 families residing in the township. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 62.8 people per square mile (24.3/km²). There were 729 housing units at an average density of 24.1/sq mi (9.3/km²). The racial makeup of the township was 96.64% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 0.31% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 0.42% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 0.58% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 0.52% from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 1.52% from two or more races. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 0.68% of the population.
</p><p>There were 672 households out of which 38.7% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 69.3% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 7.4% had a female householder with no husband present, and 19.9% were non-families. 14.6% of all households were made up of individuals and 5.2% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.82 and the average family size was 3.13.
</p><p>In the township the population was spread out with 29.1% under the age of 18, 7.2% from 18 to 24, 27.8% from 25 to 44, 25.0% from 45 to 64, and 10.8% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 37 years. For every 100 females, there were 103.3 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 101.5 males.
</p><p>The median income for a household in the township was $44,485, and the median income for a family was $50,192. Males had a median income of $35,521 versus $24,167 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the township was $18,506. About 4.2% of families and 5.9% of the population were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 5.2% of those under the age of 18 and 7.5% of those 65 and older.
</p>
