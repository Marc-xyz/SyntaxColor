<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Javi_Lara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Montoro" title="Montoro">Montoro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Province_of_C%C3%B3rdoba_(Spain)" title="Province of Córdoba (Spain)">Córdoba</a>, Lara graduated from local <a href="/wiki/C%C3%B3rdoba_CF" title="Córdoba CF">Córdoba CF</a>'s youth setup, and made his senior debut with <a href="/wiki/C%C3%B3rdoba_CF_B" title="Córdoba CF B">the reserves</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2003%E2%80%9304_Tercera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="2003–04 Tercera División">2003–04 season</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Tercera_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Tercera División">Tercera División</a>. After loan stints in <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n_B" title="Segunda División B">Segunda División B</a> with <a href="/wiki/CD_Villanueva" title="CD Villanueva">CD Villanueva</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Real_Uni%C3%B3n" title="Real Unión">Real Unión</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/%C3%89cija_Balompi%C3%A9" title="Écija Balompié">Écija Balompié</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> he moved to another <a href="/wiki/Reserve_team" title="Reserve team">reserve team</a>, <a href="/wiki/UD_Almer%C3%ADa_B" title="UD Almería B">UD Almería B</a>, in August 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>In the summer of 2008, Lara joined <a href="/wiki/RSD_Alcal%C3%A1" title="RSD Alcalá">RSD Alcalá</a> also of the fourth division. On 20 July 2009 he signed a two-year contract with <a href="/wiki/Elche_CF" title="Elche CF">Elche CF</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Segunda División">Segunda División</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He played his first match as a professional on 3 September 2009, starting and featuring the full 90 minutes in 2–3 away loss against <a href="/wiki/FC_Cartagena" title="FC Cartagena">FC Cartagena</a> for <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Copa_del_Rey" title="2009–10 Copa del Rey">the campaign</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Copa_del_Rey" title="Copa del Rey">Copa del Rey</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> he made his league debut three days later, coming on as a second-half <a href="/wiki/Substitute_(association_football)" title="Substitute (association football)">substitute</a> in a 1–4 loss at <a href="/wiki/CD_Numancia" title="CD Numancia">CD Numancia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>On 29 January 2010, after being sparingly used by the <a href="/wiki/Andalusia" title="Andalusia">Andalusians</a>, Lara was loaned to <a href="/wiki/CD_Alcoyano" title="CD Alcoyano">CD Alcoyano</a> until June.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He then moved to <a href="/wiki/Valencia_CF" title="Valencia CF">Valencia CF</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Valencia_CF_Mestalla" title="Valencia CF Mestalla">B-side</a> in January 2011 after being six months without a club,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> following which he competed in division three with <a href="/wiki/Lucena_CF" title="Lucena CF">Lucena CF</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and Alcoyano.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>On 17 July 2013, Lara signed a one-year deal with <a href="/wiki/SD_Ponferradina" title="SD Ponferradina">SD Ponferradina</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> On 24 June of the following year he moved to <a href="/wiki/SD_Eibar" title="SD Eibar">SD Eibar</a>, <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="2013–14 Segunda División">freshly promoted</a> to <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> He made his debut in the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_football_league_system" title="Spanish football league system">Spanish top flight</a> on 24 August 2014, starting and netting the game's only (also the club's first ever goal in the competition) through a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Direct_free_kick" title="Direct free kick">free kick</a> in a win against <a href="/wiki/Real_Sociedad" title="Real Sociedad">Real Sociedad</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Ipurua_Municipal_Stadium" title="Ipurua Municipal Stadium">Ipurua Municipal Stadium</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p><p>Lara moved abroad at the age of nearly 30, joining <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atl%C3%A9tico_de_Kolkata" title="Atlético de Kolkata">Atlético de Kolkata</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Super_League" title="Indian Super League">Indian Super League</a> on 17 July 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> He scored his first goal for the team on 13 October in their first home game of <a href="/wiki/2015_Indian_Super_League_season" title="2015 Indian Super League season">the season</a>, a 2–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Kerala_Blasters_FC" title="Kerala Blasters FC">Kerala Blasters FC</a> with <a href="/wiki/Pel%C3%A9" title="Pelé">Pelé</a> in attendance at the <a href="/wiki/Salt_Lake_Stadium" title="Salt Lake Stadium">Salt Lake Stadium</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2015, after being ruled out of the season due to an injury, Lara was consequently released and replaced by countryman <a href="/wiki/Jorge_Alonso" title="Jorge Alonso">Jorge Alonso</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> On 2 February of the following year, he returned to his home country after agreeing to a short-term deal with <a href="/wiki/CD_Tenerife" title="CD Tenerife">CD Tenerife</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>
</p><p>On 12 August 2016, Lara returned to India and Atlético Kolkata.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> On 10 January 2017, he signed for Córdoba until 30 June.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup>
</p><p>Lara returned to Spain in August 2019, with the 33-year-old joining <a href="/wiki/UD_Ibiza" title="UD Ibiza">UD Ibiza</a> for two seasons as a <a href="/wiki/Free_agent" title="Free agent">free agent</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup>
</p>
