<p><b>Cryers Hill</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Hamlet_(place)" title="Hamlet (place)">hamlet</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Parish" title="Parish">parish</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hughenden_Valley" title="Hughenden Valley">Hughenden</a> and in <a href="/wiki/Buckinghamshire" title="Buckinghamshire">Buckinghamshire</a>, England.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hughenden_Parish_Council_-_History_of_the_Parish_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hughenden_Parish_Council_-_History_of_the_Parish-1">[1]</a></sup> It was formerly known as Ravensmere (sometimes 'Ravening').
</p><p>The hamlet is sandwiched between <a href="/wiki/Great_Kingshill" title="Great Kingshill">Great Kingshill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hughenden_Valley" title="Hughenden Valley">Hughenden Valley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Widmer_End" title="Widmer End">Widmer End</a>.  Curiously, its primary school is called Great Kingshill school<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Great_Kingshill_CofE_Combined_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Great_Kingshill_CofE_Combined-2">[2]</a></sup>  and the crematorium at Cryers Hill is called Hughenden Crematorium. This is located in Four Ashes Lane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cryers_Hill,_Four_Ashes_Road_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cryers_Hill,_Four_Ashes_Road-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>The only pub within Cryers Hill is The White Lion, serving traditional ales and food.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-White_Lion_Cryers_Hill_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-White_Lion_Cryers_Hill-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-White_Lion_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-White_Lion-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>The hamlet and surrounding area provided the setting for <a href="/wiki/Kitty_Aldridge" title="Kitty Aldridge">Kitty Aldridge</a>'s 2007 novel called "Cryers Hill". The book partly documents the significant expansion of housing in the area during the 1960s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tele2007_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tele2007-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/Four_Ashes,_Buckinghamshire" title="Four Ashes, Buckinghamshire">Four Ashes</a> Road, there is a large country house with a Georgian facade called 'Uplands' which is set in 18 acres (73,000 m<sup>2</sup>) of gardens. It is now a 74-room De Vere Venues hotel and conference centre.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-De_Vere_Venues:_Uplands_House_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-De_Vere_Venues:_Uplands_House-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>In recent years, an apparition of the <a href="/wiki/Green_Man" title="Green Man">Green Man</a> is alleged to have been sighted on at least two occasions at Cryers Hill.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Green_Man_of_Hughenden_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Green_Man_of_Hughenden-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
