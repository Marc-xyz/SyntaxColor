<p>The <b>Spatangidae</b> are a family of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heart_urchin" title="Heart urchin">heart urchins</a>. There are four recognised genera within the family; <i><a href="/wiki/Maretia" title="Maretia">Maretia</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paramaretia" title="Paramaretia">Paramaretia</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pseudomaretia" title="Pseudomaretia">Pseudomaretia</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Spatangus" title="Spatangus">Spatangus</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-worms_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-worms-1">[1]</a></sup> Additionally, <i>Spatagus</i> and <i>Prospatangus</i> were previously recognised genera within the Spatangidae, but are now accepted as part of the genus <i>Spatangus</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-worms_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-worms-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Spatangidae are marine heart urchins that <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deposit_feeder" title="Deposit feeder">feed on subsurface deposits</a> and <a href="/wiki/Grazing" title="Grazing">graze</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-worms_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-worms-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
