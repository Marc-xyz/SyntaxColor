<p><b>Con Funk Shun</b> (formerly known as <b>Project Soul</b>) is an American <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> and <a href="/wiki/Funk" title="Funk">funk</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Band_(music)" title="Band (music)">band</a> whose popularity began in the mid-1970s and ran through the 1980s. Influences included <a href="/wiki/Earth,_Wind_%26_Fire" title="Earth, Wind &amp; Fire">Earth, Wind &amp; Fire</a>; <a href="/wiki/Commodores" title="Commodores">Commodores</a>; <a href="/wiki/Chaka_Khan" title="Chaka Khan">Chaka Khan</a>; and <a href="/wiki/Sly_and_the_Family_Stone" title="Sly and the Family Stone">Sly and the Family Stone</a>. Signed to <a href="/wiki/Mercury_Records" title="Mercury Records">Mercury Records</a> in 1976, Con Funk Shun enjoyed a decade of successful national and overseas tours, eleven chart-topping albums, and numerous hit singles, including a <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> magazine "Number One With A Bullet" hit single on the Top R&amp;B Singles chart. The group formally disbanded in 1986.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Con_Funk_Shun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The band began in 1969 as "Project Soul", formed by <a href="/wiki/Louis_A._McCall_Sr." title="Louis A. McCall Sr.">Louis A. McCall Sr.</a> (drums/percussion/vocals) and Michael Cooper (rhythm guitar/lead vocals), both of whom were <a href="/wiki/Secondary_school" title="Secondary school">high school</a> students in <a href="/wiki/Vallejo,_California" title="Vallejo, California">Vallejo, California</a>. By 1971, the band also included bassist Cedric Martin, keyboardist Danny "Sweet Man" Thomas, trumpeter Karl Fuller, and <a href="/wiki/Woodwind_instrument" title="Woodwind instrument">woodwinds</a> player Paul "Maceo" Harrell. Soon thereafter, this fledgling group's classic lineup became complete when  lead vocals/multi-instrumentalist <a href="/wiki/Felton_Pilate" title="Felton Pilate">Felton Pilate</a> came onboard. In 1971, the seven musicians formed a new band, calling themselves "Con-Funk-Shun", after a song by the instrumental ensemble <a href="/wiki/New_Birth_(band)" title="New Birth (band)">The Nite-Liters</a>. They relocated to <a href="/wiki/Memphis,_Tennessee" title="Memphis, Tennessee">Memphis, Tennessee</a> in 1973 when they were hired to back up <a href="/wiki/Stax_Records" title="Stax Records">Stax Records</a> artists <a href="/wiki/The_Soul_Children" title="The Soul Children">The Soul Children</a>. There, they came to the attention of <a href="/wiki/Estelle_Axton" title="Estelle Axton">Estelle Axton</a> who signed them to her "Fretone Records" label. Their début album, <i>Organized Con Funk Shun</i>, was released in 1973. The seven band members chose to change the name of the band to "Con Funk Shun" (dropping the hyphens) in 1974.
</p><p>In 1976, Con Funk Shun signed to <a href="/wiki/Mercury_Records" title="Mercury Records">Mercury Records</a>, and then went on to release eleven albums over a span of ten years. The band's 1977 album, <i>Secrets</i>, was certified <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gold_album" title="Gold album">gold</a> in the United States, as were the 1978 album <i>Loveshine</i>, the 1979 album <i>Candy</i>, and the 1980 album <i>Spirit of Love</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> They scored a string of <a href="/wiki/Top_40" title="Top 40">top ten</a> <a href="/wiki/Hit_song" title="Hit song">hit songs</a> on the U.S. <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_R%26B/Hip-Hop_Songs" title="Hot R&amp;B/Hip-Hop Songs">black singles</a> <a href="/wiki/Record_chart" title="Record chart">chart</a>, including "<a href="/wiki/Ffun" title="Ffun">Ffun</a>" (No. 1 in 1978), "Shake and Dance with Me" (No. 5 in 1978), "Chase Me" (No. 4 in 1979), "Got to Be Enough" (No. 8 in 1980), "Too Tight" (No. 8 in 1981), "Baby I'm Hooked (Right into Your Love)" (No. 5 in 1983), and "Electric Lady" (No. 4 in 1985). Tensions from within the group built over the 1980s, and the band's final album, <i>Burning Love</i>, was recorded without multi instrument-vocalist and songwriter Felton Pilate. After disappointing album sales, Mercury invoked its "key member clause" and dropped the band from the label. Michael Cooper then moved on to a solo run with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Records" title="Warner Bros. Records">Warner Bros</a>. The remaining original members tried to continue, but repeated efforts with new lead singers fell short, and they ceased performing and recording as Con Funk Shun in 1987.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_years">Later years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Con_Funk_Shun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Later years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pilate became the in-house record producer and songwriter for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M.C._Hammer" title="M.C. Hammer">M.C. Hammer</a> and his record label <a href="/wiki/MC_Hammer_discography" title="MC Hammer discography">Bust It Records</a>. He worked on several albums for the company, including Hammer's <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Please_Hammer,_Don%27t_Hurt_%27Em" title="Please Hammer, Don't Hurt 'Em">Please Hammer, Don't Hurt 'Em</a></i> album, as well as the bulk of <a href="/wiki/Special_Generation_(band)" title="Special Generation (band)">Special Generation</a>'s 1990 début album, <i>Take It to the Floor</i>. Lead singer Michael Cooper embarked on a successful career as a solo artist, releasing his solo début album <i>Love Is Such A Funny Game</i> on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Records" title="Warner Bros. Records">Warner Bros. Records</a> in 1987, and releasing the 1989 album <i>Just What I Like</i> and the 1992 album <i>Get Closer</i> on Warner Bros.' sister label <a href="/wiki/Reprise_Records" title="Reprise Records">Reprise Records</a>.
</p><p>As recently as 2013, their eleven Mercury Records albums, along with their <i>Greatest Hits</i> and <i>Best Of Con Funk Shun</i> albums, continue to be remastered and released digitally.
</p><p>In 2015, the <i>More Than Love</i> album was released. Three of the original band members, Michael Cooper, Felton Pilate and Karl Fuller, currently tour as Con Funk Shun.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In March 2017, they celebrated their 45th anniversary as a band with four sold-out shows at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yoshi%27s_(jazz_club)" title="Yoshi's (jazz club)">Yoshi's</a> in <a href="/wiki/Oakland,_California" title="Oakland, California">Oakland, California</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Con_Funk_Shun&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As the late Louis A. McCall Sr. was born in <a href="/wiki/Alameda,_California" title="Alameda, California">Alameda, California</a>, his name was chosen in April 2014 by the Alameda City Planning Board to be used for one of the street names in the proposed residential community, Alameda Landing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>On September 21, 2014, the seven band members were honored by the National R&amp;B Music Society with a Lifetime Achievement Award, at a black tie dinner and award ceremony in <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_City,_New_Jersey" title="Atlantic City, New Jersey">Atlantic City, New Jersey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
