<p><i><b>Ben Bowyang</b></i> was an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australian" title="Australian">Australian</a> newspaper <a href="/wiki/Comic_strip" title="Comic strip">comic strip</a> created by the cartoonist <a href="/wiki/Alexander_George_Gurney" title="Alexander George Gurney">Alex Gurney</a>. It debuted in 1936.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the misadventures of two <a href="/wiki/Larrikin" title="Larrikin">larrikin</a> soldiers, the strip was based on the "Gunn's Gully" newspaper humor columns written during the 1920s and 1930s by <a href="/wiki/C._J._Dennis" title="C. J. Dennis">C. J. Dennis</a> of the <i>Melbourne Herald</i>. Dennis wrote about a philosophical farmer, Ben Bowyang, from Gunn's Gully.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>The first six installments appeared on a full page in the <i>Picture-News</i> in November and the comic strip was moved to the <i>Sun News-Pictorial</i> on 1 February 1940.
</p><p>The strip was also drawn by Mick Armstrong, Keith Martin, Sir <a href="/wiki/Lionel_Lindsay" title="Lionel Lindsay">Lionel Lindsay</a>, Alex McRae, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Russell-Clarke" title="Peter Russell-Clarke">Peter Russell-Clarke</a> and Sam Wells. During its later years it appeared in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Melbourne_Herald" title="The Melbourne Herald">The Melbourne Herald</a></i>, where it ceased publication in 1979. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
