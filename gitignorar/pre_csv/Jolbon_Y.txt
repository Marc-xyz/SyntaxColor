	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Jolbon</h1>

		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><table class="infobox" style="width: 22em"><tbody><tr><td colspan="2" style="text-align:center;font-size: 125%; background-color: #b0c4de;">Jolbon</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" style="font-weight: normal;"><a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a></th><td><div style="display:inline;font-size:1rem;"><span lang="ko-Hang" title="Korean language text"><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%EC%A1%B8" title="wikt:졸">졸</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%EB%B3%B8" title="wikt:본">본</a></span></div></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" style="font-weight: normal;"><a href="/wiki/Hanja" title="Hanja">Hanja</a></th><td><div style="display:inline;font-size:1rem;"><span lang="ko-Hani" title="Korean language text"><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%8D%92" title="wikt:卒">卒</a><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E6%9C%AC" title="wikt:本">本</a></span></div></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" style="font-weight: normal;"><a href="/wiki/Revised_Romanization_of_Korean" title="Revised Romanization of Korean">Revised Romanization</a></th><td><i lang="ko-Latn" title="Revised Romanization of Korean transliteration">Jolbon</i></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" style="font-weight: normal;"><a href="/wiki/McCune%E2%80%93Reischauer" title="McCune–Reischauer">McCune–Reischauer</a></th><td><i lang="ko-Latn" title="McCune–Reischauer transliteration">Cholpon</i></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p><b>Jolbon</b> was the capital of a Korean tribal state which arose in the north of the Korean peninsula<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Jolbon is thought to had been in modern <a href="/wiki/Wun%C3%BC_Mountain" title="Wunü Mountain">Wunü Mountain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liaoning" title="Liaoning">Liaoning</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 37 BCE, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jumong" title="Jumong">Jumong</a> had fled from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dongbuyeo" title="Dongbuyeo">Dongbuyeo</a> to avoid death at the hands of Dongbuyeo's Crown Prince <a href="/wiki/Daeso" title="Daeso">Daeso</a>, who presented great jealousy towards Jumong.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> After he fled, Jumong established a new kingdom in 37 BCE called <a href="/wiki/Goguryeo" title="Goguryeo">Goguryeo</a> in the Jolbon region. In Jolbon, he married <a href="/wiki/Soseono" title="Soseono">Soseono</a> (or So Seo-no), who was the daughter of a local tribal leader.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Jolbon was the first capital city of the ancient Korean Kingdom of Goguryeo from 37 BCE – 3 CE. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Injae_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Injae-5">[5]</a></sup> The second ruler, the son of Jumong, <a href="/wiki/Yuri_of_Goguryeo" title="Yuri of Goguryeo">Yuri</a>, moved its capital to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gungnae_Fortress" title="Gungnae Fortress">Gungnae Fortress</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
