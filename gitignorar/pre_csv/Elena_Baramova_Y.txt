<p><b>Elena Baramova</b> (<a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_language" title="Bulgarian language">Bulgarian</a>: Елена Баръмова) is a Bulgarian-born operatic soprano.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elena_Baramova&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in Bulgaria, Elena Baramova studied opera singing at the <a href="/wiki/National_Academy_of_Music_(Bulgaria)" title="National Academy of Music (Bulgaria)">National Academy of Music (Bulgaria)</a>, Sofia, under the tutelage of Professor Konstanza Vatchkova, later at the Boris Christoff Academy in Rome, under <a href="/wiki/Mirella_Parutto" title="Mirella Parutto">Mirella Parutto</a>, and from 2001, under the guidance of <a href="/wiki/Ghena_Dimitrova" title="Ghena Dimitrova">Ghena Dimitrova</a>. In 2001-02 she sang in concerts with Ghena Dimitrova in Bulgaria, Vienna, Prague and Budapest.
</p><p>Baramova won competition prizes at the Julian Gaiare International, <a href="/wiki/Pamplona" title="Pamplona">Pamplona</a>, produced by <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Carreras" title="José Carreras">José Carreras</a>, and at the Rijeka Belcanto International Opera Competition in Croatia. She was a laureate at the Hristo Brambarov International Competition, and won first prize for best performance of Hungarian and Bulgarian songs at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hungarian_Cultural_Institute" title="Hungarian Cultural Institute">Hungarian Cultural Institute</a>, Sofia.
</p><p>She performed at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Opera_and_Ballet" title="National Opera and Ballet">Sofia National Opera</a> as "Donna Elvira" in <i><a href="/wiki/Don_Giovanni" title="Don Giovanni">Don Giovanni</a></i>, "Lady Macbeth" in <i><a href="/wiki/Macbeth_(opera)" title="Macbeth (opera)">Macbeth</a></i>, "Elvira" in <i><a href="/wiki/Ernani" title="Ernani">Ernani</a></i>, "Minnie" in <i><a href="/wiki/La_fanciulla_del_West" title="La fanciulla del West">La fanciulla del West</a></i>, and in  <i><a href="/wiki/Tosca" title="Tosca">Tosca</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Turandot" title="Turandot">Turandot</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Norma_(opera)" title="Norma (opera)">Norma</a></i>, and gave guest performances of "Aida" at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polish_National_Opera" title="Polish National Opera">Polish National Opera</a>, <a href="/wiki/Avenches" title="Avenches">Avenches</a> Opera Festival, the Classic Openair Solothurn, Switzerland, <a href="/wiki/Cairo_Opera_House" title="Cairo Opera House">Cairo Opera House</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Taipei_Arena" title="Taipei Arena">Taipei Arena</a>, Taiwan in a <a href="/wiki/Teatro_dell%27Opera_di_Roma" title="Teatro dell'Opera di Roma">Teatro dell'Opera di Roma</a> and <a href="/wiki/Taipei_Symphony_Orchestra" title="Taipei Symphony Orchestra">Taipei Symphony Orchestra</a> co-production.
</p><p>Baramova played "Lady Macbeth" in Verdi's <i>Macbeth</i> at the <a href="/wiki/Gro%C3%9Fes_Festspielhaus" title="Großes Festspielhaus">Großes Festspielhaus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Salzburg" title="Salzburg">Salzburg</a>, the Teatro Principal de Alicante, Spain, and the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Theatre,_%C5%81%C3%B3d%C5%BA" title="Grand Theatre, Łódź">Grand Theatre, Łódź</a>, "Abigaille" in <i><a href="/wiki/Nabucco" title="Nabucco">Nabucco</a></i> at Teatr Wielki,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Avoid_weasel_words" title="Wikipedia:Avoid weasel words"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too vague attribution or weasel words. (April 2013)">which?</span></a></i>]</sup> Poland, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prague_State_Opera" title="Prague State Opera">Prague State Opera</a>, <a href="/wiki/Estonian_National_Opera" title="Estonian National Opera">Estonian National Opera</a>, <a href="/wiki/Forest_National" title="Forest National">Vorst Nationaal</a>, Brussels, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stadsschouwburg_(Amsterdam)" title="Stadsschouwburg (Amsterdam)">Stadsschouwburg</a>, Amsterdam,  and the <a href="/wiki/Grenslandhallen" title="Grenslandhallen">Grenslandhallen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hasselt" title="Hasselt">Hasselt</a>, Belgium.
</p><p>With <i>Turandot</i> she performed at the Opéra de Massy, Paris, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prague_State_Opera" title="Prague State Opera">Prague State Opera</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Bunka_Kaikan" title="Tokyo Bunka Kaikan">Tokyo Bunka Kaikan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aichi_Arts_Center" title="Aichi Arts Center">Aichi Arts Center</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nagoya" title="Nagoya">Nagoya</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Sun_Yat-sen_Memorial_Hall_(Taipei)" title="Sun Yat-sen Memorial Hall (Taipei)">National Sun Yat-sen Memorial Hall</a>,  Taipei, the <a href="/wiki/Chiang_Kai-shek_Memorial_Hall" title="Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall">Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taipei" title="Taipei">Taipei</a>, and Festival Lirica, <a href="/wiki/Massa_Marittima" title="Massa Marittima">Massa Marittima</a>, Teatro delle Fonti, <a href="/wiki/Ripatransone" title="Ripatransone">Ripatransone</a>, and Teatro D'Annunzio, <a href="/wiki/Pescara" title="Pescara">Pescara</a> in Italy
</p><p>She played "Elvira" in <i>Don Giovanni</i> at Classic Openair Solothurn, Switzerland, <i>Tosca</i> at <a href="/wiki/Grand_Theatre,_%C5%81%C3%B3d%C5%BA" title="Grand Theatre, Łódź">Teatr Wielki</a>, Łodz, "Elvira" in <i><a href="/wiki/Ernani" title="Ernani">Ernani</a></i> at the <a href="/wiki/Varna" title="Varna">Varna</a> International Music Festival, Bulgaria,  and "Amelia" in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Un_Ballo_in_Maschera" title="Un Ballo in Maschera">Un Ballo in Maschera</a></i> at Cairo Opera House.
</p><p>Baramova was a guest soloist with <a href="/wiki/Orchestra_Ensemble_Kanazawa" title="Orchestra Ensemble Kanazawa">Orchestra Ensemble Kanazawa</a>, Japan, and the Orchestra of the Conservatory of Bern at Arènes d’Avenches.
</p>
