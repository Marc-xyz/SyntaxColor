<p><b>Mazim</b> is a village in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ad_Dhahirah_Region" title="Ad Dhahirah Region">Ad Dhahirah Region</a>, in northeastern <a href="/wiki/Oman" title="Oman">Oman</a>. The village lies southeast of <a href="/wiki/Abu_Silah" title="Abu Silah">Abu Silah</a> and northwest of <a href="/wiki/As_Subaykhi" title="As Subaykhi">As Subaykhi</a> along Highway 21, which connects the village to <a href="/wiki/Ibri" title="Ibri">Ibri</a>, the district capital. The village contains a large green area of trees to the west of the main road entering the village. The 1917 Gazetteer of Arabia describes Mazim as being a hamlet, located about 25 miles northwest of Ibri, and being fortified with walls.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Branch(Cairo1988_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Branch(Cairo1988-1">[1]</a></sup> Tanker filler stations have been proposed in Mazim.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>To the north of the village is the hamlet of Masharub.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
