<p>The <b>12 m<sup>2</sup> Sharpie</b> was designed in 1931 by the Kröger Brothers in Warnemünde, Germany. The peak of the class was in the <a href="/wiki/Sailing_at_the_1956_Summer_Olympics" title="Sailing at the 1956 Summer Olympics">1956 Melbourne Olympic Games</a>. To this day, the original design has been preserved, and the class is sailed competitively in the UK,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Holland,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Germany,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and Portugal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
The European Championships are rotated between these four countries every year.   
</p><p>The term 'Twelve Square Metre' evolves from the original sail area, though on modern sharpies due to modern sail designs has now reached to around sixteen square metres.   
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sharpie_12_m2_GER_122.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="990" data-file-width="852" decoding="async" height="256" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Sharpie_12_m2_GER_122.jpg/220px-Sharpie_12_m2_GER_122.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Sharpie_12_m2_GER_122.jpg/330px-Sharpie_12_m2_GER_122.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Sharpie_12_m2_GER_122.jpg/440px-Sharpie_12_m2_GER_122.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Sharpie_12_m2_GER_122.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>12 m<sup>2</sup> Sharpie</div></div></div>
<p>Past Australian champions to have passed through the ranks include <a href="/wiki/James_Hardy_(sailor)" title="James Hardy (sailor)">Sir James Hardy</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Cuneo" title="John Cuneo">John Cuneo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rolly_Tasker" title="Rolly Tasker">Rolly Tasker</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_Bertrand_(Australian_sailor)" title="John Bertrand (Australian sailor)">John Bertrand</a>. Rolly Tasker won Australia's first sailing medal at the <a href="/wiki/1956_Summer_Olympics" title="1956 Summer Olympics">1956 Olympic Games</a> in Melbourne when he and <a href="/wiki/John_Scott_(sailor)" title="John Scott (sailor)">John Scott</a> won a silver medal in their 12 m<sup>2</sup> Sharpie.
</p><p>The 12 m<sup>2</sup> Sharpie is one of the Vintage classes for the <a href="/wiki/2018_Vintage_Yachting_Games" title="2018 Vintage Yachting Games">2018 Vintage Yachting Games</a>.
</p><p>There are still a small number of original sharpies in Australia and Brasil, though they have not been sailed competitively on International level since the 1960s. In Australia, the original 'heavyweight' Sharpie has now evolved into the lightweight <a href="/wiki/Australian_Sharpie" title="Australian Sharpie">Australian Sharpie</a>.
</p><p>When racing in a mixed fleet, the 12 m<sup>2</sup> Sharpie has a <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth_Yardstick" title="Portsmouth Yardstick">Portsmouth number</a> of 1026.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RYA2007_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-RYA2007-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
