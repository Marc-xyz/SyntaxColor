<p><b>Chabad of Poway</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Orthodox_Judaism" title="Orthodox Judaism">Orthodox</a> <a href="/wiki/Synagogue" title="Synagogue">synagogue</a> and community center in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poway" title="Poway">Poway</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>. Founded in 1986, it includes a 770-seat sanctuary, preschool, afternoon Hebrew school, senior center, library, and <a href="/wiki/Mikveh" title="Mikveh">mikveh</a>, serving the Jewish communities of Poway and <a href="/wiki/Rancho_Bernardo,_San_Diego" title="Rancho Bernardo, San Diego">Rancho Bernardo</a>.
</p><p>On April 27, 2019, the synagogue was the site of <a href="/wiki/Poway_synagogue_shooting" title="Poway synagogue shooting">a shooting</a> in which one person was killed and three were injured.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chabad_of_Poway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Rabbi_Yisroel_Goldstein_(cropped).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1720" data-file-width="1376" decoding="async" height="213" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Rabbi_Yisroel_Goldstein_%28cropped%29.jpg/170px-Rabbi_Yisroel_Goldstein_%28cropped%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Rabbi_Yisroel_Goldstein_%28cropped%29.jpg/255px-Rabbi_Yisroel_Goldstein_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Rabbi_Yisroel_Goldstein_%28cropped%29.jpg/340px-Rabbi_Yisroel_Goldstein_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Rabbi_Yisroel_Goldstein_(cropped).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Rabbi Yisroel Goldstein in 2010</div></div></div>
<p>Chabad of Poway was founded in 1986 by Rabbi Yisroel Goldstein, a 24-year-old <a href="/wiki/Shaliach_(Chabad)" title="Shaliach (Chabad)">shaliach</a> from <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, New York, and his new wife Devorie.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sht_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-sht-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gala_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-gala-4">[4]</a></sup> The congregation first met in a storefront in the <a href="/wiki/Rancho_Bernardo,_San_Diego" title="Rancho Bernardo, San Diego">Rancho Bernardo</a> shopping center, and then moved to a one-acre site where it opened a <a href="/wiki/Preschool" title="Preschool">preschool</a> in mobile trailers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gala_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-gala-4">[4]</a></sup> A senior center was opened in 1990.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gala_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-gala-4">[4]</a></sup> 
</p><p>In 1997 the synagogue built a 20,000 sq ft (1,900 m<sup>2</sup>) community center containing a 770-seat sanctuary, preschool, afternoon Hebrew school, senior center, library, <a href="/wiki/Mikveh" title="Mikveh">mikveh</a>, and meeting rooms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gala_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-gala-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lat_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-lat-5">[5]</a></sup> Shluchim Rabbi Mendy and Bluma Rubenfeld joined the staff of Chabad of Poway in 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gala_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-gala-4">[4]</a></sup> In 2006 Goldstein was named the Jewish chaplain for the Poway Sheriff's Department.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sht_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-sht-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2010 the synagogue enhanced its security measures by applying for and receiving a $75,000 grant from the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Emergency_Management_Agency" title="Federal Emergency Management Agency">Federal Emergency Management Agency</a> (FEMA). It used the funds to add "security systems and alarms, a security assessment and installation of 16 cameras, fencing and lighting".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-ap-6">[6]</a></sup> In May 2018 the synagogue applied for a $150,000 FEMA grant to install gates and more secure doors. This grant was approved in September 2018, but the funds were not released until March 2019, one month before the shooting attack.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-ap-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Shooting">Shooting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chabad_of_Poway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Shooting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Poway_synagogue_shooting" title="Poway synagogue shooting">Poway synagogue shooting</a></div>
<p>On April 27, 2019, John T. Earnest, a 19-year-old male from the <a href="/wiki/San_Diego" title="San Diego">San Diego</a> suburb of <a href="/wiki/Rancho_Pe%C3%B1asquitos,_San_Diego" title="Rancho Peñasquitos, San Diego">Rancho Peñasquitos</a>, allegedly entered the synagogue building during services on the last day of the Jewish <a href="/wiki/Passover" title="Passover">Passover</a> holiday and opened fire in the foyer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-who_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-who-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-8">[8]</a></sup> One woman, 60-year-old Lori Gilbert-Kaye, was killed and three other people were wounded, including Goldstein.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> According to witnesses, Gilbert-Kaye had tried to shield Goldstein from the gunfire.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnn_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-cnn-11">[11]</a></sup> The shooter's gun jammed after releasing ten bullets<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and two congregants, one an off-duty <a href="/wiki/United_States_Border_Patrol" title="United States Border Patrol">United States Border Patrol</a> officer, chased him out of the building.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nbc_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-nbc-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Meet_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Meet-14">[14]</a></sup> A short while later, Earnest called <a href="/wiki/9-1-1" title="9-1-1">911</a> and reported the shooting.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-8">[8]</a></sup> He was apprehended approximately 2 mi (3.2 km) from the synagogue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nbc_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-nbc-13">[13]</a></sup> The suspect had an <a href="/wiki/AR-15_style_rifle" title="AR-15 style rifle">AR-style rifle</a> in his possession at the time of his arrest and had made many <a href="/wiki/Antisemitism" title="Antisemitism">antisemitic</a> and anti-Muslim comments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2019, Goldstein announced that he would give over his day-to-day duties as rabbi of the congregation to his son, Rabbi Mendel Goldstein, and eventually serve as rabbi emeritus of the synagogue. News agencies cited his decision as relating to lingering symptoms of <a href="/wiki/Posttraumatic_stress_disorder" title="Posttraumatic stress disorder">posttraumatic stress disorder</a> he has suffered since the shooting incident.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p>
