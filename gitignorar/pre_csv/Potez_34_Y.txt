<p>The <b>Potez 34</b> was a recording breaking aircraft, designed and built in <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> in the late 1920s. Only one was completed.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design_and_development">Design and development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Potez_34&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Design and development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Potez 34 was built by <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A%C3%A9roplanes_Henry_Potez" title="Aéroplanes Henry Potez">Aéroplanes Henry Potez</a></i> and was derived from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Potez_28M" title="Potez 28M">Potez 28M</a> and used the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ryan_NYP" title="Ryan NYP">Ryan NYP</a>'s blind flying /  periscope configuration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Intended to win the distance record in closed circuit and then in a straight line, the first record attempts of the Potez 34 were punctuated by tyre punctures and oil leaks. Modifications were made including the exchange of the Farman engine with a lighter Hispano-Suiza and the adoption of a metal propeller. On 18 June 1929, the French distance in closed circuit record was beaten over a course of 6,517 km (4,049 mi) flown by <a href="/wiki/Lionel_de_Marmier" title="Lionel de Marmier">Lionel de Marmier</a> and Louis Favreau, but the world record could not be broken due to a technical failure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> On 25 June 1929, the same crew attempted to break the distance record in a straight line, flying to the Far East, but propeller vibrations necessitated a landing in <a href="/wiki/Tunisia" title="Tunisia">Tunisia</a> and the failure of the attempt.
</p><p>The Potez 34 was lent by the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ministere_de_l%27Air&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ministere de l'Air (page does not exist)">Ministere de l'Air</a> to <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Le_Brix" title="Joseph Le Brix">Joseph Le Brix</a>, assisted by Maurice Rossi, for a flight from Paris to Saigon, with three stops at <a href="/wiki/Benghazi" title="Benghazi">Benghazi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Basra" title="Basra">Basra</a> and <a href="/wiki/Allahabad" title="Allahabad">Allahabad</a>. The attempt began 16 December 1929 and a breakdown occurred over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Burma" title="Burma">Burma</a> on the night of 22 to 23 December 1929, the aircraft crashed into the jungle but the crew parachuted to safety.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Specifications">Specifications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Potez_34&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Specifications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p style="font-size: 90%; margin:0;"><i>Data from</i> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p><span style="font-size: 120%;"><b>General characteristics</b></span>
</p><ul><li><b>Crew:</b> Two</li>
<li><b>Length:</b> 11.50 m (37 ft 9 in)</li>
<li><b>Wingspan:</b> 18.30 m (60 ft 0 in)</li>
<li><b>Height:</b> 3.91 m (12 ft 10 in)</li>
<li><b>Wing area:</b> 61 m<sup>2</sup> (660 sq ft)</li>
<li><b>Empty weight:</b> 1,900 kg (4,189 lb)</li>
<li><b>Gross weight:</b> 5,920 kg (13,051 lb)</li>
<li><b>Fuel capacity:</b> 350 l (77 imp gal; 92 US gal) in wing tanks.</li>
<li><b>Powerplant:</b> 1 × <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hispano-Suiza_12Lbr&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hispano-Suiza 12Lbr (page does not exist)">Hispano-Suiza 12Lbr</a> liquid cooled <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/V-12_engine" title="V-12 engine">V-12</a>, 450 kW (600 hp)</li>
<li><b>Propellers:</b> 2-bladed</li></ul>
<p><span style="font-size: 120%;"><b>Performance</b></span>
</p>
<ul><li><b>Maximum speed:</b> 242 km/h (150 mph, 131 kn) at sea level</li>
<li><b>Cruise speed:</b> 150 km/h (93 mph, 81 kn)</li>
<li><b>Ferry range:</b> 6,700 km (4,200 mi, 3,600 nmi)</li>
<li><b>Service ceiling:</b> 4,500 m (14,800 ft)</li></ul>
