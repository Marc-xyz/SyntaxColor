<p><b>Dekker Dreyer</b> (born November 16, 1980) is a filmmaker, artist, and composer also known as Phantom Astronaut.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He is a prominent creator in <a href="/wiki/Virtual_reality" title="Virtual reality">virtual</a> and <a href="/wiki/Augmented_reality" title="Augmented reality">augmented reality</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dekker_Dreyer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dreyer was born on November 16, 1980 in <a href="/wiki/Daytona_Beach,_Florida" title="Daytona Beach, Florida">Daytona Beach</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Virtual_and_Augmented_Reality">Virtual and Augmented Reality</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dekker_Dreyer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Virtual and Augmented Reality">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dreyer has been called a VR pioneer by both <a href="/wiki/Entrepreneur_(magazine)" title="Entrepreneur (magazine)">Entrepreneur</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/MovieMaker" title="MovieMaker">MovieMaker</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He is considered a prominent artist in immersive media<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and created the VR / AR Producing course at <a href="/wiki/Columbia_College_Chicago" title="Columbia College Chicago">Columbia College Chicago</a> Online.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> At the studio he co-founded, Clever Fox, he created and directed two of the first scripted original series in VR, <i>The Depths</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and <i>Broadcast</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He has also created XR experiences for musical artists <a href="/wiki/Bootsy_Collins" title="Bootsy Collins">Bootsy Collins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Devo" title="Devo">Devo</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Disturbed_(band)" title="Disturbed (band)">Disturbed</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p><p>His augmented reality live experience <i>The Summoning</i> has been called a "First of its kind" by UploadVR and received wide recognition for its innovative use of technology and creativity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Subsequent projects include the collaborative storytelling project <i>What We Leave Behind.</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_and_Television">Film and Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dekker_Dreyer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Film and Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dekker's work in independent film began when his short <i>Closed Circuit</i>, commissioned by <a href="/wiki/Miramax" title="Miramax">Miramax</a> to accompany the feature <i><a href="/wiki/Naqoyqatsi" title="Naqoyqatsi">Naqoyqatsi</a></i>, was shown at the 2002 Slamdance Film Festival. His first documentary film <i>Three Days in Orlando</i> showcased the friction both inside and out facing the yearly Gay Days event in Orlando Florida through a series of intimate profiles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> His experimental feature <i>The Arcadian</i> starring <a href="/wiki/Lance_Henriksen" title="Lance Henriksen">Lance Henriksen</a> with the music of <a href="/wiki/Perturbator" title="Perturbator">Perturbator</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> has released on collector's edition <a href="/wiki/VHS" title="VHS">VHS</a> and digital download with special international midnight screenings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> He is also the director of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mystery_Skulls" title="Mystery Skulls">Mystery Skulls</a> official music video for the single <i>Music</i>.
</p><p>In 2007 he and his partners launched the <i><a href="/wiki/Illusion_On-Demand" title="Illusion On-Demand">Illusion On-Demand</a></i> network, a science fiction channel with a large national footprint.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> He soon took on the duty of creative director and brought both anime<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> and sci-fi classics like Doctor Who<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> to the network. Dekker produced many of the original programs that appeared on Illusion including <i>Analog presents: The Science of Fiction</i> in partnership with <a href="/wiki/Analog_Science_Fiction_and_Fact" title="Analog Science Fiction and Fact">Analog Science Fiction and Fact</a>. He would later expand the channel's holdings by launching the short-lived anthology magazine <i>Transmitter</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup>
</p><p>From 2012 to 2016 he was part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Participant_Media" title="Participant Media">Participant Media's</a> online video arm at <a href="/wiki/TakePart" title="TakePart">Takepart.com</a> and on the launch team for their cable television network <a href="/wiki/Pivot_(TV_network)" title="Pivot (TV network)">Pivot</a>. Participant Media is widely known to produce content dealing with the subjects of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a>, <a href="/wiki/Journalism_ethics_and_standards" title="Journalism ethics and standards">journalistic ethics</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Social_justice" title="Social justice">social justice</a>.
</p><p>Dreyer has spoken at events such as <a href="/wiki/Cannes_Film_Festival" title="Cannes Film Festival">The Cannes Film Festival</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sundance_Film_Festival" title="Sundance Film Festival">Sundance</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Festivals">Festivals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dekker_Dreyer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Festivals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dreyer returned to the <a href="/wiki/Slamdance_Film_Festival" title="Slamdance Film Festival">Slamdance</a> Film Festival as a jurist in 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> That same year he was a major supporter of the organization's <i>DIG</i> (Digital, Interactive, and Gaming) showcase.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> In 2018 he took on the role of organizer and co-curator of the fourth annual DIG program.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup>
</p><p>Dreyer was also the founder of the touring animation festival <i>Anime After Dark<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></i> which kicked off with presenting the official 20th anniversary event for <i><a href="/wiki/Grave_of_the_Fireflies" title="Grave of the Fireflies">Grave of the Fireflies</a></i> which had a digital conversion overseen by Dreyer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Books_and_Comics">Books and Comics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dekker_Dreyer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Books and Comics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dreyer is the author of the short story anthologies, <i>Cyberpunk is Dead: Long Live Cyberpunk</i>, <i>Dark Astronomy</i>, <i>Body Horrors</i>, <i>Demonology Lessons</i>, and the novella <i>The Tea Goddess</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> He is credited as having coined the term ecopunk.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup> In comics, Dekker is a writer and illustrator. He is a writer and illustrator<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> His work includes <i>Mondo Atomic</i>, which retells the stories of <i><a href="/wiki/Plan_9_from_Outer_Space" title="Plan 9 from Outer Space">Plan 9 from Outer Space</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Robot_Monster" title="Robot Monster">Robot Monster</a></i>, and other B movies in a contemporary way.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="Art.2C_Food_.26_Cultural_Commentary"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Art,_Food_&amp;_Cultural_Commentary">Art, Food &amp; Cultural Commentary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dekker_Dreyer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Art, Food &amp; Cultural Commentary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dekker is a visual artist working in physical, digital, and non-conventional mediums. His work has been included in numerous pop culture shows in the Los Angeles art scene most notably associated with the venue Meltdown Comics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> He is an outspoken essayist on the topics of <a class="external text" href="https://thoughtcatalog.com/dekker-dreyer/2014/02/fight-or-flight-in-the-former-eastern-bloc/" rel="nofollow">human rights</a>, race, <a class="external text" href="https://thoughtcatalog.com/dekker-dreyer/2014/04/sexualization-minority-representation-and-gender-identity-how-comics-are-tackling-the-big-issues/" rel="nofollow">sexual identity</a>, and alternative economic models.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> His horror comic anthology <i>Seven Deadly Sins</i> critiques the role of technology in modern society.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup>
</p><p>Dekker's culinary projects combine immersive theatricality, dining, and absurdist cultural commentary. These projects include the restaurant Kaiju Sushi which was located in the shark attack capital of the world. Dreyer and his partner Julia Howe proclaimed that any person coming to the pop-up restaurant with proof that they were attacked by sharks would get free sushi resulting in national coverage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> and L'Aldila, a supernatural restaurant in the heart of <a href="/wiki/Cassadaga,_Florida" title="Cassadaga, Florida">Cassadaga, Florida</a> where patrons were encouraged to commune with the dead during their meals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup>
</p><p>His live event series Movie Cult, encourages audience participation around forgotten VHS movies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup>
</p><p>Dreyer teamed up with his wife to produce the art meme project <i>Tentacle Grape</i> to bring awareness to a rising tide of misogyny in pop culture in an era predating <a href="/wiki/Gamergate_controversy" title="Gamergate controversy">Gamergate</a> and related movements. The project, presented as a regular product, drew controversy about the brand's packaging and message.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> Although controversial, the product was overwhelmingly popular leading to <a href="/wiki/Cracked_(magazine)" title="Cracked (magazine)">Cracked</a> naming it #4 on their list of "Horrifying Soft Drinks Around the World".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> The unexpected popularity of the product lead to a backlog prompting watchdog site <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Consumerist" title="The Consumerist">The Consumerist</a> to question the existence of the soda.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> A retraction was subsequently published.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup>
</p><p>From 2017 to 2018 Dreyer was the co-host of the podcast <i>The Future is Virtual</i> from Embolden Entertainment (later <a class="external text" href="https://www.zerogravitymanagement.com/" rel="nofollow">Zero Gravity</a>). The show was unique in that it was live streamed as a simulcast to <a href="/wiki/AltspaceVR" title="AltspaceVR">AltspaceVR</a>, <a href="/wiki/Twitch_(service)" title="Twitch (service)">Twitch</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> 360.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> In 2015 Dreyer was featured on <a href="/wiki/TV_Tokyo" title="TV Tokyo">TV Tokyo</a>'s television series <a href="/wiki/Why_Did_You_Come_to_Japan%3F" title="Why Did You Come to Japan?">Why Did You Come to Japan</a>. He and his wife Julia took the audience on a tour of Tokyo's <a href="/wiki/Shinjuku_Golden_Gai" title="Shinjuku Golden Gai">Golden Gai</a> nightlife.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup>
</p>
