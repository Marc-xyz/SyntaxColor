<p><b>Wrestling Dontaku 2018</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling" title="Professional wrestling">professional wrestling</a> event promoted by <a href="/wiki/New_Japan_Pro-Wrestling" title="New Japan Pro-Wrestling">New Japan Pro-Wrestling</a> (NJPW). The event took place on May 3 and 4, 2018, in <a href="/wiki/Fukuoka" title="Fukuoka">Fukuoka</a>, at the Fukuoka Kokusai Center. The first night of the event featured nine matches, with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Championship_(professional_wrestling)" title="Championship (professional wrestling)">championship</a> at stake in one of these, while the second night featured two championship matches out of nine overall. The main event of the first night was <a href="/wiki/Kenny_Omega" title="Kenny Omega">Kenny Omega</a> against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hangman_Page" title="Hangman Page">Hangman Page</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WrestlingDontakuWON_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-WrestlingDontakuWON-5">[5]</a></sup> and the second night's main event was <a href="/wiki/Kazuchika_Okada" title="Kazuchika Okada">Kazuchika Okada</a> defending the <a href="/wiki/IWGP_Heavyweight_Championship" title="IWGP Heavyweight Championship">IWGP Heavyweight Championship</a> against <a href="/wiki/Hiroshi_Tanahashi" title="Hiroshi Tanahashi">Hiroshi Tanahashi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WrestlingDontakuWON_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-WrestlingDontakuWON-5">[5]</a></sup> This was the fifteenth event under the <a href="/wiki/Wrestling_Dontaku" title="Wrestling Dontaku">Wrestling Dontaku</a> name.
</p>

<p>Each night of Wrestling Dontaku 2018 featured nine <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling_match_types" title="Professional wrestling match types">professional wrestling matches</a> that involved different wrestlers from pre-existing scripted feuds and <a href="/wiki/Narrative_thread" title="Narrative thread">storylines</a>. Wrestlers portrayed <a href="/wiki/Heel_(professional_wrestling)" title="Heel (professional wrestling)">villains</a>, <a href="/wiki/Face_(professional_wrestling)" title="Face (professional wrestling)">heroes</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_professional_wrestling_terms#Tweener" title="Glossary of professional wrestling terms">less distinguishable characters</a> in the scripted events that built tension and culminated in a wrestling match or series of matches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
