<p><b>Charles John Vaughan</b> (23 April 1921 – 16 March 1989) was an English <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who played for <a href="/wiki/Charlton_Athletic_F.C." title="Charlton Athletic F.C.">Charlton Athletic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth_F.C." title="Portsmouth F.C.">Portsmouth</a> as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Centre_forward" title="Centre forward">centre forward</a> during the 1940s and 1950s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Vaughan started his career with <a href="/wiki/Sutton_United_F.C." title="Sutton United F.C.">Sutton United</a>. He helped them win numerous wartime honours, scored 42 league goals in the 1945–46 season to win the <a href="/wiki/Athenian_League" title="Athenian League">Athenian League</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and finished his Sutton United career with 248 goals in all competitions. He was inducted into the club's Hall of Fame.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1950, Vaughan toured Canada with a <a href="/wiki/The_Football_Association" title="The Football Association">Football Association</a> party,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and in March 1952, he appeared in an <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_B_team" title="England national football B team">England B</a> international against the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
