	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Mataruge</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>Mataruge</b> are an extinct tribe of non-<a href="/wiki/Slavs" title="Slavs">Slavic</a> origin recorded at the end of medieval period in the region of modern-day <a href="/wiki/Montenegro" title="Montenegro">Montenegro</a> and <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albania</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEVukanović1974228_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEVukanović1974228-1">[1]</a></sup> Different version of their name include Matranga and Matarango.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEDruštvo1965237_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDruštvo1965237-2">[2]</a></sup> The name Mataruge is derived from the name of the spears used by warriors from this tribe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEVukanović1974228_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEVukanović1974228-1">[1]</a></sup> They belonged to group of indigenous Balkan tribes which were first partially or completely <a href="/wiki/Romanization_(cultural)" title="Romanization (cultural)">Romanized</a> and then <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slavicized" title="Slavicized">Slavicized</a>. The Mataruge had a Vlach and/or Albanian background, or were a combination of both peoples, that emerged from Romanized and semi-Romanized indigenous Balkan tribes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>According to legends, Mataruge were only a part of larger, regarded the oldest, tribe of the region, the <a href="/wiki/%C5%A0panje" title="Španje">Španje</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGZM197720_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGZM197720-7">[7]</a></sup> It is assumed that ancient tribes of Balkans were already completely or partially Romanized before Slavs overwhelmed them at the end of the 6th century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEOZO1974169_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEOZO1974169-8">[8]</a></sup> When they received an influx of <a href="/wiki/Serbs" title="Serbs">Serbs</a> into their society, Mataruge mixed with them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECetinjski_muzej197796_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECetinjski_muzej197796-9">[9]</a></sup> It is assumed that they imposed name of their tribe to some groups of Serbs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECetinjski_muzej197796_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECetinjski_muzej197796-9">[9]</a></sup> Mataruge were frequently mentioned in historical documents of 14th and 15th century as a pasture society, with some of the most prominent of them being organizers of caravans for transport of salt and other goods from the seaside into mainlands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEND199374_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEND199374-10">[10]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Albanian_nobility" title="Albanian nobility">Albanian noble</a> family of Mataranga is mentioned since 13th century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEVukanović1974228_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEVukanović1974228-1">[1]</a></sup> In the regions of <a href="/wiki/Grahovo,_Nik%C5%A1i%C4%87" title="Grahovo, Nikšić">Grahovo</a>, (<a href="/wiki/Old_Herzegovina" title="Old Herzegovina">Old Herzegovina</a>) the Mataruge were mentioned between 1318 and 1398.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECvijić53_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECvijić53-11">[11]</a></sup> With the Ottoman advance in their region, Mataruge, together with other ancient tribes, such as <a href="/wiki/Kri%C4%8Di" title="Kriči">Kriči</a> and <a href="/wiki/Macure" title="Macure">Macure</a>, migrated from Montenegro to the west and north-west.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESGZ19627_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESGZ19627-12">[12]</a></sup> According to some late 19th century legends, the Mataruge were one of the brotherhoods of the <a href="/wiki/Kri%C4%8Di" title="Kriči">Kriči</a> tribe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEЗечевић96_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEЗечевић96-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p><p>Names of many toponyms in Montenegro and Serbia are derived from the name of this tribe, such as <a href="/wiki/Mataru%C5%A1ka_Banja" title="Mataruška Banja">Mataruška Banja</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mataruge,_Montenegro" title="Mataruge, Montenegro">Mataruge</a> in <a href="/wiki/Pljevlja" title="Pljevlja">Pljevlja</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mataruge_(Kraljevo)" title="Mataruge (Kraljevo)">Mataruge</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kraljevo" title="Kraljevo">Kraljevo</a>.
</p>
