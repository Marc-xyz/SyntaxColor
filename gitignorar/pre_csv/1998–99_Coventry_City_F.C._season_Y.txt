<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Season_summary">Season summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1998%E2%80%9399_Coventry_City_F.C._season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Season summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Coventry City finished 15th in the Premiership – four places lower than last season – but were never in any real danger of being relegated, despite the loss of key striker <a href="/wiki/Dion_Dublin" title="Dion Dublin">Dion Dublin</a> to local rivals <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The biggest news of Coventry's season was the announcement of a move to a new <a href="/wiki/Ricoh_Arena" title="Ricoh Arena">45,000-seat stadium</a> at <a href="/wiki/Foleshill" title="Foleshill">Foleshill</a>, which was anticipated to be ready by 2002. Manager <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Strachan" title="Gordon Strachan">Gordon Strachan</a> then signed <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Moroccan</a> international football star <a href="/wiki/Mustapha_Hadji" title="Mustapha Hadji">Mustapha Hadji</a>, knowing that it would be important to have a top quality team to match the forthcoming new home.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
