<p>The <b>Belfast Royal Adacemy</b>  (commonly shortened to <b>B.R.A</b>) is the oldest school in the city of <a href="/wiki/Belfast" title="Belfast">Belfast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>  It is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Co-educational" title="Co-educational">co-educational</a>, <a href="/wiki/Non-denominational" title="Non-denominational">non-denominational</a> voluntary <a href="/wiki/Grammar_school" title="Grammar school">grammar school</a> situated in north <a href="/wiki/Belfast" title="Belfast">Belfast</a>. The Academy is one of 8 schools in Northern Ireland whose Head is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Headmasters%27_and_Headmistresses%27_Conference" title="Headmasters' and Headmistresses' Conference">Headmasters' and Headmistresses' Conference</a>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belfast_Royal_Academy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Academy was founded in 1785 by Rev. Dr. <a href="/wiki/James_Crombie_(minister)" title="James Crombie (minister)">James Crombie</a>. Originally situated near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St_Anne%E2%80%99s_Cathedral,_Belfast" title="St Anne’s Cathedral, Belfast">St Anne's Parish Church</a> in what is now Academy Street, it moved to its current location on the Cliftonville Road in 1880. For more than a century the school was named <i>Belfast Academy</i>. On 27 November 1887, <a href="/wiki/Queen_Victoria" title="Queen Victoria">Queen Victoria</a> granted permission for the school to style itself <i>Belfast Royal Academy</i>, and its name was officially changed in November 1888.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
<h3><span id=".22Barring_out.22_incident"></span><span class="mw-headline" id='"Barring_out"_incident'>"Barring out" incident</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Belfast_Royal_Academy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title='Edit section: "Barring out" incident'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1867, a group of schoolboys (eight boarders and two day boys) barricaded themselves in the towers classroom. In doing so they “<i>declared war against the masters until their requests should be granted<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></i>”. As they expected to be holed up for some time, they had taken a quantity of provisions from the academy kitchens; further they managed to arm themselves with 5 pistols and a large quantity of gunpowder and shot. A letter, headed “Liberty Hall”, was sent by the students to their masters in which they stated they would not surrender until their demands had been met.
The academy authorities, in an attempt to break the siege, sent workmen to break down the door and pour water down the chimney, without success, as the boys opened fire on them.
Finally the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lord_Mayor_of_Belfast" title="Lord Mayor of Belfast">Sovereign of Belfast</a>, Rev. William Bristow, was summoned, he “<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Read_the_Riot_Act" title="Read the Riot Act">read the Riot Act</a>” to the boys but failed to end the <a href="/wiki/Barring_out" title="Barring out">barring out</a>, and one of the boys opened fire on him.
Later that night the siege ended; the boys were later beaten and then expelled.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>

<p>The school crest comprises the <a href="/wiki/Rose" title="Rose">rose</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Thistle" title="Thistle">thistle</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Shamrock" title="Shamrock">shamrock</a>, along with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Royal_Coat_of_Arms_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Royal Coat of Arms of the United Kingdom">Royal Coat of Arms of the United Kingdom</a>, the Arms of the City of <a href="/wiki/Belfast" title="Belfast">Belfast</a> and those of the Province of <a href="/wiki/Ulster" title="Ulster">Ulster</a>. <p>When a pupil enters the Academy he or she is placed into one of the four houses: Shaw, Currie, Pottinger or Cairns, named after distinguished past pupils: James Shaw, <a href="/wiki/Donald_Currie" title="Donald Currie">Donald Currie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Henry_Pottinger" title="Henry Pottinger">Henry Pottinger</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Cairns,_1st_Earl_Cairns" title="Hugh Cairns, 1st Earl Cairns">Hugh Cairns, 1st Earl Cairns</a>, for whom the house colours are yellow, green, red, and blue, respectively; each pupil must wear a tie with a stripe of his or her house colour on it.
</p>

<p>As a pupil progresses through the Academy, he or she can earn honours through excellence in sport and/or the arts. There are minor honours, allowing a pupil to wear a minor honours tie, (green owls) intermediate honours (blue owls) and major honours (gold owls). In addition, a pupil gaining intermediate honours is entitled to wear a black blazer with maroon braid surrounding a golden school badge. A pupil gaining major honours in sport is entitled to wear a distinctive maroon blazer with blue braid and a gold school badge. Pupils who receive major honours in the arts, be it for music, drama, or art &amp; design are entitled to wear a blue blazer with maroon braid and a gold school badge. The honour, e.g. Cricket XI 2004 or Music 2002, is stitched in gold letters under the badge for both intermediate and major honours.
</p>
