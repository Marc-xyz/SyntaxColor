<p><i><b>The Gordin Cell</b></i> is an Israeli <a href="/wiki/Psychological_thriller" title="Psychological thriller">psychological thriller</a> series. The series stars <a href="/wiki/Ran_Danker" title="Ran Danker">Ran Danker</a> as <a href="/wiki/Israeli_Air_Force" title="Israeli Air Force">Israeli Air Force</a> officer Eyal Gordin and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Ivanir" title="Mark Ivanir">Mark Ivanir</a> as <a href="/wiki/Foreign_Intelligence_Service_(Russia)" title="Foreign Intelligence Service (Russia)">Russian Intelligence</a> agent Yaakov Lundin.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Gordin_Cell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Season_1">Season 1</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Gordin_Cell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Season 1">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Diana and Mickey Gordin immigrated to <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> in the 90s and adjusted in Israel, as they join the upper middle class. In fact, both were recruited by the <a href="/wiki/KGB" title="KGB">KGB</a> Intelligence Directorate to collect data for the organization, but that was a decade ago.
</p><p>Since then it has been many years, but their past haunts them again. Their liaison agent, the legendary spy Yaacov Lundin, is back. He demands from them to transfer under his service their young son Eyal, an outstanding ex-officer of <a href="/wiki/Unit_669" title="Unit 669">Unit 669</a>, who serves now in the office of the <a href="/wiki/Israeli_Air_Force" title="Israeli Air Force">Israeli Air Force</a> commander. While they try to escape from their past, the <a href="/wiki/Shin_Bet" title="Shin Bet">Shabak</a>'s Russian Department closes in on them, and Eyal himself struggles with the dilemma of becoming a traitor of the country he swore to serve and protect or betraying his family.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Season_2">Season 2</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Gordin_Cell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Season 2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Two years after the events of the previous season, Eyal Gordin is a released prisoner and his parents live in the town in <a href="/wiki/Moscow_Oblast" title="Moscow Oblast">Moscow Oblast</a> in Russia. Members of the Russian Department of <a href="/wiki/Shin_Bet" title="Shin Bet">Shin Bet</a>, who retired from office, returned to service in order to track down Natalia Gordin, having wiped out the deputy head of <a href="/wiki/Mossad" title="Mossad">Mossad</a>. Meanwhile, Natalia works closely with a Muslim assassin of <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentine</a> origin while holding secret operation, under the nose of people from the <a href="/wiki/Foreign_Intelligence_Service_(Russia)" title="Foreign Intelligence Service (Russia)">Russian intelligence</a> and the Mossad. Eyal, freed from prison, must work with the Israelis in order to help the Israeli intelligence to find his sister and again finds himself in an internal dilemma: whether to betray his family and give away his sister to the state authorities or do nothing?
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Foreign_adaptations">Foreign adaptations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Gordin_Cell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Foreign adaptations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="American_version">American version</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Gordin_Cell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: American version">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2012, <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> purchased the broadcasting rights in U.S., and NBC approved the shooting of the pilot for the American version, which was expected to be called "M.I.C.E" (Money, Ideology, Coercion &amp; Ego) but the pilot was not brought to series.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. Later the pilot for TV series called <i><a href="/wiki/Allegiance_(TV_series)" title="Allegiance (TV series)">Allegiance</a></i> was ordered by NBC as an adaptation for the Israeli show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Allegiance</i> tells the story of Alex O'Connor, a young idealistic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CIA" title="CIA">CIA</a> analyst specializing in Russian affairs, whose close-knit, affluent family is about to be split apart when it is revealed that his parents, Mark and Katya, are covert Russian spies deactivated years ago. But now the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kremlin" title="Kremlin">Kremlin</a> has re-enlisted them into service as they plan a terrorist operation inside the U.S. border that will bring <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">America</a> to its knees.
</p><p>Years ago, Russian-born Katya was tasked by the <a href="/wiki/KGB" title="KGB">KGB</a> to recruit American businessman Mark O'Connor as a spy and the two fell in love. A deal was struck: as long as Katya remained an asset for <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, and it was agreed that her services could be called on in the future, she would be allowed to marry Mark and move to America. After years in America building a happy life and without word from <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a>, they thought they had escaped. Now it seems that the new Mother Russia has one more mission - turning Alex into a spy. For these anguished parents, the choice is clear: betray their country or risk their family.
</p><p>The cast of the series includes Margarita Levieva, <a href="/wiki/Gavin_Stenhouse" title="Gavin Stenhouse">Gavin Stenhouse</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hope_Davis" title="Hope Davis">Hope Davis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scott_Cohen_(actor)" title="Scott Cohen (actor)">Scott Cohen</a>, Morgan Spector, Annie Ilonzeh, Alexandra Peters and <a href="/wiki/Kenneth_Choi" title="Kenneth Choi">Kenneth Choi</a>. Writer-director <a href="/wiki/George_Nolfi" title="George Nolfi">George Nolfi</a> serves as executive producer with <a href="/wiki/Avi_Nir" title="Avi Nir">Avi Nir</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ron_Leshem" title="Ron Leshem">Ron Leshem</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amit_Cohen" title="Amit Cohen">Amit Cohen</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Yona_Wisenthal&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Yona Wisenthal (page does not exist)">Yona Wisenthal</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Giora_Yahalom&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Giora Yahalom (page does not exist)">Giora Yahalom</a>. The TV series is a production of <a href="/wiki/Universal_Television" title="Universal Television">Universal Television</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Keshet_Broadcasting" title="Keshet Broadcasting">Keshet Media</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Yes!_(television_company)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Yes! (television company) (page does not exist)">Yes!</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Russian_version">Russian version</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Gordin_Cell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Russian version">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In April 2013, the series was sold to J.I.T.V Productions in <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>. The Russian version was planned to be developed in cooperation with Sreda Productions based on the signed development deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="South_Korean_version">South Korean version</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Gordin_Cell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: South Korean version">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The South Korean adaptation titled <i><a href="/wiki/Spy_(2015_TV_series)" title="Spy (2015 TV series)">SPY</a></i> aired on <a href="/wiki/KBS2" title="KBS2">KBS2</a> in 2015, starring <a href="/wiki/Kim_Jae-joong" title="Kim Jae-joong">Kim Jae-joong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bae_Jong-ok" title="Bae Jong-ok">Bae Jong-ok</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yu_Oh-seong" title="Yu Oh-seong">Yu Oh-seong</a>. Although based on <i>The Gordin Cell</i>, the <a href="/wiki/Korean_drama" title="Korean drama">Korean drama</a> was tailored to the current <a href="/wiki/North_Korea%E2%80%93South_Korea_relations" title="North Korea–South Korea relations">North-South Korean political conflict</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
