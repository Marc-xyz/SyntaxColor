<p><b>Industriewerke Ludwigsfelde</b> is an automotive factory in <a href="/wiki/Ludwigsfelde" title="Ludwigsfelde">Ludwigsfelde</a> in <a href="/wiki/Brandenburg" title="Brandenburg">Brandenburg</a>, just south of <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a> in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. The factory is part of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daimler-Benz" title="Daimler-Benz">Daimler-Benz</a> <a href="/wiki/Aktiengesellschaft" title="Aktiengesellschaft">AG</a> and since 1991 it has made <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz" title="Mercedes-Benz">Mercedes-Benz</a> vans.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Supplier_of_aero_engines_for_the_Luftwaffe">Supplier of aero engines for the Luftwaffe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Industriewerke_Ludwigsfelde&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Supplier of aero engines for the Luftwaffe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:262px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:DB_603_E.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="768" data-file-width="1024" decoding="async" height="195" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/DB_603_E.jpg/260px-DB_603_E.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/DB_603_E.jpg/390px-DB_603_E.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/DB_603_E.jpg/520px-DB_603_E.jpg 2x" width="260"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:DB_603_E.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Daimler-Benz DB 603 engine, partly sectioned to show internal working.</div></div></div>
<p>The Daimler-Benz company established the Ludwigsfelde factory in 1936 to make <a href="/wiki/Daimler-Benz_DB_600" title="Daimler-Benz DB 600">DB 600</a> aero engines for new <i><a href="/wiki/Luftwaffe" title="Luftwaffe">Luftwaffe</a></i> bomber- and fighter-aircraft.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200965_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200965-1">[1]</a></sup> In the course of <a href="/wiki/German_re-armament" title="German re-armament">German re-armament</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a> the factory went on to make Daimler-Benz <a href="/wiki/Daimler-Benz_DB_601" title="Daimler-Benz DB 601">DB 601</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daimler-Benz_DB_603" title="Daimler-Benz DB 603">DB 603</a> and <a href="/wiki/Daimler-Benz_DB_605" title="Daimler-Benz DB 605">DB 605</a> engines for various <i>Luftwaffe</i> aircraft.
</p><p>During the Second World War (1939-1945) the factory used at least 10,000 prisoners of war, <a href="/wiki/Unfree_labour" title="Unfree labour">forced labourers</a> and concentration-camp prisoners to work in the factory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spiegel_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Spiegel-2">[2]</a></sup> From 1943 to 1944 it used the forced labour of about 1,100 women prisoners from the <a href="/wiki/Ravensbr%C3%BCck_concentration_camp" title="Ravensbrück concentration camp">Ravensbrück concentration camp</a> north of Berlin. Ludwigsfelde lies at least 60 miles (97 km) from Ravensbrück, so the forced labourers were housed near the factory in a subsidiary concentration camp called the <i>Deutschlandhalle</i>.
</p><p>Early in 1945 the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Air_Forces" title="United States Army Air Forces">United States Army Air Forces</a> bombed the factory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000439_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000439-3">[3]</a></sup> Then, after the <a href="/wiki/German_Instrument_of_Surrender" title="German Instrument of Surrender">surrender of Germany</a> in May 1945, what remained of the works was dismantled and taken to the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000439_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000439-3">[3]</a></sup> as part of the Allies' programme to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Industrial_plans_for_Germany#Level_of_Industry_plans" title="Industrial plans for Germany">take reparations from Germany and reduce German industrial capacity</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-war_recovery">Post-war recovery</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Industriewerke_Ludwigsfelde&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Post-war recovery">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Fotothek_df_ps_0002894_Stadt_%5E_Stadtlandschaften_%5E_Wohnh%C3%A4user_cropped.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="369" data-file-width="496" decoding="async" height="164" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Fotothek_df_ps_0002894_Stadt_%5E_Stadtlandschaften_%5E_Wohnh%C3%A4user_cropped.JPG/220px-Fotothek_df_ps_0002894_Stadt_%5E_Stadtlandschaften_%5E_Wohnh%C3%A4user_cropped.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Fotothek_df_ps_0002894_Stadt_%5E_Stadtlandschaften_%5E_Wohnh%C3%A4user_cropped.JPG/330px-Fotothek_df_ps_0002894_Stadt_%5E_Stadtlandschaften_%5E_Wohnh%C3%A4user_cropped.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Fotothek_df_ps_0002894_Stadt_%5E_Stadtlandschaften_%5E_Wohnh%C3%A4user_cropped.JPG/440px-Fotothek_df_ps_0002894_Stadt_%5E_Stadtlandschaften_%5E_Wohnh%C3%A4user_cropped.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Fotothek_df_ps_0002894_Stadt_%5E_Stadtlandschaften_%5E_Wohnh%C3%A4user_cropped.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Multicar M21 being used in the rebuilding of Leninplatz, Dresden in about 1966, photographed by <a href="/wiki/Richard_Peter" title="Richard Peter">Richard Peter</a>.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bundesarchiv_Bild_183-53500-185,_Leipzig,_Fr%C3%BChjahrsmesse,_Strahltriebwerk.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="508" data-file-width="800" decoding="async" height="140" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Bundesarchiv_Bild_183-53500-185%2C_Leipzig%2C_Fr%C3%BChjahrsmesse%2C_Strahltriebwerk.jpg/220px-Bundesarchiv_Bild_183-53500-185%2C_Leipzig%2C_Fr%C3%BChjahrsmesse%2C_Strahltriebwerk.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Bundesarchiv_Bild_183-53500-185%2C_Leipzig%2C_Fr%C3%BChjahrsmesse%2C_Strahltriebwerk.jpg/330px-Bundesarchiv_Bild_183-53500-185%2C_Leipzig%2C_Fr%C3%BChjahrsmesse%2C_Strahltriebwerk.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Bundesarchiv_Bild_183-53500-185%2C_Leipzig%2C_Fr%C3%BChjahrsmesse%2C_Strahltriebwerk.jpg/440px-Bundesarchiv_Bild_183-53500-185%2C_Leipzig%2C_Fr%C3%BChjahrsmesse%2C_Strahltriebwerk.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Bundesarchiv_Bild_183-53500-185,_Leipzig,_Fr%C3%BChjahrsmesse,_Strahltriebwerk.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Pirna 014 jet engine at the Spring <a href="/wiki/Leipzig_Trade_Fair" title="Leipzig Trade Fair">Leipzig Trade Fair</a> in March 1958</div></div></div>
<p>Brandenburg was part of the <i>Sowjetische Besatzungszone</i> (SBZ or <a href="/wiki/Soviet_occupation_zone" title="Soviet occupation zone">Soviet occupation zone</a>) from 1945 and the <i>Deutsche Demokratische Republik</i> (DDR or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_Democratic_Republic" title="German Democratic Republic">German Democratic Republic</a>) from 1949. The <i><b><a href="/wiki/Volkseigener_Betrieb" title="Volkseigener Betrieb">Volkseigener Betrieb</a> Industriewerke Ludwigsfelde</b></i> was founded on 1 March 1952.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000439_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000439-3">[3]</a></sup> Initially it made <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marine_diesel_engine" title="Marine diesel engine">marine diesel engines</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000439_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000439-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Machine_tool" title="Machine tool">machine tools</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200965_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200965-1">[1]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Machine_element" title="Machine element">machine elements</a>. Assembly of <b><a href="/wiki/Multicar" title="Multicar">Multicar</a> M21 <i>Diesel-Ameise</i></b> ("diesel ant") vehicles was transferred to Ludwigsfelde from the <i>Schmiedewerk <a href="/wiki/Ro%C3%9Fwein" title="Roßwein">Roßwein</a></i> ("Roßwein Forge Works") in <a href="/wiki/Saxony" title="Saxony">Saxony</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000439_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000439-3">[3]</a></sup> In 1953 IWL became involved in the development of <a href="/wiki/Scooter_(motorcycle)" title="Scooter (motorcycle)">motor scooters</a>, which entered production in 1954.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000439_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000439-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1958 IWL began production of <a href="/wiki/Forge#Drop_forging" title="Forge">drop forging</a>, precision-casting, jet engines, agricultural machinery and one-off special machinery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000439_3-6"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000439-3">[3]</a></sup> The jets were 32.3 kN (7,300 lb<sub>f</sub>) <b><a href="/wiki/Pirna_014" title="Pirna 014">Pirna 014</a></b> engines for the <b><a href="/wiki/Baade_152" title="Baade 152">Dresden 152</a></b> airliner,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000439_3-7"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000439-3">[3]</a></sup> whose first prototype made its first test flight in December 1958. In 1960 <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EADS_EFW" title="EADS EFW">VEB Flugzeuge Dresden</a></i> was building 20 aircraft to enter service with <i>Deutsche Lufthansa der DDR</i> (which in 1963 became <i><a href="/wiki/Interflug" title="Interflug">Interflug</a></i>), for which IWL had begun series production of the Pirna 014.
</p><p>However, the first 152 prototype crashed in March 1959 killing its crew, and the second prototype was grounded after a fuel tank malfunction caused a dangerous reduction in fuel supply to the engines. During ground testing in September 1960 the third prototype also suffered a fuel tank fault, and its ground tests were ended in December. In February 1961 the <i>SED <a href="/wiki/Politburo" title="Politburo">Politbüro</a></i> decided to terminate aircraft production,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000439_3-8"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000439-3">[3]</a></sup> which brought Pirna 014 production to an end at IWL. One engine was tested on an <a href="/wiki/Ilyushin_Il-28" title="Ilyushin Il-28">Ilyushin Il-28</a> aircraft until June 1961 but this did not lead to the Pirna 014's further use or production. In about mid-1961 all Dresden 152 aircraft were scrapped.
</p><p>Cancellation of the 152 left IWL with 30 completed jet engines<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000439_3-9"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000439-3">[3]</a></sup> and a gap in planned industrial production. The engines were later used to power <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minesweeper_(ship)" title="Minesweeper (ship)">minesweepers</a> for the <i><a href="/wiki/Volksmarine" title="Volksmarine">Volksmarine</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000439_3-10"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000439-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<div style="clear:both;"></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="IWL_motor_scooters">IWL motor scooters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Industriewerke_Ludwigsfelde&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: IWL motor scooters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bundesarchiv_Bild_183-34227-0001,_Schlage,_Einzelbauer_mit_Motorroller.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="800" data-file-width="587" decoding="async" height="232" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/48/Bundesarchiv_Bild_183-34227-0001%2C_Schlage%2C_Einzelbauer_mit_Motorroller.jpg/170px-Bundesarchiv_Bild_183-34227-0001%2C_Schlage%2C_Einzelbauer_mit_Motorroller.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/48/Bundesarchiv_Bild_183-34227-0001%2C_Schlage%2C_Einzelbauer_mit_Motorroller.jpg/255px-Bundesarchiv_Bild_183-34227-0001%2C_Schlage%2C_Einzelbauer_mit_Motorroller.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/48/Bundesarchiv_Bild_183-34227-0001%2C_Schlage%2C_Einzelbauer_mit_Motorroller.jpg/340px-Bundesarchiv_Bild_183-34227-0001%2C_Schlage%2C_Einzelbauer_mit_Motorroller.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Bundesarchiv_Bild_183-34227-0001,_Schlage,_Einzelbauer_mit_Motorroller.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A farmer on an IWL <i>Pitty</i> scooter near <a href="/wiki/Rostock" title="Rostock">Rostock</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mecklenburg" title="Mecklenburg">Mecklenburg</a> in January 1956.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Motorroller-ddr.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1232" data-file-width="1808" decoding="async" height="170" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Motorroller-ddr.jpg/250px-Motorroller-ddr.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Motorroller-ddr.jpg/375px-Motorroller-ddr.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Motorroller-ddr.jpg/500px-Motorroller-ddr.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Motorroller-ddr.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A complete sequence of IWL motor scooter models in historical order. From left to right: <i>Pitty</i>, SR 56 <i>Wiesel</i>, SR 59 <i>Berlin</i> and TR 150 <i>Troll</i> 1.</div></div></div>
<p>After the Second World War, various manufacturers — and particularly aircraft and aircraft components makers — diversified into other products including <a href="/wiki/Scooter_(motorcycle)" title="Scooter (motorcycle)">motor scooters</a>. In Italy, <a href="/wiki/Piaggio" title="Piaggio">Piaggio</a> launched the <a href="/wiki/Vespa" title="Vespa">Vespa</a> in 1946 closely followed by <a href="/wiki/Innocenti" title="Innocenti">Innocenti</a>'s launch of the <a href="/wiki/Lambretta" title="Lambretta">Lambretta</a> in 1947. The new scooters found a market among customers who could not obtain or could not afford a car but wanted a machine that was cleaner, simpler and gave more weather protection than a motorcycle. Both Piaggio and Innocenti fitted specially designed engines mounted on one side of the rear wheel, which kept the wheelbase short and maximised urban maneuverability. A bulbous rear enclosure kept the engine enclosed, which made a scooter cleaner than most motorcycles of the period. The use of aircraft industry techniques, combined with more stylish appearance made Italian scooters commercially successful, and Innocenti and Piaggio both exported large numbers of their scooters and licensed manufacturers in the <i>Bundesrepublik Deutschland</i> (BRD or <a href="/wiki/West_Germany" title="West Germany">West Germany</a>) and other countries to make them.
</p><p>Numerous European manufacturers who were not licensed to build Italian-designed scooters, developed their own models in an attempt to compete. In 1951 the automotive manufacturer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hans_Glas" title="Hans Glas">Hans Glas</a> in Bavaria launched his own design of scooter, the <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Goggo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Goggo (page does not exist)">Goggo</a></i>, initially with a 123 cc engine but from 1953 with more powerful 147 cc and 198 cc engines. In 1953 the West German aircraft maker <a href="/wiki/Heinkel" title="Heinkel">Heinkel</a> launched the <i><a href="/wiki/Heinkel_Tourist" title="Heinkel Tourist">Tourist</a></i>, initially with a 149 cc engine but from 1954 enlarged to 174 cc. However, West German designers tended to use conventional motorcycle-type engines which were not small or light enough to fit beside the rear wheel. They were therefore mounted in front of the rear wheel, which significantly lengthened the scooters' wheelbase.
</p><p>East German engineers responded to the growing market and competition by developing a number of prototype scooters between 1950 and 1954.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200965_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200965-1">[1]</a></sup> August Falz of <a href="/wiki/D%C3%B6beln" title="Döbeln">Döbeln</a> in Saxony had built a primitive motor scooter in 1950, and by 1954 had developed a prototype with streamlined bodywork and powered by a 174 cc <a href="/wiki/%C4%8Cesk%C3%A1_zbrojovka_Strakonice" title="Česká zbrojovka Strakonice">ČZ</a> engine imported from the <a href="/wiki/Czechoslovak_Socialist_Republic" title="Czechoslovak Socialist Republic">ČSSR</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200965_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200965-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>However, the DDR decided that its scooter would be made only with indigenous DDR components, so the <a href="/wiki/DKW_RT_125" title="DKW RT 125">MZ RT 125/1</a> unit was selected.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200966_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200966-4">[4]</a></sup> The engine displaced 123 cc, produced 5.5 bhp and had a three-speed <a href="/wiki/Transmission_(mechanics)" title="Transmission (mechanics)">transmission</a>. It was smaller and less powerful than 174 cc ČZ engine, and also than engines that were being fitted in West German scooters by 1954 that produced abetween 9 and 12 bhp.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200966_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200966-4">[4]</a></sup> This put the DDR's scooter at a competitive disadvantage before it even entered production. The MZ factory at <a href="/wiki/Zschopau" title="Zschopau">Zschopau</a> would increase engine production to supply the expected scooter demand.
</p><p>The DDR's established <a href="/wiki/Eisenacher_Motorenwerk" title="Eisenacher Motorenwerk">EMW</a>, <a href="/wiki/MZ_Motorrad-_und_Zweiradwerk" title="MZ Motorrad- und Zweiradwerk">MZ</a> and <a href="/wiki/Simson_(company)" title="Simson (company)">Simson</a> motorcycle factories were already fully occupied making their own products,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200965_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200965-1">[1]</a></sup> so if the country was to make its own scooter it would have to be in a different factory. The DDR's <a href="/wiki/Planned_economy" title="Planned economy">planned economy</a> could be criticised for choosing the IWL factory as thousands of MZ engines would have to be transported at least 110 miles (180 km) from Zschopau to Ludwigsfelde. However, Ludwigsfelde was in the centre of the DDR and is on the outskirts of Berlin. The capital's radial rail and road links could distribute the finished scooters efficiently, and <a href="/wiki/East_Berlin" title="East Berlin">East Berlin</a> could be expected to be one of the largest urban markets for them. If there was a mistake, it was to rely on expanding engine production at Zschopau when IWL had nearly twenty years' experience of making engines at Ludwigsfelde.
</p><p>The prototype scooter really needed further development before being put into production.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200966_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200966-4">[4]</a></sup> However, in the wake of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Uprising_of_1953_in_East_Germany" title="Uprising of 1953 in East Germany">June 1953 uprising</a> the DDR government was in a hurry to implement its <i>Neue Kurs</i> ("New Course") policy to improve the supply of consumer goods.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200966_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200966-4">[4]</a></sup> This put the <i>VEB Industriewerke Ludwigsfelde</i> under great pressure to begin scooter production by 1955.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200966_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200966-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pitty"><i>Pitty</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Industriewerke_Ludwigsfelde&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Pitty">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Pitty_rechts.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1105" data-file-width="1757" decoding="async" height="138" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Pitty_rechts.jpg/220px-Pitty_rechts.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Pitty_rechts.jpg/330px-Pitty_rechts.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Pitty_rechts.jpg/440px-Pitty_rechts.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Pitty_rechts.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>1955 IWL <i>Pitty</i></div></div></div>
<p>IWL's first production model, named the <i>Pitty</i>, was launched early in 1955 at a retail price of 2,300 <a href="/wiki/East_German_mark" title="East German mark">Marks</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERönicke200946_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERönicke200946-5">[5]</a></sup> The <i>Pitty</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s front wheel had <a href="/wiki/Motorcycle_fork#Leading_link" title="Motorcycle fork">leading link</a> suspension,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERönicke200946_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERönicke200946-5">[5]</a></sup> while its rear wheel had hydraulically damped <a href="/wiki/Swingarm" title="Swingarm">swingarm</a> suspension on which the engine and gearbox unit was mounted:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERönicke200946_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERönicke200946-5">[5]</a></sup> an arrangement that Vespa had pioneered.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200966_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200966-4">[4]</a></sup> However, IWL followed West German practice in mounting the engine in front of the rear wheel instead of beside it, thus giving the <i>Pitty</i> a much longer wheelbase than its Italian counterparts.
</p><p>On a scooter, a pressed-steel full enclosure under the seat encases the rear wheel, engine and gearbox. In order to remain within its operating temperature range, a cooling fan was added to the air-cooled MZ engine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200966_4-6"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200966-4">[4]</a></sup> Powering this fan sapped the engine's already inadequate 5 PS power output. IWL copied Glas and Heinkel by giving the <i>Pitty</i> a large fairing that enveloped the front wheel. This increased the scooter's weight to 139 kilograms (306 lb),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200966_4-7"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200966-4">[4]</a></sup> which was heavy for its class and further impeded its under-powered performance. IWL claimed a 70 km/h (43 mph) top speed, but in practice this was seldom achieved.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200967_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200967-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>At the beginning of 1955, reporters from the East German <i>Der deutsche Straßenverkehr</i> magazine tested the <i>Pitty</i> and gave IWL numerous suggestions to improve it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200967_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200967-6">[6]</a></sup> Numerous customers who bought the scooter also complained.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200967_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200967-6">[6]</a></sup> The <i>Pitty</i> has a <a href="/wiki/Motorcycle_saddle" title="Motorcycle saddle">dualseat</a> that riders complained was too hard.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200996_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200996-7">[7]</a></sup> Unlike most scooters, the <i>Pitty</i> has no <a href="/wiki/Steering_column#Steering_lock" title="Steering column">steering lock</a> for security. Its only anti-theft device is a flap in the enclosure under the seat that can be closed over the fuel tap and locked.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200996_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200996-7">[7]</a></sup> IWL reacted to criticism by quickly developing a successor model, and kept the <i>Pitty</i> in production for only just over a year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200967_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200967-6">[6]</a></sup> In this time a total of only 11,293 <i>Pitty</i> scooters were built.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERönicke200946_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERönicke200946-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="SR_56_Wiesel">SR 56 <i>Wiesel</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Industriewerke_Ludwigsfelde&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: SR 56 Wiesel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Wiesel_rechts_1-1.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1154" data-file-width="1662" decoding="async" height="153" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/Wiesel_rechts_1-1.jpg/220px-Wiesel_rechts_1-1.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/Wiesel_rechts_1-1.jpg/330px-Wiesel_rechts_1-1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/Wiesel_rechts_1-1.jpg/440px-Wiesel_rechts_1-1.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Wiesel_rechts_1-1.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>IWL SR 56 <i>Wiesel</i></div></div></div>
<p>In 1956 IWL replaced the <i>Pitty</i> with the <b>SR 56 <i>Wiesel</i></b>. "SR" stands for <i><b>S</b>tadt<b>R</b>oller</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESalzmann1999184_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESalzmann1999184-8">[8]</a></sup> ("town scooter"), 56 is the year,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERönicke200947_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERönicke200947-9">[9]</a></sup> and <i>Wiesel</i> is German for <a href="/wiki/Weasel" title="Weasel">weasel</a>. Instead of the <i>Pitty</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s huge fairing, the <i>Wiesel</i> has a more conventional scooter fairing behind the front wheel and a separate front mudguard. This reduced the weight to 124 kg (273 lb):<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200995_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200995-10">[10]</a></sup> still heavy, but 15 kg (33 lb) lighter than the <i>Pitty</i>. The rear swingarm was of a new trapezoidal design, which allowed the chain's tension to remain constant as the rear wheel travelled vertically. The fuel tank was increased to 12 L (3.2 US gal; 2.6 imp gal), which gave the scooter a 340 km (210 mi) range.
</p><p>Also in 1956 MZ introduced the RT 125/2 motorcycle, for which it marginally increased engine power to 6 PS. Both the weight reduction and the power increase were small, and the <i>Wiesel</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s <a href="/wiki/Power-to-weight_ratio" title="Power-to-weight ratio">power-to-weight ratio</a> was inferior to that of numerous western competitors. IWL claimed a 60 km/h (37 mph) cruising speed,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200996_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200996-7">[7]</a></sup> but in reality the <i>Wiesel</i> was only 3 or 4 km/h (2 mph) faster than the <i>Pitty</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200995_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200995-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>Despite customer complaints, the <i>Wiesel</i> retained its predecessor's hard upholstery, lockable fuel tap flap and lack of a steering lock.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200996_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200996-7">[7]</a></sup> It was easy for a thief to force the flap, and <i>Der deutsche Straßenverkehr</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s testers were astonished that IWL had not rectified this fault from the previous model.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200996_7-4"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200996-7">[7]</a></sup> IWL produced the <i>Wiesel</i> until 1959, by which time a total of 57,400 had been built.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERönicke200947_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERönicke200947-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
<div style="clear:both;"></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="SR_59_Berlin">SR 59 <i>Berlin</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Industriewerke_Ludwigsfelde&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: SR 59 Berlin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:IWL_scooter_orange_%26_grey.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="960" data-file-width="1280" decoding="async" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/IWL_scooter_orange_%26_grey.jpg/220px-IWL_scooter_orange_%26_grey.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/IWL_scooter_orange_%26_grey.jpg/330px-IWL_scooter_orange_%26_grey.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/IWL_scooter_orange_%26_grey.jpg/440px-IWL_scooter_orange_%26_grey.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:IWL_scooter_orange_%26_grey.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Some IWL enthusiasts keep their scooters' appearance as standard, while others such as the owner of this SR 59 <i>Berlin</i> have adopted highly individual custom colour schemes.</div></div></div>
<p>In 1959 IWL replaced the SR 56 <i>Wiesel</i> with the <b>SR 59 <i>Berlin</i></b>. This shared the same bodywork as the <i>Wiesel</i> but had well-upholstered separate saddles for the rider and pillion passenger.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200997_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200997-11">[11]</a></sup> Also in 1959, MZ introduced the RT 125/3 motorcycle, which had not only a slightly more powerful engine but also a four-speed transmission. IWL specially requested that the version of the RT 125/3 motor made for the scooter be bored out<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200996_7-5"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200996-7">[7]</a></sup> to 143 cc, which increased power output to 7.5 bhp.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERönicke200948_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERönicke200948-12">[12]</a></sup> IWL credibly claimed a top speed of 82 km/h (51 mph) and a cruising speed of 70 km/h (43 mph).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200997_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200997-11">[11]</a></sup> The four-speed transmission improved the machine's flexibility, making hill-climbing significantly less strenuous.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200996_7-6"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200996-7">[7]</a></sup> The <i>Berlin</i> at last gave riders in the DDR a scooter that was good enough for practical use. It was therefore just as well that IWL at last included a steering lock to secure the relatively desirable <i>Berlin</i> against theft.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200997_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200997-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>The <i>Berlin</i> still had less power than many of its western counterparts. In common with its predecessors, its front suspension was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Suspension_(vehicle)#Damping" title="Suspension (vehicle)">undamped</a>, which on roads as bumpy as the DDR's was a significant weak point.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200997_11-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200997-11">[11]</a></sup> Nevertheless, the <i>Berlin</i> secured IWL's first export orders.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200997_11-4"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200997-11">[11]</a></sup> IWL produced the <i>Berlin</i> until the end of 1962, by which time a total of 113,943 had been built.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERönicke200948_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERönicke200948-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Campi_trailer"><i>Campi</i> trailer</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Industriewerke_Ludwigsfelde&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Campi trailer">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Although IWL projected the <i>Berlin</i> as a "town scooter", new private motor vehicles were in such short supply in the DDR that customers made no such distinction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200998_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200998-13">[13]</a></sup> The <i>Berlin</i> was capable of longer journeys, including holidays, but a scooter has far less luggage capacity than either a small car or a motorcycle and <a href="/wiki/Sidecar" title="Sidecar">sidecar</a> combination.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200998_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200998-13">[13]</a></sup> Instead of devising a sidecar to fit the <i>Berlin</i>, IWL collaborated with Stoye of Leipzig, the DDR's sidecar manufacturer, to develop a lightweight, single-wheel trailer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200998_13-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200998-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:272px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Berliner-Rollergespann-links-1.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="885" data-file-width="1885" decoding="async" height="127" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/Berliner-Rollergespann-links-1.jpg/270px-Berliner-Rollergespann-links-1.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/Berliner-Rollergespann-links-1.jpg/405px-Berliner-Rollergespann-links-1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/Berliner-Rollergespann-links-1.jpg/540px-Berliner-Rollergespann-links-1.jpg 2x" width="270"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Berliner-Rollergespann-links-1.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>SR 59 <i>Berlin</i> with IWL-Stoye <i>Campi</i> trailer.</div></div></div>
<p>The result was the <i>Campi</i> trailer, which was styled to complement a scooter and was roomy enough to carry a set of 1960s camping equipment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200998_13-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200998-13">[13]</a></sup> IWL made the <i>Campi</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s chassis, including a tubular steel towing link that connected to the scooter behind the pillion seat and above the rear light.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERönicke200948_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERönicke200948-12">[12]</a></sup> Stoye made the bodywork, which is aluminium and contributes to the trailer weighing only 30 kg (66 lb).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERönicke200948_12-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERönicke200948-12">[12]</a></sup> This made it light enough for a <i>Berlin</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s 7.5 bhp engine to cope with the combined weight of its rider, passenger, trailer and luggage, albeit at markedly less speed than when being ridden solo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERönicke200948_12-4"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERönicke200948-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p><p>The <i>Campi</i> added inconveniently to the length of the scooter, while adding less luggage capacity than a sidecar.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200998_13-4"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200998-13">[13]</a></sup> Because of these limitations, its use was confined chiefly to holiday and leisure journeys.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder200998_13-5"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder200998-13">[13]</a></sup> The <i>Campi</i> was made until 1965, by when about 5,700 had been built.
</p>
<div style="clear:both;"></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="TR_150_Troll_1">TR 150 <i>Troll</i> 1</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Industriewerke_Ludwigsfelde&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: TR 150 Troll 1">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:257px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Troll001.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1704" data-file-width="2272" decoding="async" height="191" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Troll001.jpg/255px-Troll001.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Troll001.jpg/383px-Troll001.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Troll001.jpg/510px-Troll001.jpg 2x" width="255"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Troll001.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>1964 example of the TR 150 <i>Troll</i> 1, with original handlebar-end indicators</div></div></div>
<p>In 1963 IWL replaced the SR 59 <i>Berlin</i> with the <b>TR 150 <i>Troll</i> 1</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder2009134_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder2009134-14">[14]</a></sup> Whereas the <i>Wiesel</i> and <i>Berlin</i> had retailed for 2,300 Marks,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERönicke200947,_48_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERönicke200947,_48-15">[15]</a></sup> the same price as a <i>Pitty</i> in 1954,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERönicke200946_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERönicke200946-5">[5]</a></sup> for the <i>Troll</i> 1 the price was increased to 2,550 Marks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERönicke200949_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERönicke200949-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p><p>"TR" stands for <i><b>T</b>ouren<b>Rol</b>ler</i> and the <a href="/wiki/Backronym" title="Backronym">backronym</a> <i>"Troll"</i> stands for <i><b>T</b>ouren<b>Rol</b>ler <b>L</b>udwigsfelde</i> ("Ludwigsfelde touring scooter"),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESalzmann1999184_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESalzmann1999184-8">[8]</a></sup> emphasising the longer journeys on which IWL scooters were now being ridden. The "1" at the end evidently indicated that IWL hoped to develop a further model.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder2009134_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder2009134-14">[14]</a></sup> However, in December 1962, during the <i>Troll</i> 1's development, the <i>Ministerrat der DDR</i> ("<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Council_of_Ministers_of_the_GDR" title="Council of Ministers of the GDR">Council of Ministers of the GDR</a>") had announced that the factory would switch to making a new model of <a href="/wiki/Industrieverband_Fahrzeugbau" title="Industrieverband Fahrzeugbau">IFA</a> <a href="/wiki/Truck" title="Truck">truck</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000438_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000438-17">[17]</a></sup> This decision may have acted as a disincentive to improve the <i>Troll</i> 1 from before the model's launch in 1963 until the end of production in 1965.
</p><p>Both in Germany and in most of the European states to which IWL might have hoped to export scooters, <a href="/wiki/Troll" title="Troll">trolls</a> are known from <a href="/wiki/Norse_mythology" title="Norse mythology">Norse mythology</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scandinavian_folklore" title="Scandinavian folklore">Scandinavian folklore</a> as beings that are in many cases slow-witted, in some cases ugly, seldom friendly to humans and in a few cases inclined to kill and eat people. Why any vehicle maker would choose to name one of its models after such creatures is not clear.
</p><p>In 1962 MZ had introduced the ES 150 motorcycle, and the scooter version of its 143 cc engine supplied for the <i>Troll</i> 1 had its power increased to 9.5 bhp.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERönicke200949_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERönicke200949-16">[16]</a></sup> The rear chain runs in an oilbath in a patent MZ hard-rubber enclosure, which keeps oil in and dirt out and greatly prolongs chain life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESalzmann1999184_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESalzmann1999184-8">[8]</a></sup> IWL revised the bodywork, giving a tail unit like that of the Heinkel <i>Tourist</i> model A2<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder2009135_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder2009135-18">[18]</a></sup> and quickly detachable side panels<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESalzmann1999185_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESalzmann1999185-19">[19]</a></sup> like a <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=D%C3%BCrkopp&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dürkopp (page does not exist)">Dürkopp</a> <i>Diana</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder2009135_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder2009135-18">[18]</a></sup> Each side panel is secured by a central bayonet lock and is simple to undo and light to remove,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESalzmann1999185_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESalzmann1999185-19">[19]</a></sup> easing access to the engine, gearbox and rear chain.
</p><p>For the <i>Troll</i> 1, IWL reverted to a dualseat,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERönicke200949_16-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERönicke200949-16">[16]</a></sup> perhaps because twin saddles were becoming dated. The <i>Troll</i> 1 had 160 mm (6.3 in) diameter <a href="/wiki/Drum_brake" title="Drum brake">drum brakes</a>:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERönicke200949_16-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERönicke200949-16">[16]</a></sup> 10 mm (0.4 in) bigger than on all previous IWL models.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERönicke200946–48_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERönicke200946–48-20">[20]</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:IWL-Stadtroller_Troll.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1599" data-file-width="2399" decoding="async" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/IWL-Stadtroller_Troll.jpg/220px-IWL-Stadtroller_Troll.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/IWL-Stadtroller_Troll.jpg/330px-IWL-Stadtroller_Troll.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/IWL-Stadtroller_Troll.jpg/440px-IWL-Stadtroller_Troll.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:IWL-Stadtroller_Troll.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>On this <i>Troll</i> 1 the original indicators have been replaced with more conventionally positioned modern ones.</div></div></div>
<p>The <i>Troll</i> 1 had <a href="/wiki/Automotive_lighting#Turn_signals" title="Automotive lighting">indicators</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESalzmann1999185_19-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESalzmann1999185-19">[19]</a></sup> as a standard factory fitment. The front indicators are at the ends of the handlebars in a style that was becoming customary in both the BRD and the DDR at the time. In order to increase economies of scale IWL revised its scooter design to use other MZ ES 150 parts including the headlight <a href="/wiki/Nacelle" title="Nacelle">nacelle</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESalzmann1999186_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESalzmann1999186-21">[21]</a></sup> handlebars,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERönicke200949_16-4"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERönicke200949-16">[16]</a></sup> front and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Suspension_(motorcycle)#Shock_absorbers" title="Suspension (motorcycle)">rear shock absorbers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Motorcycle_fork#Earles" title="Motorcycle fork">Earles front fork</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder2009135_18-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder2009135-18">[18]</a></sup> This made the <i>Troll</i> 1 the first IWL scooter to have hydraulically damped front suspension.
</p><p>The front mudguard of the <i>Wiesel</i> and <i>Berlin</i> would not fit with the Earles fork assembly, so IWL designed a new one to accommodate the movement of the front suspension. This necessitated a bulbous shape that was inconsistent with the improved styling of the tail unit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder2009135_18-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder2009135-18">[18]</a></sup> In 1964 East Germany's annual book <i>Motor Jahr</i> circumspectly observed <i>"Diese Vorderradhaube liegt jetzt zwischen den Federbeinen, was vielleicht nicht jedermanns Geschmack trifft, aber deren gute Zugänglichkeit gewährleistet"</i>. ("This front mudguard now lies between the shock absorbers, which perhaps does not meet everyone's taste, but it ensures good access".) However, in February 1963 <i>Der deutsche Straßenverkehr</i> magazine had been more outspoken: <i>"Uns konnte diese ›Linie‹ nicht sonderlich begeistern, und wir beneiden das Institut für industrielle Formgebung keinesfalls um seine Mitarbeit"</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder2009135_18-4"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder2009135-18">[18]</a></sup> ("These 'lines' cannot particularly enthuse us, and we do not at all envy the Institute for Industrial Design in their collaboration.").
</p><p>The main chassis member is a welded box-section frame.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESalzmann1999184_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESalzmann1999184-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERönicke200949_16-5"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERönicke200949-16">[16]</a></sup> IWL scooters had a long <a href="/wiki/Wheelbase" title="Wheelbase">wheelbase</a> and the <i>Troll</i> 1 was taller than its predecessors, which made the seating position more comfortable but made the machine more vulnerable to crosswinds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder2009135_18-5"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder2009135-18">[18]</a></sup> In March 1964 <i>Der deutsche Straßenverkehr</i> strongly criticised this shortcoming, but in the same year <i>Motor Jahr</i> played it down by claiming <i>"Die Seitenwindempfindlichkeit des „Troll“ liegt in normalen Grenzen. Er reagiert auf Seitenböen nicht anders als durchschnittliche Zweiradfahrzeuge und is deshalb trotz seiner etwas höheren Schwerpunktlage auch in solchen Situationen risikilos zu fahren."</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESalzmann1999187_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESalzmann1999187-22">[22]</a></sup> ("The <i>Troll</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s crosswind stability lies within normal limits. It reacts to side gusts no differently than average two-wheeled vehicles and therefore despite a somewhat higher centre of gravity it is safe to ride in such conditions.") Customers were not fooled, and the <i>Troll</i> 1's crosswind instability damaged its reputation and was partly responsible for the model selling less well than its predecessors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder2009135_18-6"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder2009135-18">[18]</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tatran_S125-2012-03-02.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="720" data-file-width="960" decoding="async" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Tatran_S125-2012-03-02.jpg/220px-Tatran_S125-2012-03-02.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Tatran_S125-2012-03-02.jpg/330px-Tatran_S125-2012-03-02.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Tatran_S125-2012-03-02.jpg/440px-Tatran_S125-2012-03-02.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Tatran_S125-2012-03-02.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Czechoslovak-made Tatran S 125 in Slovakia</div></div></div>
<p>At the end of 1965 IWL ended scooter production in order to switch to making <a href="/wiki/IFA_W_50" title="IFA W 50">IFA W 50</a> trucks. By the time scooter production ended, IWL had built a total of 56,513 <i>Troll</i> 1's.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERönicke200949_16-6"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERönicke200949-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tatran_S_125">Tatran S 125</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Industriewerke_Ludwigsfelde&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Tatran S 125">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>From 1966 a scooter from the ČSSR was imported for sale in the DDR.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder2009136_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder2009136-23">[23]</a></sup> The Tatran S 125 had only a 124 cc engine producing 7 PS.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder2009136_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder2009136-23">[23]</a></sup> However, with less weight and a shorter wheelbase than any IWL model, the Tatran's power-to-weight ratio was practical and at last offered East Germans a real "town scooter".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESchröder2009136_23-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESchröder2009136-23">[23]</a></sup>
</p>
<div style="clear:both;"></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_vehicles">Commercial vehicles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Industriewerke_Ludwigsfelde&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Commercial vehicles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From 1947 IFA concentrated production of large trucks at the former <a href="/wiki/Horch" title="Horch">Horch</a> factory in <a href="/wiki/Zwickau" title="Zwickau">Zwickau</a> in Saxony.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg200081ff,_730_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg200081ff,_730-24">[24]</a></sup> In 1958 <i>VEB Waggonfabrik Werdau</i> at <a href="/wiki/Werdau" title="Werdau">Werdau</a> in Saxony (the former <i>Waggonfabrik Schumann</i>) took over as the main IFA truck factory,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000235ff_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000235ff-25">[25]</a></sup> leaving the <i><a href="/wiki/HQM_Sachsenring_GmbH" title="HQM Sachsenring GmbH">VEB Sachsenring Automobilwerke Zwickau</a></i> to expand production of the new <i><a href="/wiki/Trabant" title="Trabant">Trabant</a></i> small car. In 1966 truck production was moved again, from Werdau to IWL at Ludwigsfelde.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="IFA_W_50">IFA W 50</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Industriewerke_Ludwigsfelde&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: IFA W 50">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/IFA_W_50" title="IFA W 50">IFA W 50</a></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bundesarchiv_Bild_183-1982-0315-012,_Rostock,_%C3%9Cberseehafen,_LKW_W50.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="566" data-file-width="800" decoding="async" height="156" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Bundesarchiv_Bild_183-1982-0315-012%2C_Rostock%2C_%C3%9Cberseehafen%2C_LKW_W50.jpg/220px-Bundesarchiv_Bild_183-1982-0315-012%2C_Rostock%2C_%C3%9Cberseehafen%2C_LKW_W50.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Bundesarchiv_Bild_183-1982-0315-012%2C_Rostock%2C_%C3%9Cberseehafen%2C_LKW_W50.jpg/330px-Bundesarchiv_Bild_183-1982-0315-012%2C_Rostock%2C_%C3%9Cberseehafen%2C_LKW_W50.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Bundesarchiv_Bild_183-1982-0315-012%2C_Rostock%2C_%C3%9Cberseehafen%2C_LKW_W50.jpg/440px-Bundesarchiv_Bild_183-1982-0315-012%2C_Rostock%2C_%C3%9Cberseehafen%2C_LKW_W50.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Bundesarchiv_Bild_183-1982-0315-012,_Rostock,_%C3%9Cberseehafen,_LKW_W50.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>W 50 trucks from IWL on a quayside at <a href="/wiki/Rostock" title="Rostock">Rostock</a> in March 1982, being loaded onto a ship for export.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:257px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:W50undL60-Export.tif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1843" data-file-width="3020" decoding="async" height="156" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/W50undL60-Export.tif/lossy-page1-255px-W50undL60-Export.tif.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/W50undL60-Export.tif/lossy-page1-383px-W50undL60-Export.tif.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/W50undL60-Export.tif/lossy-page1-510px-W50undL60-Export.tif.jpg 2x" width="255"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:W50undL60-Export.tif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Ludwigsfelde truck production 1978–90: export sales in light green; domestic sales in dark red.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000761_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000761-26">[26]</a></sup></div></div></div>
<p>The <b>IFA W 50</b>, built at Ludwigsfelde from 17 July 1965 until 1990, was IFA's most important product for decades. Annual sales peaked at 32,516 in 1986.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000756_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000756-27">[27]</a></sup> A high proportion of IWL trucks was exported. Between 1978 and 1987 over 80% of W 50 trucks built each year were sold abroad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000756_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000756-27">[27]</a></sup> Total W 50 production at Ludwigsfelde from 1965 to 1990 was 571,831 vehicles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000637_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000637-28">[28]</a></sup> It continued to be built alongside its intended replacement, the L 60.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="IFA_L_60"><span id="L60"></span> IFA L 60</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Industriewerke_Ludwigsfelde&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: IFA L 60">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:IFA_Treffen_Ludwigsfelde_50.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="797" data-file-width="1200" decoding="async" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c7/IFA_Treffen_Ludwigsfelde_50.jpg/220px-IFA_Treffen_Ludwigsfelde_50.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c7/IFA_Treffen_Ludwigsfelde_50.jpg/330px-IFA_Treffen_Ludwigsfelde_50.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c7/IFA_Treffen_Ludwigsfelde_50.jpg/440px-IFA_Treffen_Ludwigsfelde_50.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:IFA_Treffen_Ludwigsfelde_50.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Former <i><a href="/wiki/National_People%27s_Army" title="National People's Army">NVA</a></i>-L60</div></div></div>

<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:257px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:IFA_L60_2.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="870" data-file-width="1200" decoding="async" height="185" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/IFA_L60_2.jpg/255px-IFA_L60_2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/IFA_L60_2.jpg/383px-IFA_L60_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/IFA_L60_2.jpg/510px-IFA_L60_2.jpg 2x" width="255"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:IFA_L60_2.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>L 60 truck</div></div></div>
<p>After the launch of the W 50, IWL undertook development work to increase engine power and reduce fuel consumption, reduce empty weight and increase payload, improve reliability and driver comfort and reduce production time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg20006_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg20006-29">[29]</a></sup> From 1967 IWL was planning a range of new trucks with sizes of three, five, six and 10 tonnes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000448_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000448-30">[30]</a></sup> Two prototypes were built: a three-tonne truck code-numbered <b>1013</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000447–448_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000447–448-31">[31]</a></sup> followed by an 11-tonne one code-numbered <b>1118</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000448–449_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000448–449-32">[32]</a></sup>
</p><p>In the first quarter of 1970 development work resumed with two new prototypes, one each of the 1013 and 1118.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000449_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000449-33">[33]</a></sup> Considerable capacity at IWL continued to be taken up with looking after the W 50, but development of the 1118 slowly continued.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000451_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000451-34">[34]</a></sup>
</p><p>The 1118 became the <b>IFA L 60</b>, and development intensified from 1974.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000634_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000634-35">[35]</a></sup> In 1978 the DDR made an agreement with <a href="/wiki/Volvo_Trucks" title="Volvo Trucks">Volvo Trucks</a> to use a Volvo cab on an L 60 prototype.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000451_34-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000451-34">[34]</a></sup> Within two years this resulted in a contract to make Volvo cabs under licence, for which a new production hall would be built at Ludwigsfelde.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000451_34-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000451-34">[34]</a></sup> The contract would include IWL supplying cab shells to the Volvo truck plant at <a href="/wiki/Ghent" title="Ghent">Ghent</a> in Belgium and also allowed IWL to supply cabs to <i><a href="/wiki/Robur_(truck)" title="Robur (truck)">VEB Robur-Werke Zittau</a></i> in Saxony, which made smaller trucks ranging from about 1.5 to 3.5 tonnes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000452_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000452-36">[36]</a></sup>
</p><p>When the preparatory work for the new assembly hall at Ludwigsfelde was well advanced, Volvo tripled the price.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000452_36-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000452-36">[36]</a></sup> In 1980<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000636_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000636-37">[37]</a></sup> the <i>Ministerrat der DDR</i> cancelled the agreement and ordered that series production of the L 60 was to go ahead without the Volvo cab.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000452_36-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000452-36">[36]</a></sup> IWL accordingly started development of a new cab, the 6400, both for the W 60 and to supply to Robur.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000452_36-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000452-36">[36]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1984 IWL again reached agreement to give the L 60 a foreign cab, this time from <a href="/wiki/Steyr-Daimler-Puch" title="Steyr-Daimler-Puch">Steyr</a> in Austria.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000636_37-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000636-37">[37]</a></sup> However, three years later the DDR could not meet Steyr's asking price for the design and the necessary production tools.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000636–637_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000636–637-38">[38]</a></sup> Instead the L 60 was given a cab based on that of the W 50, but able to be tilted forward for access to the engine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000635,_637_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000635,_637-39">[39]</a></sup>
</p><p>After the W 51 and W 52 projects were discontinued in June 1983 <i>(see above)</i>, the L 60 programme was adapted to the needs of Iran and Iraq.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000634–635_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000634–635-40">[40]</a></sup> The payload was increased to at least six tonnes and power output was increased to a range from 125 to 180 bhp.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000635_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000635-41">[41]</a></sup>
</p><p>The L 60 was finally unveiled to the public at the Autumn <a href="/wiki/Leipzig_Trade_Fair" title="Leipzig Trade Fair">Leipzig Trade Fair</a> in September 1986 and its series production began at Ludwigsfelde in June 1987.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000637_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000637-28">[28]</a></sup> L 60 development had started by 1967 so the model arguably reached the market about 15 years late, and its cab derived from that of the W 50 made it look even more out of date. L 60 sales did grow each year, and in 1989 they peaked at 8,081 vehicles for that year. Total L 60 production at Ludwigsfelde from 1987 to 1990 was 20,293 vehicles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000637_28-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000637-28">[28]</a></sup>
</p><p>Potential customers within the DDR were deterred not by its appearance but by its price, which was almost double that of the W 50.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000637_28-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000637-28">[28]</a></sup> As a result, IWL kept the W 50 in production alongside the L 60 until 1990.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000637_28-4"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000637-28">[28]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_end_of_IFA">The end of IFA</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Industriewerke_Ludwigsfelde&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: The end of IFA">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The start of L 60 sales in 1987 was a turning point, but not in the way IWL wanted. After 1986, W 50 sales fell more quickly than L 60 sales grew, so IWL's total unit sales fell from an all-time peak of 32,516 in 1986 to 28,152 in 1989.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000756_27-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000756-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000761_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000761-26">[26]</a></sup> IWL needed a more competitive product, and in the second half of 1989 it held exploratory talks with the West German commercial vehicle builder <a href="/wiki/MAN_SE" title="MAN SE">MAN</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000637_28-5"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000637-28">[28]</a></sup> However, these did not lead to a joint project.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:257px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:IFA_1318.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="926" data-file-width="1011" decoding="async" height="234" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/IFA_1318.jpg/255px-IFA_1318.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/IFA_1318.jpg/383px-IFA_1318.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/IFA_1318.jpg/510px-IFA_1318.jpg 2x" width="255"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:IFA_1318.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>IFA 1318 prototype with Mercedes cab</div></div></div>
<p>The DDR's <a href="/wiki/Peaceful_Revolution" title="Peaceful Revolution">Peaceful Revolution</a> took place in September and October 1989. This was followed by contact between IWL and <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz" title="Mercedes-Benz">Mercedes-Benz</a> in February 1990 that launched a joint project designated <b>IFA 1318</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000637_28-6"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000637-28">[28]</a></sup> A prototype IFA 1318 truck, with a <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mercedes-Benz_LN-series&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mercedes-Benz LN-series (page does not exist)">Mercedes-Benz LN2</a> cab on an L 60 chassis,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000637_28-7"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000637-28">[28]</a></sup> was completed on 3 May 1990.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spiegel_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Spiegel-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000685_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000685-42">[42]</a></sup> The <i>IFA-Kombinat Nutzkraftwagen</i> ("IFA Goods Vehicle Combine") was broken up and in June 1990 a separate <i>IFA-Automobilwerk Ludwigsfelde GmbH</i> was founded under the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Treuhand" title="Treuhand">Treuhandanstalt</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000686_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000686-43">[43]</a></sup>
</p><p>However, the DDR's <a href="/wiki/East_German_mark#Adoption_of_the_West_German_deutsche_Mark" title="East German mark">adoption of the West German <i>Deutsche Mark</i></a> on 1 July 1990 led Mercedes-Benz to withdraw from the intended deal by 18 July.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spiegel_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Spiegel-2">[2]</a></sup> As a result, IWL projected that it would reduce its workforce from 8,334 at the end of June 1990 to about 3,150 by the end of the year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spiegel_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Spiegel-2">[2]</a></sup> The <i>Treuhand</i> would help with the cost of redundancy payments and 2,514 employees would be converted to other work and would be kept on short time until mid-1991.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spiegel_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-Spiegel-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Even this plan was overtaken by the collapse of its established markets in eastern Europe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000637_28-8"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000637-28">[28]</a></sup> Orders and production plummeted and in 1990 a combined total of only 17,275 trucks were completed — the lowest number since 1969.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000756_27-4"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000756-27">[27]</a></sup> L 60 production was terminated in August 1990, and W 50 production was ended 17 December that year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000756_27-5"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000756-27">[27]</a></sup>
</p>

<p>On 5 October 1990 Mercedes-Benz and the <i>Treuhand</i> reached an agreement on the future of the IWL factory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000686_43-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000686-43">[43]</a></sup> On 1 February 1991 the <i>Nutzfahrzeuge Ludwigsfelde GmbH</i> (NLG or "Ludwigsfelde Goods Vehicles") and <i>Entwicklungsgesellschaft für Kraftfahrzeugtechnik Ludwigsfelde</i> (ELG or "Ludwigsfelde Development Company for Motor Vehicle Technology) were founded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000686_43-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000686-43">[43]</a></sup> The <i>Treuhand</i> owned 75% and Mercedes-Benz owned 25%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000686_43-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000686-43">[43]</a></sup> On 8 February 1991 Ludwigsfelde assembled its first Mercedes vehicle, an LK 814, and three months later it started making Mercedes cabs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000686_43-4"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000686-43">[43]</a></sup> Ludwigsfelde began assembly of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/T2_Transporter" title="T2 Transporter">T2 "Transporter"</a> model in September and afterwards was completely transferred there from Mercedes-Benz' <a href="/wiki/D%C3%BCsseldorf" title="Düsseldorf">Düsseldorf</a> plant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000686_43-5"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000686-43">[43]</a></sup>
</p><p>On 1 December 1993 Mercedes-Benz and the <i>Treuhand</i> made a new agreement, that from 1 January 1994 NLG and EGL would become 100% subsidiaries of Mercedes-Benz.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000687_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000687-44">[44]</a></sup> At the same time the Ludwigsfelde factory would take over all production of Mercedes goods vehicles in the 4.5 tonne to 7.5 tonne range.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000687_44-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000687-44">[44]</a></sup> On 1 July 1996 Ludwigsfelde began production of a further development of the T 2, the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_Vario" title="Mercedes-Benz Vario">W670 Vario</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000687_44-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000687-44">[44]</a></sup> On 1 July 1997 NLG and EGL were merged as <i>Daimler-Benz Ludwigsfelde GmbH</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000687_44-3"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000687-44">[44]</a></sup>
</p><p>Between December 1999 and 2001 DaimlerChrysler (the parent company between 1998 and 2007) invested in the order of 500 million <i><a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Mark" title="Deutsche Mark">Deutsche Mark</a></i> to redevelop the Ludwigsfelde plant and prepare it to make the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_Vaneo" title="Mercedes-Benz Vaneo">W414 <i>Vaneo</i></a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000687_44-4"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKirchberg2000687-44">[44]</a></sup> Ludwigsfelde made the Vaneo from 25 September 2001 until 8 July 2005. Since 2006 the plant has made <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_Sprinter" title="Mercedes-Benz Sprinter">NCV3 <i>Sprinter</i></a> vans, and major elements of the <a href="/wiki/Volkswagen_Crafter" title="Volkswagen Crafter">VW LT3 <i>Crafter</i></a>.
