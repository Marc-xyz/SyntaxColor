		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>Two ships of the United States Navy have been named <b>USS <i>Spruance</i></b>, in honor of Admiral <a href="/wiki/Raymond_A._Spruance" title="Raymond A. Spruance">Raymond A. Spruance</a> (1886–1969):
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/USS_Spruance_(DD-963)" title="USS Spruance (DD-963)">USS <i>Spruance</i> <span class="nowrap">(DD-963)</span></a> was the lead ship of <a href="/wiki/Spruance-class_destroyer" title="Spruance-class destroyer"><i>Spruance</i>-class</a> <a href="/wiki/Destroyer" title="Destroyer">destroyers</a>, launched in 1973 and struck in 2005</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/USS_Spruance_(DDG-111)" title="USS Spruance (DDG-111)">USS <i>Spruance</i> <span class="nowrap">(DDG-111)</span></a> is an <a href="/wiki/Arleigh_Burke-class_destroyer" title="Arleigh Burke-class destroyer"><i>Arleigh Burke</i>-class</a> <a href="/wiki/Destroyer" title="Destroyer">destroyer</a>, launched in 2010 and commissioned in 2011</li></ul>
