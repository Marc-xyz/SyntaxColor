<p><b>Leela Devi Dookhun Luchoomun</b> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" title="Member of Parliament">MP</a> (born ळीला डेवि डूखुन ळुचूमुन on 16 April 1961) is the former <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Minister_of_Social_Security_(Mauritius)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Minister of Social Security (Mauritius) (page does not exist)">Minister of Social Security</a> of <a href="/wiki/Mauritius" title="Mauritius">Mauritius</a> serving in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cabinet_of_Ministers_of_Mauritius" title="Cabinet of Ministers of Mauritius">cabinet</a> of <a href="/wiki/Navin_Ramgoolam" title="Navin Ramgoolam">Navin Ramgoolam</a> having been appointed on 11 May 2010 by President <a href="/wiki/Anerood_Jugnauth" title="Anerood Jugnauth">Anerood Jugnauth</a>. She is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a> representing <a href="/wiki/Constituencies_of_Mauritius" title="Constituencies of Mauritius">Constituency No 8, Moka &amp; Quartier Militaire</a>. She is a former Minister of Arts &amp; Culture serving in the cabinet of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paul_Berenger" title="Paul Berenger">Paul Berenger</a> for a short period of 6 months.
</p><p>She was appointed as <a href="/wiki/Parliamentary_Private_Secretary" title="Parliamentary Private Secretary">Parliamentary Private Secretary</a> in September 2000 after the <a href="/wiki/MSM/MMM" title="MSM/MMM">MSM/MMM</a> coalition won the elections. She firstly served in the cabinet of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sir_Anerood_Jugnauth" title="Sir Anerood Jugnauth">Sir Anerood Jugnauth</a>. She is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Militant_Socialist_Movement" title="Militant Socialist Movement">Militant Socialist Movement</a> and is the Vice President of the Party. She is also President of the Women Wing of the same political party. She is along with <a href="/wiki/Nando_Bodha" title="Nando Bodha">Nando Bodha</a> and Showkatally Soodhun to form the front bench of the MSM in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ptr-MSM-PMSD" title="Ptr-MSM-PMSD">Ptr-MSM-PMSD</a> coalition government.
</p><p>She is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Educator" title="Educator">Educator</a> by profession. She is well known for being a highly renowned Biology Teacher in the country. Leela Devi Dookhun Luchoomun has a rich political career. In 1996, she joined the <a href="/wiki/Militant_Socialist_Movement" title="Militant Socialist Movement">Militant Socialist Movement</a> and took part in the municipal elections of Vacoas-Phoenix, then stood as candidate in Constituency No 15, La Caverne &amp; Phoenix in 2000. She was elected and served as 1st Member of parliament for the constituency. In 2005, the <a href="/wiki/MSM/MMM" title="MSM/MMM">MSM/MMM</a> coalition lost to <a href="/wiki/Social_Alliance" title="Social Alliance">Social Alliance</a> and she was elected 3rd MP for the same constituency but serving in the opposition. She was member of the opposition from 2005 to 2010 before the general elections in 2010.
</p><p>She holds a BSc Hons from the University of Delhi and completed her <a href="/wiki/Postgraduate_Certificate_in_Education" title="Postgraduate Certificate in Education">Postgraduate Certificate in Education</a> at the MIE and her <a href="/wiki/Postgraduate_Diploma_in_Education" title="Postgraduate Diploma in Education">Postgraduate Diploma in Education</a> (PGDip) from the University of Brighton. She is also a Part-time lecturer at the Mauritius Institute of Education. She is married and is the mother of two children.
</p>
