<p><b>Mayo</b> is a municipality in the <a href="/wiki/Papineau_Regional_County_Municipality" title="Papineau Regional County Municipality">Papineau Regional County Municipality</a> of western Quebec, located northeast of the city of Gatineau (Buckingham sector). Mayo is south of the hamlet of <a href="/wiki/Mulgrave-et-Derry,_Quebec" title="Mulgrave-et-Derry, Quebec">Mulgrave-et-Derry</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mayo,_Quebec&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area which would become the municipality of Mayo in 1864, just north of the township of <a href="/wiki/Lochaber" title="Lochaber">Lochaber</a>, began to be settled in the nineteenth century mainly by Irish and German immigrants as well as French Canadians.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MRC_Papineau_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-MRC_Papineau-6">[6]</a></sup>  The Irish settlers arrived in Mayo between 1820-1830, pre-famine immigrants.  They would have arrived in Montreal and traveled along the Ottawa river and then along the Blanche River.  The settlers would have found great comfort in their new surroundings, the hilly terrain resembling that of Ireland, and subsequently their new home was named Mayo after <a href="/wiki/County_Mayo" title="County Mayo">County Mayo</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>  The main industries which drive the economics of the municipality were, and still are, farming and forestry as well as the manufacturing of <a href="/wiki/Potash" title="Potash">potash</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MRC_Papineau_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-MRC_Papineau-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Our_Lady_of_Knock_Shrine">Our Lady of Knock Shrine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mayo,_Quebec&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Our Lady of Knock Shrine">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The people of Mayo had a strong connection with their Irish roots.  The parish of St. Malachy in Mayo was very important in the municipality's connection with <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>.  In County Mayo, Ireland there is a town called <a href="/wiki/Knock,_County_Mayo" title="Knock, County Mayo">Knock</a> where, in 1879, there were reports of an apparition of the Virgin Mary, St. Joseph and St. John.  The story of this miracle was brought to Canada as early as 1882, and in 1949 the then pastor of St. Malachy, Father Braceland, was inspired to construct a replica of the <a href="/wiki/Knock_Shrine" title="Knock Shrine">Our Lady of Knock Shrine</a> in Mayo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="La_Blanche_Forest">La Blanche Forest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mayo,_Quebec&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: La Blanche Forest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The La Blanche forest, located on the eastern shores of <a href="/wiki/Lac_La_Blanche" title="Lac La Blanche">La Blanche Lake</a> in Mayo and Mulgrave-et-Derry, contains rare <a href="/wiki/Old-growth_forest" title="Old-growth forest">old-growth forest</a> and many endangered plant species such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Panax_quinquefolius" title="Panax quinquefolius">ginseng</a>.  The forest is protected in the <a href="/wiki/For%C3%AAt-la-Blanche_Ecological_Reserve" title="Forêt-la-Blanche Ecological Reserve">Forêt-la-Blanche Ecological Reserve</a>, a 20.52 square kilometres (7.92 sq mi) area of preservation for this forest which has been sheltered from disturbance for centuries.  The forest is also home to many species of rare birds such as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Great_Blue_Heron" title="Great Blue Heron">blue heron</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Cerulean_warbler" title="Cerulean warbler">cerulean warbler</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>  The La Blanche forest was designated a permanent protected area in October 2003 giving it the legal distinction of an ecological reserve, the highest protection possible in Quebec<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
