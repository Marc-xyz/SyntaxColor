	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Solombala Shipyard</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Solombala Shipyard</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Соломбальская верфь</span>) was the main shipyard servicing <a href="/wiki/Arkhangelsk" title="Arkhangelsk">Arkhangelsk</a>, Russia. It existed from 1693 to 1862. The town of Arkhangelsk was Russia's main seaport until the early 18th century, when <a href="/wiki/Saint_Petersburg" title="Saint Petersburg">Saint Petersburg</a> was established.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sozaev_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sozaev-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The establishment of the Solombala yard by <a href="/wiki/Peter_the_Great" title="Peter the Great">Peter the Great</a> in 1693 signed the beginning of Russian shipbuilding on a sustained basis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kotilaine_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kotilaine-2">[2]</a></sup> Despite its remoteness and harsh climate, the Arkhangelsk region provided a number of advantages for the shipbuilding industry, such as cheap and abundant timber and access to the iron ore deposits of the Urals. Between 1702 and 1855, the Solombala shipyard completed 247 vessels.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sozaev_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Sozaev-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>It was closed in 1862 when most shipbuilding work was transferred to Saint Petersburg, but it was reopened in 1887 as a ship repair yard. The yard became known as <b>Krasnaya Kuznitsa</b> (Red Forge) in Soviet times.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It is now part of the <a href="/wiki/United_Shipbuilding_Corporation" title="United Shipbuilding Corporation">United Shipbuilding Corporation</a> group.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hist_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-hist-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
