<p><i><b>Branded</b></i> is the eleventh episode of the third series of the British comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/Dad%27s_Army" title="Dad's Army">Dad's Army</a></i>. It was originally transmitted on Thursday 20 November 1969.
</p><p>On a night of programmes devoted to Dad's Army, <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Perry" title="Jimmy Perry">Jimmy Perry</a> named it his favourite episode.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> "When we told <a href="/wiki/Arnold_Ridley" title="Arnold Ridley">Arnold Ridley</a> that we'd written a part especially for him, he was absolutely delighted. When he read the script he said, 'Jimmy, its good to mention <i><a href="/wiki/Conscientious_objector" title="Conscientious objector">conchies</a></i> as they were called, because they went through hell a lot of them, and a lot of them had high principles. I'm very honoured to play it.' Both Arnold Ridley and <a href="/wiki/John_Laurie" title="John Laurie">John Laurie</a> had served in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/First_World_War" title="First World War">First World War</a> and both served in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Somme" title="Battle of the Somme">Battle of the Somme</a> where Ridley was dreadfully badly wounded. We all knew about the war - perhaps that's what gives <i>Dad's Army</i>, as <a href="/wiki/Clint_Eastwood" title="Clint Eastwood">Clint Eastwood</a> says in <i><a href="/wiki/Pale_Rider" title="Pale Rider">Pale Rider</a></i>, that 'little bit of edge'. It's [a] quite serious [episode]. In the end dear old Godfrey is proved to have more courage than the lot of them".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Synopsis">Synopsis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Branded_(Dad%27s_Army)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Synopsis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Private_Godfrey" title="Private Godfrey">Private Godfrey</a> admits to being a conscientious objector during the Great War.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Branded_(Dad%27s_Army)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Following a standard exercise on the stealthy approach to an enemy soldier, <a href="/wiki/Captain_Mainwaring" title="Captain Mainwaring">Captain Mainwaring</a> calls <a href="/wiki/Sergeant_Wilson" title="Sergeant Wilson">Sergeant Wilson</a> into his office. He has received an alarming letter from Pte. Godfrey, informing the captain of Godfrey's intent to resign from the unit at the earliest possible convenience. Given Godfrey's vital role in the platoon (as the soldier who makes the tea), Mainwaring is unwilling to let Godfrey go, and demands an explanation. Godfrey thus tells him that his decision emerges from a recent incident in which he found a mouse in his kitchen, but found himself unwilling and incapable of killing it; and if he can't kill a mouse, how can he be expected to kill a German? During the telling of the story, Godfrey reveals that in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/First_World_War" title="First World War">previous war</a>, he was a <a href="/wiki/Conscientious_objector" title="Conscientious objector">conscientious objector</a> who refused to fight.
</p><p>Mainwaring, appalled and disgusted, orders Godfrey to get out of his sight; whilst Wilson is tolerant and understanding of Godfrey's need to follow his conscience, Mainwaring is sickened at the thought of a man not wanting to fight and, assuming Godfrey to be a coward, determines to shame and humiliate him in front of the troops. With characteristic pompousness, he convenes a <a href="/wiki/Parade" title="Parade">parade</a> of the remainder of the platoon in which to inform them of Godfrey's apparent cowardice, but his thunder is stolen by the unimpressed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Air_Raid_Precautions" title="Air Raid Precautions">ARP</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ARP_Warden_William_Hodges" title="ARP Warden William Hodges">Warden Hodges</a>, who wants to discuss an upcoming ARP/<a href="/wiki/Home_Guard_(United_Kingdom)" title="Home Guard (United Kingdom)">Home Guard</a> drill.
</p><p>Once the platoon learns of Godfrey's past, he is ostracised by the other men. Whilst many - including Corporal Jones - are undecided about their response to Godfrey's decision, and some, including Ptes. Pike and Walker, do not seem to mind, Pte. Frazer is characteristically vocal in his condemnation of Godfrey's cowardice, and has no hesitation in expressing his disgust to the other man's face. It is decided that Godfrey will remain in the unit until a replacement can be found.
</p><p>The ARP/Home Guard training drill arrives, during which Warden Hodges will teach the men how to retrieve <a href="/wiki/Unconsciousness" title="Unconsciousness">unconscious</a> bodies (represented by sacks of straw) from burning buildings filled with smoke. Naturally, Mainwaring is unimpressed by the volume of smoke in the building and fills the boiler with burning rags, thus filling the building with far more smoke than safely required. He also informs Godfrey that he has no intention of letting the other man use his "conchie tricks" to get out of this exercise (not that Godfrey had any intention of trying to get out of it), and intends to follow Godfrey through the hut to make sure that he completes the exercise.
</p><p>The remainder of the unit having passed through the hut, Godfrey makes it through, being the second-to-last member to conduct the exercise. He waits outside the huts exit for Mainwaring - who doesn't come out, despite taking longer to conduct the exercise than expected. Without hesitation, Godfrey re-enters the smoke-filled hut in order to retrieve Mainwaring who <a href="/wiki/Syncope_(medicine)" title="Syncope (medicine)">fainted</a> due to <a href="/wiki/Smoke_inhalation" title="Smoke inhalation">smoke inhalation</a>.
</p><p>Later, Godfrey recuperates from the training exercise in bed, having also experienced smoke inhalation, and is visited by the entire platoon, having overcome their earlier hatred for him in concern for his health. As Mainwaring attempts to express uncomfortable gratitude to Godfrey for saving his life, he notices a photo of a younger Godfrey, in military uniform, wearing the <a href="/wiki/Military_Medal" title="Military Medal">Military Medal</a>. Whilst refusing to fight in the First World War, Godfrey instead joined the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Army_Medical_Corps" title="Royal Army Medical Corps">Royal Army Medical Corps</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Stretcher_bearer" title="Stretcher bearer">stretcher bearer</a>, and was responsible, during the Battle of the Somme, for a tremendous act of heroism in rescuing several wounded soldiers from <a href="/wiki/No_man%27s_land" title="No man's land">no man's land</a> under heavy fire (which, with characteristic modesty, he downplays, and he refuses to wear his medal on the grounds that it feels ostentatious). Embarrassed at their earlier treatment of him as a coward (although Frazer, typically, insists that he knew it would be the case all along), the platoon apologise, and at Wilson's suggestion, Mainwaring has no hesitation in declaring Godfrey the platoon's <a href="/wiki/Combat_medic" title="Combat medic">medical orderly</a>, having learnt that heroism is not a matter of appearance.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Branded_(Dad%27s_Army)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ol><li>In 1984, a few days after Arnold Ridley's death, <a href="/wiki/BBC_One" title="BBC One">BBC1</a> repeated this episode in tribute.</li>
<li>Although the entire third series of <i>Dad's Army</i> was made in colour, the early episodes were first transmitted before the launch of <a href="/wiki/BBC_One" title="BBC One">BBC One</a>'s colour service on 15 November 1969. "Branded" was the first episode to be transmitted in colour.</li></ol>
