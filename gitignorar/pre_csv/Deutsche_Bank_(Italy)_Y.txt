<p><b>Deutsche Bank S.p.A.</b> is an Italian bank based in Milan, Lombardy. It is a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Bank" title="Deutsche Bank">Deutsche Bank</a> A.G..
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Deutsche_Bank_(Italy)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Banca dell'Italia Meridionale</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Story_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Story-2">[2]</a></sup> was found in 1917.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DeutscheBank1986AR_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-DeutscheBank1986AR-3">[3]</a></sup> It was acquired by <a href="/wiki/Amadeo_Giannini" title="Amadeo Giannini">Amadeo Giannini</a>, the founder of <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_Italy_(United_States)" title="Bank of Italy (United States)">Bank of Italy (United States)</a> in 1922. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>The bank was renamed to <b>Banca d'America e d'Italia</b> (literally the <i>Bank of America and Italy</i>). In December 1986 Deutsche Bank acquired 98.3% voting rights of the bank.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DeutscheBank1986AR_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-DeutscheBank1986AR-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>71</span></sup> In 1994 the bank was renamed to <b>Deutsche Bank S.p.A.</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Story_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Story-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1994 the DB Italy also acquired <a href="/wiki/Banca_Popolare_di_Lecco" title="Banca Popolare di Lecco">Banca Popolare di Lecco</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Story_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Story-2">[2]</a></sup> In 1999 DB Italy acquired 20% shares of <a href="/wiki/Cassa_di_Risparmio_di_Asti" title="Cassa di Risparmio di Asti">Cassa di Risparmio di Asti</a> which was sold to <a href="/wiki/Banca_Popolare_di_Milano" title="Banca Popolare di Milano">Banca Popolare di Milano</a> in 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> From 2000 to 2003 the bank also had a minority interests in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Banca_di_Cividale" title="Banca di Cividale">Banca di Cividale</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
