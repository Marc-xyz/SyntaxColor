	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Haliburton Hume Leech</h1>

<p>Flight Lieutenant <b>Haliburton Hume Leech</b> (1908-1939) was a <a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Force" title="Royal Air Force">Royal Air Force</a> aviator, air racer and test pilot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Flight_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Flight-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Leech was born on 16 April 1908 in Newcastle upon Tyne, Northumberland the son of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Joseph_William_Leech&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Joseph William Leech (page does not exist)">Joseph William Leech</a> a surgeon and later mayor and member of parliament for Newcastle upon Tyne.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mar_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-mar-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Leech joined the Royal Air Force in 1925 as a cadet and on 10 April 1926 gained his Royal Aero Club Aviators Certificate (No. 7993) at the Newcastle upon Tyne Aero Club using a <a href="/wiki/De_Havilland_Moth" title="De Havilland Moth">De Havilland Moth</a>. Commissioned in 1927 he left the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Force_College_Cranwell" title="Royal Air Force College Cranwell">Royal Air Force College Cranwell</a> and was posted to <a href="/wiki/RAF_Tangmere" title="RAF Tangmere">RAF Tangmere</a> in 1927.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Flight_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Flight-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1930 he moved to the Royal Aircraft Establishment at Farnborough as a test pilot and he was selected for the <a href="/wiki/High_Speed_Flight_RAF" title="High Speed Flight RAF">High Speed Flight</a> to compete in the Schneider Trophy Race, but after training with too many pilots being selected he returned to Farnborough without joining the team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Flight_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Flight-1">[1]</a></sup> He was a keen air racer and entered the <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Cup_Race" title="King's Cup Race">King's Cup Race</a> every year between 1929 and 1934, with his experience as a test pilot he performed the first flights of the <a href="/wiki/RAE_Scarab" title="RAE Scarab">RAE Scarab</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Arrow_Active" title="Arrow Active">Arrow Active</a>.
</p><p>He married in 1937 in Eyke in Suffolk to Ruth Elliot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Times47812_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Times47812-3">[3]</a></sup> In September 1938 he retired from the Royal Air Force due to ill-health.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-retire_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-retire-4">[4]</a></sup> Leech died on 5 May 1939 at St Bartholomews Hospital in London, aged 31.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Times48298_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Times48298-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
