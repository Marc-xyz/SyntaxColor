<p><b>Ryan Benjamin Rowland-Smith</b> (born 26 January 1983), is an Australian former <a href="/wiki/Professional_baseball" title="Professional baseball">professional baseball</a> <a href="/wiki/Pitcher" title="Pitcher">pitcher</a>. He previously played in <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> (MLB) for the <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Mariners" title="Seattle Mariners">Seattle Mariners</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Diamondbacks" title="Arizona Diamondbacks">Arizona Diamondbacks</a>. He also played in the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Baseball_League" title="Australian Baseball League">Australian Baseball League</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Sydney_Blue_Sox" title="Sydney Blue Sox">Sydney Blue Sox</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Brisbane_Bandits" title="Brisbane Bandits">Brisbane Bandits</a>. Rowland-Smith pitched for Australia in the <a href="/wiki/2006_World_Baseball_Classic" title="2006 World Baseball Classic">2006 World Baseball Classic</a> and in the <a href="/wiki/Baseball_at_the_2004_Summer_Olympics" title="Baseball at the 2004 Summer Olympics">2004 Summer Olympics</a>. He was rated the number eight prospect in the Mariners organization in 2008 by <i><a href="/wiki/Baseball_America" title="Baseball America">Baseball America</a></i>.
</p><p>He graduated from <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_High_School_(Australia)" title="Newcastle High School (Australia)">Newcastle High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Newcastle,_New_South_Wales" title="Newcastle, New South Wales">Newcastle, New South Wales</a>, Australia, in December 2000. Rowland-Smith was the first player from Newcastle to be signed to a professional contract, signing as a non-drafted <a href="/wiki/Free_agent" title="Free agent">free agent</a> on 19 November 2000 by Mariners scout Barry Holland. He was the Newcastle Baseball Player of the Year as a 16 and 18-year-old.
</p><p>Rowland-Smith played most of his career in the Mariners' organisation but he was selected by the <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Twins" title="Minnesota Twins">Minnesota Twins</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League</a> portion of the <a href="/wiki/Rule_5_draft_results#2004" title="Rule 5 draft results">2004 Rule 5 draft</a>. He spent most of the 2004 <a href="/wiki/Spring_training" title="Spring training">spring training</a> with the Twins before they returned him to Seattle.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Rowland-Smith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2001.E2.80.932005_seasons"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2001–2005_seasons">2001–2005 seasons</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Rowland-Smith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2001–2005 seasons">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Mariners signed Rowland-Smith in November, 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Rowland-Smith began his pro career in 2001 with the Rookie level <a href="/wiki/Arizona_League_Mariners" title="Arizona League Mariners">Peoria Mariners</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Arizona_League" title="Arizona League">Arizona League</a>. He made 17 relief appearances, allowing just one home run in 33.1 innings pitched. He took part in the Mariners Arizona Instructional League at the end of the season.
</p><p>He split the 2002 season between the Single-A <a href="/wiki/Wisconsin_Timber_Rattlers" title="Wisconsin Timber Rattlers">Wisconsin Timber Rattlers</a> and the Short-season <a href="/wiki/Everett_AquaSox" title="Everett AquaSox">Everett AquaSox</a>. He pitched in 12 games, 8 starts, with Wisconsin to begin the season. On 24 June, Rowland-Smith was transferred to Everett. He did not allow an earned run in six consecutive games from 6 to 24 July in 17.1 innings pitched. He finished the month of July 3–0 with 2 saves and a 0.70 ERA in 25.2 innings pitched. During his time with the AquaSox, Rowland-Smith was the third-youngest player on the roster.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Rowland-Smith had a 1.90 ERA and 52 strikeouts combined between the Single-A Wisconsin Timber Rattlers and the Class-A <a href="/wiki/Inland_Empire_66ers" title="Inland Empire 66ers">Inland Empire 66ers</a>. He began the 2003 season with Wisconsin appearing in 13 games. He did not allow an earned run 15​<span class="frac nowrap"><sup>2</sup>⁄<sub>3</sub></span> innings from 21 April to 13 May. He struck out a season-high six batters in 2​<span class="frac nowrap"><sup>2</sup>⁄<sub>3</sub></span> innings. He was named to the <a href="/wiki/Midwest_League" title="Midwest League">Midwest League</a> All-Star team. Rowland-Smith was promoted to Inland Empire on 19 June and tossed 8​<span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>3</sub></span> innings in seven games without allowing an earned run from 14 July to 9 August.
</p><p>In the 2004 season played the entire season with the Class-A Inland Empire 66ers, appearing in 29 games. He split the season between the bullpen and starter, going 2–0 with three saves and a 4.15 ERA as a reliever and 3–3 with a 3.46 ERA as a starter. He struck out 119 batters in 99​<span class="frac nowrap"><sup>2</sup>⁄<sub>3</sub></span> innings. Rowland-Smith was tied for seventh among all Mariner farmhands in strikeouts. He held opponents to a .276 average, including a .246 clip against left-handers.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Athens_Summer_Games">Athens Summer Games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Rowland-Smith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Athens Summer Games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Rowland-Smith played for <a href="/wiki/Australia_national_baseball_team" title="Australia national baseball team">Australia</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Baseball_at_the_2004_Summer_Olympics" title="Baseball at the 2004 Summer Olympics">2004 Summer Olympic games</a>, making four relief appearances, going 2–0 with a 1.23 ERA allowing only six hits and five walks while striking out 5. He helped the team to a silver medal. They made it to the championship game before falling to <a href="/wiki/Cuba_national_baseball_team" title="Cuba national baseball team">Cuba</a>, 6–2.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It was said that all of Rowland-Smith's teammates were paying close attention to the Olympic baseball gold-medal game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2006_season">2006 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Rowland-Smith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2006 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rowland-Smith started the 2006 season on the disabled list with a left elbow strain. He was reinstated on 2 June. He did not allow an earned run in five of his seven appearances with the Class-A Inland Empire 66ers. He was called up to the Double-A San Antonio Missions on 21 June. He struck out a season-high 7 batters in 4.1 innings on 25 August.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He went a season-high 11.1 consecutive innings pitched without allowing an earned run from 20 August to 2 September. Rowland-Smith limited opponents to a .241 batting average, including a .188 clip against left-handers. He was 0–1 with a 1.50 ERA and a save while striking out 23 batters in nine games in August. He played with the <a href="/wiki/Peoria_Javelinas" title="Peoria Javelinas">Peoria Javelinas</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Fall_League" title="Arizona Fall League">Arizona Fall League</a>.

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2008_season">2008 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Rowland-Smith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2008 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ryan_Rowland-Smith_2008.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1945" data-file-width="1185" decoding="async" height="328" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/Ryan_Rowland-Smith_2008.jpg/200px-Ryan_Rowland-Smith_2008.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/Ryan_Rowland-Smith_2008.jpg/300px-Ryan_Rowland-Smith_2008.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/Ryan_Rowland-Smith_2008.jpg/400px-Ryan_Rowland-Smith_2008.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ryan_Rowland-Smith_2008.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Rowland-Smith pitching against the <a href="/wiki/Oakland_Athletics" title="Oakland Athletics">Oakland Athletics</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Rowland-Smith began the season exclusively as a reliever, making 32 relief appearances before making two starts prior to the <a href="/wiki/2008_Major_League_Baseball_All-Star_Game" title="2008 Major League Baseball All-Star Game">All-Star break</a>. He made three more relief appearances before being optioned to the Triple-A <a href="/wiki/Tacoma_Rainiers" title="Tacoma Rainiers">Tacoma Rainiers</a> on 20 July to begin conversion to starter. On 1 July he made first Major League start against the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Blue_Jays" title="Toronto Blue Jays">Toronto Blue Jays</a>, tossing 3.2 innings, allowing 3 runs before being removed after 62 pitches, he allowed all 3 runs in the fourth inning. He recorded 8 consecutive <a href="/wiki/Quality_start" title="Quality start">quality starts</a> from 21 August to 27 September the longest streak by a Mariners starter since Joel Piñeiro tossed 8 consecutive quality starts in 2002. The streak was also tied for the 5th-longest in the <a href="/wiki/American_League" title="American League">AL</a> in 2008. From 21 August until the end of the season, his 2.56 ERA was the best in the American League and tied for 4th-lowest amongst left-handed starters in the Majors behind <a href="/wiki/CC_Sabathia" title="CC Sabathia">CC Sabathia</a> (1.72), <a href="/wiki/Johan_Santana" title="Johan Santana">Johan Santana</a>, NYM (1.88) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jorge_De_La_Rosa" title="Jorge De La Rosa">Jorge De La Rosa</a>, COL (2.34). Rowland-Smith collected his first Major League save 8 April against the <a href="/wiki/Tampa_Bay_Rays" title="Tampa Bay Rays">Tampa Bay Rays</a> in Seattle's 6–5 win, tossing 1.2 innings and retiring all five batters he faced, while protecting 1-run lead. As a reliever, had a 1.41 ERA in 17 games and 44 innings pitched when pitching. In Tacoma, he went 2–0 with a 2.89 ERA in 3 starts with the Rainiers, he held opponents to a .185 batting average.
</p>

On 9 December 2010 the <a href="/wiki/Houston_Astros" title="Houston Astros">Houston Astros</a> signed Rowland-Smith to a one-year contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014_season">2014 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Rowland-Smith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: 2014 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2016_season">2016 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Rowland-Smith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: 2016 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rowland-Smith pitched with the <a href="/wiki/Australia_national_baseball_team" title="Australia national baseball team">Australia national baseball team</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2017_World_Baseball_Classic_%E2%80%93_Qualifier_1" title="2017 World Baseball Classic – Qualifier 1">2017 World Baseball Classic – Qualifier 1</a> from February 11–14, 2016. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2017_season">2017 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Rowland-Smith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: 2017 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rowland-Smith signed with the <a href="/wiki/Brisbane_Bandits" title="Brisbane Bandits">Brisbane Bandits</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Baseball_League" title="Australian Baseball League">Australian Baseball League</a> in January 2017, helping the club to win its second consecutive <a href="/wiki/Australian_Baseball_League" title="Australian Baseball League">ABL</a> championship in his last season with the team. In March, Rowland-Smith competed in the <a href="/wiki/World_Baseball_Classic" title="World Baseball Classic">World Baseball Classic</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Australia_national_baseball_team" title="Australia national baseball team">Australia national baseball team</a>. Australia failed to advance past the first round, going 1-2 with a win over <a href="/wiki/China_national_baseball_team" title="China national baseball team">China</a> and losses to <a href="/wiki/Japan_national_baseball_team" title="Japan national baseball team">Japan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cuba_national_baseball_team" title="Cuba national baseball team">Cuba</a>. He appeared in only one game and faced just one batter, striking out Japan's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Norichika_Aoki" title="Norichika Aoki">Norichika Aoki</a> in the 9th inning of Australia's 4-1 loss to Japan.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-playing_career">Post-playing career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Rowland-Smith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Post-playing career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rowland-Smith is currently a baseball television analyst for <a href="/wiki/Root_Sports_Northwest" title="Root Sports Northwest">Root Sports Northwest</a>, appearing on the pre- and post-game shows for <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Mariners" title="Seattle Mariners">Seattle Mariners</a> broadcasts. He is also a baseball radio analyst for <a href="/wiki/KIRO_(AM)" title="KIRO (AM)">ESPN710 Seattle</a>, appearing on Mariners post-game shows.
</p><p>Rowland-Smith and Australian outfielder <a href="/wiki/Trent_Oeltjen" title="Trent Oeltjen">Trent Oeltjen</a> co-founded NxtGen Baseball, a training camp for young baseball players.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Rowland-Smith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rowland-Smith's grandfather was a former New South Wales minister for sport and recreation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-growing_up_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-growing_up-26">[26]</a></sup> He is the son of Australian celebrity trainer Rob Rowland-Smith, known as "The Sandhill Warrior".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-growing_up_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-growing_up-26">[26]</a></sup> Rowland-Smith's mother, Julie White, is a retired high school <a href="/wiki/Physical_education" title="Physical education">physical education</a> teacher.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-growing_up_26-2"><a href="#cite_note-growing_up-26">[26]</a></sup> His parents divorced when he was three, and he ended up living with his mother and sister in Newcastle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-growing_up_26-3"><a href="#cite_note-growing_up-26">[26]</a></sup> Rowland-Smith's older sister, Rhiannon, is a competitive <a href="/wiki/Surfing" title="Surfing">surfer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-surf_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-surf-27">[27]</a></sup> He himself is an avid surfer, and spends a lot of his time in the off-season in his native Australia surfing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-surf_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-surf-27">[27]</a></sup>
</p><p>Rowland-Smith was married on January 11, 2013, to American actress Amanda Aardsma in San Diego. Amanda is the sister of former <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> pitcher <a href="/wiki/David_Aardsma" title="David Aardsma">David Aardsma</a>. Amanda and Ryan have a daughter, Kennedy, and a son, Lennox.
</p><p>Growing-up, Rowland-Smith watched baseball on video cassettes because Major League Baseball was not carried on Australian networks back then.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-growing_up_26-4"><a href="#cite_note-growing_up-26">[26]</a></sup>
</p><p>Rowland-Smith is the first player with a hyphenated last name ever to appear in a major league game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> He maintains his own blog and Twitter account to keep up with fans.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup>
</p><p>In June 2019, Rowland-Smith appeared on the television show <a href="/wiki/American_Ninja_Warrior" title="American Ninja Warrior">American Ninja Warrior</a> in the Seattle Qualifying Round but fell on the second obstacle.
