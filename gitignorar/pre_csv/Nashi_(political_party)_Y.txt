<p><b>Nashi</b> (<a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_language" title="Ukrainian language">Ukrainian</a>: <span lang="uk">Політична партія «НАШI»</span>), translated as <b>Ours</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">political party</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a> registered February 2015. Since 2018 the party leader is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Evgeny_Murayev" title="Evgeny Murayev">Evgeny Murayev</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nashi_(political_party)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In September 2018 former members of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=For_life_(political_party)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="For life (political party) (page does not exist)">For life</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Evgeny_Murayev" title="Evgeny Murayev">Yevhen Murayev</a> reconstituted the party under its current name <i>Nashi</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>In a November 2018 <a href="/wiki/Sociological_group_%22RATING%22" title='Sociological group "RATING"'>"RATING"</a> opinion poll the party scored 4.9%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>On 10 January 2019 the party elected party  leader Murayev as their candidate in the <a href="/wiki/2019_Ukrainian_presidential_election" title="2019 Ukrainian presidential election">2019 Ukrainian presidential election</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> On 7 March 2019 Muraev pulled out of the election favor of <a href="/wiki/Oleksandr_Vilkul" title="Oleksandr Vilkul">Oleksandr Vilkul</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7208637Muraevbc_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-7208637Muraevbc-7">[7]</a></sup> He also announced that Vilkul's party <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Opposition_Bloc_-_Party_for_Development_and_Peace" title="Opposition Bloc - Party for Development and Peace">Opposition Bloc - Party for Development and Peace</a> and Nashi would soon merge.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7208637Muraevbc_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-7208637Muraevbc-7">[7]</a></sup> Indeed in the <a href="/wiki/2019_Ukrainian_parliamentary_election" title="2019 Ukrainian parliamentary election">2019 Ukrainian parliamentary election</a> the party joined a united party list with the political parties of Opposition Bloc — Party for Peace and Development, <a href="/wiki/Revival_(Ukraine)" title="Revival (Ukraine)">Revival</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trust_Deeds" title="Trust Deeds">Trust Deeds</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
