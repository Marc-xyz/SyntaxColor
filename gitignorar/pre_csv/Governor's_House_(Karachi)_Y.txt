	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Governor's House (Karachi)</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Austin_mini850mk1_governorhouse.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="768" data-file-width="1024" decoding="async" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Austin_mini850mk1_governorhouse.jpg/220px-Austin_mini850mk1_governorhouse.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Austin_mini850mk1_governorhouse.jpg/330px-Austin_mini850mk1_governorhouse.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Austin_mini850mk1_governorhouse.jpg/440px-Austin_mini850mk1_governorhouse.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Austin_mini850mk1_governorhouse.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Governor's House, Karachi in 2007. <p>The <b>Governor's House</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Urdu_language" title="Urdu language">Urdu</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ur"><span class="Nastaliq" dir="rtl" style="font-family: 'Jameel Noori Nastaleeq', 'Urdu Typesetting', 'Noto Nastaliq Urdu', 'Noto Nastaliq Urdu Draft', 'Hussaini Nastaleeq', 'AlQalam Taj Nastaleeq', IranNastaliq, 'Awami Nastaliq', 'Awami Nastaliq Beta3', 'Awami Nastaliq Beta2', 'Awami Nastaliq Beta1', 'Nafees Nastaleeq', 'Nafees Nastaleeq v1.01', 'Pak Nastaleeq', 'PDMS_Jauhar', 'Alvi Lahori Nastaleeq'; font-size: 110%; font-style: normal;" title="Nastaliq">گورنر ہاؤس</span></span>‎; previously known as the <b><a href="/wiki/Government_House" title="Government House">Government House</a></b>, <b>Governor-General's House</b> and <b>President's House</b>) in <a href="/wiki/Karachi" title="Karachi">Karachi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sindh" title="Sindh">Sindh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Official_residence" title="Official residence">official residence</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Sindh" title="Governor of Sindh">Governor of Sindh</a>. The current <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Sindh" title="Governor of Sindh">Governor of Sindh</a> is <a href="/wiki/Imran_Ismail" title="Imran Ismail">Imran Ismail</a>. It is located along the Aiwan-e-Sadar Road of Karachi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Built in 1939, it is a historical building and has been the residence of several prominent figures, including the Commissioners and pre-independence British governors of Sindh, followed by the Governor-Generals of Pakistan, the President of Pakistan and then by the Governors of present-day Sindh province.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Governor%27s_House_(Karachi)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Old_Government_House">Old Government House</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Governor%27s_House_(Karachi)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Old Government House">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Sindh Governor House is located on the original site of the now-demolished Government House, which was built in the year 1843 by <a href="/wiki/Charles_James_Napier" title="Charles James Napier">Sir Charles Napier</a> while Sindh was part of the <a href="/wiki/British_Raj" title="British Raj">British Raj</a>. The Government House had been constructed for Napier's personal use. When Napier left in 1847, the house was bought by the Government of British India and used as an official residence by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commissioners_of_Sindh" title="Commissioners of Sindh">Commissioners of Sindh</a> up until 1936.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-governorsindh_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-governorsindh-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="British_Raj">British Raj</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Governor%27s_House_(Karachi)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: British Raj">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The construction of the present Governor House building started in the year 1936 by renowned architect R.T. Russel and was completed in 1939, at a cost of 700,000 <a href="/wiki/Rupee" title="Rupee">rupees</a>. The new building was built to replace the Government House, which had been in a state of disrepair and was no longer suitable for habitation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-governorsindh_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-governorsindh-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Lancelot_Graham" title="Lancelot Graham">Sir Lancelot Graham</a>, the first Governor of Sindh, laid the foundation stone of the building and started using it as his residence in 1939.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-governorsindh_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-governorsindh-2">[2]</a></sup> After Graham, the succeeding governors of Sindh, <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Dow" title="Hugh Dow">Sir Hugh Dow</a> (1941–1946) and <a href="/wiki/Francis_Mudie" title="Francis Mudie">Sir Francis Mudie</a> (1946–1947), also lived in the building.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-governorsindh_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-governorsindh-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-independence">Post-independence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Governor%27s_House_(Karachi)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Post-independence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following Pakistan's <a href="/wiki/Partition_of_India" title="Partition of India">independence</a> in August 1947 and Karachi's designation as the country's <a href="/wiki/Federal_Capital_Territory_(Pakistan)" title="Federal Capital Territory (Pakistan)">capital</a>, the building became the residence of <a href="/wiki/Muhammad_Ali_Jinnah" title="Muhammad Ali Jinnah">Muhammad Ali Jinnah</a>, the founder of Pakistan and its first <a href="/wiki/Governor-General_of_Pakistan" title="Governor-General of Pakistan">Governor-General</a>. From that point onward, the building became known as the "Governor-General's House" and was used as an official residence by all Governor-Generals of Pakistan who succeeded Jinnah. Jinnah had continued to reside in the house until his death in September 1948.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-governorsindh_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-governorsindh-2">[2]</a></sup> In 1956, the post of Governor-General was abolished and replaced by the <a href="/wiki/President_of_Pakistan" title="President of Pakistan">President of Pakistan</a>, thus making the last governor-general of Pakistan, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iskandar_Mirza" title="Iskandar Mirza">Iskandar Mirza</a>, the country's first elected president. The building was thereafter referred to as the "President's House."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-governorsindh_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-governorsindh-2">[2]</a></sup> By the 1970s, Karachi was no longer the capital of Pakistan; the federal government was shifted to <a href="/wiki/Islamabad" title="Islamabad">Islamabad</a>. With the dissolution of the <a href="/wiki/One_Unit" title="One Unit">One Unit</a> scheme and revival of provinces, Karachi was designated the capital city of Sindh. The house eventually became the residence of the Governor of Sindh and continues to be so as of present.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-governorsindh_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-governorsindh-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
