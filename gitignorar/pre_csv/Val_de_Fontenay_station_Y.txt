<p>The <a href="/wiki/R%C3%A9seau_Express_R%C3%A9gional" title="Réseau Express Régional">RER</a> station <b>Val de Fontenay</b> is a station in the Paris suburb <a href="/wiki/Fontenay-sous-Bois" title="Fontenay-sous-Bois">Fontenay-sous-Bois</a>.  It is situated on the <a href="/wiki/Paris-Est%E2%80%93Mulhouse-Ville_railway" title="Paris-Est–Mulhouse-Ville railway">Paris-Est–Mulhouse-Ville railway</a> and provides an interchange between the RER lines A and E.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_station">The station</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Val_de_Fontenay_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: The station">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station is named for a neighborhood of Fontenay-sous-Bois. It is served by both line <a href="/wiki/RER_A" title="RER A">A</a> (branch A4) and line <a href="/wiki/RER_E" title="RER E">E</a> (branch E4).
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="RER_A">RER A</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Val_de_Fontenay_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: RER A">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On the A line, Val de Fontenay is the first station on the branch A4, leading to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marne-la-Vall%C3%A9e%E2%80%93Chessy_station" title="Marne-la-Vallée–Chessy station">Marne-la-Vallée–Chessy</a> and is also the busiest in terms of trains. Val de Fontenay (like <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Noisy-le-Grand-Mont_d%27Est_station" title="Noisy-le-Grand-Mont d'Est station">Noisy-le-Grand-Mont d'Est</a>) is served by every train running on this branch, unlike others which are skipped at certain times of day (<a href="/wiki/Bry-sur-Marne_station" title="Bry-sur-Marne station">Bry-sur-Marne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lognes_station" title="Lognes station">Lognes</a>). 
</p><p>The station is served in each direction by:
</p>
<ul><li>12 to 18 trains  on <i>peak travel times</i>.</li>
<li>9 trains per hour on <i>off-peak hours</i>.</li>
<li>6 trains per hour on <i>week-ends</i>.</li>
<li>4 trains per hour in the <i>late evening</i>.</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Additional_trains_in_off-peak_times">Additional trains in off-peak times</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Val_de_Fontenay_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Additional trains in off-peak times">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Starting 4 February 2008 RATP changed the number of trains running on the RER A. In off-peak hours, there are now 12 trains per hour instead of 6. Of those 12 trains going to Marne-la-Vallée, 6 are direct between Val de Fontenay and Noisy-le-Grand (the stations of Neuilly-Plaisance and Bry-sur-Marne are skipped). This allows a passenger to save 2 minutes between Val de Fontenay and Noisy-le-Grand, as the trip drops from 7 minutes to 5 minutes without the two stops.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="RER_E">RER E</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Val_de_Fontenay_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: RER E">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Since 30 August, 1999, Val de Fontenay is also served by the <a href="/wiki/RER_E" title="RER E">RER E</a>. This is the branch E4, going between <a href="/wiki/Haussmann%E2%80%93Saint-Lazare" title="Haussmann–Saint-Lazare">Haussmann–Saint-Lazare</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tournan_Station" title="Tournan Station">Tournan</a>. Train service on the E line consists of 6 trains per hour during the week in both directions. Towards Tournan, there are 4 trains per hour to <a href="/wiki/Villiers-sur-Marne_-_Le_Plessis-Tr%C3%A9vise_station" title="Villiers-sur-Marne - Le Plessis-Trévise station">Villiers-sur-Marne</a> and 2 trains per hour to Tournan. Towards Haussmann–Saint-Lazare, trains coming from Villiers-sur-Marne stop at all stations, while trains coming from Tournan stop only at Noisy-le-Sec, Pantin, Rosa Parks and Magenta. In the evening, there are 4 trains per hour, alternating between Villiers-sur-Marne and Tournan and stopping at all stations.
</p>
