	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Operation Kedem</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>

		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Operation Kedem</b> was an action planned and carried out in July 1948, during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1948_Arab%E2%80%93Israeli_war" title="1948 Arab–Israeli war">1948 Arab–Israeli war</a>. Its purpose was to capture <a href="/wiki/East_Jerusalem" title="East Jerusalem">East Jerusalem</a> (including the <a href="/wiki/Old_City_(Jerusalem)" title="Old City (Jerusalem)">Old City</a>). After the first cease-fire of the Arab–Israeli war, which lasted for four weeks, the balance in power in <a href="/wiki/Jerusalem" title="Jerusalem">Jerusalem</a> had shifted dramatically. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jew" title="Jew">Jews</a> were now much stronger than the <a href="/wiki/Arabs" title="Arabs">Arabs</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Jerusalem" title="Jerusalem">Jerusalem</a> sector.
</p><p>The operation was to be carried out by <a href="/wiki/Irgun" title="Irgun">Irgun</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lehi_(group)" title="Lehi (group)">Lehi</a> forces on July 8 with help from the Haganah. But it was delayed by <a href="/wiki/David_Shaltiel" title="David Shaltiel">David Shaltiel</a>, possibly because he did not trust their ability after their failure to capture <a href="/wiki/Deir_Yassin" title="Deir Yassin">Deir Yassin</a> without <a href="/wiki/Haganah" title="Haganah">Haganah</a>'s assistance.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> He told them to first capture the Arab village of <a href="/wiki/Malha" title="Malha">Malha</a>. On July 14, 1948, after a fierce battle in the early hours of the morning, the Irgun occupied the village. Several hours later, the Arabs launched a counterattack, but reinforcements arrived and the village was retaken. The Irgun lost 17 men in the battle for Malha, and many were wounded.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Irgun force commanded by <a href="/wiki/Yehuda_Lapidot" title="Yehuda Lapidot">Yehuda Lapidot</a> (Nimrod) was to break through at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_New_Gate" title="The New Gate">The New Gate</a>, Lehi to break through the wall stretching from the New Gate and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Jaffa_Gate" title="The Jaffa Gate">The Jaffa Gate</a>, and the Beit Horon Battalion to strike from <a href="/wiki/Mount_Zion" title="Mount Zion">Mount Zion</a>.
</p><p>The battle was planned to begin at the <a href="/wiki/Shabbat" title="Shabbat">Sabbath</a>, 20:00 Friday July 16, a day before the second cease-fire of the Arab–Israeli war. The plan went wrong from the beginning and was postponed first to 23:00 and then to midnight. Not until 02:30 did the battle actually begin. The Irgunists managed to break through at the New Gate, but the other forces failed in their missions. At 05:45, Shaltiel ordered a retreat and cessation of hostilities.
</p>
