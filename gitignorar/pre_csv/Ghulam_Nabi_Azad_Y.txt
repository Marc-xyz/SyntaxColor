<p><b>Ghulam Nabi Azad</b> (born 7 March 1949) is an Indian politician of the  <a href="/wiki/Indian_National_Congress" title="Indian National Congress">Indian National Congress</a> and was the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minister_of_Health_and_Family_Welfare_(India)" title="Minister of Health and Family Welfare (India)">Minister of Health and Family Welfare</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Presently, he serves as the Leader of opposition in <a href="/wiki/Rajya_Sabha" title="Rajya Sabha">Rajya Sabha</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>He has served as the Parliamentary Affairs Minister of India in the government of former Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Manmohan_Singh" title="Manmohan Singh">Manmohan Singh</a> until 27 October 2005, when he was appointed as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chief_Minister_of_Jammu_and_Kashmir" title="Chief Minister of Jammu and Kashmir">Chief Minister of Jammu and Kashmir</a>. He also led the party successfully in the <a href="/wiki/2002_Jammu_and_Kashmir_Legislative_Assembly_election" title="2002 Jammu and Kashmir Legislative Assembly election">2002 Assembly election in Jammu and Kashmir</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ghulam_Nabi_Azad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gulam Nabi Azad was born in a village called <b>Soti</b> of tehsil <b><a href="/wiki/Gondoh" title="Gondoh">Gandoh</a> (Bhalessa)</b> in <a href="/wiki/Jammu_and_Kashmir" title="Jammu and Kashmir">Jammu and Kashmir</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Doda_district" title="Doda district">Doda district</a>. His parents were Rahamatullah Bhatt and Basa Begum. He attended the local school in his village.Later for higher studies he moved to <a href="/wiki/Jammu" title="Jammu">Jammu</a> and received his bachelor's of Science degree from <a href="/wiki/Govt._Gandhi_Memorial_Science_College" title="Govt. Gandhi Memorial Science College">G.G.M. Science College</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Further more he also received a Master's in <a href="/wiki/Zoology" title="Zoology">Zoology</a> degree from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Kashmir" title="University of Kashmir">University of Kashmir</a> ,Srinagar in1972.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bio_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-bio-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ghulam_Nabi_Azad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Azad started his political career soon after working as the secretary for the Block Congress Committee in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bhalessa" title="Bhalessa">Bhalessa</a> in 1973. Two years later, he was nominated as the President of the Jammu and Kashmir Pradesh Youth Congress. In 1980, he was appointed as the President of the All-India Youth Congress.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bio_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-bio-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>After being elected to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seventh_Lok_Sabha" title="Seventh Lok Sabha">Seventh Lok Sabha</a> from Maharashtra's <a href="/wiki/Washim_(Lok_Sabha_constituency)" title="Washim (Lok Sabha constituency)">Washim (Lok Sabha constituency)</a> in 1980, Azad entered into the Central government as Deputy Minister in charge of Law, Justice and Company Affairs Ministry in 1982.
</p><p>Subsequently, he was elected to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eighth_Lok_Sabha" title="Eighth Lok Sabha">Eighth Lok Sabha</a> in 1984 and was a member (1990 - 1996) from Maharashtra in <a href="/wiki/Rajya_Sabha" title="Rajya Sabha">Rajya Sabha</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RS-list_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-RS-list-10">[10]</a></sup> During Rao's government, Azad took charge of Parliamentary Affairs and Civil Aviation ministries. He was subsequently elected to Rajya Sabha from Jammu and Kashmir during the term of 30/11/1996 to 29/11/2002 and  30/11/2002 to 29/11/2008, but resigned on 29/04/2006 as he became Chief Minister of Jammu and Kashmir on 2 November 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RS-list_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-RS-list-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chief_Minister_of_Jammu_and_Kashmir">Chief Minister of Jammu and Kashmir</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ghulam_Nabi_Azad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Chief Minister of Jammu and Kashmir">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In June 2008, Azad's government announced plans to transfer land to the board of a Hindu shrine. Many Muslims were angered by this decision and protested, leading the government to cancel the transfer; however, this reversal provoked Hindu protests. Seven people were reported killed in violence that accompanied these protests. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jammu_and_Kashmir_People%27s_Democratic_Party" title="Jammu and Kashmir People's Democratic Party">People's Democratic Party</a>, a coalition partner of the Indian National Congress in Jammu and Kashmir, withdrew its support for Azad's government, and rather than attempt to sustain his government by requesting a vote of confidence, Azad resigned on 7 July 2008, and later left office on 11 July 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Union_government">Union government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ghulam_Nabi_Azad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Union government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Shri_Ghulam_Nabi_Azad_assumes_charge_of_the_Union_Minister_for_Urban_Development_in_New_Delhi_on_May_25,_2004_(Tuesday.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="591" data-file-width="900" decoding="async" height="144" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/Shri_Ghulam_Nabi_Azad_assumes_charge_of_the_Union_Minister_for_Urban_Development_in_New_Delhi_on_May_25%2C_2004_%28Tuesday.jpg/220px-Shri_Ghulam_Nabi_Azad_assumes_charge_of_the_Union_Minister_for_Urban_Development_in_New_Delhi_on_May_25%2C_2004_%28Tuesday.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/Shri_Ghulam_Nabi_Azad_assumes_charge_of_the_Union_Minister_for_Urban_Development_in_New_Delhi_on_May_25%2C_2004_%28Tuesday.jpg/330px-Shri_Ghulam_Nabi_Azad_assumes_charge_of_the_Union_Minister_for_Urban_Development_in_New_Delhi_on_May_25%2C_2004_%28Tuesday.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/Shri_Ghulam_Nabi_Azad_assumes_charge_of_the_Union_Minister_for_Urban_Development_in_New_Delhi_on_May_25%2C_2004_%28Tuesday.jpg/440px-Shri_Ghulam_Nabi_Azad_assumes_charge_of_the_Union_Minister_for_Urban_Development_in_New_Delhi_on_May_25%2C_2004_%28Tuesday.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Shri_Ghulam_Nabi_Azad_assumes_charge_of_the_Union_Minister_for_Urban_Development_in_New_Delhi_on_May_25,_2004_(Tuesday.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Ghulam Nabi Azad assumes charge of the Union Minister for Urban Development in New Delhi on May 25, 2004</div></div></div>
<p>In the second United Progressive Alliance Government, led by Dr. <a href="/wiki/Manmohan_Singh" title="Manmohan Singh">Manmohan Singh</a>, Mr. Azad, was sworn in as the Health Minister of India. He was elected to Rajya Sabha for 4th term  and 3rd term from Jammu and Kashmir during the term of 30/11/1996 to 29/11/2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RS-list_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-RS-list-10">[10]</a></sup>  He vowed to expand the National Rural Health Mission, which has mobilized half a million health workers, all across <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, and later his ministry also launched a National Urban Health Mission, to serve the slum dwelling urban poor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p><p>He has suggested a late marriage age of between 25 and 30 for population control, and has said that lack of electricity &amp; thereby absence of TV entertainment in rural areas will cause people to produce more children.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Leader_of_opposition">Leader of opposition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ghulam_Nabi_Azad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Leader of opposition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In June 2014, after the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Democratic_Alliance_(India)" title="National Democratic Alliance (India)">National Democratic Alliance</a> won a majority in the <a href="/wiki/Lok_Sabha" title="Lok Sabha">Lok Sabha</a> and formed the Union government, Azad was appointed as the leader of opposition in the <a href="/wiki/Rajya_Sabha" title="Rajya Sabha">Rajya Sabha</a>, where Congress still held the majority.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2015, Azad got re-elected to the Rajya Sabha from Jammu and Kashmir, despite the PDP-BJP alliance holding a majority of seats in the Legislative Assembly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kidnapping_of_kin">Kidnapping of kin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ghulam_Nabi_Azad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Kidnapping of kin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Between June 1991 and January 1993 Azad was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minister_of_Parliamentary_Affairs_(India)" title="Minister of Parliamentary Affairs (India)">union parliamentary affair minister</a>. His brother-in-law Tassaduq Dev was kidnapped by Al Umar Mujahideen in January 1992. Three jailed Islamic terrorists of Al Umar were released in exchange for Dev on January 17.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ghulam_Nabi_Azad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Azad married <a href="/wiki/Shameem_Dev_Azad" title="Shameem Dev Azad">Shameem Dev Azad</a>, a well known Kashmiri singer, in 1980, and they have a son Saddam Nabi Azad and a daughter Sofiya Nabi Azad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup>
</p>
