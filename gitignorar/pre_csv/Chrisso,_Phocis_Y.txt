<p><b>Chrisso</b> (<a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>: <span lang="el">Χρισσό</span>) is a village in <a href="/wiki/Phocis" title="Phocis">Phocis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>. The modern village sits north of the ancient town of <a href="/wiki/Crissa" title="Crissa">Crissa</a>, a powerful city-state of <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greece" title="Ancient Greece">ancient Greece</a> which gave its name to the Crissaean plain and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crissaean_Gulf" title="Crissaean Gulf">Crissaean Gulf</a> and lies in the southwestern foothills of <a href="/wiki/Mount_Parnassus" title="Mount Parnassus">Mount Parnassus</a>. Chrisso is 2 km southwest of <a href="/wiki/Delphi" title="Delphi">Delphi</a>, 10 km southeast of <a href="/wiki/Amfissa" title="Amfissa">Amfissa</a> and 6 km northeast of <a href="/wiki/Itea,_Phocis" title="Itea, Phocis">Itea</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Greek_National_Road_48" title="Greek National Road 48">Greek National Road 48</a> (<a href="/wiki/Nafpaktos" title="Nafpaktos">Nafpaktos</a> - Delphi - <a href="/wiki/Livadeia" title="Livadeia">Livadeia</a>) passes west of the village. Chrisso belongs to the municipality of Delphi. 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chrisso,_Phocis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Crissa" title="Crissa">Crissa</a></div>
<p>The town is first mentioned in <a href="/wiki/Homer" title="Homer">Homer</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Catalog_of_Ships" title="Catalog of Ships">Catalog of Ships</a>, as a <a href="/wiki/Phocis" title="Phocis">Phoecean</a> settlement that participated in <a href="/wiki/Trojan_War" title="Trojan War">Trojan War</a> (ca. 12th-13th century B.C.).
The rubble from the ancient wall of Krissa lies in the modern Stefani hill.  The ancient city was devastated by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amphictiones" title="Amphictiones">Amphictiones</a> in the war which lasted from around 600 BC and 590 BC.  In the Crisaean fields, an ancient hippodrome is founded.
</p>
