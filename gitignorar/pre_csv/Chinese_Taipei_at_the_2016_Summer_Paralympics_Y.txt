<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Delegation">Delegation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chinese_Taipei_at_the_2016_Summer_Paralympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Delegation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Disability_classifications">Disability classifications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chinese_Taipei_at_the_2016_Summer_Paralympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Disability classifications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Disability_sport_classification" title="Disability sport classification">Disability sport classification</a></div>
<p>Every participant at the Paralympics has their disability grouped into one of five <a href="/wiki/Summer_Paralympic_Games#Disability_categories" title="Summer Paralympic Games">disability categories</a>; <a href="/wiki/Amputation" title="Amputation">amputation</a>, the condition may be congenital or sustained through injury or illness; <a href="/wiki/Cerebral_palsy" title="Cerebral palsy">cerebral palsy</a>; <a href="/wiki/Wheelchair" title="Wheelchair">wheelchair</a> athletes, there is often overlap between this and other categories; <a href="/wiki/Visual_impairment" title="Visual impairment">visual impairment</a>, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blindness" title="Blindness">blindness</a>; <a href="/wiki/Les_Autres_sport_classification" title="Les Autres sport classification">Les autres</a>, any physical disability that does not fall strictly under one of the other categories, for example <a href="/wiki/Dwarfism" title="Dwarfism">dwarfism</a> or <a href="/wiki/Multiple_sclerosis" title="Multiple sclerosis">multiple sclerosis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-class1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-class1-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-class2_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-class2-6">[6]</a></sup> Each <a href="/wiki/Paralympic_Games#Summer_sports" title="Paralympic Games">Paralympic sport</a> then has its own classifications, dependent upon the specific physical demands of competition. Events are given a code, made of numbers and letters, describing the type of event and classification of the athletes competing. Some sports, such as <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_2016_Summer_Paralympics" title="Athletics at the 2016 Summer Paralympics">athletics</a>, divide athletes by both the category and severity of their disabilities, other sports, for example <a href="/wiki/Swimming_at_the_2016_Summer_Paralympics" title="Swimming at the 2016 Summer Paralympics">swimming</a>, group competitors from different categories together, the only separation being based on the severity of the disability.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-class3_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-class3-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Five-time Paralympian and three-time Paralympic medalist, <a href="/wiki/Lin_Tzu-hui" title="Lin Tzu-hui">Lin Tzu-hui</a> seeks to defend her title in the <a href="/wiki/Powerlifting_at_the_2016_Summer_Paralympics_%E2%80%93_Qualification#79_Kg" title="Powerlifting at the 2016 Summer Paralympics â Qualification">women's 79 kg</a> weight class. Lin was entered into the competition by virtue of her world ranking as of April 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-powerlifting_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-powerlifting-13">[13]</a></sup>  
<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lin_Ya-hsuan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lin Ya-hsuan (page does not exist)">Lin Ya-hsuan</a> will also compete in the Paralympics for the fourth consecutive time. The best outcome out of three attempts counted as the final results. The athlete who placed first in each event was allowed a fourth attempt to break the <a href="/wiki/Paralympic_powerlifting" title="Paralympic powerlifting">Paralympic or world record</a>.
