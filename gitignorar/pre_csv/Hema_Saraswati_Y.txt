	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Hema Saraswati</h1>

<table class="infobox vcard" style="width:22em"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" style="text-align:center;font-size:125%;font-weight:bold"><div class="fn" style="display:inline;">Hema Saraswati</div></th></tr><tr><th scope="row" style="padding-top:0.225em;line-height:1.1em;padding-right:0.65em;">Native name</th><td style="line-height:1.4em;"><div class="nickname">হেম সৰস্বতী</div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p><b>Hema Saraswati</b> (<a href="/wiki/Assamese_language" title="Assamese language">Assamese</a>: <span lang="as">হেম সৰস্বতী</span>) (fl. late 14th century) was amongst the earliest known <a href="/wiki/Assamese_language" title="Assamese language">Assamese</a> writers, most known for his poem, <i>Prahlada Charita</i>, the earliest known poetic work in Assamese language.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He was court poet under the patronage of <a href="/wiki/Kamtapur" title="Kamtapur">Kamtapur</a>'s King  Durlabh Narayan of <a href="/wiki/Kamata_Kingdom" title="Kamata Kingdom">Kamata Kingdom</a>, who also provided patronage to his contemporary, <a href="/wiki/Harivara_Vipra" title="Harivara Vipra">Harivara Vipra</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He wrote <i>Prahlada Charita</i> based on a story found in <a href="/wiki/Vayu_Purana" title="Vayu Purana">Vayu Purana</a> and <i>Har-Gauri-Sambaad</i>, a version of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kumarasambhava" title="Kumarasambhava">Kumarasambhava</a> by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kalidasa" title="Kalidasa">Kalidasa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>His concurrent writers are <a href="/wiki/Rudra_Kandali" title="Rudra Kandali">Rudra Kandali</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kobiranta_Saraswati&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kobiranta Saraswati (page does not exist)">Kobiranta Saraswati</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Madhav_Kandali" title="Madhav Kandali">Madhav Kandali</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Horibor_Bipro" title="Horibor Bipro">Horibor Bipro</a> etc., who gave a strong initial base to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Assamese_Literature" title="Assamese Literature">Assamese Literature</a>.
</p>
