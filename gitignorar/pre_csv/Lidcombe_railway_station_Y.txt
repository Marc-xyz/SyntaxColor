It is served by <a href="/wiki/Sydney_Trains" title="Sydney Trains">Sydney Trains</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Shore,_Northern_%26_Western_Line" title="North Shore, Northern &amp; Western Line">T1 Western</a>, <a href="/wiki/Inner_West_%26_Leppington_Line" title="Inner West &amp; Leppington Line">T2 Inner West &amp; Leppington</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bankstown_Line" title="Bankstown Line">T3 Bankstown</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olympic_Park_Line" title="Olympic Park Line">T7 Olympic</a> line services.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lidcombe_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="mbox-small" role="presentation" style="background-color:#f9f9f9;border:1px solid #aaa;color:#000">

<td class="mbox-image"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:OOjs_UI_icon_map-ltr-progressive.svg"><img alt="OOjs UI icon map-ltr-progressive.svg" class="noviewer" data-file-height="20" data-file-width="20" decoding="async" height="30" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/OOjs_UI_icon_map-ltr-progressive.svg/30px-OOjs_UI_icon_map-ltr-progressive.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/OOjs_UI_icon_map-ltr-progressive.svg/45px-OOjs_UI_icon_map-ltr-progressive.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/OOjs_UI_icon_map-ltr-progressive.svg/60px-OOjs_UI_icon_map-ltr-progressive.svg.png 2x" width="30"/></a></td>
<td class="mbox-text plainlist" style="text-align:centerno"><a class="mw-kartographer-maplink no-icon" data-mw="interface" data-overlays='["_9cd77b7fa290238e6b5dd547caaa4173fce71ddf"]' data-style="osm-intl" data-zoom="17" href="/wiki/Special:Map/17///en">Major features of the station</a></td></tr>

<p>Lidcombe station opened on 1 November 1858 as Haslams Creek after local landowner, Samuel Haslam. When in 1867 land was set aside for a cemetery nearby, the residents renamed the locality Rookwood. The official name of the station was changed in 1878. By the turn of the century the Necropolis was also called Rookwood, so on 1 January 1914 the station name was renamed again to Lidcombe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NSWEnvironment&amp;Heritage_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-NSWEnvironment&amp;Heritage-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>On 11 November 1912, Lidcombe became a junction station, with the opening of a deviation of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Main_Southern_railway_line" title="Main Southern railway line">Main South line</a> to <a href="/wiki/Regents_Park_railway_station" title="Regents Park railway station">Regents Park</a>.
</p><p>On 23 April 1999, Platform 0 was opened as part of the <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Park_railway_line" title="Olympic Park railway line">Olympic Park line</a> project.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> As part of this work, the station was refurbished and a second footbridge added at the eastern end.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NSWEnvironment&amp;Heritage_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-NSWEnvironment&amp;Heritage-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2010, an extra dock platform for terminating <a href="/wiki/Bankstown_Line" title="Bankstown Line">City via Bankstown</a> trains was opened. This was built as part of the <a href="/wiki/Rail_Clearways_Program#Clearway_2:_Bankstown" title="Rail Clearways Program">CityRail Clearways Project</a> which was intended to untangle the various lines and make the network simpler to operate. In conjunction with a companion project at <a href="/wiki/Homebush_railway_station" title="Homebush railway station">Homebush</a>, operation of the Bankstown line was separated from the former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Airport,_Inner_West_%26_South_Line#Inner_West_Line" title="Airport, Inner West &amp; South Line">Inner West line</a> in October 2013. The changes resulted in the discontinuation of almost all trains to <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_railway_station,_Sydney" title="Liverpool railway station, Sydney">Liverpool</a> via <a href="/wiki/Regents_Park_railway_station" title="Regents Park railway station">Regents Park</a> prompting a community campaign to reinstate lost services.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="Platforms_.26_services"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Platforms_&amp;_services">Platforms &amp; services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lidcombe_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Platforms &amp; services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

During times of special events, some train services may make additional calls at Lidcombe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
