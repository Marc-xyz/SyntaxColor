	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Reinhard Baumeister</h1>

<p><b>Reinhard Baumeister</b> (19 March 1833 in <a href="/wiki/Hamburg" title="Hamburg">Hamburg</a> – 11 February 1917 in <a href="/wiki/Karlsruhe" title="Karlsruhe">Karlsruhe</a>) was a German engineer and <a href="/wiki/Urban_planner" title="Urban planner">urban planner</a>, the author of one of the earliest texts on <a href="/wiki/Urban_planning" title="Urban planning">urban planning</a> <i>Stadterweiterungen in technischer, baupolizeilicher und Wirtschaftlicher Beziehung </i> (Town extensions: their links with technical and economic concerns and with building regulations) published in 1876. It was used as a textbook at the first urban planning course in Germany, at the college of technology in <a href="/wiki/Aachen" title="Aachen">Aachen</a> in 1880.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ward_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ward-1">[1]</a></sup> An early translation of one of his writings into English was <i>The Cleaning and Sewerage of Cities</i> published in New York in 1891.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Reinhard_Baumeister&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Baumeister was a professor of civil engineering with experience in railway construction when he entered the field of planning by winning the 1872 competition for the urban extension of <a href="/wiki/Mannheim" title="Mannheim">Mannheim</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ward_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ward-1">[1]</a></sup> 
</p>
