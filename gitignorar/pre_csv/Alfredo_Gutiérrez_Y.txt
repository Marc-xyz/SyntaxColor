	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Alfredo Gutiérrez</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Alfredo de Jesús Gutiérrez Vital</b> (born April 17, 1940) is a Colombian <a href="/wiki/Accordion" title="Accordion">accordion</a> player and singer famous for winning  the "<a href="/wiki/Vallenato_Legend_Festival" title="Vallenato Legend Festival">Vallenato Legend Festival</a>" three times (since then he is known as the King of Kings).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>He was born in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Palo_Quemao&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Palo Quemao (page does not exist)">Palo Quemao</a>, Colombia, and began playing accordion when he was four. In 1960 he started his professional career as a musician, but it was in 1968 when he consolidated his career when he got a deal with Codiscos record label and produced an album that gave him national acclaim.
</p><p>He was part of the famous music group <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Los_Corraleros_de_Majagual&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Los Corraleros de Majagual (page does not exist)">Los Corraleros de Majagual</a> along with <a href="/wiki/Lisandro_Meza" title="Lisandro Meza">Lisandro Meza</a>, Chico Cervantes, Cesar Castro and Calixto Ochoa. Among his musical repertoire are found hits like "Los Indios", "Los Novios", "<i>Ojos Indios</i>", "<i>Ojos Verdes</i>", "<i>Anhelos</i>" "<i>Cabellos Cortos</i>", "<i>Cabellos Largos</i>", "<i>Capullito de Rosa</i>", "<i>Ay Elena</i>"  "<i>Festival en <a href="/wiki/Guarar%C3%A9" title="Guararé">Guararé</a></i>".
</p><p>In 2000, Along with  <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Francisco_%22Pacho%22_Rada&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title='Francisco "Pacho" Rada (page does not exist)'>Francisco "Pacho" Rada</a> and others artists he starred in the documentary <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=El_Acorde%C3%B3n_del_Diablo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="El Acordeón del Diablo (page does not exist)">El Acordeón del Diablo</a> from the German director <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Stefan_Schwietert&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Stefan Schwietert (page does not exist)">Stefan Schwietert</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
