<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gagik_Karapetyan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Karapetyan was born on December 2, 1957 in Leninakan (now <a href="/wiki/Gyumri" title="Gyumri">Gyumri</a>). Horoscope signs are archer-Cock. He has graduated from two Institutions in <a href="/wiki/Yerevan" title="Yerevan">Yerevan</a> and one in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, Specialization of teacher-coach; psychologist-physiotherapist and physiotherapist-manualist.
</p><p>He studied and graduated from <a href="/wiki/USSR_Academy_of_Medical_Sciences" title="USSR Academy of Medical Sciences">USSR Academy of Medical Sciences</a> at first, post graduate study and then doctor study. He is an academician and a full member of Medico Technical Academy of Science of Russian Federation.
</p><p>Together with his working team Gagik Karapetyan provides 2500-3000 medical sessions with 300-350 clients annually. He has spread his activity in <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stambul" title="Stambul">Stambul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dubai" title="Dubai">Dubai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> and has visited different countries on business trips.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Literary_career">Literary career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gagik_Karapetyan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Literary career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gagik Karapetyan is also acknowledged for his literary activities. He is the author of over a hundred scientific works, three thousand radio and TV shows, two novels including the poem “The Hell”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> which is devoted to the victims of 1988 December 7 earthquake. The poem became a part of the National Radio “Gold Fund” performed by People’s artist, the master of recitation <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Vladimir_Abajyan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Vladimir Abajyan (page does not exist)">Vladimir Abajyan</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gagik_Karapetyan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
