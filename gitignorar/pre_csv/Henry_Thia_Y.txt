<p><b>Henry Thia</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans">程旭辉</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Chéng Xùhuī</span></i>; <a href="/wiki/Wade%E2%80%93Giles" title="Wade–Giles">Wade–Giles</a>: <i><span lang="zh-Latn-wadegile">Ch'eng<sup>2</sup> Hsu<sup>4</sup> Hui<sup>1</sup></span></i>, born 25 February 1952) is a Singaporean former actor and comedian.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Henry_Thia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Thia was a member of the main cast of <i>Comedy Nite</i> throughout its run.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Although he had many roles in various sketches, his most memorable role is probably Lion King in the sketch series <i>Liang Ximei</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He made a transition to English television in 2004, where he played the recurring role of Georgie Gan in <i><a href="/wiki/Police_%26_Thief" title="Police &amp; Thief">Police &amp; Thief</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Thia is also known to the public for his strikingly similar looks to Singaporean politician <a href="/wiki/Khaw_Boon_Wan" title="Khaw Boon Wan">Khaw Boon Wan</a>, a fact he often acknowledges by jokingly referring to Khaw as his twin.
</p>
