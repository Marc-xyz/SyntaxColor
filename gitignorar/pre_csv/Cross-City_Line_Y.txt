<p>The <b>Cross-City Line</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Commuter_rail" title="Commuter rail">commuter rail</a> line in the <a href="/wiki/West_Midlands_(region)" title="West Midlands (region)">West Midlands</a> <a href="/wiki/Regions_of_England" title="Regions of England">region</a> of <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. It runs for 32 miles (51 km) from <a href="/wiki/Redditch" title="Redditch">Redditch</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bromsgrove" title="Bromsgrove">Bromsgrove</a> in <a href="/wiki/Worcestershire" title="Worcestershire">Worcestershire</a>, its two southern termini, to <a href="/wiki/Lichfield" title="Lichfield">Lichfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Staffordshire" title="Staffordshire">Staffordshire</a>, its northern terminus, via <a href="/wiki/Birmingham_New_Street_railway_station" title="Birmingham New Street railway station">Birmingham New Street</a>, connecting the suburbs of <a href="/wiki/Birmingham" title="Birmingham">Birmingham</a> in between. Services are operated by <a href="/wiki/West_Midlands_Trains" title="West Midlands Trains">West Midlands Trains</a>.
</p><p>Cross-City Line services began in 1978, as a project of the <a href="/wiki/West_Midlands_Passenger_Transport_Executive" title="West Midlands Passenger Transport Executive">West Midlands Passenger Transport Executive</a> (WMPTE) to improve local rail services. It made use of pre-existing railways lines, which previously did not have any through services. Services were extended to <a href="/wiki/Redditch_railway_station" title="Redditch railway station">Redditch</a> in 1980, and to <a href="/wiki/Lichfield_Trent_Valley_railway_station" title="Lichfield Trent Valley railway station">Lichfield Trent Valley</a> in 1988. The route was <a href="/wiki/Railway_electrification_in_Great_Britain" title="Railway electrification in Great Britain">electrified</a> in 1993. In 2018 services were extended to <a href="/wiki/Bromsgrove_railway_station" title="Bromsgrove railway station">Bromsgrove</a>, which was added as a second southern terminus.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cross-City_Line&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Constituent_railways">Constituent railways</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cross-City_Line&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Constituent railways">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>What is now the Cross-City Line was not built as a single route; it is a combination of lines opened by different companies at different times, between 1837 and 1885.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBoynton1993_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBoynton1993-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>On the northern half of the route (Birmingham-Lichfield):
</p>
<ul><li>The oldest section is between <a href="/wiki/Duddeston_railway_station" title="Duddeston railway station">Duddeston</a> (originally named Vauxhall) and <a href="/wiki/Aston_railway_station" title="Aston railway station">Aston</a>, which was part of the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Junction_Railway" title="Grand Junction Railway">Grand Junction Railway</a> from Birmingham to <a href="/wiki/Earlestown_railway_station" title="Earlestown railway station">Earlestown</a>, opened in 1837.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific page or range of pages in which the material appears. (August 2017)">page needed</span></a></i>]</sup> This was extended towards the centre of Birmingham, at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Birmingham_Curzon_Street_railway_station_(1838-1966)" title="Birmingham Curzon Street railway station (1838-1966)">Curzon Street</a>, the following year, and into Birmingham New Street in 1854.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jowett_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Jowett-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>The line from Aston to <a href="/wiki/Sutton_Coldfield_railway_station" title="Sutton Coldfield railway station">Sutton Coldfield</a> was opened by the <a href="/wiki/London_and_North_Western_Railway" title="London and North Western Railway">London and North Western Railway</a> in 1862,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jowett_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Jowett-3">[3]</a></sup> and extended to <a href="/wiki/Lichfield_City_railway_station" title="Lichfield City railway station">Lichfield City</a> in 1884, where it connected with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Staffordshire_Line" title="South Staffordshire Line">South Staffordshire Line</a> between <a href="/wiki/Walsall_railway_station" title="Walsall railway station">Walsall</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lichfield_Trent_Valley_railway_station" title="Lichfield Trent Valley railway station">Lichfield Trent Valley</a>, which had opened in 1849.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBoynton199365-67_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBoynton199365-67-4">[4]</a></sup></li></ul>
<p>On the southern half of the route (Birmingham-Redditch):
</p>
<ul><li>The <a href="/wiki/Birmingham_and_Gloucester_Railway" title="Birmingham and Gloucester Railway">Birmingham and Gloucester Railway</a> had opened in 1840, following what is now the Cross-City Line between <a href="/wiki/Bromsgrove_railway_station" title="Bromsgrove railway station">Bromsgrove</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kings_Norton_railway_station" title="Kings Norton railway station">Kings Norton</a>, although this line ran to Curzon Street (later New Street) via what is now the <a href="/wiki/Camp_Hill_Line" title="Camp Hill Line">Camp Hill Line</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jowett_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Jowett-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBoynton199314-17_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBoynton199314-17-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li>The single-track branch line from Barnt Green to <a href="/wiki/Redditch_railway_station" title="Redditch railway station">Redditch</a> followed in 1859, as part of the <a href="/wiki/Midland_Railway" title="Midland Railway">Midland Railway</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gloucester_Loop_Line" title="Gloucester Loop Line">Gloucester Loop Line</a>, which was closed south of Redditch in 1964.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBoynton199318-20_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBoynton199318-20-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Birmingham_West_Suburban_Railway" title="Birmingham West Suburban Railway">Birmingham West Suburban Railway</a>, taking the route of the Cross-City Line between <a href="/wiki/Kings_Norton_railway_station" title="Kings Norton railway station">Kings Norton</a> and central Birmingham via <a href="/wiki/Bournville_railway_station" title="Bournville railway station">Bournville</a>, opened to its original terminus at <a href="/wiki/Granville_Street_railway_station" title="Granville Street railway station">Granville Street</a> in 1876, it was extended into Birmingham New Street in 1885, and the Granville Street terminus closed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jowett_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-Jowett-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBoynton199321-25_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBoynton199321-25-7">[7]</a></sup></li></ul>
<p>These lines from Birmingham to Barnt Green and Redditch were operated by the <a href="/wiki/Midland_Railway" title="Midland Railway">Midland Railway</a> and the line to Lichfield was operated by the <a href="/wiki/London_and_North_Western_Railway" title="London and North Western Railway">London and North Western Railway</a>, so there were no through services. This continued despite the <a href="/wiki/Railways_Act_1921" title="Railways Act 1921">Grouping of the LNW and Midland Railways</a> to form the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/London_Midland_and_Scottish_Railway" title="London Midland and Scottish Railway">London Midland and Scottish Railway</a> in 1921, and subsequent <a href="/wiki/Transport_Act_1947" title="Transport Act 1947">nationalisation</a> to form <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Railways" title="British Railways">British Railways</a>.
</p><p>Prior to the creation of the Cross-City Line, the northern half of the route from New Street to Sutton Coldfield and Lichfield had a well used regular suburban service, which had been switched from steam to <a href="/wiki/Diesel_multiple_unit" title="Diesel multiple unit">diesel multiple unit</a> (DMU) operation in 1956, leading to a large increase in usage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBoynton1993_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBoynton1993-1">[1]</a></sup> However, the southern half of the route from New Street to Redditch was a different matter: In 1964, the closure of all the stations between New Street and <a href="/wiki/Redditch_railway_station" title="Redditch railway station">Redditch</a> (along with the branch to Redditch) was proposed by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beeching_Axe" title="Beeching Axe">Beeching Axe</a>. They were reprieved from closure in 1967, however the service was cut back to a handful of trains at peak times for commuters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBoynton199359_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBoynton199359-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Birmingham_Cross-City_Line.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="9000" data-file-width="5000" decoding="async" height="396" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/Birmingham_Cross-City_Line.png/220px-Birmingham_Cross-City_Line.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/Birmingham_Cross-City_Line.png/330px-Birmingham_Cross-City_Line.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/Birmingham_Cross-City_Line.png/440px-Birmingham_Cross-City_Line.png 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Birmingham_Cross-City_Line.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Diagrammatic map of Cross-City Line, including <a href="/wiki/Bromsgrove_railway_station" title="Bromsgrove railway station">Bromsgrove</a> which was added as a second southern terminus in 2018.</div></div></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1978:_consolidated_route">1978: consolidated route</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cross-City_Line&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1978: consolidated route">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the early-1970s the <a href="/wiki/West_Midlands_Passenger_Transport_Executive" title="West Midlands Passenger Transport Executive">West Midlands Passenger Transport Executive</a> (WMPTE) gained responsibility for managing and planning the local railway network in the West Midlands, and they set about looking for ways it could be improved: The WMPTE Passenger Transport Plan of 1972 recognised the need for a cross-city rail service, and especially an improved service to the south of Birmingham with new stations to serve the growth areas in the south of the city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBoynton199386-88_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBoynton199386-88-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Cross City Line project was sanctioned by the WMPTE in May 1975 and launched on 8 May <a href="/wiki/1978_in_rail_transport" title="1978 in rail transport">1978</a>. Costing £7.4 million (equivalent to £60,970,000 in 2018),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-inflation-UK_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-inflation-UK-10">[10]</a></sup> it involved joining the services into Birmingham from north and south into a single through service, along with the re-opening of <a href="/wiki/Five_Ways_railway_station" title="Five Ways railway station">Five Ways station</a> (the original had closed in the 1940s) and new stations to serve the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_(Birmingham)_railway_station" title="University (Birmingham) railway station">University of Birmingham</a> and <a href="/wiki/Longbridge_railway_station" title="Longbridge railway station">Longbridge</a> (the former station at Longbridge was on the branch line to Halesowen and Old Hill). Most of the other stations on the southern half of the route were rebuilt at the same time, and improvements were made to signalling and junctions. Of the new stations, the only one to be officially opened was University, which the then Secretary of State for Transport <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_Rodgers" title="William Rodgers">William Rodgers</a> MP formally opened on 8 May 1978. There is a plaque on platform 2 marking this occasion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Redditch_MRC_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Redditch_MRC-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBoynton199386-88_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBoynton199386-88-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>Services initially ran on a 15-minute frequency between Longbridge and Four Oaks via Birmingham New Street, with an hourly extension to Lichfield City, using refurbished <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_116" title="British Rail Class 116">Class 116</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diesel_Multiple_Units" title="Diesel Multiple Units">Diesel Multiple Units</a>. The new service was an instant success, and by the end of the first year was carrying 30,000 passengers daily.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBoynton199389-90_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBoynton199389-90-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Redditch_MRC_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-Redditch_MRC-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1980s_developments">1980s developments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cross-City_Line&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1980s developments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Services were extended to <a href="/wiki/Redditch_railway_station" title="Redditch railway station">Redditch</a> in 1980, initially on an hourly frequency, increased to half-hourly in 1989. The service to Lichfield City was increased to half hourly in 1986, and on 28 November 1988, some services were extended to terminate at the re-opened high level platforms of <a href="/wiki/Lichfield_Trent_Valley_railway_station" title="Lichfield Trent Valley railway station">Lichfield Trent Valley</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBoynton199389-90_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBoynton199389-90-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Redditch_MRC_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-Redditch_MRC-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-southstaffsrail_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-southstaffsrail-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1990s:_Electrification">1990s: Electrification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cross-City_Line&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1990s: Electrification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>By the late-1980s, the elderly diesels operating the service were becoming increasingly unreliable, and the WMPTE (now re-branded as Centro) pressed for <a href="/wiki/Railway_electrification_in_Great_Britain" title="Railway electrification in Great Britain">electrification</a>. The decision to electrify the line was made on 7 February 1990 by the then Transport Minister <a href="/wiki/Cecil_Parkinson" title="Cecil Parkinson">Cecil Parkinson</a> during the campaign for <a href="/wiki/1990_Mid_Staffordshire_by-election" title="1990 Mid Staffordshire by-election">a by-election</a> in the Mid Staffordshire constituency.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBoynton199392-104_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBoynton199392-104-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Redditch_MRC_11-3"><a href="#cite_note-Redditch_MRC-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>Work started on electrifying the route with the <a href="/wiki/25_kV_AC_railway_electrification" title="25 kV AC railway electrification">25 kV AC</a> <a href="/wiki/Overhead_line" title="Overhead line">overhead line</a> system in May 1990, and it was completed on 6 June 1993. Redditch, Alvechurch, and Blake Street stations were rebuilt at this time, and several other stations including Barnt Green were extensively modified to accommodate the new longer electric trains. The signalling was also modernised at the same time as the electrification, as part of a parallel scheme. A new fleet of <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_323" title="British Rail Class 323">Class 323</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electric_Multiple_Units" title="Electric Multiple Units">Electric Multiple Units</a> were introduced to work the electrified line, and replace the elderly diesels. Full service with the Class 323s did not begin until 1994, due to initial reliability problems with the new units. Some elderly <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_304" title="British Rail Class 304">Class 304</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_308" title="British Rail Class 308">Class 308</a> and <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_310" title="British Rail Class 310">Class 310</a> EMUs were drafted in to operate services in the interim, along with some of the original diesel units. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBoynton199392-104_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBoynton199392-104-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Redditch_MRC_11-4"><a href="#cite_note-Redditch_MRC-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>The cost of the electrification scheme was estimated at £64.5 million (equivalent to £129,050,000 in 2018),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-inflation-UK_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-inflation-UK-10">[10]</a></sup> of which around 70% was funded by Centro, and the remainder by the <a href="/wiki/Regional_Railways" title="Regional Railways">Regional Railways</a> sector of <a href="/wiki/British_Rail" title="British Rail">British Rail</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBoynton199392-104_14-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBoynton199392-104-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="21st_century_developments">21st century developments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cross-City_Line&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 21st century developments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Day_30_-_West_Midlands_Police_-_View_from_Above_1_(8428681775).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2848" data-file-width="4288" decoding="async" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/72/Day_30_-_West_Midlands_Police_-_View_from_Above_1_%288428681775%29.jpg/220px-Day_30_-_West_Midlands_Police_-_View_from_Above_1_%288428681775%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/72/Day_30_-_West_Midlands_Police_-_View_from_Above_1_%288428681775%29.jpg/330px-Day_30_-_West_Midlands_Police_-_View_from_Above_1_%288428681775%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/72/Day_30_-_West_Midlands_Police_-_View_from_Above_1_%288428681775%29.jpg/440px-Day_30_-_West_Midlands_Police_-_View_from_Above_1_%288428681775%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Day_30_-_West_Midlands_Police_-_View_from_Above_1_(8428681775).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Aerial view of the new railway bridge (nearest to camera) and Aerial Aqueduct, over the diverted A38, taken in January 2013</div></div></div>
<p>At <a href="/wiki/Selly_Oak" title="Selly Oak">Selly Oak</a>, a new bridge was constructed in 2011 to carry the canal over a new section of the A38.
</p><p>The single track between Barnt Green and Redditch restricted the number of trains that could run to Redditch to two per hour. In November 2013 a scheme was approved to construct a new <a href="/wiki/Passing_loop" title="Passing loop">passing loop</a> at <a href="/wiki/Alvechurch_railway_station" title="Alvechurch railway station">Alvechurch</a> to allow the service to be increased to three trains per hour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Investing_Midlands_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Investing_Midlands-16">[16]</a></sup> The line between Barnt Green and Redditch was closed for eight weeks for the works to be carried out, and was reopened on 1 September 2014. The improved service began in December 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>
</p><p>Electrification was also extended from Barnt Green to <a href="/wiki/Bromsgrove_railway_station" title="Bromsgrove railway station">Bromsgrove station</a>, which was rebuilt in 2016 and was added as a second southern terminus once electrification was completed in August 2018.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> These changes allow three trains per hour to run to both Redditch and Bromsgrove.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Investing_Midlands_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-Investing_Midlands-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RE2017-1_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-RE2017-1-21">[21]</a></sup>
</p><p>Three of the ten new <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_350" title="British Rail Class 350">Class 350</a> trains that London Midland introduced in 2014 have displaced Class 323s on other routes in the West Midlands to enable an increase in service frequency and capacity between Longbridge and Redditch, and the extension of all remaining Longbridge trains to Bromsgrove once electrification is complete. Class 350s are not currently authorised to be used on the Cross City Line.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Current_services">Current services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cross-City_Line&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Current services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During Monday to Saturday daytimes, on the core section of the line between <a href="/wiki/Four_Oaks_railway_station" title="Four Oaks railway station">Four Oaks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Longbridge_railway_station" title="Longbridge railway station">Longbridge</a>, six trains per-hour operate each way, giving a ten-minute frequency of service. Of these, four northbound trains per hour continue from Four Oaks to <a href="/wiki/Lichfield_City_railway_station" title="Lichfield City railway station">Lichfield City</a>, and two of these continue to <a href="/wiki/Lichfield_Trent_Valley_railway_station" title="Lichfield Trent Valley railway station">Lichfield Trent Valley</a>. Southbound three trains per hour continue from Longbridge to <a href="/wiki/Redditch_railway_station" title="Redditch railway station">Redditch</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup>
</p><p>In detail the service consists of:
</p>
<ul><li>2tph from Lichfield Trent Valley to either Bromsgrove (1tph) or Redditch (1tph) – This service also calls at <a href="/wiki/Shenstone_railway_station" title="Shenstone railway station">Shenstone</a> which other services pass through</li>
<li>2tph from Lichfield City to Bromsgrove (1tph) or  Redditch (1tph)</li>
<li>2tph from Four Oaks to Bromsgrove (1tph) or Redditch (1tph) – This service also calls at <a href="/wiki/Duddeston_railway_station" title="Duddeston railway station">Duddeston</a> which other services pass through</li></ul>
<p>The southern half of the Cross-City line also forms part of the <a href="/wiki/Cross_Country_Route" title="Cross Country Route">Cross Country Route</a>, and some longer distance services stop at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_(Birmingham)_railway_station" title="University (Birmingham) railway station">University station</a>, including <a href="/wiki/CrossCountry" title="CrossCountry">CrossCountry</a> trains to <a href="/wiki/Cardiff_Central_railway_station" title="Cardiff Central railway station">Cardiff</a> and West Midland Railway services to <a href="/wiki/Hereford_railway_station" title="Hereford railway station">Hereford</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Future">Future</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cross-City_Line&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Future">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are long-standing proposals for the re-introduction of local trains on the <a href="/wiki/Camp_Hill_Line" title="Camp Hill Line">Camp Hill Line</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> (effectively a loop between Birmingham New Street and King's Norton).
</p><p>As part of the new <a href="/wiki/West_Midlands_Trains" title="West Midlands Trains">West Midlands Trains</a> franchise, the Class 323 units will be replaced by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aventra" title="Aventra">Aventras</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2018, as part of a 30-year strategy of Transport in the West Midlands several proposals were put forward. By 2034, there would be longer trains, electrification of the line from <a href="/wiki/Lichfield_Trent_Valley_railway_station" title="Lichfield Trent Valley railway station">Lichfield Trent Valley</a> to <a href="/wiki/Burton-on-Trent_railway_station" title="Burton-on-Trent railway station">Burton-on-Trent</a> allowing 2 services per hour to be extended to <a href="/wiki/Burton-on-Trent_railway_station" title="Burton-on-Trent railway station">Burton-on-Trent</a> via a newly reopened <a href="/wiki/Alrewas_railway_station" title="Alrewas railway station">Alrewas</a>. Beyond 2034, it was proposed that new semi-fast service could serve more larger stations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup>
</p><p>There is also a single tracked mothballed line to the former <a href="/wiki/Angelsea_Sidings" title="Angelsea Sidings">Angelsea Sidings</a> until 2001. The line remains in situ but rusty beyond repair. There is a possibility that the line which connected to Walsall from Lichfield via <a href="/wiki/Brownhills" title="Brownhills">Brownhills</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pelsall" title="Pelsall">Pelsall</a> could reopen as <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Birmingham_Mayor&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Birmingham Mayor (page does not exist)">Birmingham Mayor</a> Andy Street promised to look and make the reopening of the line feasible. <a href="/wiki/West_Midlands_Combined_Authority" title="West Midlands Combined Authority">West Midlands Combined Authority</a> also released a plan for the line as part of a 10-year transport plan called the 2026 Delivery for Transport.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Passenger_volume">Passenger volume</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cross-City_Line&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Passenger volume">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2016-17 the Cross-City Line's 24 stations (excluding New Street) had combined passenger numbers of 22.59 million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> a substantial increase on the 2015 figure of 19.95 million and the 2006 figure of 8.5 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> The busiest station on the route besides Birmingham New Street is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_(Birmingham)_railway_station" title="University (Birmingham) railway station">University</a>, with nearly three million passenger entries and exits, and the least busy station is <a href="/wiki/Alvechurch_railway_station" title="Alvechurch railway station">Alvechurch</a> with around 145,000 passenger entries and exits.
</p>

<li><a href="/wiki/Redditch_railway_station" title="Redditch railway station">Redditch railway station</a> in <a href="/wiki/Redditch" title="Redditch">Redditch</a></li></ul>
<p>A large stretch of the northern part of the line closely follows the <a href="/wiki/A5127_road" title="A5127 road">A5127 road</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media">Media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cross-City_Line&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>In 1990, Railscene produced a driver's eye view of the then-diesel line, featuring the elderly rolling stock still in operation. Many features of the line have since been changed, for example, the rebuilding of Alvechurch and Redditch stations, the abolition of Lichfield City's goods sidings and closure of the Brownhills Line and the removal of the fourth platform of Lichfield Trent Valley.</li>
<li>There was a highly publicised opening ceremony to celebrate the <a href="/wiki/Railway_electrification_in_Great_Britain" title="Railway electrification in Great Britain">electrification</a> and service enhancement at Redditch in 1993.</li>
<li>In 1995, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Video_125&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Video 125 (page does not exist)">Video 125</a> released a video of a driver's eye view of the recently electrified line, narrated by Kay Alexander. On the video near Lichfield an elderly <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_310" title="British Rail Class 310">Class 310</a> unit is used on the opposite direction service – this was due to not all 323 units being in traffic in time for the new services starting.</li>
<li>There is a reproduction of the Cross City Line available on <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Train_Simulator" title="Microsoft Train Simulator">Microsoft Train Simulator</a> (MSTS)</li>
<li>There is a highly detailed reproduction of the part between Redditch and Birmingham New Street for the free train simulators <a href="/wiki/BVE_Trainsim" title="BVE Trainsim">BVE</a> and OpenBVE.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></li></ul>
