<p><b>Rudi Vogt</b> (born 30 January 1983) is a former South African <a href="/wiki/Rugby_union" title="Rugby union">rugby union</a> footballer, who played as a <a href="/wiki/Rugby_union_positions#15._full_back" title="Rugby union positions">full-back</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rugby_union_positions#10._fly_half" title="Rugby union positions">fly-half</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rugby_union_positions#14._wing" title="Rugby union positions">wing</a>. He played for the  <a href="/wiki/Pumas_(rugby_team)" title="Pumas (rugby team)">Pumas</a> in the domestic <a href="/wiki/Currie_Cup" title="Currie Cup">Currie Cup</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vodacom_Cup" title="Vodacom Cup">Vodacom Cup</a> competitions between 2007 and 2009, for French <a href="/wiki/Rugby_Pro_D2" title="Rugby Pro D2">Rugby Pro D2</a> side <a href="/wiki/Oyonnax_Rugby" title="Oyonnax Rugby">Oyonnax</a> in <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Rugby_Pro_D2_season" title="2009–10 Rugby Pro D2 season">2009–10</a> and for  <a href="/wiki/Griquas_(rugby)" title="Griquas (rugby)">Griquas</a> from 2010 to 2012. He also made five <a href="/wiki/Super_Rugby" title="Super Rugby">Super Rugby</a> appearances for the  <a href="/wiki/Lions_(Super_Rugby)" title="Lions (Super Rugby)">Lions</a> during the <a href="/wiki/2008_Super_14_season" title="2008 Super 14 season">2008 Super 14 season</a> and played for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emerging_Springboks" title="Emerging Springboks">Emerging Springboks</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2008_IRB_Nations_Cup" title="2008 IRB Nations Cup">2008 IRB Nations Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SARU_Player_Profile_Rudi_Vogt_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-SARU_Player_Profile_Rudi_Vogt-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-It's_Rugby_Player_Profile_Rudi_Vogt_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-It's_Rugby_Player_Profile_Rudi_Vogt-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Although still contracted to  <a href="/wiki/Griquas_(rugby)" title="Griquas (rugby)">Griquas</a> in 2013, he missed the <a href="/wiki/2012_Currie_Cup_Premier_Division" title="2012 Currie Cup Premier Division">2012 Currie Cup Premier Division</a> and the entire 2013 seasons with a hip injury,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Heelagter-spens_leeg_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Heelagter-spens_leeg-3">[3]</a></sup> which culminated in his retirement after the <a href="/wiki/2013_Currie_Cup_Premier_Division" title="2013 Currie Cup Premier Division">2013 Currie Cup season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JP_en_nóg_11_klaar_met_GW_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-JP_en_nóg_11_klaar_met_GW-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
