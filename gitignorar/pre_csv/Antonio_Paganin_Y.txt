<p><b>Antonio Paganin</b> (born 18 June 1966, in <a href="/wiki/Vicenza" title="Vicenza">Vicenza</a>) is a retired <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> player, who played as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)" title="Defender (association football)">defender</a>. He is the brother of <a href="/wiki/Massimo_Paganin" title="Massimo Paganin">Massimo Paganin</a>, who was also a football player.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Antonio_Paganin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Paganin began his career with <a href="/wiki/Bologna_F.C._1909" title="Bologna F.C. 1909">Bologna</a> (1982–1984) in the lower divisions, later moving to <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> club <a href="/wiki/U.C._Sampdoria" title="U.C. Sampdoria">Sampdoria</a> (1984–88), and winning two <a href="/wiki/Coppa_Italia" title="Coppa Italia">Coppa Italia</a> trophies during his time with the club. He moved to <a href="/wiki/Udinese_Calcio" title="Udinese Calcio">Udinese</a> for two seasons (1988–90), helping the team to earn <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> promotion, before moving to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F.C._Internazionale_Milano" title="F.C. Internazionale Milano">Internazionale</a> (1990–95), where he won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UEFA_Cup" title="UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup</a> twice, in 1991 and in 1994. He later played with <a href="/wiki/Atalanta_B.C." title="Atalanta B.C.">Atalanta</a> (1995–96), and <a href="/wiki/Hellas_Verona_F.C." title="Hellas Verona F.C.">Verona</a>  (1996-97), where he concluded his <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> career, before moving to Torri Quartesolo  (1997–99), and later ending his career with <a href="/wiki/U.C._Montecchio_Maggiore" title="U.C. Montecchio Maggiore">Montecchio Maggiore</a> (1999-2003), in the lower divisions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
