<p><b>Paddy Cosgrave</b> is an Irish entrepreneur. He is a co-founder of <a href="/wiki/Web_Summit" title="Web Summit">Web Summit</a>, an annual technology conference, and CEO of the company that runs Web Summit and other events. In 2015 he was listed 18th in the <i><a href="/wiki/Wired_UK" title="Wired UK">Wired UK</a></i> list of the most influential people in Europe in the field of technology.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Paddy_Cosgrave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Patrick "Paddy" Cosgrave grew up on a farm in <a href="/wiki/County_Wicklow" title="County Wicklow">County Wicklow</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Connolly_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Connolly-1">[1]</a></sup>   He was educated at <a href="/wiki/Glenstal_Abbey_School" title="Glenstal Abbey School">Glenstal Abbey School</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trinity_College_Dublin" title="Trinity College Dublin">Trinity College Dublin</a>, where he studied Business, Economics and Social Studies (BESS).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Duggan_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Duggan-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Madeiros_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Madeiros-3">[3]</a></sup> While at Trinity he was president of the <a href="/wiki/University_Philosophical_Society" title="University Philosophical Society">University Philosophical Society</a> (The Phil) and editor of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Piranha" title="The Piranha">Piranha</a></i>, a satirical college magazine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Crowley_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Crowley-4">[4]</a></sup> During his presidency of The Phil, the society introduced <a href="/wiki/University_Philosophical_Society#Phil_Speaks" title="University Philosophical Society">Phil Speaks</a>, an outreach initiative aimed at promoting debating and public speaking in Irish secondary schools. Cosgrave graduated with a BA from Trinity College, Dublin's BESS program in 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Keenan&amp;O'Hare_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Keenan&amp;O'Hare-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WorldEF_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-WorldEF-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Paddy_Cosgrave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cosgrave was one of the organizers and the executive director of Rock the Vote Ireland, a campaign launched in April 2007 to encourage young people to vote in the May 2007 <a href="/wiki/2007_Irish_general_election" title="2007 Irish general election">Irish general election</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-celebrities_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-celebrities-7">[7]</a></sup> He was also a co-founder of MiCandidate, a website that "provided detailed information on every candidate running in the 2007 general election".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Crowley_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Crowley-4">[4]</a></sup> MiCandidate expanded its scope to become a syndicator of news and information about European politicians to media organisations. The company was sold for "an undisclosed sum" in October 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-buyout_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-buyout-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p>Cosgrave is a co-founder of the Web Summit and F.ounders conferences, and the CEO of Ci, the company that runs the two conferences and other events. F.ounders is an invitation-only event first held in Dublin in October 2009 as a result of his not getting preferential treatment for this by the Irish government. He has engaged in attacks against Ireland and its tax system, an attack which is a joke since Amaranthine, an investment fund set up by Mr Cosgrave with other Web Summit founders, is based in California, but registered for tax purposes in Delaware, an effective tax shelter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The first Dublin Web Summit in November 2010 was attended by approximately 400 people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Madeiros_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Madeiros-3">[3]</a></sup> The fourth annual Web Summit in November 2014 had an attendance of over 22,000, and 30,000 were expected to attend the event in Dublin in November 2015. Starting in 2016, the Web Summit was held in Lisbon, although Ci continued to be headquartered in Dublin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Temperton_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Temperton-11">[11]</a></sup> In 2014 Ci began introducing new events in the United States (Collision) and Hong Kong (RISE), with an event called SURGE planned for February 2016 in India.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nextchapter_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-nextchapter-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p><p>Cosgrave was awarded the 2015 Irish Exporters Association annual gold medal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IEA_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-IEA-13">[13]</a></sup> He was listed 18th in the 2015 <i><a href="/wiki/Wired_UK" title="Wired UK">Wired UK</a></i> list of the "100 most influential individuals in the wider <i>Wired</i> world".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p>
