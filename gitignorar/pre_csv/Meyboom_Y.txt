	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Meyboom</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>

<p>The <b>Meyboom</b> plantation (or <b>Meiboom</b> in Dutch, "May tree" – a bastardisation of the Dutch <i>tree of joy</i>) is the oldest tradition in <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a> (<a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>), attested since 1308. It is held every year on 9 August, the eve of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Lawrence" title="Saint Lawrence">St Lawrence's</a> Day, and consists mainly in planting a <a href="/wiki/Beech" title="Beech">beech</a> at the intersection of <span lang="fr" style="font-style:normal;" title="French language text">Rue des Sables</span>/<span lang="nl" style="font-style:normal;" title="Dutch language text">Zandstraat</span> and <span lang="fr" style="font-style:normal;" title="French language text">Rue du Marais</span>/<span lang="nl" style="font-style:normal;" title="Dutch language text">Broekstraat</span>.
</p><p>The celebration commemorates a victory of Brussels over the city of <a href="/wiki/Leuven" title="Leuven">Leuven</a> in 1213. To respect the tradition, the tree of joy must imperatively be planted before 5 p.m. It is accompanied by processions and various folk activities during the day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Since 2008, the <i>Meyboom</i> has been recognised as an expression of <a href="/wiki/Intangible_cultural_heritage" title="Intangible cultural heritage">intangible cultural heritage</a> by <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a>, as part of the binational listing of '<a href="/wiki/Processional_giants_and_dragons_in_Belgium_and_France" title="Processional giants and dragons in Belgium and France">Processional giants and dragons in Belgium and France</a>'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Celebrations">Celebrations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meyboom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Celebrations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The tree is designated by the city's Plantation Department and "chosen" by the <i>Companions of St Lawrence</i> in the <a href="/wiki/Sonian_Forest" title="Sonian Forest">Sonian Forest</a>, in the outskirts of Brussels. According to tradition, it must weigh at least 600 kg (1,300 lb), measure 12–13 m (39–43 ft) in height and be leafy. It is cut and then transported by <i>Bûûmdroegers</i> ("tree bearers"), through the municipalities of <a href="/wiki/Schaerbeek" title="Schaerbeek">Schaerbeek</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saint-Josse-ten-Noode" title="Saint-Josse-ten-Noode">Saint-Josse-ten-Noode</a> and the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Brussels" title="City of Brussels">City of Brussels</a>. At 1 p.m., a tribute is paid to the deceased <i>Companions</i>.
</p><p>The procession then forms at the corner of <span lang="fr" style="font-style:normal;" title="French language text">Rue du Marais</span>/<span lang="nl" style="font-style:normal;" title="Dutch language text">Broekstraat</span> and <span lang="fr" style="font-style:normal;" title="French language text">Rue des Comédiens</span>/<span lang="nl" style="font-style:normal;" title="Dutch language text">Komediantenstraat</span> (near the local brotherhood of the <i>Companions</i>, at 37, <span lang="fr" style="font-style:normal;" title="French language text">rue des Sables</span>/<span lang="nl" style="font-style:normal;" title="Dutch language text">Zandstraat</span>), and sets off along a fixed route: <span lang="fr" style="font-style:normal;" title="French language text">Rue du Fossé aux Loups</span>/<span lang="nl" style="font-style:normal;" title="Dutch language text">Wolvengracht</span>, <span lang="fr" style="font-style:normal;" title="French language text">Place de la Monnaie</span>/<span lang="nl" style="font-style:normal;" title="Dutch language text">Muntplein</span>, <span lang="fr" style="font-style:normal;" title="French language text">Rue des Fripiers</span>/<span lang="nl" style="font-style:normal;" title="Dutch language text">Kleerkopersstraat</span>, <span lang="fr" style="font-style:normal;" title="French language text">Rue de Tabora</span>/<span lang="nl" style="font-style:normal;" title="Dutch language text">Taborastraat</span>, <span lang="fr" style="font-style:normal;" title="French language text">Rue du Midi</span>/<span lang="nl" style="font-style:normal;" title="Dutch language text">Zuidstraat</span>, <span lang="fr" style="font-style:normal;" title="French language text">Rue du Lombard</span>/<span lang="nl" style="font-style:normal;" title="Dutch language text">Lombardstraat</span>, <span lang="fr" style="font-style:normal;" title="French language text">Rue de l'Étuve</span>/<span lang="nl" style="font-style:normal;" title="Dutch language text">Stoofstraat</span>, <span lang="fr" style="font-style:normal;" title="French language text">Rue de l'Amigo</span>/<span lang="nl" style="font-style:normal;" title="Dutch language text">Vruntstraat</span> and the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Place" title="Grand Place">Grand Place</a>. 
</p><p>At 2.45 p.m., the procession returns from the Grand Place via <span lang="fr" style="font-style:normal;" title="French language text">Rue Chair et Pain</span>/<span lang="nl" style="font-style:normal;" title="Dutch language text">Vlees-en-Broodstraat</span>, <span lang="fr" style="font-style:normal;" title="French language text">Rue du Marché aux Herbes</span>/<span lang="nl" style="font-style:normal;" title="Dutch language text">Grasmarkt</span>, <span lang="fr" style="font-style:normal;" title="French language text">Rue de la Fourche</span>/<span lang="nl" style="font-style:normal;" title="Dutch language text">Greepstraat</span>, <span lang="fr" style="font-style:normal;" title="French language text">Rue de l’Écuyer</span>/<span lang="nl" style="font-style:normal;" title="Dutch language text">Schildknaapsstraat</span>, <span lang="fr" style="font-style:normal;" title="French language text">Rue Montagne aux Herbes Potagères</span>/<span lang="nl" style="font-style:normal;" title="Dutch language text">Warmoesberg</span>, and finally returns to the corner of <span lang="fr" style="font-style:normal;" title="French language text">Rue des Comédiens</span>/<span lang="nl" style="font-style:normal;" title="Dutch language text">Komediantenstraat</span> and <span lang="fr" style="font-style:normal;" title="French language text">Rue du Marais</span>/<span lang="nl" style="font-style:normal;" title="Dutch language text">Broekstraat</span> around 4.30 p.m. Along the way, small branches of the tree are distributed, supposed to bring good fortune for the year to come. The culmination is the plantation itself, which must take place before 5 p.m. The tree is in fact not really planted but stuck in the ground and removed the next day.
</p>
