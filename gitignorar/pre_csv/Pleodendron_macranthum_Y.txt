<p><i><b>Pleodendron macranthum</b></i> (<b>chupacallos</b>) is a rare species of <a href="/wiki/Tree" title="Tree">tree</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Canellaceae" title="Canellaceae">Canellaceae</a> family. It is <a href="/wiki/Endemism" title="Endemism">endemic</a> to <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, where there are only three tiny populations remaining. Two individual plants are located in <a href="/wiki/El_Yunque_National_Forest" title="El Yunque National Forest">El Yunque</a> and 8 to 10 plants remain in <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Abajo_State_Forest" title="Río Abajo State Forest">Río Abajo State Forest</a>. This tree is a federally listed <a href="/wiki/Endangered_species" title="Endangered species">endangered species</a> of the United States.
</p><p>This <a href="/wiki/Evergreen" title="Evergreen">evergreen</a> tree grows up to 10 meters tall and 20 centimeters in diameter. The wood is hard and white in color. The leathery leaves are up to 12.5 centimeters long by 5 wide. The blades are dark green, shiny, and smooth on the edges. Flowers occur singly in the leaf axils. The flower is 2 centimeters wide and has 12 petals. The fruit ripens purple-black and is about 2 centimeters wide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rec_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-rec-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>This tree is rare and limited in distribution because of <a href="/wiki/Deforestation" title="Deforestation">deforestation</a> and habitat modification in the forests of Puerto Rico.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rec_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-rec-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
