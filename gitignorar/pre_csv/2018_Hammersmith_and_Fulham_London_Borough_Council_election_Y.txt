<p>Labour consolidated control of the council by gaining 9 seats (in addition to the 11 they gained in 2014), winning 35 in all. The Conservatives lost 9 seats, winning 11 in all.  This was the lowest number of Conservative councillors since 1986.
</p><p>There were no wards with split party representation - for only the fourth time in the fifteen elections since the council was created in 1968.  The previous occasions when this occurred was 1990, 1978 and the 1971 Labour landslide.
</p><p>Another sign of the increased homogeneity was the number of wards where the winning party got less than 50% of the vote - just three wards this time. <a href="/wiki/2018_Hammersmith_and_Fulham_London_Borough_Council_election#Ravenscourt_Park" title="2018 Hammersmith and Fulham London Borough Council election">Ravenscourt Park</a> with 46%, <a href="/wiki/2018_Hammersmith_and_Fulham_London_Borough_Council_election#Town" title="2018 Hammersmith and Fulham London Borough Council election">Town</a> with 48% and <a href="/wiki/2018_Hammersmith_and_Fulham_London_Borough_Council_election#Sands_End" title="2018 Hammersmith and Fulham London Borough Council election">Sands End</a> with a fraction under 50%.  This compares with the <a href="/wiki/2014_Hammersmith_and_Fulham_London_Borough_Council_election" title="2014 Hammersmith and Fulham London Borough Council election">2014 election</a> when there were 6 wards including <a href="/wiki/2014_Hammersmith_and_Fulham_London_Borough_Council_election#Sands_End" title="2014 Hammersmith and Fulham London Borough Council election">Sands End</a> with 38%. In the <a href="/wiki/2010_Hammersmith_and_Fulham_London_Borough_Council_election" title="2010 Hammersmith and Fulham London Borough Council election">2010 election</a> there were 10 wards including <a href="/wiki/2010_Hammersmith_and_Fulham_London_Borough_Council_election#Addison" title="2010 Hammersmith and Fulham London Borough Council election">Addison</a> with 39%. And in the <a href="/wiki/2006_Hammersmith_and_Fulham_London_Borough_Council_election" title="2006 Hammersmith and Fulham London Borough Council election">2006 election</a> there were also 10 wards including <a href="/wiki/2006_Hammersmith_and_Fulham_London_Borough_Council_election#Askew" title="2006 Hammersmith and Fulham London Borough Council election">Askew</a> with 39%.
</p><p>26 Councillors successfully retained their seats - 20 Labour and 6 Conservative.
5 sitting Councillors were defeated - all Conservative, including the previous opposition leader.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>The ward with the highest percentage vote for one party was <a href="/wiki/2018_Hammersmith_and_Fulham_London_Borough_Council_election#Wormholt_and_White_City" title="2018 Hammersmith and Fulham London Borough Council election">Wormholt and White City</a> which saw a 76.5% vote share for the Labour Party.  This is the highest percentage any party has achieved at the five elections since these wards were established in 2002.  Vote share percentages by ward for previous elections are not currently available.
</p><p>The largest percentage change was in the <a href="/wiki/2018_Hammersmith_and_Fulham_London_Borough_Council_election#Shepherds_Bush_Green" title="2018 Hammersmith and Fulham London Borough Council election">Shepherds Bush Green</a> ward, where the Labour Party increased its vote share by 20.9 percentage points - as the Green Party didn't stand a candidate this time round.  The largest percentage decrease was in the <a href="/wiki/2018_Hammersmith_and_Fulham_London_Borough_Council_election#Fulham_Reach" title="2018 Hammersmith and Fulham London Borough Council election">Fulham Reach</a> ward where the Conservative vote dropped by 14.6 percentage points.
</p><p>The candidate with the largest individual tally was Lisa Homan with 2,577 votes in the Askew ward.  This veteran councillor has been elected on all but one occasion since 1994 - losing out in 2002 by 44 votes.
</p><p>One of the successful Conservative candidates in Palace Riverside had been deselected from the same ward in 2006, and had run as an independent candidate - coming within 146 votes of winning at <a href="/wiki/2006_Hammersmith_and_Fulham_London_Borough_Council_election#Palace_Riverside" title="2006 Hammersmith and Fulham London Borough Council election">that election</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Liberal Democrats fielded a full slate of 46 candidates - in 2014 they put forward 39 candidates for election.
</p><p>UKIP fielded 3 candidates - in 2014 they had 7 candidates.
</p><p>The Green Party fielded 2 candidates - in 2014 they had 7 candidates.
</p><p>There was 1 Independent candidate - the same number as in 2014, although a different individual.  The 2018 independent had previously run in the same ward in the <a href="/wiki/2006_Hammersmith_and_Fulham_London_Borough_Council_election#Wormholt_&amp;_White_City" title="2006 Hammersmith and Fulham London Borough Council election">2006 local election</a> and in the Hammersmith constituency at the <a href="/wiki/Hammersmith_(UK_Parliament_constituency)#Elections_in_the_2010s" title="Hammersmith (UK Parliament constituency)">2017 general election</a>.
</p><p>TUSC did not stand any candidates in the 2018 local elections - they had two candidates in Hammersmith and Fulham in 2014.
</p><p>National Health Action Party did not field candidates in the 2018 local elections - they had a single candidate in Hammersmith and Fulham in 2014.
</p>
