<p><b>Emily Marie Nemmers</b> (born July 9, 1981) is an American music artist, who performs and records under the name <b>Emily West</b>. Signed to <a href="/wiki/Capitol_Records" title="Capitol Records">Capitol Records</a> Nashville, she debuted on <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard's</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> charts in early 2008 with the single "Rocks in Your Shoes". This song peaked at No. 39 early in the year, and is the first single from a self-titled EP. She entered the country charts a second time with "Blue Sky", a duet with <a href="/wiki/Keith_Urban" title="Keith Urban">Keith Urban</a>, in 2010.
</p><p>In 2012, West guest-starred in <i><a href="/wiki/Body_of_Proof" title="Body of Proof">Body of Proof</a></i>.
In 2014, she competed on <a href="/wiki/America%27s_Got_Talent_(season_9)" title="America's Got Talent (season 9)">the 9th season</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/America%27s_Got_Talent" title="America's Got Talent">America's Got Talent</a></i>, where she finished as the runner up to magician <a href="/wiki/Mat_Franco" title="Mat Franco">Mat Franco</a>. Since her success on the show, West has toured extensively throughout the United States, first with her <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/One-person_show" title="One-person show">one-woman show</a> and then with America's Got Talent Live: The All-Stars Tour!
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emily_West&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>West, the youngest of four children, was born in <a href="/wiki/Waterloo,_Iowa" title="Waterloo, Iowa">Waterloo, Iowa</a>. In July 1994 her parents took her to <a class="external text" href="http://memorybrothers.com/Bird-On-Fire%201.html" rel="nofollow">Bird-On-Fire Recording Studio</a> in West Union, Iowa where studio owner Doug Koempel worked with her producing four demos: "Sweet Dreams", "Piece Of My Heart", "Higher Love" and "Me And Bobby McGee."  She moved to Nashville, Tennessee in 2000 following her graduation from <a href="/wiki/Waterloo_West_High_School" title="Waterloo West High School">Waterloo West High School</a> in hopes of achieving her goals of becoming a country music singer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> West soon signed a deal with a publishing company in Nashville, Warner-Chapell. West was then signed to Capitol Records Nashville when producer Mike Dungan listened to her demo tapes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> One of her first appearances was as a background vocalist on <a href="/wiki/Chely_Wright" title="Chely Wright">Chely Wright</a>'s 2005 album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Metropolitan_Hotel" title="The Metropolitan Hotel">The Metropolitan Hotel</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emily_West&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2007-2012:_Music_and_television">2007-2012: Music and television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emily_West&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2007-2012: Music and television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Capitol released a self-titled EP December 11, 2007 to the <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> music store. In early 2008, West was featured in <i>People</i> magazine and completed a media tour for her debut single, "Rocks in Your Shoes".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> "Rocks in Your Shoes" peaked in the Top 40 on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> chart in early 2008. A second single, "That Kind of Happy" was released in April 2009 and failed to enter the chart.
</p><p>West appeared on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Are_You_Smarter_Than_a_5th_Grader%3F" title="Are You Smarter Than a 5th Grader?">Are You Smarter Than a 5th Grader?</a></i> on September 25, 2009, winning $25,000. In January 2010, West released a duet with labelmate <a href="/wiki/Keith_Urban" title="Keith Urban">Keith Urban</a> titled "Blue Sky." The song debuted at No. 53 on the Hot Country Songs chart for the week of February 23, 2010. Roughstock gave the song 4½ stars out of 5, stating that <span style="padding-right:.15em;">"</span>'Blue Sky' is already a contender for one of 2010's best singles."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> It spent thirteen weeks on the charts and peaked at No. 38.
</p><p>Emily appeared in the April 18, 2010 episode of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Apprentice_(U.S._season_9)" title="The Apprentice (U.S. season 9)">Celebrity Apprentice</a></i>, as the "music make-over" target for the women's team led by <a href="/wiki/Cyndi_Lauper" title="Cyndi Lauper">Cyndi Lauper</a>. Lauper won the challenge and as a result West donated 100% of the first month of <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> sales of her song "Blue Sky" to the Stonewall Community Foundation, Lauper's charity.
</p><p>She appeared in the January 10, 2012 "Shades of Blue" episode on <i><a href="/wiki/Body_of_Proof" title="Body of Proof">Body of Proof</a></i> on ABC. She played a drug addict trying to make it in the music business. She sings her single "Head On" at the end of the episode.
</p>
<h3><span id="2014-present:_America.27s_Got_Talent"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2014-present:_America's_Got_Talent">2014-present: <i>America's Got Talent</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emily_West&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2014-present: America's Got Talent">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2014, West competed on  <a href="/wiki/America%27s_Got_Talent_(season_9)" title="America's Got Talent (season 9)">the 9th season</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/America%27s_Got_Talent" title="America's Got Talent">America's Got Talent</a></i>, advancing to the finals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> She finished in second place.
</p><p>Shortly after the show ended, West signed a record deal with <a href="/wiki/Sony_Masterworks" title="Sony Masterworks">Sony Masterworks</a>, and is set to release a two-track single on December 2, 2014;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and an album in 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>On April 6, 2015, the first single, called "Bitter", off her upcoming album "All For You" was released, and the album was released in August 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>
