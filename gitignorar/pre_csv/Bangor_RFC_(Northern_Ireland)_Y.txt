<p><b>Bangor RFC</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Irish</a> <a href="/wiki/Rugby_union" title="Rugby union">rugby union</a> club from <a href="/wiki/Bangor,_County_Down" title="Bangor, County Down">Bangor, County Down</a>, playing in Division 2C of the <a href="/wiki/All-Ireland_League_(rugby_union)" title="All-Ireland League (rugby union)">All-Ireland League</a>. Joshua Greer owns the Rugby Club as well as the towns football club. Joshua is really an Icon of the Bangor area and sources his money from his fathers car dealership. 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bangor_RFC_(Northern_Ireland)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The club was founded in <a href="/wiki/1885_in_Ireland" title="1885 in Ireland">1885</a> and enjoyed senior status between 1924 and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>. The club returned to junior rugby after the war. Historically having played at <a href="/wiki/Ward_Park" title="Ward Park">Ward Park</a>, leased from Bangor Borough Council, in 1968 the club purchased eleven acres of land on the southern outskirts of Bangor and opened a new ground named Upritchard Park (after chairman Joe Upritchard) in September 1969, coinciding with the regaining of senior status. Over the next 20 years, Bangor became one of the top senior clubs in Ulster, culminating in the 1981-82 season, when it won the treble of <a href="/wiki/Ulster_Senior_League_(rugby_union)" title="Ulster Senior League (rugby union)">Ulster Senior League</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ulster_Senior_Cup_(rugby_union)" title="Ulster Senior Cup (rugby union)">Ulster Senior Cup</a> and its own floodlit competition, the Boston Cup. Bangor were inaugural members of the <a href="/wiki/All-Ireland_League_(rugby_union)" title="All-Ireland League (rugby union)">All-Ireland League</a> in 1990, but the club went into decline and was relegated back into junior rugby in 2002. In 2016, however, the club regained senior status on promotion back to the All-Ireland League. Since 1972, the club has been joined with <a href="/wiki/Bangor_Cricket_Club" title="Bangor Cricket Club">Bangor Cricket Club</a> as Bangor Rugby Football and Cricket Club. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bangorrfc1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-bangorrfc1-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
