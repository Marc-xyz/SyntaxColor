	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Adhemar da Silva Rocha</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>

		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>

		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Adhemar da Silva de Oliveira Rocha</b> (2 July 1908, <a href="/wiki/Valen%C3%A7a,_Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Valença, Rio de Janeiro">Valença, Rio de Janeiro</a> – 14 November 1975, <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a>) was a Brazilian <a href="/wiki/Chess" title="Chess">chess</a> player.
</p><p>He tied for 6-10th at Buenos Aires 1934/35 (the 5th <a href="/wiki/South_American_Chess_Championship" title="South American Chess Championship">South American Chess Championship</a>, <i>Torneo Sudamericano</i>, <a href="/wiki/Luis_Piazzini" title="Luis Piazzini">Luis Piazzini</a> won),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> took 4th in the <a href="/wiki/Montevideo_1938_chess_tournament" title="Montevideo 1938 chess tournament">Montevideo 1938 chess tournament</a> (the 8th <i>Torneio Sulamericano</i>, <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Alekhine" title="Alexander Alekhine">Alexander Alekhine</a> won),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Chess_Championship" title="Brazilian Chess Championship">Brazilian Champion</a> title for 1941.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Da Silva Rocha represented <a href="/wiki/Brazil_at_the_1936_Summer_Olympics" title="Brazil at the 1936 Summer Olympics">Brazil</a> in the <a href="/wiki/3rd_unofficial_Chess_Olympiad" title="3rd unofficial Chess Olympiad">3rd unofficial Chess Olympiad</a> at Munich 1936 and in the <a href="/wiki/8th_Chess_Olympiad" title="8th Chess Olympiad">8th Chess Olympiad</a> at Buenos Aires 1939.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Adhemar da Silva Rocha won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro_State" title="Rio de Janeiro State">Rio de Janeiro State</a> chess tournament in the years 1934, 1935 and 1937. He died on 14 November 1975 of <a href="/wiki/Cardiac_arrest" title="Cardiac arrest">cardiac arrest</a>, while walking around downtown Rio de Janeiro.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-epopeia_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-epopeia-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
