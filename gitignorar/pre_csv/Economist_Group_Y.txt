<p><b>The Economist Newspaper Limited</b>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doing_business_as" title="Doing business as">trading as</a> <b>The Economist Group</b>, is a British <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporation" title="Multinational corporation">multinational</a> media company headquartered in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> and best known as publisher of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Economist" title="The Economist">The Economist</a></i>. The Economist Group specialises in <a href="/wiki/International_business" title="International business">international business</a> and <a href="/wiki/International_relations" title="International relations">world affairs</a> information. Its principal activities are magazines, newspapers, conferences and market intelligence.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ownership">Ownership</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Economist_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Ownership">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pearson_PLC" title="Pearson PLC">Pearson PLC</a> held a 50% shareholding via <a href="/wiki/Financial_Times" title="Financial Times">The Financial Times Limited</a> until August 2015; at that time Pearson sold their share in the Economist. The <a href="/wiki/Agnelli_family" title="Agnelli family">Agnelli family</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Exor_(company)" title="Exor (company)">Exor</a> paid Â£287m to raise their stake from 4.7% to 43.4%, while the Economist paid Â£182m for the balance of 5.04m shares which will be distributed to current shareholders.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto-2">[2]</a></sup> Aside from the Agnelli family, smaller shareholders in the company include <a href="/wiki/Cadbury_family" title="Cadbury family">Cadbury</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rothschild_family" title="Rothschild family">Rothschild</a> (21%), <a href="/wiki/Schroders" title="Schroders">Schroder</a>, Layton and other family interests as well as a number of staff and former staff shareholders.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-auto-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Economist_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The origins of The Economist Group date back to the foundation of <i>The Economist: A Political, Commercial, Agricultural, &amp; Free-Trade Journal</i> by James Wilson in 1843.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ourhist_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-ourhist-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1946, the <a href="/wiki/Economist_Intelligence_Unit" title="Economist Intelligence Unit">Economist Intelligence Unit</a> began providing business intelligence to both <i>The Economist</i> magazine and external clients.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ourhist_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-ourhist-4">[4]</a></sup> In the same year, the Economist bookshop was established as a 50:50 joint venture with the London School of Economics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ourhist_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-ourhist-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Economist Conferences was established as a division of Economist Intelligence Unit in 1956 to offer government <a href="/wiki/Round_Table" title="Round Table">roundtables</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ourhist_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-ourhist-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1995, The Economist Group acquired the <i>Journal of Commerce</i>, a US-based provider of information for the shipping and <a href="/wiki/Transport" title="Transport">transportation industries</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ourhist_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-ourhist-4">[4]</a></sup> In the same year the Group launched <i>European Voice</i>, the first pan-European Union weekly newspaper.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ourhist_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-ourhist-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2004, The Economist Group launched an upmarket lifestyle magazine called <i>Intelligent Life</i>, an annual publication. This magazine was redesigned as a quarterly in September 2007, and became a bi-monthly publication in August 2011.  In March 2016, the magazine was renamed <a href="/wiki/1843_(magazine)" title="1843 (magazine)">1843</a>.
</p><p>Launched in 2010, the Ideas People Channel is a vertical online advertising network of around 50 sites defined by the mindset of the audience. The sites recruited for the network were identified by the readers of <i>The Economist</i> as their favourite online destinations for topics on business, globalisation, innovation and culture. The channel competes in the lower-cost, high-volume network advertising market, a category not previously served by The Economist online.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Also recently launched is Economist Education, providing e-learning courses.
</p><p>In March 2012, The Economist Group acquired the London-based marketing communications agency TVC Group for an undisclosed sum.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>In April 2012, the Economist Intelligence Unit expanded in Asia with the acquisition of Clearstate, a market intelligence firm specialising in customised strategic advisory and primary research in the healthcare and life sciences domains.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ourhist_4-6"><a href="#cite_note-ourhist-4">[4]</a></sup> 
</p><p>In July 2015, Canback &amp; Company, a strategy consulting firm operating in more than 70 countries was acquired. It is now known as <a href="/wiki/EIU_Canback" title="EIU Canback">EIU Canback</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">Operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Economist_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Operations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Economist Group is headquartered in Canary Wharf, London, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, and has offices worldwide, including in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brussels,_Belgium" title="Brussels, Belgium">Brussels, Belgium</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frankfurt,_Germany" title="Frankfurt, Germany">Frankfurt, Germany</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geneva,_Switzerland" title="Geneva, Switzerland">Geneva, Switzerland</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paris,_France" title="Paris, France">Paris, France</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dubai,_United_Arab_Emirates" title="Dubai, United Arab Emirates">Dubai, United Arab Emirates</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Johannesburg,_South_Africa" title="Johannesburg, South Africa">Johannesburg, South Africa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, mainland <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tokyo,_Japan" title="Tokyo, Japan">Tokyo, Japan</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> and <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>.
</p><p>The Economist Group's principal activities are magazines, newspapers, conferences and market intelligence. Publications and services delivered under The Economist brand include <i><a href="/wiki/The_Economist" title="The Economist">The Economist</a></i> magazine, <i>The Economist</i> online, <a href="/wiki/Economist_Intelligence_Unit" title="Economist Intelligence Unit">Economist Intelligence Unit</a>, Economist Conferences, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Economist_Corporate_Network" title="Economist Corporate Network">Economist Corporate Network</a>, The World In series and a bi-monthly lifestyle magazine, <a href="/wiki/1843_(magazine)" title="1843 (magazine)"><i>1843</i></a>. The Groupâs other brands include CQ Roll Call (aimed at decision-makers on Capitol Hill), <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=EuroFinance&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="EuroFinance (page does not exist)">EuroFinance</a>, a cash and treasury management event business, and a digital media agency, TVC. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_Voice" title="European Voice">European Voice</a> (Brussels), formerly a sister publication of CQ Roll Call, was sold to the French company Selectcom in 2013.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Economist_Corporate_Network">Economist Corporate Network</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Economist_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economist Corporate Network">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Economist Corporate Network is a members-only service within The Economist Group providing global and regional <a href="/wiki/Business_intelligence" title="Business intelligence">business intelligence</a> briefings, presentations, and advice to its subscribers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The Corporate Network relies on the "information, insight, and interaction" provided by regional experts in its parent organization.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> 81% of its customers are business executives at the director or senior management levels. It has offices in <a href="/wiki/Dubai" title="Dubai">Dubai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Johannesburg" title="Johannesburg">Johannesburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_affairs">Corporate affairs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Economist_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Corporate affairs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Economist_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The current members of the <a href="/wiki/Board_of_directors" title="Board of directors">board of directors</a> of The Economist Group are: Rupert Pennant-Rea (Chairman), <a href="/wiki/Zanny_Minton_Beddoes" title="Zanny Minton Beddoes">Zanny Minton Beddoes</a> (editor-in-chief of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Economist_magazine" title="Economist magazine">the Economist magazine</a>), Lady <a href="/wiki/Suzanne_Heywood" title="Suzanne Heywood">Suzanne Heywood</a>, Brent Hoberman,  <a href="/wiki/David_Bell_(publisher)" title="David Bell (publisher)">Sir David Bell</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Elkann" title="John Elkann">John Elkann</a>, Alex Karp, Sir Simon Robertson, Lady <a href="/wiki/Lynn_Forester_de_Rothschild" title="Lynn Forester de Rothschild">Lynn Forester de Rothschild</a>, Chris Stibbs and Baroness Jowell.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Economist_Group_directors_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Economist_Group_directors-12">[12]</a></sup>
Former board members include: Andrew Rashbass, Rona Fairhead, Philip Mengel<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Economist_Group_directors_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-Economist_Group_directors-12">[12]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Eric_Schmidt" title="Eric Schmidt">Eric Schmidt</a> (of Alphabet, parent company of Google).  The current trustees of the Group are: <a href="/wiki/Virginia_Bottomley" title="Virginia Bottomley">Baroness Bottomley of Nettlestone</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gus_O%27Donnell,_Baron_O%27Donnell" title="Gus O'Donnell, Baron O'Donnell">Lord O'Donnell</a>, Tim Clark and Bryan Sanderson.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p>
