<p><b>Withypool</b> (formerly <b>Widepolle</b>, <b>Widipol</b>, <b>Withypoole</b>) is a small village in <a href="/wiki/Somerset" title="Somerset">Somerset</a>, England, near the centre of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Exmoor_National_Park" title="Exmoor National Park">Exmoor National Park</a> and close to the border with <a href="/wiki/Devon" title="Devon">Devon</a>.  The word <b><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/withy" title="wiktionary:withy">Withy</a></b> means "willow". The <a href="/wiki/Civil_parish" title="Civil parish">civil parish</a>, known as <b>Withypool and Hawkridge</b>, covers 3,097 hectares (7,653 acres), includes the village of <b>Hawkridge</b> and has a population around 201.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-popn_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-popn-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Withypool is in the <a href="/wiki/Barle_Valley" title="Barle Valley">Barle Valley</a> on the <a href="/wiki/River_Barle" title="River Barle">River Barle</a>. The village lies on the route of the <a href="/wiki/Two_Moors_Way" title="Two Moors Way">Two Moors Way</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Celtic_Way" title="Celtic Way">Celtic Way</a> Exmoor Option.
</p><p>To the southwest of the village lie Withypool Common and Withypool Hill.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Withypool&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area around Withypool has been inhabited since the <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Age" title="Bronze Age">Bronze Age</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Withypool_Stone_Circle" title="Withypool Stone Circle">Withypool Stone Circle</a> can still be seen on top of Withypool Hill. The Brightworthy <a href="/wiki/Tumulus" title="Tumulus">barrows</a> lie on the Common; of three original, two survive.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Withypool is mentioned in the <a href="/wiki/Domesday_Book" title="Domesday Book">Domesday Book</a> as being tended by three foresters: Dodo, Almer and Godric.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The parishes of Hawkridge and Withypool were part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Williton_and_Freemanners_(hundred)" title="Williton and Freemanners (hundred)">Williton and Freemanners</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hundred_(county_subdivision)" title="Hundred (county subdivision)">Hundred</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>In the 14th century, <a href="/wiki/Geoffrey_Chaucer" title="Geoffrey Chaucer">Geoffrey Chaucer</a> was in charge of the village in his duties as forester of <a href="/wiki/North_Petherton" title="North Petherton">North Petherton</a>.
</p><p>The red <a href="/wiki/Sandstone" title="Sandstone">sandstone</a> <a href="/wiki/Withypool_Bridge" title="Withypool Bridge">Withypool Bridge</a> (or New Bridge) carries a small road over the <a href="/wiki/River_Barle" title="River Barle">River Barle</a>. It was built in the 19th century and is a Grade II* <a href="/wiki/Listed_building" title="Listed building">listed building</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Withypool&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parish_councils_of_England" title="Parish councils of England">parish council</a> has responsibility for local issues, including setting an annual precept (local rate) to cover the council’s operating costs and producing annual accounts for public scrutiny. The parish council evaluates local planning applications and works with the local police, district council officers, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neighbourhood_Watch_(UK)" title="Neighbourhood Watch (UK)">neighbourhood watch</a> groups on matters of crime, security, and traffic. The parish council's role also includes initiating projects for the maintenance and repair of parish facilities, as well as consulting with the district council on the maintenance, repair, and improvement of highways, drainage, footpaths, public transport, and street cleaning. Conservation matters (including trees and listed buildings) and environmental issues are also the responsibility of the council.
</p><p>The village falls within the <a href="/wiki/Non-metropolitan_district" title="Non-metropolitan district">non-metropolitan district</a> of <a href="/wiki/Somerset_West_and_Taunton" title="Somerset West and Taunton">Somerset West and Taunton</a>, which was established on 1 April 2019. It was previously in the district of <a href="/wiki/West_Somerset" title="West Somerset">West Somerset</a>, which was formed on 1 April 1974 under the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a>, and part of <a href="/wiki/Dulverton_Rural_District" title="Dulverton Rural District">Dulverton Rural District</a> before that.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dulvertonrd_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-dulvertonrd-9">[9]</a></sup> The district council is responsible for <a href="/wiki/Planning_permission" title="Planning permission">local planning</a> and <a href="/wiki/Building_regulations_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Building regulations in the United Kingdom">building control</a>, local roads, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Council_housing" title="Council housing">council housing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Environmental_health" title="Environmental health">environmental health</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Market_(place)" title="Market (place)">markets</a> and fairs, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Refuse_collection" title="Refuse collection">refuse collection</a> and <a href="/wiki/Recycling" title="Recycling">recycling</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cemeteries" title="Cemeteries">cemeteries</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crematoria" title="Crematoria">crematoria</a>, leisure services, parks, and <a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">tourism</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Somerset_County_Council" title="Somerset County Council">Somerset County Council</a> is responsible for running the largest and most expensive local services such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Local_Education_Authority" title="Local Education Authority">education</a>, <a href="/wiki/Social_services" title="Social services">social services</a>, <a href="/wiki/Library" title="Library">libraries</a>, main roads, <a href="/wiki/Public_transport" title="Public transport">public transport</a>, <a href="/wiki/Police" title="Police">policing</a> and  <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fire_service" title="Fire service">fire services</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trading_Standards" title="Trading Standards">trading standards</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Waste_disposal" title="Waste disposal">waste disposal</a> and strategic planning.
</p><p>As Withypool falls within the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Exmoor_National_Park" title="Exmoor National Park">Exmoor National Park</a> some functions normally administered by district or county councils have, since 1997, fallen under the Exmoor National Park Authority, which is known as a ‘single purpose’ authority, which aims to "conserve and enhance the natural beauty, wildlife and cultural heritage of the National Parks" and "promote opportunities for the understanding and enjoyment of the special qualities of the Parks by the public",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ENPA_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-ENPA-10">[10]</a></sup> including responsibility for the conservation of the historic environment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>It is also part of the <a href="/wiki/Bridgwater_and_West_Somerset_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Bridgwater and West Somerset (UK Parliament constituency)">Bridgwater and West Somerset</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/County_constituency" title="County constituency">county constituency</a> represented in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_House_of_Commons" title="British House of Commons">House of Commons</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Parliament of the United Kingdom">Parliament of the United Kingdom</a>. It elects one <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament (MP)</a> by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/First_past_the_post" title="First past the post">first past the post</a> system of election, and part of the <a href="/wiki/South_West_England_(European_Parliament_constituency)" title="South West England (European Parliament constituency)">South West England constituency</a> of the <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament" title="European Parliament">European Parliament</a> which elects seven <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Members_of_the_European_Parliament" title="Members of the European Parliament">MEPs</a> using the <a href="/wiki/D%27Hondt_method" title="D'Hondt method">d'Hondt method</a> of <a href="/wiki/Party-list_proportional_representation" title="Party-list proportional representation">party-list proportional representation</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Landmarks">Landmarks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Withypool&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Landmarks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>2 miles upstream of the village, the River Barle passes under a late medieval five-arch stone <a href="/wiki/Landacre_Bridge" title="Landacre Bridge">Landacre Bridge</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Royal_Oak_Inn">The Royal Oak Inn</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Withypool&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The Royal Oak Inn">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The village's Royal Oak Inn has seen its share of history since its construction in the late 17th century.  <a href="/wiki/R._D._Blackmore" title="R. D. Blackmore">R. D. Blackmore</a> wrote part of <i><a href="/wiki/Lorna_Doone" title="Lorna Doone">Lorna Doone</a></i> in the bar, and artist <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Munnings" title="Alfred Munnings">Alfred Munnings</a> had a studio in the loft.  In the 1930s, the inn was owned by Gwladys and Maxwell Knight, a spy-ring leader and radio broadcaster upon whom <a href="/wiki/Ian_Fleming" title="Ian Fleming">Ian Fleming</a> based the character of <a href="/wiki/James_Bond" title="James Bond">James Bond</a>'s boss, <a href="/wiki/M_(James_Bond)" title="M (James Bond)">M</a>.  During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, the nearby <a href="/wiki/Woolacombe" title="Woolacombe">Woolacombe</a> beach was used to simulate the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/D-Day" title="D-Day">invasion of Normandy</a>, and General <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dwight_Eisenhower" title="Dwight Eisenhower">Dwight Eisenhower</a> planned some of the operation from the Royal Oak.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Religious_sites">Religious sites</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Withypool&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Religious sites">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The late medieval <a href="/wiki/Church_of_St_Andrew,_Withypool" title="Church of St Andrew, Withypool">Church of St Andrew</a> is a Grade II* <a href="/wiki/Listed_building" title="Listed building">listed building</a>. The tower was rebuilt in the early 17th century, <a href="/wiki/Victorian_restoration" title="Victorian restoration">restored and refitted</a> in 1887, and restored extensively and rebuilt again in 1902.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Norman_architecture" title="Norman architecture">Norman</a> <a href="/wiki/Church_of_St_Giles,_Hawkridge" title="Church of St Giles, Hawkridge">Church of St Giles in Hawkridge</a> has 14th-century origins.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p>
