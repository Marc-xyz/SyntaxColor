<p><b>Adebukola Bankole, Chief Judge Adebukola Bankole</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigerian</a> Chief Judge of a Federal High Court in Abuja that sentenced two Ex-Governors in Nigeria to imprisonment - <a href="/wiki/Joshua_Dariye" title="Joshua Dariye">Joshua Dariye</a> to a 14 years in prisonment after being found guilty of some criminal charges <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Jolly_Nyame" title="Jolly Nyame">Jolly Nyame</a> to 28 years imprisonment without an option of fine <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> both on similar charges <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> in a trail that lasted over a decade.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> President <a href="/wiki/Muhammadu_Buhari" title="Muhammadu Buhari">Muhammadu Buhari</a> appointed Justice Adebukola Banjoko as Chairman of Court of Conduct Tribunal (CCT), in June 2018 as <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Adamu Abdu-Kafarati was appointed as the Chief Judge of the Federal High Court in a letter he wrote to the Senate requesting the confirmation of Mr Abdu-Kafarati’s appointment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adebukola_Banjoko&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Banjoko hails from Ogun State and has a Bachelor in Law from the University of London, where she was between 1982 and 1985. She was at the Nigerian Law School between 1985 and 1986. She practised law for a while before her appointment as a Magistrate in Oyo State where she served between May 1997 and November 2003.  She left in 2003 as Chief Magistrate, Administration and in December 2003, she became a judge of the High Court of the FCT.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adebukola_Banjoko&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Adebukola Banjoko excused herself from the trial of former Chairman, House of Representatives Ad hoc Committee on Fuel Subsidy Regime, <a href="/wiki/Farouk_Lawan" title="Farouk Lawan">Farouk Lawan</a> and one other in 2014 as she ordered the case file be returned to the court’s Chief Judge, Justice Ibrahim Bukar. She refused to continue with proceedings in the case despite the withdrawal of an application filed by Lawan, asking her to disqualify herself from the case after the Chief Judge had cleared her of the allegation in a petition as she tags the contents of the petition to be “scandalous and a challenge” on her integrity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>As of 2014, she has spent six years as a magistrate and 11 years as a judge.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Presently she is the Chairman of the Code of Conduct Tribunal in Nigeria.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p>
