<p><b>Ana Maria Brânză</b> (<small>Romanian pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Romanian" title="Help:IPA/Romanian">[ˈana maˈri.a ˈbrɨnzə]</a></span>, born 26 November 1984, current name <b>Ana Maria Popescu</b>) is a Romanian épée <a href="/wiki/Fencing" title="Fencing">fencer</a>. She earned the silver medal in the individual épée event at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympic_Games" title="2008 Summer Olympic Games">2008 Summer Olympic Games</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sports-reference_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-sports-reference-2">[2]</a></sup> and the gold medal in the <a href="/wiki/2013_European_Fencing_Championships" title="2013 European Fencing Championships">2013 European Fencing Championships</a>. She won the <a href="/wiki/Fencing_World_Cup" title="Fencing World Cup">women's épée World Cup series</a> a record-equalling three times (<a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Fencing_World_Cup" title="2007–08 Fencing World Cup">2007–08</a>, <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Fencing_World_Cup" title="2008–09 Fencing World Cup">2008–09</a>, and <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Fencing_World_Cup" title="2012–13 Fencing World Cup">2012–13</a>), with thirteen World Cup titles to her name. With the Romanian team she was twice World champion (2010 and 2011) and six-times European champion (2006, 2008, 2009, 2011, 2014, and 2015).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ana_Maria_Popescu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Brânză was born in 1984 in the <a href="/wiki/Rahova" title="Rahova">Rahova</a> district of <a href="/wiki/Bucharest" title="Bucharest">Bucharest</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ad2012_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ad2012-3">[3]</a></sup> the second of two children. A very energetic child, she was pushed by her parents towards sport. She first tried tennis because the courts were not far from home,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adevarul_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Adevarul-4">[4]</a></sup> but she left after one year because she was the only left-handed player and because of the lack of competitive events.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ad2012_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ad2012-3">[3]</a></sup> When she was ten, her elder brother Marius, who played football for a school team of <a href="/wiki/CSA_Steaua_Bucure%C8%99ti" title="CSA Steaua București">CSA Steaua București</a>, took her to his club's fencing hall in <a href="/wiki/Ghencea" title="Ghencea">Ghencea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FIE_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-FIE-5">[5]</a></sup> She did not care much for the idea at first, as she was not a fan of <i>The Three Musketeers</i>, but she was attracted to the sport as soon as she set foot in the fencing hall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ad2012_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Ad2012-3">[3]</a></sup> She turned to <a href="/wiki/%C3%89p%C3%A9e" title="Épée">épée</a> because there was no other left-handed weapon available in the club when she began training.
</p><p>Brânză became champion of Romania for her age group after only six months of training.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adevarul_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Adevarul-4">[4]</a></sup> She was noticed by national coach <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dan_Podeanu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dan Podeanu (page does not exist)">Dan Podeanu</a>, who after a trial selected her for centralized training. At 13 years old, at the beginning of class VIII, she left her family and moved to <a href="/wiki/Craiova" title="Craiova">Craiova</a> to train at the Junior Olympic Centre for épée with other athletes, much of which were older than her. She pursued her studies at the Petrache-Trișcu Sports High School, which later gave her name to one of its alleys.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GDS_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-GDS-6">[6]</a></sup> The high school offers a specific curriculum for young athletes with three or four class hours a day, the rest being devoted to sport, but the sports facilities were in a rather run-down state in post-Ceaușescu Romania. For lack of a dedicated building, they first held their training sessions in the community hall of the high school. As there was no locker room, they had to change behind curtains.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ad2012_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-Ad2012-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>After her <a href="/wiki/Romanian_Baccalaureate" title="Romanian Baccalaureate"><i>bacalaureat</i></a> she was offered an athletic scholarship from an American university, but she chose to stay in Romania.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> She first hoped to study <a href="/wiki/Psychology" title="Psychology">psychology</a> at university, before setting for the Faculty of sports and physical education of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Craiova" title="University of Craiova">University of Craiova</a>, where she obtained a master in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FIE_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-FIE-5">[5]</a></sup> The same year she was awarded the title of master emeritus in sports (<i>Maestru Emerit al Sportului</i>).
</p><p>She joined in 2001 one of the main Romanian sport clubs, CSA Steaua, which is run by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ministry_of_National_Defence_of_Romania" title="Ministry of National Defence of Romania">Ministry of National Defence of Romania</a>, and received the grade of sergeant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FIE_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-FIE-5">[5]</a></sup> She is however under no military obligation and is seconded full-time to her sport.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Being from a military family–her grandfather, father and brother served in the army–,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> she regularly appears in uniform in the media.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> She was promoted to lieutenant after her studies,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FIE_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-FIE-5">[5]</a></sup> and as of 2015<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Ana_Maria_Popescu&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup> holds the grade of major.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>Brânză is committed to promoting student sport.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> She also supports AITA, an association for <a href="/wiki/Autism" title="Autism">autistic</a> children in Bucharest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> From September 2013 to November 2014 she ran the campaign <i><i lang="ro" title="Romanian language text">Aleargă de ziua ta!</i></i> (“run on your birthday”) which encouraged people to celebrate their birthday by engaging in physical activity and gathering funds for a charitable cause.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2015 she married Pavel Popescu, who plays <a href="/wiki/Water_polo" title="Water polo">water polo</a> for CSA Steaua, and announced his intention to change her name for competition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ana_Maria_Popescu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2001.E2.80.932004"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2001–2004">2001–2004</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ana_Maria_Popescu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2001–2004">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Brânză won her first senior national championship at the age of fifteen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> She attended her first international competitions as a member of CSM–LPS Craiova. She took part in the 1999 Cadet World Championships in Keszthely, finishing only 28th, but the confrontation with fencers from countries with better training conditions spurred her to train harder: “I saw kids from France wearing immaculate white outfits, while I had a yellowed kit and sneakers ripped up at the toe. But I went after them to defeat them.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J14_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-J14-17">[17]</a></sup>
</p><p>She joined in 2001 the fencing section of CSA Steaua under coach <a href="/wiki/Cornel_Milan" title="Cornel Milan">Cornel Milan</a>. The same year she posted her first significant result with a gold medal at the Cadet World Championships and a team silver medal at the Junior World Championships, both in <a href="/wiki/Gda%C5%84sk" title="Gdańsk">Gdańsk</a>. These achievements prompted the Romanian media to compare her to Olympic foil champion <a href="/wiki/Laura_Badea-C%C3%A2rlescu" title="Laura Badea-Cârlescu">Laura Badea-Cârlescu</a>, but she took exception to the comparison, claiming she wanted to do better than Badea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2002 Brânză won the gold medal at the Junior World Championships in <a href="/wiki/Antalya" title="Antalya">Antalya</a> after prevailing over China's <a href="/wiki/Tan_Li" title="Tan Li">Tan Li</a>. She was praised for “a remarkable tactical mastery for a seventeen and a half year old girl”.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The same year she took part in her first senior major competitions. At the <a href="/wiki/2002_European_Fencing_Championships" title="2002 European Fencing Championships">European Championships</a> in Moscow she reached the quarter-finals before being eliminated 9–15 by <a href="/wiki/Lyubov_Shutova" title="Lyubov Shutova">Lyubov Shutova</a>. At the <a href="/wiki/2002_World_Fencing_Championships" title="2002 World Fencing Championships">World Championships</a> in Lisbon she made it to the semi-finals, where she was defeated 6–15 by Germany's <a href="/wiki/Imke_Duplitzer" title="Imke Duplitzer">Imke Duplitzer</a> and came away with a bronze medal. She would later call it her fondest victory, because she was very young at the time and was there without her coach, with only her sabre colleagues as companions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup>
</p><p>Having reached the top quickly, she lost her previous fearlessness and began to feel apprehensive before her bouts: “When you get on the piste, it looks to you like your opponent is out to steal your dreams.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-J14_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-J14-17">[17]</a></sup> She struggled throughout the <a href="/wiki/2002%E2%80%9303_Fencing_World_Cup" title="2002–03 Fencing World Cup">2002–03 season</a>, managing only a quarter-finals finish in Prague. A double gold medal haul at the Junior European Fencing in Porec helped her overcome the hurdle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> That season saw also the first apparition of the Romanian women's épée team in senior competitions, namely the <a href="/wiki/2003_World_Fencing_Championships" title="2003 World Fencing Championships">2003 World Fencing Championships</a>, where they finished 8th.
</p><p>In April 2004 she won the Junior World Championships again after defeating <a href="/wiki/Bianca_Del_Carretto" title="Bianca Del Carretto">Bianca Del Carretto</a> 15–14 in the final. She reached the quarter-finals in the <a href="/wiki/2004_European_Fencing_Championships" title="2004 European Fencing Championships">senior European Championships</a> in Copenhagen. She qualified for the <a href="/wiki/2004_Summer_Olympics" title="2004 Summer Olympics">2004 Summer Olympics</a> by earning a silver medal at the zonal qualifying tournament in Ghent, seeing off top-seed <a href="/wiki/Sophie_Lamon" title="Sophie Lamon">Sophie Lamon</a> of Switzerland in the table of 16, but losing in the final to <a href="/wiki/Sonja_Tol" title="Sonja Tol">Sonja Tol</a> of the Netherlands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> For her Olympic début in Athens Brânză managed to beat experienced fencer <a href="/wiki/Adrienn_Hormay" title="Adrienn Hormay">Adrienn Hormay</a> of Hungary, but stumbled in the table of 16 against China's <a href="/wiki/Zhang_Li_(fencer)" title="Zhang Li (fencer)">Zhang Li</a> and finished 16th. She later explained she felt overwhelmed by the Olympic experience<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> and like a child lost amongst big-name athletes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2004.E2.80.932008"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2004–2008">2004–2008</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ana_Maria_Popescu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2004–2008">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_Fencing_World_Cup" title="2004–05 Fencing World Cup">2004–05 season</a> she reached her first podium in the <a href="/wiki/Fencing_World_Cup" title="Fencing World Cup">Fencing World Cup</a> with a silver medal at the Budapest Grand Prix. This result, along with a quarter-final place at the <a href="/wiki/2005_World_Fencing_Championships" title="2005 World Fencing Championships">2005 World Championships</a> in Leipzig, where she lost to Estonia's <a href="/wiki/Maarika_V%C3%B5su" title="Maarika Võsu">Maarika Võsu</a>, allowed her to close the season in the Top 10 for the first time in her career. The <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_Fencing_World_Cup" title="2005–06 Fencing World Cup">next season</a> she won her first World Cup title in Budapest and posted four top-eight finishes. At the <a href="/wiki/2005_European_Fencing_Championships" title="2005 European Fencing Championships">European Championships</a> in İzmir she was defeated in the second round by teammate <a href="/wiki/Iuliana_M%C4%83ce%C8%99eanu" title="Iuliana Măceșeanu">Iuliana Măceșeanu</a>. In the team event Romania prevailed 45–43 over Russia and met Hungary in the final. Brânză defeated <a href="/wiki/T%C3%ADmea_Nagy" title="Tímea Nagy">Tímea Nagy</a> in the last leg, allowing Romania to edge out a 33–32 win. This victory over a double Olympic champion encouraged her to think she could become a champion in her own right.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> She reached the quarter-finals again at the <a href="/wiki/2006_World_Fencing_Championships" title="2006 World Fencing Championships">2006 World Championships</a> in Turin, but yielded to Nagy this time.
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Fencing_World_Cup" title="2007–08 Fencing World Cup">2007–08 season</a> Brânză claimed the gold medal at the St Petersburg World Cup, the silver in Budapest and Havana, and the bronze in Luxembourg and Barcelona. She climbed to the second place in world rankings, gaining <a href="/wiki/Fencing_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Qualification" title="Fencing at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Qualification">qualification</a> to the women's individual épée event of the <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">2008 Summer Olympics</a>–<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> women's team épée did not feature on the Olympic programme for that edition. The <a href="/wiki/2008_European_Fencing_Championships" title="2008 European Fencing Championships">2008 European Championships</a> held in Kiev in July were the last rehearsal before the Olympic Games. Brânză reached the semi-finals, where she saw off Russia's <a href="/wiki/Anna_Sivkova" title="Anna Sivkova">Anna Sivkova</a>. In the final she lost 15–10 to Hormay, whom she had defeated at the Athens Olympics, and came away with the silver–her first individual European medal. In the team event Romania overcame Poland and Hungary, then largely prevailed over Germany in the final to earn their second continental title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup>
</p><p>In August, the Romanian delegation arrived in Beijing two weeks ahead of the competition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-R12_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-R12-28">[28]</a></sup> Being the only Romanian epeist at the Games, she trained with <a href="/wiki/Foil_(fencing)" title="Foil (fencing)">foilists</a> <a href="/wiki/Cristina_Stahl" title="Cristina Stahl">Cristina Stahl</a> and <a href="/wiki/Virgil_S%C4%83li%C8%99can" title="Virgil Sălișcan">Virgil Sălișcan</a>. As No.2 seed she received a bye in the first round. She then disposed 15–11 of Japan's <a href="/wiki/Megumi_Ikeda" title="Megumi Ikeda">Megumi Harada</a> and beat 15–13 Russia's Lyubov Shutova to reach the semi-finals, where she met experienced, 39-year-old <a href="/wiki/Ildik%C3%B3_Mincza-N%C3%A9bald" title="Ildikó Mincza-Nébald">Ildikó Mincza-Nébald</a> of Hungary. After a very balanced bout where no fencer managed to gain more than a two-hit advantage, Brânză struck the decisive hit to win her ticket to the final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> After a brief respite she met world No.1 <a href="/wiki/Britta_Heidemann" title="Britta Heidemann">Britta Heidemann</a> of Germany, who acquired an early four-hit lead. Brânză rallied to 10–12 in the third and last period, but the German struck three hits in a row, closing the bout 11–15 with a double touche.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Brânza attributed Heidemann's victory to her superior physical condition, explaining that the German had "fenced like a man".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup>
</p><p>For her silver medal Brânză was awarded the Romanian order for sports merit (<i>Ordinul “Meritul Sportiv”</i>), second class.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> She finished first in the overall <a href="/wiki/Fencing_World_Cup" title="Fencing World Cup">women's épée World Cup</a> for two consecutive seasons, 2007–2008 and 2008–2009. She was designated a member of the athletes commission of the International Fencing Federation by its executive committee for the period 2009–2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2008.E2.80.932012"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2008–2012">2008–2012</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ana_Maria_Popescu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2008–2012">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the Olympics Brânză began to complain of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tendinitis" title="Tendinitis">tendon pain</a> in her weapon hand, but the competition rhythm did not allow her to attend to it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adevarul_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Adevarul-4">[4]</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/2009_European_Fencing_Championships" title="2009 European Fencing Championships">2009 European Championships</a> in Plovdiv she was defeated in the second round by fellow Romanian <a href="/wiki/Anca_M%C4%83roiu" title="Anca Măroiu">Anca Măroiu</a>, but secured her third continental team title after Romania defeated successively Russia, Germany and Poland. Teammate <a href="/wiki/Simona_Gherman" title="Simona Gherman">Simona Gherman</a> jokily referred to the team as the “<i lang="ro" title="Romanian language text">Power Praf</i> girls”, after the <i><a href="/wiki/The_Powerpuff_Girls" title="The Powerpuff Girls">Powerpuff Girls</a></i> anime, because of the hardships of the match against Russia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> The nickname stuck. They would later incorporate it in their pre-match battle cry: “We're the Powerpuff Girls and we'll make mincemeat of you!” (<a href="/wiki/Romanian_language" title="Romanian language">Romanian</a>: <i lang="ro">Suntem fetițele PowerPraf și am venit să vă facem pilaf!</i>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup>
</p><p>Brânză was rested for the Romanian national championships to spare her hand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> She arrived with a No.1 ranking to the <a href="/wiki/2010_World_Fencing_Championships" title="2010 World Fencing Championships">World Championships</a> in Antalya, but warned beforehand that she was only fencing at 50 to 60% of her ability due to her wrist problems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> She was eliminated 9–10 in the table of 16 by Canada's <a href="/wiki/Sherraine_Schalm" title="Sherraine Schalm">Sherraine Schalm</a>. Romania were defeated in the first round by Italy, again by a single hit, and finished sixth after the placement matches.
</p><p>After this failure Brânză resolved to address her wrist issues. Medical investigations in Romania and in France showed that her condition was operable, but that the surgery would likely end her fencing career. She chose instead to take a break in her career to follow a strict recovery program.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> After a pause of several months, she earned a gold medal in her first two competitions, the Florina and Nanjing 2010 World Cup events.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Adevarul_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-Adevarul-4">[4]</a></sup> Up to 2012 she suffered from left-wrist pains and had to get medical attention after each competition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Escrime21-2012_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-Escrime21-2012-39">[39]</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Fetele_Power_Praf_2015_Ciutat_de_Barcelona_t125724.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2667" data-file-width="4000" decoding="async" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Fetele_Power_Praf_2015_Ciutat_de_Barcelona_t125724.jpg/220px-Fetele_Power_Praf_2015_Ciutat_de_Barcelona_t125724.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Fetele_Power_Praf_2015_Ciutat_de_Barcelona_t125724.jpg/330px-Fetele_Power_Praf_2015_Ciutat_de_Barcelona_t125724.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Fetele_Power_Praf_2015_Ciutat_de_Barcelona_t125724.jpg/440px-Fetele_Power_Praf_2015_Ciutat_de_Barcelona_t125724.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Fetele_Power_Praf_2015_Ciutat_de_Barcelona_t125724.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The twice-world champion “Power Praf” girls: from L to R, <a href="/wiki/Simona_Gherman" title="Simona Gherman">Gherman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anca_M%C4%83roiu" title="Anca Măroiu">Măroiu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Loredana_Dinu" title="Loredana Dinu">Dinu</a> and Brânză</div></div></div>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/2010_World_Fencing_Championships" title="2010 World Fencing Championships">2010 World Fencing Championships</a> in Paris, Brânză was again eliminated early in the individual event, but won the first Romanian team gold in épée with Simona Gherman, <a href="/wiki/Loredana_Dinu" title="Loredana Dinu">Loredana Dinu</a> and Anca Măroiu. Brânză contributed more than half the hits scored by Romania in the final against Germany.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> Her last relay against <a href="/wiki/Imke_Duplitzer" title="Imke Duplitzer">Imke Duplitzer</a>, which she won 18–10, was described as “a real example of technique and tactical intelligence” in an otherwise “quite monotonous” match.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2011 Brânză took the bronze medal in the individual event of the <a href="/wiki/2011_European_Fencing_Championships" title="2011 European Fencing Championships">2011 European Fencing Championships</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sheffield" title="Sheffield">Sheffield</a> and the gold medal in the team event after defeating Russia 45-31. The victory ceremony was marred by an incident when <i><a href="/wiki/Trei_culori" title="Trei culori">Trei culori</a></i>, the former, communist-era <a href="/wiki/National_anthem" title="National anthem">national anthem</a> of Romania, was played instead of the current <i><a href="/wiki/De%C8%99teapt%C4%83-te,_rom%C3%A2ne!" title="Deșteaptă-te, române!">Deșteaptă-te, române!</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sheffield_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sheffield-42">[42]</a></sup> Brânză decided to walk off in protest and convinced her teammates of following her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sheffield_42-1"><a href="#cite_note-Sheffield-42">[42]</a></sup>
</p><p>At the <a href="/wiki/2011_World_Fencing_Championships" title="2011 World Fencing Championships">2011 World Fencing Championships</a> in <a href="/wiki/Catania" title="Catania">Catania</a>, Brânză was stopped in the semifinals by <a href="/wiki/Sun_Yujie" title="Sun Yujie">Sun Yujie</a> of China and was doomed to a bronze medal, the second World Championships medal in her career. In the <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_team_%C3%A9p%C3%A9e_at_the_2011_World_Fencing_Championships" title="Women's team épée at the 2011 World Fencing Championships">team event</a>, the “<i lang="ro" title="Romanian language text">Power Praf</i> girls” saw off easily Great Britain then Poland, but Brânză was hit on her weapon hand during the quarter-finals against Poland and had to get medical attention. She fenced with a bandaged hand and only one relay in the semifinal against Germany, which Romania won with a tight score of 24–23. She was to be rested during the final against China, but was called eventually to replace a stressed out Măroiu. She lost her first relay 1–4 to Sun Yujie, but her 4–1 against Xu Anqi contributed to Romania's sudden-death victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> Brânză was crowned team world champion for the second time in a row. For this performance she, Măroiu and Alexandru, as members of the military club CSA Steaua, received the emblem of honour of the Romanian Army (<a href="/wiki/Romanian_language" title="Romanian language">Romanian</a>: <i lang="ro">emblema de onoare a Armatei României</i>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> and all members of the team received <a href="/wiki/Honorary_citizenship" title="Honorary citizenship">honorary citizenship</a> of Craiova.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2012, the Romanian team received new kit from their equipment sponsor PBT, including red, yellow, and blue coloured masks which allow them to form as a team the tricolour <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Romania" title="Flag of Romania">flag of Romania</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-galben_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-galben-46">[46]</a></sup> Because nobody else wanted it, Brânză opted for the yellow mask,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-galben_46-1"><a href="#cite_note-galben-46">[46]</a></sup> which has since become her trademark.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> She was considered to <a href="/wiki/List_of_flag_bearers_for_Romania_at_the_Olympics" title="List of flag bearers for Romania at the Olympics">carry the flag for Romania</a> at the Olympics, but she declined, citing her left hand problem.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup>
</p><p>Number 1 in world rankings in April 2012,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> she went for gold in the <a href="/wiki/Fencing_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics" title="Fencing at the 2012 Summer Olympics">London 2012 Summer Olympics</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-R12_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-R12-28">[28]</a></sup> but she was beaten 14–13 in the round of 16 by Ukraine's <a href="/wiki/Yana_Shemyakina" title="Yana Shemyakina">Yana Shemyakina</a>, who eventually took the gold medal. The top-seed Romanian team she led was defeated 45–38 by 10th-ranked <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> in the quarter-finals and fell short of the podium too. Brânză was quoted saying: "This is the most painful moment in my life."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> After the Games three Romanian team members retired from sport, but Brânză decided to continue her career and announced her new goal was the <a href="/wiki/2016_Summer_Olympics" title="2016 Summer Olympics">2016 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> After the resignation of her lifelong coach Dan Podeanu, she began training under <a href="/wiki/Octavian_Zidaru" title="Octavian Zidaru">Octavian Zidaru</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="After_the_London_Games">After the London Games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ana_Maria_Popescu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: After the London Games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Romania_v_France_EFS_2013_Fencing_WCH_t173400.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3337" data-file-width="5000" decoding="async" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/Romania_v_France_EFS_2013_Fencing_WCH_t173400.jpg/220px-Romania_v_France_EFS_2013_Fencing_WCH_t173400.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/Romania_v_France_EFS_2013_Fencing_WCH_t173400.jpg/330px-Romania_v_France_EFS_2013_Fencing_WCH_t173400.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/Romania_v_France_EFS_2013_Fencing_WCH_t173400.jpg/440px-Romania_v_France_EFS_2013_Fencing_WCH_t173400.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Romania_v_France_EFS_2013_Fencing_WCH_t173400.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Brânză (left) and teammates celebrate Romania's bronze medal in the <a href="/wiki/2013_World_Fencing_Championships" title="2013 World Fencing Championships">2013 World Fencing Championships</a></div></div></div>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Fencing_World_Cup" title="2012–13 Fencing World Cup">2012–13 season</a> Brânză made the podium in six out of eight entries in World Cup events, including gold medals in the Challenge International de Saint-Maur and the Havana Grand Prix.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> She led CSA Steaua to a silver medal in the European Champion Clubs' Cup in Naples.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> In May she won her 8th national title as well as the team event.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ager_54-0"><a href="#cite_note-ager-54">[54]</a></sup>
</p><p>Number one in the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_d%27Escrime" title="Fédération Internationale d'Escrime">FIE</a> world rankings, she went to the <a href="/wiki/2013_European_Fencing_Championships" title="2013 European Fencing Championships">2013 European Fencing Championships</a> as favourite. She had a tight 15–14 win in the table of 32 against 17-year-old Alona Komarov of Israel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ZagrebGPS_55-0"><a href="#cite_note-ZagrebGPS-55">[55]</a></sup> Her later bouts were easier victories and she eventually earned the gold after defeating Italia's <a href="/wiki/Francesca_Quondamcarlo" title="Francesca Quondamcarlo">Francesca Quondamcarlo</a> 15–11 in the final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ZagrebGPS_55-1"><a href="#cite_note-ZagrebGPS-55">[55]</a></sup> In the team event she led a largely renewed Romanian side, now nicknamed ”Poky Power”, scoring the decisive hit in the 44–43 quarter-final against Sweden; Romania then disposed of Hungary, but failed against Estonia in the final and came away with a silver medal.
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/2013_World_Fencing_Championships" title="2013 World Fencing Championships">2013 World Fencing Championships</a> in Budapest, Brânză made her way comfortably to the table of eight, but she was defeated in a tight 14–15 bout by Estonia's <a href="/wiki/Julia_Beljajeva" title="Julia Beljajeva">Julia Beljajeva</a>, who eventually won the competition. In <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_team_%C3%A9p%C3%A9e_at_the_2013_World_Fencing_Championships" title="Women's team épée at the 2013 World Fencing Championships">the team event</a>, Romania defeated successively Denmark, Venezuela, and South Korea, reaping revenge on the defeat in the Olympic Games. They were stopped in the semifinals by Russia, which largely prevailed with a 44–33 victory. Romania then fought France in the match for the third place; Brânză entered the last leg on a 25–25 draw and beat <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9phine_Jacques-Andr%C3%A9-Coquin" title="Joséphine Jacques-André-Coquin">Joséphine Jacques-André-Coquin</a> 8–3 to secure the bronze medal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> Brânză was also elected a member of the athletes' commission in the elections held during the world championships.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> She finished the year No.1 for a record-equalling third time and received a gold medal from the FIE during its centenary gala dinner in Paris at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Automobile_Club_de_France" title="Automobile Club de France">Automobile Club de France</a> on 30 November 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Romania_v_Italy_2014_European_Championships_EFS-EQ_t122627.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2662" data-file-width="4000" decoding="async" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/Romania_v_Italy_2014_European_Championships_EFS-EQ_t122627.jpg/220px-Romania_v_Italy_2014_European_Championships_EFS-EQ_t122627.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/Romania_v_Italy_2014_European_Championships_EFS-EQ_t122627.jpg/330px-Romania_v_Italy_2014_European_Championships_EFS-EQ_t122627.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/Romania_v_Italy_2014_European_Championships_EFS-EQ_t122627.jpg/440px-Romania_v_Italy_2014_European_Championships_EFS-EQ_t122627.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Romania_v_Italy_2014_European_Championships_EFS-EQ_t122627.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Brânză (left) and teammates at the semi-finals of the <a href="/wiki/2014_European_Fencing_Championships" title="2014 European Fencing Championships">2014 European Fencing Championships</a></div></div></div>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Fencing_World_Cup" title="2013–14 Fencing World Cup">2013–14 season</a>, Brânză took part in the <a href="/wiki/2013_World_Combat_Games" title="2013 World Combat Games">World Combat Games</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint-Petersburg" title="Saint-Petersburg">Saint-Petersburg</a>, but failed to earn a medal after defeats against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emese_Sz%C3%A1sz" title="Emese Szász">Emese Szász</a> and <a href="/wiki/Xu_Anqi" title="Xu Anqi">Xu Anqi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> She finished fifth in the first World Cup event of the season in Doha after <a href="/wiki/Julianna_R%C3%A9v%C3%A9sz" title="Julianna Révész">Julianna Révész</a> defeated her 7–8 in quarter-finals, but won the gold medal in the team event, scoring the decisive hit in additional time in the last three matches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> She went on to win the Budapest Grand Prix, prevailing 15–7 against <a href="/wiki/Irina_Embrich" title="Irina Embrich">Irina Embrich</a> in the final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> She later earned a bronze medal in Saint-Maur after being stopped in semifinals by China's <a href="/wiki/Xu_Anqi" title="Xu Anqi">Xu Anqi</a>, who eventually won the competition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> After three podiums in Barcelona in 2011, 2012, and 2013, she exited the competition in the table of 8 after a 15–11 defeat against <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Qin_Xue&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Qin Xue (page does not exist)">Qin Xue</a> of China. In the team event, she led her team to the semi-finals, where Romania were defeated 35–20 by Russia. Romania then met the United States; Brânză scored the winning hit in additional time to earn bronze.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> The rest of the season brought her no podium placings and she lost in May her world No.1 ranking to Emese Szász.
</p><p>At the <a href="/wiki/2014_European_Fencing_Championships" title="2014 European Fencing Championships">European Championships</a> in Strasbourg Brânză could not defend her title as she was beaten 8–9 in the table of 32 by teammate Simona Gherman, who eventually won a bronze medal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> In the team competition, No.2 seed Romania received a bye, then disposed of Ukraine 45–31, beat Italy 29–24 in the semi-final and overcame Russia 38–34 in a very tight and tactical final, allowing Brânză to win her fifth team European gold.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/2014_World_Fencing_Championships" title="2014 World Fencing Championships">World Championships</a> in Kazan proved very disappointing as Brânză was stopped in the table of 16 by Estonia's <a href="/wiki/Irina_Embrich" title="Irina Embrich">Irina Embrich</a> and slid to the fifth place in FIE rankings. In <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_team_%C3%A9p%C3%A9e_at_the_2014_World_Fencing_Championships" title="Women's team épée at the 2014 World Fencing Championships">the team event</a>, Romania prevailed over Germany, but were defeated in the table of 8 by Italy. Brânză entered on 25–24 for Italy in the last leg and was overcome 16–8 by newly crowned World champion <a href="/wiki/Rossella_Fiamingo" title="Rossella Fiamingo">Rossella Fiamingo</a>. Romania entered the placement rounds and first defeated China, then the United States to take the fifth place in the competition. Following this failure, Octavian Zidaru was replaced as coach by Dan Podeanu,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup> whom Brânză regards as a second father<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup> and who was persuaded to come back for a second stint.
</p><p>Brânză entered the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Fencing_World_Cup" title="2014–15 Fencing World Cup">2014–15 season</a> with a silver medal at the Legnano World Cup after a 13–12 defeat in additional time against Ukraine's <a href="/wiki/Anfisa_Pochkalova" title="Anfisa Pochkalova">Anfisa Pochkalova</a>. In the team event, she scored the winning hit in extra time in the semi-finals against Italy, but Romania were overcome by Estonia in the final and doomed to silver.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> She posted another second place in Xuzhou after failing in the final against Emese Szász.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup> At the Doha Grand Prix her way to the podium was again blocked by Gherman, who defeated her 4–3 in the quarter-finals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Barcelona World Cup in January 2015 proved disappointing as Brânză lost in the second round to Poland's <a href="/wiki/Renata_Knapik-Miazga" title="Renata Knapik-Miazga">Renata Knapik-Miazga</a>, ranked 57th in the world. She subsequently slid to the 8th place in FIE rankings. In the team event, Romania saw off Israel, then China in the quarter-finals. However, Brânză injured her weapon hand after <a href="/wiki/Sun_Yujie" title="Sun Yujie">Sun Yujie</a> accidentally collided with her. She fenced only part of the semifinal with Sweden, which overcame Romania 45-33. She did not fence at all in the small final, where a last-bout rally by Gherman allowed Romania to take the bronze medal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> After being rested for ten days, she reached the quarter-finals in the Buenos Aires World Cup, but lost to Korea's <a href="/wiki/Shin_A-lam" title="Shin A-lam">Shin A-lam</a>. In the team event Romania prevailed over Venezuela and Estonia, then crushed Russia 45–24 before yielding 30–26 to Italy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup> Their silver medal allowed them to climb to the third place in the World rankings. In March Brânză earned the silver medal at the Budapest after losing again to Shin in the final.
<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup> She subsequently reached the third place in FIE rankings. In May she was defeated by Anca Măroiu in the Romanian national championship and came away with a bronze medal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup> She was rested for the Rio de Janeiro Grand Prix, but Shin A-lam's average result at the competition allowed Brânză to reach the second place in world rankings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup>
</p><p>At the <a href="/wiki/2015_European_Fencing_Championships" title="2015 European Fencing Championships">2015 European Championships</a> in <a href="/wiki/Montreux" title="Montreux">Montreux</a> Brânză switched her yellow mask for a tricolour one bearing Romanian colours. In the individual event she stopped short of the podium after a defeat in the quarter-finals to world No.1 Emese Szász.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup> In the team event, Romania saw off Ukraine, then met Sweden, coached by Romanian <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Pop" title="Adrian Pop">Adrian Pop</a>. Brânză entered the last relay on a draw and defeating Sweden's captain <a href="/wiki/Emma_Samuelsson" title="Emma Samuelsson">Emma Samuelsson</a> to bring her team in the final. As in the 2013 edition they met Estonia, which they defeated this time on 45-30.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup> Brânză claimed her sixth continental title, a record in women's épée. A few weeks later, she reached the final at the <a href="/wiki/2015_European_Games" title="2015 European Games">2015 European Games</a> in Baku after a sudden-death victory over Estonia's <a href="/wiki/Erika_Kirpu" title="Erika Kirpu">Erika Kirpu</a>. She proceeded to defeat Russia's <a href="/wiki/Yana_Zvereva" title="Yana Zvereva">Yana Zvereva</a>, winning Romania's first gold medal at the Games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup> In the team event Romania reached the final, where they met the same Estonian team as at the European Championships and produced a repeat performance to allow Brânză a double gold haul.
</p><p>At the <a href="/wiki/2015_World_Fencing_Championships" title="2015 World Fencing Championships">2015 World Championships</a> in Moscow Brânză was eliminated in the second round by Estonia's <a href="/wiki/Katrina_Lehis" title="Katrina Lehis">Katrina Lehis</a>, whom she had always defeated before.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_team_%C3%A9p%C3%A9e_at_the_2015_World_Fencing_Championships" title="Women's team épée at the 2015 World Fencing Championships">team event</a> Romania defeated successively Turkey, Switzerland, Hungary and Ukraine to meet China in the final. Brânză opened the match against Xu Anqi, but failed to find an edge and was defeated 2-5. The following bouts proved as difficult and Brânză entered the last relay on 28-36. She could not bridge the gap and Romania lost eventually on 36-45, taking the silver medal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-80"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup> Brânză finished the season World No.4.
</p>
