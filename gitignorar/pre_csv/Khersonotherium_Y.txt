<p><i><b>Khersonotherium</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prehistoric" title="Prehistoric">prehistoric</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Giraffes" title="Giraffes">giraffes</a>. Its remains were found in <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paleomus_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-paleomus-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paleomus2_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-paleomus2-2">[2]</a></sup> and in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Khersonotherium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first part of the generic name, "<a href="/wiki/Kherson" title="Kherson">Kherson</a>" refers to a city in <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>, where the first specimens were recovered. The second part, <i>therium</i>, comes from the <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>, <i>θηρίον</i> which means "beast".
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Khersonotherium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Most fossils of <i>Khersonotherium</i> are found in <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>. In Russia and Ukraine the fauna in which it lived is called "hipparionova fauna" - the fauna in which <i><a href="/wiki/Hipparion" title="Hipparion">Hipparion</a></i> was a dominant member. A skeleton of <i>Khersonotherium</i>, found in Odessa, a city in <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>, is displayed in Kyiv Paleontological Museum.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paleomus2_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-paleomus2-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
