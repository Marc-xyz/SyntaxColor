<p><b>Bartow</b> is a town in <a href="/wiki/Jefferson_County,_Georgia" title="Jefferson County, Georgia">Jefferson County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a>, United States. As of the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 census</a>, the city had a population of 286.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Initially the town was known as "Spier's Turnout", but was changed to honor the first Confederate officer to die in battle, Colonel <a href="/wiki/Francis_S._Bartow" title="Francis S. Bartow">Francis S. Bartow</a> of <a href="/wiki/Savannah,_Georgia" title="Savannah, Georgia">Savannah, Georgia</a>, who was killed at the Battle of Manassas, Virginia on July 21, 1861.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>

<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-2">[2]</a></sup> of 2000, there were 223 people, 95 households, and 68 families residing in the town.  The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 196.1 people per square mile (75.5/km²).  There were 106 housing units at an average density of 93.2 per square mile (35.9/km²).  The racial makeup of the town was 40.36% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a> and 59.64% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>.  <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 1.35% of the population.
</p><p>There were 95 households out of which 27.4% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 48.4% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 20.0% had a female householder with no husband present, and 28.4% were non-families. 26.3% of all households were made up of individuals and 7.4% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older.  The average household size was 2.35 and the average family size was 2.82.
</p><p>In the town, the population was spread out with 23.8% under the age of 18, 4.5% from 18 to 24, 30.5% from 25 to 44, 27.4% from 45 to 64, and 13.9% who were 65 years of age or older.  The median age was 38 years. For every 100 females, there were 99.1 males.  For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 91.0 males.
</p><p>The median income for a household in the town was $23,750, and the median income for a family was $31,250. Males had a median income of $32,083 versus $16,667 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the town was $11,873.  About 25.4% of families and 30.0% of the population were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 44.9% of those under the age of 18 and 22.9% ages 65 or older.
</p>
