<p><b>Franz Kenneth Stahl</b> (born  October 30, 1962) is an American <a href="/wiki/Guitarist" title="Guitarist">guitarist</a>, best known for being a member of both the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Washington,_DC" title="Washington, DC">Washington, DC</a> <a href="/wiki/Hardcore_punk" title="Hardcore punk">hardcore punk</a> band <a href="/wiki/Scream_(band)" title="Scream (band)">Scream</a> with his brother <a href="/wiki/Peter_Stahl" title="Peter Stahl">Peter Stahl</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Alternative_rock" title="Alternative rock">alternative rock</a> band <a href="/wiki/Foo_Fighters" title="Foo Fighters">Foo Fighters</a>, led by former Scream and <a href="/wiki/Nirvana_(band)" title="Nirvana (band)">Nirvana</a> drummer <a href="/wiki/Dave_Grohl" title="Dave Grohl">Dave Grohl</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Franz_Stahl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Franz and his brother Peter were raised in <a href="/wiki/Bailey%27s_Crossroads,_Virginia" title="Bailey's Crossroads, Virginia">Bailey's Crossroads, Virginia</a>, which they reference by putting the letters "BXR" in Scream's albums. Their father, Arnold, was a lawyer and also managed the DC rock band The Hangmen, who scored a regional hit with 1965's "What a Girl Can't Do".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> After learning guitar, Franz started playing in local <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginian</a> bands with bassist <a href="/wiki/Skeeter_Thompson" title="Skeeter Thompson">Skeeter Thompson</a>. In 1981 Franz and Thompson formed Scream in <a href="/wiki/Alexandria,_Virginia" title="Alexandria, Virginia">Alexandria</a>, along with Peter and drummer <a href="/wiki/Kent_Stax" title="Kent Stax">Kent Stax</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>After Scream broke up in 1990, Franz and Peter formed the band <a href="/wiki/Wool_(band)" title="Wool (band)">Wool</a>, which even released the album <i>Box Set</i> for <a href="/wiki/London_Records" title="London Records">London Records</a> but broke up in 1996.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> As Stahl visited a friend who was producing Japanese musician <a href="/wiki/J_(musician)" title="J (musician)">J</a>, he found out that the drummer was fellow DC musician <a href="/wiki/Scott_Garrett_(musician)" title="Scott Garrett (musician)">Scott Garrett</a>, and that they needed a guitarist for the tour. Stahl accepted, and played around Japan as a member of J's backing band. During the tour, Stahl was invited by Grohl to replace the Foo Fighters' original guitarist <a href="/wiki/Pat_Smear" title="Pat Smear">Pat Smear</a>, who had decided to leave the band. After the tour was finished, he flew in just two days before his debut performance at the <a href="/wiki/Radio_City_Music_Hall" title="Radio City Music Hall">Radio City Music Hall</a> in September 1997, having just one day of rehearsal. Stahl started his short lived Foo Fighters career with their hit "Everlong" from their second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Colour_and_the_Shape" title="The Colour and the Shape">The Colour and the Shape</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-post_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-post-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Stahl performed on the track "A320", which was featured in <i><a href="/wiki/Godzilla:_The_Album" title="Godzilla: The Album">Godzilla: The Album</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and the re-recorded version of the Foo Fighters song "<a href="/wiki/Walking_After_You" title="Walking After You">Walking After You</a>", which was released as a single and is included on the soundtrack for <i><a href="/wiki/The_X-Files_(film)" title="The X-Files (film)">The X-Files</a></i> movie. Stahl also appeared in the music video for the Foo Fighters song "<a href="/wiki/My_Hero_(song)" title="My Hero (song)">My Hero</a>", even though he did not play on the recording. He was released from the band in 1999, before the recording of their third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/There_Is_Nothing_Left_to_Lose" title="There Is Nothing Left to Lose">There Is Nothing Left to Lose</a>,</i> due to creative differences. Stahl still said his tenure in Foo Fighters were "the best two years of my life".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-back_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-back-6">[6]</a></sup> After leaving, J invited Stahl back, and he remained as his guitarist for another seven years until 2005. In 2009, Franz was invited by Peter to play for <a href="/wiki/Smith_%26_Pyle" title="Smith &amp; Pyle">Smith &amp; Pyle</a>, and during the tour the brothers decided to reform Scream with Thompson and Stax.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-post_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-post-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Stahl is a big fan of the <a href="/wiki/Gibson_Les_Paul" title="Gibson Les Paul">Gibson Les Paul</a>, which he is usually seen using. However, in the music video for Foo Fighters's "<a href="/wiki/My_Hero_(song)" title="My Hero (song)">My Hero</a>", Franz is seen using a <a href="/wiki/Fender_Stratocaster" title="Fender Stratocaster">Fender Stratocaster</a>.
</p><p>Stahl currently lives in <a href="/wiki/Hollywood" title="Hollywood">Hollywood</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>. Along with Scream, he also recently has been gigging, writing and recording with the Boston Hardcore band <a href="/wiki/DYS_(band)" title="DYS (band)">DYS</a>, while also writing, and composing for film and television.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In 2007 he composed the title track for the film <i><a href="/wiki/One_California_Day" title="One California Day">One California Day</a></i>.
</p>
