<p><b>The Sunsets Farewell Tour</b> was a 2010 concert tour of Australian rock band <a href="/wiki/Powderfinger" title="Powderfinger">Powderfinger</a>. It was their last concert tour following the announcement on 9 April 2010 that they would be breaking up after the tour, saying it would be their last ever as the band had musically said everything they wanted to say.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-breakup_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-breakup-1">[1]</a></sup> It coincided with the release of their final album, <i><a href="/wiki/Golden_Rule_(album)" title="Golden Rule (album)">Golden Rule</a></i>, on 13 November 2009.
</p><p>In a statement read by Bernard Fanning, the band said:
</p>
<blockquote><p><i>"With the completion of our last album, Golden Rule, we feel that we have said all that we want to say as a musical group. We firmly believe that it is our most complete and satisfying album and can't think of a better way to farewell our fans than with music that we all believe in and also with, hopefully, our best tour to date."</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-powderfinger.com_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-powderfinger.com-2">[2]</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>The band also stated, regarding the set list, that their first album <i><a href="/wiki/Parables_for_Wooden_Ears" title="Parables for Wooden Ears">Parables for Wooden Ears</a></i> probably wouldn't feature so much; Fanning laughed while saying, "we're going to play that in its entirety." Most of the songs performed were from their most successful albums – <i><a href="/wiki/Internationalist_(album)" title="Internationalist (album)">Internationalist</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Odyssey_Number_Five" title="Odyssey Number Five">Odyssey Number Five</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Vulture_Street_(album)" title="Vulture Street (album)">Vulture Street</a></i> – as well as singles "<a href="/wiki/These_Days_(Powderfinger_song)" title="These Days (Powderfinger song)">These Days</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Bless_My_Soul" title="Bless My Soul">Bless My Soul</a>".
</p>
