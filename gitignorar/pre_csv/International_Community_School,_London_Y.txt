<p><b>International Community School (ICS)</b> is a co-educational World <a href="/wiki/International_Baccalaureate" title="International Baccalaureate">International Baccalaureate</a> School<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> located in <a href="/wiki/Marylebone" title="Marylebone">Marylebone</a>, <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, teaching students aged from three to 18 years old.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Community_School,_London&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Toettcher family established International Community School (ICS)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> in 1979. The school was set up to teach children English within a full curriculum International School. Since then the School has broadened its scope to offer a full curriculum for children between 3 and 18 years old.
</p><p>In 2008, the <a href="/wiki/International_Baccalaureate" title="International Baccalaureate">International Baccalaureate</a> made ICS an IB World School, accredited to teach the <a href="/wiki/IB_Primary_Years_Programme" title="IB Primary Years Programme">Primary Years Programme (PYP)</a> and <a href="/wiki/IB_Middle_Years_Programme" title="IB Middle Years Programme">Middle Years Programme (MYP, and Diploma Programme (DP).</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2010, ICS was awarded accreditation to teach the <a href="/wiki/IB_Diploma_Programme" title="IB Diploma Programme">IB Diploma</a>, becoming one of only five schools in the UK to teach all three school-age levels of the IB.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>ICS was acquired by the NACE Schools group in 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>  ICS is a non-selective Ofsted-inspected<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Community_School,_London&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A supervised school trip to Brazil in 2013 ended in tragedy when one pupil was killed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Curriculum">Curriculum</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Community_School,_London&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Curriculum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>ICS teaches the International Baccalaureate curriculum through the three programmes:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/IB_Primary_Years_Programme" title="IB Primary Years Programme">PYP</a> for students aged 3–11 years</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/IB_Middle_Years_Programme" title="IB Middle Years Programme">MYP</a> for students aged 11–16 years</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/IB_Diploma_Programme" title="IB Diploma Programme">IB Diploma</a> for students aged 16 and over</li></ul>
