<p>The <b>2016 Poinsettia Bowl</b> was a <a href="/wiki/College_football" title="College football">college football</a> <a href="/wiki/Bowl_game" title="Bowl game">bowl game</a> played on December 21, 2016 at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qualcomm_Stadium" title="Qualcomm Stadium">Qualcomm Stadium</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Diego,_California" title="San Diego, California">San Diego, California</a>.  The twelfth and final edition of the <a href="/wiki/Poinsettia_Bowl" title="Poinsettia Bowl">Poinsettia Bowl</a> featured the <a href="/wiki/2016_BYU_Cougars_football_team" title="2016 BYU Cougars football team">BYU Cougars</a> against the <a href="/wiki/2016_Wyoming_Cowboys_football_team" title="2016 Wyoming Cowboys football team">Wyoming Cowboys</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>  It began at 6:00 PM <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Standard_Time_(North_America)" title="Pacific Standard Time (North America)">PST</a> and aired <a href="/wiki/ESPN" title="ESPN">ESPN</a>.  It was one of the <a href="/wiki/2016%E2%80%9317_NCAA_football_bowl_games" title="2016–17 NCAA football bowl games">2016–17 bowl games</a> concluding the <a href="/wiki/2016_NCAA_Division_I_FBS_football_season" title="2016 NCAA Division I FBS football season">2016 FBS football season</a>.  Sponsored by <a href="/wiki/San_Diego_County_Credit_Union" title="San Diego County Credit Union">San Diego County Credit Union</a>, the game was officially known as the <b>San Diego County Credit Union Poinsettia Bowl</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-byuaccepts_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-byuaccepts-7">[7]</a></sup> This was the final edition of the Poinsettia Bowl as the bowl's operator, the San Diego Bowl Game Association, elected to cancel the game to focus its efforts on the <a href="/wiki/Holiday_Bowl" title="Holiday Bowl">Holiday Bowl</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-End_of_Poinsetta_Bowl_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-End_of_Poinsetta_Bowl-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Teams">Teams</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Poinsettia_Bowl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Teams">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The game featured the <a href="/wiki/2016_BYU_Cougars_football_team" title="2016 BYU Cougars football team">BYU Cougars</a> against the <a href="/wiki/2016_Wyoming_Cowboys_football_team" title="2016 Wyoming Cowboys football team">Wyoming Cowboys</a>.
</p><p>This was the 78th meeting between the schools, with BYU leading the all-time series 44–30–3.  From 1922 until 2010, BYU and Wyoming had been in the same conference, being together in the <a href="/wiki/Rocky_Mountain_Athletic_Conference" title="Rocky Mountain Athletic Conference">Rocky Mountain Athletic Conference</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mountain_States_Conference" title="Mountain States Conference">Mountain States Conference</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Western_Athletic_Conference" title="Western Athletic Conference">Western Athletic Conference</a>, and the Mountain West Conference until BYU football went independent after 2010.
</p><p>The most recent meeting was on October 23, 2010, where the <a href="/wiki/2010_BYU_Cougars_football_team" title="2010 BYU Cougars football team">Cougars</a> defeated the <a href="/wiki/2010_Wyoming_Cowboys_football_team" title="2010 Wyoming Cowboys football team">Cowboys</a> by a score of 25–20.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="BYU_Cougars">BYU Cougars</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Poinsettia_Bowl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: BYU Cougars">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/2016_BYU_Cougars_football_team" title="2016 BYU Cougars football team">2016 BYU Cougars football team</a></div>
<p>On November 6, 2013, it was announced that BYU would participate in the 2016 and 2018 Poinsettia Bowls should they be bowl eligible and not selected for one of the <a href="/wiki/College_Football_Playoff" title="College Football Playoff">College Football Playoff</a> bowl games.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-byu1618_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-byu1618-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>After their 37–7 victory over <a href="/wiki/2016_Southern_Utah_Thunderbirds_football_team" title="2016 Southern Utah Thunderbirds football team">Southern Utah</a> (which improved their record to 6–4 and made them bowl-eligible), bowl director Ted Tollner (who, incidentally, served as quarterbacks coach at BYU for the 1981 season) extended an invitation for the Cougars to play in the game, which they accepted.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-poinsettiacougars_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-poinsettiacougars-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>This was the Cougars' second Poinsettia Bowl appearance, following <a href="/wiki/2012_BYU_Cougars_football_team" title="2012 BYU Cougars football team">their</a> victory over the <a href="/wiki/2012_San_Diego_State_Aztecs_football_team" title="2012 San Diego State Aztecs football team">San Diego State Aztecs</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2012_Poinsettia_Bowl" title="2012 Poinsettia Bowl">2012 Poinsettia Bowl</a> by a score of 23–6.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wyoming_Cowboys">Wyoming Cowboys</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Poinsettia_Bowl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Wyoming Cowboys">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/2016_Wyoming_Cowboys_football_team" title="2016 Wyoming Cowboys football team">2016 Wyoming Cowboys football team</a></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/2016_Wyoming_Cowboys_football_team" title="2016 Wyoming Cowboys football team">Wyoming Cowboys</a> became bowl eligible with an upset 30–28 win over the then-13th ranked <a href="/wiki/2016_Boise_State_Broncos_football_team" title="2016 Boise State Broncos football team">Boise State Broncos</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>  After starting the season 2–2, the Cowboys won five consecutive games.  They then had a 69–66 triple-overtime loss to UNLV en route to a 1–3 record in their final four games, one of which was a 27–24 loss to <a href="/wiki/2016_San_Diego_State_Aztecs_football_team" title="2016 San Diego State Aztecs football team">San Diego State</a> in the Mountain West Conference title game.
</p><p>This was the Cowboys' first Poinsettia Bowl appearance.
</p>

<p>The 2016 San Diego County Credit Union Poinsettia Bowl kicked off at 6:10 PT (9:10 ET)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> in the driving rain and was broadcast on <a href="/wiki/ESPN" title="ESPN">ESPN</a>. Both teams punted on their first drives; the first drives for both teams combined for just 43 yards (BYU's for 7 and Wyoming's for 36). The Cougars second drive ended abruptly after running back Jamaal Williams fumbled after 36- and 23-yard rushes to start the drive; Wyoming took over on their own 17 and went three-and-out and punted back to the Cougars, who took over on their own 43 and went three-and-out themselves. They punted back to Wyoming with 7:45 in the first quarter, who took over on their own 16. Wyoming's third drive ended in another three-and-out, and BYU started their fourth drive on their own 32. The first injury of the game came with 5:33 left in the first quarter when Wyoming safety Marcus Epps collided with BYU RB Jamaal Williams - Epps was able to walk off with little to no help from the trainers. BYU's fourth drive ended with a third punt, which was returned to the Wyoming 20. The Cowboys went three-and-out but the snap on the punt was fumbled and a Wyoming defender jumped on the ball on the Wyoming 3. It took 2 plays for BYU to find the end zone for the first points of the game; QB <a href="/wiki/Tanner_Mangum" title="Tanner Mangum">Tanner Mangum</a> scrambled for the 3-yard touchdown rush. The first quarter ended BYU 7, Wyoming 0 and the teams combined to go 0/7 on third downs in the first quarter.
</p><p>Wyoming's first drive of the second quarter ended in yet another punt; BYU's Logan Taele was injured on this drive, which was BYU's first injury of the game - he was carted off the field. BYU's second turnover came on their next drive when a Tanner Mangum pass was intercepted by Wyoming's Andrew Wingard. The Cowboys stalled on this drive and were set to attempt a 42-yard field goal when holder Nick Szpor fumbled the snap; he attempted to throw for the first down but the pass was batted away and BYU took over on downs at their own 25. BYU's next drive was their longest yet; they marched 66 yards down the field and upped their lead to 10 after kicker Rhett Almond converted a 27-yard field goal. Wyoming punted on their final drive of the half and the score at halftime was BYU 10, Wyoming 0, making it the first time all season Wyoming was shut out in the first half.
</p><p>BYU kicked off to Wyoming to start the third quarter and the Cowboys started on their own 40. UW's first drive of the second half featured two third down conversions and two fourth down conversions, and resulted in the Cowboys' first touchdown of the game, which cut the lead to 3. BYU's first drive of the second half saw them reach the red zone in five plays after starting at their own 43; the drive was capped by a touchdown pass that was batted around four times by both offensive and defensive players and eventually caught by BYU's Tanner Balderree in the back of the end zone. With 1:59 left in the 3rd, BYU defender Micah Hannemann was disqualified after getting called on a targeting penalty. On the very next play, Wyoming QB Josh Allen's pass was intercepted and returned by BYU's Dayan Lake to the BYU 45. The third quarter ended one play later, with the score BYU 17, Wyoming 7.
</p><p>BYU started the fourth quarter with the ball on the UW 36, up by ten - two plays later, they found the end zone to up their lead to 17. The Cowboys' first drive of the fourth quarter started on their own 24, included four first downs, and resulted in a Wyoming touchdown. BYU's next drive resulted in a three-and-out, and UW got the ball back at their own 19. Wyoming's next drive was highlighted by a 3rd &amp; 20 conversion and a touchdown pass on 3rd &amp; 4 to pull the Cowboys back within 3. Wyoming kicked off to BYU rather than attempting an onside kick, and after a late hit penalty was inforced, BYU started their drive on their own 16. That drive went for a total for -6 yards and BYU punted back to Wyoming, who started their potential game-winning drive on their own 49 with 1:44 left in the game. The first play of the UW drive was an Allen to Hill pass for 19 yards and a first down; the next play saw a Josh Allen pass downfield intercepted by BYU's Kai Nacua to end the game.
</p><p>With the final snap, BYU became the 2016 <a href="/wiki/Poinsettia_Bowl" title="Poinsettia Bowl">Poinsettia Bowl</a> champion, with the final score BYU 24, Wyoming 21.
</p>
