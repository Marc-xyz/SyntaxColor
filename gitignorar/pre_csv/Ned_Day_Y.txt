<p><b>Edward Gately "Ned" Day, Jr.</b> (April 5, 1945 – September 3, 1987) was an American <a href="/wiki/Journalism" title="Journalism">journalist</a> and newspaper reporter who was known for taking on mobsters who dominated Las Vegas <a href="/wiki/Casino" title="Casino">casinos</a> in the 1970s and '80s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Presence_still_felt_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Presence_still_felt-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ned_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Day's father, also named Ned Day, was a professional bowler.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> After several attempts to follow his father's career path, the younger Day moved to Las Vegas in the mid-1970s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ned_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After moving to Las Vegas, Day began working as a reporter for the now-closed North Las Vegas <i><a href="/wiki/Valley_Times_(North_Las_Vegas)" title="Valley Times (North Las Vegas)">Valley Times</a></i> newspaper. He later wrote columns for the <i><a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Review-Journal" title="Las Vegas Review-Journal">Las Vegas Review-Journal</a></i>. In the late 1970s, he became the managing editor and a reporter for <a href="/wiki/KLAS-TV" title="KLAS-TV">KLAS-TV</a>. His trademark on-air signoff was, "I thought you'd like to know, I'm Ned Day."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1986, Day's car was bombed (Day was not in it, only his golf clubs). Day reportedly described it as "the happiest day in my life, when the mob firebombed my car."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Presence_still_felt_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Presence_still_felt-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>On September 3, 1987, Day died on vacation while <a href="/wiki/Snorkeling" title="Snorkeling">snorkeling</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a>, at the age of 42.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The coroner's office ruled it a natural death from a <a href="/wiki/Myocardial_infarction" title="Myocardial infarction">heart attack</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
