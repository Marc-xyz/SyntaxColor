<p><i><b>Erigeron procumbens</b></i> is a North American species of flowering plant in the <a href="/wiki/Asteraceae" title="Asteraceae">daisy family</a> known by the common name <b>Corpus Christi fleabane</b>, the name referring to a coastal city in Texas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sarah_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-sarah-2">[2]</a></sup> The species grows along the <a href="/wiki/Coastal_plain" title="Coastal plain">coastal plain</a> and <a href="/wiki/Coastal_Strand" title="Coastal Strand">coastal strand</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a>, in the states of <a href="/wiki/Veracruz" title="Veracruz">Veracruz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tamaulipas" title="Tamaulipas">Tamaulipas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">Mississippi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Erigeron procumbens</i> is a perennial herb up to 40 centimeters (16 inches) tall, the stems very often procumbent (lying on the ground instead of growing straight up). The plant generally produces only one <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flower_head" title="Flower head">flower head</a> per stem. Each head has 225â350 pink or white <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ray_florets" title="Ray florets">ray florets</a> surrounding numerous yellow <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disc_floret" title="Disc floret">disc florets</a>. The species grows in wet depressions between sand dunes as well as on roadsides, and on the edges of mudflats and salt marshes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sarah_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-sarah-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
