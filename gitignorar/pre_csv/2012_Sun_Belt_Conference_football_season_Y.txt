<p>The <b>2012 Sun Belt Conference football season</b> was a <a href="/wiki/College_football" title="College football">college football</a> season for the <a href="/wiki/Sun_Belt_Conference" title="Sun Belt Conference">Sun Belt Conference</a> that ran from 30 August to 1 December. Ten teams participated in the competition: <a href="/wiki/2012_Arkansas_State_Red_Wolves_football_team" title="2012 Arkansas State Red Wolves football team">Arkansas State</a>, <a href="/wiki/2012_Florida_Atlantic_Owls_football_team" title="2012 Florida Atlantic Owls football team">Florida Atlantic</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2012_FIU_Golden_Panthers_football_team" title="2012 FIU Golden Panthers football team">Florida International</a>, <a href="/wiki/2012_Louisiana%E2%80%93Lafayette_Ragin%27_Cajuns_football_team" title="2012 Louisiana–Lafayette Ragin' Cajuns football team">Louisiana–Lafayette</a>, <a href="/wiki/2012_Louisiana%E2%80%93Monroe_Warhawks_football_team" title="2012 Louisiana–Monroe Warhawks football team">Louisiana–Monroe</a>, <a href="/wiki/2012_Middle_Tennessee_Blue_Raiders_football_team" title="2012 Middle Tennessee Blue Raiders football team">Middle Tennessee State</a>, <a href="/wiki/2012_North_Texas_Mean_Green_football_team" title="2012 North Texas Mean Green football team">North Texas</a>, <a href="/wiki/2012_South_Alabama_Jaguars_football_team" title="2012 South Alabama Jaguars football team">South Alabama</a>, <a href="/wiki/2012_Troy_Trojans_football_team" title="2012 Troy Trojans football team">Troy</a> and  <a href="/wiki/2012_WKU_Hilltoppers_football_team" title="2012 WKU Hilltoppers football team">Western Kentucky</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/South_Alabama_Jaguars_football" title="South Alabama Jaguars football">South Alabama</a> competed in the Sun Belt for the first time and became the conference's tenth team. Due to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NCAA" title="NCAA">NCAA</a> transitional rules, South Alabama was not eligible for a conference championship or postseason play.
</p>

<p>The Sun Belt media day was held on 16 July, in New Orleans, Louisiana. Florida International received five first place votes and were selected by the coaches as the favorites to win the conference.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Arkansas State and Louisiana-Lafayette each received two first place votes, while Western Kentucky received one.
