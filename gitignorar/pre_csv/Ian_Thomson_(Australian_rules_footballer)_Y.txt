<p><b>Ian Peter Thomson</b> (born 6 September 1949) is a former <a href="/wiki/Australian_rules_football" title="Australian rules football">Australian rules footballer</a> who played with <a href="/wiki/Sydney_Swans" title="Sydney Swans">South Melbourne</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Football_League" title="Australian Football League">Victorian Football League</a> (VFL). He also played for <a href="/wiki/East_Perth_Football_Club" title="East Perth Football Club">East Perth</a> and <a href="/wiki/East_Fremantle_Football_Club" title="East Fremantle Football Club">East Fremantle</a> in the <a href="/wiki/West_Australian_Football_League" title="West Australian Football League">West Australian Football League</a> (WAFL).
</p><p>Thomson, a key position player, started his career at East Perth in 1967.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-East_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-East-2">[2]</a></sup> He represented <a href="/wiki/Western_Australia_Australian_rules_football_team" title="Western Australia Australian rules football team">Western Australia</a> in an interstate match against Victoria at <a href="/wiki/Subiaco_Oval" title="Subiaco Oval">Subiaco Oval</a> on 17 July 1971.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Before the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/1973_VFL_season" title="1973 VFL season">1973 VFL season</a>, Thomson was in talks to join <a href="/wiki/Fitzroy_Football_Club" title="Fitzroy Football Club">Fitzroy</a>, but was signed by South Melbourne.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Swans_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Swans-4">[4]</a></sup> He played 20 games in his first year, as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Centre_half-back" title="Centre half-back">centre half-back</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Swans_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Swans-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AFLTables_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-AFLTables-5">[5]</a></sup> In 1974 he appeared in all 22 rounds and took a club high 120 marks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AFLTables_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-AFLTables-5">[5]</a></sup> Used up forward for much of the year, Thomson also kicked 24 goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AFLTables_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-AFLTables-5">[5]</a></sup> He broke an ankle bone in a 1975 pre-season practice match, which kept him out of the side until round five, then towards the end of the season suffered a knee injury, which restricted him to 13 games that season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AFLTables_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-AFLTables-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The next year he played 19 games and had a six goal haul against <a href="/wiki/St_Kilda_Football_Club" title="St Kilda Football Club">St Kilda</a> at <a href="/wiki/Moorabbin_Oval" title="Moorabbin Oval">Moorabbin Oval</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AFLTables_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-AFLTables-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1977 and 1978, Thomson was back at East Perth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-East_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-East-2">[2]</a></sup> He was only playing suburban football early in the 1979 season when he got recruited by East Fremantle, where he was used as a forward.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Swans_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Swans-4">[4]</a></sup> It was at <a href="/wiki/Full-forward" title="Full-forward">full-forward</a> that he kicked four goals for East Fremantle in their <a href="/wiki/1979_WANFL_Grand_Final" title="1979 WANFL Grand Final">1979 WANFL Grand Final</a> win over <a href="/wiki/South_Fremantle_Football_Club" title="South Fremantle Football Club">South Fremantle</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> With prolific goal scoring team mate <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Taylor_(Australian_footballer)" title="Kevin Taylor (Australian footballer)">Kevin Taylor</a> at South Melbourne, Thomson topped East Fremantle's goal-kicking in 1981.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
