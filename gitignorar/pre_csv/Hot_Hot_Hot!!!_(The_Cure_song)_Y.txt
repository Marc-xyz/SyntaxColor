<p>"<b>Hot Hot Hot!!!</b>" is the name of a 1988 <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">single</a> by British <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> band <a href="/wiki/The_Cure" title="The Cure">The Cure</a> from their album <i><a href="/wiki/Kiss_Me,_Kiss_Me,_Kiss_Me" title="Kiss Me, Kiss Me, Kiss Me">Kiss Me, Kiss Me, Kiss Me</a></i>. The song reached number 45 in the UK whereas it was more successful in Ireland, where it reached number 18, and in Spain, entering the Top 10.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hot_Hot_Hot!!!_(The_Cure_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Hot Hot Hot!!!" was the fourth single released from the album <i>Kiss Me, Kiss Me, Kiss Me</i>—the band's seventh LP. In early 1988, it spent three weeks in the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>, peaking at number 45 on 20 February of that year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In the United States, the song reached number 68 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>, while a remix of the track by <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Kevorkian" title="François Kevorkian">François Kevorkian</a> charted at numbers 11 and 50 on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_Dance_Club_Songs" title="Hot Dance Club Songs">Dance Music/Club Play Singles</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Dance/Electronic_Singles_Sales" title="Dance/Electronic Singles Sales">Hot Dance Music/Maxi-Singles Sales</a> charts, respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> It reached its highest chart position in Spain, peaking at number 8; the song was also a hit in Ireland, spending two weeks there and making number 18 on 18 February 1988.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> It spent three weeks on the Dutch charts, reaching number 79 there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> As with many other singles, the video was directed by <a href="/wiki/Tim_Pope" title="Tim Pope">Tim Pope</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and has been described as "intentionally ridiculous".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-amcps_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-amcps-8">[8]</a></sup> Filmed in black and white, it featured the band as "dwarves" dressed in 1950's clothes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hot_Hot_Hot!!!_(The_Cure_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In a review of the single for <i><a href="/wiki/NME" title="NME">NME</a></i> in 1988, <a href="/wiki/Steve_Lamacq" title="Steve Lamacq">Steve Lamacq</a> said that the song's dance mix was "spuriously welcoming, but basically a tragedy of trenchfoot" and concluded, "Even I know [Smith has] better stuff hidden in that mop of his".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In an undated review, Stewart Mason of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Allmusic" title="Allmusic">Allmusic</a> described the song as the "weakest" of the singles from the album, adding that it has markedly dated and criticised both Smith's lyrics and his vocal performance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-masonam_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-masonam-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Re-recordings_and_cover_versions">Re-recordings and cover versions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hot_Hot_Hot!!!_(The_Cure_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Re-recordings and cover versions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song appears on disc two of the live album <i><a href="/wiki/Bestival_Live_2011" title="Bestival Live 2011">Bestival Live 2011</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Trivia">Trivia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hot_Hot_Hot!!!_(The_Cure_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Trivia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Before the song starts, Robert Smith sings the first lines of <a href="/wiki/She_(Charles_Aznavour_song)" title="She (Charles Aznavour song)">She</a> by <a href="/wiki/Charles_Aznavour" title="Charles Aznavour">Charles Aznavour</a>, <i>"She may be the face I can't forget(...)"</i>.
</p>
