<p><i><b>Kane no naru oka: Dai-san hen, kuro no maki</b></i><span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">鐘の鳴る丘 第三篇クロの巻</span><span style="display:none">,</span> <i>Slope of the Ringing Bell Hill: the third volume</i>)</span> is  <a href="/wiki/1949_in_film" title="1949 in film">1949</a> <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> film and the final installment of a wartime film trilogy, directed by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Keisuke_Sasaki&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Keisuke Sasaki (page does not exist)">Keisuke Sasaki</a>, featuring the cast from the previous two <i>Ringing Bell Hill</i> films.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production_Notes">Production Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kane_no_naru_oka:_Dai_san_hen,_kuro_no_maki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Production Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The success of the <i>Ringing Bell Hill</i> trilogy spawned a weekend radio series of 15-minute episodes, produced by NHK, featuring further adventures of a demobilized soldier and a group of war orphans under his care on Ringing Bell Hill.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<li><i>鐘の鳴る丘 第二篇 修吉の巻</i> (Kane no naru oka: dai-ni hen, shūkichi no maki; Slope of the Ringing Bell Hill: the second volume) (1949)</li>
<li><i>鐘の鳴る丘 第三篇 クロの巻</i> (Kane no naru oka: dai-san hen, kuro no maki; Slope of the Ringing Bell Hill: the third volume) (1949)</li></ol>
