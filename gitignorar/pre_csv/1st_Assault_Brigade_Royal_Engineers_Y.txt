<p>The <b>1st Assault Brigade Royal Engineers</b> was a specialisied armoured formation of the <a href="/wiki/British_Army" title="British Army">British Army</a> active in World War II. It was formed in mid-1943 and its structure was three <b>Assault Regiments</b> of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Engineers" title="Royal Engineers">Royal Engineers</a>. It was assigned to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/79th_Armoured_Division" title="79th Armoured Division">79th Armoured Division</a> in preparation for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Normandy_invasion" title="Normandy invasion">Normandy invasion</a> of 6 June 1944. The unit comprised armoured vehicles modified for specialist roles (also known as <a href="/wiki/Hobart%27s_Funnies" title="Hobart's Funnies">Hobart's Funnies</a>), intended to assist with the landing phase of the operation.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1st_Assault_Brigade_Royal_Engineers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:AVRE-Bobbin.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="796" data-file-width="800" decoding="async" height="299" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/AVRE-Bobbin.jpg/300px-AVRE-Bobbin.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/AVRE-Bobbin.jpg/450px-AVRE-Bobbin.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/AVRE-Bobbin.jpg/600px-AVRE-Bobbin.jpg 2x" width="300"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:AVRE-Bobbin.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Churchill AVRE with a bobbin.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Churchill_AVRE_Petard.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="451" data-file-width="800" decoding="async" height="169" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/Churchill_AVRE_Petard.jpg/300px-Churchill_AVRE_Petard.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/Churchill_AVRE_Petard.jpg/450px-Churchill_AVRE_Petard.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/Churchill_AVRE_Petard.jpg/600px-Churchill_AVRE_Petard.jpg 2x" width="300"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Churchill_AVRE_Petard.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Close-up of an AVRE's Petard Mortar.</div></div></div>
<p>By the end of October 1943, various engineer units had been renamed and transferred into the Brigade. This brigade comprised 3 Assault Regiments, each with 4 Assault Squadrons, plus an Assault Park Squadron. This structure remained unchanged up to the Normandy landings in June 1944.
</p><p>The basic vehicle of the assault unit was the Assault Vehicle Royal Engineers (AVREs). This was a Churchill tank with its turret modified or removed and to which were attached certain special equipments.
</p><p>The Brigade was employed during the assault landings in Normandy, <a href="/wiki/Operation_Overlord" title="Operation Overlord">Operation Overlord</a> and further used during the battle for the Roer Triangle (<a href="/wiki/Operation_Blackcock" title="Operation Blackcock">Operation Blackcock</a>), the Rhine crossings (<i><a href="/wiki/Operation_Plunder" title="Operation Plunder">Operation Plunder</a></i>) and the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Elbe_crossing&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Elbe crossing (page does not exist)">Elbe crossing</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Equipment">Equipment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1st_Assault_Brigade_Royal_Engineers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Equipment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Armoured_Vehicle_Royal_Engineers" title="Armoured Vehicle Royal Engineers">AVRE</a></b> - <i>Assault Vehicle Royal Engineers</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> was a Churchill tank adapted to attack German defensive fortifications. The crew included two Royal Engineers who could easily enter the tank through its side hatches. The AVRE had the main gun replaced by a <a href="/wiki/Petard" title="Petard">Petard</a> <a href="/wiki/Mortar_(weapon)#Spigot_mortar" title="Mortar (weapon)">Spigot Mortar</a>. This fired a forty-pound (18 kg) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/High_explosive" title="High explosive">HE</a>-filled projectile (nicknamed the <i>Flying Dustbin</i>) 150 yards (137 metres). The "Dustbin" could destroy concrete obstacles such as roadblocks and <a href="/wiki/Bunker" title="Bunker">bunkers</a>. This weapon was unusual in that it had to be reloaded externally - by opening a hatch and sliding a round into the mortar tube from the hull. AVREs were also used to carry and operate equipment such as:
<ul><li><b>Bobbin</b> - A reel of 10-foot-wide (3.0 m) canvas cloth reinforced with steel poles carried in front of the tank and unrolled onto the ground to form a "path", so that following vehicles (and itself) would not sink into the soft ground of the beaches during the <a href="/wiki/Amphibious_warfare" title="Amphibious warfare">amphibious landing</a>.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Fascine" title="Fascine">Fascine</a></b> - A bundle of wooden poles or rough brushwood lashed together with wires carried in front of the tank that could be released to fill a ditch or form a step. Metal pipes in the center of the fascine allowed water to flow through.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Small_Box_Girder" title="Small Box Girder">Small Box Girder</a></b> was an assault bridge that was carried in front of the tank and could be dropped to span a 30-foot (9.1 m) gap in 30 seconds.</li>
<li><b>Skid Bailey</b> was a larger assault bridge made of normal <a href="/wiki/Bailey_bridge" title="Bailey bridge">Bailey bridge</a> on skids. This was towed and then pushed by an AVRE over gaps up-to 80-foot (24 m).</li>
<li><b>Bullshorn Plough</b>. A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mine_plough" title="Mine plough">mine plough</a> intended to excavate the ground in front of the tank, to expose and make harmless any <a href="/wiki/Land_mine" title="Land mine">land mines</a>.</li>
<li><b>Double Onion</b> two large demolition charges on a metal frame that could be placed against a concrete wall and then detonated from a safe distance. It was the successor to the single charge <b>Carrot</b> device.</li>
<li><b>Snake</b> was a pipe based mine clearance device carried on the trackguards. The pipes were filled with explosive, propelled across the field, and then detonated.</li></ul></li>
<li><b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Churchill_Ark" title="Churchill Ark">ARK</a></b> Armoured Ramp Carrier was a Churchill tank modified with the turret removed and pivoting ramps fitted fore and aft. The vehicle was driven into gaps and ditches where the ramps were deployed. The vehicle itself then formed a bridge over which other vehicles could pass.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Armored_bulldozer" title="Armored bulldozer">D7 (Armd) Dozer</a></b> was a normal bulldozer fitted with light armour to protect crew members during beach operations.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Cromwell_tank" title="Cromwell tank">Centaur Dozer</a></b> was a larger fully armoured bulldozer built on the hull of the Centaur (Cromwell) tank.</li>
<li><b>Conger</b> was a hose based mine clearance device built in the hull of a <a href="/wiki/Universal_Carrier" title="Universal Carrier">Universal Carrier</a>. This had no engine; it was towed by another vehicle. The hose was propelled across the field by rocket, and then liquid high explosive pumped through and detonated.</li>
<li><b>Buffalo</b> was an amphibious <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LVT4" title="LVT4">LVT4</a> tracked vehicle used by 5 Regiment for inland water crossings.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Terrapin_(amphibious_vehicle)" title="Terrapin (amphibious vehicle)">Terrapin</a></b> was a wheeled amphibious vehicle used with Buffalo.</li></ul>
<h2><span id="D-Day_.2C_Operation_Overlord"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="D-Day_,_Operation_Overlord">D-Day , Operation Overlord</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1st_Assault_Brigade_Royal_Engineers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: D-Day , Operation Overlord">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Normandy_Landings" title="Normandy Landings">Normandy Landings</a></div>
<p>Each of the three Infantry Divisions involved in the first assault on the British and Canadian front - <a href="/wiki/Gold_Beach" title="Gold Beach">Gold Beach</a>, <a href="/wiki/Juno_Beach" title="Juno Beach">Juno Beach</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sword_Beach" title="Sword Beach">Sword Beach</a> - included two Assault Squadrons from <b>5 Assault Regiment</b> and <b>6 Assault Regiment</b>, Royal Engineers.
</p><p>The objective of each pair of Assault Squadrons was to clear lanes across the beaches and to establish up to eight exits from the beaches onto the first inland road, suitable for tracked vehicles. This involved:
</p>
<ul><li>removing beach obstacles, which were exposed at low tide, so that they were not a hazard as the tide rose.</li>
<li>disarming and removing mines and other explosive charges from these obstacles</li>
<li>breaching the sea wall, where one existed</li>
<li>creating and maintaining ramps and firm access routes through the soft sand above the high water line</li>
<li>removing knocked-out vehicles that were obstructing these exits</li></ul>
<p>All this had to be done under fire from buildings on the seafront, and under mortar and artillery fire from further inland.
</p><p>Each Assault Squadron was equipped with a number of Armoured Vehicles Royal Engineers (<a href="/wiki/Armoured_Vehicle_Royal_Engineers" title="Armoured Vehicle Royal Engineers">AVREs</a>) - which were <a href="/wiki/Churchill_tank" title="Churchill tank">Churchill tanks</a>, modified in various ways to suit the needs of Assault Engineers. In addition, the assault teams included a number of <a href="/wiki/M4_Sherman" title="M4 Sherman">Sherman</a> 'Crabs' from <a href="/wiki/30th_Armoured_Brigade" title="30th Armoured Brigade">30th Armoured Brigade</a> - these were Sherman tanks, with a full-width, heavy duty, rotary chain flail mounted in front, which destroyed any mines in its path, thus clearing the lanes up the beach.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:D-day_-_British_Forces_during_the_Invasion_of_Normandy_6_June_1944_B5095.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="800" data-file-width="798" decoding="async" height="301" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/D-day_-_British_Forces_during_the_Invasion_of_Normandy_6_June_1944_B5095.jpg/300px-D-day_-_British_Forces_during_the_Invasion_of_Normandy_6_June_1944_B5095.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/D-day_-_British_Forces_during_the_Invasion_of_Normandy_6_June_1944_B5095.jpg/450px-D-day_-_British_Forces_during_the_Invasion_of_Normandy_6_June_1944_B5095.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/D-day_-_British_Forces_during_the_Invasion_of_Normandy_6_June_1944_B5095.jpg/600px-D-day_-_British_Forces_during_the_Invasion_of_Normandy_6_June_1944_B5095.jpg 2x" width="300"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:D-day_-_British_Forces_during_the_Invasion_of_Normandy_6_June_1944_B5095.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Medics are attending to wounded in the shelter of a Churchill AVRE from 5th Assault Regiment, Royal Engineers - Sword Beach, 6 June 1944</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:D-day_-_British_Forces_during_the_Invasion_of_Normandy_6_June_1944_B5040.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1772" data-file-width="1799" decoding="async" height="295" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/D-day_-_British_Forces_during_the_Invasion_of_Normandy_6_June_1944_B5040.jpg/300px-D-day_-_British_Forces_during_the_Invasion_of_Normandy_6_June_1944_B5040.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/D-day_-_British_Forces_during_the_Invasion_of_Normandy_6_June_1944_B5040.jpg/450px-D-day_-_British_Forces_during_the_Invasion_of_Normandy_6_June_1944_B5040.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/D-day_-_British_Forces_during_the_Invasion_of_Normandy_6_June_1944_B5040.jpg/600px-D-day_-_British_Forces_during_the_Invasion_of_Normandy_6_June_1944_B5040.jpg 2x" width="300"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:D-day_-_British_Forces_during_the_Invasion_of_Normandy_6_June_1944_B5040.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Universal carriers with deep wading screens pass through Lion sur Mer. A Churchill AVRE can be seen in the background, 6 June 1944.</div></div></div>
<p>The AVREs then turned their attention to the first line of land defences, using their spigot mortars, and with self-propelled guns providing artillery support. More Sappers landed to continue the clearance of the beaches. All the time, infantry were moving through into the growing bridgehead.
</p><p>Several exits were open within an hour of the first landings, allowing the Armoured Brigades to start moving inland.
</p><p>The Assault Regiments suffered their share of casualties. In some sectors, up to 50% of the flail tanks, AVREs and armoured bulldozers had been knocked out, with high casualties among the crews. But many of the objectives for D-Day had been achieved, which may not have been the case without 1st Assault Brigade R.E.
</p><p>After Normandy, the Brigade was used in <a href="/wiki/Operation_Blackcock" title="Operation Blackcock">Operation Blackcock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Operation_Infatuate" title="Operation Infatuate">Operation Infatuate</a> and <a href="/wiki/Operation_Plunder" title="Operation Plunder">Operation Plunder</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Assault_Engineers_after_the_War">Assault Engineers after the War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1st_Assault_Brigade_Royal_Engineers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Assault Engineers after the War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Disbanding_of_1st_Assault_Brigade">Disbanding of 1st Assault Brigade</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1st_Assault_Brigade_Royal_Engineers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Disbanding of 1st Assault Brigade">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the end of the Second World War, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/79th_Armoured_Division" title="79th Armoured Division">79th Armoured Division</a> was disbanded in August 1945. The remaining Assault Engineer Regiment was disbanded in July 1957.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reformation_as_32_Armoured_Engineer_Regiment">Reformation as 32 Armoured Engineer Regiment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1st_Assault_Brigade_Royal_Engineers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Reformation as 32 Armoured Engineer Regiment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The unit was briefly reformed as 32 Armoured Engineer Regiment in 1964 before a further reorganisation in 1969 reduced the Armoured Engineers to one squadron.
</p><p>In 1980, 32 Armoured Engineer Regiment was again reformed and was the largest tank regiment in the British Army. The Regiment was the only unit equipped with specialist Sapper tanks and heir to the traditions of past assault and armoured engineers. The regimental crest included the bull's head device of 79th Armoured Division.
</p><p>32 Armoured Engineer Regiment had three squadrons each of four troops, a total of 72 tanks. Each troop had three <a href="/wiki/Armoured_Vehicle_Royal_Engineers" title="Armoured Vehicle Royal Engineers">AVREs</a> and three <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AVLB" title="AVLB">AVLBs</a> (bridge layers).  These were more modern types, evolved from the original Churchill, but based on the newer Centurion and Chieftain tanks.
</p><p>As of 2008, Engineer Regiments attached to Armoured or Mechanised Brigades in the <a href="/wiki/British_Army" title="British Army">British Army</a> have Armoured Engineer Squadrons included within their organisation.
</p>
