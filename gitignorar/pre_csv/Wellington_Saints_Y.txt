<p>The <b>Wellington Saints</b> are a New Zealand <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a> team based in <a href="/wiki/Wellington" title="Wellington">Wellington</a>. The Saints compete in the <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_League_(New_Zealand)" title="National Basketball League (New Zealand)">National Basketball League</a> (NBL) and play their home games at <a href="/wiki/TSB_Bank_Arena" title="TSB Bank Arena">TSB Bank Arena</a>. For sponsorship reasons, they are known as the <b>Cigna Saints</b>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Team_history">Team history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wellington_Saints&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Team history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Wellington Saints were founded in 1981. In 1982, the <b>Exchequer Saints</b> won the <a href="/wiki/Conference_Basketball_League" title="Conference Basketball League">second division</a> title, which promoted them to the <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_League_(New_Zealand)" title="National Basketball League (New Zealand)">first division</a> for the <a href="/wiki/1983_New_Zealand_NBL_season" title="1983 New Zealand NBL season">1983 season</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The Saints went on to play in six straight championship games upon joining the NBL, winning titles in <a href="/wiki/1984_New_Zealand_NBL_season" title="1984 New Zealand NBL season">1984</a>, <a href="/wiki/1985_New_Zealand_NBL_season" title="1985 New Zealand NBL season">1985</a>, <a href="/wiki/1987_New_Zealand_NBL_season" title="1987 New Zealand NBL season">1987</a> and <a href="/wiki/1988_New_Zealand_NBL_season" title="1988 New Zealand NBL season">1988</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> They played in their seventh final in <a href="/wiki/1991_New_Zealand_NBL_season" title="1991 New Zealand NBL season">1991</a>, where they lost to the <a href="/wiki/Hutt_Valley_Lakers" title="Hutt Valley Lakers">Hutt Valley Lakers</a>.
</p><p>At the end of the <a href="/wiki/1996_New_Zealand_NBL_season" title="1996 New Zealand NBL season">1996 season</a>, both the Saints and the Lakers were struggling to survive. They amalgamated for the <a href="/wiki/1997_New_Zealand_NBL_season" title="1997 New Zealand NBL season">1997 season</a>, becoming TransAlta Wellington. With fans wanting a 'real' name, for the <a href="/wiki/1998_New_Zealand_NBL_season" title="1998 New Zealand NBL season">1998 season</a>, they became the TransAlta Wizards. For the <a href="/wiki/1999_New_Zealand_NBL_season" title="1999 New Zealand NBL season">1999 season</a>, they returned to the original identity of Saints.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The Saints returned to title contenders in the 2000s, as they finished runners-up in <a href="/wiki/2001_New_Zealand_NBL_season" title="2001 New Zealand NBL season">2001</a> and <a href="/wiki/2008_New_Zealand_NBL_season" title="2008 New Zealand NBL season">2008</a>, and won their fifth title in <a href="/wiki/2003_New_Zealand_NBL_season" title="2003 New Zealand NBL season">2003</a>.
</p><p>The 2010s were dominated by the Saints, as they played in every championship game between 2010 and 2019, except 2013. They claimed back-to-back championships in <a href="/wiki/2010_New_Zealand_NBL_season" title="2010 New Zealand NBL season">2010</a> and <a href="/wiki/2011_New_Zealand_NBL_season" title="2011 New Zealand NBL season">2011</a>, before claiming their eighth title in <a href="/wiki/2014_New_Zealand_NBL_season" title="2014 New Zealand NBL season">2014</a>. With their ninth title in <a href="/wiki/2016_New_Zealand_NBL_season" title="2016 New Zealand NBL season">2016</a>, the Saints matched the <a href="/wiki/Auckland_Stars" title="Auckland Stars">Auckland Stars</a> for most championships in NBL history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/2017_New_Zealand_NBL_season" title="2017 New Zealand NBL season">2017</a>, the Saints made NBL history by becoming the first team to complete a perfect regular season, going 18–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> They went on to defeat the <a href="/wiki/Canterbury_Rams" title="Canterbury Rams">Canterbury Rams</a> in the semi-finals before becoming the first team to complete an unbeaten season, capping off a perfect 20–0 campaign by beating the <a href="/wiki/Southland_Sharks" title="Southland Sharks">Southland Sharks</a> 108–75 in the final. Their tenth championship set a new record for most in NBL history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The Saints returned to the final in <a href="/wiki/2018_New_Zealand_NBL_season" title="2018 New Zealand NBL season">2018</a>, but missed the chance at their first three-peat with a 98–96 loss to the Sharks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/2019_New_Zealand_NBL_season" title="2019 New Zealand NBL season">2019</a>, the Saints completed their second undefeated regular season in three years, going 18–0,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> before completing another perfect 20–0 campaign by defeating the <a href="/wiki/Hawke%27s_Bay_Hawks" title="Hawke's Bay Hawks">Hawke's Bay Hawks</a> 78–68 in the final to win their 11th title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="CBL_team">CBL team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wellington_Saints&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: CBL team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Saints began in the second-tiered <a href="/wiki/Conference_Basketball_League" title="Conference Basketball League">Conference Basketball League</a> (CBL), winning the CBL title in 1982. In 1997, Wellington's second division team finished as runners-up in the CBL, and in 2001, the Wellington College Saints finished as CBL runners-up.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In 2008, the Saints' second division team won the CBL championship with a 90–84 win over the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Waikato_Titans" title="Waikato Titans">Waikato Titans</a> in the final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>
</p>
