<p><b>Gunnar Atle Pettersen</b> (born 30 July 1955) is a <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norwegian</a> former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Team_handball" title="Team handball">team handball</a> player, and a former head coach of the <a href="/wiki/Norway_national_handball_team" title="Norway national handball team">Norwegian national team</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Playing_career">Playing career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gunnar_Pettersen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Playing career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pettersen played 132 matches and scored 445 goals for the <a href="/wiki/Norway_national_handball_team" title="Norway national handball team">Norway national handball team</a> between 1978 and 1987.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nhf_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-nhf-1">[1]</a></sup> He has his education from the <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_School_of_Sport_Sciences" title="Norwegian School of Sport Sciences">Norwegian School of Sport Sciences</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coaching_career">Coaching career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gunnar_Pettersen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Coaching career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He coached the national team from 1989 to 1994, and again from 2001 to 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-handball-no_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-handball-no-3">[3]</a></sup> His best international achievements are the 7th place at the <a href="/wiki/2005_World_Men%27s_Handball_Championship" title="2005 World Men's Handball Championship">2005 World Men's Handball Championship</a> and the 6th place at the <a href="/wiki/2008_European_Men%27s_Handball_Championship" title="2008 European Men's Handball Championship">2008 European Championships</a>. After securing Norway a place in the <a href="/wiki/2009_World_Men%27s_Handball_Championship" title="2009 World Men's Handball Championship">2009 World Championship</a>, he announced that he would not continue as the national team head coach when his contract ran out, 313 October 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2008_European_Championships">2008 European Championships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gunnar_Pettersen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2008 European Championships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Pettersen's team won the preliminary group B of the <a href="/wiki/2008_European_Men%27s_Handball_Championship" title="2008 European Men's Handball Championship">2008 European Men's Handball Championship</a> and qualified for the Main group, after beating later champions <a href="/wiki/Denmark_national_handball_team" title="Denmark national handball team">Denmark</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia_national_handball_team" title="Russia national handball team">Russia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Montenegro_national_handball_team" title="Montenegro national handball team">Montenegro</a>. After tying later silver medalists <a href="/wiki/Croatia_national_handball_team" title="Croatia national handball team">Croatia</a> in their last match in the Main group, the team missed the semifinals.
</p>
