	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Chilubi District</h1>

<p><b>Chilubi District</b> with headquarters at <a href="/wiki/Chilubi" title="Chilubi">Chilubi</a> is located in <a href="/wiki/Northern_Province,_Zambia" title="Northern Province, Zambia">Northern Province, Zambia</a>. It covers the north-east <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bangweulu_Lake&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bangweulu Lake (page does not exist)">Bangweulu Lake</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bangweulu_swamps&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bangweulu swamps (page does not exist)">Bangweulu swamps</a> containing several islands including <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Chilubi_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Chilubi Island (page does not exist)">Chilubi Island</a>, and some of the mainland northeast of the lake. As of the 2000 Zambian Census, the district had a population of 66,338 people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Like <a href="/wiki/Samfya_District" title="Samfya District">Samfya District</a> most of it is lake or swamp, and the rest rural and less developed than Samfya, lacking roads and infrastructure. The only road connections for its mainland part is a dirt road from <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Luwingu&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Luwingu (page does not exist)">Luwingu</a> in the north to <a href="/wiki/Mofu" title="Mofu">Mofu</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Chaba,_Zambia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Chaba, Zambia (page does not exist)">Chaba</a>. Swamps of the Lukulu, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lubansenshi_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lubansenshi River (page does not exist)">Lubansenshi</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Chambeshi_River" title="Chambeshi River">Chambeshi</a> rivers cut the district off on its eastern side.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Google_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Google-2">[2]</a></sup>  Channels have been cut through the swamps in many places to aid navigation by boat. Since they share the lake and swamps, geographically Chilubi District has more in common with Samfya District in Luapula Province than it does with the rest of Northern Province. Most travellers leaving the district go by boat to Nsombo then by gravel road to <a href="/wiki/Kasama,_Zambia" title="Kasama, Zambia">Kasama</a> via Luwingu, or by boat to Samfya then by all-weather road to <a href="/wiki/Mansa,_Zambia" title="Mansa, Zambia">Mansa</a> or <a href="/wiki/Serenje" title="Serenje">Serenje</a>.
</p><p>Other islands in Chilubi District include <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Chichile&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Chichile (page does not exist)">Chichile</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kasansa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kasansa (page does not exist)">Kasansa</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Panyo" title="Panyo">Panyo</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Luangwa_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Luangwa Island (page does not exist)">Luangwa</a>, all at the edges of the swamps where they dry out sufficiently in the dry season to form a connection to the mainland.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DCS_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-DCS-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Isangano_National_Park" title="Isangano National Park">Isangano National Park</a> lies in the east of the district but it has no management and most of the wildlife has been poached out.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Spectrum_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Spectrum-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
