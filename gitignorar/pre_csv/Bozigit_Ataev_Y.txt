<p><b>Bazigit Atavovich  Atajev</b> (<a href="/wiki/Kumyk_language" title="Kumyk language">Kumyk</a>: <span lang="kum">Bozigit Atay Atawnu ulanı</span>, <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Бозигит Атавович Атаев</span>) is a Russian <a href="/wiki/Mixed_martial_arts" title="Mixed martial arts">mixed martial artist</a> and <i><a href="/wiki/Sanshou" title="Sanshou">sanshou</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Kickboxing" title="Kickboxing">kickboxer</a> of Kumyk heritage . He has competed in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RINGS" title="RINGS">RINGS</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pride_Fighting_Championships" title="Pride Fighting Championships">Pride Fighting Championships</a>. He is known for a fierce spinning heel kick and <a href="/wiki/Sambo_(martial_art)" title="Sambo (martial art)">Sambo</a> style grappling. Atajev holds notable wins against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tsuyoshi_Kohsaka" title="Tsuyoshi Kohsaka">Tsuyoshi Kohsaka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roman_Zentsov" title="Roman Zentsov">Roman Zentsov</a>, Valdas Pocevicius, and <a href="/wiki/Aaron_Brink" title="Aaron Brink">Aaron Brink</a>. Atajev is currently fighting in the Light heavyweight of the <a href="/wiki/Professional_Fighters_League" title="Professional Fighters League">Professional Fighters League</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport_career">Sport career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bozigit_Ataev&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Sport career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Atajev debuted in <a href="/wiki/Pride_Fighting_Championships" title="Pride Fighting Championships">PRIDE</a> in December 2002 and suffered his only loss against Dutchman <a href="/wiki/Alistair_Overeem" title="Alistair Overeem">Alistair Overeem</a> at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pride_24" title="Pride 24">Pride 24</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Atajev planned to make his return to Pride at their final event <a href="/wiki/Pride_34" title="Pride 34">Pride 34</a> against <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_Yvel" title="Gilbert Yvel">Gilbert Yvel</a>. However, due to a stomach illness, the bout was cancelled.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2008, he faced the eventual K-1 champion <a href="/wiki/Remy_Bonjasky" title="Remy Bonjasky">Remy Bonjasky</a> in his kickboxing debut. He was defeated by knockout early in the third round.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>He won the gold medal at the <a href="/wiki/2013_World_Wushu_Championships" title="2013 World Wushu Championships">2013 World Wushu Championships</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_Fighters_League">Professional Fighters League</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bozigit_Ataev&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Professional Fighters League">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Atajev made his debut in North America on 21 June 2018 at <a href="/wiki/PFL_2_(2018_season)" title="PFL 2 (2018 season)">PFL 2</a> against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dan_Spohn" title="Dan Spohn">Dan Spohn</a> in Light heavyweight tournaments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He lost the fight by technical knockout in the third round.
</p><p>In his second fight in the tournament, Atajev faced <a href="/wiki/Sean_O%27Connell_(fighter)" title="Sean O'Connell (fighter)">Sean O'Connell</a> at <a href="/wiki/PFL_7_(2018_season)" title="PFL 7 (2018 season)">PFL 7</a> on August 30, 2018.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He won the fight via TKO in the first round.
</p>
