<p><b>Wojciech Kowalczyk</b> (born 14 April 1972) is a Polish retired <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who played as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Striker" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a>.
</p><p>The bulk of his professional career was spent with <a href="/wiki/Legia_Warsaw" title="Legia Warsaw">Legia Warsaw</a> and in <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>. Early into his international career, he helped the <a href="/wiki/Poland_national_football_team" title="Poland national football team">Polish national team</a> win <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_1992_Summer_Olympics" title="Football at the 1992 Summer Olympics">the silver medal</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1992_Summer_Olympics" title="1992 Summer Olympics">1992 Summer Olympics</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wojciech_Kowalczyk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Warsaw" title="Warsaw">Warsaw</a>, Kowalczyk played his youth football with local Olimpia and Polonez, joining city giants <a href="/wiki/Legia_Warsaw" title="Legia Warsaw">Legia Warsaw</a> in 1990 at the age of 18. Almost from the very beginning, he was an automatic first-choice; in his weakest <a href="/wiki/1990%E2%80%9391_Ekstraklasa" title="1990–91 Ekstraklasa">season, his first</a>, he only appeared in 11 <a href="/wiki/Ekstraklasa" title="Ekstraklasa">Ekstraklasa</a> games, but was crucial in helping oust <a href="/wiki/U.C._Sampdoria" title="U.C. Sampdoria">U.C. Sampdoria</a> in <a href="/wiki/1990%E2%80%9391_European_Cup_Winners%27_Cup" title="1990–91 European Cup Winners' Cup">the quarter-finals</a> of the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Cup_Winners%27_Cup" title="UEFA Cup Winners' Cup">UEFA Cup Winners' Cup</a> by scoring twice in <a href="/wiki/Genoa" title="Genoa">Genoa</a> for the 2–2 draw and qualification for the semifinals 3–2 on aggregate.
</p><p>After having started the <a href="/wiki/1994%E2%80%9395_Ekstraklasa" title="1994–95 Ekstraklasa">1994–95 campaign</a> with Legia (five matches, three goals, another league title), Kowalczyk moved abroad and signed with <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> side <a href="/wiki/Real_Betis" title="Real Betis">Real Betis</a>, but was never able to reproduce his previous form. He finished his five-year spell in Spain with <a href="/wiki/UD_Las_Palmas" title="UD Las Palmas">UD Las Palmas</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Segunda División">second division</a>, where he also appeared sparingly.
</p><p>After almost one year out of football, Kowalczyk returned to his country and his main club, Legia. In late 2001 he changed countries again, joining <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a>' <a href="/wiki/Anorthosis_Famagusta_FC" title="Anorthosis Famagusta FC">Anorthosis Famagusta FC</a> and netting a career-best 24 goals; after a nearly non-existent second season he finished his professional career in the same country, with <a href="/wiki/APOEL_FC" title="APOEL FC">APOEL FC</a>, retiring at 32 – afterwards, he would play in amateur football until 2006, with AZS Absolwent UW Warszawa.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wojciech_Kowalczyk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kowalczyk gained 39 <a href="/wiki/Cap_(sport)" title="Cap (sport)">caps</a> for <a href="/wiki/Poland_national_football_team" title="Poland national football team">Poland</a>, scoring 11 goals. His debut came at the age of 19 on 21 August 1991, against <a href="/wiki/Sweden_national_football_team" title="Sweden national football team">Sweden</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>His biggest international highlight was helping the Olympic squad <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_1992_Summer_Olympics" title="Football at the 1992 Summer Olympics">win silver</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1992_Summer_Olympics" title="1992 Summer Olympics">1992 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>: he did not score at all in the group stage, but eventually ranked third in the charts at four, three behind compatriot <a href="/wiki/Andrzej_Juskowiak" title="Andrzej Juskowiak">Andrzej Juskowiak</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
