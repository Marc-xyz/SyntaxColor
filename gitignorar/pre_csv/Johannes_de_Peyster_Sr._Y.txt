<p><b>Johannes de Peyster Sr.</b> (born in <a href="/wiki/Haarlem" title="Haarlem">Haarlem</a>, <a href="/wiki/Holland" title="Holland">Holland</a>, about 1600; died in <a href="/wiki/New_Amsterdam" title="New Amsterdam">New Amsterdam</a> (now <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>) about 1685) was a Dutch merchant who emigrated to <a href="/wiki/New_Amsterdam" title="New Amsterdam">New Amsterdam</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Johannes_de_Peyster_Sr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The family name was originally spelled "Peijster," "Peister," or "Pester." De Peyster came of a French <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Huguenot" title="Huguenot">Huguenot</a> family that took refuge in the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Republic" title="Dutch Republic">United Provinces</a> about the time of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Bartholomew%27s_Day_Massacre" title="St. Bartholomew's Day Massacre">St. Bartholomew's Day Massacre</a>. He emigrated to New Amsterdam on account of religious persecution. During the brief period in 1673-74 in which the Dutch regained possession of New Netherland, he took a prominent part in the conduct of public affairs, and he was one of the last to take the oath of allegiance to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_crown" title="British crown">British crown</a> on the final cession of the province to that power.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWilson_&amp;_Fiske1900_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWilson_&amp;_Fiske1900-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Notwithstanding his tardiness in taking an oath of allegiance, he still continued active in municipal affairs under English rule. At different times, he served as alderman and deputy mayor, but he refused the mayoralty on account of his ignorance of English. At the time of his death, he was one of the wealthiest citizens of the province.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Johannes_de_Peyster_Sr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Johannes married Cornelia "Neeltje" Lubberts (aka de Peijster, de Peyster, van Elburch) in 1651 in New York City. Of his sons, <a href="/wiki/Abraham_de_Peyster" title="Abraham de Peyster">Abraham</a> became mayor of New York City and chief justice of the province, <a href="/wiki/Johannes_de_Peyster" title="Johannes de Peyster">Johannes</a> also filled the mayor's chair, Isaac was a member of the provincial legislature, and Cornelius was the first chamberlain of New York City, besides acting in various other public capacities. His son-in-law <a href="/wiki/David_Provost" title="David Provost">David Provost</a> also served as the city's mayor, succeeding Johannes de Peyster.
</p>
