<p>The <b>City of Stoke-on-Trent Sixth Form College</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Mixed-sex_education" title="Mixed-sex education">mixed</a> <a href="/wiki/Sixth_form_college" title="Sixth form college">sixth form college</a> on <a href="/wiki/A52_road" title="A52 road">Leek Road</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stoke-on-Trent" title="Stoke-on-Trent">Stoke-on-Trent</a>. It opened its new building on Leek Road in September 2010 having previously been located on Victoria Road, <a href="/wiki/Fenton,_Staffordshire" title="Fenton, Staffordshire">Fenton</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mar09_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mar09-1">[1]</a></sup> The college is also known as <b>Stoke-on-Trent Sixth Form College</b>, and - prior to its relocation - <b>Fenton Sixth Form College</b>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Admissions">Admissions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=City_of_Stoke-on-Trent_Sixth_Form_College&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Admissions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The college specialises in educating years <a href="/wiki/Year_Twelve" title="Year Twelve">12</a> and <a href="/wiki/Year_Thirteen" title="Year Thirteen">13</a>, where students can gain AS, A2 and BTEC qualifications. More than 95% of students are aged between 16 and 19.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mar09_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Mar09-1">[1]</a></sup> About 90% of students are of <a href="/wiki/White_British" title="White British">White British</a> heritage,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dec04_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dec04-2">[2]</a></sup> with a large minority being <a href="/wiki/British_Pakistanis" title="British Pakistanis">British Pakistanis</a>.  About 70% of students come from the city, with the rest journeying in from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Staffordshire" title="North Staffordshire">North Staffordshire</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dec04_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Dec04-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>The college requires a minimum of four GCSEs at A* to C grades, rather than the minimum five usually required.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nov06_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nov06-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=City_of_Stoke-on-Trent_Sixth_Form_College&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The college was established in 1970 on Victoria Road, <a href="/wiki/Fenton,_Staffordshire" title="Fenton, Staffordshire">Fenton</a>; making it the first purpose-built sixth form college in the country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dec04_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Dec04-2">[2]</a></sup> Next to it was the Fenton Manor Sports Complex. Originally build with a capacity of 700 students, it eventually came to teach more than double that figure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dec04_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Dec04-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>The idea of a 'University Quarter' was first announced in 2004,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and was developed for the next few years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> A December 2004 report gave most teaching staff a grade of good or better.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dec04_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-Dec04-2">[2]</a></sup> In November 2006 an <a href="/wiki/Ofsted" title="Ofsted">Ofsted</a> report gave the college a 'satisfactory' grade, and gave a good or better teaching and learning in 72% of lessons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nov06_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Nov06-3">[3]</a></sup> In late 2008 <a href="/wiki/Planning_permission" title="Planning permission">planning permission</a> was given for the college to build a new campus on Leek Road,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> as part of a £1.5 billion regeneration project in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Staffordshire" title="North Staffordshire">North Staffordshire</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The project was suspended in January 2009,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> and concerns were made if sufficient funds could be found.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The site was handed over to developers in March 2009,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> just as an Ofsted report gave the college a 'satisfactory' grade.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mar09_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Mar09-1">[1]</a></sup> In September 2010 the college officially moved to Leek Road, <a href="/wiki/Stoke-on-Trent" title="Stoke-on-Trent">Stoke-on-Trent</a>.
</p><p>The college was named in July 2019 as a computing hub for the <a href="/wiki/National_Centre_for_Computing_Education" title="National Centre for Computing Education">National Centre for Computing Education</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Academic_standards">Academic standards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=City_of_Stoke-on-Trent_Sixth_Form_College&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Academic standards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2><p>
The school competes with four other institutes in the city: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Joseph%27s_College,_Stoke-on-Trent" title="St. Joseph's College, Stoke-on-Trent">St Joseph's College</a>, <a href="/wiki/St_Margaret_Ward_Catholic_Academy" title="St Margaret Ward Catholic Academy">St Margaret Ward Catholic Academy</a>, <a href="/wiki/St_Thomas_More_Catholic_Academy" title="St Thomas More Catholic Academy">St Thomas More Catholic Academy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stoke-on-Trent_College" title="Stoke-on-Trent College">Stoke-on-Trent College</a>. Of these only <a href="/wiki/Shelton,_Staffordshire" title="Shelton, Staffordshire">Shelton</a> based Stoke-on-Trent College is not attached to a school. </p><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:352px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:City_of_Stoke_on_Trent_Sixth_Form_College.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="427" data-file-width="640" decoding="async" height="234" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/City_of_Stoke_on_Trent_Sixth_Form_College.jpg/350px-City_of_Stoke_on_Trent_Sixth_Form_College.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/City_of_Stoke_on_Trent_Sixth_Form_College.jpg/525px-City_of_Stoke_on_Trent_Sixth_Form_College.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/9/9d/City_of_Stoke_on_Trent_Sixth_Form_College.jpg 2x" width="350"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:City_of_Stoke_on_Trent_Sixth_Form_College.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The front of the new college building.</div></div></div>

<p>The college has feeder schools <a href="/wiki/List_of_schools_in_Stoke-on-Trent" title="List of schools in Stoke-on-Trent">throughout the city</a>.
</p><p>Many of the students go on to local Universities <a href="/wiki/Staffordshire_University" title="Staffordshire University">Staffordshire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Keele_University" title="Keele University">Keele</a>.
