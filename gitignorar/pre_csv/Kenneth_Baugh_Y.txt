<p><b>Kenneth Lee O'Neil Baugh</b> (24 February 1941 – 1 September 2019)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaican</a> politician, who was an Opposition Leader, Foreign minister and Deputy Prime Minister of Jamaica. He served in the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Jamaica" title="Parliament of Jamaica">Parliament of Jamaica</a>, was a general secretary and chairman of the <a href="/wiki/Jamaica_Labour_Party" title="Jamaica Labour Party">Jamaica Labour Party</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He also served as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minister_of_Health" title="Minister of Health">Minister of Health</a>. After his party lost the 2011 general election he served as Member of Parliament and opposition spokesman on health and quality of life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Baugh retired from politics in 2015 due to ill health.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dead_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-dead-4">[4]</a></sup> He had <a href="/wiki/Brain" title="Brain">brain</a> <a href="/wiki/Surgery" title="Surgery">surgery</a> later that year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dead_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-dead-4">[4]</a></sup> Baugh died on 1 September 2019 from a long-illness at the age of 78.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dead_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-dead-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
