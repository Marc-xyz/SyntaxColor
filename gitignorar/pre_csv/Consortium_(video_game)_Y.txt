<p><i><b>Consortium</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/First-person_shooter" title="First-person shooter">first-person shooter</a> <a href="/wiki/Role-playing_video_game" title="Role-playing video game">role-playing video game</a> developed by Interdimensional Games for <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">Microsoft Windows</a>. The game is set almost entirely on a futuristic aircraft called Zenlil, manned by a crew with chess-themed ranks.  The game was principally financed by the <a href="/wiki/Canada_Media_Fund" title="Canada Media Fund">Canada Media Fund</a> by awarding a grant for $500,000 CAD in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> After a first failed <a href="/wiki/Kickstarter" title="Kickstarter">Kickstarter</a> campaign in 2012, $70,435 USD was raised through Kickstarter on April 18, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Consortium was released on January 8, 2014.  A sequel, <a href="/wiki/Consortium:_The_Tower" title="Consortium: The Tower">Consortium: The Tower</a>, was released to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Steam_(software)" title="Steam (software)">Steam</a> Early Access on September 21, 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gameplay">Gameplay</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Consortium_(video_game)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Gameplay">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The game features <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dialog_tree" title="Dialog tree">conversational mechanics</a> which allow gameplay to be uninterrupted by conversations: replies are bound to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Function_keys" title="Function keys">function keys</a> and an in-game device allows for semi-telepathic communication. The other major feature is a branching story, similar in execution to the <i><a href="/wiki/Mass_Effect" title="Mass Effect">Mass Effect</a></i> trilogy, and a character alignment system: both of these allow for every playthrough to be different, even to the point of allowing the player to reveal the meta-plot of the game. While the game is a first-person shooter, combat can be entirely avoided, non-lethal or lethal (in a similar style to <i><a href="/wiki/Deus_Ex_(video_game)" title="Deus Ex (video game)">Deus Ex</a></i>), and is limited due to the size of the game world.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Consortium_(video_game)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The game is set in the mid-21st century, in a timeline where most world militaries have been disbanded in favour of a technologically advanced world police force called the Consortium. The player assumes control of Bishop 6, an elite Consortium soldier.  The game's fiction acknowledges that the player and Bishop 6 are different characters, connected through the interdimensional link of the video game.  <a href="/wiki/Non-player_character" title="Non-player character">Non-player characters</a> may not be aware that Bishop 6 is being controlled from afar.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RPSPrev_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-RPSPrev-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>During a flight from <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a> to <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> on the aircraft Zenlil, Bishop 6 must deal with hostile mercenaries while trying to uncover the identity of a traitor within the crew.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Consortium_(video_game)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The game received mixed reviews, with a score of 66/100 on reviews aggregation website <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MC_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-MC-7">[7]</a></sup>  Reviews praised the storytelling but noted the amount of <a href="/wiki/Software_bug" title="Software bug">software bugs</a> in the game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGN_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-IGN-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GS_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-GS-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VG_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-VG-10">[10]</a></sup>  Interdimensional Games sought to address those bugs with the Master Edition patch, released in April 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1.2_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-1.2-11">[11]</a></sup>  A retrospective at <i><a href="/wiki/Rock,_Paper,_Shotgun" title="Rock, Paper, Shotgun">Rock, Paper, Shotgun</a></i> reflected on the game's <a href="/wiki/Fourth_wall" title="Fourth wall">fourth wall</a> breaking central conceit, and how it was supported by the conversation and dialogue options.  It concluded that "None of this would work in another medium. <i>Consortium</i> offers a solid sample of what we’ve been saying games have the potential to do for a generation."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RPSRetro_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-RPSRetro-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
