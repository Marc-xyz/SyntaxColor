<p><b>Sankhol</b> is a village in Jhajjar district, town Bahadurgarh located along NH 9 (formerly NH 10). Sankhol comes in Assembly constituency 64-Bahadurgarh. The village has four polling stations (polling station no. 56, 57, 58, 59). Today the village is 403 years old (as in 2018) with 25-30 generation of people born .
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sankhol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of 2001<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Sankhol&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup> India <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Sankhol had a population of 5178. Males constitute 54% of the population and females 46%. Sankhol has an average literacy rate of 70%, higher than the national average of 59.5%: male literacy is 78%, and female literacy is 60%. In Sankhol, 13% of the population is under 6 years of age.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sankhol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In Samwat 1672 (i.e. On 5TH NOVEMBER 1615 ; on Guru Nanak Jayanti ) Kartika month on Thursday Purnima  Ch. SUKHLA RATHI <a class="external text" href="https://www.jatland.com/home/Rathi" rel="nofollow"><u>RATHI</u></a> established the village SANKHOL along with people from 7 different cast. Ch. Sukhla Rathi was a Kshatriya originally from Tungdesh (now Prabhas Patan, Somnath, Gujrat) and moved to Sankhol in search for a better place for prosperity while halting at Hastinapur, Tamargarh, Delhi (Mehrauli), Gopalgarh, Hariyapur, Kasar, Parnala, Sarfabad (now Bahadurgarh) and finally at Sankhol.
</p><p>The village was internally divided into three Pannas by the three brothers of 6-7 generation becoming the head of each Panna i.e. Bhortha, Gullar and Baggri.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_Persons">Notable Persons</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sankhol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Notable Persons">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Brigadier_Hoshier_Singh_Rathee">Brigadier Hoshier Singh Rathee</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sankhol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Brigadier Hoshier Singh Rathee">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Hoshier_Singh" title="Hoshier Singh">Brigadier <b>Hoshiar Singh</b></a> <i>IOM, IDSM, Croix De Guerre</i> was the commander of the Indian 62 Brigade stationed at <a href="/wiki/Sela_Pass" title="Sela Pass">Sela Pass</a>. Brigadier Singh was <a href="/wiki/Killed_in_action" title="Killed in action">killed in action</a>, along with a few Indian troops during the 1962 <a href="/wiki/Sino-Indian_War" title="Sino-Indian War">Sino-Indian War</a>. Brigadier Hoshiar Singh was commended for his bravery in the war. Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Jawaharlal_Nehru" title="Jawaharlal Nehru">Jawaharlal Nehru</a>, along with <a href="/wiki/Partap_Singh_Kairon" title="Partap Singh Kairon">Pratap Singh Kairon</a>, the then Chief Minister of Punjab, had visited this village personally to pay homage to the late Brigadier.
</p>
