<p><b>Museo Storico Alfa Romeo</b> (<b>Alfa Romeo Historical Museum</b>; subsequently called <b>Museo Alfa Romeo - La macchina del tempo</b>, literally <i>Alfa Romeo Museum - The time machine</i>) is <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo" title="Alfa Romeo">Alfa Romeo</a>'s official museum, located in <a href="/wiki/Arese" title="Arese">Arese</a> (<a href="/wiki/Province_of_Milan" title="Province of Milan">Milan</a>), and displaying a permanent collection of Alfa Romeo cars and engines.
</p><p>After being closed down in 2011, the museum reopened in June 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ilmessaggero_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ilmessaggero-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alfa_Romeo_Museum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The museum was officially inaugurated on 18 December 1976,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tafter.it_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-tafter.it-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-alfaromeo1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-alfaromeo1-3">[3]</a></sup> and is located in the former <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_Arese_Plant" title="Alfa Romeo Arese Plant">Alfa Romeo Arese factory</a> area. Production of cars ended in 2002 and engine production in 2006 in Arese factory complex.
</p><p>At the beginning of 2009 the museum was closed down a first time for renovations and opened in the end of the year, to celebrate Alfa Romeo's 100th birthday in 2010. It was closed once more in February 2011, reportedly for renovation work again.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
The renovation project was laid down at the end of 2013, and restoration work only started in Summer 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ilmessaggero_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-ilmessaggero-2">[2]</a></sup>
Centerpiece of the renewed structure are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rosso_Corsa" title="Rosso Corsa">Alfa red</a> projecting roofs added to the original 1970s structure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ilmessaggero_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-ilmessaggero-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>After four years the Museum officially reopened on 24 June 2015, when it hosted the press unveiling of the all-new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_Giulia_(2015)" title="Alfa Romeo Giulia (2015)">Alfa Romeo Giulia</a> and Alfa Romeo logo, both key steps in the relaunch of the brand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ilmessaggero_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-ilmessaggero-2">[2]</a></sup>
On 30 June 2015 the museum reopened to the public.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ilmessaggero_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-ilmessaggero-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alfa_Romeo_Museum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The museum is dedicated to over 100 years of history of the Alfa Romeo marque, whose production included automobiles, commercial vehicles, railway locomotives, tractors, buses, trams, marine and aircraft engines.
</p><p>The museum spreads over 4,800 square metres (52,000 sq ft). Its six floors are divided into four theme areas, including a historical review of all Alfa Romeo road cars produced since 1910, prototypes and <a href="/wiki/Concept_car" title="Concept car">dream cars</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aircraft" title="Aircraft">aircraft</a> and aeronautical projects, and scale models and awards.
</p><p>The museum collection numbers over 250 cars and 150 engines, of which approximately half were on display. These included at least one example of each model produced, plus prototypes and racing cars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-alfaromeo1_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-alfaromeo1-3">[3]</a></sup>
Some of the museum cars are regularly on loan for festivals and historical events, like <a href="/wiki/Pebble_Beach_Concours_d%27Elegance" title="Pebble Beach Concours d'Elegance">Pebble Beach Concours d'Elegance</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Goodwood_Festival_of_Speed" title="Goodwood Festival of Speed">Goodwood Festival of Speed</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mille_Miglia" title="Mille Miglia">Mille Miglia</a>.
</p><p>The renewed 2015 exhibit includes 69 cars.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ilmessaggero_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-ilmessaggero-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Scuderia_del_Portello">Scuderia del Portello</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alfa_Romeo_Museum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Scuderia del Portello">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:292px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Arturo_Merzario_con_l%27Alfa_Romeo_33_TT_a_Monza.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1333" data-file-width="2000" decoding="async" height="193" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Arturo_Merzario_con_l%27Alfa_Romeo_33_TT_a_Monza.JPG/290px-Arturo_Merzario_con_l%27Alfa_Romeo_33_TT_a_Monza.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Arturo_Merzario_con_l%27Alfa_Romeo_33_TT_a_Monza.JPG/435px-Arturo_Merzario_con_l%27Alfa_Romeo_33_TT_a_Monza.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Arturo_Merzario_con_l%27Alfa_Romeo_33_TT_a_Monza.JPG/580px-Arturo_Merzario_con_l%27Alfa_Romeo_33_TT_a_Monza.JPG 2x" width="290"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Arturo_Merzario_con_l%27Alfa_Romeo_33_TT_a_Monza.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Arturo Merzario with Alfa Romeo 33 TT in Monza</div></div></div>
<p>Scuderia del Portello is an <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> <a href="/wiki/Sports_club" title="Sports club">sports association</a> dedicated to the activities of <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo" title="Alfa Romeo">Alfa Romeo</a> cars and historic models in motorsport and rallies; the official club is located at the Alfa Romeo Museum in <a href="/wiki/Arese" title="Arese">Arese</a>.
</p><p>On February 3, 1982, the journalist Luca Grandori, together with Edilberto Mandelli, Pietro Rondo, Giorgio Schoen, Stefano Senin and Renato Ughi, founded the Scuderia in Arese as part of the Centro Direzionale Alfa Romeo with the patronage of the mother company. The name recalled the suburbs of Milan where the company's first factories were built. The Scuderia was conceived as a structure for giving support and technical assistance to drivers racing Alfa Romeo cars which were no longer in production, but the articles of association stated that its aim was also to promote the brand, as well as to safeguard its historical and technological heritage, as a contribution to the history of <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">car racing</a>. It immediately became the official Alfa Romeo team for historical car races.
</p><p>In 1990 Scuderia del Portello changed its statute and broadened its activity to also include modern racing cars, becoming an official club of the communication of the brand management. On that occasion the logo was changed as well, taking on its present appearance which recalls the form of the <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_1900" title="Alfa Romeo 1900">Alfa Romeo 1900</a> radiator.
</p><p>The current president and team principal is Marco Cajani.
</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_races">Other races</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alfa_Romeo_Museum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Other races">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In addition to the titles won under the aegis of the FIA, Scuderia del Portello has won eight Italian Historic Car Championships, and over 300 overall class victories in historical car races both in European and National championships.
</p><p>In 1997 the Scuderia won the Tour Auto (revival of the <a href="/wiki/Tour_de_France_Automobile" title="Tour de France Automobile">Tour de France Automobile</a>) with Cajani-Faraci (<a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_Giulietta_Sprint_Speciale" title="Alfa Romeo Giulietta Sprint Speciale">Alfa Romeo Giulietta Sprint</a> Veloce), and in 2004, 2006, and 2007 the Tour de España, respectively with Jean Sage (<a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_Giulietta_(750/101)" title="Alfa Romeo Giulietta (750/101)">Alfa Romeo Giulietta</a> Ti), Sage-Entremont (<a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_1900" title="Alfa Romeo 1900">Alfa Romeo 1900</a> Ti) and Cajani-Confaloni (<a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_1900" title="Alfa Romeo 1900">Alfa Romeo 1900</a> CSS Zagato).
</p><p>The Scuderia took part in the commemorations of important historical races such as the <a href="/wiki/Carrera_Panamericana" title="Carrera Panamericana">Carrera Panamericana</a> (1990, 1991, 2002), where in 1991 it was awarded “best participating team”, and in 2002 won in the <i>Sport Menor</i> category with Arturo Merzario and Vinicio Marta (Alfa Romeo Giulietta Spider Veloce), and in the <i>Original Panamericana</i> category with Prisca Taruffi and Antonio Maglione (<a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_Giulietta_Sprint_Speciale" title="Alfa Romeo Giulietta Sprint Speciale">Alfa Romeo Giulietta Sprint</a> Veloce). The Scuderia took part in two editions of the <a href="/wiki/London%E2%80%93Sydney_Marathon" title="London–Sydney Marathon">London–Sydney Marathon</a> (1993 and 2000) and in the <a href="/wiki/Peking_to_Paris" title="Peking to Paris">Peking to Paris</a> in 2007. In 2004 it was the only racing team to take part in all six classes at <i>Le Mans Classic</i>, the historical reconstruction of the <a href="/wiki/24_Hours_of_Le_Mans" title="24 Hours of Le Mans">24 Hours of Le Mans</a>, and with Phil Hill it gained class victory with an Alfa Romeo 6C 3000 CMM.
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, Scuderia del Portello members and cars usually participate in commemorations of the historical <a href="/wiki/Mille_Miglia" title="Mille Miglia">Mille Miglia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Targa_Florio" title="Targa Florio">Targa Florio</a>.
</p><p>The Scuderia has achieved relevant results with modern racing cars too, including a Drivers and Constructors Championship ISCS International Series (<a href="/wiki/Group_N" title="Group N">Group N</a>), an Italian Superproduction Championship, two CIVT Italian Touring Car Championships (<a href="/wiki/Group_N" title="Group N">Group N</a>), an ETCS Superdiesel Challenge (Class up to 2.5cc), two Italian Cups for women, one victory in the Alfa Romeo Challenge, a class victory at the <a href="/wiki/24_Hours_N%C3%BCrburgring" title="24 Hours Nürburgring">24 Hours Nürburgring</a> and two victories in the <a href="/wiki/Italian_Superturismo_Championship" title="Italian Superturismo Championship">Italian Superturismo Championship</a> (Private trophies).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
