<p>The <b>Archipelago of Lake Saint Pierre</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> counts 103 islands in the western part of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Saint_Pierre" title="Lake Saint Pierre">Lake Saint Pierre</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>. There are two groups: Islands of <a href="/wiki/Berthierville" title="Berthierville">Berthier</a> to the north and the islands of <a href="/wiki/Sorel-Tracy" title="Sorel-Tracy">Sorel</a> to the south.
</p><p>The shores of this archipelago has twenty-two coastal villages. Around 2600 people were living on those islands in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Islands <a href="/wiki/La_Visitation-de-l%27%C3%8Ele-Dupas,_Quebec" title="La Visitation-de-l'Île-Dupas, Quebec">Dupas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saint-Ignace-de-Loyola,_Quebec" title="Saint-Ignace-de-Loyola, Quebec">Saint-Ignace-de-Loyola</a> are partially urbanized. The majority of the other islands are used for agriculture and grazing farm animals.
</p><p>A network of small roads, often unpaved crosses the riparian environment of the islands. The islands of Saint-Ignace-de-Loyola, Dupas and Castors (Beavers) are connected to the north side of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Lawrence_River" title="St. Lawrence River">St. Lawrence River</a> by a small network of bridges. The connection between the two sides of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Lawrence_River" title="St. Lawrence River">St. Lawrence River</a>) is provided by a ferry service between <a href="/wiki/Sorel-Tracy" title="Sorel-Tracy">Sorel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saint-Ignace-de-Loyola,_Quebec" title="Saint-Ignace-de-Loyola, Quebec">Saint-Ignace</a> Island (the largest island of the archipelago). Most of the other islands are accessible only by boat. The south side of the river, the "Île des Embarras" (Embarrassment Island) is the smallest of the inhabited islands.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geology">Geology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archipelago_of_Saint-Pierre_Lake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Archipelago of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Saint_Pierre" title="Lake Saint Pierre">Lake Saint Pierre</a> is a delta from the last glaciation and modified by the alluvium of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Lawrence_River" title="St. Lawrence River">St. Lawrence River</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Following the withdrawal of the ice cap, the Sorel Islands region was invaded by the <a href="/wiki/Champlain_Sea" title="Champlain Sea">Champlain Sea</a> which is then withdrawn gradually, about 12000 years ago. Clayey marine sediments were deposited at the bottom of the water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>The visible part of the islands of the Archipelago is formed of recent alluvium. These deposits with a maximum height of 7 to 8 metres (23 to 26 feet) above the river level consist mainly of silt deposited by the Champlain Sea at the back that forced marine waters this time to integrate the current riverbed. Today, the same phenomenon is repeated annually during spring flooding<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> while the waters of the river cover a portion of the Sorel Islands and will leave a layer alluvium. Although they can cause problems and damage to the island for a few weeks to travel, the spring floods are beneficial to agriculture.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Topography">Topography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archipelago_of_Saint-Pierre_Lake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Topography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Lawrence_River" title="St. Lawrence River">St. Lawrence River</a> widens the entrance of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Saint_Pierre" title="Lake Saint Pierre">Lake Saint Pierre</a> which forms a delta archipelago of hundreds of islands and islets. Several channels crisscross between these islands. This region is characterized by a very few marked relief. The slope is 3 to 5 metres (9.8 to 16.4 feet) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Above_sea_level" title="Above sea level">above sea level</a> Most of the islands are widely flooded every spring.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archipelago_of_Saint-Pierre_Lake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This region plays a northern continental climate, slightly cooler<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> than area <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a> but much more sunny.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Frost-free period is long, which favors cereal crop production. In winter there is not too severe with rainfall<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and bit excessive cold.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hydrography">Hydrography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archipelago_of_Saint-Pierre_Lake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Hydrography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Chenal_du_Moine.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1326" data-file-width="2048" decoding="async" height="142" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Chenal_du_Moine.jpg/220px-Chenal_du_Moine.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Chenal_du_Moine.jpg/330px-Chenal_du_Moine.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Chenal_du_Moine.jpg/440px-Chenal_du_Moine.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Chenal_du_Moine.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><center>Chenal du Moine, South</center></div></div></div>
<p>The area of the Archipelago has 14 tributaries draining an area of 46,075 square kilometres (17,790 square miles), for a monthly average flow of 777 <a href="/wiki/Cubic_metre" title="Cubic metre">cubic metres</a> (27,400 <a href="/wiki/Cubic_foot" title="Cubic foot">cubic feet</a>) per second. The disproportion between the contribution of banks is impressive; the report is favourable to ten against one for the south shore to the Archipelago. The shore south of the Archipelago with only 4 rivers has a pool of 42,250 square kilometres (16,310 square miles) for a flow 712 cubic metres (25,100 cubic feet) per second while the bank on the north side of Archipelago with ten rivers covers only 3,825 square kilometres (1,477 square miles) and flows that 65.2 cubic metres (2,300 cubic feet) per second.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Wildlife">Wildlife</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archipelago_of_Saint-Pierre_Lake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Wildlife">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Islands and <a href="/wiki/Lake_Saint_Pierre" title="Lake Saint Pierre">Lake Saint Pierre</a> is the most important migratory stopover in eastern Canada for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Waterfowl" title="Waterfowl">waterfowl</a>. The ecological value of these wetlands has been recognized by the <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a> in 2000.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Human_history_on_islands">Human history on islands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archipelago_of_Saint-Pierre_Lake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Human history on islands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>These are the first <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native Americans</a> who inhabited and frequented the Archipelago are deriving their livelihood from the fauna of the islands. <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Cartier" title="Jacques Cartier">Jacques Cartier</a> mentions in his travel in September 1535<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> <b>·</b> .<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> On 29 June 1603, it was the turn of <a href="/wiki/Samuel_de_Champlain" title="Samuel de Champlain">Samuel de Champlain</a> to observe Amerindian.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Then in August 1642, is the foundation of a wooden fort. This in order to repel the attacks of <a href="/wiki/Iroquois" title="Iroquois">Iroquois</a> on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trois-Rivi%C3%A8res,_Quebec" title="Trois-Rivières, Quebec">Trois-Rivières</a>. The fort was burned by the Iroquois in 1647. It was not until 1665 that a new fort was rebuilt by the company of Captain <a href="/wiki/Pierre_de_Saurel" title="Pierre de Saurel">Pierre de Saurel</a> from <a href="/wiki/Carignan" title="Carignan">Carignan</a>.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Vaches_ile_dupas.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="570" data-file-width="900" decoding="async" height="158" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Vaches_ile_dupas.jpg/250px-Vaches_ile_dupas.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Vaches_ile_dupas.jpg/375px-Vaches_ile_dupas.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Vaches_ile_dupas.jpg/500px-Vaches_ile_dupas.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Vaches_ile_dupas.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><center>In <a href="/wiki/La_Visitation-de-l%27%C3%8Ele-Dupas,_Quebec" title="La Visitation-de-l'Île-Dupas, Quebec">La Visitation-de-l'Île-Dupas, Quebec</a> in the summer, the islands are grazing in the open, no fences.</center></div></div></div>
<p>A few years later, in 1672, demobilized soldiers are the first settlers in the Archipelago.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The exploitation of communal grazing on the islands finds its origin here. Agriculture fits this Archipelago of floodplains and in 1710 the population of the manor is 112 people. The population is concentrated in the Beaver Islands, Dupas, Saint-Ignace and Isle Madame at the north of the Archipelago.
</p><p>In the late 1920s, several dams stones were built upstream channels to raise the water level in the shipping channel that runs south of the Archipelago.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p><p>Many summer cottages on pillories were erected along the "chenal aux corbeaux" (Ravens channel), between the Crows and Lapierre Islands. This is the corner of the country <i>Le Survenant (roman)</i> (French novel) described in the two novels written by <a href="/wiki/Germaine_Gu%C3%A8vremont" title="Germaine Guèvremont">Germaine Guèvremont</a> <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=The_Outlander_(novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="The Outlander (novel) (page does not exist)">The Outlander</a></i> (written in 1945) and <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Marie-Didace&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Marie-Didace (page does not exist)">Marie-Didace</a></i> (written in 1947).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The characters in these two novels are associated with the Sorel Islands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Germaine Guèvremont lived for nearly 30 years on the Îlette Pé<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> (near the "Chenal du moyne" - Channel of the monk) and her house became for some years the Museum of Scripture.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup>
</p>
