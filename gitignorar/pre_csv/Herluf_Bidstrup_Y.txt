<p><b>Herluf Bidstrup</b> (10 September 1912 – 26 December 1988) was a <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Danish</a> cartoonist and illustrator. 
</p><p>Bidstrup was educated as a painter at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Danish_Academy_of_Fine_Arts" title="Royal Danish Academy of Fine Arts">Royal Danish Academy of Fine Arts</a> and throughout his career he authored more than 5,000 cartoons. He was a devout <a href="/wiki/Communism" title="Communism">communist</a> and very much concerned with the international affairs of his time and social satire. However, not all his work revolves around politics and ideology.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Artwork">Artwork</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Herluf_Bidstrup&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Artwork">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bidstrup published his first drawings on his 20-years birthday in a notable Danish newspaper, but had exhibited already at the age of 14 in a children's art exhibition in Copenhagen.
</p><p>As a communist, Bidstrup drew lots of cartoons about international politics and social themes, as well as subject matter related to the effects of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. Large parts of his work however, just captures and presents the humour in everyday life situations. He was employed as a cartoonist at the Danish communist newspaper <i>"<a href="/wiki/Land_og_Folk" title="Land og Folk">Land og Folk</a>"</i> from after the war until his death in 1988 and he caricatured many politicians among his political and satirical cartoons.
</p><p>Bidstrup travelled extensively throughout his career, in particular in the socialist countries of the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Bloc" title="Eastern Bloc">Eastern Bloc</a>, most notably <a href="/wiki/East_Germany" title="East Germany">East Germany</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> where he also produced animated movies and exhibited. During the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/German_occupation_of_Denmark" title="German occupation of Denmark">German occupation of Denmark</a> in World War II he lived in the US, and in his time he was among the most popular and well-known Danish artists abroad. His works are still very popular in Russia and China today.
</p><p>In addition to his prolific production of cartoons to many different media, Bidstrup also produced posters and illustrated books, including children's books, educational material and his own travelogues. His book <i>"Kinarejse"</i> (1956) about his travels in China has been translated to Russian, Chinese, German and English ("Herluf Bidstrup in China").
</p><p>Some of his artwork can be experienced at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Worker%27s_Museum_(Copenhagen)" title="Worker's Museum (Copenhagen)">Worker's Museum</a> in Copenhagen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Prizes_and_honors">Prizes and honors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Herluf_Bidstrup&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Prizes and honors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bidstrup was awarded many prizes and honors, many of which were related to the international socialist and communist movements. 
</p>
<ul><li>The <a href="/wiki/Lenin_Peace_Prize" title="Lenin Peace Prize">International Lenin Peace Prize</a>, 1964</li>
<li>Honorable member of <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCOs</a> <a href="/wiki/International_Association_of_Art" title="International Association of Art">International Association of Art</a> (IAA/AIAP), 1974</li>
<li>Honorable member of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USSR_Academy_of_Arts" title="USSR Academy of Arts">USSR Academy of Arts</a>, 1974</li>
<li>Kulturfondens hæderspris, 1981. A notable Danish art prize.</li></ul>
