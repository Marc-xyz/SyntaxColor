<p><b>Evans, Clark and Wollatt</b> was an architectural practice based in <a href="/wiki/Nottingham" title="Nottingham">Nottingham</a> from the early 1920s to 1948.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evans,_Clark_and_Woollatt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The practice was established by <a href="/wiki/Robert_Evans_(Jun)" title="Robert Evans (Jun)">Robert Evans</a>, John Thomas Clark and John Woollatt by 1921. Robert Evans died in 1927, and John Thomas Clark retired in 1940.
</p><p>In 1948 the practice changed its name and became <a href="/wiki/Evans,_Cartwright_and_Woollatt" title="Evans, Cartwright and Woollatt">Evans, Cartwright and Woollatt</a> until 1961 when it was <i>Cartwright, Woollatt and Partners</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
