<p><b>Taylor Dayne</b> (born <b>Leslie Wunderman</b>; March 7, 1962<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-google_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-google-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-google2_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-google2-3">[3]</a></sup>) is an American singer, songwriter, and actress.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-elyrics1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-elyrics1-5">[5]</a></sup> She rose to fame in 1987 after her debut single "<a href="/wiki/Tell_It_to_My_Heart" title="Tell It to My Heart">Tell It to My Heart</a>" reached the top 10 across fourteen international music charts. Dayne achieved six additional U.S. top 10 singles, including "<a href="/wiki/Love_Will_Lead_You_Back" title="Love Will Lead You Back">Love Will Lead You Back</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Prove_Your_Love" title="Prove Your Love">Prove Your Love</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/I%27ll_Always_Love_You_(Taylor_Dayne_song)" title="I'll Always Love You (Taylor Dayne song)">I'll Always Love You</a>".
</p><p>Dayne has sold over 75 million records worldwide, making her one of the world's <a href="/wiki/List_of_best-selling_music_artists" title="List of best-selling music artists">best-selling music artists</a>. Additionally, she has earned three <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> nominations, an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_Music_Award" title="American Music Award">American Music Award</a>, and multiple New York Music Awards. She has also received New York Hall of Fame honors and was ranked at number 18 on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone_(magazine)" title="Rolling Stone (magazine)">Rolling Stone</a>'</i>s list of the best female dance artists of all-time. In December 2016, <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> magazine ranked her as the 28th most successful dance artist of all-time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Singing_and_acting_career">Singing and acting career</span></h2>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_success">Early success</span></h3>
<p>Leslie Wunderman began singing professionally after graduating from high school in <a href="/wiki/Baldwin,_Nassau_County,_New_York" title="Baldwin, Nassau County, New York">Baldwin, Nassau County, New York</a>, singing in little-known rock bands such as Felony and Next.  She began singing solo after finishing college and recorded two <a href="/wiki/Electronic_dance_music" title="Electronic dance music">dance</a> singles under the name <b>Les Lee</b> called "I'm The One You Want" (1985) and "Tell Me Can You Love Me" (1986), which were released on New York indie label Mega Bolt.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>Signed to <a href="/wiki/Arista_Records" title="Arista Records">Arista Records</a> as Taylor Dayne, her first song to crack the <a href="/wiki/Top_40" title="Top 40">top ten</a> was the <a href="/wiki/Dance-pop" title="Dance-pop">dance-pop</a> hit "<a href="/wiki/Tell_It_to_My_Heart" title="Tell It to My Heart">Tell It to My Heart</a>" in late 1987. The song was an instant smash worldwide, peaking in the top five of most major markets worldwide, and achieving a peak of number one in many countries, including <a href="/wiki/West_Germany" title="West Germany">West Germany</a>.  Six more U.S. top-ten hits followed including "<a href="/wiki/Prove_Your_Love" title="Prove Your Love">Prove Your Love</a>", "<a href="/wiki/I%27ll_Always_Love_You_(Taylor_Dayne_song)" title="I'll Always Love You (Taylor Dayne song)">I'll Always Love You</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Rush_Me" title="Don't Rush Me">Don't Rush Me</a>", "<a href="/wiki/With_Every_Beat_of_My_Heart" title="With Every Beat of My Heart">With Every Beat of My Heart</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Love_Will_Lead_You_Back" title="Love Will Lead You Back">Love Will Lead You Back</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/I%27ll_Be_Your_Shelter" title="I'll Be Your Shelter">I'll Be Your Shelter</a>".  These were followed by the No. 12 hit "<a href="/wiki/Heart_of_Stone_(Taylor_Dayne_song)" title="Heart of Stone (Taylor Dayne song)">Heart of Stone</a>".
</p><p>In the United States, she achieved three <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gold_single" title="Gold single">gold singles</a>, with "Tell It to My Heart", "I'll Always Love You," and "Love Will Lead You Back." "I'll Always Love You," a change-of-pace ballad compared to Dayne's earlier releases, was her breakout hit on the <a href="/wiki/Adult_Contemporary_(chart)" title="Adult Contemporary (chart)">Adult Contemporary</a> charts in 1988 and paved the way for future uptempo dance tracks such as "Don't Rush Me" (1988) and "With Every Beat Of My Heart" (1989) to gain acceptance at Adult Contemporary radio.  In 1990, "Love Will Lead You Back", a <a href="/wiki/Diane_Warren" title="Diane Warren">Diane Warren</a> composition, peaked at No. 1 on both the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_100" title="Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_Adult_Contemporary_Tracks" title="Hot Adult Contemporary Tracks">Hot Adult Contemporary Tracks</a> chart.
</p><p>The string of hits from her first two albums, <i><a href="/wiki/Tell_It_to_My_Heart_(album)" title="Tell It to My Heart (album)">Tell It to My Heart</a></i> (1988) and <i><a href="/wiki/Can%27t_Fight_Fate" title="Can't Fight Fate">Can't Fight Fate</a></i> (1989), would prove to be the peak of her music career.  Although she went on to release two more albums in the 1990s, she had only one more U.S. Top 40 hit, her 1993 cover of <a href="/wiki/Barry_White" title="Barry White">Barry White</a>' 1974 hit "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Can%27t_Get_Enough_of_Your_Love" title="Can't Get Enough of Your Love">Can't Get Enough of Your Love</a>" (from her 1993 album <i><a href="/wiki/Soul_Dancing" title="Soul Dancing">Soul Dancing</a></i>) which reached No. 2 in the <a href="/wiki/ARIA_Charts" title="ARIA Charts">Aria</a> Charts in Australia but only No. 20 in the U.S.
</p><p>Together with Arthur Baker and <a href="/wiki/Fred_Zarr" title="Fred Zarr">Fred Zarr</a>, Dayne wrote "<a href="/wiki/Whatever_You_Want_(Tina_Turner_song)" title="Whatever You Want (Tina Turner song)">Whatever You Want</a>" for <a href="/wiki/Tina_Turner" title="Tina Turner">Tina Turner</a>'s 1996 album <i><a href="/wiki/Wildest_Dreams_(Tina_Turner_album)" title="Wildest Dreams (Tina Turner album)">Wildest Dreams</a>.</i> Dayne later recorded and released the song herself in 1998 as a single from her album <i><a href="/wiki/Naked_Without_You_(album)" title="Naked Without You (album)">Naked Without You</a>.</i>  That album failed to chart, but Dayne continued to have hits on the dance charts into the 2000s.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_singing_and_acting_career">Later singing and acting career</span></h3>
<p>As an actress, Dayne appeared in the 1997 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sci-fi" title="Sci-fi">sci-fi</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_series" title="Television series">television series</a> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nightman" title="Nightman">Nightman</a></i> as Carla Day. She has had roles in independent films such as <i>Fool's Paradise</i> (1997), <i>Stag</i> (also in 1997) and <i>Jesus the Driver</i> (2004), as well as the <a href="/wiki/Warren_Beatty" title="Warren Beatty">Warren Beatty</a>-produced 1994 remake of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Love_Affair_(1994)" title="Love Affair (1994)">Love Affair</a>.</i> Dayne also had a recurring role on the <a href="/wiki/Showtime_(TV_network)" title="Showtime (TV network)">Showtime</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Rude_Awakening_(TV_series)" title="Rude Awakening (TV series)">Rude Awakening</a>.</i>
</p><p>She has performed alongside <a href="/wiki/Marc_Bonilla" title="Marc Bonilla">Marc Bonilla and Dragonchoir</a>. Later on, she performed on <a href="/wiki/Broadway_theatre" title="Broadway theatre">Broadway</a> in Elton John's <i><a href="/wiki/Aida_(musical)" title="Aida (musical)">Aida</a></i> in 2001. In 2005, Dayne was featured in the <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a> series <i>Remaking</i>, which featured her close friends, <a href="/wiki/Leah_Remini" title="Leah Remini">Leah Remini</a> and Michelle Reid (also referred to as the Don on VH1). The series documents Dayne's return to music after taking time off to have a family (she has twins via a <a href="/wiki/Surrogacy" title="Surrogacy">surrogate mother</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>), and premiered her newest song, "Right Now," a result of her collaboration with <a href="/wiki/Rodney_Jerkins" title="Rodney Jerkins">Rodney Jerkins</a>.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Taylor_Dayne_Performing.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="403" data-file-width="287" decoding="async" height="309" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Taylor_Dayne_Performing.JPG/220px-Taylor_Dayne_Performing.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/51/Taylor_Dayne_Performing.JPG 1.5x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Taylor_Dayne_Performing.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Dayne performing in concert, January 15, 2006</div></div></div>
<p>On December 11, 2007, Dayne released the first single "<a href="/wiki/Beautiful_(Taylor_Dayne_song)" title="Beautiful (Taylor Dayne song)">Beautiful</a>" from her studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Satisfied_(Taylor_Dayne_album)" title="Satisfied (Taylor Dayne album)">Satisfied</a></i>. The song was written by Taylor Dayne and Hitesh Ceon of production team 3Elementz. Dayne has been promoting the single on many radio stations, including top markets including New York's WKTU. "Beautiful" went to No. 1 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> Dance Charts. She also appeared on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Today_(NBC_program)" title="Today (NBC program)">The Today Show</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Chelsea_Lately" title="Chelsea Lately">Chelsea Lately</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wake_Up_With_Whoopi" title="Wake Up With Whoopi">Wake Up With Whoopi</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> for the single and the <i>Satisfied</i> album cover were shot by <a href="/wiki/Tony_Duran_(photographer)" title="Tony Duran (photographer)">Tony Duran</a>, who has also shot album covers for <a href="/wiki/Justin_Timberlake" title="Justin Timberlake">Justin Timberlake</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Lopez" title="Jennifer Lopez">Jennifer Lopez</a>.
</p><p>She released her fifth album <i><a href="/wiki/Satisfied_(Taylor_Dayne_album)" title="Satisfied (Taylor Dayne album)">Satisfied</a></i> in February 2008 — her first <a href="/wiki/Recording_studio" title="Recording studio">recording studio</a> album in nearly a decade. Producers on this record include <a href="/wiki/Rick_Nowels" title="Rick Nowels">Rick Nowels</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gregg_Alexander" title="Gregg Alexander">Gregg Alexander</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peter_Wade_Keusch" title="Peter Wade Keusch">Peter Wade</a>. The album included both new material and recreations of some of her favorite contemporary songs.
</p><p>In May 2010, she lobbied congressional leaders on <a href="/wiki/Capitol_Hill" title="Capitol Hill">Capitol Hill</a> on behalf of NAMM (National Association of Music Merchants) and VH1's Save The Music Foundation to reduce cuts in music education in America's schools.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2010, she released "<a href="/wiki/Facing_a_Miracle" title="Facing a Miracle">Facing a Miracle</a>", the official theme song to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gay_Games_VIII" title="Gay Games VIII">2010 Gay Games</a>. Dayne performed the song to an audience of 50,000 fans on July 31, 2010 in <a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. It ended the year at No. 9 on Perfect Beat's Top Worldwide Singles of 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Five years before, however, she replied to the question what her thoughts on gay marriage were: "In the eyes of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kaballah" title="Kaballah">Kaballah</a>, [gays] can't be 'married.' That requires actual male and female energy and DNA. In the bigger picture, I think that 'marriage' is about a certain circuitry. I'm as married to my business manager as I would be any husband." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pratt_40_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pratt_40-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>Taylor Dayne remastered her <i><a href="/wiki/Greatest_Hits_(Taylor_Dayne_album)" title="Greatest Hits (Taylor Dayne album)">Greatest Hits</a></i> album. She released a new single called "Floor on Fire" on 22 June 2011, which broke the <i>Billboard</i> Dance/Club Charts Top 10.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p><p>In September 2014, Dayne released a new single called "Dreaming".
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span></h2>
<p>Dayne has never married.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-davidatlanta_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-davidatlanta-13">[13]</a></sup> She has twin children via a <a href="/wiki/Surrogacy" title="Surrogacy">surrogate</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-people_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-people-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-glt_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-glt-15">[15]</a></sup> Dayne became a supporter of <a href="/wiki/Same-sex_marriage" title="Same-sex marriage">same-sex marriage</a> (after having been opposed to it) in 2005,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pratt_40_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-Pratt_40-11">[11]</a></sup> and stated in 2014 that she considers the <a href="/wiki/LGBT_community" title="LGBT community">LGBT community</a> to be her most loyal fanbase.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-davidatlanta_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-davidatlanta-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p><p>Dayne is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jewish" title="Jewish">Jewish</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup>  She grew up in Baldwin and <a href="/wiki/Freeport,_New_York" title="Freeport, New York">Freeport, New York</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Poplife_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Poplife-18">[18]</a></sup>
</p>
