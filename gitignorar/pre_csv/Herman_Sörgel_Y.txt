	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Herman Sörgel</h1>

<p><b>Herman Sörgel</b> (2 April 1885 – 25 December 1952) was a German architect from Bavaria. He was known for pioneering the <a href="/wiki/Atlantropa" title="Atlantropa">Atlantropa</a> project which was initially conceived as a solution to the economic and political turmoil gripping Europe in the early 20th century. Atlantropa called for dams built across the <a href="/wiki/Strait_of_Gibraltar" title="Strait of Gibraltar">Strait of Gibraltar</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Dardanelles" title="Dardanelles">Dardanelles</a>, and between <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">Sicily</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tunisia" title="Tunisia">Tunisia</a>. The dams would provide hydroelectric power and would be overseen by a newly formed independent body with the authority to discontinue energy to any country posing a threat to peace. Sörgel actively promoted his ideas until his death in 1952.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Herman_S%C3%B6rgel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Herman Sörgel was born in <a href="/wiki/Regensburg" title="Regensburg">Regensburg</a>, Bavaria, Germany in 1885 to Bavarian parents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> From 1904 to 1908 Sörgel studied architecture at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Technische_Universit%C3%A4t_M%C3%BCnchen" title="Technische Universität München">The Technical University in Munich</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Atlantropa">Atlantropa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Herman_S%C3%B6rgel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Atlantropa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sörgel was the originator of the idea of <a href="/wiki/Atlantropa" title="Atlantropa">Atlantropa</a>—a utopian continent created by damming the <a href="/wiki/Strait_of_Gibraltar" title="Strait of Gibraltar">Strait of Gibraltar</a>, the Dardanelles, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Congo_river" title="Congo river">Congo river</a>. His idea called for the damming, and thus lowering, of the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a> level and then making use of the difference between the Mediterranean and the Atlantic sea levels to generate <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hydro-electric_power" title="Hydro-electric power">hydro-electric power</a>. Sorgel's idea to lower sea levels would increase the dry land areas around the Mediterranean and provide overland access to Africa. Damming the Congo river would refill the basin surrounding <a href="/wiki/Lake_Chad" title="Lake Chad">Lake Chad</a> providing fresh water to irrigate the <a href="/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">Sahara</a> and shipping access to the African interior.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Besides creating new bodies of land, the mass amounts of hydro-electric energy that would be generating, could account for 50% of Europe's energy needs at the time. While Sörgel was dreaming up the idea, he never took into consideration how other countries would react or change. The land mass of the Levant, for example, would increase by 50% due to the water levels dropping. Sörgel would also have to go through multiple Middle Eastern countries to get to Africa where most of the major changes would take place.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Herman_S%C3%B6rgel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sörgel died at the age of 67 shortly after having been struck by a car while on his bicycle en route to a lecture at a German university in Munich. The accident happened on a road "as straight as a die" and the driver of the car was never found.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-datum_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-datum-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
