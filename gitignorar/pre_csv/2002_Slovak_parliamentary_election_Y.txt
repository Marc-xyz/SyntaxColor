<p><b>Parliamentary elections</b> were held in <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovakia</a> on 20 and 21 September 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NS_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-NS-1">[1]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Party_%E2%80%93_Movement_for_a_Democratic_Slovakia" title="People's Party – Movement for a Democratic Slovakia">People's Party – Movement for a Democratic Slovakia</a> remained the largest party in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Council_of_the_Slovak_Republic" title="National Council of the Slovak Republic">National Council</a>, winning 36 of the 150 seats.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Mikul%C3%A1%C5%A1_Dzurinda" title="Mikuláš Dzurinda">Mikuláš Dzurinda</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Slovak_Democratic_and_Christian_Union_%E2%80%93_Democratic_Party" title="Slovak Democratic and Christian Union – Democratic Party">Slovak Democratic and Christian Union</a> remained <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Slovakia" title="Prime Minister of Slovakia">Prime Minister</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
