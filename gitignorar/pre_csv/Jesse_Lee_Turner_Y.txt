	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Jesse Lee Turner</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Jesse Lee Turner</b> (born 1938)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is a former American pop singer. He recorded songs for several labels in the late 1950s, including the <a href="/wiki/One-hit_wonder" title="One-hit wonder">one-hit wonder</a> "Little Space Girl".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Larkin50_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Larkin50-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Turner was born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Addicks,_Texas" title="Addicks, Texas">Addicks, Texas</a>, United States, and graduated from high school in <a href="/wiki/Boling,_Texas" title="Boling, Texas">Boling, Texas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMG_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-AMG-3">[3]</a></sup> His first recording was the single "Teenage Misery" for <a href="/wiki/Fraternity_Records" title="Fraternity Records">Fraternity Records</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMG_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-AMG-3">[3]</a></sup> In 1958, he released the single "Little Space Girl" b/w "Shake Baby Shake" on <a href="/wiki/Carlton_Records" title="Carlton Records">Carlton Records</a>; the tune hit No. 20 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> in 1959.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Larkin50_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Larkin50-2">[2]</a></sup> The follow-up "Thinkin'" failed to chart, and Turner switched to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Top_Rank_Records" title="Top Rank Records">Top Rank Records</a> to release "Do I Worry", which also sold poorly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMG_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-AMG-3">[3]</a></sup> Moving to Sudden Records, he wrote and issued "The Elopers" to no success; <a href="/wiki/GNP_Records" title="GNP Records">GNP Records</a> also released "Ballad of Billy Sol Estes" and a second single.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Larkin50_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Larkin50-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Turner's output comprises 15 known songs; most of which have been reissued on the CD, <i>Shake, Baby, Shake</i>.  He is now an evangelist who sings rock and roll songs with Christian lyrics. On his homepage he tells the story of his bad luck in becoming a star.
</p><p>He currently has an online TV show titled "Our Land USA, Where The Adventure Begins",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and lives in Texas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMG_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-AMG-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
