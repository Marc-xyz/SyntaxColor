<p><b>Hayden Kain Groves</b> (born 21 July 1974) is an English chef and amateur cyclist. He is the author of the Cure Leukaemia Charity cook book <i>Back in the Saddle</i> and won the title of <i>National Chef of the Year in 2013</i>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hayden_Groves&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Groves started his kitchen career as a <a href="/wiki/Chef#Kitchen_assistants" title="Chef">kitchen porter</a>, aged 15. He worked in a number of small local restaurants and hotels, at the same time studying at <a href="/wiki/Hertford_Regional_College" title="Hertford Regional College">Hertford Regional College</a> earning two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/City_%26_Guilds" title="City &amp; Guilds">City &amp; Guilds</a> qualifications.
</p><p>He had stints at <a href="/wiki/Hanbury_Manor" title="Hanbury Manor">Hanbury Manor</a>, under Rory Thompson and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Le_Manoir_aux_Quat%27_Saisons" title="Le Manoir aux Quat' Saisons">Le Manoir aux Quat' Saisons</a> before moving to <a href="/wiki/Tesco" title="Tesco">Tesco</a> head office to cook for the Chairman <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ian_MacLaurin" title="Ian MacLaurin">Ian MacLaurin</a>.
</p><p>In 2004, he took on a role as executive chef<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> for <a href="/wiki/Lloyd%27s_of_London" title="Lloyd's of London">Lloyd's of London</a> an <a href="/wiki/Insurance" title="Insurance">insurance</a> market located in the <a href="/wiki/City_of_London" title="City of London">City of London</a>. During an eight-year tenure, he was in charge of four kitchens and cooked for several members of the <a href="/wiki/Monarchy_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Monarchy of the United Kingdom">royal family</a> including Queen <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">Elizabeth II</a> in an event for the <a href="/wiki/British_Red_Cross" title="British Red Cross">British Red Cross</a> in 2005.
</p><p>In 2012, Groves joined Foodservice provider BaxterStorey.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He has since cooked for senior executives in the banking world, including <a href="/wiki/Bob_Diamond_(banker)" title="Bob Diamond (banker)">Bob Diamond</a> at <a href="/wiki/Barclays" title="Barclays">Barclays</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lloyd_Blankfein" title="Lloyd Blankfein">Lloyd Blankfein</a> of <a href="/wiki/Goldman_Sachs" title="Goldman Sachs">Goldman Sachs</a>.
</p><p>In 2013 Groves was awarded National chef of the Year,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> whose previous winners included <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Ramsay" title="Gordon Ramsay">Gordon Ramsay</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Sargeant" title="Mark Sargeant">Mark Sargeant</a>.
</p><p>In 2015 he was awarded <i><a href="/wiki/The_Caterer" title="The Caterer">The Caterer</a></i> foodservice Chef Catey at <a href="/wiki/The_Catey_Awards" title="The Catey Awards">The Catey Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Since 2012 he is also a consultant and resident demonstration chef at <a href="/wiki/Borough_Market" title="Borough Market">Borough Market</a> and sits as a London board member of the industry benevolent charity Hospitality Action.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Book">Book</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hayden_Groves&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Book">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Back_in_the_Saddle_front_cover_shot.jpg"><img alt="Back in the Saddle front cover shot.jpg" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="746" data-file-width="750" decoding="async" height="219" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/e/eb/Back_in_the_Saddle_front_cover_shot.jpg/220px-Back_in_the_Saddle_front_cover_shot.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/e/eb/Back_in_the_Saddle_front_cover_shot.jpg/330px-Back_in_the_Saddle_front_cover_shot.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/e/eb/Back_in_the_Saddle_front_cover_shot.jpg/440px-Back_in_the_Saddle_front_cover_shot.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Back_in_the_Saddle_front_cover_shot.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div></div></div></div><p>'Back in the Saddle<i> (2017), is the 280-page coffee-table book Groves wrote to accompany the </i>Three Tour Charity Challenge<i> by publisher Anthony Hodgson of Face Publications. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> 100% of the proceeds from the sale of the book  go to  CureLeukaemia.</i>
</p><p><i>Back in the Saddle</i> by Hayden Groves represented the UK at the prestigious Gourmand World Cookbook Awards. The ceremony which took place in <a href="/wiki/Yantai" title="Yantai">Yantai</a>, China 
May 25-28, 2018; saw the book crowned 'Best in the World' in both the <i>Sports Nutrition</i> and <i>Charity Fundraising</i> categories.
<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Gourmand World Cookbook Awards, aka the Oscars of the cookbook awards, were founded in 1995 by  <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edouard_Cointreau" title="Edouard Cointreau">Edouard Cointreau</a>. Every year the Awards honour the best food and wine books. Books from 205 countries participate in these prestigious awards, the largest international cookbook competition in the world.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hayden_Groves&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Groves has two daughters: Keira and Elsie. He was an amateur cyclist who raced at a regional and national level in his teens and twenties.
</p><p>In 2015, he took on the <a href="/wiki/Tour_de_France" title="Tour de France">Tour de France</a> route one day before the professionals to help raise money for blood cancer <a href="/wiki/Charitable_organization" title="Charitable organization">charity</a> Cureleukaemia.
</p><p>In 2017, Groves rode all three Grand Tour routes as part of a team of four, including <a href="/wiki/Geoff_Thomas_(footballer,_born_1964)" title="Geoff Thomas (footballer, born 1964)">Geoff Thomas</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> one day before the professional <a href="/wiki/Peloton" title="Peloton">peloton</a> – the <a href="/wiki/Giro_d%27Italia" title="Giro d'Italia">Giro d'Italia</a>, Tour de France and the  <a href="/wiki/Vuelta_a_Espa%C3%B1a" title="Vuelta a España">Vuelta a España</a>; the 10,403 km (6,464 mi) event was called the Three Tours Challenge.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> which helped raise further funds for the expansion of the Birmingham Centre of Clinical Haematology which opened in January 2018.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> <br/>
In recognition of his fundraising Groves was made a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Patron" title="Patron">Patron</a> of the charity Cureleukaemia in 2019.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>
