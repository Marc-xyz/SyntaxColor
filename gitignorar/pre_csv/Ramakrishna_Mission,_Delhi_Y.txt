<p><b>Ramakrishna Mission, Delhi</b> is the Delhi branch of <a href="/wiki/Ramakrishna_Mission" title="Ramakrishna Mission">Ramakrishna Mission</a>, an spiritual organisation established by <a href="/wiki/Swami_Vivekananda" title="Swami Vivekananda">Swami Vivekananda</a> in 1897.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The Delhi branch was established at Ramakrishna Ashram Marg on 4 May 1927.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-abt_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-abt-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dec_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-dec-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ramakrishna_Mission,_Delhi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Delhi mission has its origins in the Ramakrishna Math which was opened in a rented house on 966, <a href="/wiki/Garstin_Bastion_Road,_New_Delhi" title="Garstin Bastion Road, New Delhi">Garstin Baston Road</a> in 1927. The construction work on the present premises started in 1934 after the first foundation brick was brought in from <a href="/wiki/Belur_Math" title="Belur Math">Belur Math</a> to Delhi, and consecrated by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swami_Shivananda" title="Swami Shivananda">Swami Shivananda</a>, a direct disciple of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sri_Ramakrishna" title="Sri Ramakrishna">Sri Ramakrishna</a> and the second President of the Ramakrishna Math and the Ramakrishna Mission. Thereafter the Delhi Centre was formally opened in 1935. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dec_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-dec-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-abt_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-abt-2">[2]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swami_Ranganathananda" title="Swami Ranganathananda">Swami Ranganathananda</a>, who later became the 13th president of the Ramakrishna Math and Mission and was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Padma_Vibhushan" title="Padma Vibhushan">Padma Vibhushan</a>, served as the secretary at the Delhi Mission from 1949 to 1962. Swamis Sharvananda, <a href="/wiki/Swami_Satprakashananda" title="Swami Satprakashananda">Satprakashananda</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swahananda" title="Swahananda">Swahananda</a>, Budhananda, Vandanananda and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Gokulananda&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Gokulananda (page does not exist)">Gokulananda</a> were also associated with the Delhi Mission.
</p><p>In 2013, the Mission started working with <a href="/wiki/Central_Board_of_Secondary_Education" title="Central Board of Secondary Education">CBSE</a> to provide value-based education to students of classes VII, VIII and IX. For this purpose the mission is developing a three-year <a href="/wiki/Values_education" title="Values education">value education</a> programme around the teachings of <a href="/wiki/Swami_Vivekananda" title="Swami Vivekananda">Swami Vivekananda</a> to be integrated with regular curriculum.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ramakrishna_Mission,_Delhi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The mission is accessible by the <a href="/wiki/Ramakrishna_Ashram_Marg_metro_station" title="Ramakrishna Ashram Marg metro station">Ramakrishna Ashram Marg metro station</a> of <a href="/wiki/Delhi_Metro" title="Delhi Metro">Delhi Metro</a>.
</p>
