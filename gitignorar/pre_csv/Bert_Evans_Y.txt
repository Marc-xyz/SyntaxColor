<p><b>Bert Evans</b> was a Welsh-American <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> player who earned one <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cap_(sports)" title="Cap (sports)">cap</a> with the <a href="/wiki/United_States_men%27s_national_soccer_team" title="United States men's national soccer team">U.S. national team</a> in an 8-1 loss to <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a> on May 28, 1959.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Evans signed professional forms with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swansea_City_F.C." title="Swansea City F.C.">Swansea Town</a> on his 17th birthday in August 1939. Just three weeks later, however, war broke out and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League" title="Football League">Football League</a> suspended operations. Evans would never get the chance to shine for his hometown team.  He joined the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Force" title="Royal Air Force">Royal Air Force</a> and played for their representative XI when stationed in Egypt. After the hostilities, Evans rebuilt his football career, signing for <a href="/wiki/Lovell%27s_Athletic_F.C." title="Lovell's Athletic F.C.">Lovell's Athletic</a>. After winning the Welsh Football League, the club graduated to the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Football_League" title="Southern Football League">Southern League</a> in 1947.  In April 1951, Hubert Evans signed for <a href="/wiki/Newport_County_A.F.C." title="Newport County A.F.C.">Newport County</a>, at long last making his debut in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League" title="Football League">Football League</a> at 28 years of age.  After 14 appearances (1 goal), Evans returned to the Southern League with another Welsh club, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Llanelli_A.F.C." title="Llanelli A.F.C.">Llanelli</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>  In 1957, Evans moved to the United States and settled in California.  When he arrived he joined the semi-professional San Pedro McIlvaine Canvasbacks, playing as a central defender and team captain when they won the <a href="/wiki/1959_National_Challenge_Cup" title="1959 National Challenge Cup">1959 National Challenge Cup</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>  In 1961, he moved to the San Pedro Toros.
</p>
