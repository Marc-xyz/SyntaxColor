<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Objectors_who_moved_to_Canada">Objectors who moved to Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Iraq_War_resisters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Objectors who moved to Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Canada_and_Iraq_War_resisters" title="Canada and Iraq War resisters">Canada and Iraq War resisters</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_of_Iraq_War_resister">Background of Iraq War resister</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Iraq_War_resisters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background of Iraq War resister">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Some deserters chose <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> as the place of refuge in part of the closeness of the US-Canada Border, entry to Canada is easy and because of the prior decision by the <a href="/wiki/Cabinet_of_Canada" title="Cabinet of Canada">Canadian Cabinet</a>, when headed by Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Trudeau" title="Pierre Trudeau">Pierre Trudeau</a>, to allow <a href="/wiki/Canada_and_the_Vietnam_War#Draft_dodgers" title="Canada and the Vietnam War">draft dodgers</a> into Canada without prosecution during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/History_of_Canada_(1960-1981)#Trudeau_and_the_1970s" title="History of Canada (1960-1981)">Vietnam War</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_and_political">Legal and political</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Iraq_War_resisters&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Legal and political">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Pursuant to the Treaty between the Government of Canada and the Government of the United States of America on Mutual Legal assistance in Criminal Matters, US authorities can request Canadian authorities to identify, locate, and take into custody of US nationals who have committed crime that carries a possible sentence of more than a year<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and subsequently be extradited back to US, pursuant to Extradition Treaty Between the United States of America and Canada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> However, the government of the United States must promise those extradited will not receive death penalty, in accordance with the ruling of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_v._Burns" title="United States v. Burns">United States v. Burns</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_Canada" title="Supreme Court of Canada">Supreme Court of Canada</a>.
</p><p>Because the possibilities that <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Deserters" title="Deserters">deserters</a> have been issued with arrest warrant back in the United States and pursuant to those two agreements above, they are liable for arrest in Canada unless they legalize their status. This can be done by pursuing a refugee claim, about which the <a href="/wiki/Immigration_and_Refugee_Board_of_Canada" title="Immigration and Refugee Board of Canada">Immigration and Refugee Board of Canada</a> (IRB) will hold a hearing and determine the validity of the claim. If refused, the claimant can appeal to the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Court_(Canada)" title="Federal Court (Canada)">Federal Court</a>, <a href="/wiki/Federal_Court_of_Appeal" title="Federal Court of Appeal">Federal Court of Appeal</a>, and finally the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_Canada" title="Supreme Court of Canada">Supreme Court of Canada</a>, if leave is granted.
</p><p>However, if the refugee claim is refused and subsequent appeals do not overturn the decision made by IRB, the claimant must leave Canada within 30 days under a removal order.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> If the claimant does not leave Canada within 30 days or failed to confirm departure details with <a href="/wiki/Canada_Border_Services_Agency" title="Canada Border Services Agency">Canada Border Services Agency</a>, it automatically becomes a deportation order, enforceable by any peace officer in Canada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>

<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Robin_Long" title="Robin Long">Robin Long</a> – On July 16, 2008, the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Star" title="Toronto Star">Toronto Star</a> reported that Long "was ordered out of [Canada] last week after he failed to comply with bail conditions imposed when he missed an immigration hearing last year. <ul><li><a href="/wiki/Daniel_Sandate" title="Daniel Sandate">Daniel Sandate</a> – Deported at Niagara Falls, Canada on July 16, 2008,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> after having been in Canada for over 2 years. Carson and given an 8-month prison sentence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-couragetoresist.org_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-couragetoresist.org-38">[38]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wri-irg.org_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-wri-irg.org-39">[39]</a></sup> Sandate released a written statement in prison about his opposition to the war in Iraq.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jmbzine.com_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-jmbzine.com-40">[40]</a></sup> He was released on January 20, 2009, at Ft. Sill, OK, and spoke publicly about his experience at a press conference in Oklahoma City on January 22, 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blueoklahoma.org_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-blueoklahoma.org-41">[41]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dean_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dean-42">[42]</a></sup></li>
<li>James Ashley – On December 23, 2008, the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Star" title="Toronto Star">Toronto Star</a> reported the following:"A 28-year-old man accused of deserting the U.S. army has been caught by <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canadian</a> police and turned over to U.S. authorities in <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a>. James Ashley was returned to the United States on Monday at the <a href="/wiki/Blue_Water_Bridge" title="Blue Water Bridge">Blue Water Bridge</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Port_Huron" title="Port Huron">Port Huron</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Customs" title="U.S. Customs">U.S. Customs</a> spokesman Ron Smith says it's not clear if police caught Ashley at the bridge or elsewhere in <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>. Smith says there's an arrest warrant for Ashley out of <a href="/wiki/Fort_Riley" title="Fort Riley">Fort Riley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kansas" title="Kansas">Kansas</a>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> It is unclear whether or not  Ashley had intended to legalize his status by pursuing a refugee claim with the Immigration and Refugee Board of Canada, or whether he had yet to do so.  (See <a href="/wiki/Immigration_and_Refugee_Protection_Act" title="Immigration and Refugee Protection Act">Immigration and Refugee Protection Act</a>)</li></ul>

<p>For purposes of this list, the determination of <a href="/wiki/Conscientious_objector" title="Conscientious objector">conscientious objection</a> is made by the individual, not a government. It is frequently the case that individuals and governments disagree on the status.
