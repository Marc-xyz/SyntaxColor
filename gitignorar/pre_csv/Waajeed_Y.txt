<p><b>Waajeed</b> (born <b>Robert O'Bryant</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Detroit" title="Detroit">Detroit</a>-born <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Music_producer" title="Music producer">music producer</a>, and one half of the <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/R%26B" title="R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> group <a href="/wiki/Platinum_Pied_Pipers" title="Platinum Pied Pipers">Platinum Pied Pipers</a>, and a founding member of <a href="/wiki/Tiny_Hearts" title="Tiny Hearts">Tiny Hearts</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jeedowp_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-jeedowp-1">[1]</a></sup> He formed the Bling47 record label in 2002, which has released projects by <a href="/wiki/J_Dilla" title="J Dilla">J Dilla</a>, Waajeed himself, and others. Since 2013, he has preferred the name Jeedo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>The name "Waajeed" means "finder" or "seeker" in <a href="/wiki/Arabic" title="Arabic">Arabic</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Waajeed&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Waajeed&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Before he began producing records, Waajeed was studying art and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DJ" title="DJ">DJing</a> in and around the Detroit music circuit.  He was a prominent photographer in the local hip-hop scene and designed the cover for both <a href="/wiki/Slum_Village" title="Slum Village">Slum Village's</a> "<a href="/wiki/Fan-Tas-Tic_(Vol._1)" title="Fan-Tas-Tic (Vol. 1)">Fan-Tas-Tic (Vol. 1)</a>" and <i><a href="/wiki/Fantastic,_Vol._2" title="Fantastic, Vol. 2">Fantastic, Vol. 2</a>,</i> among others.
</p><p>Waajeed was an executive producer on <a href="/wiki/Dwele" title="Dwele">Dwele's</a> pre-debut project, <i>Rize,</i> which ultimately led to Dwele getting signed to <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Records" title="Virgin Records">Virgin Records</a> shortly thereafter.  He has been a close friend of Slum Village since its earliest days, DJ'ing for them on their earliest tours, convincing the group to release their first album/demo, <i><a href="/wiki/Fan-Tas-Tic_(Vol._1)" title="Fan-Tas-Tic (Vol. 1)">Fan-Tas-Tic (Vol. 1)</a></i>, choosing the group's name, and introducing them to fourth member <a href="/wiki/Elzhi" title="Elzhi">Elzhi</a>.
</p><p>Since his earlier records, Waajeed has made a shift towards a "darker" sound which he explains in a 2013 interview. "People often ask me what my music is about and they see it as dark or bleak and yes, I do think it’s dark, but it’s also hopeful and optimistic. I make dark music to be a better person. I make dark music so I don’t go out in a temper and take a son from a mother. You have to camouflage yourself. You have to lay in the cut…. My music is dark because it’s a reflection of the times too. These are serious times we live in so even at its brightest moments there’s a taint to my work. What’s going on with all the gunplay – this is not a time for happy sing-along shit." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Waajeed&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As a producer, Waajeed began comparatively late (2000), but before long had landed a handful of tracks on his longtime friends, Slum Village's <i><a href="/wiki/Trinity_(Past,_Present_and_Future)" title="Trinity (Past, Present and Future)">Trinity (Past, Present and Future)</a></i> album.  According to Waajeed, his first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MIDI_Production_Center" title="MIDI Production Center">MPC</a> had previously belonged to J Dilla.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> After the hardware was broken by <a href="/wiki/Questlove" title="Questlove">Questlove</a>, J Dilla said that Waajeed could keep it if he could fix it.  His first beats were made on that very same MPC after managing to correct the problem.
</p><p>Waajeed's productions have led to him working with some of the industry's most respected artists such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cee_Lo_Green" title="Cee Lo Green">Cee Lo Green</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/John_Legend" title="John Legend">John Legend</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mayer_Hawthorne" title="Mayer Hawthorne">Mayer Hawthorne</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and more.
</p><p>In 2007 he released an album called <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=The_War_LP&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="The War LP (page does not exist)">The War LP</a></i> which included solo instrumentals as well as new production for <a href="/wiki/Invincible_(rapper)" title="Invincible (rapper)">Invincible</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tiombe_Lockhart" title="Tiombe Lockhart">Tiombe Lockhart</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=TaRaach&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="TaRaach (page does not exist)">TaRaach</a>, and the late J Dilla.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>Waajeed has been a featured guest on many international radio shows, including KCRW,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> BBC Radio 1, and more.
</p><p>In 2010, he released preview of his remix of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DEDE_(American_Band)" title="DEDE (American Band)">DEDE (American Band)</a>'s "Phantom" on Soundcloud.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2011, he joined and co-founded music group <a href="/wiki/Tiny_Hearts" title="Tiny Hearts">Tiny Hearts</a> with producer <a href="/wiki/Tim_K" title="Tim K">Tim K</a> and vocalist Dede Reynolds of <a href="/wiki/Dede_(band)" title="Dede (band)">DEDE</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jeedowp_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-jeedowp-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, he established his second independent label, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dirt_Tech_Reck&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dirt Tech Reck (page does not exist)">Dirt Tech Reck</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>He is currently working on new solo material, as well as a collaborative album with Invincible.
</p>
