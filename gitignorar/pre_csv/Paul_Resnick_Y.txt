<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Paul_Resnick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Paul Resnick was born in <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a> and attended the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Michigan" title="University of Michigan">University of Michigan</a> for his undergraduate studies. He received a <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy">Ph.D.</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology" title="Massachusetts Institute of Technology">Massachusetts Institute of Technology</a> (MIT) in 1992 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Computer_Science" title="Computer Science">Computer Science</a>. After graduating from MIT, Resnick worked at <a href="/wiki/AT%26T_Labs" title="AT&amp;T Labs">AT&amp;T Labs</a> and AT&amp;T <a href="/wiki/Bell_Labs" title="Bell Labs">Bell Labs</a> and was an assistant <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">Professor</a> at the <a href="/wiki/MIT_Sloan_School_of_Management" title="MIT Sloan School of Management">MIT Sloan School of Management</a>. Resnick became an assistant <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">professor</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Michigan" title="University of Michigan">University of Michigan</a> in 1997, and subsequently became Associate Professor, Professor, and then Associate Dean.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Paul_Resnick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Resnick was elected to the CHI Academy in 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He received the 2010 ACM Software Systems Award for his work on the GroupLens Collaborative Filtering Recommender System<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> which showed how distributed users could personalize recommendations via ratings. He also received the ACM Special Interest Group on E-commerce Test of Time Award for the paper titled "The Social Cost of Cheap Pseudonyms".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He received the 2016 University of Michigan Distinguished Faculty Achievement Award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
