<p><b>HMCS <i>Dawson</i></b> was a <a href="/wiki/Flower-class_corvette" title="Flower-class corvette">Flower-class</a> <a href="/wiki/Corvette" title="Corvette">corvette</a> that served in the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Canadian_Navy" title="Royal Canadian Navy">Royal Canadian Navy</a> (RCN) during the <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">Second World War</a>. She was one of the few <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canadian</a> corvettes to serve in action in both oceans. She was named for <a href="/wiki/Dawson_City" title="Dawson City">Dawson City, Yukon</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=HMCS_Dawson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Flower-class_corvette" title="Flower-class corvette">Flower-class corvette</a></div>
<p>Flower-class corvettes like <i>Dawson</i> serving with the Royal Canadian Navy during the Second World War were different from earlier and more traditional sail-driven corvettes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jane_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Jane-6">[6]</a></sup>  The "corvette" designation was created by the French as a class of small warships; the Royal Navy borrowed the term for a period but discontinued its use in 1877.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> During the hurried preparations for war in the late 1930s, <a href="/wiki/Winston_Churchill" title="Winston Churchill">Winston Churchill</a> reactivated the corvette class, needing a name for smaller ships used in an escort capacity, in this case based on a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Whaling_ship" title="Whaling ship">whaling ship</a> design.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The generic name "flower" was used to designate the class of these ships, which – in the Royal Navy – were named after flowering plants.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Milner_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Milner-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>Corvettes commissioned by the Royal Canadian Navy during the Second World War were named after communities for the most part, to better represent the people who took part in building them. This idea was put forth by Admiral <a href="/wiki/Percy_W._Nelles" title="Percy W. Nelles">Percy W. Nelles</a>. Sponsors were commonly associated with the community for which the ship was named. Royal Navy corvettes were designed as open sea escorts, while Canadian corvettes were developed for coastal auxiliary roles which was exemplified by their minesweeping gear. Eventually the Canadian corvettes would be modified to allow them to perform better on the open seas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Macpherson2_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Macpherson2-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Construction">Construction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=HMCS_Dawson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Construction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Dawson</i> was ordered on 14 February 1940 from <a href="/wiki/Victoria_Machinery_Depot" title="Victoria Machinery Depot">Victoria Machinery Depot</a>, <a href="/wiki/Victoria,_British_Columbia" title="Victoria, British Columbia">Victoria, British Columbia</a> as part of the 1939-1940 Flower-class building program. She was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Laid_down" title="Laid down">laid down</a> on 7 September 1940 and was launched on 8 February 1941. She was commissioned into the RCN on 6 October 1941 at Victoria.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uboat_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-uboat-11">[11]</a></sup> In September 1943 <i>Dawson</i> began a major refit at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vancouver,_British_Columbia" title="Vancouver, British Columbia">Vancouver</a> that saw her <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fo%27c%27sle" title="Fo'c'sle">fo'c'sle</a> extended. She had a second refit at <a href="/wiki/Dartmouth,_Nova_Scotia" title="Dartmouth, Nova Scotia">Dartmouth</a> that began in January 1945 that lasted until April.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Macpherson_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Macpherson-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Wartime_service">Wartime service</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=HMCS_Dawson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Wartime service">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Dawson</i> was one of the few RCN ships that played an active role in both the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_War" title="Pacific War">Pacific War</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Atlantic" title="Battle of the Atlantic">Battle of the Atlantic</a>. After initial workups, she was assigned to Esquimalt Force.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Macpherson_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-Macpherson-12">[12]</a></sup> From 1942 to 1944 <i>Dawson</i> was deployed in an <a href="/wiki/Anti-submarine_warfare" title="Anti-submarine warfare">anti-submarine</a> role on the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Northwest" title="Pacific Northwest">Pacific coast</a> as a local escort. In August 1942 she and her sister, <a href="/wiki/HMCS_Vancouver_(K240)" title="HMCS Vancouver (K240)">HMCS <i>Vancouver</i></a> were assigned to the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aleutian_Islands_Campaign" title="Aleutian Islands Campaign">invasion</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Aleutian_Islands" title="Aleutian Islands">Aleutian Islands</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Macpherson_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-Macpherson-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> There was little risk to the corvettes as they met very little resistance at sea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-German_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-German-14">[14]</a></sup> On 4 November 1942 she returned to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Esquimalt,_British_Columbia" title="Esquimalt, British Columbia">Esquimalt</a>. In February 1943 she returned to work with the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a> in <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a> until the end of May.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Macpherson_12-3"><a href="#cite_note-Macpherson-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p><p>After completing her major refit <i>Dawson</i> transferred to the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic</a> in February 1944. Upon arrival in March she joined escort group W-7 as part of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Western_Escort_Force&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Western Escort Force (page does not exist)">Western Escort Force</a>. She escorted convoys from <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> to the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">UK</a> for the remainder of her war.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-navhi_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-navhi-15">[15]</a></sup> After undergoing a second major refit, <i>Dawson</i> worked up in <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a> in April 1945 but did not return to active service before the European part of the war ended.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Macpherson_12-4"><a href="#cite_note-Macpherson-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-war_service">Post-war service</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=HMCS_Dawson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Post-war service">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Dawson</i> was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paid_off" title="Paid off">paid off</a> on 19 June 1945 at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sorel,_Quebec" title="Sorel, Quebec">Sorel, Quebec</a>. The ship was sold for <a href="/wiki/Ship_breaking" title="Ship breaking">scrap</a> on 5 October 1945.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-miramar_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-miramar-16">[16]</a></sup> On the way to the breakers, she foundered near <a href="/wiki/Hamilton,_Ontario" title="Hamilton, Ontario">Hamilton, Ontario</a> on 22 March 1946. She was raised and broken up.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Macpherson_12-5"><a href="#cite_note-Macpherson-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
