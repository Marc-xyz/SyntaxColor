<p>The <b>Battle of Agüi</b> was fought at <a href="/wiki/Lacuy_Peninsula" title="Lacuy Peninsula">Fuerte de Agüi</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Ancud" title="Ancud">Ancud</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chilo%C3%A9_Island" title="Chiloé Island">Chiloé</a> on February 18, 1820 between Chilean patriots and Spanish royalists, during the <a href="/wiki/Chilean_War_of_Independence" title="Chilean War of Independence">Chilean War of Independence</a>. After the successful <a href="/wiki/Capture_of_Valdivia" title="Capture of Valdivia">capture of Valdivia</a> <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Cochrane,_10th_Earl_of_Dundonald" title="Thomas Cochrane, 10th Earl of Dundonald">Lord Cochrane</a> sought to occupy the <a href="/wiki/Chilo%C3%A9_Archipelago" title="Chiloé Archipelago">Chiloé Archipelago</a> which was the last stronghold of the Spanish in Chile. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_Miller_(Peruvian_general)" title="William Miller (Peruvian general)">William Miller</a> disembarked for this task but had to reembark after had been severely wounded in his leg and lost 20 men.<span style="font-size: small;"><span id="coordinates"><a href="/wiki/Geographic_coordinate_system" title="Geographic coordinate system">Coordinates</a>: <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Battle_of_Ag%C3%BCi&amp;params=41_49_46.48_S_73_51_18.26_W_"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">41°49′46.48″S</span> <span class="longitude">73°51′18.26″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">41.8295778°S 73.8550722°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">-41.8295778; -73.8550722</span></span></span></a></span></span></span>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_battle">The battle</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Ag%C3%BCi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: The battle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The battle was part of an effort to drive Spanish forces from the Chiloé archipelago, some of which were fortified in the San Miguel de Agüi Castle.  When Commander Saturino García arrived at the castle to reinforce the Spanish army, he found that Chilean Major William Miller had already begun combat. The patriots felt the urgency of this arrival and a force of 60 men assaulted the fort but 38 men, including Miller, were disabled by <a href="/wiki/Shot_(pellet)" title="Shot (pellet)">shot</a> from within.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Assuming command, Captain Francisco Eréscano formed a new column, but canon and gun fire from within the castle made it difficult to advance towards it over a narrow, broken road.  The patriots found their situation made more difficult when a <a href="/wiki/Gunboat" title="Gunboat">gunboat</a> dispatched from <a href="/wiki/Ancud" title="Ancud">San Carlos</a> by colonial governor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antonio_Quintanilla" title="Antonio Quintanilla">Antonio Quintanilla</a> arrived and began firing on their flank.
</p><p>The patriot <a href="/wiki/Column_(formation)" title="Column (formation)">column</a> was forced to fall back. Captain Eréscano carried it out in an orderly fashion, evacuating the wounded and assuring that the rear of his column would be protected from the garrison inside the castle, now being reinforced by Captain García’s troops.  Nevertheless, Eréscano’s forces still had to repel three attacks from the Spanish.  The Chilean forces marched down Huechucucuy Beach and arrived at the cove were their ships were anchored.  By this point the Spanish had ceased their pursuit of the patriot force, probably out of a fear of being surrounded by a larger force.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
