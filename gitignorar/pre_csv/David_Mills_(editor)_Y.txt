		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>David Mills</b> (born 1957) is an American editor and writer who is known for his work within <a href="/wiki/Christian_media" title="Christian media">Christian media</a>. He was the executive editor of the journal <i><a href="/wiki/First_Things" title="First Things">First Things</a></i> and was the editor of <i><a href="/wiki/Touchstone_(magazine)" title="Touchstone (magazine)">Touchstone</a></i> magazine from 2003 to 2008.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Mills_(editor)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>An activist in the conservative Episcopal movement, he was a long-time member of the board of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Evangelical_and_Catholic_Mission" title="Evangelical and Catholic Mission">Evangelical and Catholic Mission</a> and of its successors the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Episcopal_Synod_of_America" title="Episcopal Synod of America">Episcopal Synod of America</a> and <a href="/wiki/Forward_in_Faith" title="Forward in Faith">Forward in Faith</a>, editing the groups' theological <i>The Evangelical Catholic</i> from 1986 to 1998. He was also a member of the board of NOEL (the National Organization of Episcopalians for Life). His "A Hope for Collapsing Churches" was widely reprinted in the <a href="/wiki/Anglicanism" title="Anglicanism">Anglican</a> world, and for many years he wrote a "Letter from America" for the English Anglican magazine <i>New Directions</i>, published by Forward in Faith/UK.
</p><p>Mills, his wife, and their four children were received into the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic Church</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Easter_Vigil" title="Easter Vigil">Easter Vigil</a> in 2001, sponsored by the Catholic writer <a href="/wiki/Mike_Aquilina" title="Mike Aquilina">Mike Aquilina</a> and his wife. He writes a column for the Inside Catholic website, <i>Lay Witness</i> magazine, and the diocesan newspaper <i>The Pittsburgh Catholic</i>, and has also written for <i>Columbia</i>, the <i><a href="/wiki/National_Catholic_Register" title="National Catholic Register">National Catholic Register</a></i>, the <i><a href="/wiki/New_Oxford_Review" title="New Oxford Review">New Oxford Review</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/First_Things" title="First Things">First Things</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Our_Sunday_Visitor" title="Our Sunday Visitor">Our Sunday Visitor</a></i>.
</p><p>Under his editorship, <i><a href="/wiki/Touchstone_(magazine)" title="Touchstone (magazine)">Touchstone</a></i> magazine won the Associated Church Press' "Award of Excellence" (First place) for journals the five years it was member, from 2004 to 2008, and his own articles won several awards. Before becoming editor of Touchstone, he served as director of publications at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trinity_Episcopal_School_for_Ministry" title="Trinity Episcopal School for Ministry">Trinity Episcopal School for Ministry</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ambridge,_Pennsylvania" title="Ambridge, Pennsylvania">Ambridge, Pennsylvania</a>, where he edited the seminary's journal <i>Mission &amp; Ministry</i>.
</p>
