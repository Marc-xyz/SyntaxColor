<p><b>Brian Hennessy</b> (born 29 August 1969) is an Irish retired <a href="/wiki/Hurling" title="Hurling">hurler</a> who played as a right corner-back for the <a href="/wiki/Offaly_GAA" title="Offaly GAA">Offaly</a> senior team.
</p><p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Birr,_County_Offaly" title="Birr, County Offaly">Birr, County Offaly</a>, Hennessy first played competitive hurling in his youth. He first came to prominence on the inter-county scene when he first linked up with the Offaly minor team, before later joining the under-21 side. He made his senior debut during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Hurling_League_1990-91" title="National Hurling League 1990-91">1990-91 National League</a> and immediately became a regular member of the team. During his career he won one <a href="/wiki/National_Hurling_League" title="National Hurling League">National Hurling League</a> medal on the field of play.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>At club level Hennessy is a one-time <a href="/wiki/All-Ireland_Senior_Club_Hurling_Championship" title="All-Ireland Senior Club Hurling Championship">All-Ireland</a> medallist with <a href="/wiki/Birr_GAA" title="Birr GAA">Birr</a>. In addition to this he also won four <a href="/wiki/Leinster_Senior_Club_Hurling_Championship" title="Leinster Senior Club Hurling Championship">Leinster</a> medals and five <a href="/wiki/Offaly_Senior_Hurling_Championship" title="Offaly Senior Hurling Championship">championship</a> medals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>His retirement came following the conclusion of the <a href="/wiki/1995_All-Ireland_Senior_Hurling_Championship" title="1995 All-Ireland Senior Hurling Championship">1995 championship</a>.
</p>
