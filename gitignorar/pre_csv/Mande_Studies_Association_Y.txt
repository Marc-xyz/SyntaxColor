<p>The <b>Mande Studies Association</b> (MANSA, also <i>Association des Études Mandé</i>) is an international organisation with an academic or professional interest in the <a href="/wiki/Mand%C3%A9_peoples" title="Mandé peoples">Mande region</a> of <a href="/wiki/West_Africa" title="West Africa">West Africa</a>. The organization was founded at the University of Wisconsin in 1986; it operates in both English and French. A newsletter, the <i>MANSA Newsletter</i>, was published from to 1986 to 2014; its official academic journal is <i>Mande Studies</i>. The acronym of the organization, <i>MANSA</i>, has an additional meaning of "king" in the <a href="/wiki/Bambara_language" title="Bambara language">Bambara language</a>. MANSA has been an associate organization of the <a href="/wiki/African_Studies_Association" title="African Studies Association">African Studies Association</a>, a broader academic organisation related to African studies, since 1993.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-m_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-m-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mande_Studies"><i>Mande Studies</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mande_Studies_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Mande Studies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Mande Studies</i> is an annual <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peer-reviewed" title="Peer-reviewed">peer-reviewed</a> <a href="/wiki/Academic_journal" title="Academic journal">academic journal</a> covering all aspects of the <a href="/wiki/Mand%C3%A9_peoples" title="Mandé peoples">Mandé peoples</a> of Africa. It is published by the Indiana University Press on behalf of the Mande Studies Association.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ms_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ms-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
