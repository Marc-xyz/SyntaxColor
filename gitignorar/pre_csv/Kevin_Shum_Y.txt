<p><b>Kevin Shum</b> (born July 3, 1997) is an American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Figure_skater" title="Figure skater">figure skater</a>. He competed at the <a href="/wiki/2015_World_Junior_Figure_Skating_Championships" title="2015 World Junior Figure Skating Championships">2015 World Junior Championships</a> and qualified for the free skate.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kevin_Shum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shum was born on July 3, 1997 in <a href="/wiki/Walnut_Creek,_California" title="Walnut Creek, California">Walnut Creek, California</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ISU-1617_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-ISU-1617-1">[1]</a></sup> He is studying computer science and engineering at the <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology" title="Massachusetts Institute of Technology">Massachusetts Institute of Technology</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IN170303_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-IN170303-2">[2]</a></sup> where he also works as an admissions blogger.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MIT-Adm_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-MIT-Adm-3">[3]</a></sup> He is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Beta_Theta_Pi" title="Beta Theta Pi">Beta Theta Pi</a> fraternity.
</p><p>He credits his success to inspiration found in Brink's recent album <i>Ginger Ale.</i> Brink is also a brother of Beta Theta Pi.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kevin_Shum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shum began learning to skate in 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ISU-1617_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-ISU-1617-1">[1]</a></sup> His international debut came in March 2014 at the <a href="/wiki/International_Challenge_Cup" title="International Challenge Cup">International Challenge Cup</a> in <a href="/wiki/The_Hague" title="The Hague">The Hague</a>, Netherlands. In September of the same year, he made his first <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_ISU_Junior_Grand_Prix" title="2014–15 ISU Junior Grand Prix">Junior Grand Prix</a> (JGP) appearance, placing 7th in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aichi" title="Aichi">Aichi</a>, Japan.
</p><p>After winning the junior silver medal at the <a href="/wiki/2015_U.S._Figure_Skating_Championships" title="2015 U.S. Figure Skating Championships">2015 U.S. Championships</a>, Shum was assigned to the <a href="/wiki/2015_World_Junior_Figure_Skating_Championships" title="2015 World Junior Figure Skating Championships">2015 World Junior Championships</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tallinn" title="Tallinn">Tallinn</a>, Estonia. He qualified for the free skate after placing 16th in the short program and went on to finish 20th overall.
</p><p>Shum is coached by <a href="/wiki/Peter_Johansson_(figure_skater)" title="Peter Johansson (figure skater)">Peter Johansson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Mitchell_(American_figure_skater)" title="Mark Mitchell (American figure skater)">Mark Mitchell</a> at the Skating Club of Boston.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ISU-1617_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-ISU-1617-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
