<p><b>Charles Cameron Cherry</b> (19 November 1872 – 2 September 1931) was a British born actor. He was born to James Frederick Cherry (died 1883) and his wife, Lady Emily Louisa Haworth-Leslie (died 1936) at Greenwich, Kent, England. His mother was a relation to <a href="/wiki/Norman_Leslie,_19th_Earl_of_Rothes" title="Norman Leslie, 19th Earl of Rothes">Norman Leslie, 19th Earl of Rothes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> He spent a large part of his career in the United States often as leading man to many beautiful star actresses i.e. <a href="/wiki/Elsie_de_Wolfe" title="Elsie de Wolfe">Elsie de Wolfe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maxine_Elliott" title="Maxine Elliott">Maxine Elliott</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ethel_Barrymore" title="Ethel Barrymore">Ethel Barrymore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marie_Doro" title="Marie Doro">Marie Doro</a> and <a href="/wiki/Elsie_Ferguson" title="Elsie Ferguson">Elsie Ferguson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>He appeared in two silent motion pictures, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Mummy_and_the_Hummingbird" title="The Mummy and the Hummingbird">The Mummy and the Hummingbird</a></i> (1915) and <i>Passers By</i> (1916).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>His sisters were Miriam Audrey Cherry (died 1954) (Mrs. Herbert Owen-Taylor) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gladys_Cherry" title="Gladys Cherry">Gladys Cherry</a> (1881–1965) (Mrs. George O. S. Pringle), a <a href="/wiki/RMS_Titanic" title="RMS Titanic"><i>Titanic</i></a> survivor. His spouse was Grace Dudley.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
