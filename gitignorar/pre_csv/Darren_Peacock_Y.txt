<p><b>Darren Peacock</b> (born 3 February 1968) is an English football manager and former professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who played as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Centre-back" title="Defender (association football)">centre-back</a>. From 2013 to 2015, he was the manager of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northern_Premier_League_Division_One_North" title="Northern Premier League Division One North">Northern Premier League Division One North</a> club <a href="/wiki/Lancaster_City_F.C." title="Lancaster City F.C.">Lancaster City</a>.
</p><p>He made nearly 400 league appearances, beginning his career in the lower reaches of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Football_League" title="The Football League">the Football League</a>, before spending eight seasons in the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> for <a href="/wiki/Queens_Park_Rangers_F.C." title="Queens Park Rangers F.C.">Queens Park Rangers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a> and Blackburn. Peacock retired to the Algarve after injury ended his career .
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Football_career">Football career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Darren_Peacock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Football career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After being released by <a href="/wiki/Bristol_Rovers_F.C." title="Bristol Rovers F.C.">Bristol Rovers</a>, Peacock started his career in the lower leagues at <a href="/wiki/Newport_County_A.F.C." title="Newport County A.F.C.">Newport County</a>, and when the club folded in 1989 he signed for <a href="/wiki/Hereford_United_F.C." title="Hereford United F.C.">Hereford United</a>. In his first full season with the Bulls, he was voted player of the year, featuring regularly in the team that won the <a href="/wiki/Welsh_Cup" title="Welsh Cup">Welsh Cup</a> in 1990.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Peacock's performances with Hereford earned him a move to <a href="/wiki/Football_League_First_Division" title="Football League First Division">First Division</a> club <a href="/wiki/Queens_Park_Rangers_F.C." title="Queens Park Rangers F.C.">Queens Park Rangers</a> in 1990 for £200,000. He was a member of the QPR team that beat <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a> 4–1 at <a href="/wiki/Old_Trafford" title="Old Trafford">Old Trafford</a> in front of a live television audience on New Year's Day 1992.
</p><p>After having made over 100 league appearances for QPR, Peacock was purchased by <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Keegan" title="Kevin Keegan">Kevin Keegan</a> of <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a> on 24 March 1994, for £2.7 million, to strengthen their defence in <a href="/wiki/1993%E2%80%9394_FA_Premier_League" title="1993–94 FA Premier League">their first season</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a>. A sell-on clause meant that Hereford received a total fee of £440,000 for the player, which remains, as of February 2010, their record transfer fee received.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He helped the Magpies to a third-place finish, which earned the club <a href="/wiki/1994%E2%80%9395_UEFA_Cup" title="1994–95 UEFA Cup">qualification for the UEFA Cup</a>.
</p><p>Newcastle won their first six league games in the <a href="/wiki/1994%E2%80%9395_FA_Premier_League" title="1994–95 FA Premier League">1994–95 season</a>, and there was talk of a first top-flight title since 1927, but they lost form and finished in sixth position, out of the European qualification zone. The following season, Peacock was part of the team that lost the Premier League title to Manchester United after having a 10-point lead at Christmas. He scored only twice for the Magpies, one of those coming in a 5–0 home defeat of Manchester United on 20 October 1996. This was Keegan's final season as club manager, and his replacement, <a href="/wiki/Kenny_Dalglish" title="Kenny Dalglish">Kenny Dalglish</a>, gradually broke up his predecessor's team.
</p><p>Peacock made 176 appearances for Newcastle in all competitions, scoring 4 goals, and moved on to <a href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C." title="Blackburn Rovers F.C.">Blackburn Rovers</a> in 1998, where his serious injury problems began.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He scored one goal for Rovers, against <a href="/wiki/Southampton_F.C." title="Southampton F.C.">Southampton</a> in April 1999,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> but soon lost his place in the team and served loan spells at <a href="/wiki/West_Ham_United_F.C." title="West Ham United F.C.">West Ham United</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wolverhampton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.">Wolverhampton Wanderers</a>.
</p><p>While on loan at Wolves, Peacock collided with his own <a href="/wiki/Goalkeeper_(association_football)" title="Goalkeeper (association football)">goalkeeper</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Oakes" title="Michael Oakes">Michael Oakes</a>, in a match against <a href="/wiki/Fulham_F.C." title="Fulham F.C.">Fulham</a>, suffering damaged vertebrae in his neck and compression to his spine. He played on in the match despite being knocked unconscious, and it was only on his return to Blackburn that it became clear how close he had come to <a href="/wiki/Paralysis" title="Paralysis">paralysis</a>. He retired from football in December 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Managerial_career">Managerial career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Darren_Peacock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Managerial career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

</p>
