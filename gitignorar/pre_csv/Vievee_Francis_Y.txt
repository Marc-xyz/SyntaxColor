<p><b>Vievee Elaure Francis</b> (<span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="'v' in 'vie'">v</span><span title="/aɪ/: 'i' in 'tide'">aɪ</span><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'v' in 'vie'">v</span><span title="/iː/: 'ee' in 'fleece'">iː</span></span>/</a></span></span>) is an American <a href="/wiki/Poet" title="Poet">poet</a>. She is an associate professor of English at <a href="/wiki/Dartmouth_College" title="Dartmouth College">Dartmouth College</a>. She earned an MFA from the University of Michigan in 2009, and she received a Rona Jaffe Award the same year. She is the author of three collections of the poetry, the third of which, <i>Forest Primeval</i>, won the 2016 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurston/Wright_Legacy_Award" title="Hurston/Wright Legacy Award">Hurston/Wright Legacy Award</a> for poetry<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and the 2017 Kingsley Tufts (<a href="/wiki/The_Kingsley_and_Kate_Tufts_Poetry_Awards" title="The Kingsley and Kate Tufts Poetry Awards">Kingsley and Kate Tufts Poetry Awards</a>) poetry award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vievee_Francis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Francis is a native of Texas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> She lived and worked in Detroit, Michigan for fifteen years where she was instrumental in fostering a literary community for youth, young-adult and adult poets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> From there, she moved to Swannanoa, North Carolina while teaching at Warren Wilson College (undergraduate) and eventually <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina_State_University" title="North Carolina State University">North Carolina State University</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Francis is married to poet <a href="/wiki/Matthew_Olzmann" title="Matthew Olzmann">Matthew Olzmann</a>, author of <i>Mezzanines</i> (Alice James Press) and <i>Contradictions in the Design</i> (Alice James Press) a native of Detroit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vievee_Francis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Francis is an associate professor in the department of English and Creative Writing at Dartmouth College,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> where she continues to write poetry. She is also an associate editor of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Callaloo_(journal)" title="Callaloo (journal)">Callaloo (journal)</a>, and a faculty member at the 2018 Conference on Poetry at <a href="/wiki/The_Frost_Place" title="The Frost Place">The Frost Place</a> in Franconia, NH. Prior to joining Dartmouth, she taught writing and poetry at North Carolina State University among other colleges and universities.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vievee_Francis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Writing for the judging committee for the <a href="/wiki/The_Kingsley_and_Kate_Tufts_Poetry_Awards" title="The Kingsley and Kate Tufts Poetry Awards">Kingsley and Kate Tufts Poetry Awards</a>, Don Share, editor of <a href="/wiki/Poetry_(magazine)" title="Poetry (magazine)"><i>Poetry</i> magazine</a> praised <i>Forest Primeval</i> as “an intense work, dark … Dantean … dreamlike in its visions.... Francis is reclaiming modernist and feminist legacies of poetry, and it takes great courage to do that.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
