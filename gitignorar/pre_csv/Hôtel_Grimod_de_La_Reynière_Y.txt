	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Hôtel Grimod de La Reynière</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>Hôtel Grimod de La Reynière</b> was an <a href="/wiki/H%C3%B4tel_particulier" title="Hôtel particulier">hôtel particulier</a> in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, in the corner between Avenue Gabriel and Rue Boissy d'Anglas.
</p><p>It was built in 1775 in a <a href="/wiki/Neoclassicism" title="Neoclassicism">Neo-Classical</a> style by <a href="/wiki/Jean-Beno%C3%AEt-Vincent_Barr%C3%A9" title="Jean-Benoît-Vincent Barré">Jean-Benoît-Vincent Barré</a> for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fermier_g%C3%A9n%C3%A9ral" title="Fermier général">fermier général</a> <a href="/wiki/Laurent_Grimod_de_La_Reyni%C3%A8re" title="Laurent Grimod de La Reynière">Laurent Grimod de La Reynière</a> (1733–1793).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Milam2011_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Milam2011-1">[1]</a></sup> It used a plot occupied by a store for ancient statues in the royal collection, on which Grimod de La Reynière had obtained a royal concession to construct a building similar to the hôtel de Saint-Florentin (which had been constructed in the northeastern corner of the new Place Louis XV, now <a href="/wiki/Place_de_la_Concorde" title="Place de la Concorde">Place de la Concorde</a>, to plans by <a href="/wiki/Ange-Jacques_Gabriel" title="Ange-Jacques Gabriel">Ange-Jacques Gabriel</a>).  
</p><p>The layout of the rooms is known from a relief by the architect <a href="/wiki/Johann_Christian_Kammsetzer" title="Johann Christian Kammsetzer">Johann Christian Kammsetzer</a>, preserved at <a href="/wiki/Krak%C3%B3w" title="Kraków">Cracow</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grand_salon" title="Grand salon">grand salon</a> and the state rooms gave onto an English garden spread between the south facade and the gardens of the <a href="/wiki/Champs-%C3%89lys%C3%A9es" title="Champs-Élysées">Champs-Élysées</a>.  The dining room was located in the west wing, between two courtyards and a small, oval internal garden, with heating.  Two fountains were placed in a gallery between the kitchen and the buffet, a gallery reached through a billiards room and an octagonal hall.  On the other side of the main courtyard was a picture gallery and a library, which gave onto Rue de la Bonne-Morue.  
</p><p>In the interior, <a href="/wiki/Charles-Louis_Cl%C3%A9risseau" title="Charles-Louis Clérisseau">Charles-Louis Clérisseau</a> and <a href="/wiki/%C3%89tienne_de_La_Vall%C3%A9e_Poussin" title="Étienne de La Vallée Poussin">Étienne de La Vallée Poussin</a> executed the first decorative scheme in Europe to be inspired by the new archaeological discoveries at <a href="/wiki/Pompeii" title="Pompeii">Pompeii</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Herculanum" title="Herculanum">Herculanum</a>.  The painted <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boiserie" title="Boiserie">boiseries</a></i> were sold in 1850 and may be seen in part at the <a href="/wiki/Victoria_and_Albert_Museum" title="Victoria and Albert Museum">Victoria and Albert Museum</a> in London.
</p><p>Up until the 19th century, the Hôtel housed the imperial Cercle, then the Cercle de l'Union artistique - the latter held some exhibitions by the Society of Watercolourists here in 1914.  Disfigured by successive additions, it was razed to the ground in 1932 and replaced by a neoclassical pastiche, built between 1931 and 1933 by the architects <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_Delano" title="William Delano">William Delano</a> and <a href="/wiki/Victor_Laloux" title="Victor Laloux">Victor Laloux</a> to house the <a href="/wiki/Embassy_of_the_United_States,_Paris" title="Embassy of the United States, Paris">US embassy</a>.
</p>
