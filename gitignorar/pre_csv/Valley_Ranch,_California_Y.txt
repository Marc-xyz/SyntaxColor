<p><b>Valley Ranch</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Census-designated_place" title="Census-designated place">census-designated place</a> in <a href="/wiki/Plumas_County,_California" title="Plumas County, California">Plumas County</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, United States. The population was 109 at the 2010 census, up from 92 at the 2000 census.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Valley_Ranch,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Valley Ranch is located at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Valley_Ranch,_California&amp;params=39_44_18_N_120_34_4_W_type:city"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">39°44′18″N</span> <span class="longitude">120°34′4″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">39.73833°N 120.56778°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">39.73833; -120.56778</span></span></span></a></span> (39.738392, -120.567839).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR1-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the CDP has a total area of 1.3 square miles (3.4 km<sup>2</sup>), all of it land.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Valley_Ranch,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010">2010</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Valley_Ranch,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2010">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 United States Census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> reported that Valley Ranch had a population of 109. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 95.8 people per square mile (37.0/km²). The racial makeup of Valley Ranch was 107 (98.2%) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 0 (0.0%) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 0 (0.0%) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 0 (0.0%) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 0 (0.0%) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander_(U.S._Census)" title="Pacific Islander (U.S. Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 1 (0.9%) from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 1 (0.9%) from two or more races.  <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 2 persons (1.8%).
</p><p>The Census reported that 109 people (100% of the population) lived in households, 0 (0%) lived in non-institutionalized group quarters, and 0 (0%) were institutionalized.
</p><p>There were 58 households, out of which 5 (8.6%) had children under the age of 18 living in them, 39 (67.2%) were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">opposite-sex married couples</a> living together, 2 (3.4%) had a female householder with no husband present, 2 (3.4%) had a male householder with no wife present.  There were 1 (1.7%) <a href="/wiki/POSSLQ" title="POSSLQ">unmarried opposite-sex partnerships</a>, and 2 (3.4%) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Same-sex_partnerships" title="Same-sex partnerships">same-sex married couples or partnerships</a>. 11 households (19.0%) were made up of individuals and 4 (6.9%) had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 1.88.  There were 43 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Family_(U.S._Census)" title="Family (U.S. Census)">families</a> (74.1% of all households); the average family size was 2.09.
</p><p>The population was spread out with 5 people (4.6%) under the age of 18, 0 people (0%) aged 18 to 24, 6 people (5.5%) aged 25 to 44, 49 people (45.0%) aged 45 to 64, and 49 people (45.0%) who were 65 years of age or older.  The median age was 63.8 years. For every 100 females, there were 101.9 males.  For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 92.6 males.
</p><p>There were 82 housing units at an average density of 72.1 per square mile (27.8/km²), of which 54 (93.1%) were owner-occupied, and 4 (6.9%) were occupied by renters. The homeowner vacancy rate was 0%; the rental vacancy rate was 33.3%.  104 people (95.4% of the population) lived in owner-occupied housing units and 5 people (4.6%) lived in rental housing units.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000">2000</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Valley_Ranch,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2000">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-4">[4]</a></sup> of 2000, there were 92 people, 42 households, and 39 families residing in the CDP. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 54.6 people per square mile (21.0/km²). There were 52 housing units at an average density of 30.9 per square mile (11.9/km²). The racial makeup of the CDP was 100.00% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 2.17% of the population.
</p><p>There were 42 households out of which 11.9% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 88.1% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 2.4% had a female householder with no husband present, and 4.8% were non-families. 4.8% of all households were made up of individuals and none had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.19 and the average family size was 2.20.
</p><p>In the CDP, the population was spread out with 7.6% under the age of 18, 5.4% from 18 to 24, 8.7% from 25 to 44, 52.2% from 45 to 64, and 26.1% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 57 years. For every 100 females, there were 114.0 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 107.3 males.
</p><p>The median income for a household in the CDP was $82,736, and the median income for a family was $82,736. Males had a median income of $61,250 versus $17,679 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the CDP was $32,505. None of the population and none of the families were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Valley_Ranch,_California&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/California_State_Legislature" title="California State Legislature">state legislature</a>, Valley Ranch is in  <a href="/wiki/California%27s_1st_State_Senate_district" title="California's 1st State Senate district">the  1st Senate District</a>, represented by <a href="/wiki/California_Republican_Party" title="California Republican Party">Republican</a> <a href="/wiki/Brian_Dahle" title="Brian Dahle">Brian Dahle</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/California%27s_1st_State_Assembly_district" title="California's 1st State Assembly district">the  1st Assembly District</a>, represented by <a href="/wiki/California_Republican_Party" title="California Republican Party">Republican</a> <a href="/wiki/Megan_Dahle" title="Megan Dahle">Megan Dahle</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>Federally, Valley Ranch is in <a href="/wiki/California%27s_1st_congressional_district" title="California's 1st congressional district">California's  1st congressional district</a>, represented by <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republican</a> <a href="/wiki/Doug_LaMalfa" title="Doug LaMalfa">Doug LaMalfa</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
