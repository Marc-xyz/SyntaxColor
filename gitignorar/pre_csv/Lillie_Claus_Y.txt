<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lillie_Claus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She received  a musical education in her youth (vocals, piano, <a href="/wiki/Dance_studio" title="Dance studio">dance school</a>).  When she was 16 she entered the Vienna <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Academy_of_Music_(Vienna)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Academy of Music (Vienna) (page does not exist)">Academy of Music</a>.  She debuted as Papagena in Mozart's <a href="/wiki/The_Magic_Flute" title="The Magic Flute">Magic Flute</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Vienna_State_Opera" title="Vienna State Opera">Vienna State Opera</a>.
</p><p>After engagements at several theatres, she returned to the Vienna State Opera.  There, she heard the composer <a href="/wiki/Nico_Dostal" title="Nico Dostal">Nico Dostal</a> was in search a leading singer for his operetta "Clivia".  On 23 December 1933, she took part in the premiere in the Berlin theatre on Nollendorfplatz.
</p><p>On 30 November 1934, she sang the 'Lied der Lulu' (part of the five-movement concert suite version of Alban Berg's opera <i><a href="/wiki/Lulu_(opera)" title="Lulu (opera)">Lulu</a></i>) at the <a href="/wiki/Berlin_State_Opera" title="Berlin State Opera">Berlin Staatsoper</a> <a href="/wiki/Unter_den_Linden" title="Unter den Linden">Unter den Linden</a> under <a href="/wiki/Erich_Kleiber" title="Erich Kleiber">Erich Kleiber</a>.
</p><p>In the ensuing period Lillie Claus focused increasingly on operetta. <a href="/wiki/Nico_Dostal" title="Nico Dostal">Nico Dostal</a> wrote several of his operettas for her voice.
</p>
<ul><li>1933 <a href="/wiki/Clivia_(opera)" title="Clivia (opera)">Clivia (opera)</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li>1935 <a href="/wiki/Die_Vielgeliebte" title="Die Vielgeliebte">Die Vielgeliebte</a> ( "I am in love")</li>
<li>1936 <a href="/wiki/Prinzessin_Nofretete" title="Prinzessin Nofretete">Prinzessin Nofretete</a></li>
<li>1937 Extra leaves ( "I forgot my heart away")</li>
<li>1937 <a href="/wiki/Monika_(opera)" title="Monika (opera)">Monika</a> ( "Home Song")</li>
<li>1939 <a href="/wiki/Die_ungarische_Hochzeit" title="Die ungarische Hochzeit">Die ungarische Hochzeit</a> ( "I play the song of happiness and litter")</li></ul>
<p>In 1942 she married Nico Dostal and retired from the stage.  Their son  Roman Dostal, later to be a conductor, was born in 1943 and the family lived in Salzburg. At Nico Dostal's 100th birthday celebration in 1995, the 90-year-old Lillie Claus Dostal was still in the best of health and participated in the events. She made several records.
</p>
