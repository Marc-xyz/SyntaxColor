<p><b>Narechen</b> (<a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_language" title="Bulgarian language">Bulgarian</a>: <span lang="bg">Наречен</span>), also called Narechenski Bani (<a href="/wiki/Bulgarian_language" title="Bulgarian language">Bulgarian</a>: <span lang="bg">Нареченски Бани</span>), is a village in  <a href="/wiki/Plovdiv_Province" title="Plovdiv Province">Plovdiv Province</a>, central <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a>. As of 2013<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Narechen&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup> it had 719 inhabitants. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It is set amid pine forest on the Asenitsa River (formerly named the Chepelare River) in the Rhodope mountains., 42 km southwest of <a href="/wiki/Plovdiv" title="Plovdiv">Plovdiv</a>. The village is notable for its hypothermal mineral springs which have transformed it into an important spa resort. Neli Andreeva, well-known Bulgarian folk singer, is from Narechen. 
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Narechen_Glacier" title="Narechen Glacier">Narechen Glacier</a> on <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Island" title="Alexander Island">Alexander Island</a> in <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">Antarctica</a> is named after Narechen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
