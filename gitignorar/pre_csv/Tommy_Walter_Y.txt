<p><b>Thomas Edward "Tommy" Walter</b> (born October 30, 1970, <a href="/wiki/Thousand_Oaks,_California" title="Thousand Oaks, California">Thousand Oaks, California</a>) is an American musician and songwriter, best known for his <a href="/wiki/Alternative_rock" title="Alternative rock">alternative rock</a> band, <b>Abandoned Pools</b>, as well as being the former bassist and one of the founding members of <a href="/wiki/Eels_(band)" title="Eels (band)">Eels</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years_and_the_Eels">Early years and the Eels</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tommy_Walter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early years and the Eels">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tommy Walter was raised in Westlake Village. His father was an airplane pilot; his mother a stewardess. His father is from Canada,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and was almost 47 when Tommy was born. He grew up in a modest, middle-class household. He began playing bass at a young age, and was formally trained on the French horn in college. He attended the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Southern_California" title="University of Southern California">University of Southern California</a>, followed by <a href="/wiki/Pacific_University" title="Pacific University">Pacific University</a>. He began teaching <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_classical_music" title="European classical music">classical music</a> <a href="/wiki/Music_theory" title="Music theory">theory</a>, and worked with local Los Angeles area musicians.
</p><p>He met with singer-songwriter <a href="/wiki/Mark_Oliver_Everett" title="Mark Oliver Everett">Mark Oliver Everett</a> (known as E), and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Butch_Norton" title="Butch Norton">Butch Norton</a> and formed Eels. Prior to their founding, E had already released two records by himself, under his single-letter pseudonym, and their name was decided upon so that their music would be placed next to E's solo works. However, it was in retrospect that they realized that there were bands that would be placed in between, such as <a href="/wiki/Eagles_(band)" title="Eagles (band)">Eagles</a>.
</p><p>Their collaborations led to refining songs that E had previously written, as well as creating new material. They released their debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Beautiful_Freak" title="Beautiful Freak">Beautiful Freak</a></i>, in 1996. However, after a year of touring, Walter left the band. In an interview, he stated his dissatisfaction with E's personality, and the loss of the amount of creative input that both Butch and himself had prior to the success of Beautiful Freak.
</p><p>Soon after, he formed a band called Metromax. They shortly changed their name to Tely, and released an album over the Internet. However, the band didn't garner much success, and it soon evolved into Walter's solo project, Abandoned Pools.
</p>
<h2><span id="Humanistic_.282001.E2.80.932004.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Humanistic_(2001–2004)"><i>Humanistic</i> (2001–2004)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tommy_Walter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Humanistic (2001–2004)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Walter, using new material, as well as several songs he had worked on beforehand in both Tely and Metromax, composed, recorded, and released his solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/Humanistic_(album)" title="Humanistic (album)">Humanistic</a></i>, in 2001 on <a href="/wiki/Extasy_Records" title="Extasy Records">Extasy Records</a>. While being the creative force behind the project, other musicians contributed to the project, such as Angie Hart of <a href="/wiki/Frente!" title="Frente!">Frente!</a>, who sang backing vocals to "Start Over," "Ruin Your Life," "Sunny Day," and "Seed."
</p><p>Although a solo project, he recruited a touring band, featuring <a href="/wiki/Leah_Randi" title="Leah Randi">Leah Randi</a> and Bryan Head filling out the ranks of bassist and drummer, respectively. They had several successful tours, going across the country and headlining with acts such as <a href="/wiki/Garbage_(band)" title="Garbage (band)">Garbage</a>, <a href="/wiki/A_Perfect_Circle" title="A Perfect Circle">A Perfect Circle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lenny_Kravitz" title="Lenny Kravitz">Lenny Kravitz</a>. They also appeared on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Late_Late_Show_(CBS_TV_series)" title="The Late Late Show (CBS TV series)">The Late Late Show with Craig Kilborn</a></i> and two videos, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mercy_Kiss" title="Mercy Kiss">Mercy Kiss</a>", and "The Remedy", were released. Monster, another video, was composed of shots taken while on touring, but wasn't released for years, via MTV's website.
</p><p>In 2002 they performed the theme song for the short-lived <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Teletoon_(Canada)" title="Teletoon (Canada)">Teletoon</a> (also broadcast on <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>) animated series <i><a href="/wiki/Clone_High" title="Clone High">Clone High</a></i>.   Many of their songs can be heard in the background during the show. Walter also voiced himself in a brief appearance in the final episode. After <i>Clone High</i> was dropped by MTV, however, the band stopped its touring (largely due to the dissolution of their label, <a href="/wiki/Extasy_Records" title="Extasy Records">Extasy Records</a>).
</p>
<h2><span id="Armed_to_the_Teeth_.282005.E2.80.932007.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Armed_to_the_Teeth_(2005–2007)"><i>Armed to the Teeth</i> (2005–2007)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tommy_Walter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Armed to the Teeth (2005–2007)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>While keeping in touch with his fanbase and caring for his sick cat, Iggy, Walter began working on material over the next few years, preparing to release another album. After a relationship of his came to a rough conclusion, he was picked up by <a href="/wiki/Universal_Music_Group" title="Universal Music Group">Universal</a>, and wrote several new songs for his next album, citing his personal life, as well as politics, as his main inspirations.
</p><p>Teaming up with Bryan Head once more and bringing in guitarist Sean Woolstenhulme, Walter started work in 2004 to record his next album. In June 2005 they released an EP, consisting of a few songs from the new album along with b-sides and demos, called <i>The Reverb EP</i>. This was followed by the full album, <i><a href="/wiki/Armed_to_the_Teeth_(Abandoned_Pools_album)" title="Armed to the Teeth (Abandoned Pools album)">Armed to the Teeth</a></i>, in September 2005
</p><p>In January 2006, Walter wrote in his online journal that <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universal_Records" title="Universal Records">Universal Records</a> had stopped promoting the album. He subsequently quit the label, also noting that Abandoned Pools is once again a one-man project. He has since created two side projects—Glacier Hiking, an alternative rock band, and Oliver the Penguin, an electronica project.
</p>
<h2><span id="Sublime_Currency_.282011.E2.80.932012.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Sublime_Currency_(2011–2012)"><i>Sublime Currency</i> (2011–2012)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tommy_Walter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sublime Currency (2011–2012)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On May 3, 2011, Abandoned Pools released the song "In Silence", available in all digital stores, as the first single from the upcoming album <i>Sublime Currency</i>.  On June 7, 2011, the second single, "Marigolds" was released in all digital stores.
</p><p>On January 30, 2012, Abandoned Pools revealed that they had signed onto a new record label, <a href="/wiki/Tooth_%26_Nail_Records" title="Tooth &amp; Nail Records">Tooth &amp; Nail Records</a>, and that <i>Sublime Currency</i>  would be released under this label. They also mentioned that the release date of the third album will be revealed "soon".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>It was revealed on May 17, 2012, that <i>Sublime Currency</i> would feature 11 tracks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> On July 10, 2012, it was revealed that <i>Sublime Currency</i> would be released on August 28, 2012, and the final artwork for the album was shown.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 26, 2012, the album's title track premiered on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alternative_Press_(music_magazine)" title="Alternative Press (music magazine)">Alternative Press</a>' website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> On August 14, 2012, the track was officially released as the album's third single.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The next day, the track "Unrehearsed" was made free to download on <a href="/wiki/RCRD_LBL" title="RCRD LBL">RCRD LBL</a>'s website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> On August 22, 2012, the track "Behemoth" premiered on <a href="/wiki/CMJ" title="CMJ">CMJ</a>'s website.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Sublime Currency</i> was released on August 28, 2012, and a music video for the single "Sublime Currency" was released on September 7.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="Somnambulist_.282013.E2.80.93present.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Somnambulist_(2013–present)"><i>Somnambulist</i> (2013–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tommy_Walter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Somnambulist (2013–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On June 6, 2013, the Abandoned Pools Twitter account announced that the band's next LP, entitled <i>Somnambulist</i>, will be released on July 2, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Shortly after, it was announced that album will be delayed by a week, and will instead be released on July 9, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>On November 26, 2013, Abandoned Pools released a cover of the Christmas song "<a href="/wiki/The_First_Noel" title="The First Noel">The First Noel</a>" through the album <i>Hype Music Presents Holidays, Vol. 1</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
