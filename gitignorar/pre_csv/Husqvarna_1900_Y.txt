<p><b>Husqvarna 1900</b>, later <b>Carl Gustaf 1900</b>, is a bolt action rifle developed in Sweden in the 1960s by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Husqvarna_Vapenfabriks" title="Husqvarna Vapenfabriks">Husqvarna Vapenfabrik</a>, which also produced the rifle from 1967 to 1970.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jof_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-jof-1">[1]</a></sup> The rifle was produced in the variants Standard, Monte Carlo, Monte Carlo Lux and a sport model, and got a reputation for having a very high quality.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jof_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-jof-1">[1]</a></sup> In 1970 the department producing the rifle was sold to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=F%C3%B6renade_Fabriksverken&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Förenade Fabriksverken (page does not exist)">Förenade Fabriksverken</a> (FFV), and the production was moved to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carl_Gustafs_Gev%C3%A4rsfaktori" title="Carl Gustafs Gevärsfaktori">Carl Gustafs Gevärsfaktori</a> in <a href="/wiki/Eskilstuna" title="Eskilstuna">Eskilstuna</a> without large changes. Production in Eskilstuna was ended in 1979.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Copies">Copies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Husqvarna_1900&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Copies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It has been claimed that the factory towards the end of the 1970s was instructed to reduce costs, resulting in parts of the production being moved to subcontractors in southern Europe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jof_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-jof-1">[1]</a></sup> The quality of the assembled firearm is then claimed to have varied a lot from year to year. After a while copies of the rifle also started appearing on the market, for example under the names "Viking 1900" and "SG 1900" (SG meansing <i>svenskt gevär</i>, lit. <i>Swedish Rifle</i>). All these were allegedly produced or assembled in the Zoli factory in Italy.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> One of these copies is still sold today under the name <b>Antonio Zoli 1900</b>. Antonio Zoli has since bought the design.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Technical">Technical</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Husqvarna_1900&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Technical">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Stock">Stock</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Husqvarna_1900&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Stock">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Husqvarna 1900 stock was made of walnut, and had a Monte Carlo shape. The internal magazine had a staggered feed, and the firearm safety was placed on the side.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Action">Action</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Husqvarna_1900&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Action">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The action was made in one length only, but with four different lengths of bolt travel depending on the caliber. The bolt travel and magazine was thus fitted according to the cartridge length. These lengths were fitted to calibers such as <a href="/wiki/.222_Remington" title=".222 Remington">.222</a>/<a href="/wiki/.223_Remington" title=".223 Remington">.223 Rem</a>, <a href="/wiki/.243_Winchester" title=".243 Winchester">.243 Win</a>/<a href="/wiki/.308_Winchester" title=".308 Winchester">.308 Win</a>, <a href="/wiki/6.5%C3%9755mm_Swedish" title="6.5×55mm Swedish">6.5×55mm</a> and <a href="/wiki/.30-06_Springfield" title=".30-06 Springfield">30-06</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Rails for mounting a scope to the receiver are available, for instance <a href="/wiki/Weaver_rail_mount" title="Weaver rail mount">Weaver</a> or <a href="/wiki/Picatinny_rail" title="Picatinny rail">Picatinny</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Barrel">Barrel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Husqvarna_1900&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Barrel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The barrel is attached to the receiver using traditional <a href="/wiki/Action_threads" title="Action threads">action threads</a>. The rifle was delivered from factory in calibers such as <a href="/wiki/.222_Remington" title=".222 Remington">.222 Rem</a>, <a href="/wiki/.223_Remington" title=".223 Remington">.223 Rem</a>, <a href="/wiki/.22-250_Remington" title=".22-250 Remington">.22-250</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/.243_Win" title=".243 Win">.243 Win</a>, <a href="/wiki/6.5%C3%9755mm_Swedish" title="6.5×55mm Swedish">6,5 × 55 mm</a>, <a href="/wiki/.270_Winchester" title=".270 Winchester">.270 Win</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/7_mm_Remington_Magnum" title="7 mm Remington Magnum">7 mm Rem Mag</a>, <a href="/wiki/.300_Winchester_Magnum" title=".300 Winchester Magnum">.300 Win Mag</a>, <a href="/wiki/.308_Winchester" title=".308 Winchester">.308 Win</a>, <a href="/wiki/.30-06_Springfield" title=".30-06 Springfield">.30-06</a> and <a href="/wiki/9.3%C3%9762mm" title="9.3×62mm">9,3 × 62 mm</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jof_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-jof-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Trigger">Trigger</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Husqvarna_1900&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Trigger">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The standard trigger was a single stage, but the rifle was also available with a set trigger.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The trigger pull weight was set to 1500 grams from the factory.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Carl_Gustaf_2000">Carl Gustaf 2000</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Husqvarna_1900&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Carl Gustaf 2000">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Carl Gustaf CG 2000 is a successor of the CG 1900.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>Just when CG 2000 was about to be launched, Carl Gustaf received a large order from the <a href="/wiki/Swedish_Armed_Forces" title="Swedish Armed Forces">Swedish Armed Forces</a> for the Kulspruta 58 (ksp m/58, a licensed <a href="/wiki/FN_MAG" title="FN MAG">FN MAG</a> copy). The civilian production of CG 2000 was therefore postponed, and Carl Gustaf instead purchased the rights to import finished and semi-finished rifles from Sauer. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sauer_80" title="Sauer 80">Sauer 80</a> was sold under the name <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carl_Gustaf_3000" title="Carl Gustaf 3000">Carl Gustaf 3000</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Sauer_200" title="Sauer 200">Sauer 200</a> was sold as <a href="/wiki/Sauer_200" title="Sauer 200">Carl Gustaf 4000</a>. The receivers were made in Germany and had <a href="/wiki/House_mark" title="House mark">house marks</a> from Sauer. The barrel and stock was usually produced and fitted in Sweden. Some models were produced entirely in Germany. CG 3000 and CG 4000 was sold by Carl Gustaf until they decided to resume production of the model 2000 towards the end of the last century.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>There are two generations of the CG 2000, which are called MKI and MKII respectively. MKI is said to have had some recurring minor issues, like a weak <a href="/wiki/Bolt_(firearms)" title="Bolt (firearms)">bolt</a> spring and oil on the bolt spraying towards the shooter when pulling the trigger. Both generations had a detachable single stack magazine. The trigger was made using rollers, and has received some criticism.
</p>
