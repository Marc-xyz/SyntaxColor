<p><b>Barbara Joy Stewart</b> (born 1952) is a New Zealand politician. She is a member of the <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_First" title="New Zealand First">New Zealand First</a> party.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barbara_Stewart&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Stewart was born in <a href="/wiki/Wairoa" title="Wairoa">Wairoa</a> in 1952. She obtained a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Education" title="Bachelor of Education">BEd</a> and a Postgraduate Diploma in Management Studies, both from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Waikato" title="University of Waikato">University of Waikato</a>. Before entering politics, she was a <a href="/wiki/Teacher" title="Teacher">teacher</a> and a company training officer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MP_parliamentary_bio_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-MP_parliamentary_bio-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barbara_Stewart&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Stewart was initially a member of the <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_National_Party" title="New Zealand National Party">National Party</a> and acted as secretary for the <a href="/wiki/Feilding" title="Feilding">Feilding</a> branch 1987–90.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MP_parliamentary_bio_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-MP_parliamentary_bio-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Stewart was elected to Parliament in the <a href="/wiki/2002_New_Zealand_general_election" title="2002 New Zealand general election">2002 election</a> as a <a href="/wiki/List_MP" title="List MP">list MP</a>, having been ranked sixth on the New Zealand First <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Party_list" title="Party list">party list</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MP_parliamentary_bio_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-MP_parliamentary_bio-1">[1]</a></sup> In 2006, she introduced a Member's bill to Parliament to reduce the size of the New Zealand House of Representatives from 120 members to 100 members. The bill passed its first reading 61 votes to 60 on 16 March 2006, but was defeated at its Second Reading on 8 November 2006.
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/2008_New_Zealand_general_election" title="2008 New Zealand general election">2008 general election</a> Stewart was fifth on the New Zealand First list, but the party lost all its parliamentary seats, winning no electorates and polling below the 5% threshold.
</p><p>At the <a href="/wiki/2011_New_Zealand_general_election" title="2011 New Zealand general election">2011 election</a> Stewart was re-elected to Parliament following a resurgence in the New Zealand First vote, where she had been ranked fifth on the party list. Stewart had stood in the Waikato seat but was unsuccessful in defeating <a href="/wiki/Lindsay_Tisch" title="Lindsay Tisch">Lindsay Tisch</a>, the incumbent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Party_of_New_Zealand" title="National Party of New Zealand">National Party of New Zealand</a> MP. Stewart was elected whip by the new NZ First caucus following the 2011 election and remained in this role following her re-election in <a href="/wiki/2014_New_Zealand_general_election" title="2014 New Zealand general election">2014</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2013, Stewart voted against the <a href="/wiki/Marriage_(Definition_of_Marriage)_Amendment_Act_2013" title="Marriage (Definition of Marriage) Amendment Act 2013">Marriage Amendment Bill</a>, which aims to permit same sex marriage in New Zealand, with all of her follow New Zealand first MPs.
</p><p>Stewart announced that, following the death of her husband, she will not stand for re-election in <a href="/wiki/2017_New_Zealand_general_election" title="2017 New Zealand general election">2017</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
