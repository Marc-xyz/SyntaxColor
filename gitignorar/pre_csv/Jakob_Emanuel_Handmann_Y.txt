	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Jakob Emanuel Handmann</h1>

<p><b>Jakob Emanuel Handmann</b> (16 August 1718 in <a href="/wiki/Basel" title="Basel">Basel</a> – 3 November 1781 in <a href="/wiki/Bern" title="Bern">Bern</a>) was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swiss" title="Swiss">Swiss</a> <a href="/wiki/Painting" title="Painting">painter</a> specialised in <a href="/wiki/Portrait_painting" title="Portrait painting">portrait painting</a>. He was a contemporary of the Swiss painters <a href="/wiki/Anton_Graff" title="Anton Graff">Anton Graff</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jean_Preudhomme" title="Jean Preudhomme">Jean Preudhomme</a>, <a href="/wiki/Angelica_Kauffman" title="Angelica Kauffman">Angelica Kauffman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Johann_Jakob_Schalch" title="Johann Jakob Schalch">Johann Jakob Schalch</a>,  <a href="/wiki/Johann_Caspar_F%C3%BCssli" title="Johann Caspar Füssli">Johann Caspar Füssli</a> and his son <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Johann_Heinrich_F%C3%BCssli" title="Johann Heinrich Füssli">Johann Heinrich Füssli</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_work">Life and work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jakob_Emanuel_Handmann&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Leonhard_Euler.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="461" decoding="async" height="221" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Leonhard_Euler.jpg/170px-Leonhard_Euler.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Leonhard_Euler.jpg/255px-Leonhard_Euler.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Leonhard_Euler.jpg/340px-Leonhard_Euler.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Leonhard_Euler.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Portrait of <a href="/wiki/Leonhard_Euler" title="Leonhard Euler">Leonhard Euler</a> by Jakob Emanuel Handmann (1753)</div></div></div>
<p>Jakob Emanuel Handmann was the 9th child of Johann Jakob Handmann, a baker and later <a href="/wiki/Bailiff" title="Bailiff">bailiff</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Waldenburg_BL" title="Waldenburg BL">Waldenburg</a>, and his wife Anna Maria Rispach. Between 1735 and 1739 he made an apprenticeship as a <a href="/wiki/Stucco" title="Stucco">stucco</a> <a href="/wiki/Plasterer" title="Plasterer">plasterer</a> and studied painting in <a href="/wiki/Schaffhausen" title="Schaffhausen">Schaffhausen</a> with the painter and <a href="/wiki/Stucco" title="Stucco">stucco</a> <a href="/wiki/Plasterer" title="Plasterer">plasterer</a> Johann Ulrich Schnetzler. He made study trips to <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a> and <a href="/wiki/Naples" title="Naples">Naples</a>. In Paris he worked at the studio of Jean Restout II who influenced his work. In 1742 Handmann travelled through France finding employment in a portrait studio partnership with the painter Hörling. In the business partnership with Hörling Handmann was mainly responsible for painting the heads of the sitters. In <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> he worked among others in the studios of <a href="/wiki/Marco_Benefial" title="Marco Benefial">Marco Benefial</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Subleyras" title="Pierre Subleyras">Pierre Subleyras</a> in Rome. There he mainly copied masterpieces from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Classical_Antiquity" title="Classical Antiquity">Classical Antiquity</a> and <a href="/wiki/Renaissance" title="Renaissance">Renaissance</a> periods.
</p><p>By June 1746 he was back in Switzerland. In 1747 he settled down in <a href="/wiki/Basel" title="Basel">Basel</a>. In Basel he opened his own studio. However, many of his clients were <a href="/wiki/Patrician_(post-Roman_Europe)" title="Patrician (post-Roman Europe)">patricians</a> from the city and the area of <a href="/wiki/Bern" title="Bern">Bern</a>. Thanks to the acquaintance of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Estonian_people" title="Estonian people">Estonian</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nobleman" title="Nobleman">nobleman</a> <a href="/wiki/Colonel" title="Colonel">Colonel</a> Carl Friedrich von Staal, Handmann became a member of the “<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Accademia_Clementina" title="Accademia Clementina">Accademia Clementina</a>” of <a href="/wiki/Bologna" title="Bologna">Bologna</a> in 1773. Apart from a trip to <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> in 1753 he never again left his native country.
</p>
