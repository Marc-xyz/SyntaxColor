<p><b>Buckcherry</b> is an American rock band from <a href="/wiki/Anaheim,_California" title="Anaheim, California">Anaheim, California</a>, formed in 1995. The band released two albums, <i><a href="/wiki/Buckcherry_(album)" title="Buckcherry (album)">Buckcherry</a></i> (1999) and <i><a href="/wiki/Time_Bomb_(Buckcherry_album)" title="Time Bomb (Buckcherry album)">Time Bomb</a></i> (2001), before dissolving in 2002. In 2005, lead vocalist <a href="/wiki/Josh_Todd" title="Josh Todd">Josh Todd</a> and lead guitarist Keith Nelson reformed Buckcherry with a new lineup and released a new album in 2006, <i><a href="/wiki/15_(Buckcherry_album)" title="15 (Buckcherry album)">15</a></i>. It contained Buckcherry's biggest crossover hits to date, "<a href="/wiki/Crazy_Bitch" title="Crazy Bitch">Crazy Bitch</a>", and their first <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> top ten hit, "<a href="/wiki/Sorry_(Buckcherry_song)" title="Sorry (Buckcherry song)">Sorry</a>". Their fourth album, <i><a href="/wiki/Black_Butterfly_(Buckcherry_album)" title="Black Butterfly (Buckcherry album)">Black Butterfly</a></i>, was released in 2008, and their fifth album, <i><a href="/wiki/All_Night_Long_(Buckcherry_album)" title="All Night Long (Buckcherry album)">All Night Long</a></i>, was released in 2010. Buckcherry released their sixth album, <i><a href="/wiki/Confessions_(Buckcherry_album)" title="Confessions (Buckcherry album)">Confessions</a></i>, in 2013, followed by their seventh album <i><a href="/wiki/Rock_%27n%27_Roll_(Buckcherry_album)" title="Rock 'n' Roll (Buckcherry album)">Rock 'n' Roll</a></i> in 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-loudwire_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-loudwire-2">[2]</a></sup> They released their eighth album <i><a href="/wiki/Warpaint_(Buckcherry_album)" title="Warpaint (Buckcherry album)">Warpaint</a></i> in 2019.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buckcherry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Formation:_1995.E2.80.931999"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation:_1995–1999">Formation: 1995–1999</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buckcherry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Formation: 1995–1999">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Josh_Todd" title="Josh Todd">Josh Todd</a> grew up in the <a href="/wiki/Anaheim_Hills" title="Anaheim Hills">Anaheim Hills</a> neighborhoood of <a href="/wiki/Anaheim,_California" title="Anaheim, California">Anaheim, California</a> and later moved to <a href="/wiki/Lake_Forest,_California" title="Lake Forest, California">Lake Forest, California</a>. Early on, Todd fronted the Hollywood glam rock band Slamhound.  He eventually met lead guitarist Keith Nelson through their tattoo artist (Kevin Quinn).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The duo made a few demos before being joined by bassist <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jonathan_Brightman" title="Jonathan Brightman">Jonathan Brightman</a> and drummer Devon Glenn, calling themselves Sparrow. Sparrow began performing around the Hollywood club scene, receiving a strong, local following due to their old school <a href="/wiki/Rock_and_roll" title="Rock and roll">rock and roll</a> vibe, and were signed to <a href="/wiki/DreamWorks_Records" title="DreamWorks Records">DreamWorks Records</a> shortly after. The group changed its name to Buckcherry after receiving a cease-and-desist letter from a record label called <a href="/wiki/Sparrow_Records" title="Sparrow Records">Sparrow</a> (owned by <a href="/wiki/EMI" title="EMI">EMI</a>). Although the band's name is a <a href="/wiki/Spoonerism" title="Spoonerism">spoonerism</a> of the late <a href="/wiki/Chuck_Berry" title="Chuck Berry">Chuck Berry</a>, the group said it was inspired by a drag queen acquaintance of theirs named Buck Cherry. Buck Cherry was also the <a href="/wiki/Pseudonym" title="Pseudonym">pseudonym</a> of John Armstrong, guitarist for 1980s new-wave group <a href="/wiki/Canadian_punk_rock" title="Canadian punk rock">The Modernettes</a>; it is not known if there is any inspiration or relation.
</p>
<h3><span id="Commercial_success:_1999.E2.80.932002"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_success:_1999–2002">Commercial success: 1999–2002</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buckcherry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Commercial success: 1999–2002">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The band released its <a href="/wiki/Buckcherry_(album)" title="Buckcherry (album)">self-titled debut</a> in 1999 to critical praise and certified gold sales. The album, produced by <a href="/wiki/Terry_Date" title="Terry Date">Terry Date</a> (<a href="/wiki/Soundgarden" title="Soundgarden">Soundgarden</a>/<a href="/wiki/Mother_Love_Bone" title="Mother Love Bone">Mother Love Bone</a>/<a href="/wiki/Pantera" title="Pantera">Pantera</a>/<a href="/wiki/Steve_Jones_(musician)" title="Steve Jones (musician)">Steve Jones</a>/<a href="/wiki/Deftones" title="Deftones">Deftones</a>), included the hit singles "<a href="/wiki/Lit_Up" title="Lit Up">Lit Up</a>", "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/For_the_Movies" title="For the Movies">For the Movies</a>", "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dead_Again_(song)" title="Dead Again (song)">Dead Again</a>", and "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Check_Your_Head_(song)" title="Check Your Head (song)">Check Your Head</a>".
</p><p>After adding rhythm guitarist Yogi Lonich to the lineup in 1999, Buckcherry toured nonstop in support of the debut album, including opening for <a href="/wiki/Lenny_Kravitz" title="Lenny Kravitz">Lenny Kravitz</a> on his Freedom Tour (1999) and participating at the infamous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Woodstock_99" title="Woodstock 99">Woodstock 99</a>, before going back into the studio in 2000 to work on a follow-up, <i><a href="/wiki/Time_Bomb_(Buckcherry_album)" title="Time Bomb (Buckcherry album)">Time Bomb</a></i> (2001). The second album was considered a disappointment by many critics,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-4">[4]</a></sup> and it did not fare as well on the charts. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Allmusic" title="Allmusic">Allmusic</a> noted that "For a second record, it's surprising how jaded and <a href="/wiki/Nihilism" title="Nihilism">nihilistic</a> they are already.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-4">[4]</a></sup>
Buckcherry performed opening dates for <a href="/wiki/AC/DC" title="AC/DC">AC/DC</a> in the spring 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> From August 2001 through January 2002, as tension over the musical direction set in, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jonathan_Brightman" title="Jonathan Brightman">Jonathan Brightman</a>, Yogi Lonich, and Devon Glenn each successively left Buckcherry, and were replaced by Dave Markasky, Josh Fleeger, and Matt Lawrence, respectively. Josh Todd and Keith Nelson still planned to continue the band, and began the writing process for a third album. The album was never completed, however, and the band was put on hiatus in July 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Other_projects:_2002.E2.80.9305"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_projects:_2002–05">Other projects: 2002–05</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buckcherry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Other projects: 2002–05">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Josh_Todd" title="Josh Todd">Josh Todd</a> and Keith Nelson performed together along with ex-<a href="/wiki/Guns_N%27_Roses" title="Guns N' Roses">Guns N' Roses</a> members <a href="/wiki/Slash_(musician)" title="Slash (musician)">Slash</a>, <a href="/wiki/Duff_McKagan" title="Duff McKagan">Duff McKagan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Matt_Sorum" title="Matt Sorum">Matt Sorum</a> (also of <a href="/wiki/The_Cult" title="The Cult">The Cult</a>) at the <a href="/wiki/Randy_Castillo" title="Randy Castillo">Randy Castillo</a> tribute concert. Speculation ensued that Josh Todd would become the lead vocalist of the Project headed up by Slash, Duff McKagan, and Matt Sorum. Josh Todd spent a month in the studio, reportedly completing 10 songs, but was abruptly dropped from the project by Slash. Todd was quoted as saying, "It was amazing, the band was slamming, and then Slash just came in one day and just shit-canned the whole thing". In his autobiography, Slash says the reason was because he was displeased with how Todd's voice sounded when they played the material back. <a href="/wiki/Scott_Weiland" title="Scott Weiland">Scott Weiland</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Stone_Temple_Pilots" title="Stone Temple Pilots">Stone Temple Pilots</a> was eventually chosen to helm the band that would become known as <a href="/wiki/Velvet_Revolver" title="Velvet Revolver">Velvet Revolver</a>. Keith Nelson was given a songwriting credit on Velvet Revolver's track "<a href="/wiki/Dirty_Little_Thing" title="Dirty Little Thing">Dirty Little Thing</a>". Nelson then went on to write and produce on various records.
</p>
<h3><span id="Commercial_Success_and_Cr.C3.BCe_Fest:_2005.E2.80.9309"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_Success_and_Crüe_Fest:_2005–09">Commercial Success and <i>Crüe Fest</i>: 2005–09</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buckcherry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Commercial Success and Crüe Fest: 2005–09">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2005, Josh Todd reformed Buckcherry with Keith Nelson and new members Stevie D. of <a href="/wiki/Automatic_Black" title="Automatic Black">Automatic Black</a>, Jimmy Ashhurst, and Xavier Muriel.
In 2006, the band released the album <i><a href="/wiki/15_(Buckcherry_album)" title="15 (Buckcherry album)">15</a></i> to commercial and critical success. It resurrected the band with hits "<a href="/wiki/Crazy_Bitch" title="Crazy Bitch">Crazy Bitch</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Sorry_(Buckcherry_song)" title="Sorry (Buckcherry song)">Sorry</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Everything_(Buckcherry_song)" title="Everything (Buckcherry song)">Everything</a>" and "Next 2 You".
</p><p>Back on September 11, 2006, another lawsuit had been filed against the band, their current record label, and other related companies on behalf of a sixteen-year-old girl who claimed she was coerced into appearing topless and in various sexual scenarios at a concert while the band was playing a sexually-related song. According to sources, however, all participants in the video had to show proof that they were over eighteen years old and sign a release form further reaffirming their age. Band attorney Skip Miller is quoted in several news stories as stating, "This woman filled out a release form with false information. And once it was determined this woman was underage, the video was removed." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>On April 15, 2008, Buckcherry announced they would be touring as part of <a href="/wiki/M%C3%B6tley_Cr%C3%BCe" title="Mötley Crüe">Mötley Crüe</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Cr%C3%BCe_Fest" title="Crüe Fest">Crüe Fest</a></i>, along with <a href="/wiki/Papa_Roach" title="Papa Roach">Papa Roach</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sixx:A.M." title="Sixx:A.M.">Sixx:A.M.</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Trapt" title="Trapt">Trapt</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The tour began July 1, 2008, in <a href="/wiki/West_Palm_Beach,_Florida" title="West Palm Beach, Florida">West Palm Beach, Florida</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>On September 16, 2008, the band released their fourth album, <i><a href="/wiki/Black_Butterfly_(Buckcherry_album)" title="Black Butterfly (Buckcherry album)">Black Butterfly</a></i>, which debuted at number 8 on the top 200 charts. It was the number one rock album that week. That year, <a href="/wiki/Josh_Todd" title="Josh Todd">Josh Todd</a> was featured on the album <i><a href="/wiki/This_War_Is_Ours" title="This War Is Ours">This War Is Ours</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Escape_the_Fate" title="Escape the Fate">Escape the Fate</a>. He sang co-lead vocals in the song "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=10_Miles_Wide&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="10 Miles Wide (page does not exist)">10 Miles Wide</a>" and backing vocals on "Harder Than You Know". In 2009, he was featured in the music video for "10 Miles Wide".
</p><p>In May 2009, <a href="/wiki/Kiss_(band)" title="Kiss (band)">KISS</a> announced that Buckcherry would be the opening band in their 2009/10 US and Canadian tour. On July 30, Buckcherry was featured in the Kerrang! Week Of Rock Tour with <a href="/wiki/The_69_Eyes" title="The 69 Eyes">The 69 Eyes</a> and Dear Superstar at the HMV Forum in London. Other bands in the Week Of Rock included <a href="/wiki/Limp_Bizkit" title="Limp Bizkit">Limp Bizkit</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Youmeatsix" title="Youmeatsix">Youmeatsix</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lacuna_Coil" title="Lacuna Coil">Lacuna Coil</a>. <a href="/wiki/DragonForce" title="DragonForce">DragonForce</a> was also supposed to play during the Week Of Rock, but pulled out.
</p><p>On September 29, 2009, Buckcherry released their first live album, <i><a href="/wiki/Live_%26_Loud_2009" title="Live &amp; Loud 2009">Live &amp; Loud 2009</a></i>, which they played some of their biggest songs such as "<a href="/wiki/Sorry_(Buckcherry_song)" title="Sorry (Buckcherry song)">Sorry</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Lit_Up" title="Lit Up">Lit Up</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Crazy_Bitch" title="Crazy Bitch">Crazy Bitch</a>", and much more. The album was recorded in May 2009 during their Canadian tour in <a href="/wiki/Edmonton" title="Edmonton">Edmonton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Calgary" title="Calgary">Calgary</a>, <a href="/wiki/Medicine_Hat" title="Medicine Hat">Medicine Hat</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Regina,_Saskatchewan" title="Regina, Saskatchewan">Regina</a>.
</p>
<h3><span id="All_Night_Long_and_Confessions:_2010.E2.80.932013"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="All_Night_Long_and_Confessions:_2010–2013"><i>All Night Long</i> and <i>Confessions</i>: 2010–2013</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buckcherry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: All Night Long and Confessions: 2010–2013">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>It was announced on February 22, 2010 that Buckcherry would be a part of Rocklahoma 2010 in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pryor,_Oklahoma" title="Pryor, Oklahoma">Pryor, Oklahoma</a>.
</p><p>Bassist Jimmy Ashhurst is one of the characters in the book <i>Sex Tips from Rock Stars</i> by Paul Miles, published by <a href="/wiki/Omnibus_Press" title="Omnibus Press">Omnibus Press</a> in July 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>Buckcherry released their fifth studio album, titled <i><a href="/wiki/All_Night_Long_(Buckcherry_album)" title="All Night Long (Buckcherry album)">All Night Long</a></i>, on August 3, 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> The album was mixed by <a href="/wiki/Mike_Fraser_(record_producer)" title="Mike Fraser (record producer)">Mike Fraser</a>. The first single from the album, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/All_Night_Long_(Buckcherry_song)" title="All Night Long (Buckcherry song)">All Night Long</a>", was originally going to be released to rock radio on May 3, but was pushed back to May 24.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> It was released as a free download to all fans who posted a pre-defined "tweet" on <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> or a similar message on <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> on May 4. The album has debuted at No. 10 on the Top Billboard Top 200 Album chart and was the No. 1 Rock debut. The single has just<sup>[when?]</sup> broken into the Top 10 of the Mainstream Rock radio chart, landing at No. 9.
</p><p>The band released the holiday themed single "Christmas is Here" as a digital download on November 30, 2010.
</p><p>From January 19 to March 18, 2011, Buckcherry toured with <a href="/wiki/Hellyeah" title="Hellyeah">Hellyeah</a> and <a href="/wiki/All_That_Remains_(band)" title="All That Remains (band)">All That Remains</a> on the Jägermeister Music Tour.
</p><p>As of June 2011, according to Keith Nelson, the band were already writing new material for their next album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p><p>The band contributed the song, "Wherever I Go," for the <i>Avengers Assemble</i> soundtrack.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p><p>Buckcherry's sixth album, <i><a href="/wiki/Confessions_(Buckcherry_album)" title="Confessions (Buckcherry album)">Confessions</a></i>, was released February 19, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gluttonyloudwire_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-gluttonyloudwire-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>  The band is also planning on releasing a  short film that ties in with the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gluttonyloudwire_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-gluttonyloudwire-15">[15]</a></sup> The first single from <i>Confessions</i>, "Gluttony", was made available for streaming on December 7, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gluttonyloudwire_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-gluttonyloudwire-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p><p>Later that year, Buckcherry announced that they will be releasing their first compilation on October 29, 2013 titled "The Best Of", featuring 12 of their best songs.
</p>
<h3><span id="Fuck_EP.2C_Rock_.27N.27_Roll_and_Warpaint:_2014.E2.80.93Present"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Fuck_EP,_Rock_'N'_Roll_and_Warpaint:_2014–Present"><i>Fuck EP</i>, <i>Rock 'N' Roll</i> and <i>Warpaint</i>: 2014–Present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buckcherry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Fuck EP, Rock 'N' Roll and Warpaint: 2014–Present">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Buckcherry announced that they will be releasing an EP in the summer of 2014 entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Fuck_(EP)" title="Fuck (EP)">Fuck</a></i>. Every title of every song on the EP includes the word "Fuck". "Say Fuck It", one of the songs on the EP that has been played by the band at their live shows. This song is a cover of the 2012 <a href="/wiki/Icona_Pop" title="Icona Pop">Icona Pop</a> song "<a href="/wiki/I_Love_It_(Icona_Pop_song)" title="I Love It (Icona Pop song)">I Love It</a>", with a few modifications in the lyrics. <i>Fuck</i> was released on August 19, 2014.
</p><p>They took part in VampPark Fest on February 19, 2015, hosted by the rock band <a href="/wiki/Vamps_(band)" title="Vamps (band)">Vamps</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Nippon_Budokan" title="Nippon Budokan">Nippon Budokan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup>
</p><p>Their seventh studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Rock_%27n%27_Roll_(Buckcherry_album)" title="Rock 'n' Roll (Buckcherry album)">Rock 'n' Roll</a></i> was released on August 21, 2015. On June 2, 2015, "Bring It On Back" was released as the first single from the album. A second single, "The Feeling Never Dies," which features country singer <a href="/wiki/Gretchen_Wilson" title="Gretchen Wilson">Gretchen Wilson</a>, was released February 17, 2016.
</p><p>In May 2017, Keith Nelson and Xavier Muriel left the band due to disagreements with Josh Todd over the band's direction. The band was said by Jimmy Ashhurst to have over-toured.  This was the reason Keith and X left the band.  The band brought in new members guitarist Kevin Roentgen and drummer Sean Winchester shortly afterwards. Nelson's departure left Josh Todd as the only remaining original member of Buckcherry.
</p><p>In November 2018, Buckcherry announced that they will release their eighth studio album <i>Warpaint</i> on March 8, 2019. The title track was released on February 8, 2019.
</p>
