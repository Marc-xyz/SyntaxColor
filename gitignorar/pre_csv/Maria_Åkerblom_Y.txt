<p><b>Ida Maria Åkerblom</b> (September 14, 1898 – February 25, 1981) was the leader of the <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finnish</a> <a href="/wiki/%C3%85kerblom_Movement" title="Åkerblom Movement">Åkerblom Movement</a>, an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Evangelical" title="Evangelical">evangelical</a> movement sometimes called a "<a href="/wiki/Cult" title="Cult">cult</a>". She had also served some time in <a href="/wiki/Prison" title="Prison">prison</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Her movement deemed her a <a href="/wiki/Prophet" title="Prophet">prophet</a> and began in the 1920s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Maria Åkerblom is characterized by Aarni Voipio as a "<a href="/wiki/Sleeping_preacher" title="Sleeping preacher">sleeping preacher</a>", that is, a person who peaches in a state of <a href="/wiki/Trance" title="Trance">trance</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
