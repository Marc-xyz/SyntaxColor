<p><i><b>Ganbare Goemon</b></i><span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">がんばれゴエモン</span>, <i>"Go for it, Goemon!"</i>)</span>, known as <i><b>Goemon</b></i> and <i><b>Mystical Ninja</b></i> internationally, is a <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">video game</a> series created and produced by <a href="/wiki/Konami" title="Konami">Konami</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Etsunobu_Ebisu" title="Etsunobu Ebisu">Etsunobu Ebisu</a> is the joint producer of the franchise.
</p><p>These games revolve around the main character, Goemon, and his exploits. His character is loosely based on <a href="/wiki/Ishikawa_Goemon" title="Ishikawa Goemon">Ishikawa Goemon</a>, the noble thief of Japanese <a href="/wiki/Folklore" title="Folklore">folklore</a>. While the early games emphasized Goemon as a noble thief, he eventually becomes more of a standard video game hero character. His trademarks are his blue bushy hair and weapon of choice, the <a href="/wiki/Kiseru" title="Kiseru">kiseru</a>. The games are set in a cartoonlike, mystical <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Feudal_Japan" title="Feudal Japan">Feudal Japan</a>, with many references to Japanese folklore. Although the series has its roots in action-adventure, the <i>Ganbare Goemon</i> series has features from genres including <a href="/wiki/Role-playing_video_game" title="Role-playing video game">role-playing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puzzle_video_game" title="Puzzle video game">puzzle video games</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Board_game" title="Board game">board games</a>. <i>Ganbare Goemon</i> is popular in Japan. The series consists of video games, with its success spawning a wide series of merchandise and an <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> and <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> series.
</p><p>Konami has generally regarded the Goemon games as too specific to the Japanese market to be released worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> However, five of them have been released overseas: <i><a href="/wiki/The_Legend_of_the_Mystical_Ninja" title="The Legend of the Mystical Ninja">The Legend of the Mystical Ninja</a></i> for the <a href="/wiki/Super_Nintendo_Entertainment_System" title="Super Nintendo Entertainment System">Super NES</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Mystical_Ninja_Starring_Goemon" title="Mystical Ninja Starring Goemon">Mystical Ninja Starring Goemon</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Goemon%27s_Great_Adventure" title="Goemon's Great Adventure">Goemon's Great Adventure</a></i> for the <a href="/wiki/Nintendo_64" title="Nintendo 64">Nintendo 64</a> and two titles for the <a href="/wiki/Game_Boy" title="Game Boy">Game Boy</a>.
</p><p>In 2002-2003, a mobile phone was released for the titled <i>Ganbare Goemon: Tsūkai Game Apli</i> series.
</p><p>The latest original game of the series was <i><a href="/wiki/Ganbare_Goemon:_T%C5%8Dkai_D%C5%8Dch%C5%AB_%C5%8Cedo_Tengu_ri_Kaeshi_no_Maki" title="Ganbare Goemon: Tōkai Dōchū Ōedo Tengu ri Kaeshi no Maki">Ganbare Goemon: Tōkai Dōchū Ōedo Tengu ri Kaeshi no Maki</a></i>, released in Japan for the <a href="/wiki/Nintendo_DS" title="Nintendo DS">Nintendo DS</a> in <a href="/wiki/2005_in_video_gaming" title="2005 in video gaming">2005</a>. Since then, the series has been used primarily as themes for Konami's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pachislot" title="Pachislot">pachislot</a> machines.
</p><p>The series is represented in <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Smash_Bros._Ultimate" title="Super Smash Bros. Ultimate">Super Smash Bros. Ultimate</a></i> with a purchasable Goemon costume for <a href="/wiki/Mii" title="Mii">Mii</a> fighters, revealed during a <a href="/wiki/Nintendo_Direct" title="Nintendo Direct">Nintendo Direct</a> in September 2019.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Original_video_animation">Original video animation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Goemon_(series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Original video animation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<ul><li>A single-episode thirty-minute <a href="/wiki/Original_video_animation" title="Original video animation">OVA</a> was released in Japan in 1993 titled <i><b>Ganbare Goemon: Jigen Jō no Akumu</b></i><span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja"><a class="extiw" href="https://ja.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%81%8C%E3%82%93%E3%81%B0%E3%82%8C%E3%82%B4%E3%82%A8%E3%83%A2%E3%83%B3_%E6%AC%A1%E5%85%83%E5%9F%8E%E3%81%AE%E6%82%AA%E5%A4%A2" title="ja:がんばれゴエモン 次元城の悪夢">がんばれゴエモン 次元城の悪夢</a></span>, "Ganbare Goemon: The Nightmare of the Dimensional Castle")</span>. The OVA starred the voice of <a href="/wiki/Daiki_Nakamura" title="Daiki Nakamura">Daiki Nakamura</a> as Goemon and <a href="/wiki/Hideyuki_Umezu" title="Hideyuki Umezu">Hideyuki Umezu</a> as Ebisumaru and featured segments parodying <i><a href="/wiki/Gradius" title="Gradius">Gradius</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Castlevania" title="Castlevania">Akumajō Dracula</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/TwinBee" title="TwinBee">TwinBee</a></i>.</li>
<li>A second-episode thirty-minute <a href="/wiki/Original_video_animation" title="Original video animation">OVA</a> was released in Japan in 1998 titled <i><b>Ganbare Goemon: Chikyuu Kyuushutsu Sakusen</b></i><span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">がんばれゴエモン地球救出作戦</span>, "Ganbare Goemon: Global Rescue Operation")</span>.</li></ul>

<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Anime_Ganbare_Goemon" title="Anime Ganbare Goemon">Anime Ganbare Goemon</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manga">Manga</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Goemon_(series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Manga">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Goemon is the protagonist of many manga based on the video game series. There are several series, each one based on a different game. Most of the manga were illustrated by artist Hiroshi Obi and were published between 1991 and 1998 to accompany the release of each new game.
</p><p>Obi died from a brain stem hemorrhage on August 3, 2014, at the age of 54.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
