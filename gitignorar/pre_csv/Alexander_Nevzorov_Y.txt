<p><b>Alexander Glebovich Nevzorov</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Алекса́ндр Гле́бович Невзо́ров</span>; born on August 3, 1958) is a <a href="/wiki/Russians" title="Russians">Russian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet</a> <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">television</a> journalist, a <a href="/wiki/Film_director" title="Film director">film director</a> and a former member of the <a href="/wiki/State_Duma" title="State Duma">Russian parliament</a>. He is the founder of a horsemanship school, Nevzorov Haute École. Also engaged in criticism of religion.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alexander_Nevzorov&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nevzorov started working for the Leningrad television in 1985.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-celebirthday_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-celebirthday-1">[1]</a></sup> From December 1987 – 1993 he hosted the program <i><a href="/wiki/600_Seconds" title="600 Seconds">600 Seconds</a></i> on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leningrad_TV" title="Leningrad TV">Leningrad TV</a> channel, aired then all over the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zaks.ru_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-zaks.ru-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> On December 12, 1990, Nevzorov was shot and wounded in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leningrad" title="Leningrad">Leningrad</a> during a meeting with someone who pretended to have sensitive documents to offer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zaks.ru_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-zaks.ru-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In late 1991 his program was taken off the air twice and later gradually lost its popularity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1tvnetru_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-1tvnetru-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bulvar.com.ua_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-bulvar.com.ua-7">[7]</a></sup> During <a href="/wiki/1991_Soviet_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat_attempt" title="1991 Soviet coup d'état attempt">August putsch</a> Nevzorov supported <a href="/wiki/State_Committee_on_the_State_of_Emergency" title="State Committee on the State of Emergency">State Committee on the State of Emergency</a>, the organ of the coupists.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Nevzorov formed the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nashi_(1991)" title="Nashi (1991)">Nashi movement</a> (not to be confused with the later <a href="/wiki/Nashi_(youth_movement)" title="Nashi (youth movement)">pro-Putin youth movement</a> of the same name). The broadcast was finally closed down in <a href="/wiki/1993_Russian_constitutional_crisis" title="1993 Russian constitutional crisis">the aftermath of Yeltsin's victory</a> in his confrontation with the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Soviet_of_Russia" title="Supreme Soviet of Russia">Russian Supreme Soviet</a> (Nevzorov had supported the anti-Yeltsin side).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1tvnetru_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-1tvnetru-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>Nevzorov worked as a reporter in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yugoslav_wars" title="Yugoslav wars">Yugoslav wars</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/War_of_Transnistria" title="War of Transnistria">War of Transnistria</a> in 1992–1993.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bulvar.com.ua_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-bulvar.com.ua-7">[7]</a></sup> In 1994 he was a vocal supporter of the initiation of the <a href="/wiki/First_Chechen_War" title="First Chechen War">First Chechen War</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-zaks.ru_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-zaks.ru-2">[2]</a></sup> In 1997 he wrote and directed the TV film <i>Chistilishche</i> ("Purgatory") about the Chechen war, co-produced with <a href="/wiki/Boris_Berezovsky_(businessman)" title="Boris Berezovsky (businessman)">Boris Berezovsky</a> and released in March 1998.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/1993_Russian_legislative_election" title="1993 Russian legislative election">1993 campaign</a> he was elected deputy in the <a href="/wiki/State_Duma" title="State Duma">State Duma</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_Federation" title="Russian Federation">Russian Federation</a> for the first time, and since then has been re-elected as an independent deputy three times, serving until the <a href="/wiki/2007_Russian_legislative_election" title="2007 Russian legislative election">2007 elections</a> when the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Single_constituency&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Single constituency (page does not exist)">single constituency</a> seats were abolished.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1tvnetru_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-1tvnetru-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>He served as an adviser on film, TV and radio to <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Anatolyevich_Yakovlev" title="Vladimir Anatolyevich Yakovlev">Vladimir Yakovlev</a> during the latter's tenure as the <a href="/wiki/Governor" title="Governor">Governor</a> (mayor) of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St_Petersburg" title="St Petersburg">St Petersburg</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1999 Nevzorov collaborated with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Channel_One_(Russia)" title="Channel One (Russia)">ORT</a> TV channel and often appeared as a political commentator on <a href="/wiki/Sergey_Dorenko" title="Sergey Dorenko">Sergey Dorenko</a>'s Saturday night news show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>Later he abandoned <a href="/wiki/Political_journalism" title="Political journalism">political journalism</a> and devoted himself to horses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> He founded a horsemanship school, Nevzorov Haute Ecole, and wrote the book <i>The Horse: Crucified and Risen</i>. He continues to make occasional excursions in politics. He directed two documentaries, produced several journal articles and publications,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and maintains a blog where he expresses his views of fervent atheism and bitter criticism towards the Russian government.
</p><p>Was recognized as a <a href="/wiki/Saint" title="Saint">saint</a> by Russian Church of the <a href="/wiki/Flying_Spaghetti_Monster" title="Flying Spaghetti Monster">Flying Spaghetti Monster</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p>
