	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Main Dyke</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Main Dyke</b> is a water channel running through <a href="/wiki/The_Fylde" title="The Fylde">the Fylde</a> area of <a href="/wiki/Lancashire" title="Lancashire">Lancashire</a> in England.
</p><p>The Main Dyke is an outflow of the largely drained <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marton_Mere" title="Marton Mere">Marton Mere</a>,  <a href="/wiki/Blackpool" title="Blackpool">Blackpool</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> though Marton Mere originally released its waters via Spen Brook in the opposite direction<a class="external autonumber" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20070929121306/http://www.btinternet.com/~stephen.yarwood/Blackpool_history.htm" rel="nofollow">[1]</a>.
</p><p>The Main Dyke joins the <a href="/wiki/River_Wyre" title="River Wyre">River Wyre</a> at <a href="/wiki/Skippool" title="Skippool">Skippool</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> appearing to follow the course of an earlier "Skippon River" (named as such on John Speed's map of the county)<a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.lancashire.gov.uk/environment/oldmap/speed/speed.jpg" rel="nofollow">[2]</a>.
</p>
