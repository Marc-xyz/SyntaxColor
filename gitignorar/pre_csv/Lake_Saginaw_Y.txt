	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Lake Saginaw</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><span style="font-size: small;"><span id="coordinates"><a href="/wiki/Geographic_coordinate_system" title="Geographic coordinate system">Coordinates</a>: <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Lake_Saginaw&amp;params=43_55_N_83_35_W_scale:500000"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">43°55′N</span> <span class="longitude">83°35′W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">43.917°N 83.583°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">43.917; -83.583</span></span></span></a></span></span></span>
</p><p><b>Lake Saginaw</b> occupied the basin of <a href="/wiki/Saginaw_Bay" title="Saginaw Bay">Saginaw Bay</a>.  There were two periods when it was an independent lake, not associated with a larger body of water in the <a href="/wiki/Lake_Huron" title="Lake Huron">Huron basin</a>. The first Lake Saginaw was a contemporary of the last stages of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Maumee" title="Lake Maumee">Lake Maumee</a>. When the ice border opened allowing these two lakes to become one, it entered the period of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Arkona" title="Lake Arkona">Lake Arkona</a>. Then, the ice advanced, closing the link forming the second Lake Saginaw.  This was during the time of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Whittlesey" title="Lake Whittlesey">Lake Whittlesey</a>.  When the ice margin retreated northward for the last time, it became  the western bay of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Wayne" title="Lake Wayne">Lake Wayne</a> and then of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Warren" title="Lake Warren">Lake Warren</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lake_Lundy&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lake Lundy (page does not exist)">Lake Lundy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leverett1915-53_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Leverett1915-53-1">[1]</a></sup> During its periods as an independent lake, its outlet was west through <a href="/wiki/Grand_River_(Michigan)" title="Grand River (Michigan)">Grand River</a> channel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leverett1915-53_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Leverett1915-53-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="First_Lake_Saginaw">First Lake Saginaw</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lake_Saginaw&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: First Lake Saginaw">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Plate_16_-_Glacial_lakes_Whittlesey,_Sagniaw_and_Chicago.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="816" data-file-width="1052" decoding="async" height="171" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Plate_16_-_Glacial_lakes_Whittlesey%2C_Sagniaw_and_Chicago.JPG/220px-Plate_16_-_Glacial_lakes_Whittlesey%2C_Sagniaw_and_Chicago.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Plate_16_-_Glacial_lakes_Whittlesey%2C_Sagniaw_and_Chicago.JPG/330px-Plate_16_-_Glacial_lakes_Whittlesey%2C_Sagniaw_and_Chicago.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Plate_16_-_Glacial_lakes_Whittlesey%2C_Sagniaw_and_Chicago.JPG/440px-Plate_16_-_Glacial_lakes_Whittlesey%2C_Sagniaw_and_Chicago.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Plate_16_-_Glacial_lakes_Whittlesey,_Sagniaw_and_Chicago.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>First Lake Saginaw - Map of Glacial lakes Whittlesey, Sagniaw and Chicago, based on the USGS Report of 1915</div></div></div><p>Lake Saginaw began after the building of the Flint moraine and the creation of the Imlay channel (from Flint to Maple Rapids) from glacial runoff.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leverett1915-53_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Leverett1915-53-1">[1]</a></sup> When the ice sheet had retreated into the Saginaw basin, exposing land, water began to accumulate and the First Lake Saginaw was formed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leverett19119-7_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Leverett19119-7-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>The outlet of the lake was through <a href="/wiki/Grand_River_(Michigan)" title="Grand River (Michigan)">Grand River</a> to <a href="/wiki/Lake_Chicago" title="Lake Chicago">Lake Chicago</a>, which as served the run-off from the glacier. Well-defined beaches surround the outlet at an altitude 700 feet (210 m) above sea level,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leverett19119-7_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Leverett19119-7-2">[2]</a></sup>  To the north and west, they are higher because of isostatic rebound once the glaciers weight had been removed. The bed of the lake is nearly equally divided between a sandy and a clayey soil. Sand due to lake waves and currents is restricted to the border of the lake, while glacial outwash may be present where there was a considerable depth of water,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leverett19119-7_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Leverett19119-7-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lakes_Saginaw_and_Arkona">Lakes Saginaw and Arkona</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lake_Saginaw&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Lakes Saginaw and Arkona">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>When the ice barrier withdrew from the "thumb," a strait was opened across its northern low part and the waters on both sides merged at one level, making Lake Arkona and adding to it the area of Lake Saginaw. In this change Lake Saginaw, with its undisturbed outlet, became the controlling factor. The new lake became merely a greatly enlarged Lake Saginaw, using the same outlet and retaining the same shorelines. The <a href="/wiki/Lake_Arkona" title="Lake Arkona">Arkona</a> beaches began to be formed.  The first one merged with the existing second beach of Lake Saginaw to form a single shore.,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> thus the second Saginaw beach merged into the first beach of Lake Arkona.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leverett1915-53_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Leverett1915-53-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_Lake_Saginaw">Second Lake Saginaw</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lake_Saginaw&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Second Lake Saginaw">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Plate_53_-_Glacial_Lakes_Duluth,_Chicago,_and_Lundy_(USGS_1915).JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="850" data-file-width="1094" decoding="async" height="171" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Plate_53_-_Glacial_Lakes_Duluth%2C_Chicago%2C_and_Lundy_%28USGS_1915%29.JPG/220px-Plate_53_-_Glacial_Lakes_Duluth%2C_Chicago%2C_and_Lundy_%28USGS_1915%29.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Plate_53_-_Glacial_Lakes_Duluth%2C_Chicago%2C_and_Lundy_%28USGS_1915%29.JPG/330px-Plate_53_-_Glacial_Lakes_Duluth%2C_Chicago%2C_and_Lundy_%28USGS_1915%29.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Plate_53_-_Glacial_Lakes_Duluth%2C_Chicago%2C_and_Lundy_%28USGS_1915%29.JPG/440px-Plate_53_-_Glacial_Lakes_Duluth%2C_Chicago%2C_and_Lundy_%28USGS_1915%29.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Plate_53_-_Glacial_Lakes_Duluth,_Chicago,_and_Lundy_(USGS_1915).JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Second Lake Saginaw - Map of Glacial Lakes Duluth, Chicago, and Lundy (USGS 1915)</div></div></div><p>The ice front advanced from its position as the barrier of Lake Arkona it closed the strait around the "thumb" and raised the waters east of it about 44 feet, thus inaugurating Lake Whittlesey. The change, however, caused no notable alteration in the discharge from Lake Saginaw, because from its beginning to the time of Lake Arkona Lake Maumee discharged into it, and after Lake Arkona ceased Lake Whittlesey discharged into it. Hence, the volume of water passing out of Lake Saginaw was not affected by the changes. During the time of Lake Whittlesey, therefore, Lake Saginaw had a second period of independent existence with its area almost at a maximum. It seems certain that the lower Arkona ridge of the Saginaw basin was made chiefly by the waves of Lake Arkona, and it seems equally sure that during the time of Lake Whittlesey the waves of Lake Saginaw beat upon this same beach, although nothing clearly establishing this relation has been observed. In the absence of positive evidence one way or the other, these inferences seem natural and necessary.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leverett1915-53_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-Leverett1915-53-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Later Lake Saginaw extended about to the limits of the First Lake Saginaw on the west and south, for its outlet was through the same channel, along Grand River to Lake Chicago, and at but a few feet lower level. The limits on the northeast or ice-ward side were at the Port Huron moraine, and are thus more definitely fixed than the limits of the Earlier Lake Saginaw. The lake extended up the Cass river valley about to Cass City and up the <a href="/wiki/Tittabawassee_River" title="Tittabawassee River">Tittabawassee valley</a> at least to northeastern Gladwin and possibly into southeastern Ogemaw county.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leverett19119-7_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Leverett19119-7-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lakes_Saginaw_and_Wayne">Lakes Saginaw and Wayne</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lake_Saginaw&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Lakes Saginaw and Wayne">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>When the waters fell from Lake Whittlesey they were lowered about 80 feet, not to the Warren beach, as one might suppose, but to the Wayne beach and perhaps later to a lower level.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leverett1915-53_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-Leverett1915-53-1">[1]</a></sup> The Wayne beach lies about 20 feet below the Warren and shows modification bj^ submergence after it was made, in this respect resembling the Arkona beaches. There is much reason for thinking that this lake fell to still lower levels before its end. No proof of this has been found, but its outlet was near Syracuse, N. Y., where the situation favored large changes of lake level with relatively small oscillations of the ice front. While Lake Saginaw formed a part of Lake Wayne it abandoned the Grand River channel, its water standing a little below the head of that outlet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leverett1915-53_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-Leverett1915-53-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lakes_Saginaw_and_Warren">Lakes Saginaw and Warren</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lake_Saginaw&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Lakes Saginaw and Warren">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>When the ice barrier advanced into <a href="/wiki/Lake_Wayne" title="Lake Wayne">Lake Wayne</a>, the water rose to the level of the <a href="/wiki/Lake_Warren" title="Lake Warren">Warren beach</a>.  Once again the <a href="/wiki/Grand_River_(Michigan)" title="Grand River (Michigan)">Grand River</a> became the outlet. <a href="/wiki/Lake_Warren" title="Lake Warren">Lake Warren</a> has been the name given to this state, with shorelines 25 feet (7.6 m) or 30 feet (9.1 m) lower than the Arkona ridges.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leverett1915-53_1-7"><a href="#cite_note-Leverett1915-53-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Lakes_Saginaw_and_Lundy_.28Lake_Dana_.26_Lake_Elkton.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Lakes_Saginaw_and_Lundy_(Lake_Dana_&amp;_Lake_Elkton)">Lakes Saginaw and Lundy (Lake Dana &amp; Lake Elkton)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lake_Saginaw&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Lakes Saginaw and Lundy (Lake Dana &amp; Lake Elkton)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lake_Lundy&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lake Lundy (page does not exist)">Lake Lundy</a> followed Lake Warren when a new outlet to the east opened in the <a href="/wiki/Mohawk_River" title="Mohawk River">Mohawk River</a> Valley.  The Grand River outlet was again abandoned for the final time. Lake Saginaw had ended with the onset of the Lake Warren stage.  But the opening of a lower outlet along the Mohawk, ended the chance for another independent lake to arise in Saginaw Bay.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leverett1915-53_1-8"><a href="#cite_note-Leverett1915-53-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Grand_River_Channel">Grand River Channel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lake_Saginaw&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Grand River Channel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Grand_River_(Michigan)" title="Grand River (Michigan)">Grand River</a> channel was in use a much longer time than any other channel in <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a>. It began to carry the lake waters when the outlet of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Maumee" title="Lake Maumee">Lake Maumee</a> first opened near <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Imlay,_Michigan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Imlay, Michigan (page does not exist)">Imlay</a>, before the beginning of Lake Saginaw. Later it received the discharge from Lake Maumee through the first Lake Saginaw. Next it received the overflow of the merged <a href="/wiki/Lake_Arkona" title="Lake Arkona">Lakes Arkona</a> and Saginaw. Later it received the waters of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Whittlesey" title="Lake Whittlesey">Lake Whittlesey</a> and Saginaw. Only when the ice retreated from the Port Huron morainic system and Lake Saginaw merged again eastward and became part of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Wayne" title="Lake Wayne">Lake Wayne</a> was the Grand River channel temporarily abandoned. Through all previous changes, apparently without break or intermission from the first opening of the Imlay outlet, it had carried the overflow. Later the ice advanced, closing the eastern outlet past Syracuse creating Lake Warren, which discharged through the Grand River channel. The Warren beaches lie 20 feet (6.1 m) or 25 feet (7.6 m) above the broad, swampy, dune-covered divide at the head of the channel north of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bannister,_Michigan" title="Bannister, Michigan">Bannister</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Leverett1915-53_1-9"><a href="#cite_note-Leverett1915-53-1">[1]</a></sup>
