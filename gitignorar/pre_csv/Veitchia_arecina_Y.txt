<p><i><b>Veitchia arecina</b></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> commonly known as <b>Montgomery palm</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> is a species of <a href="/wiki/Flowering_plant" title="Flowering plant">flowering plant</a> in the family <a href="/wiki/Arecaceae" title="Arecaceae">Arecaceae</a>. It grows to between 25' to 35' (7.60m to 10.60m) and has white or yellow blooms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>It is found only in <a href="/wiki/Vanuatu" title="Vanuatu">Vanuatu</a>. It is threatened by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Habitat_loss" title="Habitat loss">habitat loss</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IUCN_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-1">[1]</a></sup> It is being offered as a landscape design suggestion in areas of Florida where the annual low temperatures do not exclude it as a choice.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
