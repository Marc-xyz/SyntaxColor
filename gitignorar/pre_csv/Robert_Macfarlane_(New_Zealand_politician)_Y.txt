<p><b>Sir Robert Mafeking Macfarlane</b> <span class="noexcerpt nowraplinks" style="font-size:85%;"><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Knight_Commander_of_The_Most_Distinguished_Order_Of_Saint_Michael_And_Saint_George" title="Knight Commander of The Most Distinguished Order Of Saint Michael And Saint George">KCMG</a></span> (né <b>Haynes</b>, 17 May 1900 – 2 December 1981) was a New Zealand politician of the <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_Labour_Party" title="New Zealand Labour Party">Labour Party</a>. He was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament#New_Zealand" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a>, served as <a href="/wiki/Speaker_of_the_New_Zealand_House_of_Representatives" title="Speaker of the New Zealand House of Representatives">Speaker of the House of Representatives</a> and was a <a href="/wiki/Mayor_of_Christchurch" title="Mayor of Christchurch">Mayor of Christchurch</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Macfarlane_(New_Zealand_politician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Macfarlane was born in Christchurch on 17 May 1900, the son of Emma Rose King Haynes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DNZB_Macfarlane_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-DNZB_Macfarlane-1">[1]</a></sup> In 1904, he took the surname Macfarlane after his mother married Hugh Macfarlane, a labourer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DNZB_Macfarlane_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-DNZB_Macfarlane-1">[1]</a></sup> He married Louisa Jacobs in 1932.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DNZB_Macfarlane_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-DNZB_Macfarlane-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Local_body_politics">Local body politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Macfarlane_(New_Zealand_politician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Local body politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Macfarlane was on the <a href="/wiki/Christchurch_City_Council" title="Christchurch City Council">Christchurch City Council</a> (1927–1929, <a href="/wiki/1936_Christchurch_mayoral_by-election" title="1936 Christchurch mayoral by-election">1936</a>–1941, 1947–1959, and 1961–1981),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Councillors_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Councillors-2">[2]</a></sup> and was Mayor of Christchurch twice, from <a href="/wiki/1938_Christchurch_mayoral_election" title="1938 Christchurch mayoral election">1938</a> to 1941 and from <a href="/wiki/1950_Christchurch_mayoral_election" title="1950 Christchurch mayoral election">1950</a> to 1958.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CCC_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-CCC-3">[3]</a></sup> He was at various times a member of the <a href="/wiki/Lyttelton_Harbour_Board" title="Lyttelton Harbour Board">Lyttelton Harbour Board</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DNZB_Macfarlane_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-DNZB_Macfarlane-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Macfarlane entered Parliament in 1939 following a by-election, replacing <a href="/wiki/Ted_Howard_(politician)" title="Ted Howard (politician)">Ted Howard</a> (although Howard's daughter <a href="/wiki/Mabel_Howard" title="Mabel Howard">Mabel Howard</a> had hoped to replace him following his death). He was the Member of Parliament for <a href="/wiki/Christchurch_South" title="Christchurch South">Christchurch South</a> from 1939 to 1946, then for <a href="/wiki/Christchurch_Central" title="Christchurch Central">Christchurch Central</a> from 1946 to 1969, when he retired.
</p><p>From May 1947 until September 1947 he was the Labour Party's junior <a href="/wiki/Whip_(politics)" title="Whip (politics)">whip</a>. He was subsequently Labour's senior whip from September 1947 until June 1951.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWilson1985281_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWilson1985281-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Speaker_of_the_House_of_Representatives">Speaker of the House of Representatives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Macfarlane_(New_Zealand_politician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Speaker of the House of Representatives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He was the 14th Speaker of the House of Representatives, from 1958 to 1960.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Honours">Honours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Macfarlane_(New_Zealand_politician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Honours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/1954_Birthday_Honours" title="1954 Birthday Honours">1954 Queen's Birthday Honours</a>, Macfarlane was appointed a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Companion_of_the_Order_of_St_Michael_and_St_George" title="Companion of the Order of St Michael and St George">Companion of the Order of St Michael and St George</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and in the <a href="/wiki/1974_New_Year_Honours_(New_Zealand)" title="1974 New Year Honours (New Zealand)">1974 New Year Honours</a> he was elevated to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Order_of_Saint_Michael_and_Saint_George" title="Order of Saint Michael and Saint George">Knight Commander</a> of the same order.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/1985_New_Year_Honours" title="1985 New Year Honours">1985 New Year Honours</a>, his wife, Louisa, Lady Macfarlane, was appointed a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Companion_of_the_Queen%27s_Service_Order" title="Companion of the Queen's Service Order">Companion of the Queen's Service Order</a> for community service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Military_service">Military service</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Macfarlane_(New_Zealand_politician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Military service">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In World War II he served in the Middle East in the ASC of the 2nd New Zealand Expeditionary Force for 2½ years.
</p>
