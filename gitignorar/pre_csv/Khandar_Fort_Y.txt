<p><b>Khandar Fort</b> is an ancient fort located in <a href="/wiki/Khandar" title="Khandar">Khandar Tehsil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sawai_Madhopur" title="Sawai Madhopur">Sawai Madhopur</a> district, <a href="/wiki/Rajasthan" title="Rajasthan">Rajasthan</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>. This fort is on the boundary of <a href="/wiki/Ranthambore_National_Park" title="Ranthambore National Park">Ranthambore National Park</a>. The fort has three big entrances, but they are damaged.
</p>

<p>It is located at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Khandar_Fort&amp;params=26_1_24_N_76_36_25_E_"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">26°1′24″N</span> <span class="longitude">76°36′25″E</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">26.02333°N 76.60694°E</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">26.02333; 76.60694</span></span></span></a></span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Khandar_Fort&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Kangure.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="800" decoding="async" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/Kangure.jpg/220px-Kangure.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/Kangure.jpg/330px-Kangure.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/Kangure.jpg/440px-Kangure.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Kangure.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Walls of Khandar Fort</div></div></div>
<p>Located on top of a strategic vertical hill, the Khandar Fort in Sawai Madhopur could never come under an easy attack and thus was truly regarded as invincible and was a favorite of many dynasties in India. It is also said that king of this fort never lost in the war. This fort also tells some historical stories by the damaged walls and gates.
</p><p>The fort was long ruled by the Sisodia kings of Mewar after which it was taken over by the Mughals for a short while before coming under the direct control of the Maharajas of Jaipur in the 18th century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>This fort also belonged to <a href="/wiki/Bargujar" title="Bargujar">Bargujar</a> Kings and was attacked by Allaudin Khilji. It is very near to the rivers as Chambal &amp; Banas.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Attractions_within_the_Khandar_Fort">Attractions within the Khandar Fort</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Khandar_Fort&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Attractions within the Khandar Fort">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are seven temples within the Khandar Fort. There is an old Jain temple which displays an exquisite work of carved rock idols of Jain Gurus. There is also a Hanuman temple with a single stone idol of Lord Hanuman with a demon under its feet. The other temples at the Khandar Fort are Chaturbhuja Temple, Gobind Devji Temple, Jagatpalji Temple, and Jayanti Mata Temple. Chaturbhuja Temple houses an impressive idol of the four-armed God. An annual fair is held at the Jayanti Mata Temple. There is also the beautiful Rani Temple of the Khandar Fort located on top of the highest platform of the fort and perhaps the most magnificent part of the Fort. There are a lot of damaged sculptures here.
</p><p>Other attractions at the Khandar Fort are the two huge water tanks of Ramkunda and Laxmankunda. Seven small ponds are also there but due to lack of maintenance, only four ponds are clearly visible. Tigers, leopards, and many other wild animals can be easily seen in the fort.
</p>
