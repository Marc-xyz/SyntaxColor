<p><i><b>Matter</b></i> is the online magazine published by Matter Studios, a multi-platform content studio owned by <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> founder <a href="/wiki/Evan_Williams_(Internet_entrepreneur)" title="Evan Williams (Internet entrepreneur)">Evan Williams</a> (Internet entrepreneur)
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_profile">History and profile</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Matter_(magazine)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History and profile">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Matter</i> began life as an online publication specializing in <a href="/wiki/Long-form_journalism" title="Long-form journalism">long-form</a> articles about science, technology, medicine and the environment. The founders, journalists Bobbie Johnson and <a href="/wiki/Jim_Giles_(reporter)" title="Jim Giles (reporter)">Jim Giles</a>, funded the project by raising $140,000 on <a href="/wiki/Kickstarter" title="Kickstarter">Kickstarter</a> in March 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Following the successful campaign, an initial release of the online magazine was designed by Paul Lloyd and <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Keith_(web_developer)" title="Jeremy Keith (web developer)">Jeremy Keith</a> of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Clearleft&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Clearleft (page does not exist)">Clearleft</a>, and developed by Phil Gyford.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The success of the campaign generated discussion about new business models in journalism.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>In April 2013, Johnson and Giles announced that <i>Matter</i> had been acquired by Medium, a new publishing platform established by Twitter founder Ev Williams.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In June 2014, <i>Matter</i> was relaunched as a general interest publication under editor in chief Mark Lotto.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2016, <i>Matter</i> became the first digital publication to win a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Magazine_Award" title="National Magazine Award">National Magazine Award</a> in the prestigious category of reporting, for Joshua Hammer's story on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ebola_epidemic" title="Ebola epidemic">Ebola epidemic</a>, "My Nurses Are Dead, and I Don’t Know If I’m Already Infected."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2016, <i>Medium</i> spun off <i>Matter</i> into an independent company, Matter Studios, owned by Medium founder <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ev_Williams" title="Ev Williams">Ev Williams</a>. As part of its expansion into Matter Studios, under co-chief creative officers Mark Lotto and Hillary Frey, it produces digital journalism, podcasts, books, live events, documentaries and web series.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
