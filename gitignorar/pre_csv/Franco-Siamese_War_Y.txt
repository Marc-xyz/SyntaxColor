<p>The <b>Franco-Siamese War</b> of 1893 was a conflict between the <a href="/wiki/French_Third_Republic" title="French Third Republic">French Third Republic</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rattanakosin_Kingdom" title="Rattanakosin Kingdom">Kingdom of Siam</a>. <a href="/wiki/Auguste_Pavie" title="Auguste Pavie">Auguste Pavie</a>, French vice consul in <a href="/wiki/Luang_Prabang" title="Luang Prabang">Luang Prabang</a> in 1886, was the chief agent in furthering French interests in <a href="/wiki/Laos" title="Laos">Laos</a>. His intrigues, which took advantage of Siamese weakness in the region and periodic invasions by <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnamese</a> rebels from <a href="/wiki/Tonkin" title="Tonkin">Tonkin</a>, increased tensions between <a href="/wiki/Bangkok" title="Bangkok">Bangkok</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>. Following the conflict, the Siamese agreed to cede <a href="/wiki/Laos" title="Laos">Laos</a> to France, an act that led to the significant expansion of <a href="/wiki/French_Indochina" title="French Indochina">French Indochina</a>.
</p><p>This conflict succeeded the <a href="/wiki/Haw_wars" title="Haw wars">Haw wars</a> (1865–1890), in which the Siamese attempted to pacify northern Siam and Tonkin.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Context">Context</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Franco-Siamese_War&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Context">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/France%E2%80%93Thailand_relations" title="France–Thailand relations">France–Thailand relations</a></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:The_French_Wolf_and_The_Siamese_Lamb.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="579" data-file-width="412" decoding="async" height="281" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/The_French_Wolf_and_The_Siamese_Lamb.jpg/200px-The_French_Wolf_and_The_Siamese_Lamb.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/The_French_Wolf_and_The_Siamese_Lamb.jpg/300px-The_French_Wolf_and_The_Siamese_Lamb.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/The_French_Wolf_and_The_Siamese_Lamb.jpg/400px-The_French_Wolf_and_The_Siamese_Lamb.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:The_French_Wolf_and_The_Siamese_Lamb.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Punch_Magazine" title="Punch Magazine">Punch Magazine</a></i> cartoon showing the "French wolf" looking across the Mekong towards the "Siamese lamb"</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Franco-Siamese_War_Cartoon.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="871" data-file-width="548" decoding="async" height="350" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Franco-Siamese_War_Cartoon.jpg/220px-Franco-Siamese_War_Cartoon.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Franco-Siamese_War_Cartoon.jpg/330px-Franco-Siamese_War_Cartoon.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Franco-Siamese_War_Cartoon.jpg/440px-Franco-Siamese_War_Cartoon.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Franco-Siamese_War_Cartoon.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A cartoon from the British newspaper <i>The Sketch</i> shows a French soldier attacking a Siamese soldier depicted as a harmless wooden figure, reflecting the technological superiority of the French troops.</div></div></div>
<p>The conflict started when <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Governor-General_of_French_Indochina" title="Governor-General of French Indochina">French Indochina's Governor-General</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jean_de_Lanessan" title="Jean de Lanessan">Jean de Lanessan</a> sent Auguste Pavie as consul to Bangkok to bring Laos under French rule. The government in Bangkok, mistakenly believing that they would be supported by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_government" title="British government">British government</a>, refused to concede territory east of the <a href="/wiki/Mekong" title="Mekong">Mekong</a> and instead reinforced their military and administrative presence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stuart-Fox_1997_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stuart-Fox_1997-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Events were brought to a head by two separate incidents when Siamese governors in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Khammuan" title="Khammuan">Khammuan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nong_Khai" title="Nong Khai">Nong Khai</a> expelled three French merchants from the middle Mekong in September 1892, two of them, Champenois and Esquilot, on suspicion of <a href="/wiki/Opium" title="Opium">opium smuggling</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stuart-Fox_1997_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Stuart-Fox_1997-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Simms_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Simms-2">[2]</a></sup> Shortly afterwards, the French consul in <a href="/wiki/Luang_Prabang" title="Luang Prabang">Luang Prabang</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=M._Massie&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="M. Massie (page does not exist)">M. Massie</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (October 2016)">who?</span></a></i>]</sup>, feverish and discouraged, committed suicide on his way back to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saigon" title="Saigon">Saigon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stuart-Fox_1997_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Stuart-Fox_1997-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Simms_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Simms-2">[2]</a></sup> Back in France, these incidents were used by the colonial lobby (<i>Parti Colonial</i>) to stir up nationalistic anti-Siamese sentiment, as a pretext for intervention.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Simms_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Simms-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ooi_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ooi-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>The death of Massie left <a href="/wiki/Auguste_Pavie" title="Auguste Pavie">Auguste Pavie</a> as the new French Consul. In March 1893 Pavie demanded that the Siamese evacuate all military posts on the east side of the Mekong River south of Khammuan, claiming that the land belonged to Vietnam. To back up these demands, the French sent the gunboat <i><a href="/wiki/Lutin" title="Lutin">Lutin</a></i> to <a href="/wiki/Bangkok" title="Bangkok">Bangkok</a>, where it was moored on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chao_Phraya" title="Chao Phraya">Chao Phraya</a> next to the French legation.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conflict">Conflict</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Franco-Siamese_War&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Conflict">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>When Siam rejected the French demands, de Lanessan sent three military columns into the disputed region to assert French control in April 1893. Eight small Siamese garrisons west of the Mekong withdrew upon the arrival of the central column, but the advance of the other columns met with resistance. In the north, the French came under siege on the <a href="/wiki/Khong_Island" title="Khong Island">island of Khoung</a>, with the capture of an officer, Thoreaux. In the south the occupation proceeded smoothly until an ambush by the Siamese on the village of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Keng_Kert&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Keng Kert (page does not exist)">Keng Kert</a> resulted in the killing of French police inspector <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grosgurin" title="Grosgurin">Grosgurin</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dommen_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dommen-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Killing_of_Inspector_Grosgurin">Killing of Inspector Grosgurin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Franco-Siamese_War&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Killing of Inspector Grosgurin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Inspector Grosgurin was a French inspector and commander of a Vietnamese militia in <a href="/wiki/Laos" title="Laos">Laos</a>. Like <a href="/wiki/Auguste_Pavie" title="Auguste Pavie">Auguste Pavie</a>, he had been engaged in several exploratory expeditions in the region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dommen_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Dommen-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>18</span></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pouvou_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pouvou-5">[5]</a></sup> He was a member of one of the French armed columns dispatched in April 1893 by Lassenan to cross the <a href="/wiki/Annamite_Range" title="Annamite Range">Annamite Range</a> into the Lao area of <a href="/wiki/Khammouane_Province" title="Khammouane Province">Khammuan</a> (modern <a href="/wiki/Thakhek" title="Thakhek">Thakhek</a>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Simms_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Simms-2">[2]</a></sup> and to occupy the disputed territory. The column was at first successful in evicting the Siamese commissioner at Khammuan by 25 May.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dommen_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Dommen-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pouvou_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Pouvou-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>Shortly afterwards on 5 June, the Siamese commissioner organized a surprise ambush on the village of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kien_Ket&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kien Ket (page does not exist)">Kien Ket</a>, where Grosgurin, confined to his sickbed, had encamped with his militia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dommen_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-Dommen-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pouvou_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-Pouvou-5">[5]</a></sup> The commissioner had apparently been instructed by Siamese government representatives to "compel their [French troops] retirement, by fighting, if necessary, to the utmost of their strength".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Simms_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-Simms-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Norman_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Norman-6">[6]</a></sup> The ambush resulted in the razing of the village and the killing of Grosgurin and 17 Vietnamese.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Norman_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Norman-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>The incident and the death of Grosgurin became known as the "Affair of Kham Muon (Kien Chek)" and was ultimately used as a pretext for strong French intervention.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Simms_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-Simms-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Case_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Case-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Paknam_incident">Paknam incident</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Franco-Siamese_War&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Paknam incident">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Paknam_incident" title="Paknam incident">Paknam incident</a></div>
<p>As a result, relations between Bangkok and the West soured, with France demanding reparations. The British sent three navy ships to the mouth of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chao_Phraya" title="Chao Phraya">Chao Phraya</a>, in case evacuation of British citizens became necessary.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stuart-Fox_1997_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Stuart-Fox_1997-1">[1]</a></sup> In turn the French went one step further in July 1893 by ordering two of their ships, the sloop <i>Inconstant</i> and the gunboat <i>Comète</i>, to sail up the Chao Phraya towards Bangkok, without the permission of the Siamese. They came under fire from the fort at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amphoe_Mueang_Samut_Prakan" title="Amphoe Mueang Samut Prakan">Paknam</a> on 13 July 1893.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The French returned fire and forced their way to Bangkok.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Simms_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-Simms-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>209–210</span></sup>
</p><p>With guns trained on the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Palace" title="Grand Palace">Grand Palace</a> in Bangkok, the French delivered an ultimatum to the Siamese on 20 July to hand over the territory east of the Mekong and withdraw their garrisons there, to pay an indemnity of three million francs in reparation for the fighting at Paknam, and to punish those responsible for the killings in the disputed territory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Simms_2-7"><a href="#cite_note-Simms-2">[2]</a></sup> When Siam did not immediately comply unconditionally to the ultimatum, the French blockaded the Siamese coast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Simms_2-8"><a href="#cite_note-Simms-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>In the end the Siamese submitted fully to the French conditions after finding no support from the British.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ooi_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ooi-3">[3]</a></sup> In addition, the French demanded as guarantees the temporary occupation of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chantaburi" title="Chantaburi">Chantaburi</a> and the demilitarisation of <a href="/wiki/Battambang" title="Battambang">Battambang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Siem_Reap" title="Siem Reap">Siem Reap</a> and a 25 kilometre-wide zone on the west bank of the Mekong.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Simms_2-9"><a href="#cite_note-Simms-2">[2]</a></sup> The conflict led to the signature of the Franco-Siamese Treaty, on 3 October 1893.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Simms_2-10"><a href="#cite_note-Simms-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Franco-Siamese_trial">Franco-Siamese trial</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Franco-Siamese_War&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Franco-Siamese trial">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following the killing of Grosgurin, the Commissioner of the Kammuon District, Phra Yot, was acknowledged by his government to have been the responsible official, although was he initially acquitted of wrongdoing in a trial in March 1894.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Norman_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-Norman-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> A "Franco-Siamese Mixed Court" was subsequently convened in June 1894.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Norman_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-Norman-6">[6]</a></sup> The court determined that Phra Yot had brought extra forces to surround the house in Kien Ket occupied by the ill Grosgurin, outnumbering his small Vietnamese militia; that Grosgurin and those Vietnamese who had not managed to escape had been killed and the house subsequently set on fire on the orders of Phra Yot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Case_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Case-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>In a joint agreement between the Siamese and the French, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Phra_Yot&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Phra Yot (page does not exist)">Phra Yot</a> was condemned to 20 years of penal servitude.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Norman_6-4"><a href="#cite_note-Norman-6">[6]</a></sup> The solicitor for the defense was the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ceylonese" title="Ceylonese">Ceylonese</a> lawyer <a href="/wiki/Tilleke_%26_Gibbins" title="Tilleke &amp; Gibbins">William Alfred Tilleke</a>, who was later appointed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Attorney_General" title="Attorney General">Attorney General of Siam</a> and granted a peerage by the king.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Case_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-Case-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Loos_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Loos-12">[12]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Royal_Thai_Army" title="Royal Thai Army">Royal Thai Army fort</a> Phra Yot Muang Khwan in <a href="/wiki/Nakhon_Phanom_Province" title="Nakhon Phanom Province">Nakhon Phanom Province</a> on the border between Thailand and Laos commemorates Phra Yot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Loos_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-Loos-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Consequences">Consequences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Franco-Siamese_War&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Consequences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Siamese_territorial_concessions_(1867-1909)_with_flags.gif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="514" data-file-width="301" decoding="async" height="376" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Siamese_territorial_concessions_%281867-1909%29_with_flags.gif/220px-Siamese_territorial_concessions_%281867-1909%29_with_flags.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/9/91/Siamese_territorial_concessions_%281867-1909%29_with_flags.gif 1.5x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Siamese_territorial_concessions_(1867-1909)_with_flags.gif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Territorial claims abandoned by Siam in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. The result of the Franco-Siamese War was the cession of Laos (dark blue) to France in 1893.</div></div></div>
<p>The Siamese agreed to cede <a href="/wiki/Laos" title="Laos">Laos</a> to France, significantly expanding <a href="/wiki/French_Indochina" title="French Indochina">French Indochina</a>. In 1896, France signed a treaty with Britain defining the border between Laos and British territory in Upper Burma. The Kingdom of Laos became a protectorate, initially placed under the Governor General of Indochina in Hanoi. Pavie, who almost single-handedly brought Laos under French rule, saw to the officialization in <a href="/wiki/Hanoi" title="Hanoi">Hanoi</a>.
</p><p>The French and British both had strong interests in controlling parts of Indochina. Twice in the 1890s, they were on the verge of war over two different routes leading to <a href="/wiki/Yunnan" title="Yunnan">Yunnan</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> But several difficulties discouraged them from war. The geography of the land made troop movements difficult, making warfare more costly and less effective. Both countries were fighting a difficult conflict within their respective colonies.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> <a href="/wiki/Malaria" title="Malaria">Malaria</a> was common and deadly. Ultimately, the imagined trade routes never really came into use. In 1904, the French and the British put aside their many differences with the <a href="/wiki/Entente_Cordiale" title="Entente Cordiale">Entente Cordiale</a>, ending this dispute in southeastern Asia.
</p><p>France continued to occupy Chanthaburi and Trat up until 1907, when Siam ceded to it the provinces of Battambang, Siem Reap and Sisophon.
</p>
