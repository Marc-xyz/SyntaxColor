<p>The <b>Småvatna Hydroelectric Power Station</b> (<a href="/wiki/Norwegian_language" title="Norwegian language">Norwegian</a>: <i lang="no">Småvatna kraftverk</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Hydroelectricity" title="Hydroelectricity">hydroelectric</a> <a href="/wiki/Power_station" title="Power station">power station</a> in the municipality of <a href="/wiki/Kv%C3%A6nangen" title="Kvænangen">Kvænangen</a> in <a href="/wiki/Troms" title="Troms">Troms</a> <a href="/wiki/Counties_of_Norway" title="Counties of Norway">county</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>. The plant utilizes a 283-meter (928 ft) drop between Lake Lassa (<a href="/wiki/Kven_language" title="Kven language">Kven</a>: <i lang="fkv">Lassojärvi</i>, <a href="/wiki/Northern_Sami_language" title="Northern Sami language">Northern Sami</a>: <i lang="se">Lássájávri</i>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and Little Lakes (<a href="/wiki/Norwegian_language" title="Norwegian language">Norwegian</a>: <i lang="no">Småvatnan</i>, <a href="/wiki/Kven_language" title="Kven language">Kven</a>: <i lang="fkv">Pikkujärvet</i>, <a href="/wiki/Northern_Sami_language" title="Northern Sami language">Northern Sami</a>: <i lang="se">Čorrojávrrit</i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Lake Lassa is regulated at a level between 543 m (1,781 ft) and 519 m (1,703 ft), and Little Lakes serves as the reservoir for the <a href="/wiki/Kv%C3%A6nangsbotn_Hydroelectric_Power_Station" title="Kvænangsbotn Hydroelectric Power Station">Kvænangsbotn Hydroelectric Power Station</a>. The Småvatna plant also utilizes water from Abo River<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> (<a href="/wiki/Norwegian_language" title="Norwegian language">Norwegian</a>: <i lang="no">Aboelva</i>, <a href="/wiki/Kven_language" title="Kven language">Kven</a>: <i lang="fkv">Aapujoki</i>, <a href="/wiki/Northern_Sami_language" title="Northern Sami language">Northern Sami</a>: <i lang="se">Ábojohka</i>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Lassajavre_Hydroelectric_Power_Station" title="Lassajavre Hydroelectric Power Station">Lassajavre Hydroelectric Power Station</a>. The plant came into operation in 1969. It has a <a href="/wiki/Francis_turbine" title="Francis turbine">Francis turbine</a> and operates at an installed capacity of <span class="nowrap">18.8 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Megawatt" title="Megawatt">MW</a></span>, with an average annual production of about 61 GWh. The plant is controlled by Kvænangen Kraftverk AS, with a 48.2% share owned by <a href="/wiki/Troms_Kraft" title="Troms Kraft">Troms Kraft</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
