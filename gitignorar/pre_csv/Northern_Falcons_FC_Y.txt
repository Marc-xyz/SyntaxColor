<p><b>Northern Falcons Football Club</b> is a semi-professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">association football (soccer)</a> club that is based in the northern <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a> suburb of <a href="/wiki/Thornbury,_Victoria" title="Thornbury, Victoria">Thornbury</a>. Founded in 1986 by the <a href="/wiki/Italian_community_of_Melbourne" title="Italian community of Melbourne">Italian community of Melbourne</a>, the Falcons currently compete in the northern sector of the <a href="/wiki/Victorian_State_League_Division_4" title="Victorian State League Division 4">Victorian State League Division 4</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Northern_Falcons_FC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The football club was established under the name of <i>Flemington Floridia Soccer Club</i> in October 1986 as the sporting arm of the Floridia Social Club. The club's inaugural home venue was the parkland at the foot of the <a href="/wiki/Flemington,_Victoria" title="Flemington, Victoria">Flemington</a> commission housing on <a href="/wiki/Flemington_Road,_Melbourne" title="Flemington Road, Melbourne">Flemington Road</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>The club is led by president Frank Pizzo<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> who has earned recognition for the club from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_Federation_Victoria" title="Football Federation Victoria">Football Federation Victoria</a> for numerous workings. In 2008, Frank Pizzo with support from the Darebin Council established the club's 'All Abilities' program for players with disabilities in weekly competitive competitions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In 2013, Football Federation Victoria awarded Pizzo with the federations’s annual volunteer of the year award for his &amp; the club's efforts in the 'all abilities' program.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
