<p>The <b>2019 Norrbil Swedish FIM Speedway Grand Prix</b> was the fourth race of the <a href="/wiki/2019_Speedway_Grand_Prix" title="2019 Speedway Grand Prix">2019 Speedway Grand Prix</a> season. It took place on July 6th at the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=HZ_Bygg_Arena&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="HZ Bygg Arena (page does not exist)">HZ Bygg Arena</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hallstavik" title="Hallstavik">Hallstavik</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Riders">Riders</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_Speedway_Grand_Prix_of_Sweden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Riders">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>First reserve <a href="/wiki/Robert_Lambert_(speedway_rider)" title="Robert Lambert (speedway rider)">Robert Lambert</a> replaced the injured world champion <a href="/wiki/Tai_Woffinden" title="Tai Woffinden">Tai Woffinden</a>, while second reserve <a href="/wiki/Max_Fricke" title="Max Fricke">Max Fricke</a> replaced <a href="/wiki/Greg_Hancock" title="Greg Hancock">Greg Hancock</a>. The Speedway Grand Prix Commission nominated <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Oliver_Berntzon&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Oliver Berntzon (page does not exist)">Oliver Berntzon</a> as the wild card, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pontus_Aspgren&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Pontus Aspgren (page does not exist)">Pontus Aspgren</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kim_Nilsson_(speedway_rider)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kim Nilsson (speedway rider) (page does not exist)">Kim Nilsson</a> both as Track Reserves. 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Results">Results</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_Speedway_Grand_Prix_of_Sweden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Results">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Grand Prix was won by <a href="/wiki/Emil_Sayfutdinov" title="Emil Sayfutdinov">Emil Sayfutdinov</a>, who beat <a href="/wiki/Martin_Vacul%C3%ADk" title="Martin Vaculík">Martin Vaculík</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maciej_Janowski" title="Maciej Janowski">Maciej Janowski</a> and <a href="/wiki/Max_Fricke" title="Max Fricke">Max Fricke</a> in the final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It was the seventh win of Sayfutdinov's career, but his first since <a href="/wiki/2013_Speedway_Grand_Prix" title="2013 Speedway Grand Prix">2013</a>. The win also saw him move to the joint-lead of the overall standings, tied on 47 points with <a href="/wiki/Patryk_Dudek" title="Patryk Dudek">Patryk Dudek</a> and <a href="/wiki/Leon_Madsen" title="Leon Madsen">Leon Madsen</a>, who both failed to make the semi-finals.
</p>
