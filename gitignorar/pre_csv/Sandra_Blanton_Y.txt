<p><b>Sandra Clark Blanton</b>, known as <b>Sandy Blanton</b> (born 1949),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is an <a href="/wiki/Accountant" title="Accountant">accountant</a> from <a href="/wiki/Paoli,_Indiana" title="Paoli, Indiana">Paoli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">Indiana</a>, who served from 2007 to 2011 as a <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">Democratic</a>  member of the <a href="/wiki/Indiana_House_of_Representatives" title="Indiana House of Representatives">Indiana House of Representatives</a> for the 62nd District, which includes her own <a href="/wiki/Orange_County,_Indiana" title="Orange County, Indiana">Orange County</a> in the southern portion of her state.
</p><p>In 2008, Mrs. Blanton won her second term in the legislature by defeating the <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republican</a> Daniel Brook Tarr, 13,608 votes (57.4 percent) to his 10,118 (42.6 percent). In 2010, she was unseated in her quest for a third term by the Republican Matt Ubelhor, 9,962 (57.4 percent) to her 7,400 (42.6 percent). She sought a comeback in 2012 in District 73 but was defeated by the incumbent Republican Steve Davisson, 13,357 votes (54.5 percent) to 11,160 (45.5 percent).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ballot_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ballot-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Blanton is the oldest of four children of Robert Scott Clark, Sr. (1931-2013), and the former Lillian Bruner (1931-2015), both natives of <a href="/wiki/Shelby_County,_Kentucky" title="Shelby County, Kentucky">Shelby County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kentucky" title="Kentucky">Kentucky</a>. Her father was a pastor, <a href="/wiki/History" title="History">history</a> professor, and a long-term vice president of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southern_Baptist" title="Southern Baptist">Southern Baptist</a>-affiliated <a href="/wiki/Campbellsville_University" title="Campbellsville University">Campbellsville University</a> in <a href="/wiki/Campbellsville,_Kentucky" title="Campbellsville, Kentucky">Campbellsville</a>, Kentucky; her mother was a retired <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> and <a href="/wiki/Speech" title="Speech">speech</a> teacher who served for many years on the <a href="/wiki/Taylor_County,_Kentucky" title="Taylor County, Kentucky">Tayor County</a> Board of Education in Campbellsville and was a former <a href="/wiki/Adjunct_professor" title="Adjunct professor">adjunct professor</a> at Campbellsville University.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Blanton graduated from Campbellsville University in 1977. Her husband, Larry R. Blanton (born 1944), an Indiana circuit judge, graduated in 1969 from CU. In 1987, Blanton received the <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Business_Administration" title="Master of Business Administration">Master of Business Administration</a> degree from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholicism" title="Roman Catholicism">Roman Catholic</a>-affiliated <a href="/wiki/Bellarmine_University" title="Bellarmine University">Bellarmine University</a> in <a href="/wiki/Louisville,_Kentucky" title="Louisville, Kentucky">Louisville</a>, Kentucky. In 2000, she received the Master of Health Care Administration degree from <a href="/wiki/Western_Kentucky_University" title="Western Kentucky University">Western Kentucky University</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bowling_Green,_Kentucky" title="Bowling Green, Kentucky">Bowling Green</a>, Kentucky.
</p><p>An accountant with Southern Indiana Community Healthcare, Blanton is a member of the Mount Pleasant Baptist Church, Phi Beta Psi, and the Democrat Women's Club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ballot_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-ballot-2">[2]</a></sup> Her siblings are Renee Clark Kessler (born 1952) and husband, Marc, of <a href="/wiki/Greensburg,_Kentucky" title="Greensburg, Kentucky">Greensburg</a>, Kentucky, Beverly Clark Manley (born 1957) and husband, Gerald, of <a href="/wiki/Waddy,_Kentucky" title="Waddy, Kentucky">Waddy</a>, Kentucky, and Robert S. Clark, Jr. (born 1962), and wife, Mary, of Campbellsville.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Robert "Bob" Clark, Jr., was named in April 2016 to succeed his mother on the Campbellsville School Board.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
