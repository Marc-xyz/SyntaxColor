	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en"><i>Saturday Playhouse</i></h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><i><b>Saturday Playhouse</b></i> was a 60-minute <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UK" title="UK">UK</a> anthology television series produced by and airing on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="British Broadcasting Corporation">British Broadcasting Corporation</a> (BBC) from 4 January 1958 until 1 April 1961. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IMDb1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-IMDb1-1">[1]</a></sup> There were sixty-eight episodes, among them adaptations of the plays <i><a href="/wiki/The_Man_Who_Came_to_Dinner" title="The Man Who Came to Dinner">The Man Who Came to Dinner</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cat_and_the_Canary_(play)" title="The Cat and the Canary (play)">The Cat and the Canary</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IMDb1_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-IMDb1-1">[1]</a></sup> One of the episodes, <a href="/wiki/Alex_Atkinson" title="Alex Atkinson">Alex Atkinson</a>’s classic thriller <i>Design for Murder</i>, was featured twice on the BBC: first on <i>Saturday Playhouse</i> (Saturday, 15 March 1958; S1/Ep.6) and again from the BBC’s own theatre in Bristol (Thursday, 6 July 1961).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ScreenPlaysDfM_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ScreenPlaysDfM-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Many actors performed for <i>Saturday Playhouse</i>, including:
<a href="/wiki/Maxine_Audley" title="Maxine Audley">Maxine Audley</a>, 
<a href="/wiki/John_Barrie" title="John Barrie">John Barrie</a>, 
<a href="/wiki/Michael_Bates_(actor)" title="Michael Bates (actor)">Michael Bates</a>, 
<a href="/wiki/Brian_Blessed" title="Brian Blessed">Brian Blessed</a>, 
<a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Brett" title="Jeremy Brett">Jeremy Brett</a>, 
<a href="/wiki/Michael_Crawford" title="Michael Crawford">Michael Crawford</a>,
<a href="/wiki/Anton_Diffring" title="Anton Diffring">Anton Diffring</a>,
<a href="/wiki/Paul_Eddington" title="Paul Eddington">Paul Eddington</a>, 
<a href="/wiki/Denholm_Elliott" title="Denholm Elliott">Denholm Elliott</a>, 
<a href="/wiki/Thora_Hird" title="Thora Hird">Thora Hird</a>,
<a href="/wiki/Desmond_Llewelyn" title="Desmond Llewelyn">Desmond Llewelyn</a>,
<a href="/wiki/Margaret_Lockwood" title="Margaret Lockwood">Margaret Lockwood</a>,
<a href="/wiki/Leo_McKern" title="Leo McKern">Leo McKern</a>, 
<a href="/wiki/Bob_Monkhouse" title="Bob Monkhouse">Bob Monkhouse</a>, 
<a href="/wiki/Leslie_Phillips" title="Leslie Phillips">Leslie Phillips</a>,
<a href="/wiki/Prunella_Scales" title="Prunella Scales">Prunella Scales</a> and
<a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_Shepherd" title="Elizabeth Shepherd">Elizabeth Shepherd</a>, among others.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IMDb2_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-IMDb2-3">[3]</a></sup> 
</p><p>Only a single episode is believed to have survived.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LostShows_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-LostShows-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
