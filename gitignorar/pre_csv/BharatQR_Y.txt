<p><b>BharatQR</b>, developed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NPCI" title="NPCI">NPCI</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mastercard" title="Mastercard">Mastercard</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Visa_Inc." title="Visa Inc.">Visa</a>, is an integrated <a href="/wiki/Payment_system" title="Payment system">payment system</a> in <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>. The system, which was launched in September 2016, facilitates users to transfer their money from one source to another. The money transferred through BharatQR is received directly in the user's linked bank account. It provides a common interface between RuPay, Mastercard, Visa, American Express as opposed to other such systems used by startups and is interoperable with all the banks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Currently, BharatQR is supported on <a href="/wiki/Android_(operating_system)" title="Android (operating system)">Android</a> and <a href="/wiki/IOS" title="IOS">iOS</a> devices.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BharatQR&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>BharatQR was launched in September 2016 as per the directions set by the <a href="/wiki/Reserve_Bank_of_India" title="Reserve Bank of India">Reserve Bank of India</a> with aim of facilitating India in transition to a less-cash society. The system helps in enabling digital payments to reduce the usage of card swiping machines for payment. A number of banks supported BharatQR, before the system was launched, and got ready to deploy it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Although, BharatQR primarily works by scanning <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/QR_codes" title="QR codes">QR codes</a>, it is not the only way to make payments. It allows users to pay through <a href="/wiki/Aadhaar" title="Aadhaar">Aadhaar</a> number, <a href="/wiki/Unified_Payments_Interface" title="Unified Payments Interface">UPI</a> payment address or through account number and IFSC code, thus, minimizing the need to pay using debit and credit card which are, supposedly, less secure than BharatQR.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The system, also, supports Dynamic QR code generation which eliminates the need of entering the amount for payment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>This payment system supports <a href="/wiki/RuPay" title="RuPay">RuPay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mastercard" title="Mastercard">Mastercard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Visa_Inc." title="Visa Inc.">Visa</a>, <a href="/wiki/American_Express" title="American Express">American Express</a> and 14 other national banks. Multiple cards can be linked to BharatQR supported bank apps and can be selected any one of them to handle transactions conveniently for bank customers, partners, merchants. BharatQR enables faster &amp; secure mode of transactions with <a href="/wiki/Immediate_Payment_Service" title="Immediate Payment Service">Immediate Payment Service (IMPS)</a>. There is no need to share mobile number or account details, CVV with anyone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>NPCI, Mastercard and Visa developed BharatQR with provisions for three additional fields: bank account and IFS (Indian Financial System) code, Unified Payments Interface (UPI) and Aadhaar. These additional fields provide banks the option to populate them as QR-based payments scale in the country. American Express is also on board to adopt these standards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Usage">Usage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BharatQR&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Usage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bijlipay became the first Indian Point-of-Sale (PoS) devices providing company to enable it on the screens of PoS machines. Bijlipay, an end to end digital payment company, has enabled the QR code for 12,000 merchants and targets to make the facility available to another 50,000 merchants by the end of 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> 
</p><p>ItzCash announced rolling-out Bharat QR code feature across its retail network to scan and pay money with ItzCash's Point-of-Sale devices by simply flashing cell phone across our robust network of 75,000 outlets in 3,000 cities and towns across India.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Samsung_Pay" title="Samsung Pay">Samsung Pay</a>, which earlier in 2017, integrated Samsung Pay mobile wallet with <a href="/wiki/Unified_Payments_Interface" title="Unified Payments Interface">Unified Payments Interface</a>, integrated BharatQR to pay at merchants supporting UPI payment system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>By early 2018, <a href="/wiki/Delhi_Metro_Rail_Corporation" title="Delhi Metro Rail Corporation">Delhi Metro Rail Corporation</a> is planning to introduce BharatQR code enabled <a href="/wiki/Automated_fare_collection" title="Automated fare collection">AFC</a> gates at all the stations of the Airport Express Line allowing passengers to use the system while using the Line. Two entry points at <a href="/wiki/Lal_Qila_metro_station" title="Lal Qila metro station">Lal Qila metro station</a> and two exit points at <a href="/wiki/Jama_Masjid_metro_station" title="Jama Masjid metro station">Jama Masjid metro station</a> are set by DMRC providing commuters to enter and exit using QR codes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
