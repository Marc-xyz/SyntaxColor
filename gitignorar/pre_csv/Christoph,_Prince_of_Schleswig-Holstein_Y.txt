<p><b>Christoph, Prince of Schleswig-Holstein</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thePeerage_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-thePeerage-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OnlineGotha_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-OnlineGotha-2">[2]</a></sup> (legal name: <i>Christoph Prinz zu Schleswig-Holstein</i>; born 22 August 1949)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thePeerage_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-thePeerage-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OnlineGotha_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-OnlineGotha-2">[2]</a></sup> has been the head of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Gl%C3%BCcksburg" title="House of Glücksburg">House of Schleswig-Holstein-Sonderburg-Glücksburg</a> (commonly known as the House of Glücksburg) and, by agnatic <a href="/wiki/Primogeniture" title="Primogeniture">primogeniture</a>, of the entire <a href="/wiki/House_of_Oldenburg" title="House of Oldenburg">House of Oldenburg</a> since 1980.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ghda_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-ghda-3">[3]</a></sup>  He is the current titular <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Duke_of_Schleswig-Holstein" title="Duke of Schleswig-Holstein">Duke of Schleswig-Holstein</a> and Duke of <a href="/wiki/Gl%C3%BCcksburg" title="Glücksburg">Glücksburg</a>, traditionally <a href="/wiki/Style_(manner_of_address)" title="Style (manner of address)">styled</a> as <i><a href="/wiki/Highness" title="Highness">His Highness</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ghda_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-ghda-3">[3]</a></sup>  He is a male-line descendant of <a href="/wiki/Christian_I_of_Denmark" title="Christian I of Denmark">Christian I of Denmark</a>, and is also descended <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cognate_(kinship)" title="Cognate (kinship)">cognatically</a> from numerous more recent monarchs, including <a href="/wiki/Queen_Victoria" title="Queen Victoria">Queen Victoria</a>, Emperor <a href="/wiki/Alexander_II_of_Russia" title="Alexander II of Russia">Alexander II of Russia</a> and several more recent Danish kings.
</p><p>The members of the house he heads include the reigning monarchs of Denmark and Norway, the deposed monarch of Greece, and the heir-apparent to the British throne.
</p>

<h2><span id="House_of_Schleswig-Holstein-Sonderburg-Gl.C3.BCcksburg"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="House_of_Schleswig-Holstein-Sonderburg-Glücksburg">House of Schleswig-Holstein-Sonderburg-Glücksburg</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Christoph,_Prince_of_Schleswig-Holstein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: House of Schleswig-Holstein-Sonderburg-Glücksburg">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The House of Oldenburg — in one of its cadet branches — is <a href="/wiki/Patrilineality" title="Patrilineality">patrilineally</a> the royal house of Denmark (since 1448) and Norway (1450–1818 and since 1905), has been the reigning dynasty of several other countries including Greece, Sweden and Russia,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ghda_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-ghda-3">[3]</a></sup> and also includes the heir to the throne of the United Kingdom. As such, Christoph is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Agnatic_primogeniture" title="Agnatic primogeniture">agnatic head</a> of the family that today includes <a href="/wiki/Margrethe_II_of_Denmark" title="Margrethe II of Denmark">Margrethe II of Denmark</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Harald_V_of_Norway" title="Harald V of Norway">Harald V of Norway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Constantine_II_of_Greece" title="Constantine II of Greece">Constantine II of Greece</a> and, <a href="/wiki/Patrilineality" title="Patrilineality">patrilineally</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Charles,_Prince_of_Wales" title="Charles, Prince of Wales">Charles, Prince of Wales</a>. His great-great-grandfather, <a href="/wiki/Friedrich,_Duke_of_Schleswig-Holstein-Sonderburg-Gl%C3%BCcksburg" title="Friedrich, Duke of Schleswig-Holstein-Sonderburg-Glücksburg">Friedrich, Duke of Schleswig-Holstein-Sonderburg-Glücksburg</a>, was the older brother of <a href="/wiki/Christian_IX_of_Denmark" title="Christian IX of Denmark">Christian IX of Denmark</a>, and through him Christoph is heir by male <a href="/wiki/Primogeniture" title="Primogeniture">primogeniture</a> to the Danish title Duke of Glücksburg conferred by the Danish crown in 1825.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ghda_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-ghda-3">[3]</a></sup> Christoph is also, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cognatic" title="Cognatic">cognatically</a>, a descendant of <a href="/wiki/Queen_Victoria" title="Queen Victoria">Queen Victoria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alexander_II_of_Russia" title="Alexander II of Russia">Alexander II of Russia</a>, and is in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Line_of_succession_to_the_British_throne" title="Line of succession to the British throne">line of succession to the British throne</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Christoph,_Prince_of_Schleswig-Holstein&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Christoph was born in Louisenlund Castle in <a href="/wiki/G%C3%BCby" title="Güby">Güby</a>, Germany,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thePeerage_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-thePeerage-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OnlineGotha_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-OnlineGotha-2">[2]</a></sup> the eldest son of <a href="/wiki/Peter,_Duke_of_Schleswig-Holstein" title="Peter, Duke of Schleswig-Holstein">Peter, Duke of Schleswig-Holstein</a> (1922–1980) and his wife <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Princess_Marie_Alix_of_Schaumburg-Lippe" title="Princess Marie Alix of Schaumburg-Lippe">Princess Marie Alix of Schaumburg-Lippe</a> (b. 1923).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thePeerage_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-thePeerage-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OnlineGotha_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-OnlineGotha-2">[2]</a></sup> He has a <a href="/wiki/Diploma" title="Diploma">diploma</a> in Agricultural Engineering.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ghda_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-ghda-3">[3]</a></sup> Christoph served as a <a href="/wiki/Reservist" title="Reservist">Reservist</a> in the <a href="/wiki/German_Army" title="German Army">German Army</a> for two years holding the rank of <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant" title="Lieutenant">lieutenant</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>He succeeded to the headship of the ducal house on 30 September 1980 following the death of his father. While possession of the united duchies of Schleswig and Holstein had been allocated by a series of wars and treaties since the <a href="/wiki/First_Schleswig_War" title="First Schleswig War">First Schleswig War</a> of 1848 and the <a href="/wiki/London_Protocol_(1852)" title="London Protocol (1852)">London Protocol of 1852</a>, the ducal title was borne by Christoph's father and paternal grandfather (as inherited from his great-grandfather, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Friedrich_Ferdinand,_Duke_of_Schleswig-Holstein-Sonderburg-Gl%C3%BCcksburg" title="Friedrich Ferdinand, Duke of Schleswig-Holstein-Sonderburg-Glücksburg">Friedrich Ferdinand, Duke of Schleswig-Holstein-Sonderburg-Glücksburg</a> in 1934). However Christoph is known also by the title which is shared by male <a href="/wiki/Cadet_(genealogy)" title="Cadet (genealogy)">cadets</a> of the dynasty, "Prince of Schleswig-Holstein-Sonderburg-Glücksburg".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ghda_3-5"><a href="#cite_note-ghda-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Since 1980, Christoph chairs the board of the family foundation that owns the ancestral castle, <a href="/wiki/Gl%C3%BCcksburg_Castle" title="Glücksburg Castle">Glücksburg Castle</a>. He is a founding member of the GLC Glücksburg Consulting Group and serves as chairman of its advisory board. He resides in Grünholz near <a href="/wiki/Schwansen" title="Schwansen">Schwansen</a> where he has business interests in agriculture, forestry and real estate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He is the owner of the Grünholz and Bienebek estates and is one of the largest landowners of Schleswig-Holstein. His sister, Princess Ingeborg, chairs the board of a further family foundation, the <i><a href="/wiki/Stiftung_Louisenlund" title="Stiftung Louisenlund">Stiftung Louisenlund</a></i>.
</p>
