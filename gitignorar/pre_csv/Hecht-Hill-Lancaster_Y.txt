	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Hecht-Hill-Lancaster</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>

		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Hecht-Hill-Lancaster</b> was a production company formed by the actor <a href="/wiki/Burt_Lancaster" title="Burt Lancaster">Burt Lancaster</a> in association with his agent, <a href="/wiki/Harold_Hecht" title="Harold Hecht">Harold Hecht</a>, and <a href="/wiki/James_Hill_(producer)" title="James Hill (producer)">James Hill</a>. In 1948 Lancaster and Hecht formed <b>Norma Productions</b>, which later became <b>Hecht-Lancaster</b>. Hill joined in the mid-1950s. The company produced some of the most notable American films of the 1950s.
</p><p>In 1956 they renewed their deal with <a href="/wiki/United_Artists" title="United Artists">United Artists</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In late 1957 they announced they would make ten films worth $14 million in 1958.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
