<p><i><b>VideoGaiden</b></i> is a Scottish computer games <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">television</a> show that was broadcast by <a href="/wiki/BBC_Two_Scotland" title="BBC Two Scotland">BBC Two Scotland</a>. Its creators and presenters, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Florence" title="Robert Florence">Robert Florence</a> ("Rab") and Ryan Macleod, were responsible for the internet-distributed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Videogaming" title="Videogaming">videogaming</a> show <i><a href="/wiki/Consolevania" title="Consolevania">Consolevania</a></i>, upon which the show is based.
</p><p>The show began as six ten-minute episodes on BBC Two Scotland, broadcast at around midnight on Fridays starting in December 2005. The episodes were also able to be viewed online from the BBC's web site. A second series, consisting of six half-hour episodes, was commissioned by popular demand and began broadcast on Sunday 5 November 2006 at 11:10pm, with episodes once again available on the BBC's website. A third series consisting of eighteen weekly 11-minute online episodes began in December 2007, with three half-hour TV specials episodes also being produced. A Christmas special aired on 23 December 2007.
</p><p>A fourth series of the show was announced on 31 December 2015. Series 4 began in March 2016, and consists of six online episodes and one televised special.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Gaiden</i> (外伝) is a <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a> word meaning 'side-story', a reference to the show's relationship to its predecessor <i>Consolevania</i>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Format">Format</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=VideoGaiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Format">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Series_One_.282005.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Series_One_(2005)">Series One (2005)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=VideoGaiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Series One (2005)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Each episode opened with a brief satirical mention of the show's status within the BBC and the feedback it has received. This was typically followed by the first of many discursive game reviews. These featured one of the two lead presenters walking around an indoor or outdoor setting discussing the game in question. Footage from the game was superimposed on background objects and occasionally cut to directly during the discussion.
</p><p>Somewhat in keeping with the way in which <i><a href="/wiki/Consolevania" title="Consolevania">Consolevania</a></i> was shot on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MiniDV" title="MiniDV">MiniDV</a>, the majority of series was filmed on a Sony HVR-Z1U <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.oxm.co.uk/__data/assets/image/329164/BBCShoot.JPG" rel="nofollow">[1]</a>.  This small scale camera made in possible to shoot series in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/16:9" title="16:9">16:9</a>, but without an expensive <a href="/wiki/Betacam" title="Betacam">DigiBeta</a> kit and crew; something that the small production budget would not allow.
</p><p>Recurring features unique to the first series were sketches about terrible video game pitches (like <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_and_White_Minstrel_Show" title="Black and White Minstrel Show">Black and White Minstrel Show</a> 2006</i>), Glasgow hard-man Barry Anoki reporting on video game news, and a spoof soap opera about video game development called <i>DevCo</i>. The "Gaiden Top 100" was ostensibly a series of talking-head interviews on the best games of all time, but in fact provided thinly veiled mockery or game-inspired jokes.
</p><p>Each episode in this series ended with a brief sketch which ran over the end credits, featuring a fictional world-famous video gamer called Legend (a carry-over from <i>Consolevania</i>) discussing his childhood.
</p>
<h3><span id="Series_Two_.282006.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Series_Two_(2006)">Series Two (2006)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=VideoGaiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Series Two (2006)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The second series had less of a sketch basis. While the opening comments and the style of reviews were the same and the Top 100 returned, most of the comedy was changed. This series also included a wide range of special guests, such as journalist <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Brooker" title="Charlie Brooker">Charlie Brooker</a> and actor <a href="/wiki/Colin_Baker" title="Colin Baker">Colin Baker</a> appearing in character as the <a href="/wiki/Sixth_Doctor" title="Sixth Doctor">Sixth Doctor</a>.
</p><p>Non-game reviews were featured in the style of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Games_Journalism" title="New Games Journalism">new games journalism</a> panel discussion called "Eternal Pixel" (compare to <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Newsnight_Review" title="Newsnight Review">Newsnight Review</a></i>). More episodes of Eternal Pixel were filmed than aired, with plans to release these additional episodes online; however, these did not come to fruition.<a class="external autonumber" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20070928193106/http://thefraudcast.com/site/?p=271" rel="nofollow">[2]</a> In the first three episodes there was a "funeral" for a now-deceased console, followed by recommendations of notable games. Each episode included "A Warning From History", showcasing appalling old gaming action, followed by a game recommendation from Socrates, a "games criticism robot" made from cardboard. Of particular note was the "Gaiden Guide to..." series by Robert Florence, sternly calling for change in a particular aspect of gaming culture (for example standards of journalism). Each episode of the second series signed off with a song about the series' far-from-universal appeal.
</p><p>The show also ran an (apparently BBC-sanctioned) campaign to have the <i><a href="/wiki/Shenmue" title="Shenmue">Shenmue</a></i> series either continued or permanently consigned to history. Rab and Ryan called on gamers to produce <i>Shenmue</i> tributes or outright mockery, respectively.<a class="external autonumber" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20061208111856/http://www.bbc.co.uk/scotland/tv/videogaiden/campaign/index.shtml" rel="nofollow">[3]</a> Updates on the progress of the campaign including notable comments on the internet were included in each episode. Rab won overall, with the public favouring the return of <i>Shenmue</i>. However, an unofficial statement from Sega warned them not to expect a new installment for some time.
</p><p>The series ended with "The VideoGaiden Awards 2006" in which Rab and Ryan chose the best game of 2006. The winner of this award was <i><a href="/wiki/Hitman:_Blood_Money" title="Hitman: Blood Money">Hitman: Blood Money</a></i>. It was also in this episode that Rab fell on Socrates, crushing him beyond repair in the process.
</p>
<h3><span id="Series_Three_.282007-2008.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Series_Three_(2007-2008)">Series Three (2007-2008)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=VideoGaiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Series Three (2007-2008)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Series three was broadcast online on <a class="external text" href="http://www.bbc.co.uk/videogaiden/" rel="nofollow">the BBC's VideoGaiden website</a>, with each weekly episode lasting around 11 to 13 minutes. The series generally contained two regular reviews lasting a couple of minutes each, and one shorter review comprising only video game footage and voice overs. These would be interspersed with short comedy sketches. Each episode would also have a bookending pair of sketches that would only rarely be returned to during the course of the series as a whole.
</p><p>An ongoing serial called Hunter and Rayorg featuring CGI versions of Rab and Ryan was one of only two recurring sketches. The other was VideoGaiden Worldwide News, which had Rab and Ryan interviewing a number of correspondents: a manager from a games store in Scotland (G-Force, Glasgow) who announced that week's top-selling titles; an English correspondent who is clearly Scottish; and a woman in Japan, who would always end up reviewing Japanese cuisine, despite claiming that she was talking about video games. Each week would also see Rab and Ryan trying to contact a representative in the US, before announcing that they couldn't get the satellite link-up working and giving up. This was changed briefly in episodes four and five, when US comedians <a href="/wiki/Tim_Heidecker" title="Tim Heidecker">Tim Heidecker</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eric_Wareheim" title="Eric Wareheim">Eric Wareheim</a> (both of <i><a href="/wiki/Tom_Goes_to_the_Mayor" title="Tom Goes to the Mayor">Tom Goes to the Mayor</a></i> fame) made guest appearances.
</p>
<h3><span id="Series_Four_.282016.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Series_Four_(2016)">Series Four (2016)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=VideoGaiden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Series Four (2016)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On New Year's Eve 2015, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Macleod&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ryan Macleod (page does not exist)">Ryan Macleod</a> and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Florence" title="Robert Florence">Robert Florence</a> via <a href="/wiki/Periscope_(app)" title="Periscope (app)">Periscope</a> announced a return of <i>videoGaiden</i>, after eight years. The first episode was released on YouTube on 3 March 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Series 4 features review segments, the videoGaiden top 100, Ryan's coverage of indie games, Rab's coverage of board games and segments of the two realising they are trapped in an existential prison.
</p>
