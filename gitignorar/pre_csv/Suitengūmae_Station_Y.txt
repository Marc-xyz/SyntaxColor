<p><b>Suitengūmae Station</b><span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">水天宮前駅</span><span style="display:none">,</span> <i>Suitengūmae-eki</i>)</span> is a subway station on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tokyo_Metro_Hanzomon_Line" title="Tokyo Metro Hanzomon Line">Tokyo Metro Hanzomon Line</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ch%C5%AB%C5%8D,_Tokyo" title="Chūō, Tokyo">Chūō, Tokyo</a>, operated by the Tokyo subway operator <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Metro" title="Tokyo Metro">Tokyo Metro</a>. It is connected by <a href="/wiki/Moving_walkway" title="Moving walkway">moving walkways</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_City_Air_Terminal" title="Tokyo City Air Terminal">Tokyo City Air Terminal</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ningyocho_Station" title="Ningyocho Station">Ningyocho Station</a> is located 500 meters to the east (although there is no transfer corridor between the two stations).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Suiteng%C5%ABmae_Station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Suitengūmae Station opened on 28 November 1990.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-terada2013_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-terada2013-1">[1]</a></sup> It was the eastern terminus of the Hanzomon Line until 2003, when the line was extended to <a href="/wiki/Oshiage_Station" title="Oshiage Station">Oshiage Station</a>.
</p>
