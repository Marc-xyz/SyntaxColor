<p>The <b>Church of the Holy Trinity</b> at Chantry, in the parish of <a href="/wiki/Whatley,_Mendip" title="Whatley, Mendip">Whatley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Somerset" title="Somerset">Somerset</a>, England, dates from 1844–46.  It was designed by <a href="/wiki/George_Gilbert_Scott" title="George Gilbert Scott">George Gilbert Scott</a> and William Moffatt, with further work by William George Brown of <a href="/wiki/Frome" title="Frome">Frome</a>, for <a href="/wiki/James_Fussell_IV" title="James Fussell IV">James Fussell</a>, who owned the <a href="/wiki/Old_Iron_Works,_Mells" title="Old Iron Works, Mells">Old Iron Works, Mells</a>. It is a Grade I <a href="/wiki/Listed_building" title="Listed building">listed building</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The small <a href="/wiki/Doulting" title="Doulting">Doulting</a> stone church has a <a href="/wiki/Nave" title="Nave">nave</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chancel" title="Chancel">chancel</a>, porch and <a href="/wiki/Vestry" title="Vestry">vestry</a>. The roof is covered by 400 slates each of which is 6 feet (1.8 m) by 1 foot 9 inches (0.53 m) with the gaps covered by rolls of slate. The small spire contains a single bell.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1858 <a href="/wiki/Richard_William_Church" title="Richard William Church">Richard William Church</a> was amongst the clergy of the church.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Nearby is the <a href="/wiki/Manor_Farmhouse_Gatehouse,_Whatley" title="Manor Farmhouse Gatehouse, Whatley">Manor Farmhouse Gatehouse</a> which was built around 1500 and is also Grade I listed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IoE_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-IoE-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>The parish is within the <a href="/wiki/Benefice" title="Benefice">benefice</a> of <a href="/wiki/Mells,_Somerset" title="Mells, Somerset">Mells</a> with <a href="/wiki/Buckland_Dinham" title="Buckland Dinham">Buckland Dinham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Great_Elm" title="Great Elm">Great Elm</a> and Whatley within the <a href="/wiki/Frome" title="Frome">Frome</a> deanery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
