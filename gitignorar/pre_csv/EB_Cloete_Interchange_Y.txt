<p>The <b>EB Cloete Interchange</b> near <a href="/wiki/Durban" title="Durban">Durban</a> in <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Interchange_(road)" title="Interchange (road)">interchange</a> between the two national roads that pass through Durban: the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/N2_road_(South_Africa)" title="N2 road (South Africa)">N2</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/N3_highway,_South_Africa" title="N3 highway, South Africa">N3</a>. It has been given various nicknames, the most famous one is "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spaghetti_Junction" title="Spaghetti Junction">Spaghetti Junction</a>". The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/N3_highway,_South_Africa" title="N3 highway, South Africa">N3</a> is the busiest highway in South Africa and is a very busy truck route. Since Johannesburg is not located near a body of water such as a river or an ocean, most of the city's exports rely on the Port of <a href="/wiki/Durban" title="Durban">Durban</a> to export its goods. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/N2_road_(South_Africa)" title="N2 road (South Africa)">N2</a> connects <a href="/wiki/Cape_Town" title="Cape Town">Cape Town</a> with <a href="/wiki/Durban" title="Durban">Durban</a>, and it serves other South African cities as well, such as <a href="/wiki/Port_Elizabeth" title="Port Elizabeth">Port Elizabeth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Plettenberg_Bay" title="Plettenberg Bay">Plettenberg Bay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Margate,_KwaZulu-Natal" title="Margate, KwaZulu-Natal">Margate, KwaZulu-Natal</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greater_St._Lucia_Wetland_Park" title="Greater St. Lucia Wetland Park">Greater St. Lucia Wetland Park</a>. Two very busy roads intersect at this junction, thus leading to a very busy junction. A <a href="/wiki/Stack_interchange#Four-level_stack" title="Stack interchange">four level stack interchange</a> was chosen for this junction to serve the high volumes of traffic. This is the only 4 level interchange in South Africa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dict01_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dict01-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>6</span></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origin_of_nickname">Origin of nickname</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=EB_Cloete_Interchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origin of nickname">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The interchange's nickname comes from that of <a href="/wiki/Gravelly_Hill_Interchange" title="Gravelly Hill Interchange">Gravelly Hill Junction</a> in <a href="/wiki/Birmingham" title="Birmingham">Birmingham</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UK" title="UK">UK</a>, which had opened fifteen years previously and been given the nickname "Spaghetti Junction" by locals.
</p>
