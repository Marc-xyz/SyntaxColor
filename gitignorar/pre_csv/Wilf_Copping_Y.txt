<p><b>Wilfred Copping</b> (17 August 1909 – June 1980) was an English <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who played for <a href="/wiki/Leeds_United_F.C." title="Leeds United F.C.">Leeds United</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> and the England national team.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wilf_Copping&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Leeds_United">Leeds United</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wilf_Copping&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Leeds United">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Copping was born in <a href="/wiki/Middlecliffe" title="Middlecliffe">Middlecliffe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barnsley" title="Barnsley">Barnsley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a>, but was rejected by his local team <a href="/wiki/Barnsley_F.C." title="Barnsley F.C.">Barnsley</a>, and worked for a time in the pits while playing for Middlecliffe Rovers. He eventually joined <a href="/wiki/Leeds_United_F.C." title="Leeds United F.C.">Leeds United</a> in 1929, and made his debut at the start of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1930-31_in_English_football" title="1930-31 in English football">1930-31</a> season, soon becoming a regular in the Leeds half-back line.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bio_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bio-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Together with his Leeds United teammate <a href="/wiki/Billy_Furness" title="Billy Furness">Billy Furness</a>, he made his debut for <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a>, against <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy</a> on 13 May 1933. Copping played over 160 League games for Leeds in five seasons, and won six England caps during that time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bio_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Bio-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Arsenal">Arsenal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wilf_Copping&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Arsenal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Copping was signed by Arsenal in the summer of 1934 for £8,000, as a replacement for <a href="/wiki/Bob_John" title="Bob John">Bob John</a>, and immediately took a first-team place at left half. He made his debut against <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth_F.C." title="Portsmouth F.C.">Portsmouth</a> on 25 August 1934, and was an ever-present in his first season for the club, until suffering a serious knee injury in the third-last match of the season against <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a>. Copping soldiered on and remained on the pitch to ensure Arsenal won the game 2-0; this ensured the club won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1934-35_in_English_football" title="1934-35 in English football">1934-35</a> <a href="/wiki/Football_League_First_Division" title="Football League First Division">First Division</a> title.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Arsenal_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Arsenal-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Copping recovered from his injury to continue playing for Arsenal, making over 35 appearances in each of his first four seasons with the club. During his time at Highbury, Arsenal won the <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1935-36_in_English_football" title="1935-36 in English football">1935-36</a>, another League title in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1937-38_in_English_football" title="1937-38 in English football">1937-38</a>, and two <a href="/wiki/FA_Community_Shield" title="FA Community Shield">Charity Shields</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Arsenal_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Arsenal-2">[2]</a></sup> His England career also continued, and he was one of seven Arsenal players to start England's match against Italy in November 1934 (the "<a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Highbury" title="Battle of Highbury">Battle of Highbury</a>") - a game for which he named man of the match. In total he won 20 caps for England between 1933 and 1939.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bio_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Bio-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>He remained a near-ever present for Arsenal until March 1939; with <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> looming, Copping put in a <a href="/wiki/Transfer_market#Transfer_request" title="Transfer market">transfer request</a> so that he could return to the North with his family. Arsenal duly complied, and Copping re-signed for Leeds United. In all he played 189 matches for Arsenal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Arsenal_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Arsenal-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Leeds_United">Return to Leeds United</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wilf_Copping&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Return to Leeds United">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Copping played out the rest of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1938-39_in_English_football" title="1938-39 in English football">1938-39</a> season with Leeds United, but war broke out in September 1939 and all first-class football was cancelled. Copping joined the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_army" title="British army">Army</a> at the start of the war, and served in <a href="/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a>, eventually becoming a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sergeant_Major" title="Sergeant Major">Sergeant Major</a>. He played the occasional wartime game for Leeds, but retired from playing football in 1942.
</p><p>Copping was known as a hard and strong player; his most famous quote was "the first man in a tackle never gets hurt". Despite his nickname of "The Iron Man", and his image (he took to the field unshaven, a remarkably unusual practice at the time), Copping was never booked or sent off in his career.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_career">Later career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wilf_Copping&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Later career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the war ended, Copping became a <a href="/wiki/Athletic_trainer" title="Athletic trainer">trainer</a> and coach at various clubs, including <a href="/wiki/K._Beerschot_V.A.C." title="K. Beerschot V.A.C.">Beerschot</a> in Belgium, <a href="/wiki/Southend_United_F.C." title="Southend United F.C.">Southend United</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bristol_City_F.C." title="Bristol City F.C.">Bristol City</a> and <a href="/wiki/Coventry_City_F.C." title="Coventry City F.C.">Coventry City</a>. He retired to live in <a href="/wiki/Southend-on-Sea" title="Southend-on-Sea">Southend-on-Sea</a>. He died in 1980, aged 70.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bio_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Bio-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1998, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Football_League" title="The Football League">the Football League</a>, as part of its centenary season celebrations, included Copping on its list of <a href="/wiki/Football_League_100_Legends" title="Football League 100 Legends">100 League Legends</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Arsenal_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Arsenal-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
