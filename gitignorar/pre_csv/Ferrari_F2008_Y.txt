<p>The <b>Ferrari F2008</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Motor_racing" title="Motor racing">motor racing</a> car, which <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Scuderia Ferrari</a> used to compete in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2008_Formula_One_season" title="2008 Formula One season">2008 Formula One season</a>.
</p><p>The car was unveiled to the public on January 6, 2008.  It features a new standard <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electronic_Control_Unit" title="Electronic Control Unit">Electronic Control Unit</a> (ECU), which is the electronic system that controls all the cars, produced by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/McLaren_Electronic_Systems" title="McLaren Electronic Systems">McLaren Electronic Systems</a>. This was included to comply with the new regulations. The ECU also removes most of the driver aids used in previous seasons, including traction control, engine braking and electronically assisted starting system. It also makes the management of the differential, engine and gearchanges easier.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>The car also weighs more than last season's <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F2007" title="Ferrari F2007">F2007</a> chassis due to rule changes which includes the gearbox which has to be used for four consecutive races, higher side protection around the drivers helmet etc.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>World champion <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> gave the car its first <a href="/wiki/Shakedown_(testing)" title="Shakedown (testing)">shakedown</a> at Ferrari's <a href="/wiki/Fiorano_Circuit" title="Fiorano Circuit">Fiorano</a> test track on January 7, 2008.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Incidents_and_accidents">Incidents and accidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ferrari_F2008&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Incidents and accidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>In the <a href="/wiki/2008_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Australian Grand Prix">2008 Australian Grand Prix</a>, <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> suffered nose cone and front wing damage after an incident with <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">Coulthard</a>.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/2008_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Monaco Grand Prix">2008 Monaco Grand Prix</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> lost his front wing after sliding into the back of <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> at the Nouvelle chicane.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/2008_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Canadian Grand Prix">2008 Canadian Grand Prix</a>, Räikkönen lost his rear wing in the pit lane, after <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> collided with him while the red light still on.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/2008_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Belgian Grand Prix">2008 Belgian Grand Prix</a>, Räikkönen suffered front wing damage after running wide then spinning before the chicane.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Singapore Grand Prix">2008 Singapore Grand Prix</a>, Massa was unsafely released from his pit box with the fuel hose still attached; Räikkönen suffered a suspension problem then hit the wall in the kerbs.</li>
<li>In the <a href="/wiki/2008_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Hungarian Grand Prix">2008 Hungarian Grand Prix</a>, Massa's engine failed without warning, forcing him to retire from the lead with three laps remaining.</li></ul>
