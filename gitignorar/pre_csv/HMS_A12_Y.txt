<p><b>HMS <i>A12</i></b> was an <a href="/wiki/A-class_submarine_(1903)" title="A-class submarine (1903)">A-class</a> <a href="/wiki/Submarine" title="Submarine">submarine</a> built for the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> in the first decade of the 20th century. After surviving World War I, she was sold for <a href="/wiki/Ship_breaking" title="Ship breaking">scrap</a> in 1920.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design_and_description">Design and description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=HMS_A12&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Design and description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>A12</i> was a member of the <a href="/wiki/A-class_submarine_(1903)" title="A-class submarine (1903)">first British class of submarines</a>, although slightly larger, faster and more heavily armed than the <a href="/wiki/Lead_ship" title="Lead ship">lead ship</a>, <a href="/wiki/HMS_A1" title="HMS A1">HMS <i>A1</i></a>. The submarine had a length of 105 feet 1 inch (32.0 m) <a href="/wiki/Length_overall" title="Length overall">overall</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Beam_(nautical)" title="Beam (nautical)">beam</a> of 12 feet 9 inches (3.9 m) and a mean <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Draft_(ship)" title="Draft (ship)">draft</a> of 10 feet 8 inches (3.3 m). They <a href="/wiki/Displacement_(ship)" title="Displacement (ship)">displaced</a> 190 long tons (190 t) on the surface and 206 long tons (209 t) submerged. The A-class submarines had a crew of 2 officers and 11 <a href="/wiki/Naval_rating" title="Naval rating">ratings</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gg9_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-gg9-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>For surface running, the boats were powered by a single 16-cylinder 600-<a href="/wiki/Horsepower#Brake_horsepower" title="Horsepower">brake-horsepower</a> (447 kW) <a href="/wiki/Wolseley_Motors" title="Wolseley Motors">Wolseley</a> <a href="/wiki/Petrol_engine" title="Petrol engine">petrol engine</a> that drove one <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Propeller_shaft" title="Propeller shaft">propeller shaft</a>. When submerged the propeller was driven by a 150-horsepower (112 kW) <a href="/wiki/Electric_motor" title="Electric motor">electric motor</a>. They could reach 11 <a href="/wiki/Knot_(unit)" title="Knot (unit)">knots</a> (20 km/h; 13 mph) on the surface and 6 knots (11 km/h; 6.9 mph) underwater.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gg9_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-gg9-1">[1]</a></sup> On the surface, <i>A12</i> had a range of 500 <a href="/wiki/Nautical_mile" title="Nautical mile">nautical miles</a> (930 km; 580 mi) at 10 knots (19 km/h; 12 mph); submerged the boat had a range of 30 <a href="/wiki/Nautical_mile" title="Nautical mile">nautical miles</a> (56 km; 35 mi) at 5 knots (9.3 km/h; 5.8 mph).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-a0_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-a0-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>The boats were armed with two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_18_inch_torpedo" title="British 18 inch torpedo">18-inch (45 cm)</a> <a href="/wiki/Torpedo_tube" title="Torpedo tube">torpedo tubes</a> in the bow. They could carry a pair of reload torpedoes, but generally did not as they would have to remove an equal weight of fuel in compensation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Construction_and_career">Construction and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=HMS_A12&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Construction and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>A12</i> was ordered as part of the 1903–04 Naval Programme from at <a href="/wiki/Vickers" title="Vickers">Vickers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> She was laid down at the <a href="/wiki/Shipyard" title="Shipyard">shipyard</a> in <a href="/wiki/Barrow-in-Furness" title="Barrow-in-Furness">Barrow-in-Furness</a> in 1903, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ship_naming_and_launching" title="Ship naming and launching">launched</a> on 3 March 1905 and completed on 23 September.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-a0_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-a0-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
