<p><b>Central Oregon Community College</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Community_college" title="Community college">community college</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bend,_Oregon" title="Bend, Oregon">Bend, Oregon</a>. It primarily serves residents of <a href="/wiki/Deschutes_County,_Oregon" title="Deschutes County, Oregon">Deschutes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jefferson_County,_Oregon" title="Jefferson County, Oregon">Jefferson</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Crook_County,_Oregon" title="Crook County, Oregon">Crook</a> Counties.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Central_Oregon_Community_College&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Central Oregon Community College is the oldest two-year college in the state of Oregon. The college was originally founded in 1949 making it almost a decade older than <a href="/wiki/Clatsop_Community_College" title="Clatsop Community College">Clatsop Community College</a>, the next oldest Community College in Oregon. The main campus is located on the west side of <a href="/wiki/Bend,_Oregon" title="Bend, Oregon">Bend, Oregon</a> on 200-acres.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The campus has a total of 26 different buildings, the most recently built being the residence hall in 2015. The location provides views of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oregon_Cascades" title="Oregon Cascades">Oregon Cascades</a>, as well as access to lots of what Bend has to offer. The main campus is located near the <a href="/wiki/Deschutes_National_Forest" title="Deschutes National Forest">Deschutes National Forest</a> and Shevlin Park providing access to miles of trails and outdoor recreation opportunities. Central Oregon Community College also has campuses in <a href="/wiki/Redmond,_Oregon" title="Redmond, Oregon">Redmond, Oregon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prineville,_Oregon" title="Prineville, Oregon">Prineville, Oregon</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Madras,_Oregon" title="Madras, Oregon">Madras, Oregon</a> providing educational opportunities across the Central Oregon Region.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Academics">Academics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Central_Oregon_Community_College&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Academics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Central Oregon Community College provides students the opportunity to pursue an Associates 2 year degree or a Certificate. Programs will either prepare students to enter directly into the workforce or to continue their education as a transfer student to a four-year college for a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor's degree">Bachelor's degree</a>. As of 2018, Central Oregon Community College offers 78 different academic programs,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> including programs like Automotive Technology,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Aviation,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Culinary Arts,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Criminal Justice,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Dentistry,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Fire Science,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Massage Therapy,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Nursing,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Outdoor Leadership,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> and Veterinarian.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Outside of coursework, Central Oregon Community College also provides opportunities for students to become involved in clubs and activities. Clubs provide opportunities for students to become involved in groups focused on their area of study as well as become more involved with the student body. Students also have the opportunity to participate in sports.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Partnership_with_Oregon_State_University">Partnership with Oregon State University</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Central_Oregon_Community_College&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Partnership with Oregon State University">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Central Oregon Community College has a 2+2 partnership with <a href="/wiki/Oregon_State_University_Cascades_Campus" title="Oregon State University Cascades Campus">Oregon State University Cascades Campus</a> offering lower-division and prerequisite coursework that can be used toward the goal of earning a bachelor's degree. Starting Fall 2015, Oregon State University Cascades hosted its first freshman class of students seeking a bachelor's degree.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup>
</p>
