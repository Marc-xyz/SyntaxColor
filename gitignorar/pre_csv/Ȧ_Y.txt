		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="floatright"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:A_with_dot_above.svg"><img alt="A with dot above.svg" data-file-height="405" data-file-width="1053" decoding="async" height="58" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/A_with_dot_above.svg/150px-A_with_dot_above.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/A_with_dot_above.svg/225px-A_with_dot_above.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/A_with_dot_above.svg/300px-A_with_dot_above.svg.png 2x" width="150"/></a></div>
<p><b>Ȧ</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lower_case" title="Lower case">minuscule</a>: <b>ȧ</b>) is a letter of the <a href="/wiki/Latin_alphabet" title="Latin alphabet">Latin alphabet</a>, derived from <a href="/wiki/A" title="A">A</a> with the addition of a <a href="/wiki/Dot_(diacritic)" title="Dot (diacritic)">dot</a> above the letter. It is occasionally used as a <a href="/wiki/Phonetic_transcription" title="Phonetic transcription">phonetic symbol</a> for a low central vowel, <span class="IPA nowrap" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">/<a href="/wiki/Open_central_unrounded_vowel" title="Open central unrounded vowel">ä</a>/</span>. As a character in a computer file, it can be represented in the <a href="/wiki/Unicode" title="Unicode">Unicode</a> <a href="/wiki/Character_encoding" title="Character encoding">character encoding</a>, but not the standard <a href="/wiki/ASCII" title="ASCII">ASCII</a> character encoding.
</p>
