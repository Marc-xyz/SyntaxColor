<p>The <b>flag of <a href="/wiki/Ascension_Island" title="Ascension Island">Ascension Island</a></b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_overseas_territory" title="British overseas territory">British overseas territory</a> of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Helena,_Ascension_and_Tristan_da_Cunha" title="Saint Helena, Ascension and Tristan da Cunha">Saint Helena, Ascension and Tristan da Cunha</a>, was adopted on 11 May 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The flag is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blue_ensign" title="Blue ensign">blue ensign</a> design, defaced with the <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms_of_Ascension_Island" title="Coat of arms of Ascension Island">coat of arms of Ascension Island</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Flag_history">Flag history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Ascension_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Flag history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The current flag was flown for the first time on 11 May 2013. Prior to the adoption of this flag, the island used the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Union_Flag" title="Union Flag">Union Flag</a> of the United Kingdom for official purposes. At its meeting on 3 March 2009, the <a href="/wiki/Ascension_Island_Council" title="Ascension Island Council">Ascension Island Council</a> discussed the idea of a new flag and agreed to develop a unique flag for the island in consultation with the <a href="/wiki/Foreign_and_Commonwealth_Office" title="Foreign and Commonwealth Office">Foreign and Commonwealth Office</a>. Members also suggested that a public competition could be held to determine the design of the new flag.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_2010_proposals">The 2010 proposals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Ascension_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The 2010 proposals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Two draft proposals for an Ascension Island flag were presented to the Island Council at its meeting on 30 July 2010. Following a public consultation, a final design was published in January 2012 and if approved by the Governor,  will be submitted to the <a href="/wiki/College_of_Arms" title="College of Arms">College of Arms</a> for approval.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>The draft proposals were made public in September 2010. Both are blue ensigns defaced with the proposed coats of arms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012_proposal">2012 proposal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Ascension_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2012 proposal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Ascension Island Council approved a final design for a <a href="/wiki/Flags_of_Africa" title="Flags of Africa">coat of arms</a> in January 2012. Following the agreement of the Governor of St Helena, Ascension and Tristan de Cunha, the design was submitted to the College of Arms for formal  be a blue ensign defaced with the proposed arms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The arms received royal approval in August 2012 and are now in official use by the Ascension Island Government.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>An unofficial flag was used to represent Ascension Island at the <a href="/wiki/Thames_Diamond_Jubilee_Pageant" title="Thames Diamond Jubilee Pageant">Thames Diamond Jubilee Pageant</a> in June 2012. The flag was a blue ensign defaced with the coat of arms proposed in January 2012. An image of this flag appeared in the official flag guide to the event published by the <a href="/wiki/Flag_Institute" title="Flag Institute">Flag Institute</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>A final design for the island's flag was agreed by the Ascension Island Council in November 2012 and received approval from HM the Queen in April 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> This flag was raised for the first time on 11 May 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p>
