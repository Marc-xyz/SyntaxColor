	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Karl Moser</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>

		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Karl Moser</b> (August 10, 1860 – February 28, 1936) was an <a href="/wiki/Architect" title="Architect">architect</a> from <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>.  
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:ETH-BIB-Moser,_Karl_(1860-1936)-Portrait-Portr_00350.tif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2130" data-file-width="1421" decoding="async" height="330" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dc/ETH-BIB-Moser%2C_Karl_%281860-1936%29-Portrait-Portr_00350.tif/lossy-page1-220px-ETH-BIB-Moser%2C_Karl_%281860-1936%29-Portrait-Portr_00350.tif.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dc/ETH-BIB-Moser%2C_Karl_%281860-1936%29-Portrait-Portr_00350.tif/lossy-page1-330px-ETH-BIB-Moser%2C_Karl_%281860-1936%29-Portrait-Portr_00350.tif.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dc/ETH-BIB-Moser%2C_Karl_%281860-1936%29-Portrait-Portr_00350.tif/lossy-page1-440px-ETH-BIB-Moser%2C_Karl_%281860-1936%29-Portrait-Portr_00350.tif.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:ETH-BIB-Moser,_Karl_(1860-1936)-Portrait-Portr_00350.tif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Karl Moser</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Antoniuskirche_Basel.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2400" data-file-width="1091" decoding="async" height="330" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Antoniuskirche_Basel.jpg/150px-Antoniuskirche_Basel.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Antoniuskirche_Basel.jpg/225px-Antoniuskirche_Basel.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Antoniuskirche_Basel.jpg/300px-Antoniuskirche_Basel.jpg 2x" width="150"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Antoniuskirche_Basel.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Kirche St. Anton in Basel (1927) in the modernist style</div></div></div>
<p>Between 1887 and 1915 he worked together with <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Curjel&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Robert Curjel (page does not exist)">Robert Curjel</a> in <a href="/wiki/Karlsruhe" title="Karlsruhe">Karlsruhe</a>. Some of their works are:
</p>
<ul><li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kunsthaus_Zurich" title="Kunsthaus Zurich">Kunsthaus Zurich</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/University_of_Zurich" title="University of Zurich">University of Zurich</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Basel_Badischer_Bahnhof" title="Basel Badischer Bahnhof">Basel Badischer Bahnhof</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/St._Paul%27s_Church,_Bern" title="St. Paul's Church, Bern">St. Paul's Church, Bern</a></li>
<li>St. Anthony's (Anoniuskirche), Basel</li>
<li>several Protestant churches</li></ul>
<p>From 1915 to 1928 he was <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">professor</a> at <a href="/wiki/ETH_Zurich" title="ETH Zurich">ETH Zurich</a>.
</p><p>In 1928 he was president of the newly founded <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Congr%C3%A8s_International_d%27Architecture_Moderne" title="Congrès International d'Architecture Moderne">Congrès International d'Architecture Moderne</a>, an organisation, steered prominently by the pioneers of modernism, architects <a href="/wiki/Le_Corbusier" title="Le Corbusier">Le Corbusier</a> and <a href="/wiki/Walter_Gropius" title="Walter Gropius">Walter Gropius</a>, which championed rational and functionalist architecture, while critiquing the type of revivalist architecture typified by Moser's own work. Indeed, at was at this time that Moser's own work changed radically towards modernism, exemplified in the St. Anthony's (Antoniuskirche) in Basel (1925-27), built in reinforced concrete rather than brick and stone typical for his earlier works.
</p><p>His son <a href="/wiki/Werner_M._Moser" title="Werner M. Moser">Werner M. Moser</a> also became a notable architect.
</p>
