	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Mearley Brook</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>

		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Holmes_Mill_Clitheroe_8468.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2448" data-file-width="3264" decoding="async" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Holmes_Mill_Clitheroe_8468.JPG/220px-Holmes_Mill_Clitheroe_8468.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Holmes_Mill_Clitheroe_8468.JPG/330px-Holmes_Mill_Clitheroe_8468.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Holmes_Mill_Clitheroe_8468.JPG/440px-Holmes_Mill_Clitheroe_8468.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Holmes_Mill_Clitheroe_8468.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Mearley Brook at <a href="/wiki/Clitheroe" title="Clitheroe">Clitheroe</a></div></div></div>
<p><b>Mearley Brook</b> is a minor <a href="/wiki/River" title="River">river</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lancashire" title="Lancashire">Lancashire</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. It is approximately 4.5 miles (7.2 km) long and has a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Catchment" title="Catchment">catchment</a> area of 8.79 square miles (22.77 km<sup>2</sup>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Length_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Length-1">[a]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The stream rises on <a href="/wiki/Pendle_Hill" title="Pendle Hill">Pendle Hill</a> and heads northwest, descending through Mearley Clough, passing Little Mearley Hall and crossing under the <a href="/wiki/A59_road" title="A59 road">A59</a> Clitheroe Bypass. Close to Upbrooks Farm it is met by Worston Brook and turns to the southeast. Nearby it met by a small brook from Bracken Hey and the <a href="/wiki/Culvert" title="Culvert">culvert</a> of another at the Waterloo Bridge, as it enters the centre of <a href="/wiki/Clitheroe" title="Clitheroe">Clitheroe</a>. At the Shaw Bridge it is joined by Shaw Brook and continues to the east of the hill topped by <a href="/wiki/Clitheroe_Castle" title="Clitheroe Castle">Clitheroe Castle</a>. In the south of the town, the river flows into the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mill_lodge" title="Mill lodge">mill lodge</a> of the old <a href="/wiki/List_of_mills_in_Clitheroe" title="List of mills in Clitheroe">Primrose Mill</a> and then into <a href="/wiki/Pendleton_Brook" title="Pendleton Brook">Pendleton Brook</a> nearby at Lower Standen Hey, just before that river joins the <a href="/wiki/River_Ribble" title="River Ribble">River Ribble</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Mearley (also the name of the <a href="/wiki/Civil_parish" title="Civil parish">civil parish</a> where the river begins) is thought to be from the <a href="/wiki/Old_English" title="Old English">Old English</a> <i lang="ang" title="Old English language text">mǣre</i> <i lang="ang" title="Old English language text">lēah</i>, meaning the meadow or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clearing_(forest)" title="Clearing (forest)">clearing</a> at the boundary.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ek_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ek-4">[3]</a></sup> Brook (OE <i lang="ang" title="Old English language text">broc</i>) is a common name for a stream, most often found in southern and central England.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-about-6">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
