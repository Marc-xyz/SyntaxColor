<p><b>Amazon Lab126</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> (sometimes known as <b>Lab126</b>) is an American <a href="/wiki/Research_and_development" title="Research and development">research and development</a> and <a href="/wiki/Computer_hardware" title="Computer hardware">computer hardware</a> company owned by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amazon.com" title="Amazon.com">Amazon.com</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fastcompany.com_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-fastcompany.com-5">[5]</a></sup> It was founded in 2004 by Gregg Zehr, previously Vice President of Hardware Engineering at <a href="/wiki/Palm,_Inc." title="Palm, Inc.">Palm</a>, and is based in <a href="/wiki/Sunnyvale,_California" title="Sunnyvale, California">Sunnyvale, California</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> It is widely known for developing Amazon's <a href="/wiki/Amazon_Kindle" title="Amazon Kindle">Kindle</a> line of <a href="/wiki/E-reader" title="E-reader">e-readers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tablet_computer" title="Tablet computer">tablets</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amazon_Lab126&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lab126's name derives from the arrow in Amazon's logo, which points from A to Z—the 1st and 26th letters in the English language <a href="/wiki/Alphabet" title="Alphabet">alphabet</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Products">Products</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Amazon_Lab126&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Products">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>In 2007, after three years of research and development by Lab126, the <a href="/wiki/Amazon_Kindle" title="Amazon Kindle">Amazon Kindle</a> e-reader was released. Newer models of the Kindle continue to be released, with the latest model called the Kindle Oasis in mid-2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></li></ul>
<ul><li>In 2011, Lab126 released the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kindle_Fire" title="Kindle Fire">Kindle Fire</a> <a href="/wiki/Tablet_computer" title="Tablet computer">tablet</a> and in 2012 released two new models of the Fire tablet called the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kindle_Fire_HD" title="Kindle Fire HD">Kindle Fire HD</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> In 2013, it released the <a href="/wiki/Fire_HDX" title="Fire HDX">Fire HDX</a>, a high-end tablet.</li></ul>
<ul><li>In 2014, Lab126 released the <a href="/wiki/Amazon_Fire_TV" title="Amazon Fire TV">Amazon Fire TV</a> <a href="/wiki/Digital_media_player" title="Digital media player">digital media player</a> and in late 2014 released the smaller <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fire_TV_Stick" title="Fire TV Stick">Fire TV Stick</a>.</li></ul>
<ul><li>In 2014, Lab126 released the <a href="/wiki/Fire_Phone" title="Fire Phone">Fire Phone</a> that was not commercially successful.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fastcompany.com_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-fastcompany.com-5">[5]</a></sup></li></ul>
<ul><li>In 2015, Lab126 released the <a href="/wiki/Amazon_Echo" title="Amazon Echo">Amazon Echo</a>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Voice_command_device" title="Voice command device">voice command device</a>.</li></ul>
<ul><li>In 2016, it released the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Echo_Dot" title="Echo Dot">Echo Dot</a>, which is a hockey puck sized version of the Echo and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amazon_Tap" title="Amazon Tap">Amazon Tap</a>, a smaller, portable version of the Echo.</li></ul>
