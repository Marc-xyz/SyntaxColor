<p><b>WLEW-FM</b> (102.1 FM) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radio_station" title="Radio station">radio station</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bad_Axe,_Michigan" title="Bad Axe, Michigan">Bad Axe, Michigan</a>, serving the Thumb area of Michigan.  It is owned by Thumb Broadcasting and broadcasts from studios on South <a href="/wiki/M-53_(Michigan_highway)" title="M-53 (Michigan highway)">Van Dyke Road</a> in Bad Axe.  Unusual for small-town radio stations, WLEW-FM is locally programmed and does not rely on satellite feeds for any of their music programming.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>  
</p><p>WLEW-FM, known as "Cruise 102.1" ("The Thumb's Power Station"), broadcasts at 102.1Â MHz <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/FM_radio" title="FM radio">FM</a> with 50,000 watts of power.  The station's <a href="/wiki/Adult_hits" title="Adult hits">adult hits</a> format is a combination of classic hits and adult contemporary titles from the 1960s through the present.  WLEW-FM was formerly a 3,000-watt <a href="/wiki/Easy_listening" title="Easy listening">easy listening</a> music station at 92.1 until it changed frequencies and boosted its power in the late 1980s (with <a href="/wiki/WIDL" title="WIDL">WIDL</a> in <a href="/wiki/Caro,_Michigan" title="Caro, Michigan">Caro</a>, which had been at 104.9, taking the 92.1 frequency).
</p>
