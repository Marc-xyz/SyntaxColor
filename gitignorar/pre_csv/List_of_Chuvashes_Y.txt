<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Leaders_and_politicians">Leaders and politicians</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_Chuvashes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Leaders and politicians">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:%D0%AD%D0%BB%D1%8C%D0%BC%D0%B5%D0%BD%D1%8C%D0%94%D0%A1.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1309" data-file-width="928" decoding="async" height="310" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/%D0%AD%D0%BB%D1%8C%D0%BC%D0%B5%D0%BD%D1%8C%D0%94%D0%A1.jpg/220px-%D0%AD%D0%BB%D1%8C%D0%BC%D0%B5%D0%BD%D1%8C%D0%94%D0%A1.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/%D0%AD%D0%BB%D1%8C%D0%BC%D0%B5%D0%BD%D1%8C%D0%94%D0%A1.jpg/330px-%D0%AD%D0%BB%D1%8C%D0%BC%D0%B5%D0%BD%D1%8C%D0%94%D0%A1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/%D0%AD%D0%BB%D1%8C%D0%BC%D0%B5%D0%BD%D1%8C%D0%94%D0%A1.jpg/440px-%D0%AD%D0%BB%D1%8C%D0%BC%D0%B5%D0%BD%D1%8C%D0%94%D0%A1.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:%D0%AD%D0%BB%D1%8C%D0%BC%D0%B5%D0%BD%D1%8C%D0%94%D0%A1.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Daniil Elmen</div></div></div>
<ul><li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Vakrim&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Vakrim (page does not exist)">Vakrim</a> (15th century) - Chuvash noble who left <a href="/wiki/Golden_Horde" title="Golden Horde">Golden Horde</a> to become a vassal of <a href="/wiki/Vasily_II_of_Moscow" title="Vasily II of Moscow">Vasily the Blind</a>.</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=M%C4%95tri_C%C4%83va%C5%A1&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mĕtri Căvaš (page does not exist)">Mĕtri Căvaš</a> (16th century) - hero of the <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Kazan" title="Siege of Kazan">siege of Kazan</a>. Descendant of <a href="/wiki/Morza" title="Morza">mărsa</a> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Vakrim&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Vakrim (page does not exist)">Vakrim</a>, ancestor of <a href="/wiki/Gavrila_Derzhavin" title="Gavrila Derzhavin">Gavrila Derzhavin</a>.</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kamaj&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kamaj (page does not exist)">Kamaj</a> (16th century) - Chuvash <a href="/wiki/Morza" title="Morza">mărsa</a> at the service of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Khan_of_Kazan" title="Khan of Kazan">Khan of Kazan</a> who defected to the Russian side.</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Aransajpik&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Aransajpik (page does not exist)">Aransajpik</a> (17th century) - Chuvash noble at Russian service who was granted 300 <a href="/wiki/Obsolete_Russian_units_of_measurement#Area" title="Obsolete Russian units of measurement">desiatinas</a> of land around Şĕrpü (<a href="/wiki/Tsivilsk" title="Tsivilsk">Tsivilsk</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Daniel_Elmen" title="Daniel Elmen">Daniil Elmen</a> (1885–1932) - Chuvash statesman, first leader of <a href="/wiki/Chuvash_Autonomous_Oblast" title="Chuvash Autonomous Oblast">Chuvash autonomy</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Union_of_Soviet_Socialist_Republics" title="Union of Soviet Socialist Republics">Soviet Russia</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Nikolay_Fyodorov_(politician)" title="Nikolay Fyodorov (politician)">Nikolay Fyodorov</a> (b. 1958) - former president of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chuvashia_Republic" title="Chuvashia Republic">Chuvashia</a>.</li></ul>

<ul><li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Emine_(Chuvash_poet)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Emine (Chuvash poet) (page does not exist)">Emine</a> (19th century) - most famous Chuvash woman poet.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Konstantin_Vasilyevich_Ivanov" title="Konstantin Vasilyevich Ivanov">Kĕştenttin Ivanov</a> (1890–1915) - outstanding poet, author of "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Narspi_(poem)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Narspi (poem) (page does not exist)">Narspi</a>".</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mi%C5%A1%C5%A1i_%C5%9Ae%C5%9Bp%C4%95l" title="Mišši Śeśpĕl">Mišši Śeśpĕl</a> (1899–1922) - pioneer of Chuvash <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Syllabotonic_poetry&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Syllabotonic poetry (page does not exist)">syllabotonic poetry</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Dimitri_Isayev_(writer)" title="Dimitri Isayev (writer)">Dimitri Isayev</a> (1905–1930) - prolific writer and literary critic.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ille_Toktash" title="Ille Toktash">Ille Tuktaš</a> (1907–1957) - Chuvash writer, poet and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Folkloristics" title="Folkloristics">folklorist</a>, author of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=National_Anthem_of_the_Chuvash_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="National Anthem of the Chuvash Republic (page does not exist)">anthem of Chuvashia</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Anatoly_Serep" title="Anatoly Serep">Anat Serep</a> (1920–2003) - <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> veteran, writer.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Alexander_Artemiev" title="Alexander Artemiev">Alexander Artemiev</a> (1924–1998) - people's poet, writer and translator.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Arkady_Malov" title="Arkady Malov">Arkady Malov</a> (1928–1995) - Chuvash writer, journalist and translator.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gennadiy_Aygi" title="Gennadiy Aygi">Gennady Aygi</a> (1934–2006) - famous poet and translator, father of Russian <a href="/wiki/Surrealism" title="Surrealism">surrealist</a> poetry.</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Boris_C%C4%83nt%C4%83k&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Boris Căntăk (page does not exist)">Boris Căntăk</a> (b. 1960) - talented writer, playwright and poet.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vasley_Mitta" title="Vasley Mitta">Vasley Mitta</a> (1908–1957) - poet and translator.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Nikifor_Mran%27ka" title="Nikifor Mran'ka">Nikifor Mran'ka</a> (1901-1973) - poet and dramatist</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lyudmila_Vasilyeva" title="Lyudmila Vasilyeva">Lyudmila Vasilyeva</a> (1930-1949) - poet</li></ul>
