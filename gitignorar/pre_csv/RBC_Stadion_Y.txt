	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">RBC Stadion</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>RBC Stadion</b>, former name among others <i>Vast &amp; Goed Stadion</i>, <i>Rosada Stadion</i> and <i>MariFlex Stadion</i>, is a multi-use <a href="/wiki/Stadium" title="Stadium">stadium</a> in <a href="/wiki/Roosendaal" title="Roosendaal">Roosendaal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>. It is used mostly for <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> matches. The stadium is able to hold 4,995 people and was built in 2000. It was the home of the club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RBC_Roosendaal" title="RBC Roosendaal">RBC Roosendaal</a> until the club went bankrupt in June 2011. From the 2013‑14 season on it is again the home stadium of <a href="/wiki/RBC_(football_club)" title="RBC (football club)">RBC</a> now playing as an amateur club.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
The stadium is renamed to Herstaco Stadion. It also houses the national football Museum: voetbalexperience.
</p>
