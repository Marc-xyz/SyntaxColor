<p><b>Jack William Wintle</b> (18 April 1908 - 13 November 1942) was a <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a> Officer and a posthumous recipient of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Navy_Cross_(United_States)" title="Navy Cross (United States)">Navy Cross</a>, which was awarded for his actions in mid November 1942, during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Guadalcanal" title="Battle of Guadalcanal">Battle of Guadalcanal</a>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Military_career">Military career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jack_William_Wintle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Military career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Wintle was appointed a <a href="/wiki/Midshipman" title="Midshipman">midshipman</a> at the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Naval_Academy" title="United States Naval Academy">Naval Academy</a> on 14 June 1928 and graduated on 2 June 1932. He reported for duty in <a href="/wiki/USS_California_(BB-44)" title="USS California (BB-44)">USS <i>California</i> <span class="nowrap">(BB-44)</span></a> on the 30th and completed a three-year tour of duty in the <a href="/wiki/Battleship" title="Battleship">battleship</a> before being transferred to <a href="/wiki/Submarine_tender" title="Submarine tender">submarine tender</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USS_Bushnell_(AS-2)" title="USS Bushnell (AS-2)">USS <i>Bushnell</i> <span class="nowrap">(AS-2)</span></a>. That assignment lasted 17 months.
</p><p>On 7 August 1936, Wintle reported to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Puget_Sound_Navy_Yard" title="Puget Sound Navy Yard">Puget Sound Navy Yard</a> to help supervise the fitting out of <a href="/wiki/USS_Perkins_(DD-377)" title="USS Perkins (DD-377)">USS <i>Perkins</i> <span class="nowrap">(DD-377)</span></a>; and he remained in the <a href="/wiki/Destroyer" title="Destroyer">destroyer</a> after she went into commission on 18 September 1936. In the summer of 1939, he received postgraduate instruction at the Naval Academy before reporting for duty at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Navy_Yard" title="Philadelphia Navy Yard">Philadelphia Navy Yard</a> to help prepare <a href="/wiki/USS_Du_Pont_(DD-152)" title="USS Du Pont (DD-152)">USS <i>Du Pont</i> <span class="nowrap">(DD-152)</span></a> for recommissioning and service on the <a href="/wiki/Neutrality_Patrol" title="Neutrality Patrol">Neutrality Patrol</a>. His tour of duty in that destroyer, one of the first in the Atlantic Squadrons to be fitted with <a href="/wiki/Sonar" title="Sonar">sonar</a>, ended in August 1940 when he was sent to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Orleans,_Louisiana" title="New Orleans, Louisiana">New Orleans, Louisiana</a>, where he served almost two years instructing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NROTC" title="NROTC">NROTC</a> midshipmen.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="World_War_II">World War II</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jack_William_Wintle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: World War II">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Late in April 1942, Wintle reported to the <a href="/wiki/Bureau_of_Navigation" title="Bureau of Navigation">Bureau of Navigation</a> in Washington where he learned that his next assignment was to be aide and flag lieutenant to the Commander, South Pacific Area and South Pacific Force. On 15 June 1942, Wintle received his promotion to <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant_commander" title="Lieutenant commander">lieutenant commander</a> and, four days later, reported for duty in his new assignment in the South Pacific.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Service_Aboard_the_USS_San_Francisco">Service Aboard the USS San Francisco</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jack_William_Wintle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Service Aboard the USS San Francisco">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Promoted to <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant_commander" title="Lieutenant commander">lieutenant commander</a>, Wintle served under Rear Admiral <a href="/wiki/Daniel_J._Callaghan" title="Daniel J. Callaghan">Daniel J. Callaghan</a>, Chief of Staff to the Commander South Pacific Area and South Pacific Force, through the early months of the struggle for <a href="/wiki/Guadalcanal" title="Guadalcanal">Guadalcanal</a> in late 1942. Late in October 1942, when Callaghan went to sea as the commander of a <a href="/wiki/Cruiser" title="Cruiser">cruiser</a>-<a href="/wiki/Destroyer" title="Destroyer">destroyer</a> force, Wintle joined him in his <a href="/wiki/Flagship" title="Flagship">flagship</a> <a href="/wiki/USS_San_Francisco_(CA-38)" title="USS San Francisco (CA-38)">USS <i>San Francisco</i> <span class="nowrap">(CA-38)</span></a> as a member of his staff.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awarding_of_the_Navy_Cross">Awarding of the Navy Cross</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jack_William_Wintle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Awarding of the Navy Cross">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On the night of 12 and 13 November, Callaghan's force met a Japanese raiding force built around battleships <a href="/wiki/Japanese_battleship_Hiei" title="Japanese battleship Hiei">Hiei</a> and <a href="/wiki/Japanese_battleship_Kirishima" title="Japanese battleship Kirishima">Kirishima</a>. During the confused melee off <a href="/wiki/Savo_Island" title="Savo Island">Savo Island</a>, San Francisco suffered a terrific pounding from enemy ships-and briefly lost power completely. At that point, several Japanese <a href="/wiki/Salvo" title="Salvo">salvos</a> scored on her superstructure, obliterating her flag and navigating bridges. All but one member of the admiral's staff were killed, and Wintle was among the casualties. For this sacrifice, he was awarded the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Navy_Cross_(United_States)" title="Navy Cross (United States)">Navy Cross</a>, posthumously.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jack_William_Wintle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The USS Wintle, Destroyer Escort (DE-25), commissioned 10 July 1943, was named in his honor.
</p>
