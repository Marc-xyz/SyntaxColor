<p><b>Eduard "Edi" Martini</b> (born 2 January 1975) is an <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albanian</a> <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> manager and former player, who is currently the head coach <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KF_Teuta_Durr%C3%ABs" title="KF Teuta Durrës">Teuta</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Playing_career">Playing career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edi_Martini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Playing career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Club">Club</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edi_Martini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Club">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During his playing career Martini represented mostly <a href="/wiki/KF_Vllaznia_Shkod%C3%ABr" title="KF Vllaznia Shkodër">Vllaznia Shkodër</a> but also played abroad with Slovenian <a href="/wiki/NK_Slavija_Vev%C4%8De" title="NK Slavija Vevče">Vevce</a>, Austrian <a href="/wiki/FC_K%C3%A4rnten" title="FC Kärnten">SAK Klagenfurt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eintracht_Frankfurt" title="Eintracht Frankfurt">Eintracht Frankfurt</a> in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. He fled <a href="/wiki/Albanian_Civil_War" title="Albanian Civil War">war-torn</a> Albania for Germany in 1997 after a match in Spain along with teammate <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Adrian_Dashi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Adrian Dashi (page does not exist)">Adrian Dashi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International">International</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edi_Martini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: International">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Martini made his international debut for <a href="/wiki/Albania_national_football_team" title="Albania national football team">Albania</a> on 14 May 1994 during the 5–1 away defeat to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Macedonia_national_football_team" title="Macedonia national football team">Macedonia</a>, appearing as a first-half substitute for <a href="/wiki/Gentian_Stojku" title="Gentian Stojku">Gentian Stojku</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> A year later, on 16 August 1995, he made his second appearance in a 2–1 upset to <a href="/wiki/Malta_national_football_team" title="Malta national football team">Malta</a>, playing full-90 minutes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Managerial_career">Managerial career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edi_Martini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Managerial career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Vllaznia_Shkod.C3.ABr"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Vllaznia_Shkodër">Vllaznia Shkodër</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edi_Martini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Vllaznia Shkodër">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 29 December 2009, Martini was named as the new manager of his boyhood club <a href="/wiki/KF_Vllaznia_Shkod%C3%ABr" title="KF Vllaznia Shkodër">Vllaznia Shkodër</a>, and started the work with the team on 4 January 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> He remained in the duty until the end of <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Albanian_Superliga" title="2009–10 Albanian Superliga">2009–10 campaign</a>, with Vllaznia Shkodër who missed the European competitions for only four points and was runner-up in <a href="/wiki/Albanian_Cup" title="Albanian Cup">Albanian Cup</a>, losing in the final to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Besa_Kavaj%C3%AB" title="Besa Kavajë">Besa Kavajë</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Teuta_Durr.C3.ABs"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Teuta_Durrës">Teuta Durrës</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edi_Martini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Teuta Durrës">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 9 June 2010, Martini become the new coach of fellow <a href="/wiki/Albanian_Superliga" title="Albanian Superliga">Albanian Superliga</a> side <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KF_Teuta_Durr%C3%ABs" title="KF Teuta Durrës">Teuta Durrës</a>, signing a contract until the end of the season, taking the duty over <a href="/wiki/Ylli_Shehu" title="Ylli Shehu">Ylli Shehu</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> During the season, Teuta Durrës struggled to find results and finished league in 7th place, tied with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KS_Kastrioti" title="KS Kastrioti">Kastrioti Krujë</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shkumbini_Peqin" title="Shkumbini Peqin">Shkumbini Peqin</a> with 42 points. In <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Albanian_Cup" title="2010–11 Albanian Cup">2010–11 Albanian Cup</a>, Teuta didn't go more than the second round, where they were eliminated by <a href="/wiki/KF_Sk%C3%ABnderbeu_Kor%C3%A7%C3%AB" title="KF Skënderbeu Korçë">Skënderbeu Korçë</a> 2–0 on aggregate. Following the end of the season, Martini left the team after the club's president <a href="/wiki/Fatmir_Hysenbelliu" title="Fatmir Hysenbelliu">Fatmir Hysenbelliu</a> decided not to extend his contract for the next season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Luft.C3.ABtari_Gjirokast.C3.ABr"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Luftëtari_Gjirokastër">Luftëtari Gjirokastër</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Edi_Martini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Luftëtari Gjirokastër">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 30 October 2012, Martini was appointed as head coach at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luft%C3%ABtari_Gjirokast%C3%ABr" title="Luftëtari Gjirokastër">Luftëtari Gjirokastër</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
