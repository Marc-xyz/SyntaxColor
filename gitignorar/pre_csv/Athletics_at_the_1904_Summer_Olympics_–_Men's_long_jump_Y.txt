<p>The <b>men's long jump</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Track_and_field" title="Track and field">track and field athletics</a> event held as part of the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_1904_Summer_Olympics" title="Athletics at the 1904 Summer Olympics">athletics at the 1904 Summer Olympics</a> programme. It was the third time the event was held. Nine athletes from three nations participated. The competition was held on Thursday, September 1, 1904.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SR_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-SR-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Myer_Prinstein" title="Myer Prinstein">Myer Prinstein</a>, who had taken second place in 1900 after leading in the first round of jumping but declining to compete in the second due to it being held on a Sunday, won the contest and set a new Olympic record for distance.
</p>
