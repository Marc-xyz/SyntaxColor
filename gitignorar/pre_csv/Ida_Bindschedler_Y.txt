<p><b>Ida Bindschedler</b> (born 6 July 1854  – 28 June 1919) was a Swiss children's writer.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ida_Bindschedler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bindschedler was born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Z%C3%BCrich,_Switzerland" title="Zürich, Switzerland">Zürich, Switzerland</a> to cotton merchant Friedrich Rudolf and mother Anna Tauber.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-x_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-x-1">[1]</a></sup> Her family spent their summers in Villa Bellerive, which she later used as inspiration for her books.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bindschedler_Geburtshaus.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2573" data-file-width="1754" decoding="async" height="323" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Bindschedler_Geburtshaus.jpg/220px-Bindschedler_Geburtshaus.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Bindschedler_Geburtshaus.jpg/330px-Bindschedler_Geburtshaus.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Bindschedler_Geburtshaus.jpg/440px-Bindschedler_Geburtshaus.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Bindschedler_Geburtshaus.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>House of Ida Bindschedler's birth</div></div></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ida_Bindschedler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bindschedler was trained as a teacher under the guidance of Joseph Viktor Widmann, who would later recommend her to teach at a private school in Zurich.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> She taught at that school for 24 years before leaving due to a heart condition. While at the school, she advanced from primary school teacher to secondary school teacher and also taught in Paris. She worked approximately 40 hours a week both in school and in additional lessons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>After retiring, she moved to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Augsburg,_Germany" title="Augsburg, Germany">Augsburg, Germany</a>, where she wrote her first book, "<i>The gymnastic children in summer.</i>"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> 
</p><p>Bindschedler's books were very successful in Switzerland, tying <a href="/wiki/Johanna_Spyri" title="Johanna Spyri">Johanna Spyri</a>'s book <i><a href="/wiki/Heidi" title="Heidi">Heidi</a></i> in popularity there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death_and_legacy">Death and legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ida_Bindschedler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Death and legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ida_Bindschedler-Strasse.jpeg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1530" data-file-width="3796" decoding="async" height="89" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/Ida_Bindschedler-Strasse.jpeg/220px-Ida_Bindschedler-Strasse.jpeg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/Ida_Bindschedler-Strasse.jpeg/330px-Ida_Bindschedler-Strasse.jpeg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/Ida_Bindschedler-Strasse.jpeg/440px-Ida_Bindschedler-Strasse.jpeg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ida_Bindschedler-Strasse.jpeg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Street named in her honour</div></div></div>
<p>Bindschedler died on 28 June 1919.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> One of the main location of her books took place in Seeweid in the <a href="/wiki/Riesbach" title="Riesbach">Riesbach</a> district of Zurich. The distract named a street in her honour.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-x_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-x-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
