<p><b>Seth Chaim Firkins</b> (August 5, 1981 – September 23, 2017) was an American <a href="/wiki/Audio_engineer" title="Audio engineer">audio engineer</a>. Firkins was known for engineering songs for major artists such as <a href="/wiki/Rihanna" title="Rihanna">Rihanna</a>, <a href="/wiki/Future_(rapper)" title="Future (rapper)">Future</a>, <a href="/wiki/Young_Thug" title="Young Thug">Young Thug</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trina" title="Trina">Trina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ciara" title="Ciara">Ciara</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Gucci_Mane" title="Gucci Mane">Gucci Mane</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seth_Firkins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Firkins was exposed at an early age to a diverse range of music, from 1970s rock like <a href="/wiki/America_(band)" title="America (band)">America</a> and <a href="/wiki/Steely_Dan" title="Steely Dan">Steely Dan</a>, to classical composers such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chopin" title="Chopin">Chopin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ludwig_van_Beethoven" title="Ludwig van Beethoven">Beethoven</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-srm_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-srm-1">[1]</a></sup> He started his career in the <a href="/wiki/Rock_and_roll" title="Rock and roll">Rock and roll</a> genre with the Louisville-based band Heaven Hill, originally known as Element H and later Breckinridge.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-element_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-element-2">[2]</a></sup> Firkins created the band's <a href="/wiki/Street_team" title="Street team">street team</a> which grew into a group of nearly 400 workers and volunteers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-element_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-element-2">[2]</a></sup> In 2002, Firkins worked on his first rap album with artist Hurra Season, a Louisville-based artist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-srm_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-srm-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2006, he relocated to Atlanta, which became his permanent place of residence, and headquarters for 5X5 Media Group, a consulting music firm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-srm_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-srm-1">[1]</a></sup> 5X5 Media Group consults with artists to offer post-production services.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Firkins is credited to over 2.5 million records sold worldwide and was part of the Grammy nominated team which produced the Jay-Z album <a href="/wiki/American_Gangster_(album)" title="American Gangster (album)">American Gangster</a> which is certified platinum in the United States by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RIAA" title="RIAA">RIAA</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Firkins was credited with engineering and mixing the <a href="/wiki/Rihanna" title="Rihanna">Rihanna</a> track "<a href="/wiki/Loveeeeeee_Song" title="Loveeeeeee Song">Loveeeeeee Song</a>", featuring Atlanta rap artist Future, on the album <i><a href="/wiki/Unapologetic" title="Unapologetic">Unapologetic</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fye_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-fye-6">[6]</a></sup>  The album was released on November 19, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fye_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-fye-6">[6]</a></sup> 
</p><p>Firkins continued his work with Future, and is credited for engineering and mixing for Future's second <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album">studio album</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Pluto_(Future_album)" title="Pluto (Future album)">Pluto 3D</a></i>, released on November 27, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> He is also credited for the 2 albums Future released back to back in 2017: Future and HNDRXX. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seth_Firkins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
