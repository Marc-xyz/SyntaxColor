<p><b>Lionel Abrahams</b> (11 April 1928 – 31 May 2004)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-obit_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-obit-1">[1]</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South African</a> novelist, poet, editor, critic, essayist and publisher. He was born in <a href="/wiki/Johannesburg" title="Johannesburg">Johannesburg</a>, where he lived his entire life.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He was born with <a href="/wiki/Cerebral_palsy" title="Cerebral palsy">cerebral palsy</a> and had to use a wheelchair until 11 years of age.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-obit_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-obit-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Best known for his poetry, he was mentored by <a href="/wiki/Herman_Charles_Bosman" title="Herman Charles Bosman">Herman Charles Bosman</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-obit_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-obit-1">[1]</a></sup> and he and later edited seven volumes of Bosman's posthumously published works.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Abrahams went on to become one of the most influential figures in South African literature in his own right,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> publishing numerous poems, essays, and two novels.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-obit_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-obit-1">[1]</a></sup> Through Renoster Books, which he started in 1956, he published works by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oswald_Mtshali" title="Oswald Mtshali">Oswald Mtshali</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mongane_Wally_Serote" title="Mongane Wally Serote">Mongane Wally Serote</a> heralding the emergence of black poetry during the <a href="/wiki/Apartheid" title="Apartheid">apartheid</a> era.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-obit_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-obit-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gikandi_2003_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gikandi_2003-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1986, he married Jane Fox.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-obit_1-6"><a href="#cite_note-obit-1">[1]</a></sup> That year, he was awarded honorary doctorates of literature by the <a href="/wiki/University_of_the_Witwatersrand" title="University of the Witwatersrand">University of the Witwatersrand</a> and the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Natal" title="University of Natal">University of Natal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
