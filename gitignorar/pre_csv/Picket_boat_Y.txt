	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Picket boat</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Not to be confused with <a href="/wiki/Radar_picket" title="Radar picket">Radar picket</a> or <a href="/wiki/Packet_boat" title="Packet boat">Packet boat</a>.</div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:36_foot_coast_guard_picket_boat_circa_1920s.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="377" data-file-width="695" decoding="async" height="119" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/36_foot_coast_guard_picket_boat_circa_1920s.png/220px-36_foot_coast_guard_picket_boat_circa_1920s.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/36_foot_coast_guard_picket_boat_circa_1920s.png/330px-36_foot_coast_guard_picket_boat_circa_1920s.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/36_foot_coast_guard_picket_boat_circa_1920s.png/440px-36_foot_coast_guard_picket_boat_circa_1920s.png 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:36_foot_coast_guard_picket_boat_circa_1920s.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>US Coast Guard 36-foot (11 m) open cockpit picket boat of the 1920s</div></div></div>
<p>A <b>picket boat</b> is a type of small naval craft. These are used for harbor patrol and other close inshore work, and have often been carried by larger warships as a <a href="/wiki/Ship%27s_boat" title="Ship's boat">ship's boat</a>. They range in size between 30 and 55 feet.
</p><p>Patrol boats, or any craft engaged in sentinel duty, are sometimes referred to as picket boats, using "picket" in the generic sense, even if much larger than actual picket boats.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Picket_boat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Union's 45-foot (14 m) long steam-powered Picket Boat Number One sank the Confederate ironclad <i><a href="/wiki/CSS_Albemarle" title="CSS Albemarle">Albemarle</a></i> in 1864. (Although named "Picket Boat", this craft has also been called a <a href="/wiki/Launch_(boat)" title="Launch (boat)">steam launch</a>). The boat was armed with a 12-pounder <a href="/wiki/Dahlgren_gun" title="Dahlgren gun">Dahlgren gun</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Spar_torpedo" title="Spar torpedo">spar torpedo</a>, of which the latter was employed in sinking <i>Albemarle</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Priolo_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Priolo-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CYAH_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-CYAH-2">[2]</a></sup> The Union's <a href="/wiki/Potomac_Flotilla" title="Potomac Flotilla">Potomac Flotilla</a> also employed some picket boats.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>A number of American warships of the 19th century carried picket boats, such as the <a href="/wiki/USS_Vulcan_(1884)" title="USS Vulcan (1884)">USS <i>Vulcan</i></a> (her picket boat was heavily engaged by Spanish small-caliber shore fire during one incident in the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish–American War">Spanish–American War</a>), and others.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>In the early 1920s, during <a href="/wiki/Prohibition_in_the_United_States" title="Prohibition in the United States">Prohibition</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">United States Coast Guard</a> built a fleet of picket boats to intercept <a href="/wiki/Rum-running" title="Rum-running">rum runners</a>, supplementing the larger and more seaworthy <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard_Cutter" title="United States Coast Guard Cutter">cutters</a> and <a href="/wiki/Patrol_boat" title="Patrol boat">patrol boats</a>. These boats were about 36 feet (11 m) long, had no main fixed armament, and cost about <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>8,800 (about $128,651<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-inflation-US_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-inflation-US-3">[3]</a></sup> in 2018 dollars) to build.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USCG_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-USCG-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>A later picket boat, built between 1932 and 1943, was 38 feet (12 m) long and also had no large main armament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dring_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dring-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Britain">Britain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Picket_boat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Britain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:HMS_Triumph_picket_boat.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1133" data-file-width="1642" decoding="async" height="152" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/HMS_Triumph_picket_boat.jpg/220px-HMS_Triumph_picket_boat.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/HMS_Triumph_picket_boat.jpg/330px-HMS_Triumph_picket_boat.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/HMS_Triumph_picket_boat.jpg/440px-HMS_Triumph_picket_boat.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:HMS_Triumph_picket_boat.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A British 56-foot (17 m) picket boat, returning to its mothership (HMS <i>Triumph</i>) after participating in action on April 18, 1915</div></div></div>
<p>A long-serving 19th-century British picket boat, carried on capital ships, was a 50-foot (15 m) model introduced in 1867 which saw wide service in World War I and even some limited service in World War II. The typical main armament during most of this boat's service life was a <a href="/wiki/QF_3-pounder_Hotchkiss" title="QF 3-pounder Hotchkiss">Hotchkiss 3–pounder</a>, adopted by the Royal Navy in 1886.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kemp_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kemp-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>British pre-dreadnoughts, including <a href="/wiki/HMS_Majestic_(1895)" title="HMS Majestic (1895)">HMS <i>Majestic</i></a> and <a href="/wiki/HMS_Triumph_(1903)" title="HMS Triumph (1903)">HMS <i>Triumph</i></a>, carried picket boats.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stapleton_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stapleton-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/P1000_Class_Picket_Boat" title="P1000 Class Picket Boat">P1000 Class Picket Boat</a> is a current British <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> boat, 42 feet (13 m) in length, formerly carried on destroyers but now used only for training.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Saumarez_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Saumarez-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Germany">Germany</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Picket_boat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Germany">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The <i><a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6nigsberg-class_cruiser_(1905)" title="Königsberg-class cruiser (1905)">Königsberg</a></i>–class cruisers of 1905 and <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6nigsberg-class_cruiser_(1927)" title="Königsberg-class cruiser (1927)">1915</a> carried a picket boat; the <i><a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6nigsberg-class_cruiser_(1927)" title="Königsberg-class cruiser (1927)">Königsbergs</a></i> of 1927 carried two. The <i><a href="/wiki/Dresden-class_cruiser" title="Dresden-class cruiser">Dresden</a></i> class of a similar era carried one, as did the <i><a href="/wiki/Wiesbaden-class_cruiser" title="Wiesbaden-class cruiser">Wiesbaden</a></i>–class.
</p><p>Larger ships also carried picket boats: The <i><a href="/wiki/Nassau-class_battleship" title="Nassau-class battleship">Nassau</a></i>–, <i><a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6nig-class_battleship" title="König-class battleship">König</a></i>–, and <i><a href="/wiki/Bayern-class_battleship" title="Bayern-class battleship">Bayern</a></i>–class battleships, the <i><a href="/wiki/SMS_Seydlitz" title="SMS Seydlitz">SMS Seydlitz</a></i>, and the <i><a href="/wiki/Derfflinger-class_battlecruiser" title="Derfflinger-class battlecruiser">Derfflinger</a></i>–class battlecruisers all carried one each.
</p>
