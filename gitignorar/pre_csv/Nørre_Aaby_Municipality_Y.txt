	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Nørre Aaby Municipality</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>

		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>Until January 1, 2007 <b>Nørre Aaby municipality</b> was a municipality (<a href="/wiki/Danish_language" title="Danish language">Danish</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Denmark" title="Municipalities of Denmark">kommune</a></i>) in the former <a href="/wiki/Funen_County" title="Funen County">Funen County</a> on the north and west coasts of the island of <a href="/wiki/Funen" title="Funen">Funen</a> in southern <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>.  The municipality covered an area of 65 km², and had a total population of 5,626 (2006).  Its last mayor was Hanne Christensen, a member of the <a href="/wiki/Venstre_(Denmark)" title="Venstre (Denmark)">Venstre</a> (Liberal Party) <a href="/wiki/Politics_of_Denmark" title="Politics of Denmark">political party</a>. The main town and the site of its municipal council was the town of <a href="/wiki/N%C3%B8rre_Aaby" title="Nørre Aaby">Nørre Aaby</a>. To the north are the waters of Båring Cove (<i>Båring Vig</i>) and beyond that the <a href="/wiki/Kattegat" title="Kattegat">Kattegat</a>.  To the west are the waters of Fem's Cove (<i>Fems Vig</i>) and beyond that the <a href="/wiki/Little_Belt" title="Little Belt">Little Belt</a>.
</p><p>Part of the former municipality formed a <a href="/wiki/Spit_(landform)" title="Spit (landform)">spit</a> of land that juts out into the surrounding waters of the Little Belt.  The water between the spit and the main part of the municipality is called Gamborg Fjord.  Close to the head of the spit is the island of <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A6n%C3%B8" title="Fænø">Fænø</a>, and beyond that the Jutland mainland, all in proximity to each other.  The waters to the north of this area are called <i>Snævringen</i> ("The Narrowing"); and to the south as there becomes more room they are called <i>Bredningen</i> ("The Broadening").
</p><p>Nørre Aaby  municipality ceased to exist as the result of <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Denmark#Municipal_Reform_2007" title="Municipalities of Denmark"><i>Kommunalreformen</i> ("The Municipality Reform" of 2007)</a>.  It was merged with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ejby_municipality" title="Ejby municipality">Ejby</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Middelfart_municipality" title="Middelfart municipality">Middelfart</a> municipalities to form an enlarged Middelfart municipality.  This created a municipality with an area of 297 km² and a total population of 36,417 (2006).  The municipality belong to <a href="/wiki/Region_of_Southern_Denmark" title="Region of Southern Denmark">Region of Southern Denmark</a>.
</p>
