	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Wiiija</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>

<b>Wiiija</b> was a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Independent_record_label" title="Independent record label">independent record label</a> founded in 1988 by staff from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rough_Trade_Shop" title="Rough Trade Shop">Rough Trade Shop</a> in <a href="/wiki/Notting_Hill" title="Notting Hill">Notting Hill</a>, London. The name Wiiija is a corruption of W11 1JA, the postcode of the Rough Trade Shop in Talbot Street. 
</p><p>The label was notable for introducing the band <a href="/wiki/Therapy%3F" title="Therapy?">Therapy?</a> and releasing the first records by <a href="/wiki/Silverfish_(band)" title="Silverfish (band)">Silverfish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Huggy_Bear_(band)" title="Huggy Bear (band)">Huggy Bear</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cornershop_(band)" title="Cornershop (band)">Cornershop</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Notable later releases included <a href="/wiki/Free_Kitten" title="Free Kitten">Free Kitten</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bis_(band)" title="Bis (band)">Bis</a> while the commercial success of Cornershop from 1997 rewarded the label's long-term support. 
</p><p>Wiiija became loosely associated with the early UK <a href="/wiki/Riot_grrrl" title="Riot grrrl">riot grrrl</a> scene as a result of releases by Huggy Bear and <a href="/wiki/Blood_Sausage_(band)" title="Blood Sausage (band)">Blood Sausage</a>, as well as the <i>Some Hearts Paid To Lie</i> EP which featured <a href="/wiki/Comet_Gain" title="Comet Gain">Comet Gain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Skinned_Teen" title="Skinned Teen">Skinned Teen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Linus_(band)" title="Linus (band)">Linus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Red_Monkey_(band)" title="Red Monkey (band)">Pussycat Trash</a>. Labelmates Cornershop can be seen along with Huggy Bear and Blood Sausage in the 1994 tour documentary <i>Getting Close To Nothing</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1996 it became a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Beggars_Banquet_Records" title="Beggars Banquet Records">Beggars Banquet Records</a> and remained active until the early 2000s.
</p>
