<p><b>Chan Chak-to</b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh">陳澤滔</span>; born 1984) is a Hong Kong politician and activist. He is known to be his outright pro-<a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_independence" title="Hong Kong independence">Hong Kong independence</a> stance and his active involvement in the electoral politics as a <a href="/wiki/Localism_in_Hong_Kong" title="Localism in Hong Kong">localist</a>. He is currently the convenor of the district-based <a href="/wiki/Kowloon_East_Community" title="Kowloon East Community">Kowloon East Community</a>, which commands one seat in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/District_Councils_of_Hong_Kong" title="District Councils of Hong Kong">District Councils</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_activism">Early life and activism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chan_Chak-to&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and activism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Chan was born in Hong Kong in 1984 to a single parent. His mother single-handedly raised him and his two sisters as his father died before he was born. He was educated at the <a href="/wiki/Sing_Yin_Secondary_School" title="Sing Yin Secondary School">Sing Yin Secondary School</a> and studied Computer Engineering at the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_University_of_Hong_Kong" title="Chinese University of Hong Kong">Chinese University of Hong Kong</a>. He went to Australia through a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Working_holiday" title="Working holiday">working holiday</a> scheme for a year before he worked as an engineer at the <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_Disneyland" title="Hong Kong Disneyland">Hong Kong Disneyland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stand_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-stand-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>He was first arrested for rehearing the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Occupy_Central_with_Peace_and_Love" title="Occupy Central with Peace and Love">Occupy Central plan</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_July_1_marches#2014" title="Hong Kong July 1 marches">2014 July 1 protest</a> among the total number of 511 protesters. In the massive <a href="/wiki/2014_Hong_Kong_protests" title="2014 Hong Kong protests">2014 Hong Kong protests</a>, he and other netizens from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_Golden_Forum" title="Hong Kong Golden Forum">Hong Kong Golden Forum</a> formed the district-based <a href="/wiki/Kowloon_East_Community" title="Kowloon East Community">Kowloon East Community</a> and planned to run in the <a href="/wiki/2015_Hong_Kong_local_elections" title="2015 Hong Kong local elections">2015 District Council elections</a>, the first election after the historic protest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stand_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-stand-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Electoral_politics">Electoral politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chan_Chak-to&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Electoral politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Community filled in three candidates in the <a href="/wiki/Kwun_Tong_District_Council" title="Kwun Tong District Council">Kwun Tong District Council</a>, in which Chan ran in the <a href="/wiki/Hong_Lok_(constituency)" title="Hong Lok (constituency)">Hong Lok</a> against the <a href="/wiki/Pro-Beijing_camp_(Hong_Kong)" title="Pro-Beijing camp (Hong Kong)">pro-Beijing</a> incumbent 	Ma Yat-chiu. He received 1,525 votes, narrowly defeated by 99 votes, while another member Wong Chin-ken ousted the incumbent Fung Kam-yuen in <a href="/wiki/Lok_Wah_North_(constituency)" title="Lok Wah North (constituency)">Lok Wah North</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/2016_Hong_Kong_legislative_election" title="2016 Hong Kong legislative election">2016 Legislative Council election</a>, Kowloon East Community formed the <a href="/wiki/Localism_in_Hong_Kong" title="Localism in Hong Kong">localist</a> electoral alliance "<a href="/wiki/ALLinHK" title="ALLinHK">ALLinHK</a>" with <a href="/wiki/Youngspiration" title="Youngspiration">Youngspiration</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tin_Shui_Wai_New_Force" title="Tin Shui Wai New Force">Tin Shui Wai New Force</a>, Cheung Sha Wan Community Establishment Power, [[[Tsz Wan Shan Constructive Power]] and Tuen Mun Community with the slogan "Hong Kong nation's self-determination" where Chan led a one-man ticket in <a href="/wiki/Kowloon_East_(constituency)" title="Kowloon East (constituency)">Kowloon East</a> constituency. Despite he and other candidates were required to sign the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_LegCo_candidates%27_disqualification_controversy" title="Hong Kong LegCo candidates' disqualification controversy">extra confirmation form</a> to declare their understanding of Hong Kong being an inalienable part of China as stipulated in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Basic_Law_of_Hong_Kong" title="Basic Law of Hong Kong">Basic Law of Hong Kong</a>. Although refusing to sign the form, Chan's candidacy was confirmed by the <a href="/wiki/Electoral_Affairs_Commission" title="Electoral Affairs Commission">Electoral Affairs Commission</a> while some others were disqualified from running.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>After serval warnings against the pro-independence candidates that they might still be disqualified, Chan spoke for the <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong_independence" title="Hong Kong independence">Hong Kong independence</a> in several electoral forums: "I support Hong Kong independence ... it’s the only way out." He also demanded the Basic Law to be scrapped.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He eventually received 12,854 votes and was not elected.
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/2016_Hong_Kong_Election_Committee_Subsector_elections" title="2016 Hong Kong Election Committee Subsector elections">2016 Election Committee Subsector elections</a>, Chan joined the <a href="/wiki/Pro-democracy_camp_(Hong_Kong)" title="Pro-democracy camp (Hong Kong)">pro-democracy</a> IT Vision to run in the Information Technology subsector against incumbent Chief Executive <a href="/wiki/Leung_Chun-ying" title="Leung Chun-ying">Leung Chun-ying</a> from running for second term. He was elected with 4,238 votes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/2017_Hong_Kong_Chief_Executive_election" title="2017 Hong Kong Chief Executive election">2017 Chief Executive election</a>, Chan nominated retired judge <a href="/wiki/Woo_Kwok-hing" title="Woo Kwok-hing">Woo Kwok-hing</a>.
</p>
