<p>
The subject of <b><a href="/wiki/Mental_disorder" title="Mental disorder">mental health</a> in <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">association football</a></b> has been described as a "stigma" in the sport,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>  although other professional sports are also affected.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Professional footballers suffer more from symptoms of <a href="/wiki/Depression_(mood)" title="Depression (mood)">depression</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anxiety_disorder" title="Anxiety disorder">anxiety</a> than the general public.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Examples">Examples</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mental_health_in_association_football&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Examples">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After suffering from depression, former England international <a href="/wiki/George_Harrison_(footballer)" title="George Harrison (footballer)">George Harrison</a> committed suicide at the age of 46.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Agostino_Di_Bartolomei" title="Agostino Di Bartolomei">Agostino Di Bartolomei</a> suffered from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clinical_depression" title="Clinical depression">clinical depression</a> after retiring from professional football, and eventually committed suicide by shooting himself in his villa in <a href="/wiki/Castellabate" title="Castellabate">San Marco di Castellabate</a>, on 30 May 1994, ten years to the day after his former club <a href="/wiki/A.S._Roma" title="A.S. Roma">Roma</a> had lost the <a href="/wiki/1984_European_Cup_Final" title="1984 European Cup Final">European Cup final</a> to <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> on penalties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>During the first part of the <a href="/wiki/2000%E2%80%9301_Everton_F.C._season" title="2000–01 Everton F.C. season">2000–01 season</a> with <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Gascoigne" title="Paul Gascoigne">Paul Gascoigne</a> struggled with depression after losing his place in the first team following a series of injuries and a lack of fitness.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Later in his career, during his time with Chinese club <a href="/wiki/Gansu_Tianma_F.C." title="Gansu Tianma F.C.">Gansu Tianma</a>, he went to America in April 2003 for treatment against <a href="/wiki/Alcoholism" title="Alcoholism">alcoholism</a> and depression.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>Former professional players <a href="/wiki/Robert_Enke" title="Robert Enke">Robert Enke</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gary_Speed" title="Gary Speed">Gary Speed</a> have committed suicide; both suffered from depression. Enke threw himself in front of a train.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Andersson_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Andersson-13">[13]</a></sup> Five years after Enke's death his wife stated that she believed depression in sport was no longer a taboo subject.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Speed hanged himself.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> In September 2018 his wife said that she a discovered a letter written by Speed as a teenager which hinted at his long-term depression.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> In 2019 a 10-year memorial event for Enke was held a theatre hall in Hannover.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Clarke_Carlisle" title="Clarke Carlisle">Clarke Carlisle</a> stated that he contemplated suicide after becoming injured early in his career,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> and in December 2014 attempted suicide due to the severe depression he was suffering from.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Deisler" title="Sebastian Deisler">Sebastian Deisler</a> was hospitalized after being unable to cope with the pressures of professional football, eventually retiring from the sport at the age of 27.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Andersson_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-Andersson-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Jean-Marc_Bosman" title="Jean-Marc Bosman">Jean-Marc Bosman</a> struggled to find work after his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Judicial" title="Judicial">judicial</a> challenge of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_transfer" title="Football transfer">football transfer</a> rules led to the <a href="/wiki/Bosman_ruling" title="Bosman ruling">Bosman ruling</a> in 1995. He ended up living off of welfare, and as a result of his financial difficulties and his claimed ostracism by the world of football, he fell into depression and also struggled with alcoholism.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bosman,_dalla_sentenza_all'alcol_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bosman,_dalla_sentenza_all'alcol-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20_years_on_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-20_years_on-25">[25]</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2008, <a href="/wiki/Gianluigi_Buffon" title="Gianluigi Buffon">Gianluigi Buffon</a> revealed in his autobiography <i>Numero 1</i> that he had suffered from depression between December 2003 and June 2004, and that he even saw a <a href="/wiki/Psychologist" title="Psychologist">psychologist</a> during this period in order to overcome it;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Buffon:_Ricco_e_famoso_la_depressione_mi_prese_lo_stesso_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-Buffon:_Ricco_e_famoso_la_depressione_mi_prese_lo_stesso-26">[26]</a></sup> he later elaborated further on his struggles with depression in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gianluigi_Buffon:_from_depression_to_Italy's_record_books_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gianluigi_Buffon:_from_depression_to_Italy's_record_books-27">[27]</a></sup> In January 2019, he also revealed that because of his depression, he had suffered from panic attacks during his early career with <a href="/wiki/Juventus_F.C." title="Juventus F.C.">Juventus</a>, even missing a game as a result during the 2003–04 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup>
</p><p>Ex-player <a href="/wiki/Mickey_Bennett" title="Mickey Bennett">Mickey Bennett</a> set up an organization called Unique Sports Counselling to help footballers deal with mental health issues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2016 <a href="/wiki/Steve_Harper" title="Steve Harper">Steve Harper</a> spoke out about his mental health problems while in between clubs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Professional_Footballer%27s_Association" title="Professional Footballer's Association">PFA</a> had to apologise after initially criticising his comments.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2016, professional footballer <a href="/wiki/Chris_Mitchell_(Scottish_footballer)" title="Chris Mitchell (Scottish footballer)">Chris Mitchell</a> committed suicide. In October 2018 his family spoke out about his struggles with mental health.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2017, <a href="/wiki/Aaron_Lennon" title="Aaron Lennon">Aaron Lennon</a> was detained under the Mental Health Act, and he received treatment for a "stress-related illness".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> In March 2019 he spoke about the incident, and said that other players had asked him for advice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup>
</p><p>In June 2017, <a href="/wiki/Steven_Caulker" title="Steven Caulker">Steven Caulker</a> spoke about his struggles with depression, and his addiction to alcohol and gambling.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2018 <a href="/wiki/David_Cox_(footballer)" title="David Cox (footballer)">David Cox</a> stated that he had been mocked by both players and fans for discussing his mental health problems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2018, the UK government announced plans to deal with mental health in professional sports.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> Later that month, <a href="/wiki/Harry_Smith_(footballer,_born_1995)" title="Harry Smith (footballer, born 1995)">Harry Smith</a> released a statement stating that he was seeking help for mental health and gambling addiction problems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> That same month, Danish player <a href="/wiki/Jannik_Skov_Hansen" title="Jannik Skov Hansen">Jannik Skov Hansen</a> talked about the depression and suicidal thoughts he had experienced earlier in his career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup>
</p><p>In April 2018 <a href="/wiki/The_Secret_Footballer" title="The Secret Footballer">The Secret Footballer</a>, an anonymous former Premier League player, talked about his depression.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2018, <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9s_Iniesta" title="Andrés Iniesta">Andrés Iniesta</a> revealed that he suffered from depression before the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a> due to his injuries and the death of his friend <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Jarque" title="Daniel Jarque">Daniel Jarque</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup>
</p><p>In June 2018, England international <a href="/wiki/Danny_Rose_(footballer,_born_1990)" title="Danny Rose (footballer, born 1990)">Danny Rose</a> stated that he had been diagnosed with depression.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup>
</p><p>In September 2018, professional footballer <a href="/wiki/Marvin_Sordell" title="Marvin Sordell">Marvin Sordell</a> suggested that clubs should have full-time counsellors to assist players with depression and other mental health issues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> When Sordell retired from football in July 2019 he cited his mental health.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> Later that month <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Knockaert" title="Anthony Knockaert">Anthony Knockaert</a> also revealed his struggles with mental health issues,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> as did <a href="/wiki/David_Cotterill" title="David Cotterill">David Cotterill</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2018, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Carrick" title="Michael Carrick">Michael Carrick</a> announced that he had suffered from depression for 2 years following the defeat in the <a href="/wiki/2009_UEFA_Champions_League_Final" title="2009 UEFA Champions League Final">2009 UEFA Champions League Final</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> Later that month <a href="/wiki/Christian_Nad%C3%A9" title="Christian Nadé">Christian Nadé</a> revealed that he had tried to commit suicide in 2014 following struggles with depression.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup>
</p><p>In June 2019, former player <a href="/wiki/Emmanuel_Ebou%C3%A9" title="Emmanuel Eboué">Emmanuel Eboué</a> spoke about his ongoing struggles with mental health.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> In August 2019 <a href="/wiki/Martin_Ling" title="Martin Ling">Martin Ling</a> discussed his mental health problems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Billy_Kee" title="Billy Kee">Billy Kee</a> has spoken publicly about his struggles with anxiety and depression, which led him to considering giving up football.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> In September 2019 it was revealed that Kee was seeking treatment for depression, anxiety, and bulimia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> During that same month, the <a href="/wiki/English_Football_League" title="English Football League">English Football League</a> announced a scheme in conjunction with mental health charity <a href="/wiki/Mind_(charity)" title="Mind (charity)">Mind</a> called 'Get Set to Go'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> This included a series of street art pieces to raise awareness about mental health.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup> Later that month, Motherwell manager <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Robinson_(footballer)" title="Stephen Robinson (footballer)">Stephen Robinson</a> said clubs had a duty of care in relation to players' mental well-being if they were injured.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2019, <a href="/wiki/Francesco_Acerbi" title="Francesco Acerbi">Francesco Acerbi</a> revealed that during his time in Milan he had suffered from following the death of his father, and fell into alcoholism as a result of the loss and his personal struggles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2019 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prince_William" title="Prince William">Prince William</a> met with <a href="/wiki/West_Bromwich_Albion_F.C." title="West Bromwich Albion F.C.">West Bromwich Albion</a> players to discuss the importance of mental health.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup>
</p><p>In December 2019 ex-professional <a href="/wiki/Rhodri_Jones" title="Rhodri Jones">Rhodri Jones</a> spoke out about the pressures that young players face.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup>
</p>
