<p><b>Mairangi Bay</b> is a coastal suburb of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Shore,_Auckland" title="North Shore, Auckland">North Shore, Auckland</a>, located in the northern North Island of <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>, on the south-east-facing peninsula forming the northern side of the <a href="/wiki/Waitemat%C4%81_Harbour" title="Waitematā Harbour">Waitematā Harbour</a>. Mairangi Bay came under the local governance of the <a href="/wiki/North_Shore,_New_Zealand" title="North Shore, New Zealand">North Shore City Council</a> until subsumed into the <a href="/wiki/Auckland_Council" title="Auckland Council">Auckland Council</a> in 2010. The population was 5,346 in the <a href="/wiki/2013_New_Zealand_census" title="2013 New Zealand census">2013 census</a>, an increase of 69 from 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Bay's beach itself has, along with adjacent Murrays and <a href="/wiki/Campbells_Bay" title="Campbells Bay">Campbells Bays</a>, undergone civil works projects since 2004 to improve stormwater management.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mairangi_Bay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mairangi Bay School and St John's School are coeducational contributing primary (years 1-6) schools with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Socio-Economic_Decile" title="Socio-Economic Decile">decile ratings</a> of 10 and rolls of 403<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and 315,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> respectively. Mairangi Bay School was founded in 1967.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> St John's is a state integrated Catholic School, which was founded in 1961.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Rangitoto_College" title="Rangitoto College">Rangitoto College</a> is a large secondary school in Windsor Park, to the west of Mairangi Bay.
</p>
