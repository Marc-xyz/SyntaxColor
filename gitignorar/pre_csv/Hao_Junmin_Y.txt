<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hao_Junmin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hao Junmin started his football career with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tianjin_Teda_F.C." title="Tianjin Teda F.C.">Tianjin Teda</a> when he made his debut for the club on 15 September 2004 in a 2-0 win against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Liaoning_Zhongyu" title="Liaoning Zhongyu">Liaoning Zhongyu</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[1]</a></sup> This was then followed by his first goal for the club on 28 November 2004 in a 5-1 win against Liaoning Zhongyu.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[2]</a></sup> He would establish himself as a regular for the club and aided them to a fourth-place finish in the 2005 season. This saw not only his importance towards the team but the following seasons would see him personally rise towards predominance when he would win the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Football_Association" title="Chinese Football Association">Chinese Football Association</a> Young Player of the Year award in both 2006 and 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[3]</a></sup> His talent would then start to shine when he aided Tianjin to finish high enough for them to qualify for the club's first ever <a href="/wiki/AFC_Champions_League" title="AFC Champions League">AFC Champions League</a> campaign during the 2008 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>On 22 January 2010, Hao left <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tianjin_Teda_F.C." title="Tianjin Teda F.C.">Tianjin Teda</a> to sign with <a href="/wiki/Bundesliga" title="Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a> side <a href="/wiki/FC_Schalke_04" title="FC Schalke 04">Schalke 04</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[6]</a></sup> He made his debut for the club on 6 March 2010 in a 4-1 win against <a href="/wiki/Eintracht_Frankfurt" title="Eintracht Frankfurt">Eintracht Frankfurt</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[7]</a></sup> becoming the first Chinese footballer to ever play for Schalke 04.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[8]</a></sup>
He was originally given the shirt number 7, but was asked to surrender it when <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ra%C3%BAl_Gonz%C3%A1lez" title="Raúl González">Raúl</a> was signed on 28 July 2010. Hao, being a longtime fan of the legendary Spanish striker, happily obliged and received the number 8 shirt instead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>On 8 July 2011, Hao transferred to <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Super_League" title="Chinese Super League">Chinese Super League</a> side <a href="/wiki/Shandong_Luneng_Taishan_F.C." title="Shandong Luneng Taishan F.C.">Shandong Luneng</a> even though he still had one year remaining on his contract with Schalke 04.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[10]</a></sup> Hao suffered an injury during preseason training and was out for the majority of the 2013 season. He made his first appearance of the season back from injury on 10 August 2013 in a 3-2 win against <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shanghai_Shenhua_F.C." title="Shanghai Shenhua F.C.">Shanghai Shenhua</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hao_Junmin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hao worked his way up by first playing for the <a href="/wiki/China_national_under-17_football_team" title="China national under-17 football team">Chinese under-17 national team</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2003_FIFA_U-17_World_Championship" title="2003 FIFA U-17 World Championship">2003 FIFA U-17 World Championship</a> and then progressed to the <a href="/wiki/China_national_under-20_football_team" title="China national under-20 football team">Chinese under-20 national team</a> that took part in <a href="/wiki/2005_FIFA_World_Youth_Championship" title="2005 FIFA World Youth Championship">2005 FIFA World Youth Championship</a>. His performances were good enough for him to join the national team to play in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2005_East_Asian_Cup" title="2005 East Asian Cup">2005 East Asian Cup</a> where he made his debut on 3 August 2005 in a 2-2 draw against <a href="/wiki/Japan_national_football_team" title="Japan national football team">Japan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[11]</a></sup> Under then manager <a href="/wiki/Zhu_Guanghu" title="Zhu Guanghu">Zhu Guanghu</a>, his international career would flourish; however, he was not called up for the <a href="/wiki/2007_AFC_Asian_Cup" title="2007 AFC Asian Cup">2007 AFC Asian Cup</a> due to illness.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[12]</a></sup> In 2008, Hao was eligible to play for the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_tournament" title="Football at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament">2008 Summer Olympics</a> squad where he started two of the three group games in the tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[13]</a></sup>
</p>
