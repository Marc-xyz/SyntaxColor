<p>The <b>2008 Cork Senior Hurling Championship</b> was the 120th staging of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cork_Senior_Hurling_Championship" title="Cork Senior Hurling Championship">Cork Senior Hurling Championship</a> since its establishment by the <a href="/wiki/Cork_GAA" title="Cork GAA">Cork County Board</a> in 1887. The draw for the 2017 fixtures took place at the County Convention in December 2007. The championship began on 3 May 2008 and ended on 28 September 2008.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Erin%27s_Own_GAA_(Cork)" title="Erin's Own GAA (Cork)">Erin's Own</a> were the <a href="/wiki/2007_Cork_Senior_Hurling_Championship" title="2007 Cork Senior Hurling Championship">defending champions</a>, however, they were defeated by <a href="/wiki/Bride_Rovers_GAA" title="Bride Rovers GAA">Bride Rovers</a> in the semi-final.
</p><p>On 16 August 2008, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Catherine%27s_GAA" title="St. Catherine's GAA">St. Catherine's</a> were relegated from the championship following a 1-10 to 1-12 defeat by <a href="/wiki/Carrigtwohill_GAA" title="Carrigtwohill GAA">Carrigtwohill</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>On 28 September 2008, <a href="/wiki/Sarsfields_GAA_(Cork)" title="Sarsfields GAA (Cork)">Sarsfields</a> won the championship following a 2-14 to 2-13 defeat of Bride Rovers in the final.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> This was their third championship title overall and their first in 51 championship seasons.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Glen_Rovers_GAA" title="Glen Rovers GAA">Glen Rovers</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Horgan" title="Patrick Horgan">Patrick Horgan</a> was the championship's top scorer with 1-43.
</p>
