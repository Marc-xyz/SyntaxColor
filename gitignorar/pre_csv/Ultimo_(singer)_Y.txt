<p><b>Ultimo</b>, pseudonym of <b>Niccolò Moriconi</b> (born 27 January 1996), is an Italian pop singer/songwriter and winner of the <a href="/wiki/Sanremo_Music_Festival_2018" title="Sanremo Music Festival 2018">Sanremo Music Festival 2018</a> in the Newcomers category.
</p><p>Moriconi was born in Rome, where he began studying piano at age eight and writing songs at age fourteen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-music_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-music-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
In 2016, he won the <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" title="Hip hop">hip hop</a> music competition "One Shot Game," promoted by the independent record company Honiro Label, which began to produce the singer shortly after.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>He was confirmed as a participant of the <a href="/wiki/Sanremo_Music_Festival_2018" title="Sanremo Music Festival 2018">Sanremo Music Festival 2018</a> in the "new proposal" category with the song "Il ballo delle incertezze". He won the contest and received the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Premio_Lunezia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Premio Lunezia (page does not exist)">Premio Lunezia</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>.
</p>
