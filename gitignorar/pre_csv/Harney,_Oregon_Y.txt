<p><b>Harney</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Unincorporated_area" title="Unincorporated area">unincorporated community</a> in <a href="/wiki/Harney_County,_Oregon" title="Harney County, Oregon">Harney County</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">U.S. state</a> of <a href="/wiki/Oregon" title="Oregon">Oregon</a>. It lies north of <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_20_in_Oregon" title="U.S. Route 20 in Oregon">U.S. Route 20</a> between <a href="/wiki/Burns,_Oregon" title="Burns, Oregon">Burns</a> and <a href="/wiki/Buchanan,_Oregon" title="Buchanan, Oregon">Buchanan</a> near the site of historic <a href="/wiki/Fort_Harney" title="Fort Harney">Fort Harney</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-topo_map_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-topo_map-2">[2]</a></sup> The community, county, and nearby geographic features such as <a href="/wiki/Harney_Lake" title="Harney Lake">Harney Lake</a> are named for <a href="/wiki/William_S._Harney" title="William S. Harney">William S. Harney</a>, a military officer popular in the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Northwest" title="Pacific Northwest">Pacific Northwest</a> in the mid-19th century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OGN_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-OGN-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>The fort, part of a military establishment that fought with <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Indians</a> off and on in the 1860s and 1870s, was at a place called variously <i>Rattlesnake Camp</i>, <i>Camp Steele</i>, <i>Camp Harney</i>, and finally <i>Fort Harney</i>. The fort and the land around it were abandoned as military property in 1889. Meanwhile, the civilian community had developed about 2 miles (3 km) south of the fort, along Rattlesnake Creek.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OGN_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-OGN-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>A post office named <i>Camp Harney</i> was established at Fort Harney in 1874. William T. Stevens was the first postmaster. The post office name was changed to <i>Harney</i> in 1885 and relocated to the Harney civilian community. Robert J. Ives was the first postmaster at the new location.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OGN_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-OGN-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
