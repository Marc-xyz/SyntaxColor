	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Brantford Public Utilities Commission</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>Brantford Public Utilities Commission</b> is a former municipal commission in <a href="/wiki/Brantford" title="Brantford">Brantford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>. Once a powerful body in the city, it was dismantled in 1996 and eliminated entirely in 2001.
</p><p>The commission oversaw hydro and water services, and after 1935 it also looked after the city's public transportation system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It was overseen for many years by a team of elected commissioners, who represented different wards in the city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Brantford_City_Council" title="Brantford City Council">Brantford City Council</a> passed a by-law in 1996 that dismembered the commission. After heated discussions and a series of lawsuits, the city took over water and transit services directly and set up the Brantford Hydro-Electric Commission to oversee hydro services. 
</p><p>City council shut down the latter commission in 2001, two years after the provincial government of <a href="/wiki/Mike_Harris" title="Mike Harris">Mike Harris</a> changed the oversight of public utilities to encourage deregulation and privatization.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Brantford originally planned to sell the service outright, but instead set up <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Brantford_Power&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Brantford Power (page does not exist)">Brantford Power</a> and related corporations to run it. City councillor Richard Carpenter criticized this decision, saying that it could result in private-sector control and higher rates without proper oversight.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
