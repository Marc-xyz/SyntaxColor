	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Lost history</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>Many significant locations, cultures/groups, and objects throughout history have been <b>lost</b>, inspiring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archaeologist" title="Archaeologist">archaeologists</a> and treasure-hunters around the world to search for them.  
The existence of some of these places or items, particularly those from ancient history, is legendary and remains in doubt.  
Excluded from these lists are <a href="/wiki/Lost_work" title="Lost work">lost works</a>—literary, historical, or mythological writings which have no current complete copies but are referenced by their contemporaries.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Previously_lost">Previously lost</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lost_history&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Previously lost">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>The ancient city <a href="/wiki/Troy" title="Troy">Troy</a> (found by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Schliemann" title="Schliemann">Schliemann</a>, though this has been disputed) (found in the 1870s; lost 12th century BC–14th century BC)</li>
<li>The Greek place of worship <a href="/wiki/Olympia,_Greece" title="Olympia, Greece">Olympia</a> (found by German archaeologists in 1875)</li>
<li>The Lost Legions Of <a href="/wiki/Publius_Quinctilius_Varus" title="Publius Quinctilius Varus">Varus</a> (<a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Teutoburg_Forest" title="Battle of the Teutoburg Forest">Battle of the Teutoburg Forest</a>) (last seen 15 AD, found 1987)</li>
<li>Roman cities of <a href="/wiki/Pompeii" title="Pompeii">Pompeii</a>, <a href="/wiki/Herculaneum" title="Herculaneum">Herculaneum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stabiae" title="Stabiae">Stabiae</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Oplontis" title="Oplontis">Oplontis</a> all buried in the eruption of <a href="/wiki/Mount_Vesuvius" title="Mount Vesuvius">Mount Vesuvius</a>.  (Lost 79 AD, rediscovered in 1748)</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Nuestra_Se%C3%B1ora_de_Atocha" title="Nuestra Señora de Atocha">Nuestra Señora de Atocha</a> Spanish treasure ship (lost 1622, found 1985)</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/RMS_Titanic" title="RMS Titanic">RMS <i>Titanic</i></a> (lost 1912, found 1985)</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Still_lost_-_some_are_legendary_or_dubious">Still lost - some are legendary or dubious</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lost_history&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Still lost - some are legendary or dubious">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Ten_Lost_Tribes" title="Ten Lost Tribes">Ten Lost Tribes</a> of Israel, lost following the invasion by Assyria in 722 BC</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cambyses_II#The_lost_army_of_Cambyses" title="Cambyses II">The Lost Army of Cambyses</a> — an army of 50,000 soldiers that disappeared in a sandstorm in the Egyptian desert (lost 525? BC)</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Ark_of_the_Covenant" title="Ark of the Covenant">Ark of the Covenant</a> – biblical repository for the <a href="/wiki/Ten_Commandments" title="Ten Commandments">Ten Commandments</a> (lost after the <a href="/wiki/Neo-Babylonian_Empire" title="Neo-Babylonian Empire">Babylonian</a> invasion of <a href="/wiki/Jerusalem" title="Jerusalem">Jerusalem</a>)</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Holy_Chalice" title="Holy Chalice">Holy Chalice</a> — biblical chalice</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Legio_IX_Hispana" title="Legio IX Hispana">The Ninth Roman Legion</a></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Roanoke_Colony" title="Roanoke Colony">Roanoke Colony</a> of Roanoke Island, <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a> (Lost 1587–88) Settlers of the first English colony in <a href="/wiki/New_World" title="New World">the New World</a> vanish; leaving an abandoned settlement and the word "Croatoan," the name of a nearby island, carved into a post.</li>
<li>The Money Pit on <a href="/wiki/Oak_Island" title="Oak Island">Oak Island</a> (lost treasure pre-1795?)</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Mahogany_Ship" title="Mahogany Ship">Mahogany Ship</a> — an ancient shipwreck somewhere near <a href="/wiki/Warrnambool" title="Warrnambool">Warrnambool</a>, <a href="/wiki/Victoria_(Australia)" title="Victoria (Australia)">Victoria (Australia)</a> (last seen 1880)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Twin_Sisters_(cannons)" title="Twin Sisters (cannons)">Twin Sisters</a> — a pair of cannons used by <a href="/wiki/Texas_Military_Forces" title="Texas Military Forces">Texas Military Forces</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Texas_Revolution" title="Texas Revolution">Texas Revolution</a> and <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>. (last seen 1865)</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Lost_Dutchman%27s_Gold_Mine" title="Lost Dutchman's Gold Mine">Lost Dutchman's Gold Mine</a> (lost 1891)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Victoria_(Australia)" title="Victoria (Australia)">Victoria</a>'s Parliamentary Mace (lost 1891)</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Irish_Crown_Jewels" title="Irish Crown Jewels">Irish Crown Jewels</a> (lost 1907)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Amelia_Earhart" title="Amelia Earhart">Amelia Earhart</a> and her aircraft (lost 1937)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Saranow_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Saranow-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Amber_Room" title="Amber Room">Amber Room</a> (lost 1941?)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Saranow_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Saranow-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Flight_19" title="Flight 19">Flight 19</a> World War II era military aircraft training flight (lost 1945)</li>
<li>Lord Nelson's <a href="/wiki/Chelengk" title="Chelengk">Chelengk</a> (lost 1951)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup_Trophy" title="FIFA World Cup Trophy">FIFA World Cup Trophy</a> Jules Rimet World Cup Trophy (lost 1983)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lost_asteroid" title="Lost asteroid">Lost asteroids</a> and planets, lost after having been once discovered (multiple incidents) — some rediscovered but others still lost</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Tomb_of_Alexander_the_Great" title="Tomb of Alexander the Great">Tomb of Alexander the Great</a></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Library_of_Alexandria" title="Library of Alexandria">Library of Alexandria</a></li></ul>
