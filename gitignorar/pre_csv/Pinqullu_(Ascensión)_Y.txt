<p><b>Pinqullu</b> (<a href="/wiki/Aymara_language" title="Aymara language">Aymara</a> for a kind of <a href="/wiki/Pinkillu" title="Pinkillu">flute</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Andes" title="Andes">Andes</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Hispanicized spelling <i>Pincullo</i>) is a 5,096-metre (16,719 ft) mountain in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chunta_mountain_range" title="Chunta mountain range">Chunta</a> mountain range in the Andes of <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>. It lies in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Huancavelica_Region" title="Huancavelica Region">Huancavelica Region</a>, <a href="/wiki/Huancavelica_Province" title="Huancavelica Province">Huancavelica Province</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ascensi%C3%B3n_District" title="Ascensión District">Ascensión District</a>, northwest of <a href="/wiki/Antarasu" title="Antarasu">Antarasu</a>. The river <a href="/wiki/Kachimayu_(Huancavelica)" title="Kachimayu (Huancavelica)">Kachimayu</a> originates southwest of the mountain. It flows along the southern slopes of Pinqullu. Yawarqucha ("blood lake", <i>Yahuarcocha</i>) is the name of the little lake southwest of the peak.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ign_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-ign-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-escale_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-escale-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
