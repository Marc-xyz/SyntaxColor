<p><b>Folldal</b> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Norway" title="Municipalities of Norway">municipality</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hedmark" title="Hedmark">Hedmark</a> <a href="/wiki/Counties_of_Norway" title="Counties of Norway">county</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>.  It is part of the <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Norway" title="Districts of Norway">traditional region</a> of <a href="/wiki/%C3%98sterdalen" title="Østerdalen">Østerdalen</a>.  It is bordered on the north by <a href="/wiki/Oppdal" title="Oppdal">Oppdal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tynset" title="Tynset">Tynset</a> municipalities, in the east by <a href="/wiki/Alvdal" title="Alvdal">Alvdal</a> municipality, in the south by <a href="/wiki/Stor-Elvdal" title="Stor-Elvdal">Stor-Elvdal</a>, <a href="/wiki/S%C3%B8r-Fron" title="Sør-Fron">Sør-Fron</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sel" title="Sel">Sel</a> municipalities, and in the west by <a href="/wiki/Dovre" title="Dovre">Dovre</a> municipality. The administrative centre of the municipality is the village of Folldal.  Mining at the Folldal mines was the main industry for Folldal's residents from the 18th century until the last mine related operation in 1993.  The new municipality of Folldal was separated from <a href="/wiki/Alvdal" title="Alvdal">Alvdal</a> in 1914.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Folldal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first element is the river name <i>Folla</i> and the last element is <i>dal</i> which means "<a href="/wiki/Valley" title="Valley">valley</a>" or "dale". The meaning of the river name is unknown (perhaps "the broad one").
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Coat-of-arms">Coat-of-arms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Folldal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Coat-of-arms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">coat-of-arms</a> is from modern times.  They were granted in 1988. The arms show a gold-colored <a href="/wiki/Pickaxe" title="Pickaxe">pickaxe</a> on a red background.  This was chosen to represent <a href="/wiki/Mining" title="Mining">mining</a> and <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agriculture</a> in the municipality.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Folldal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Folldalgruver_gammeltbilde.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="218" data-file-width="300" decoding="async" height="218" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/4/49/Folldalgruver_gammeltbilde.jpg" width="300"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption">Interior view of the <i>Gammelgruva</i> mine.</div></div></div><p>  Mining has been important to Folldal’s development from the 18th century to the present. The Folldal works <i>(Folldal Verk)</i> was founded in 1748.  Folldal’s main <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Copper_extraction_techniques" title="Copper extraction techniques">copper mine</a>, <i>Gammelgruva</i>, also opened in 1748.  The mine employed up to 550 people. Until the 1878 ore was transported by horse-drawn vehicles to the Lovise <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Smelter" title="Smelter">smelter</a> located in neighboring <a href="/wiki/Alvdal" title="Alvdal">Alvdal</a> municipality.  From 1878 to 1906 there was a lull in operations.  Operations were restarted in 1906 with ore was transported by a 34 km  long cable car (Northern Europe's longest) to the smelter. The main <a href="/wiki/Mining" title="Mining">mine</a> played out and was closed in 1941.  Smelting continues using ore from several other deposits in the area until 1968. Then the mining and ore dressing moved to Tverrfjellet at Hjerkinn, in <a href="/wiki/Dovre" title="Dovre">Dovre</a> municipality, approximately 30 km from Folldal. The business was closed in 1993.
</p><p><br/>
</p><p>
The church in Folldal was a satellite church of that in <a href="/wiki/Lesja_Church" title="Lesja Church">Lesja</a>, as was the church at the <a href="/wiki/Iron_ore" title="Iron ore">iron ore</a> mines at <a href="/wiki/Lesjaverk" title="Lesjaverk">Lesjaverk</a> (Lesja Iron Works). Per Berg reports, </p><blockquote><p>“When the <a href="/wiki/Sexton_(office)" title="Sexton (office)">sexton</a> Ola Kring died in Lesja in 1751, Frederik Wiborg was appointed sexton there and was presented to the congregation on the third Sunday following Trinity. There certainly wasn't anything very special about being a teacher in <a href="/wiki/Ringsaker" title="Ringsaker">Ringsaker</a>, if he was willing to change that for being a sexton. No house or land went with the post and the work was hard. In the Lesja parish there were three satellite churches - Lesjeverk, <a href="/wiki/Dovre" title="Dovre">Dovre</a>, and Folldal. The travel distances were great and it could be very difficult in the winter.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Folldal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Togetfolldalgruver.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="800" decoding="async" height="150" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Togetfolldalgruver.jpg/200px-Togetfolldalgruver.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Togetfolldalgruver.jpg/300px-Togetfolldalgruver.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Togetfolldalgruver.jpg/400px-Togetfolldalgruver.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Togetfolldalgruver.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Train with tourists heading into the mine. Reprinted with permission from Foundation Folldal Mines</div></div></div>
<p>Folldal is located along the northwestern border of Hedmark county.  It is bordered in the north by <a href="/wiki/Oppdal" title="Oppdal">Oppdal</a> (in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%B8r-Tr%C3%B8ndelag" title="Sør-Trøndelag">Sør-Trøndelag</a> county) and <a href="/wiki/Tynset" title="Tynset">Tynset</a>, in the east by <a href="/wiki/Alvdal" title="Alvdal">Alvdal</a>, in the southeast by <a href="/wiki/Stor-Elvdal" title="Stor-Elvdal">Stor-Elvdal</a>, and to the southwest and west by <a href="/wiki/S%C3%B8r-Fron" title="Sør-Fron">Sør-Fron</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sel" title="Sel">Sel</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Dovre" title="Dovre">Dovre</a> (all in <a href="/wiki/Oppland" title="Oppland">Oppland</a> county). Folldal Municipality has the country's highest administrative center (712.5 meters above sea level) and has picturesque scenery with mountains and valleys. The village lies at the foot of <a href="/wiki/Rondeslottet" title="Rondeslottet">Rondeslottet</a> mountain and <a href="/wiki/Sn%C3%B8hetta" title="Snøhetta">Snøhetta</a> mountain. The area also has many interesting geologic features from the last Ice Age. Norway’s longest <a href="/wiki/Transhumance" title="Transhumance">seter</a> valley, the  55 kilometer long Einunndalen, lies within the municipality. The Einunndalen is still actively used for summer grazing at the seters (mountain summer farms) established in the 1700s. Almost half of the municipal land is protected as conservation areas and national parks, including parts that are included in the <a href="/wiki/Rondane_National_Park" title="Rondane National Park">Rondane National Park</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dovrefjell%E2%80%93Sunndalsfjella_National_Park" title="Dovrefjell–Sunndalsfjella National Park">Dovrefjell–Sunndalsfjella National Park</a>.
</p><p>A national tourist road, the RV 27, runs through the municipality.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Outdoors">Outdoors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Folldal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Outdoors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Folldal offers a wide variety of outdoor activities. Hiking is good with marked trails and several cabins to visit. Fishing can be done in the rivers Folla, Einunna or Glomma. The opportunities for hunting in the area are also good.
</p>
