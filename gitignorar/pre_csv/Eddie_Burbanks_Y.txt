	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Eddie Burbanks</h1>

<b>Eddie Burbanks</b> (1 April 1913 – 26 July 1983)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> was an English <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who played for <a href="/wiki/Sunderland_A.F.C." title="Sunderland A.F.C.">Sunderland</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Midfield#Winger" title="Midfield">left winger</a>. He was born in <a href="/wiki/Norton,_Doncaster" title="Norton, Doncaster">Campsall</a>, England.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ALS_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ALS-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eddie_Burbanks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Burbanks made his debut for Sunderland on 27 April 1935 in a 4-1 win against <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth_F.C." title="Portsmouth F.C.">Portsmouth</a> at <a href="/wiki/Roker_Park" title="Roker Park">Roker Park</a>, where he scored one goal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stat_Cat_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stat_Cat-3">[3]</a></sup>  In Sunderland's first FA Cup win in 1937, Burbanks scored the third goal to wrap up the victory.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ALS_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-ALS-2">[2]</a></sup> In his 156 appearances for Sunderland, he scored 30 goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stat_Cat_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Stat_Cat-3">[3]</a></sup> He played also in Leeds United (1953–1954) when he became (until today) the oldest player to have ever played for the club when he captained the side against Hull on 24 April 1954. In 13 appearances with the Elland Road club he scored 1 goal.
</p>
