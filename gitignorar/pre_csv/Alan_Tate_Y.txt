<p><b>Alan Tate</b> (born 2 September 1982) is an English professional football coach and retired <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who is an assistant first-team and academy coach at <a href="/wiki/Swansea_City_A.F.C." title="Swansea City A.F.C.">Swansea City</a>. A former <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)" title="Defender (association football)">defender</a>, Tate spent the majority of his playing career at Swansea.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Playing_career">Playing career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Tate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Playing career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Easington,_County_Durham" title="Easington, County Durham">Easington</a>, County Durham,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hugman_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Hugman-1">[1]</a></sup> Tate began his career as a trainee at <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a>, later earning himself a professional contract. Although he went on to captain United's reserve team, which included such players as <a href="/wiki/Quinton_Fortune" title="Quinton Fortune">Quinton Fortune</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luke_Chadwick" title="Luke Chadwick">Luke Chadwick</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chris_Eagles" title="Chris Eagles">Chris Eagles</a>, he failed to break into the first team. Tate went on loan to <a href="/wiki/Royal_Antwerp_F.C." title="Royal Antwerp F.C.">Royal Antwerp</a>, Manchester United's feeder club, in 2002, making seven appearances in the Belgian First Division.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> He did not score for Royal Antwerp in the two months he was there, but he did score an own goal against <a href="/wiki/K.A.A._Gent" title="K.A.A. Gent">Gent</a> on 17 February 2002, the first goal in a 3–3 draw.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Swansea_City">Swansea City</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Tate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Swansea City">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Tate spent two loan periods at <a href="/wiki/Swansea_City_A.F.C." title="Swansea City A.F.C.">Swansea City</a>. He played a major role in the club's battle against relegation from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Football_League" title="The Football League">the Football League</a> in the 2002–03 season, before joining the club on a permanent basis on 6 February 2004.
</p><p>Initially a centre-back, Tate made a successful transition to the right-back slot. He further showed his versatility by playing several matches in the centre of midfield during an injury crisis at the club during the 2005–06 season, which saw him named as the club's Player of the Year. Tate was in the Swansea City team which won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Trophy" title="Football League Trophy">Football League Trophy</a> final against <a href="/wiki/Carlisle_United_F.C." title="Carlisle United F.C.">Carlisle United</a> on 2 April 2006. After the final whistle, he and <a href="/wiki/Lee_Trundle" title="Lee Trundle">Lee Trundle</a> were photographed holding a Welsh flag bearing the words "Fuck off Cardiff," which had been handed to them by Swansea supporters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The pair claimed to have been unaware of the message written on the flag. On 24 June 2006, both players were fined a week's wages and warned as to their future conduct by the <a href="/wiki/Football_Association_of_Wales" title="Football Association of Wales">Football Association of Wales</a>, having already received police cautions for public order offences.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>Swansea City reached the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_One" title="Football League One">League One</a> play-off final against <a href="/wiki/Barnsley_F.C." title="Barnsley F.C.">Barnsley</a> on 27 May 2006. The match finished at 2–2 after extra time, and went to a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Penalty_shootout_(association_football)" title="Penalty shootout (association football)">penalty shootout</a>. With Swansea trailing 4–3, Tate saw his penalty saved, sealing Barnsley's promotion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>He continued mainly playing at right-back under <a href="/wiki/Kenny_Jackett" title="Kenny Jackett">Kenny Jackett</a> and occasionally midfield to cover for injuries in the 2006–07 season. When former Swansea captain <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Mart%C3%ADnez" title="Roberto Martínez">Roberto Martínez</a> took over as the new manager by the 2007–08 season, Tate had lost his place at right-back to new arrival <a href="/wiki/%C3%80ngel_Rangel" title="Àngel Rangel">Àngel Rangel</a>.
</p><p>During the 2008–09 season, Tate covered all positions in defence, including one appearance as a goalkeeper against <a href="/wiki/Queens_Park_Rangers_F.C." title="Queens Park Rangers F.C.">Queens Park Rangers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> After injuries to <a href="/wiki/Marcos_Painter" title="Marcos Painter">Marcos Painter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Federico_Bessone" title="Federico Bessone">Federico Bessone</a>, Tate played at left-back for the rest of the 2008–09 season. Tate had a superb <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Swansea_City_A.F.C._season" title="2009–10 Swansea City A.F.C. season">2009–10</a> season as he played most of his matches in his favourite position at centre-back due to <a href="/wiki/Garry_Monk" title="Garry Monk">Garry Monk</a>'s injury-laden season, as well as captaining the side for most of the season. For the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Swansea_City_A.F.C._season" title="2010–11 Swansea City A.F.C. season">2010–11</a> season, under new manager Brendan Rodgers, he resumed his position at left-back, although he has played centre-back ever since Monk got injured in an FA Cup tie against Colchester United on 8 January 2011.
</p><p>On 28 August 2011, Tate broke his leg in a <a href="/wiki/Golf_cart" title="Golf cart">golf cart</a> accident, managing to lodge his leg between the cart and a tree. The injury was estimated to rule him out for up to six months. Despite this, Tate signed a new contract with Swansea on 14 September 2011, keeping him at the club until June 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Tate made his comeback as a late substitute for Swansea in their penultimate match of the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Swansea_City_A.F.C._season" title="2011–12 Swansea City A.F.C. season">2011–12</a> season at <a href="/wiki/Old_Trafford" title="Old Trafford">Old Trafford</a> appearing against his former club, Manchester United.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>A <a href="/wiki/Testimonial_match" title="Testimonial match">testimonial match</a> for Tate against <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a> was granted by Swansea in the summer of 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> However, it was postponed because Swansea were scheduled to play Manchester United in their first <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> fixture of the 2013–14 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> His testimonial was played four years later, on 9 August 2017, with a Swansea legends team facing a Manchester United legends team at the <a href="/wiki/Liberty_Stadium" title="Liberty Stadium">Liberty Stadium</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p><p>Tate had made 340 appearances for Swansea, scoring five goals in all competitions, which excludes the <a href="/wiki/FAW_Premier_Cup" title="FAW Premier Cup">FAW Premier Cup</a>, which Swansea competed in from 1997 to 2008.
</p><p>Tate revealed that he was to exit the club in the summer 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> This was confirmed by Swansea on 28 May 2015.
</p><p>On 21 July 2016 it was announced that Tate would take up an Academy U18 coaching role, helping to coach the Swans' Under-18s team alongside Eric Ramsay and Chris Llewellyn as well as taking charge of any U17s matches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Loan_spells">Loan spells</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Tate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Loan spells">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>After a falling out with Swansea manager <a href="/wiki/Michael_Laudrup" title="Michael Laudrup">Michael Laudrup</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Tate joined <a href="/wiki/Leeds_United_F.C." title="Leeds United F.C.">Leeds United</a> on loan on 22 November 2012 until January 2013. Tate represented the first signing by Leeds after the takeover by GFH Capital the previous day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Tate made his debut for Leeds against <a href="/wiki/Crystal_Palace_F.C." title="Crystal Palace F.C.">Crystal Palace</a> on 24 November and was handed the squad number 15.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Tate received praise for his performance's against Crystal Palace and <a href="/wiki/Leicester_City_F.C." title="Leicester City F.C.">Leicester City</a>, helping Leeds earn two wins in his first two matches.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup>
</p><p>After the takeover of Leeds by GFH Capital on 21 December, new Leeds director Salem Patel revealed Leeds were in negotiations with Swansea City to sign Tate on a permanent deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> On 1 January, Tate extended his loan spell at Leeds until 26 January.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup>
</p><p>On 23 July 2013, Tate joined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" title="Football League Championship">Championship</a> side <a href="/wiki/Yeovil_Town_F.C." title="Yeovil Town F.C.">Yeovil Town</a> on loan until 2 January 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Upon joining the club, the club made the announcement of the signing by producing a mocked-up picture of an easel outside <a href="/wiki/Huish_Park" title="Huish Park">Huish Park</a> following the arrival of the <a href="/wiki/Prince_George_of_Cambridge" title="Prince George of Cambridge">Duke and Duchess of Cambridge's son</a> the day before.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Tate made his Yeovil debut in their 1–0 victory over <a href="/wiki/Millwall_F.C." title="Millwall F.C.">Millwall</a> on 3 August 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Tate made six appearances for Yeovil before returning to Swansea early on 5 September due to a hamstring injury.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup>
</p><p>On 3 January 2014, Tate joined <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Premiership" title="Scottish Premiership">Scottish Premiership</a> side <a href="/wiki/Aberdeen_F.C." title="Aberdeen F.C.">Aberdeen</a> as an emergency signing to replace recently departed <a href="/wiki/Reading_F.C." title="Reading F.C.">Reading</a> defender <a href="/wiki/Michael_Hector" title="Michael Hector">Michael Hector</a>, on loan until the end of the 2013–14 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> He made his Aberdeen debut on 10 January in a 1–0 home win against <a href="/wiki/Hibernian_F.C." title="Hibernian F.C.">Hibernian</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup>
</p><p>Tate joined <a href="/wiki/Crewe_Alexandra_F.C." title="Crewe Alexandra F.C.">Crewe Alexandra</a> on 24 September 2014 for an emergency 93-day loan,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> which was extended in January 2015 to the end of the 2014–15 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SWEP_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-SWEP-29">[29]</a></sup> Tate made 26 appearances for The Railwaymen, helping them to maintain their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_One" title="Football League One">League One</a> status.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Port_Talbot_Town">Port Talbot Town</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Tate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Port Talbot Town">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 2 November 2015, it was announced Tate would be joining <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Welsh_Premier_League" title="Welsh Premier League">Welsh Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Port_Talbot_Town_F.C." title="Port Talbot Town F.C.">Port Talbot Town</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coaching_career">Coaching career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Tate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Coaching career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Following his retirement as a player, Tate began coaching at <a href="/wiki/Swansea_City_A.F.C." title="Swansea City A.F.C.">Swansea City</a>'s academy, where he worked with the under-16s and under-18s. He became an assistant first-team coach to <a href="/wiki/Steve_Cooper_(football_manager)" title="Steve Cooper (football manager)">Steve Cooper</a> in September 2019, while continuing his work as a youth coach.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup>
</p>
