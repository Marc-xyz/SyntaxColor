	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Delaware Blue Hen</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p>The <b>Delaware Blue Hen</b> is a <a href="/wiki/List_of_chicken_colours" title="List of chicken colours">blue</a> strain of <a href="/wiki/American_Game" title="American Game">American gamecock</a>. It was adopted on April 14, 1939, as the <a href="/wiki/List_of_U.S._state_birds" title="List of U.S. state birds">state bird</a> of <a href="/wiki/Delaware" title="Delaware">Delaware</a>. It is one of three <a href="/wiki/List_of_U.S._state_birds" title="List of U.S. state birds">U.S. state birds</a> that is not a species native to the United States.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Delaware_Blue_Hen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Blue Hen is not a recognized chicken breed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paul_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-paul-2">[2]</a></sup> According to one story, during the <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">Revolutionary War</a>, the men of Captain Jonathan Caldwell's company, recruited in <a href="/wiki/Kent_County,_Delaware" title="Kent County, Delaware">Kent County</a>, took with them <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cockfighting" title="Cockfighting">game chickens</a> that were said to be of the brood of a famous Blue Hen and were noted for their fighting ability.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ekarius_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-ekarius-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap">:<span>76</span></sup>
</p><p>The sports teams of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Delaware" title="University of Delaware">University of Delaware</a> are called the <a href="/wiki/Delaware_Fightin%27_Blue_Hens" title="Delaware Fightin' Blue Hens">Blue Hens</a>, and their <a href="/wiki/Mascot" title="Mascot">mascot</a>, <a href="/wiki/YoUDee" title="YoUDee">YoUDee</a>, derives from the bird.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In the 1960s <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=S._Hallock_duPont&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="S. Hallock duPont (page does not exist)">S. Hallock duPont</a>, who bred Blue Hens (though not derived from the original Kent County stock), gave twelve birds to the University, which keeps a small flock at its College of Agriculture &amp; Natural Resources.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ekarius_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-ekarius-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap">:<span>76</span></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paul_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-paul-2">[2]</a></sup> The birds have been <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cross-bred" title="Cross-bred">cross-bred</a> with birds of the <a href="/wiki/Andalusian_chicken" title="Andalusian chicken">Blue Andalusian</a> breed from Spain, and have acquired many of its characteristics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ekarius_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-ekarius-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap">:<span>76</span></sup> They are Mediterranean in appearance, and no longer resemble the original fighting birds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paul_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-paul-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
