<p><b>Blackheath Hill</b> was a railway station between the <a href="/wiki/Greenwich" title="Greenwich">Greenwich</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lewisham" title="Lewisham">Lewisham</a> areas in south-east London. It was opened in 1871 by the <a href="/wiki/London,_Chatham_and_Dover_Railway" title="London, Chatham and Dover Railway">London, Chatham and Dover Railway</a> (LCDR) on its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greenwich_Park_Branch_Line" title="Greenwich Park Branch Line">Greenwich Park Branch Line</a>. Blackheath Hill was at the end of the line until an extension to <a href="/wiki/Greenwich_Park_railway_station" title="Greenwich Park railway station">Greenwich Park</a> opened in 1888.
</p><p>The station closed in 1917 along with the rest of the line, owing to low usage and economy measures during <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>. The station building beside the <a href="/wiki/A2_road_(England)" title="A2 road (England)">A2 road</a> was demolished in the mid-1980s, and the site has since been entirely built over. Whilst the building work was being undertaken the retaining wall and tunnel under the A2 were visible.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
