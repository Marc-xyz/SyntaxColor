<p><b>William Andrew Tavaré</b> (born 1 January 1990) is an English former <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">cricketer</a> who played for <a href="/wiki/Gloucestershire_County_Cricket_Club" title="Gloucestershire County Cricket Club">Gloucestershire County Cricket Club</a>.  Tavaré is a right-handed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Batsman_(cricket)" title="Batsman (cricket)">batsman</a> who bowls right-arm <a href="/wiki/Seam_bowling" title="Seam bowling">medium pace</a>.  He was born in <a href="/wiki/Bristol" title="Bristol">Bristol</a> and educated at <a href="/wiki/Bristol_Grammar_School" title="Bristol Grammar School">Bristol Grammar School</a>. He retired from cricket following the <a href="/wiki/2019_County_Championship" title="2019 County Championship">2019 County Championship</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>While studying for his degree in human biology at <a href="/wiki/Loughborough_University" title="Loughborough University">Loughborough University</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Tavaré made his <a href="/wiki/First-class_cricket" title="First-class cricket">first-class</a> debut for <a href="/wiki/Loughborough_MCC_University" title="Loughborough MCC University">Loughborough MCCU</a> against <a href="/wiki/Kent_County_Cricket_Club" title="Kent County Cricket Club">Kent</a> in 2010.  He has made two further first-class appearances for Loughborough MCCU, against <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_County_Cricket_Club" title="Yorkshire County Cricket Club">Yorkshire</a> in 2010 and Kent in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>  His three first-class appearances have so far seen him score 172 runs at an <a href="/wiki/Batting_average_(cricket)" title="Batting average (cricket)">average</a> of 34.40, with two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Half_century" title="Half century">fifties</a> and a high score of 59.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>On 28 September 2013, Tavaré signed for <a href="/wiki/Gloucestershire_County_Cricket_Club" title="Gloucestershire County Cricket Club">Gloucestershire County Cricket Club</a> after impressing for the county's second XI during the 2013 season.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>One of his uncles is the former <a href="/wiki/England_cricket_team" title="England cricket team">England</a> <a href="/wiki/Test_cricket" title="Test cricket">Test</a> and <a href="/wiki/One_Day_International" title="One Day International">One Day International</a> cricketer <a href="/wiki/Chris_Tavar%C3%A9" title="Chris Tavaré">Chris Tavaré</a> and another is Business Executive Simon Lee
</p>
