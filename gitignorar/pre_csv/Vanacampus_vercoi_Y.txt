<p><i><b>Vanacampus vercoi,</b></i> also known as <b>Verco's pipefish</b> is a species of marine fish belonging to the family <a href="/wiki/Syngnathidae" title="Syngnathidae">Syngnathidae</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IUCN_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-1">[1]</a></sup> They can be found inhabiting <a href="/wiki/Seaweed" title="Seaweed">seaweed</a> and <a href="/wiki/Seagrass" title="Seagrass">seagrass</a> beds in addition to <a href="/wiki/Tide_pool" title="Tide pool">tidepools</a> at depths between 2–4 meters from <a href="/wiki/Spencer_Gulf" title="Spencer Gulf">Spencer's Gulf</a> to <a href="/wiki/Encounter_Bay,_South_Australia" title="Encounter Bay, South Australia">Encounter Bay</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Australia" title="South Australia">South Australia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pogonoski2002_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pogonoski2002-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Baker2008_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Baker2008-3">[3]</a></sup> Their diet likely consists of small <a href="/wiki/Crustacean" title="Crustacean">crustaceans</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Amphipoda" title="Amphipoda">amphipods</a> and <a href="/wiki/Copepod" title="Copepod">copepods</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kendrick2005_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kendrick2005-4">[4]</a></sup> Reproduction occurs through <a href="/wiki/Ovoviviparity" title="Ovoviviparity">ovoviviparity</a> in which the males brood eggs before giving live birth.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dawson1985_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dawson1985-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
