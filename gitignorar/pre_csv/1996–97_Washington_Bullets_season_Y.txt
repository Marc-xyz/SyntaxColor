<p>The <b><a href="/wiki/1996%E2%80%9397_NBA_season" title="1996–97 NBA season">1996–97 NBA season</a></b> was the Bullets' 36th season in the <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_Association" title="National Basketball Association">National Basketball Association</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> During the offseason, the Bullets acquired <a href="/wiki/Rod_Strickland" title="Rod Strickland">Rod Strickland</a> from the <a href="/wiki/1995%E2%80%9396_Portland_Trail_Blazers_season" title="1995–96 Portland Trail Blazers season">Portland Trail Blazers</a>, and signed free agent <a href="/wiki/Tracy_Murray" title="Tracy Murray">Tracy Murray</a>. Despite a stellar season last year, <a href="/wiki/Juwan_Howard" title="Juwan Howard">Juwan Howard</a> signed a 7-year $100 million contract with the <a href="/wiki/1996%E2%80%9397_Miami_Heat_season" title="1996–97 Miami Heat season">Miami Heat</a>. However, the deal was voided claiming that Miami exceeded their salary cap. The Bullets quickly re-signed Howard, but would lose their first round draft pick next year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>With a record of 22–24 at midseason, the Bullets fired head coach <a href="/wiki/Jim_Lynam" title="Jim Lynam">Jim Lynam</a>, then after playing one game under assistant <a href="/wiki/Bob_Staak" title="Bob Staak">Bob Staak</a>, they hired <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Bickerstaff" title="Bernie Bickerstaff">Bernie Bickerstaff</a> as their new coach. Under Bickerstaff, the Bullets finished the season winning 16 of their final 21 games. On the final day of the regular season, the Bullets defeated the <a href="/wiki/1996%E2%80%9397_Cleveland_Cavaliers_season" title="1996–97 Cleveland Cavaliers season">Cleveland Cavaliers</a> 85–81 at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gund_Arena" title="Gund Arena">Gund Arena</a> to capture the #8 seed in the Eastern Conference. They finished fourth in the Atlantic Division with a 44–38 record, ending an eight-year playoff drought. <a href="/wiki/Chris_Webber" title="Chris Webber">Chris Webber</a> led the team in scoring, rebounding and block shots. He was also selected for the <a href="/wiki/1997_NBA_All-Star_Game" title="1997 NBA All-Star Game">1997 NBA All-Star Game</a>.
</p><p>In the first round of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1997_NBA_Playoffs" title="1997 NBA Playoffs">playoffs</a>, the Bullets were swept by the defending champion <a href="/wiki/1996%E2%80%9397_Chicago_Bulls_season" title="1996–97 Chicago Bulls season">Chicago Bulls</a> in three straight games. This would be their final playoff appearance until <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_Washington_Wizards_season" title="2004–05 Washington Wizards season">2005</a>. Game 3 of that series at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_Airways_Arena_(Washington,_D.C.)" title="US Airways Arena (Washington, D.C.)">US Airways Arena</a> was officially their final game as the "Bullets". Following the season, the team changed its name to the "Wizards".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
