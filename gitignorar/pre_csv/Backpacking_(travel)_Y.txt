	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Backpacking (travel)</h1>

<p><b>Backpacking</b> is a form of low-cost, independent travel. Once considered a marginal activity undertaken by society’s drop-outs, it has gradually entered the tourism mainstream.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>While backpacker tourism is generally a form of <a href="/wiki/Youth_travel" title="Youth travel">youth travel</a>, primarily undertaken by young people during <a href="/wiki/Gap_year" title="Gap year">gap years</a>, it is also undertaken by older people during a career break or retirement. Backpackers tend to be from <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, the <a href="/wiki/English-speaking_world" title="English-speaking world">English-speaking world</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characteristics">Characteristics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Backpacking_(travel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Characteristics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Backpacker tourism generally, but does not always, include:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>Traveling mostly via <a href="/wiki/Public_transport" title="Public transport">public transport</a>.</li>
<li>Using inexpensive lodging such as <a href="/wiki/Hostel" title="Hostel">hostels</a>.</li>
<li>A longer duration trip when compared with conventional vacations.</li>
<li>An interest in meeting locals as well as seeing sights.</li>
<li>An interest in meeting other travelers.</li>
<li>Use of <a href="/wiki/Smartphone" title="Smartphone">smartphones</a> and online resources for planning.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li>Carrying belongings in a <a href="/wiki/Backpack" title="Backpack">backpack</a>. Backpackers have traditionally carried their possessions in 30 liter to 60 liter backpacks, but roller-wheeled suitcases and some less-traditional carrying methods have become more common, and there has been a trend towards keeping pack weights under the 8-to-10 kilogram carry-on limit of most airlines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li>Backpacker tourism may also include working in various countries for short stints, depending on <a href="/wiki/Work_permit" title="Work permit">work permit</a> laws.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BTAP_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-BTAP-7">[7]</a></sup> It can also be undertaken by <a href="/wiki/Digital_nomad" title="Digital nomad">digital nomads</a>, people who work using technology while living a nomadic lifestyle.</li>
<li>A sense of <a href="/wiki/Authenticity_(philosophy)" title="Authenticity (philosophy)">authenticity</a>. Backpacking is perceived not only as a form of <a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">tourism</a>, but a means of education.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Backpackers want to experience what they consider the "real" destination rather than a packaged version often associated with mass tourism.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Backpacking_(travel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>People have travelled for thousands of years with their possessions on their backs, but usually out of need rather than for recreation.
</p><p>In the 17th century, Italian adventurer <a href="/wiki/Giovanni_Francesco_Gemelli_Careri" title="Giovanni Francesco Gemelli Careri">Giovanni Francesco Gemelli Careri</a> was likely one of the first people to engage in backpacker tourism.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>The modern popularity of backpacking can be traced, at least partially, to the <a href="/wiki/Hippie_trail" title="Hippie trail">hippie trail</a> of the 1960s and 1970s,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Diversity_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Diversity-11">[11]</a></sup> which in turn followed sections of the old <a href="/wiki/Silk_Road" title="Silk Road">Silk Road</a>. Some backpackers follow the same trail today.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p><p>Since the late-20th century, backpackers have visited <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asia</a> in large numbers<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Benefits">Benefits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Backpacking_(travel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Benefits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="For_participants">For participants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Backpacking_(travel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: For participants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>A 2018 study of over 500 backpackers conducted by researchers at <a href="/wiki/Sun_Yat-sen_University" title="Sun Yat-sen University">Sun Yat-sen University</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shaanxi_Normal_University" title="Shaanxi Normal University">Shaanxi Normal University</a> in China and <a href="/wiki/Edith_Cowan_University" title="Edith Cowan University">Edith Cowan University</a> in Australia showed that for Westerners, backpacking leads to acquired capabilities like effective communication, <a href="/wiki/Decision-making" title="Decision-making">decision-making</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adaptability" title="Adaptability">adaptability</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Problem_solving" title="Problem solving">problem solving</a>, all of which contribute to an increase in <a href="/wiki/Self-efficacy" title="Self-efficacy">self-efficacy</a>, and for Chinese backpackers, acquiring skills like time and <a href="/wiki/Money_management" title="Money management">money management</a>, language development, <a href="/wiki/Stress_management" title="Stress management">stress management</a>, and self-motivation provided the biggest increase in self-efficacy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="For_communities">For communities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Backpacking_(travel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: For communities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>For many low-income communities in the developing world, the economic benefits of hosting backpackers outweigh their negative impacts. Since backpackers tend to consume local products, stay in small guest houses, and use locally owned ground transport, more of their expenditure is retained in-country than in conventional mass tourism. businesses that cater to backpackers are usually locally owned and profits tend to be retained within the country rather than flowing overseas to international hotel groups.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism">Criticism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Backpacking_(travel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Criticism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Effect_on_culture">Effect on culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Backpacking_(travel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Effect on culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Backpacker tourism of the <a href="/wiki/Hippie_trail" title="Hippie trail">hippie trail</a> has been criticized for possibly encouraging urban liberal minorities while insulting <a href="/wiki/Traditionalist_theology_(Islam)" title="Traditionalist theology (Islam)">Islamic traditionalist theology</a>, possibly leading to the Islamic reawakening in the late 1970s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Lack_of_cultural_immersion">Lack of cultural immersion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Backpacking_(travel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Lack of cultural immersion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Even though one of the primary aims of backpacking is to seek the "authentic", the majority of backpackers spend most of their time interacting with other backpackers, and interactions with locals are of "secondary importance".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Diversity_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-Diversity-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Transformation_of_localities">Transformation of localities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Backpacking_(travel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Transformation of localities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Backpacker tourism has been criticized for the transformation of some sleepy towns and the creation of the <a href="/wiki/Full_Moon_Party" title="Full Moon Party">Full Moon Party</a> on <a href="/wiki/Ko_Pha-ngan" title="Ko Pha-ngan">Ko Pha-ngan</a> in <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>, which includes "scores of topless teenagers urinating into the ocean".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Variants">Variants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Backpacking_(travel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Variants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Flashpacking</b> and <b>Poshpacking</b> refer to backpacking with more money and resources. The words combine <i>backpacking</i> with <i>flash</i>, a slang term for being fancy, or <i>posh</i>, an informal adjective for <a href="/wiki/Upper_class" title="Upper class">upper class</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup>
</p><p><b>Begpacking</b> combines <i>begging</i> and <i>backpacking</i> in reference to individuals who <a href="/wiki/Begging" title="Begging">beg</a> (ask directly or indirectly for money), solicit money during <a href="/wiki/Street_performance" title="Street performance">street performances</a>, or vend (sell postcards or other small items) as a way to extend their overseas travel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The trend has drawn criticism for taking money away from people in actual need, with one known begpacker barred from entering <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> Begpacking is most common in Southeast Asia and is a growing trend in South America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup>
</p>
