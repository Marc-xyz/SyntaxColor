<p><b><a href="/wiki/Ada_(name)" title="Ada (name)">Ada</a></b> (c. 1188 – 1234/37) was <a href="/wiki/Count_of_Holland" title="Count of Holland">Countess</a> of <a href="/wiki/County_of_Holland" title="County of Holland">Holland</a> between 1203 and 1207.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ada,_Countess_of_Holland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:De_burcht_leiden_2003.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="273" data-file-width="400" decoding="async" height="150" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/De_burcht_leiden_2003.jpg/220px-De_burcht_leiden_2003.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/De_burcht_leiden_2003.jpg/330px-De_burcht_leiden_2003.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/f5/De_burcht_leiden_2003.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:De_burcht_leiden_2003.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Burcht_van_Leiden" title="Burcht van Leiden">Burcht van Leiden</a>, remnants of the castle where Ada was first taken prisoner after the death of her father.</div></div></div>
<p>Ada was the only surviving daughter of <a href="/wiki/Dirk_VII,_Count_of_Holland" title="Dirk VII, Count of Holland">Dirk VII, Count of Holland</a> and his wife Adelaide of Cleves. She succeeded her father but immediately had to deal with her uncle <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_I_of_Holland" title="William I of Holland">William</a>, who claimed Holland for his own. Ada married <a href="/wiki/Louis_II,_Count_of_Loon" title="Louis II, Count of Loon">Louis II</a>, count of <a href="/wiki/County_of_Loon" title="County of Loon">Loon</a> to strengthen her position. She was in such a hurry, that she married even before her father was buried, which caused a scandal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> These events led to the outbreak of the <a href="/wiki/Loon_War" title="Loon War">Loon War</a> (1203–1206).
</p><p>Ada was quickly captured by the supporters of William and taken prisoner in the citadel of <a href="/wiki/Leiden" title="Leiden">Leiden</a>. She was first imprisoned on the island of <a href="/wiki/Texel" title="Texel">Texel</a> and afterwards she was taken to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_Lackland" title="John Lackland">John Lackland</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_England" title="Kingdom of England">Kingdom of England</a>. William had to accept Louis and Ada as count and countess at a treaty of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brugge" title="Brugge">Brugge</a> in 1206. Louis managed to get Ada free in 1206, and the couple returned to Loon in 1207. Their reign was short-lived and William was appointed Count of Holland by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Otto_IV" title="Otto IV">Otto IV</a> in 1208. She did not accept the loss of her county, and Ada and Louis continued the fight. Ada remained childless. Louis died in 1218, leaving Ada to live out the rest of her life in obscurity. She was buried next to her husband in <a href="/wiki/Herkenrode_Abbey" title="Herkenrode Abbey">Herkenrode Abbey</a>.
</p><p>The civil war in Holland became part of a major international war between on one side <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Hohenstaufen" title="Hohenstaufen">Hohenstaufen</a> dynasty and on the other side England and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Welfs" title="Welfs">Welfs</a>. William could get Holland through good maneuvering between both sides. Louis and Ada had to give up their claims. History has been particularly unkind to her, and many period histories up to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Protestant_Reformation" title="Protestant Reformation">Protestant Reformation</a> don't even count her as a countess, but call William I the next in line from Dirk VII, as if she never existed.
</p>
