	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en"><i>Saturday Night Live with Howard Cosell</i></h1>

<p><i><b>Saturday Night Live with Howard Cosell</b></i> was an American television comedy-variety program that ran on <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> from September <a href="/wiki/1975_in_television" title="1975 in television">1975</a> to January <a href="/wiki/1976_in_television" title="1976 in television">1976</a>, hosted by <a href="/wiki/Howard_Cosell" title="Howard Cosell">Howard Cosell</a> and executive-produced by <a href="/wiki/Roone_Arledge" title="Roone Arledge">Roone Arledge</a>. The series ran for 18 episodes before being cancelled.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-youtubemischer_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-youtubemischer-1">[1]</a></sup> The show was later remembered by its director <a href="/wiki/Don_Mischer" title="Don Mischer">Don Mischer</a> as "one of the greatest disasters in the history of television", largely because Cosell and Arledge—both veterans of sports broadcasting—were entirely unfamiliar with comedy and variety programming.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-youtubemischer_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-youtubemischer-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Despite having highly notable celebrities both as cast members and guests, <i>Saturday Night Live with Howard Cosell</i> has never been made available on home video.
</p><p><i>Saturday Night Live with Howard Cosell</i>  is consistently confused with the <a href="/wiki/Sketch_comedy" title="Sketch comedy">sketch comedy</a> program <i><a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live" title="Saturday Night Live">Saturday Night Live</a></i>. In October 1975, rival network <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> began airing the <a href="/wiki/Late_night_television" title="Late night television">late night</a> comedy show <i>NBC's Saturday Night</i>, the  creation of producer  <a href="/wiki/Lorne_Michaels" title="Lorne Michaels">Lorne Michaels</a>. The shows did not compete for the same time slot. Cosell's <i>Saturday Night Live</i> aired at 8 p.m. <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Time_Zone" title="Eastern Time Zone">ET</a>/<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Standard_Time" title="Pacific Standard Time">PT</a>, whereas <i>NBC's Saturday Night</i> aired at 11:30 p.m. After Cosell's show was cancelled, the NBC show was renamed <i>Saturday Night Live</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-snl_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-snl-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saturday_Night_Live_with_Howard_Cosell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cast_and_guests">Cast and guests</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saturday_Night_Live_with_Howard_Cosell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Cast and guests">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The premiere episode featured celebrity guests <a href="/wiki/Frank_Sinatra" title="Frank Sinatra">Frank Sinatra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shirley_Bassey" title="Shirley Bassey">Shirley Bassey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Anka" title="Paul Anka">Paul Anka</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Siegfried_and_Roy" title="Siegfried and Roy">Siegfried and Roy</a>, the cast of the <a href="/wiki/Broadway_theatre" title="Broadway theatre">Broadway</a> version of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wiz" title="The Wiz">The Wiz</a></i>, tennis pro <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Connors" title="Jimmy Connors">Jimmy Connors</a> (who sang, while profusely sweating, Anka's "Girl, You Turn Me On" as a dedication to his girlfriend <a href="/wiki/Chris_Evert" title="Chris Evert">Chris Evert</a>.  Anka played the piano to accompany Connors), and <a href="/wiki/John_Denver" title="John Denver">John Denver</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-snl-1_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-snl-1-3">[3]</a></sup>  The episode's musical guest was the <a href="/wiki/Bay_City_Rollers" title="Bay City Rollers">Bay City Rollers</a>, from Scotland, whom Cosell dubbed "the next" British phenomenon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pittpress_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-pittpress-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>The show featured <a href="/wiki/Bill_Murray" title="Bill Murray">Bill Murray</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brian_Doyle-Murray" title="Brian Doyle-Murray">Brian Doyle-Murray</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Guest" title="Christopher Guest">Christopher Guest</a> as regular comedy performers, dubbed "The Prime Time Players". In response, NBC's show <i>Saturday Night</i> called its regular performers "The Not-Ready-For-Prime-Time Players" (especially since the show didn't air in prime time, but late-night). Eventually, Murray, Doyle-Murray, and Guest would all work on the NBC program.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Billy_Crystal" title="Billy Crystal">Billy Crystal</a>, who appeared on the premiere episode of Cosell's program, was also scheduled to appear on the premiere episode of the NBC show, but was bumped when the show ran long; he later joined the NBC program's cast, along with Guest, during <a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live_(season_10)" title="Saturday Night Live (season 10)">Season 10</a> a decade later. Also that season, Cosell himself guest-hosted the NBC program in its season finale on April 13, 1985.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cancellation">Cancellation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saturday_Night_Live_with_Howard_Cosell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cancellation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Mischer described the show as chronically hectic and unprepared. He recalled one particular episode wherein executive producer Roone Arledge discovered that jazz icon <a href="/wiki/Lionel_Hampton" title="Lionel Hampton">Lionel Hampton</a> was in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, and invited the musician to appear on the show an hour before airtime.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-youtubemischer_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-youtubemischer-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The show fared poorly among critics and audiences alike, with <i><a href="/wiki/TV_Guide" title="TV Guide">TV Guide</a></i> calling it "<a href="/wiki/Dead_on_arrival" title="Dead on arrival">dead on arrival</a>, with a cringingly awkward host".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvguide_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-tvguide-6">[6]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alan_King_(comedian)" title="Alan King (comedian)">Alan King</a>—the show's "executive in charge of comedy"—later admitted that it was difficult trying to turn Cosell into a variety show host, saying that he "made <a href="/wiki/Ed_Sullivan" title="Ed Sullivan">Ed Sullivan</a> look like <a href="/wiki/Buster_Keaton" title="Buster Keaton">Buster Keaton</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tvguide_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-tvguide-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Saturday Night Live with Howard Cosell</i> was canceled on January 17, 1976, after only 18 episodes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-youtubemischer_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-youtubemischer-1">[1]</a></sup> A year later, in 1977, NBC's <i>Saturday Night</i> appropriated the name of its former (indirect) competition.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saturday_Night_Live_with_Howard_Cosell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2002, <i><a href="/wiki/TV_Guide" title="TV Guide">TV Guide</a></i> ranked the series number 37 on its "50 Worst TV Shows of All Time" list.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In his book <i>What Were They Thinking? The 100 Dumbest Events in Television History</i>, author David Hofstede ranked the series at #30 on the list.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Episodes">Episodes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saturday_Night_Live_with_Howard_Cosell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Episodes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Only three episodes (video) are known to survive:<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>All 14 original episodes telecast exist as AUDIO in the Archival Television Audio archive (www.atvaudio.com). 
</p>
<ul><li>September 20, 1975: <a href="/wiki/Billy_Crystal" title="Billy Crystal">Billy Crystal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Frank_Sinatra" title="Frank Sinatra">Frank Sinatra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bay_City_Rollers" title="Bay City Rollers">Bay City Rollers</a></li>
<li>October 11, 1975: <a href="/wiki/Barry_Manilow" title="Barry Manilow">Barry Manilow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aretha_Franklin" title="Aretha Franklin">Aretha Franklin</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Rockettes" title="The Rockettes">The Rockettes</a></li>
<li>November 8, 1975: <a href="/wiki/Roy_Clark" title="Roy Clark">Roy Clark</a>, <a href="/wiki/Billy_Crystal" title="Billy Crystal">Billy Crystal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ted_Kennedy" title="Ted Kennedy">Ted Kennedy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chita_Rivera" title="Chita Rivera">Chita Rivera</a> as Herself/Velma Kelly, and <a href="/wiki/Gwen_Verdon" title="Gwen Verdon">Gwen Verdon</a> as Herself/Roxie Hart</li></ul>
