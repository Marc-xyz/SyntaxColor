	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Duke of Gandía</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Duke of Gandía</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Valencian_language" title="Valencian language">Valencian</a>: <i lang="ca-valencia">Ducat de Gandia</i>, <small>IPA: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Catalan" title="Help:IPA/Catalan">[duˈkad de ɣanˈdi.a]</a></span>) is a title of <a href="/wiki/Spanish_nobility" title="Spanish nobility">Spanish nobility</a> that was first created in 1399 by <a href="/wiki/Martin_of_Aragon" title="Martin of Aragon">Martin of Aragon</a> and granted to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alfonso_of_Aragon_and_Foix" title="Alfonso of Aragon and Foix">Alfonso of Aragon and Foix</a>. It has its origin in the <a href="/wiki/Manorialism" title="Manorialism">lordship</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gand%C3%ADa" title="Gandía">Gandía</a> created in 1323 by <a href="/wiki/James_II_of_Aragon" title="James II of Aragon">James II of Aragon</a>. Later, having no direct descendants, the title passed from the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Aragon" title="House of Aragon">House of Aragon</a> to the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Trast%C3%A1mara" title="House of Trastámara">House of Trastámara</a>.
</p><p>The title was re-established in 1483 by <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_II_of_Aragon" title="Ferdinand II of Aragon">Ferdinand II of Aragon</a> as a favour to <a href="/wiki/Pope_Alexander_VI" title="Pope Alexander VI">Cardinal Rodrigo Borgia</a> for his son <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pier_Luigi_Borgia" title="Pier Luigi Borgia">Pedro Luis</a> (Pier Luigi de Borgia).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The dukedom then went to Pier Luigi's half-brother <a href="/wiki/Giovanni_Borgia,_2nd_Duke_of_Gandia" title="Giovanni Borgia, 2nd Duke of Gandia">Juan</a> (Giovanni Borgia). He was assassinated, and his young son inherited the title. The fourth duke was the religious figure <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Francesco_Borgia" title="Francesco Borgia">Francesco Borgia</a>. After the death of his wife, with whom he had a large family, he became a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jesuit" title="Jesuit">Jesuit</a>.
</p>
