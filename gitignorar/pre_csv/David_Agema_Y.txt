<p><b>David Agema</b> (born February 11, 1949) is a politician from the U.S. state of <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a>. Presently the chairman of the Top Gun Republican <a href="/wiki/Political_action_committee" title="Political action committee">political action committee</a>, he is a former member of the <a href="/wiki/Michigan_House_of_Representatives" title="Michigan House of Representatives">Michigan House of Representatives</a> and of the <a href="/wiki/Republican_National_Committee" title="Republican National Committee">Republican National Committee</a>. Agema, formerly a commercial pilot for <a href="/wiki/American_Airlines" title="American Airlines">American Airlines</a>, is an Air Force Reserve veteran. He currently resides in <a href="/wiki/Grandville,_Michigan" title="Grandville, Michigan">Grandville</a>, Michigan.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Agema&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Agema was born in <a href="/wiki/Grand_Rapids,_Michigan" title="Grand Rapids, Michigan">Grand Rapids</a>, Michigan, on February 11, 1949, in an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dutch_American" title="Dutch American">American family of Dutch origin</a>. He received a Bachelor of Arts from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Calvin_College" title="Calvin College">Calvin College</a> in 1971 and an M.B.A. from <a href="/wiki/Central_Michigan_University" title="Central Michigan University">Central Michigan University</a> in 1975.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-official_website_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-official_website-1">[1]</a></sup> He served as a <a href="/wiki/Fighter_pilot" title="Fighter pilot">fighter pilot</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Air_Force" title="United States Air Force">United States Air Force</a> but never served overseas during the conflict.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-official_website_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-official_website-1">[1]</a></sup> In 1978, he joined the <a href="/wiki/Air_National_Guard" title="Air National Guard">Air National Guard</a>, retiring as a lieutenant colonel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-official_website_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-official_website-1">[1]</a></sup> Agema then worked for <a href="/wiki/American_Airlines" title="American Airlines">American Airlines</a> and later as a business consultant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-official_website_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-official_website-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_involvement">Political involvement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Agema&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Political involvement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Agema is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pro-life" title="Pro-life">pro-life</a> Republican, who is actively vocal in his opposition to <a href="/wiki/Same-sex_marriage" title="Same-sex marriage">same-sex marriage</a> and <a href="/wiki/Civil_union" title="Civil union">civil unions</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gaybenefits_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-gaybenefits-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Agema served three terms (the constitutional limit) in the <a href="/wiki/Michigan_House_of_Representatives" title="Michigan House of Representatives">Michigan House of Representatives</a>, from 2007 to 2013, representing parts of the Grand Rapids area.  The <a href="/wiki/American_Conservative_Union" title="American Conservative Union">American Conservative Union</a> gave him a 100% rating.  He was elected at the 2012 Michigan Republican State Convention to the Republican National Committee, defeating incumbent <a href="/wiki/Saul_Anuzis" title="Saul Anuzis">Saul Anuzis</a>. In 2016 he was replaced by Robert Steele.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_controversies">Political controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Agema&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Political controversies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2013, he faced calls to resign from the Republican National Committee by only 11 members of Chairman Priebus's executive committee after he posted extracts from an article allegedly written by a doctor entitled "Everyone Should Know These Statistics On Homosexuals" which said that homosexuals lived a "filthy lifestyle", were responsible for 50 percent of U.S. murders in certain cities as stated by a magistrate that year and that many are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paedophilia" title="Paedophilia">pedophiles</a>. Agema was rebuked by RNC  chairman <a href="/wiki/Reince_Priebus" title="Reince Priebus">Reince Priebus</a>, who drew a distinction between Republican policies on gay rights and the language used in Agema's post, adding "all human beings deserve to be treated with dignity and respect."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The Republican platform opposes gay marriage.
</p><p>Later that year, Agema was condemned by <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Michigan" title="Governor of Michigan">Michigan Governor</a> <a href="/wiki/Rick_Snyder" title="Rick Snyder">Rick Snyder</a> after saying that homosexuals were in favor of healthcare reform because "some are dying between 30 and 44 years old."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Agema posted several statistics on his web page from newer biased sources confirming earlier death.
</p><p>In addition to his comments about homosexuals, Agema quoted an article written by another in response to Obama's statement that Muslim have a rich fabric in our nations history, "Have you ever seen a Muslim do anything that contributes positively to the American way of life?"(These were not his words). This was in reference to the lack of historical record of Obama's statement. Muslim Republicans took offense to this and cited contribution from their families and friends. Additionally, his bill to ban foreign law and Sharia Law (ALAC)( passed by several other states) was seen as an attempt to engender paranoia against Muslims.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Agema's comments on Muslims, "styled as an open missive to President <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Obama</a>", led to the resignation in 2015 of a Maine private-academy lacrosse coach who had re-posted them on Facebook.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>On December 31, 2014, Agema reposted an article printed by former Congressman and retired Army lieutenant colonel Allen West in his news letter. West stated he had personally observed some of this and that it would seem racists but needed to be addressed. The article was written pseudonymously that ran in the white nationalist magazine <i><a href="/wiki/American_Renaissance_(magazine)" title="American Renaissance (magazine)">American Renaissance</a></i>, which included sentences by a public defender concerning certain felons with multiple convictions he had to defend  stating certain  "[B]lacks are different by almost any measure to all other people. They cannot reason as well ( referring to these felons).  They cannot communicate as well. They cannot control their impulses as well. They are a threat to all who cross their paths, black and non-black alike."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Chairman <a href="/wiki/Reince_Priebus" title="Reince Priebus">Reince Priebus</a> subsequently called on him to resign his RNC position.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> He did not resign.
</p>
