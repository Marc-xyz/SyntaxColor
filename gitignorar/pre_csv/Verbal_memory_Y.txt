<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Verbal_encoding">Verbal encoding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Verbal_memory&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Verbal encoding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Verbal encoding refers to the interpretation of verbal stimuli. Verbal encoding appears to be strongly left-<a href="/wiki/Lateralization_of_brain_function" title="Lateralization of brain function">lateralized</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medial_temporal_lobe" title="Medial temporal lobe">medial temporal lobe</a> of the human brain; however, its functional neuroanatomy can vary between individuals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Verbal_recall">Verbal recall</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Verbal_memory&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Verbal recall">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Verbal recall refers to the recollection of verbal information. Although left-lateralization is typically associated with language, studies suggest that symmetrical bi-lateralization of language in the brain is advantageous to verbal recall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
