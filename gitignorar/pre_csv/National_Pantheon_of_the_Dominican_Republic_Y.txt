<p><b>The National Pantheon</b> was built from 1714-1746 by the Spaniard Geronimo Quezada y Garçon and was originally a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jesuit" title="Jesuit">Jesuit</a> church.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-History_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-History-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jesuits_in_DR_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Jesuits_in_DR-2">[2]</a></sup> The structure was constructed in the <a href="/wiki/Neoclassical_architecture" title="Neoclassical architecture">neoclassic</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renaissance_architecture" title="Renaissance architecture">renaissance</a> style. Today, the structure stands as a national symbol of the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a> and serves as the final resting place of the Republic's most honored citizens.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Pantheon_of_the_Dominican_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jesuits held mass here until 1767. After 1767, it was used as a tobacco warehouse and then as the first Dominican theater for purely artistic purposes by the society <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jose_Gabriel_Garc%C3%ADa#Writing" title="Jose Gabriel García">Amantes de las Letras</a> in 1860 until 1878 when it became theater La Republicana which operated until 1917.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It housed governmental offices until 1956.
</p><p>In 1956, Spanish architect Javier Borroso renovated the structure to serve its new purpose as a national mausoleum, by order of then dictator <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Trujillo" title="Rafael Trujillo">Rafael Trujillo</a>. Originally, Trujillo envisioned being interred at the National Pantheon, yet today it is the place where the country's most famous persons are honored, among others Trujillo's assassins.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-History_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-History-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Other notables that are buried at the National Pantheon include <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Gregorio_Billini" title="Francisco Gregorio Billini">Francisco Gregorio Billini</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gregorio_Luper%C3%B3n" title="Gregorio Luperón">Gregorio Luperón</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eugenio_Mar%C3%ADa_de_Hostos" title="Eugenio María de Hostos">Eugenio María de Hostos</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jose_Gabriel_Garc%C3%ADa" title="Jose Gabriel García">Jose Gabriel García</a>.
</p>
