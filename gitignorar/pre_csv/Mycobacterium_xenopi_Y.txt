<p><i><b>Mycobacterium xenopi</b></i> is a slow-growing <a href="/wiki/Scotochromogenic" title="Scotochromogenic">scotochromogenic</a> species of <i><a href="/wiki/Mycobacterium" title="Mycobacterium">Mycobacterium</a></i>. It was first reported by Schwabacher<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> in 1959, having been isolated in <a href="/wiki/Lesion" title="Lesion">lesions</a> found on a <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xenopus_laevis" title="Xenopus laevis">Xenopus laevis</a></i>, but the possibility of human infection was not confirmed until 1965.
</p><p>It has low <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pathogenicity" title="Pathogenicity">pathogenicity</a> in humans,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and where infections have been found they are closely associated with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Immunocompromised" title="Immunocompromised">immunocompromised</a> individuals.
</p><p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biological_type" title="Biological type">Type strain</a>: strain <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/American_Type_Culture_Collection" title="American Type Culture Collection">ATCC</a> 19250 = CCUG 28011 = CCUG 31306 = CIP 104035 = DSM 43995 = NCTC 10042.
</p>
