<p>A <b>king-emperor</b>, the female equivalent being <b>queen-empress</b>, is a sovereign ruler who is simultaneously a <a href="/wiki/King" title="King">king</a> of one territory and <a href="/wiki/Emperor" title="Emperor">emperor</a> of another. This title usually results from a merger of a royal and imperial crown, but recognises that the two territories are different politically or culturally and in status (emperor being a higher rank than king). It also denotes a king's imperial status through the acquisition of an <a href="/wiki/Empire" title="Empire">empire</a> or vice versa.
</p><p>The dual title signifies a sovereign's dual role, but may also be created to improve a ruler's prestige. Both cases, however, show that the merging of rule was not simply a case of annexation where one state is swallowed by another, but rather of unification and almost equal status, though in the case of the <a href="/wiki/Monarchy_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Monarchy of the United Kingdom">British monarchy</a> the suggestion that an emperor is higher in rank than a king was avoided by creating the title "king-emperor" ("queen-empress") instead of "emperor-king" ("empress-queen").
</p>

<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:INDIAGeorge_V_King_Emperor.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="900" data-file-width="900" decoding="async" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/INDIAGeorge_V_King_Emperor.jpg/220px-INDIAGeorge_V_King_Emperor.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/INDIAGeorge_V_King_Emperor.jpg/330px-INDIAGeorge_V_King_Emperor.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/INDIAGeorge_V_King_Emperor.jpg/440px-INDIAGeorge_V_King_Emperor.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:INDIAGeorge_V_King_Emperor.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Coins of the <a href="/wiki/British_Raj" title="British Raj">British Raj</a> featured the title "King Emperor", such as this <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/George_V_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="George V of the United Kingdom">George V</a> coin.</div></div></div>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Crown" title="British Crown">British Crown</a> had officially taken over the governing of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_India" title="British India">British India</a> from the <a href="/wiki/East_India_Company" title="East India Company">East India Company</a> in 1858, in the aftermath of what the British called 'the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indian_Mutiny" title="Indian Mutiny">Indian Mutiny</a>'. Henceforth, the new <a href="/wiki/British_Raj" title="British Raj">British Raj</a> was ruled directly from <a href="/wiki/Whitehall" title="Whitehall">Whitehall</a> via the <a href="/wiki/India_Office" title="India Office">India Office</a>. Following the <a href="/wiki/Delhi_Durbar" title="Delhi Durbar">Delhi Durbar</a> in 1877, <a href="/wiki/Queen_Victoria" title="Queen Victoria">Queen Victoria</a> was given Imperial status by the British Government, and she assumed the title <a href="/wiki/Emperor_of_India" title="Emperor of India">Empress of India</a>. She was thus the Queen-Empress, and her successors, until <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/George_VI_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="George VI of the United Kingdom">George VI</a>, were known as King-Emperors. This title was the shortened form of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Style_of_the_British_Sovereign#Styles_of_British_sovereigns" title="Style of the British Sovereign">the full title</a>, and in widespread popular use.
</p><p>The reigning King-Emperors or Queen-Empress used the initials <i>R I</i> (<i><a href="/wiki/Rex_(title)" title="Rex (title)">Rex</a> <a href="/wiki/Imperator" title="Imperator">Imperator</a></i> or <i>Regina Imperatrix</i>) or the abbreviation <i>Ind. Imp.</i> (<i>Indiae Imperator/Imperatrix</i>) after their name (while the one reigning Queen-Empress, Victoria, used the initials <i>R I</i>, the three consorts of the married King-Emperors simply used <i>R</i>).
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Coins_of_the_pound_sterling" title="Coins of the pound sterling">British coins</a>, and those of the <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British Empire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth</a> <a href="/wiki/Dominion" title="Dominion">dominions</a>, routinely included some variation of the titles <i>Rex Ind. Imp.</i>, although in India itself the coins said "Empress", and later "King Emperor."  When, in August 1947, India became independent, all dies had to be changed to remove the latter two abbreviations, in some cases taking up to a year.  In <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>, coins of George VI carried the title up to 1948.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_Austria-Hungary">In Austria-Hungary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=King-Emperor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: In Austria-Hungary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Another use of this dual title was when, in 1867, the multi-national <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Empire" title="Austrian Empire">Austrian Empire</a>, which was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ethnic_German" title="Ethnic German">German</a>-ruled and facing growing <a href="/wiki/Nationalism" title="Nationalism">nationalism</a>, undertook a reform that gave nominal and factual rights to <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungarian</a> <a href="/wiki/Nobility" title="Nobility">nobility</a>. This reform revived the Austrian-annexed <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Hungary" title="Kingdom of Hungary">Kingdom of Hungary</a>, and therefore created the <a href="/wiki/Personal_union" title="Personal union">dual-monarchic union</a> state of <a href="/wiki/Austria-Hungary" title="Austria-Hungary">Austria-Hungary</a> and the dual title of "emperor-king" (in <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a> <i>Kaiser und König</i>).
</p><p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Habsburg" title="Habsburg">Habsburg</a> dynasty therefore ruled as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emperors_of_Austria" title="Emperors of Austria">Emperors of Austria</a> over the western and northern half of the Empire (<a href="/wiki/Cisleithania" title="Cisleithania">Cisleithania</a>), and as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kings_of_Hungary" title="Kings of Hungary">Kings of Hungary</a> over the Kingdom of Hungary and much of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transleithania" title="Transleithania">Transleithania</a>. Hungary enjoyed some degree of self-government and representation in joint affairs (principally foreign relations and defence). The federation bore the full name of "The Kingdoms and Lands Represented in the Imperial Council and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lands_of_the_Holy_Crown_of_St._Stephen" title="Lands of the Holy Crown of St. Stephen">Lands of the Crown of St. Stephen</a>".
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_the_Italian_colonial_empire">In the Italian colonial empire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=King-Emperor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: In the Italian colonial empire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1936, with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Italian_conquest_of_Ethiopia" title="Italian conquest of Ethiopia">Italian conquest of Ethiopia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Victor_Emmanuel_III_of_Italy#Emperor_of_Ethiopia" title="Victor Emmanuel III of Italy">Victor Emmanuel III</a> was proclaimed <a href="/wiki/Emperor_of_Ethiopia" title="Emperor of Ethiopia">Emperor of Ethiopia</a>. Thus he became King-Emperor (in Italian <i>Re Imperatore</i>), i.e., <a href="/wiki/King_of_Italy" title="King of Italy">King of Italy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Emperor_of_Ethiopia" title="Emperor of Ethiopia">Emperor of Ethiopia</a>, because conquered Ethiopia was then an Empire.  In the following years foreign  countries recognized the Italian rule in Ethiopia (UK in 1937).
</p><p>The King-Emperor was represented by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Viceroy_of_Italian_East_Africa" title="Viceroy of Italian East Africa">Viceroy</a>, who was also appointed as Governor-General of <a href="/wiki/Italian_East_Africa" title="Italian East Africa">Italian East Africa</a> (AOI - Africa Orientale Italiana). The capital city of the Viceroy and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Governor-General" title="Governor-General">Governor-General</a> was <a href="/wiki/Addis_Ababa" title="Addis Ababa">Addis Ababa</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Titles">Titles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=King-Emperor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Titles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>The Serbian emperor <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Du%C5%A1an" title="Stefan Dušan">Stefan Dušan</a> (r. 1346–55), earlier king (1331–45), is attested with the title "Emperor of Greece and King of All Serb Lands and the Maritime" in a document dating to between 1347–56 (see also <a href="/wiki/Emperor_of_the_Serbs" title="Emperor of the Serbs">Emperor of the Serbs</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Dušan has been described as a "king-emperor".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li>The German Empire was also ruled by an Emperor-King, as the German <a href="/wiki/House_of_Hohenzollern" title="House of Hohenzollern">Hohenzollern</a> <a href="/wiki/List_of_German_monarchs" title="List of German monarchs">Emperor</a> was also <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_rulers_of_Prussia" title="List of rulers of Prussia">King</a> of <a href="/wiki/Prussia" title="Prussia">Prussia</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Holy Roman Emperor">Holy Roman Emperors</a> were also Kings of Italy, Germany and Burgundy for most of the time that title existed.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Napol%C3%A9on_I_of_France" title="Napoléon I of France">Emperor Napoléon I of the French</a> was also <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Italy_(Napoleonic)" title="Kingdom of Italy (Napoleonic)">King of Italy</a>. His title was shortened in "Emperor-King" (<i>Empereur-Roi</i> or <i>l'Empereur et Roi</i>) rather than "King-Emperor".</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/John_VI_of_Portugal" title="John VI of Portugal">John VI of Portugal</a> was made titular <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emperor_of_Brazil" title="Emperor of Brazil">Emperor of Brazil</a> alongside being <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_of_Portugal" title="King of Portugal">King of Portugal</a> and was titled as King-Emperor until his death. After John VI's death, his son <a href="/wiki/Pedro_I_of_Brazil" title="Pedro I of Brazil">Pedro</a> briefly acceded him as King of Portugal while reigning as Emperor of Brazil.</li></ul>
