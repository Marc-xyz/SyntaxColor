	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Farid Nazha</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>

		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><table class="infobox biography vcard" style="width:22em"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" style="text-align:center;font-size:125%;font-weight:bold"><div class="fn" style="display:inline">Farid Elias Nazha<br/>ܦܪܝܕ ܐܠܝܐܣ ܢܙܗܝ</div></th></tr><tr><td colspan="2" style="text-align:center"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Farid_Nazha.jpg"><img alt="Farid Nazha.jpg" data-file-height="114" data-file-width="115" decoding="async" height="198" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/df/Farid_Nazha.jpg" width="200"/></a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Born</th><td><span style="display:none">(<span class="bday">1894-01-10</span>)</span>10 January 1894<br/><div class="birthplace" style="display:inline"><a href="/wiki/Hama" title="Hama">Hama</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a></div></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Died</th><td>19 October 1970<span style="display:none">(1970-10-19)</span> (aged 76)<br/><div class="deathplace" style="display:inline"><a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a></div></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Occupation</th><td class="role">Journalist</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Movement</th><td class="category"><a href="/wiki/Assyrian_nationalism" title="Assyrian nationalism">Assyrian nationalism</a></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p><b>Farid Elias Nazha</b> (<a href="/wiki/Syriac_language" title="Syriac language">Syriac</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="syr">ܦܪܝܕ ܐܠܝܐܣ ܢܙܗܝ</span>‎, <small>pronounced </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA" title="Help:IPA">[fɑriːd nɑzhe]</a></span>; 10 January 1894 in <a href="/wiki/Hama" title="Hama">Hama</a>, <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a> – 19 October 1970)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hujada_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-hujada-1">[1]</a></sup> was an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Assyrian_Nationalist" title="Assyrian Nationalist">Assyrian Nationalist</a> and a journalist. He was known for his criticism to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Syriac_Christian" title="Syriac Christian">Syriac Christian</a> Clergy which led to his <a href="/wiki/Excommunication" title="Excommunication">excommunication</a> by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Syriac_Orthodox" title="Syriac Orthodox">Syriac Orthodox</a> Patriarch <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ignatius_Afram_I_Barsoum" title="Ignatius Afram I Barsoum">Ignatius Afram I Barsoum</a>. He is considered one of founders of modern <a href="/wiki/Assyrian_people" title="Assyrian people">Assyrian</a> nationalism.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bethnahrin_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-bethnahrin-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Farid_Nazha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Farid was born on 10 January 1894 to a family which traces its roots back to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kharput" title="Kharput">Kharput</a> in modern-day <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>. In 1911 a number of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Syriac_Orthodox" title="Syriac Orthodox">Syriac Orthodox</a> members of his family converted to <a href="/wiki/Syriac_Catholic_Church" title="Syriac Catholic Church">Syriac Catholicism</a> which initiated a bitter conflict between the Assyrians of <a href="/wiki/Hama" title="Hama">Hama</a>. Farid's father decided to send his son to <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a> to prevent him from getting involved in the conflict.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bethnahrin_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-bethnahrin-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 1911 he arrived in <a href="/wiki/Buenos_Aires" title="Buenos Aires">Buenos Aires</a>, where he studied Mathematics and Economy. He got married and moved to <a href="/wiki/Santiago_del_Estero" title="Santiago del Estero">Santiago del Estero</a> in March 1920 where he worked there, and after 11 years he went back to <a href="/wiki/Buenos_Aires" title="Buenos Aires">Buenos Aires</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bethnahrin_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-bethnahrin-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Journalism_and_activism">Journalism and activism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Farid_Nazha&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Journalism and activism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Influenced by <a href="/wiki/Naum_Faiq" title="Naum Faiq">Naum Faiq</a>'s writing, Nazha established a Syriac cultural club, the "Ephremic Society", in 1934. It included Assyrian/Syriac immigrants in Argentina. A newspaper, "Syriac University" (<a href="/wiki/Syriac_language" title="Syriac language">Syriac</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="syr">ܚܕܥܝܘܬܐ ܣܘܪܝܝܬܐ</span>‎ <i lang="syr-Latn" title="Syriac-language romanization"><i>Ḥdoʻyauṯo Suryoyṯo</i></i>, <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">Asociacion  Asiria</i>), was published by the society in September of the same year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bethnahrin_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-bethnahrin-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>The newspaper was initially aimed at the Assyrian/Syriac immigrants in Argentina, but its popularity grew as Assyrian journalists from other parts of the world started contributing to it. Nazha himself had a permanent column in the newspaper and he often attacked the clergy in what he saw as "consecration of separation" of different <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Syriac_Churches" title="Syriac Churches">Syriac Churches</a>. For him nationality was above religion, and archbishops shouldn't have a leading role in the Assyrian/Syriac society. His views about the church worsened as the latter started adopting Arabic in its liturgies. The clash with the clergy reached its peak when the Syriac Orthodox patriarch <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ignatius_Afram_I_Barsoum" title="Ignatius Afram I Barsoum">Ignatius Afram I Barsoum</a> formally excommunicated him, although his successor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ignatius_Ya%60qub_III" title="Ignatius Ya`qub III">Ignatius Ya`qub III</a> retracted this decision in the late 1950s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bethnahrin_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-bethnahrin-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Farid Nazha continued publishing his newspaper until his death in Spain on 19 October 1970.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bethnahrin_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-bethnahrin-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
