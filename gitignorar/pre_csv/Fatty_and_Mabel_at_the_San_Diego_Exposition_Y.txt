<p><i><b>Fatty and Mabel at the San Diego Exposition</b></i> is a 1915 American <a href="/wiki/Silent_film" title="Silent film">silent</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black-and-white" title="Black-and-white">black-and-white</a> <a href="/wiki/Short_film" title="Short film">short</a> <a href="/wiki/Comedy_film" title="Comedy film">comedy film</a>, directed by  <a href="/wiki/Roscoe_Arbuckle" title="Roscoe Arbuckle">Fatty Arbuckle</a> and starring Arbuckle and <a href="/wiki/Mabel_Normand" title="Mabel Normand">Mabel Normand</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-silentera_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-silentera-1">[1]</a></sup> It was produced by <a href="/wiki/Keystone_Studios" title="Keystone Studios">Keystone Studios</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fatty_and_Mabel_at_the_San_Diego_Exposition&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fatty (Roscoe Arbuckle) and Mabel (Mabel Normand) are a married couple visiting the Exposition. Fatty gets in trouble by flirting with a passing woman (Minta Durfee) while Mabel shops. He chases the woman into a hula pavilion and makes approaches to the dancers. He is accosted by both Mabel and the woman's husband; eventually the police are called to straighten the whole thing out.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production_background">Production background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fatty_and_Mabel_at_the_San_Diego_Exposition&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Arbuckle and Normand followed the Keystone tradition of showing up at an actual event and using that as background for a largely improvised film. The event in this case was the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Panama-California_Exposition_(1915)" title="Panama-California Exposition (1915)">Panama-California Exposition</a>, held in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Balboa_Park,_San_Diego,_California" title="Balboa Park, San Diego, California">Balboa Park</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/San_Diego,_California" title="San Diego, California">San Diego, California</a> in 1915-1916. The film is 14 minutes long. It was released on January 23, 1915.
</p>
