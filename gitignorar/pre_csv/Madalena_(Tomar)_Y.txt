	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Madalena (Tomar)</h1>

		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:TMR-madalena.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="432" data-file-width="413" decoding="async" height="230" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/TMR-madalena.png/220px-TMR-madalena.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/TMR-madalena.png/330px-TMR-madalena.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/57/TMR-madalena.png 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:TMR-madalena.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>CoA</div></div></div>
<p><b>Madalena</b> is a former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freguesia_(Portugal)" title="Freguesia (Portugal)">civil parish</a> in the municipality of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tomar_Municipality" title="Tomar Municipality">Tomar</a>, Portugal. In 2013, the parish merged into the new parish <a href="/wiki/Madalena_e_Beselga" title="Madalena e Beselga">Madalena e Beselga</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It has a total area of 30.56 km² and a population of 3,466 inhabitants (2001). Its population density is 113.4 people per km².
</p><p>The parish includes the following villages: Caldelas, Caniçal, Carvalhal Grande, Carvalhal Pequeno, Cem Soldos, Charneca do Maxial, Madalena, Maxial, Paço da Comenda, Porto da Lage, Porto Mendo and São Miguel.
</p><p>According to Pinho Leal in "Portugal Antigo e Moderno" (<i>Portugal Past and Present</i>), the parish was heavily populated in the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman</a> era. For this author the modern Caldelas is built upon the site of the ancient city of the same name. Other writers say that Caldele was the exact name of the Roman city.
</p>
