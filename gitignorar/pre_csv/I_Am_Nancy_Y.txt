<p><i><b>I Am Nancy</b></i> is a 2011 American documentary that follows actress <a href="/wiki/Heather_Langenkamp" title="Heather Langenkamp">Heather Langenkamp</a> as she explores her role as <a href="/wiki/Nancy_Thompson_(A_Nightmare_on_Elm_Street)" title="Nancy Thompson (A Nightmare on Elm Street)">Nancy Thompson</a> in the <a href="/wiki/A_Nightmare_on_Elm_Street_(franchise)" title="A Nightmare on Elm Street (franchise)"><i>A Nightmare on Elm Street</i> films</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Fandom" title="Fandom">fandom</a> that surrounds the franchise, and why most of it focuses on <a href="/wiki/Freddy_Krueger" title="Freddy Krueger">Freddy Krueger</a>, rather than Nancy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I_Am_Nancy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Actress Heather Langenkamp attends several <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Horror_conventions" title="Horror conventions">horror conventions</a> across the globe as she interacts with fans to gain insight as to why people are so drawn to the series and the characters of Nancy and Freddy. Additionally, she interviews <a href="/wiki/Robert_Englund" title="Robert Englund">Robert Englund</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wes_Craven" title="Wes Craven">Wes Craven</a> to understand what qualities make Nancy a definitive film hero.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I_Am_Nancy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Langenkamp was inspired to make a documentary about herself and Nancy after a receptionist rebuffed her efforts to contact director <a href="/wiki/Wes_Craven" title="Wes Craven">Wes Craven</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>  The film was originally planned to coincide with the 25th anniversary of <i>A Nightmare on Elm Street</i> but was delayed for her participation in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Never_Sleep_Again" title="Never Sleep Again">Never Sleep Again</a></i>.  Langenkamp felt that documentary did not answer all the questions that she had.  Production took two years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I_Am_Nancy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>I Am Nancy</i> premiered at the Days of the Dead film festival.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-premiere_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-premiere-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I_Am_Nancy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Emilie Noetzel of <a href="/wiki/Dread_Central" title="Dread Central">Dread Central</a> rated it 4/5 stars and wrote, "<i>I Am Nancy</i> is a must-see for fans of the <i>A Nightmare on Elm Street</i> series and of Heather Langenkamp."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
