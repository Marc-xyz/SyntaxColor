<p><b>Donji Petrovići</b> (<a href="/wiki/Serbian_Cyrillic_alphabet" title="Serbian Cyrillic alphabet">Serbian Cyrillic</a>: <span lang="sr-Cyrl">Доњи Петровићи</span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Village" title="Village">village</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="Municipalities of Bosnia and Herzegovina">municipality</a> of <a href="/wiki/Krupa_na_Uni" title="Krupa na Uni">Krupa na Uni</a>, part of the <a href="/wiki/Republika_Srpska" title="Republika Srpska">Republika Srpska</a> entity of <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina">Bosnia and Herzegovina</a>. The village is made up of mostly ethnic Serbs. The closest city to Donji Petrovici is <a href="/wiki/Novi_Grad,_Republika_Srpska" title="Novi Grad, Republika Srpska">Novi Grad</a> (Formerly Bosanski Novi).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The population of the village has decreased drastically during the time of the <a href="/wiki/Yugoslav_Wars" title="Yugoslav Wars">Yugoslav Wars</a>. From the village, there is a great view of <a href="/wiki/Grme%C4%8D" title="Grmeč">Grmec</a>, one of Bosnia's famous mountains.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Donji_Petrovi%C4%87i&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It was previously part of the <a href="/wiki/Bosanska_Krupa" title="Bosanska Krupa">Bosanska Krupa</a> municipality.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It is approximately 20 minutes from The Una-Sana River. The village is known for their annual celebration for the village patron Saint- Pantaleon, which takes place in August of every year. Family members from all over Europe (sometimes even the world) return to Donji Petrovici for the one weekend in August for celebrations.
</p><p>Saint Pantaleon is also honoured in the village with having the church named after him—Crkva Sveti Velikom Pantelejmona.
</p>
