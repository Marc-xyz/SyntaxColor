	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">LaserScope</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:LaserScope.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="960" data-file-width="1280" decoding="async" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/4b/LaserScope.jpg/220px-LaserScope.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/4b/LaserScope.jpg/330px-LaserScope.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/4b/LaserScope.jpg/440px-LaserScope.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:LaserScope.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Konami LaserScope</div></div></div>
<p>The <b>Konami LaserScope</b> is a head-mounted <a href="/wiki/Light_gun" title="Light gun">light gun</a> used with and licensed for the <a href="/wiki/Nintendo_Entertainment_System" title="Nintendo Entertainment System">Nintendo Entertainment System</a> <a href="/wiki/Video_game_console" title="Video game console">video game console</a>. It was originally released in 1991 in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Nintendo_Entertainment_System" title="Nintendo Entertainment System">Famicom</a> under the name <b>Gun Sight</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>It was designed for the game <i><a href="/wiki/Laser_Invasion" title="Laser Invasion">Laser Invasion</a></i>, but works with any game compatible with the <a href="/wiki/NES_Zapper" title="NES Zapper">NES Zapper</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-forbes_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-forbes-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In the United States, <i>Laser Invasion</i> came with a coupon for a $5 discount for the LaserScope.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>It is voice-activated, firing a shot whenever the wearer says "fire" (although some reviewers criticized its ability to do so<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-forbes_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-forbes-2">[2]</a></sup>). The headset also includes an eyepiece with a crosshair that sits in front of the wearer's right eye.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-i-mockery_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-i-mockery-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>The LaserScope is powered through the audio port of the NES, allowing it to function as headphones for the NES.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-i-mockery_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-i-mockery-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LaserScope&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The LaserScope received negative reviews for its poor microphone technology; users complained that saying anything into the microphone would cause the LaserScope to fire, including background noise.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-forbes_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-forbes-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
