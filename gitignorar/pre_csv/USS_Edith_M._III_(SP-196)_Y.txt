<p><b>USS <i>Edith M. III</i> (SP-196)</b> was a <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Patrol_vessel" title="Patrol vessel">patrol vessel</a> in commission from 1917 to 1919.
</p><p><i>Edith M. III</i> was built by V. J. Osborn at <a href="/wiki/Croton-on-Hudson,_New_York" title="Croton-on-Hudson, New York">Croton-on-Hudson</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>, as a <a href="/wiki/Civilian" title="Civilian">civilian</a> <a href="/wiki/Motorboat" title="Motorboat">motorboat</a> of the same name in 1909. The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a> purchased her for <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> service in June 1917 and <a href="/wiki/Ship_commissioning" title="Ship commissioning">commissioned</a> her on 5 November 1917 as USS <i>Edith M. III'</i> (SP-196) with <a href="/wiki/Boatswain" title="Boatswain">Boatswain</a> A. R. Mulkins, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USNRF" title="USNRF">USNRF</a>, in command.
</p><p><i>Edith M. III</i> was assigned to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3rd_Naval_District" title="3rd Naval District">3rd Naval District</a>, where she spent the remainder of World War I carrying men and provisions around <a href="/wiki/New_York_Harbor" title="New York Harbor">New York Harbor</a>.
</p>

Hazzard of the Edith M. III</div></div></div>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ship_decommissioning" title="Ship decommissioning">Decommissioned</a> on 8 May 1919, <i>Edith M. III</i> was sold on 2 July 1919<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and entered passenger service in New York Harbor captained by Louis H. Hazzard.
</p>
