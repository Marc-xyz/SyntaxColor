<p><i><b>Stomopteryx basalis</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Moth" title="Moth">moth</a> of the family <a href="/wiki/Gelechiidae" title="Gelechiidae">Gelechiidae</a>. It was described by Staudinger in 1876. It is found in <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, and on <a href="/wiki/Corsica" title="Corsica">Corsica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">Sicily</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crete" title="Crete">Crete</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Wingspan" title="Wingspan">wingspan</a> is about 15 mm. The forewings are grey irrorated dark grey with a trapezoidal basal blotch occupying the dorsal half to one-fourth, with its upper posterior angle acutely prominent on the fold to the middle, and a subcostal streak to near one-third conﬂuent, with this at the base bronzy-ochreous. There is a small obscure spot of darker grey suffusion representing the second discal stigma and a suffused white spot on the costa at three-fourths. The hindwings are grey.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
