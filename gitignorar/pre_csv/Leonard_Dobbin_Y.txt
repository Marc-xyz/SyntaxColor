	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Leonard Dobbin</h1>

		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">For the chemist and author, see <a href="/wiki/Leonard_Dobbin_(chemist)" title="Leonard Dobbin (chemist)">Leonard Dobbin (chemist)</a>.</div>
<p>

<b>Leonard Dobbin</b> (29 September 1762<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> – 19 February 1844) was an Irish <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_(UK)" title="Liberal Party (UK)">Liberal</a>  politician who sat in the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Commons_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="House of Commons of the United Kingdom">House of Commons</a> from 1832 to 1837.
</p><p>Dobbin was a clerk of the peace in <a href="/wiki/Armagh" title="Armagh">Armagh</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/1832_United_Kingdom_general_election" title="1832 United Kingdom general election">1832 general election</a> Dobbin was elected as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a> (MP) for <a href="/wiki/Armagh_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Armagh (UK Parliament constituency)">Armagh</a>. He held the seat until 1837.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Dobbin then became <a href="/wiki/High_Sheriff_of_Armagh" title="High Sheriff of Armagh">High Sheriff of Armagh</a> in 1838.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Dobbin gave to the city an area of parkland beside the Ballinahone River in Armagh.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> This is commemorated in the song <i>Dobbin's Flowery Vale</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
