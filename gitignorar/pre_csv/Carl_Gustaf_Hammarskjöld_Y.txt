<p><b>Carl Gustaf Valdemar Hammarskjöld</b> (22 April 1865 – 27 February 1940), was a Swedish <a href="/wiki/Officer" title="Officer">military officer</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conservative" title="Conservative">conservative</a> <a href="/wiki/Politician" title="Politician">politician</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carl_Gustaf_Hammarskj%C3%B6ld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hammarskjöld was born in Väderum in <a href="/wiki/Tuna,_Vimmerby" title="Tuna, Vimmerby">Tuna</a>, Sweden and was son to <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant" title="Lieutenant">lieutenant</a> Knut Hammarskjöld (1818-1891) and Wilhelmina <i>Mimmi</i> Hammarskjöld, born Cöster (1832-1901). Carl Gustaf Hammarskjöld was brother to <a href="/wiki/Hjalmar_Hammarskj%C3%B6ld" title="Hjalmar Hammarskjöld">Hjalmar Hammarskjöld</a>, cousine to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Carl_Hammarskj%C3%B6ld&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Carl Hammarskjöld (page does not exist)">Carl Hammarskjöld</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hugo_Hammarskj%C3%B6ld" title="Hugo Hammarskjöld">Hugo Hammarskjöld</a>, also uncle to <a href="/wiki/Dag_Hammarskj%C3%B6ld" title="Dag Hammarskjöld">Dag Hammarskjöld</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carl_Gustaf_Hammarskj%C3%B6ld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hammarskjöld graduated from <a href="/wiki/Uppsala" title="Uppsala">Uppsala</a> 1883, graduated as an officer 1885, became second lieutenant in <a href="/wiki/V%C3%A4rmland" title="Värmland">Värmlands</a> Field Volunteer Corps 1885, lieutenant 1891, <a href="/wiki/General_Staff_(Sweden)" title="General Staff (Sweden)">general staff</a> 1895, captain 1899 in the <a href="/wiki/Vaxholm" title="Vaxholm">Vaxholms</a> branch. Regiment 1903, <a href="/wiki/Major" title="Major">major</a> at the general staff 1906, <a href="/wiki/Colonel" title="Colonel">colonel</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Swedish</a> army 1909. Regiment 1910, <a href="/wiki/Colonel" title="Colonel">colonel (2)</a> in the army 1913, <a href="/wiki/Colonel" title="Colonel">colonel</a> and commander of the <a href="/wiki/1st_Life_Grenadier_Regiment_(Sweden)" title="1st Life Grenadier Regiment (Sweden)">1st Life Grenadier Regiment</a> 1914, commander of the 3rd infantery brigade 1917, <a href="/wiki/Major_general" title="Major general">major general</a> and chief for the 6th Army Division in <a href="/wiki/%C3%96stersund" title="Östersund">Östersund</a> 1918.
</p><p>Hammarskjöld implemented extra repetitions at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Swedish_Army_Staff_College" title="Royal Swedish Army Staff College">Royal Swedish Army Staff College</a> from 1892 to 1893, got promoted to company officer at the <a href="/wiki/Military_Academy_Karlberg" title="Military Academy Karlberg">Royal Military Academy</a> in 1895, assisting teacher at the Royal Swedish Army Staff College 1897-98 and teacher 1899-03, general staff officer at the staff of the 2nd Army Division 1903-05, chief of staff of the 1st Army Division 1905-06, under chief <i>(Swedish: souschef)</i> at Country of Defences commission expedition 1906-10. He got promoted in 1922 to Chief of the General Staff and 1930 to general, at the same time he left all his other positions.
</p><p>Hammarskjöld represented the Swedish conservative right party from 1918 to 1920 in the lower house of the <a href="/wiki/Riksdag" title="Riksdag">Riksdag</a>) and 1925-1932 in the upper house. When <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Louis_De_Geer_(1854%E2%80%931935)" title="Louis De Geer (1854–1935)">Louis De Geer</a> formed his caretaker government Hammarskjöld got appointed to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minister_for_Defence_(Sweden)" title="Minister for Defence (Sweden)">Minister for Defence</a> on the 27 October 1920 and remained in the position until the 6 June 1921. As the Minister for Defence he wanted to increase the defense spending, opposite to the opposition in the Riksdag. Hammarskjöld was, like the most in the right party, critical to the Social Democrat's proposal to women's right to vote and the most other big reforms that the social democrats proposed. He also supported the resistance when the Riksdag put up a proposal to increase the public military service and later left the conservatives party.
</p><p>Hammarskjöld was a member in the city council of <a href="/wiki/Link%C3%B6ping" title="Linköping">Linköping</a> from 1916 to 1919.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carl_Gustaf_Hammarskj%C3%B6ld&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On the 28 November 1903 he married with Dora Almgren, daughter to director general Fredrik Almgren and Johanna <i>Hanna</i>, née Wasenius. He was the father of Carl (born 1904) and Bengt (born 1905). He's buried in <a href="/wiki/Norra_begravningsplatsen" title="Norra begravningsplatsen">Norra begravningsplatsen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Solna_Municipality" title="Solna Municipality">Solna Municipality</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
