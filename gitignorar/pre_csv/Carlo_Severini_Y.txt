<p><b>Carlo Severini</b> (10 March 1872 – 11 May 1951) was an <a href="/wiki/Italians" title="Italians">Italian</a> <a href="/wiki/Mathematician" title="Mathematician">mathematician</a>: he was born in <a href="/wiki/Arcevia" title="Arcevia">Arcevia</a> (<a href="/wiki/Province_of_Ancona" title="Province of Ancona">Province of Ancona</a>) and died in <a href="/wiki/Pesaro" title="Pesaro">Pesaro</a>. Severini, independently from <a href="/wiki/Dmitri_Egorov" title="Dmitri Egorov">Dmitri Fyodorovich Egorov</a>, proved and published earlier a proof of the theorem now known as <a href="/wiki/Egorov%27s_theorem" title="Egorov's theorem">Egorov's theorem</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carlo_Severini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He graduated in <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">Mathematics</a> from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Bologna" title="University of Bologna">University of Bologna</a> on November 30, 1897:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> the title of his "<a href="/wiki/Laurea" title="Laurea">Laurea</a>" <a href="/wiki/Thesis" title="Thesis">thesis</a> was "<i>Sulla rappresentazione analitica delle funzioni arbitrarie di variabili reali</i>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> After obtaining his <a href="/wiki/Academic_degree" title="Academic degree">degree</a>, he worked in <a href="/wiki/Bologna" title="Bologna">Bologna</a> as an assistant to the chair of <a href="/wiki/Salvatore_Pincherle" title="Salvatore Pincherle">Salvatore Pincherle</a> until 1900.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> From 1900 to 1906, he was a senior high school teacher, first teaching in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Institute_of_Technology" title="Institute of Technology">Institute of Technology</a> of <a href="/wiki/La_Spezia" title="La Spezia">La Spezia</a> and then in the <a href="/wiki/Lyceum" title="Lyceum">lyceums</a> of <a href="/wiki/Foggia" title="Foggia">Foggia</a> and of <a href="/wiki/Turin" title="Turin">Turin</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Str1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Str1-5">[5]</a></sup> then, in 1906 he became full professor of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Infinitesimal_Calculus" title="Infinitesimal Calculus">Infinitesimal Calculus</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Catania" title="University of Catania">University of Catania</a>. He worked in <a href="/wiki/Catania" title="Catania">Catania</a> until 1918, then he went to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_Genova" title="University of Genova">University of Genova</a>, where he stayed until his retirement in 1942.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Str1_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Str1-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Work">Work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carlo_Severini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He authored more than 60 papers, mainly in the areas of <a href="/wiki/Real_analysis" title="Real analysis">real analysis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Approximation_theory" title="Approximation theory">approximation theory</a> and <a href="/wiki/Partial_differential_equation" title="Partial differential equation">partial differential equations</a>, according to <a href="#CITEREFTricomi1962">Tricomi (1962)</a>. His main contributions belong to the following fields of <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">mathematics</a>:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Approximation_theory">Approximation theory</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carlo_Severini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Approximation theory">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In this field, Severini proved a generalized version of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weierstrass_approximation_theorem" title="Weierstrass approximation theorem">Weierstrass approximation theorem</a>. Precisely, he extended the original result of <a href="/wiki/Karl_Weierstrass" title="Karl Weierstrass">Karl Weierstrass</a> to the class of <a href="/wiki/Bounded_function" title="Bounded function">bounded</a> <a href="/wiki/Locally_integrable_function" title="Locally integrable function">locally integrable functions</a>, which is a class including particular <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Discontinuous_function" title="Discontinuous function">discontinuous functions</a> as members.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Measure_theory_and_integration">Measure theory and integration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carlo_Severini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Measure theory and integration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Severini proved <a href="/wiki/Egorov%27s_theorem" title="Egorov's theorem">Egorov's theorem</a> one year earlier than <a href="/wiki/Dmitri_Egorov" title="Dmitri Egorov">Dmitri Egorov</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> in the paper (<a href="#CITEREFSeverini1910">Severini 1910</a>), whose main theme is however <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sequence_(mathematics)" title="Sequence (mathematics)">sequences</a> of <a href="/wiki/Orthogonal_functions" title="Orthogonal functions">orthogonal functions</a> and their properties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Partial_differential_equations">Partial differential equations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carlo_Severini&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Partial differential equations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Severini proved an <a href="/wiki/Existence_theorem" title="Existence theorem">existence theorem</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Cauchy_problem" title="Cauchy problem">Cauchy problem</a> for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Non-linear_partial_differential_equation" title="Non-linear partial differential equation">non linear</a> <a href="/wiki/Hyperbolic_partial_differential_equation" title="Hyperbolic partial differential equation">hyperbolic partial differential equation</a> of first order
</p>

<p>According to <a href="#CITEREFStraneo1952">Straneo (1952</a>, p. 99), he worked also on the foundations of the theory of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Real_function" title="Real function">real functions</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> Severini also left an unpublished and unfinished <a href="/wiki/Treatise" title="Treatise">treatise</a> on the theory of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Real_function" title="Real function">real functions</a>, whose title was planned to be "<i>Fondamenti dell'analisi nel campo reale e i suoi sviluppi"</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p>
