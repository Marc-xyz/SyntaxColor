<p><i><b>Survivor: Kaôh Rōng</b></i> is the 32nd season of the American <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> competitive <a href="/wiki/Reality_television" title="Reality television">reality television</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Survivor_(American_TV_series)" title="Survivor (American TV series)">Survivor</a></i>. As with <i><a href="/wiki/Survivor:_Cagayan" title="Survivor: Cagayan">Survivor: Cagayan</a></i>, the season featured players initially divided into three tribes of six based on dominant attribute: "Brains" (intelligence), "Brawn" (athleticism), and "Beauty" (attractiveness and charisma).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RossIntel_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-RossIntel-1">[1]</a></sup> It is known as "The most punishing season of Survivor", due to the record breaking number of medical evacuations. The competition was filmed in <a href="/wiki/Koh_Rong" title="Koh Rong">Koh Rong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cambodia" title="Cambodia">Cambodia</a> during the spring of 2015 and aired from February 17, 2016 until May 18, 2016, when <a href="/wiki/Michele_Fitzgerald" title="Michele Fitzgerald">Michele Fitzgerald</a> was named the Sole Survivor over Aubry Bracco and Tai Trang in a vote of 5–2–0.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-location_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-location-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> 
</p><p>Though this was the 32nd season to air, it was the 31st to shoot, having been filmed before <i><a href="/wiki/Survivor:_Cambodia" title="Survivor: Cambodia">Survivor: Cambodia</a></i>, which aired first; the two seasons were filmed back-to-back in the same location.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RossIntel_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-RossIntel-1">[1]</a></sup> Like all seasons from <i><a href="/wiki/Survivor:_Fiji" title="Survivor: Fiji">Survivor: Fiji</a></i> onward, this season featured hidden immunity idols that could be played after the votes were cast but before they were read; this season also introduced the feature whereby two of these idols could be combined into one single "super idol" that could be played after the votes were read, similar to the idols found in <i><a href="/wiki/Survivor:_Panama" title="Survivor: Panama">Panama</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Survivor:_Cook_Islands" title="Survivor: Cook Islands">Cook Islands</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Survivor:_Cagayan" title="Survivor: Cagayan">Cagayan</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> This season also introduced the power to remove a member of the jury, which was given to the finalist who won the final reward challenge.
</p><p>This season was released on <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> through CBS DVD, via <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amazon.com" title="Amazon.com">Amazon.com</a>'s CreateSpace, on November 21, 2018.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Future_appearances">Future appearances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Survivor:_Ka%C3%B4h_R%C5%8Dng&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Future appearances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Caleb Reynolds, Debbie Wanner, Tai Trang, and Aubry Bracco returned for <i><a href="/wiki/Survivor:_Game_Changers" title="Survivor: Game Changers">Survivor: Game Changers</a></i>, the 34th season of the show. Bracco returned again for <i><a href="/wiki/Survivor:_Edge_of_Extinction" title="Survivor: Edge of Extinction">Survivor: Edge of Extinction</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[7]</a></sup> Michele Fitzgerald will return for <i><a href="/wiki/Survivor:_Winners_at_War" title="Survivor: Winners at War">Survivor: Winners at War</a></i>, the 40th season of the show.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Season_summary">Season summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Survivor:_Ka%C3%B4h_R%C5%8Dng&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Season summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 18 new castaways were divided into three tribes based on primary attribute: Chan Loh (Brains), To Tang (Brawn) and Gondol (Beauty). Though the Brawn lost three of the first four immunity challenges, being reduced to only Cydney, Jason, and Scot, all three survived through the tribe swap, reaching the merge alongside four Brains and four Beauties.
</p><p>Upon merging, the Brawns and Beauties initially aligned against the Brains but, put off by their male allies’ attitudes, the Brawn-Beauty women Cydney, Julia and Michele decided to team up with the Brains instead to form a new majority alliance. Though the minority alliance of Jason, Scot and Tai had two hidden immunity idols, Aubry convinced Tai to betray his allies, getting rid of one of the idols and joining the majority.
</p><p>Aubry and Cydney were the primary strategists of their alliance, and turned against each other when there were four players remaining; Aubry won the tiebreaker to join Michele and Tai in the finals. While all three were praised and criticized by the jury, the jury applauded Michele's more relaxed, yet effective, social game over Aubry's outwardly strategic game play and Tai's erratic strategy, and she was awarded the title of Sole Survivor.
</p>

<p>The eighteen new castaways, divided into three tribes of six, were given two minutes to ransack a boat filled with supplies and load them onto their rafts. Upon arriving at camp, Jason from the Brawn tribe formed multiple alliances, including one with Jennifer and Scot and another with Cydney, and targeted Alecia for her perceived weakness. Later, a bug crawled into Jennifer's ear, which caused her pain, but Scot was able to pull it out. On the Brains tribe, Debbie was unable to back up her claim about knowing how to make fire without flint, and Aubry, Liz, Neal and Peter formed an alliance against the older Debbie and Joe. Aubry had a panic attack and was consoled by her tribe mates, but they worried about her behaviour. The women of the Beauty tribe—Anna, Julia and Michele—quickly bonded. Though they considered recruiting Tai to be the fourth member of their alliance, they lost trust in him after they caught him looking for a hidden immunity idol.
</p>
<ul><li><b>Reward/immunity challenge:</b> The tribes swam out to a boat, where members dove down one at a time to retrieve paddles. They then paddled to shore where they converted their boat to a cart, then pulled it up a track. They then chose between two options: assembling a puzzle, or stacking balls while standing on a wobbly platform. The first two tribes to complete their chosen final tasks won immunity. The first tribe to win also won a fire-making kit and a tarp, while the second-place tribe won flint.</li></ul>
<p>Darnell lost the Brawn tribe's diving mask, which caused them to struggle retrieving their paddles; though they recovered during the cart-pulling portion, Alecia panicked on the puzzle and they lost the challenge. Back at camp, Alecia and Darnell were targeted due to their struggles in the challenge. Scot told Alecia not to scheme because they were voting against Darnell, but she continued to campaign for her safety, which angered him and Cydney. At Tribal Council, Darnell's pleas convinced Cydney and Jason to vote against Alecia, which caused a tie between her and Darnell. But on the revote, Darnell was unanimously voted out.

<tr class="vevent" style="text-align:center;background:#F2F2F2"><th id="ep481" rowspan="1" scope="row" style="text-align:center">481</th><td style="text-align:center">11</td><td class="summary" style="text-align:left">"It’s a ‘Me’ Game, Not a ‘We’ Game"</td><td style="text-align:center"><a class="external text" href="http://www.cbs.com/shows/survivor/episodes/215174" rel="nofollow">Recap</a></td><td style="text-align:center">2.1/8<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-April_27_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-April_27-36">[28]</a></sup></td><td style="text-align:center">April 27, 2016<span style="display:none"> (<span class="bday dtstart published updated">2016-04-27</span>)</span></td><td style="text-align:center">9.47<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-April_27_36-1"><a href="#cite_note-April_27-36">[28]</a></sup></td><td style="text-align:center">3<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-May_3,_2016_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-May_3,_2016-37">[29]</a></sup></td></tr><tr class="expand-child"><td class="description" colspan="9" style="border-bottom:solid 3px #130089">
<p>After eliminating Scot and taking out Jason's idol, the new majority alliance of Aubry, Cydney, Joe and Tai celebrated their victory, with Jason as their next target, while a confused Julia and Michele lamented being left out of the plan. The next day, Tai tried to rationalize his betrayal to Jason, explaining that he was afraid that he and Scot were going to betray him, but it failed to diffuse Jason's anger.
</p>
<ul><li><b>Reward challenge:</b> Divided into pairs and tethered together at the waist, the teams raced through an obstacle course to retrieve three rings. The first pair to throw the rings onto a hook won a helicopter ride followed by a picnic.</li></ul>
<p>The pairs were divided by random draw; Jason did not compete. Cydney and Michele narrowly defeated Joe and Tai, and brought Aubry with them on the reward. On the reward, Michele tried to reintegrate with her old alliance, and Aubry and Cydney discussed teaming up with Michele to eliminate Tai for being too likable and powerful, but only once Jason and Julia were gone. At camp, Jason and Julia plotted to recruit Cydney and Michele to eliminate Tai at the next Tribal Council.
</p>
<ul><li><b>Immunity challenge:</b> The castaways raced out to a platform that featured a series of symbols with corresponding numbers. They then raced back to their stations and used the numbers that corresponded with the symbols provided to select one of eight keys; the correct key would unlock a series of puzzle pieces with letters on them. The first castaway to spell the correct word won immunity.</li></ul>
<p>Michele beat Julia to win immunity. Back at camp, the majority alliance — Michele included — chose to target Julia over Jason for her challenge and social prowess, but Cydney and Michele later debated between voting against Julia or Tai. At Tribal Council, a nervous Tai considered playing his idol; ultimately, Tai kept it, and Cydney and Michele voted against Julia, sending her to the jury.
</p>

<tr class="vevent" style="text-align:center;background:#F2F2F2"><th id="ep482" rowspan="1" scope="row" style="text-align:center">482</th><td style="text-align:center">12</td><td class="summary" style="text-align:left">"Now’s the Time to Start Scheming"</td><td style="text-align:center"><a class="external text" href="http://www.cbs.com/shows/survivor/episodes/215208/" rel="nofollow">Recap</a></td><td style="text-align:center">2.1/8<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-May_4_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-May_4-38">[30]</a></sup></td><td style="text-align:center">May 4, 2016<span style="display:none"> (<span class="bday dtstart published updated">2016-05-04</span>)</span></td><td style="text-align:center">9.48<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-May_4_38-1"><a href="#cite_note-May_4-38">[30]</a></sup></td><td style="text-align:center">4<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-May_10,_2016_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-May_10,_2016-39">[31]</a></sup></td></tr><tr class="expand-child"><td class="description" colspan="9" style="border-bottom:solid 3px #130089">
<p>After Julia's elimination, Tai revealed his advantage to Aubry and Joe, while sole outsider Jason urged Cydney and Michele to think about taking him to the end over Aubry or Tai. Michele hoped that, by voting out her closest ally in Julia, she had gained trust within her alliance, but Tai considered blindsiding Michele for her likability.
</p>
<ul><li><b>Reward challenge:</b> Separated into groups of three, castaways raced one by one through a water obstacle course to reach a large, floating table maze. Once all members of the team were present, the castaways untied a ball and, using only themselves as balance points, manoeuvred it into the centre of the maze. First team to lodge all three balls won a picnic and a trip to a local sanctuary to visit rescued animals.</li></ul>
<p>Jason, Michele and Tai won the challenge. On the trip, Jason's positive attitude strengthened Tai's resolve to eliminate Michele. At camp, Joe's bossy demeanour irritated Cydney.
</p>
<ul><li><b>Immunity challenge:</b> The castaways held a rope attached to an unbalanced table to keep the table level, while maneuvering between two platforms retrieving and stacking blocks with letters on them; if their stack dropped, they had to restart. The first castaway to stack all eight blocks in correct order, spelling "IMMUNITY", won the challenge.</li></ul>
<p>Cydney won the challenge. Back at camp, Tai reinforced his desire to vote Michele out; though Aubry agreed, Cydney didn't appreciate Tai's brusque attitude, and she revealed his scheme to Michele. At Tribal Council, Tai claimed Michele was at the bottom of their alliance; he used his extra vote advantage to vote against Michele twice, but the rest of his alliance abandoned his plan and sent Jason to the jury.
</p>

<tr class="vevent" style="text-align:center;background:#F2F2F2"><th id="ep483" rowspan="1" scope="row" style="text-align:center">483</th><td style="text-align:center">13</td><td class="summary" style="text-align:left">"With Me or Not With Me"</td><td style="text-align:center"><a class="external text" href="http://www.cbs.com/shows/survivor/episodes/215234/" rel="nofollow">Recap</a></td><td style="text-align:center">2.1/8<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-May_11_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-May_11-40">[32]</a></sup></td><td style="text-align:center">May 11, 2016<span style="display:none"> (<span class="bday dtstart published updated">2016-05-11</span>)</span></td><td style="text-align:center">9.51<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-May_11_40-1"><a href="#cite_note-May_11-40">[32]</a></sup></td><td style="text-align:center">3<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-May_17,_2016_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-May_17,_2016-41">[33]</a></sup></td></tr><tr class="expand-child"><td class="description" colspan="9" style="border-bottom:solid 3px #130089">
<p>After Jason's elimination, Tai tried to make amends with his alliance after he was left out of the plan, and confronted Aubry about her betrayal.
</p>
<ul><li><b>Reward challenge:</b> Castaways raced to a wooden ladder in the jungle to retrieve sandbags, and then raced back to a series of targets. They then launched the sandbags into the targets; once a castaway ran out of sandbags, they had to wait until all others ran out before retrieving them. The first castaway to launch a sandbag into each of their five targets won an overnight trip to a spa, where they enjoyed a feast.</li></ul>
<p>After everyone else ran out of sandbags, Joe took his time and won the challenge with his final toss. He chose Aubry to join him on the trip; when given the chance to bring another castaway, Joe deferred to Aubry, who picked Cydney. On the trip, Joe proposed a final three alliance, and claimed that Aubry would automatically win against him and Cydney; Aubry privately realized that Cydney was a bigger threat to win than Tai, and considered betraying her. Back at camp, Michele and Tai attempted to reconnect, and the two decided to team up against Aubry. However, after returning to camp the next morning, Aubry reconciled with Tai, and the two decided to go to the end with Joe, while Cydney and Michele teamed up against Aubry and Joe. Joe began feeling ill as a result of the previous day's feasting; after his condition kept worsening, the medical team withdrew him from the game.
</p>

<tr class="vevent" style="text-align:center;background:#F2F2F2"><th id="ep484" rowspan="1" scope="row" style="text-align:center">484</th><td style="text-align:center">14</td><td class="summary" style="text-align:left">"Not Going Down Without a Fight"</td><td style="text-align:center"><a class="external text" href="http://www.cbs.com/shows/survivor/episodes/215256/" rel="nofollow">Recap</a></td><td style="text-align:center">2.1/7<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-May_18_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-May_18-42">[34]</a></sup></td><td style="text-align:center">May 18, 2016<span style="display:none"> (<span class="bday dtstart published updated">2016-05-18</span>)</span></td><td style="text-align:center">9.54<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-May_18_42-1"><a href="#cite_note-May_18-42">[34]</a></sup></td><td style="text-align:center">5<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVBytheNumbers_20160524_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-TVBytheNumbers_20160524-43">[35]</a></sup></td></tr><tr class="expand-child"><td class="description" colspan="9" style="border-bottom:solid 3px #130089">
<p>Having lost Joe and, as a result, their majority alliance, Aubry and Tai decided to target Michele for her close personal relationships with the jurors, while Cydney and Michele targeted Tai.
</p>
<ul><li><b>Reward challenge:</b> The castaways crawled through a net and slid tiles down a <a href="/wiki/Shuffleboard" title="Shuffleboard">shuffleboard</a>, then balanced the tiles on a machete through an obstacle course; once all the tiles were through, the castaway used them to solve a combination lock. The winning castaway won a protein-rich meal.</li></ul>
<p>Aubry won the challenge, and chose to share her reward with Cydney to ensure Michele lost the next immunity challenge but while they enjoyed the meal, Michele talked to Tai about breaking up Aubry and Cydney's partnership.
</p>
<ul><li><b>Immunity challenge:</b> The castaways raced between a platform in the ocean and a tower to retrieve three keys to unlock the two higher levels of the tower and then a bag of puzzle pieces. The first castaway to solve the puzzle won immunity.</li></ul>
<p>Michele came from behind to win the challenge, narrowly beating Tai. Aubry changed her target to Cydney and pled with Tai to vote with her and force a tie, while Michele and Cydney proposed getting rid of Aubry as the best likely speaker in front of the jury. At Tribal Council, Tai voted against Cydney, forcing a tie between her and Aubry. Aubry defeated Cydney in the fire-making tiebreaker to make Cydney the final member of the jury, making Aubry, Michele and Tai the game's three finalists.
</p>
<ul><li><b>Reward challenge:</b> The castaways balanced on an unstable beam while using a <a href="/wiki/Military_fork" title="Military fork">forked pole</a> to stack balls and stands. The first castaway to stack all six balls won the power to eliminate a juror.</li></ul>
<p>Michele narrowly defeated Aubry to win the challenge. She considered eliminating Joe for his unfaltering loyalty to Aubry, Neal for his loyalty to Aubry and persuasiveness, or Scot for being the victim of Tai's biggest move in the game. At Tribal Council, Michele chose to vote Neal off the jury.
</p><p>The next night, at the final Tribal Council, Tai was criticized for his erratic gameplay and misplaying his advantages, Aubry was praised for her transformation throughout the game, and Michele was reprimanded for coasting through the beginning of the game but was praised for working her way back into the majority after being left out of Scot's elimination.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Survivor:_Ka%C3%B4h_R%C5%8Dng&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Survivor:_Ka%C3%B4h_R%C5%8Dng&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Daniel Fienberg praised the season's unpredictability but panned the final Tribal Council, saying "this was one of the rare seasons that came into its last leg with no inherently 'wrong' winner;" but "a dismal jury delivered a verdict that came so close to wrong it may retroactively dampen my feelings for the season as a whole."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[39]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Andy_Dehnart" title="Andy Dehnart">Andy Dehnart</a> of <i>Reality Blurred</i> echoed this sentiment, stating that it "ended up being a strong season overall, with some exceptionally dramatic moments and interesting game play throughout" but the finale was a "disappointing three hours."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[40]</a></sup> Survivor fans agreed with the final outcome, voting for Fitzgerald over Bracco by a margin of 3% in a poll conducted by <a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a> which asked who should've won every season of Survivor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[41]</a></sup>
</p><p>Contestant Tai Trang also received positive attention for his treatment of animals throughout the game, most notably for <a href="/wiki/Domestication" title="Domestication">domesticating</a> a chicken named Mark, after series creator and executive producer <a href="/wiki/Mark_Burnett" title="Mark Burnett">Mark Burnett</a>, over the course of the game.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[42]</a></sup> Trang's actions prompted musician <a href="/wiki/Sia_(musician)" title="Sia (musician)">Sia</a> to make a surprise appearance at the live reunion show to donate $50,000 each to Trang and an animal charity of his choice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[43]</a></sup> Aubry Bracco was also a fan-favorite, and her popularity resulted in her returning to play on two future seasons-<a href="/wiki/Survivor:_Game_Changers" title="Survivor: Game Changers">Game Changers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Survivor:_Edge_of_Extinction" title="Survivor: Edge of Extinction">Edge of Extinction</a>.  Across those three seasons, she would become the eighth player to have played over one hundred days. 
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ratings">Ratings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Survivor:_Ka%C3%B4h_R%C5%8Dng&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Ratings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ratings for the premiere were down considerably from both the <i><a href="/wiki/Survivor:_Worlds_Apart" title="Survivor: Worlds Apart">Worlds Apart</a></i> season and <i><a href="/wiki/Survivor:_Cambodia" title="Survivor: Cambodia">Cambodia</a></i> season, attaining a 1.9/7 rating/share among adults 18-49; this was largely due to <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a></i> airing at the same time as the show and attaining a 2.0/7 rating.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-February_18_16-2"><a href="#cite_note-February_18-16">[8]</a></sup> Including DVR playback, the premiere was watched by 10 million viewers and got a 2.6 rating in the 18-49 demographic, slightly surpassing <i>American Idol'</i>s 9.76 million viewers and 2.5 rating.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-premierelive_63-0"><a href="#cite_note-premierelive-63">[44]</a></sup>
</p><p>It was often in the top ten most watched broadcast shows among adults 18-49, a trend that started with episode three. By April, the show became the No. 1 unscripted program among adults in all demographics, beating <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Voice_(U.S._TV_series)" title="The Voice (U.S. TV series)">The Voice</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Variety_Ratings_64-0"><a href="#cite_note-Variety_Ratings-64">[45]</a></sup>
