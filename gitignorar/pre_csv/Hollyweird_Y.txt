<p><b>Hollyweird</b> is the sixth studio album by American <a href="/wiki/Glam_metal" title="Glam metal">glam metal</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> band <a href="/wiki/Poison_(American_band)" title="Poison (American band)">Poison</a>, released on May 21, 2002 through <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cyanide_Music&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cyanide Music (page does not exist)">Cyanide Music</a> and debuted at No. 103 on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Billboard_200" title="The Billboard 200">The Billboard 200</a> chart and No. 8 on the <a href="/wiki/Independent_Albums" title="Independent Albums">Independent Albums</a> chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The album sold 11,000 copies in its first week with a No. 103 debut on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200">Billboard 200</a> chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_style">Musical style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hollyweird&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Musical style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The record brought together Poison's original line-up for the first full studio project since Flesh &amp; Blood. The result was a combination of old and new sounds, with guitarist <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/C._C._DeVille" title="C. C. DeVille">C. C. DeVille</a> frequently preferring <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Punk_rock" title="Punk rock">punk rock</a> sounds to the <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">heavy metal</a> style which had characterized Poison's previous records.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The band's new style was particularly evident in the songs "Emperor's New Clothes", "Livin' In the Now" and "Home (C.C's Story)" which featured DeVille on lead vocals.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production_and_marketing">Production and marketing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hollyweird&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Production and marketing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the face of major label offers which would have required the band to re-record old material, the band decided to release the album through their own label, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cyanide_Music&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cyanide Music (page does not exist)">Cyanide Music</a>. <i>Hollyweird</i> was recorded at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Henson_Recording_Studios" title="Henson Recording Studios">Henson Recording Studios</a>, in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hollywood,_California" title="Hollywood, California">Hollywood, California</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Rock_Central_Studios&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Rock Central Studios (page does not exist)">Rock Central Studios</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sherman_Oaks,_California" title="Sherman Oaks, California">Sherman Oaks, California</a> with producer <a href="/wiki/Thom_Panunzio" title="Thom Panunzio">Thom Panunzio</a> and engineer <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bob_koszela&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bob koszela (page does not exist)">bob koszela</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Songs">Songs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hollyweird&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Songs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The album's themes revolve almost exclusively around the trials and tribulations of success in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hollywood,_California" title="Hollywood, California">Hollywood, California</a>; a theme which had previously been explored in the band's single "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fallen_Angel_(Poison_single)" title="Fallen Angel (Poison single)">Fallen Angel</a>" from their second album <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Open_Up_And_Say..._Ahh!" title="Open Up And Say... Ahh!">Open Up And Say... Ahh!</a></i>.
</p><p>The first single released from the album was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rockstar_(Poison_single)" title="Rockstar (Poison single)">Rockstar</a> which was released on the web as a preview for the album, the 2nd single was a cover of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Who" title="The Who">The Who</a></i> song "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Squeeze_Box_(Poison_single)" title="Squeeze Box (Poison single)">Squeeze Box</a>".
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Bobby_Dall" title="Bobby Dall">Bobby Dall</a> on <a href="/wiki/The_Who" title="The Who">The Who</a> cover "Squeeze Box"
</p>
<table class="cquote pullquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">
<tbody><tr>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">“
</td>
<td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;">...Actually, that was Rikki's idea. When we came into rehearsals ... to break the ice for the new record ... before we started fightin' and arguin' about songs, we went through a list of covers and remakes. ...It was the song that stuck and I think it fits perfectly for our band. It has a great melody and a great vocal, but it's also kinda sparse and undefined ... it was kinda like an open pallet [<i>sic.</i>] for us.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>

</p>
