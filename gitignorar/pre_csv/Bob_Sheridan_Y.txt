<p><b>Bob Sheridan</b> (born 1944 in <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>) is an American <a href="/wiki/Boxing" title="Boxing">boxing</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mixed_Martial_Arts" title="Mixed Martial Arts">MMA</a> <a href="/wiki/Sports_commentator" title="Sports commentator">commentator</a>. He has broadcast over 10,000 fights on radio and television.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-keeppunching.com_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-keeppunching.com-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> 
</p><p>Sheridan attended the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Miami" title="University of Miami">University of Miami</a> on a baseball scholarship and briefly played for the Class-A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miami_Marlins_(1962%E2%80%9370)" title="Miami Marlins (1962–70)">Miami Marlins</a> after graduating in 1966. While working as a gym teacher in Miami, Sheridan paid for air time on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WDER-FM" title="WDER-FM">WDER-FM</a> on Sunday mornings. Before the end of the year, he started calling boxing matches on <a href="/wiki/WAQI" title="WAQI">WGBS</a>. He went on to announce several of <a href="/wiki/Muhammad_Ali" title="Muhammad Ali">Muhammad Ali</a>'s title fights, including the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rumble_in_the_Jungle" title="Rumble in the Jungle">Rumble in the Jungle</a> against <a href="/wiki/George_Foreman" title="George Foreman">George Foreman</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Thrilla_in_Manila" title="Thrilla in Manila">Thrilla in Manila</a> which saw Ali take on <a href="/wiki/Joe_Frazier" title="Joe Frazier">Joe Frazier</a>. He anchored telecasts for <a href="/wiki/Mike_Tyson" title="Mike Tyson">Mike Tyson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Evander_Holyfield" title="Evander Holyfield">Evander Holyfield</a>'s bouts. He was inducted into the World Boxing Hall of Fame in 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-keeppunching.com_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-keeppunching.com-3">[3]</a></sup> In 2007 he succeeded Lon McEachern as the main play-by-play commentator for the bodogFIGHT mixed martial arts promotion, calling fights alongside Paul Lazenby, Jeff Osborne and <a href="/wiki/Royce_Gracie" title="Royce Gracie">Royce Gracie</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bob_Sheridan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bob_Sheridan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sheridan was born in 1944, in Boston, Massachusetts, after his parents moved to the United States from Ireland as toddlers. His mother came from County Mayo and his father came from County Longford; both were born in 1905. Sheridan didn't come from a highly educated background. His grandfather worked as a sheet-metal worker and passed that trade onto Sheridan's father. Bob's knowledge of Irish culture was provided to him by Andrew Dougherty, his maternal grandfather.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto1-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto2_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto2-6">[6]</a></sup> Sheridan praised his family for being "wise" and "family-orientated".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto1_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-auto1-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="College_and_early_adulthood">College and early adulthood</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bob_Sheridan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: College and early adulthood">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sheridan graduated from Lexington High School and continued on to college (<a href="/wiki/University_of_Miami" title="University of Miami">University of Miami</a>) on a baseball scholarship. After graduating from college in 1966, Sheridan found a spot on a Minor League team called the Miami Marlins. By his own account, Sheridan wasn't very good at baseball and had only been brought on the team to fill a spot until a younger player arrived.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-auto-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>Soon after college Bob began his amateur career as an announcer. He hosted games for the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Miami" title="University of Miami">University of Miami</a> football team and the <a href="/wiki/Miami_Dolphins" title="Miami Dolphins">Miami Dolphins</a> as well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-7">[7]</a></sup> Sheridan also spent time during early adulthood traveling between Ireland and Boston while managing a cattle farm in Ireland. At the same time, Sheridan managed his broadcasting career and a part-time job as a rodeo bull rider. In 1982 Sheridan left the cattle business, along with the rodeo bull-riding business, and moved back to the States to focus on his career as an announcer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto2_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-auto2-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto3_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto3-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Broadcasting">Broadcasting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bob_Sheridan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Broadcasting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After graduating from college Sheridan worked as a <a href="/wiki/Physical_education" title="Physical education">P.E.</a> teacher in Dade County. He also paid for airtime on a local broadcasting station (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WDER-FM" title="WDER-FM">WDER-FM</a>) and, thus, began his career as a broadcaster. At the time boxing was just gaining traction in his area and Sheridan made a name for himself early. He was able to earn himself the rights to broadcast games for two notable Florida football teams; <a href="/wiki/University_of_Miami" title="University of Miami">University of Miami</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Miami_Dolphins" title="Miami Dolphins">Miami Dolphins</a>. He gained recognition from promoter Chris Dundee and eventually went on to broadcast boxing bouts for Dundee's weekly cards. While working with Dundee, Sheridan got a chance to announce his first title fight which was between WBA heavyweight fighters Jerry Quarry and Jimmy Ellis in 1968.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>Not long after he started calling fights for Dundee, Sheridan got a shot at sparking his career. In 1972, when he was only about 29 years old, Sheridan was offered an opportunity to work with Video Techniques broadcasting a world title fight between Frankie Otero and Ken Buchanan for the Lightweight Championship. His talent hosting that fight earned him a position with famed fight promoter <a href="/wiki/Don_King_(boxing_promoter)" title="Don King (boxing promoter)">Don King</a>. A couple years later Bob was hosting one of the most well-known fights in boxing history: <i><a href="/wiki/The_Rumble_in_the_Jungle" title="The Rumble in the Jungle">The Rumble in the Jungle</a></i>, the heavyweight title bout between former champion/contender <a href="/wiki/Muhammad_Ali" title="Muhammad Ali">Muhammad Ali</a>  and  Heavyweight Champion <a href="/wiki/George_Foreman" title="George Foreman">George Foreman</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-auto-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto4_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto4-10">[10]</a></sup> The fight, in Zaire, Africa, was telecast to an audience of over 1 billion people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-robertdailys.blogspot.com_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-robertdailys.blogspot.com-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>Sheridan was an announcer at several notable fights throughout boxing's history, some of which include the <i><a href="/wiki/Thrilla_in_Manila" title="Thrilla in Manila">Thrilla in Manila</a></i> between Joe Frazier and Ali (which was the first heavyweight bout hosted in the Philippines), the first heavyweight fight hosted in Japan which was between <a href="/wiki/Mike_Tyson" title="Mike Tyson">Mike Tyson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Buster_Douglas" title="Buster Douglas">Buster Douglas</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-robertdailys.blogspot.com_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-robertdailys.blogspot.com-11">[11]</a></sup> as well as <a href="/wiki/Evander_Holyfield_vs._Mike_Tyson_II" title="Evander Holyfield vs. Mike Tyson II">the rematch between Mike Tyson and Evander Holyfield</a> where Holyfield's ear was bitten off.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Health_issues">Health issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bob_Sheridan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Health issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Since 1973, Sheridan has hosted every title fight for promoter <a href="/wiki/Don_King_(boxing_promoter)" title="Don King (boxing promoter)">Don King</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-auto-1">[1]</a></sup> Staying dedicated to his duties, Bob agreed to announce the aforementioned 1997 rematch between Holyfield and Tyson, set to take place in Las Vegas, Nevada. By that time in his career Sheridan was quite overweight (around 320 pounds) and had already had two heart attacks. After indulging on a large portion of <a href="/wiki/Red_meat" title="Red meat">red meat</a> the night prior, Sheridan experienced his third heart attack while in Vegas. Under the care of Dr. Ram K. Singh, Sheridan was able to make it through the incident. However, his dedication to the sport caused him to argue strongly for his release from the hospital so he would be able to broadcast the nights fight. After signing himself out (specifically signing that he was leaving against the recommendation of hospital staff), Sheridan returned to the arena in order to announce the historic fight.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto4_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-auto4-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>Sheridan's perceived the errors and frustration experienced by Tyson that eventually led to his Biting off Holyfield's ear. The commotion and excitement that proceeded from that event led to Sheridan revisiting the hospital later that night. Although he claimed he felt fine, he was later told that he would  need quadruple bypass surgery. The next morning however, the medical team opted for two angioplasties instead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto4_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-auto4-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>Despite almost losing his life in order to announce that fight, Sheridan claims he wouldn't reverse his decision to attend. Sheridan submits that he "has never worked a day in his life", adhering to the words of his father which read, "If you love what you do, you'll never work a day in your life."  Sheridan told Dr. Ram K. Singh, that night, that If he died, at least he'd be doing what he loved.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-espn.com_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-espn.com-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p><p>In addition to his heart trouble in the late-1990s, by 2010 Sheridan had already had four heart attacks and twelve angioplasties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto3_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-auto3-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2010, the bank foreclosed on his house, after which Sheridan ended up in an induced coma for 30 days. Even after waking from the coma Sheridan had to deal with life as a paraplegic for some time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_accomplishments">Awards and accomplishments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bob_Sheridan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Awards and accomplishments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Later in his career, Sheridan took his turn as a banker after receiving an offer to work at Olympic Bank. Sheridan was thought to be a valuable associate due to his charisma and his connections with athletes and their agents. As this was Olympic Bank's primary target, Sheridan could bring in valuable customers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>World Boxing Hall of Fame (2004)</li>
<li>Boxing Writers' Association of America Sam Taub Award</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Nevada_Boxing_Hall_of_Fame" title="Nevada Boxing Hall of Fame">Nevada Boxing Hall of Fame</a> (Dec. 19, 2012)</li>
<li>Voted <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Irish_America_Magazine" title="Irish America Magazine">Irish America Magazine</a>'s 50 Most Influential Irish-Americans</li>
<li>Over 10,000 career broadcast TV and radio fights</li>
