<p><b>Lautaro Murúa</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation: </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">[lauˈtaɾo muˈɾu.a]</a></span>; 29 December 1926 in <a href="/wiki/Tacna" title="Tacna">Tacna</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a> – 3 December 1995 in <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chilean</a>-<a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentine</a> <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Film_director" title="Film director">film director</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Screenwriter" title="Screenwriter">screenwriter</a>. He is one of the best<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> known actors in the <a href="/wiki/Cinema_of_Argentina" title="Cinema of Argentina">cinema of Argentina</a>.
</p><p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a>, Murúa moved to <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a> at the beginning of the fifties. He studied architecture and fine arts before entering the film industry. He worked primarily as an actor and appeared in over 80 films between 1949 and his death in 1995 although he also directed a handful of important films such as <i><a href="/wiki/Shunko" title="Shunko">Shunko</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alias_Gardelito" title="Alias Gardelito">Alias Gardelito</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/La_Raulito" title="La Raulito">La Raulito</a></i>, all with stories usually revolving around <a href="/wiki/Social" title="Social">social</a> topics.
</p><p>As an actor, Murúa participated in the 1960s film industry revival, acting in the movies of <a href="/wiki/Leopoldo_Torre_Nilsson" title="Leopoldo Torre Nilsson">Leopoldo Torre Nilsson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rodolfo_Kuhn" title="Rodolfo Kuhn">Rodolfo Kuhn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manuel_Ant%C3%ADn" title="Manuel Antín">Manuel Antín</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Jos%C3%A9_Kohon" title="David José Kohon">David José Kohon</a>. 
</p><p>He left to live in <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> for political reasons in the 1970s, and then he returned to <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a> to film several movies until sickness set in and he died in <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> in 1995.
</p>
