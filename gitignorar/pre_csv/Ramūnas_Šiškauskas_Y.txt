<p><b>Ramūnas Šiškauskas</b> (<small></small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Lithuanian" title="Help:IPA/Lithuanian">[rɐˈmûːnɐs ɕɪʃˈkɐ̂ˑʊskɐs]</a></span> <span class="nowrap" style="font-size:85%">(<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap;margin-right:.25em;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ramunas_Siskauskas.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" data-file-height="20" data-file-width="20" decoding="async" height="11" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" width="11"/></a></span><a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/1b/Ramunas_Siskauskas.ogg" title="Ramunas Siskauskas.ogg">listen</a></span></span>)</span>, born September 10, 1978) is a former Lithuanian professional <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a> player and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Basketball_coach" title="Basketball coach">basketball coach</a>. At a listed height of 6'6" (1.98 m) tall,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> 
he could play at both the <a href="/wiki/Shooting_guard" title="Shooting guard">shooting guard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Small_forward" title="Small forward">small forward</a> positions. His individual accolades as a player include a <a href="/wiki/EuroLeague_MVP" title="EuroLeague MVP">EuroLeague MVP</a> award, four <a href="/wiki/All-EuroLeague_Team" title="All-EuroLeague Team">All-EuroLeague Team</a> selections, as well as an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EuroBasket_All-Tournament_Team" title="EuroBasket All-Tournament Team">All-EuroBasket Team</a> designation. On May 16, 2014, Šiškauskas was named a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EuroLeague_Basketball_Legend" title="EuroLeague Basketball Legend">EuroLeague Basketball Legend</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>During his playing career, Šiškauskas won two <a href="/wiki/EuroLeague" title="EuroLeague">EuroLeague</a> titles, one each with <a href="/wiki/Panathinaikos_B.C." title="Panathinaikos B.C.">Panathinaikos Athens</a> and <a href="/wiki/PBC_CSKA_Moscow" title="PBC CSKA Moscow">CSKA Moscow</a>, in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007_EuroLeague_Final_Four" title="2007 EuroLeague Final Four">2007</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2008_EuroLeague_Final_Four" title="2008 EuroLeague Final Four">2008</a>, and reached two more <a href="/wiki/EuroLeague_Finals" title="EuroLeague Finals">EuroLeague Finals</a> with CSKA, in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009_EuroLeague_Final_Four" title="2009 EuroLeague Final Four">2009</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2012_EuroLeague_Final_Four" title="2012 EuroLeague Final Four">2012</a>. He was a member of the senior <a href="/wiki/Lithuania_men%27s_national_basketball_team" title="Lithuania men's national basketball team">Lithuanian national team</a> that won the gold medal at the <a href="/wiki/EuroBasket_2003" title="EuroBasket 2003">EuroBasket 2003</a>. As a member of Lithuania's national team, he also won the bronze medal at the <a href="/wiki/Basketball_at_the_2000_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_tournament" title="Basketball at the 2000 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament">2000 Summer Olympics</a>, and the bronze medal at the <a href="/wiki/EuroBasket_2007" title="EuroBasket 2007">EuroBasket 2007</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_playing_career">Professional playing career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ram%C5%ABnas_%C5%A0i%C5%A1kauskas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Professional playing career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lithuania">Lithuania</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ram%C5%ABnas_%C5%A0i%C5%A1kauskas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Lithuania">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Šiškauskas made his pro debut with <a href="/wiki/BC_Sakalai" title="BC Sakalai">Sakalai</a> in 1996. He played two seasons with the club, averaging 11.3 points, on 60 percent shooting.
</p><p>In 1998, Šiškauskas signed with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BC_Lietuvos_rytas" title="BC Lietuvos rytas">Lietuvos Rytas</a> of <a href="/wiki/Vilnius" title="Vilnius">Vilnius</a>. He played there until the 2003–04 season, and led the team to <a href="/wiki/Lietuvos_krep%C5%A1inio_lyga" title="Lietuvos krepšinio lyga">Lithuanian League</a> titles in 2000 and 2002, and a <a href="/wiki/North_European_Basketball_League" title="North European Basketball League">Northern Europe League</a> title in 2002. In 2002–03, he had his most successful season, averaging 16.4 points, on 68 percent shooting, and 3.4 rebounds.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Italy">Italy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ram%C5%ABnas_%C5%A0i%C5%A1kauskas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Italy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Šiškauskas joined <a href="/wiki/Pallacanestro_Treviso" title="Pallacanestro Treviso">Benetton Treviso</a> in 2004. He led them to an <a href="/wiki/Lega_Basket_Serie_A" title="Lega Basket Serie A">Italian League</a> title in 2006, where he was named <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lega_Basket_Serie_A_Finals_MVP" title="Lega Basket Serie A Finals MVP">the Finals MVP</a>, and to an <a href="/wiki/Italian_Basketball_Cup" title="Italian Basketball Cup">Italian Cup</a> title in 2005. He played in 32 <a href="/wiki/EuroLeague" title="EuroLeague">EuroLeague</a> games over two seasons with Benetton, and averaged 12.3 points, on 60 percent shooting.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Greece">Greece</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ram%C5%ABnas_%C5%A0i%C5%A1kauskas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Greece">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2006, Šiškauskas signed with <a href="/wiki/Panathinaikos_B.C." title="Panathinaikos B.C.">Panathinaikos</a>, and he helped them win the <a href="/wiki/EuroLeague" title="EuroLeague">EuroLeague</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007_EuroLeague_Final_Four" title="2007 EuroLeague Final Four">2007</a>. He averaged 11 points, on 51 percent shooting for the championship team. With Panathinaikos, he also won a <a href="/wiki/Greek_Basket_League" title="Greek Basket League">Greek League</a> title, and the <a href="/wiki/Greek_Basketball_Cup" title="Greek Basketball Cup">Greek Cup</a>, in 2007.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Russia">Russia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ram%C5%ABnas_%C5%A0i%C5%A1kauskas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Russia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Šiškauskas joined <a href="/wiki/PBC_CSKA_Moscow" title="PBC CSKA Moscow">CSKA Moscow</a> in 2007. He helped to lead them to a <a href="/wiki/EuroLeague" title="EuroLeague">EuroLeague</a> title in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2008_EuroLeague_Final_Four" title="2008 EuroLeague Final Four">2008</a>, and helped them reach the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EuroLeague_Final" title="EuroLeague Final">EuroLeague Final</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009_EuroLeague_Final_Four" title="2009 EuroLeague Final Four">2009</a>. Which, however, CSKA lost to Šiškauskas' former team, Panathinaikos. On May 1, 2009, he scored a career-high 29 points against <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona_B%C3%A0squet" title="FC Barcelona Bàsquet">Barcelona</a>. Šiškauskas also won five <a href="/wiki/Russian_Professional_Basketball_Championship" title="Russian Professional Basketball Championship">Russian Championship</a> titles (2008, 2009, 2010, 2011, 2012).
</p><p>On May 13, 2012, Šiškauskas missed two vital free-throws, with 10 seconds remaining in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2012_EuroLeague_Final_Four" title="2012 EuroLeague Final Four">CSKA's EuroLeague Finals game</a> against <a href="/wiki/Olympiacos_B.C." title="Olympiacos B.C.">Olympiacos Piraeus</a>, which proved crucial in CSKA's 62 to 61 loss. He also missed the last second three-point shot in CSKA's EuroLeague Finals game against <a href="/wiki/Panathinaikos_B.C." title="Panathinaikos B.C.">Panathinaikos</a>, on May 3, 2009, which meant that CSKA lost that final, by a score of 73 to 71.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Only one week after the 2012 EuroLeague Finals game, on May 21, 2012, Šiškauskas announced his retirement from playing professional basketball.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> On May 16, 2014, Šiškauskas was named a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EuroLeague_Basketball_Legend" title="EuroLeague Basketball Legend">EuroLeague Basketball Legend</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_team_career">National team career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ram%C5%ABnas_%C5%A0i%C5%A1kauskas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: National team career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lithuanian_junior_national_team">Lithuanian junior national team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ram%C5%ABnas_%C5%A0i%C5%A1kauskas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Lithuanian junior national team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Šiškauskas played with the junior national teams of Lithuania. With Lithuania's junior national team, he played at the <a href="/wiki/1998_FIBA_Europe_Under-20_Championship" title="1998 FIBA Europe Under-20 Championship">1998 FIBA Europe Under-20 Championship</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lithuanian_senior_national_team">Lithuanian senior national team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ram%C5%ABnas_%C5%A0i%C5%A1kauskas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Lithuanian senior national team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Šiškauskas was a member of the senior men's <a href="/wiki/Lithuania_men%27s_national_basketball_team" title="Lithuania men's national basketball team">Lithuanian national basketball teams</a> that won the bronze medal at the <a href="/wiki/Basketball_at_the_2000_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_tournament" title="Basketball at the 2000 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament">2000 Summer Olympics</a>, and the bronze medal at the <a href="/wiki/EuroBasket_2007" title="EuroBasket 2007">EuroBasket 2007</a>. He was also a member of the Lithuanian team that won the gold medal at the <a href="/wiki/EuroBasket_2003" title="EuroBasket 2003">EuroBasket 2003</a>. He also played at the <a href="/wiki/EuroBasket_2001" title="EuroBasket 2001">EuroBasket 2001</a>, at the <a href="/wiki/Basketball_at_the_2004_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_tournament" title="Basketball at the 2004 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament">2004 Summer Olympics</a>, at the <a href="/wiki/EuroBasket_2005" title="EuroBasket 2005">EuroBasket 2005</a>, and at the <a href="/wiki/Basketball_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_tournament" title="Basketball at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament">2008 Summer Olympics</a>.
</p><p>After the 2008 Summer Olympics, he officially announced that he was stepping down from the Lithuanian National Team, as a player.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Player_profile">Player profile</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ram%C5%ABnas_%C5%A0i%C5%A1kauskas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Player profile">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Šiškauskas was a physical <a href="/wiki/Small_forward" title="Small forward">small forward</a> (198 cm or 6'6" tall), who could also easily play, both on offense and defense, at the both <a href="/wiki/Point_guard" title="Point guard">point guard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shooting_guard" title="Shooting guard">shooting guard</a> positions, on the wing, and also in the paint. His excellent leaping ability, and his quickness, made him a surprising <a href="/wiki/Block_(basketball)" title="Block (basketball)">shot blocker</a>, and one of the best one-on-one players and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swingmen" title="Swingmen">swingmen</a> in Europe. He was considered the best defensive player of the senior <a href="/wiki/Lithuania_men%27s_national_basketball_team" title="Lithuania men's national basketball team">Lithuanian national basketball team</a>. He led the 2003–04 season of the <a href="/wiki/Lietuvos_krep%C5%A1inio_lyga" title="Lietuvos krepšinio lyga">Lithuanian League</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Free_throw" title="Free throw">free throw</a> percentage (90.6%).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coaching_career">Coaching career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ram%C5%ABnas_%C5%A0i%C5%A1kauskas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Coaching career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Šiškauskas began his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Basketball_coach" title="Basketball coach">basketball coaching</a> career in 2017, when he became an assistant coach of the senior men's <a href="/wiki/Lithuania_men%27s_national_basketball_team" title="Lithuania men's national basketball team">Lithuanian national basketball team</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
