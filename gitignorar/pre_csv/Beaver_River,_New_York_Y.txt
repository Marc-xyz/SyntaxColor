<p><b>Beaver River</b> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hamlet_(New_York)" title="Hamlet (New York)">hamlet</a> that is six tenths of a mile square, at the east end of <a href="/wiki/Stillwater_Reservoir" title="Stillwater Reservoir">Stillwater Reservoir</a>, in the town of <a href="/wiki/Webb,_New_York" title="Webb, New York">Webb</a> in <a href="/wiki/Herkimer_County,_New_York" title="Herkimer County, New York">Herkimer County</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>, United States. The hamlet is surrounded by the <a href="/wiki/Adirondack_Park" title="Adirondack Park">Adirondack Park</a>. The hamlet has a year-round population of eight, that increases during the summer as many people have camps in this wilderness area. There are 125 private properties, and three commercial businesses.  No roads lead to the hamlet; it is accessible only by hiking, rail car or boat in the summer and by snowmobile, snowshoes or cross country skis in the winter.  There is no electrical service. The town is named for the <a href="/wiki/Beaver_River_(New_York)" title="Beaver River (New York)">Beaver River</a>, which was impounded to form the Stillwater Reservoir. The Beaver River is a west-flowing tributary of the <a href="/wiki/Black_River_(New_York)" title="Black River (New York)">Black River</a> and part of the <a href="/wiki/Lake_Ontario" title="Lake Ontario">Lake Ontario</a> watershed. The <a href="/wiki/New_York_Central_Railroad" title="New York Central Railroad">New York Central Railroad</a> right of way, on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> passes through the hamlet; an existing bunkhouse is a part of the historic property.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beaver_River,_New_York&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Beaver River was created in 1893 by railroad builder Dr. <a href="/wiki/William_Seward_Webb" title="William Seward Webb">William Seward Webb</a> as a stop on his newly completed <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Adirondack_and_St._Lawrence_Railway&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Adirondack and St. Lawrence Railway (page does not exist)">Adirondack and St. Lawrence Railway</a>, which became the <a href="/wiki/Mohawk_and_Malone_Railway" title="Mohawk and Malone Railway">Mohawk and Malone Railway</a> and ultimately, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_Central" title="New York Central">New York Central</a>.  Webb owned some 200,000 acres (810 km<sup>2</sup>) in the area, which was popular with hunters and fishermen.  Extensive logging began at the turn of the century.  In 1902, the Norridgewock Hotel was built, which became the social hub of the community.  A school house was built in 1912 by early settler Ben Bullock; the school continued to operate until 1965.
</p>
