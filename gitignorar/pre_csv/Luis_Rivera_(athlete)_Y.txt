<p><b>Luis Alberto Rivera Morales</b> (born 21 June 1987, in <a href="/wiki/Agua_Prieta" title="Agua Prieta">Agua Prieta</a>) is a Mexican <a href="/wiki/Long_jump" title="Long jump">long jumper</a>. His personal best is 8.46 metres, achieved in July 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>He attended <a href="/wiki/Central_Arizona_College" title="Central Arizona College">Central Arizona College</a>, where he learned English. After finishing his General Studies degree, he attended the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Arizona" title="University of Arizona">University of Arizona</a>, where he was named all-American in long jump.  He finished his Bachelor in Science in Industrial Engineering.
</p><p>Currently, he is studying his PhD in Systems and Industrial Engineering.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (December 2016)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>In 2013, Luis Rivera competed at the <a href="/wiki/2013_Summer_Universiade" title="2013 Summer Universiade">World University Games</a> held in Kazan, Russia, where he jumped 8.46 meters to take the gold medal in a head to head battle with Russian national favorite <a href="/wiki/Aleksandr_Menkov" title="Aleksandr Menkov">Aleksandr Menkov</a>, who finished 4 cm behind. Rivera's winning jump became the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mexican_records_in_athletics" title="Mexican records in athletics">Mexican national record</a>, and the University Games record.
</p><p>Just over a month later, at the <a href="/wiki/2013_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="2013 World Championships in Athletics">World Championships</a> in Moscow, Russia, Menkov was again in the competition. This time Menkov took the gold with the Russian National record of 8.56 m while Rivera took the bronze medal with a jump of 8.27 m.
</p><p>His brother, <a href="/wiki/Edgar_Rivera" title="Edgar Rivera">Edgar Rivera</a>, competes in the high jump. He has participated in various international competitions, including the World Universiade in Shenzen, China, Kazan, Russia. He also participated in two world championships, in Daegu, Korea 2011 and Moscow, Russia 2013.
</p>
