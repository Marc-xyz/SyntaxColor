	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Gerald Rose</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Gerald Hembdon Seymour Rose</b> (born 1935) is a <a href="/wiki/British_people" title="British people">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Illustrator" title="Illustrator">illustrator</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Children%27s_books" title="Children's books">children's books</a>. He won the 1960 <a href="/wiki/Kate_Greenaway_Medal" title="Kate Greenaway Medal">Kate Greenaway Medal</a> from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CILIP" title="CILIP">Library Association</a>, recognising the year's best children's book illustration by a <a href="/wiki/British_subject" title="British subject">British subject</a>. The book was <i>Old Winkle and the Seagulls</i>, written by his wife Elizabeth Rose and published by <a href="/wiki/Faber_and_Faber" title="Faber and Faber">Faber and Faber</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-medal1960_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-medal1960-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Rose was born in <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>. During the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a> his father became a prisoner of war and his sister and mother were interned at Stanley civilian detention camp.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bloomsbury_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-bloomsbury-2">[2]</a></sup>
He studied at Lowestoft School of Art and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Royal_Academy" title="Royal Academy">Royal Academy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bloomsbury_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-bloomsbury-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Elizabeth (n√©e Elizabeth Jane Pretty) and Gerald Rose began to produce children's books, she writing and he illustrating. <i>How St. Francis Tamed the Wolf</i> was published by Faber in 1958. Gerald was a commended runner-up for the Greenaway Medal next year, when the librarians introduced the distinction, recognising <i>Wuffles Goes to Town</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ccsu_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-ccsu-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HC_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-HC-4">[a]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1979 Gerald Rose won the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Premio_Critici_in_Erba&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Premio Critici in Erba (page does not exist)">Premio Critici in Erba</a> in Italy for <i>"Ahhh!" said Stork</i> (Faber, 1977).
</p><p>Rose lives in <a href="/wiki/Hove" title="Hove">Hove</a>, East Sussex.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bloomsbury_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-bloomsbury-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (May 2013)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
