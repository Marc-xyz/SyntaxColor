<p><i><b>The Octonauts</b></i> is a British <a href="/wiki/Children%27s_television_series" title="Children's television series">children's television series</a>, produced by <a href="/wiki/Silvergate_Media" title="Silvergate Media">Silvergate Media</a> for the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> channel <a href="/wiki/CBeebies" title="CBeebies">CBeebies</a>. 
</p><p>The series is animated in Ireland by <a href="/wiki/Brown_Bag_Films" title="Brown Bag Films">Brown Bag Films</a> and uses British voice actors. The TV series is based on American-Canadian children's books written by Vicki Wong and Michael C. Murphy of <a href="/wiki/Meomi_Design" title="Meomi Design">Meomi Design</a> Inc.
</p><p>The show follows an underwater exploring crew made up of stylized <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anthropomorphic" title="Anthropomorphic">anthropomorphic</a> animals, a team of eight adventurers who live in an undersea base, the Octopod, from which they go on undersea adventures with the help of a fleet of aquatic vehicles. Although its technology is fictional, the exotic creatures and locations that the crew encounter are based on real marine animals in their natural habitats. 
</p><p><i>The Octonauts</i> has been renewed for a 5th series that will "introduce new environments, [...] characters and a new fleet of vehicles" to be released sometime in 2020.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The subject matter is reminiscent of <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek" title="Star Trek">Star Trek</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Thunderbirds_(TV_series)" title="Thunderbirds (TV series)">Thunderbirds</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc-mueller_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-mueller-2">[2]</a></sup> blended with <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Cousteau" title="Jacques Cousteau">Jacques Cousteau</a>. 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Books">Books</span></h2>
<p>The original books were first published in the US by Immedium in 2006, and were republished in the UK by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harper_Collins" title="Harper Collins">Harper Collins</a> in 2009. Six titles by Meomi have been published:
</p>
<ul><li><i>The Octonauts &amp; the Only Lonely Monster</i>, about an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Architeuthis" title="Architeuthis">Architeuthis</a> (Giant Squid).</li>
<li><i>The Octonauts &amp; the Sea of Shade</i>, about a world where everyone's shadows have gone missing.</li>
<li><i>The Octonauts &amp; the Frown Fish</i>, about a glum-looking <a href="/wiki/Catfish" title="Catfish">catfish</a>.</li>
<li><i>The Octonauts &amp; the Great Ghost Reef</i>, about <a href="/wiki/Coral_bleaching" title="Coral bleaching">coral bleaching</a>.</li>
<li><i>The Octonauts Explore the Great Big Ocean</i>, about the search for Tunip the Vegimal's home.</li>
<li><i>The Octonauts &amp; the Growing Goldfish</i>, about Dunkie, a giant goldfish who won't stop growing.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="TV_series">TV series</span></h2>
<p>In 2010, <i>The Octonauts</i> was adapted into a 52 episode <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CGI_animation" title="CGI animation">CGI animated</a> television series co-produced by <a href="/wiki/Chorion_(company)" title="Chorion (company)">Chorion</a> and Brown Bag Films. It first aired in the UK on 4 October 2010 on CBeebies, a <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> television channel for the under 7's. The first series ended in February 2011, but continued to be aired as repeats. A second series of 22 episodes commenced in November 2012.
</p><p><i>The Octonauts</i> had its US premiere on the <a href="/wiki/Disney_Channel" title="Disney Channel">Disney Channel</a> in January 2012, however many of the characters' voices were redubbed to give them American accents—hispanic in the case of Peso and Pinto—although Captain Barnacles, Kwazii and Prof. Inkling retained their original British voices.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It has also been shown in Ireland (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RT%C3%89_Two" title="RTÉ Two">RTÉ Two</a>), Canada (<a href="/wiki/Treehouse_TV" title="Treehouse TV">Treehouse TV</a>), and Australia (<a href="/wiki/ABC_Television" title="ABC Television">ABC Television</a>). The show has been translated and shown in French (<a href="/wiki/TF1" title="TF1">TF1</a>), Spanish and Portuguese (<a href="/wiki/Discovery_Kids_(Latin_America)" title="Discovery Kids (Latin America)">Discovery Kids Latin America</a>), Finnish (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/YLE_TV2" title="YLE TV2">YLE TV2</a>), Welsh (<a href="/wiki/S4C" title="S4C">S4C</a>) and Slovakian (<a href="/wiki/Dvojka" title="Dvojka">STV 2</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>The series was made with the help of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marine_biologist" title="Marine biologist">marine biologists</a> Dr. Lara A. Ferry-Graham and Dr. Michael H. Graham, who had previously worked on <i><a href="/wiki/Finding_Nemo" title="Finding Nemo">Finding Nemo</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc-mueller_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-mueller-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>The TV show is episodic, with each 11 minute episode seeing them encountering an unusual but real sea creature as they explore strange underwater worlds. Often they must discover a vital biological or behavioral fact about that creature to rescue it or themselves from danger. The stories usually feature three main characters: Captain Barnacles, Kwazii and Peso, with the other five Octonauts acting in supporting roles.
</p><p>In the TV series they use five sea-creature-inspired underwater vehicles called GUP-A to GUP-E, the pedal driven GUP-F, driven by Captain Barnacles and Kwazii, which appears in Episode 10 of Series 1 (<i>The Speedy Sailfish</i>), the narwhal-shaped submarine Gup-S that can split so one half goes over land, the train-shaped vegimal-driven Gup-V, and the powerful and sturdy tracked GUP-X, first appearing in Episode 52 of Series 1 (<i>The Great Christmas Rescue</i>).
</p><p>The opening theme tune finishes with the chant "Explore! - Rescue! - Protect!", the Octonauts’ motto.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="TV_spin-off_books">TV spin-off books</span></h3>
<p>A series of shorter books based on individual episodes from the TV series is being published by <a href="/wiki/Simon_%26_Schuster" title="Simon &amp; Schuster">Simon &amp; Schuster</a>.
These titles can be distinguished by the "As seen on TV" label on the covers and are not written or illustrated by Meomi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The books have the same titles as the episodes they are based on.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span></h2>
<p>The TV series was well received when shown on CBeebies in the UK and by <a href="/wiki/Australian_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Australian Broadcasting Corporation">ABC</a> in Australia. According to the authors' website, it was the No. 1 pre-school age show in the UK, and the first pre-school show to be ranked in the top ten by older audiences (aged 3–4 and up).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The show was the top-ranked ABC programme for 5-12 year olds in January 2011 in Australia, with the ABC calling its first month an "unprecedented success online and on-air".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
It has been rated 7.4 out of 10 on <a href="/wiki/IMDb" title="IMDb">IMDb</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2013, Octonauts was nominated for (but did not win) two awards: an <a href="/wiki/International_Emmy_Award" title="International Emmy Award">International Emmy Award</a> in the <i>"Kids—Preschool"</i> category<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
and an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Irish_Film_and_Television_Award" title="Irish Film and Television Award">Irish Film and Television Award</a> in the <i>"Best Children's/Youth Programme"</i> category.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
The Octonauts DVD boxed set was 4th best-selling Children's TV DVD at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amazon.com" title="Amazon.com">Amazon</a> UK as of 7 April 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Recurrent_minor_characters">Recurrent minor characters</span></h3>
<ul><li><b>Calico Jack</b>, a pirate cat and Kwazii's grandfather. His exploits are frequently talked about but he is assumed long-dead until he finally makes an appearance in the <a href="#Special_episodes">Amazon Adventure special</a>. In real life, the pirate <a href="/wiki/Calico_Jack" title="Calico Jack">Calico Jack</a> was the creator of the <a href="/wiki/Jolly_Roger" title="Jolly Roger">Jolly Roger</a>, though the Octonauts character's name might also be an allusion to the <a href="/wiki/Calico_cat" title="Calico cat">Calico cat</a>.</li>
<li><b>Pinto</b>, Peso's little brother, a young penguin who visits in <a href="#Series_1">episode 24</a>, <a href="#Series_1">episode 32</a> and the <a href="#Special_episodes">Great Penguin Race special</a>. He initially aspires to be a pirate like Kwazii but later decides he'd rather be a medic like Peso.</li>
<li><b>Sandy</b> the sea <a href="/wiki/Turtle" title="Turtle">turtle</a>, a good friend of Tweak's. She swims long distances and makes appointments to meet the Octonauts in several episodes.</li>
<li><b>Boris</b> the <a href="/wiki/Narwhal" title="Narwhal">narwhal</a>, a friend of Captain Barnacle's from when he was young. Boris remains in the <a href="/wiki/Arctic" title="Arctic">Arctic</a> but the Octonauts meet him in several episodes.</li>
<li><b>Pearl</b> the <a href="/wiki/Sea_otter" title="Sea otter">sea otter</a> and Shellington's married sister who recently had a baby named <b>Periwinkle</b>, first seen in the episode The Urchin Invasion.</li>
<li><b>Bianca</b> the <a href="/wiki/Polar_bear" title="Polar bear">polar bear</a> is the twin sister of Captain Barnacles, she and her cubs appeared in the <a href="#Special_episodes">Great Arctic Adventure</a>.</li>
<li><b>Orson</b> and <b>Ursa</b>, Bianca's cubs and the niece and nephew of Captain Barnacles, they first in the Great Arctic Adventure and <a href="#Series_4">episode 99</a>.</li>
<li><b>Professor Natquik</b> the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Artic_fox" title="Artic fox">artic fox</a> who is also a polar explorer and Captain Barnacles's old friend, first seen in <a href="#Special_episodes">Operation Deep Freeze</a>.</li>
<li><b>Ranger Marsh</b>, a park ranger and Tweak's father,  first seen in the <a href="#Special_episodes">Great Swamp Search</a>.</li>
<li><b>Koshi</b>, Dashi's little sister, first seen in the episode The Kelp Monster Mystery.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Vehicles">Vehicles</span></h2>
<ul><li>The <i>Octopod</i> is their mobile home base and the biggest vehicle. It has almost unlimited range, as it may be nuclear powered. It is built to look like a giant <a href="/wiki/Octopus" title="Octopus">octopus</a>.</li>
<li>The <i>GUP-A</i> is propeller-powered and Captain Barnacles' favorite. It is built to look like an <a href="/wiki/Anglerfish" title="Anglerfish">anglerfish</a>.</li>
<li>Kwazii's favorite, the <i>GUP-B</i> is a turbo sub so is the fastest of all GUPs. It is built to look like a <a href="/wiki/Tiger_shark" title="Tiger shark">tiger shark</a>.</li>
<li>The strong <i>GUP-C</i> has a towline and is powerful enough to tow an elephant seal. It can also be used as an icebreaker when fitted with a special reinforced prow. It is built to look like a <a href="/wiki/Blue_whale" title="Blue whale">blue whale</a>.</li>
<li>Multi-purpose <i>GUP-D</i> has two claws, two drills and six legs. It is also Tweak's favorite. It is built to look like a <a href="/wiki/Manta_ray" title="Manta ray">manta ray</a> (or a crab when legged).</li>
<li>The <i>GUP-E</i> is an ambulance GUP and colored green. A tank behind can be filled with water for creatures with gills. It is built to look like a <a href="/wiki/Guppy" title="Guppy">guppy</a>.</li>
<li>The <i>GUP-F</i> was a pedal-powered metal-framed vehicle only seen in series 1, episode 10, <i>Octonauts and the Speedy <a href="/wiki/Sailfish" title="Sailfish">Sailfish</a></i>, used to round up the runaway GUPs A-E. It was built to look like a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Clownfish" title="Clownfish">clownfish</a>. In the episode "The Artificial Reef" the GUP-F is destroyed by a hurricane and is converted into an artificial reef. In the same episode, it is also revealed by Tweak that it was the very first prototype GUP built.</li>
<li>The <i>GUP-H</i> is a helicopter that is built to look like a dragonfly. It's used to soar above the sea, and watch the animals hop in and out of the water. It is first seen in series 4, episode 1, <i>The Poison Dart Frogs</i>.</li>
<li>The <i>GUP-I</i> is an polar exploration station and rugged vehicle first seen in the 8th special, <i>Operation Deep Freeze</i>. It is built to look like a water bear.</li>
<li>The <i>GUP-K</i> is a half air-boat, half-tank vehicle that can float on surface and is first seen in the 9th and latest special, <i>The Great Swamp Search</i> to save Tweak's hometown the Everglades from fire. It is built to look like a crocodile or an alligator.</li>
<li>The <i>GUP-S</i> is an Antarctic exploration vehicle first seen in the 6th special episode <i>The Over Under Adventure</i>. Like the GUP-X, it can split in 2 sections, with the front part forming an amphibious snowmobile called the Octo-Sled. The Gup-S is built to look like a swordfish or narwhal, with a tusk-like ice-penetrating heated drill at the front.</li>
<li>The <i>GUP-V</i> is a vehicle designed by the Vegimals in the 5th special episode, <i>A Very Vegimal Christmas</i>. It is shaped like a train, and made from improvised materials.</li>
<li>The <i>GUP-X</i> is an armored, powerful and sturdy tracked vehicle presented to Captain Barnacles as a Christmas present from Tweak. It can split into 3 sections, for instance, a glider, the 'Octo-Ski' and its central main section with the caterpillar tracks. It is first seen in series 1, episode 52, <i>The Great Christmas Rescue</i>. It is built to look like a <a href="/wiki/Horseshoe_crab" title="Horseshoe crab">horseshoe crab</a>.</li>
<li>The <i>Deep Sea Octolab</i> is a moveable base from which the deepest parts of the ocean can be studied and first appears in the 4th special episode, <i>The Mariana Trench Adventure</i>. It is dome-shaped and stands on springy legs to cope with subterranean tremors.</li>
<li>The <i>Octo Servicing Station</i> is a vehicle servicing scaffold constructed on the seabed, seen in various episodes in season 4, most notably in the special episode <i>The Great Arctic Adventure</i>. It's used to refurbish the Octopod and manufacture the GUP K and GUP H.</li></ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Spin-offs">Spin-offs</span></h2>
<p>The Octonauts brand has been licensed by the production company for numerous spin-off activities. These include <a href="/wiki/Octonauts_Rollercoaster_Adventure" title="Octonauts Rollercoaster Adventure">Octonauts Rollercoaster Adventure</a>, a themed rollercoaster within the <a href="/wiki/CBeebies#CBeebies_Land" title="CBeebies">CBeebies Land</a> of <a href="/wiki/Alton_Towers" title="Alton Towers">Alton Towers</a> theme park and themed bedrooms with the CBeebies Land Hotel. There is also a travelling live show called <i>Octonauts Live</i>, which has toured in the U.S.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> and other countries.
</p><p>An <i>Octonauts</i> app was launched for iOS on 17 November 2016, produced in partnership with Night and Day Studios.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup>
