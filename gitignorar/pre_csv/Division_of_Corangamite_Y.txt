<p>The <b>Division of Corangamite</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Divisions_of_the_Australian_House_of_Representatives" title="Divisions of the Australian House of Representatives">Australian electoral division</a> in the <a href="/wiki/States_and_territories_of_Australia" title="States and territories of Australia">state</a> of <a href="/wiki/Victoria_(Australia)" title="Victoria (Australia)">Victoria</a>. The division was proclaimed in 1900, and was one of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Australian_electorates_contested_at_every_election" title="List of Australian electorates contested at every election">original 65 divisions</a> to be contested at the <a href="/wiki/1901_Australian_federal_election" title="1901 Australian federal election">first federal election</a>. It is named for <a href="/wiki/Lake_Corangamite" title="Lake Corangamite">Lake Corangamite</a>, although the lake no longer falls within the division's boundaries.
</p><p>The division is located in the <a href="/wiki/Western_District_(Victoria)" title="Western District (Victoria)">Western district</a> of Victoria covering 5,441 square kilometres (2,101 sq mi). It is a mixed electorate, including the growing surf coast area, the southern suburbs of <a href="/wiki/Geelong" title="Geelong">Geelong</a> as well as rural areas to the west. Starting at <a href="/wiki/Queenscliff,_Victoria" title="Queenscliff, Victoria">Queenscliff</a> in the east, the electorate takes in the entire <a href="/wiki/Bellarine_Peninsula" title="Bellarine Peninsula">Bellarine Peninsula</a>, then runs down the surf coast to include <a href="/wiki/Aireys_Inlet" title="Aireys Inlet">Aireys Inlet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anglesea,_Victoria" title="Anglesea, Victoria">Anglesea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Apollo_Bay" title="Apollo Bay">Apollo Bay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barwon_Heads,_Victoria" title="Barwon Heads, Victoria">Barwon Heads</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lorne,_Victoria" title="Lorne, Victoria">Lorne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ocean_Grove,_Victoria" title="Ocean Grove, Victoria">Ocean Grove</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wye_River,_Victoria" title="Wye River, Victoria">Wye River</a> and <a href="/wiki/Torquay,_Victoria" title="Torquay, Victoria">Torquay</a>. The electorate then extends north into the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Plains_Shire" title="Golden Plains Shire">Golden Plains Shire</a>, where it includes the towns of <a href="/wiki/Bannockburn,_Victoria" title="Bannockburn, Victoria">Bannockburn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lethbridge,_Victoria" title="Lethbridge, Victoria">Lethbridge</a> and part of <a href="/wiki/Meredith,_Victoria" title="Meredith, Victoria">Meredith</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aecprofile_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-aecprofile-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Since the <a href="/wiki/2019_Australian_federal_election" title="2019 Australian federal election">2019 federal election</a>, the current Member for Corangamite is <a href="/wiki/Libby_Coker" title="Libby Coker">Libby Coker</a>, a member of the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Labor_Party" title="Australian Labor Party">Australian Labor Party</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Division_of_Corangamite&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:View_from_Red_Rock_Victoria.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="356" data-file-width="1373" decoding="async" height="57" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/View_from_Red_Rock_Victoria.jpg/220px-View_from_Red_Rock_Victoria.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/View_from_Red_Rock_Victoria.jpg/330px-View_from_Red_Rock_Victoria.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/View_from_Red_Rock_Victoria.jpg/440px-View_from_Red_Rock_Victoria.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:View_from_Red_Rock_Victoria.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Lake_Corangamite" title="Lake Corangamite">Lake Corangamite</a> (in the background), the division's namesake</div></div></div>
<p>Until the 1930s it was usually a marginal seat which leaned toward the conservative parties, but was won by the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Labor_Party" title="Australian Labor Party">Australian Labor Party</a> during high-tide elections. In <a href="/wiki/1918_Corangamite_by-election" title="1918 Corangamite by-election">1918</a>, it was the first seat won by what would become the <a href="/wiki/National_Party_of_Australia" title="National Party of Australia">Country Party</a>.
</p><p>It was held by the Liberals (and their immediate predecessor, the <a href="/wiki/United_Australia_Party" title="United Australia Party">United Australia Party</a>) without interruption from 1934 to 2007. A reasonably safe seat for most of the time from the 1950s to the 1990s, it became increasingly less safe from 1998 onward as successive redistributions pushed it further into Geelong. This resulted in the seat falling to <a href="/wiki/Darren_Cheeseman" title="Darren Cheeseman">Darren Cheeseman</a>, the Labor candidate, by less than one percent at the <a href="/wiki/2007_Australian_federal_election" title="2007 Australian federal election">2007 federal election</a> for the first time since <a href="/wiki/1929_Australian_federal_election" title="1929 Australian federal election">1929</a>. Cheeseman was only the third Labor member ever to win the seat. Labor retained the seat in <a href="/wiki/2010_Australian_federal_election" title="2010 Australian federal election">2010 election</a> against former journalist <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Henderson" title="Sarah Henderson">Sarah Henderson</a>, making Cheeseman the first Labor MP to win re-election in the seat. Henderson sought a rematch in <a href="/wiki/2013_Australian_federal_election" title="2013 Australian federal election">2013</a>, and won.
</p><p>Henderson retained her seat in <a href="/wiki/2016_Australian_federal_election" title="2016 Australian federal election">2016</a> but a redistribution completed prior to the <a href="/wiki/2019_Australian_federal_election" title="2019 Australian federal election">2019</a> resulted in the seat becoming notionally Labor, albeit with a very narrow margin. As Henderson failed to gain a swing towards her at the election, she lost the seat to the Labor candidate, <a href="/wiki/Libby_Coker" title="Libby Coker">Libby Coker</a>. Coker's win in 2019 was historically significant, as it marked the first time that the non-Labor parties have been in government without holding Corangamite.
</p><p>Prominent members include <a href="/wiki/James_Scullin" title="James Scullin">James Scullin</a>, who later became the <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Australia" title="Prime Minister of Australia">Prime Minister of Australia</a> in 1929-32; Fraser Government Minister <a href="/wiki/Tony_Street" title="Tony Street">Tony Street</a>, and longtime Liberal backbencher <a href="/wiki/Stewart_McArthur" title="Stewart McArthur">Stewart McArthur</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-green_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-green-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2018, the Australian Electoral Commission (AEC) published its report on the proposed redistribution of Victoria's federal divisions. The report proposed renaming Corangamite to Cox, after swimming instructor May Cox. Incumbent MP <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Henderson" title="Sarah Henderson">Sarah Henderson</a> said the new name "has already prompted some ridicule on social media".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-coxproposal_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-coxproposal-3">[3]</a></sup> In the commission's final determination, the decision was made to retain the name of Corangamite.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nochange_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-nochange-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
