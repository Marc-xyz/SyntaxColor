<p><b>Glyncorrwg</b> is a village in the <a href="/wiki/River_Afan" title="River Afan">Afan Valley</a>, in southern <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>.
</p><p>Glyncorrwg is also the name of an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ward_(politics)" title="Ward (politics)">electoral ward</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Community_(Wales)" title="Community (Wales)">community</a> covering the village and surrounding countryside, in <a href="/wiki/Neath_Port_Talbot" title="Neath Port Talbot">Neath Port Talbot</a> <a href="/wiki/County_borough" title="County borough">county borough</a>. Glyncorrwg community contains the villages of <a href="/wiki/Abergwynfi" title="Abergwynfi">Abergwynfi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blaengwynfi" title="Blaengwynfi">Blaengwynfi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Croeserw" title="Croeserw">Croeserw</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cymmer,_Neath_Port_Talbot" title="Cymmer, Neath Port Talbot">Cymmer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Abercregan" title="Abercregan">Abercregan</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Duffryn,_Neath_Port_Talbot&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Duffryn, Neath Port Talbot (page does not exist)">Duffryn</a> and Glyncorwg itself. The population of Glyncorrwg as a community, was recorded as 5,544 in the 2001 census,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> reducing to 5,283 at the 2011 census.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The population of the electoral ward mentioned above was 1,096 only at the 2011 census.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glyncorrwg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Glyncorrwg was once an important <a href="/wiki/Coal_mining" title="Coal mining">coal mining</a> centre, typical of the <a href="/wiki/South_Wales_Valleys" title="South Wales Valleys">South Wales Valleys</a>. With the end of the coal mining industry during the 1970s, buildings were cleared away, factories closed and people left the area to find new employment.
</p><p>In 1990 the local community decided to take advantage of the local scenery and complemented it with a series of <a href="/wiki/Pond" title="Pond">ponds</a> along the narrow valley. <a href="/wiki/Trout" title="Trout">Trout</a> fishing, <a href="/wiki/Coarse_fishing" title="Coarse fishing">coarse fishing</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Canoeing" title="Canoeing">canoeing</a> are now popular sports in the area, plus miles of old flat railway trackbed lines and steep <a href="/wiki/Mountain" title="Mountain">mountain</a> slopes providing opportunities for <a href="/wiki/Cycling" title="Cycling">cycling</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hillwalking" title="Hillwalking">hillwalking</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mountain_biking" title="Mountain biking">mountain biking</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Glyncorrwg_Health_Centre">Glyncorrwg Health Centre</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glyncorrwg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Glyncorrwg Health Centre">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dr <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Julian_Tudor_Hart" title="Julian Tudor Hart">Julian Tudor Hart</a> established a famous research and teaching practice in Glyncorrwg.  He arrived in 1961 and conducted a series of epidemiological studies on the practice population - a population of about 1900 which was relatively stable and which he got to know well.  He established a health centre committee with a public health focus and demonstrated that systematic case-finding and regular follow-up  produced real benefits. In 1987 he found age-standardised death rates under 65 were 28% lower in Glyncorrwg than in nearby <a href="/wiki/Blaengwynfi" title="Blaengwynfi">Blaengwynfi</a> over the previous five-year period. This approach, which was the subject of many publications, led eventually to the introduction of the <a href="/wiki/Quality_and_Outcomes_Framework" title="Quality and Outcomes Framework">Quality and Outcomes Framework</a> which rewarded GPs for meeting targets for intervention in the management of disease.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Glyncorrwg_Coal_Company">Glyncorrwg Coal Company</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glyncorrwg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Glyncorrwg Coal Company">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/South_Wales_Mineral_Railway" title="South Wales Mineral Railway">South Wales Mineral Railway</a> reached Glyncorrwg in 1863. It was always short of funds so was leased to the Glyncorrwg Coal Company, which became the Glyncorrwg Colliery Company Limited in 1870. This company operated a local coal mine and needed the railway to cheaply transport coal down to <a href="/wiki/Briton_Ferry" title="Briton Ferry">Briton Ferry</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government_and_politics">Government and politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glyncorrwg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Government and politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electoral_ward" title="Electoral ward">electoral ward</a> of Glyncorrwg elects a county councillor to <a href="/wiki/Neath_Port_Talbot_County_Borough_Council" title="Neath Port Talbot County Borough Council">Neath Port Talbot County Borough Council</a> and falls within the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parliamentary_constituency" title="Parliamentary constituency">parliamentary constituency</a> of <a href="/wiki/Aberavon_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Aberavon (UK Parliament constituency)">Aberavon</a>.  The ward is surrounded by <a href="/wiki/Moorland" title="Moorland">moorland</a> which in turn is planted with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coniferous_forest" title="Coniferous forest">coniferous forest</a>. Glyncorrwg is bounded by the wards of <a href="/wiki/Blaengwrach" title="Blaengwrach">Blaengwrach</a> in the Vale of Neath to the North; <a href="/wiki/Treherbert" title="Treherbert">Treherbert</a> in the County of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rhondda_Cynon_Taff" title="Rhondda Cynon Taff">Rhondda Cynon Taff</a> to the East; and to the South by <a href="/wiki/Gwynfi" title="Gwynfi">Gwynfi</a> and Cymmer in the (Afan Valley)and finally, by <a href="/wiki/Resolven_(electoral_ward)" title="Resolven (electoral ward)">Resolven</a> to the west.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pen_y_Cymoedd">Pen y Cymoedd</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glyncorrwg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Pen y Cymoedd">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Pen_y_Cymoedd" title="Pen y Cymoedd">Pen y Cymoedd</a></div>
<p>In May 2012 it was announced that the <a href="/wiki/Pen_y_Cymoedd" title="Pen y Cymoedd">Pen y Cymoedd</a> windfarm, the highest-generating onshore wind farm in Wales, had received government backing, and that the 76 turbines would be operational by 2016. This was being developed by Swedish-based Vattenfall.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
