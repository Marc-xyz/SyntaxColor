	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Bachelor party</h1>

<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Wes%27_Bachelor_Party_005.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1365" data-file-width="2047" decoding="async" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/Wes%27_Bachelor_Party_005.jpg/220px-Wes%27_Bachelor_Party_005.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/Wes%27_Bachelor_Party_005.jpg/330px-Wes%27_Bachelor_Party_005.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/Wes%27_Bachelor_Party_005.jpg/440px-Wes%27_Bachelor_Party_005.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Wes%27_Bachelor_Party_005.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A bachelor being led to his party</div></div></div>
<p>A <b>stag night</b>, also known as a <b>stag weekend</b>, <b>stag do</b> or <b>stag party</b> (in the United Kingdom, <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth countries</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>), a <b>buck's night</b> (in Australia) or <b>bachelor party</b> (in United States)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Party" title="Party">party</a> held for the man who is to shortly enter <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">marriage</a>.
</p><p>A stag night is usually planned by the <a href="/wiki/Groomsman" title="Groomsman">groomsman</a>, occasionally with the assistance of a <a href="/wiki/Party_service" title="Party service">bachelor party planning company</a>. 
</p><p>The first references to Western stag nights in the <i><a href="/wiki/Oxford_English_Dictionary" title="Oxford English Dictionary">Oxford English Dictionary</a></i> date to the 19th century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BradshawBishop2007_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-BradshawBishop2007-2">[2]</a></sup> Traditionally, stag nights involved a <a href="/wiki/Black_tie" title="Black tie">black tie</a> banquet hosted by the father of the groom that included a <a href="/wiki/Toast_(honor)" title="Toast (honor)">toast</a> in honour of the groom and bride.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Haire2009_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Haire2009-3">[3]</a></sup> Since the 1980s, bachelor parties in the United States have involved vacationing to a foreign destination,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Haire2009_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Haire2009-3">[3]</a></sup> or have featured female company such as <a href="/wiki/Stripper" title="Stripper">strippers</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Topless" title="Topless">topless</a> waitresses.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bachelor_party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The bachelor party dates back as early as the 5th century B.C. The ancient <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spartans" title="Spartans">Spartans</a> celebrated the groom's last night as a single man in which they held a dinner and made toasts on his behalf.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs a reliable source (October 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>In 1896, Herbert Barnum Seeley, a grandson of <a href="/wiki/P._T._Barnum" title="P. T. Barnum">P. T. Barnum</a>, threw a stag party (known as the "Awful Seeley Dinner") for his brother at restaurant <a href="/wiki/Sherry%27s" title="Sherry's">Sherry's</a> in New York City. The party had a dancer, nicknamed "<a href="/wiki/Little_Egypt_(dancer)" title="Little Egypt (dancer)">Little Egypt</a>", who allegedly danced naked in desserts. The party was dissolved in the early morning by an officer. Afterwards, the Seeley family brought the police officer to the police board trial for "conduct unbecoming to an officer of the law."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> At that time, that incident brought the light to the "behind closed doors" matters with bachelor parties.
</p><p>The term "bachelor", originally meaning "a young knight-in-training", was first mentioned in the 14th century to refer to an unmarried man in <a href="/wiki/Geoffrey_Chaucer" title="Geoffrey Chaucer">Geoffrey Chaucer</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Canterbury_Tales" title="The Canterbury Tales">The Canterbury Tales</a></i>. In 1922, the term "bachelor party" was published in <a href="/wiki/William_Chambers_(publisher)" title="William Chambers (publisher)">William Chambers</a>'s <i>Journal of Literature, Science and Arts</i> and was described as a "jolly old" party.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Variations">Variations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bachelor_party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Variations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The equivalent event for the bride-to-be is known as a <a href="/wiki/Bachelorette_party" title="Bachelorette party">bachelorette party</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hen_night" title="Hen night">hen night</a>.
</p><p>Some also choose instead to hold a <i><a href="/wiki/Stag_and_doe" title="Stag and doe">stag and doe</a></i> party in Canada, or a <i>hag party</i> or <i>hag do</i> in the UK ("hag" being a combination of the words "hen" and "stag"), in which both the bride and groom attend.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Germany">Germany</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bachelor_party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Germany">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Germany, this event is called <i>Junggesellenabschied</i>, which literally means "bachelor farewell". There is also a separate event that the couple celebrates together on the evening prior to their wedding, called <i><a href="/wiki/Polterabend" title="Polterabend">Polterabend</a></i>. At the Polterabend, the guests break old porcelain and earthenware to bring luck to the couple's marriage. The tradition is said to go back to pre-Christian times; by noisily breaking ceramics, evil spirits – especially spirits of envy – are supposed to be driven out. In the last couple of years, Anglo-style bachelor parties have become more and more popular among bachelors. In parts of northern Germany that lack a <a href="/wiki/Carnival" title="Carnival">Carnival</a> tradition, funny costuming has become a popular part of bachelor or bachelorette parties.
</p><p>Some parts of Germany have a related custom, in which a person who is not yet married by their 30th birthday, is made to dress up in an embarrassing fashion by their friends and to do silly tasks that most often include some kind of cleaning work.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="France">France</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bachelor_party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: France">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In France and in many French-speaking regions such as <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>, the bachelor party is called <i><i lang="fr" title="French language text">enterrement de vie de garçon</i></i>, which literally means "(the) burial of the life as a boy" or "burial/funeral of the life as a bachelor". For women it is <i><i lang="fr" title="French language text">enterrement de vie de jeune fille</i></i>, translated as "burial/funeral of the life as a young girl/maiden".
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Israel">Israel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bachelor_party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Israel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Israel, the bachelor party is called <i><span dir="rtl" lang="he" title="Hebrew language text">מסיבת רווקים</span></i> (mesibat ravakim), literally meaning a party of single men.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (August 2016)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Such parties may feature heavy drinking and sometimes the presence of strippers,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> or else other recreational bonding activities undertaken together, such as paintball or an overseas trip lasting a few days.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom_and_Ireland">United Kingdom and Ireland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bachelor_party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: United Kingdom and Ireland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the United Kingdom, it is now common for the party to last for more than one evening, hence the increasing prevalence of the phrase "stag weekend", or "stag do". A spin-off has been the growth of the stag weekend industry in the UK with various companies taking over the preparation of the event.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>In the UK, stag weekend trips are becoming mini-holidays with the groups taking part in various day-time activities as well as the expected night out on the town. They may involve travelling to another location in the UK or going abroad,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-boyer_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-boyer-8">[8]</a></sup> with <a href="/wiki/Krak%C3%B3w" title="Kraków">Kraków</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Riga" title="Riga">Riga</a> topping the list, followed by <a href="/wiki/Prague" title="Prague">Prague</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bratislava" title="Bratislava">Bratislava</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Stag parties abroad have been known to involve visits to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brothels" title="Brothels">brothels</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prostitutes" title="Prostitutes">prostitutes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States_and_Canada">United States and Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bachelor_party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: United States and Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the United States, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Las_Vegas,_Nevada" title="Las Vegas, Nevada">Las Vegas</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> is both a popular bachelor party destination and location for the wedding itself.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-boyer_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-boyer-8">[8]</a></sup> Increasingly, "destination bachelor parties" are replacing standard nights out, with Americans traveling to <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quebec_City" title="Quebec City">Quebec City</a> or <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p><p>Bachelor parties in the US stereotypically entail the mass consumption of alcohol, hiring a <a href="/wiki/Stripper" title="Stripper">stripper</a>, and general rowdiness to which the bride might not have a positive reaction; in fact, the defining feature of the bachelor party is that the fiancée is not present. Increasingly, bachelor parties have come to symbolize the last time when the groom is free of the influence of his new wife/partner.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>  <a href="/wiki/Pop_out_cake" title="Pop out cake">Pop out cakes</a> are sometimes associated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
Canadian bachelor parties generally consist of a group of bachelors celebrating their singleness.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="South_Africa">South Africa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bachelor_party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: South Africa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bachelor parties in South Africa are expected to be a surprise, which is a unique regional variant. The party is planned without the groom’s knowledge and is typically a couple days before the wedding. It often includes a traditional <a href="/wiki/Regional_variations_of_barbecue#South_Africa" title="Regional variations of barbecue">braai</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Thailand">Thailand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bachelor_party&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Thailand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bachelor parties in Thailand became increasingly popular after huge success of the <a href="/wiki/The_Hangover_Part_II" title="The Hangover Part II"><i>The Hangover 2 Movie</i></a> which was filmed and based in Bangkok. 
</p><p>According to <a href="/wiki/GQ_(Indian_edition)" title="GQ (Indian edition)"><i>GQ</i> India</a>, this multimillion-dollar bachelor party industry is being "fuelled by a steady stream of raging testosterone from India".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p>
