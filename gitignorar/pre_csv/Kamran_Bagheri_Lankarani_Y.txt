<p><b>Kamran Bagheri Lankarani</b> (<a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="fa">کامران باقری لنکرانی</span>‎) is an Iranian physician and politician who was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Minister_of_Health_and_Medical_Education" title="Minister of Health and Medical Education">Minister of Health and Medical Education</a> from 2005 until 2009.
</p><p>Born in 1965, he finished medical school at <a href="/wiki/Shiraz_University_of_Medical_Sciences" title="Shiraz University of Medical Sciences">Shiraz University of Medical Sciences</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and attained an advanced fellowship degree in Medicine from the same university. He specializes in gastroenterology.
</p><p>Baqeri-Lankarani got his doctorate from <a href="/wiki/Shiraz_University_of_Medical_Sciences" title="Shiraz University of Medical Sciences">Shiraz University of Medical Sciences</a> in 1989. He received post-doctorate certificate in 1992 from the same university.
He served as head of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Shiraz_Namazi_Hospital&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Shiraz Namazi Hospital (page does not exist)">Shiraz Namazi Hospital</a> (1993–94), deputy chancellor of Shiraz Medical Sciences
University (1994–1996) and manager of the university's internal medicine department (since 1996).
He then served as a member of the presiding board of Shiraz Medical System Administration (1996–2000),
secretary of Islamic Association of <a href="/wiki/Fars_Province" title="Fars Province">Fars</a> province physicians and a member of the Islamic Society of Shiraz University Students.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
