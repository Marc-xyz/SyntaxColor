<p>The <b>Student Welfare Organisation in Trondheim</b> (<a href="/wiki/Norwegian_language" title="Norwegian language">Norwegian</a>: <i lang="no">Studensamskipnaden i Trondheim</i>) or <b>SiT</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Student_welfare_organisation" title="Student welfare organisation">student welfare organisation</a> in <a href="/wiki/Trondheim" title="Trondheim">Trondheim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a> and is responsible for the welfare of about 25,000 students at the <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_University_of_Science_and_Technology" title="Norwegian University of Science and Technology">Norwegian University of Science and Technology</a> (NTNU), and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queen_Maud%27s_College_of_Early_Childhood_Education" title="Queen Maud's College of Early Childhood Education">Queen Maud's College of Early Childhood Education</a> (DMMH).
</p><p>Among the responsibilities of SiT include housing for 3000 students, two gyms, <a href="/wiki/Kindergarten" title="Kindergarten">kindergarten</a> places, ten <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caf%C3%A9" title="Café">cafés</a>, the seven <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Book_store" title="Book store">book stores</a> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=SiT_Tapir&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="SiT Tapir (page does not exist)">SiT Tapir</a>, <a href="/wiki/Psychologist" title="Psychologist">psychologists</a>, advisors, career centre, heath station and economic support arrangements.  The housing for students is located at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Berg,_Trondheim&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Berg, Trondheim (page does not exist)">Berg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Singsaker" title="Singsaker">Singsaker</a>, <a href="/wiki/%C3%98ya" title="Øya">Øya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Moholt" title="Moholt">Moholt</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Jakobsli&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Jakobsli (page does not exist)">Jakobsli</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lerkendal,_Trondheim" title="Lerkendal, Trondheim">Lerkendal</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Teknobyen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Teknobyen (page does not exist)">Teknobyen</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nedre_Berg&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nedre Berg (page does not exist)">Nedre Berg</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Steinan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Steinan (page does not exist)">Steinan</a>. About 20% of students in Trondheim have accommodation through SiT. Facilities are located on ten campuses in town, including <a href="/wiki/Gl%C3%B8shaugen" title="Gløshaugen">Gløshaugen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dragvoll" title="Dragvoll">Dragvoll</a>, <a href="/wiki/%C3%98ya" title="Øya">Øya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tyholt" title="Tyholt">Tyholt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kalvskinnet" title="Kalvskinnet">Kalvskinnet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Moholt" title="Moholt">Moholt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leangen" title="Leangen">Leangen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rotvoll" title="Rotvoll">Rotvoll</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Tunga,_Trondheim&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Tunga, Trondheim (page does not exist)">Tunga, Trondheim</a> and DMMH.
</p><p>SiT is led by a <a href="/wiki/Board_of_directors" title="Board of directors">board</a>, with the majority and chairman being students. Other representatives are from NTNU and HiST as well as the employees in SiT. The highest organ for welfare activities is <i>Velferdstinget</i> (<i>the welfare parliament</i>) that has 17 student members.
</p>
