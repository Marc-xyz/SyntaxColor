<p><b>General Belgrano</b> is a central <a href="/wiki/Departments_of_Argentina" title="Departments of Argentina">department</a> of <a href="/wiki/Chaco_Province" title="Chaco Province">Chaco Province</a> in <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>.
</p><p>The provincial subdivision has a <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of about 10,500 inhabitants in an area of  1,218 km², and its capital city is <a href="/wiki/Corzuela" title="Corzuela">Corzuela</a>, which is located around 1,250 km from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Capital_federal" title="Capital federal">Capital federal</a>.
</p><p>The department is named in honour of <a href="/wiki/Manuel_Belgrano" title="Manuel Belgrano">General Manuel Belgrano</a> (June 3, 1770 – June 20, 1820), an Argentine economist, Lawyer, Politician and military leader.
</p>
