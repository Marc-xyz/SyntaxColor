	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en"><i>Hits</i> (The Beach Boys EP)</h1>

<p><i><b>Hits</b></i> was an <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> by <a href="/wiki/The_Beach_Boys" title="The Beach Boys">The Beach Boys</a>, released in May 1966 and containing four of the band's most recent hit singles up to that point. The EP was released as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/7-inch" title="7-inch">7-inch</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vinyl_record" title="Vinyl record">vinyl record</a> in <a href="/wiki/Monaural" title="Monaural">mono</a> with the catalogue number <a href="/wiki/Capitol_Records" title="Capitol Records">Capitol</a> EAP1-20781.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> <i>Hits</i> was the UK <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_number-one_EPs_(UK)" title="List of number-one EPs (UK)">number-one EP</a> for 34 weeks, having eight separate stints at the top of the chart from June 1966 until December 1967 – this is the highest number of weeks as number-one EP. <i>Hits</i> was the incumbent number one when the chart ceased on 16 December 1967.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hits_(The_Beach_Boys_EP)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Each of the four songs on the EP had been released as singles. In America all charted on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard Hot</i> 100</a>: "Help Me, Rhonda" had reached number one on 29 May 1965, "California Girls" had peaked at number three on 28 August 1965, "The Little Girl I Once Knew" peaked at number twenty on 1 January 1966, and "Barbara Ann" peaked at number two on 29 January 1966.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEElliott2003420_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEElliott2003420-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Three of the singles had also been previously released in the UK, with "Help Me, Rhonda" and "California Girls"  making the top thirty of the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart"><i>Record Retailer</i></a> chart and "Barbara Ann" peaking at number three in March 1966.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chart_performance">Chart performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hits_(The_Beach_Boys_EP)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Chart performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Beginning in 1960s, in addition to publishing a <a href="/wiki/LP_record" title="LP record">long play (LP)</a> chart, <i>Record Retailer</i> also ran an EP chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWarwickKutnerBrown2004viii_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWarwickKutnerBrown2004viii-5">[5]</a></sup> <i>Hits</i> was released in May 1966 and became a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_number-one_EPs_(UK)" title="List of number-one EPs (UK)">number-one EP</a> on 4 June.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWarwickKutnerBrown200428_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWarwickKutnerBrown200428-7">[7]</a></sup> It stayed at the top for 4 weeks and was then displaced by <a href="/wiki/The_Walker_Brothers" title="The Walker Brothers">The Walker Brothers</a> EP <i>I Need You</i>. <i>Hits</i> returned to the top again on 10 September for another four weeks, being displaced again by the same Walker Brothers EP. On 29 October it returned for a third stint at number-one of seven weeks. <a href="/wiki/The_Who" title="The Who">The Who</a> tooks the number-one spot over Christmas and in the New Year of 1967, with <i>Hits</i> reclaiming number-one on 21 January for another seven weeks. After five months of the top, <i>Hits</i> returned on 12 August for another six weeks at the top in its fifth stint at number-one.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWarwickKutnerBrown200428_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWarwickKutnerBrown200428-7">[7]</a></sup> The EP alternated weeks at number-one with the <i><a href="/wiki/Four_Tops" title="Four Tops">Four Tops</a> Hits</i> EP in late September and early October and at this point EPs were dying out.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWarwickKutnerBrown200428_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWarwickKutnerBrown200428-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> <i>Hits</i> returned for its eighth and final period atop the chart on 25 November and remained there for four weeks; it was the incumbent number one when the chart ceased on 16 December 1967.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWarwickKutnerBrown2004viii,_28_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWarwickKutnerBrown2004viii,_28-9">[9]</a></sup> In total, <i>Hits</i> amassed 34 weeks at the top of the chart – more than any other EP.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWarwickKutnerBrown200424&amp;ndash;28_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWarwickKutnerBrown200424&amp;ndash;28-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
