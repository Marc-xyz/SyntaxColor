<p><i><b>Thryptomene wittweri</b></i> is a shrub species in the family <a href="/wiki/Myrtaceae" title="Myrtaceae">Myrtaceae</a> that is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Endemic" title="Endemic">endemic</a> to Western Australia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FloraBase_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FloraBase-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The spreading to rounded shrub typically grows to a height of 0.5 to 1.5 metres (2 to 5 ft). It blooms between April and August producing white-cream flowers.
</p><p>It is found on breakaways and creek beds in the <a href="/wiki/Gascoyne" title="Gascoyne">Gascoyne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pilbara" title="Pilbara">Pilbara</a> regions of Western Australia where it grows in skeletal stony soils.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FloraBase_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-FloraBase-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
