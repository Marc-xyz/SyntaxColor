		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>HealthCap</b> is a specialized provider of <a href="/wiki/Venture_capital" title="Venture capital">venture capital</a> within <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Life_sciences" title="Life sciences">life sciences</a>. HealthCap invests in innovative companies with focus on therapeutics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> As of 2017, HealthCap has invested in over 100 companies since inception and completed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Initial_public_offerings" title="Initial public offerings">initial public offerings</a> of more than 40 companies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> HealthCap has offices in <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oslo" title="Oslo">Oslo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lausanne" title="Lausanne">Lausanne</a>.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=HealthCap&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The firm was founded in 1996 by Björn Odlander<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>  and Peder Fredrikson,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and the first fund was started the same year. As of 2017, HealthCap has established seven funds and financed 106 companies, where 39 have been taken public on nine different markets. The most recent fund, HealthCap VII, was established in the fall of 2015.
In 2017, HealthCap had approximately 23 employees out of which ten are partners. The team combines venture capital investing experience with competencies and work experiences from small as well as large companies across the healthcare industry, spanning disciplines of scientific research, drug development, clinical practice, investment banking, and industry management.
</p><p><br/>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Investments">Investments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=HealthCap&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Investments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>HealthCap has raised seven main funds. HealthCap IV-VII are Delaware Limited Partnerships with Swiss general partners. Investors in HealthCap funds include, among others, <a href="/wiki/European_Investment_Fund" title="European Investment Fund">European Investment Fund</a>, Skandia Life Insurance, the 4th and 6th Swedish National Pension Funds, <a href="/wiki/The_Kresge_Foundation" title="The Kresge Foundation">The Kresge Foundation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mayo_Clinic" title="Mayo Clinic">Mayo Clinic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Northwestern_University" title="Northwestern University">Northwestern University</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Michigan" title="University of Michigan">University of Michigan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vanderbilt_University" title="Vanderbilt University">Vanderbilt University</a> and <a href="/wiki/Washington_University_in_St._Louis" title="Washington University in St. Louis">Washington University</a>. HealthCap has committed capital exceeding EUR 1 billion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>HealthCap invests in companies developing disruptive technologies that hold the potential to change clinical practice. Over the years HealthCap has invested in more than 100 companies. The portfolio companies have developed more than 20 pharmaceutical products and over 40 med-tech products to the market.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Many of these products, such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Firazyr" title="Firazyr">Firazyr</a>®, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Xofigo" title="Xofigo">Xofigo</a>®, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tracleer" title="Tracleer">Tracleer</a>®, are breakthrough therapies addressing life-threatening diseases.
