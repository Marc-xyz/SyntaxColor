<p><b>Auguste Charles Eugène Napoléon, Duke of Leuchtenberg</b> (9 December 1810 – 28 March 1835) was the first <a href="/wiki/Prince_consort" title="Prince consort">prince consort</a> of <a href="/wiki/Maria_II_of_Portugal" title="Maria II of Portugal">Maria II of Portugal</a>. Besides being the <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Leuchtenberg" title="Duke of Leuchtenberg">2nd Duke of Leuchtenberg</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prince_of_Eichst%C3%A4tt" title="Prince of Eichstätt">2nd Prince of Eichstätt</a>, he also held the <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_nobility" title="Brazilian nobility">Brazilian noble title</a> of <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Santa_Cruz" title="Duke of Santa Cruz">Duke of Santa Cruz</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Auguste,_Duke_of_Leuchtenberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Being born in <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a>, Lombardy, Auguste was the eldest son of <a href="/wiki/Eug%C3%A8ne_de_Beauharnais" title="Eugène de Beauharnais">Eugène de Beauharnais</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Napoleon_I" title="Napoleon I">Napoleon I</a>'s stepson, and <a href="/wiki/Princess_Augusta_of_Bavaria" title="Princess Augusta of Bavaria">Princess Augusta of Bavaria</a>. His <a href="/wiki/Dynasty" title="Dynasty">dynastic</a> connections were exceptional, considering his paternal lineage: among his sisters were <a href="/wiki/Josephine_of_Leuchtenberg" title="Josephine of Leuchtenberg">Joséphine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Queen_consort" title="Queen consort">Queen consort</a> of <a href="/wiki/Oscar_I_of_Sweden" title="Oscar I of Sweden">Oscar I of Sweden</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Am%C3%A9lie_of_Leuchtenberg" title="Amélie of Leuchtenberg">Amélie</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Empress_consort" title="Empress consort">Empress consort</a> of his future father-in-law <a href="/wiki/Pedro_I_of_Brazil" title="Pedro I of Brazil">Pedro I of Brazil</a>. Later, his brother <a href="/wiki/Maximilian_de_Beauharnais,_3rd_Duke_of_Leuchtenberg" title="Maximilian de Beauharnais, 3rd Duke of Leuchtenberg">Maximilian</a> would wed <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchess_Maria_Nikolaevna_of_Russia_(1819%E2%80%931876)" title="Grand Duchess Maria Nikolaevna of Russia (1819–1876)">Grand Duchess Maria Nikolaevna of Russia</a>, eldest daughter of <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_I_of_Russia" title="Nicholas I of Russia">Tsar Nicholas I</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Duke_of_Leuchtenberg">Duke of Leuchtenberg</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Auguste,_Duke_of_Leuchtenberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Duke of Leuchtenberg">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>His maternal grandfather, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_Maximilian_I_of_Bavaria" title="King Maximilian I of Bavaria">King Maximilian I of Bavaria</a>, had given Eugène the title "Duke of Leuchtenberg" on 14 November 1817, after the loss in 1815 of his Napoleonic titles and the associated expectancies of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Italy_(Napoleonic)" title="Kingdom of Italy (Napoleonic)">Kingdom of Italy</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Frankfurt" title="Grand Duchy of Frankfurt">Grand Duchy of Frankfurt</a>. Despite the promise of an independent principality inserted into the final treaty, the <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_Vienna" title="Congress of Vienna">Congress of Vienna</a> adjourned without creating a state for Eugène, so Auguste and his siblings had no inheritance. To the empty Leuchtenberg ducal title had been added the estate of <a href="/wiki/Eichst%C3%A4tt" title="Eichstätt">Eichstätt</a> in <a href="/wiki/Dowry" title="Dowry">dowry</a>, made a nominal principality, also by King Maximilian. Eugène's eldest son Auguste was heir to this modest property, which he inherited when Eugène died on 21 February 1824.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>On 4 February 1831 Leuchtenberg was one of three candidates for the throne of the newly independent <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>, his Napoleonic connections allaying the concerns of some of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Great_Powers" title="Great Powers">Great Powers</a> worried that the breakaway Roman Catholic realm might otherwise ally itself too closely with the likewise Catholic and revolutionary "bourgeois monarchy" of <a href="/wiki/House_of_Orl%C3%A9ans" title="House of Orléans">Orléans</a> France. But in the election by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Belgian_National_Congress" title="Belgian National Congress">Belgian National Congress</a>, Auguste came in second after the younger son of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Louis_Philippe,_King_of_the_French" title="Louis Philippe, King of the French">King of the French</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prince_Louis,_Duke_of_Nemours" title="Prince Louis, Duke of Nemours">Prince Louis, Duke of Nemours</a>, though ahead of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Habsburg" title="House of Habsburg">Habsburg</a> candidate, <a href="/wiki/Archduke_Charles,_Duke_of_Teschen" title="Archduke Charles, Duke of Teschen">Archduke Charles, Duke of Teschen</a>. In the event, none of these men attained the Belgian throne, which went to Britain's candidate, <a href="/wiki/Leopold_I_of_Belgium" title="Leopold I of Belgium">Prince Leopold of Saxe-Coburg</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>He escorted his sister <a href="/wiki/Am%C3%A9lie_of_Leuchtenberg" title="Amélie of Leuchtenberg">Amélie</a> to Brazil for her marriage to Emperor Pedro I and was created by his new brother-in-law, <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Santa_Cruz" title="Duke of Santa Cruz">Duke of Santa Cruz</a> on 5 November 1829.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Prince_consort_of_Portugal">Prince consort of Portugal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Auguste,_Duke_of_Leuchtenberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Prince consort of Portugal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Blason_d%27Auguste_de_Leuchtenberg.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1134" data-file-width="979" decoding="async" height="255" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Blason_d%27Auguste_de_Leuchtenberg.svg/220px-Blason_d%27Auguste_de_Leuchtenberg.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Blason_d%27Auguste_de_Leuchtenberg.svg/330px-Blason_d%27Auguste_de_Leuchtenberg.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Blason_d%27Auguste_de_Leuchtenberg.svg/440px-Blason_d%27Auguste_de_Leuchtenberg.svg.png 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Blason_d%27Auguste_de_Leuchtenberg.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Coat of Arms of Auguste.</div></div></div>
<p>On 26 May 1834, young Queen <a href="/wiki/Maria_II_of_Portugal" title="Maria II of Portugal">Maria II of Portugal</a> was restored to the throne of Portugal, gifted to her by the abdication – and subsequent conquest in war – of her father, Emperor Pedro I of Brazil, who had to do battle against the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Usurpation" title="Usurpation">usurpation</a> of his rebellious younger brother, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miguel_of_Portugal" title="Miguel of Portugal">Dom Miguel</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>Maria's childhood betrothal to Dom Miguel was broken so that a more pliant husband could be found to beget a new Portuguese dynasty, one whose loyalty might prove more trustworthy if he had no other prospects, such that he would be entirely beholden for his dynastic fortune to Portugal's constitutional regime. The Queen obligingly settled on Auguste de Beauharnais who, once again, proved unthreatening to the Great Powers because of his lack of membership in an already reigning dynasty and lack of conflicting foreign obligations or ambitions. He was also the eldest brother of Maria's stepmother Empress Amélie, her late father's second wife.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>Auguste and Maria II were married by proxy in <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a> on 1 December 1834. The groom was almost twenty-four years old and the bride fifteen years old. On his wedding day his bride conferred upon him the Portuguese style of "His Royal Highness The Prince Consort of Portugal".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>He arrived in Portugal shortly thereafter, and the couple were wed in person in Lisbon on 26 January 1835. However Auguste fell ill and died only two months later.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>Childless at the time of his death, Auguste left as heir in Bavaria his younger brother, who became the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maximilian,_Duke_of_Leuchtenberg" title="Maximilian, Duke of Leuchtenberg">3rd Duke of Leuchtenberg</a>, and briefly Auguste's successor in ownership of Eichstätt which, however, he returned to the Bavarian king in 1855 upon deciding to make his home in Russia, the realm of his own father-in-law.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>A year later Maria II married <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_II_of_Portugal" title="Ferdinand II of Portugal">Ferdinand of Saxe-Coburg-Gotha</a>, a nephew of the Coburg prince who had beat out her first husband in competition for the constitutional crown of Belgium.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>Because Auguste died before fathering an heir to the Portuguese throne, he never became Maria's co-monarch, which Maria's next husband did in 1837, becoming founder of the Coburg-Braganza dynasty.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
