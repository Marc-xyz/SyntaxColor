	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Gino Vinicio Gentili</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>

		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>
<b>Gino Vinicio Gentili</b> (<a href="/wiki/Osimo" title="Osimo">Osimo</a>, 27 September 1914 – <a href="/wiki/Bologna" title="Bologna">Bologna</a>, 29 July 2006) was an Italian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archaeologist" title="Archaeologist">archaeologist</a>.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:232px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Prof._Gino_Vinicio_Gentili.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="448" data-file-width="336" decoding="async" height="307" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Prof._Gino_Vinicio_Gentili.jpg/230px-Prof._Gino_Vinicio_Gentili.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/f1/Prof._Gino_Vinicio_Gentili.jpg 1.5x" width="230"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Prof._Gino_Vinicio_Gentili.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Gino Vinicio Gentili.</div></div></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gino_Vinicio_Gentili&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He earned his degree in Fine Arts and Letters at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_Rome_La_Sapienza" title="University of Rome La Sapienza">University of Rome La Sapienza</a> and graduated in Archaeology at the Italian School of Archaeology in <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>. He earned a Doctorate in Archaeology and in History of <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greek</a> and Roman arts, first at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Catania" title="University of Catania">University of Catania</a> and later at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Bologna" title="University of Bologna">University of Bologna</a>, where he taught <a href="/wiki/Etruscology" title="Etruscology">Etruscology</a> and ancient Italy's art.
</p><p>In 1945 he was elected mayor of Osimo. He  was also an official in the administration of the Antiquities and Fine Arts of the Ministry of Education and Chief Inspector and Superintendent of Antiquities for Eastern Sicily (1946 to 1963) and for <a href="/wiki/Emilia-Romagna" title="Emilia-Romagna">Emilia-Romagna</a>.
In 1973 he was awarded the Gold Medal of Civic Merit by the municipality of Osimo "for having paid tribute to the city with the brightest fame acquired in the field of archaeology, and especially for his valuable and irreplaceable contribution to the knowledge of the past in Osimo".
In 1979 he retired from his official position, but continued with his research.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Major_works_and_researches">Major works and researches</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gino_Vinicio_Gentili&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Major works and researches">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1959–60, Gino Vinicio Gentili excavated a mosaic on the floor of the room, later dubbed the "Chamber of the Ten Maidens", at the <a href="/wiki/Villa_Romana_del_Casale" title="Villa Romana del Casale">Villa Romana del Casale</a> near <a href="/wiki/Piazza_Armerina" title="Piazza Armerina">Piazza Armerina</a>. Informally called "the bikini girls", the maidens appear in a mosaic artwork which scholars named "Coronation of the Winner". The young women perform sports including weight-lifting, discus throwing, running and ball-games. A woman in a rich dress is depicted with a crown in her hand; one of the maidens holds a palm frond. The Villa in 1997 became a UNESCO <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">World Heritage Site</a>.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:232px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bikinimaedchen.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1600" data-file-width="1262" decoding="async" height="292" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Bikinimaedchen.jpg/230px-Bikinimaedchen.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Bikinimaedchen.jpg/345px-Bikinimaedchen.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Bikinimaedchen.jpg/460px-Bikinimaedchen.jpg 2x" width="230"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Bikinimaedchen.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Detail of the "<a href="/wiki/Bikini" title="Bikini">bikini</a> girls" mosaic.</div></div></div>
<p>Explorations and archaeological research conducted by Gentili include:
</p>
<ul><li>Identification of the great Ionic temple of  <a href="/wiki/Syracuse,_Sicily" title="Syracuse, Sicily">Syracuse</a></li>
<li>Researches in the Roman <a href="/wiki/Amphitheatre" title="Amphitheatre">amphitheatre</a> in Syracuse</li>
<li>Discovery of a small <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Byzantine" title="Byzantine">Byzantine</a> <a href="/wiki/Basilica" title="Basilica">basilica</a> at <a href="/wiki/Santa_Croce_Camerina" title="Santa Croce Camerina">Santa Croce Camerina</a></li>
<li>Exploration of archaic funerary monuments in Syracuse and recovery of the great statue of a "Mother" <a href="/wiki/Kourotrophos" title="Kourotrophos">Kourotrophos</a> in the site of <a href="/wiki/Megara_Hyblaea" title="Megara Hyblaea">Megara Hyblaea</a> at <a href="/wiki/Augusta,_Sicily" title="Augusta, Sicily">Augusta</a></li>
<li>Identification near <a href="/wiki/Mineo" title="Mineo">Mineo</a> of the ancient <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">Sicilian</a> center of Palikè</li>
<li>Early excavations at <a href="/wiki/Naxos" title="Naxos">Naxos</a>, the first Greek colony in Sicily, near <a href="/wiki/Messina" title="Messina">Messina</a></li>
<li>Discovery of a Roman villa of the early <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman Empire</a> in San Biagio di <a href="/wiki/Castroreale" title="Castroreale">Castroreale</a> Bagni</li>
<li>Researches in the <a href="/wiki/Etruscan_civilization" title="Etruscan civilization">Etruscan</a> town of <a href="/wiki/Marzabotto" title="Marzabotto">Marzabotto</a></li>
<li>Identification of the Etruscan town <a href="/wiki/Spina" title="Spina">Spina</a> in the province of <a href="/wiki/Ferrara" title="Ferrara">Ferrara</a></li>
<li>Discovery of valuable Roman mosaics in <a href="/wiki/Faenza" title="Faenza">Faenza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sarsina" title="Sarsina">Sarsina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rimini" title="Rimini">Rimini</a> and of remains of Byzantine basilicas in <a href="/wiki/Ravenna" title="Ravenna">Ravenna</a>.</li>
<li>A series of excavations of prehistoric culture in <a href="/wiki/Verucchio" title="Verucchio">Verucchio</a>, recovering burial objects of <a href="/wiki/Villanovan_culture" title="Villanovan culture">Villanovan culture</a> (8th–7th century B.C.) and others from the <a href="/wiki/Sabellians" title="Sabellians">Sabellians</a> (5th–4th century circa B.C.). He brought to light an exceptionally well-preserved artifact, the "throne" with scenes relating to the processing of wool, along with finely carved wooden items, now in the Museo Civico Archeologico di <a href="/wiki/Bologna" title="Bologna">Bologna</a>.</li></ul>
<p>Gino Vinicio Gentili is the author of essays about his researches at Piazza Armerina, Verucchio, Osimo.
</p>
