<p><b>Xolane Howard Mnisi</b> (born 13 July 1989) is a South African <a href="/wiki/Rugby_union" title="Rugby union">rugby union</a> player for the  <a href="/wiki/Southern_Kings" title="Southern Kings">Southern Kings</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Pro14" title="Pro14">Pro14</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SARU_Player_Profile_Howard_Mnisi_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-SARU_Player_Profile_Howard_Mnisi-1">[1]</a></sup> His regular position is centre.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Howard_Mnisi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Youth">Youth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Howard_Mnisi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Youth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He represented the  <a href="/wiki/Pumas_(rugby_team)" title="Pumas (rugby team)">Pumas</a> at the 2006 and 2007 Under–18 <a href="/wiki/Craven_Week" title="Craven Week">Craven Week</a> tournaments, which earned him a place in the  <a href="/wiki/Sharks_(Currie_Cup)" title="Sharks (Currie Cup)">Sharks</a> academy. He played for their Under–19 team in 2008 and their Under–21 team in 2010.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sharks">Sharks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Howard_Mnisi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sharks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2011, he was included in the <a href="/wiki/2011_Vodacom_Cup" title="2011 Vodacom Cup">2011 Vodacom Cup</a> squad and made his debut against the  <a href="/wiki/Welwitschias" title="Welwitschias">Welwitschias</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Welwitschias_5-25_Sharks_XV_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Welwitschias_5-25_Sharks_XV-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="NMMU_Madibaz">NMMU Madibaz</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Howard_Mnisi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: NMMU Madibaz">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>However, he failed to break into the <a href="/wiki/Currie_Cup" title="Currie Cup">Currie Cup</a> team and joined <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Varsity_Cup" title="Varsity Cup">Varsity Cup</a> side  <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Mandela_University#Sports" title="Nelson Mandela University">NMMU Madibaz</a> instead. He was included in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_African_Universities_rugby_union_team" title="South African Universities rugby union team">South Africa Students</a> team in 2012 and 2013<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SA_Universities_squad_named_to_face_Namibia_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-SA_Universities_squad_named_to_face_Namibia-3">[3]</a></sup> and he was also voted as the "Back That Rocks" for the <a href="/wiki/2013_Varsity_Cup" title="2013 Varsity Cup">2013 Varsity Cup</a> tournament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Players_that_Rock_2013_Winners_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Players_that_Rock_2013_Winners-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Griquas">Griquas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Howard_Mnisi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Griquas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He had a trial with the  <a href="/wiki/Free_State_Cheetahs" title="Free State Cheetahs">Free State Cheetahs</a> in 2013,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rhule_in_bulstryd_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rhule_in_bulstryd-5">[5]</a></sup> but joined the  <a href="/wiki/Griquas_(rugby)" title="Griquas (rugby)">Griquas</a> instead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Madiba_'n_Griekwa_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Madiba_'n_Griekwa-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Golden_Lions">Golden Lions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Howard_Mnisi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Golden Lions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He moved to <a href="/wiki/Johannesburg" title="Johannesburg">Johannesburg</a> to join the  <a href="/wiki/Golden_Lions" title="Golden Lions">Golden Lions</a> prior to the <a href="/wiki/2014_Currie_Cup_Premier_Division" title="2014 Currie Cup Premier Division">2014 Currie Cup Premier Division</a> season. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="South_Africa_.27A.27"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="South_Africa_'A'">South Africa 'A'</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Howard_Mnisi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: South Africa 'A'">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2016, Mnisi was included in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Africa_%27A%27_national_rugby_union_team" title="South Africa 'A' national rugby union team">South Africa 'A'</a> squad that played a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2016_mid-year_rugby_union_internationals" title="2016 mid-year rugby union internationals">two-match series</a> against a touring <a href="/wiki/England_Saxons" title="England Saxons">England Saxons</a> team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ackermann_to_coach_SA_‘A’_against_Saxons_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ackermann_to_coach_SA_‘A’_against_Saxons-8">[8]</a></sup> He was named in the starting line-up for their first match in <a href="/wiki/Bloemfontein" title="Bloemfontein">Bloemfontein</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Youth_and_experience_for_SA_‘A’_opener_against_Saxons_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Youth_and_experience_for_SA_‘A’_opener_against_Saxons-9">[9]</a></sup> but ended on the losing side as the visitors ran out 32–24 winners.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-South_Africa_A_24-32_England_Saxons_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-South_Africa_A_24-32_England_Saxons-10">[10]</a></sup> He was named on the bench for the second match of the series, coming on as a second-half replacement in a 26–29 defeat to the Saxons in <a href="/wiki/George,_Western_Cape" title="George, Western Cape">George</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-South_Africa_A_26-29_England_Saxons_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-South_Africa_A_26-29_England_Saxons-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>
