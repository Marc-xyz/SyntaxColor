	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Djurgårdens IF Fotboll in European football</h1>

		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><table class="infobox vcard" style="width:22em"><caption class="fn org">Djurgårdens IF in European football</caption><tbody><tr><th scope="row" style="white-space: nowrap; text-align:left">Club</th><td><a href="/wiki/Djurg%C3%A5rdens_IF_Fotboll" title="Djurgårdens IF Fotboll">Djurgårdens IF</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" style="white-space: nowrap; text-align:left">First entry</th><td><a href="/wiki/1955%E2%80%9356_European_Cup" title="1955–56 European Cup">1955–56 European Cup</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" style="white-space: nowrap; text-align:left">Latest entry</th><td><a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2018–19 UEFA Europa League">2018–19 UEFA Europa League</a></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p><a href="/wiki/Djurg%C3%A5rdens_IF_Fotboll" title="Djurgårdens IF Fotboll">Djurgårdens Idrottsförening</a>, also known simply as Djurgårdens IF, is a Swedish professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football club</a> based in <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a>. The club have participated in 17 editions of the club competitions governed by <a href="/wiki/UEFA" title="UEFA">UEFA</a>, the chief authority for football across Europe. These include six seasons in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">European Cup and Champions League</a>, seven seasons in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Cup and Europa League</a>, three seasons in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Cup_Winners%27_Cup" title="UEFA Cup Winners' Cup">Cup Winners' Cup</a> and one season in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Intertoto_Cup" title="UEFA Intertoto Cup">Intertoto Cup</a>. Counting all of the 50 games the side have played in UEFA competitions since their first entry into the European Cup in the <a href="/wiki/1955%E2%80%9356_European_Cup" title="1955–56 European Cup">1955–56</a> season, the team's record stands at 15 wins, 15 draws and 20 defeats.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[a]</a></sup> 
</p><p>Djurgården's 8–0 victory over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apollon_Limassol" title="Apollon Limassol">Apollon Limassol</a> of Cyprus in the <a href="/wiki/1996_UEFA_Intertoto_Cup" title="1996 UEFA Intertoto Cup">1996 UEFA Intertoto Cup</a> is the club's most decisive win in European competitions, while the team's heaviest defeat is 6–0, against Bulgarian club <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Levski_Sofia" title="Levski Sofia">Levski Sofia</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1965%E2%80%9366_European_Cup" title="1965–66 European Cup">1965–66 European Cup</a>. With 15 caps, <a href="/wiki/Toni_Kuivasto" title="Toni Kuivasto">Toni Kuivasto</a> has appeared in the most UEFA matches for Malmö, while <a href="/wiki/Fredrik_Dahlstr%C3%B6m" title="Fredrik Dahlström">Fredrik Dahlström</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kaj_Eskelinen" title="Kaj Eskelinen">Kaj Eskelinen</a> has scored the most goals with 5.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stats_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-stats-2">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
