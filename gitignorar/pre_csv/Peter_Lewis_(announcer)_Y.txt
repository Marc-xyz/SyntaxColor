	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Peter Lewis (announcer)</h1>
	
	<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
		<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>

		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Peter Lewis</b> is a former <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> television continuity announcer. He was the first person to broadcast from <a href="/wiki/London_Weekend_Television" title="London Weekend Television">London Weekend Television</a>'s new centre on the south bank of the <a href="/wiki/River_Thames" title="River Thames">River Thames</a>, when it opened in 1971. He was promoted to become senior announcer in 1977 and stayed in this role until 1997 when he left the station to pursue a management consultancy career in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Peter_Lewis_(announcer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lewis was educated at school in his native city of <a href="/wiki/Cardiff" title="Cardiff">Cardiff</a> in Wales. Before his career had begun, his family were already well known in Wales and the West of England. His father, Bruce Lewis, had already been a highly popular presenter for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_Wales_and_West" title="Television Wales and West">Television Wales and West</a> (TWW), where Peter Lewis began announcing during the mid to late 1960s. During this period, he became one of the youngest people on British television to present his own programme - it was <i>Movie Magazine</i> (the precursor to <i>Cinema</i> and <i>Film Night</i>) - an original idea devised by his father Bruce and Peter Duval.
</p><p>Lewis joined <a href="/wiki/London_Weekend_Television" title="London Weekend Television">London Weekend Television</a> during its opening month in August 1968 and around the same time, freelanced for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Television" title="Yorkshire Television">Yorkshire Television</a>. Later, he chose to concentrate on his work for LWT, becoming the first person to broadcast from the company's then-new headquarters on the south bank of the River Thames in 1971.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1974, Lewis helped to launch the first independent commercial radio station in the North East of England - <a href="/wiki/Metro_Radio" title="Metro Radio">Metro Radio</a>. Lewis was the station's first programming controller while his father was the first managing director and wrote the original proposal to the <a href="/wiki/Independent_Broadcasting_Authority" title="Independent Broadcasting Authority">IBA</a> for Metro and later for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GWR_Group" title="GWR Group">GWR</a> in Swindon.
</p><p>By 1977, Lewis had been made chief announcer with LWT - a role which he remained in for 20 years until leaving the station in 1997. He returned to the station briefly in 1998 to record continuity links for the 30th anniversary weekend.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>During his time at LWT, Lewis was a regular newsreader for <i>London News Headlines</i> (later <i>LWT News Headlines</i>) between 1982 and 1988. He also announced for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HTV_West" title="HTV West">HTV West</a> on weekdays. 
</p><p>Lewis then moved to Tampa, Florida after a brief stint in Houston, Texas. He now runs a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Management_consultancy" title="Management consultancy">management consultancy</a> company in <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>.
</p>
