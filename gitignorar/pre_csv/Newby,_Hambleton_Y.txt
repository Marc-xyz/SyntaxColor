<p><b>Newby</b> is a village and <a href="/wiki/Civil_parish" title="Civil parish">civil parish</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Hambleton_District" title="Hambleton District">Hambleton</a> district of <a href="/wiki/North_Yorkshire" title="North Yorkshire">North Yorkshire</a>, England. Close to the border with the borough of <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough" title="Middlesbrough">Middlesbrough</a>
and 7 miles (11 km) from the centre of <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough" title="Middlesbrough">Middlesbrough</a>. Newby is located 19 miles (31 km) from <a href="/wiki/North_York_Moors" title="North York Moors">North York Moors</a>, which became a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Park" title="National Park">National Park</a> in 1952.
According to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Census_2011" title="United Kingdom Census 2011">United Kingdom Census 2011</a> Newby had a total population of 214.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2011_census_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-2011_census-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Newby,_Hambleton&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Newby_population_time_series_1881-2011.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="282" data-file-width="478" decoding="async" height="130" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/Newby_population_time_series_1881-2011.png/220px-Newby_population_time_series_1881-2011.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/Newby_population_time_series_1881-2011.png/330px-Newby_population_time_series_1881-2011.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/Newby_population_time_series_1881-2011.png/440px-Newby_population_time_series_1881-2011.png 2x" width="220"/></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Newby_population_time_series_1881-2011.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Timescale showing population of Newby 1881–2011</div></div></div><p> 
In 1870 <a href="/wiki/John_Marius_Wilson" title="John Marius Wilson">John Marius Wilson</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Gazetteer_of_England_and_Wales" title="Imperial Gazetteer of England and Wales">Imperial Gazetteer of England and Wales</a> described Newby as: </p><blockquote><p>"Newby, a township in Stokesley parish, N. R. Yorkshire; 2½ miles N of Stokesley. Acres, 1, 211. Real property, £1, 464. Pop., 129. Houses, 29. There is a Wesleyan chapel; and there was formerly a slightly-endowed school."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> 
</p></blockquote>
<p>Census data since 1881 can help to show how Newby has changed throughout the years. The main occupation for residents in 1881 was <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agriculture</a> with 24 males and 1 female employed in that sector. Another main employment sector in 1881 was domestic services with 6 females working in that sector at the time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In 1881 the population of Newby was 115 and the total number of houses was 29.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> This has increased since 1881 with the total number of houses significantly increasing between 1931 and 1951 from 22 to 45.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>The population of Newby has fluctuated between 109 and 214 people between 1881 and 2011. The population of Newby in the 20th Century hit a peak of 183 in 1961.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>  The population of Newby in 2011 is 214 and covers a wide range of age groups.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Listed_buildings">Listed buildings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Newby,_Hambleton&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Listed buildings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Wesleyan_Methodist_Church_(Great_Britain)" title="Wesleyan Methodist Church (Great Britain)">Wesleyan Methodist Church</a> in Newby was first built in 1826 and is a grade II <a href="/wiki/Listed_building" title="Listed building">Listed building</a>, after it was listed on 4 August 1983.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> This is not the only listed building in Newby, there are seven others. These include Antelope Lodge, Long Farm House, The Haven and Newby Grange. All of the <a href="/wiki/Listed_building" title="Listed building">Listed buildings</a> within Newby are listed at a grade II level and were added to the British listed buildings catalogue in 1983.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Newby,_Hambleton&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The village is on the B1365 and is 2 miles (3.2 km) from the A172. It is also served by the 83 route to Stokesley.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Nunthorpe_railway_station" title="Nunthorpe railway station">Nunthorpe railway station</a> is the located 3 miles (4.8 km) east of the village, with frequent trains running along the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Esk_Valley_Line" title="Esk Valley Line">Esk Valley Line</a>.
</p>
