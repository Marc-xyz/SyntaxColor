<p><b>Gary Bellamy</b> (born 4 July 1962) is an English former professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a>. He began his career at <a href="/wiki/Chesterfield_F.C." title="Chesterfield F.C.">Chesterfield</a>, making over 150 appearances for the club before joining <a href="/wiki/Wolverhampton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.">Wolverhampton Wanderers</a> in 1987. He helped the club to back-to-back promotions from <a href="/wiki/Football_League_Fourth_Division" title="Football League Fourth Division">Division Four</a> to <a href="/wiki/Football_League_Second_Division" title="Football League Second Division">Division Two</a>, as well as winning the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Trophy" title="Football League Trophy">Football League Trophy</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1988_Football_League_Trophy_Final" title="1988 Football League Trophy Final">1988</a>. He had a brief spell on loan at <a href="/wiki/Cardiff_City_F.C." title="Cardiff City F.C.">Cardiff City</a> in 1982 before moving to <a href="/wiki/Leyton_Orient_F.C." title="Leyton Orient F.C.">Leyton Orient</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Bellamy later joined <a href="/wiki/Chelmsford_City_F.C." title="Chelmsford City F.C.">Chelmsford City</a> as player-manager and also managed <a href="/wiki/Dover_Athletic_F.C." title="Dover Athletic F.C.">Dover Athletic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Braintree_Town_F.C." title="Braintree Town F.C.">Braintree Town</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gary_Bellamy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chesterfield">Chesterfield</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gary_Bellamy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Chesterfield">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bellamy had a spell at <a href="/wiki/Nottinghamshire_County_Cricket_Club" title="Nottinghamshire County Cricket Club">Nottinghamshire County Cricket Club</a> as a teenager, but decided to pursue a career in football instead, joining <a href="/wiki/Chesterfield_F.C." title="Chesterfield F.C.">Chesterfield</a> as a trainee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chesterfieldhistory_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Chesterfieldhistory-3">[3]</a></sup> He broke into the first team and played seven seasons at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Recreation_Ground_(Chesterfield)" title="Recreation Ground (Chesterfield)">Saltergate</a>. He won the <a href="/wiki/Football_League_Fourth_Division" title="Football League Fourth Division">(old) Fourth Division championship</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1984-85_in_English_football#Fourth_Division_2" title="1984-85 in English football">1984–85</a> with the club and made a total of 207 appearances in all competitions for the Spirites, despite missing 15 months through injury. Following his return from injury, Bellamy was set to move to <a href="/wiki/Birmingham_City_F.C." title="Birmingham City F.C.">Birmingham City</a> but the transfer collapsed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Interview_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Interview-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wolverhampton_Wanderers">Wolverhampton Wanderers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gary_Bellamy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Wolverhampton Wanderers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He left the club in July 1987 after becoming embroiled in a contract dispute to join then-fourth tier <a href="/wiki/Wolverhampton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.">Wolverhampton Wanderers</a> for a £17,500 fee set at tribunal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chesterfieldhistory_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Chesterfieldhistory-3">[3]</a></sup> After making his debut for the club on 12 September 1987 in a 2–2 draw with <a href="/wiki/Crewe_Alexandra_F.C." title="Crewe Alexandra F.C.">Crewe Alexandra</a>, he won the Fourth Division championship for the second time in his career. The defender also got to experience a <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium_(1923)" title="Wembley Stadium (1923)">Wembley</a> final as the club lifted the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Football_League_Trophy" title="Football League Trophy">Football League Trophy</a> at the end of the season too, defeating <a href="/wiki/Burnley_F.C." title="Burnley F.C.">Burnley</a> 2–0 in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1988_Football_League_Trophy_Final" title="1988 Football League Trophy Final">final</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Interview_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Interview-1">[1]</a></sup> His second season with Wolves saw him add another medal as the club immediately won the <a href="/wiki/Football_League_Third_Division" title="Football League Third Division">(old) Third Division</a>. He remained a first choice player in his first season at the second flight level in 1989–90 but subsequently was used only sparingly in the following two seasons.
</p><p>At the start of the 1991–92 season, Bellamy contracted <a href="/wiki/Lyme_disease" title="Lyme disease">Lyme disease</a> on a pre-season tour of <a href="/wiki/Scandinavia" title="Scandinavia">Scandinavia</a> which kept him out for the opening weeks of the season and he was unable to force his way back into the side on his return.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Interview_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Interview-1">[1]</a></sup> He joined <a href="/wiki/Cardiff_City_F.C." title="Cardiff City F.C.">Cardiff City</a> on loan in a bid to regain fitness toward the end of the season, making nine league appearances for the <i>Bluebirds</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_career">Later career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gary_Bellamy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Later career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The following season, Bellamy was sold to Third Division <a href="/wiki/Leyton_Orient_F.C." title="Leyton Orient F.C.">Leyton Orient</a> for £30,000 in September 1992. Here, he played as a first choice player for four seasons, despite continued friction with then manager <a href="/wiki/Peter_Eustace" title="Peter Eustace">Peter Eustace</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Interview_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-Interview-1">[1]</a></sup> before leaving for non-league <a href="/wiki/Chelmsford_City_F.C." title="Chelmsford City F.C.">Chelmsford City</a> in June 1996. Just five months later, he took up a joint manager role for the club and spent five years in this capacity, winning promotion to the <a href="/wiki/Isthmian_League" title="Isthmian League">Isthmian League</a> Premier Division in 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chesterfieldhistory_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Chesterfieldhistory-3">[3]</a></sup> He had earlier guided them to promotion in 1998, but their ground was deemed not to meet the requirements of the higher division at that time.
</p><p>This promotion success alerted <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conference_National" title="Conference National">Conference</a> side <a href="/wiki/Dover_Athletic_F.C." title="Dover Athletic F.C.">Dover Athletic</a> to him and he took up the reins there following this in June 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> However, he was sacked only months later in November as the club struggled. He became manager of <a href="/wiki/Braintree_Town_F.C." title="Braintree Town F.C.">Braintree Town</a>, where he had had a loan spell as a player, but was later fired in November 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chesterfieldhistory_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-Chesterfieldhistory-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
