		<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">This is a <a href="/wiki/Chinese_name" title="Chinese name">Chinese name</a>; the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_surname" title="Chinese surname">family name</a> is <a href="/wiki/Wu_(surname)" title="Wu (surname)">Wu</a>.</div>
<p><b>Wu Ting-yu</b> (<a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hant">吳庭毓</span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Taiwanese_people" title="Taiwanese people">Taiwanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Violin" title="Violin">violinist</a>. He currently serves as the <a href="/wiki/Concertmaster" title="Concertmaster">concertmaster</a> in Taiwan's leading <a href="/wiki/National_Symphony_Orchestra_(Taiwan)" title="National Symphony Orchestra (Taiwan)">National Symphony Orchestra</a>.
</p><p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Kaohsiung" title="Kaohsiung">Kaohsiung</a>, Wu began to study violin at age of 5 with his father, Wu Lian-jing, a prestigious painter and violinist. Since then, he had studied with some of <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>'s most renowned violinists. In 1982, Wu graduated with the highest honor from the National Academy of Arts (later renamed as <a href="/wiki/National_Taiwan_University_of_Arts" title="National Taiwan University of Arts">National Taiwan University of Arts</a>). He later served as the concertmaster of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=National_Defense_Symphony_Orchestra&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="National Defense Symphony Orchestra (page does not exist)">National Defense Symphony Orchestra</a> and <a href="/wiki/Taipei_Symphony_Orchestra" title="Taipei Symphony Orchestra">Taipei Symphony Orchestra</a>. In 1988, he entered the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ville_de_Rueil-Malmaison_Conservatoire&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ville de Rueil-Malmaison Conservatoire (page does not exist)">Ville de Rueil-Malmaison Conservatoire</a> in <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> under the guidance of Prof. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Melle_Brignon&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Melle Brignon (page does not exist)">Melle Brignon</a>. Upon returning to Taiwan, he was appointed as the concert master of National Symphony Orchestra. He has since then performed as a soloist with many orchestras throughout the world. In 2000, Wu was invited to perform at the presidential palace for the President of R.O.C. He was also invited by the French Consulate to perform at the Art Exhibition festival in Taipei. In 2002, he recorded, as the soloist, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vivaldi" title="Vivaldi">Vivaldi</a>'s <a href="/wiki/The_Four_Seasons_(Vivaldi)" title="The Four Seasons (Vivaldi)">The Four Seasons</a> with <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Philharmonic_Chamber_Orchestra_of_Moscow&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Philharmonic Chamber Orchestra of Moscow (page does not exist)">Philharmonic Chamber Orchestra of Moscow</a>. At the same time, Wu is committed to educate the young talents in Taiwan. He is currently an associate professor at <a href="/wiki/Fu_Jen_Catholic_University" title="Fu Jen Catholic University">Fu Jen Catholic University</a>, <a href="/wiki/National_Chiao_Tung_University" title="National Chiao Tung University">National Chiao Tung University</a>, and National Taiwan University of Arts.
</p>
