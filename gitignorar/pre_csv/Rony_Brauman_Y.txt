	<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Rony Brauman</h1>

<p><b>Rony Brauman</b> (born June 19, 1950, in <a href="/wiki/Jerusalem" title="Jerusalem">Jerusalem</a>) is a French <a href="/wiki/Physician" title="Physician">physician</a> specializing in <a href="/wiki/Tropical_disease" title="Tropical disease">tropical diseases</a>. 
</p><p>He was one of the early members of <i><a href="/wiki/M%C3%A9decins_Sans_Fronti%C3%A8res" title="Médecins Sans Frontières">Médecins sans frontières</a></i> (Doctors without Borders), and was its president from 1982 to 1994. He was a professor at the <i><a href="/wiki/Sciences_Po" title="Sciences Po">Institut d'études politiques de Paris</a></i> (<i>Sciences po</i>) from 1994 to 1997 and is now scientific advisor in the <a href="/wiki/Paris_School_of_International_Affairs" title="Paris School of International Affairs">school of international affairs of <i>Sciences po</i></a>. 
</p><p>With <a href="/wiki/Israelis" title="Israelis">Israeli</a> director <a href="/wiki/Eyal_Sivan" title="Eyal Sivan">Eyal Sivan</a>, his cousin, he co-directed a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Documentary" title="Documentary">documentary</a> (1999) on the trial of <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Eichmann" title="Adolf Eichmann">Adolf Eichmann</a> (1961) based on <a href="/wiki/Hannah_Arendt" title="Hannah Arendt">Hannah Arendt</a>'s 1963 book <i><a href="/wiki/Eichmann_in_Jerusalem" title="Eichmann in Jerusalem">Eichmann in Jerusalem</a></i>.
</p><p>Brauman is also Director of the <a class="external text" href="http://www.hcri.manchester.ac.uk" rel="nofollow"></a><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Humanitarian_and_Conflict_Response_Institute&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Humanitarian and Conflict Response Institute (page does not exist)">Humanitarian and Conflict Response Institute</a> (HCRI) at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Manchester" title="University of Manchester">University of Manchester</a>.
</p>
