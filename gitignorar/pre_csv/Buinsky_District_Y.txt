<p><b>Buinsky District</b> (<a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru">Буинский райо́н</span>; <a href="/wiki/Tatar_language" title="Tatar language">Tatar</a>: <span lang="tt" title="Tatar language text">Буа районы</span>, <i>Bua rayonı</i>) is an administrative<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Constitution_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Constitution-1">[1]</a></sup> and municipal<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RTatarstanBuinskyD_mun_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-RTatarstanBuinskyD_mun-6">[6]</a></sup> district (<a href="/wiki/Raion" title="Raion">raion</a>), one of the <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_the_Republic_of_Tatarstan" title="Administrative divisions of the Republic of Tatarstan">forty-three</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Tatarstan" title="Republic of Tatarstan">Republic of Tatarstan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>. It is located in the southwest of the republic. The area of the district is 1,543 square kilometers (596 sq mi).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-about_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-about-3">[3]</a></sup> Its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a> is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Types_of_inhabited_localities_in_Russia" title="Types of inhabited localities in Russia">town</a> of <a href="/wiki/Buinsk,_Republic_of_Tatarstan" title="Buinsk, Republic of Tatarstan">Buinsk</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RTatarstan_admlist_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-RTatarstan_admlist-2">[2]</a></sup> (which is not administratively a part of the district).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RTatarstan_admlaw_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-RTatarstan_admlaw-8">[8]</a></sup> Population: <span class="nowrap">25,101 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2010)" title="Russian Census (2010)">2010 Census</a>)</span>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2010Census_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-2010Census-4">[4]</a></sup> 27,800 (<a href="/wiki/Russian_Census_(2002)" title="Russian Census (2002)">2002 Census</a>);<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2002Census_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-2002Census-9">[9]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap">46,664 (<a href="/wiki/Soviet_Census_(1989)" title="Soviet Census (1989)">1989 Census</a>)</span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census1989_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Census1989-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_and_municipal_status">Administrative and municipal status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buinsky_District&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Administrative and municipal status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Within the <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Administrative_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">framework of administrative divisions</a>, Buinsky District is one of the <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_the_Republic_of_Tatarstan" title="Administrative divisions of the Republic of Tatarstan">forty-three</a> in the republic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RTatarstan_admlist_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-RTatarstan_admlist-2">[2]</a></sup> The town of <a href="/wiki/Buinsk,_Republic_of_Tatarstan" title="Buinsk, Republic of Tatarstan">Buinsk</a> serves as its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Administrative_center" title="Administrative center">administrative center</a>, despite being incorporated separately as a <a href="/wiki/City_of_federal_subject_significance" title="City of federal subject significance">town of republic significance</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RTatarstan_admlist_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-RTatarstan_admlist-2">[2]</a></sup>—an administrative unit with the status equal to that of the districts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RTatarstan_admlaw_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-RTatarstan_admlaw-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p>As a <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Russia#Municipal_divisions" title="Subdivisions of Russia">municipal division</a>, the district is incorporated as <b>Buinsky Municipal District</b>, with the town of republic significance of Buinsk being incorporated within it as Buinsk Urban Settlement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RTatarstanBuinskyD_mun_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-RTatarstanBuinskyD_mun-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
